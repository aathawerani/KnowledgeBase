MINUTES OF 310TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON TUESDAY, AUGUST 26, 2014

The 310th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Tuesday, August 26, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at CEE, IBA City Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Hussain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FCS
3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
4. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

The following attended the meeting on special invitation:

 

1. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan Dean & Director
2. Dr. Nasir Afghan Associate Dean, FCS
3. Dr. Amber Gul Rashid Registrar
4. Mr. Imran of NBCL Director Finance

 

The following items were discussed:  

 

Before discussing the agenda item the Dean & Director thanked Mr. Zaffar A. Khan for finding time for the meeting.

 

Item No.1       To consider proposals on Performance Evaluation and KPIs.

 

1.1       The Dean & Director said that earlier we set objectives of all departments and along with that we prepared KPS. However, over the time it was realized that further differentiation was needed. For example, those which are purely service units should have KPIs to determine the satisfaction level of those whom they serve.  Although we have got AC Nielson survey for the last four years yet it is broad based.  He said that he thought that we should focus the survey of AC Nielson for those departments where we have to measure KPIs and the rest ought to be dropped.  For each department there should be different KPI depending upon their objectives; some will be done through evaluation and some will have quantitative measures. The vision, mission and objective of the institute and the objectives of the service units should be aligned together. He said that major units are admin department, which include general administration, facilities management, residence management, security, transport.  For security the evaluation will be different and not confined to students response only as compared to transport department. Along with the evaluation of the services, it will also be evaluated whether they have been able to control the cost, if the expenses have exceeded the budget or remained within the budget etc.  In that way we may design the objectives and KPIs because currently that is not reflected in the KPIs. The service units were asked to do cost saving which some of them did (reduction in electricity bills for example) but it is not reflected in the KPI. He said that it is very necessary that we set KPIs and objectives of every department and unit anew. Now we can link the survey results of AC Nielson with KPI and performance evaluation.

 

1.2       Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that generally what worked well that once a year every department manager was given goals and targets of which some were tangible and some were intangible. It used to be a formal presentation. Then they had to use judgment, selected those which were absolutely critical and important and then would discuss and disseminate the selected objectives and KPIs so that everybody knows it. Those KPIs  of various departments is submitted to the HOD and after discussion a pyramid is set. As far as measures are concerned, he said that there are many other things for which you have to use perception type feedback or 360 degree feedback. He hoped that that type of process is being followed at IBA. 

 

1.3       The Dean & Director said that we have done this in the context of the strategic plan for which we had given KPIs and reported to the Board after every six month.  Those KPIs were focused on physical infrastructure, IT, faculty development, and HR policies. Now we are in a different phase altogether. Now it is maintenance of existing facilities, academic excellence, development of managerial pool for which there has to be objectives and KPIs. There has to be synchronization among the objectives of the institution, of the department and of the units and all should be consistent. This is missing in this institution.  Everybody is working in silos. He said that he wants to get rid of that culture and foster team work. We have to have the structure which facilitates communication and flow of information so that there is a team work and synergy. 

 

1.4       Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that organizational structure has to be re-examined although there would not be a radical shift. The Dean & Director said that he is open to it.  Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that he would present it in the strategy paper.  The Dean & Director asked if we should wait for the strategy document 2020 or carry on. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested not to hold up and keep working along the line because there may be further discussion on the paper.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director asked Dr. Amber Gul Rashid as to where we are according to AACSB standard for internal organization. Dr. Amber Gul said that there are two or three standards; one of them is the relations among faculty, staff and administration and how they are supporting each other.  She said that in this area we need to focus on.  Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that we need to make sure that there is an interaction between the faculty and administration and work as a team as both have important roles to play.

 

1.6       The Dean & Director said that survey will not be done for every department. It will be done for only those departments whose KPIs are to be considered. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that one of the feedbacks that he has received is that faculty and admin are disconnected at IBA. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan asked if we hold town hall meetings. The Dean & Director said that he does hold meetings with the faculty but not with administration and faculty jointly which he will do.

 

1.7       Mr. Moeid Sultan, Dr. Nasir Afghan, and Mr. Imran Batada presented the KPIs of their respective departments. Dr. Nasir Afghan said that he has prepared the first draft in which he has divided program related, students, and faculty related KPIs.  He said that the idea was to align the KPI with our mission and objectives.

 

1.8       The Dean & Director advised Dr. Nasir Afghan to keep the few KPIs which are most important and more encompassing.  He further advised that KPIs have to be related to the variables upon which one has the control. He advised to start from ground zero and first discuss the KPIs with department staff; tell them what has been summarized and bring the final one to the EC for consideration and approval. 

 

1.9       Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that there is a lot of bench mark information available.  He said that there are critical questions and issues. He suggested to know how other comparable quality schools deal with such issues.  The Dean & Director said that Dr. Amber Gul has been working on it. Dr. Amber Gul said that they have done bench marking for courses but not for KPIs.   

 

1.10     The Dean & Director said that there are three building blocks for the revival of the institution.  These are: (i) systems and procedures which have to be totally transparent, (ii) HR quality which has to be excellent, and (iii) IT Technology information which is generated on time to make the decisions.

 

1.11     Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested that there should be different levels of appreciation starting from ‘well done’, formal letter of appreciation to lunch dinner which will help in change of culture.

 

1.12     The Dean & Director said that there is a communication gap on which we have to work.  He advised Mr. Adnan Hameed to arrange frequent meetings of faculty and administration to bring them together.

 

1.13     The Dean & Director said that as advised by Mr. Zaffar A. Khan, the EC members/HODs should hold brain storming session with the whole department and discuss as to what will be the objectives and KPIs of their departments.  Those who have prepared their KPIs (Finance, IT, HR, MBA Program) will further refine it. Dr. Amber Gul was advised to provide guidance to them. 

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.14     It was decided that each department will formulate their KPIs in light of the guidelines provided above and present them in the EC on Friday, September 26, 2014. The EC will review and finalize the objectives and KPIs of each department and when finalized it will be disseminated by the concerned HoD among their staff. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan would again be requested to attend this meeting.

 

1.15     The Committee thanked Mr. Zaffar A. Khan for his very valuable guidance.

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the additional work of face lifting of NBP      Technology Centre at Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the NBP Technology Centre project was awarded to M/s. Badar Engineering Works on March 31, 2014 at a cost of Rs.2,809,631/- The project commenced on April 26, 2014 and it was to be completed in six months time on October 26, 2014.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that they faced the following impediments / bottlenecks on timely completion of the project:

 

o   Work affected due to unforeseen problem in shifting of outer units of split air conditioners and some modification has been done to Architectural and Structural design.

 

o   Extraordinary time is being spent on shifting of old Air Conditioners. Because of being very old their functioning is giving problem, their cables and drain pipes were damaged. All holes in slabs Ground Floor and Roof have been done through core driller.

 

o   Due to existing site conditions two exterior walls (Northern side flanking wall and signage wall) locations have been changed and redesigned the structure.  One window had to be relocated.

 

2.3       The Director Projects reported that the Architects were requested to review the design of the northern elevation wall where there are maximum utilities services including outer units, sewer pipes, water supply pipes, electrical cables and manholes. Mr. Zayd Bilgrami visited the site on August 8, 2014 to review the situation and proposed some solution for which they have given the estimates of Rs.478,711/-.  The proposed solution is either to shift the outer unit on top or on the ground. 

 

2.4       The Director Projects reported that during the execution following works required to be done as per the site condition which was not taken care during the design and some modifications have been done to Architectural and Structural detail which was essentially required to complete the work. The cost of the following work is Rs.860,000/-:

 

·         Increase of thickness of base plaster to maintain level and plumb due to old building.

·         Chemical dowel to join new and old structure.

·         Dismantling of existing column on northern side.

·         CC pads for placing of outer units on roof.

·         Relocation of one window due to change of location of signage wall.

·         Termite proofing in foundations

·         Replacement of power and control cable of air-conditions due to damage of existing cable.

·         UPVC channels for running of copper pipe and cables in office area.

·         Shifting of five numbers outer units other than quantity mentioned in BOQ.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that in addition to above following more works required to be executed to complete the project for which the estimated cost is Rs.523,900/-:

 

·         Weather proof DB for air-conditions on roof.

·         Shifting of outer units from eastern and western side.

 

2.6       The Director Projects said that the total additional cost of above mentioned works (Rs.478,711 + Rs.860,000 + Rs.523,900)  is Rs.1.862 million which is 66.3% above the contract price.

 

2.7       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the total additional cost of Rs.1.862 million and two months extension in time till December 26, 2014 for completion of additional work and time lapse due to already executed additional works.

 

2.8       To a question the Director Projects reported that there are 48 ACs in the MP Building which are to be shifted either on the top or on the ground floor. The Dean & Director asked if the requirements of Mr. Imran Batada have been met.  The Director Projects said that the ACs of IT would not be shifted; that would remain on the ground floor and the outer units would also be there. The Dean & Director asked as to why we need 48 ACs to be shifted when the ACs at IT would remain in place. Moreover, the offices of H.R., Audit, EMBA will be shifted to the Admin Block when ready. The ACs of these offices need not be shifted; they would become redundant. He further said that out of Rs.478,711/- a sum of Rs.300,000/- is required for shifting of ACs.  If the number of ACs to be shifted is reduced, the cost would also come down.  He requested the Registrar to visit the MP Building along with the Director Projects and the Architect to find out how many ACs would be required after some of the offices are shifted to the Admin. Building.

 

2.9       For the work listed in 2.4 above, the Dean & Director observed that those are small work for which Rs.860,000 is too much.  He again requested the Registrar to see the details of expenses and give his report to the EC.  For the estimate of Rs.523,900 as detailed in 2.5 above he said that the cost depends upon the number of ACs required to be shifted.

 

2.10     The Dean & Director agreed that the sewage pipes have to be hidden by creating cavity wall at the northern side as proposed and that the ACs should be shifted to the top but the cost would be revised depending upon the need of ACs.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

2.11     The Committee requested the Registrar and the Director Projects to revisit the requirements of ACs in the MP Building after some of the offices are shifted to Admin. Building based on which the Director Projects would present the revised cost estimate to the EC.

 

2.12     The Committee allowed extension in time till December 26, 2014 for completion of additional work at MP Building.

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve the amendment in contract for special advance for   the renovation and refurbishment of existing work at Admin. Building at          Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that Renovation and Refurbishment of Existing Administration Building work was awarded to M/s Al-Shafi on February 13, 2012. The actual contract duration was 18 months from the date of commencement. The 1st extension was granted for six months and then the 2nd extension was granted for six months uptil September 15, 2014.  In order to further expedite the works and to facilitate the contractor financially 1st amendment in contract for payment of special advance of Rs.20 million was approved and then the 2nd amendment in contract for increasing the adhoc payment limit from 50% to 70% was approved by the IBA Management.

 

3.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that on August 5, 2014 during progress review meeting the contractor was given the deadline to complete all the works by September 30, 2014 as per the 3rd extension. The contractor showed his concern regarding the delay in last bill which was submitted to NESPAK on June 03, 2014 and the required documents on June 25, 2014. After verification at site the same was submitted by NESPAK on June 30, 2014 to IBA. The Projects Department after necessary process (Preparation of Measurement book) submitted the bill to Finance Department on July 21, 2014. IBA Finance Department released the payment to contractor on August 16, 2014. The whole process took 73 days for payment of running bills. The contractors requested to release another amount of Rs.40 million as an special advance against the insurance guarantee and on condition that previous special advance may be completely deducted from their upcoming bill and they will raise only one running bill before the final bill.  Their request was scrutinized by M/s NESPAK and advisor (Construction).  NESPAK sent a note of recommendation to IBA for release of Rs.40 million advance in order to expedite the works and to meet the deadline.

 

3.3       Based on the reasons for delayed payments which effected the progress of work and the recommendation of M/s NESPAK the case was presented in Procurement Committee to approve an amendment in contract for release of Rs.20 million in order to expedite the works and to meet the final deadline for completion of all works. The deduction of this advance shall be made from their pre-final bill.  The Director Projects stated that although the request from the Contractor and the recommendation of NESPAK was for Rs.40 million, he has recommended to release Rs.20 million to avoid any risk.

 

3.4       The Director Projects reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on August 23, 2014 recommended amendment in contract for Special Advance of Rs.20 million (against Insurance Bond) to M/s Al-Shafi on renovation and refurbishment of existing Admin Block, Main Campus, considering that the previous special advance of Rs.20 million is fully adjusted before this advance is released.

 

3.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the amendment in    contract for Special advance of Rs.20 million to M/s Al-Shafi based on the recommendations of Procurement Committee, Project Manager NESPAK and IBA Projects Department.

      

3.6       The Dean & Director said that the disbursements have been slow and as evidenced above it takes 73 days for us to process and release the funds to the contractors.  The natural  outcome will be a slow down in physical works due to cash flow shortages on part of the contractor.  We have to get the Admin. Building completed and commissioned on time and we have a large undisbursed amount due to the contractor with us. He said that as 50% of their bills against physical works already completed remain unpaid, there is no risk in giving Rs.40 million as advance against bank guarantee in order to get the work    completed on time. He further said that we work on evidence and logic. The evidence is that 50% of the contract value is with us.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee allowed amendment in contract for special advance of Rs.40 million         against insurance bond to M/s. Al Shafi.

 

3.8       The Committee requested the Director Projects to get the projects completed speedily.
