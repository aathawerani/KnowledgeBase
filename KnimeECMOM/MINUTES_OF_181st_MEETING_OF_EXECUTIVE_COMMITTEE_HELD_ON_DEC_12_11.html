3.2     The Director Projects informed the Committee that the submitted documents were sent to M/s KP Associates in order scrutinize and evaluate the contractors for Pre-qualification. M/s KP associates submitted their report and recommended the following six contractors for pre-qualification:

 

1.  Areva T & D (Pakistan)                         4.  Electric Services

2.  Gulf Constructors                                5.  Iqbal Engineering House

3.  MIMA Constructors                              6.  Siemens Pakistan               

 

3.3      The TEC in its meeting held on 30-11-2011 excluded Siemens Pakistan on the suggestion of Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects as he is not satisfied with Siemens performance and recommended the remaining five to be Pre-qualified as Electrical Contractor to CPC. The CPC in its meeting held on December 8, 2011 endorsed the recommendation of TEC. 

 

3.4       Based on the recommendation of CPC, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the Pre-qualification of five Contractors listed in 3.2 above excluding Siemens Pakistan. 

 

3.5       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to provide cogent reasons for not being satisfied with Siemens.

 

3.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that right from the very beginning Siemens attitude has been that of a bureaucratic organization, typical of a large corporation. He cited numerous instances where the action / response from Siemens was very slow and of a bureaucratic style. He said that in their (IBA-HEC Project Department) letter of June 2, 2011, addressed to all potential bidders, including Siemens, they clearly pointed out that “All bids must be unconditional and strictly conform to the terms and conditions given in the tender documents”. Despite that, Siemens included numerous additional technical and commercial terms and conditions of their own in their bid. It was for this reason that our consultant EA Consulting (Pvt.) Ltd. evaluated the Siemens bid as conditional and therefore non-responsive. However, for practical and economical reasons IBA accepted the Siemens bid, being the lowest in price, on their withdrawal of all additional terms and conditions except the two special conditions which they had insisted to be included in the contract. A Letter of Acceptance was issued to Siemens on August 20, 2011. They took as many as 10 days to sign and return the duplicate of the letter. In the Letter of Acceptance, Siemens was given 15 days to start the work. But they took more than two months to mobilize their resources and physically start the work at site.

 

3.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that since the start of the work on November 4, 2011, Siemens has been working at a snail’s pace, despite verbal and written exhortations by Project Manager NESPAK. While about 80% of the foundation work only at the generators’ site has been completed, at the sub-station site Siemens sub-contractor is still struggling to emerge out of the ground. Even the plinth is still incomplete.  He also presented the Minutes of the Weekly Progress Review Meetings’ which establishes that Siemens’ response to our project manager NESPAK’s instructions is not timely and fully responsive, which is the major reason for the delays so far.

 

3.8       Citing another example of the overall casual attitude of Siemens’ people toward completion of the contract, Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that his Department prepared and signed a formal Contract Agreement on November 4, 2011 and asked Siemens to collect it from them, sign and return it to them. They took about two weeks to collect it and another five weeks to sign and return it, and that too with defects.

 

3.9       Mr. Shameem Ahmed concluded that it is a case of “too many cooks spoil the broth”. There are so many people involved on behalf of Siemens, with distributed responsibilities, that the Client is at a loss to determine who he should take his complaints to.

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Committee endorsed the recommendation of the CPC for pre-qualification of first five contractors listed in 3.2 above for electrical infrastructure system at IBA Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel, & Staff Colony.

Shah explained the whole requirements of Tier 3 Standard’s Data Centre.  He said that as the previous budget was allocated on a design that did not meet the standard that is required for a Tier 3 Data Centre, the budget has to be revised.  Accordingly, it was decided in the meeting that H.K. Presentation by H.K. Shah:

 

8.3       Mr. Mujtaba of H.K. Shah after introducing his team members gave an overview of the Scope of Work they have been assigned which was as under:

 

  •   To design Tier 3 Data Centre based on TIA – 942 Data Centre Standards

  •   Assisting IBA in finalizing and selecting of Contractor for this Project

  •   Project Management

  •   Assisting IBA in taking complete handover of Data Centre from Contractor

 

8.4       Mr. Mujtaba also presented the proposed Structural Layout of IBA City Campus Data Centre and Section-wise Project Cost which was divided into five parts summary of which is as under:

 

a.      Total Electrical Cost                                            Rs. 15.8 million

b.      Total Fire Detection and Security Cost                   Rs. 12.7 million

c.      Total ICT Part Cost                                             Rs. 22.3 million

d.      Total Cooling and Environmental Cost                     Rs. 14.8 million

e.      Total Civil Part Cost                                            Rs. 04.2 million

Total Cost of the Project:                Rs. 69.8 million

                        Cost of Electrical work outside Data Centre                 Rs. 11.51 million

 

                                                            Total Cost of Data Centre:     Rs. 81.31million
