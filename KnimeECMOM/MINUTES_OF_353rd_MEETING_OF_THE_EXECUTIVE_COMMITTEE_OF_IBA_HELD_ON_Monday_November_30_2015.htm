Item No.1       To consider Grievance Committee report on reward and recognition 2015.

 

1.1              The Dean & Director said that the Grievance Committee has recommended to exclude the following five from the Rewards earlier recommended by the Executive Committee for the reasons explained in their note:

 

1.      Mr. Muhammad Ather Rana

2.      Mr. Muhammad Munawar

3.      Mr. Muhammad Zahid

4.      Mr. Abdul Khaliq

5.      Mr. Muhammad Sharif Jamali

 

1.2              Mr. Moeid Sultan said that in case of Mr. Muhammad Zahid the reasons given by the GC are not based on facts.  The GC’s finding is that Mr. Muhammad Zahid has already got Activity Based Reward (@ one standard gross salary) and that he was being rewarded for the same responsibility. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that it was on GL Module implementation but another one was on fixed assets register tagging which was a very cumbersome process which he did during the month of Ramazan. He said that if this exercise had been outsourced, it would have involved millions of rupees.

 

1.3              The Dean & Director said that in a year we cannot give two rewards; however, he said that as Director Finance Mr. Moeid can give a letter of appreciation to Muhammad Zahid.

 

1.4              Mr. Imran Batada said that Mr. Muhammad Sharif Jamali was recommended for a letter of appreciation and not for cash reward. The Dean & Director said that Mr. Jamali also has got Activity Based Reward and as such he should not get a Certificate as recommended by the EC.

 

1.5              The Committee considered the recommendation of the GC that the following should not be eligible for rewards:

 

o   60 or more late comings in a year (According to the Policy show cause will be issued after seven late(s) in the same month)

o   Absences/leaves in a year exceeding the allowed leaves as per the IBA leave policy

o   Less than one year working tenure

o   Inquiry or disciplinary action from the competent authority in job tenure

o   Any compensatory allowance other than standard gross salary.

 

1.6       The Registrar suggested that instead of 60 late comings in a year, it should be 30 or more late comings in a year.  The Committee agreed with the Registrar.

 

1.7       Mr. Adnan Hameed suggested that one should be ineligible for the reward only on disciplinary action and not on inquiry only or issuance of warning letter and the period of ineligibility should be five years instead of job tenure. 

 

1.8       The Dean & Director said that if an inquiry is in process, the award for the concerned person should be pended for that year.

 

DECISIONS & ACTIONS

 

1.9       The Executive Committee accepted the recommendations of the GC to exclude the five names noted at 1.1 above from the list of employees recommended for Cash Reward 2015.

 

1.10     The Executive Committee also accepted the recommendation of the GC with noted modifications that the employees with the following shortcomings would not be eligible for Reward:

 

a)      30 or more late comings in a year.

b)      absences / leaves in a year exceeding the allowed leaves as per the IBA leave policy.

c)      less than one year working tenure at IBA

d)     major disciplinary action has been taken during the last 5 years of service at IBA.   

e)      Any compensatory allowance other than standard gross salary. 

 

1.11     Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri would convey the above decisions of the Executive Committee to the Grievance Committee.

 

Item No.2       Status Report from Director Finance.

 

2.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan to examine the financial implications on allowing scholarships to top 10% students of BS-A&F only: Mr. Moeid Sultan said that Dr. Nasir Touheed had suggested that those who are on Dean’s List should be given merit scholarship to the extent that the cost remains the same as that of BBA and MBA scholarships. He said that he worked out on that basis and found that there were 76 such students. The financial impact is of Rs.5.5 million and an additional amount of Rs.4.495 million would have to re-appropriated.

 

2.2       The Dean & Director said that first we have to make a policy. He said that in 2nd and 3rd year only those who are on Dean’s List would get the merit scholarship. The merit based scholarship in first year is being used to attract students while for year 2 to 4 the merit based scholarships are to improve performance. He said that first we have to see as to what would be the financial impact if we give 50% scholarships. He said that from year 2 to year 4 whosoever comes in Dean’s list will be given 50% of scholarships of the applicable fee.  He said that the existing system of merit based scholarship list of first year would become redundant for years two to year four. He advised to work out the financial impact on the basis of the Deans’ List and it should not be campus wise or semester wise; it would be year-wise.   

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

2.3       The Director Finance would work out the financial impact of merit based scholarship on the basis of the Deans’ Lists for year 2 to year 4.  

 

Item No.3       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Change in Leave Encashment Rules for non-teaching staff.

 

3.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that in 2011, deferred benefits were introduced for IBA contract employees including Provident Fund and Gratuity. The A&FC advised that an option may be given to employees to opt between leave encashment & gratuity. More than 98% employees opted for leave encashment. However, later it was decided that for all new appointments after July 2011, leave encashment facility will be suspended and they will be entitled for Gratuity (applicable on 48 staff members).

 

3.2       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that Earned Leaves are paid leaves and therefore they are compensated in monetary terms at the end of employment, irrespective of the fact whether the organization has leave encashment policy or not.  Therefore, the term leave encashment refers to the encashment of accumulated leaves during the employment (up to 30 days per year) and not leave encashment at the end of employment. He said that only non-teaching employees are entitled for leave encashment, whereas faculty members meeting the defined criteria i.e. official assignment given during summers are entitled for leave encashment.

 

3.3       Mr. Adnan Hameed proposed that IBA non-teaching employees who joined after July 2011 (total 48, while other approx. 300 employees have this facility) may also be allowed leave encashment and the benefit of gratuity may be withdrawn from these employees for the following reasons:

 

·         Request from employees

·         Avoids loan burden on employees in case of financial need

·         Significant reduction in institute’s future financial burden of deferred benefits

 

3.4       Mr. Moeid Sultan supported this proposal and suggested that option should be taken from these employees whether they would like to continue with the Gratuity Scheme or opt for leave encashment.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.5       The Executive Committee agreed to the proposal of allowing leave encashment to and withdrawing gratuity for the 48 employees who were earlier not allowed leave encashment. However, option will be given to concerned employees in this regard.

 

3.6       The Head of HR would present the proposal to the A&FC for approval.

 

b)     Accumulation of leaves of teaching staff.

 

3.7       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that IBA is expected to bear a huge financial burden of deferred benefits in case of faculty members who are entitled for Provident Fund, Gratuity and Leave Encashment (at the end of employment). He also mentioned that in other academic institutions faculty members are not entitled for earned leaves instead they have their summer semester off.  He suggested that all full time faculty members and specially those who are not involved in teaching during summer semester should avail mandatory 30 days earned leave during the year and preferably during summer semester; this will not only facilitate faculty members in availing their earned leaves but will also help IBA in controlling its future financial liabilities.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.8       The Committee agreed to the proposal.  The Head of HR would present the proposal to the A&FC for approval.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
