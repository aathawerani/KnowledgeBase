MINUTES OF THE 54th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON JUNE 29, 2009 

The 54th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 29, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat Associate Dean
2. 2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean
3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN Registrar
4. Mr. Mobin Khalili Controller of Examinations
5. Mr. Ali Taha Director Finance
6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director I.C.T
8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai Director H.R

 

The following items were discussed:

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the tender documents along with the design of furniture of existing academic block at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects presented the indicative designs of the furniture selected by the Furniture Committee for various rooms of the existing Academic Block at the Main Campus.

 

1.2              The Committee reviewed the design of furniture and made suggestions for certain changes which included:

 

·         that the top of the chairs should be of any hard material and not of wood as it may be misused by the students.   

·         cushions should be provided on the back and seats of the chairs

 

1.3              The Director Projects presented the main features of the tender for the design and manufacturing of furniture which included the following:

 

·         The tenders will be issued to the pre-qualified contractors

·         Technical and financial bids will be received in two different envelopes

·         Technical bid will comprise the designs drawings and specifications suggested by the tenderer based on the indicative designs

·         Technical bids will be opened first, which will be evaluated by the Furniture Committee. Based on the approval of technically approved bidders, the financial bids of the approved bidders will be opened. The composite evaluation (technical and financial) will be submitted to the evaluation committee for their approval.

 

1.4              The Director Projects also presented the following time line:

 

·         Issuance of tenders                                   July 1, 2009

·         Submission                                                July 15, 2009

·         Evaluation & Award                                 July 20, 2009

·         Manufacturing & Supply:

A)    Seminar rooms including Rostrums

Teachers tables & Chairs                               September 20, 2009

 

B)    Classroom teachers tables, teachers chairs

And Rostrums                                                 August 20, 2009

 

                 C)   Breakout rooms and lounges                          August 20, 2009

 

1.5              The Director Projects stated that in order to make sure that the required furniture is delivered on time, they will split the tender so that seminar room furniture may be given to one and syndicate room furniture may be given to any other.  He said that the completion of furniture is dependent on the selected design.

 

DECISION

 

1.6              The Committee approved the tender for the design and manufacturing of furniture for the existing Academic Block at Main Campus selected by the Furniture Committee incorporating the suggested changes.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Director Projects will issue the tender for the design and manufacturing of furniture for the existing Academic Block at Main Campus. 

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve design and estimate for the development of training facility for MBA (Banking and Financial Services) in APWA Building.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that Mr. Muhammad Saleem Umer, Coordinator MBA (Banking & Financial Services) has requested to provide high class training facilities in two rooms of APWA building.  He said that Mr. Saleem Umer has particularly desired that the ceiling, walls and floors, furnishing, lighting, air-conditioning and sound proofing should be excellent. He presented the estimates of Rs.2.068 million for classroom tables and chairs, coffee tables, sofa chairs, teacher table, false ceiling, wall panelling, flooring, sound proof insulation, multimedia projector with screen and white board.    

 

2.2       The Director Projects requested the Committee to approve the desired work and the estimates so that he could float the tenders and the work could be started as only 8 weeks were left to do the procurement process and to complete the installation and procurement of furniture.

 

2.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to why so much money would be spent at APWA if the classes are to be started on the ground floor building of the old academic block at City Campus. The Director Projects replied that the demolishing work of library and auditorium will commence in September this year and there will be a lot of disturbance in the City Campus.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee approved the design and estimate of around Rs.2 million for the development of training facility for MBA (Banking and Financial Services) in APWA Building as presented by the Director Projects.  This amount will be recovered from the income generated by the donations given by the banks to the Endowment Fund.

 

2.5       The Committee requested the Director ICT to provide multimedia projector with screen so that the estimate may be reduced by Rs.200,000/-

 

ACTION

 

2.6       The Director Projects would float the tender for the desired work and the Director ICT would arrange multimedia projector with screen.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider the proposal from Director Projects regarding renovation of Girls Hostel A&B.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the Girls Hostel A & B are in poor condition from maintenance point of view. He said that elaborated work is required in both the hostels. He mentioned some of the important works that need to be undertaken which included:

 

·         Paintwork of both Hostels

·         Changing old wooden windows with aluminum windows (Windows which are inside )

·         Tiling of  all Toilets with associated features

·         Tiling of both kitchens

·         Kitchen cabinets

·         Removing cement net and replacing it with Aluminum partition and shutters. The place can be used as sitting place.

·         Construction of two rooms as stores

·         Fixing clothes washing double tub sink

·         Converting one small room into Office for Hostel Warden

·         Fly Proofing in some windows

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported that in order to carry out the above mentioned work, quotations were invited from eight contractors. However, M/s. The Turnkey Group and M/s ME & Construction did not participate. The cost quoted by the following six contractors is as under:

 

1.      M/s  Strength                                Rs 1,907,990/- 1st    Lowest

2.      M/s  Link International                 Rs 1,990,098/-

3.      M/s  R R Associates                      Rs 2,195,886/-

4.      M/s  Five Star Associates              Rs 2,256,200/-

5.      M/s  Electro Systems                    Rs 2,375,200/-

6.      M/s  Jhangir Corporation              Rs 2,704,510/- 6th Highest                  

 

3.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the renovation of Girls hostel A&B in principle so that the Purchase Committee could be requested to scrutinize and award the work to the lowest bidder. He informed the Committee that as per TAP, the transaction is under the competency of Director IBA.

 

3.4       The Registrar said that the students would start coming by August 15, 2009. He requested that there should be a time line for the completion of the renovation work.

 

3.5       The Director Projects said that the tendering has been done and four weeks time has been given. He said that if the EC and the Purchase Committee approves it, the Work Order can be issued in a day or two.

     

DECISION

 

3.6              The Committee agreed to the proposal for the renovation of Girls Hostel A & B and to award the work to the lowest bidder after scrutiny by the Purchase Committee.

 

ACTION

 

3.7              The Director Projects would issue work order to the lowest bidder after the scrutiny by the Purchase Committee.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the award of work for construction of students canteen and lounge at City Campus.

 

4.1            The Director Projects reported that the Audit and Finance Committee already approved the award of work for detail designing, tender documents and top supervision to M/s ICON and detail supervision and project management work to M/s Integ for the students canteen and lounge at City Campus. The detail design and Tender Documents Completed in April 2009. After preparation of Tender Documents and drawing, tender documents were issued to the following five pre-qualified Contractors on May 02, 2009:

 

1.      M/s. Project Managers Constructor

2.      M/s. Steel Man

3.      M/s. Prefab Systems

4.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.

5.      M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprise.

4.2       The Director Projects reported that M/s. Steel Man refused to collect the tender documents so only four contractors collected the tender documents. A technical proforma was issued to contractors during pre-bid meeting as per instruction of BoG. The contractors were advised to submit the technical and financial proposals separately duly signed and sealed. Three Contractors submitted their bids on May 19, 2009, one contractor M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises regretted to submit the bid.

4.3       The Director Projects reported that technical proposals of three bidders M/s. Project Managers Constructor, M/s. Prefab Systems and M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co. were opened on May 19, 2009 and sent to Project Manager and Consultants for their evaluation. He said that the Project Manager and Consultant submitted their reports on June 2, 2009.              Based on the technical evaluation report the financial bids were opened on June 3, 2009 in the presence of Consultant, Project Manager, IBA official and representative of contractors. The following were the standings of the contractors after opening of financial bids:

 

1.  M/s. Project Managers-Constructors          Rs.8,752,782          1st lowest 

1.      M/s.  Prefab Systems                                Rs.10,372,523        2nd lowest

2.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.                    Rs.11,705,597        Highest

4.4              The Director Projects reported that the financial bids were sent to Consultant and Project Manager for their evaluation on June 3, 2009. The Project Manager and Consultant submitted their report on June 24, 2009.

 

4.5              The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Project Manager scrutinized the bids in detail and after arithmetical check found the following corrected bid amounts of the contractors:

 

 

1.      M/s. Project Manager Constructor            Rs.  8,763,582/-

2.      M/s. Prefab Systems                                 Rs.10,372,523/-

3.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.                    Rs.11,705,597/-

4.6              The Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s. Project Manager offered a rebate of 3% vide their letter# PM/IBA/08/09 dated June 16, 2009 for the subject work. After rebate the bid price of M/s Project Manager Constructor is as follows:

 

Corrected Bid Cost:                            Rs. 8,763,582/-

Rebate:                                                Rs.262, 908/- (3% of Bid Cost)

Bid Cost after Rebate:                                    Rs.8, 500,674/-

 

4.7              The Director Projects reported that the final bid amount is 1.06% higher than the budgeted amount which is Rs.8,000,000/-  Based on conclusion of Project Manager M/s. INTEG, recommendation of Architect and rebate letter of contractor, the Director Projects recommended that the work may be awarded to M/s Project Manager constructor at a cost of Rs.8,500,674/-

 

4.8              The Director Projects clarified that the cost does not include the cost of furniture.

 

4.9              The Registrar said that the proposed canteen will be very close to the construction work and dusts may come into the canteen. He suggested to construct the less costly canteen as is done in Expo Centre.  According to his estimate it would not cost more than Rs.2.5 million.

 

4.10          It was also suggested that the canteen should be arranged at APWA.

DECISION

 

4.11          The Committee decided that temporary arrangement for students canteen should be made which should cost not more than Rs.2.5 million. The Committee further decided that the A&FC should be re-addressed with the recommendation of Executive Committee.

 

4.12          The Registrar and the Director ICT volunteered to redesign the canteen and present before the Executive Committee.

 

ACTION

 

4.13          The Registrar and the Director ICT will design the canteen and present the design before the EC for consideration.

 

4.14     The Director Projects would place the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the    A&FC to review the decision regarding construction of students canteen and lounge at City Campus for which Rs.8 million has been approved by the A&FC/BoG.

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and accord approval in principle to initiate the urgent work of (a) External Development, and (b) Infrastructure Development needed for existing academic block and multi-storied building at Main Campus.

 

A.   External development

 

5.1       The Director Projects said that there is a need for constructing a pathway between the existing Academic Block and the Multi Purpose Building. This will comprise the pathway to be developed through the existing lawn using portions of existing pathway, development of earthwork between the Administration Building and the Multi Purpose Building to lay down the pathway, providing the fibreglass sunshade over the entire pathways. These canopies will be used for the permanent covered parking.  A shed for students near the Academic Block is also required to be constructed.

 

5.2              The Director Projects reported that to undertake the construction of the pathways and the shed, the architectural details are being provided by the Architect. He said that the work of tendering, procurement process and detailed supervision will be done in-house.  He said that the estimate has not been obtained and at this stage he just wanted an approval in principle.

 

5.3              The Registrar suggested that only a pavement, if it is at all necessary, may be enough.  He suggested not to erect roof on the pathway.

DECISION

 

5.4              The Committee agreed with the Registrar and decided that in the first phase laying paves will be enough. However, fibre glass roof may be constructed in an area in the middle of approximately 10 x 20 so that the pedestrians may have a break while walking under the sun.

 

5.5              The Committee also decided that the shed for students should be constructed at the back side of the existing academic block.

 

ACTION

 

5.6              The Director Projects would initiate the process of laying down pathway only and present estimates for the pathway and the students shed.

 

 

B  Infrastructure Development

 

5.7       The Director Projects reported that there is an urgent need to replace one power cable coming from Karachi University of size 70 mm to the existing Academic block which has been found unserviceable, when exposed during the renovation works. He said that according to the electrical consultants M/s S.K Consultants of the project (working for ICON), this needs to be replaced. He stated that the Consultant has also advised to lay down a new cable to the building from KESC sub-station to the Academic Block to meet the energy requirement of the building. This new cable will be approximately 460 Meter long.

 

5.8       The Director Projects further stated that there is an urgent need of laying new cable 95 mm, 160 Meter long for the Multi Purpose Building from KESC Sub Station.

 

5.9       The Director Projects informed the Committee that there is a need for study of stand by generation for Academic Block and the Multi purpose building.  He said that the Consultants already engaged for the electrical audit has a different scope of services under the present agreement with IBA. The study of infrastructure development falls in the second phase which will take longer time.

 

5.10     The Director Projects said that the above works are part of the infrastructure development, but have to be done now as both buildings need the power specially the Academic Block where ground floor has been added with the addition of Air-conditioning.

 

5.11     The Director Projects requested the Committee to accord approval for the engagement of the electrical consultant of the Buildings (who has designed the building electrical system) to design the electrical infrastructure for both the buildings, prepare tender documents, assist IBA during the procurement process and carry out top supervision. He said that M/s. Integ will provide the detailed supervision during the construction as the system has to be integrated with the buildings. He informed the Committee that provision of expenditure exists in the Budget.

 

 

DECISION

 

5.12     The Committee approved the proposal for engaging the electrical consultant of the Buildings M/s. S.K. Consultants (who has designed the building electrical system) to design the electrical infrastructure for both the buildings, prepare tender documents, assist IBA during the procurement process and carry out top supervision.

Mr. Imran Batada served as both Senior Manager MIS & Manager IT from December 16, 2008 to March 03, 2009. He reported that during this period, he was responsible for the following activities from IT side:

 

1.         Prepared ICT Budget

2.         Completed the leg-work for Supplier for Networking Services Tender

3.         Supervising tender floating for Supplier for Networking Services

4.         Completing the leg-work for Software Applications and Licenses Process

5.         Floating tender for Pre-Qualification of Suppliers for Software Applications and Licenses

6.         Improving Email Infrastructure (Done changes in the business process, to make it more reliable)

7.         Working and managing daily operational activities of IT Side

8.         Looking after the helpdesk and labs operation

6.2       The Director ICT said that Mr. Batada has given extra time and effort in order to avoid any mishap of ICT Department. During this process all the deadline project of MIS has been fulfilled on time. Mr. Batada looked after both the departments exceptionally well without giving a cause of concern to his superiors.

 

6.3       The Director ICT recommended that at least Rs.30,000/- or half of the salary that he was drawing during that time, whichever is higher should be given to him as double duty allowance on per month basis for the mentioned period.

 

6.4       The Controller of Examinations said that there are many people who are doing additional duty.  He said that there has to be a policy and this policy should be implemented across the board.

 

6.5              The H.R. Manager said that Mr. Mohammad Rafiq, Assistant Admin. Officer has been doing additional duty ever since Mr. Qaisar Abbas, the then Admin. Officer resigned and left IBA. He said that as per policy Mr. Rafiq should also be compensated. Similarly, he said that Mr. M. Sohail Khan, Purchase Officer, has been arranging Purchases for the whole IBA. The function has now been divided into three purchase offices and the Purchase Officer for Projects Department has not yet been appointed. Therefore, he should also be compensated for double duty. The Registrar agreed with Mr. Jafri.

 

6.6       The Director H.R. said that the Departmental Head has been requesting for the officiating allowance for Mr. Batada from day one.  She said that reviewing past cases is not possible.    

 

6.7       The Director Finance said that his understanding was that at the time of evaluation, considering the factors mentioned above, Mr. Batada has been given a reasonable raise in his salary w.e.f December 1, 2008. He said that the policy should be such that if a person at responsible position goes on leave and his job is performed by another person, he / she should be compensated for the additional job.  

 

6.8       The Director Projects said that his Quantity Surveyor went on leave and disappeared. Mr. Abdul Rehman is working alone in his place.  He suggested that the policy should be to give ex-gratia immediately after the job is over.

6.10     Regarding precedent of giving officiating allowance, the H.R. Manager clarified that Mr. Moeid Sultan has been given officiating allowance on two occasions with the approval of the A&FC. On the first occasion the amount of officiating allowance was suggested by Mr. Danishmand, the then Director IBA to the A&FC and on the second occasion, almost the same percent of the salary as was given earlier was allowed as officiating allowance.

6.11          The Committee decided that the same percentage of the salary that was given to Mr. Moeid Sultan as officiating allowance will be given to Mr. Imran Batada as officiating allowance for the period he performed the additional duty of I.T. Manager.

 

ACTION

 

6.12          The Director Finance will communicate the percentage to the Director H.R. for determining the amount of officiating allowance for Mr. Imran Batada.

 

 

Item No. 7      To consider and approve draft of the Code of Conduct presented by the Director H.R.

 

7.1       The Director H.R. presented the draft version of the Code of Conduct which she said, if approved, will be a governing document of behaviors and ways of working at IBA. She said that it would be helpful in setting the ethos or Tone at the Top as well as clearly articulating how we want to achieve our objectives. It is designed to be easy to read, comprehend and follow at all levels in the organization. She said that the Code once approved, will be translated into Urdu and disbursed and communicated extensively. Each individual segment can be built upon and further elaborated in the times to come with detailed policies and SOPs.

 

7.2       The Director H.R. requested the EC to give their valuable thoughts so that we can move towards finalization. She said that the Code will form part of the IBA Code Book.      

 

DECISION

 

7.3              The members will give their feedback on the draft of Code of Conduct in the next meeting of the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 8      Follow up on the IBA Code Book  Delivery Status.

 

8.1              The Director H.R. said that she has identified the topics from the IBA Code Book on which up-date from all concerned is required. She said that if the members find any gap in the topic / area, they may point it out.  She requested the members to give a time line for providing up-date in their areas of the Code Book.

9.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that he had pointed it out in the beginning and Mr. Shameem Ahmed had said that he would take care of it.

 

9.3              The Registrar advised that the APWA should be approached and the Maintenance Manager should go there to resolve that problem.

a)         Construction of Pedestrian Bridge between APWA and City Campus Buildings.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
