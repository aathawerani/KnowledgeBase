MINUTES OF THE 110TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JULY 5, 2010

 

The 110th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 5, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.  Dr. Ishrat Husain                                   Director

2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

3.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

4.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

5.  Mr. Mobin Khalili                                     Controller of Examinations

6.  Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

7.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

8.  Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                               Director CED

9.  Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                              Director H.R.

10.Dr. Arshad Siddiqi,                                  Director ICT (Represented by Mr. Faisal Zia)

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects and Mr. Khalid Rashid Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit attended the meeting by special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve Annual Audit Plan 2010-11

 

1.1       The Director informed the Committee that there is a structural change in the Internal Audit Department.  He said that the Board has decided to transfer the pre-audit function, which is required as per IBA Act, to the Finance Department. He said that this has been decided to avoid conflict of interest because once the Internal Audit Department signs a bill for payment, how can it be involved in post-audit. He said that the Audit Plan being presented by the Head of Internal Audit should be endorsed by each department and the timing should be fixed in consultation with the concerned department.      

 

1.2       Mr. Khalid Rashid Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit presented the Annual Audit Plan 2010-11.  He said that he had divided the plan into the following areas:

 

·         Risk based Audits

·         Cyclical Reviews

·         On-going Activity

 

1.3       Mr. Jhumra said that his Department has ensured that the following broad categories are covered by audit function when planning its annual audit activities:

 

·         Safeguarding of Assets

·         Effectiveness and efficiency of operations

·         Reliability and timely preparation of financial statements

·         Compliance with laws and regulations

 

1.4       Mr. Jhumra presented a list of all auditable areas in IBA. He said that auditable areas have been categorized into three risk levels; high (H), moderate (M) and low (Low) risk. He said that High Risk areas would receive audit coverage every year, Moderate Risk would receive audit coverage every two years, and Low Risk areas would receive audit coverage every 3 years.

 

1.5       Mr. Jhumra clarified that a rating as High Risk does not mean that the area is perceived to have control problems.  It means that the area is critical, complex, involves major cash outflows and has increased compliance risk.

 

1.6       Mr. Jhumra presented the schedule of audit coverage which included planned audits, description, areas to be covered and planned timing.

 

1.7       Mr. Jhumra presented list of auditable areas along with their risk rating.

 

1.8       Mr. Faisal Zia said that the timing of February to April for the audit of IT/IS does not suit them and requested to move it to January to March.

 

1.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that audit of Data Security should also be included.

 

1.10     The Director H.R. pointed out that “Staff Town / Residency” has been shown in the audit plan under the Human Resources whereas it is looked after by the Registrar’s Office and as such it should be shifted to Administration.  The Committee agreed with the Director H.R. 

 

1.11     The Director Projects wanted to know as to why Maintenance is on “Moderate Risk”. In his opinion it should be placed on “High Risk”.

 

1.12     The Director said that instead of department-wise, the risk should be determined on the basis of the activity.  He advised Mr. Jhumra to identify the level of risk of each activity in consultation with the departmental heads.

 

1.13     The Director asked the Director Finance and the Head of Internal Audit to analyse the nature and concern of the Government audit and satisfy that also.

 

1.14     The Director suggested that the Head of Internal Audit should have manual of every department plus Procurement Manual, and Transaction Authority Policy which would serve as bench mark to ensure that the transactions are according to the approved manual / policy.  He said that the manual and policies should be placed on the portal and their hard copies should be made available to each department.  

 

1.15     The Director H.R. observed that in terms of organization’s core business, she does not find any provision for Academic audit.

 

1.16     The Director replied that the Audit department does not have the competence, but he added that IBA has an External Review Panel and the Academic Board for this purpose.

 

DECISION

 

1.17     The Committee approved the Annual Audit Plan incorporating the suggestions made above. 

 

1.18     The Committee decided that the audit timing of IT/IS would be interchanged with Finance Department’s audit timing i.e. the audit timing of IT/IS would be from January to March and that of Finance would be from March to April.

  

ACTION

 

1.19     The Head of Internal Audit would revise the Annual Audit Plan 2010-11 by incorporating the above suggestions/decisions.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve payment for revision of designs and drawings by M/s. NBCL for the Project of Centre for Entrepreneurship Development.

 

2.1       The Director Projects stated that in the second week of June 2010, Mr. Zafar Ahmad Siddiqui Director CED, Mr. Moeid Sultan, General Manager Finance and Syed Jahanzeb, Project Accountant proposed changes in the overall spatial planning to match the budget approved by the USAID. The planning was checked internally by the Projects Department and it was found that planning can be changed without making major changes in the structure of the building. However, on the drawings all the related Architectural layout, furniture plans, electrical, ICT, MEP and HVAC drawings have to be changed.  He reported that based on the approval by the Director CED, Architects were given go ahead to change drawings to save time.

 

2.2       The Director Projects stated that the Architects have requested for an extra payment for the revision of 80-100 drawings at a total cost of Rs.750,000/-  He said that this is a reasonable cost and is in the ambit of the agreement wherein  it is provided that payment on revision of design and drawings will be based on actual expenses to be decided mutually.

 

2.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the additional payment of Rs.750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand only) to be made to the Architects and their Consultants.

 

2.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the structure, room numbers and basic thing will remain the same so there is no need to change the designs and drawings.

 

2.5       The Director also agreed with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and said that he would not allow additional expense.  He said that if NBCL has not changed the drawings then it should be left as it is.

 

2.6       The Director Projects contacted NBCL and confirmed that they had not changed the drawings.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee did not approve revision of designs and drawings of Centre for Entrepreneurial Development.

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Relocation of various offices / departments at Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director said that after detailed discussion with the Architect, Director Projects and concurrence of Director ICT, an agreement has been reached that the Data Centre will be located on the ground floor of multipurpose building and all the servers and other equipment will be shifted to the Data Centre.

 

3.2       The Library will temporarily be shifted to the ground floor (wooden offices) of the Admin. Building at Main Campus and all the offices of all the Directors would be shifted to the first floor of Multi-purpose building. The Director asked the Director Projects to show the ground floor offices to the Librarian. He suggested to construct the Record Rooms on the top floor of the Admin. Building. He also said that there is no need to keep Computer Lab in the Admin. Building and asked the Director Projects to tell M/s. Icon to remove this from tender document. 

 

3.3       The Director suggested that the idea of shifting some of the new batches of the EMBA programs to the Main Campus should be explored. However, he said that his first priority is to get the Data Centre ready as soon as possible and asked the Director Projects to start the work of Data Centre immediately. 

 

3.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that he would call an immediate meeting of the Architects and the Director ICT to agree on the re- planning of the existing ground floor of the Multi Purpose Building. He further informed that the Architect should be able to prepare the revised layout of the Data Centre within 10 days.  

 

3.5       The Director wanted to know as to whose responsibility it is for the design of the Data Centre.  To this Mr. Faisal Zia said that it is his responsibility. The Director asked the Director Projects to show the design of the Data Centre to Mr. Faisal Zia and fit in his requirements and he must be given assurance that cabling and wiring are available at that place.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee noted and approved the relocation of some offices / departments as noted above. The Director Projects will consult Mr. Faisal Zia to make sure that the Data Centre meets the requirements of ICT Department and the Head Librarian about the shifting of the Library.

 

b)     Extension of FCS Building.

 

3.7       The Director Projects reported that due to expansion in area, the cost for renovation / expansion of FCS Building would be Rs.120 million.

 

3.8       The Director said that there was no need to extend the area and asked the Director Projects to complete the extension of two wings in the FCS Building in Rs.40 million. He said that they would have plenty of space in the multi-storied building and new Academic Block so there is no need to extend the areas of FCS Building.

 

3.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that his original requirement has not changed.  He wondered how the cost estimate increased three times.

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Director asked the Director Projects and Dr. Sayeed Ghani to work out the design of the FCS extension with M/s. Icon within the limit of Rs.40 million.

 

c)      Space for Data Centre at City Campus

 

3.11     Mr. Faisal Zia requested for some space at City Campus so that they may start the work of Data Centre.  He said that the Director Projects has assured that the required space would be ready by February 2011 but he said that it would be too late.

 

3.12     The Director Projects said that the existing ladies bath rooms at City Campus would be converted into faculty rooms and an alternate would be ready within 3 to 4 weeks. He suggested that a portion of the faculty rooms could temporarily be given to the Data Centre.

 

DECISION

 

3.13     The Committee decided that Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia, Mr. Faisal Zia and Mr. Mobin Khalili would sort it out.

 

d)     Training on new I.T. packages

 

3.14     The Director said that because of introduction / installation of new software packages i.e. Microsoft, people face difficulty in operating it.  He suggested that a training session should be arranged as and when new software and packages are installed.

 

3.15     Mr. Faisal Zia said that Mr. Mohsin Naqvi is conversant with the new packages and he can provide training. Mr.Faisal Zia also shared the importance of Training of ISO 27001:2005 standard for all departmental heads/staff. The Director asked Mr. Faisal Zia to obtain details of the upcoming training program in Karachi.

 

3.16     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that visiting faculty also have the expertise and their services can be hired.

 

DECISION

 

3.17     The Director ICT would look into it and see how training sessions can be arranged for those who have difficulty in operating the new software / package. 

 

e)      Renaming of “Faculty Development Fund”.

 

3.18     The Director said that they have started Student Exchange Program and three students are going to a University in China under this program but some of them do not have the resources to meet the expenses. He said that it is not a two-way exchange program because Chinese do not want to come to Pakistan.  He suggested that Faculty Development Fund may be broadened as “Faculty Development and Student Exchange Program” so that this sort of problem is resolved.

 

3.19     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that it should be named as “Capacity Development Fund (Faculty and Students)” and 80% of the fund should be used for Faculty and 20% for students. The unutilized students fund may also be transferred to this fund.

 

DECISION

 

3.20     The Committee decided that the title of Faculty Development Fund be changed to “Capacity Development Fund for Faculty and Students” and presented to the A&FC/Board for approval.

 

ACTION

 

3.21     The Associate Deans would present a proposal for the A&FC by redefining the scope of the proposed “Capacity Development Fund for Faculty and Students”.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
