MINUTES OF 255th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, JULY 1, 2013

The 255th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 1, 2013 at 11:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                          Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                    Director Projects

4.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                 Director Finance

5.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                            Director H.R.

6.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                 Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Shakeel Anwar, Engineer HEC Projects, Mr. Jawed Islam, Projects Engineer, Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager General Maintenance / Security attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1 To consider and approve the Laptop Ownership Policy.

1.1 Mr. Imran Batada said that he has prepared the Laptop Ownership / Issuance Policy and also incorporated the suggestions from HR & Finance. The Director H.R. said that when the new faculty whose laptop was to be issued were conveyed the decision of the 252nd meeting of the Executive Committee held on June 10, 2013 that the ownership of the laptop would be transferred to them on payment of 50% of the cost in 36 instalments, a few faculty members were uncomfortable with this decision.

1.2 To a question from the Director Projects, Mr. Imran Batada said that if HOD is given a new laptop in replacement then 50% payment will apply to him/her also. Dr. Mohammad Nishat wanted to know if a faculty member does not want to keep ownership for three years, then how such cases should be treated. Mr. Imran Batada said that there is no option given by the Dean & Director.

1.3 Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know as to what is the cost of a laptop and how many laptops are required to be purchased. Mr. Imran Batada said that 75 new laptops are required and the cost of a lap is around Rs.60,000/- Thus around Rs.4.5 million would be required out of which 50% would be recovered from the faculty in three years time.

1.4 Dr. Mohammad Nishat suggested that in view of the feedback of faculty, the Dean & Director may be requested to give option to the faculty either to take the ownership of the laptop or not.

1.5 The Director H.R. said that for the existing laptop, faculty will be given an option of buying back at the existing market value but they will not be given replacement. A representative from IT and Finance would assess the market value. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the market price should be assessed on the basis of sale price not on purchase price.

1.6 Mr. Imran Batada said that this is the right time to replace Toshiba Laptops with HP as there SLA for Toshiba has already expired but SLA with HP is still valid.

DECISION

1.7 The Committee asked Mr. Imran Batada to present the policy again to the EC supported by the discussions as a recommendation.

ACTION

1.8 Mr. Imran Batada would present the policy again along with the recommendations made above.


Item No. 2 To consider and approve the extension of time period of the project “Visiting Faculty Hostel”.

2.1 Mr. Shakeel Anwar, Engineer, HEC Projects, reported that the construction of Visiting Faculty Hostel started on 1st December 2011 and its completion date was 30th November 2012. The first extension for completing the project was given to the Contractor for six Months starting from 1st December 2012 to 31st May 2013. But Contractor was unable to complete the project due to the following changes by Architect and approval of cost impact by Project Manager:

• Staircase Terrazzo steps replaced by Silbatta Stone.
• Door polish approval.
• Water Spout Cost impact
• Door Hardware Cost Impact
• Anti shatter Film and Frosted Film Cost Impact.
• Change of Aluminium Windows colour
• Kitchen Cost Impact
• Fire House Cabinet SS panel Cost Impact

2.2 Mr. Shakeel stated that the Contractor Al Shafi Enterprises submitted a letter dated 25th April for 2nd extension for four months. A meeting of Architect, Project Managers, client and contractor was held on June 6, 2013 regarding 2nd Extension issue. After a brief discussion Project Manager, Architect and Client agreed on 2.5 months extension.

2.3 Mr. Shakeel requested the EC to approve a second extension of 2.5 months i.e. till August 15, 2013 in the time of completion of the project. He reported that the CPC has recommended second extension in time in the meeting held on June 29, 2013.

2.4 The Director Projects said that he has assessed it and it is possible to complete the project by August 15, 2013. However, he said that they cannot afford any changes in the design.

2.5 Dr. Mohammad Nishat said that there are termites in the campus. He suggested to make treatment for termites specially in wooden work. The Director Projects said that treatment for termites would be done. Dr. Nishat further said that in September this year two or three professors from Germany would be coming who would be provided accommodation at the VPR. He requested that it should be ensured that the project is completed by August 15, 2013.

2.6 The Director Projects said that sewerage and landscaping work has not been awarded as yet but it would be ordered if the proposal presented separately is approved by the EC. However, he requested not to provide accommodation to foreigners until the external work is completed which is expected to be completed by November this year.

2.7 Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know if there is any cost impact because of extension. The Director Projects replied that the CPC has approved 2.5 months extension on the condition that if the project is not completed in 2.5 months i.e. by August 15, 2013, the original 6 months plus 2.5 months = 8.5 months penalty would be imposed. However, he said that escalation is the part of the contract which we have to give. The Consultant pointed out that the escalation is allowed on cement and steel which have already been used and completed. Other items are not included in the contract.

DECISION

2.8 The Committee approved the extension in completion of Visiting Faculty Hostel project for 2.5 months i.e. till August 15, 2013, failing which as recommended by CPC 8½ months penalty would be imposed on the Contractor.


Item No.3 Approval of Variation Order of Miscellaneous Sewerage Works at Visiting Faculty Hostel.

3.1 The Director Projects stated that the External Sewerage System of the Visiting Faculty Hostel was not in the scope of the work of the Architects. Lately, when the building’s own sewerage system had been constructed and levels were known, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (NBCL) were asked to send the drawings for the external sewerage system. This was not constructed earlier as the work outside the periphery of the building was not available. As the construction machinery and material have been removed, the work can be done now.

3.2 The Director Projects said that as the Visiting Faculty Hostel is to be handed over to the Management for occupation by the moving in faculty from abroad, it was considered essential that the work could be made part of existing work in the building. This would save the mobilization and de-mobilization of a new contractor and also avoid the clash of two contractors at one site. Secondly, the urgency of work is quite evident from the above. He reported that in this regard BOQ was prepared by the HEC Projects Department and given to the existing contractors M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises with the request to quote their rates of these items. They have submitted their quotations of Rs.2.58 million. All the rates were scrutinized by the Engineers & Quantity Surveyors of HEC Projects Department and subsequently by the Sr. Quantity Surveyor of Projects Department. The Contractors were invited to accept the reduced cost which was at Rs.2.074 million and over & above they were asked to give further rebate. They had offered a rebate of 5% and final cost would be Rs.1.971 million. The Director Projects confirmed that the amount of variation order is within 15% of the project cost.

3.3 The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on June 29, 2013 has approved the amount of Rs.1,971,193/- for miscellaneous Sewerage works at Visiting Faculty Hostel.

3.4 Mr. Moeid suggested that the Contractor should deploy additional resources in order to complete the project on time. Mr. Jawed Islam, Project Engineer confirmed that the contractors would deploy additional resources.

DECISION

3.5 The Committee approved the Variation Order for Miscellaneous Sewerage Works at Visiting Faculty Hostel at a cost of Rs.1,971,193/- to the existing contractor M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises.


Item No. 4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Ex-Post Facto approval for Maintenance Works.

4.1 Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager General Maintenance / Security stated that the activities of the Facilities Maintenance Department include immediate maintenance requirements in addition to the regular planned activities. Due to the heavy work load resulting because of currently underway construction, all the facilities are heavily booked and therefore periodical maintenance is done only during the semester breaks. This creates frequent urgent client requests to do maintenance work while classes are on. Maintenance Department has to take care of such urgent requests speedily through any available vendor and contractor. He reported that during FY 2012-13 various maintenance jobs requested by Building In-Charges have been completed on urgent need basis. These were executed / completed through available contractors after completing due process. However, Finance Department approval was initiated afterwards due to urgency in majority of the works. He requested the Executive Committee to grant EX-Post Facto approval to settle those Bills of contractors (14 altogether amounting to Rs.898,155/-) before closing of Current Financial Year.

4.2 Syed Fahim Uddin said that due to urgency of work they skipped some of the procedural requirements and awarded the work. However, he said that all bills are available. He assured that in future he would ensure that the due process is completed before award of the work. He said that there was no item which would have required tendering. He said that there was only item for which splitting orders were issued and that was due to under estimation of the requirement of the material. He said that there were only three days available for laying and fixing of tiles, painting and white washing of ICT Computer Lab. At the last moment tile fixing bond became short, therefore the same vendor was asked to provide the material from local market as the Maintenance staff was too busy to procure it at a very short notice.

4.3 Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested that there should be a system whereby such problems may not come up again. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the Dean & Director has the emergency power which he can exercise but it has to be a real urgency. He said that the Dean & Director had wanted to discuss the justifications of each one of the 14 items. Syed Fahim Uddin reported that the Registrar had discussed with the Dean & Director and then the Dean & Director advised to place the matter before the EC.

4.4 Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that in the largest interest of IBA and to save the image of IBA in the market, EC should regularize the maintenance work carried out in urgency and grant ex-post facto approval.

DECISION

4.5 The Committee granted ex-post factor approval for the work carried out in urgency. The total amount involved is Rs.898,155/-

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
