Item No. 2      To consider note from the Director Projects regarding conveyance                    arrangements for maintenance staff and purchase officer for City   Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects in his note of January 31, 2009 stated that there is no conveyance available to any of the maintenance staff stationed at City Campus who have to ply between City Campus and Main Campus especially when the construction site is to be visited to attend electrical problems at Main Campus, Academic and Admin Blocks as well as staff colony and boys hostel.  He stated that as the purchase of maintenance is to be managed by the             Maintenance Department, there is a need for the availability of a vehicle at the City Campus which can be used for maintenance purchases for both campuses.

2.2       The Director Projects also stated in his note that Mr. Muhammad Jawad Khan,                Electrical Engineer has sent an application stating that he was given an understanding at the time of his appointment that he would be provided a pick and drop facility. The Director Projects said that in the organogram prepared by him it was proposed to create a Central Electrical Maintenance Section headed by Mr. Muhammad Jawad Khan who would look after the overall electrical maintenance works of both the Campuses. He requested that one vehicle (Suzuki Bolan, preferably new) be allocated specially for the maintenance section stationed at City Campus. b)     To consider   email from   Mr.  M.  Akmal   Khan   Assistant Admin Officer CCS Office and reply from Dr. Sayeed Ghani and Dr Nasir  Touheed on the issue of enrolment of two BCS students.

8.5       Mr. M. Akmal Khan Assistant Admin Officer, CCS informed Dr. Sayeed Ghani that the Enrolment Section raised the objection that Ms. Amina Tahir  and Mr. Anwar-ul-Hassan (both students of BCS 6th semester of IBA) have no  mathematics in their intermediate/ A level.

8.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani referred the issue to the Executive Committee with the comments that the enrolment office has determined that the two students have not satisfied one of the requirements of admissions i.e. having maths at inter or A level. He requested the Testing / Admission department to check as to how this happened.  He also requested the enrolment department to complete the process of checking faster as if that had been checked within the same Semester then it would have a different impact. He further requested that the student be allowed to continue as they were given admission to IBA based on interview and documents checking. If they have not satisfied an eligibility criterion, IBA cannot hold them responsible after a lapse of two and a half years.

8.7       Mr. Mobin Khalili and the Registrar pointed out the fool-proof method of obtaining all missing information from selected candidates prior to issuing the admission letter as used to be practiced in the past and suggested that thorough information gathering about selected students must be done by the Admission Committee to avoid any such lapses.

DECISION

8.8       The Committee decided that since it was not the fault of the students, therefore, the student cannot be punished.  The students were allowed to continue.

8.9       For future it was decided that the Testing Department will be responsible for conducting the test and handover the result to Admission Office and the Admission Committee will take decisions about the cut off point etc. The Admission Committee, before issuing admission letters through Registrars Office, will ensure that all the requirements have been met.

c)        Discrepancies in the new Toshiba Laptop pointed out by Mr. Irfan Nabi, faculty member.

8.10     Dr. Arshad Siddiqi reported that he has received a letter from the vendors of Toshiba laptop in which they have clarified that they have provided SQL 2005 free of cost which other laptop brands do not provide. They have also stated that the standard model has 1GB of RAM whereas they have installed 2GM RAM free of cost. They have also stated that the Centrino stickers are provided by Intel to all the OEMs.

8.11     The Director wanted to know if the laptops are brand new or not to which the Director I.T. said that he had investigated and is satisfied that the Toshiba laptops were brand new.

DECISION

8.12     The Committee decided that comments from Mr. Irfan Nabi should be obtained on the letter received from the Vendors (O&A Business Machines (Pvt.) Limited).

8.13     The Committee requested Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to send the report of the Committee he had formed to investigate and evaluate conformity with the specifications of the laptops and its being brand new along with comments from Mr. Irfan Nabi to the Executive Committee.

ACTION

8.14     Dr. Arshad Siddiqi will send report of the Committee along with comments from Mr. Irfan Nabi on the Toshiba laptops to the Executive Committee.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
