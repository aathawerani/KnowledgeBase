MINUTES OF THE 140TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, FEBRUARY 7, 2011

 

The 140th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, February 7, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

3.      Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

4.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

5.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

6.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                               Director CED

 

The following attended on special invitation:

 

1.      Mr. Shameem Ahmed       Director HEC Projects

2.      Mr. Mehmood Bughio       Project Engineer

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve appointment of Project Manager for Detailed Supervision and Project Management of Renovation & Refurbishment of Existing Administration Building at IBA Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that a meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee was held on Saturday, January 1, 2011 to discuss and decide the appointment of Project Managers for Detailed Supervision and Project Management of Renovation & Refurbishment of existing Administration Building at IBA Main Campus. Out of ten Project Managers, there were six Project Managers who submitted the Technical Proposals and out of 100, they scored the following points mentioned against each of the six Project Managers:

 

·         M/s. AAA Partnership (Private) Limited              85 Points

·         M/s. Consult-Tech International                      88 Points         

·         M/s. Shahzad Associates                                62 Points

·         M/s. NESPAK                                                88 Points         

·         M/s. INTEG                                                  88 Points         

·         M/s. Arif Shah Associates                               77 Points         

 

1.2       After the Technical Evaluation, the Financial Proposals were opened in the presence of the Technical Evaluation Committee. The financial proposal of M/s. Shahzad Associates was not opened as they scored less than 75 points and were not able to meet the minimum technical criteria. The rates quoted by the five Project Managers are mentioned as under:

 

o   M/s. AAA Partnership (Pvt.) Limited            3.23% of the total cost of Project

o   M/s. Consult-Tech International                 3.53% of the total cost of Project

o   M/s. NESPAK                                           3.00% of the total cost of Project

o   M/s. INTEG                                             3.00% of the total cost of Project

o   M/s. Arif Shah Associates                          1.50% of the total cost of Project

 

1.3       The Director Projects presented the Comparative Statement prepared on the basis of 80:20 rules as per the IBAs Procurement, Policy & Procedure based on the Single Stage Two Envelop Bidding Procedure. According to the Comparative Statement, M/s. NESPAK & M/s. INTEG received equal scores of 88 points and their financial bids were also equal at 3%; whereas M/s. Arif Shah Associates had scored 77 points in technical evaluation but emerged as the lowest bidder in financial proposal.

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that in order to select the best Project Managers based on their technical capability, the three Project Managers namely; M/s. NESPAK, M/s. Integ, and M/s. Arif Shah Associates were invited to make a presentation before the TEC on the following aspects of the proposals:

 

·         Methodology of Work

·         Competency of their design review team and their resumes

·         Detailed input schedules

·         The financial composition of their financial proposal with charge rate of their proposed staff.

 

1.5       The Director Projects reported that on the recommendation of TEC for considering the award of work to M/s. NESPAK, a meeting of the Central Purchase Committee (CPC) was held on 29th January, 2011 and the CPC also endorsed the recommendation of the TEC to award the work to M/s. NESPAK. He requested the Executive Committee to consider and recommend the award of work to M/s. NESPAK at a rate of 3.0% of the total cost of the Project. 

 

1.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know the basis on which M/s. NESPAK were recommended and M/s. Integ were not considered.

 

1.7       The Director Projects referred to the minutes of the meeting of Technical Evaluation Committee held on January 24, 2011 in which justifications for recommending M/s. NESPAK and not considering M/s. Integ, and M/s. Arif Shah Associates were noted.

 

1.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to include working relation of the Project Management firm with the Consultant in the Technical Evaluation.  He also suggested that IBA should go on the merit of the organization no matter if IBA has to pay extra cost for the quality work.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee approved the appointment of M/s. NESPAK as Project Managers for detailed supervision and project management of renovation and refurbishment of existing Administration Building at IBA Main Campus at 3% of the total cost of the Project.

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the estimated cost for construction of two new KESC sub-stations at the Main Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that the proposed locations for construction of two new KESC sub-stations were considered and approved by the Executive Committee in its meeting held on August 23, 2010. He said that one of the sub-stations was required to be built at the Boys Hostel premises, and the other at Staff Colony, as per KESC drawing and specifications.  He informed the Committee that the KESC has now given the cost estimates for providing the two HT connections and now they require IBA to have the two sub-stations ready as soon as possible. He said that the two connections would be given to IBA from KESCs new grid, located in Gulistan-e-Jauhar, which is almost ready.
2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the in-house Quantity Surveyor has estimated that the total cost of construction of the two sub-stations will be Rs.. million. He presented the break-up of the estimated cost. He clarified that this estimate is for civil engineering work only. The cost of electrical equipment will be over and above it and will be estimated by the newly appointed electrical consultant who will also be responsible for designing the distribution network. He said that as the building work is a small project costing about Rs. million only, its tender documents will be prepared in-house. The construction work will also be supervised by IBAs own engineers/supervisors. For inviting bids the tenders will be issued to 18 petty contractors who were pre-qualified last year (2010). He requested the Executive Committee to approve the small project as well as its aforesaid estimated cost.
2.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that they would do their best to have the two sub-stations ready before the end of April 2011 so that faculty members and other staff residing in the Staff Colony as well as the hostel residents may benefit from the enhanced power supply in the coming summer.
 
2.4       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia clarified that the Electrical Consultant has been appointed only for the Main Campus. For designing the distribution network for the Staff Town and the Boys Hostel their scope of work has to be increased.
 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee approved the small project (construction of two new KESC sub-stations) and its estimated cost of Rs......million.

 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Concern over the delay in award of contract for new Academic Block.

 

3.1       The Director Projects referring to the decision of the EC meeting held on January 31, 2011 on getting the LOI vetted through the legal advisor of IBA said that the matter needs to be decided expeditiously as four months have passed since the bid was submitted and the Consultant was engaged. He said that during the last four months the cost of various items has increased considerably. He informed the Committee that the Contractor has warned that they can ask escalation compensation against these items before signing LOI/Contract. He said that they cannot bind the Contractor beyond the validity period of the offer which is of 90 days. He further said that if the lowest bidder runs away then the next bidder will be higher by Rs.20 million.     

           

DECISION

 

3.2       The Committee noted the concern of the Director Projects and requested the Director Finance to ask the legal advisor to expedite the vetting of the draft so that it could be sent to the A&FC for approval.

 

b)         Request for extension in the deadline given by the EC for shifting of DB.

 

3.3       The Director ICT reported that because of the complexities involved, the shifting of DB from the old Telephone Operators Room cannot be completed in 10 days time as given by the EC in its last meeting.  He said that it would take at least a month and requested the EC to extend the time.

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee requested the Director ICT and the Director Projects to see the Dean & Director and inform him of the complexities involved in shifting the DB.    

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
