Item No. 1      To consider and approve the revised ICT Policies.

1.1       The Director ICT reported that the changes suggested by the EC in its meeting held on January 3, 2011 have been incorporated in the ICT Policies.

DECISION

1.2       The Committee reviewed the modified draft policy with reference to the changes that were suggested in earlier EC meeting and took the following decisions:

·         On the basis of the duties the J3 staff are performing, they were made eligible for emailing facilities.

·         5.2(c) “Lower” was deleted from the title “Lower/Junior Staff”. This section was rephrased as under:

“Staff in category J1 & J2 shall not be authorized emailing facilities and thus no email addresses will be provided to them.  However, they may be given PC, Printer or Scanner on their job requirements. As an exception staff in J2, including ICT junior staff (Lab Assistants, Lab Attendants and Specialists falling in J2,) who are required to use email facilities as part of their duties, shall be authorized to use emailing facilities on the recommendation of the HOD.”

·         11.1 Eligibility:  Junior and J3 to be deleted from the first sentence.

·         Procedures for Computer Disposal:  After point 4, the following 5th point was added in the Procedures for Computer Disposal:

“5.  IBA’s Fixed Assets Disposal Committee will regulate all matters in regard to disposal of surplus and obsolete ICT equipments.”

1.3       The Director H.R. was advised to make allocate a space in each issue of the Internal Publication and include the names, contact numbers and email addresses of helpdesk persons in that space/box.  

ACTION

1.4       The Director ICT would present the revised ICT Policy incorporating the changes mentioned in 1.2 above to the A&FC for consideration. 

 

1.5       The Director H.R. would ensure that the names, contact numbers and email addresses of helpdesk persons are published in every issue of the Internal Publication.

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      CNBC’s program “Badalta Pakistan” in collaboration with IBA.

 

3.1       The Registrar reported that CNBC has proposed to record a 52 serial program “Badalta Pakistan” with IBA. He said that as per draft agreement proposed by CNBC, IBA will be required to provide facilities of auditorium and students as audience. The sponsorship for the program will also be required to be arranged by IBA in coordination with VNTV - the Production Unit. Presently a sum of Rs.4 million in sponsorship is targeted of which Rs.1 million has been committed by Silk Bank. The Registrar informed that IBA would benefit in this through the media coverage that its students and IBA’s name would get. However, he foresees some problems in terms for arranging the sponsors as all payments are to be made through IBA. He also informed that the agreement has been vetted by the Finance Directorate and IBA’ Legal Advisor who had proposed some corrections, a meeting in this regard to be held with CNBC shortly.

DECISION

3.2       The Director said that as IBA will get free publicity through this program and the IBA students will also get the opportunity of projection and interaction with eminent personalities, he thought it was a good venture. However, he suggested to include a termination clause in the agreement that if no sponsorship is received then the agreement may be terminated. He also suggested that the sponsors may pay directly to the VNTV and the IBA would provide facilities and the students for their program.  The Committee agreed with the Director.         

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
