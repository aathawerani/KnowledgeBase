MINUTES OF THE  42nd MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON APRIL 8, 2009

The 42nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Wednesday, April 8, 2009 at 9:00 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Dean & Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dea
  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN            Registrar
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  6. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  7. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                            Director I.T.
  8. Ms. Ayesha Menai                               Director H.R.
  9. Mr. Shameem Ahmed                           G.M. (P&D) by special invitation

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To discuss draft IBA Budget 2009-10

1.1              The Director Finance presented the draft IBA Budget 2009-10. The Committee thoroughly discussed the draft budget.  It was noted that the major portion of the capital expenditure relates to I.T. and Projects Departments. The Director I.T. and the Director Projects assured that they would be able to complete the assigned projects on time and utilize the amount allocated to their respective departments.  

1.2              The Committee noted that sufficient fund is available for I.T. up-gradation in the current budget. The Committee advised the Director Projects to upgrade the bandwidth this year.

The Director said that technology up-gradation would be done out of Rs.105 million of Aman Foundation.

The Director I.T. assured that all items could be procured but requested for a warehouse.  The Director Projects was advised to arrange a store for I.T.

DECISION

1.3       The Committee recommended the revised draft IBA Budget 2009-10 to the A&FC for consideration.

ACTION

1.4       The Director Finance will present the revised draft of IBA Budget 2009-10 to the A&FC for consideration.

Item No. 2      Bids for ERP

2.1       The Director I.T. reported that M/s. Siemens have given the lowest bid for ERP/SAP. He said that the SAP has not been tried and tested in any of the campuses of Pakistan whereas PeopleSoft is working in Aga Khan University, Dow University, LUMS and many other universities and they are tested and tried. He said that although SAP has done industry projects, yet they have not done campus solution which is the core of our ERP. He said that M/s. Siemens might have given their bids below their own cost in order to penetrate in the Pakistani market.

2.2       The Head of Internal Audit was requested to advise what options are available to IBA on this issue. The Head of Internal Audit said that re-tendering will require reprocessing and that would be more time consuming. He said that the only option is that we can cancel the bid but there has to be a cogent ground to cancel the bid. 

2.3       It was suggested that those experts of SAP who have implemented campus solution in other countries will have to be on the team. The Head of Internal Audit suggested that we should take that in writing from them.

2.4       The Director I.T. reported that Siemens have given in writing that every cost including maintenance and up-gradation cost is included in the bid.

2.5       The Registrar said that if the bidder agrees that they would meet all the requirements then the risk is minimised. He said that the IBA will benefit from Siemens because they would hopefully provide better services in order to market their products.

2.6       The Director Projects wanted to know if Siemens would give us professional indemnity bond in case of failure.    

2.7       The Director I.T said that Siemens have been communicated what we need and they have agreed to provide what we need.  He further said that as we are part of the SAP Alliance and we are training our faculty and students in SAP, we will be able to operate the system.

DECISION

2.8       The Committee decided that Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Dr. Arshad Siddiqi, and Dr. Sayeed Ghani would find and recommend a solution on ERP issue.

ACTION

2.9       Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Dr. Arshad Siddiqi, and Dr. Sayeed Ghani would submit their recommendation to the EC on ERP issue.   

Item No. 3      Approval for acquiring Mittha Hall of APWA on rent for shifting the City Campus library.

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the APWA administration has agreed to rent out to IBA the Mittha Hall which is the main office of APWA but he said that they are demanding a rent of Rs.200,000/- per month for this Hall. He said that the total area of the Mittha Hall is around 4500 sqft. whereas the third floor which has an area of 20,000 sq.ft. they are giving us on a monthly rent of Rs.400,000/-

3.2       The Director said that we need the hall also as we have to shift the library. He further said that APWA administration was initially not willing to rent out the Mittha Hall but on our request they have agreed. He said that the monthly rent of Rs.600,000/- (Rs.400,000 + Rs.200,000) for around 24,500 sq.ft that we would be paying to APWA is far below the market rate. As it comes to around Rs.24/- per sq.ft.

DECISION

3.3       The Committee decided to acquire the third floor as well as the Mittha Hall of APWA at a monthly rent of Rs.600,000/-

ACTION

3.4       The G.M. (P&D) will finalize the tenancy agreement with APWA.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
