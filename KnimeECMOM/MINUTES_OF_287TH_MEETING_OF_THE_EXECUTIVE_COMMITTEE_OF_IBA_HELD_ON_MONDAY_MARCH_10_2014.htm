The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1 Review of Promotion Recommendations.

1.1 Before presenting the promotion recommendations, Mr. Adnan Hameed, presented the highlights from the Promotion Rule for Non-Teaching Employees for three types of promotions; Promotion through appointment, Promotion through up-gradation of position, and Seniority based promotion. He also presented the promotion ladder for IBA Scale as well as BPS Scale Employees.

1.2 The Dean & Director pointed out that the Promotion Policy on Promotion through Up-gradation of Position states that the Director IBA approves promotion in BPS 1-15 / Job Band J3 to 1 but the Seniority based Promotion policy which is applicable to BPS 1-15, is applicable to J2 and J1 employees only. He said that there seems to be some discrepancy and inconsistency; as it allows J3 to 1 at one place and J2 to J1 at another place while there is no change in the BPS group of 1 to 15. He suggested to revise the Promotion Policy striking equivalence in BPS and Job Band and get it approved by the Board.

1.3 The Dean & Director said that in future the HR should present details of how many employees have been recommended for up-gradation, and how many for promotion on seniority basis, how many were promoted last year and how many of them have been recommended for promotion/up-gradation for the period under review. He further said that the HR must ensure before presenting the cases to the EC that the recommended cases meet the eligibility criteria.

1.4 The Committee considered the justifications given by the concerned departmental heads of Alumni Affairs, Admin (Boys Hostel), Administration (General), General Repair & Maintenance, Graduate Program Office, Undergraduate Program Office, Associate Dean Office, Finance, ICT, and Projects on each of the recommendations for promotion/up-gradation.

1.5 The Dean & Director said that he is thinking of restructuring the teaching Departments on the pattern of LUMS where for every department there is an Admin. Assistant who works for the faculty of the entire department rather than working for the chairperson only. He suggested to keep the recommendation of Associate Dean’s office on hold as the position would be reconfigured according to the new structure.

1.6 On the recommendation for upgrading the position of Assistant Admin. Officer (Undergraduate Program Office), to Admin. Officer, it was pointed out that since there is already a Manager in the Undergraduate Program Office, how the up-graded position would fit in into the structure. The Dean & Director said that the structure would not be changed but if the individual is eligible for promotion on merit cum seniority, he can be considered for promotion. He further said that individuals to be promoted as Managers / Senior Managers on the basis of merit plus seniority would not continue to be necessarily working in the same office; rather he/she would be on the pool of resources and transferred to any department where he / she can be of greater use. Mr. Adnan Hameed informed that there is a vacancy of Manager Graduate Program Office. The recommended individual can be considered for that position. The Dean & Director said that in this case the position is not up-graded; rather the individual is recommended to the Selection Board for promotion under the category of Seniority-cum-Merit based promotion. If the Selection Board approves his promotion, he would be promoted accordingly, and if the position of Manager Graduate Program is commensurate with his new grade he could be posted against that vacancy.

1.7 The Dean & Director said that the same rule of Seniority-cum-Merit should be applied on the recommendation for promotion of Manager (Undergraduate Program Office) to Senior Manager. Mr. Adnan Hameed was asked to present a list of eligible IBA non-teaching employees in the cohorts of BPS-16 and above, and Job Band 2 and above along with dates of their last promotion, annual performance evaluation, and other relevant information for consideration in the next EC meeting. The recommendations for promotion of Assistant Admin. Officer (Undergraduate Program Office), and Manager (Undergraduate Program Office) would be considered in the next EC meeting.

Item No. 3 To consider and approve the amendment in payment terms for the contract with M/s. Al-Shafi for the project “Renovation and Refurbishment of existing Administration Building, Main Campus.

3.1 The Director Projects reported that contract for renovation and refurbishment of Existing Administration building was awarded to M/s Al-Shafi Enterprises on February 13, 2012. The total time duration for completion of the project was eighteen (18) months as per contract. However, as the building was operative at the time of start of work therefore IBA couldn’t hand over the required areas on time which caused the delays in execution of work. An extension of four (04) months up till December 2013 was given to contractor but due to law and order situation and the shortage of manpower and logistics because of CNG load shedding and changes in various floors, planned finish date was extended to June 30th 2014 after approval of the Executive Committee.

3.2 The Director Projects reported that M/s Al-Shafi have also completed Visiting Faculty Hostel project but due to some reasons timely payments could not be processed for the said project which caused the cash flow problem with their firm. He said that in order to complete the subject project in a smooth way up till June 2014 they need to match the cash flow input for speedy works and delivery at site. This will address their cash flow problems at the same time and it will ensure completion by June 2014. The Director Projects reported that the Contractor has requested that the limit of adhoc payment for their running bills be increased to 70% against the checked running bills instead of 50%. He said that Nespak has already recommended the limit of 70% as the payment is based on actual joint measurements of the actual physical works done at site.

3.3 The Director Projects stated that after analyzing the conditions and cash flow of M/s Al-Shafi the projects Department recommended to the Procurement Committee that the limit of adhoc payment to M/s. Al-Shafi for running bills may be increased to 70%. In its meeting held on March, 2014, the matter was considered by the Procurement Committee, which approved the amendment in contract to increase the payment upto 70% of the running bill checked by the Project Managers and the Consultants, while the balance 30% to be released after the recording of measurement book. Based on the approval of the Procurement Committee, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve this amendment in contract.

DECISION

3.4 The Committee approved the amendment in contract to make adhoc payment upto 70% of the running bills and the balance 30% to be released after the recording of measurement book.


Item No.4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Approval for Underground water tank, plastic water tank of 500 gallons and fixing of safety valves at the kitchen of New Girls’ Hostel.

4.1 The Registrar reported that there is no underground and overhead water tank at the new Girls Hostel. He said that the water pipe line got burst and there was no check valve. The Registrar said that although he had temporarily arranged an old plastic water tank, there is an urgent need of an underground water tank and a plastic water tank of 500 galls at the kitchen of new Girls’ Hostel, and fixing of four safety valves which would cost around Rs.800,000/-.

DECISION

4.2 The Committee requested the Director Projects to get these works done through the External Development contractor.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
