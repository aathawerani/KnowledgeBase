The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      Up-date on payment of electricity bills to Karachi University.

 

1.1       The Registrar gave a background of the IBA – KU electricity set up as it was over the years and stated that during the last three years IBA has initiated a series of works aimed at rebuilding its antiquated infrastructure. To meet the much enhanced demands of power for new / rebuilt infrastructure, IBA has acquired several independent KESC electric connections at both campuses. He said that the newly acquired connections at Main Campus also aim at eventually reducing IBA’s dependence for electricity on Karachi University.  However, to enable the eventual delinking from Karachi University it was essential that IBA settles its almost seven year long outstanding electricity dues with Karachi University.

 

1.2       The Internal Audit in their Audit Report presented to the A&FC on April 4, 2011 raised the issue of non-settlement of electricity bills of Karachi University. The A&FC suggested that if there is no dispute on the number of units consumed, then IBA should pay the bills as per year-wise KESC tariff for the periods 2001-2007 and make the liability zero. The A&FC also suggested to make payment for whatever mount IBA thinks is correct for the bills pertaining to the period of 2007 onward. Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects was assigned this task. 

 

1.3       The Registrar reported that Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects worked on this project for about six months but expressed his dissatisfaction on his contacts with KU officials. According to his findings, the Karachi University had insufficient record and were non-cooperative. He (Mr. Shameem) nevertheless identified an amount of Rs.387,180/- payable to KU for 2003 time frame in respect of electricity dues for all power provided to IBA.  For 2004 to 2010 no record was available either with KU or IBA. The A&FC got annoyed that nobody at IBA was taking / having the responsibility of keeping track of electricity bills. The Director then pointed out that it was the responsibility of the Finance dept to ensure that bills were collected and paid. The Registrar having Maintenance Dept under him was tasked by the A&FC to resolve the matter of unpaid bills for the period 2004 – 2010.  The Registrar informed the EC that he had tasked his Maintenance Manager and Electrical Engineer  to do a thorough analysis of the issue and had prepared a presentation which he showed to the EC members along with findings of his committee which are as under:  

 

·         No system of bill collection from Karachi University ever existed.

·         The IBA has been deducting electricity charges @Re.1/- per unit from IBA Staff Town residents on regular basis and depositing the same with KU through the period 2004-2011. A sum of Rs.1,476,989/- has been paid to KU for which payment receipts from KU are available with Finance. The subsidized rate of Re.1/- per unit at which electricity charges were to be collected from residents were revised in December 2005 by the KU Syndicate and enhanced to Rs.2/- per unit which was subsequently enhanced to Rs.4/- per unit w.e.f. March 2007.  Intimation of this was never received by the IBA and we have to-date continued to collect and deposit electricity charges at the old rate.

·         No record exists with Finance / Audit Departments of any payments made to KU for the 4 bulk power provided by them at Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel during the period 2004-2011. As a result of successive meetings of Cdr. Faheem Uddin, Manager General Maintenance / Security, with KU officials, they have provided data on total amount due against total units consumed individually by 5 bulk power connections provided at our three facilities namely; Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel, and Staff Town.

·         Karachi University is unable to share with us the data on the total units consumed by each connection – their engineering department has however provided us year and month wise details of the amount payable by IBA in respect of electricity charges per year for each connection.

·         As per observation the arrears reported by Mr. Shameem Ahmed do not account for multiplication factor applied as a standard feature in all KESC bills, thus the meagre amount for 2003. Likewise the arrears shown for 2009 and 2010 are Rs.2,723,425/- and Rs.1,947,577/-  The average monthly payment to KESC for Main Campus along with meter readings for the 400 KW connection is Rs.1,237,469/- Thus justifying the accumulated arrears of 8 years of KU to be Rs.24,419,525/-  Details presented in the Breakdown of Outstanding Payments.

 

1.5       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to why we are delinking power connections with the Karachi University when the KU is providing electricity at a highly subsidized rate.

 

1.6       The Director Projects informed Mr. Khalili that KU is providing electricity to IBA Staff Town with a very weak feeder. He further said that KU had advised that they were laying all their cables  underground so IBA should also do that which we did for Boys’ Hostel. However, he said that since our electricity demand is increasing, KU does not have extra electricity to meet our increasing demands. He further said that we have applied for a new connection from KESC for IBA Staff Town.

 

1.7       The Registrar said that until we get power supply from KESC at the new substations constructed at the IBA Staff town and Boys Hostel, we would continue to take electricity from Karachi University for the IBA Staff Town and the Boys’ hostel. But the residents of IBA Staff Town will be charged at the enhanced rate.

 

1.8       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know if the KESC tariff is the same for Karachi University as well as IBA or it is different.

 

1.9       The Registrar said that he has no knowledge about it but he opined that KESC would probably be charging the same rate from all educational institutions.

 

1.10     Cdr. Faheem Uddin said that KU is receiving bulk supply for which the rate is lesser than supplies in small units.

 

DECISION

 

1.11     The Committee decided that the outstanding amount which has been worked out at Rs.24.42 million is reasonable as the tariff as well as utilization of power at IBA Main Campus had gone through many changes and much increased over the years. The Director Finance recommended that the arrear amount bill should be paid to Karachi University in instalments. The Committee also suggested that in order to ensure that no subsequent claim is received from KU for payment of electricity bills, the settlement should be made at a higher level and be duly receipted by KU.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve commencement of works at Aman Tower – Arrangement of Alternate parking space.

 

4.1       The Director Projects in his email addressed to Mr. Mobin Khalili stated that the vacant ground in the east of existing Academic Block of the City Campus which is being used for parking now is the place where Aman Tower and Auditorium will be built. The contractor will mobilize to the site on 15th November 2011. Therefore, they needed the ground to be vacated by 15th November 2011.

 

4.2       In response to the email of the Director Projects, Mr. Khalili replied that the ground is used for parking of IBA buses, vans and also for faculty and students’ cars. Once this ground is cordoned off by the contractor, serious problem already faced by them in managing heavy movements of vehicles will further aggravate and it will become unmanageable. He said that he had raised this issue a number of times in EC meetings. He requested to find a solution to this problem before the area is handed over to the contractor. He further stated that the Dean & Director had advised to explore the possibility to use the adjacent vacant plot of old Income Tax Building for parking of IBA vehicles. He requested the Registrar to use his contacts in this regard.

 

4.3       The Registrar said that he had talked to the Chowkidar of the old Income Tax Building for parking of IBA vehicles and the Chowkidar said that he would talk to the owner of the building who is settled in Australia.

 

4.4.      The Registrar requested the Director Projects to ask the Contractors to let us know how much minimum space they would require. He said that to make parking space available, the IBA buses would not be parked at City Campus and would be brought back to Main Campus.

 

4.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to talk to concerned person of Prince Cinema for allowing us to park the cars for which IBA should pay.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee requested the Registrar to explore all those possibilities mentioned above and present his findings in the next EC meeting.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
