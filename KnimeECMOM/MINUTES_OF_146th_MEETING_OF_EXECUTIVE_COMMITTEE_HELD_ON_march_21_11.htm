MINUTES OF THE 146TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MARCH 21, 2011

 

The 146th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 21, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                    Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

9.      Ms. Ayesha A. Minai                                Director H.R.

 

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve formation of special committees:

i)        for evaluation of RFP for appointment of consultant / project managers for Data Centre, and

ii)      for evaluation of Technical and Financial bids for Data Centre.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the advertisement for the selection of Data Centre Consultants / Project Managers was floated on 3rd March 2011. The Technical and Financial Bids of the Consultants / Project Manager would be received on or before   29th March 2011. He also reported that the advertisement for obtaining Technical & Financial bids was floated on 9th March 2011 which would be received till 31st March 2011.  He said that these actions were taken in accordance with the directives of the Executive Committee. He further said that while the above decisions were taken, the Executive Committee was requested to form a special committee for evaluation of Technical and Financial Bids wherein it was also recommended to include an independent Data Centre Expert from any of the prestigious institution in Pakistan.

 

1.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that it was a single stage two envelope system. The Technical Evaluation in both cases will also include the prequalification process of the Consultants / Project Managers in case (i), and the prequalification of the data centre Contractors in case (ii). In both the cases the financial bids will be opened only of those companies which are prequalified during Technical Evaluation.  He further said that the Projects Department does not have qualified persons to evaluate the technical bids as ICT Department has already rejected the Consultants of ICON and the INTEG who had designed and prepared the tender documents. He said that Mr. Umar Makhdoomi provided extensive co-ordination during design stage and tender documentation stage and obtained approval from the ICT Department.  

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that the Design and Tender Documents of Data Centre of Main Campus were also being designed by ICON Consultants supported by INTEG Engineers. The Data Centre Consultants/Project Managers to be appointed would scrutinize the design, provide detailed supervision and Project Management of the data centre at City Campus and Main Campus. 

 

1.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the formation of two independent committees having expertise for the evaluation of the RFP to be received from Consultants / Projects Managers and a committee having expertise in evaluation of the Technical and Financial bids of the Data Centres construction.

 

1.5       The Director suggested that instead of forming special committees, the matter should be resolved in two stages.  In the first stage after evaluating the Consultants bid, it should be sent to the Technical Evaluation Committee which will select the Consultant. In the second stage, the selected Consultant will help in selecting the Contractors.

 

1.6       The Director Projects agreeing to the suggestion said that in the sequence of the bidding and evaluation process, there would be a delay of at least 2 to 3 weeks in the selection of the contractors.

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee agreed with the Director that there was no need to form special committees as two experts namely Dr. Shakeel Khoja and Mr. M. Faisal Zia are already on the Technical Evaluation Committee who can evaluate and select the Consultant and the Consultant thus selected would help in selecting the Contractors.  The Committee allowed the delay of at least 2 to 3 weeks in the process but requested the Director Projects to try to speed up the process of selection of consultants.               

 

 

Item No. 2           Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider the purport of discussions initiated and suggestions made through the string of emails exchanged on the subject of Faiz Day.

 

2.1       The Committee discussed the string of emails on the subject of Faiz Day and observed as under:

 

(i)         Whereas the discussion through emails had allowed free exchange of diverse views / opinions on the said topic it lacked direction; and had a lot of repetition.

 

(ii)        The use of IBA email system as a medium overburdened the same and also caused inconvenience to majority of the users, particularly those who had no interest in the matter.

 

DECISION

 

2.2       The Committee decided that for the future, a separate discussion group /blog (e.g. “IBA Forum / Blog”) will be created to facilitate such discussions on various important and useful topics.  However faculty and students who are not interested will have the option of removing their name from the discussion group or having such e-mails routed to a different part of their e-mail inbox. 

 

b)     Vacating of house at IBA Staff Town occupied Mr. Mohammad Younus, ex-Daftary of IBA.

 

2.3      The Registrar reported that one of the quarters in Staff Town was occupied by Mr. Mohammad Younus who retired from service on attaining the age of superannuation in 2008.  However, Mr. Younus filed a petition in the Court that his date of birth was wrongly entered by his parents and he has ten years more to reach the age of 60.  His case was dismissed by the Honourable Court but he again filed a case which was also dismissed in October last year. The Registrar thereafter consulted the IBA Lawyers on whose advice Mr. Younus was issued instructions to vacate the IBA quarter as he was no longer entitled to retain the same. On failing to comply with IBA’s instructions; in January on advice of IBA lawyers; orders for eviction of Mr. Younus were issued and the procedure comprising phased cutting of utilities has been initiated.  Mr. Younus has yet to vacate the premises and is in fact resisting the same. Efforts to counsel Mr. Younus through his neighbours and other residents in the area have failed too to convince him to leave; his plea is that he is a poor man and has no place to shift to.  The Registrar sought advice from the EC on this issue.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee requested the Registrar to pursue the matter allowing some additional time not exceeding 30 days from the date of the meeting i.e. March 21, 2011, to Mr. Mohammad Younus to vacate the house.  The Committee on humanitarian ground also requested the Registrar to examine the feasibility of assisting Mr. Mohammad Younus in getting alternate accommodation outside IBA premises with limited assistance from the IBA Welfare Fund.

 

ACTION

 

2.5        The Registrar would get the above decisions implemented.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
