MINUTES OF THE 136TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JANUARY 10, 2011

 

The 136th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, January 10, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at the CEE, IBA City Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                       Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                      Associate Dean

3.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                Associate Dean

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                   Registrar

5.      Mobin Khalili                                             Controller of Examinations

6.      Mr. Ali Taha                                             Director Finance

7.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                          Director Projects

8.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                      Director ICT

9.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                  Director CED

10.    Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                  Director H.R.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Post Production Support of ERP System.

 

1.1       The Director ICT stated that IBA ICT has implemented nine modules of PeopleSoft Campus Solutions and they are in operation and being used effectively in IBA. He said that it was necessary to have the Post Production Support contract for the ERP System. He reported that the post production support was started by Techlogix from November 2010 after the registration process of the Fall 2010 semester.  He informed the Committee that the HEC subsidized cost of this support agreement is Rs.750,000/- per year. This subsidization depends on the other universities continuing the Support Contract through HEC otherwise IBA will have to negotiate the cost of the Support Contract with Techlogix, which will be higher than this cost.  He said that the present contract will be for next 14 month - November 01, 2010 to December 31, 2011. The cost of the contract will be:

 

·         For November – December 2010:                         Rs.125,000/-

(As per HEC Contract for two months)

·         For January  - December 2011:                             Rs.750,000/-

(If other universities stay with HEC)

Total for Nov. 2010 – Dec. 2011:              

1.2       The Director ICT requested the Executive Committee to approve the total contract for  Rs.875,000/- for fourteen months. Further he sought approval for negotiating with Techlogix for the revised cost of the contract for January to December 2011 and report to the EC in case other universities do not continue with HEC.

                       

1.3       The Director ICT also requested the EC to approve payment of Rs.125,000/- for the 2 months support contract for Nov-Dec 2010.

   

1.4       The Director wanted to know as to what is the scope of their (Techlogix) services.  He suggested to minimise the post installation cost and develop in-house capabilities so that maximum problems are solved in-house.

 

1.5       The Director ICT said that the scope is to resolve problems and make sure that all new releases are installed and staff are trained.  He said that with the passage of time they will gain experience and after a year or so minimum external help would be needed.  He clarified that this contract does not cover finance and HRM and for that another contract will have to be signed.

 

1.6       The Director said that they should have package for all modules and for that he advised the Director ICT to talk to HEC.

 

1.7       The Director Finance suggested that the modules acquired by Aga Khan University or other universities should be seen if they are working properly because they have acquired it in bits and pieces.

 

1.8       The Director said that the whole purpose of ERP is that the CMS, Finance, HR, Financial Aid, Resource Mobilization modules are all linked together and system integrated which can make adjustments automatically.

    

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee approved the payment of Rs.125,000/- for the 2 months support contract for Nov-Dec 2010.

 

1.10     In case other universities do not continue with HEC, the Director ICT would negotiate with Techlogix for the revised cost of the contract for January to December 2011 including HRM and Finance modules and report to the EC for approval.

 

1.11     The Director ICT would obtain separate approval from EC for January to December 2011.

DECISION

ACTION

2.10     The Director ICT would ask Mr. Imran Batada to get a system generated form prepared and send it to all for up-dating mobile phones, landline phone numbers and email addresses after every three months.

3.2       To a question the Director Projects clarified that at the time of external development this facility will be broken.

 

DECISION

Item No. 4      To consider and approve estimate for construction of a Store room at rear end of Academic Block in City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that in the Architectural plan for Academic Block, dated May 05, 2010, one Battery Room and one UPS Room were shown to cater for one UPS of Academic Block only. Thereafter, in a number of meetings with ICT, it was decided that the one UPS room for Academic Block will now incorporate one additional UPS for Admin Block as well due to limited availability of space in Admin Block; so there would be now two UPS in Academic Block UPS Room.

 

4.2       The Director Projects reported that after comprehensive meetings between ICT and Projects Department, ICT furnished the requirement of another separate Battery Room at the end of November 2010; in addition to the one already planned and constructed for Academic Block UPS. This additional Battery Room would be utilized to charge the Admin Block UPS, which had been added in Academic Block UPS Room.   As there was not enough space available for another Battery Room, it was advised by Projects Department that considering the criticality of this requirement and to ensure smooth and unhindered functioning of electrical services, there is no alternative but to turn the planned Store Room adjacent to UPS Room into Battery Room No. 2 to cater for Admin Block UPS.  As the requirement was essential, ICT was allowed to take over the store on the condition that a new store will have to be built for Administration Department.

 

4.3       The Director Projects said that on December 22, 2010, it was decided that a new store room can be built in the rear corridor area behind Academic Block and adjacent to CEE emergency exit stair case - it is the area just to the left of main gate entrance. As the planned Store Room had undergone a “change of status” by virtue of the aforementioned arrangement, the Administrative Staff at City Campus put a requirement of a substitute Store Room at some proper place at the City Campus. He said that the estimated cost estimate of the proposed Store Room would be Rs....../- . He presented the relevant BOQ items and plan for the proposed store room for review and approval of Executive Committee. He said that the total area of the store shall be 90 sft. The store being permanent it will be framed reinforced structure with RCC roof.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee approved the estimated cost of Rs......../- for construction of a Store room at rear end of Academic Block at City Campus.

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and approve appointment of Architects / Engineering Consultants for Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of Sports Grounds and Ancillary Works at IBA Main Campus.

 

5.1       The Director Projects reported that a meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee was held on December 24, 2010 to discuss and decide the Appointment of Architects / Engineering Consultants for Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of Sports Grounds & Ancillary Works at IBA Main Campus. Out of six the following four Architects submitted their Technical Proposals. The points out of 100 scored by them are also mentioned against their names:

 

·         M/s. Habib Fida Ali                      82 Points         

·         M/s. NBCL                                 90 Points         

·         M/s. NESPAK                              75 Points         

·         M/s. Ahed Associates                  75 Points         

 

5.2       The Director Projects reported that after the Technical Evaluation, the Financial Proposals were opened in the presence of the Technical Evaluation Committee and all the Architects except the representative of M/s. Ahed Associates. The rates quoted by the four Architects are as under:

 

·         M/s. Habib Fida Ali                      4.75% of the total cost of Project

·         M/s. NBCL                                 4.00% of the total cost of Project

·         M/s. NESPAK                              5.00% of the total cost of Project

·         M/s. Ahed Associates                  6.00% of the total cost of Project

5.3       The Director Projects stated that as per the Comparative Statement which was prepared on the basis of 80:20 rule, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Limited got a combined valuation of 92 points and obtained 1st Rank. 

 

5.4       The Director Projects reported that he briefed the CPC on January 5, 2011 that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Limited have offered to change the fee from 4% to 3.0% of the total cost of Project in case they are awarded the Contract for Master Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of External Development and Infrastructure at IBA Main Campus. He reported that this was considered and approved by the CPC.

 

5.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the award of work to M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Limited at a rate of 3.0% of the total cost of the Project if they are also awarded the Contract for Master Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of External Development and Infrastructure at IBA Main Campus.

 

5.6       The Director said that he wants to make sure that rigorous scrutiny of the bids are carried out as he does not want to give an impression that all contracts are being given to one Consultant/Architect.  He further said that the fee offer should not be made conditional on the award of other project and must be settled independently.

 

5.7       The Director Projects explained that the prescribed process of procurement has been strictly followed.  He said that the process is absolutely transparent as it has to undergo scrutiny by technical Evaluation Committee, Central Purchase Committee and the Executive Committee. He said that after this process M/s. NBCL have emerged as the lowest evaluated responsive bidder.

 

DECISION

 

5.8       The Committee recommended the appointment of M/s. NBCL to A&FC for the Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of Sports Grounds at 3% of the total cost of the project.
