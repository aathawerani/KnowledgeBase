2.2          The Director Projects said that the old houses were not properly maintained and with the passage of time have deteriorated to an extent where every house needs to be fully renovated. He said that a plan was developed to undertake phase-wise renovation project. Under this plan survey of houses was conducted and details of renovation requirements were listed. He said that detailed drawings were prepared based on which bills of quantities were developed along with detailed specification of the material and workmanship. Tendering was done for four D type houses. As a result of the tendering process the lowest bid price came out as Rs.1503/- per sqft.  However, the Executive Committee rejected the renovation of houses in view of the high cost and advised Projects Department to carry out a pre-feasibility of construction of Ground + 2 Apartments for A & B Type housings and Ground + 3 for C & D Type houses.

 

2.3          The Director Projects said that the most appropriate methodology considered for the preparation of the feasibility could be to compare the cost of new construction as against the cost of renovation and up-gradation of the old houses. In this regard a block of each type A, B, C and D was selected for the construction with three scenarios wherein the built up area of the apartments was varied. In all these three scenarios cost of new construction was compared with the cost of the similar areas for renovation of houses for a true comparison. The income from the rental of the houses was calculated and the pay-back periods were obtained for each scenario by dividing the cash outflow by income per year. The rents given in the Housing Policy of IBA were used.  He said that the pay-back period for scenarios 1, 2, & 3 for one block each of A, B, C & D came to 11 years, 10 years and 9 years respectively. He said that from the above the Executive Committee may choose for any one of the three options for apartments in type A, B, C & D.  He said that in scenario 1 where the total area of all apartments is 29,700 sqft., the estimated total construction cost of 14 new apartments would be Rs.74,250,000/-  In Scenario 2 with a total area of 26,200 sqft, the estimated cost would be Rs.65,500,000/- and in scenario 3 having total area of 24,650 sqft. the estimated cost would be Rs.61,625,000/-  He said that the following may be the benefits of new construction:

 

·         Efficient use of the land mass

·         Availability of ample space for future expansion and also for construction of the facilities for the colony residences such as community centre, parks for children and ladies, jogging track, community club etc.

·         Life of the new construction is 50 years whereas the life of old structure will remain as 10 years because the structure is not being strengthened

Item No.3       To consider and recommend modifications in the Registered Graduates Statutes.

 

3.1       The Registrar highlighted the proposed changes in the rules and regulations for Registered Graduates. He proposed that the functions related to Registered Graduates be transferred from the Registrar to the Director Alumni as the Alumni department has more authentic, complete and up-dated data of IBA alumni as compared to a list of 729 registered graduates which is not up-dated.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director clarified that under IBA Act there is a term called “Registered Graduates”. One member of the Registered Graduates of IBA is elected from amongst themselves to be a member on IBA Board of Governors.  On the contrary IBAINS is a separate body which is established under its Memorandum of Association and it has nothing to do with the “Registered Graduates”.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director agreed that the function of Registration of Graduates should be transferred from the Registrar to the Director Alumni who would become custodian of the Register of all Registered Graduates.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director said that since all IBA graduates are automatically registered with the Alumni Office, there is no need for the students to apply for enrolment in the Register of Registered Graduates. However, he said that there should be a system through which after three years the graduates of IBA Karachi, become eligible to elect or to be elected as member BoG. He also agreed to the proposed change that the Register of Registered Graduates should be closed for purposes of election at least 30 days before the date of election which is currently 40 days.

 

3.5       The Dean & Director said that for the election of Registered Graduates for IBA Board of Governors’ seat each Registered Graduate should be given a unique password to cast her/her vote electronically. He requested the Director ICT to depute some IT person to assign password to the Registered Graduates and arrange casting of votes electronically as and when needed. The deputed person should also devise a system through which the IBA alumni who complete three years post graduation period are included in the list of Registered Graduates. He clarified that the election will be conducted by the Director Alumni.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee requested the Registrar to amend the rules and regulations for Registered Graduates in light of the guidelines noted above and in consultation with the Director Alumni and present the draft to the A&FC/BoG for approval.

7.5       The Director ICT said that later M/s. AMFCO replaced the cheque with Pay Order.  He said that he consulted Mr. Khalid Jhumra who agreed that the Rule 37 applies in this case. He said that the CPC has also approved it.
