MINUTES OF 254th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON TUESDAY, JUNE 25, 2013

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                           Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                 Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                   Controller of Examinations

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                     Director Projects

6.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                  Director Finance

7.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                             Director CED

8.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                            Director H.R.

9.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                 Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Head Librarian, and Mr. Noshad of Icon attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Procurement of Toners for 2013-14.

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Batada stated that the ICT Department, as a custodian of printer toners, has worked on IBA’s toner requirements for 2013-14.  He said that a detailed analysis has been performed for the toner’s requirements for 2013-14. He said that they have proposed two options for the procurement of toners to the Executive Committee for discussion. He presented details of the paper utilization for the last two years, stock in hand, toner purchased last year and the requirements along with options for 2013-14.  He said that the consumption of Laser Printing Paper AA(A-4) in 2011-12 was 1613 and in 2012-13 it was 1596. The average per ream cost is Rs.475/- He presented department-wise consumption of papers during 2011-12, and 2012-13.  He informed the Committee that during 2012-13 Rs.5.687 million was spent on the purchase of toner out of which Rs.2.3 million alone was for the purchase of toner for color printers.

 

1.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that department-wise toner consumption information was not available.  He said that this information could have provided an idea as to which department consumed how much toner. He further said that details have also not been provided as to which departments have been provided with color printers.  He suggested that all color printers should be withdrawn.

 

1.3       Mr. Batada said that in total there are 141 printers including 24 color printers and all of them have completed their warranty period of 3 years. He said that there are two options; Option 1 is either to procure OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer), and Option 2 is to procure Compatible with ‘X’ Series.  He said that Option 2 is most viable option because of the savings in cost, and increased number of print pages.

 

1.4       The Dean & Director said that if the option is viable, is technically sound and comparable to the OEM and there is a saving and if the Head of ICT is satisfied, he should do it on his own rather than bringing it to the EC.  He suggested to place centralised color printers at both the campuses at each building under the ICT Department. ICT Department will keep a record of the requisitions for color prints. He suggested that access to the printers should be through a Code to be provided by the ICT department so it could be identified who prints how much. He said that from September this year the faculty will have to do all their work through LMS. 

 

1.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know if a complete set of four color cartridges has to be purchased for color printers or individual grey/black cartridge can be purchased. Mr. Imran Batada said that he would check it.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee agreed to the suggestions of the Dean & Director as noted at 1.4 above.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Acting Head of ICT would take action according to the decisions noted at 1.4 above.  He would also check if the color printer cartridges are to be purchased in a set of four or only grey/black color cartridge can also be purchased.

 

Item No. 2      Presentation on furniture design for Library by M/s. Icon.

 

2.1       Mr. Noshad of Icon presented the furniture design of Library at Main Campus. The design and its specifications were presented for the Periodical Pamphlets and Newspaper Racks, Tables and Chairs, Lounge Seating-Collaborative Learning, Reading Hall, Carrels, Media Learning, Book Shelves – Stacks, Collaborative Learning,

 

2.2       The Dean & Director asked the Librarian about the trend he has observed on the usage and occupancy of library by the students during two years. Mr. Muhammad Anwar said that during exams the library remained fully occupied because there were no other places.  The Dean & Director said that when the Students’ Centre is ready, the students will get enough space over there besides breakout rooms which they can use during exams. He wanted to know as to who would be using the library in the normal working days.  He asked as to what is the present seating capacity at the Library.  Mr. Noshad replied that there is a seating capacity of more than 250 students in the Library. The Dean & Director suggested that we should do indoor furnishing of the Library according to its usage. Mr. Anwar said that currently there is no library focused teaching but once we get all the facilities, we can make proper utilization of the library services as an aid to teaching.  He further said that faculty members are also showing interest in sitting in the library.

 

2.3       At the Carrels, the Dean & Director suggested to place wooden plank instead of frosted glass if there is significant cost difference.  He also suggested to fix sensor lights if there is not much difference in the cost between sensor and normal light. Mr. Noashad said that sensor light is a bit costly, however, he said that he would check it.

 

2.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to what would be the per square cost of furnishing the library. Mr. Noshad said that they are working on it and would convey it to the Director Projects. 

       

2.5       The Dean & Director said that in the future after a certain time period journals and newspapers will no longer dominate the academic scene; there would be digitalized books, journals and newspapers which would all be available on-line. He wanted to know as to how much flexibility and adaptability is there to switch to those systems and what would be the attraction for the students to come to the library.  Mr. Noshad said that they have provided maximum area with power and connectivity of IT system and have provided casual, normal and gadget area. The Dean & Director asked as to what is the estimated expenditure on furnishing the Library with furniture and fixtures and the recurrent cost. He said that we have to spend minimum on the wear and tear.  Mr. Noshad said that the estimated cost would be between Rs.18 to Rs.20 million.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee approved the furniture design for the Library as presented by Mr. Noshad.  

 

ACTION

 

2.7       Mr. Noshad would take actions on the suggestions noted at 2.3 and 2.4 above.

 

  

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair. 

 

a)      Outsourcing of vacant ICT positions.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Batada reported that the Executive Committee in its 235th meeting held on January 28, 2013 had allowed to outsource four lab staff and one lab engineer. Subsequently, hiring of five persons was also approved by CPC on 9th February 2013.  However, due to recent decision by the EC of keeping the lab functional in NBP Technology Centre, requirement of lab incharges has been increased by one, as compared to the previous requirement of five.

3.2       Mr. Batada reported that a meeting was held on June 3, 2013 between ICT and Admin to discuss the issue of provision of ICT support in classrooms. It was decided in the meeting that since messengers are incapable of providing ICT support, ICT department shall continue to provide ICT support in classrooms.  He said that a request for allowing to outsource three additional staff (one for Lab at Tabba Building, one for FCS Classroom, one for Faysal Bank Academic Block Classroom) was made to the CPC on June 15, 2013. The CPC approved the request for three additional staff and advised to get it approved from EC as well. Mr. Batada said that the requirements have also been discussed with the HR and they have incorporated the same in their upcoming tender. Mr. Batada requested the Executive Committee to approve 3 more staff members till the time of finalizing the tender of HR Dept. He said that they can give notice of 30 days as per contract, in-case they like to have resources through HR tender. 

 

3.3       The Dean & Director said that the support required in classrooms is generally of non-technical nature e.g. switching and turning on multimedia etc which the faculty or students can do. The faculty may contact help desk if there is any issue. He said that the Lab Incharge should also work as a trouble shooter and should work with the Building Incharge so that if there is any technical issue, he may resolve that.  Mr. Imran Batada said that he has only three staff whereas there are 3 labs and 4th will be at Tabba Building plus one staff required for Evening Shift at NBP Technology Center.  The Dean & Director said that this is a management issue and there is no need to outsource three additional staff.  He asked the Director H.R. to have a meeting with the buildings incharge and ICT and sort it out. He suggested to invite classroom support staff also to know what they do. He said that for evening shift the Lab Incharge should work from 3:30 to 9:30 p.m. He clarified that if the lab incharge goes for trouble shooting to any class, the building incharge may take care of the lab. Help desk people can also do classroom trouble shooting. He requested the Director H.R. to develop a SOP for the ICT staff and give it to them. 

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee decided that there was no need to hire 3 additional ICT staff through outsourcing. The Committee agreed to the suggestions of the Dean & Director noted at 3.3 above.

 

ACTION

 

3.5       Mr. Imran Batada would hold meetings with the Registrar, Administrator City Campus along with the Building Incharges. He should assign this task of non-technical support to a person in the Building Incharge office.

 

3.6       The Director H.R. would hold a meeting with all the Building Incharge, Lab Incharge, Help Desk Staff of ICT, ICT Support staff at classrooms and the Acting Head of ICT and resolve the issue of providing IT related support in classrooms. 

 

3.7       The Director H.R. would develop a SOP for the ICT Staff.

     

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
