Item No. 3      To consider proposal of hiring Security Services or Hostel Superintendent for IBA Girls Hostel.

3.1              The Assistant to Registrar submitted a proposal for hiring security services or hostel superintendent for IBA Girls Hostel. She stated that at present there is no proper security arrangement at IBA Girls Hostel. Currently male security guards are performing duties outside the girls hostels which are not appropriate at all from security point. She suggested two options (1) hiring the security services from well-known security companies, or (2) hiring hostel superintendent / matron. She stated that hiring female security guards for girls hostel would cost around Rs.84,000/- per month in addition to transport facility which IBA will have to provide to the lady guards for pick and drop from university gate to hostels at every shift.  She suggested that instead of hiring the services of security company, we should hire a lady superintendent / matron and provide her accommodation facility so that she can look after the hostelites in the Girls Hostel. It would cost around Rs.35,000/- per month towards the salary of the superintendent / matron.

3.2              The Registrar informed the Committee that there is an annexe in which girls are residing but they do not want to stay in that.  He proposed that the annexe may be given to the superintendent / matron and the girls may be shifted to the hostel.

DECISION

3.3              The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Registrar and requested the Director H.R. to prepare an advertisement for a full time Matron.

ACTION

3.4              The Director H.R. would place an ad for a full time Matron.

Item No. 8      Request from Controller of Examinations for two additional security guards for City Campus.

8.1              The Registrar in his note of January 3, 2009 stated that the Controller of Examinations has expressed his distress in the management of car parking at the City Campus. The Controller of Examinations had suggested to remove the point buses parked in the evening at the City Campus or provide him with two additional security guards to manage the said parking.

8.2              The Registrar said that removing the point buses from the City Campus to the Main Campus is a very expensive proposition. The other alternative is more practical. However, he said that we are short of security guards at present as we had to leave some positions unmanned at the Main Campus to transfer three security guards to the City Campus.

8.3              He requested the Executive Committee to give approval for hiring six security guards and three messengers urgently needed for both the Campuses on contract basis.

8.4              It was suggested that during peak hours the buses may be made to stand on the roads outside the IBA premises and later may be brought back.

8.5              The Controller of Examinations said that the roads remain occupied and also the drivers may not be available at that time to move the buses.

8.6              The Controller of Examinations said that another issue is of shortage of Security Guard. He said that some cases of theft have been reported.  To check this, two additional guards; one for morning and another for evening shift may be deployed who can roam around.

8.7              It was also suggested that the IBA buses can be parked in the ground adjacent to Gul Plaza.

DECISION

8.8              The Committee decided that the Controller of Examinations would see if the space outside IBA is available or not to park the buses. He would also see if the buses can be parked on the ground adjacent to Gul Plaza.

8.9              Regarding additional Security Guards and other security arrangements, the Dean & Director said that we should wait till the Security Officer joins IBA

ACTION

8.10          The Controller of Examinations would see if the IBA buses could be parked outside IBA premises or on the ground adjacent to Gul Plaza.

Item No. 9      To consider note from Dr. Nasir Touheed regarding uniform timing.

9.1              Dr. Nasir Touheed in his email of January 1, 2009 stated that last year the classes at City Campus were starting at 8:30 a.m. and at Main Campus the timing was 9:00 a.m. He stated that when teaching starts at 9:00 a.m., every staff member, teaching as well as non-teaching plans to reach the campus by 9:00 a.m. and that means each person is wasting daily half an hour at Main Campus. He said that this year the classes at both the campuses are starting from 9:00 a.m. which means we are wasting time across the board.  He suggested that we should start teaching at 8:30 a.m. at both the campuses and save 800 hours of precious time daily.

9.2              He further stated that changing the timings at City Campus from 8:30 a.m. to 9:00 a.m. has another disadvantage  many part time faculty members from the industry like to take 1st period in the morning. Now they will find it difficult to adjust with the new timings.

9.3              He requested the Executive Committee to revise the schedule of teaching and consider starting teaching at both the campuses at 8:30 a.m.

9.4              The Controller of Examinations said that 9:00 a.m. as the ideal time for classes to start because staff members come between 8:30 a.m to 8:45 a.m.  However, he said that for people coming from industry it would not be suitable.

DECISION

9.5              The Committee decided that class timings of both campuses would be changed to 8:30 a.m. from next semester.

9.6              The Committee further decided that the office timings of maintenance, lab and shift staff will start earlier so that they may have a look of the arrangements before the start of the classes.

ACTION

9.7              The Registrar would notify about the class timings for Fall Semester 2009 onward at an appropriate time.

9.8              The Registrar would also announce office timings of maintenance, lab and shift staff which would be earlier than the class timings.
