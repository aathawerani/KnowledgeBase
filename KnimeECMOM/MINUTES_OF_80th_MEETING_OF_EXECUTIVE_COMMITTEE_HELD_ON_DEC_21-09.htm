Item No. 1     To consider and approve Cell Phones & Communication Expenditures Policy.

 

1.1       The Director H.R. presented the Cell Phones & Communication Expenditures policy which included the scope, responsibilities, entitlement and eligibility and other rules and procedures. Giving details of the entitlement and eligibility, the Director H.R. said that there would be two types of entitlement; (1) Company Maintained Cell Phones, and (2) Cell Phone Communication Expenditures. 

 

Company Maintained Cell Phones

·         Director IBA and his/her direct reports.

·         Positions whose job responsibilities require constant accessibility due to reasons of security, field work or special projects e.g.: Security Coordinators, Maintenance Manager,  Protocol Officer, Help Desk IT Manager, Manager Corporate Affairs etc. Director IBA approval will be required before company maintained job related cell phones are sanctioned.

 

Cell Phone Communication Expenditures

·         Staff members who need to make an extraordinary number of outside calls, within and beyond office hours, due to certain peak seasons requirements will, after the approval of their function head, be allowed reimbursement of actual communication expenditures not exceeding the approved limit upon submission of receipts. This facility will prevail only for the specific duration of the period for which the peak season continues. Examples of such events include, but are not confined to, the IBA convocation, recruitment drives, job fairs, events related to faculty coordination work etc.

·         Project related senior staff whose jobs require them to be present in the field for a major part of the working day, on a regular basis where direct lines are not accessible to them. This includes Project Engineer and Supervisor Civil Works.

 

1.2       The Director H.R. presented the maximum limits for company provided mobile sets and for monthly expenditure limits (including line subscription and call charges) for eligible positions.

 

1.3              Mr. M. Faisal Zia, Senior Manager I.T. said that they have not been provided cell phone or phone bills reimbursement facility which they have to make for official purposes. He suggested that Senior Managers, Managers and Assistant Manages with reimbursement limits of Rs.3000/-, Rs.2000/- and Rs.1500/- respectively should be included in the eligible positions.  He said that on more than one occasion he has to make official international calls from his own cell phone but did not charge to IBA and that he had to do because the Telephone Operator at such late hours was not available. He also said that they have the requirements of black berry for official purposes as they have no internet facilities at their home. 

 

1.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that if IT people are given this facility then persons at other departments will also have to be provided this facility.  The Director Finance agreed with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui.

 

1.5              The Director H.R. said that the entitlement shown in the Rules & Regulations is the existing condition and it is not a fresh proposal.\

 

1.6              The Director Projects said that ERP is Rs.300 million project and expenses on phone and internet should be part of the projects because this is necessary for them. He said that the expenditure should be project specific and it should be governed by a general policy which should deal with project specific requirements. He suggested that the ICT department should prepare a budget for their logistics along with a policy and present it to the EC for approval.

 

1.7              The Director Finance said that they can use the Infinity Boxes for emails from their house.  He wanted to know as to who are the persons who have been given Infinity Boxes provided by Mobilink.        

 

DECISION

 

1.8              The Committee decided that the request for reimbursement of cell phone bills may be considered separately in accordance with a policy to be approved. The Committee further decided that the expenses on such account may be charged to the Project. However, it is to be ensured that it is not misused.

 

1.9              The Committee asked Mr. M. Faisal Zia to prepare a budget for their logistic support along with a policy on reimbursement of Cell Phones and Communication Expenditures and present it to the EC for consideration.

 

1.10          The Committee decided that reimbursement on international roaming will be allowed to Dean & Director IBA only. 

 

1.11          The Rules and Regulations on the Cell Phones & Communication Expenditures as presented by the Director H.R. were approved by the Executive Committee.

 

ACTION

 

1.12          Mr. M. Faisal Zia would present a budget for logistic support for ICT department along with a draft policy and present it to the EC for consideration.  He would also provide a list of persons who have been issued Infinity Boxes.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider requests from IBA graduates interested in MBA(Evening Program) for relaxation in condition of one employer for six months work experience prior to admission to MBA(Evening Program).

 

2.1       Mr. Aamir Anwar Ali in his email stated that he had completed his BBA (MIS & Marketing) from IBA in May 2009 and now he is willing to register for MBA Evening Program. He stated that he fulfills all the requirements except six months experience with one employer.  He mentioned in his request that he was offered job by Standard Chartered Bank (Pakistan) Limited on July 10, 2009. At that time, the expected joining date was in August. That could have resulted in 6 months of experience with one employer by the cut-off date of January 31st, 2009, had the induction program of the Bank actually started in August. However, it was later communicated that the program has been delayed. This resulted in eventual delay of the induction program. Due to tough job market situation this year, he did not have any option other than waiting. However, to ensure that he completes six months requirement by the end of January 31, 2009, he started an internship of two months. His work experience at SCB by the end of the said date would be four months. Thus, he meets the criteria of interning for six months, but that is with two employers. He pointed out that this is the last time a BBA student from IBA can start MBA after six months experience. Thus, this is his last chance to continue his studies further with IBA, without any delay. He requested that considering tough job market this year, delay on part of the organization he joined as well as the special circumstances regarding the change of rule from the Fall 2010, he be allowed to register for the MBA Evening Program by relaxing the condition of one employer.

 

2.2       Similar request was made by another candidate Ms. Farzana Siddique Narejo who has been working with Silkbank Limited since October 2009 and before that she served with AKD Securities.

 

2.3              The Registrar said that one of the requirements is that students should have six months experience for admission to MBA but some students have problem that they do not have six months experience in one organization.

 

2.4              Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that this is for the last batch and afterwards two years experience would be required for admission to MBA program.

 

2.5              The Director H.R. said that six months experience is against 18 credit hours and the six months experience is assessed by the faculty.

 

2.6              Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that there should be some tougher requirements for those who have six months experience by working with two different organizations.

 

2.7              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the same evaluation criteria should be applied.

 

DECISION

 

2.8              The Committee accepted six months work experience gained by the candidates at two different organizations for the purpose of admission in MBA(Evening Program.)

 

ACTION

 

2.9       The Registrar would convey the decision to the concerned students.

Item No. 4      To witness the façades finished sample at the City Campus Academic Block and approve or otherwise.

 

4.1       The Director Projects stated that the matter of color and texture of the buildings under renovation and that of the new construction has not yet been resolved as the Architects could not fully explain the ultimate look of the facades through perspectives and even through 3d presentation made to the joint meeting of the EC members and the members of A&FC..

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that as the works on the Academic Block Main Campus and Academic Block City Campus reached the stage where the facade work could be undertaken, Architects M/s ICON had asked contractors of both the buildings to put on the samples of the "washed terrazzo" and "polished Terrazzo". The contractor of Main Campus (M/s Project Managers CC) could not produce satisfactory samples during the past three months, however the contractor of City Campus (M/s. Abaseen) was able to produce samples acceptable to the Architects. He said that as these samples will determine the overall texture of IBA buildings, it is necessary that the Executive Committee may see the samples and approve the same or otherwise. He said that the samples are located at the City Campus building walls. He said that the Architect is also available to explain the overall building facade.

 

4.3              The members of the Executive Committee witnessed various samples of the washed and polished terrazzo and approved the sample with grey color.  The Registrar said that washed terrazzo would be very suitable but the  grinding operation before the polishing the terrazzo upto 10 ft height was not acceptable because it would result in excessive dust which would be hazardous to the health of students. He quoted example of the day the contractor prepared a sample in the academic block at main campus. The dust from that small sample was so fine that it was mixed with the air and could be seen on the equipments of the class rooms of first floor. He reiterated that it will be a health hazard to students some of whom complained that they are suffering from Asthma. The Director Projects while agreeing with the Registrar on this point stated that similar work of grinding and polishing of façade upto 10 ft level on other buildings such as Academic Block at City Campus and the Administration Block at Main Campus will have to be carried out when the parts of the buildings will remain occupied. As such the polished terrazzo was not practically possible.

 

DECISION

 

4.4              The Committee approved the recommendation of the Registrar of grey coloured washed terrazzo for the entire buildings.     

 

ACTION

 

4.5              The Director Projects would convey the decision of the selection of grey coloured washed terrazzo for the entire building to the concerned architects.
