MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON NOVEMBER 10, 2008 (TWENTY FIRST MEETING)

 

The twenty first meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, November 10, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Chairman, CS& MIS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                          Director H.R.
  8. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                              Director IT
  9. Mr. Shameem Ahmad                          General Manager, Planning  & Development

Before the commencement of the meeting, the Director introduced Dr. Arshad Siddiqi who joined IBA as Director I.T. The Executive Committee welcomed Dr. Arshad Siddiqi.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider email from Director Finance regarding Gold Medal.

1.1       The Director Finance vide his email of November 6, 2008 presented the details of the gold medals given by the following institutions:

a)  Aga Khan University         7 tola pure gold (84 gram)

b)  Karachi University             18 to 22 carat pure gold

c)  LUMS.                            9 carat.

a)  Sterling silver gold plated              Rs.  6,500/-

b)  9 carat pure gold                            Rs.12,500/-

c)  12 carat pure gold                          Rs.15,500/-

d)  14 carat pure gold                          Rs.17,500/-

e)  18 carat pure gold                          Rs.24,500/-

DECISION

ACTION

1.5              The Registrar will arrange 9 carat gold medals for the top position holders.

Item No. 2     To consider note from the General Manager (Projects & Development) regarding construction of the low cost building (temporary shed) at the Main Campus.

2.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (Projects & Development) said that 15 applications from contractors who desired to be prequalified for bidding, were received. Out of 15, 10 were recommended by M/s. ICON for further consideration while five of them were eliminated on the ground that they were either not registered with Pakistan Engineering Council in the appropriate category or because their registration with PEC was no longer valid. He said that out of 10 applicants considered for stage 2, ICON recommended six contractors for pre-qualification.

2.2              Mr. Shameem Ahmad further said that in view of their own personal knowledge of the performance of two of the eliminated contractors in the past, he and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia decided to consider M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor and M/s. A.S. Enterprises prequalifed for bidding.  Thus in all eight contractors were pre-qualified for participation in the bidding.  They were informed accordingly and asked to purchase the tender documents. One of them (National Constructions Limited) did not respond. Seven of them purchased the tender documents and submitted their bids which were opened on November 4, 2008. He further stated that of the two contractors who had been eliminated by the consultant, one of them namely M/s. A.S. Enterprises has quoted the highest price (Rs.10,326,849) while the other one M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor is the lowest bidder (Rs.7,464,080) for the work.

2.3              Mr. Shameem said that there were following two issues for which he would recommend as under:

a)         The Budget 2008-09 allows a maximum expenditure of Rs.6.00 million only. Mr. Shameem said that what they had in mind was that Rs.5.00 million would be spent on the civil work and Rs.1.00 million would be used for furniture, air-conditioners etc. But now the civil work only requires Rs.7.5 million. In view of the urgency of office space required for both faculty and staff he recommended that instead of getting the building redesigned and restarting the tendering process we should accept the lowest bid, complete the process of issuing a work order and ask the contractor to commence the work by the end of this month. The building will be a permanent asset, and addition of another floor to it sometime later will enhance its utility in the future. As far as the additional cost of Rs.1.5 million is concerned, we have enough approved money available in the two on-going projects from where some re-appropriation can be done, with or without A&FC’s approval.

b)         Mr. Shameem suggested that the issue of the lowest bidder’s registration with PEC may be resolved by asking him to produce a valid registration certificate within 10 days. Besides, in order to verify his financial capacity, he suggested that we should ask him to submit his bank statements for at least two years.

2.4       Dr. Arshad Siddiqui said that not pre-qualifying the contractors by the Consultant on the ground of their not having a valid PEC licence does not seem to be a valid reason.  He said that every contractor should have to be given a chance to get valid PEC licence.  He said that according to PPRA rules we should consider the lowest bidder.    

DECISION

2.5       The Committee formed a sub-committee comprising Director IT, Director Finance and General Manager (P&D) which would verify the validity of PEC Licence and financial strength & credentials of the lowest bidder, M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructors. The Committee would also ensure that there is no violation of PPRA rules.  If the sub-committee is satisfied in all respect, then the contract would be awarded to the lowest bidder.

2.6       The Committee further decided that instead of naming the low cost building as “temporary shed”, it should be retained as a permanent structure and be called as multi-purpose building which may be used for warehousing and for other unforeseen and unanticipated purposes.

  ACTION

2.7       The Sub-Committee as constituted in 2.5 above would submit its recommendations to the Executive Committee.

Item No. 3      To consider note from General Manager (P&D) regarding construction of wooden partition offices at the ground floor of Administration Building, Main Campus.

3.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (P&D) said that the proposal of construction of wooden partition offices at the ground floor of Administration Building, Main Campus was discussed in the Executive Committee meeting of September 8, 2008.  In that meeting he had given a guess estimate of Rs.500,000/- for the proposed offices. However, the Committee had asked for a revised cost based on actual costs of the material, labour, bricks and windows.  He said that the total estimated cost of Rs.1,207,147/- now submitted was based on actual quotations obtained. It includes 16% GST and 6% WHT as well where applicable.  He further said that certain material would be reusable somewhere else when the temporary offices were no longer needed.

DECISION

3.2       The Committee decided that Dr. Arshad Siddiqui and Mr. Shameem Ahmad would see what additional work / equipment related to electrification is required including UPS.

3.3       The Committee approved the estimated cost of Rs.1,207,147/- as presented by Mr. Shameem Ahmad.  The Committee further approved Rs.400,000/- for contingency including UPS.

ACTION

3.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmad would initiate the construction of wooden partition offices at the ground floor on Administration Building at the Main Campus.

Item No. 4      To consider operational staff requirements for the PABX at IBA City Campus and installation of additional trunk links at IBA City Campus submitted by Manager I.T.

4.1              Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager stated in his note of November 7, 2008 that a new PABX has been installed at IBA City Campus. ACTION

Item No.  5     To consider note from the Registrar regarding re-appropriation of budget for Convocation 2008.

5.1              The Registrar stated that the budget for IBA’s Convocation 2008 including that of the MBA(Tax Management) graduates is Rs.1.2 million. The addition of lunch and seven gold medals as added items requires a budget of Rs.700,000 to Rs.800,000/- Keeping this in view and the great price hike, he requested that an additional amount of Rs.500,000/- to Rs.600,000/- may be appropriated for the Convocation expenses.

5.2              The Director Finance reported that there is a balance of Rs.96,000/- of previous year on account of Gold Medal which may be transferred to the Convocation 2008 fund.

DECISION

5.3              The Committee decided that in view of the decision to award 9 carat gold medals, the cost on gold medals would be reduced. Moreover, the balance of Rs.96,000/- may be transferred to the Convocation 2008 fund.

5.4              The Committee decided that the Convocation 2008 be arranged within the budget.

ACTION

5.5            The Registrar will ensure that the Convocation 2008 is arranged within the budget which would also include Rs.96,000/- of the previous year.

Item No. 6      To consider input from Dr. Nasir Touheed on the proposal for compensation for faculty and staff for additional duties during examinations.

6.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed suggested that exams of two classes can be arranged in one room at the same time; the classes may be different. He said that this would reduce the staff and faculty requirement. He also suggested that the class teacher should not be present there or may be available for say 5 to 10 minutes to clarify any question.  He also suggested that instead of printing the exam paper at the City Campus, the papers should also be printed at the Main Campus. He said that a few faculty members have some reservations about the maintenance of secrecy.   

6.2              The Controller of Examinations said that it was previously done but some teachers did not like it as if a teacher explains a point to a student of a class, others are disturbed.  Regarding printing of papers he said that it is the prerogative of the Controller of Examinations. It is his job to ensure confidentiality, secrecy and on time printing of papers.  He requested that the allegations about leakage of question papers should be investigated as according to him, this is for the first time that he heard in 25 years of his service.   He said that some teachers get photo copy from photo copier at the last moment.

DECISION

6.3       The Committee decided that henceforth there would be exams for two different papers in one room at the same time and the concerned class teacher will move to other class or will be available in his office after starting the exam and staying in the examination room for initial 5 to 10 minutes to clarify any point.

6.4       The Committee also decided that every teacher would be given a date for submission of question paper within which he / she would have to submit the paper.

6.5       All teachers would be instructed that they are not allowed to get photo copy done commercially. They would have to submit the paper within the given time to the Controller of Examinations. It would be the responsibility of the Controller of Examinations to arrange multiple copies of the paper.

6.6       The Director said that he would listen to the teachers who have some reservations about the maintenance of secrecy and see if there is any substance in that.

ACTION

6.7       The Controller of Examinations would ensure that exams for two different papers at the same time are arranged in one room.

6.8       The Controller of Examinations would notify to all faculty members about the decisions of 6.2 to 6.4 above.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)                To consider request from Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin for Daily Allowance.

7.1              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that an approval was earlier given by the Executive Committee for travelling to a workshop in Bangkok for Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin. However, the earlier Daily Allowance requested was lower than the IBA approved rates for travel in that region.  He further said that it is felt that the costs may not be covered under the previous approval.  He requested for allowing the travel with the IBA approved rates for Daily Allowance.

7.2              Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri, H.R. Manager clarified that the Daily Allowance he was referring to is for the government servants and is not approved by the IBA.

7.3              The Director said that we should differentiate between representation and training.  He said that the daily allowances are not applicable on training.

DECISION

7.4              The Committee allowed reimbursement of actual expenses over and above US$40/- per day to Ms. Quratulain.

7.5              The Director requested the Director H.R and Director Finance to develop some parameters as to what will be the daily allowance on training or representation for Europe, Africa, Far East, the Middle East.

ACTION

7.6              Dr. Sayeed Ghani will convey the decision of 7.4 above to Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin.

7.7              The Director H.R. in consultation with Director Finance will develop a daily allowance policy.

 There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
