MINUTES OF THE 142ND MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, FEBRUARY 21, 2011

 

The 142nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, February 21, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                   Dean & Director
2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                            Associate Dean
3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                  Associate Dean
4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN               Registrar
5.      Mr. Ali Taha                                         Director Finance
6.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
7.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                  Director ICT
8.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                              Director CED
9.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                              Director H.R.

 

Before considering the agenda items, the Committee welcomed Dr. Kristiana Raube, Adjunct Professor, and Executive Director, Graduate Program in Health Management, and International Business Development Program at Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley.

After the introduction of each member of the Executive Committee to Dr. Raube, Dr. Sayeed Ghani made a presentation on IBA’s history, heritage; its vision, core values, academic programs, strategy, objectives and challenges, talent hunt programs & financial aid, internal management, infrastructure development and technology up-gradation, faculty development and appraisal, and academic initiatives.

The Director said that they have kept stretched goals for the management and every year they review their strategy to know where they have reached.  However, he said that due to heavy flood in the country last year, there has been a set-back but now they are back on their strategy.

The Director said that international exposure is a very critical factor but IBA is way behind this. However, he expressed his satisfaction over the participation of faculty members in various conferences, seminars, workshops. He said that in 2010 as many as 35 faculty members went abroad under various programs / activities compared to 13 in 2009 and 10 in 2008.  He further said that 19 faculty members presented research papers at conferences abroad compared to 6 in 2009 and 4 in 2008. This upsurge has been recorded mainly in the Faculty of Business Administration. He said that in the local programs the number was even more impressive -- 67 compared to 36 in 2009 and 12 in 2008.

After the presentation, Dr. Kristiana Raube accompanied by Dr. Muhammad Nishat, Capt. Ahmed Zaheer and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui visited the classrooms, and hostels.

The following agenda items were discussed:

Director Finance:

Director H.R.

She said that Mr. Abdul Qadir from IT side and Mr. Muhammad Asif from H.R would be coordinating for the training of new inductees.

1.6       Computation of Attendance Reward by Incharge Leave Record: The Director H.R. reported that the Incharge Leave Record has been briefed about the decision which also included in the note from Dr. Ishrat Husain regarding actions taken on decisions made in his meetings with staff.  However, she reported that until the Attendance Device properly works, the revised Attendance Reward policy cannot be implemented.

He requested to depute an IT person who could train people how to handle it and also help him in preparing a report.

1.8       Physical fitness tests of security staff: The Director H.R. reported that they worked with Major (R) Qamar Abbas but he too was not satisfied and has suggested to get it done from outside.  She said that Armed forces physical fitness college would be contacted to conduct the physical test of Security Guards. 1.9       Rates of Panel Hospitals: The Director H.R. reported that the rate list has been placed on the IBA Portal.

1.11     Dr. Muhammad Nishat and the Director H.R to discuss the TAs assignments with the faculty and department chairs and on the basis of the findings present a proposal to the EC: The Director H.R reported that the issue was discussed and addressed in the last Associate Dean meeting.

DECISION

1.12     The Committee noted and approved the up-dates along with the decisions noted above.

Item No. 2      To consider and approve Data Cabling Work for Research Centre on First Floor, Academic Block at City Campus.

2.1       The Director Projects stated that in the meeting held on 14th February 2011, the Executive Committee had in principle approved the award of Data Cabling Work for Research Centre to the lowest bidder M/s. International Electrical Services at the cost of Rs.222,120/-. Later, the matter was presented to the Technical Evaluation Committee on 17th February 2011. TEC advised that the lowest limit for obtaining quotations as per SPPRA now is Rs.100,000/- instead of Rs.300,000/- ( as done previously under PPPRA). Further as the Project Manager at City Campus had obtained the quotations, they were not accepted by the TEC.

2.2       The Director Projects said that tendering under SPPRA was considered which would need at least 30 days, as 15 days’ notice is to be given under the SPPRA and 15 days are required for decisions of EC, TEC and CPC and as such it was decided that the work may be done through the existing contractors on the price established through market quotations.  In this case a variation order will have to be issued to M/s.  Ziauddin Ahmed & Co. (Pvt) Limited (ZCL) under whom one of the contractors M/s. MIMA is already working at the site of City Campus at existing Administration Building. In this case, the Director Projects said that they would have to pay the taxes and overheads of M/s. ZCL over and above the market price.  He clarified that the cost would not go beyond 15% of the total cost for the work for existing Administration Building. He requested the Executive Committee to approve this change in process as advised by the Technical Evaluation Committee.

DECISION

2.3       The Committee approved the change in process as advised by the Technical Evaluation Committee i.e. the work may be done through the existing contractors on the price established through market quotations.  

Item No.3       To consider and approve delegation of the authority to Mr. Shameem Ahmad, Director HEC Projects.

3.1       The Director Projects stated that under the Transaction Authority Policy of IBA, the Director Projects is the only authorized person to sign and execute the contracts of projects, Architects, Consultants and Projects Managers. He said that the Projects which are under the direct control of Mr. Shameem Ahmad, Director HEC Projects, need to be signed by him and executed as per the contracts.  He further said that the control of all the process from procurement to the final execution of the projects lies with the Director HEC Projects under whom a complete team is already working.

3.2       The Director Projects reported that the authority to sign the contracts of all Architects, Consultants, Projects Managers and contractors of all those projects which lie under the jurisdiction of Mr. Shameem Ahmad, Director HEC Projects has been delegated to Mr. Shameem Ahmad as per the Transaction Authority Policy.

DECISION

3.3       The Committee noted the delegation of authority to Mr. Shameem Ahmed by Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

 

Item No. 4      Half yearly accounts – Income and Expenditure overview.

4.1       The Director Finance presented the half yearly accounts. He presented details of revenue and major expenses during this period. On the revenue side he presented details of the tuition fee and other charges, Interest Income, and Government Grant.  However, he informed the Committee that no grant from Sindh Government was received during this period. Of the Operating Expenses he presented details of the employment cost, hospitalization expenses, training and development, student transport, communication expenses, electricity expenses, and repair and maintenance expenses on Computer Hardware and Software.   

DECISION

4.2       The Director observed that the hospitalization cost is increasing. He suggested that somebody should keep track of the hospitalization cases and control the expenses. He asked the Director H.R. to look at the exceptional cases.      

4.3       The Director showed his concern over the increasing overtime. He asked the Director ICT to control the overtime of his department. He asked the Director Finance to arrange a separate meeting of the Registrar, and Maintenance Manager with him. He also requested the Associate Dean to request Dr. Ahmad Ali Shah to control the overtime expenses of the library staff.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
