MINUTES OF THE 95TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, APRIL 5, 2010

 

The 95th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, April 5, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

  7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Clarity on the additional designation of the Controller of Examinations – “Administrator City Campus” or “Coordinator City Campus”.

 

1.1       This item was deferred and would be discussed in the next meeting of the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider the make up exam request from Ms. Ghazala Kamal on the basis of medical certificate provided by the State Bank’s Doctor.

 

2.1       Ms. Ghazala Kamal of MBA-II of City Campus submitted a medical certificate issued by Dr. Zeba Alvi, the CMO, SBP on March 15.  The CMO had certified that Ms. Ghazala Kamal had been suffering from allergic Rhinitis and Bronchial Asthma for about a year and on March 13 she had fever and shortness of breath as well as throat congestion. She was prescribed certain medicines and advised to take rest and stay home.

ACTION

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the prequalification of Architects and Engineering Consultants for IBA Projects.

3.1       The Director Projects reported that pre-qualification of Architects and Engineering Consultants was done in the 1st quarter of the year 2009. As one year had passed new applications were invited through advertisement published in the daily Dawn on 8th November 2009. In all 30 applications were received till due date. He said that IBA had asked all Architects and Engineering Consultants to submit pre-qualification papers of their associates specially Consultants for pre-qualification by IBA. The intent was to develop a list of specialty consultants also who could be given independent assignments, if required.

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported that on first scrutiny it was found that the pre-qualification documents were also submitted by specialty consultants independently. As such they were treated separately and specialty consultants list would be developed separately wherein those applicants would also be included for the preset exercise. The Consultants who are associated with qualified Architects and Engineers Firms are also included in the list.

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that all documents were thoroughly studied first to check if the firms had submitted all the required documents. Ten (10) percent marks were allocated for the particular requirements. Detailed evaluation was then carried out under the set criteria. He presented the factors for which marks were allocated. Based on details provided by the firms grading was done and the following firms stood first to sixth in the ranking order:

 

1.         M/s. Habib Fida Ali                                           88 %

2.         M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Ltd       84%

3.         M/s. Nespak                                                   84%

4.         M/s. Meinhardt (Pakistan) Ltd                            84 %

5.         M/s. EA Consulting (Private) Ltd                         83 %

6.         M/s. Ahed Associates                                       82%

 

3.4       The Director Projects explained that the above firms secured higher marks because they have large offices, better resources, vast experience related to the works such as preparation of master planning and designing of external development works including utility plan, designing of all the infrastructure facilities.

 

3.5       The Director Projects presented a list of the following second tier firms which have secured marks within the range of 70% to 80%. He said that they are smaller firms and will be considered for building designs and related works:

 

·         M/s. Anwar Ali & Associates                               77%

·         M/s. Progressive Consultants (Pvt) Limited            76%

·         M/s. Consult-Tech International                          76%

·         M/s. FNMH Architecture                                     76%

·         M/s. FIRCO International (Pvt) Limited                  75%

 

3.6       The Director Projects requested that the six firms listed at 3.3 above be pre-qualified as top tier firms and the firms listed in 3.5 above to be pre-qualified as 2nd tier firms. He said that M/s. ICON will remain pre-qualified as they are already engaged on eight (08) IBA Projects under agreement signed in 2007. However, they will not be issued RFP for master planning and designing of external development works of both campuses of IBA as they do not have resources.

 

3.7       The Director Projects reported that the case along with the recommendations was presented to the Technical Evaluation Committee in its meeting held on 22nd March, 2010 and it was approved by the TEC.

 

3.8       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the above Architects & Engineering Consultants to be Pre-Qualified.

 

3.9              Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to what projects the pre-qualified contractors would have when all the projects have already been assigned to the contractors.

 

3.10     The Director Projects stated that utility Master Plan and Master Planning of external development has not yet been done which is a major project.

 

3.11     To a question from the Director Finance regarding allocation of maintenance cost for the projects being completed, the Director Projects said that maintenance will need to be properly organized and sizable amount will have to be allocated in the budget for maintenance of buildings in future by the maintenance department as soon as the buildings get completed and they take over the complete buildings or portions.

 

3.12     The Director Projects said that during this prequalification process those Electrical and Mechanical Consultants whose capability documents were scrutinized along with their lead Architects /Engineering Consultants, stand prequalified. He further stated that in response to the advertisement a few Electrical and Mechanical Consultants had applied independently, their documents have also been scrutinized and a list of prequalified Electrical and Mechanical Consultants will also be submitted for approval.

 

3.13     However, the Director Projects reported that Mr Shameem Ahmed suggested that fresh advertisement should be floated for prequalification because many consultants might not have applied. Agreeing to the proposal, the Director Projects suggested that while asking for capability documents of the Electrical Consultants, they should ask for their capabilities in the ICT discipline. This will greatly help in carrying out detailing of ICT Equipment drawings which is missing altogether.

 

The Executive Committee agreed to the proposal of floating the advertisement for prequalification of Electrical and Mechanical Consultants with special emphasis on ICT design and detailing capabilities.

 

DECISION

 

3.14     The Committee approved the pre-qualification of Architects & Engineering Consultants as suggested by the Director Projects in 3.3 and 3.5 above.

3.15     The Committee approved floating the advertisement for prequalification of Electrical and Mechanical Consultants with special emphasis on the capabilities of Design and detailing of ICT works.

 

3.16     Director Finance said that he had earlier suggested that there should be a Master Plan for ICT. To this the Director ICT agreed that there should be a Master Plan for the ICT works.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the prequalification of contractors for IBA Projects.

 

4.1       The Director Projects stated that IBA invited applications for pre-qualification of reputed contractors on 8th November, 2009 vide their advertisement in local newspapers. Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Request from Ms. Mehwish Amanullah Jangda of BBA-8 for re-scheduling her exams.
