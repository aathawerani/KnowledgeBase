The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Demonstration of Lecture Recording System.

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that the Dean & Director has plans to offer distance learning programs for EMBA for which they have to record all the lectures. Mr. Batada said that he has invited Mr. Naseem of Argos to make a demonstration of Lecture Recording System and its various features and functions. Accordingly, Mr. Naseem demonstrated the Lecture Recording System and its various functions and features.

 

1.2       Dr. Nishat wanted to know as to how interaction between students and faculty would take place under distance learning program. Mr. Naseem replied that students cannot interact directly but they can type their question which would pop up on the screen. The Registrar wanted to know if the recording equipments are to be permanently fixed or they are portable. Mr. Naseem replied that it is portable but adjustment of camera according to the individual room set up would be required.

 

1.3       Mr. Imran Batada said that they have planned to implement Lecture Recording System before starting the upcoming semester. Initial suggestion is to procure 5 licences, 2 for each campuses and 1 for CEE. The purpose of lecture recording system is not only to record the lectures but also to build the library of our Online Lectures. These recorded lectures can be viewed by enrolled student during exams time or later at any time. The Lecture Recording System will be integrated with the LMS (SAKAI) as well. The Registrar said that initially they should get one licence for a semester for trial purpose and record lectures of one particular course and see its utility then take a decision.  Mr. Naseem said that the company does not offer one license because it would cost US$7500 for one license. The minimum number of licences that is offered is 5 and because of special discount for educational institutions, the cost would come down to US$3500 per license. The price of a camera which is very sophisticated is US$1700.  Software would also cost around US$1700. He said that the per licence cost would come down with the increase in the number of licences. For more than 10 licences it would cost around US$1500 per license. He said that the deployment cost is fixed irrespective of the number of licenses. The Registrar again reiterated that we should acquire one license for one semester then depending upon its utility and benefit, we may increase the number.

 

1.4       Mr. Izhar Husain said that when cases are being discussed in a class room, there is no lecture and the recording of case based learning may not add value to those who could not attend the class.  He said that it will have to be looked into in which subjects there would be value addition through lecture recording. The faculty members will need orientation for lecture recording so that they could modify their contents accordingly. It would be more beneficial for academic programs than for non-degree training workshops. Mr. Naseem said that the program can accept four different inputs. He said that for students’ coverage in a class, another camera would be required and that can be an ordinary video camera.  Mr. Naseem reported that at LUMS they are capturing all the lectures but they are using ordinary camera. To a question he said that LUMS had acquired three licenses almost three years ago. The Registrar said that if LUMS is working with three licences , there is no reason for IBA to get the entire lot. He suggested that in one seminar room we should use it on trial basis and see the results. Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that there should be a trial version option. Dr. Nishat wanted to know if LUMS made further order and how they utilized it. Mr. Naseem said that their lectures are on the website.  Mr. Naseem, to a question from the Director Projects, said that camera, microphone and a controller are required for this system.  He said that any amendment or changes in the lecture can only be made by those who are authorised to do this.

 

1.5       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that for distance learning program, there is a utility of this system but not for our regular programs where presence of faculty and students in a class is compulsory. Mr. Imran Batada said that those students who miss their class may be able to capture those lectures through recorded lectures. The Registrar suggested to get input from Dr. Shakeel Khoja also about the requirements of distance learning.

 

1.6       The members were shown the video of recorded lectures of LUMS placed on their web and the lectures recorded at IBA.  However, due to the use of EVO (IBA net was not working on Mr. Naseem’s machine), which had limited bandwidth, the speed was slow and there was frequent buffering. It was reported that at KSBL also the Lecture Recording system has been functioning.  Mr. Imran Batada said that he can arrange a visit of EC members to KSBL for this purpose.

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee decided that KSBL would be visited and feedback from Dr. Shakeel Khoja would be obtained before taking any decision.

 

ACTION

 

1.8       Mr. Imran Batada would arrange a visit of some EC members to KSBL and would also obtain feedback from Dr. Shakeel Khoja.

Item No.3       To consider and approve the Technical Qualifications provided by the lowest evaluated responsive bidder for the work of Supply and Installation of four passenger lifts and one Service Lift at Aman Tower, City Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects stated that in its meeting held on 28th October 2013, the Executive Committee had considered the evaluation report of the supply and installation of four passenger elevators and one service elevator at Aman Tower, City Campus. The Executive Committee had recommended that the elevators’ motors and related moving parts should be Korean made and not Chinese made and if that is acceptable by the vendors then recommendations can be sent to Audit & Finance Committee for their approval.  In this regard the Director Projects reported that a meeting was convened on Wednesday, October 30, 2013, wherein the following participated:

 

1.      Engr. Rehan A. Riaz                      Director Projects IBA

2.      Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed                  Manager Maintenance & Operation IBA

3.      Mr. Najamul Islam,                        Resident Engineer (Aman Tower) M/s. Halcrow

4.      Mr. Khasif Khan                            Electrical Engineer M/s. Halcrow

5.      Mr. Muhammad Farooq                 Electrical Design Engineer, M/s. E.A. Consulting

6.      Mr. Salman Zia                              Proprietor, M/s. ZIAFCO

7.      Syed Mushtaq Haider Abidi  Manager Marketing, M/s. ZIAFCO

8.      Mr. Hassan Ahmed                        General Manager M/s. ZIAFCO

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported in this meeting he conveyed the concern of the Executive Committee regarding Chinese products specially the motors and related moving parts. M/s. Ziafco was informed that all the elevators should be provided with the motors and related moving parts made in Korea. M/s. Ziafaco informed that the bid was based on the complete elevators manufactured in China under the certification of German (TUV) and Norweign (DNV), which are third party certifying agencies. M/s. Ziafaco further informed that the Chinese manufacturers do not allow mix and match of the parts from various resources which is a deviation from the production line. They are going to supply elevators from Dalian Factory, which is OTIS Elevators Korean prime export base in China from where the elevators are exported to 65 countries including USA and Middle East. They further clarified that the quality and warranty of elevators manufacturers are guaranteed by OTIS elevator Korea, the owner and manufacturer of “SIGMA Elevator”.

 

3.3       The Director Projects informed the EC that although the Chinese motors warranty period is 18 months yet in view of the concern showed by IBA, M/s. Ziafco are ready to extend the warranty period to five years. The Director Projects further said that M/s. Ziafco offered that, if IBA so desires, they can get the complete elevator supplied from Korea, but its cost will be 20%  higher than the current bid prices.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that M/s. Ziafco have informed that they have installed ‘Sigma Elevators’ in Ocean Mall Karachi in their 9 storied portion and 27 storied tower, Neuplex Karachi a 9 storied building and Islamabad Stock Exchange Building, a 22 storied building.

 

3.5       The Director Projects reported that the above technical clarifications were acceptable to the Project Manager M/s. Halcrow and IBA (Projects Department and Manager, Maintenance & Operation).  He said that he has invited the owner of ZiafcoMr. Zia and his team, Electrical Engineer of Halcrow, Mr. Kashif Khan, and Mr. Muhammad Farooq, Electrical Design Engineer, M/s. E.A. Consulting, to give a brief presentation to the EC.

 

3.6       Mr. Zia of Ziafco presented the profile of his company. He informed the members that they have installed the same brand and same origin high speed elevator at several high rise buildings including Technocity, Shaheen Complex, Creek Vista, Faisal House, Al-Tijarah, The Ocean Tower, Saudi Pak Towers, Islamabad, and Avari Towers Islamabad and providing maintenance services also. He briefed the members on the technical aspects of the elevator and related equipment. He also briefed about the safety features. When asked how long it would take to complete the project, Mr. Zia said that it would take around ten months time.  The Registrar said that they have eight months time to complete the entire project. Mr. Zia said that they could do some preliminary work before the arrival of the shipment in order to squeeze time. The Director Projects said that one year’s maintenance warranty has been given by Ziafco. The Registrar suggested that after the warranty they may give three years maintenance contract to Ziafco for which Ziafco have to give special discount. Mr. Zia agreed to do so.

 

3.7       The Director Projects requested the EC that in view of the clarifications given by Ziafcoas noted at 3.2 and 3.3 above and accepted by the Project Manager and Consultant, the EC may accept the clarifications and recommend to the A&FC to approve the award of work for supply and installation of 4 passenger elevators and 1 service elevator to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Ziafco at Rs.33,146,870/-.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The EC accepted the clarifications and recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of work for supply and installation of 4 passenger elevators and 1 service elevator to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Ziafco at a cost of Rs.33,146,870/-.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       The Director Projects would send the recommendations of EC to the A&FC for consideration and approval.

 

Item No.4       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Replacement of Blackberry with Smart Phone.

 

4.1       Mr. Imran Batada reported that as decided in the 268th EC meeting held on October 7, 2013, approval from the Dean & Director on the financial implication on replacement of Blackberry with Smart Phone has been obtained.  He said that the price of the set is Rs.27,500/- but they would get it at Rs.25,000/-  He showed the Smart Phone set to the members.

 

4.2    Mr. Mobin Khalili expressed his reservation on this expenditure as according to him blackberry sets are working fine. Mr. Imran Batada explained that he has given presentation in the earlier meeting of EC where he mentioned the benefits of Cloud Computing over hosting exchange in-house. The cloud computing solution doesn’t support the email functionality in the existing sets; secondly this switchover will save IBA millions in the long run.

 

4.3       Mr. Batada said that the procedure for disposal would be followed for the old blackberry. Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that as we do on laptop, we should get the market value of the old set assessed and give it to the users. It was suggested that since the blackberry was provided free of cost to IBA, it should be given free of cost.  Mr. Moeid clarified that the sets were given free of cost to IBA not to individuals as such it has to be disposed off.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee approved the replacement of Blackberry with Smart Phone and agreed with Mr. Moeid Sultan that the old blackberry should be given to its respective users at the market assessed price.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
