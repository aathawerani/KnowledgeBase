MINUTES OF THE 73rd MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 2, 2009  

The 73rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 2, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  5. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                             Director CED

  6. Dr. Arshad Siddiqui                            Director ICT

  7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

 

The following attended by special invitation:

 

1.      Mr. Saleem Umar

2.      Mr. Shahid Zaki

3.      Mr. Shameem Ahmed

4.      Dr. Ahmad Ali Shah

5.      Mirza Izhar Hussain

6.      Mr. Muhammad Ghufran

7.      Ms. Erum Hafeez

8.      Ms. Shafaq Yaqub

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     Presentation on Communication.

 

1.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui, Director CED made a presentation on the Communication Strategy of IBA. He said that there was a need to revise and improve the communication system at IBA. He talked about where we are in terms of communication strategy, where we want to, and how to get there.

 

1.2              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui felt that Communication has to be centralized.  He suggested that the three societies namely; Media and Publication Society, Literary Society and Web Society should report directly to the Communication Department.

 

1.3              The Director said that instead of reporting directly to the Communication Department the three societies should work in close coordination with Communication Department.  He also asked Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to show those societies in broken line in the organization chart.

 

1.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggesting solutions to the communication gap stated that we should revisit the communication system by targeting our internal audience.  One of the solutions was to have regular town meetings.

 

1.5              The Director and other members appreciated the idea and suggested that every month each Director should hold a lecture and tell the students what they are doing.  Similarly the projects pictures should be placed at both the campuses.

 

1.6              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui talked about the contents of the information placed on the IBA Website and the Portal. He also presented the websites of LUMS and IBA for comparison purposes.

 

1.7              The Director said that contents management is the responsibility of concerned departments. He said that unless we assign the responsibility of contents management and frequent up-dating of the information to the concerned department, the problem will persist. He also said that another issue is of aesthetic look which may be improved through competition among students and the third issue is the technical support which is the responsibility of the IT department.

 

1.8              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui proposed to appoint a student for six months in the Communication Department who will basically be responsible for redesigning the IBA website.  

 

1.9              Commenting on the IBA ads, he said that the first and foremost requirement for an ad is that it should be attractive. He said that we need to tell our advertising agency to be a little more creative.  He also said that he wants one ad instead of ads with a number of variations. He said that the process and procedures for placing ads need to be redesigned. He said that the new PID rules require publishing date, size of the ad, number and languages of newspapers without mentioning the name of the newspapers as it is for them to decide. He proposed that every department should have a communication calendar and they should plan for ads to be placed upto June 2010 but the deadline for providing details of ads to the Communication Department is December 15, 2009.  He said that request on short notices particularly for major items will not be entertained.

 

1.10          The Director requested the department heads to start with the planning calendar and provide details of ads and dates of publications to the Communication Department. 

 

1.11          The Director said that a small ad should be placed in the newspaper and details should be provided in the IBA Website which should be mentioned in the newspaper ad. He also said that we have to preserve the history of IBA through photographs.  He requested the Registrar to talk to Mr. Inayat N. Din in this regard.    

 

1.12          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui required the following resources in order to effectively run the Communication Department and achieve the set objectives:

 

·         A student to work on six months plan

(Fixed cost for six months @Rs.30,000 per month)               Rs.180,000  

·         Two laptops                                                                            Rs.130,000

·         Four-in-one machines (fax, scanner, printer, copier)               Rs.  40,000

·         Miscellaneous expenses for six months                                  Rs.  50,000

 

1.13          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the Communication Department should be aware of the publications and they should be informed before printing.

 

1.14          The Registrar suggested that as students are interested in infrastructure, the Director Projects should publish projects details with the help of Communication Department and include that in the quarterly newsletter. He requested that for admission ads, he should be involved and consulted because he has no information of EMBA admission and other admissions.  He also suggested to increase the number of insertions from two to three for admissions ads as is done by LUMS

 

DECISION

 

1.15          The Committee appreciated Mr. Zafar Siddiqui for making very excellent and optimistic presentation and approved the provision of resources as listed in 1.12 above.

 

1.16          The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Registrar for making three insertions instead of two for admissions ads and that the Registrar would be informed about the admission ads before they are published.

 

1.17          The Committee decided that every last Saturday of the month, a corner meeting of the Directors will be arranged at APWA building for dissemination of related information among the students.

 

1.18          The Director Projects will get clay models of the buildings and schematic designs prepared and have them displayed at both the campuses.

 

ACTION

 

1.19          The Director ICT will arrange two laptops and one four-in-one machines (fax, scanner, printer, copier) for the Communication Department.

 

1.20          Every Director in coordination with the Registrar will brief the students about their projects.

 

1.21          The Director Projects will arrange for models of clay of the buildings and schematic designs and have them displayed at both the campuses.  He will also get photographs of the existing buildings / facilities.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve extra-items for multipurpose building.

 

2.1              The Director Projects reported that the work of Multipurpose building Main Campus was awarded to M/s. B.G Civil Electrical and Mechanical Company at a cost of Rs.8,523,142 via competitive bidding. The work completed 30 days before the completion date and handed over to IBA on August 15, 2009. He said that during the execution of work due to site conditions, design changes and requirement of IBA, eleven (11) extra items were executed. Major extra items were plinth protection, widow, rollup blinds and first floor structure. He presented details of extra items. He said that the cost of extra items as claimed by contractor was Rs.1,957,137 and submitted to IBA. IBA sent their request to Project Manager and Consultant for scrutiny and verification. The Project Manager and Consultant submitted their recommendations and they recommended the Cost of Rs.1,345,438/- against the claim of contractor.

 

2.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the amount of Rs.1,345,438/- as the work has been completed and the Contractor executed the work satisfactorily and the extra items claimed and recommended by the contractors are justified.  He said that the cost is within the budget and provided the following additional details:

 

The value of original Contract is                     Rs.8,523,142/-

 

Total Variation order Cost                              Rs.1,345,438/-

 

Variation Percentage                                       15.786%

 

Budgeted Amount                                          Rs.10.0M

 

Total Completion Cost

Including partial Structural    

Cost of first Floor                                           Rs.9,868,580/-

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee approved the amount of Rs.1,345,438/- for the extra items for multipurpose building.

 

 

Item No. 3      Presentation by the Architect on renovation of Main Campus Library façade.

 

3.1              Mr. Hanif Daud presented the design already approved by the A&FC and the proposed design for the Main Campus Library façade.

 

3.2              Mr. Muhammad Ghufran said that the existing building does not need any change except renovation and a stack room on the back side needs to be constructed.  He said that the library needs to be covered as dusts come in.

 

3.3              The Registrar did not agree to the proposal for changing the existing façade of the library and suggested that it should be retained with changes in the glass windows and replacement of the existing windows to bring it in consonance with the façade of the Admin Building once that is renovated. 

 

3.4              The Director said that he is not satisfied with this library and it will have to be modernised and as the students population is increasing so requirements are also increasing. He said that he wants a good library for the students.     

 

3.5              Mr. Hanif Daud said that he will make provisions for computers room, reception office, photocopier room, research area, computer lab and audio-visual rooms and will make provisions for anything else so required.

 

3.6              The Director wanted to know as to what is the cost estimate of the new proposal and how long time will it take to be complete. To this Mr. Hanif Daud replied that it would take around 8 months time and it will cost Rs.59 million. He said that the new as well as the old design has the same cost estimate. 

 

3.7              The Director said that they had requested to change the design so that there is less destruction and minimum disruption and cost savings too.

 

DECISION

 

3.8              After a detailed discussion, the Committee decided to retain the design of the Main Campus Library as approved by A&FC.

 

3.9              Mr. Hanif Daud will have a meeting with Mr. Muhammad Ghufran and Dr. Ahmed Ali Shah and incorporate the internal requirements to be provided by them.

 

ACTION

 

3.10          The Director Projects will arrange a meeting of Mr. Hanif Daud with Mr. Muhammad Ghufran and Dr. Ahmed Ali Shah for incorporating the internal requirements of the library.

 

 

Item No. 4      Approval for cancellation and re-issuance of tenders for Servers and Storage.

 

4.1              The Director ICT reported that Mr. Faisal Zia, Senior Manager IT of IBA had a meeting with the Director CB SPPRA and discussed among others, the evaluation criteria adopted by IBA.  The recommendation of the Director CB SPPRA was that the contracts should be awarded to the technically selected vendor whose bid is the lowest. Accordingly, the Director ICT sought approval of the Executive Committee for cancellation of the following two tenders which do not fall into this category and issuance of new tenders for the same:

 

a)      Servers and Services

b)      Storage Solutions

 

4.2              The Director ICT further informed that M/s. Intel have suggested to procure the new IT infrastructure procurement with virtualization using the Nehalem-Ex architecture.

 

DECISION

 

4.3              The Committee approved the cancellation of the two tenders and allowed issuance of new tenders for Servers and Services, and Storage Solutions.

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and approve Testing Services Policy and remuneration.

 

5.1              Dr. Nasir Touheed presented the proposed Testing Services Policy and remuneration for faculty and staff involved in testing process.

 

5.2              Dr. Nasir Touheed said that the faculty and staff desire that the testing remuneration be increased as the interview time has increased, written test has been added to the test and remedial test has been given to the testing service.  He clarified that the number of candidates has been reduced because of high eligibility criteria.

 

5.3              The Director said that the testing remuneration has been enhanced last year and he cannot enhance the remuneration every year. He also suggested to delete the list of existing as well as prospective corporate clients from the policy.

 

DECISION

 

5.4              The Committee approved the Testing Policy for inclusion in the IBA Code Book.  However, the Committee did not approve the proposed remuneration package for testing services.

 

ACTION

 

5.5              Dr. Nasir Touheed will send the Testing Services Policy to Director H.R. for inclusion in the IBA Code Book.

 

 

Item No. 6      Request for space for ERP Implementation.

 

6.1              The Director ICT said that he needs space for ERP Implementation team.  He said that one option may be to shift the Business English Program to APWA so that three rooms in a row (two already with the IT department) could be available for IT people.

 

6.2              The Director suggested to shift the IT staff in the two rooms attached to the Business English Program to the Hall at APWA building and accommodate the ERP Implementation team also in the same hall.

 

DECISION

 

6.3              The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Director and requested the Registrar to provide a hall at APWA building for the IT Staff as well as the ERP Implementation team.

 

6.4              The Committee further decided that the Director ICT would get the two rooms attached to the Business English Program vacated.

 

 

Item No. 7      To consider and approve the proposal from Director ICT to stop development activities in order to be able to devote complete time to ERP Implementation.

 

7.1              The Director ICT reported that they are receiving requests for certain work due to which they are unable to devote complete time to ERP Implementation. He requested that approval for stoppage of development activities be given.

 

DECISION

 

7.2              The Committee decided that on exceptional basis requests for development activities may be entertained but not on a routine basis.

 

 

Item No. 8      Decisions Implementation Status.

     

8.1       The decisions implementation status was reported to the Executive Committee through email.  However, it was not discussed in the meeting.

 

 

Item No. 9      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider and approve development of (a) Left & right sides of Pathway from Admin. Building to multipurpose building, Main Campus, and (b) Rare side of Administration Building, Main Campus.

 

9.1              The Director Projects reported that as per the request of the Registrar for the development of left and right sides of pathway from Administration Building to Multipurpose building Main Campus due to the visit of Governor at IBA Main Campus on December 12, 2009 and the shifting of parking area near to the Administration Building, detailed survey was done and Bill of Quantities (BOQ) was prepared by Projects Department.  He said that based on quantum of work, the estimated cost comes to approximately Rs.1 million.  He presented the details and sketch.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the work package so that the tendering could be held.

 

9.2              The Director observed that the estimate of Rs.1 million was very high. The Director Projects said that he would try to reduce the cost.

 

DECISION

 

9.3              The Committee approved the development of (a) Left & right sides of Pathway from Admin. Building to multipurpose building, Main Campus, and (b) Rare side of Administration Building, Main Campus as proposed by the Director Projects.

 

ACTION

 

9.4              The Director Projects would initiate the tendering process.

 

b)      To approve schedule of Admissions 2010.

 

9.5              Dr. Nasir Touheed presented the schedule of admissions 2010 for BBA/BS and MBA programs.

 

DECISIONS

 

9.6              The Committee decided that:

a)      Open House should start from February 2010 and the ad for Open House should be placed in January 2010.

b)      BS admissions be conducted separately and the papers should also be different from the BBA program.  Dr. Nasir Touheed will prepare a schedule of admission for BS Program.

c)      Open House for BS Program will also be conducted.

d)     The BS program may be offered on discount as compared to BBA for the reason that the net present value of BBA and BS is different.

e)      The eligibility criteria for BS program may be lowered with the approval of the Academic Board.

 

9.7       The Committee approved the schedule for admissions for BBA and MBA programs incorporating the above decisions.

 

ACTION

 

9.7              Dr. Nasir Touheed will prepare a proposal for lowering the eligibility criteria for approval by the Academic Board

 

 

c)         Approval for photographs and modals.

 

9.8              The Director Projects reported that he has received quotations of Rs.60,000/- for photographs for IBA Buildings and Rs.265,000/- for preparing clay modals of the buildings.  He said that he would further negotiate the fee and requested the EC to accord approval of the quotations for the photographs and modals.

 

 

DECISION

 

9.9              The Committee approved the proposal of the Director Projects for the photographs of IBA Buildings and clay modals of the projects buildings and the quotations.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
