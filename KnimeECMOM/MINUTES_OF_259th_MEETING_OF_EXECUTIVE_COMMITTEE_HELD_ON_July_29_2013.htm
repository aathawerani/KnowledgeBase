MINUTES OF 259TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, JULY 29, 2013

 

The 259th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 29, 2013 at 9:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                           Acting Dean & Director

Cdr (R) Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager, General Maintenance & Security, attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Status Report from Director HEC Projects.

 

1.1       Payment of Rs.9 million to Sui Gas Company: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the required payment was made to SSGC on May 15, 2013. He said that he has been pursuing SSGC to provide the Sui Gas connection as soon as possible. He hoped that the engineer of SSGC would survey the connection points the coming week.

 

The Registrar wanted to know as to when the connection from SSGC is expected. He said that on outsourcing the cafeteria at Students Centre, the first question that is asked by the vendors is if we would provide the gas. If IBA provides the gas then the rates would be different and if not then the rates would be higher. Mr. Shameem replied that he cannot say as to when the connection would be available because they have to get route permission from Karachi University and before that survey is to be done. He said that he is rigorously pursuing for the survey and once the survey is done, and route is approved by the Karachi University, it will take about 3 months for getting the connection. The Registrar suggested that if we get the connection from the outlet near Maskan, it would be better.

 

To a question Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that SSGC has not given approval for gas for gas fired chiller for City Campus. The Registrar said that in his opinion the gas fired chiller is not the best option.  Mr. Shameem said that the Director Projects has not yet decided about the gas fired chiller and is in consultation with the Consultant.

 

DECISION

 

1.2       The Committee noted and approved the up-date.

 

Item No.2       Approval of SLA for garbage disposal and sludge / sewerage water removal from IBA facilities.

 

2.1       Cdr (R) Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager, General Maintenance & Security informed the Committee members that the garbage disposal and sewage water / sludge removal was done through RFQ process on as and when required basis.  He said that as per present practice the garbage is either burnt at Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel and Staff Town or disposed off through Contractors. The garbage at Main Campus and Staff Town was disposed off through Contractor and recently through Afghan Kachra Collector also, which now termed as security risk. However, he said that after commissioning of various new facilities such as AMAN & Tabba blocks, New Boys’ Hostel and about to come the Students’ Centre and Girls’ Hostel; it is felt that Garbage / Sewage water removal may not be further possible through RFQ process due to increased annual costs.  He said that there is a need to stream line this procedure and to have a SLA so that Garbage / Sewage water is disposed of as per required environmental standards. He said that until the infrastructure and the external development work is completed, they would have to go for this arrangement. He requested the EC to accord approval for SLA for garbage disposal and sludge/sewage water removal from IBA facilities so that the process can be completed through Expression of Interest / Tender processed through Manager Purchases.  He said that the current budget allocation has availability of fund for the SLA.

 

2.2       Dr. Nishat wanted to know as to what arrangement Karachi University has for the disposal of their garbage and sewage water.  Cdr. Fahim replied that Karachi University has made Soakage Pits (as now planned by Mr Shameem Ahmed Director HEC) instead of septic tanks for this purpose.

 

2.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to how much money was spent last year on account of disposal of garbage and sludge / sewage water removal.  Cdr. Fahim replied that previously about Rs.175,000/- was spent on this account. He said that they had to pay Rs.4000/- per tanker for the removal of sludge / sewage water. Cdr. Fahim said that due to increased facilities approximately Rs.50,000/- per month would be required on the disposal of garbage/removal of sludge/sewage water from Boys and Girls Hostels, Staff Town, and Main Campus facilities.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that if the amount has been provided in the budget then there is no issue. Mr. Moeid confirmed that Rs.500,000/- has been allocated in the budget on this account.  

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee granted approval for the SLA for garbage disposal and sludge / sewage water removal from IBA facilities.

 

ACTION

 

2.5       Cdr. Fahim in coordination with the Manager Purchase would follow the required procedure for inviting EOI for the above job.

Item No. 4      Implementation of SOP on Fixed Assets.

 

4.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the SOP on Fixed Assets Register was circulated in March 2012 to all concerned.  However, he said that the SOP is not being complied properly. He said that since the Director Finance is the custodian of assets, as per SOP any movement / transfer in fixed assets, should immediately be intimated to the Finance Department but it is not being done. He said that there is a form for Transfer of Assets and one copy of the filled in form should be forwarded to the Finance Department.

 

4.2       Mr. Moeid said that there is a Surplus Property Declaration Form which should be properly filled in if a certain item is no more useful for IBA and should be disposed off. He specially requested Mr. Imran Batada and the Registrar to ensure that the Surplus Property Declaration Form is filled in before disposal of surplus property in order to avoid any audit objection.

 

4.3       Presenting the SOPs for disposal of fixed assets, Mr. Moeid Sultan reiterated that, as per IBA PP&P, only the Director Finance is authorized to transfer or sell surplus property to any inside and outside party. He requested that the “completed form” along with the “surplus property declaration including itemized listing” by the concerned departmental heads must be submitted to the Director Finance through an Asset Disposal Committee. He further said that if the assessed cumulative value of items is estimated within Rs.100,000 then sealed quotations have to be obtained by relevant Purchase office by placing Ad on IBA Website else for sale items above Rs.100,000, sealed quotations be obtained by relevant Purchase office by publishing advertisement preferably in the “classified section” of two newspapers (English/Urdu/Sindhi) as well as hoisting the Ad on IBA Website. A sale request must be submitted by the concerned purchase office to the Director Finance with the suggested vendor name indicated on the request as “SURPLUS” for approval.

 

4.4       The Registrar suggested that as in armed forces, there should be a list of fixed assets placed at every room/hall so that if any item is missing, it could easily be identified and movement of fixed assets could be restricted.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee noted the SOP on fixed assets and its disposal and would ensure its compliance.

 

Item No.5       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
