The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider note from the Director Projects regarding appointment of M/s. A.A. Associates as Design and Supervision Consultants for Pedestrian Bridge between IBA City Campus and APWA Building.

 

1.1       The Director Projects stated that as desired by the IBA Management, he contacted a highly experienced and specialist firm of consulting engineers specializing in Bridge design, to provide professional services for the project.  He said that as the time was limited and an expert opinion was solicited, he invited Mr. Aleem Khan, Principal Partner of M/s. A.A. Associates to the project site.  He said that on his advice site survey was conducted and sent to him and further surveys as required by him will also be done. He requested the Executive Committee to consider the proposal received from M/s. A.A. Associates and approve the award of the work to M/s. A.A. Associates for the design and advisory supervision of the pedestrian bridge connecting the IBA City Campus with the APWA. He stated that M/s. A.A. Associates have a track record of about 50 years of experience in the design of roads and bridges in Pakistan.

1.2      In their proposal given by M/s. A.A. Associates, they stated that the proposed bridge shall be 70 feet long in structural steelwork comprising two spans each 35 ft. supported on a central column in the middle of the road and two end columns to be situated at the ends of the footpaths. Before the final design is taken up an accurate survey of the existing overhead and underground services shall be necessary to determine which of the services need to be diverted and/or relocated. Geotechnical investigations report at the site of the campus in the proximity of the proposed structure would also be required for the design of foundations. The scope of their services shall be:

1.         Preparation of the detailed design and construction drawings.

2.         Reparation of Tender and Contract Documents comprising Instructions to Bidders, General and Particular Conditions of Contract, Specifications and Bills of Quantities.

3.         Assistance in prequalification of Bidders and after the bids are received in the evaluation of bids.

4.         During construction advisory services comprising weekly site visits and checking and certification of the Contractor's bills.

1.3       The design with tender and contract documents is expected to be complete in four weeks of the signing of the Agreement. For the design and preparation of tender and contract documents  they will charge a lump sum fee of two hundred thousand Rupees and fifty thousand rupees per month for the advisory services during construction; payable as follows:

·         A sum of Rupees 50,000 shall be payable on the signing of the Agreement

·         A sum of Rupees 150,000 shall be payable on completion and submission of the drawings and tender and contract documents.

·         For advisory services during construction a sum of Rupees 50,000 shall be payable at the end of each month of the construction of the works.

1.4              The Director said that the tender documents and estimates are to be provided to the City Nazim for which there is a need to engage a Consultant but he said that there is no need to engage and pay for supervision. The City Government, if needed, may engage a Consultant for supervision.

 

1.5       In reply to a query from Director IBA, the Director Projects stated that IBA does not have the design engineers to design the work related to steel structural engineering.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee decided that as proposed by the Director Projects M/s. A.A. Associates may be engaged for preparing designs, estimates and tender for the pedestrian bridge against a lump sum payment of Rs.200,000/-    

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider note from the Director Projects for assigning the following work related to Aman Building to Mr. Shahab Ghani.

 

·         Survey and analysis of users needs

·         Development of area requirements and zoning for various activities

·         Development of design brief including spatial planning and general specifications to be given to the Turnkey contractor to enable him prepare a comprehensive technical and financial bid for the building.

2.1       The Director Projects stated that in the last Executive Committee Meeting it was decided to contact Mr Shahab Ghani, who was already engaged as Consulting Architect for Technical Evaluation of Design Competition, to furnish his proposal for the following works for Aman Foundation Building stage 1. (For stage 2, proposals will be invited after identifying the details of its scope of works).

·         Survey and analysis of users needs

·         Development of area requirements and zoning for various activities

·         Development of design brief including spatial planning and general specifications to be given to the Turnkey contractor to enable him prepare a comprehensive technical and financial bid for the building.

2.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that earlier two Architects had quoted Rs.5.0 Million and Rs.1.6 Million respectively whereas the baseline was Rs.350,000 for phase 1. He presented Mr Shahab Ghanis proposal which he received through E mail.

2.3       Mr. Shahab Ghani in his proposal mentioned that the fee for Phase 1 will be Rs.250,000 and would take approximately 6 to 8 weeks. The break up of Rs.250,000/- is as under:

 

·         Rs.50,000/- for User Need Analysis   

·         Rs.50,000/- for development of area requirements and building zoning for various activities and their inter relation

·         Rs.150,000/- for development of design brief with spatial requirement, general specs of building to be given to the Turnkey Contractor to enable him to design the building and prepare a comprehensive technical and financial proposal. 

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that in his opinion the proposal is technically and financially feasible and recommended that the work be assigned to Mr. Shahab Ghani.

DECISION

2.5       The Committee approved the recommendation of the Director Projects for assigning the work mentioned in 2.1 above to Mr. Shahab Ghani at a total fee of Rs.250,000/- 

2.6       The Director informed Mr. Shahab Ghani about the donation received from Aman Foundation and requested Mr. Shahab Ghani to protect IBAs interest and be representative of IBA as far as the whole construction is concerned and ensure that work is done according to the specifications and no unnecessary cost is involved.

4.2       The Director ICT informed the Committee that the HEC has put IBA on top of the World Bank and USAID and they feel that these grants would be coming forth very soon. He suggested that to avail the HEC prices quoted above we will have to cancel the tender received for the ERP on grounds that all bids received were over our budget. Then IBA will go with HEC and for the implementation of the PeopleSoft, award the contract to the lowest bidder.  Additionally, IBA will also get College Net Scheduling Module and Oracle RAC at subsidized rates if we contract with HEC

 

4.3              The Director ICT informed the Committee that the ERP has been installed at six public sector universities by the HEC. The Director said that the HEC wants to have a common database. They wanted a uniform ERP System and on trial basis they did that for six universities but in the meantime funding dried up. Now they want to apply in all the universities in Pakistan. The Director said that if the HEC gets the fund and donates the ERP system to IBA as a gift then there is no problem, alternatively IBA will have to spend Rs.20 million from its own fund which is currently available from Aman Foundation Fund.  However, he clarified that despite this they have to go according to the PPRA Rules. He said that in his opinion as the HEC is giving this package, they may cancel the advertised tender. 

 

4.4              Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Head of Internal Audit, said that we can cancel the tender on certain grounds. He said that we are not sure what the implementation cost would be. He said that according to PPRA rules for the sake of transparency everything should be advertised. He further said that the PPRA allows direct contracting when the price of foods, services and work is fixed by government or any other authority / agency of body duly authorized by the government on its behalf.    

 

4.5              Mr. Moiz Hassanali also informed that software and implementation by HEC must meet all IBA requirements for example; campus management, financials, human resource projects, payroll, material management / procurements etc.

 

4.6              The Director ICT asserted that all inclusive and total prices of ERP project are fixed by HEC which is the government organization and HEC offered ERP module meet the entire IBA requirements.    

 

4.7              Mr. Moiz Hassanali informed the Committee that approval for cancellation could be obtained from the same authority that approved the procurement and suggested that ground for cancellation is needed to be informed to bidders and published through notice. In this case he said that the apparent ground for cancellation is that the IBA ERP project requirement can be met by a substantially less expensive alternative from Higher Education commission (HEC).

 

DECISION

 

4.8              The Committee agreed to the suggestion given by Mr. Moiz Hassanali in 4.7 above.

 

ACTION

 

4.9       The Director ICT will take action according to the suggestion given by Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Head of Internal Auditor.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
