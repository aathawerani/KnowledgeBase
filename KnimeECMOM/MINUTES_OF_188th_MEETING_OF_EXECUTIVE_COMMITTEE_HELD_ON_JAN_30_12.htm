Coordinator Testing Services – Dr. Nasir Touheed:

Item No.2       To consider and approve recommendations for positions up-gradation.

 

2.1       The Dean & Director said that there are two different types of career advancements in an organization – one in a pyramid structure there are structured positions of higher responsibilities and they are filled in by promotion and the second is through professional growth. Every department has got a structure – some people will be promoted for example from Executive to Senior Executive position. Post up-gradation on the other hand is a change in the scope and the responsibility of that particular post. So promotion is the most favourable part of a human resource policy because it provides the satisfaction and the self-esteem goes up.  H.R. department has to do screening whether it is a case of promotion or up-gradation.

 

2.2       The Registrar said that there is a miscommunication on this issue. We should have made it clear that the up-gradation is not related to an individual’s performance and promotion to the next ladder. He said that a person who is looking after the Academic Block and the scope of work of that person is expanded to look after another academic block, in this case the position has to be up-graded due to expanded job responsibilities.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that in the organization structure there are departments with critical mass where career progression will proceed through normal promotion according to the eligibility criteria. Departments such as Facilities Management where facilities and scope of work are expanding, naturally there will be additional responsibilities and the upgraded job will become open to competition. But in other departments where there is no such expansion, the employees of those departments may apply for the position opened up because they do not have much of a chance for promotion within their department. We will give preference to our internal persons and if the internal candidates do not match the requirements then we will seek candidates from outside IBA. He said that in Maintenance and Facilities Management posts may have to be upgraded.

 

2.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the staff of the whole organization cannot be up-graded.  He said that 10 to 15 percent is the rule of thumb in the corporate world for promotion.

 

2.5       The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. to screen all the recommendations received from the departments. He said that we have to separate promotion cases from those of up-gradation. He asked the Director H.R to send back the recommendations for promotion and up-gradation to the EC after the concerned departments have identified promotion cases within the organization structure and the posts which are to be up-graded with justifications for each case.  He asked the Director H.R. that after the promotion and up-gradation cases are identified, she should discuss the cases with each departmental head and come to a conclusion and if there is a difference of opinion then come to the EC.

 

2.6       Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested that as agreed in earlier meeting not only the scale but designation should also be changed e.g. if an individual is promoted from J-1 to J2 level, then his or her designation should also change.

 

2.7       The Director H.R. said that this would be done in March this year.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee agreed to the suggestion that each department will look at its approved organogram and send promotion proposals to H.R. The H.R. will present the recommended cases to the Promotion Committee which will interview the candidates and recommend the cases to the Dean & Director / Selection Board as the case may be.  The cases for up-gradation of posts will be presented to the EC with full justifications.
