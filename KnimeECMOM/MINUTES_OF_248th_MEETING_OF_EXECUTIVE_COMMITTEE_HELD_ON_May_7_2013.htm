Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Project Management fee of renovation and refurbishment of existing Library for extended period.

 

1.1       The Director Projects said that the Central Purchase Committee and the Executive Committee approved the extension of the Project’s contractor from February 01, 2013 to July 3, 2013 in their meetings held on March 16, 2013 and March 25, 2013 respectively. The Executive Committee in its meeting held on April 23, 2013 further extended the completion time up-till August 03, 2013(6 Months).

 

1.2       The Director Projects said that the Project Management Services of M/s NESPAK also require to be extended along with contractor’s extension and the monthly remuneration will also require to be approved in order to get the continuous services of M/s NESPAK. He said that as this project requires very close monitoring and coordination, therefore, the monthly remuneration charges for NESPAK will remain same as we are paying currently as per contract. As per the contract per month remuneration of Project Management Firm is Rs.122,500/-  Therefore, the total fee up till Extended Period of 6 Months would be (6 months X Rs.122,500) = Rs.735,000/-

 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on Saturday, May 4, 2013 has scrutinized and recommended the extension for Project Management services and the fee for the approval of the Executive Committee. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the Project Management Fee of Rs.735,000/- up till August 3, 2013.  He said that this cost is only for the extended period other terms and conditions of the contract will remain unchanged.

 

1.4       The Dean & Director said that it should be made clear to the Project Manager and the Consultant that no extension after August 3, 2013 will be given and they will have to complete the residual work without any fee.

 

1.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the quantum of work has reduced so their fee should also be reduced in that proportion. The Director Projects said that the work will be on the peak because of installation of AC and HVAC systems. Their mechanical and electrical engineers will also be coming for the installation of the systems.

 

1.6       The Dean & Director said that their efforts and manpower be accelerated so that the project is completed by August 3, 2013.    

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee approved the Project Management Fee of Rs.735,000/- for NESPAK up till the extended period of August 3, 2013. The Committee decided that no further extension would be granted after August 3, 2013 and the Project Manager (NESPAK) would complete the residual work, if any, after August 3, 2013 without charging any fee to IBA.

 

ACTION

 

1.8       The Director Projects would ensure that the Project is completed by August 3, 2013. And if there is any residual work after August 3, 2013, he would get it completed by M/s. NESPAK without making any additional payment.

Item No.3       Presentation on Interior design of Aman Tower.

 

3.1       Mr. Hasnain Lotia of EA Consulting presented the floor-wise interior design of Aman Tower. The following comments / suggestions were made on the interior design of Aman Tower:

 

·         The Dean & Director said that as payment for the Interior works and furniture is to be made through IBA budget therefore, it is mandatory to keep it as economical as possible.

·         The Dean & Director suggested that instead of sofas, reading tables should be extended in the Library. Data /electrical points be provided in the tables that will be used for standing laptop usage.

·         Horse shoe arrangements should be made for graduate classes.

·         The training rooms should be made with flexible partitioning. 

·         At CEE there will not be fixed seating classrooms, it will be multipurpose room that can be reconfigured according to the requirements.

·         The Director CEE proposed that two rooms should be combined and the walls in between the columns should be made flexible. However, Mr. Lotia said that because of pillars it will not look good.  Mr. Lotia suggested to visit the site before taking any decision which was agreed. The Dean and Director, Director CEE, Mr.Mobin Khalili

and Mr. Ahmed Zaheer will visit.

·         It was suggested to see if natural light can be provided in the building especially the CEE classes at 3rd and 4th floor. The Dean and Director emphasiozed the need of natural light where the reading and writing activities are conducted. He further added that there is a need to improve the ambiance by the addition of natural light.

·         Sufficient space should be provided in the CEE lounge for more interaction and networking.  There should be minimum sofa sets in the lounge.

·         The same spec furniture and tablet chairs should be arranged for the Aman Tower class rooms as done for the Students Centre.

·         One of the Conference rooms at the faculty floor may be converted into faculty lounge.  The Dean & Director said that the faculty members would be asked if they want close or open faculty lounge.

·         The Conference room at the faculty floor will be moved to the centre and the Assistants will be provided separate work stations instead of making them sit together.

·         At 12th floor seminar halls, ailes be given in the center. Mr. Husnain lotia informed that the present arrangement has been made for the students/audience to have a better view of the boards/screens.

·         The Dean and Director advised that the cost estimate of the interior works be submitted as early as possible.

·         MA TV points, CCTV points, PA system and sound system in class rooms, seminar rooms were presented by EA, which were approved.

·         The Dean and Director observed that the designing of spaces lack the facilities like book shop, resource centre, prayer areas for ladies and gents, indoor games (table tennis, pool table). The Architect should consider to include all these facilities.

·         Mr. Jawad requested that instead of having two generators of 1700 KVA, they should have three generators of 600 KVA each so that they may use it according to the need.

·         The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why additional requirements for UPS have been made while we have got standby generators and are also exempted from load shedding. He asked Mr. Jawad to recalculate the standby generator capacity and UPS requirements and to find out the circumstances under which UPS would be needed.   Mr. Imran Batada suggested that they will review the UPS requirements and as such UPS backup shall be provided only for the switch rooms and multimedia projectors.

·         The Dean & Director said that when there was no KESC power supply, the ACs should not be on.  He asked the Maintenance Manager to prepare a policy in this regard and get it approved from him.

·         The number of LCD/LED Plasma at cafeteria be reduced from five to two.

·         Wiring for PA system will be done but the system will be acquired later.

 

DECISION

 

3.2       The Committee approved the suggestions noted above.

 

ACTION

 

3.3       The Director Projects would get the above suggestions implemented.

 

3.4       Mr. M. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer would find out the circumstances under which UPS would be needed and the probability of its use.  On the basis of his findings he would recalculate the standby generator capacity and UPS requirements and inform the EC accordingly.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Approval of paying 40% Mobilization Advance to SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd., in respect of the contract for procurement of furniture for New Girls' Hostel.

 

5.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the girls’ hostel is under construction at IBA Staff Town at a cost of Rs.94.484 million, out of which a funding of Rs.57.450 million has been provided by the Government of Sindh, under its Sindh Talent Hunt Program.  He said that while part of the hostel is likely to be completed by the end of June and the rest in July/August, 2013, the funds provided by the Sindh Govt. are required to be utilized by the middle of June 2013.

 

5.2       He said that the major issue faced by them at the moment is the utilization of funds allocated for procurement of furniture for the hostel.  After following all procedure and taking all approvals they placed an order on SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd. for manufacture and supply of furniture worth Rs.6,229,437/-. The supply is required to be completed by June 14, 2013. However, as the contractor is facing liquidity problem, it is most unlikely that he will be able to deliver the furniture by the given deadline unless we facilitate him financially.  In case of his default IBA will also suffer as we will be unable to utilize the funds made available to us by Sindh Government and the contractor’s payment will have to be made from IBA’s own resources.

 

5.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that in February 2013 the contractor requested them to pay him 40% mobilization advance but they refused it. However, since they face the problem of timely utilization of STHP funds as well as the likelihood of the contractor’s failure to supply the furniture in time, it is in IBA’s interest that we accede to the contractor’s request for mobilization advance. He informed the Committee that the contractor has been working with IBA on various projects since 2008 and they have found him a trustworthy person. He said that they may demand that the contractor furnishes an Insurance Guarantee for the Mobilization Advance.

 

5.4       He said that he had requested CPC to approve making the 40 % advance payment to the contractor. Forty percent of Rs.6,229,437/- works out to Rs.2,491,775/-.  The CPC considered and approved it in its meeting held on April 13, 2013. The approval has, however, been given subject to the following conditions:

 

(a)    The advance payment will be made to the contractor in two instalments, each of 20%, against a total insurance bond of 40% which will have an expiry of six months from the date of issue. The second instalment will be paid after supply of items worth the amount of the first instalment.

 

(b)   The contractor SIMAB be not given any additional project till completion of all supplies for the New Girls’ Hostel

 

(c)    Conceptual approval be obtained from the EC before proceeding on the CPC approval.

 

5.5       The Dean & Director said that the mobilization advance should be made only if the furniture is delivered by the deadline given to the Contractor.  Mr. Shameem Ahmed confirmed that it would be delivered.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that if the payment is on delivery then there is no issue. However, he said that if we are paying in advance, then insurance guarantee will be needed. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the CPC has also advised the same and they would do that. The Registrar suggested to give them the delivery schedule against the payment.  Mr. Shameem replied that they have already done that.

 

DECISION

 

5.6       The Committee approved the payment of 40% Mobilization Advance to SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd., against an insurance guarantee for the furniture for New Girls' Hostel.

 

a) Proposal for raising of boundary wall around IBA Main Campus and Staff Town & Boys Hostel.

 

5.7       Major (Retd.) Qamar Abbas, Security Coordinator proposed that in view of the possible security threats, volatile environments of Karachi City and to safeguard Institute’s costly and precious physical as well as intellectual properties and valuables, it is necessary to enhance its physical security assets by raising the existing boundary wall of its Main Campus, Staff Town and Boys Hostel premises and its reinforcement with barbed wire.  He said that the following are the perceived threats due to short height of boundary wall and broken walls of Karachi University:

 

o   Trespassing

o   Infiltration by undesired elements inside campus premises

o   Intruders’ movement

o   Theft and pilferages

o   Smuggling of contraband items inside campus premises

o   Throw of explosive / sound crackers etc.

 

5.8       The Dean & Director said that the external perimeter of the Main Campus wall is secured. When the projects are completed the labour will also not be entering the premises.  He said that the perceived threats listed above are of temporary nature.

 

5.9       The Registrar said that it has been reported that construction material such as saria, wire etc. is thrown outside the wall because of its low height. The wall itself is not very strong as it does not have strong foundation. He said that there are two options; one is to start building the foundation of the wall but that would be very expensive and the second option is to erect a pillar in between the joining of two walls and raise its height.

 

5.10     The Dean & Director said that security needed to be strengthened.  He said that watch tower would be more effective and if needed search light can also be installed. He said that we have to examine our priorities.  At this stage IBA does not have the resources for such an expenditure on the boundary wall. He said that he would speak to the Governor of Sindh about the broken walls of Karachi University but at this stage he is not convinced with expensive solution presented. He said that a better approach would be to have the existing wall demolished and replace it with wall with proper foundation and height raised with barbed wire.

 

5.11     About the boundary wall of the Boys’ Hostel, Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the contractor refused to work on this additional project so they have no option but to tender for it.  The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to do the tender for raising the boundary wall of the Boys’ Hostel.  He suggested that the number of security guards at Boys’ Hostel should be increased; three at night and two in day time and they should keep on patrolling. He asked the Security Coordinator to take special care of the security arrangements of Boys’ and Girls Hostels.

 

5.12     The Dean & Director suggested that the boundary wall of the Staff Town should be raised. He asked as to how much it would cost.  

 

5.13     Major (R) Qamar Abbas suggested that Karachi University does not easily allow heavy vehicles with construction material to enter through the Maskan Gate.  They suggest to enter from the gate opposite Kaneez Fatima Society. He suggested that an entrance can be made for heavy vehicles with construction material from the rear side of our boundary wall and the gate dismantled from the City Campus can be fixed there. 

 

DECISION

 

5.14     The Committee decided that instead of raising walls at the Main Campus, Watch Towers would be made and search light would be installed.

 

5.15     The Committee approved the opening of an entrance from the rear side of the IBA boundary wall for the heavy vehicles.  The Registrar would seek approval from Karachi University, if needed, for the use of the University gate opposite Kaneez Fatima society.    

 

5.16     The Committee decided that the number of security guards at the Boys Hostel would be two in day time and three at night. 

 

ACTION

 

5.17     The Registrar and the Director Projects would get the above decisions implemented.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
