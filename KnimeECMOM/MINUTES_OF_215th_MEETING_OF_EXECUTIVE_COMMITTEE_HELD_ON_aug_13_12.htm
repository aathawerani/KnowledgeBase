MINUTES OF 215TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, AUGUST 13, 2012

 

The 215th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 13, 2012 at 9:00 a.m. at Societies Meeting Room, Adamjee Academic Centre, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1.       Dr. Ishrat Husain                                        Dean & Director

2.       Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                 Associate Dean, FBA

3.       Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                       Associate Dean, FCS

4.       Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                        Registrar

5.       Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                           Director Projects

6.       Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                       Director ICT

7.       Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                   Director CED

8.       Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                   Director H.R.

9.       Mr. Moeid Sultan                                        Acting Director Finance

 

The following attended the meeting on special invitation:

1)    Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects

2)    Mr. Kumail Raza Hemani, Program Office Manager

3)    Mr. Khalid Pervez of M/s. KP & Associates

 

The following items were discussed:      

Item No.1      To consider and approve cost for emergent temporary supply of 100 KW from existing panel of Admin. Building to Aman CED (Option ‘B’) and the additional cost of emergent supply of total demand of Aman CED and new Academic Block, from KESC new power connection (Option ‘A’).

1.1     The Director Projects reported that it was decided in the meeting held in the office of Dean  & Director of IBA, that: 

·         100KW be taken from existing LT panel of Existing Administration Building to Aman CED building on emergency basis 

·         and then implementing the works to energise both the buildings i.e. Aman–CED and New academic Block from KESC bulk connection next when KESC will be available, until the work on Main Electrical Infrastructure works is undertaken.

1.2     He reported that subsequently detailed planning, designing and costing work were undertaken by the consultants. The following options were arrived at:

·         Emergent temporary supply of 100 KW from existing panel of Administration Building to Aman-CED Building (Plan B). 

·         Emergent supply of total demand load of Aman-CED and New Academic Block (Plan ‘A’).

1.3     The Director Projects said that this was discussed with Mr. M. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer and, Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed, Maintenance Manager of IBA. He presented a line diagram of both the systems. He said that complete BOQ were prepared for the combined option ’B’ and option ‘A’, which were arrived at after the detailed scrutiny of contractor’s offer and the existing BOQ of awarded contract to M/s. National Engineering Co.

1.4     The combined cost of option ‘B’ and ‘A’ comes to Rs.13,846,524/- out of which the cost of Rs.5,954,063/- is chargeable to existing BOQ of the  main Contract given to M/s. National Engineering Co. He said that after both the options are exercised, the additional cost would be Rs.7,892,461/-

1.5     He said that the cost of Rs.5,435,475/- will be a complete extra item. However, ATS cost of Rs.1.67 million will be for permanent installation and is essentially required to be there when Plan A is executed. He said that a BoQ item for ATS amounting to Rs.0.8 million existed in Aman CED original contract but this one would be a bigger one. The 95 mm cables (1416 feet), costing Rs.2.9 million can be re-used in future. He said that the Contractor can complete the work on Plan B by 31st August, 2012, if the approval is given on 13th August.

1.6     He said that in case only option ‘A’ is to be executed then its cost would be Rs.10.946 million (Cost of Options B&A = Rs.13,846,524/- minus the cost of 95 mm cable Rs.2,889,855/- = Rs.10.946 million.  He said that when item chargeable to BOQ (Rs.5,954,063/-) is deducted from Rs.10.946 million, the  additional amount required to implement option ‘A’ would be Rs.4.992 million which can further be reduced to Rs.4.2 million by deducting Rs.0.8 million provided for the ATS in the original contract.

1.7     The Director Projects informed the Committee that the contractor has committed that he would implement option ‘A’ by 20th September, if the approval is given to him on 13th August 2012. The Contractor has also required that the KESC power at VCB should be available on 15th September to complete the connections.

1.8     The Director Projects informed the Committee that the cost of running generator for 10 hours per day is Rs.112,310/- Thus for 20 days it would cost Rs.2,246,200/- He said that in implementation of both options ‘B’ & ‘A’, the approval for an extra cost of Rs.7.892 million would be required.  In implementing option ‘A’ only, Rs.4.992 million additional cost would be required that would mean that we can achieve only a saving of Rs.2.9 million in foregoing option ‘B’, under which only 100KW shall be available on 1st September 2012. In this case the generator will have to be operated from 1st September 2012 to 20th September, the cost of which will come to Rs.2.246 million. He said that if we wait for execution of the infrastructure works then they would have to run the generator for 5 to 6 months; the expenses of which would be around Rs.15 million plus cost of wear and tear and above all there would be no back up available.

1.9     Based on the above, the Director Projects recommended that the approval for implementation of both the options be given which has also been recommended by the Consultants.

1.10    The Registrar said that we have two breakers available which can be used and that would further reduce the cost.

1.11    The Dean & Director wanted to know if, when the KESC supply is available, the 1416 feet 95 mm cable which is estimated to cost Rs.2.9 million can be used or sold if not needed.  The Director Projects replied that the cable can either be used in our own project or it can be sold if not needed.   

1.12    The Dean & Director said that the ICT equipments are to be energized for which it has been suggested by the Consultant that 100 KW will be enough for the building except the chiller. He said that for the time being (till the KESC supply is available) there would be no AC in the offices and faculty and staff would be requested to bear this for some time i.e. till October 2012. He said that our priority is IT server and ICT equipments which should be energized.  He asked Mr. Khalid Pervez if we can do the work with 100 KW.

1.13    The Registrar said that he has 60 pedestal fans in his stock which can be provided to the offices.  Mr. Khalid Pervez confirmed that 60 pedestal fans, 36 lap tops, 25 desk tops and 04 split ACs (in the server room) would require 60 KW and still there would 40 KW available for miscellaneous requirements. The Director ICT said that 30 KW alone would be required for the Server and UPS.  He suggested that to manage the load they may switch off the servers after 5:00 p.m.  However, the Registrar said that he has alternate arrangement and he would take care of the uninterrupted power requirement of the servers and UPS for 24 hours.  He suggested that Option ‘A’ only be approved as there is huge difference of cost between the two options. Mr. Khalid Pervez also agreed with the Registrar.

1.14    The Director Projects said that on Aman CED the contractor on the electrical works is the same and as such a separate variation order can be issued for the ATS against Aman CED contract. The value of the variation order would be determined after deducting the amount of the two breakers to be furnished by the Maintenance Manager and adding an approximate cost of Rs.200,000/- being the amount needed for making ATS 100% automatic.  He said that the value of the ATS cost as recommended by the Consultant is Rs.1,657,416/- Thus the value of variation order for the electrical infrastructure shall be (Rs.4.992 – Rs.1.657) = Rs.3.335 million and the total value of both the variation order should not exceed Rs.4.2 million.

1.15    Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there are many opportunities of energy conservation and he offered his services free of cost. The Committee appreciated the offer.  The Dean & Director suggested that energy conservation measures should be initiated now as Mr. Khalid is giving his professional expertise free of cost for the job. 

DECISION

1.16    The Committee approved Option ‘A’ which would cost around Rs.4.2 million as compared to Option ‘B’ which would not serve the purpose effectively as the Servers and UPS will have to be shut down at night. Option ‘A’ would require the generator to be operational from 1st September 2012 to 20th September, 2012 for 8 working hours per day.  The decision was based on (a) cost effectiveness, (b) Classes can be held on time in September 2012, (c) Admin Building occupants can be shifted to Aman CED on time. 

ACTION

1.17    The Maintenance Manager would provide two breakers available at his department to the Projects Department. The Director Projects would have the cost of ATS provided in the BoQ deleted on receiving the breaker.

1.18    The Director Projects would issue a separate variation order to the existing contractor for electrical works at Aman CED for the ATS. The value to be determined after deducting the amount of two breakers. 

1.19    The Registrar would take care of and arrange the power supply for Server and UPS at Aman CED Building from alternate sources.

Item No. 2     International Resource Centre (IRC) Launch Plan.

2.1     Mr. Kumail Raza Hemani, Program Office Manager, while presenting the International Resource Centre (IRC) Launch Plan, said that the purpose of IBA’s IRC is to:

o    Open our doors to the world, and

o    Expand the scope of our academics beyond our geographical borders. 

 2.2     Mr. Hemani also presented the requirements of the IRC which included:

·         development of faculty expertise,

·         increasing international students on campus,

·         developing research and entrepreneurial linkages and partnerships

·         development funding sources for international programming

·         increasing our presence and name recognition globally

·         focusing on specific regional opportunities that advance IBA’s international vision

2.3     Mr. Hemani informed the Committee that the proposed IRC Launch date is October 23, 2012. The ceremony would be graced by the Country Head of British Council. The proposed venue is either G&T Auditorium, Main Campus or CEE at City Campus.  The proposed invitees are educational attaches/ambassadors from Sri Lanka, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, USA and UK.  Faculty and students who have gone abroad on exchange programs would also be invited. He proposed that Pakistani students who have received degrees from abroad e.g. Warwick, to speak to the gathering about importance of an international office.

2.4     The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why educational attaches/ambassadors of other countries would come to this program. He said that many of the proposed countries do not have their educational attaches.

2.5     Mr. Hemani said that the British Council is working with different universities of these proposed countries and those universities are also focusing on expanding the scope as we are doing.

2.6     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that the educational attaches/ambassadors of the proposed countries should be extended invitation by the British Council. 

2.7     Mr. Hemani requested that a space be allocated for the placement of shelves from British Council and Physical Office Space for IRC, and a student life/IBA video be made showcasing students, infrastructure, IBA tradition etc.

2.8     The Registrar suggested that break out rooms at CED can be allocated for this purpose. However, the Dean & Director said that the front office at City Campus where Mr. Habib-ur-Rahman and Ms. Mahwish Butt are seated can be provided to the IRC team and Mr. Habib and Ms. Mahwish may be placed somewhere else. He requested the Registrar to find out some alternate place for Mr. Habib and Ms. Mahwish.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that the IRC, CDC and CED teams should sit together.     

2.9     Mr. Hemani said that the Student Life/IBA video would be prepared by the students.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he is also getting a video prepared. The Dean & Director suggested that there should be a provision in the tender for video that from time to time we can update the video.

2.10    About the proposed IRC Road Shows Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that because of language barrier, Egypt should be excluded from the suggested countries. He requested the Director H.R. to get a separate website of IRC prepared which should be linked to the IBA Website.  The Director ICT assured that he would get the website of IRC prepared by October 23, 2012.

2.11    About the fee structure for foreign students the Dean & Director suggested to visit Dow University of Health Sciences and see the students body and the fee structure for Pakistani expatriates. He further suggested to see the arrangements of the Pakistan Government’s Technical Assistance provided to the students from Sudan, and Ethiopia studying at Karachi University. He suggested to invite Karachi University, NED, and Dow University.

2.12    About the IRC Documentation and Budgets, the Director H.R. said that the budget for IRC would be accommodated in the already approved budget for Communication so there is no need to prepare a separate budget for IRC.  She said that the total estimated cost for the printed material, video and social media would be around Rs.250,000 to Rs.300,000/-

2.13    The Registrar suggested to move the launch plan a month ahead so that civil work at FCS Building is also complete where the faculty offices are to be moved and the Convocation date will also be very close to the event. The Director H.R. said that they would see if that is also suitable for the British Council.

DECISION

2.14    The Committee approved the Launch Plan of the International Resource Centre along with the suggestions noted above.

ACTION

2.15    The Registrar would arrange to accommodate the offices of IRC, CDC and CED teams in the front office at City Campus and find out some alternate place for Mr. Habib and Ms. Mahwish. 

2.16    Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would make a provision of up-dating the video from time to time in the tender to be issued for IBA documentary.

2.17    The Director ICT would get a separate website of IRC prepared by October 23, 2012. The IRC website would be linked with the main website of IBA.

2.18    Mr. Kumail Hemani would visit Dow University of Health Sciences to see their students’ body and their fee structure for Pakistani expatriates. He would also see the arrangements of the Pakistan Government’s Technical Assistance provided to the students from Sudan, and Ethiopia studying at Karachi University.

2.19    The Director H.R. would check with the British Council if they can move the IRC launch plan a month ahead i.e. sometime towards end of November 2012 instead of the proposed date of October 23, 2012.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
