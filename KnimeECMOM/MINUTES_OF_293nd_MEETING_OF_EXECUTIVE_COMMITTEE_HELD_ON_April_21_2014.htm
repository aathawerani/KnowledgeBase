 Item No. 3      To consider and approve the Extension time for completion of Sewage Treatment Plant at IBA Main Campus.  

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the contract for sewage treatment plant was awarded to M/s RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractors (Pvt.) Ltd. on January 15, 2013 at a cost of Rs.20,866,109/-. The Commencement date was fixed as March 18, 2013. The total contract duration was 12 calendar months hence the completion date was March 17, 2014. However, the project got delayed due to the following reasons:

  • Delay in possession of site

  • Excavation of Hard Strata and De-watering

  • Rain, Scarcity of SR cement, CNG Closure,  Power Load Shedding and law and order Situation.

     Delay in Possession of site

The possession of site was delayed due to already laid concrete debris, tiles and scattered scrap, un-useable items and old furniture.

The contractor claimed an extension of 32 days on account of delay due to above reasons which were totally beyond the control of contractor hence the extension of 32 days for this delay are justifiable and recommended. M/s NESPAK (Project Manager) have also recommended the extension. 

     Excavation of hard strata and de-watering

Although the excavation in all types of soil was in scope of contractor but during the execution of work it was observed that hard rock and continuous de-watering caused the work pace very slow in this regard contractor claimed 57 days but the justified and reasonable extension is 20 days. Hence recommended for 20 days extension due to this reason. The Project Manager M/s NESPAK also recommended 20 days for the above mentioned reason.

     Rain, Scarcity of SR Cement, CNG Closure, Power Load shedding and law and order situation.

The contractor claimed the 90 days extension of time due to unpredictable climate conditions, CNG, Power load shedding at Hub (the plant was in fabrication process at their factory situated in Hub) and law and order situation of the city. After detailed scrutiny and verification it was observed that 15 days are justified for above given reasons due to labor transportation problem and law and order of the city during one year.

Hence only 15 days are recommended for the extension of time due to above mentioned reason.

3.2       The Director Projects said that total extension of 67 days is recommended for above mentioned reason. He said that the Project Manager also recommended the 67 days vide their recommendation letter.

3.3       The Director Projects reported that in its meeting held on 19th April 2014, the  Procurement Committee considered the reasons to be genuine and approved the  extension of time up till May 23, 2014 and that no escalation will be given due to time extension. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to accord its approval for the extension of 67 days till May 23rd 2014.

3.4       The Dean & Director said that the A&FC has to be convinced of these reasons for the delay. The Director Projects said that the Contractor has not claimed any escalation for it but only extension is needed. The Dean & Director said that the Project Management proposes the overall External Development Project and not separate components should be taken into account as cost of components proceeding simultaneously.

3.5       The Registrar asked as to when the connection to the main trunk will be given.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that by May 5, 2014 connection to the main trunk will be given. He said that the irrigation system will be operational when the treatment plant is hopefully ready by July this year. The Dean & Director said that he wants the treated sewage water to be used first for the Sports Ground.

DECISION

3.6       The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the extension of 67 days for completion of Sewage Treatment Plant at IBA Main Campus till May 23rd 2014.

ACTION

3.7       The Director Projects would present the recommendation for extension to the A&FC for approval.

Item No. 5      To consider and approve further time extension of renovation and up-gradation of existing Administration Building at Main Campus.

5.1       The Director Projects said that the contract of renovation and up-gradation of existing Administration Building at Main Campus was given to M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises.  The commencement date of the project was March 15, 2012 and it was to be completed in 18 months i.e by September 14, 2013. However, due to the following reasons six months extension in completion time of this project i.e. upto March 15, 2014 was given:

Partial handing over of the floors to the Contractor:

It is a difficult task to keep the normal academic activities running without any interruption while renovating, modernizing, and expanding existing buildings. The temporary Library was located on the ground floor of the Admin Building and could not be moved in absence of a suitable alternate accommodation. This took some time till the possession of Tabba Building. The Academic Block was taken over and the Library was shifted there at a time when the students were on academic break.  Hence there was time slippage on handing over the building to the Contractor.  At City Campus we were able to manage because we had alternate accommodation available by renting APWA Building floors but at the Main Campus we did not have alternate arrangement. The shifting of Admin offices was also delayed because the CED building was delayed where the offices were to be shifted temporarily. This is a kind of dependence syndrome in running uninterrupted academic programs at the same time renovating and remodelling the existing buildings. 

Technical reasons:

During excavation, existing footing of foundation was encountered due to which location of lift well was revised. Due to hard rock, design of footing was revised and pace of excavation was slowed and excavation was performed manually without using mechanical equipment to avoid noise and disturbance in existing operational library at ground floor.

The building was designed and redesigned three times because of changing user requirements, budget constraints and value engineering. The continuous law and order situation and shut down of CNG caused non availability of public transport which affected arrival of labour at site and procurement of material.

5.2       The Director Projects said that a further extension of six months upto September 15, 2014 is requested for the following reasons:

  • Changes have been made in the design of Ground Floor to accommodate the Program offices requirements and those of Security/CCTV room, Procurement and re-adjustment of UPS & electrical room.
     

  • Due to these intermittent changes in design interlinked activities such as electrical works, HVAC works, false ceiling and finishing works were also revised. Procedural approvals such as preparation of shop drawing and material submittal and its approval also took some time.
     

  • Testing Department was kept at 3rd floor because of their time sensitive activities.  If we had disturbed the Testing Department, Admission cycle would have been affected.  Because of their presence some reordering work had to be done.  In the beginning the Data Centre was to be located on the 4th floor of this building but later it was discovered that it was not technically possible to do so. Thus the Data Centre had to be relocated in the MP Building and the 4th floor had to be reconfigured.

5.3       The Director Projects said that on the basis of the above facts and as per recommendation of Project Managers M/s. Nespak the extended time required is up till September 15, 2014. He said that the Project Department also recommended the extension of time as stated above. He further said that in its meeting held on April 12, 2014, the Procurement Committee checked and approved the extension. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till September 15, 2014.

5.4       The Director Projects said that the services of the Project Managers will be needed till September 15, 2014 to complete the work and witness the handing/taking over of the building including testing and commissioning of the MEP works. The monthly fee of Project Manager at present is Rs.180,833/-. The cost impact will thus be Rs.1,084,998/- for additional six months which he said will be negotiated with Nespak. He further said that the extension of time shall be without reimbursement of any escalation claim, the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall have to extend all insurances and guarantees, related to the Contract, accordingly.

DECISION & ACTION

5.5       In view of the urgency of the matter and as there are no substantial cost implications, the Committee decided that the Director Projects should issue the extension letter to the Contractor and extension to Project Manager Nespak at negotiated rate and report to the A&FC for their approval.

Item No. 8      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      Procurement of Library Security and Material Management System.

8.1       Mr. Imran Batada stated that as per technology road map of IBA, ICT has initiated a project of Procurement of Library Security and Material Management system. The Library security system is now an industry standard and every international library / University has such a system. They have now been deployed in major local universities as well such as LUMS, NED, IBA Sukkur etc. 

8.2       He said that it is a hybrid solution which is based on the combination of RFID / EM strips. This solution allows a tracking system that combines security with efficient tracking of materials throughout the library. This includes easier and faster charge and discharge, inventory management and materials handling. Core Features of the system are:

  • Security

  • Library Management

  • Book Management

  • Rack Management

  • Circulation Management

  • Searching & Reporting

8.3       Mr. Batada said that this procurement is budgeted in 2013-14 budget and is part of ICT procurement plan and they have submitted this case in EC for conceptual approval.

8.4       Mr. Mohammad Anwar, the Head Librarian said that for every book they would not use RFID as it is very costly, EM strips will be used for security while RFID will be used for materials management. He said that LUMS used RFID which has not been successful installation for theft detection, while IBA Sukkur has moved from RFID to hybrid solution for the same reason. He said that the units we have proposed cover barcode, EM and RFID. He showed some videos on the functioning of the system. He said that there is self check-in and self check-out system but they have not included self-check-in in the proposal at the moment as it will cost around Rs.3 to 4 million, which may consider in next budgets after the successful implementation of the suggested components of the system.

8.5       Mr. Batada said that the proposal has been approved by the Procurement Committee and has been referred to the EC for conceptual approval and it has to be tendered now.  The Dean & Director suggested that it should be ensured in technical evaluation that industry standard is available.

DECISON & ACTION

8.6       The Committee granted conceptual approval for the procurement of Library Security and Material Management System.  The Head of ICT would float tender for the procurement of the system.
