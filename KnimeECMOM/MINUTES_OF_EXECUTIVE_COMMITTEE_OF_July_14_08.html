MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON JULY 14, 2008 (FOURTH MEETING)

The fourth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, July 14, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr. Moeid Sultan                               Acting Head of Finance & Accounts
  6. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                        H.R. Manager

Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Chairman, MIS & CS being on leave could not attend the meeting.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Transfer of Cases of Students

1.1       The Registrar stated that 21 students have applied for transfer from the City Campus study programs of BBA and MBA to the same programs of the Main Campus for different personal reasons. He said that some students have applied for their transfer from the morning programs to the evening programs of study for their own personal reasons. He referred to the decision of the 37th meeting of the Academic Board held on July 22, 2006 allowing transfer of students from Main Campus to City Campus and vice-a-versa provided a student transferred from Main Campus to the City Campus pays the full fee charged from City Campus students and will not be eligible for financial assistance. Similarly, a student transferred from City Campus to the Main Campus, would have to give an undertaking that he / she would continue to pay full fee and will not be eligible for concessional fee or any financial aid.

1.2       The Registrar further stated that as per the existing practice of IBA any student wishing a transfer from a higher cut off point to a lower cut off point program is generally not denied such a transfer and therefore the transfer of a student from the morning regular program to the evening MBA program should be allowed as per existing practice. He proposed that the applications of students admitted to the City Campus for transfer to the Main Campus should be considered in light of the number of seats accommodatable in the Main Campus. 

1.3       The Registrar said that presently there are two students who want to move to City Campus from Main Campus and four students from the Main Campus to the Evening Program and they agree to the terms and conditions.

DECISION:

1.4       The Committee decided to follow the approved policy and transfer will base on the availability of vacancy and fulfilment of conditions.

1.5       The Committee further decided that in case of more than required applications, he student who has obtained highest marks would be given preference.  

1.6       The Committee approved the transfer of two students from Main Campus to the City Campus and four students from the Main Campus to the Evening program subject to fulfilment of the conditions.

Item No. 2      Extraordinary Delays in Semester Final Results

2.1       The Controller of Examinations stated that normally one week is given to the faculty members for submission of examination results. Faculty members usually follow the schedule and submit the results on time. However, he said that some faculty members do not follow the schedule despite repeated requests and take weeks and sometimes months for submission of their results. He said that two teachers have still not submitted three results of Spring Semester 2008. He said that the delay in results has a chain-affect on other crucial activities at the Program Offices level which are badly hampered for want of results. He requested the Executive Committee to think about some procedure to ensure timely submission of results.  He proposed that the Chairpersons who are also informed about delays in submission of results, can play a very effective role in this regard and warning letters to the concerned teacher may also be issued by the Chairpersons/Director.

DECISION:

2.2       The Committee decided that those who do not submit the results after a week of the due date to the concerned Program Office, the respective Program Office would bring it to the notice of the Director and the Director would issue a Show Cause Notice to the concerned faculty member.

Item No. 3      To consider note from Manager Projects regarding selection of Public Address System for City Campus Rooms: Centralized vis-à-vis separate for each classroom.

3.1       The Manager Projects in his note of July 9, 2008 presented cost estimates for Centralized Public Address System and Desk-Mounted System for each Class Room for 6 rooms in Academic Block and 12 rooms in CCS Building. 

DECISION:

3.2       The Committee in principle agreed that Desk Mounted System for each class room would be more suitable than a Centralized Public Address System. The Committee however, wanted complete documents.

Item No. 4      To consider Business Plan for IBA National Talent Hunt Programme.

4.1       The Director said that the IBA National Talent Hunt Program needs to be further strengthened. He said that he has a plan to hunt 40 students from backward areas of all the provinces including FATA. The program will be based on merit cum poverty consideration. The merit criteria will be determined by making only those students who secure top 20 positions in merit list at the Intermediate examination conducted by various Boards of Intermediate Education. Those who express interest in pursuing higher studies in Business Administration and Computer Science at IBA and meet the eligibility criteria, will be invited to participate in an orientation program of two months duration in General Knowledge, Mathematics, I.Q, and Business English.  Those who clear the test and get admission would be offered four years full scholarship. He said that the IBA will share the cost and provide hostel, and living expenses for which the Board has approved allocation of Rs. 6 million.  The Director said that Dr. Zeenat Ismail would the supervisor of this Program. The program will be funded for four years by different donor organizations along with IBA.

DECISION

4.2       The Committee appreciated the proposal of strengthening the existing National Talent Hunt Program of IBA and decided that all the rules applicable to other students of the IBA will also be applicable to the students admitted under this Program.

4.3       The Committee decided to widely publicise the program and a brief should be prepared for advertisement. Brief of the program should also be given in IBA’s Open House sessions.

ACTION

4.4              Dr. Zeenat Ismail will prepare a brief for advertisement and for Open House sessions.

Item No. 5      Fee Receivables

5.1       The note from the Finance Department regarding fee receivables amounting to Rs.10,207,998/-, appearing in the accounts as on June 30, 2008 along with the reasons mentioned therein was discussed.

5.2       It was suggested that we should identify those students who are really needy and cannot pay the fee, their cases should be put up before the A&FC for writing off the amount recoverable from them.

DECISION

5.3              The Committee decided that the Finance Department should obtain the following details and present them to the Executive Committee:

a)      Number of students who were enrolled in BS, BBA and MBA programs in 2007-08.

b)      Number of students who applied for scholarships in each program

c)      Number of students provided full scholarships from various sources

d)     Number of students given partial scholarships and

e)      The remaining balance of the needy students who could not receive any financial assistance.

5.4       The Committee decided to empower the Financial Aid Officer to deal with these issues.  

ACTION

5.5       The Registrar will empower the Financial Aid Officer to deal with such issues. 

Item No. 6      Scheduling of classes for Fall Semester 2008

6.1       The Administrator City Campus said that due to shortage of rooms owing to construction work, he is constrained to schedule lecture duration from 50 minutes to 75 minutes twice a week at the City Campus. This would also need to be followed at the Main Campus as some faculty members who would be teaching in both the campuses on the same days will be able to adjust the timing. 

DECISION

6.2       The Committee approved the rescheduling of classes from 50 minutes to 75 minutes at both the campuses for Fall Semester 2008.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

7.1       Orientation for new faculty.  The Committee decided that there would be an orientation session for new teachers. The department head will arrange such orientation.  The Director will also inform them about the guidelines and expectations from them.          

7.2              Installation of Smoke Detectors:  The Manager Projects wanted to know if there was a need to have smoke detectors in every room of the Boys Hostel irrespective of the cost involved.

7.3              As there was no cost estimate available, the Committee did not suggest anything.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
