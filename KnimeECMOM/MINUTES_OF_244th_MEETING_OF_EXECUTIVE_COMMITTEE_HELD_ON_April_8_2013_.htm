3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                   Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                    Registrar

5.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                     Controller of Examinations

6.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                       Director Projects

The following items were discussed:   

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Contract Agreement of M/s. ICON.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that after the approval of the Executive Committee (192nd meeting of EC held on February 27, 2012) for extension of contract agreement with M/s. Icon on the current projects till March 2014, with specific conditions, the Projects Department included all the provisions such as manpower, delivery of drawings and documents and clause of penalty due to delay.  The Agreement and Extension letter was sent to ICON on March 12, 2012 in order to review and send their comments several reminders were sent but no response received from M/s ICON in this regard. The Director Projects said that he advised M/s ICON to visit his office and resolve the matter. Accordingly, a meeting was scheduled on March 26, 2013 in this regard which was also attended by Mr. Noshad and Mr. Faisal. In this meeting they showed their concerns over penalty clause and after detailed discussion they agreed for the penalty clause to be included in the contract but they also requested to include another clause that if the delays occur due to client or any other factor then in that case the Consultant should be reimbursed for their man-hour which they will be putting in order to manage the continuity of Project.

 

1.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the inclusion of reimbursement clause in their extension letter. 

 

1.3       The Dean & Director did not agree to the counterproposal of M/s. Icon and said that the Consultant knows that during the course of execution many unanticipated changes take place.  Also there are exigencies beyond the control of IBA. However, he said that as the projects given to Icon are about to come to closure there is not much point in entering into a protracted negotiation with them at this stage. We are more interested in getting our projects completed and cannot afford any delays. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that if we insert compensation clause then double benefit will be passed on to the Consultant; reimbursement plus increase of amount of fee due to increase in completion cost.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee did not agree to the inclusion of reimbursement clause and the penalty clause in the extension letter for this case.    

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve Transport Policy of IBA.

 

2.1       The Registrar presented the Transport Policy of IBA. He said that IBA has got a large fleet of vehicles at its disposal. These vehicles are operated by a transport pool located at the Main Campus. The transport pool is a “Service Unit” operating under the aegis of the office of the Registrar. Motor vehicles, purchased by the IBA, are managed as ‘Fleet Vehicles’ under one of the following two arrangements. Each of these arrangements is governed by a distinct policy as indicated below. Although each of the policies has common elements, there are important differences. Thus, before applying a policy, it is necessary to know the category of vehicle:

 

a.       General Pool refers to conditions for management of vehicles purchased by the IBA for pool or special purpose use such as courtesy vehicle, utility vehicle or point / shuttle use vehicle.

 

b.      Executive Pool where the conditions for provision of vehicles to senior staff by virtue of their office or in accordance with their salary package as determined individually by HR Dept.

 

2.2       The Registrar said that IBA has adopted this Transport Policy for the acquisition, use, control, maintenance, repair and disposal of the IBA’s motor vehicle fleet, and for the administration of vehicles in the General Pool and Executive Pool and vehicles / transportation services acquired through outsourcing / lease arrangements. This document identifies the policy and procedures required for ensuring that the resources of the transport pool are efficiently utilized and responsive to the needs of the IBA community. It also documents the duties and responsibilities of the transport staff in respect of the operation and maintenance of the vehicles of the pool.

 

2.3       The Registrar covered the following in the proposed Transport Policy:

 

            Purpose, Definitions, Authority and Responsibility,         Purchase/Disposal/Replacement, requisitioning of Vehicles, Repair & Maintenance of Vehicles, Vehicle Security and Documentation, Payment of Charges, Responsibilities and duties of Transport Staff, Use of Private Vehicles on Institute Business, Insurance, Actions in case of Accidents, Fuel, Action in case of breakdown of Vehicles, Dos and Donts, and FAQs. He also presented annexures on Duty Slip, Transport Requisition Form, Complaint/Repair & Maintenance Form, Student Registration Form, Point Routes, Shuttle Timings, and Organization Chart.

 

2.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the Executive Pool is open ended. In public sector universities there is a list of eligible positions that are entitled to the Executive Pool.  The Registrar said that he has referred to the transport rules governing the transport of other universities but he did not find such a list.

 

2.5       About the proposed Transport Incharge to be reporting to the Manager Transport, the Dean & Director said that since this position is not approved, it cannot be included in the policy because through policy we do not create positions.  It has to be through the H.R. The Director H.R. said that the proposed two positions have not been approved.    

 

2.6       The Dean & Director suggested to separate point service from the Community Service. He also suggested that the ‘Community Vehicle’ be titled as “Community Service Vehicle”.  He further said that all Requisitioning of Vehicle should be signed by the Head of the Department not by a senior staff as proposed.  He asked to delete the proposed rule that “Vehicle will not be provided to a department on a particular duty more than once in a day” as it is not practicable. He suggested to divide the document prepared and submitted to the EC into three parts; Policy, Rules and the Procedures (SOP). The policy would be broad based while the rules will provide the details for application of the policy. The procedures will define the process of acquisition, maintenance, allocation etc. The duties and responsibilities should be attached as annexures and the Dos and Donts, and the FAQs should be included in the Manual. Against the Transport Fee it should be mentioned “As Applicable”, not the exact amount. 

 

2.7       The Director Projects suggested that our internal auto mechanic should inspect every vehicle every month and refix the small problem then and there.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee appreciated the work that the Registrar has done in preparing a comprehensive document. However, as it was presented at a very short notice, the members could not go through it thoroughly, the Registrar was requested to send it again to the EC after incorporating the suggestions noted above.  The members would send their comments / suggestions and if there is any disagreement on any issue, then it would be referred to the Dean & Director who would decide that issue.

 

ACTION

 

2.9       The Registrar would revise the draft of Transport Policy in the light of above discussion and suggestions and circulate it to the EC members for their comments / suggestions. After incorporating the changes, if any, he would send the final draft to the A&FC for approval.

 

Item No. 3      Presentation of Revised Architectural Design of Cricket Stadium by Architects M/s. NBCL.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the estimated cost of the Cricket Stadium was Rs.18 million which was requested to be brought down to Rs.10 million after re-designing. He said that the Consultants M/s. NBCL have accordingly, redesigned the Cricket Stadium.

 

3.2       The Consultants presented the revised design of Cricket Stadium explaining how they brought the cost down from Rs.18 million to Rs.10 million. He said that they have reduced the tier seating at the bottom and have reduced the usage capacity of washroom; now it is for one person at one time and at up-stairs also they have done similar changes. He said that they just shrank the area.  Earlier the roof was concrete now it will be fabric ceiling.

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee in principle approved the revised design. However, the Committee requested the Director Projects to show the presentation of the design to Mr. Asad Ilyas and get his approval.       

    

 

Item No. 4      To consider and recommend to the Board to approve issuing “Expression of Interest” for allocation of one of the amenity plots out of prime land of Makli Society for the proposed IBA Community College at Makli.

     

4.1       The Registrar said that the concept of Community College is very popular outside Pakistan. He said that Sukkur IBA is also running three Community Colleges which provide preparatory and undergraduate courses.  Students from the underprivileged areas are given education at intermediate level.  He said that a group from Thatta district has contacted IBA, Karachi for establishing Community College at Makli. He clarified that there would be no financial obligation on the part of IBA.

 

4.2      The Dean & Director said that this proposal should be examined in light of our Community Outreach objective. He said that we started Sindh Talent Hunt Program in which we brought students from rural areas and trained them for two months. Under the new Foundation Program we have increased the duration of training to 6 months. In spite of this, some of the students admitted to BBA class are finding it difficult to maintain minimum CGPA of 2.2 He said that the next logical step is to have a community college which will serve as a feeder for our undergraduate talent hunt program. He said that he wanted the Community of Thatta to understand that everything at the Community College will be done on merit; whether it is admission of students, appointment of teachers or any other matter. We will not entertain any recommendation. And if they are prepared to adhere to our merit based system then we will go otherwise not. He clarified that we will not be giving degree or transcript to the students of the proposed IBA Community College; we will only ensure quality and standard of education.

 

4.3      Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the management of the proposed IBA Community College should be very strong. The Dean & Director said that it will have an Academic Board, Selection Board and a Board of Governors. He said that if we in principle agree to go for IBA Community College then the Registrar would prepare a concept paper for our Board of Governors approval. They Registrar said that we have been requested to give letter for Expression of Interest for the piece of land by April 28, 2013.  Dr. Nishat suggested that there should be a clause of detachment.  The Dean & Director agreed with Dr. Nishat and said that we should have unilateral power to exit and that should be included in the clause.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Executive Committee provisionally accepted the proposal and requested the Registrar to issue a ‘tentative letter of intent’ of availing the offer of land for establishment of community college of the IBA at Makli making it clear that this is subject to approval of the Concept Paper by the Board of Governors of IBA. 

 

ACTION

4.5       The Registrar would issue the tentative letter of intent, to the Chairman, Makli Cooperative Housing Society Ltd., and prepare a concept paper for the consideration and approval of the Board of Governors of IBA.

 

   

Item No. 5      To consider a request from Major Aqeel Ahmed Awan, a student of EMBA for make-up examination.

 

5.1       Major Aqueel Ahmed Awan, a student of EMBA had submitted an application on March 24, 2013 for a make-up exam as he could not appear in the final exam of QMDM paper held on March 23, 2013 due to medical reasons and his hospitalization at CMH.  The Controller of Examinations had remarked that no make-up of final is allowed on medical ground. An ‘I’ Incomplete grade should be allowed after verification of the hospitalization of the student. Accordingly, the Registrar got it verified from the CMH Malir.

 

DECISION

 

5.2       The Committee decided to allow ‘I’ (Incomplete) to Major Aqeel Ahmed Awan in the QMDM paper.

 

ACTION

 

5.3       The Program Director EMBA would convey the decision to Major Aqeel Ahmed Awan.
