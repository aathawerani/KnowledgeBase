Item No.2 To consider and approve the installation of Glass Vestibule on Entrance of Aman CED Building, Main Campus.

2.1 The Director Projects reported that mould growth on corridor walls, ground and 1st floor was observed in Aman-CED Building. He said that to investigate the reasons a joint visit was held with Architect Mr. Zayd Bilgrami of NBCL and Mr. Farooq Mehboob of M/s.SMC on August 26, 2013. Architect submitted their report on August 27, 2013 by email. According to the report, the Mould Growth is due to the fact that doors and windows are being left open specially frequent use of main entrance glass door allowing hot humid air to enter, mix with the cool air and create condensation. In the darker areas of the building it is creating mould growth.

2.2 The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Architect has suggested that Air Curtain on front door should be operative all the time. Furthermore, they have proposed that double door (Vestibule) or revolving door on main entrance may be provided to prevent hot/humid air in the building to avoid mould growth in future and frequent breakdown of the glass door. He said that revolving door would be very costly – around Rs.6 million. He said that the Architect has submitted drawing and Engineer’s estimate of glass vestibule. The drawing and Engineer’s estimate were presented to the EC. He said that the estimated cost of work is Rs.444,550/- He requested the Executive Committee to approve estimated cost of the work in order to proceed work as per procurement procedure.

2.3 The Dean & Director wanted to know about the aesthetical look of the proposed vestibule. The Director Projects said that the Architect has designed it and have confirmed that aesthetically it would be O.K. The Director Projects further said that the proposed vestibule would stop dust and moisture and at the same time will help in maintaining the air-conditioning cooling which would result in reduction in cooling expenses.

DECISION

2.4 The Committee approved the installation of Glass Vestibule on Entrance of Aman CED Building, Main Campus as proposed by the Director Projects. The Committee also approved the engineers estimate of Rs.444,550/-.

ACTION

2.5 The Director Projects would float tender for the above mentioned work.

Item No. 4 To consider and approve second special secured advance to M/s. Project Managers, the Contractors for renovation of existing Library at the Main Campus.

4.1 The Director Projects said that the progress of work at the existing library at IBA Main Campus had been slow because of the deficiency of the Contractor M/s. Project Managers. He said that liquidated damages had already been imposed on the contractor, even after which his progress did not improve. The contractor did not renew the Performance Guarantee as well. The Director Projects informed the Committee that this matter was discussed with the Dean & Director, Projects Department, M/s. NESPAK and the Finance Department to find out a solution so that the matter would not go into litigation. The Dean & Director advised them to find out a solution so the work is completed as early as possible without going into legal action. The Director Projects reported that a special meeting in this regard was held on 1st January 2014, in NESPAK’s office, which was attended by Mr. Farhat Adil, Vice President NESPAK & his full team, Engr. Rehan A. Riaz, Director Projects IBA & his team and Mr. Said Hussain, Director M/s. Project Managers and his team. After detailed discussion and deliberation, the following course of action was agreed by all present:

a) The Contractor should be facilitated by releasing his bills as early as possible in a manner that the previous special advance given to him is fully adjusted.

b) Insurance companies be informed that the contractor is continuing the work and for that period plus one year period defect liability be provided by the insurance companies (Engr. Abdul Rehman, Sr. Manager Projects spoke with the Management of Adamjee Insurance who accepted to issue the performance guarantee). Contractor was informed and is in touch with the Adamjee Insurance.

c) In order to ease the financial problems of the contractor a second special secured advance of Rs.5.00 (Rupees five million only) be provided to the contractor as his previous advance stands fully adjusted. In this regard M/s. NESPAK have given their recommendations.

4.2 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve issuance of special advance of Rs.5 million subject to the condition that the contractor would revalidate his previous guarantee with the amendment. He said that if approved by the EC, necessary Amendment will be issued by the IBA. He informed that the contractor has undertaken that he would complete the work by the 30th March 2014.

4.3 The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what was the total awarded cost of the project and how much has so far been spent. Mr. Jehanzeb when contacted on phone informed that the awarded cost of the project was Rs.127.6 million out of which payment of Rs.74.8 million including advance has been made. Mr. Abdul Rahman was asked as to bill for how much amount against the work done by the contractor has been submitted. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that a bill of Rs.12 million has been submitted by the Contractor out of which Rs.6 million would be adjusted to advances. He said that carpet of Rs.3 million has been delivered against which they have asked for a reimbursement of 70%. Similarly, he said that lighting also costs Rs.3 to Rs.4 million. The Dean & Director said that he wanted zero risk for IBA in making this advance. The Director Projects said that the second secured advance of Rs.5 million is risk free because Rs.5 million is already available as the Retention Money and a performance bond is also being taken.

DECISION

4.4 The Committee approved the second secured advance of Rs.5 million to M/s. Project Managers, the Contractors for renovation and refurbishment of existing Library at the Main Campus.

Item No. 5 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Approval for construction of boundary wall and ramp road on the southern side of the under-construction Auditorium at City Campus.

5.1 The Director Projects reported that the construction of the boundary wall and ramp road at City Campus has been delayed due to the reason that the owner of adjacent plot did not agree that his wall should be demolished and replaced by new wall of IBA. He said that MJS Foundation is pressing for quite sometimes that the boundary wall was hindering their work and progress. In this regard he said that they asked the contractor M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprise who is working on the Auditorium, to engage the Structural Engineer to design the wall and the ramp road. The design has been completed on the basis of which this wall will be constructed in the property line of IBA and without demolishing boundary wall of neighbouring plot. He said that the boundary wall and ramp road would be part of the external development work at City Campus. He presented the drawing which was already checked by the Projects Department and found correct.

5.2 The Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprise have accepted the responsibility for complete designing and supervision. He presented their Technical & Financial proposals. The total cost of the work is Rs.3,058,245/-. M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprise is ready to do the job at Rs.3.00 million. The Director Projects said that the cost is reasonable and the item-wise BoQ has been checked by Sr. Manager Project of IBA City Campus.

5.3 The Director Projects requested the EC that the work of construction of boundary wall and the ramp may be approved and be awarded to M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprise at a lump sum cost of Rs.3.00 million whcih includes the supervision charges of their Consultant as well. He said that it will be time consuming to go for tendering process and will further impact the construction of auditorium. Secondly it is very economical if the job is done by the same Contractor who is already working there. Thirdly, it will be very difficult to get a third contractor at Aman Tower site because of the non availability of space for material storage and construction work very close to the existing building.

5.4 As there is no provision of Rs.3 million in the budget for this work, the Director Projects suggested that this work may be charged to Aman Tower. However, the Dean & Director suggested to charge it to Auditorium as Al-Shafi is working for the auditorium. He said that it would be a temporary arrangement for accounting purpose and when the budget is approved for this, the amount would be reimbursed by IBA.

5.5 The Director Projects said that the Architect of Aman Tower wants solid wall while that of MSJ wants Jangla around the buildings. The Dean & Director said that all should be Jangla so that the buildings are visible from outside. He further said that the Jangla should be such that people cannot trespass it. However, he asked the Director Projects that before taking a final decision he should consult with the Security Incharge if jangla is advisable from security point of view.

DECISION

5.6 The Committee approved the construction of boundary wall and ramp road on the southern side of the under-construction Auditorium at City Campus through Al-Shafi Enterprises at a lump sum cost of Rs.3 million.

5.7 The Committee decided that subject to security clearance, there would be Jangla of appropriate height around the Aman Tower and MJS Auditorium.

b) Solution to broken/damaged sewerage lines at IBA Staff Town.

5.8 The Director Projects reported that as decided by EC, he consulted with Mr. Abdul Mateen of NESPAK on the issue of sewerage line at IBA Staff Town. He said that the immediate solution is to put 550’ line and the line of a bungalow be annexed to it because the sewerage line of other bungalows is not upto the required level. The line would be connected to HEJ Line. He said that he reviewed the design which was prepared by NESPAK in 2009 but because of its cost it was not executed. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that they had assumed that when the sewerage line of Karachi University would be ready, they would get the IBA Staff Town sewerage line connected to it but Karachi University does not have the resources to complete it. The Dean & Director said that we should not go for the Karachi University sewerage line as they may not meet our future needs. The Director Projects said that the total cost for the sewerage line from Gate to Old Girls’ Hostel as suggested by M/s Al-Shafi Enterprises is Rs.2,194,499/- for which he would find budget from Admin. Building. Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know if we are following the procurement rule. The Director Projects said that if we charge Admin Building, it would be within 15% of the project cost and would be carried out by Al-Shafi Enterprises. The Dean & Director asked as to who is doing the external development work at Main Campus. The Director Projects replied that M/s. Venture Crest are working on the External Development project. The Dean & Director suggested that the proper course of action would be that the sewerage work of IBA Staff Town is a component of the External Development. The winner of the contract for External Development M/s. Venture Crest may be issued variation order for carrying out this work. Venture Crest may choose to sub-contract it to Al-Shafi as they are already mobilized there and Venture Crest does not have the resources. The Director Projects said that he would follow this process.

5.9 The Director Projects suggested to get the sewerage design of Staff Town prepared by NESPAK back in 2009 reviewed by them keeping in mind the future requirements including faculty apartments and included in the Master Plan for future use. He said that if approved by EC, he would prepare and advertise the RFP.

DECSION & ACTION

5.10 The Committee approved the award of External Sewerage system of IBA Staff Town to Venture Crest who may sub-contract the work to M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises at a cost of Rs.2.194 million.

5.11 The Director Projects would prepare and advertise the RFP for the sewerage of Staff Town.
