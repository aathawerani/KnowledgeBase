The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve Ceramic / Porcelain tiles for installation in the pool of Mosaic tiles originally provided in the BoQ.

 

1.1       The Director Projects stated that in the original design Architects had provided Mosaic tiles in the water body in the front lawn. As the Mosaic tiles get permanent stains while submerged in water, Projects department had advised the Architects to suggest ceramic/ porcelain tiles for easy maintenance. He presented a comparative table showing cost of various tiles suggested by the Architects and the contractor for review and decision.  The EC members were also shown the tile samples to the EC members. The Director Projects said that there were two additional works which were proposed by the Registrar as an improvement to the water body, the cost of which is Rs.185,000/-.

 

1.2       The Registrar said that he had suggested ordinary wall between the pond so that its maintenance can be easy on cleaning the pool. He said that he is not in favour of major expenditure and is ready to forego the fountain he had suggested in order to save cost. He said that if the cost of ceramic/porcelain tiles is the same as that of mosaic tiles then it should be O.K. He further said that the tiles proposed for the pool have been placed at the water body at the Boys’ Hostel and there has been some problem because of uneven level. He suggested that its level should be good.

 

1.3       The Dean & Director said that items which are under the Transaction Authority of the initiator should not be brought to the EC.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee decided that as there is no cost difference, the proposed tile stands approved.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the Secured advance of 30% against imported material for Existing Library Project.

 

3.1       The Director Projects stated that the project of existing Library was awarded to M/s Project Manager. During the execution of works on request of Head Librarian the floor tiles were replaced with carpet in order to reduce the noise level. The selected and approved brand of carpet has to be imported. In this regard the contractor requested for the 30% secured advance. The request was put up to the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on 24th August 2013. After detailed scrutiny the CPC has approved the issuance of the advance against the insurance guarantee for this imported material. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the issuance of the advance.  He stated that the precedence of approving 30% advance for imported material against insurance guarantee exists at IBA.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director wanted to as to why we are getting imported carpet tiles. The Director Projects replied that tiles are not local, they are imported. The Registrar suggested to see what is at KSBL as they have a large area covered with carpet tiles. The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects again to visit LUMS and see their material, configuration, space utilization etc. The Dean & Director said that we would give 30% advance against insurance guarantee. However, he suggested to see if the carpet at KSBL is cheaper then we should procure that kind of carpet tile.

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee approved 30% secured advance (against insurance guarantee) to the contractor for carpet tiles for the Library at Main Campus.

 

ACTION

 

3.4       The Director Projects would visit KSBL and find out its cost and quality. He would also visit LUMS to see the material & configuration that has been used over there and how the space has been utilized.

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve extension of time for completion of existing Library.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the Contractor claimed 267 days’ extension in time for completion of the existing library but after detailed study by M/s NESPAK and IBA project department it was recommended that 152 days were justified and hence recommended the extension of time up till July 03, 2013 along with one month grace period.  The case of extension was presented in 242nd Executive Committee meeting held on March 25, 2013. In its meeting the Executive Committee approved extension of time up till July 03, 2013 along with one month grace period.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that in 246th meeting of Executive Committee the case for the special secured advance for the project of existing Library was presented. The Executive Committee further increased the extension of time up till October 2013 along with one month grace period and advised the Director Projects to obtain the approval of A&FC up till November 30, 2013.

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that in light of the minutes of 242nd meeting and approval of special secured advance, an amendment in contract was signed between contractor and IBA after approval of CPC and EC. The EC had asked that the contractor be advised to complete the work by August 03, 2013 and the special advance should be adjusted from running bills on or before August 03, 2013. He said that as the contractor could not complete the work on August 03, 2013 no bill was raised by him during this period.

 

4.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Contractor has requested for further extension in time. He said that Nespak had scrutinized the case in great detail and recommended the extension for a period of 3 months. In the opinion of Projects Department the extension be granted till 30th November 2013, including the grace period.

 

4.5       The Director Projects said that the Contractor has requested to adjust the special secured advance in upcoming running bills. In its meeting held on 24th August 2013 CPC had scrutinized the case and approved the extension and further amendment in contract. They had advised as follows:

 

1.      No more extension should be granted beyond 30th November 2013.

2.      No Escalation shall be allowed during the extended period

3.      All Guarantees should be  extended by the contractor

 

4.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension of the contract so that the amendment may be issued.

 

4.7       The Dean & Director said that since the A&FC which is a superior body has already approved extension till November 30, 2013 and the procedural requirement of obtaining approval from CPC has been taken, there was no need to bring it to the EC. He further said that for any procedural change, rather than making amendment in the contract a side letter should be issued which would be an integral part of the contract. He suggested that an omni clause ought to be inserted in the contract which says that side letters will be part of the contract.  He clarified that the Director Projects is authorized to issue side letters as it will cut down the time taken to get the work done.

 

4.8       The Registrar requested that the facade of the Library should be completed before the convocation. The Director Projects assured the EC that the facades of both the Library and Admin buildings would be ready before the Convocation is held. He will issue instructions to the contractors and the project managers of the two projects.

 

DECISION

 

4.9       The Committee noted that the procedural requirement of obtaining approval from CPC for extension of time for completion of existing Library has been completed, and the A&FC which is a superior body to EC has also given approval for extension till November 30, 2013.

 

ACTION

 

4.10     The Director Projects would issue side letter to the contractor which would cover the recovery of advancement payment schedule.

Item No.6       To consider and approve the migration of Email Exchange Server to Office 365.

 

6.1       Mr. Imran Batada reported that Microsoft is providing Office 365 solutions for different industries along with Education Sector. Number of universities like Georgia State, Queen’s, UTSA, FURMAN etc. have already migrated their email services to Office 365. He said that these universities have started saving thousands/millions of dollars on operating expenses. He said that ICT team at IBA has also successfully finalized the migration process on its test server.  He requested the EC to give formal approval, so that they can start the migration process on production environment.  He said that once moved to the office 365, IBA will definitely be able to save money in terms of storage, servers, maintenance, service level agreements etc.

 

6.2       To a question Mr. Batada clarified that for education purposes Office 365 is provided free of cost. He said that there would be much saving on account of yearly SLA, Windows Service Licence.  Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know if in the long run we would have to pay certain fee. Mr. Batada said that there is remote possibility and if it so happens, they may go back to the existing one.

 

6.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to involve computer science faculty also in the test run and obtain their feedback.

 

DECISION

 

6.4       The Committee in principle approved the migration of Email Exchange Server to Office 365.

 

ACTION

 

6.5       Mr. Imran Batada would involve computer science faculty also in the test run and obtain their feedback.
