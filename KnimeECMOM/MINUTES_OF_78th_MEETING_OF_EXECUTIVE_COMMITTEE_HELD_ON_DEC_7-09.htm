MINUTES OF THE 78th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 7, 2009

The 78th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 7, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

  1.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Associate Dean (Acting Director)
  2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                      Associate Dean
  3.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN       Registrar
  4.  Mr. Mobin Khalili                              Controller of Examination
  5.  Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  6.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT
  7.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                Director Projects
  8.  Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                         Director CED
  9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

Before discussing the agenda items the Executive Committee congratulated Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz on performing Hajj and welcomed him back to IBA. 

The Registrar informed the Committee of the untimely death of Mr. Lutuf Husain Minhas, Assistant Librarian. The Committee offered Fateha for late Mr. Lutuf Husain, lauded his services and offered heart felt condolences to the bereaved family. Dr. Sayeed Ghani requested the Director Finance to settle all the dues of late Mr. Lutuf Husain on priority basis.

The following items were discussed: 

Item No. 1     To consider and approve the proposal from Director ICT for procurement of internet connection under PERN 2.

1.1       The Director ICT stated that IT infrastructure at IBA has been expanding continuously for the last few years. New services are planned to be offered to faculty, students and staff to help them in their study, research and administrative work.  He informed the Committee that at present they have acquired two E3 (35 Mb/s) connections at both the campuses from Wateen and they were also using 2 Mb/s internet connection at Main Campus provided under PERN by HEC. He said that recently the connection was upgraded to PERN2. The concerned department at HEC has allocated 24 Mb/s for IBA.             The Director ICT said that in view of market price, PERN2 was very expensive but the justification of such exorbitant service charges of PERN2 was that there were also a number of services offered by HEC, e.g. digital library, Voice over IP and video conferencing etc, bundled with PERN2 connectivity. He stated that in a meeting of IBA ICT team with HEC regional team on December 04, 2009, the following points were clarified:

a)      Those universities wishing to benefit from such services are bound to subscribe to PERN2, no matter they are using another internet connection from any other ISP.

b)      If the university decides to terminate the PERN2 connection, the offered services might be revoked.

c)      Winding up the PERN2 service but still utilising the PERN2 IP addresses, on payment of nominal charges, also wouldn’t be possible.

1.3       The Director ICT suggested to subscribe to two PERN2 connections, one at each campus, so that faculty and students at IBA can continue using HEC services and be a part of a broader spectrum of universities, sharing lectures and research material. He said that PERN2 connection will also serve as a backup connection, so that in case of any service outage from Wateen, the IBA community doesn’t have to suffer. He said that it is not necessary to subscribe to a full fledged E3 connection. Rather, a small bandwidth (min 8 Mb/s, as restricted by PERN2) at each campus would be sufficient. 1.4       The Director ICT requested the Executive Committee to approve:

a)         Re-appropriation of the funds for the internet service charges for the period from January 2010 to December 2010.

b)         Procurement of two (02) internet connections, of 8 Mb/s capacity each, from HEC under PERN2.

1.5              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that if we have to procure PERN2, then the bandwidth obtained from Wateen may be reduced.  To this the Director ICT said that it could be possible.

1.6              The Director Finance wanted to know if the HEC would go on adding similar items in future. The Director ICT clarified that as IBA is not taking anything else from them, they would not be adding similar items.

1.7              The Director ICT said that the payment will be made in Pak Rupee and that is not provided for in the budget.  He also said that IBA is not paying any fee for the digital library.

1.8              Dr. Muhammad Nishat wanted to know as to what are the reactions of other universities on this issue.

DECISION

1.9              The Committee decided to reduce the bandwidth provided by Wateen so that 16 Mb/s if obtained from PERN2 could be adjusted.

1.10          The Committee also requested the Director ICT to request the HEC to give the proposal in writing about the stoppage of facility in case PERN2 is not procured from them.

1.11          The Committee decided to see the reactions of other universities on this issue.

ACTION

1.12     The Director ICT would report on the implementation of the above decisions to the Executive Committee.

Item No. 2      To consider major concerns regarding ERP implementation raised by Mr. Imran Batada.

2.1       Mr. Imran Batada, Senior Manager IS, in his email of December 5, 2009 raised the following concerns regarding ERP Implementation:

a)         Techlogix procedure wants to do UAT (User’s Acceptance Test) testing without having the complete student grading data whereas during the UAT stage it is necessary to have complete data in it including the student grading data

b)         Techlogix is giving two (2) options for data migration of Student Grading. Option one is to enter the data in excel sheet and another option is to enter the data directly into the PeopleSoft. There is no interface available to transfer data from the existing system to PeopleSoft. In both options the data has to be entered manually, which is not the best practice.

2.2              The Director ICT said that UAT is done when everything is in the system but when the data is not available UAT cannot be done. He said that they say that the data may be entered later.

 

2.3              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that they had initially agreed that the data on CMS would be transferred automatically but now they disagree. He said that if they redo the data entry, it would take a year or so.

2.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to what had been agreed upon.

2.5              The Director ICT said that he was going to HEC on December 8, 2009 and would discuss this issue with the universities using PeopleSoft but apprehended that the HEC may not accept it.  He said that the contract has not been signed directly by IBA; it has been signed by HEC. He said that when they agreed to get the work done by Techlogix through HEC, they were not aware of the contents of the contract signed by HEC. He said that it is mentioned that they would do UAT but it is not defined.    

2.6              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the Director ICT should have informed them about the requirements.   

2.7              The Director ICT said that his assumption was that when Techlogix had done for 7 universities specially for LUMS they would have included it.

2.8              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the Director ICT is raising this issue very late. He suggested to negotiate and convince the HEC to get the data available on the CMS transferred on ERP before UAT is done.  However, he said that if HEC is not convinced, then IBA will have to have a separate contract with Techlogix for this.

2.9              The Director Projects wanted to know if they can have a copy of the HEC contract.  The ICT assured that he would provide a copy of the contract but clarified that the specs are not included in the HEC contract.

DECISION

2.10          The Committee agreed to the suggestion of Mr. Zafar Siddiqui that the Director ICT would try to convince the HEC to get the data available on the CMS transferred on ERP before UAT is done and if the HEC is not convinced, then IBA will have to have a separate contract with Technogix for this.

ACTION

2.11          The Director ICT would try to convince the HEC for transfer of data at CMS on to ERP.

2.12          The Director ICT would send a copy of the HEC contract to the Director Projects.

Item No. 3      To consider recommendation from the Director ICT for payment of the monthly telephone bills for official calls.

3.1              The Director ICT stated that due to the increased work load of interaction with the vendors and IBA staff, Senior Manager IT I/O and Senior Manager IS are requesting for the payments of their telephone bills for the calls they are making for official work. The Director ICT recommended that a limit of Rs.2000/month may be granted to them for the duration of the ERP implementation project.

3.2              The Registrar did not agree to the suggestion of the Director ICT and said that allowing reimbursement of telephone bill to SM IT I/O and SM IS would set a wrong precedent and open a Pandora box.  

3.3              The Director HR proposed that they can use official land line for official calls instead of their personal cell phones.

3.4              The Director ICT said that it takes time to get connected through exchange or the zero dial system.

3.5              The Director Projects suggested that if it is a genuine expenditure then we should reimburse that.

3.6              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that we have to do something but not in the proposed way.

3.7              The Director H.R. said that it is to be done across the board but she said that the Dean & Director has said that at this time we cannot consider people who are second-in-command. 

3.8              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that land line costs more and it may be misused too. But he said that there are some hidden costs which one has to bear.  He said that before getting the official blackberry they were using their own cell phones and making official phone calls at their own expense. He said that since we are hiring people at such a high salary they should bear such costs until a policy is made.

3.9              The Registrar suggested to carry out a random survey of the uses of official calls and on the basis of that a decision could be taken.

DECISION

3.10     The Committee did not approve the proposal of Director ICT for payment of monthly bills and decided that immediate solution for such requests is greater reliance on land lines – direct or through zero dialing facilities.

3.11     The Committee requested the Registrar to gather more data on the uses of official calls for arriving at a decision.

ACTION

3.12     The Registrar would arrange to gather data on the uses of official calls on cell phones.

Item No. 4      Matters arising out of the previous EC meeting.

a)      Rescheduling of classes due to Convocation 

4.1              The Committee decided that there would be no classes for morning and evening on the Convocation day i.e. December 12, 2009.  The Committee further decided that in future it will be mentioned in the academic calendar that there would b no classes on the Convocation day.

b)     Review of fee defaulters.

4.2              The Director Finance reported that there has been a very good response towards fee recovery. He said that out of 54, thirty eight have settled their dues and now there are only 16 fee defaulters. He said that there are only two among the students registered for Convocation who have not yet cleared their dues.

DECISION

4.3              The Committee noted the fee recovery status with satisfaction.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

            a)   Live coverage of the IBA Convocation 2009 on the IBA Website.

5.1       Mr. Imran Abdul Rahman Batada, Senior Manager IS proposed that he would like to make a live coverage of the IBA Convocation 2009 on the IBA website. He said that it would enable anyone with internet access to be able to view the Convocation Ceremony in real time. He further said that it would not only be for the first time in the history of IBA but also in Pakistan.  He reported that he and his team has done all the technical work and even a successful live experimental testing too.

5.2              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to what would be its utility.  In his opinion it would have extra cost and time on the team.

5.3              The Director H.R. said that it may not have so high visibility.

5.4              Mr. Imran Batada clarified that there would be no extra cost.  He required only the internet connection on the site. Regarding visibility he said that the video appearing on the screen will be shown on the internet.

5.5              The Registrar appreciated the idea and said that if Mr. Batada and his team have any urgent work which may suffer because of this, then they should not do it.

DECISION

5.6              The Committee approved the proposal of live coverage of the IBA Convocation 2009 on the IBA website.

5.7              The Committee requested Mr. Imran Batada to send email about the live presentation to the students and faculty.

b)    Relocation of the Cafeteria shed at IBA City Campus.

5.8              The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Executive Committee had decided to cut the tree for re-location of the Cafeteria shed at IBA City Campus. However, he said that the Consultants have given an alternate plan by saving the tree and have shifted the entire shed towards the wall.  He said that the ducts, IT cables and sewage line would be shifted.

DECISION

5.9              Noted and approved by the Committee.

c)      Visit of Dr. Shahid Ansari, Provost and Dean of Faculty , Babson College.

5.10          The Registrar informed the Committee that Dr. Shahid Ansari, Provost and Dean of Faculty, Babson College would be visiting IBA Main Campus on December 11, 2009 at 10:00 a.m. and meet the members of the Executive Committee and the Academic Committee. He requested to members Executive Committee to attend the meeting.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
