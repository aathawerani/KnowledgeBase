MINUTES OF 275TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 2, 2013

 

The 275th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 2, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                             Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Mohammad Nishat                                                      Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                            Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                         Registrar

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                             Director Finance

6.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                            Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Abdul Rahman, Senior Manager Contracts attended the meeting as nominee of the Director Projects who could not attend the meeting due to his hospitalization.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve (a) the fee for Project Managers on Sports Ground at Main Campus & (b) Construction and Refurbishment of existing library at Main campus during the extended period of execution.

 

a)      Sports Ground & Ancillary Works:

 

1.1       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that the scheduled date for completion of Sports Ground and Ancillary Works was 28th May 2013. However, due to various reasons the completion period was extended to 31st August 2013. He said that while extension was approved by the Executive Committee, it was advised that services of M/s. NESPAK, Project Managers, should be terminated for the extended period. As per advice of Executive Committee, the Projects Department terminated the services of M/s. NESPAK beyond 30th June 2013 vide Projects Department letter of 5th July 2013. M/s. NESPAK in reply has contended that under sub-clauses 2.73 and 2.74 of the Consultancy Agreement they are entitled for the payment during extension of time under the terms of Contract, on prorata basis.

 

1.2       Mr. Abdul Rahman said that as the project has to be supervised in the final stages of construction involving specialist works like preparation of Cricket Pitch, Surfacing of Jogging Track, Tennis Court and Volley Ball cum Basket Ball Court,  M/s. NESPAK have provided their detailed supervision as well as back up support of their Head Office experts. In view of the above he said that payment to M/s. NESPAK is justified. The amount of additional payment claimed by M/s. NESPAK for 3 months is Rs.187,671/-.

 

1.3       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that the case was presented to the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on 5th November 2013. The Central Purchase Committee was of the view that the services were concluded by IBA.  M/s. NESPAK may be asked to withdraw this claim. Subsequently the Director Projects had a meeting with the General Manager NESPAK Mr Abdul Mateen, who did not agree to withdraw the claim and insisted that the services of Project Managers were provided as per the contract agreement in the best interest of project specially under the circumstances when the services of the contractor were extended and the work executed by the contractors involved specialised work. They, however, offered that the pro-rata payment for their services may be decided by the Executive Committee.

 

1.4       The Dean & Director said that for such a meagre amount of Rs.187,671/- NESPAK should not have insisted for payment and the case should not have been brought to the EC as it is not good for their image.  He advised Mr. Abdul Rahman to ask NESPAK to withdraw this claim.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.5       The Director Projects would ask NESPAK to withdraw their claim for an additional amount of Rs.187,671/-

        

b)     Construction and Refurbishment of Existing Library at Main Campus:          

 

1.6       Mr. Abdul Rahman stated that the scheduled completion date of Renovation & Refurbishment of existing Library at Main Campus was February 2013. However, because of various reasons the period of completion was extended to November 2013. He said that while extension was approved by the Executive Committee, it advised that services of M/s. NESPAK should be terminated for the extended period. As per advice of the Executive Committee, the Projects Department terminated the services of M/s. NESPAK beyond 3rd August 2013 vide email dated 26th August 2013.  He reported that at the time of approval for extension in the contract of execution of Library, following works were in hand for which M/s. NESPAK provided detailed supervision on full time basis:

 

a)      Installation, testing and commission of air-conditioning ducts and equipment

b)      Glazing works on both faces.

c)      False ceiling

d)     Special  Lamination Works

e)      Installation, testing and commissioning of lighting fixtures

f)       Washed terrazzo

g)      Interior finishing works

 

1.7       Mr. Abdul Rahman further reported that M/s. NESPAK also contended that their services needed to be compensated on the extension as per reasons mentioned in (a) to (g) above. He informed the Committee that M/s. NESPAK have provided their full support on the following matters to IBA for which they had not charged any fee:

 

·         Structural stability review and coordinating with the external experts.

·         Design review of the HVAC and Electrical Works and detailed coordination with the designers.

·         Special expert advices on intricate contractual matters.

 

1.8       Based on above, Mr. Abdul Rahman recommended that payment of fee to M/s. NESPAK for the extended period may also be considered. The amount of fee claimed by M/s. NESPAK is for the following period:

 

a)      For 1st extended period January – August 2013                  =    Rs.1,102,500/-

b)      Period August 3rd – Nov. 30, 2013                                   =    Rs.   490,000/-               Total:                                                                                 Rs.1,592,500/-

 

1.9       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that the case was presented to the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on 15th November 2013. The CPC recommended that M/s. NESPAK may be asked to reduce their fee at least 31% pertaining to the extended period. He reported that in this regard a meeting was held with the General Manager NESPAK, Mr Abdul Mateen who did not agree to withdraw their claim from August 3, 2013 to November 30, 2013, as they have provided their services diligently on the project specially under the circumstances when the contractors period was extended and they had to supervise special work such as installation, testing, commissioning of Air-conditioning and electrical works for which IBA did not have proper detailed supervision staff. NESPAK however, have agreed to accept the pro-rata payment as is decided by the Executive Committee based on its fair judgement. He reported that the Project has further been delayed due to the fault of the contractor for which the notice of application of Liquidated Damages has been served upon them.

 

1.10     Mr. Abdul Rahman said that as 30% Project is incomplete involving mostly critical works such Electrical, Mechanical and HVAC works which may take additional time estimate of which cannot be done at present. He said that all possible actions are being taken by Projects Department and the Project Managers to pursue and facilitate the contractor so that he could complete the balance works within shortest possible time. In this scenario, he said that the  presence of Nespak is highly necessary for additional period of at least 3 months, specially to witness and certify the completion of façade works, false ceiling  works, interior ducting works, testing and commissioning of HVAC plant and equipment, Electrical system  and other systems such as fire alarm, fire fighting, ICT, etc. Further they are required to verify and approve the final bill and to manage the technical and contractual closures, as well as to handle any contractual problems pertaining to action taken against the contractor. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension of the contract of Nespak till 28th February 2014 at the fee decided as per the submission in preceding paras.

 

1.11     The Dean & Director said that major work has already been completed. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that testing and commissioning of equipment (Chiller and ACs) will be completed by the end of December, 2013. He further said that as per contract for one month after completion of the project IBA will not have to make any payment to the Project Manager. 

 

1.12     The Dean & Director said that the extension from January to August 3, 2013 has already been approved as such it should be reported as a background information. Mr. Abdul Rahman clarified that extension in time (from January to August 3, 2013) was approved but the financial impact was not approved. He further said that the proposal should have been presented with the amount after reduction of 31 per cent as recommended by CPC.  Mr. Abdul Rahman said that the payable amount after deduction of 31% from Rs.1,592,500/- would be around Rs.1 million/-

 

1.13     The Dean & Director said that we should accept the recommendation of CPC to reduce the fee by 31% for the extended period from January 2013 to November 30, 2013.  The Committee did not agree to extend the contract of NESPAK till February 28, 2013 as recommended by the Director Projects but only upto December 31, 2013. The NESPAK will continue to supervise the project for one month beyond that.

 

DECISION

 

1.14     The Committee endorsed the recommendation of the Central Purchase Committee that M/s. NESPAK should reduce their fee by 31% for the extended period from January 2013 to December 31, 2013.

 

ACTION

 

1.15     The Director Projects would ask NESPAK to reduce their fee by 31% for the extended period from January to December 31, 2013 and bring it at Rs.1 million.

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the amendment in contract of M/s. Venture Crest for the external development works at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that the Project of External Development works at IBA Main Campus was awarded to M/s Venture Crest. The Contractor requested for the secured advance of the following material, as the material has been delivered at site:

 

1.      Kerb Stone

2.      Pavers

3.      UPVC pipe and Fittings

4.      G.I Pipe & Fittings

5.      Pumps, Valves and Fittings

 

2.2       Mr. Abdul Rahman said that as per the precedence they had paid secured advances on the material which was delivered on site to different contractors. He reported that the case was presented in Central Purchase Committee meeting held on November 30, 2013. The CPC recommended the Executive Committee for approval. He requested the Executive Committee to allow an amendment in contract for the payment of secured advances against the above mentioned items which have been delivered at site.

 

2.3       To a query, Mr. Abdul Rahman said that as per contract secured advance can be given for cement and steel but these have been used in lesser quantity. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that he has been told by Mr. Jahanzeb that PEC allows secured advance against an indemnity bond in respect of non-perishable material brought at Site but not yet incorporated in the permanent work; it is not restricted to cement and steel only. 

 

2.4       The Dean & Director said that for every project the mix of material, equipment, labour varies. He asked Mr. Abdul Rahman as to why the same type of contract document is used in which only steel and cement are included for cost escalation. In other projects, equipment or other materials may form dominant component of cost. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that they make changes in the contact as per requirement of the project.  The Dean & Director said that as a matter of principle if we are not to amend contract, it should be between finance and projects departments to facilitate advance payment and should not have been brought to the EC.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee approved amendment in contract of M/s Venture Crest for the external development works at IBA Main Campus as recommended by CPC to facilitate secured advance to M/s. Venture Crest.

 

Item No.3       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

    

a)                  Replacement of Blackberry with Smartphone.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that in 271st meeting of EC held on November 4, 2013 he had shown the Smartphone to all EC members for their feedback and the EC members were comfortable with it.  He said that when the proposal along with approval of EC was taken to the CPC, it wanted the approval of EC with brand name of the Smart Phone. He reported that Smart Phone Model is SAMSUNG GALAXY CORE, and its price (Unit/without any applicable taxes) is Rs.25,650/-

 

3.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that its price is exceeding the approved limit of Rs.25,000/-  Mr. Imran Batada said that the next model is available at around Rs.18,000/- but it does not have so many features as are in the proposed one. The Dean & Director advised to increase the limit of amount for mobile set as it was approved few years back.

 

3.3       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that he had suggested in the 271st EC meeting that the users may be given option to take the old blackberry at market price and if they do not take it, the same should be disposed off.  He said that market price may be searched and the best-selling option available in the market would be the final disposal price. In this way, there will be no loss to IBA as compared to disposal of assets at Market Price. The Dean & Director agreed.

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee noted and approved the model of Smart phone which is SAMSUNG GALAXY CORE and increased the approved limit to Rs.25,650/- instead of Rs.25,000/-.

 

3.5       The Committee approved the proposal of Mr. Moeid Sultan as suggested in 3.3 above.

 

ACTION

 

3.6          HR department would enhance the entitlement limit for the mobile phone set.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
