Item No. 1 Authorization of re-imbursement of registration cost.

1.1 Dr. Sayeed Ghani informed the Committee that Mr. Zain Ali is an aspirant for our MS-CS program.
 

He is a very bright candidate from his transcripts perspective, and possesses a BS in Mathematics from Lafayette University, US. To enter IBA’s MS-CS degree program he requires to pass the entrance test which has an essential CS component in it, which Mr. Zain cannot clear with his current Math-only skill-set. To overcome this, Mr. Zain has pursued options vigorously since Summer 2013. He was advised to cover up for his entry-level deficiency by Dr. Hisham bin Zubair, by enrolling in the relevant courses from the BS degree program. Mr. Zain took the initiative and cleared the entrance test for BS-CS, but applied to the Academic Committee, that in his special case, (as he is not mainly interested in BS-CS), he be allowed to register in BS-CS but also be allowed to bypass any pre-requisite requirements, and additionally, just enrol in courses that he chooses. The Academic Committee in its 48th meeting recommended (after a careful perusal of his case), that he be allowed to just pay for and enrol in any courses that he wishes from the BS-CS program, without actually enrolling as a formal BS student. Mr. Zain needs to know if he may actually enrol in six courses this semester, since currently, there is a cap of 2 courses per semester for non-degree students. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that they plan to raise this issue and procure permission from the Academic Board for the same.


However, at present, Mr. Zain Ali has been advised by Dr. Hisham bin Zubair to enrol in 2 courses, and to wait for registration in the remaining 4 courses, until EC authorizes reimbursement of the registration cost in the case of a negative decision by the Academic Board.

1.2 Mr. Mobin Khalili said that for a non-degree student there is no such provision. He said that he can do courses but exemptions cannot be given for those courses in MS program. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that Mr. Zain wants to take necessary courses to expedite the process; he does not want to do all pre-requisites. He said that if the Executive Committee allows him to take six courses but the Academic Board does not approve it can the fee be reimbursed to him. He said that the policy does not allow reimbursement of fee if a student has attended even a single session. The Dean & Director said that Mr. Zain should pay the fee when the Academic Board approves the process i.e taking six courses.

DECISION

1.3 The Committee decided that Mr. Zain should be allowed to pay the fee after the approval of the process by the Academic Board.

ACTION

1.4 Dr. Sayeed Ghani would present the case of Mr. Zain Ali to the Academic Board for approval.


Item No.2 To consider and approve re-designing of seating platform at IBA Main Campus – Sports and Ancillary works.

2.1 The Director Projects stated that although the original design of the seating platform at IBA Main Campus in the Sports Ground was approved by IBA yet later on it was found that the design was not workable from the view point of IBA. He said that he had given certain suggestions for the permanent seating inclusion / expansion in that Platform because of which the number of seats was increased three times. He reported that based on his instructions, the complete seating platform was re-designed by M/s. NBCL for a lump sum fee of Rs.150,000/- which was agreed upon by IBA based on man-hours spent on the re-designing job. He said that this being part and parcel of the agreement was not submitted to the Executive Committee for its approval as re-designing fee in the similar circumstances has been paid to the other Architects in the past.

2.2 The Director Projects said that as it was the requirements of Finance Department, the case was submitted to the Procurement Committee for consideration and approval of re-designing fee. The PC in its meeting held on 25th January has approved the payment. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the release of payment which has been pending since October 2012 due to certain objections raised by Finance Department.

2.3 The Dean & Director said that he would like to know the nature of objection raised by the Finance Department on payment of Rs.150,000 to NBCL. As Mr. Moeid Sultan could not attend the meeting due to indisposition, Mr. Jahanzeb was asked to explain the reason.

2.4 The Director Projects reported that the Project Cost was Rs.48 million but it was completed at Rs. 44 million (final bill is under process). Thus the Architects fee will be paid on Rs.44 million. He said that in total they have saved Rs.5.15 million on the project including Architect fee. He presented the following calculation/clarification:

2.5 Mr. Jahanzeb said that as per original contract NBCL is being paid 3% of the project cost. However, he said that the proposed amount is over and above that fee. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that if we suggest changes in the proposed design then we should not pay for the revised design. The Director Projects clarified that the contract was awarded and then the originally approved design was changed during the execution and the consultant prepared revised detailed design. The Dean & Director appreciated that the redesign had resulted in expansion of the number of seats by three times compared to the original design. He said that if the Director Projects on behalf of the client suggests certain changes at the time of submission of the proposal that is included in the 3% fee contrary to this if the design is approved and subsequently the architect is asked to redesign or redo the work during the execution to incorporate certain changes are made for various reasons (cost saving, value engineering, or any other client requirement) , then payment has to be made for the additional work assigned to the architect provided IBA is able to benefit from such redesign. In this case the platform has acquired a much higher seating capacity. The Dean and Director appreciated the efforts of the Projects team in achieving the savings and completion of the project.

DECISION

2.6 The Committee approved the payment of redesigning fee of Rs.150,000 for seating platform at IBA Main Campus – Sports and Ancillary works to M/s. NBCL.


Item No.3 Approval of Passport: a Global Market Research Database.

3.1 Mr. Mohammad Anwar, Head Librarian and Incharge Research Data Centre said that they have proposed to subscribe Industry Analysis Market Research Database. He reported that the proposal has been approved by the Library Committee and was also presented to the Procurement Committee which referred it to the EC for conceptual approval. He said that all top business schools subscribe to this because it is helpful in doing market research on different industries. It includes 27 industries across 80 markets of the world. He said that they had planned to subscribe to this database last year and had also budgeted for this but could not subscribe.

3.2 The Dean & Director asked as to how much are the practical usages of such facilities at IBA. He said that we have to see its utilization and application also. Mr. Anwar said that the feedback received from faculty and students for the proposed database is satisfactory. He reported that they have had 22 in-class sessions for the students with the help of concerned faculty, and students were also given graded assignments to enhance the use of already subscribed databases. He said that they would integrate all electronic databases including Passport GMID into the course contents and then measure its utility at the end of the year, the role of faculty is the key driving force for this. The Dean & Director said that he wants to make the use of such databases for application of theory and concepts. He wanted to know as to how many teachers use the available database and have given assignments to the students. He asked the Head Librarian to make a survey to find out how many teachers are using the existing databases for the purpose of assignment and report writing. He suggested Dr. Sayeed Ghani to include this in his academic audit. He also asked Dr. Sayeed Ghani to send young faculty for training on data analytics.

3.3 To a query the Head Librarian said that the annual subscription fee of the Global Market Research Database is $10,000/- He further informed the Committee that they have also submitted the proposal to HEC for the funding. He suggested to put the usage of database on evaluation on quarterly basis and if it is being heavily used then its subscription may be continued.

DECISION

3.4 The Committee approved the subscription of Global Market Research Database for an initial period of one year which may be renewed further subject to its fullest utilization.

ACTION

3.5 The Head Librarian would pursue the HEC for funding for the Global Market Research Database.

3.6 The Head Librarian would conduct a survey to find out how many teachers are using the existing database for the purpose of assignment and report writing.

3.7 Dr. Sayeed Ghani would include the usage of database in his academic audit.

3.8 Dr. Sayeed Ghani would send young faculty for training on database analytics.
