1.  Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

3.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

4.  Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

5.  Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

6.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

7.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

8.  Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                         Director CED

9.  Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                        Director H.R.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects and Mr. Asad Ilyas, Faculty member attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider EC decisions implementation Reports:

 

Status Report from the Controller of Examinations:

1.2       The Controller of Examinations to convey the decision to Mr. Muhammad Zuhaib Shaikh about the retake of the course of Information Industry Structure and Competitive Strategy: The Controller of Examinations reported that it has been done.

 

 

DECISION

 

1.3       The up-dates given above were noted.

 

1.4       The Committee decided that the Status Reports from the Registrar would be considered in his presence in the next EC meeting.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider requests from the following three students of EMBA Corporate Manager Program for make-up exams on the grounds mentioned in their applications:

 

a)      Mr. M. Tarique Siraj

b)      Mr. Ahsan Ahmed

c)      Ms. Sana Sarwar

a)      Mr. M. Tarique Siraj: 

 

2.1       Mr. M. Tarique Siraj in his application reported that he was unable to sit for his final exam of Statistics because he suffered from gastroenteritis.  He also submitted medical certificate along with his application for allowing him make-up for the paper of Statistics that he could not take.

 

2.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that as per policy make up is not allowed but Mr. M. Tarique Siraj may be allowed “I” (Incomplete) in the course of Statistics he missed.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee agreed with Mr. Mobin Khalili and allowed “Incomplete” to Mr. M. Tarique Siraj.

 

ACTION

 

2.4       The Registrar would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. M. Tarique Siraj, and to Mr. Shahid Zaki, Director EMBA Program.

 

b)     Mr. Ahsan Ahmed:     

 

2.5       The employer of Mr. Ahsan Ahmed requested for make-up exam for Mathematics & Statistics paper after August 31, 2010 as Mr. Ahsan was given an important assignment with deadline by the organization which he had to complete anyhow.

 

2.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that as per rule make up is allowed to a student who is sent outside city by his employer but in this case Mr. Ahsan is not leaving the city.

 

2.7       The Director said that it is the official assignment because of which a student is unable to take the exam. He further said that in case of Mr. Ahsan they have verification from the employer too.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee allowed make-up examination to Mr. Ahsan Ahmed.

 

ACTION

 

2.9       The Registrar would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Ahsan Ahmed, and to Mr. Shahid Zaki, Director EMBA Program.

 

2.10     The Controller of Examinations would arrange make up examination for Mathematics & Statistics for Mr. Ahsan Ahmed.           

 

c)      Ms. Sana Sarwar:

 

2.11     Ms. Sana Sarwar reported in her application that she could not appear in the paper of Business Finance in the final examination as she had to take her mother to the emergency of LNH.  She reported that her mother is Hepatitis ‘C” positive.

 

2.12     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the policy does not allow make-up in such cases.  He suggested that Ms. Sana Sarwar may be allowed “I” (Incomplete) in the course of Business Finance of which she could not take the final exam.

 

DECISION

 

2.13     The Committee agreed to the suggestion of Mr. Khalili and allowed “Incomplete” in the course of Business Finance.

 

ACTION

 

2.14     The Registrar would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Ms. Sana Sarwar, and to Mr. Shahid Zaki, Director EMBA Program. 

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve appointment of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Limited as Architects for planning, designing and top supervision of Playing Grounds.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee in its meeting held in June 2010 had advised Director Projects to contact M/s. Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt) Ltd (NBCL) to undertake preliminary planning for the development of Cricket and Football Grounds, Tennis, Volleyball and Basketball  courts,  in the Main Campus and award them this assignment as an extension of their contract at the Students Centre.  He said that M/s. NBCL were provided with the basic criteria for the grounds after consultation with Mr. Asad Ilyas and Mr. Saad Warraich.

 

3.2       The Director Projects said that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have prepared conceptual plans which they have been invited to present in the EC meeting for approval by the Executive Committee.

 

3.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the appointment of M/s. NBCL as Architects of this project at the fee of 4% of the cost of Project on the same terms and conditions.

 

3.4       The representatives of M/s. NBCL presented the conceptual plans of the sports grounds.

 

3.5       The Director said that they needed cricket, football and volley ball grounds/courts in the first instance.

 

3.6       Mr. Asad Ilyas suggested to merge the volley ball and basket ball courts.

 

3.7       The Committee was informed that the estimated cost of the sports ground would be around Rs.67 million.  The Committee was also shown the break up of the cost.

 

3.8       The Director noted that the estimated cost of pavilion and canteen is around Rs.30 million. He suggested to reduce it as much as possible.

 

3.9       It was suggested to see if jogging track can be made around the sports ground.

 

3.10     The Director Finance said that there was a provision of only Rs.15 million in the budget against the estimated cost of Rs.67 million.

 

3.11     The Director said that budget will have to be reallocated but the final cost would not be Rs 67 million after reduction that has been proposed.

 

3.12     The Director Projects reported that M/s. NBCL have agreed to work at the same rate of 4% which they have been allowed for Students’ Centre. He said that the Sports Ground may be treated as extension of the Students’ Centre and the cost of the Sports Ground will be added to the Students’ Centre.

 

3.13     The Director said that they should follow the procedure. He asked the Director Projects to seek advice from the Head of Internal Audit if they can include this project as an extension of Students’ Centre or a separate tender will have to be issued. He said that in case this is not permissible under the PPRA rules, then tender will have to be issued to all pre-qualified architects.

 

DECISION

 

3.14     The Committee agreed with the Director and decided that the Director Projects should consult the Head of Internal Audit for opinion whether the Sports Ground can be included as an extension in the Students’ Centre.

 

ACTION

 

3.15     The Director Projects would consult the Head of Internal Audit and in case the Head of Internal Audit does not advise to include the Sports Ground as an extension of Students’ Centre, then he would issue a tender for this.   

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider the following proposals / presentations from the Career Development Centre:

 

·         Responsible Citizen

·         Strategic Alliances with Industry Leaders

·         Boot Camp

 

4.1       The Director H.R. said that the Career Development Centre has three proposals which will be presented by Mr. Imran Zawwar and Mr. Kamran Abdullah and they would need feedback from the EC:

 

Responsible Citizen:

 

4.2       Mr. Kamran Abdullah apprized the members about the purpose, objectives, advantages, process and targeted NGOs of the Project.  He said that the focus of the project is to take the students outside of their comfort zone and connect them with different NGOs, so they get a glimpse of a variety of environments which impact the economy and businesses and to develop a sense of social responsibility in students so that they can be effective future leaders.  He said that it would be mandatory for all the BBA/BS students who completed four semesters at IBA and it would be launched in June 2011.

 

4.3       During the presentation the following observations were made by the EC Members:

 

·         The Director suggested that it should not be restricted to all kinds of NGOs only credible organizations should be included.  He suggested to consult the website of Pakistan Centre for Philanthropy. He instructed not to link IBA with the non-credible organizations. He advised to see who is working in education, special education, water supply, sanitation, urbanization, children and women programs so that they may have diversified universe.

 

·         Dr. Muhammad Nishat and Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to start this project after the first year so that students are not overburdened towards the end of the program.

 

·         It will be a mandatory non credit internship in social sector in addition to regular internship program in place.

 

·         The role of faculty would be voluntary.  Interested faculty may join and contribute to it.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The CDC would incorporate the above observations in their plan.

 

4.5       The students would be consulted on the timing for the start of the project i.e. after fourth semester or after the second semester and on the basis of the convenience of the students towards the work load, the project would be started either after second semester or after fourth semester.

 

ACTION

 

4.6       Mr. Imran Zawwar and Mr. Kamran Abdullah would implement the above decisions.

 

Strategic Alliances with Industry Leaders:

 

4.7       Mr. Imran Zawwar said that the idea is to establish formal understanding with various organizations / industries for providing (a) free training sessions, and (b) extra-curricular modules to IBA students. It would be branded as “Student Development Initiative – IBA”.  He said that the top management of P&G, Nielsen, ICAP have agreed to provide free training to IBA students in Supply Chain, MBR, Skill Development, Interview Techniques etc.  He said that AC Nielsen have developed curriculum for LUMS and they may also design curriculum for IBA.  He further said that the extra-curricular modules would include sailing/rowing, learning music, photography, out campus tours.

4.8       The Director observed that Supply Chain, and MBR are already being taught at IBA. There is no need to arrange separate training sessions for these courses.  They may come as guest speakers to such classes. 

 

4.9       The Director further said that there is a society for every activity at IBA.  He advised to link the societies with these organization.  He suggested to integrate the modules into the activities of various societies and clubs.  He opined that the students should not be overburdened as they have to take courses, mandatory language classes, internship, proposed social organization internship and the module. He said that the CDC should act as intermediary between the organizations and the societies and bring the societies and the organizations together.

 

DECISION

 

4.10     The Committee decided that the proposed training  will  be provided in the Guest Speaker Sessions and the proposed extra curricular modules be made part of the activities of various societies.  The CDC to act as intermediary in between the two – the organizations and the societies.   

 

IBA Entrepreneurship Boot Camp:

 

4.11     Mr. Imran Zawwar said that the main idea behind the IBA Entrepreneurship Boot Camp is to provide a platform to produce “Ideas for Tomorrow”.  He said that the idea would be implemented in the following three phases:

 

            Phase I                        Business ideas invited from institutes all over Pakistan

            Phase II           Preliminary Round Presentations

            Phase III         Final Round

 

4.12     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that his department is already doing this type of activity and recently they had arranged all Pakistan Business Competition.

 

DECISION

 

4.13     The Committee advised Mr. Imran Zawwar to coordinate with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui for the proposed IBA Entrepreneurship Boot Camp.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
