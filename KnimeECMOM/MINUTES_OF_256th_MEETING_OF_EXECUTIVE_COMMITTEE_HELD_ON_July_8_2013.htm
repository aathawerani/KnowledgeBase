Item No. 1      Presentation of Customer Satisfaction Survey by A.C. Nielson.

 

1.1              The Director H.R. reported that the first Customer Satisfaction Survey was done in 2010, second in 2012 and the third one in 2013. She said that Ms. Jameela Kidwai of A.C. Nielson has been invited to present the survey report of 2013 to the EC.

 

1.2              Ms. Jameela Kidwai presented the summary of the survey report, EQ Index, Net Promoter Score (NPS) and the graphical presentation of the trend in the satisfaction level of service units.

 

1.3              The members felt that the number of respondents (base) to the survey on some service units was very low which could not be the true representation. Ms. Kidwai agreed that there were some service units where the base is very low.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       This item was deferred. The Committee requested Ms. Jameela Kidwai to re-submit the report after conducting the survey on the increased base of respondents.

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the relocation of the existing Shed behind the Adamjee Block at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that a Shed and the Canteen are coming in the way of construction of road behind the ‘Adamjee Academic Block (Existing Academic Block) at Main Campus.  He said that the shed has to be removed at the earliest.  He proposed that the best place for re-location would be the space between the Auditorium and the Academic Block.  He said that this will have double advantage of having access from the Academic Block for the students to continue their outdoor activities as well as for use of the Auditorium. The structure is column-less and as such cars can pass through in case of emergency.

 

2.2       The Director H.R. reported that the Registrar has sent an email on this issue in which he has opposed the proposal of shifting the sunshade to the proposed location i.e. between the Adamjee Building and the Auditorium, on the following grounds:

        

a.       It will fairly mask the view of the beautiful buildings built in the rear of the auditorium - this masking by the sunshade structure will be more so once the sunshade is occupied by the students which are bound to be using it for bulk of the time.

 

b.      The students relax in that area and litter it with a lot of garbage - which will become an eye sore for all specially visitors using the auditorium during important events in the auditorium.

 

c.       Being a relaxing area for students - the sunshade area is very noisy due to being the location of Foosball etc - which is not conducive for the events being held in the auditorium. Also it is the venue of a lot of the promotional activity arranged by students - the noise of these events is well known to all.

 

d.      The entire facade of the built up area is affected - especially on occasions such as convocation etc - when we want to present an openness look to the 1800 people who assemble on such occasions.

 

e.       Also quite importantly the proposed location being in the immediate proximity of the noisy technical spaces i.e. AC plant room, generator space will become inappropriate for students to relax.

 

f.    Finally - but most importantly it is in the immediate proximity of the main switch room / electric room of the Adamjee Block which makes sitting of a common user facility in the immediate proximity - a very dangerous and risky from safety point of view. 

     

2.3       In the light of the above the Registrar suggested that the sunshade structure should be left at its current location.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director said that since we are providing indoor sports facilities at the Students’ Centre, there is no need to keep the space occupied with Foosball table and shed. He said that the removed shed can be used for any other work or for vehicle parking.  Mr. Khalili suggested that the removed shed should be used for vehicle parking.   

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee decided that instead of placing the removed shed in between the space of Adamjee Building and the Auditorium, as proposed by the Director Projects, it should be used for shed for vehicles at the parking space.

 

ACTION

 

2.6       The Director Projects would get the removed shed placed at the parking space for vehicles at the Main Campus.

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the extension of time for completion of additional works and operation of Students’ Centre project.

    

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the completion date of the Students’ Centre was February 15, 2013 and on contractor’s request an extension was given upto June 30, 2013. The project has almost been completed. However, he said that on 3rd July 2013 few amendments / additions were proposed by the IBA Administration in kitchen areas. These will be undertaken and it would take at least 45 working days after the drawings are given to them by the Architects. The works are necessary to be incorporated for maintaining the area as hygienic. The preparation of punch list, its clearance and handing - taking over process has commenced and will be completed by August 30, 2013 including the additional works as mentioned above.

 

3.2       The Director Projects further said that the power will not be available for operations of the building. The temporary connection from rental generator of Auditorium may be available after arrival of required cable by end of September 2013. As per the electrical infrastructure contract permanent KESC connection will only be possible in December 2013 after completion of power centre building.  He said that if desired, the Students’ Centre can be made functional by using rental generator, when Auditorium remains closed.

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that the services of the Project Managers will be needed till 30th August to get the additional works completed and witness the handing/taking over of the building including testing commissioning of the MEP works. The monthly fee at present is Rs.165,000/. However, he said that he has negotiated with them to work at Rs.100,000/- per month including tax. The cost impact will thus be Rs.200,000/-. He reported that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on July 6, 2013 has recommended (a) the approval of extension for completion of amendments / additional works by August 30, 2013, (b) operation of Students’ Centre with temporary power supply by September 30, 2013 and (c) with permanent power supply by end of December 2013. The Committee  also recommended the approval of extension of services of the Project Managers till August 30, 2013 with negotiated cost of Rs.100,000/- per month with a total cost impact of Rs.200,000/-. Based on the recommendation of Project Department and Central Purchase Committee the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the above recommendations.

     

3.4       To a question as to what are the additional work, the Director Projects reported that they were advised to provide changing room, and a bath room which were not there. However, he said that since these were included in the BoQ, they would not cost much.

       

3.5       The Dean & Director said that there is no need to get a generator on rent as the generator of 250 kVA which is for the data centre can be used for the Students Centre till such time the data centre is ready. He further said that there is also no need of retaining Project Manager. He asked Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi, Project Manager to do the job of project management also of the Students’ Centre.

 

DECISION

      

3.6       The Committee approved the extension for completion of additional works and operation of Students’ Centre project till August 30, 2013.

 

3.7       The Committee decided that instead of acquiring a generator on rent, the generator for Data Centre would be used for the Students’ Centre till such time the data centre or the power supply building is completed and power is received from KESC.

 

3.8       The Committee further decided that no extension would be given to the Project Management firm.  The project management work of the Students’ Centre would be taken care of by Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi.
