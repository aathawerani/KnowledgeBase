Item No. 1      To consider and approve award of contract for Construction of three KESC sub-stations at Main Campus, Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects stated that his proposal for location of two new KESC sub-stations and request for approval of their cost estimates were approved by the Executive Committee in its meetings held on August 23, 2010 and February 2, 2011.  He reported that with a view to inviting bids for construction of the two sub-stations, they requested 18 pre-qualified petty contractors to confirm if they would be interested in participating in the competitive bidding. Seven of them expressed their interest and purchased the tender documents. However, only the following five of them participated in the bidding:

 

1.      M/s. Link International

2.      M/s. Electro Systems

3.      M/s. Blue Sky International

4.      M/s. Venture Crest Private Ltd.

5.      M/s. Haque & Sons

 

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the five bids received were opened on March 26, 2011. He presented a copy of the Comparative Statement of the bids.  He said that the bids were evaluated in-house by their Quantity Surveyor. He also presented a copy of the Bid Evaluation Report.

 

1.3       Mr. Shameem informed the Committee that the lowest bidder, namely, M/s. Haque & Sons, who had quoted a price of Rs......./- were disqualified because they had not submitted the required Earnest Money Pay Order along with the bid. He said that the second lowest bidder namely, M/s. Link International who quoted a price of Rs........./- have subsequently given a voluntary discount of 6.7%, thereby reducing the price to Rs........../-.  

 

1.4       Mr. Shameem said that on KESC’s demand they had earlier planned to build two Sub-Stations only; one at the Staff Colony and the other at the Boys’ Hostels’ area. Recently, however, KESC has asked to provide a new Sub-Station at the Main Campus also for the third HT connection of 2,500 kW. He said that the proposal for the third Sub-Station was approved by the Executive Committee in its meeting held on April 11, 2011. Thus he said that they are required to award a contract for construction of three Sub-Stations, and not two. He said that all three are of the same size, design and specifications given by KESC.

 

1.5       Mr. Shameem said that the Technical Evaluation Committee was requested to approve award of the contract to Link International for all three sub-stations at the same price, namely Rs....... ÷ 2 = Rs....../- for each sub-station. In other words, TEC was requested to approve award of a contract to Link International at a total price of Rs....... x 3 = Rs......../- for all three sub-stations. The approval was given by TEC accordingly in its meeting held on April 13, 2011. He further said that the Central Purchase Committee considered and endorsed the TEC’s approval in its meeting held on April 19, 2011. However, he said that the minutes of the meeting have not yet been issued. He requested the Executive Committee to give its final approval for award of the contract to Link International for construction of the three sub-stations at a total price of Rs......../-.

 

1.6       The Director wanted to know as to why the power distribution cannot be done through one sub-station. Mr. Shameem replied that it would be very costly.  

 

1.7       The Director wanted to know if there is any provision in the procurement rule for increasing the number without mentioning in the advertisement. The Director Projects and the Director ICT said that there was no such provision in the procurement rule.

 

1.8       The Director advised Mr. Shameem Ahmed to award the work for construction of two sub-stations to the lowest responsive bidder i.e. M/s. Link International at the agreed price of Rs........./- in accordance with the tender notice given in the newspapers. He advised Mr. Shameem Ahmed to invite bids from pre-qualified Petty Contractors for the construction of the third sub-station for which M/s. Link International is at liberty to participate.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee agreed with the Director that the work for construction of two sub-stations be awarded to the lowest responsive bidder i.e. M/s. Link International at the agreed price of Rs......./- and Mr. Shameem to invite bids from pre-qualified Petty Contractors for the construction of the third sub-station.  M/s. Link International may also participate in the bid. 

 

ACTION

 

1.10     Mr. Shameem Ahmed would award the work for construction of two sub-stations to the lowest responsive bidder i.e. M/s. Link International at the agreed price of Rs......../- and would also invite bids from pre-qualified Petty Contractors for the construction of the third sub-station.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve Registration System of students.

 

3.1                   The Director ICT said that as per the decision made by the Director IBA in the monthly meeting to have the registration of students via bidding instead of first come first basis, they have analyzed the same and found the following outcomes:

 

Hardware Perspective:

 

·         Currently IBA is paying around US$5,000 monthly for the cloud hosting and paying around US$7,860 additional during the time of registration. The bidding process registration requires more hardware (specifically Web Server and Application Server). The overall costing during the registration process will be doubled.

 

Software Perspective:

 

·         PeopleSoft is not providing any feature related to bidding so they need to do the customization along with Techlogix Support. Techlogix is recommending to continue with existing flow since it is delivered by the PeopleSoft. Student Enrollment is the main component of ERP and it is not recommended to have customization on this module. If they do so then they might face problems during the implementation process and may not be able to have new patches in the System.

 

3.2       Based on the above perspective, the Director ICT recommended to continue the existing flow. He said that it was also observed that the major problem used to occur due to run time changes in the time table. If IBA functional departments overcome this problem then the first come first basis solution will be widely acceptable to all the students of IBA.

 

3.3       The Director said that since the IBA will get new server, there was no need to spend money on additional hardware. However, he said that Mr. Imran Batada had told him that minor customization in the module would be needed and that would not disturb the structure of the system. He said that he would discuss this issue with Mr. Imran Batada and requested the Director ICT to join him in the meeting with Mr. Batada.

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee agreed that the Director would discuss this issue with Mr. Imran Batada in the presence of the Director ICT and resole it.

 

 

Item No. 4                  To consider and approve External Development work at City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects stated that M/s. ICON are the  appointed Architects for the Administration Building, Academic Block, FCS Building and the Canteen & Lounge at the City Campus. He said that they had earlier submitted a landscape plan for the area falling in between the Administration Building and Academic Block which is now outdated as new boundaries of the Aman Tower and Auditorium have been finally defined.  He said that M/s. Icon have prepared a new conceptual plan which they have been requested to present in the EC meeting. Accordingly, the representatives of M/s. Icon presented the landscape development plan for the City Campus.

 

4.2       The Directed observed as to how landscaping can be done when the major construction work was going on. He said that when the multi-storeyed building and the auditorium are ready only then the whole path will be clear and then the landscaping can be done. He said that although the concept is good yet at this stage he would not spend money on landscaping.

 

4.3       The Registrar agreed with the Director and added that at this stage by isolating the area, they can do the landscaping at their own but when the building is complete then proper landscaping can be done.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee decided to postpone the landscaping at the City Campus till the construction of Aman Tower and the Auditorium is complete.

 

ACTION

 

4.5       The Director Projects would forward the landscape development plan prepared by M/s. Icon to M/s. E.A. Consultant and would also arrange a meeting of the representatives of Icon with Mr. Hasan Lotia. 

 

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Clarification on arranging official farewell parties for the faculty and staff who leave IBA.

 

5.1     The Registrar thanked the Director for allowing IBA funds to be used for arranging farewell parties for the faculty and staff who leave IBA. However, he sought clarification as to who should be given official farewell party and what should be the minimum length of service at IBA after which if anyone leaves, he / she should be given an official farewell party.

 

DECISION

 

5.2      The Committee decided that official farewell parties will only be arranged in respect of IBA employees proceeding on retirement from IBA after completing their full tenure of service. For others the concerned department staff can arrange farewell party on their own.

 

b)      Request for ensuring transparency in all processes.

 

5.3       The Director suggested that while opening tenders/bids, besides the officials from the department which has issued the tender, it should be ensured that members from other departments are also present so that the process becomes absolutely transparent.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
