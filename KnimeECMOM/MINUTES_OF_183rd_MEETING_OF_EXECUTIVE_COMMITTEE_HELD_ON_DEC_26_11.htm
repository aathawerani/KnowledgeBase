MINUTES OF 183RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

MONDAY, DECEMBER 26, 2011

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                 Associate Dean, FCS

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

  

Item No.1       To consider and approve the award of work for Renovation and Refurbishment of existing Administration Building at IBA Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects said that the recommendations of TEC, and CPC on the above subject were presented in the Executive Committee in its meeting held on December 12, 2011. In this EC meeting the Director Project himself had suggested for value engineering as the price quoted by the lowest bidder was Rs.194,685,586 whereas the provision in the budget is only Rs.147 million.  The Executive Committee had agreed with the Director Projects and advised him to re-submit the case after value engineering. Accordingly, a meeting was conducted with Architect and Project Manager in order to review the engineering estimate and then discuss on the technicalities associated with the Contractor’s bid with the lowest bidder.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that in the first session of the meeting, NESPAK and Project department arrived at the following conclusion:

 

1.      The Engineering Estimate approved by Executive Committee in October 2010 was Rs.155 million including a provisional amount of Rs.5 million for Elevator.

 

2.      The Architects submitted their Engineering Estimate in October 2011 of Rs.179 million excluding elevator cost, therefore the net increase in engineering estimate is Rs.29 million.

1.5       The Director Projects said that after this exercise the lowest bidder’s amount becomes 6.3% above the detailed estimate (after excluding the CCTV system) i.e. Rs.180.978 million vs Rs.170.2 million. He said that the contractors were asked to accept the deletion of the above stated items, to which they agreed. He said that the Architects had further assured that they will conduct a detailed value engineering wherein they expect to reduce the cost by another Rs.5.00 million which will be done during the execution process.

 

1.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to recommend to the A&FC the award of work to the lowest Evaluated Responsive bidder M/s Al-Shafi Enterprises at a cost of Rs.194,685,586 with the condition that the amount will be reduced to Rs.180.98 million after award of work.

 

1.7       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why the items mentioned above such as CCTV, electrical infrastructure, relocation etc. were not included earlier despite revision of designs several times.

 

1.8       The Director Projects replied that the technical designs were revised several times and the items included were not related to technical designs.  He said that the building will be given to the Contractor on six stages – half of the each floor at one time and it will be completed in 24 months. 

 

1.9       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what will be the per square foot construction cost.  The Director Projects replied that the per sft cost would be around Rs.4000/-

 

1.10     The Dean & Director was very much concerned about the very high per sft cost and wanted to know as to why this is so high while earlier the per sft cost was around Rs.3000/- and the new buildings such as Academic Building under construction was costing about Rs.4000/- per sq.ft. This was a renovation project using the existing structure.     

 

1.11     The Director Projects said that they would get the work done at night for which they have to pay the premium and the cost of copper and aluminium has also increased almost 100 times. Moreover, around 30% new areas have been added and two years built-in escalation is also included in the cost.

 

1.12     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the work which is being deleted or withheld will ultimately have to be done at a later stage and that would add to the cost of the project.   

 

1.13     The Dean & Director wanted to know if the Director Projects would be able to get the cost of the project reduced further by Rs.20 million without any decline in quality of construction. The Director Projects replied that it would not be possible because when NESPAK had asked the contractors to reduce the cost, they clearly refused to do so.  However, he said that the only method through which the cost may be reduced is that they may challenge the contractors on the rates given on the BoQ through which it may be possible that Rs.5 million is further reduced.

 

1.14     Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested to make a comparison of the component-wise cost of the projects completed and this one in order to find where the cost has gone high and for what.

 

DECISION

 

1.15     The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of work for Renovation and Refurbishment of existing Administration Building at IBA Main Campus to the lowest responsive bidder at Rs.194,685,586/-.  The Committee further decided that the Director Projects would reduce 15% of the cost of the project through value engineering so that the project is completed at Rs.165 million.

 

ACTION

 

1.16     The Director Projects would present the recommendation of the EC to the A&FC by bringing the cost of the project at Rs.165 million through value engineering.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve engagement of Interior Design Consultant on the following projects:

 

a)         New Academic Building at Main Campus

b)         Students Centre at Main Campus

 

2.1       The Director Projects said that in the case of Aman-CED Building, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Ltd (NBCL), the Interior Designers were appointed for the reasons that the finishing items of original Civil, Architectural, HVAC and Electrical Systems could be harmonized with the overall interior design of the walls, floors ceiling and the furniture so that the overall theme of the colour, texture and material could be maintained. He said that the design has been completed. It ensured continuity of civil and architectural works in accordance with the theme created by the designer for the flooring, walls and ceilings and ceiling lights. The Director Projects said that the same methodology was adopted in the two Academic Buildings (City and Main Campuses) where the Architects ( ICON ) were paid a little   amount to design the furniture which was as per the interior  theme of the architectural and aesthetic of class rooms and offices. The Architect was not involved in any stage of procurement, and quality control.  It was done by the Furniture Committee.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that on Administration Building, efforts were made by the Administration and Purchase department to procure new furniture using the old design without any regard to the overall interior color, texture and material of the rooms. This has resulted in a mismatch situation. He said that the New Academic Block and the Students Centre which are under construction need to be handled very carefully so that the furniture may blend with the architectural aesthetics of the class rooms and offices which has been designed by M/s. NBCL. The basic reason is that walls of Academic Block have been kept simple and expensive wooden cladding has been avoided. This will include the review of the old design of furniture and to improve upon it.  In order to do that M/s. NBCL were asked to quote their Interior Design fee for the above two buildings as they had already developed basic theme and final designs for Aman-CED Building. Three buildings are in a row and it will be advisable to follow similar kind of concepts.

 

2.3       The Director Projects said that M/s. NBCL had originally asked for a fee @Rs.40 /- per sft. That comes to a total of Rs.1.89 million and Rs.1.25 million for New Academic Block (47,139 sft ) and Students Centre (31,186 sft ), respectively. However, on request they reduced the fee to Rs.1.24 million (lump sum) for the New Academic Block and Rs.0.758 million for Students Centre, which is 31% lower than the fee at Aman CED Building. This Interior Design will also include the complete design of the kitchen in the Students Centre. The services will include the preparation of drawings, specifications, tender documents, pre-qualification of contractors, evaluation of bids and then supervision during the furniture making. The Director Projects reported that they have already floated the advertisement for pre-qualification of furniture contractors and Kitchen fabricators in the newspapers.

 

2.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the above arrangements and approve the engagement of M/s. NBCL as Interior Designer on the two above named buildings at fixed fee of Rs.1.24 million for New Academic Block and Rs.0.758 million for Students Centre. This will be a Direct Selection Method as per SPPRA Clause-72.4(a) “Methods for Selection of Consultants….. Direct Selection Method….. for tasks which are natural continuation of previous assignment and where continuity of technical services is required.”

 

2.5       The Dean & Director said that for Academic Block interior design is not needed.  He said that he would not spend money on the design of furniture. However, he would go for the interior design of kitchen only and Dining facilities in the Student Centre.

 

2.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that for quality assurance of the furniture there should be someone responsible for this because the quality of material used in the furniture is very poor.  He also pointed out that the space provided in between the chairs in the class rooms is insufficient. He suggested that the design of furniture should be approved and the specifications should be documented.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee approved the engagement of M/s. NBCL as Interior Designer on the two buildings at fixed fee of Rs.1.24 million for New Academic Block and Rs.0.758 million for Students Centre.  The Committee further decided that in order to maintain the quality of the furniture specifications would be documented and ensured that the furniture conform to the specifications. 

 

ACTION

 

2.8       The Director Projects would ensure compliance of the decisions noted above.

 

Item No.3       A presentation on “Non Functioning of the Chiller at Administration Building”.

 

3.1       Mr. Irfan Azad, Electrical Consultant, giving reasons for non-functioning of the Chiller at Admin Building, City Campus reported that there was manufacturing fault because of which the condenser leaked. He said that the manufacturers Carrier have agreed to replace it. They are shipping the equipment through airlift from France which is expected to reach here within two to three weeks time and its installation and airlift will be free of cost.      

 

3.2       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Irfan Azad as to how certain he was that after the rectification of the fault due to leaked condenser, there would be no further problem.

 

3.3       Mr. Azad said that he is hopeful that there would be no such problem again because the same type of unit has been installed at other places and they have been in operation for the last 15 to 20 years but no any problem has been reported.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what are the contingency plans if the existing one does not work and what are the domestically available options.

 

3.5       Mr. Azad said that there will no major problem because of which there would be a major break down and if there is any problem they would immediately be resolved because the spare parts are easily available.

 

3.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that we should get some guarantee from Carrier that if there is any such problem again there would be some standby arrangement.

 

3.7       Mr. Irfan Azad said that the Chiller will be operational by 3rd week of January 2012 and as suggested, for operational supervision for one year, he would talk to Carrier the manufacturer.

 

3.8       The Dean & Director said that the Warranty Period will start from the date the Chiller is operational i.e. from 3rd week of January 2012.

 

3.9       Mr. Irfan Azad said that he would get the Warranty Period started from the 3rd week of January 2012 and try to arrange standby arrangement.

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Committee noted the up-date given by Mr. Irfan Azad and advised the Director Projects to ensure that the Chiller is operational by 3rd week of January 2012.

 

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve the following policies from ICT Department:

 

a)         ICT Consumables Inventory Management Policy

b)         ICT Operational Security Policy

 

a)         ICT Consumables Inventory Management Policy

           

4.1       The Director ICT said that for consumable items such as toners etc. there was no policy so the draft ICT Consumable Inventory Management Policy has been prepared. It includes purpose and scope, Toners Procurement Policy, Toners stocking and supply Policy, Disposal of used toners, Creation of records, sharing of information, maintenance of information, General Control Policy, Procurement Policy (according to PPRA), Maintaining Stock and Supply Policy, Disposal of Used Equipment Policy, and roles and responsibilities.

           

4.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to who will be responsible for disposal of used items. 

 

4.3       The Director ICT said that the users can get replenishment only if they return the used items. Such used items would be disposed of through the Disposal Committee.

 

4.4       It was pointed out that used toners and other used items are piled up in various offices because the Store due to shortage of space cannot take it back.

 

4.5       The Dean & Director asked the Director ICT to centralise the disposal process by assigning a person to collect those used items and dispose them off.

 

4.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to who would ensure that this Policy is implemented.

 

4.7       The Director ICT said that Mr. Asjad Asad will be responsible for the implementation of this Policy.

 

 

b)         ICT Operational Security Policy.

 

4.8       The Director ICT briefed about the ICT Operational Security Policy which was divided into two sections; (1) Password Attributes and ICT Services, and (2) System and Data Backup Strategy.   Section 1 covered password guidelines, internet usage, VPN for home, outside publishing, Wi-fi, IP telephony, and application/software usage.  Section 2 included Tape Cartridges required, Tape Rotation Strategy, On-site Storage of tape cartridges, Off-site Storage of tape cartridges, Hardware required, and Storage Safe Requirement.    

 

4.9       The Dean & Director said that limit on phone calls as proposed in the policy cannot be made arbitrarily and centrally because it depends on situations; there may be peak and lean periods. These limits can be enforced by the Head of the Departments as they have better knowledge about the business needs for which phone is being used.

  

DECISION

 

4.10     The Committee recommended the two policies i.e. ICT Consumables Inventory Management Policy, and ICT Operational Security Policy to the A&FC for approval.

 

ACTION

 

4.11     The Director ICT would present the two policies to the A&FC for approval.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  Shifting of Computer Lab from Main Campus Library (under renovation).

 

7.1       The Director Projects said that well before the commencement of the renovation and up- gradation of the Library the shifting of computer lab was discussed at length with the Associate Dean, FCS, the Director ICT and at Executive Committee level. He said that he had conveyed his concerns of the hazardous situations under which the students would be subjected at the laboratory while going in and out and while working inside. But the final decision was to maintain the lab till some alternate was available.

 

7.2       The Director Projects said that although the contractor has taken all preventive measures and is trying to maintain that, yet the contractor has now approached him that the continuous flow of the human traffic through  the construction site is seriously hazardous and  accident may happen ( God Forbid ) at any time.  He said that the Project Managers, M/s Nespak have also critically evaluated the ground situation and recommended immediate re-location of the lab to some other place. He requested that the concerned departments be advised to take immediate actions to relocate the computer lab as early as possible.

 

7.3       The Dean & Director said that the EC has already decided that the Computer Lab would not be shifted from the Library. He said that in the design also it is mentioned that the lab will exist at its premises even after renovation of the Library. The Lab has a separate entry for the users the way to which has been properly paved and a lot of money has been spent on the refurbishment of the Lab.  He said that the work of the floor if required should be done when the library is ready and it can be done in lean period.

 

DECISION

 

7.4       The Committee agreed with the Dean & Director as noted in 7.3 above.

 

 

b)         To consider and approve proposed ICT Organogram.

 

7.5       The Director ICT presented separately organograms of IS, and IT Operations, and a combined organogram of the two sections. He highlighted the approved vacant positions, their proposed levels and a proposed vacant position of Store Keeper.

 

7.6       The Dean & Director clarified that he was not going to fill in any vacant position at this moment and asked the Director ICT to tell how he was going to deploy the existing staff.

 

7.7       The Director ICT said that Mr. Mansoor Ali will be the Manager of the Data Centres, and the Executive Secretary Mr. S. Ali Mohsin Naqvi, would be ICT Coordinator.  He said that he does not have any staff for IT Store.

 

7.8       The Dean & Director said that the existing Store Keeper should be assigned the responsibility to maintain IT Store also but separately. 

 

7.9       The Director Projects suggested that a Senior Manager should be hired in place of Mr. M. Faisal Zia.

 

7.10     The Dean & Director said that he has reviewed the Department’s structure and agrees with the Director ICT that there is no need of a senior manager.  He said that our hub is the Data Centre for which he wants an individual who will be accountable for operating both the data centres.

 

 

DECISION

 

7.11     The Committee gave conceptual approval of the division of ICT Department into two sections and the proposed ICT Organogram. The Committee decided that the levels of the positions will be approved according to Promotion Policy guidelines. The Committee further decided that no fresh hiring would be made for the vacant positions and the IT Store would be assigned to the existing Store Keeper.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
