Item No. 7      To consider and approve annual increment for faculty on study leave and new IBA employees.

 

7.1       The Director H.R. reported that as per current IBA policy, all employees who have not served the institution for at least six months in the assessment year are not entitled for annual increment; this policy also applies on faculty members who are away from IBA for more than six months of the assessment year due to study leave. The Director H.R. stated that after reviewing various relevant cases, the HR department proposes the following for consideration and approval by the EC:

 

A)    For Teaching Employees on Study Leave:

All teaching employees on study leave may be given the approved fixed increment rate against inflation for the year in which the faculty member is on study leave. This condition will apply in case the faculty member is in active service for less than six months and also in case of non-active service during the assessment year.

 

B)    For Teaching & Non-Teaching Employees with less than 6 months service:

All new teaching and non-teaching employees, who successfully complete their probationary period but serve IBA for less than six months of the assessment year, may be given a prorated increment which will be based on the approved increment rate against inflation for that particular year.

 

7.2       The Director H.R. said that the above proposal is expected to have a minimal financial impact; however, they are expected to have significant impact on the motivation of such employees.

 

DECISION

 

7.3       The Committee agreed to the proposal and recommended it to the Board for approval.

 

ACTION

 

7.4       The Director H.R. would present the recommendation to the Board for approval.

Item No. 10    Porting of Mobilink BlackBerry numbers to Warid.

 

10.1     The Director ICT said that IBA has provided BlackBerry email and cell phone services to the Senior Employees through the Mobilink Service Provider. However, it was noticed that the Mobilink service is not very good in certain areas of Karachi especially the DHA area. For better quality services and cost effective solution ICT had recommended to port these numbers to Warid as they have better service coverage and more favourable rates. The EC had decided that the numbers of Director ICT and the Registrar should be ported as a test case. Accordingly, IBA Registrar’s and Director ICT’s cell numbers were ported from Mobilink to Warid for one month review which was extended by additional 15 days. He said that he as well as the Registrar is satisfied with the performance and quality of cell phone and BlackBerry Services. He requested that to port rest of the eleven numbers from Mobilink to Warid to avail the discounted rates and better service.

 

10.2     The Registrar said that he had problem with Warid. Every month he is told by Warid people that the 80% limit exceeded and outgoing calls would be closed.

 

10.3     The Director said that he has no problem with Mobilink and is satisfied with its services.

 

DECISION

 

10.4     The Committee decided that since Dr. Arshad Siddiqi has problem with Mobilink due to non-coverage of his area, he may use Warid; others are satisfied with Mobilink and they should continue to use Mobilink services.
