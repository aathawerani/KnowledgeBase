ON MONDAY, JULY 22, 2013

The following item was discussed:   

 

Item No. 1       To consider an email from Mr. Amanullah Saiyed regarding termination of admissions of two students because of low grades.

 

1.1       Dr. Mohammad Nishat said that although the issue on which Mr. Amanullah Saiyed has sent an email pertains to the Academic Board, yet due to urgency as the exams have started and the affected students are to sit in the exam, he has brought this case to the EC for guidance. 

 

1.2       Dr. Nishat said that as reported by Mr. Amanullah Saiyed in his email that two students came to him (Mr. Amanullah) complaining that their admission was being terminated because of low grades. The plea of the concerned students was that they had dropped the courses in Fall 2012 and to take them in Spring 2013. However, those courses were not offered in Spring 2013. They therefore could not take those courses to improve their grades. Dr. Nishat said that Mr. Amanullah was of the view that as we offer most of our core courses every other semester, the students therefore have a justified reason not to take the courses in the subsequent semester after dropping them and they should not be punished for something that is beyond their control. Mr. Amanullah has suggested that before issuing termination letters to the students it should have been ensured that the courses were offered in Spring 2013 and the students did not take them. He suggested that our policy to expel such students should be extended to a whole year and not to the subsequent semester.

 

1.3       Dr. Nishat said that there are two issues; interpretation of two subsequent semesters, and not offering the courses of the first semester in subsequent semesters.

 

1.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is no issue on the interpretation of subsequent semesters. However, he said that the decision at the Academic Board was taken without analysing what its implications would be. He said that as a result of the Academic Board's decision total 18 students, who should have been dropped out or placed on probation in Fall Semester 2012, were saved. Out of those 18 students, the current situation is that with the exception of four who are dropping, cases of remaining 14 students are clear in the sense that they are clearly dropped out or are on probation. He said that the drop out letters have been issued to the students in the light of the decision of the Academic Board. However, he said that he is ready to allow those two students who approached the Associate Dean to sit in the exam as suggested by the Associate Dean Dr. Muhammad Nishat with the conditions that their papers would be ceased and would be graded only if the decision of the Academic Board is in favour of the affected students. However he emphasized that if those students are given another chance to complete their Ist semester required courses in Fall 2013, the result of 1st semester would be finalized and communicated to those students at the end of their third semester and if any student gets dropped out at that stage, it would put the Institute in a very awkward position. Mr. Khalili suggested that action should also be taken against those who did not offer the courses in the subsequent semesters. Mr. Moeid Sultan asked if it would be cost effective to offer courses for two or four students. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that if the course was not offered then it was not the fault of the students. He said that the Program Director should have offered those courses to accommodate such cases irrespective of the number of students registered for that particular course as the affected students had the assurance that the courses would be offered in subsequent semesters. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that for future even for one student, the course(s) should be offered in the subsequent semesters. He said that the Academic Board should have given direction to the Program Directors to write to the students of first semester who had not completed the required six courses to take those courses in the subsequent semesters and the Program Directors should have ensured that the required courses of first semester are offered in subsequent two semesters. 

         

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee noted that conditional permission was given to the affected students to sit in the exam.

 

ACTION

 

1.6       Dr. Mohammad Nishat would present the case to the Academic Board for clarification on (a) counting of subsequent semesters from, and (b) if the drop out letters issued are in compliance to the decision of the Academic Board or not.   

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
