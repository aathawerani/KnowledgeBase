MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON SEPTEMBER 15, 2008 (THIRTEENTH MEETING)

The thirteenth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, September 15, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Professor
  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Chairman, CS & MIS
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                             Registrar
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance
  6. Mr. Moeid Sultan                              General Manager, Finance & Accounts
  7. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                       H.R. Manager

The following attended by special invitation:

1.  Mr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                           Director Projects

2.  Mr. Shameem Ahmad                                General Manager Projects

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider note from Director Projects regarding Construction of Centre for Entrepreneurship

1.1       The Director Projects stated that the Director IBA has emphasized the importance and need to commence construction of Centre for Entrepreneurship at the Main Campus on top priority. He said that there were two options to speed up further work on this building:

a)      M/s. Icon be given a go ahead to undertake detailed design and preparation of tender documents for civil, mechanical, HVAC and IT related works immediately.

b)   Prequalification of Master Planners be done as early as possible and they should be assigned the job under the existing agreement.

1.2              In case of option (a) where the Architect is already in place and the conceptual design is already approved he estimated that the work can commence latest by 15th December 2008.

1.3              In case of option (b) where the new Architects and Engineers are selected and appointed, the process of commissioning of architects / engineers, conceptual designing and its approval, detailed designing of the Centre, procurement process and approval will result in delayed commencement of work by three months.

1.4              The Director Projects further said that they have already invited applications from the Architects and Engineers for HEC projects and are preparing evaluation report. He requested the Committee to accord approval for commencement of work on the above facts.

1.5              The Registrar said that there have been some lacuna in the selection process of M/s. Icon about which the HEC has frequently objected in writing. He said that we are paying higher rate of 5.85% of the cost to M/s. Icon and still have to pay money for supervision also. He proposed that the IBA should have another Consultant who meets the HEC requirements for HEC funded projectrs.

Decision

1.6              The Committee decided that the IBA should pay to M/s. Icon for the work they have already done.

1.7              The Committee decided that HEC funded projects would be discussed in the next meeting of the Executive Committee when the Director Projects and the General Manager Projects would be able to complete details about the earliest  commencement of work if a new Architect is appointed.

ACTION

1.8              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz and Mr. Shameem Ahmed will obtain details about the earliest commencement of work and brief the Executive Committee in its next meeting.

Item No. 2      To consider application from Dr. Huma Baqai regarding joining after leaves in Summer 2008

2.1       Dr. Huma Baqai, Associate Professor, stated that she had applied for 30 days leave and joined the department after availing eleven days for which she had forwarded an application addressed to Director stating through proper channel. After that she was called in by the IBA to take interviews for BBA. She took it as indicator of the acceptance of her joining report as she was specifically told by her Chairperson that the faculty on leave is not called in to perform such duties.

2.2              She said that, later, on the demise of her father when she took three days leave she was informed that she had no leave to her credit because her joining w.e.f. June 16, 2008 was not accepted.  She stated that the reason given to her was that the Chairperson had recalled her application from the Leave Department and had its acceptance terminated in spite of the fact that it was initialled by the Director.

2.3              The Chairperson of the Social Sciences Department had written to the Register on the joining note of Dr. Huma Baqai as under:

a)      She was allowed leaves from 5th June to 4th July 2008. Once the Summer allocations are done the faculty joins back to avoid Summer Classes.

b)      If Dr. Huma Baqai wanted to join why was the letter sent to the Directors office directly without comments from the Chairperson.

c)      The letter was not sent to her (Chairperson) till she inquired on July 8, 2008.

2.4       The Registrar said that Dr. Huma Baqai had submitted her leave application through proper channel but she did not submit her joining through proper channel although she had mentioned Through Proper Channel.  The joining report was submitted to the Executive Secretary to the Director and the Director only initialled it.     

DECISION

2.5       The Committee noted that Dr. Huma Baqai did not follow the proper procedure of forwarding her joining through proper channel which is the responsibility of the applicant.

2.6       The Committee allowed Dr. Huma Baqai one time waiver and decided because of the misunderstanding, to grant her leave for three days at her fathers death and three days for the Chehlum of her father in Singapore.

2.7      The Committee further decided that all applications should be routed through proper channel and with valid reasons / justifications for the request made.

ACTION

2.8       The Registrar will communicate the decision of the Executive Committee to Dr. Huma Baqai as well as Chairperson of Social Sciences Department besides sending a circular to all faculty about availing earned leave during summer.

Item No. 3      To consider proposal from Manager I.T. regarding BlackBerry Enterprise Server (BES) for IBA.

3.1       The Manager I.T. Mr. Azam Sardar stated that M/s. Mobilink Communications Limited have offered BlackBerry Enterprise Server for IBA community. He said that it is a platform that provides a secure wireless access to a full suite of business applications including EMAIL, Phone, SMS, Web, Organizer features. He said that those who are mobile may use this service by using BlackBerry He said that MOBILINK has been offering BlackBerry Server in Pakistan since 2005 and most recently some other mobile operators such as WARID and UFONE have also started to offer BlackBerry Service in Pakistan but their level of service as compared to MOBILINK is very limited. He said that MOBILINK have agreed to bear the cost of 20 user BlackBerry Enterprise Server for IBA and 100 user licences. They are offering various BlackBerry Handsets on discounted rates for IBA students, faculty, alumni and staff.  He said that the only liability on the part of the IBA would be to pay licence fee for over 100 users.

3.2       He sought approval of the Executive Committee to proceed with an agreement with M/s. Mobilink for providing BlackBerry services and products to IBA staff, faculty and students at the discounted rates.

3.3       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz suggested that as MOBILINK would be doing business through the IBA, they should provide some profit to IBA also.

DECISION:

3.4       The Committee requested Mr. Azam Sardar to obtain proposal and draft agreement from MOBILINK for consideration by the Executive Committee.

3.5       The Committee also agreed with Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz that MOBILINK should pay something to IBA for doing business through IBA.

ACTION

3.6       Mr. Azam Sardar will arrange a copy of the proposal and draft agreement and send them to the Executive Committee.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

            a)  Make Up Examination

4.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that a student of BCS-V met with an accident and her driver received serious injuries so he had to be taken to the hospital by the student.  As a result she missed her exam on that day.  He said that as per policy no make up is allowed to morning program students. However, on some grounds such as medical, a make up is allowed.  She had requested for a make up examination.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee did not allow the student for a make up examination and decided that she be given option of withdrawing from the course.

ACTION

4.3              Mr. Mobin Khalili will give option to the student either to continue studying the course for only 80% marks or withdraw from the course.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
