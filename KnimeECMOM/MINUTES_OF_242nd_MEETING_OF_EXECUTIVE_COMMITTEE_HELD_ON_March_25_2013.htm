3.2       The Director Projects said that Rs.32 million is already available in the budget for ID work and Furniture budget head while the expenditures on these account would be Rs.25.710 million (Rs.12.110 m + Rs.13.6 m).

 

3.3       In conclusion he said that the total cost of the project is around Rs.270 million against which the total budget available including Rs.32 million IBA budget for Interior Decorations and Works and Furniture is Rs.260 million. Thus there is a budget overrun of around Rs.8 million.

 

3.4       In reply to a query by the Registrar IBA, the Director Projects said that the Sound System for class room and seminar hall was not included in the BoQ and was executed through variation order, already accounted for  in the above account. Also the UPS was to be procured by ICT but it was procured by the Projects Department. The Registrar suggested to check the status of UPS. The Director Projects said that UPS which were procured for the girls’ and boys’ hostels were given to the Projects Department but later they were returned as soon as the UPSs of Aman CED were received.  The Dean & Director requested Mr. Imran Batada and Mr. Moeid Sultan to check the status of UPS.  The Registrar observed that Rs.1.6 million for the Sound System is on the higher side.  The Director Projects said that the BOSE sound system has been installed which is the top of the line product. The TOA system which was cheaper and installed in the existing Academic Block did not give good results.  He added that the systems had been checked thoroughly by the Architects, consultants and the Project Managers before it was handed over to the Maintenance Division. Till now there have been no complaints received from the users.

 

3.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the total cost of the project is Rs.278 million.  The Director Projects said that Rs.8 million is charged to Architect and Project Manager fees but the Director Finance wants to charge it to the total cost of the project.  The Dean & Director suggested that the practice has been to charge the Architect and Project Manager fees under a separate budget head which exists for this purpose.

 

3.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that we are going for direct contracting for works amounting to Rs.26.162 million. The contract has been reviewed and scrutinized by IBA Aman-Committee but needs EC approval. Since the Executive Committee is satisfied with the procedure followed, it stands approved.

 

3.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed pointed out that some HEC fund may be available in the budget which may be utilized to reduce the budget overrun of Rs.8 million.  It was reported that Rs.3.367 million from HEC fund is available.  Thus utilizing this amount, the budget overrun would be (Rs.8.213 – Rs.3.367) = Rs.4.846 million.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee approved Rs.26.162 million for the work that has been done through direct contracting by IBA Aman-Committee.

    

3.9       The Committee further noted that the budget overrun after utilizing the available HEC fund would be Rs.4.846 million.

 

ACTION

 

3.10     Mr. Imran Batada and Mr. Moeid Sultan would check the status of UPS and report to the EC. 

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the exemption of Project Manager’s signature on the final bills of Ziauddin Construction Ltd.  for the completed works of Administration Building, City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that after the advice of Executive Committee (Item No. 3 of 238th EC meeting held on February 18, 2013) that the Director Projects should write a letter to the contractor to rectify those works with which they are not satisfied, two meetings were held by Projects Department team headed by the Director Projects with the ZCL team headed by their Managing Director. M/s ICON and their Consultants were also present. The agenda was to resolve the final bill payment issue on which ZCL had contended that they will not accept the objections raised by the Project Managers on their final bill and also they challenged their legal entity as part of the contract.  He said that in the  meeting held on 19th March 2013, Mr. Noshad Rizvi of M/s ICON  and Mr. Irfan Azad, the HVAC Consultants explained that all the observations and objections were raised by them and only conveyed to M/s INTEG which were subsequently incorporated in the objections sent to M/s ZCL by IBA. On the basis of the above clarifications given by Mr. Noshad and Mr. Irfan Azad  it was mutually agreed that the final bill will be certified by ICON and their Consultants after fulfilment of all the observations as decided by the Architects and the  consultants.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that as all the bills of completed projects and ongoing projects are signed by the Project Managers, he sought special approval of Executive Committee for verification of the final bill by the Architects and the Consultants. As decided, the Project Architect and consultant will check and provide their observations and M/s ZCL will comply all the requisites as per the instructions.

 

4.3       The Dean & Director said that the final outcome that all the issues raised by the Architects and the Consultants have been resolved to our satisfaction should be communicated in writing to the EC.  Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that M/s. Integ be asked to sign and check the bill if the objections are finally fulfilled by the contractors. The Dean & Director agreed and advised the Director Projects that Integ be invited to jointly sign and check the bill with the Architects and the Consultants.  EC may be apprised of their observations / reservations, if any. 

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee decided to first ascertain the reservations / observations of M/s Integ and the Architects.

 

ACTION

 

4.5       The Director Projects would invite M/s. Integ and the Architects to apprise the EC about their observations / reservations.

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider and approve the Extension of Time of existing library.

 

6.1       The Director Projects said that the request for extension of time for completion of renovation of existing library was presented in the last EC meeting. The Dean & Director had advised that the case should be presented with complete details including financial implications and the commitment of the contractor to complete the project by July 3, 2013. Accordingly, he said that he has invited Mr. Kaleem, Resident Engineer of NESPAK, and the Contractor’s representative Mr. Shahid to brief the EC about the reasons for the delay, the possibility of completing the project by July 3, 2013 and the financial implications, if any.

 

6.2       Mr. Shahid said that Change in Design due to conversion of hollow columns to composite column, additional work or stability of existing structure, operational Computer lab are some of the major reasons for the delay.

 

6.3       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shahid if the project can be completed by July 3, 2013 and what are the major works left to be done.  Mr. Shahid said that half of the HVAC equipment will be delivered by end of April and half of it will be delivered in end of May.  After the delivery of HVAC it will take 45 days to install it. He said that except HVAC which is being imported from Italy, all other equipments have been procured and will be reaching within 20 days. He said that the preparatory work is being done and work is progressing satisfactorily.  He said that as they had to pay the cost of HVAC in advance, he requested for an advance of Rs.10 million so that the work may progress smoothly.  The Director Projects said that he is waiting for Mr. Mateen’s (NESPAK) advice on the issuance of the advance. The Dean & Director said that the extension date and advance are interdependent. 

 

6.4       Mr. Mahmood said that March is almost over, but the structure is not yet ready.  In his opinion three months’ time is unrealistic.  Mr. Shahid said that he had initially requested for extension for nine months but he was told that extension upto July 3, 2013 was being considered. However, Mr. Shahid said that he would try his best to complete the project by July 3, 2013, but he would need a grace period of one month to cover up any extraordinary circumstances.

 

6.5       The Dean & Director said that one month’s grace period can be allowed at Contractor’s cost i.e. we will not pay for any escalation and we will terminate the Project Manager on July 3, 2013. He requested NESPAK representative to prepare a Gantt chart, give it to the Contractor, monitor the progress regularly and inform the Contractor of any slippages on time so that he can rectify.
