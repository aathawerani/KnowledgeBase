Item No.2       To consider and give conceptual approval for the GEM 2012 Research Project.

 

2.1       Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Associate Director CED, said that the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) is an international research effort to promote entrepreneurship, implemented in more than 80 nations. The GEM studies are conducted under auspices of the Global Entrepreneurship Research Association, which is a not-for-profit academic forum. GEM results are being used by entrepreneurs, and policy makers to create awareness and enhance learning about the entrepreneurial process with the aim of improving support for entrepreneurs and innovative business ventures.  After securing Pakistan’s membership in this prestigious world forum of entrepreneurially conscious nations, GEM Pakistan project was launched in June 2010 under the sponsorship of IBA-Karachi.  An in-depth analysis of the state of entrepreneurship and start-up business activity in Pakistan was carried out.  This included a comprehensive adult population survey (APS) throughout Pakistan to understand the entrepreneurial attitudes, activities and aspirations of the Pakistanis using a large representative sample.  

 

2.2       Dr. Shahid said that as part of this nation-wide effort, detailed interviews were conducted with the top national experts to discuss key conditions that govern a country’s entrepreneurial development, such as government policies and programs, entrepreneurship education and training, financial support for entrepreneurs, R&D and technology transfer facilities, access to professional services, enabling physical infrastructure, market openness, cultural and social norms that impact entrepreneurial activity. IBA conducted the nationwide research activity for the year 2010 and 2011. The expenditure for the 2010 research was about Rs.3.5 million (75% was sponsored by IBA and 25% sponsored by USAID). The expenses for the 2011 research project were solely covered by IBA. 

 

2.3       Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that the CED-IBA would like to carry out the GEM 2012 research project in collaboration with the partner universities with which IBA has signed an MOU. The partner universities will contribute towards the research expenses and gain the following benefits:

 

·         The partner universities will become the official partners in this national level research on entrepreneurship and become a part of the international community carrying out research on Entrepreneurship.

·         The partner universities will have access to the data and can work on joint publications.

·         This collaboration will help in the capacity building of faculty members in the area of Entrepreneurship Research.

 

2.4       Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that the total cost of the GEM 2012 Pakistan project is expected to be around Rs.3.7 million. The break-up is as under:

 

·         Coordination Fee to be paid to the GEM consortium         USD ($11,850)  

·         Survey cost and Report preparation                                     Rs.2.6 million

 

2.5       Dr. Shahid informed the Committee that each of the 7 partners in the consortium (MOU signing universities) will contribute Rs.0.4 million each. IBA being the lead coordinator of the project will bear the main cost of Rs.0.8 to Rs.1.0 million.

 

2.6       Dr. Shahid requested the EC to accord conceptual approval to proceed with the research project and initiate the tender process for the Adult Population Survey (APS) and related activities.

 

2.7       The Registrar wanted to know if the earlier two years’ reports have been compiled.  Dr. Qureshi replied that one year’s report has been compiled and is also available on the IBA website. He informed the Committee that some students are using the data for their Ph.D research too. 

 

2.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that after this survey which will be third in sequence they would stop further survey for a few years because three years’ data will be available to see the trend.

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee granted conceptual approval for the GEM 2012 Research Project.

Item No.4       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that he had a meeting with the Consultant H.K. Shah, and Mr. Maroof, CEO of AMFCO who participated through phone from U.K. to find out how they can further bring down their cost close to the IBA budget.
