MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON AUGUST 18, 2008 (NINTH MEETING)

The ninth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, August 18, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                    Professor
  2. Mr. Inayat N. Din                                          Registrar
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                          Chairman, CS & MIS
  4. Mr. Moeid Sultan                                           Acting Head of Finance & Accounts
  5. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                                    H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1       Aftab Associates Gold Medal

1.1       The Registrar said that M/s. Aftab Associates have requested that the IBA should give Aftab Associates Gold Medal to the best student in Marketing Research.  He also informed the Committee that at present the IBA policy is to award Dr. I.A. Mukhtar Gold Medal only to the best student of BBA and to the best student of MBA. He suggested that if subject-wise additional gold medals have to be instituted, the matter will have to be discussed and finalized at the Academic Board level which is the competent body.

DECISION

1.2       The Committee referred this issue to the Academic Board for making a policy on awarding / not awarding gold medal(s) on various subjects taught at IBA.

ACTION

1.3       The Manager H.R. will ensure that the issue of gold medal is included in the agenda of the next meeting of the Academic Board.

Item No. 2       To consider request of Dr. S. Awais Ahmad Tipu, ex-faculty member for increment and leave encashment.

2.1       Dr. S. Awais Ahmed Tipu, ex-faculty member of the IBA resigned and left the IBA on August 7, 2008 after serving proper notice and obtaining clearance. He had 53 days earned leave to his credit. He requested for encashment of his earned leave.

2.2              The IBA Leave Rules do not allow faculty members’ encashment of leave and therefore, Dr. Awais Tipu’s case will be handled as per the existing policy. 

2.3       The Annual Increments to faculty members effective December 1, 2007 were approved by the Board in May 2008. Dr. Awais Tipu served notice for his resignation in May 2008.  Therefore, as per past practice of not giving any increment to those who resign or have left the IBA, Dr. Tipu’s increment was also withheld.

DECISION:

2.4       The Committee decided to allow approved increments to Dr. Awais Tipu on the basis of his satisfactory performance for the period he served and clearance before leaving the IBA.

ACTION

2.5      The Acting Head of Finance & Accounts will release the increment after he receives clearance from concerned departments.

Item No. 3     To consider request from a student from Quetta for withdrawal from the program and refund of fee.

3.1       The Registrar informed that a student from Quetta who had recently been admitted into BBA program of the IBA wanted to withdraw from the program due to some family problems and had also requested for refund of the fee. He said that he belonged to a poor family and his father was merely a labourer in Iran.  He had arranged the fee through a loan from his relatives.

DECISION:

3.2       The Committee, although sympathized with the student, regretted that it cannot change the policy of refund of fee if the student withdraws on his own.

Item No. 4       To decide the hourly rate for hiring the services of IBA students.

4.1       In view of the earlier decision of the Executive Committee to hire the services of IBA students for jobs which can be done efficiently and effectively by students, the Committee agreed to fix the following hourly rates for hiring the services of IBA students:

DECISION

4.2       Class                                                                                                                      Rate per hour

            Students of BBA-3rd to 6th semesters                                                                     Rs.150/- per hour

            Students of BBA-7th and 8th semestersand MBA 3rd and 4th semesters                   Rs.200/- per hour

4.3              The Committee also decided that a student will be allowed to work for upto 20 hours per week so that this has little affect on the academic pursuits.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
