MINUTES OF 210TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, JULY 2, 2012

 

The 210th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 2, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Societies’ Meeting Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                           Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Shakeel Khoja                                                 Acting Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                     Director Projects

5.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                                 Director ICT

6.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                                                 Director CED

7.      Ayesha A. Menai                                                  Director H.R.

8.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                  Acting Director Finance

 

Mr. Imran Khan, Faculty member, attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve the replacement of old Toshiba laptops with new ones of HP.

 

1.1       The Director ICT said that there are over three years old Toshiba Laptops, for which they do not have any SLA as the three year warranty period is over.  He said that these laptops are operational but are slower than the new HP EliteBook Dual Core Laptops which were bought later on. He said that when they issue these Toshiba laptops to the faculty, they are reluctant to accept them as they want the new ones. He said that they cannot junk them out as they are still useful.  He proposed the following two solutions:

 

1.      Get SLA of the Toshiba and continue to use them.

a.       The market value of each Toshiba is Rs.14000 – 15000 depending on condition.

b.      The IBA book value is about Rs.20,000.

c.       Toshiba is not willing to provide SLA.

d.      Other vendors are quoting about Rs.9,500 per unit including Battery and Adopter.

 

2.      Buy new HP EliteBook laptops for the Faculty / staff and use the Toshibas for seminars, workshops etc. The cost of each new HP EliteBook Core i5 from market is approximately Rs.78,000 - 80,000, if these are imported in IBA Name, it will cost us about Rs.66,000.

 

1.2       The Director ICT said that if we import the new HP EliteBook 6930P Core i5 we will have to replace all 65 Toshiba laptops which are being used by the faculty presently. The cost will be about Rs 4.3 Million.

 

1.3       The Dean & Director said that we do not have money to buy new laptops and discard the old ones which are still useful.  He said that a few may be having some problems which can be fixed. He said that he wants some analysis to be done before presenting the proposal.  He asked the Director ICT to find out how many batteries and adapters are required to be changed, what are their costs, and on an average what will be total annual cost of replacement/repair and what will be the cost through SLA. 

 

1.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to find out how many of the 65 laptops are not working and need repair. He said that it can be found out from the number of complaints that have been received during the last two months.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Director ICT would do an analysis as suggested in 1.3 and 1.4 and present his recommendations based on the analysis.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the purchase of WSN equipment for Telecom Research.

 

2.1       The Director ICT said that in order to conduct research and training in the area of telecommunication, FCS last year bought Wireless Sensor Network equipment from Crossbow Inc. of USA. The bought equipment is a classroom kit containing IRIS motes and is designed to conduct training and research activities for a small group of people.  That was a first step to explore the horizon of Wireless Sensor Network (WSN), and later during Fall 2011, FCS offered a PhD level course under the supervision of Dr. Sayeed Ghani in which PhD & MS students were benefitted. He informed the Committee that one of the MS students is presently working on a project that requires some specialized WSN equipment. In addition to that four PhD students are at present actively conducting research in WSN area, the research requires a WSN MICAz motes and different varieties of sensor boards.

 

2.2       To enhance the research activities and to build a larger test bed to conduct research activity by utilizing different types of sensors and WSN motes, Dr. Sayeed Ghani has suggested to buy additional set of equipment from different vendors.  He said that FCS is planning to continue the academic activities utilizing the desired WSN
equipment in the upcoming Fall 2012 semester, therefore it is highly imperative to complete the purchase before the start of the Fall 2012 semester.

 

2.3       The Director ICT presented the list of the desired equipment from two different vendors and requested the EC to give conceptual approval for the purchase of WSN equipment for Telecom Research. He said that the total cost of the equipment would be Rs.995,275/- and a budget of Rs.1.0 million has already been approved recently in the FCS Budget.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what are the plans for future utilization of WSN equipment and what is the potential for expansion of this lab and for how many students will we additionally attract and how long the equipment will last.  He said that for 3 students it is not viable. 

 

2.5       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that the normal life of the equipment is 5 years but if used carefully, it can be useful for 7 years. He said that Dr. Sayeed Ghani will develop some new undergraduate courses and some industrial liaison has also been built on this project.  He said that this lab would strengthen our MS Program and majority of the students are opting for telecom.  Another reason is for establishing this lab is to offer more electives.

 

2.6       The Dean & Director advised Dr. Shakeel Khoja to make a comprehensive note describing the future plan for the next five year.

 

2.7       Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that we can approach HEC for funding for this project. Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that in the first stage when this program was started HEC funds were not used. Now the program is in second stage, it is not possible to approach them.

 

2.8       The Dean and Director said that if there are alternative pockets of money we should utilize them first before requesting IBA funds. He advised to present the proposal to the TEC and CPC.

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee granted in principle approval for the purchase of WSN equipment for Telecom Research.  

 

ACTION

 

2.10     Dr. Shakeel Khoja and Dr. Sayeed Ghani after obtaining approval from TEC and CPC present the comprehensive proposal to the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 3      Status Report on SAP by Mr. Imran Khan, faculty member.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Khan referred to the EC decision of 198th meeting held on April 9, 2012 wherein he was suggested to increase the number of workshops and use CEE as a platform for marketing SAP program and to provide a statement of the estimated expenditure and revenue through this program. He presented details of the projected revenue and expenditure.  He said that the program was offered in Summer but only nine; five internal and 4 external, registered in this program so due to low registration the program office did not allow this course to be offered.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director said that Associate Deans are responsible of all academic work and not the Program Office.  Program Office is just the executor of academic policies. They do not have any power to interpret the rules on their own and make unilateral decisions. The IBA organogram clearly shows that program offices work under the supervision of Associate Deans. Decisions lie first in the domain of program directors and then the  Associate Deans. He said that we should have offered this program even with the registration of nine students. He said that we are offering this program for externals and if we have break even on 5 even then we should offer.  He asked Mr. Imran to see if the nine students who had enrolled earlier can come back.

 

3.3       Mr. Imran reported that IBM will be providing five days training from July 16 to 20, 2012 to five of our faculty members free of cost, of which the actual fee is US$5000.  He said that the trainer for this program would be coming from Dubai to conduct training at IBA City Campus.

 

3.4       The Committee appreciated the offer of IBM for providing training to our faculty free of cost.

 

3.5       To a point raised by Mr. Imran Khan, the Dean & Director advised to bring part time faculty if internal faculty is not available for conducting the SAP course.  He also said that visiting faculty can also be trained.

 

3.6       Mr. Imran Khan suggested that we should pay the same amount to our faculty which we pay to our external faculty for the SAP program as an extra course.

 

3.7       The Dean & Director did not agree with Mr. Imran. He said that the academic faculty has to do research and case studies so if they are involved in teaching for the sake of additional remuneration then the research and case studies would be neglected. However, he agreed that for Executive workshops the faculty members can be paid as is the case with CEE and CED.   

 

3.8       Mr. Imran Khan presented details of the programs that have been planned for Fall, and Spring semesters including workshops during 2012-13. He was of the view that we should not have profit making approach for justifying expenditure on SAP training. It should be the net value added which should be taken as the criterion for justification. 

 

DECISION

 

3.9       The Committee noted and approved the up-dates along with the observations noted above.

 

 

Item No. 4      Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that after approval by the Board on June 23, 2012, they had a meeting with CEO of AMFCO in which they discussed the BoG approval for the value engineered BoQ. He said that AMFCO has been informed that the project is to be completed in 109 days. He said that tender evaluation report has been placed on the PPRA/SPPRA website. Preparation of documents for signing the agreement has been started and this week they would issue the work order. He said that by November 2012 the project would be completed.

 

4.2       The Director Projects suggested to obtain written agreement from AMFCO that we would award the contract on original bid of Rs.77.946 million but it is to be completed at Rs.62.789 million through agreed value engineering.

 

4.3       The Director ICT said that he would do as suggested by the Director Projects. He said that he has invited Mr. Zia of H.K. Shah to brief the EC about the decision of the value engineering.

 

4.4       Mr. Zia provided the details of the value engineering carried out and agreed upon. He said that (a) electrical work – the redundancy has been kept which is required for Tier 3, (b) cable for gen set has been reduced by placing it near the sub-station, and (c) Grounding & Bonding that has been taken out as it is not required, (d) for fire alarm the brand has been changed which is still human friendly, (e) for Security and IP Surveillance European brand has been substituted by Taiwanese or Korean brand. To a query he clarified that the new security and surveillance can be integrated with the existing surveillance system. None of these changes would impair the standards of Tier 3 requirements. Mr. Zia said that the cost of cooling system of data centre has also been reduced by replacing Crac units with floor standing ACs on places except at Main Server Room.

 

4.5       The Director Projects wanted to know if pre-shipment inspection condition has been included in the agreement or not.  He further said that it is their dual responsibility as the Project Consultant as well as the Project managers to ensure pre-shipment inspection done by the authorised inspectors like Lords, Bureau Veritas etc and this condition be  included in the suppliers contract.  Mr. Zia said that he has kept that for some parts but he said that he does not remember exactly.  However, he agreed that they (H.K. Shah’s) will take care of this as part of their responsibility. 

 

4.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know if Tier 4 comes to Pakistan, can Tier 3 be upgraded.  He also wanted to know if we can rent our data centre facilities out to others and earn some money.  Mr. Zia said that there would be no change in the system; only supply from two different sources has to be provided.  He also said that there are few set ups which are renting out their services.

 

4.7       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Zia to let him know the tariff rates for renting out data centre facilities.  Mr. Zia agreed to provide the tariff rates.  However, he said that before renting the facilities competency is needed to run the system and provide services.   He said that deputed staff of IBA would be provided training by H.K. Shah.            The Dean & Director asked the Director ICT to depute people for training by H.K. Shah who would work on the data centre.

 

4.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui asked Mr. Zia to give a write up what is done with Tier 3 so that he may write to the potential organizations for availing data centre facilities. The Dean & Director said that once the system is up and running smoothly without any bugs then we will write to potential organizations for availing our services.

 

4.9       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to who would do civil work of the data centre. Mr. Zia said that the Contractor (M/s. AMFCO) will get the civil work done; maybe through sub-contracting.

 

4.10     To a question the Director ICT said that value engineering commitment will be taken from the Contractor in writing although it has verbally been agreed by Mr. Maroof, CEO of AMFCO. Mr. Zia said that a meeting has been arranged with AMFCO to finalize all these things.

 

DECISION

 

4.11     The Committee noted and approved the up-date along with the suggestions noted above.

 

ACTION

 

4.12     The Director ICT would get a written agreement from AMFCO that we would award the contract on original bid of Rs.77.946 million but it is to be completed at Rs.62.789 million through agreed value engineering.

 

4.13     The Consultant would include pre-shipment condition in the agreement to be signed by the Contractor.

 

4.14     Mr. Zia of H.K. Shah would provide tariff rates to the Dean & Director for renting data centre facilities.

 

4.15     The ICT staff to be deputed by the Director ICT for the Data Centre would be provided training by H.K. Shah.
