Item No. 5      To consider and approve post production support for ERP system.

 

5.1       The Director ICT said that the contract with Techlogix for post production support for ERP system expired on 31st December 2011. He said that IBA was paying Rs.750,000/- on annual basis. Now Techlogix wants to increase the prices since other universities are not interested to continue the plan. He informed the Committee that he discussed the same with HEC and finally got the reply from Ms. Aneela (Project Manager ERP of HEC) that IBA Karachi is required to sign contract directly with Techlogix since other universities are not paying. He said that Techlogix have sent the following two options:

 

Option 1

PKR 600,000 on Quarterly basis. In this plan they will dedicate resources for IBA. The yearly cost of this plan would be Rs.2.4 million.

 

Option 2

Incident based support in which Techlogix will charge:

o   A monthly resource assurance fee @ USD500/- (payable upfront every quarter)

o   Per incident rate: Offsite @ USD20 per hour

o   Onsite @ USD25 per hour plus travel and lodging (if required)

 

5.2       The Director ICT said that they have evaluated both options and also reviewed first quarter incident support. The details of this are as under received from Techlogix:

 

The dollar conversion rate used is PKR 90.

            IBA raised 22 tickets in 2012 in the first 3 months.

Total estimated quarterly cost for IBA Karachi would be:

-          Total time to fix is approx. 60 hours * 20 = US$ 1200

-          The assurance charges @ US$500 per month for 3 months= US$1500

-          Total 1st Quarter cost approximately = 1200 + 1500 = US$ 2,700 (approximately PKR 243,000)

 

If multiplied by four, the annual cost will be Rs.243,000 x 4 = Rs.973,000 as compared to Option No. 1 where it will be Rs.2.4 million.

 

 

5.3       The Director ICT requested the EC to give approval for incident based support (Option No.2) as the IS team is quite capable to take care operations matters and this support  is very much cost effective as compared to option No 1.

 

5.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to get the charges reduced as much as possible.

 

DECISION

 

5.5       Based on the workings shown by the Director ICT, the Committee approved Option No.2 for post production support for ERP System.

 

ACTION

 

5.6       The Director ICT would try to get the charges reduced as much as possible.

 

 

Item No. 6      To consider and approve Waste Water Disposal System at the Boys’ Hostel, and Staff Colony.

 

6.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed referring to the recommendation of the TEC and CPC for issuing tender for the waste water disposal system which was noted by the EC in its meeting held on April 23, 2012 requested the Executive Committee to review the decision as issuing a tender for the work involves the following steps, each of which takes its own time, even if accomplished with extra-ordinary haste:

 

(a)    Advertisement inviting applications for pre-qualification of consultants

(b)   Preparation of an RFP and issuing to pre-qualified consultants, asking them to submit their bids for providing the required consultancy service.

(c)    Evaluation of the bids

(d)   Approval of the responsive, lowest bid by TEC, CPC and EC

(e)    Award of a contract to the approved consultant

(f)    Designing of the Waste Water Disposal System and preparation of Tender Documents by the consultant

(g)   Issuance of Tender to the previously pre-qualified contractors

(h)   Evaluation of the contractors’ bids for selection of the most responsive, lowest bid.

(i)     Approval of the lowest responsive bid by TEC, CPC and EC

(j)     Award of a contract to the approved bidder

 

6.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that if they follow the tender procedure the Waste Water System for the Boys’ Hostel that they need before the end of August this year cannot be built in-time. He further said that as the total estimated cost of the project, as approved by EC in its meeting held on April 2, 2012, is Rs.5.6 million only, he was doubtful to get reasonable response to the advertisement for pre-qualification of consultants. Therefore, in his opinion, adopting the Tender Procedure will not only be unnecessarily lengthy, it may eventually prove to be fruitless.

 

6.3       As an alternative Mr. Shameem Ahmed suggested to add the required consultancy service as an additional service to the scope of services of one of the consultants already working for IBA. They include NESPAK, NBCL and FNMH.  Likewise, the actual construction work (septic tanks + soakage pits) may be added as an extra item in the work being executed by the respective contractors at the Boys’ Hostel and Staff Colony (Visiting Faculty Hostel and New Girls’ Hostel).

 

DECISION

 

6.4       The Committee after a detailed discussion, decided that in view of the time constraints the work is to be allocated to the same consultant who is working on the project as an additional work to the current scope.

 

ACTION

 

6.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects would take action as per decision noted in 6.4 above.

 

           

Item No.7       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

7.1       The Director ICT reported that M/s. H.K. Shah have submitted a summary sheet in which they have tried to clarify certain queries. He said that he has invited Mr. Zia of M/s H.K. Shah to present their findings and recommendations. Mr. Zia reported that:

 

o   AMFCO have done the cost calculation of imported items at the exchange rate of US$1 : PKR98 whereas H.K. Shah did the same at US$1 : PKR87.

 

o   Based on the original budget outlay of Rs.20 million, the BoQ prepared by H.K. Shah is already optimized on the basis of value and therefore further value engineering is not possible.  Value engineering is not about cutting down the BoQ as it compromises not only quality but also risks the attainment of Tier III certification. As a result of an exercise to see what difference could be made, an amount of Rs.1.84 million could be reduced.

 

o   All brands have been selected keeping the original budget of Rs.20 million in mind, hence the possibility of replacing them with brands that may deliver the same efficiency and output but cost lesser will only occur if Chinese projects are used – which is not recommended.

 

o   M/s. H.K. Shah have not been informed about the internal fixed costs such as man hours being incurred by AMFCO or as to what their funding model is. However, H.K. Shah did intense deliberation and counselling sessions on ways and means to cut down the original estimate. Resultantly, AMFCO have agreed to a discount of 4.5% of the project cost which comes to around Rs.3.5 million. AMFCO have expressly stated that neither are they in a position to offer further discount nor are they willing to disclose their costs and profit outlays for the project.

 

o   H.K. Shah requested IBA to raise the budget to meet the quoted price otherwise the only option would be to cut down the BoQ which will not only impact quality, but also risk the acceptance of the data centre at the Tier III level.       

 

7.2       Mr. Zia presented break up of Consultant’s estimates before addendum which in total was Rs.55.82 million and after addendum that was Rs.68.86 million.  He also presented the section-wise summary of AMFCO’s financial bid according to which the total cost excluding services and profit was Rs.77.9 million and after discount of 4.5%, it stood at Rs.74.4 million. Thus the difference between the Consultant’s estimate and the bid price after discount was of around Rs.6 million.

 

7.3       The Director Projects said that civil work cost would be transferred to the Projects. Similarly, cost of generator will go to the overall infrastructure of City Campus.

 

7.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the AMFCO can reduce Rs.6 million further then we may proceed.

 

DECISION

 

7.5       The Committee requested the Director ICT and Mr. Zia of H.K. Shah to have a meeting with AMFCO and convince them to reduce the cost by another Rs.6 million and give the final figure.

 

 

Item No. 8      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  Installation and synchronization of four generators.

 

8.1       The Registrar invited Mr. Muhammad Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer to suggest solution to the issue raised by Mr. Shameem Ahmed. Mr. Shameem Ahmed had stated that the site of the existing KESC S/S could not be made available until the four generators were installed, and the four generators cannot be Synchronized until the 100 kVA generator has also been overhauled and shifted to its new location.

 

8.2       Mr. Jawad explained the reasons due to which the overhauling of 100 kva generator was delayed. This included the issue of advance payment to M/s. S.M Jaffer, the sole distributors of the F.G Wilson generators. He said that as we do not have any policy to make advance payment, we communicated this issue to them & then after prolonged exchange of correspondence a way out was sorted out. M/s. S.M. Jaffer agreed to deliver overhauling parts to IBA on a Work/Purchase order. M/s. S.M. Jaffer will take 15 to 20 working days to complete the job. He said that he worked & found a more convenient & feasible solution of placing 135 kva dg set in place of 100 kva to resolve the issue & get rid of this unwanted delay. The advantages of placing 135 kva are:

 

a.       Instant solution. No needs to mount electronic governor (already fitted in it).

b.      No need to wait for canopy. It is already housed in canopy.

c.       RCC Foundation built for 100 kva can be used for 135 kva (no size issue).

d.      No major issue / modification in the Switchgear panel. Only one Motorized breaker (160 A) in the synchronization panel needed to be replaced with 230 A motorized breaker. 

DECISION

8.3       The Committee including Mr. Shameem Ahmed agreed to the solution proposed by Mr. M. Jawad Khan.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
