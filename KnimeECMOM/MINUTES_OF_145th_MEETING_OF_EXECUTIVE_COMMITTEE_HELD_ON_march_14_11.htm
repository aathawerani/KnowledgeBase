MINUTES OF THE 145TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MARCH 14, 2011

 

The 145th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 14, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                    Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

3.      Dr.Sayeed Ghani                                    Associate Dean

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                     Controller of Examinations

5.      Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

6.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

7.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                               Director CED

8.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

9.      Ms. Ayesha A. Minai                                Director H.R.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects and Dr. Nasir Afghan, Director MBA Program attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      Presentation on the facade and elevations of IBA Girls’ Hostel by FNMH.

 

1.1       Mr. Mukhtar Husain of FNMH Architecture presented the pictorial views of the designed facade and elevation of the proposed new Girls Hostel. He said that the rooms were located in four blocks which could be constructed block by block.  Every block has separate kitchen and laundry. One block may be used for Sindh Talent Hunt Program. The blocks consist of G+2. The total capacity of all the four blocks is for 168 students.   

 

1.2       The Director said that the per sq. ft cost estimate of Rs...../- was very high.  He said that he wants the Girls’ Hostel to be as modest as Boys’ Hostel.  He suggested to find the ways to reduce the cost. He also said that the common area which is around 12000 sft is quite substantial and suggested to reduce it also if possible.  He advised the Consultant to modify the design in terms of standard specifications and covered area without compromising on aesthetics. He said that the Fire Alarm System which is estimated to cost around Rs.5 million is not needed. He said that incremental blocks will be constructed as and when needed and this year construction of one block would be started. He informed the Committee that Project Department has requested the Sindh Government for financing and if the Sindh Government approves it then it would be a viable project because he said that due to flood it has become difficult to find sponsors for the project.

 

1.3       To a point raised by a member, Mr. Mukhtar Husain said that they would try to maintain the similarity with other buildings.

 

1.4       The Director requested the Director Projects to brief Mr. Mukhtar about the colour, texture and character of other buildings of IBA already renovated or are under construction. The Director Projects said that the colour and character of each building will be slightly different from other buildings of IBA.

 

1.5       The Director wanted to know the time plan. To this Mr. Mukhtar said that it would take 2 to 3 months to finalize the tender documents.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee approved the facade and elevations of the IBA Girls’ Hostel as presented by Mr. Mukhtar Husain of FNMH.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Consultant would reduce the per sft. estimated cost and try to bring it in the range of Rs...../- to Rs....../- and also remove the low priority high cost items such as fire alarm system. The Director HEC Projects and the Consultant would revisit the area under common spaces. 

 

1.8       The Consultant would ensure that the incremental work (block by block) may not give the feeling of incompleteness.

 

1.9       Mr. Shameem Ahmed and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia would discuss the theme of the facade with the architects and agree on a uniform theme.          

 

 

Item No. 2      Presentation on “IBA Case Unit”.

 

2.1       Dr. Nasir Afghan, Director MBA Program stated that they were working on the proposed IBA Case E-Journal and Case Clearing Centre for quite some time. He said that he had discussed this project with some professors within and outside Pakistan and all of them have given a very positive feedback. He said that the idea is to set up case unit and facilitate faculty to write cases.

 

2.2       The Director said that so far only two faculty members are teaching MBA students through case method.  He said that three foreign professors, experts in case method, were invited at IBA who provided guidance to IBA faculty on case writing.  Some faculty members had agreed to write cases but so far there is no result.  He said that he was in favour of IBA Case E-Journal and Case Clearing Centre but he wanted to know as to who would be going to do that. He further said that for IBA’s own E-journal, critical mass is needed otherwise the journal would irregularly be available. He also said that he wants to give preference to IBA’s faculty and publication of their research work in recognized journals even of LUMS or Indian journals would serve the purpose.   

 

2.3       Dr. Nasir Afghan said that at LUMS it took 18 to 19 years to establish case method.  For two years Harvard Professors designed case method for LUMS.  He said that at IBA there are many good faculty with great potential. He hoped that gradually faculty members would switch to case method. He proposed to make it mandatory for the faculty members teaching in MBA programs to write two cases which may be considered at the time of performance evaluation.

2.4       The Director said that writing cases may be optional, he cannot make it mandatory as it is against the academic freedom.

 

2.5       Dr. Mohammad Nishat said that first faculty members should understand case study, learn it and then start teaching. Confidence is required first and then it is following by case writing and case teaching.

 

2.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that case teaching is a long process and it will take time. He suggested that MBA courses as well as their outlines are known to faculty and students. Faculty should be told about the cases to be included in the course outline so that they may know the cases and lectures they are to teach. Books may be used as reference. He suggested that MBA faculty should sit with Director MBA Program and the Associate Deans and decide the cases to be taught in each course.

 

2.7       Dr. Nasir Afghan said that they did exactly what Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested. He further said that they down loaded cases and provided them to the faculty.

 

2.8       The Director said that there should be some evidence on which credit of 1 unit is to be given to MBA faculty. It should be seen how and what a faculty member was to teach and how and what he/she really taught. If there is no mismatch only then the credit should be given.   

 

2.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to constitute a Surveillance Committee for faculty to see what a faculty member is teaching and what he was supposed to.      

 

2.10     After the above discussions, Dr. Nasir Afghan made the presentation on the Case Unit at IBA.  He gave rationale, mission and aims and objectives of the Case Unit at IBA.  He also briefed about the methodology, flow chart, proposed Editorial Board, and Review Board. He also presented the comments and feedback received from faculty members of IBA, LUMS and some foreign professors including Dr. Wasim Azhar.  He suggested that there should be a defined budget for the Case Unit to purchase cases as the current process is extremely cumbersome and time consuming. He said that there should be a team of research associates who could assist the faculty members in developing the cases during data collection. He also talked about the need for diversity on Board for Review and the possibility of including the International Advisory Board convened by GBSN to act as reviewers.

 

2.11     The Director suggested that at the beginning instead of providing access to the cases through website to all students across the globe, IBA should focus on its own students; the emphasis should be on IBA students and the quality of the cases to be written.   

 

2.12     Dr. Sayeed Ghani observed that there was no mention of mentorship (feedback) in the flow chart.  Dr. Nasir Afghan agreed that feedback should be there in the flow chart. 

 

DECISION

 

2.13     The Committee decided that the Academic Committee will specify that credit of 1 unit will be given to MBA faculty only if the concerned faculty teaches cases according to the course outline presented by him / her to the Academic Committee before the start of the semester. The Academic Committee would randomly check and audit the class and present its report before the Calibration Committee.  

 

2.14     The Committee decided that there would be two budgets; one for the library and one for the Case Unit Projects.

 

2.15     IBA students may be hired as Research Assistants for providing assistance to the faculty members in developing the cases during data collection.

 

2.16     The Committee noted and approved the proposal for Case Unit at IBA as presented by Dr. Nasir Afghan along with the observations noted above.

 

ACTION

 

2.17     Dr. Nasir Afghan would request the Department Chairs to ask their full time MBA faculty to present their course outlines in the Academic Committee meeting to be held in end of April 2011.

 

2.18     The Chairman Academic Committee will notify that credit of one unit would be given to MBA faculty only if the concerned faculty teaches cases according to the course outlines approved by the Academic Committee.

 

2.19     Dr. Muhammad Nishat will get the budget allocated for Case Unit at IBA.

 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Approval for Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN).

 

3.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that Mr. Raza H. Abedi, faculty member of FCS, has requested for procurement of MEMSIC Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN) Kit which he will use for his research that he has started. Giving details of the WSN, he said that these are tiny devices that are programmed to sense different type of activities such as heat, humidity, pressure, seismic activity, motion, vibration and real time audio/video events etc. The applications of Wireless Sensor Networks are enormous, such as wild life/environmental monitoring, industrial control, surveillance, military applications and more. In the Telecommunication domain Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN) is very progressive research area and there is enormous room for research in specific sub area such as fault tolerance, security and multimedia applications.

 

3.2       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the FCS is planning to build a small WSN lab to conduct research in WSN. The equipment they are planning to acquire for the lab is open source, versatile and flexible to use. The equipment will allow them to conduct research by improving existing algorithms and also working towards building new protocols for future applications. The equipment would also helps them in launching research oriented courses at MS and PhD levels.

 

3.3       Dr. Arshad Siddiqui said that the price of the WSN equipment (Class room kit) excluding shipment and clearing/forwarding charges is US$7310.00. The shipment and local clearing charges are approximately US$500.00 He said that the total amount converting in Pak rupee will be around Rs.650,000 which will be within the budgeted amount of Rs.1.00 million for such equipments.  However, he said that some of the equipments they want to procure are not readily available so the remaining budgeted amount may have to be carried forward to the next year’s budget. He requested principle approval for procurement of WSN equipments.   

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee granted principle approval for the purchase of WSN equipments. 

 

ACTION

 

3.5       Dr. Arshad Siddiqi would ensure that the WSN equipment is procured by following the proper procedure.

 

 

b)     Clarification on part-time job opportunities for BBA students. 

 

3.6       A BBA student had raised the question whether his part-time job during BBA studies would be recognized as an experience to be eligible for applying for MBA.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee clarified that the job experience gained during BBA studies would not be counted towards the requirement of experience to be eligible for applying for MBA Program.  It has to be a full time job after BBA/Graduation.

 

 

c)      List of Registered Graduates.

 

3.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui informed the Committee that the Alumni Department has prepared a large data base of the IBA alumni.  He suggested that this list may be used for election of Registered Graduates for BoG seat instead of the list of only 730 registered graduates being maintained by Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri.

 

3.9       Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri clarified that he was maintaining the Register of Registered Graduates in accordance with the IBA Code Book which requires that an IBA graduate must have an standing of three years before getting himself / herself registered and the Register is closed at least 40 days before the election date. He said that the data based prepared by the Alumni Department may be used for election purposes.  However, it has to be ensured that the graduates already registered are also included in the list of the alumni maintained by the Alumni Department and the procedural changes may be reported to the Board.

 

DECISON

 

3.10     The Committee agreed to the suggestions of Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri.

 

ACTION

 

3.11     The Manager Corporate Affairs would report the procedural changes to the Board.    

 

 

d)     Proposal for Executive Medical Check-Up.

 

3.12     A member of the Executive Committee suggested that IBA should arrange yearly Executive Medical Check-up for its directors / EC members.

 

DECISION

 

3.13     The Director did not agree to the proposal and advised that individuals should get it done at their own.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
