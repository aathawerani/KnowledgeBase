MINUTES OF THE 49th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MAY 27, 2009

Item No. 1      Selection of Consulting Architect for Multi-storeyed Building Complex at IBA.

1.1    The Senior Manager IT I&O in his note submitted through Director ICT stated that IBA had published advertisement in leading newspapers in May 2009 for pre-qualification of Technology Partner Agreement for IT Equipment, Telecom Equipment, Data Center / DRC Equipment, consultancy and implementation etc., Service and Maintenance, Green Technology providers, Power monitoring and future planning.  He stated that 49 vendors participated in the pre-qualification. The minimum criteria for eligibility as published in the newspapers were:

·    Must have been in similar business for the last three years with annual turnover of minimum Rs.10 million.

·    Must be registered with Income Tax and Sales Tax departments.

·   Should not be in litigation or blacklisted by any client, government, semi-government   department or autonomous body. 

1.2     The Senior Manager IT I&O reported that after evaluating each and every application, they have short-listed 24 vendors having 70% and above scores on the following criteria:

1.3     The Director said that the evaluation criteria should be developed and presented before the Executive Committee for approval before the opening of the tender. He further said that IT Equipment, Telecom Equipment, Service and Maintenance, Power Monitoring and Future Planning should not have been lumped under the same list; each should have been separate otherwise all will get mixed up. He reminded that he had said that there should be a committee consisting of the department heads and outsiders for evaluation so that sanctity of the project is maintained.

1.4     The Director I.C.T. clarified that at this time he did not do that and assured that next time he would get the evaluation done through a committee. He further clarified that they are not looking for different vendors for different equipments. There will be a consortium which will provide the required equipments and services.

1.5     Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know if tendering will be needed or not in case of bulk purchases of IT equipments. 

1.6     The Director I.C.T. clarified that the pre-qualified would be given the tender. The Director ICT also clarified that the vendors will supply the equipments in IBAs name and they will be bound to provide after sales service.  He said that the IBA will be getting discount available to educational institutions and will also be saving import and sales tax duty.  He said that there would be three partners; local vendors, the principal (manufacturers of H.P., CISCO for example) and the IBA. The price will be negotiated with the principal and not with the local vendors. The agreement will be made with the principal but will include vendors as facilitators who will also provide after sales service and resolve the problem if any. LC will be opened by the facilitator and they will deliver orders on the premises. The commission to facilitator will be paid by the principal and not by IBA. It is a tri-partner relationship.  He said that he would provide the draft agreement to the Head of Internal Audit for his comments.   

1.7    The Director said that we can get projects related equipments also by opening LC.  He requested the Director Projects to look into this option. 

DECISION 

1.8     The Committee approved the prequalification of 24 companies as recommended by the Director ICT. The Committee also decided that next year the number of pre-qualified companies may be reduced.

ACTION

1.9     The Director ICT will provide a copy of the draft agreement to the Head of Internal Audit for his comments.

1.10   The Director Projects will look into the possibility of getting projects related equipments by opening LC at IBA. 

Item No. 2    To consider email from Mr. Ejaz A. Mian regarding his Ph.D progress and request for IBA help.

2.1     Mr. Ejaz A. Mian, Assistant Professor (on leave) stated in his email that he would be defending his findings in July this year. After that he has to finish his write up and submit and wait for the viva.  He stated that it would save his lot of time and expense if he finishes the entire program before he returns home. If he comes back to Pakistan in July this year, it will delay his PhD award. In his opinion it would be the best if he stays there and completes his write up for submission and then takes the viva and comes back to Pakistan.  He stated that in doing this the problem is that his earned leave would expire on July 23, 2009. After that he has no money to carry on. He said that he was totally depending on his earnings from salary for his PhD and he has spent his entire savings in the process.

2.2     Mr. Ejaz Mian wanted to know if there was any way IBA would be interested in helping him out in some way for these remaining last few months of his program.  He stated that he intends to carry on serving IBA upon his return to Pakistan and would be happy to continue to serve as long as IBA wants him to do so. But at this crucial juncture he needed support from IBA.  

2.3     Mr. Ejaz Mian requested the Finance Department to release his last instalment of salary for June to July, 2009 so that he may be able to register for the next semester by paying his fee. He clarified that his earned leave is from December 24, 2008 for 211 days i.e. up to July 27, 2009. He has been paid salary for 6 months that is up to June 23, 2009.  He requested that he be paid salary for June 23 to July 27, 2009 as soon as possible as he has to face many expenses there with regard to registration and other items.

2.4     The Committee discussed the possibility of giving loan from his Provident Fund. However, the Director Finance informed the Committee that Mr. Ejaz A. Mian will be reaching the age of superannuation in February 2010.  He said that at this stage he cannot be given loan against his Provident Fund. Moreover, the fund will serve as collateral against the financial support the IBA has already provided to Mr. Ejaz Mian.

DECISION

2.5     The Committee agreed with the Director Finance that the Provident Fund should be released at the time of final settlement on his retirement. However, to facilitate Mr. Ejaz Mian in completing his Ph.D the Committee decided to give Qarz-e-Hasna equivalent to three months salary from the Welfare Fund to Mr. Ejaz A. Mian against a bond for repayment of the loan.

ACTION 

2.6    The Director Finance will arrange to transfer in the account of Mr. Ejaz Mian Qarz-e-Hasna equivalent to three months salary from Welfare Fund against a bond for repayment of the loan.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair. 

(a)     To consider note from Director Projects regarding Academic Block, Main Campus, Academic Block, City Campus and Auditorium, Main Campus. 

7.1     The Director Projects stated that continuous deteriorating situation of law and order resulting loss of working days and a sense of uncertainty have posed a serious threat to the execution projects at site as well as pre-execution activities. The projects how well planned see slippages of targets.  He said that it has become extremely necessary to consider these risks in the overall planning and provide for alternate means to achieve the desired results. We need to review our plans for scheduled targets and be prepared for any eventualities. 

7.2     The Director Projects said that we had set a very tight schedule of 90 days for class rooms of Ground Floor and First Floor of Academic Block of the Main Campus Academic Block and that of City Campus. Also 90 days were planned for completion of interior of the Auditorium. On two projects bidding problems were encountered. He said that some systems compliance issues also take considerable time which in turn becomes detrimental to the project progress.  In view of the above, he said that they have worked out the possible solutions project wise as follows:

 I.          Academic Block, Main Campus :     

7.3    The Director Projects said that there is a requirement of 12 class rooms. It will thus be necessary to have alternate places for 6 class rooms. Four (4) can be built on the roof of multipurpose building. Two (2) class rooms can be accommodated in the existing library, construction of which can be delayed for 3 months. The loss of 3 months in library can be made up as it will be an independent construction site. The target completion of January, 2011 will be maintained. Remaining 8 class rooms will be completed while working in the night shift by 31st December, 2009. Overall project will be completed by March 2010 (original completion was October 2010). The Director Projects also presented the summary of the schedule.  

II.        Academic Block, City Campus 

7.4    The Director Projects reported that tender documents were issued to the following four pre-qualified contractors on 2nd May, 2009.           

1.                  M/s. Project Managers, Construction Engineers

2.                  M/s. Prefab Ltd.,

3.                  M/s. Ziauddin Construction Ltd.,

4.                  M/s. Al-Shafi

 

7.5       He stated that all had attended the pre-bid meeting and understood the job. However, on submission date (19th May, 2009) only 2 contractors at # 1 & # 2 turned up. Technical evaluation of both the bidders revealed that technically bids of both bidders were deficient in many respects. M/s. P.M. Engineers & Construction secured 63% while Prefab Ltd., secured 42% marks.

 

7.6       The Director Projects said that based on the fact that the bidding process will not be competitive and both the bidders technical proposals are not upto the mark, they have no option but to re-bid the project in which case the project can commence by 20th June, as approval from A&FC will have to be obtained.

 

7.7       One seminar hall can be built by August 15, but it will be of no use as construction activity will seriously affect the classes. There will be video conferencing room available on first floor and one big hall in APWA which can be used for MBA classes.

 

III.       Auditorium,  Main Campus

 

7.8       The Director Projects reported that tender documents were issued to following contractors on May 02, 2009:

 

1.      M/s. Abaseen Construction Co.

2.      M/s. Gulf Construction Co.,

3.      M/s. A. S. Construction Co.

4.      M/s. Mughal Construction Co.

 

7.9       He said that Contractors from #1 to #3 submitted the technical proposal as well as the financial proposals, both separately on 22nd May, 2009. Technical proposals were opened and are being evaluated by the Architects, Consultants and Project Managers. At the first review, all the three proposals have been given the time of completion of the entire project 7 to 7 ½ months. All the contractors were approached to explain this. They have responded in negative stating that the work cannot be done in 3 months due to technical reason.

 

7.10     The Director Projects stated that on the results of technical evaluations, the financial bids will be opened and on the basis of analysis of financial bids the recommendations will be submitted to the Audit & Finance Committee for approval. Under the above scenario the Auditorium will not be ready before December 31, 2009.

7.11     The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the situation and accord its approval for the above arrangements based on the actual eventuality.

 

DECISION

 

7.12     The Committee considering the situation as explained by the Director Projects, accorded approval of the arrangements as proposed above.
