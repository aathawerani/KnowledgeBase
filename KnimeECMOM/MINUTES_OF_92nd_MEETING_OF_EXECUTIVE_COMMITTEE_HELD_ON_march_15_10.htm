Item No. 2      To accept new samples for façade applied on the wall of existing Academic Block at Main Campus and to approve one of the samples.

 

2.1              The Executive Committee went to the site of the existing Academic Block where samples of new facade, texture  and colour were installed by the contractor as per the requirements of the Architects M/s. ICON. There were four sets of samples with the colour variation in the shade of light grey and the dark grey.

 

DECISION

 

2.2              After detailed inspection, discussion and taking into consideration individual members choice, the Executive Committee approved the sample having fine grains of marble chips in light grey colour with the combination of a dark grey sample.

 

2.3              During this discussion, the matter of the rejected facade of washed terrazzo was also considered. It was decided that the facade in wash terrazzo could be  allowed to be continued on the existing Academic Block at City Campus in view of the fact that the application of facade was in advance stages and that the removal and application of a new facade material would cost huge amount of money. It was also decided that the same facade will be continued with Administration Building and the FCS Building which is under design.

 

ACTION

 

2.4              The Director Projects would get the above decisions (2.2 & 2.3) implemented.

 

 

Item No. 3      Approval for Project Management Services for Construction of First Floor, Multi-purpose Building at Main Campus.

Post-bid Services for 4 months @ Rs.60,000/month              Rs. 240,000/-  

Total Fee                                                                                Rs. 300,000/-

 

3.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the Project Management Fee of Rs.300,000/- for the above mentioned project to M/s. INTEG.

 

3.3       The Registrar said that at the Academic Block of Main Campus the quality of work was inferior.He said that the Project Manager did not identify the faults.

Item No. 4      Approval for re-routing of existing cables at IBA City Campus.

        

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the power cables which were lying below the existing platform of Academic Block were required to be shifted at safer location as the work on the platform had completed and the cables were lying naked without any conduit which were posing life and safety threats for the students and faculty members. He said that to avoid any accident a meeting was held between Project Manager, Consultant and IBA Engineers and Director Projects and discussed various options in a meeting. During the discussions three options were derived out for the above work:

 

Option-1          Rs.1,356,544   Required shortest duration of shutdown (4 hrs)

Option-2          Rs.744,798      Required longer duration for shutdown (8 hrs)

Option-3          Rs.325,282      Required longest duration for shut down (16 hrs)

 

4.2       The Director Projects stated that from above options, the option-3 was selected as this option was cheapest and arrangements for 16 hours shutdown were permitted by City Campus management. The contractor was thus given a go ahead for option-3 because delay in work might cause a big accident. This emergency work was done through the already mobilized contractor M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Company. He said that the scope of work and amount as mentioned above were duly scrutinized and verified by Project Managers M/s. INTEG and the Electrical Consultant M/s SK Engineers, who recommended the final amount of Rs.278,005/-as the cost of work.

 

4.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve an amount of Rs.278,005/-for the subject work.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Executive Committee approved the amount of Rs.278,005/- for re-routing of existing cables at IBA City Campus.

 

 

Item No. 5      Approval for Project Management Services for Power Supply System from Karachi University Feeder to Boys Hostel & Admin Block, Main Campus. (Conversion of overhead to underground network)

5.1       The Director Projects stated that work (conversion of overhead electrical network to underground network) for study and preparation of feasibility, tender documents & BOQ and top supervision were given to M/s SK Engineers. The contract for the subject work was awarded to M/s B.G. Civil Electrical Mechanical Company at a cost of Rs.16,059,607/-. He said that the contractor has mobilized at site and started the work but work for detail supervision, verification of cable tests, inspection of material and quality control was not awarded to any firm. He said that the cost of project being over Rs.1.0 million required it to be independently supervised by Project Manager. Accordingly, M/s INTEG who are already working at the Main Campus were asked to submit their Proposal. M/s INTEG submitted their financial proposal and they asked for the fee of 1.75% of the contract price which is Rs.281,043./- He said that they are already working at Fee of 2.5% of the contract value on different projects therefore they quoted discounted fee due to long relation and better business with IBA.

 

5.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the fee of 1.75% of the contract value which is lower than what they are charging on other projects.

 

5.3              The Director asked the Director Projects if he was satisfied with the expertise they have.  The Director Projects said that he was satisfied with their expertise.

 

DECISION

 

5.4              The Committee approved the fee of 1.75% of the contract value which is Rs.281,043/- for Project Management to M/s. Integ for Project Management Services for Power Supply System from Karachi University Feeder to Boys Hostel & Admin Block, Main Campus. (Conversion of overhead to underground network).

 

 

Item No. 6      Presentation by Dr. Arshad Siddiqi on Emergency Response and Crises Management.

 

6.1              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi made a detailed presentation on Emergency Response and Crises Management. The presentation covered Prevention and Mitigation Measures, Primary Objectives of Crisis Management, Crisis Management Planning, Establishing Crisis Management Team and Centre, Emergency and Crisis Management Tools, etc.

ACTION

 

6.3              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi will get a plan for Crisis Management prepared and present it before the Executive Committee within three months.
