Item No. 2      Anticipated problems in the current semester term and final exams and its solutions.

 

2.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili stated that the following is the Campus-wise uneven distribution of students:

 

City Campus:        225 (new)   +  818 (old)   =  1043

Main Campus:       706 (new)   + 1018 (old)  =  1724

 

This uneven distribution caused serious difficulties in scheduling Ist Term exams at the Main Campus. Requirements of rooms and invigilators have doubled. Situation will further aggravate during finals and it will be very difficult to finish exams in 10 days. He said that students may not get appropriate breaks during finals of 3 hours each.

 

2.2       Mr. Khalili reported that faculty members do not like to invigilate as they consider it a menial job.  They schedule other activities during exams and simply email that they will not be available to conduct exams.  Even those who come, do not stay for the whole time and like to leave after ten minutes.  He recommended that:

 

o   A clear message should go to the faculty that examination is an integral part of teaching and they should take it as seriously as teaching itself.

o   Faculty should not schedule their other activities during exams unless unavoidable. In that case they should arrange substitute faculty to conduct their exams.

o   Number of additional invigilations for each regular faculty may be increased from 1 to at least 3. This will ease up increasing burden on staff.

o   EC should take a decision as to who are exempted from additional invigilations.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that he has informed the faculty members earlier too that examination is an integral part of teaching and they should take it seriously. He clarified that except Associate Deans, all faculty members including chairpersons, program directors, program coordinators have to do invigilation. He said that he would send an email to all faculty asking them to perform invigilation for their papers for the whole duration not just for 10 minutes. He asked the Director H.R. to keep exam duty in faculty’s performance evaluation also. He asked Dr. Sayeed Ghani to get examination duty included in the quantitative rating of faculty. The Dean & Director said that we should continue with one invigilation duty for regular faculty but involve our MS and PhD students also in invigilation.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director asked the Controller of Examinations to prepare a chart keeping in view the new policy and see the total number of faculty available for invigilation duty including MS and Ph.D students.  He hoped that the 30 additional invigilators (students of MS and Ph.D) will solve the problem. However, he said that even then if there is any problem that could be revisited.        

                    

2.5       Mr. Khalili said that on an average 40 staff members will be required daily in 4 shifts at the Main Campus and 20 at the City Campus if each staff member performs 2 invigilations a day. However, he said that most of the staff members are not motivated to do invigilation and many of them do not turn up on time which causes serious problems in starting exams on time.  Centre Incharge has to run from pillar to post to arrange invigilators so that exam could be started.  Some Department Heads send emails informing that certain staff members are working on “important” assignments therefore they cannot invigilate as if supervision of exam is the most unimportant work at IBA.

          

2.6       Mr. Khalili said that in order to motivate staff to take interest in invigilation duties rather than considering it drudgery, there is no other way but to approve some compensation for staff like they are paid for participating in admission tests. He recommended the following rates:

 

Rs.400/= for invigilating in Term Exams (each shift)

Rs.800/= for invigilating in Final Exams (each Shift)

 

He said that it would not cost more than Rs.1.5 million per annum to compensate staff for performing invigilation duties at both campuses in 4 term exams, one midterm and three finals in a calendar year (total 48 exam days of regular programs excluding EMBA exams).  He said that considering the sensitivity and importance of exams this amount should not be taken as a financial burden. He suggested that the cost can easily be recovered by charging students Rs.1000/= per semester as examination fee.

 

2.7       The Dean & Director said that in admission tests we get fee for the test as such there is no difficulty in paying for invigilation or other testing duties. About the exemption from invigilation, the Dean & Director said that if anyone is doing an important assignment then it is the discretion of the department head to get him/her exempted from invigilation duty but he must provide a substitute.

 

2.8       Dr. Sayeed Ghani endorsed the recommendation of Mr. Khalili that we may increase the tuition fee by Rs.1000/- which, for our internal accounting purpose, may be treated as examination fee to cover the examination related cost.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that we should check if examination fee is charged separately in other universities or it is taken as tuition fee.

 

2.9       The Dean & Director showed his strong reservation on demand of money for every assignment given to the staff or faculty. He said that we talk of values but there is a disconnect between what we are saying and what we are doing because we are not prepared to do anything unless we are compensated for it. Where is our sense of dedication and commitment which we try to imbibe in our students. We should be the role models for the students.  He asked Mr. Moeid Sultan to examine this proposal in light of the practice followed in other universities and report to the EC for a final decision.

 

DECISIONS

 

2.10     The Committee decided that:

 

a)      Examination is an integral part of teaching and faculty members have to perform invigilation for their papers for the whole duration of exam not just for 10 minutes.

b)      Except the Associate Deans, all faculty members including chairpersons, program directors, program coordinators have to per invigilation duty.

c)      MS and PhD students would also be assigned invigilation duties.

 

ACTION

 

2.11     The Director H.R. would include exam duty also in faculty’s performance evaluation, and Dr. Sayeed Ghani would get examination duty included in the quantitative rating of faculty.

 

2.12     Mr. Moeid Sultan would examine the proposal of compensation to staff for their involvement in invigilation duty and charging examination fee from students in light of the practice followed in other universities and report to the EC for a final decision.  However, the Dean & Director expressed his personal reservation to additional compensation for invigilation to staff.  He would, however, go along if the majority of the EC favoured this.

 

Item No. 3      To discuss issue on Admission on SAT Score / IBA Test.

 

3.1       The Registrar said that the subject issue emerged following an email from a prospective candidate. The text of the email was, “I checked your website and it said that the aptitude test is most probably on the 17th of November, 2013. I wish to apply on the basis of my SAT score.  I am registered for November 2, 2013 and the result will be available probably after the aptitude test. Is it possible for me to take the aptitude test as well as exempt myself from it after I receive my SAT result?”.  

 

3.2       The Registrar said that he, as Chairman, Admission Committee, sought opinion from members Admission Committee. Three members which included the Chairman Admission Committee also, were of the opinion that the student has a choice, at the time of applying for IBA, to either give his SAT score or sit for the test. This choice is clear to him at the time of application. If he chooses to forego his SAT score then he is making a conscious decision to select one alternative over another. Giving him the choice at a later stage when students have already taken the test and gotten a place above the cut-off point will deny them the opportunity to enter IBA by someone who deliberately did not disclose his SAT score. The decision to process students for interviews is made at the time the test scores are announced, by publicly issuing the successful candidate list which contains information on both IBA test and exemptions because of the SAT score. This list is based on prior information about the SAT Scores.  

 

3.3       The Registrar said that the response from other two members on the Admission Committee was as under:

 

“An applicant who has committed himself / herself for the written aptitude test stream of entry into IBA and then fails the aptitude test should be allowed to appear in the interview on the basis of the SAT Score. The reason is very simple, many applicants who are allowed to participate in the interview processes on the basis of SAT score will not be able to clear IBA test if they were asked to appear in the written aptitude test”.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director said that when the issue of admission on SAT Score / IBA Test was brought to him, he decided to take it to the EC because Executive Committee is the highest decision making body at the management level. He said that as all committees’ minutes’ are signed by him, the ultimate responsibility lies on him, he thought it appropriate to seek EC’s view on this issue. He said that in his opinion it was a very simple case but it has been unnecessarily complicated. He said that it is a matter of ‘either - or’.  If a person has met the threshold of 1900 SAT Score, that person should be allowed exemption from the IBA admission test. But if the candidate appears at the IBA Admission Test in that case he / she does no longer have a choice to fall back on the SAT score if he / she does not clear the IBA Admission test.  As the choice has been explicitly and knowingly exercised in favour of IBA test the consequences of that choice will have to be borne too. The candidate has to exercise his / her judgement right at the beginning whether he/she wants to be allowed exemption on the basis of SAT score or wants to appear in the Aptitude Test.  In case of the latter option the SAT Score will no longer be taken into account.  

 

3.5       Dr. Nasir Touheed referred to the Admission Policy which says, “Qualify the Aptitude Test OR provide proof for exemption from the aptitude test by providing SAT I/SAT II / GMAT / GRE score transcript, whichever is applicable”.  His interpretation was that if a candidate passes our Aptitude Test then there is no issue. However, if a candidate fails in Aptitude Test but submits before interview his SAT Score which meets our eligibility requirement, he should be considered for interview.

 

3.6       The Registrar said that one of the most important sources from which they draw merit is the documentation. He asked if the name of a candidate is not in the list of qualified candidates and is also not mentioned as exempted due to required SAT score, how can the name of a particular candidate be included at the time of interview.

 

3.7       The Dean & Director said that if the Admission Policy Dr. Nasir has referred to is creating semantic problem, then it can be changed to amplify as “every candidate at the time of applying for admission at IBA will exercise option that either his or her SAT score will be considered for admission purpose or he / she will appear at the Aptitude Test whichever he / she prefers”.      

 

3.8       Dr. Hisham bin Zubair said that a candidate with 1900+ SAT score fails in Aptitude Test, he after some time in the next round of admission becomes eligible for admission because of his required SAT score. He wanted to know as to how it would make any qualitative difference.  The Dean & Director said that the composition of the cohorts against whom he was competing has changed, and a new cohort has come in its place.  The student has now exercised the option of exemption from SAT score by disclosing his / her choice explicitly at the time of applying for admission.   

 

3.9       After a detailed discussion the issue was put to vote by the EC members whether they were in favour of allowing an applicant, who has committed himself / herself for the written aptitude test stream of entry into IBA and then fails the aptitude test, to appear in the interview on the basis of the required SAT Score.  Majority of the EC members did not approve it.

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Committee decided that a candidate will be asked to exercise the options either to appear at the IBA Aptitude test or claim exemption on the basis of SAT score at the time of filling in the application for admission. Once he / she has appeared and failed at the IBA Aptitude Test and subsequently produces SAT score for claiming exemption at the time of interview that person would not be admitted to IBA in that semester.

 

3.11     The Committee decided that in order to remove semantic problem, the admission policy be amplified as “every candidate at the time of applying for admission at IBA will exercise option that either his or her SAT score be considered for admission purpose or he/she will appear at the IBA Aptitude Test”.     

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve the time extension of electrical infrastructure works.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the project of Electrical Infrastructure works was awarded to M/s. National Engineering Company on July 10, 2012 however, due to value engineering the project remained on hold and started on December 15, 2012.  The project was to be completed in six months i.e. by June 14, 2013.  However, due to the following reasons the project could not be completed on time and therefore approval for an extension of 8½ months i.e. till February 28, 2014 is required:

 

A)    Design Review

 

Due to design review and observation of M/s. NESPAK on design, all the technical submittals were kept on hold by the Consultants (M/s. KP Associates).  After detailed review meetings by IBA with NESPAK and Consultants, a consensus had to be developed wherein it was decided that the design by KP Associates was their responsibility and that NESPAK’s observations were kept on record.  KP Associates confirmed that their design philosophy will remain unchanged. In these meetings opinions of IBA Maintenance Department were also solicited who agreed to the design of KP Associates.  Based on above consensus, IBA issued instructions to Projects Managers for execution of works as per the design finalized by KP Associates. Due to this hold up 45 valuable days were lost.  NESPAK also recommended extension time of 45 days for this delay.

 

B)    Delay due to Construction of Power Centre

 

As the Power Centre construction was dependant on finalization of the Electrical Infrastructure Work, its work had to remain on hold for 182 days, as confirmed by NESPAK. Due to this reason the LV2 room (Low Voltage -2 Room) and substation building were also not ready and contractor was asked not to bring the equipment.

 

C)    Delays caused due to additional works of trenches because of site conditions

                                   

Six days

 

D)    Delays caused due to Law & Order situation

                                

This delay was caused due to General Election and the strikes on different occasions. The Contractors claimed 50 days extension due to above reason. NESPAK has only recommended 13 days.

                      

4.2       The Director Projects stated that against total of 532 days because of all above, NESPAK has recommended 246 days extension with effect from June 14, 2013. The revised date of completion works shall therefore be 28th February 2014.

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on 28th September 2013 considered the matter and after due deliberations approved it and recommended to the Executive Committee for their consideration and approval. He clarified that there will be cost impact due to this delay because of Rupee – Dollar Parity as well as the extension to the Consultants and the Project Managers. He informed that endeavour is being undertaken that the cost be contained as far as possible with the help of the Consultants and the Project Managers.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till 28th February, 2014.

 

4.4       The Dean & Director said that he could not understand as to why power station design has changed.  The Director Projects said that earlier eight generators were being placed but because of value engineering, eight generators are not to be placed but they were asked to make space available for eight generators as in future eight generators may be needed for 100% back up and we have to provide full load because ACs do not work and faculty members demand AC. The Dean & Director said that although he has no problem for providing space in the Power Station but eight generators are not needed. He said that there are many universities where there is no AC. He said that he had said several times that Air Conditioning will be for essential equipments only such as ICT servers, switches, UPSs and laboratory equipment. Moreover, there is no load shedding from KESC for IBA. The Registrar said that he has arranged pedestal fans which can be used in case KESC supply is not available and power is supplied through backup generators. The Dean and Director said that if the extension is required till February 28, 2014, it means the power will not be available by December 2013. The Director Projects said that in December 2013 Sub-Station-I (SS-I) will be energized. At the request of the Registrar he said that he would try to energize the Sub-Station-I by end of November 2013.

 

4.5       The Dean & Director observed that KP Associates have given the design which is being executed by the Contractor and supervised by the Project Manager so why KP Associates are to be given extension. The Director Projects said that as per contract they can give one month’s notice to KP Associates provided NESPAK is ready to take over the responsibility after consultation with his Chief Engineer Electrical, if they would own the KPA design on which they had certain reservations. There may be a possibility that NESPAK may not own the design of KP Associates.  Engr. Abdul Mateen also said that it is the Consultant’s responsibility to check and approve the submittals of all the equipments and also witness and certify all the submittals. 

 

4.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that there may be audit observation for giving extension in completion time and payment of fee to the Consultant and Project Managers. The Dean & Director said that although there is a clause of force majure under which circumstances beyond control can be covered, we cannot afford to spend additional 3 to 4 million rupees on account of fee for the extended period. He said that he would have a meeting with Khalid Pervez of KP Associates.  He also asked Engr. Mateen to talk to his MD in this regard.

 

DECISION

 

4.7       The Committee approved time extension for completion of electrical infrastructure works for 8.5 months i.e. till February 28, 2014.

 

4.8       The issue of payment of fee to the Consultant and Project Manager was deferred.

 

ACTION

 

4.9       The Director Projects would arrange a meeting of Engr. Khalid Pervez of KP Associates with the Dean & Director.  

 

4.10     Engr. Abdul Mateen of NESPAK would propose their supervision fee for the extended period after discussing it with their MD.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
