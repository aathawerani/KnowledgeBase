MINUTES OF 159TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

MONDAY, JUNE 27, 2011

 

The 159th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 27, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                     Controller of Examinations

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

6.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                              Director H.R.

7.      Mr. Moeid Sultan, G.M (Finance)               Nominee of D.F.

 

Dr. Hisham-bin-Zubair, Assistant Professor attended on special invitation.

 

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider the request of the students who forgetfully missed their Final Exams.

 

1.1              At the advice of Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Dr. Hisham-bin-Zubair, Assistant Professor presented the case of a student who forgetfully missed her final exams.  He reported that a student of BBA (Manal Zubair), who is registered in his course, Calculus 1, missed her final exam on Saturday (18.06.2011) thinking that it would commence during 14:00 – 17:00 hours, while the right time was 09:30 a.m. – 12:30 p.m. He reported that she came running to him in distress at around 11:30 a.m. and asked to be allowed to sit for the remaining time of the exam. He said that when he contacted the Controller of Examinations to know if she could be allowed to sit in the exam, the Controller of Examinations provisioned that she may be allowed the remaining time if (and only if), none of the students have left the exam room. However, as a few students had left by then, this provision could not be exercised. He further reported that a similar happening had occurred exactly one day earlier with another BBA student (Laraib Zehra) in the Financial Modeling exam given by Ms. Sana Fatima.

 

1.2       Dr. Hisham wanted to know if there was any provision via which the students can escape a bad grade owing to a zero in the final exam because both the students are “A” graders in his Calculus course (Ms. Manal has scored 59 out of 60). He also requested to review the policy.

 

1.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that Ms. Manal is not the only student, there are two other students who also missed the final examinations. He said that the policy and practice so far has been that no make-up examination was allowed under these circumstances. He said that in rules and regulations there should not be any exemption.

 

1.4       The Registrar referring to the policy of Make-up examination said that under normal circumstances no make-up examination is allowed for missing term or semester final examination. However, on medical grounds in extremely serious cases, authenticated by recognized hospitals, the committee may consider to allow make-up in term exams only and that too for only one of the two term examinations in a semester. No make-up of semester final exam is allowed on medical grounds of any kind.  He agreed that they may review the policy but regretted that under the existing policy there is no such provision.

 

1.5       Dr. Hisham said that the students he is recommending for, have never been careless; they were very regular and punctual.  He requested that IBA should not penalize them to that extent.

 

1.6       The Director HR said that these are parts of students grooming and they have to bear the consequences of even such honest mistakes.

 

1.7       Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that they may think of extending the scope of giving “I” (Incomplete) under such circumstances but that, if approved by the Academic Board, would be applicable for the cases to come in the future.

 

1.8       Dr. Sayeed Ghani thanked Dr. Hisham for showing concern for the students and presenting their cases in the EC in person. However, he said that since it is totally a fault of the student because the student was forgetful, and there is no issue of compassion, he would not recommend for make-up.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee agreed with Dr. Sayeed Ghani that it was a fault of the student and in view of the fact that the existing policy does not allow make up examinations under such circumstances, the request was not acceded to.

 

ACTION

 

1.10     Dr. Hisham would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to the concerned student.

 

 

Item No. 2      Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that in compliance with the EC decision to hold a meeting with the Consultant, they convened a meeting just after the EC meeting with Mr. Irfan Azad, one of the Data centre designers of M/s ICON. The meeting was held in the presence of Dr Arshad Siddiqi and Mr. M Faisal Zia of ICT department and M/s. Rehan A. Riaz and Mehmood Bughio from the Projects department. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi asked Mr. Azad if the design of Data Centre complied with the requirement of the certification criteria as available on the internet. To this Mr. Irfan replied that he had designed the HVAC part of the data centre based on the tier 3 criteria. He however pointed out that the certification criteria points as mentioned by the Director ICT are not available on the internet as they are different for different certifying organizations. That may vary in number. It could be 2600 or 2400. He added that they only give the points once they are engaged and their fee is in the range of 50 to 60 thousand dollars. After detailed discussion it was agreed that all the team members of the designers must have a meeting with the Director ICT on 21st June 2011. Accordingly, another meeting was held on June 21, 2011 which was attended by the following but the meeting ended inconclusively:

 

o   Dr. Arshad Siddiqi, Director ICT and Task Manager, Data Centre.

o   Mr. M. Faisal Zia, Sr. Mgr ICT

o   Mr. Mansoor, Manager Data Network

o   Engr. Rehan A. Riaz, Director Projects

o   Engr. Umar Makhdoomi, Project Manager IBA , City Campus

o   Mr. Hanif Daud, Principal Architect ICON

o   Mr. Noshad Ahmad Senior, Architect ICON

o   Mr. Rukhshan, Electrical Consultants

o   Mr. Irfan Azad, HVAC Consultants

 

2.2       The Director Projects requested that if an independent consultant is to be appointed it should be done without any further delay. He said that the Consultant was to be appointed by 19th March (revised to 3rd April) as per the time line. It is now delayed by 85 days.

 

2.3       The Director Projects said that the Projects department had completed the design and tender documents through M/s ICON who were the consultants of Administration building City Campus in complete form and issued to the prospective bidders after approval of the tenders by ICT. ICT had given a go ahead to ICON to design the data centre of Main Campus. He said that if they were not satisfied with their work on City Campus data centre then why a go ahead was given to them to design the main campus data centre.  He wanted to know if they should retain ICON as the data centre consultants or should they conclude their contracts on both the data centres.

 

2.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the EC is not aware of the concerns of the ICT towards the tender documents and since the Director ICT is not present in the meeting he suggested that this item should be deferred.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee deferred this item and requested the Director ICT to specifically define the shortcomings they find in the tender documents for the Data Centre and present them to the EC in its next meeting.

 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider request from Mr. Amir Reza Husseini, a student of BBA-II.

 

3.1       The Registrar reported that Mr. Amir Reza Husseini, a student of BBA-II has sent an email requesting the EC to give him the chance of taking the exams of three papers at some other time as he could not take exams due to emergency situation (threatening phone calls) and had to leave the country overnight.  The Registrar said that in support of his claim he had sent a copy of the letter his father had submitted at the CPLC and a copy of the Passport showing exit from Jinnah International Airport, Karachi. 

 

3.2       The Registrar said that he personally feels that in the email and in the letter to CPLC there was no mention of the ground because of which he had to leave Pakistan.  Mr. Khalili also agreed that the email and the letter are insufficient proofs in support of the claim.

 

3.3       Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested obtaining further details / evidence from Mr. Husseini’s family members if they are in Karachi.

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee decided that as no concrete evidence was available to justify the necessity for Mr. Husseini to leave the country in emergency, and in the absence of any provision in the rules for make-up examinations for semester final, the request of Mr. Husseini cannot be presently entertained.

 

ACTION

 

3.5       The Committee requested the Registrar to convey the decision of the EC to Mr. Husseini and ask Mr. Husseini to provide any other concrete evidence related to his case, if available for further scrutiny by the EC.

 

 

b)     To consider email sent by Ms. Sana Khan about an IBA related article placed on The Express Tribune Blog.

 

3.6       The Registrar said that Mr. Bilal Asad, reportedly a student of IBA, placed an article on The Express Tribute Blog the summary of which was that during the last few days of his BBA programme, his father fell extremely ill and passed away. His father’s illness and his second set of midterms clashed. So he missed three exams because his father lay unconscious in the Intensive Care Unit. When he requested the management at IBA to let him retake his exams, he was not allowed even after submitting all medical certificates and applications. He reportedly met with many office bearers – lowest to highest. Each conversation started with a condolence and ended with an apology. They could do nothing.

 

3.7       The EC members said that this case was not brought to their knowledge earlier. Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested to approach the concerned student to know what were the specific circumstances and to whom had he approached.  However, he clarified that under such circumstances where the father or mother or near relative of a student is in hospital, the policy neither allows the student extra absences nor any make-up exam.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee requested the Registrar to look into the matter and obtain facts from the concerned student.     

 

 

c)      To consider email from Mr. Muhammad Asif, faculty member, regarding Mr. Muhammad Saad, a student of IBA who is reportedly leaving IBA.

 

3.9       Mr. Muhammad Asif in his email reported that one of his “A” grade students whose GPA is 3.5 plus is leaving IBA for the reason that he cannot afford IBA expenses and he has been refused scholarships either on merit or on deserving basis.

 

3.10     The Registrar reported that Mr. Muhammad Saad sent his first email on January 10, 2011 requesting for approval to pay fees of Spring Semester 2011 in five equal instalments. His request for instalment was immediately approved by the Director Finance. Then in his second email on February 28, 2011 Mr. Saad requested for grant of scholarship, but the scholarship application which is the tool for calculation of need level did not accompany his request. Since the date of receipt of application forms for review of scholarship cases had already passed, he was advised to immediately apply for the National Bank of Pakistan (NBP) loan facility which was available at that time and could have been arranged for him by IBA but he failed to respond to this offer; therefore he was advised to apply for Scholarship in May 2011. Even then no application was submitted by him. Instead he went to the Dean & Director to say that he could not continue his studies. The Dean & Director obtained the facts and then sent an email to Mr. Muhammad Saad on May 22, 2011 advising him to submit his application to the Financial Aid Officer for assessment of his need level and placing of his case before the scholarship committee for consideration and possible award of scholarship in the forthcoming academic year. The Dean & Director had also conveyed to him that filling the application form is a prerequisite for considering his case for award of scholarship. He was also advised to keep a check on the notice board every now and then, as notices are displayed at all notice boards of both campuses of IBA whenever any new scholarship is announced. 

 

3.11     The Registrar further informed the Committee that he had suggested to Mr. Saad that he would get his fee deferred till his Convocation but Mr. Saad did not agree to this.

 

3.12     Mr. Moeid Sultan said that students are repeatedly advised to apply for scholarships through notices.

 

DECISION

 

3.13     The Committee noted the factual position as presented by the Registrar.

 

 

d)     Retaining a retiring Messenger through MOP

 

3.14     The Registrar reported that four IBA messengers would be retiring this year.  He said that he had discussed about the hiring of some messengers in replacement of the retiring messengers but the Dean & Director was not prepared to increase head count. However, he had suggested that if needed a messenger may be hired through MOP Cleaning and Janitorial Services. The Registrar requested that since Mr. Yousuf Khan is working very efficiently, he may be retained through MOP for a year.

 

3.15     The Director H.R. observed that if exemption is given in one case, many other retiring people will approach for such exemption.

 

3.16     The Registrar clarified that Mr. Yousuf Khan would no longer be an IBA employee; he would be on the payroll of MOP but at their request he would serve at the faculty floor of IBA Main Campus. So there is no issue of exemption in policy. He further said that he had discussed the possibility of retaining some retiring janitorial staff as well through MOP but MOP regretted to accommodate them.

 

3.17     Mr. Mobin Khalili observed that outsourced persons may not be so committed as compared to the regular employees. Therefore, he suggested that a regular vacated post should be filled by a regular appointment. He did not agree to the proposed retention of a Messenger through MOP. In his opinion if any lower cadre employee is in good health and his service record is excellent he should be allowed to continue on contract after retirement otherwise a proper replacement should be provided.

 

DECISION

 

3.18     The Committee noted and approved the retention of Mr. Yousuf Khan, as Messenger through MOP.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
