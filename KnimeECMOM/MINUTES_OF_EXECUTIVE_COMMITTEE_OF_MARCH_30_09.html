Many of them submitted the required documents and certificates and validity proof of their PEC certification. Item No. 3      To consider and approve construction of:

            a)   Washrooms and pantry for ground floor temporary offices.

3.1              The Director Projects stated that the ground floor offices which will be occupied by about twenty officers and staff need to have at least one washroom for ladies and one washroom for gents. He said that the existing tea preparation and dispensing facilities are totally inadequate even for the first and second floors. As such, he said that a provision of a small pantry is being made at the ground floor. He said that the total area of the proposed temporary facility will be approximately 144 sq.ft and the estimated cost shall be Rs.72,000/- (144 sft @Rs.500 per sft.). He also presented the proposed drawing for washrooms and pantry. 

3.2              The Director Projects further said that the pantry will be equipped with a tea dispenser either from Tapal or Brooke Bond and one similar tea dispenser would be provided on the first floor.  He requested for approval for the tea dispenser.

DECISION

3.3              The Committee approved the construction of two washrooms; one for gents and one for ladies and a pantry as suggested by the Director Projects at an estimated cost of Rs.72,000/- (144 sq.ft @ Rs.500/- per sq.ft.).

3.4              The Committee requested the Director Projects to request Tapal / Brooke Bond for installing tea dispensers which they usually provide free of cost.

b)         Spaces for gardeners, drivers, electricians, janitors, and washroom facilities

3.5       The Director Projects informed the Committee that gardeners, drivers, electricians and janitorial staff do not have proper places and storage facilities for their tools and plants. At the same time the existing washroom facilities are too unhygienic.  He proposed to build a temporary facility with adequate washroom facilities for these staff. He said that the total area shall be approximately 1138 sq.ft and the estimated cost would be Rs.295,880/- (1138 sqft @ Rs.260/- per sqft.). He also presented the proposed drawing for the temporary facilities.

3.6     The Director advised the Director Projects to arrange spaces and washroom facilities on permanent and not on temporary basis.  He said that minimum temporary facilities should be constructed. The Director Projects suggested that the permanent facility can be created besides the under-construction multipurpose building.

DECISION

3.7       The Committee did not approve the proposal of the Director Projects for construction of spaces for gardeners, drivers, electricians, janitors and washroom facilities and advised the Director Projects to construct these facilities besides the multipurpose building.

            c)   Construction of offices for maintenance staff at the City Campus

3.8       The Director Projects stated that at present there is no office space available to maintenance engineer and his staff at the City Campus. They do not have any phone and even a cabinet to store their documents and tools & plants. They are operating from a small table located in a verandah outside the auditorium.

3.9       The Director Projects proposed to construct temporary offices for the maintenance staff consisting of two rooms of 10'x10', wash room 6'x10'and a meeting room of about 19'x10'. The temporary offices will be adequately equipped with furniture, phone line, data line etc. the total approximate area shall be 460 sq.ft. and the estimated cost shall be Rs.161,000/- (460 sq.ft @Rs.350 per sq.ft.).  Furniture cost shall be of approx Rs.100,000/-

DECISION

3.10          The Director said that he wants offices to be constructed on permanent basis and the new Administration Block will have rooms for such people.  He advised the Director Projects to get a room at APWA facilities till such time permanent offices in new Administration Block are ready. The Committee agreed with the Director.

ACTION

3.11          The Director Projects would arrange a room at APWA facilities which are being rented by IBA.

Item No. 4      To consider note from G.M.(P&D) regarding renting APWA Auditorium.

4.1       The G.M. (P&D) stated that IBA plans to rent APWA premises as a part of preparations for construction of two multi-storey buildings at the City Campus. The premises include APWA’s auditorium with a capacity of about 300 seats. He informed the Committee that as a result of their negotiations, APWA has agreed to rent out the auditorium to IBA at a monthly rent of Rs.25,000/- (Rs.300,000/- per year). In addition, he said that we will bear the running and maintenance costs of the diesel engine generator that supplies power exclusively to the auditorium. There is no KESC supply. Besides, we will be responsible for security also. He said that our rental agreement will be for at least three years. It will be extendable with mutual consent.  He further said that APWA will not give the auditorium to anyone else when they rent it out to IBA.

4.2       He requested the Executive Committee to approve the arrangement and the costs involved.

4.3              The Director said that he did not want to increase expenditure on a building which was going to be demolished and as such would not spend 3 to 4 lacs rupees on the existing auditorium. 

DECISION

4.4              The Committee approved the proposal of acquiring auditorium of APWA at a monthly rent of Rs.25,000/- for three years period.

ACTION

4.5       The G.M. (P&D) will sign agreement for acquiring the auditorium of APWA on rent.

d)  To consider note from Director I.T. regarding Tender for Technology Partnership Agreement / Long Term Agreement.

8.13     The Director I.T. stated that the IBA is at the point where we are going to purchase lot of IT and telecom equipment and software. He said that we have already pre-qualified vendors for these, however as the technology is changing very rapidly, it is not enough to rely on the local vendors. He suggested that IBA should get into Technology Partnerships and Long Term Agreements with companies which can provide us “tomorrow’s technology today” and keep us abreast with the future technologies. He said that we need technology partners who can provide us the complete solutions encompassing of: IT Equipment; Servers; Storages and Tape Libraries; Laptops, PCs, Thin clients; peripherals, monitoring systems through security cameras, sensors, RFID etc.; printers, projectors, scanners, copiers, etc., Telecom Equipment, Routers, Switches, Telephony equipment, Cabling, Data Center /DRC Equipment, Consultancy and implementation etc.; Service and Maintenance, Green Technology providers, Power monitoring and future planning.

8.14     The Director I.T. proposed that we should sign Technology Partnership Agreements (TPA) with strong IT companies who are “Premium Business Partners” of original equipment manufacturers at the level of “Authorized Service, Delivery Partners (ASDP)”. We should also sign Long Term Agreements (LTA) with original equipment manufacturers like IBM, HP, Siemens, Microsoft, Intel etc. In LTA the purchase order is placed directly with the manufacture and their business partners import the equipment in the name of IBA, this would save about 21% import duties and GST. The local partner clears the consignment from customs and delivers it to IBA. We would also sign University Alliance Program (UAP) Agreements with the equipment manufacturers, thus get up to 70% discounts.

8.15     The Director I.T. said that the net effect for IBA will be:

·         Heavy discounts on equipment/software purchases

·         Procurement of latest technology

·         Insight of future technologies as IBA would be on the forums of the technology road  map of the equipment manufacturers

·         One window shopping

·         Buy back option

8.16     The Director I.T. also demonstrated the latest PC provided by HP which has not yet been marketed in Pakistan.

8.17     To avail the above mentioned benefits, the Director I.T. requested the EC to allow him to float a tender for TPA and LTA.

DECISION

8.18     The Committee allowed the Director I.T. to float tender for Technology Partnership Agreement and Long Term Agreement.

ACTION

8.19     The Director I.T. would float tender for Technology Partnership Agreement and Long Term Agreement.
