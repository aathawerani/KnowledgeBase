Item No.1       To consider Scholarship for ACF Students (Fall 2013) and SSLA Students.

 

1.1        Dr. Nasir Touheed suggested that in order to bring at par with other top 10% students, the top 10% students of BS Accounting & Finance should also be given merit scholarship of Rs 61,500 at present. Similar merit scholarship should also be given to BS (CS, EM, SSLA) students. Only then every student of top 10% category will be paying Rs.76,500 as tuition fee (which is the bench mark set for BBA program). He further said that in Computer Science we have allowed scholarship to top 10% of new intake but those in second year have not been allowed the top 10% Scholarships.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that the marketing principle is that when you launch a new product, you give discount, the same was practiced at IBA. The situation changed for BS-A&F as the demand far exceeded the supply. Hence the tuition fee was raised by 30% to bring it at par with that of BBA.  He clarified that the fee of existing students of BS-CS and BS Social Sciences has been increased only by 10% and thus still contains an element of subsidy relative to BBA and BS-A&F.

1.4       The Registrar said that the students were informed at the orientation that since we have introduced the new program we have kept the fee low.  He was of the view that we can give scholarship to top 8 to 10% students of BS-A&F but not to BS-CS.

1.5       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Moeid Sultan to examine the financial implications on allowing scholarships to top 10% students of BS-A&F only.

Item No. 4      To consider dual appointments issue in IBA.

 

4.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the dual appointment of IBA non-teaching employees within IBA for different externally financed projects is an informal arrangement that has existed in IBA for quite some time. However, the appointment of the employees for this purpose has never been officially routed through HR Department.  The matter was highlighted by the Internal Audit Department, which suggested the development of a formal policy in this regard. He said that there are 3 different types of dual appointments of IBA non-teaching employees as outlined below:

 

I) Employees' Appointment in Research Projects:

 

IBA employees are working part time in research projects and paid fixed remuneration on monthly and / or on variable remuneration on assignment basis out of the project funds. These remunerations are not reflected in monthly payroll of employees.

 

II) Involvement of Non-teaching Employees in Teaching Assignments:

 

Few IBA non-teaching employees are engaged in teaching in IBA regular programs, short term programs such as English Language / NTHP & SFP as well as in different training programs organized with the support of HEC. None of these staff members have gone through the prescribed process for faculty recruitment, which is required for appointment of visiting faculty, whereas they are receiving remuneration based on the rates applicable to the Visiting Faculty.

 

III) Special Purpose Appointments:

 

In this case, services of employee are hired by another department for completion of special assignment, which can vary in terms of its duration.  For example, an evening shift employee is requested by another department to help them in their work during morning hours and such employees are compensated through overtime for the work done in the morning shift.

 

4.2        Mr. Adnan Hameed said that involvement of employees in such projects causes the           following issues:

 

-   Ensuring / monitoring that there is no conflict of interest between IBA and the project, in terms of use of official time and resources.

 

-   Equitable distribution of such opportunities among eligible employees.

 

-   Absence of a designated authority to approve such appointments.

 

-   Absence of any proper recruitment and remuneration revision processes; raising questions on the basis of selection of individuals for these assignments.

 

-   Heartburning among those who are not selected / given / considered and even not eligible for such assignments; hence negatively affecting the work environment.

 

-   Diversion of employees' interest and focus which is likely to move from his/her own job towards additional assignment (extra remuneration). Consequently, lapses/delays in official position responsibilities occur.

 

4.3        Mr. Adnan Hameed made the following suggestions for consideration of the EC:

 

a.      The first type of appointments should not be allowed. However, in case the EC wants to consider this, fair and transparent recruitment process should be devised and followed.

 

b.      Staff members should not be engaged in teaching assignments, without going through the recruitment process for faculty, particularly for the regular degree programs. Different methods such as different rates, limit on number of courses, compulsion of completing office hours, can be considered for this purpose. However, teaching in short courses, training programs can be allowed for employees in Managerial and above levels.

 

c.       Under type 3 appointments, only those employees who are eligible for overtime can be assigned such responsibilities; with the mutual consent of the concerned HODs and Managers.  Duration of such assignments and number of times an employee can be engaged in such assignment should be capped.

 

4.4       There was discussion about the pros and cons of allowing non-teaching staff to engage in remunerative duties in addition to their regular duties. There was no support for the idea of engagement in research projects.

 

4.5       In so far as the training programs organized with the support of HEC,it was suggested by the Dean and Director that if a non-teaching staff is asked to conduct the training because of his / her professional excellence there should be no payment.  He said that he had given permission to Mr. Imran Batada to teach but in retrospect he realized that it was not a right decision.

 

4.6        Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that for Point No.I noted above, we should check what other universities do.  Mr. Adnan Hameed said that he has checked it. He said that it is allowed at AERC but we cannot follow it at IBA because at AERC the number of staff involved in research projects consisted of two persons while here our Finance Department has   more than a dozen such staff who can do this kind of work and transparent distribution of assignments would simply be not possible.

 

4.7       The Dean & Director said that in Research Projects we can involve the students under Work Study Program. This would help those students who are otherwise not eligible to receive scholarships / stipends on need based criteria but are unable to afford the full burden of fees.

 

DECISION

 

4.8       The Committee decided that non-teaching staff would not be allowed additional remuneration for helping with the research projects. In case any one wishes to teach a regular class or training course, he / she shall do it without being compensated.

 

Item No.5       Proposed coverage of Hepatitis C treatment of dependents in OPD setting.

 

5.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that in Health Insurance Policy outpatient for Hepatitis is covered but in IBA Medical Policy only hospitalization is covered. He said that the recommendation is that treatment of Hepatitis as outpatient should be included in the medical policy. He sought clarification if dependents should be included or not in the outpatient treatment for Hepatitis. 

 

DECISION

 

5.2       The Committee decided that the employees will be provided treatment for Hepatitis as outpatient. However, in case of treatment for Hepatitis for dependents as outpatient, the cases would be presented to the EC and decided on case to case basis.
