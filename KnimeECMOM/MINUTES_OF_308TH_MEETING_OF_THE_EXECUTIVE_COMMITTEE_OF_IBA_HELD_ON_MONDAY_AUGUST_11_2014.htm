MINUTES OF 308TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

The 308th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 11, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA

Item No.1 a) Status Reports from:

Director Projects:

1.1 The Director Projects to reduce the area and the per sft cost so that the transport facilities building is completed within Rs.5 million: The Director Projects reported that the revised requirements were given to the Architects. The Architects would give the revised design towards the end of this month.

Registrar:

1.7 The Registrar to obtain estimate for the wire fencing: The Registrar reported that the work approved by CPC in its meeting on August 9, 2014. The tender has been floated and the work is expected to commence in 6-8 weeks time.

1.13 Library: The Director Projects said that they are trying their best to complete the library by the end of September 2014.

1.14 Multipurpose Building (Facade): The Director Projects said that they have to shift the old ACs but the tubes and wirings of the old ACs are not useable. They need new wires, cables, tubes and new ACs. The Contractor was demanding Rs.1.7 million for the work. He said that he had invited Mr. Zayd Bilgrami and it was decided that the ACs should be left on the ground floor. However, for the silbatta wall on the Northern side of the building, there would be additional cost. He said that the Architects are redesigning the Northern Wall to accommodate the shifting of the split air-conditioners outer units. He said that there would be cost over-run and he cannot give any time frame. He said that he had suggested that instead of making the wall, plants can be placed to cover the wall but the Architect did not agree.

The Registrar said that he had pointed out a year ago that ACs cannot be shifted because the design was wrong. He said that the ACs are working properly there. He suggested not to disturb the existing fittings and the wall towards Northern side of the MP Building. He suggested that in view of the very high cost which is likely to be incurred in designing and making of the Northern wall of the MP Building, it should remain as it is. Only color coding may be done. He further said that plants may be planted to hide the wall.

Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know as to how it would look like as it is to be shown to the donor. The Registrar said that the Northern wall is not in the viewing area. Dr. Mohammad Nishat suggested that we should also keep the expectation of the donor in mind. He asked if there would be any inconsistency. The Registrar suggested to visit the site and then take a decision.

The members Executive Committee visited the site and were of the view that the final decision on re-doing of the Northern wall should be taken by the Dean & Director as it requires additional amount.

1.5 Old Mosque: The Director Projects said that they would assign the work of old mosque to the Admin. Building contractor as a variation order. Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that instead of issuing variation order, if we get a donor who could be requested to make direct payment to the Contractor then there would be no issue. The Registrar said that it is not so urgent. He requested the Director Projects to focus on the pathways towards the mosque and the library so that it is completed before the Convocation. The mosque can be taken at a later point of time. The Committee agreed with the Registrar.

1.6 Entry – Exit Gates: The Director Projects said that the estimated cost of the entry-exit gates is Rs.20 million. He said that this includes breaking of the existing boundary wall and re-building according to the new design. However, he said that he had asked not to break the wall. He said that they can get the three gates completed at a cost of Rs.10 million. He said that if they place tender it would take time but if he gets approval of placing variation order in the external development work, it can be done within three months. However, he informed the Committee that they have already charged Rs.10.5 million i.e. 12.5% million to External Development and now they can avail 2.5% only which is around Rs.3 million. The Registrar suggested to get one gate i.e. Gate # 4 ready before convocation. He said that this can be done within 15% of the cost. He further said that Gate # 4 is the first gate from Maskan and it also serves the ring road.

Item No. 2 To consider and approve the Variation Orders for FCS Building at City Campus.

2.1 The Director Projects reported that the work for Renovation and Refurbishment of FCS Building Phase-I was awarded to M/s Prefab at a cost of Rs.61.0 million. Later it was decided by the Executive Committee to award the additional works of Phase-II of FCS Building to same contractor in its meeting held on November 28, 2011. The cost of both the phases was approved as Rs.70.00 million in Executive Committee meeting held on April 02, 2012. The Executive Committee also approved an additional cost of Rs.3.00 million for same contractor as variation order. The total cost of Rs.73 million was approved by the Executive Committee in its 209th meeting respectively for the same contractor for the works on Renovation and Refurbishment of FCS building.

2.2 After approval of above cost the work was executed as per the design. During the execution at the request of clients several additional works were also executed the detail of which was presented. The cost of additional work done during the execution is Rs.6.348 million which is 8.6% of the total approved cost of Rs.73.00 million for the Renovation and Refurbishment works of FCS building at City Campus.

2.3 The Director Projects reported that the Procurement Committee was requested to approve the additional works of Rs.6.348 million which were undertaken and completed on emergent basis during execution as they were required by the users as well as pointed out by the ICT and Maintenance Department. The Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 9th August 2014 advised that the project cost can only be taken as Rs. 61 million and completion cost be taken as Rs.79.348 million as such the variation comes to 30%. Out of this 19.91% has already been approved by the Executive Committee in their respective meetings. He said that the Procurement Committee further advised that Executive Committee may consider approval of the 10.09 % variation in the original contract cost.

2.4 The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Projects Department had already submitted the cost of Project as Rs.79.120 million to the Executive Committee in its meeting held on April 2, 2012. The Director Projects explained that the project team at site considered it advisable to take up the HVAC works of Phase-I and Phase-II in continuation of the on-going work with the idea that the same cost may be absorbed through value engineering. He said that this was achieved successfully and the HVAC works of Rs.8.1 million were completed within Rs.70.515. This was done through value engineering of on-going works. At that time the quantity of any extra works was not visualized as the work was under execution. He requested the Executive Committee to consider the fact that the separate tenders for HVAC were not floated in the interest of the project but the amount of Rs.8.1 million was saved from the total approved amount of Rs.79.12 million. As such the extra amount of Rs.6.304 million may be considered as cost over-run on project cost of Rs.73.454 million because HVAC works cost was saved. He requested the Executive Committee to consider the additional variation of 10.09%, considering the fact that the cost of Project has remained within the vicinity of Rs.79 million.

2.5 Mr. Moeid Sultan said that as per his understanding the 15% is taken on the basis of contract cost and not on the basis of project cost. The contract was for Rs.61 million on which 15% would apply.

DECISION & ACTION

2.6 The members Executive Committee observed that separate tendering of HVAC should have been done by the projects department. Since it was not done for the reasons explained by the Director Projects and that the cost of Rs.8.1 million was absorbed in the cost of all 3 phases, the approval for Rs.6.304 million is to be obtained by the A&FC.

2.7 The Director Projects would obtain approval for the additional cost of Rs.6.304 million from A&FC.


Item No. 3 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) To consider request from Mr. Hammad Ezad, student of Economics and Maths for allowing him to appear in the Summer Semester Final exam.

3.1 The Registrar reported that Mr. Hammad Ezad, a student of Economics and Maths, has requested for a waiver of 4th absence that he had accumulated in the Summer Semester. The student had stated that he was registered in “Principles of Accounting - I” course late by one week. He missed first two classes because of the unavailability of flight on June 22 (due to landing of Tarir-ul-Quadri’s plane). He had requested to allow him to appear in the semester final exam. His teacher has endorsed his statement.

DECISION

3.2 The Committee decided that as per rules no exemptions can be given. The Committee did not accede to the request of Mr. Hammad Ezad.


b) Locking of Attendance Register in ERP.

3.3 Mr. Imran Batada said that Attendance Register is being used by faculty through ERP. The Controller of Examinations has suggested that as we have done customization in grades and once grades are entered in ERP, they cannot be changed, similarly the attendance register should be customized and locked in ERP. Mr. Batada clarified that lock option is available to faculty but nobody is utilizing it. He requested the EC to decide as to when the Attendance Register should be locked in ERP. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to link it with the exams i.e. it should be locked two or three days before the exam. Mr. Batada said that currently it is being done so but it is being misused.

DECISION

3.4 The Committee decided that the locking of Attendance Register in ERP should be done one week after a class.
