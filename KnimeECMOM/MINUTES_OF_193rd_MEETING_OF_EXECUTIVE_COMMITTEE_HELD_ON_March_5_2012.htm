Item No.2       To consider and approve the changes in Procurement Policy & Procedure Manual due to incorporation of SPP Rules.

 

2.1       Mr. Khalid Rashid Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit said that they have modified the IBA Procurement Policy & Procedure in accordance with the SPP Rules 2010 notified by the SPPRA.  He further said that they obtained comments from some of the EC members which have also been incorporated in the manual.  He said that the Dean & Director had suggested to obtain comments from SPPRA which they did, and received some (unofficial) comments.  He said that the final draft covers all SPPRA rules as well as their feedback.

 

2.2       Mr. Jhumra said that IBA is already following the SPPRA rules and audits are also being done according to the SPP Rules. However, there are some structural changes which will have to be done. He referred to the SPP Rule 7 & 8 which states that Central Purchase Committee (CPC) will also be responsible for technical and financial evaluation. This may eliminate the need for a separate Technical Evaluation Committee. Further, CPC members to include officers from the agency or department other than IBA and comprising at least one third of the membership of committee.

 

2.3       The Registrar said that there would be great difficulty in getting people from the agency or department other than IBA on the CPC.  He requested to find out some solution to it or increase the limit of threshold for taking items to CPC, say Rs.1 million or above.

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that we should separate purchase from process, tendering process etc. for example.

 

2.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know if NED and Karachi University are following the SPP Rule referred to above.  Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested to know if KU and NED are following this rule or not and if there is a requirement for a quorum.

 

2.6       Mr. Jhumra said that the SPPRA under Rule 52 has clearly banned negotiations with the bidder having submitted the lowest evaluated bid or with any other bidder, if it is held with the intention of making substantial changes to a contract or obtaining price reductions from the lowest evaluated responsive bidder. However, he said that there are exceptions in two different cases where negotiations are allowed. He explained them in detail to the EC members.

 

2.7       Mr. Jhumra said that another change is in the amount of bid security. The SPP Rule says that the bid security should be in the range of 1% to 5% of bid price, and it has been standardized        at 2.5% of the bidder’s offer in single stage tendering. Accordingly bid security deposit for two-envelope bidding shall be 2.5% of the item purchasing budget, which is to be submitted along with the financial proposal and kept unopened till the financial proposal envelope is sealed.

 

2.8       The Director Projects suggested that instead of percentage, there should be fixed amount.   

 

2.9       Mr. Khalid Jhumra said that in SPP Rule the financial limit for petty purchases is less than Rs.25,000/- and RFQ can be used if cost is below Rs. 100,000/- and above Rs. 25,000/-. 

 

2.10     The Registrar wanted to be clarified if there would be no quotation requirement for items valuing less than Rs.25,000/-   The Head of Internal Audit replied that as per SPP rule 16 (1) (d) for items of less than Rs.25,000/- value no quotation would be required.

 

2.11     Mr. Jhumra said that for procurement of Rs.1 million or more, advertisement on SPPRA website as well as print media (at least 3 daily newspapers English, Urdu, and Sindhi) is required. He said that earlier requirement was of two newspapers only for procurements of Rs. 2 million or more.

 

2.12     Mr. Jhumra highlighted an important change in prequalification process of suppliers and contractors.  He said that prequalification of bidders will be carried out for large and complex works and services, expensive and technically complex equipment, turnkey / design and build, or management contracts.  Provided that pre-qualification may be carried out only for specific procurement contract and shall be applicable only to that particular assignment.  He said that prequalification was previously done annually and not carried out for specific procurement contract. 

 

2.13     Mr. Jhumra said that “Consulting Services” have been defined in detail and some new clauses have been added into it.

 

2.14     Giving details of posting of contract awards on website, Mr. Jhumra said that within 7 days of award of contract, evaluation report and contract agreement have to be made public through hoisting on SPPRA, PPRA and IBA websites provided disclosure is against public interest or national security. He said that this is being done through Director Alumni, Public Affairs & Resource Mobilization.

 

2.15     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that his department cannot do that because the people at SPPRA do not respond; and secondly in the absence of Communication Manager, there would be none to get this done.

 

2.16     The Director Projects said that they get the evaluation report and contract agreement placed on the SPPRA, PPRA and IBA websites through their own (Projects) department staff.

 

2.17     The Committee decided that the Sr. Web Administrator who would be sent such documents for IBA website, would forward the same to the SPPRA, PPRA for placing on their respective websites.  The Director ICT will ensure that this job is done by the Sr. Web Administrator.

 

2.18     The Committee decided that if the EC members have any other comments or observations to make, they would convey directly to the Head of Internal Audit.

 

DECISION

 

2.19     The Committee appreciated the efforts of the Head of Internal Audit and his team in highlighting the changes in the Procurement Policy & Procedure Manual due to incorporation of SPP Rules.

 

2.20     The Committee recommended the changes in the Procurement Policy & Procedure Manual incorporating the observations noted above and any other observation if given by the EC members, to the A&FC for consideration / approval.

Item No.6       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

6.1       The Director ICT reported that in the weekly meeting held on February 28, 2012 he asked M/s. H.K. Shah to explain the reasons for which they are behind the given timelines.  He said that Mr. Zia of M/s H.K. Shah explained that the first three months were totally wasted in defining the roles and responsibilities with M/s. Icon and IBA. The issues were finally resolved after the meeting with the Dean & Director, and H.K. Shah officially started working from end of November 2011. The Director ICT said that he asked HK Shah to submit a timeline chart clearly defining the proposed and actual timelines with genuine reasons for the delays which they did.  The Director ICT presented timeline chart according to which the final completion date will remain the same i.e. June 30, 2012.  The reasons given for the delay in tender bidding period which was to be completed on January 16 but was completed on January 25, 2012 and the Technical Evaluation which was completed on January 18 instead of January 4, 2012, were as under:

 

·         The extra time was taken because the extension was given to the contractors on their special request.

·         The submitted documents from the bidder firms were incomplete.  They were requested to resubmit the missing documents for which 10 extra days were given.

     

6.2       The Director ICT said that as per the revised timeline chart, the selection of vendor would be finalized by CPC and EC on March 16 and 17 respectively and the evaluation report would be placed on the SPPRA and IBA Websites on March 18 (according to the previous timeline it should have been placed on the website by February 12, 2012). The Director ICT said that the kickoff project, completion and handover would be completed in 106 days instead of 135 days. The kick off will be on March 23, 2012 and the project would be completed on June 30, 2012.

 

6.3       The Director ICT said that H.K. Shah has clarified that the time line is indicative to the best of present conditions and may vary because of law and order condition of the city, unforeseen issues in import of different material and approvals from IBA.

 

6.4       The Director ICT further said that the data centre at the City Campus cannot be made operational if the sub-station at the City Campus is not ready.

 

6.5       The Registrar advised the Director ICT to request Mr. Shameem Ahmed to expedite and make the sub-station at City Campus operational as soon as possible. 

 

DECISION

 

6.6       The Committee noted the up-date given by the Director ICT.

 

 

Item No. 7      To consider and approve the return of IBA Notebooks.

 

7.1       The Director ICT reported that a large number of “IBA” branded notebooks that were imported from China by Ora-Tech are still stored in our ICT warehouse. These are now getting obsolete and better branded Notebooks are now available in the market with same or better features for almost the same price. He said that M/s Ora-tech have a large amount of funds stuck in these notebooks, so they have discussed with the manufacturer in China to take them back and replace them with more advanced technology and to make them available to IBA when we ask for them. He said that M/s. Ora-tech are guarantying that these notebooks will not be sold in Pakistani Market, as these are IBA branded. He requested the EC to allow the return of all remaining notebooks to M/s. Ora-tech.

 

7.2       The Registrar suggested that in future they should not get the notebooks with IBA logo.

 

7.3       The Director ICT explained that due to IBA logo no custom duty is paid.

 

DECISION

 

7.4       The Committee approved the return of IBA Notebooks as proposed by the Director ICT.
