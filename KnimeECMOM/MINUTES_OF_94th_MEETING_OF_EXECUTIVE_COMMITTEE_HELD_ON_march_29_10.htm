MINUTES OF THE 94TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MARCH 29, 2010

 

The 94th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 29, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

  7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider request from Director ICT for Post-Facto approval of the hardware delivered by New Horizon Computers in advance.

 

1.1              The Director ICT reported that the ICT department published an advertisement in the newspaper for pre-qualification of their Technology Partners.  The process was started in April 2009. During this period IBA required computers, printers, scanners and toners for their users to carry on their regular duties.  Due to the emergent requirements and to avoid further delay, ICT Department took initiative and talked to some vendors which had applied to IBA for pre-qualification to deliver the hardware on emergency basis. This had the verbal consent of Director IBA. He reported that one of the vendors M/s. New Horizon Computers offered to deliver all these hardware to IBA in advance and agreed for payment after the completion of pre-qualification process.  He said that all the hardware delivered by New Horizon Computers were installed in different departments. To evaluate the prices of M/s. New Horizon Computer, quotations were received from authorized vendors of HP.  A price comparison statement was prepared on the basis of these quotations. He said that M/s. New Horizon Computers not only delivered all the hardware to IBA in advance but also have quoted the lowest prices.  He presented the list of all hardware received at IBA as well as price comparison statement.

 

1.2              The Director ICT further stated that the procedure has been followed but it was in emergency and Mr. Khurram Khalid of Internal Audit Department has advised to get Central Purchase Committee’s approval. 

 

1.3              The Director said that the IBA’s Procurement Policy was under formulation process so it is a legitimate case for approval as these are consumable items and if such items are not arranged, the whole system may come to a halt. However, he asked the Director ICT to provide description and justification for the hardware.

 

DECISION

 

1.4              The Executive Committee cleared the matter and referred it to the Central Purchase Committee.

ACTION

 

1.5              The Director ICT would provide complete details and justification to the CPC and will ensure that proper documentation is done.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider note from Director ICT regarding CMS Payments to HEC.

 

2.1       The Director ICT stated that the HEC had started a program of CMS implementation at various universities. This was intimated to the universities on January 25, 2008, however it was not received by IBA and the ICT department floated a tender for the procurement of CMS. Three vendors responded. However, when HEC found out about it, they contacted IBA and informed us about the HEC policy for the procurement of CMS. The total cost is Rs.10,970,365/- According to the HEC scheme:

 

  • HEC will pay for the license cost

  • HEC will provide the subsidized rate for the implementation cost.

  • HEC will pay for the Implementation by either of the two ways:

    • There is a grant expected from the US Aid

    • HEC will deduct the payment at source from the grant for IBA

 

2.2       However, the Director ICT reported that on December 11, 2009, HEC sent them the invoice for the 25% payment of the implementation for the amount of Rs.1,562,500/-. After his communications with HEC finally they sent them a letter dated 22nd March, 2010 with the following notification: 

 

“I am directed to convey that according to Federal Government of Pakistan advice HEC maintained Assignment Account and it is not practicable to make deductions at source. Therefore, all the concerned universities / institutions who have agreed to implement CMS are requested to arrange the payments against the claims through crossed cheques drawn in favour of Higher Education Commission, Islamabad.”

2.3       The Director ICT said that the Implementation cost of Rs 6,250,000/- was not budgeted as these were to be deducted at source, however now they have to pay this amount. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the payment of the Installation charges of Rs.6,250,000/- in three installments as shown above as and when the invoice is sent to IBA.

 

2.4              The Director said that in the 52nd meeting of the EC held on June 15, 2009, the implementation cost was approved and it was subsequently put up to the A&FC.  He said that the Director ICT’s statement to the CPC meeting held on September 12, 2009 that the HEC would meet the implementation cost was inaccurate as the budget for ERP included implementation cost.

 

DECISION

 

2.5              The Committee decided that in light of the earlier approval, the first installment for CMS should be made to HEC and the subsequent payments should be made on satisfactory installation and achievements of the milestones.

Item No. 4      To witness presentation by M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative Limited on the proposed façade elements, color and texture of the buildings being designed by them.

 

4.1       M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative Limited presented samples of the façade elements, color and texture of the buildings being designed by them.

 

4.2              The Director said that there should be uniformity in the color, and texture of the façade.

 

4.3       Mr. Bilgrami said that it is difficult to match the exact material because of the nature of job i.e. renovation and new construction but he said that their color can be matched. 

 

4.4       The Director said that it should match with the approved one which was presented by M/s. Icon.  He requested the Director Projects to arrange to send the approved sample to M/s. Najmi Bilgrami.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Director Projects would send the approved sample of the façade to M/s. Najmi Bilgrami.

 

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

5.1              The Director ICT said that he wanted a secured base to store Video Conferencing equipments at both City and Main Campuses. However, he said that as Video Conferencing at City Campus is operational, first priority to be given to City Campus. He suggested that there should be an almirah according to the dimension of the equipment in which equipments can be locked.

 

5.2              The Registrar suggested that for City Campus a glass cubicle can be made at the Video Conferencing room where synthesizers are placed and for Main Campus he said that there is a big cabinet which can be used for storage purpose.

 

5.3              The Director Projects suggested that a meeting of Director ICT, Consultant Mr. Hanif Daud may be arranged to sort it out. He suggested that he would also attend the meeting.

 

DECISION

 

5.4              The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Director Projects and requested him to arrange a meeting accordingly.

 

5.5              The Committee decided that for City Campus one break room may be used for installation of ICT equipments.

  

ACTION

 

5.6              The Director Projects would arrange a meeting of Mr. Hanif Daud with the Director ICT so that the storage of ICT equipments including UPS and batteries and their location may be discussed and finalized.

    

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
