1.9 The Director Projects in coordination with the Registrar would arrange the kitchen/counters for operating four vendors: The Director Projects reported that Physical Construction which was possible is in progress. He said that the Registrar had suggested that there should be a combined exhaust but it is not possible / advisable because if at any stage one or two of the exhausts are closed the energy of the single big fan will be wasted. As such it has been proposed by the Consultants that independent exhaust fans will be provided to provide flexibility of use as well as to conserve the energy.

The Registrar said that he has emailed to the Director Projects on December 1, 2012 the requirements of utilities for storage, preparation, cooking, and service equipment in the kitchens. He said that the counter of the outlet is very high because of which the display of food stuff may not be so prominent. The Director Projects said that he would check the height of the counter and take the standard one. The Registrar suggested that each of the four kitchens must have IP phone facility, points for electronic cash register and gas connection.

Mr.Shameem Ahmed informed the Committee that they are not getting gas from SSGC for the time being. The Registrar said that they have to arrange LPG cylinders if gas is not available. The Registrar requested the Director Projects to arrange conduits for the LPG and electricity load of 35 amps (three phase) for the kitchen equipment.

The Registrar reported that he contacted the vendors including KFC but none of them have committed because running an outlet at IBA is not financially viable for them. Therefore, instead of vendor specific he recommended that the kitchens be generic ones. Each kitchen would be divided into two parts; rear which will be functionary area accommodating de-freezer, storage, bottle cooler and garbage storage, and the serving area i.e. the counter. He felt that bulk cooking cannot be done at the kitchen because of space constraints. The Director Projects said that Mr.ZafarSiddiqui had suggested that all the cooking should be done in the kitchen. He requested the EC to confirm whether bulk cooking would be done in the kitchen or not. He said that the EC had decided that there would be four different vendors with different food items but now EC is told that none of the proposed vendors are coming and now the kitchen would be a generic one, which any food vendor could take and improvise / amend / alter at own cost to meet any specific requirements that the same vendor may have.

The Director Projects gave the example of the properly designed kitchen built by Projects Department in the City Campus based on the requirements of the canteen. Once it was completed the PEPSI bottlers visited and got it completely changed because of which cooking had to be taken out for which a special cooking room was built separately.

The Director Projects stated that the original design of the Students’ Centre Kitchen was changed into 4 kitchens and again the the usage of the four kitchens is not known as no vendor is coming. He further pointed out that there was no cooking facility in the AMAN-CED and as such it is imperative that the kitchens should have proper place for cooking which will require floor mounted burners as done in the hostel for cooking. He reiterated that EC should establish what is needed as the end products from the kitchens to serve the mixed users. In the absence of any defined usage power and gas requirements cannot be worked out.

The Registrar insisted that there was no major change and he had provided all the inputs required. Now since the Director Projects was not satisfied with the information gathered; the Registrar requested the EC members to allow him to dis-associate himself with the kitchen related matters and leave it in the hands of the Director Projects and Consultants who could better solve the queries.

Dr.SayeedGhani suggested that the item related to kitchen planning should be deferred and discussed in the presence of Mr.ZafarSiddiqui and the Dean & Director.

2.4 Mr.Shameem Ahmed said that although the two complete Evaluation Reports are available for review by the members of theExecutive Committee,he has attached copies of the relevant pages of the two reports containing their recommendations. The undertaking suggested by NESPAK to be taken from Iqbal Engineering House will be taken before the award of the work. 2.5 The Director Projects wanted to know if this item has been approved by the TEC and CPC. He also suggested that complete evaluation report should also be sent to the EC. Mr.Shameem Ahmed said that the proposal would be discussed in the CPC on December 13, 2012.

Item No. 3 To consider and approve the estimated cost of the project on which 3% fee is to be paid to the Electrical Consultant M/s. KP Associates.

3.1 Mr.Shameem Ahmed stated that the original cost estimate of Rs.42.193million of the project “Development of Electrical Infrastructure for the Boys' Hostel, Girls’ Hostel and Staff Colony”was presented by the Consultant in the EC meeting held on April 2, 2012. However, it was subsequently revised to Rs.36.25million in the light of EC’s decisions and presented again in the EC meeting held on May 28, 2012 in which the revised cost estimate was approved. The revision included (a) removal of the cost of 400 kVA generator that would be funded by STHP funds, (b) incorporating the two cables that would be retrieved and reused, (c) addition of necessary civil work for distribution at the New Girls’ Hostel/Staff Colony.

3.2 Mr.Shameem reported that the lowest bid that was received for the project and at which the work would be awarded after necessary approvals is Rs.29,710,800/-. However, he said that the Consultant has demanded that he is entitled to a fee on the cost of the generator as well as the owner-furnished (retrieved) cable. The lowest bid for the generator is Rs.6,688,000/- while the cost of the old, retrieved cable works out to Rs.8.92million.Thus the total estimated cost of the project on which the 3% fee will be payable to the Consultant is as follows:


(a) Contract price :                           Rs. 29,710,800/-
(b) Cost of the generator :                  6,688,000/-
(c) Cost of the owner-furnished cable : 8,200,000/-
                                         _____________________________
Total estimated cost of the project : Rs.44,598,800/-

3.3 Mr.Shameem Ahmed said that as the Dean & Director wanted to know the justification for asking to include generator and cable costs in this estimate, he has invited Mr. Khalid Pervez, the Consultant to justify his claim.

3.4 Mr. Khalid Pervez in support of his claim said that he had prepared the tender documents, specifications for generator and presented the design to the EC. But the EC said that IBA has some cables which could be used. So he had to redesign the design to incorporate the existing cable.

3.5 Mr.Moeid Sultan said that if the contract price is Rs.29.7 million and we have the agreement with KP Associates that we would pay the fee on the contract price, then if we pay the additional fee, there would be audit objection. The Director Projects said that in such caseamendments in the contract can be made. However, it was reported that the contract has not yet been finalized.

3.6 Mr. Khalid Pervez said that if he were told earlier that the two items would not be included then he would not have reduced his fee from 5% to 3%. He said that owner furnished items are always part and parcel of the contract if they are in the design. The installation, commissioning and testing of the generator would still be his responsibility.

DECISION

3.7 The Committee approvedthe total estimated cost of the project as noted in 3.2 above on which 3% fee will be payable to the Consultant.


Item No.4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Career Development Centre Up-date.

4.1 The Director H.R. said that she has invited Mr.IshfaqueLashari, Assistant Manager CDC to up-date the EC the activities of Career Development Centre.

4.2 Mr.IshfaqueLashari said that the up-date on the activities of CDC includes Employment Survey, 2012, Internship, Up-date on Skill Development Program (SDP) which covers Faculty Advising, Developmental Coaching, Counseling – Personal Counseling, Career Counseling and Training Modules, and the Way forward.

4.3 Presenting the status of BBA graduates of 2012, he said that there were total 231 students who completed their BBA; 196 of them sought jobs and 170 were employed till first week of December, 2012. Twenty-one went for higher education, and 14 are not interested in job. He also presented the statistics of male and female students. The mean and median salary which was Rs.43,200 and Rs.38,000 respectively. The survey also covered industry-wise employment data, the average salary and male and female ratio. It was noted that students employed at FMCG got the highest salary followed by Telecom industry. Mr.Lashari presented the statistics of similar survey conducted in 2010, 2011, along with the survey of 2012.

4.4 Mr.Lashari presented the employment status of 12 students who completed their BS-CS in 2012. Nine of the 12 have been employed at an average salary of Rs.41,000/- He then presented the statistics of 48 students who completed their MBA in 2012. Forty-two of them sought the jobs, two went for higher education, and four were not interested in job. Of the 42, 38 have been employed at an average salary of Rs.66,400/- The average starting salary of the MBA in 2010, and 2011 was Rs.43,518 and Rs.53,500 respectively. Majority of them (26%) went to Sales & Distribution, followed by FMCG (18.4%).

4.5 Mr.Lashari presented details of the Corporate Internship done by the students of BBA, BS-CS, and MBA programs. He also presented details of the Responsible Citizen Internships completed by 277 students in 2012. In 2011 199 students completed RCI. He presented the list of the organizations where the students did their RCI.

4.6 SDP-Faculty Advising- Mr.Lashari informed the Committee that 661 students have been assigned to 46 Faculty Advisors. Nine Faculty Advisors meetings for 135 students have been scheduled for November – December 2012. He also presented results of the SDP Developmental Coaching of 398 BBA and BS-CS students, and 57 MBA students.
