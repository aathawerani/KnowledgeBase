Item No. 1      To consider and approve “Shifting of gen-set from FCS site”.

 

1.1        The Registrar said that as Mr. Mobin Khalili is concerned about the shifting plan of the gen-set from FCS site, he has invited Mr. Shameem Ahmed, and Mr. M. Jawad Khan so that this issue can be resolved.

 

1.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili referred to the EC decision wherein he was requested to hold weekly meetings with Mr. Jawad, Siemen’s team working on the project at IBA, Mr. Makhdumi and Mr. Mansoor to monitor the progress. He pointed out recent exchange of a series of emails between the Project Dept. and Mr. Shameem Ahmed in which the Project Dept. requested that in order to avoid delay in FCS renovation work the Gen-Set should be removed from FCS site latest by 20th November 2011.

 

1.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that he should have been consulted before fixing the date because despite their best efforts, the action from Siemens team to start the work urgently is not very encouraging. He reported that from September 26, 2011 onward NESPAK, who is the Project Manager, will be stationed on site and use all possible pressures to expedite the work. He reiterated that meeting the deadline of November 20, 2011 is very difficult because the work will be done according to a sequence. It is a 5 month contract which includes civil work, cabling and their synchronization, installation of equipments, although some work will go on simultaneously. 

 

1.4       Mr. M. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer was asked to present his proposal for shifting of Gen-Set by 20th of November. He reported that as the nature of work is very critical & urgent, he has the following proposal with regard to shifting of the gen set from FCS site for consideration by the EC:

 

o   If we put the Generator in the corner of the boundary wall opposite to Academic block it will require the minimum possible cabling to connect the Generator to the system. For this purpose cable had to be procured to connect the generator with the existing system. Amount of the Cable to be procured is approximately Rs.1 million. Its laying, commissioning & installation material & labor cost is not included. Siemens admitted that they have to procure the cable & the lead time required for their supplier is two months.

 

o   However, later it was found that one existing cable connected with the system had some provision & we can cut desired piece of the cable from it & only have to procure one piece of cable & hence can connect the Generator to the System & the system can be operated in the existing condition.

 

o   The cable feeding the 500 Kva DG Set is overloaded (130%) & after overheating this results in voltage drop & the system safeties operate resulting in the random supply on-off.  This phenomenon is more pronounced in the summers & we face this problem almost every day in the peak hours. This problem is being identified for a long time & if no attention is paid, it can damage the cable resulting in the overall breakdown of the KESC in the campus & heavy running cost of the Generator on prime mode. It is recommended to enhance the cable size of the KESC Feeder of 500 Kva DG Set. For this we have to increase the size of the cable which itself is a major cost. I suggest that this can be done in this project by introducing extra switchgear combination. This combination can (1) enhance the overall operating efficiency to 100% on KESC & we can get rid of the overloading, voltage drop resulting in the random supply on-off & extended operation of the Generator resulting in heavy fuel consumption, and (2) due to enhanced capacity the Chiller system of Admin block can be put into service. He presented the following cost estimate including taxes and Contractor’s profit @15% for each option:

 

1)      Relocation of 500 Kva dg set with no provision of the Chiller plant operation = Rs.470,000 Approx.

2)      Relocation with Chiller plant provision = Rs.645,000 approximately.

 

1.5       Mr. Jawad said that work of either of the above options would take at least three weeks’ time to complete which he can do at his own through the Contractor.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee in principle approved the second option for relocation of 500 Kva dg set with Chiller Plant provision. 

ACTION

 

1.7       Mr. M. Jawad Khan following the laid down procedures would get the work completed by the 1st week of November 2011.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve Review of Leave Rules.

 

4.1       The Director H.R stated that as part of the institution-wise exercise of periodic review of rules and regulations; based on approximately 3 years’ experience since the implementation of these rules, the HR Department suggests some modifications in the existing Earned Leave Rules of IBA. She said that the Leave Rules of IBA allow non-teaching employees to encash their earned leave upto a maximum of 30 days in a calendar year, subject to availability of sufficient leave in their balance.  Following is the procedure for leave encashment:

 

Leave encashment is done once a year for non-faculty staff, towards the end of the year, and if possible around the time of the month of Ramazan and Eid. A circular/notice is taken out by Incharge Leave Record to inform employees that they can apply for leave encashment if they have Earned Leave balances available.

           

Proposed Modification 1:

 

4.2       The Director HR proposed the following modification in the above mentioned clause on procedure for leave encashment:

 

a)         Employees can request for encashment of their earned leave upto the prescribed limit (i.e. 30 days) in a calendar year, any time during the calendar year, subject to availability of sufficient earned leave balance. 

b)         An employee can submit maximum two leave encashment requests in calendar year; however, the combined total of these requests should not exceed 30 days encashment in a calendar year.”

 

 

4.3       The Director H.R. presented the following justifications for the modification proposed above:

 

a)      The proposed change will allow flexibility to employees to encash their leaves at the time of need, rather than waiting for the year end.

b)      There is no additional financial impact of this change, as the maximum encashment allowed in a year will remain 30 days.

 

 

Proposed Modification 2:

 

4.4       The Director H.R. said that the leave rules currently have a condition of keeping 15 days earned leave of contract employees as security deposit which is applicable both in case of availing the leave and in case of leave encashment.  Following is the relevant clause:

 

c)         For contract employees, a balance of 15 days will be kept as a ‘security deposit’, and any remaining balance up to a maximum of 30 calendar days can be encashed.

 

4.5       The Director HR recommended removal of the ‘security deposit’ requirement for contract employees who have completed at least one year of their membership in the IBA Provident Fund Scheme. Her recommendation was based on the fact that after the introduction of Provident Fund and Gratuity schemes in January 2011, the Institute now has employees’ security in the form of their Provident Fund balances, which will exceed one gross salary by the end of 2011.  Whereas, the required security deposit in the form of leave equals to 50% of an employee’s monthly gross salary. Since, an alternate security is now available; she proposed that the above clause may be modified as follows:

 

c)         For contract employees, a balance of 15 days will be kept as a ‘security deposit’, and any remaining balance up to a maximum of 30 calendar days can be encashed. This requirement will not be applicable on employees who have completed one year of their membership in the IBA Provident Fund Scheme.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee agreed to the proposed Modification 1(a) i.e. employees can request for encashment of their earned leave upto the prescribed limit (i.e. 30 days) in a calendar year, any time during the calendar year, subject to availability of sufficient earned leave balance.  Proposed Modifications 1(b) and 2 were not approved.

 

ACTION

 

4.7       The Director H.R. would report the recommended modification in the Procedure for Leave Encashment in the Leave Rules of IBA to the A&FC.
