MINUTES OF 276TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 16, 2013

The 276th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 16, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                             Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                          Registrar

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                               Controller of Examinations

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                              Director Finance

6.      Mr. Adnan Hameed                                                            Head of H.R.

 

Mr. Khalid Javed Rishi, Manager Security and Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Status Report on time bound project from Director HEC Projects.

 

1.1       Water Pipeline Project to be completed by end of November 2013:

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that there was no up-date from Mr. Mateen of NESPAK. However, he said that the pipeline has been laid and the dug out trenches backfilled.  The road carpeting is in progress. He said that the delay in completion of project is due to transporters’ strike for about 10 days and procurement of a water meter. He said that the Consultant had suggested electro-magnetic meter which costs around Rs.700,000/-  but he said that he consulted the DMD of KW&SB who agreed to provide their own meter which would cost around Rs.30,000 to Rs.35,000/-  He hoped that the project would be completed by December 26, 2013.

 

The Dean & Director said that the Board had allowed extension uptill November 30, 2013. He asked Mr. Shameem to prepare a note for the BoG when the project is completed providing justifications for the delay in the completion of the project.

 

Item No.2      To consider and approve the Restructuring of Security Department.

 

2.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that minor changes have been suggested in the Security Department which would be briefed by Mr. Khalid Javed Rishi, Manager Security.  The Dean & Director asked the status of CCTV Cameras. Mr. Khalid Rishi replied that within two or three days, tender would be floated for the CCTV cameras. The Registrar said that the number of CCTV cameras has increased from 60 to 82. The Registrar said that power centre, new car parking, boundary wall, and MP Building have been added for the CCTV Cameras. Mr. Khalid Rishi provided details with regard to locations, number of cameras and their pixels (1.3 MP and 2.0 MP), that they proposed to be placed. The Dean & Director asked as to why three cameras in computer lab have been placed. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that one camera cannot cover the whole area. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there should be cameras of high resolution so that they may serve the purpose specially for covering the open area. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that 1.3 MP IP based cameras are better than analog cameras.

 

2.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili asked about the status of CCTV cameras at City Campus which he said is more vulnerable as compared to the Main Campus.  Mr. Khalid Rishi said that there are already 16 cameras installed at the City Campus.  He said that when the Aman-Tower is ready they would revisit the requirements. He said that at FCS Block there is no monitoring. Mr. Khalili said that besides FCS Building, there are other places too such as Admin Block where there is no CCTV camera.  He said that all sensitive points should be covered. The Dean & Director said that at sensitive points there should be high resolution cameras. He said that uncovered zones should be covered adding a few cameras. The Registrar said that they would have a look at City Campus to determine the requirements.            Mr. Khalili stressed the need to strengthen the security arrangements for the City Campus. He said that on his instruction Mr. Khalid Rishi prepared a detail security plan with recommendations for the City Campus few months back which Mr. Khalili had forwarded to the Registrar for his input and presentation to the Executive Committee for consideration and approval.

 

2.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the Multipurpose Building Lab has not been covered with CCTV camera. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that he had the understanding that the lab would not be there.  The Registrar confirmed that the lab would continue to be at the MP Building.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director asked about the barriers and swapping cards for entry and exit. The Registrar said that they are waiting for the completion of External Development work. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Khalid Rishi to work on Swap Card for entry and exit.  Mr. Adnan Hameed said that RFID card would cost Rs.250 each through outsourcing and if they purchase their own equipment and printer, it would initially cost Rs.200 each and in subsequent years it would cost Rs.70 to Rs.80 per card. The Registrar suggested to get the RFID through outsource.  The Dean & Director agreed with the Registrar because he said that our technology may become outdated but on outsourcing they keep on updating.

He requested that they should officially be designated as Searchers/CCTV Operators. He clarified that no additional manpower would be required. However, he said that Lady searcher/CCTV operators would be paid additional salary due to nature of duty at the following rate:

The Dean & Director said that we should have the flexibility to deploy the best person. He further said that if we are getting equipment, we need technical manpower but at the same time the number of untrained and unskilled manpower should be reduced. The Registrar said that our contract is ending on December 31, 2013 and Mr. Khalid Rishi wants to formally create these positions. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that he would reduce the number of guards when the CCTV cameras are installed. 

 

2.6       Mr. Khalid Rishi presented the organogram of existing security staff and the proposed organogram.  He proposed that there should be an Assistant Security Supervisor reporting to Security Supervisor at the Main Campus.  He clarified that the rank of Assistant Security Supervisor will not increase the head count and there would be no financial impact. The Dean & Director said that the Board has approved the financial strategy for IBA for IBA i.e. our operational expenditures should not exceed our operational revenues. He said that if there is no financial implication, he has no problem. Mr. Khalid Rishi presented the job responsibility of Assistant Security Supervisor. The Dean & Director said that in such case conditional approval of the organizational chart may be given.

 

2.7       Mr. Khalid Rishi said that the establishment of CCTV control room is very essential to communicate any relevant and critical information to all stakeholders immediately in order to reduce the response time in case of an incident.  He said that the control room at the Main Campus will be located at the Admin Building.  He proposed the following three shifts for the Operators of CCTV:

 

            A Shift  0800 hours to 1700 hours

            B Shift  1400 hours to 2300 hours

            C Shift  2300 hours to 0800 hours

 

2.8       Mr. Khalid Rishi said that it is necessary to retain the Analog cameras along with IP Based cameras so that whenever IP System is down, Anolog would be working.  The Dean & Director suggested to mix match the Analog and IP cameras and see where Analog or IP is needed. He further advised Mr. Khalid Rishi to revisit the requirements of CCTV cameras at City Campus.  Mr. Khalili said that they have to do a mapping of the area which is covered and which is not and their sensitivity based on this they would determine the requirements for CCTV Camera at City Campus. 

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee approved the Revised Organogram with the condition that there would be some saving due to reduction in the number of security guards when all the facilities are ready.

 

2.10     The Committee approved the procurement of 82 CCTV cameras as proposed by Mr. Khalid Rishi and the additional CCTV cameras that would be required for the City Campus.

 

ACTION

 

2.11     Mr. Khalid Rishi in consultation with the Controller of Examinations would determine the requirement for additional CCTV cameras at City Campus.

 

2.12     Mr. Khalid Rishi would start working on barriers and the swapping card and present his proposal to the EC.

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve the proposed SOP for Recruitment of outsourced Support Services Staff.

 

3.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that he has developed a SOP for hiring of outsourced support services staff. However, he clarified that the SOP would not be applicable on outsourced services agreements in which support staff would work under the supervision of the service vendor. He said that there are some pre-requisites which need to be fulfilled in order to implement this SOP.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director observed that the overtime for outsource staff is not IBA’s responsibility; it is the responsibility of company. Mr. Adnan Hameed said that concerned department maintains attendance as well as over time record of individual outsourced staff. He said that overtime rate is mentioned in the agreement. The Dean & Director said that each service that needs to be outsourced (janitorial, catering, landscaping, sports ground, etc.) should be identified and the scope of work to be done defined in the agreement. He advised Mr. Adnan Hameed to see AKU, SBP and others agreements for outsourcing for guidance.  He further asked Mr. Adnan Hameed to revisit the SOP in light of the guidance provided and the information to be obtained from AKU and others. He said that the whole rationale of the BoG in outsourcing these services is that the professional service provider can do better job in a more cost effective manner in comparison to regular job holders of IBA.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.3       Mr. Adnan Hameed in consultation with the Registrar would revisit the proposal in light of the suggestions noted above.    

 

Item No.4       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  Make-up Exam Request – Note from Dr. Nadya Qamar Chishty Mujahid.

4.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili presented applications of four students who applied for make up exams of 2nd term papers missed by them. The Registrar referred to the email from Dr. Nadya Qamar Chishty Mujahid regarding make-up exams for two of her students who missed their second hourly and applied for make-up exam. Dr. Nadya Chishty in her email requested for a detailed report as to why make up is not allowed. In reply Mr. Khalili said that he had informed by email to Dr. Chishty that “all rules and regulations pertaining to examination are clearly laid down in the Program Announcement under the chapter Rules & Regulations. Regarding make up exam it is stated that there is no provision of make-up exam for regular students. Only in extremely serious medical condition which requires hospitalization the Executive Committee may consider after verification of hospitalization certificates. Applications of those students would be presented to the Executive Committee in its meeting on Monday and Dr. Chishty would be informed about the decision accordingly”. Mr. Khalili informed the EC that there are four such cases and none of them is a hospitalization case.

 

4.2       In reply to the email from Mr. Khalili, Dr. Chishty wrote, “I am a new faculty member [who has just got off probation] and until this point [well after my students missing the exam] had absolutely no idea of the gravity of this issue, or the Program Announcement/Rules and Regulations that you mention. I will not have the students pay for my deficiency--that isn't fair. The Director is welcome to place this entire email trail in my official file--it does not show me in a good light, but I'd rather take the blame for much of this situation than have my students do so...................”

 

4.3       The Committee thoroughly discussed the email of Dr. Chishty; specially with regard to honouring the commitment she had, unknowingly the rules, made to the students for make-up examination.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee decided that although they appreciate that Dr. Chishty has committed to the students for a make-up exam not knowing the rule, the make-up examination rule is very clear and it cannot be violated.  Any exception on the basis of ignorance of law and rule can set bad precedents in the future.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
