MINUTES OF 295TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY,

MAY 5, 2014

 

The 295th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 5, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Mohammad Nishat                                           Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                 Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                  Registrar

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                  Director Finance

6.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                     Director Projects

7.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                 Head of ICT Department

 

Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects, and Mr. Syed Jahanzeb, Assistant Manager Finance attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve the engagement of Single Contractor to do balance miscellaneous works of Library at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects gave a brief background of the project “Renovation and refurbishment of Library” which was awarded to Contractor M/s Project Managers, Engineers, Constructors & Fabricators (PMECF) in October 2011. The commencement date was fixed as November 15, 2011 and completion time was February 15, 2013. However, the Contractor could not complete the work on the scheduled completion date and requested for extension in time.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that extensions in time for completion were granted to the contractor twice based on verified reasons including intricacies in old structure, structural analysis for structural stability, complications in joining old structure with new structure, design issues in HVAC System, scarcity of manpower and material due to law and order situation in Karachi. The revised completion date was fixed as September 2013. The contractor was not able to achieve the completion date due to two major reasons; (1) Lack of technical resources, and (2) Lack of financial resources. The request of Contractor was examined and he was provided financial resources due to him. This enabled them to complete 80% of the work till January 2014.  He said that even after repeated promises and assurance, the Contractor did not attempt to undertake the balance works except the HVAC works.

 

1.3       A letter was issued to M/s. Project Managers asking for his explanation but no response was received. Subsequently another letter was issued for invoking the clause of liquidity damages. Even after the notice of liquidated damages, the Contractor did not improve his performance and almost abandoned the site.  Only a few labourers were seen working and works on all activities except HVAC were suspended. Detailed meetings were held by M/s. NESPAK, M/s. ICON and IBA Projects Department with the Contractor to address the critical situation and to resume work. The Contractor showed his inability to carry out any further work because of his very poor financial condition as he claimed he had incurred heavy losses on this project.

 

1.4       The Director Projects said that under the extra ordinary circumstances only two choices were available to IBA; one to terminate the Contract and get the balance work done through some other contractors at his risk and cost. This would have resulted into litigation and even the possibility of the injunction of the stay order. The project completion would have dragged on indefinitely. The second option was to get the work done through the existing sub-contractors of the main contractor and make special arrangements whereby payments are made directly to sub-contractors against the bills of the main contractor with his prior authorization and consent.

 

1.5       The Director Projects reported that the above two options were discussed with the Dean and Director IBA, who advised to go ahead with the second option while maintaining minimum financial risk to IBA. He said that M/s. Project Managers were given final chance by negotiating with their sub-contractors to complete the balance works of the project. The methodology for release of payments of the balance works directly to the sub- contractors was developed with the consent of M/s. Project Managers.  In this regard a meeting was arranged on 24th March, 2014 with their sub-contractors in which all sub-contractors expressed their willingness and agreement to complete balance works. However, the sub-contractors were adamant that advance may be given to them in order to complete the balance works of the Project. Projects Department clarified that the advance payment was not possible in accordance with the terms of the contract.

 

1.6       The Director Projects reported that in order to resolve the problem of payment of the sub-contractors, Projects Department, with the consent of M/s. NESPAK obtained an undertaking from Project Managers wherein the following clauses were included in the contract:

 

“IBA shall make the payment directly to sub-Contractors with the consent of M/s Project Managers for the actual work done at site duly checked & certified by M/s NESPAK & Consultant M/s ICON, which will be acceptable and adjusted in final bill of M/s Project Managers unconditionally.”

 

“In order to avoid further delay the project & contractual implication on our part, we therefore authorize IBA to get the remaining contractual work done / completed at their end at our risk and cost through our Sub-Contractors / Vendors OR any other Contractors whichever they prefer as per our BOQ Cost.”

 

1.7       The Director Projects reported that the following are the main works for which IBA can make direct payments with the consent of M/s Project Manager as per above paragraph:

 

1.      Electrical works

2.      Formica works

3.      Aluminium & Glass works

4.      Carpet works

5.      HVAC works

 

1.8     The Director Projects reported that detailed inspection revealed that the main contractor was executing miscellaneous works through his Petty Contractors in addition to the above work. These are as follows:

 

1.      Misc civil works (Internal & External)

2.      Carpentry works.

3.      False ceiling works.

4.      Stainless steel works.

5.      Plumbing works.

6.      Paint works.

 

1.9       The Director Projects said that the timing of completion now being very critical miscellaneous works activities forming close interface with sub-contractors works could not be undertaken through fresh competitive bidding. The estimated cost of all different types of miscellaneous works is only Rs.7.50 million. In order to resolve this issue and avoid further delay the work can be subcontracted to a contractor who is already working within Main Campus Premises. He will be able to provide labour for above mentioned works as per BOQ on complete risk and cost of M/s. Project Management Ltd. The risk and costs shall be adjusted in final bill and from the retention money of M/s Project Manager.

 

1.10     The Director Projects requested the EC to accord approval for engaging one or more contractors  to immediately undertake the listed works so that the project be completed within three months.

 

1.11     The Dean & Director said that he has advised to amend the original agreement incorporating the authorization of the Contractor to make payment directly to the sub-contractor. The General Contractor M/s Project Managers, Engineers, Constructors & Fabricators should authorize IBA to make payment directly to the sub-contractor/petty contractor.  He said that the selected Contractor will sign agreement with the sub-contractors / Petty Contractors so that SPPRA procedure is fulfilled.  He said that the responsibility would still rest with the General Contractor with whom we have already signed the original contract.

 

1.12     Mr. Jahanzeb said that the BoQ rates are three year old which may change. The Dean & Director said that we may give lump sum contract. He said that in the side letter the Director Projects will give specifications not the rate and the rates given in BoQ will be multiplied by the quantity. 

 

DECISION

 

1.13     The Executive Committee authorized amendment in the original contract so that the residual work which the Contractor is not doing can be done by sub-contractor/petty contractor and the payment will be made directly to them.

 

ACTION

 

1.14     The Director Projects would make amendment in the original contract and obtain authorization from the Contractor to make direct payment to the sub-contractor / petty contractor.

 

Item No. 2      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)       Status Up-dates.

 

2.1       Power Hub: The Director Projects reported that the Power Hub is fully functional. The Registrar observed that now it takes about double the time for the generator to be on in case of power breakdown. (Earlier by the counting of one to seven the generator was on but now it is on by the counting of 18). The Director Projects replied that due to heavy cost on ATS, it was dropped from the budget and now the switching is being done manually, as such it is taking longer time.  The Director Projects said that the power is being supplied by KESC and the main line is on KESC.  It is independent of Karachi University.

 

2.2       Water Pipeline: The Director Projects reported that the underground tank of Main Campus is ready.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that there is an IT store which has to be shifted because water pipeline will pass through it. He said that the pump for the underground tank will be ready by next month.  He said that water connections to all buildings will be provided in three months time i.e. by August 5, 2014. About the Girls’ Hostel underground water tank, the Director projects said that he has not taken any initiative. The Registrar said that there is no need of underground water tank as the living quarters have overhead tanks. The Dean & Director said that if water is easily reaching overhead tank, then there is no need of underground tank. For the Staff Colony the Director Projects said that according to the design prepared by Nespak they have to change the water line because the old pipes cannot bear too much pressure.  The Registrar suggested to use valves to reduce pressure.  The Director Projects said that he would see if check valves may be placed at some places.

 

2.3       Gas Line:  the Director Projects said that pipe fell short and that has not been arranged so far.  As soon as the pipe is available the gas pipeline will be completed and connection would be available. 

 

2.4       ATM Machine/Bank Branch: The Dean & Director asked the status of ATM Machine /Bank Branch from Mr. Moeid Sultan.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that HBL has not yet given the rates to be charged on loan to employees.  The Dean & Director said that he has authorized the Director Finance to get an ATM machine installed at Main Campus with HBL.  He said that UBL at City Campus branch wants to have its opening inside IBA besides the front side. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that he has no knowledge of it. The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to ask Mr. Rizwan Rizvi to get up-date on it.

 

2.5       Sports Facilities at City Campus: The Dean & Director said that students at City Campus also want sports facilities. He said that we have to think about it. Various options / locations were suggested for Squash court / Badminton court; one of them was the lounge along with the canteen that can be converted into badminton court. The Director Projects will look into various options.   

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
