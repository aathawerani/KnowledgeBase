MINUTES OF 223RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 15, 2012 

The 223rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 15, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1.       Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                       Associate Dean, FCS

2.       Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                 Associate Dean, FBA

3.       Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                           Director Projects

4.       Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                   Director CED

5.       Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                   Director H.R.

6.       Mr. Moeid Sultan                                        Acting Director Finance

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:   

Item No.1      To consider and approve the procurement, installation, testing and commissioning of 400 KVA Diesel Engine Generator for new Girls’ Hostel, Phase 1 (STHP).

1.1     Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the 400 kVA Diesel Generator is a part of our PC-1 for new Girls’ Hostel Phase 1 (STHP) approved by Government of Sindh. He said that the procurement process for the 400 KVA Diesel Engine Generator started with publication of Tender Notice in dailies Jang and Business Recorder on August 7, 2012.  Altogether 10 prospective bidders purchased the Tender Documents. A pre-bid meeting was held on August 18, 2012. The Technical Proposals of the 5 bids submitted were opened on August 30, 2012 and subsequently evaluated by our consultant KP & Associates. All 5 were evaluated to be technically responsive and eligible. The Financial Proposals that had been submitted by the 5 bidders were opened on September 22, 2012, in the presence of those bidders who chose to attend the opening.  An Evaluation of the Financial Bids was submitted for TEC-PROJ and CPC’s consideration in their joint meeting held on Thursday, October 11, 2012. TEC-Projects and CPC in their joint meeting approved awarding the contract to the lowest, responsive bidder, namely, Orient Energy Systems (Pvt.) Ltd., at the bid price of Rs.6,688,000/-. The Consultant has also recommended that the generator be ordered from the lowest and technically responsive bidder, namely, Orient Energy Systems (Pvt.) Ltd., at the total bid price of Rs.6,688,000/- 

1.2     Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve the procurement, installation, testing and commissioning of 400 KVA Diesel Engine Generator for new Girls’ Hostel, Phase 1 (STHP) from the lowest bidder M/s. Orient Energy Systems (Pvt.) Ltd. at the bid price of Rs.6,688,000/-. He said that the government will provide Rs.6.0 million for its procurement. The balance amount of Rs.688,000/- will either be paid by IBA from its own resources or adjusted against the contingency of Rs.1.0 million which is also provided in the PC-1.  

1.3     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know if the bidder can reduce the cost if we make 50% advance payment so that the difference which is to be paid either by IBA or from the contingency is minimised. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that it will be against the rule as it was clearly mentioned in the tender document that no advance will be given. He further said that it is on IBA’s discretion to place certain items on contingency.  He clarified that the cabling cost is also covered in the bid price.   

1.4     The Director Projects pointed out that the Consultants have raised two points in their evaluation report about the lowest bidder; one being his weakness of  after sales service and the other the difficulty in arranging the spare parts. Mr Shameem apprised that both the aspects have been taken care of. 

DECISION 

1.5     The Committee approved the procurement, installation, testing and commissioning of 400 KVA Diesel Engine Generator for new Girls’ Hostel, Phase 1 (STHP) from the lowest responsive bidder M/s. Orient Energy Systems (Pvt.) Ltd. at the bid price of Rs.6,688,000/-. 

Item No. 2     To consider survey result to determine the choices of faculty / students and management for inviting vendors for four kitchens to be established in the Alumni Students’ Centre, Main Campus.  

2.1     The Director Projects reported that as desired by the Executive Committee in its 221st  meeting held on 1st October, 2012, a survey was conducted in order to find out the choices / preferences of faculty, students and the Management staff for inviting vendors for four kitchens being established in Students’ Centre. In all 732 votes were received, following are the choices and preferences in descending order with reference to the percentage of votes received: 

·         McDonald                                                                36.74 %

·         Subway                                                                  25.68 %

·         Conventional Pak Food/LaRosh & Student Biryani            15.71 %

·         KFC                                                                        6.96 %

·         Pizza Hut                                                                 5.87 %

·         Salad Bar                                                                 3.68 %

·         Chachaji                                                                  2.04 % 

2.2     The Director Projects said that only one voter from the faculty has suggested that the opportunity may be provided to MBA Students to run food outlet.  One of the faculty members has pointed out that the foreign food franchises will definitely attract attention of miscreants and IBA may be a collateral damage.  Following received less than 1%: 

Canteen - Mr. Burger – Hardees - Dunken Donuts – Abdul Ghaffar Kabab  -  City Campus Canteen Contractor  - Karachi Broast  -  Domino Pizza – Thai Food and Chinese. 

2.3     The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to review, discuss and decide the food outlets to be brought in the Alumni Students’ Centre.

2.4     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that since Pepsi is operating at the Main Campus, McDonald which uses Coke, cannot run its outlet here. He said that Larosh has already been given the contract for the Aman-CED but he is not satisfied with their services.  

DECISION 

2.5     The Committee decided that Subway, Students Biryani, one Chinese plus Kabab Roll, and a fresh juice counter may be allowed to be set up in the coffee bar area. 

ACTION 

2.6     The Director Projects in coordination with the Registrar would arrange the kitchen/counters for the four vendors mentioned above.  

Item No. 3     To consider and approve the disposal of Staircase Tower removed from Academic Block, City Campus. 

3.1     The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee in its 216th meeting held on August 27, 2012 had decided that the Director Projects would arrange to dispose off the dismantled Staircase Tower by following laid down disposal procedure.  Accordingly, he said that the case was presented to the Central Purchase Committee but the CPC referred it back to the EC asking him to re submit the case to the EC to consider its utility at some other place so that the loss on disposal as scrap can be minimised. 

3.2     The Director Projects said that the future use of the dismantled stair case cannot be predicted and that it may be disposed off as “Spare Staircase”.  To a question, he said that the dismantled staircase is two year old and it had cost around Rs.5 lac.  After its depreciation @5% per annum, its book value is Rs.450,000/-.  If it is sold as scrap it could fetch around Rs.180,000/-  

DECISION 

3.3     The Committee requested the Director Projects to see where the stair case can be used at IBA.  He should also see the possibility of using it as “fire staircase” for any building of IBA, or disposing it as staircase not as scrap so that there is minimum loss.      

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
