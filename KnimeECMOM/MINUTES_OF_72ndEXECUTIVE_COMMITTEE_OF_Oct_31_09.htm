MINUTES OF THE 72nd MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON SATURDAY, OCTOBER 31, 2009  

The 72nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Saturday, October 31, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.   Dr. Ishrat Husain                            Director

2.   Dr. Sayeed Ghani                           Associate Dean

3.   Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

4.   Mr. Mobin Khalili                             Controller of Examinations

5.   Mr. Ali Taha                                  Director Finance

6.   Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                           Director CED

7.   Dr. Arshad Siddiqui                         Director ICT

8.   Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                       Director HR

 

Dr. Qazi Masood and Mirza Izhar Husain attended on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     Up-date on IBA Code Book.

 

1.1       The Director thanked all those who had provided up-dated information relating to their department for the IBA Code Book.  However, he said that some information from Finance and IT departments was not received. He said that he wants all relevant information to be included in the code book. He said that there should be some flexibility in the compensation packages for teaching courses by experts (Gurus), visiting faculty or foreign experts, in the CEE and the Executive Program. But they should be reflected in the code book without specifying the exact amounts. He clarified that EMBA is no longer a part of CEE but forms part of our academic programs. In case of EMBA-BFS the industry has asked us to pay the market rates as they are funding this program from their own resources. He said that if our own faculty is equally qualified then he / she should be paid the same rate as is paid to outsiders for teaching in the CEE.  He asked Mirza Izhar Hussain to include the compensation component in the policy for CEE.

 

1.2              The following draft policies were presented by the Director Finance, Director ICT and the Director H.R. for consideration and approval by the Executive Committee:

 

a)      Draft SOP presented by the Director Finance

 

1.3              The Director Finance presented draft SOP/Financial Policies and Procedures on the following to be included in the IBA Code Book after approval:

 

·         Fee Payment and Refund Policy

·         Students’ Bodies Funds

·         Investment Policy

·         Financial Assistance to Students

·         Pension & its Commutation

·         Staff Provident Fund

·         Taxation

·         Payroll and Taxation

·         Petty Cash Policy

·         Car Policy

 

1.4              The Director requested the Director Finance to highlight major changes in the revised SOP/Financial Policies and Procedures.

 

1.5              The Director Finance said that there is no clear policy on the fee receivable.  The proposal is that students should not be allowed for the next semester if he / she has not paid in full the fee for the previous semester(s). Further if a student does not pay all the dues till the completion of the program, he / she shall not be issued provisional transcript, transcript and degree till the time he / she clears all the dues.

 

1.6              Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that we should decide on the limit of amount to be receivable and the overdue period.  He suggested that a grace period of one semester be allowed.

 

1.7              Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that if a student does not pay the fee without prior permission, he / she should be considered as defaulter.

 

1.8              The Director said that those who are going to get or have applied for financial aid their cases should be separated from those who are neither eligible for nor have applied for financial aid. He further said that there should be a distinction between circumstantial defaulter and wilful defaulter.

 

1.9              The Registrar said that there are two types of students; one who have never defaulted and another is habitual defaulter. He said that in Bahria University students who have not cleared their dues are not allowed to complete their last semester.

 

1.10          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that taking action in the last semester would be too late.  He suggested that if a student applies for deferment then his / her request may be considered by assessing its genuineness but those who do not apply should not be considered.

 

DECISION

 

1.11          The Committee decided that a grace period of one semester only for payment of fee will be allowed to those students who cannot pay the fee on time.  However, this fee deferment policy will not be applicable for Evening Program students.

 

1.12          The instalment plan for fee payment will be followed and it will be allowed to EMBA students also.

 

1.13          The Committee approved the SOPs / Financial Policies and Procedures as presented by the Director Finance incorporating the clarification in the Fee Payment and Refund Policy as stated in 1.11 and 1.12 above.

 

 

ACTION

 

1.14          The Director Finance will forward the revised policy incorporating the above decisions to the Director H.R. for inclusion in the IBA Code Book.

 

1.15          The Registrar will include the Fee Payment and Refund Policy in the Students Handbook and will also get it placed on the IBA Portal.

 

 

b)     ICT Policy & Procedures.

 

1.16          The Director said that any policy which is related to its users should be circulated among all to get their feedback and ownership. He suggested that the policy on ICT should be circulated among all users.

 

1.17          The Registrar wanted to know about the Computer replacement policy. He also wanted to know about the entitlement of computer peripherals such as colour printers and its location. The Director ICT replied that generally in 3 years computers become obsolete.  

 

1.18          The Director said that we should start sharing resources. He said that we should go for multi-purpose gadgets such as fax, copier, scanner and printer all in one and those should be located at a common Resources room to be dedicated with someone made responsible for managing them.

 

1.19          Mr. Mobin Khalili reported that maintenance service is not being provided properly to junior level people.

 

1.20          The Director said that Service Centres performance evaluation will be on the feedback of users.  The Performance Management System will have higher weightage for the evaluation by the users.

 

1.21          The Director said that the Library Management System also needs to be up-graded.  He requested the Director ICT to depute someone for up-gradation of LMS.

 

DECISION

 

1.22          The Director ICT would send the ICT Policy & Procedures to the users with a request to send their feedback, if any, within a week  

 

1.23          The Members Executive Committee would send their comments on the policy, if any, within a week to the Director ICT and if there are substantive issues related to this policy then it would be discussed in the EC otherwise the Director ICT would forward the policy to the Director H.R. for inclusion in the IBA Code Book.

 

ACTION

 

1.24          The Director ICT would send the ICT Policy & Procedures to the users to get their comments within a week.

 

1.25          The Director ICT would forward the ICT Policy & Procedures to Director H.R. for inclusion in the IBA Code Book after obtaining comments from the uses and Executive Committee.

 

1.26          The Director ICT would depute someone for up-gradation of Library Management System.

 

 

c)      HR Rules and Regulations including Staff Compensation, Job Description, Job Evaluation, Remuneration Structure Design.

 

1.27          The Director H.R. said that she has presented two pieces of rules; one is Staff Compensation which includes job description, job evaluation, and remuneration structure design and the second is the Performance Management System. She said that based on the study, six job bands have been proposed for management and three for non-management staff.  The band has been divided into five families, academic (which is separate), managerial specialists, administrative, operational and technical.  She said that the placements have been made on the basis of the job description and analysis done by the Consultants.

 

1.28          Referring to the job band, the Registrar pointed out that the position of Registrar has been placed on job band 5.    

 

1.29          Mr. Mobin Khalili also observed that the Registrar and the Controller of Examinations have been placed with Associate Professors which is grate 19 post whereas the Controller of Examinations is of grade 20.  He also said that MIS people placed in band 5 along with the Registrar and the Controller of Examinations are quite junior. He said that the categorization is discriminatory.  

 

1.30          The Director H.R. clarified that the Consultants have analysed the job and on the basis of this the placements have been done.

 

1.31          The Director agreed that in a university the positions of Vice Chancellor, Registrar, Controller of Examinations are significant. He said that as an individual he would place Mr. Khalili and the Capt. (Retd) Ahmed Zaheer in band 6.  He stated that by creating the positions of Director Projects, Director H.R. Director Programs, and so on, he has diluted the responsibilities of the Registrar and the Controller of Examinations. He said that if it is decided that there would be no Director Projects, Director H.R. then the band of Controller of Examinations and the Registrar will automatically go to band six.

 

1.32          Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that Option “A” employees feel quite frustrated because the increments they get is very insignificant.

 

1.33          The Director said that he has sympathies with Option “A” employees but he would not be able to carry forward the renewal agenda if he reopens the previous system which would be a Pandora box.    

 

 

DECISION

 

1.34          The Committee approved the H.R. rules and regulations as presented by the Director H.R. The Committee decided that Mr. Mobin Khalili and Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed would be placed in band 6.

 

 

d)         Performance Management System:

 

1.35          The Director H.R. reported that they have taken segmented approach for the Performance Management of non-teaching staff and have divided it into two; one for J-1 to J-3 category and the second one for 1 to 6 band. For J-1 to J-3 there is no major change in the existing appraisal system except that the factors have been included in details and the immediate supervisors will assess and assign certain ratings to the individuals.

 

1.36          The Performance Management of band 1-6 is based on MBO (Management By Objectives) system. The Director H.R. said that 70% weightage will be given to performance and 30% to the competencies.

 

1.37          The Director asked the Director H.R. to make a distinction between the staff of Service Units and other staff and assign more weightage (50%) to the evaluation by the users and 20% to the evaluation by managers.  He said that it may be clarified that since this is the transitional year and they have not had the opportunity to provide service for the whole year, therefore, it cannot be used for this year’s appraisal but in future the performance will be evaluated on the basis of users satisfaction survey. The overall ratings will be discussed in the calibration committee which would consist of departmental heads and after discussion final ranking based on normal distribution will be communicated along with adequate information and feedback to the concerned individuals and will be signed by all concerned. The process will start in December with self appraisal and end in March.

 

1.38          The Director ICT said that it is Performance Appraisal instead of Performance Management because in Performance Management, the performance is reviewed regularly on a weekly or monthly basis to improve the performance.

 

1.39          The Director H.R said that it is mentioned in the note that regular informal contacts between staff and managers are encouraged.  Moreover, there is mid-year review also.  So it is very much Performance Management.

 

1.40          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed that it is Performance Management also as it includes objectives for future and training and development.  However, he did not consider it appropriate to give evaluation result of an individual after the decision of the calibration committee.

 

1.41     The Director H.R. explained the process of evaluation in detail and also informed about the working of the Calibration Committee. Over this Mr. Zafar Siddiqui was satisfied.

 

1.42     The Director said that there has to be a sliding scale of the weightage but at the same time feed back from the sub-ordinate staff for directors and managers will have to be obtained.  He further said that at the Director’s level there would be no bell curve but for lower level there would be a bell curve.  He requested the Director H.R. to revise the Performance Management system accordingly.

 

DECISION

 

1.43     The Committee approved the Performance Management System for non-teaching staff as presented by the Director H.R. However, the Committee decided that the Director H.R. would incorporate the changes related to the weightage given to the first part of evaluation i.e. self-appraisal and assign more weightage to the service users in case of service centres.

 

d)     Format of the presentation of IBA Code Book.

 

1.44     The Director requested that each concerned department should divide their related chapters for Code Book into the following three parts and send them to the Director H.R: 

 

i)                    Where there is no change

ii)                  Where there are major changes without any material alterations

iii)                Where there are substantive changes or new additions

 

1.45     Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested to send the draft Code Book to the legal advisor before presenting it to the Board.

 

1.46     The Director said that instead of sending it to the legal advisor, it should be sent to the visiting faculty for review. He requested the Director H.R. to give the draft Code Book to a visiting faculty to review it.  He also requested the Registrar to consult with Mr. Major (R.) Aftab Lodhi on the Code Book.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
