Registrar:

The Director ICT said that they can do it through NADRA.

 

The Dean & Director said that he wants to tighten the undersirable entry and requested the Registrar to get the visitors / parents / persons accompanying students screened.  He also advised the Registrar to talk to NADRA and get another machine meeting the requirements of NADRA for screening purposes.

Item No. 3      Presentation on Electrical Infrastructure of Main Campus by Electrical Consultant M/s. K.P. Associates.

 

3.1       Mr. Khalid Pervez, after introducing himself and his firm reported that he is currently handling the following consultancy assignments:

 

·         Feasibility Study of Captive Generation, Design and Supervision of Electric Power Infrastructure, Central Fire Alarm System and External Lighting.

 

·         Design, Tendering & Supervision of Electrical Systems of IBA Girls Hostel.

 

·         Design, Tendering and Supervision of Lifts for Admin Building at IBA City Campus.  

 

3.2       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that he would be giving presentation on the following:

 

a.       Captive Generation

b.      Power Infrastructure, and

c.       Other Tasks

 

3.3       The Dean & Director said that the primary source of power supply to IBA is KESC so captive generation is not needed. He asked Mr. Khalid Pervez to exclude Captive Generation from his feasibility study and the presentation.

 

3.4       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that in captive generation the only viable option was to go for co-gen but he said that the system does not match with it.  Giving details of the power centre he said that it would house the main distribution switch gear, back-up generators, and other equipments and will be fully computerized. He clarified that the Power Centre will have nothing to do with the existing system. He said that the existing supply will cater lesser area after the renovation work of buildings is complete. He showed the proposed location of the Power Centre.

 

3.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that since the Aman Centre is very close to the proposed location of power centre, it will be affected by the creation of noise from the Power Centre. He suggested to move it around 50 feet ahead of the Aman Centre.

 

3.6       The Registrar said that instead of 750 kva generator at the Power Centre as proposed by Mr. Khalid Pervez, there should be a 375 kva generator. The Registrar said that he has hired a 375 kva generator and its log shows that in full blog it has not gone above 180 kva. The Dean & Director agreed with the Registrar that such a big generator of 750 kva is not needed.

 

3.7       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that it is not a captive generator; it is a standby generator.

 

3.8       The Dean & Director said that for standby such a big generator is not needed because all the buildings and places at the Main Campus will not require power supply at the same time. He said that there would be emergency need and normal need for which they have to do rationing of the power supply. He further said that they would have an agreement with KESC for exemption from load shedding as the KESC has done with Karachi University. 

 

3.9       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that they need to see the internal design of the power supply for control of power supply.

 

3.10     The Registrar requested to install 500 kva’s two generators at the new Academic Block and CED because they will soon be completed.       

 

3.11     The Dean & Director advised Mr. Khalid Pervez to coordinate with M/s. NBCL for power centres for the two buildings. He said that the Data Centre should be given priority in power supply.

 

3.12     Mr. Khalid Pervez presented the following cost estimate for this project:

 

            Cost of 11 kva                             Rs.......  million

            Power Centre                              Rs.......  million

            400 volt power cable (aluminium)    Rs.......  million

            Total Cost                                          Rs....... million

 

3.13     The Dean & Director suggested to reduce the cost. He asked the Director Projects to sit with Mr. Khalid Pervez and work out the cost as the Power Centre cost would be reduced due to exclusion of Captive Generation. The Dean & Director said that he would arrange to procure the cable directly from Pakistan Cables.  He requested Mr. Pervez to let him know the total length of cables that would be required.

 

3.14     Giving details of other tasks Mr. Khalid Pervez said that for central fire alarm system only cable cost would be incurred which would be around Rs.6 lacs.

 

3.15     The Director ICT suggested that at the networking operation centre, the monitor for fire alarm system can be placed which would be monitored and controlled by a dedicated security person.  The suggestion was accepted by the Committee. 

3.16     Mr. Khalid Pervez said that they have not yet worked on the Sports Centre lighting and other electric system designs. The Dean & Director suggested to find out low cost solution for this.     

 

3.17     Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the external lighting would cover pathways, security lighting, facade lighting etc. He suggested to use standardized energy efficient system for the new buildings.

 

3.18     The Dean & Director asked Mr. Sohail Ahmed, Supervisor, to ensure that the type of light should be the same for all buildings. 

 

DECISION

 

3.19     The Committee appreciated the proposal for Power Centre through which the power supply distribution would be controlled. The following decisions were taken:

 

·         The Consultant would exclude Captive Generation from his assignment.

·         The Power Centre would be located around 50 feet away from the Aman Centre.

·         There would be a control room for central fire alarm system located in the Data Centre which would be monitored and controlled by a security person.

·         IBA will procure cable directly from Pakistan Cables.

 

ACTION

 

3.20     Mr. Khalid Pervez will work in coordination with M/s. NBCL and see the internal electrical design.

 

3.21     Mr. Sohail Ahmed, Supervisor, would ensure that the type of light is the same for all buildings.

 

3.22     Mr. Khalid Pervez would inform about the total length of cables that would be required for the project.

 

3.23     Mr. Khalid Pervez and the Director Projects would revise the cost estimate of the project in light of the decisions noted above.

 

3.24     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would get the additional cost reimbursed by Aman Foundation for in moving ahead the Power Centre from the Aman Centre.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve proposal for outsourcing the Operations and Maintenance of HVAC system at Academic Block and Auditorium at IBA Main Campus.

 

4.1       The Registrar reported that as per contract, the post installation operations and maintenance service of the air-condition plant will end on July 31, 2011 and after that they have two options; (1) either to manage it by IBA, or (2) outsource this service. He said that due to limited resources, it is not possible to internally manage it and if it is outsourced, then the process of tendering etc. would take another four months. He suggested that the vendors who are providing this post installation service should be allowed to continue their services for four months by that time bidding process will be completed. He said that the existing vendor is ready to provide this service on the following terms and conditions:

 

List of equipment to be operated and maintained:

           

Ø  Donkey Pump                                                 2nos

Ø  Control Panel                                                 2nos

Ø  Fieder Panel                                                   2nos

Ø  Electrical Chiller                                              2nos

Ø  Cooling Towers                                               2nos

Ø  Self Package Unit                                           1no (Faculty Room & Security Room)

Ø  FCUs                                                            24nos (Admin block)

Ø  AHUs                                                            21nos (Auditorium)

Ø  Pumps chiller water                                         12nos

Ø  Pumps condenser water                                   12nos

Ø  Chill water pipe, ducting & insulation                   (varied length)

 

Scope of Work

 

The scope of work shall be as follows:

 

Ø  Project Managers shall operate and maintain the equipment as listed above

Ø  All kind of material and spare parts shall be provided by IBA.

Ø  If any machining work / repair work is required from outside, IBA shall pay the cost while PM shall provide the technical assistance.

 

Manpower Details

 

Total no of persons                             4

Total no of shifts                                2

 

There will be a shutdown after 15days in convenience with IBA management.

 

Amount

 

Total amount / month              Rs.275,000/-

(Rupees: Two lacs Seventy five thousand)

 

Payment Details

 

Monthly invoices is to be provided within one week of the start of the month

 

Duration of contract four month assuming from 1st August 2011

 

DECISION

 

4.2       The Executive Committee in principle gave approval for hiring the services of the existing vendor for a maximum of four months during which time tendering process is to be completed for formal outsourcing of the job. The Committee asked the Registrar to negotiate the service charges as Rs.275,000/- per month is on the higher side.

 

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  Proposal for increasing the scope of services required from Electrical Consultant.

 

5.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the proposed addition in the scope of services required from Electrical Consultant was submitted by him to the EC for review and approval which was considered in the EC meeting held on April 19, 2011. The said that the EC had asked him to obtain comments from the Head of Internal Audit on his proposal for increasing the scope and present them to the EC for consideration.  He said that the Director Projects discussed the matter with Head of Internal Audit, as an outcome of which it has been concluded that the additional services required in respect of Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel may be included in the scope of services required from the electrical consultant KP & Associates. b)                 Career Development Centre Progress and way forward.

 

5.4       Mr. Ishfaque Ahmed Lashari, Assistant Manager, Career Development Center presented the progress report of Career Development Centre (CDC), covering Internship status, jobs and placement status, Responsible Citizen Internship (RCI) status, on campus recruitments, and other activities of CDC.  He presented the number of students in MBA, BBA, and BS programs passed out in 2011 along with the number of students placed till July 16, 2011.  He also reported that the average salary offered to the MBA students passed out in 2011 was Rs.56,302; to BBA was Rs.46,900; and to the graduates of BS program was Rs.49,429. 

 

5.5       The Dean & Director wanted to know the up-date on the employment status of last year’s graduate so that the Committee may have a benchmark with which it can compare this year’s employment of IBA graduates. He expressed his satisfaction over the average salary and market acceptance of BS graduates and advised that the new class of BS be told about the market acceptability of the previous batch during the Orientation session.

 

5.6       Mr. Lashari reported that there were 167 MBA students, and 231 BBA students who passed out last year and all of them have been placed.

 

5.7       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to how many out of 231 students of BBA program who passed out in 2010 are interested to join MBA(Morning Program) in 2012 after completing two years’ industry experience.  He suggested that they (BBA students passed out in 2010) be asked if they are interested to join MBA(Morning Program) in 2012.

 

5.8       Mr. Lashari presented the placement status of BS, BBA and MBA classes 2011 Industry-wise according to which 31% of BS and BBA class was absorbed in Banking and related services followed by IT where 23% mostly BS students were placed. 38% of the MBA class 2011 joined FMCG, followed by banking and related services, and others. He also provided status of the summer Internship 2011 and the status of Responsible Citizen Internship (RCI).  

 

5.9       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Lashari to provide him with the list of the banks which are not providing internship. He suggested to make surprise visits to the organizations where the IBA students are doing summer internship/RCI to know if they are really working over there or not.  He also suggested to find out how many organizations which provided internship/project to IBA students also offered them jobs after their graduation. He advised to allow students after their second semester to do Responsible Citizen Internship. He further said that students coming from outside Karachi should be allowed to do the RCI at their hometown, if it is available there.

 

5.10     The Dean & Director asked Mr. Lashari to make an effort to bring in more organizations to IBA to present their recruitment policy and collect resumes.

 

5.11     Mr. Lashari presented details of the programs they want to implement in the future.  This included CDC Website, Corporate Relations, Students Guide Book, and Developing Career Counseling Model.

 

5.12     The Dean & Director asked the Director ICT to depute someone from his department who could provide guidance to CDC on Web issue.

 

DECISION

 

5.13     The CDC progress as presented by Mr. Ishfaque Lashari was noted and approved along with the comments / observations noted above.

 

ACTION

 

5.14     Mr. Lashari would implement the decisions noted above.

    

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
