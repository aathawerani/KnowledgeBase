Item No. 4      Presentation on Internal Auditing by Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Head of Internal Audit.

4.1              Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Head of Internal Audit gave a presentation on Internal Auditing, Internal Control, Types of Internal Audits, and the Procedures to be followed.  He also highlighted the reasons of failure of Internal Controls which included mistakes or poor judgement by employees, collusion between two or more individuals, inappropriately overriding established policies or procedures by Management. He stated that only Management is accountable for assurance that appropriate internal controls are in place whereas everyone is responsible for the performance of internal control activities.  He said that for IBA Internal Audit Services plan to conduct three different types of Audit works (a) Compliance Audit or Internal Audit of Payments, (b) Audit of Information system, and (c) Consultancy Services.  Elaborating on  Compliance Audit or Internal Audit of Payments, he said that Internal Auditor considers financial functions when determining if they conform to established organizational policies in the following priority:

a)   Compliance with Auditor General of Pakistan instructions and observations which are agreed by IBA.

b)   Compliance with Income Tax laws,

c)   Compliance with PPRA Ordinance,

d)   Compliance with IBA Act 1994,

e)   Compliance of Rules framed by Board of Governors for carrying out the purposes of IBA Act.

4.2       Commenting on Consulting Services he said that the Internal Auditor acts as In-house Consultant on the effectiveness of an IBA’s Risk Management activities to help ensure key business risks are being managed appropriately and that the system of internal control is operating effectively. It is the responsibility of the Director IBA to outline areas where Consulting Services are required keeping in view that it should not affect the independence of the Internal Auditor.

4.3       The Director Finance said that at Karachi University and NED they have the procedure of pre-audit before payment.  So for every transaction pre-audit is carried out.

DECISION

4.4              The Director said that we have to go by law of IBA and not by what is practiced at Karachi University or NED and the law says, he emphasised that  “No expenditure shall be made from the funds unless a bill for its payment has been audited by the Audit Officer of the Institute in accordance with the rules made under this Act.”  He further said that if someone comes to the Head of Internal Audit for review, it does not mean that the Head of Internal Audit has the authority of transaction.  He will just give his best advice.

4.5              At the request of the Director Finance, the Dean & Director clarified the flow  process of work which is:

Transaction Request → Transaction Approval → Budget/funds/Budget Re-appropriation approval → Procurement Process (quotations, tenders, purchase committee) → Payment  Approval (verification and compliance with Transaction Authority Policy and other laws/rules) → Internal Audit (The Audit will ensure that compliance with IBA rules, PPRA and government rules have been observed). The Internal Auditor will send it back to the Director Finance if there is any short coming otherwise advise for making payment and the Director Finance will make the payment.

4.6              The Director said that pre-audit is now the function of the Finance Department.

4.7.            Regarding consultancy from Internal Audit Services the Director said that if any Department Head wants to take consultancy for amount exceeding Rs.100,000/- it is his prerogative – not compulsory.

4.8.            Regarding reply to Government Audit, the Director clarified that Director Finance in consultation with the Head of Internal Audit would reply to Government auditor.
