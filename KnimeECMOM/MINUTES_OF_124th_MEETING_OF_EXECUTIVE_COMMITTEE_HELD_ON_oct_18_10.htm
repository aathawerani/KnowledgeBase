E.A. Consulting (Pvt.) Item No. 2      To consider and approve the Renovation and Refurbishment of existing FCS Building at IBA City Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that as IBA intends to undertake the Renovation and Refurbishment of Existing FCS Building at City Campus, M/s. ICON being the Architect for the subject work initially submitted the first cost estimates in October, 2008 for Ground + 04 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 10,050 Sq-ft which came to Rs.42.835 million. The above cost was purely based on the refurbishment works and no new extension work was included. Later on, the Executive Committee in its 81st meeting held on 31st December, 2009 decided not to construct 4th Floor because of technical reasons concerned to the structural safety, as the structure of the building was not strong enough to resist any additional load. The same decision was conveyed to M/s. ICON on 6th January, 2010.

 

2.2       The second cost estimate was submitted by M/s. ICON in May, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 28,620 Sq-ft which comes to Rs.110.675 Million. The above cost was based on both the refurbishment works as well as new extension works. Major errors were found in the same estimates which were then communicated to M/s. ICON on 15th June, 2010. The third cost estimate was submitted by M/s. ICON in June, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 32,719 Sq-ft which came to Rs.101.472 Million. The case for approval was taken to the Executive Committee in its 107th meeting held on 14th June, 2010. The Committee did not approve the cost estimate of the building as submitted by M/s. ICON and advised that the renovation cost of the old area be restricted to Rs. 2,000/- per Sq-ft and the new area construction should be restricted to a maximum of 3,600/- per Sq-ft. The same decision was conveyed to M/s. ICON on 28th June, 2010.

 

2.3       The fourth cost estimate was received from M/s. ICON in July, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors which includes the refurbishment as well as new construction with the three (03) options. This was presented to the EC in its 118th meeting held on 30th August, 2010. The Committee did not approve the cost estimate of the building and advised that the refurbishment and renovation may be done at the whole building i.e. Ground + 03 Floors; whereas the new extension may be done on only the 3rd Floor where the two labs are to be constructed. The same decision was conveyed to M/s. ICON on 1st September, 2010. So the fifth cost estimate was received from M/s. ICON in August, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors with the following details:

DECISION

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Approval for revised date of commencement and completion of Administration Block, Main Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects informed the members that the Projects Department had planned the commencement of the construction of Administration Block Main Campus from September 2010 and its completion by August 2012. In order to maintain these targets all efforts were made to get the planning approved by the stakeholders. But as the overall area and budget was exceeding the limits, work was undertaken on re-planning in June 2010 and the whole process was completed on July 28, 2010, by which date the final approval of Architectural layout was given to the Architects. The tender documents were expected to be received in September 2010 and on completion of the procurement process they expected to commence the construction in January 2011.  In spite of continuous follow up, the Architects could not provide them a firm date for the tender drawings and the documents. In the meantime the Architect Mr. Hanif Daud left for USA on a day's notice, for three months. On further follow up Mr. Hanif Daud communicated on 6th October the dates for furnishing of Tender drawings and documents by 9th December 2010. The scrutiny and complete procurement process will take at least 14 weeks. As such the construction can only commence in the 1st week of April 2011. Originally the construction period was kept as 24 months. The completion date thus will be March 2013 i.e. a delay of 6 months.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that depending upon the amount of renovation and reconstruction works, period of construction will be recalculated with the advice of the Project Managers after receipt and checking of the drawings. It will be endeavoured to reduce the construction period to 21 months, thus aiming at the completion date of Administration building renovation by December 2012. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the revised dates in view of the above mentioned facts.

 

DECISION

 

4.3       In view of the circumstances stated by the Director Projects the Committee approved the extension in the completion date of Administration Building renovation till December 2012.

 

b)     UBL Gold Medal

 

4.4       The Registrar stated that it was agreed that the Gold Medals would be gold plated medals instead of pure gold.  However, he said that the UBL has given Rs.25,000/- for the gold medal and they want that the medal should be of Rs.25,000/-

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee requested the Registrar to continue with giving gold plated medals to the students.  However, for UBL gold medal, silver may be used as its base and gold plate of the remaining amount of Rs.25,000/- may be used.

 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
