Item No. 1     Address by Mr. Guy Pfeffermann, former Chief Economist of ICT and presently the Head of the Global Business Schools Network.

 

1.1       Before the address by Mr. Guy Pfeffermann, the Director introduced Mr. Guy Pfeffermann to the Executive Committee and the Academic Committee. The members Executive Committee and Academic Committee also introduced themselves to Mr. Guy Pfeffermann.

 

1.2            Mr. Pfeffermann presented an overview of the Global Business School Network (GBSN). He said that GBSN, based in Washington DC, is an international non-profit network of leading business schools dedicated to strengthening local business education in emerging markets. GBSN and their network members are engaged in all aspects of business and management education including the design and development of new business schools and building the capacity of existing business schools. Activities include faculty development, modernising curricula and pedagogy, developing strategic plans, and strengthening management education for particular sectors such as health, tourism, NGOs and entrepreneurship. GBSN brings access to international expertise and cutting edge practices of management education through their network of top ranked business schools.     

 

1.3            He said that GBSN harnesses the power of networks to tackle the issues of poor human capacity in the developing world. This is accomplished by organizing collaborative professional activities between faculty from leading global business schools and faculty in emerging markets, while strengthening links between local management schools, the private sector, and international organizations. These relationships form the platform for the creation of new ideas and the dissemination of knowledge, engaging GBSN and its network members in all aspects of business and management education.  In addition to carrying out capacity building projects, GBSN network members meet annually to share ideas, global expertise and to develop partnerships with colleagues from diverse backgrounds who have a stake in education and developing managers. 

 

1.4            Giving program overview, he said that GBSN has been working closely with Goldman Sachs on the development of a new initiative to support business and entrepreneurship education for women across the developing world known as 10,000 Women. GBSN provides advisory services to the Goldman Sachs team, consults Goldman Sachs on best practices in capacity building for management and entrepreneurship education in developing countries, assists in the development of sister school partnerships, and advises on the development or expansion of certificate programs in business and entrepreneurship training.

 

1.5            After the address of Mr. Guy Pfefferman, question answer session started. Ms. Yasmin Zafar wanted to know as to how IBA can collaborate with GBSN and how IBA can have access to them for faculty development.

 

1.6            Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know how joint research projects can be done and how GBSN can help IBA in developing case studies.

 

1.7            Mr. Pfefferman clarified that the GSBN does not provide any funding or arrange any funding for research or training but helps in identifying the sources from where such financing can be secured. 

 

1.8            The members of the Executive Committee and the Academic Board thanked Mr. Guy Pfefferman for coming and addressing them and providing useful information.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider suggestions on Security arrangements given by Dr. Sayeed Ghani.

 

2.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested the following with regard to security arrangements at both the Campuses:

 

a)         The frequent movement of students and faculty between APWA and City Campus poses the biggest challenge. One of the more vulnerable points of the campus is the temporary new canteen that has just been built. It has a sit-in area where quite a few number of students typically gather. He suggested that the SIT-IN area of this canteen be immediately closed, and the canteen should only provide delivery service. Students should be asked not to gather around that, or any other area, to avoid being seen in large numbers.

 

b)         Some kind of a biometric system should be immediately implemented, such as a hand scanner, or thumb/finger based scanner, which is fast and effective. He suggested that all students, staff and faculty should be required to get scanned to enter the premises. Any other ID system, he felt, could be easily bypassed. However, he said that it has to be ensured that such a system is fast, and deployed at several entry points so that queues of students do not build up. Guests’ entry should be extremely selective and closely guarded. Class room security is also an additional concern.

 

2.2              The Director agreed to the suggestions made by Dr. Sayeed Ghani.  He said that the message he gave to the students is that 2000 eyes are more important than the 2 eyes of a security guard. He requested all of them to act as their own security guards and report any suspicious activity or objects or persons immediately to the Security Staff. By remaining alert all the time and taking over the responsibilities themselves the chances of untoward incident are minimized.  

 

DECISION

 

2.3              The Registrar through Security Officers will ensure that the suggestions made by Dr. Sayeed Ghani are taken care of. The Registrar along with the Security Officers will decide where and at what places the CCTV should be placed and see how bio-metric system of scanning can be installed.

3.2       The Director ICT reported that the special discounts are being offered by Principals not only to IBA but all educational institutions unsolicited and without any negotiations. These have been given to IBA by the Technology Partners after the due process was completed and they were declared as the highest ranked through the evaluation criteria of 60% Technical and 40% Financial weightages.  He said that as IBA is the leading educational Institute of Pakistan, the Technology Partners are directly passing the whole discounts from the Principals to IBA as they would do so in case of other universities also.

 

3.3       The Director ICT requested the Executive Committee to approve the acceptance of the discounts as they do not violate any PPRA rules, since ICT Department has not solicited any discounts after the bids were opened.

 

3.4       The Director agreed that as per PPRA rules IBA cannot avail the discount.  However, he said that it was not correct that the discounts were being offered by the Technology Partners. The price given by the Technology Partners is not discounted price rather it is a special price offered to all educational institutions in Pakistan.  It is different from the corporate price to all other buyers.

 

DECISION

 

3.5              The Committee agreed with the Director that the price quoted by the Technology Partners is a special price offered to all educational institutions in Pakistan and it cannot be termed as discounted price as such there is no violation of PPRA rules.

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider proposal from Mr. Guy Pfeffermann, Founder and CEO of Global Business School Network.

 

4.1       The CEO of GBSN, in collaboration with Professor Ron Schramm of Columbia University, proposed to support IBA in building the capacity of current faculty in finance through the following proposed activities. The faculty mentor will spend five days on the following faculty capacity building activities:

 

A)        Provide Guidance and Instruction to the Finance Faculty of IBA in Karachi for the following two broad areas:

 

·         Program Structure of a Finance Department in an MBA program.

·         Finance Curriculum of MBA program.

·         Core Finance Courses – Topics, Syllabus and Reading Requirements.

·         Elective Finance Courses – Topics, Syllabus and Reading Requirements.

·         PhD Program Structure, Courses, Content and Reading Requirements.

 

·         Sample Courses: Introductory Finance. Advanced Corporate Finance.

Valuation. International Financial Management. . Financial Derivatives. Emerging Market Finance. Finance and Strategy. Entrepreneurial Finance.

·         Departmental Research Seminars.

·         Building Excellence into a Finance Department.

 

B)        Provide One-on-One Faculty Guidance in the Following Areas:

·         Individual Faculty Mentoring on Research Program.

·         International Collaboration.

·         International Conference Participation

 

C)        Provide a 2-Day Executive Development course in one of the following areas (based on IBA preference and audience background):

·         Corporate Finance

·         International Financial Management

·         Emerging Market Finance

 

(Professor Schramm will be assisted by IBA faculty in delivering this 2-day executive development course.

 

4.2       Proposed Budget:

 

·         Travel                                                  US$   5,500

·         Food and Lodging                              (Covered Locally)

·         Five-day Faculty Development          US$ 25,000

·         Two-day Exec. Ed Seminar                US$   9,500

·         Facilitation Fee                                   US$   2,000

TOTAL                                              US$ 42,000

 

4.3              The Committee discussed the proposal for collaboration for faculty development and capacity building.  It was suggested that IBA should get a survey of the gap so that it may know what its requirements are for future faculty development.

 

4.4              The Director said that there should be an academic plan and it should be synchronized with the training plan. He said that the purpose of faculty development is to acquire new tools and techniques and bring changes by applying those in their teaching. He suggested the Director H.R. to build in the incentive structure that if a faculty introduces a new course which he was previously not teaching that should be reflected in the performance evaluation and should be aligned with incentives. However, he said that initially our expectations should be lower. He said that there should be a system of weeding out courses and replacement with new ones; electives spectrum should also be increased.   

 

4.5              Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that after the proposed 5-day training is provided to our faculty by a Professor, then after a gap of four to six months, the same Professor should be invited and he should inquire as to what extent the participants have applied the knowledge and training they were provided.

 

4.6              The Committee identified the areas in which training is needed which included Finance, Management, Entrepreneurship and Marketing. However, on priority the Committee agreed that IBA should invite twice two Professors; one for Management and others for Marketing and for Finance. 

 

4.7              It was also suggested that IBA should include in its future programs the collaboration with some other universities for Health Management, Rural Management, and Infrastructure Management.

 

DECISION

 

4.8              The Committee approved the proposal as presented by Mr. Guy Pfeffermann along with the budget for faculty capacity building in collaboration with the GBSN for Management, Marketing and Finance. The Committee approved an amount of US dollars 126,000 out of the current year’s budget to fund the three courses.  The priority will be given to Finance as the GSBN has already lined up Prof. Ron Schramm of Columbia University.

 

4.9              The Executive Committee and the Academic Committee decided to approach the GBSN to help in the formation of an External Advisory Council of high powered individuals drawn from all over the world. The expenses in connection with the Council will be borne out of Faculty Development budget.

 

4.10          The Director H.R. was requested to provide for Advisory Council, faculty development, and local training in the next year’s budget.

 

 

ACTION

 

4.11          The Associate Deans and the Director H.R. would finalize the faculty capacity building activities.

 

4.12          The Director H.R. will provide for Advisory Council, faculty development, and local training in the next year’s budget.

 

b)      To consider request from Mr. Ammad Aleem, student of MBA-I (Main Campus) for make up examinations for the three mid-term papers he missed due to illness.

 

4.13          Mr. Ammad Aleem in his email addressed to Dr. Nasir Afghan stated that he has a long history of Tourette’s Syndrome which is characterized by physical and vocal tics and infrequent panic attacks during which he cannot function properly and his tics increase exponentially. He stated that on October 11, 2009 he suffered from a panic attack which did not subside till October 13 and as a result of that he was not able to take the mid term papers for Managerial Finance, Accounting for Decision Making, and Marketing Management. He also attached medical certificate from Aga Khan University Hospital.

 

DECISION

 

4.14          The Committee considering it as an extra-ordinary case, allowed make up examination for the three courses that Mr. Ammad Aleem missed in the mid-term examinations.

 

ACTION

 

4.15          The Controller of Examinations will arrange make up examinations for the three courses that Mr. Ammad Aleem missed in the mid-term examinations.  

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
