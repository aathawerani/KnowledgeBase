MINUTES OF 257TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON WEDNESDAY, JULY 17, 2013

 

The 257th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Wednesday, July 17, 2013 at 9:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                     Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Shakeel Khoja                                                          Acting Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                        Registrar

4.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                            Director Finance

5.      Mr. Shameem Ahmed (Rep. Mr. Rehanul Ambia)                    Director HEC Projects

6.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                                      Director H.R.

7.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                           Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Jawed Islam, Engineer HEC Projects, Mr. Abdul Rahman, Sr. Manager (Contracts), and Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi, Manager Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the amount exceeding BoQ Contract price of New Boys’ Hostel final bill.

 

1.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the contract for construction of the New Boys’ Hostel was awarded to M/s. Mughal Constructors (Pvt.) Ltd., for Rs.137,630,817/-. He said that with some additional items and escalation (as per conditions of contract), the final cost of the project amounts to Rs.143,238,067/- which represents an increase of Rs.5,607,250/- or 4% of the awarded cost. Mr. Shameem informed the Committee that the matter was considered by CPC first on June 29 and then on July 6, 2013. CPC has approved it with the condition that the contractor should not be allowed a cost escalation during the time extension. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the amount of Rs.5,607,250/- which has exceeded the BoQ contract price of new Boys’ Hostel.

 

1.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the BoG approved cost for this project was Rs.137,630,817/- whereas its completion cost is Rs.143,238,067/-  He said that since the amount has exceeded the budgeted amount, it would have to be presented to the Board as a reporting item. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that cost of IT related items is also included into the total cost of Rs.143 million while the financing for IT related items is done through HEC fund.

 

1.3       Mr. Imran Batada wanted to know till what time variation order is acceptable. Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that it was not variable order that was issued after completion of the project. He said that the project has been completed and the final completion certificate has also been taken.

 

1.4       The Registrar said that the bulbs at the Boys’ Hostel get fused very frequently due to which large amount is spent on its replacement and maintenance.  He advised Mr. Shameem Ahmed to keep in mind while working for phase 2 of the new Boys’ Hostel that the existing light fittings and savers are very expensive. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that bulbs have a warranty of 10 months or 30,000 hours but there is no guarantee for imported bulbs.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee approved the amount of Rs.5,607,250/- which has exceeded the BoQ contract price of new Boys’ Hostel.

 

ACTION

 

1.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would report to the A&FC/BoG about the amount exceeded the Board’s approval.

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the time extension for construction works for Sports Ground and ancillary works at Main Campus.

 

2.1                   Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that the project of Sports Ground and Ancillary Works at Main Campus was started on January 23, 2012 and was to be completed in 16 months on May 22, 2013. However he said that the completion of the project would be delayed by 98 days due to the following reasons:

 

Ø  Following changes in design:

 

· Revision of finished ground levels due to non availability of storm drainage system of Karachi University on the north side

· Addition and deletion of storm water trenches. (site requirements)

· Design changes in seating platform (by IBA) and change in retaining wall finishes. (by IBA/Architects)

 

Ø  Addition in scope of work of foundations of 12 meters floodlight poles for cricket ground and 4 meters high masts for tennis courts and volley ball courts (additional work and amount approved by Executive Committee on the request of the play area advisor Mr. Asad Ilyas, faculty member of IBA).

 

Ø  Addition of Cable trenches for cable laying up to the flood light poles and manholes (additional work and amount approved by the Executive Committee on the request of the play area advisor Mr. Asad Ilyas).

 

Ø  Delay caused due to law and order situation, unforeseen closure of CNG stations and complete closure of Karachi commercial markets  (52 days loss of recorded closures).

 

2.2       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that based on the above facts Nespak had recommended extension up to July 18, 2013. However, the Projects Department after reviewing Nespak’s recommendations and keeping in view the following facts recommended that the extension be approved till 30th August 2013:

 

· The Cricket ground is not yet acceptable to Mr. Asad Ilyas who observed that grass be allowed to grow thick during monsoon and that it will be fit for play after one monsoon rains.  He stated that they will take over the ground by end of August 2013.

 

· During site visit of project department, Architect, project managers and contractor on July 10, 2013 also observed and proposed erection of BRC mesh fencing to protect jogging track, volley ball and basket ball courts from tress passing and ongoing external development works adjacent to sports ground.

 

2.3       Mr. Abdul Rahman requested that extension in time up till August 30, 2013 may be accorded. He clarified that there would be no cost implication against fee of project managers as their contract has been concluded as of 30th June 2013 and Mr. Amin Qureshi is now the supervisor of this project. However, he said that there would be nominal expenses to be paid to the contractors for providing the manpower for maintenance of the grounds as they had completed the ground works by June 2013. He said that in its meeting held on July 13, 2013 the Central Purchase Committee recommended the approval of the extension in time up till August 31, 2013. Based on the recommendation of Project Department, Project Manager M/s NESPAK and Central Purchase Committee, Mr. Abdul Rahman requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till August 31, 2013.

 

2.4       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that the law and order situation in Karachi is bad for last so many years and the contractor must have kept cushion for such factors. He wanted to know if Projects Department keeps track of the strikes and CNG closures due to which delay is caused. Mr. Abdul Rahman replied that they keep track of such delays. He said that any strike or closure of market impacts badly on the schedule and it affects the schedule three times due to one unplanned or unforeseen stoppage of work.

 

2.5       The Registrar said that because of rain and Ramazan the work may be slowed down. He said that the request for extension should be realistic.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee allowed time extension for construction works for Sports Ground and ancillary works at Main Campus till August 31, 2013.

 

Item No. 3      Outsourcing of Cafeteria Services – Conceptual approval for floating tender.

       

3.1       The Registrar reported that following the approval of the Executive Committee (252nd meeting) to form the “Food Services Management Committee”, the said committee has met a number of times with Mr. Ejaz Mian in Chair. A proposal for Outsourcing the Cafeteria Services to experienced vendors has been evolved. He said that the draft EOI document incorporating the deliberations of the Committee and suggestions received from the stakeholders has been sent to the EC for their comments/observations, if any. He said that it has been planned to issue the advertisement inviting EOI early next week so that necessary formalities can be completed in respect of tendering and procuring the subject services in time for inauguration of the student centre. The Registrar said that the Food Services Management Committee prepared a Survey Form and circulated among 200 students, but only 22 students responded. He said that the Survey Form was circulated thrice between June 1 to June 30, 2013. He said that the main concern of the respondents was on hygiene.  He said that at the advice of the Dean & Director he invited the expert who runs the canteen at Aga Khan to see our facilities and give his advice.  He said that he also invited the vendors who run their canteens at Bahria, and CBM.

 

3.2       The Registrar said that they have prepared a tender document and got it vetted from Mr. Jahanzeb who prepares contract. He requested the EC to give conceptual approval to float the tender.  He said that they want to do this expeditiously so that the canteen can also be made functional when the Students’ Centre is ready which is expected to be ready by the end of August 2013. He said that the FSM Committee has prepared an Evaluation Sheet also for the services.

 

3.3       Dr. Shakeel Khoja wanted to know if there is any provision for “economy meal” for the low paid employees. The Registrar said that at the moment there is no such provision. However, he said that they would try to provide economy meals.

 

3.4       The Director H.R. wanted to know if we can prequalify the contractors as been done for ad agencies, so that if one canteen contractor does not come up to our expectations, we may engage other prequalified contractor.  Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that his understanding is that PPRA/SPPRA does not allow this. There has to be two stages; first prequalification, and then asking the prequalified ones to bid for the tender. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that prequalification is allowed for one year and is linked with the budget. Mr. Sohail Khan, Manager Purchase who was consulted on this issue, said that after prequalification, tender documents will have to be floated among the prequalified.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee accorded conceptual approval for floating tender for outsourcing of cafeteria services.
