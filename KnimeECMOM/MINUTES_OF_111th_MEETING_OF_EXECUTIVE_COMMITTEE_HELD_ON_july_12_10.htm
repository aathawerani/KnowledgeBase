Item No. 2      To consider and approve proposed Addendum in Leave Encashment Policy for Teaching Staff.

 

2.1       The Director H.R. stated that after the dissemination of the BOG approved HR Rules & Regulations in October 2009, various cases and queries related to this policy have been raised by the faculty that have highlighted the need for further clarity on this policy. Specifically, there is an ambiguity about the term “mandatory activity”, which is interpreted differently by different individuals; particularly in the presence of a “mandatory” teaching assignment during the summer break.  She said that the addendum proposes that the term “mandatory activity” may be replaced with the term “special assignment”, and a brief description of “special assignment” may also be included in the policy.

 

Current Policy Statement:

 

Teaching Staff:

Teaching staff will not be able to encash their leave or obtain any benefit in place thereof, unless the IBA engages them in some mandatory activity during the semester break.

 

Proposed Policy Statement:

 

Teaching Staff:

“Teaching staff will not be able to encash their leave or obtain any benefit in place thereof, unless a faculty member is given a special assignment during the summer break by the Director IBA, with defined terms of reference, agreed timeframe and deliverables. The assignment timelines must cover at least 80% of the official time during the summer break.

 

Mandatory teaching assignments during the summer break, as per the terms of employment, and any other paid short-term assignment of any nature, will not be considered as special assignment.”   

 

DECISION

 

2.2       The Director said that there is no change in the policy but it is clarification and elaboration of the existing policy.  He asked the Director H.R. to circulate the clarification among the faculty members for their feedback.

 

ACTION

 

2.3       The Director H.R. would circulate the clarification/elaboration on the policy on encashment of leave for faculty among the faculty members for their feedback.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Selection of Consultant for GEM Adult Population Survey.

 

7.1       Dr. Shahid Raza Mir, Chairman, Department of Management informed the Committee that after securing Pakistan’s membership in Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) this year, Adult Population survey (APS) and National Expert Survey (NES) have been launched in coordination with LUMS and with financial assistance of USAID.  He said that the survey would help IBA faculty to use the data for research, policy makers to understand current level/impediments of entrepreneurial efforts and at the same time place IBA-CED at the global arena. Moreover, APS is the primary research tool of GEM that will be used by IBA-CED to build up resources and initiatives like seminars, conferences and curriculum development. He said that a bidding process was carried out by IBA/LUMS experts including Prof. Dr. Sarfraz Mian, Principal Investigator-GEM project, and top tier marketing research firms were contacted for their proposal to carry out APS.

 

7.2       Giving details of the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) Dr. Mir said that GEM is the most prestigious international research effort on entrepreneurship, implemented in more than 60 countries across the globe.  The GEM studies are conducted under the auspices of the Global Entrepreneurship Research Association, which is headquartered in two prominent US and UK universities, The Babson College, USA and London Business School, UK. The Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) is a not-for-profit academic research consortium established to collect, analyze and disseminate high quality information on global entrepreneurial activity.  GEM is the largest single study of entrepreneurial activity in the world. Policy makers around the world are using GEM results, conclusions and recommendations to improve support for entrepreneurs. The details of the GEM research activities are available at its website: www.gemconsortium.org. 

 

7.3       Dr. Shahid Mir said that IBA-CED with the financial help of USAID got involved in March 2010 to finance the project. To restrict the competition between top tier marketing research firms and considering the deadline for the survey (Mid-August 10), only the following selected and highly reputable research firms were contacted which is permissible under PP&P:

 

1.      ACNielsen

2.      TNS-Aftab

3.      Oasis International

7.4       Dr. Mir said that GEM standard RFP was emailed to the consultants on 17th March 2010 and they were requested to propose a GEM compatible methodology that will be applicable to Pakistan. An evaluation committee was established to assess the vendor proposal.  The committee issued its report on 24th May 2010. Committee members included:

 

1.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui, Director CED, IBA

2.      Dr. Shahid Mir, Chairperson Management, IBA

3.      Dr. Sarafaz A Mian, Principal Investigator, GEM, and Professor at SUNY, USA

4.      Dr. Arif Iqbal, Director Entrepreneurship S&ME Center, LUMS

 

7.5       Dr. Mir informed the Committee that the Proposal of TNS-Aftab was rejected as it did not conform to GEM Methodology. Following were the cost quoted by other two technically qualified consultants:

b)     Emergency repairs of roof slab of Administration Block and the repairs of failed waterproofing on the roof.

 

7.8       With regard to the falling of concrete chunks from the roof slab of the old Administration building at the City Campus, the Director Projects reported that on 10th July a detailed inspection was carried out by him along with the IBA Engineers, Maintenance Manager IBA and his team, IBA Project Managers on the project and a water proofing expert.  He said that the building had two serious problems; (1) Leaking roof, and (2) Spalled concrete of the roof slab and both imposed serious threats to life and property.  He reported that the following actions were taken immediately on emergent basis.

 

·         Cleaning and inspection started on the roof to check leakage points. Sealing them with hot bitumen.  At present long terms water proofing cannot be applied as it is feared that the structural slab may have to removed if it is established that it is weak.

 

·         Inspection and removal of spalled concrete from underside of the slab room by room and sealing it with SBR Cement paste.

 

7.9       The Director Projects reported that as they could not afford to waste a single minute, the repair works were ordered to be done by the contractor M/s ZCL and a water proofing specialist recommended by M/s. INTEG. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the emergency repair works.

 

7.10     The Director said that he had asked several times to prepare Maintenance Schedule. He said that if there had been periodic and routine maintenance, then the need for emergency maintenance would not have arisen.

 

7.11     The Registrar said that they do not have the capacity for survey for repair and maintenance need. He said that for the water proofing work they may pay from the Maintenance budget.  

 

7.12     The Director said that the Maintenance Department may seek help of the technical people in the Projects Department. 

 

DECISION

 

7.13     The Committee approved the actions taken in emergency by the Projects Department.

 

7.14     A maintenance Manual showing the periodic, routine maintenance schedule should be prepared by the Maintenance Department and presented to the EC or approval.

 

ACTION

 

7.15     The Maintenance Manager would prepare periodic, routine maintenance schedule and present it to the EC for approval.
