Item No.1       Presentation on “Implementation of Online IBA Alumni Election System”.

 

1.1       Mr. Haris Siddiqui, Manager Alumni Affairs, said that according to the Minutes of the Meeting of the Audit & Finance Committee held on April 7, 2012, and approved by the BoG the following decisions were made:

 

·         A system would be developed through which after three years of graduation, the graduates of IBA Karachi, would automatically become eligible to elect or to be elected as member BoG.  Dr. Ishrat Husain had said that the Registered Graduates would be able to cast their votes electronically.  For this they would be given a unique password.

 

·         All graduates of the IBA Karachi of three years’ standing and upward shall automatically be registered in the Register of Registered Graduates to be maintained by the Alumni Affairs Office (in soft copy form).

 

The Minutes of the 71st meeting of the Board of Governors of IBA held on June 23, 2012 further recorded the following additional decisions:

 

·         There would be no registration fee (for Registered Graduates).

 

·         A complete list of Registered Graduates would be made available by the Alumni Department to each candidate contesting for the elections on payment of Rs.1000/-.  

 

·         In view of automatic registration there is no need of closing the Register of Registered Graduates 30 days before the election.

 

1.2       Mr. Haris Siddiqui said that the Alumni and ICT departments have developed an on-line election system which will have the following benefits:

 

o   Convenience of voters

o   Increased Efficiency and Accuracy

o   Improved Turnout

 

1.3       Mr. Imran Batada briefed the EC on the technical specifications of Online Alumni Election System and its features which are: 

 

A.        Ensuring Anonymity of Vote: Anonymity of ballot should be preserved.

-  Once a Voter clicks on one of the Candidates for voting purposes, an authentication code is generated and sent to the voter via email.

 

-  Once the Voter plugs in the authentication code and submits the vote button, the following actions take place:

 

a) Association of authentication code would be disconnected with the VoterID. Only one authentication code can be used for accessing the system.

 

b) Authentication code is NOT stored anywhere.

 

c)  Vote counter of Candidate increases by 1.

 

B.        Tamper-resistant System: The voting system must also be tamper-proof to    thwart a wide range of cyber-attacks and hacking.

 

Multi-layered security is provided in the Online Alumni Election on two levels:

 

End User:

·         The Alumni website is SSL secured which ensures encrypted two-way communication between the server and the user.

 

·         While voting, the user is required to first login to the Alumni website, insert an authentication code sent on email address against his/her vote and finally submit vote.

 

Admin:

A dedicated public IP address, functioning only from within the IBA Premises, will be designated to conduct all the stages of the Election.

 

C.         User-friendly System: A voting system must be comprehensible and usable by the entire voting population, regardless of age, infirmity or disability.

 

-  All the stages of the Election are clearly defined and stated on the Home page of the Election portal.

-  The instructions for each step are highlighted in each user’s profile.

-  An Activity History is placed so that the user can be aware of the activities conducted.

-  During the 3-day voting, a 12 hour helpline and email support will be provided from 9:00 a.m. – 9:00 p.m. to facilitate the users.

-  During the 3-day voting, the computer labs of both the campuses will be made available from 9:00 a.m. – 9:00 p.m. for Alumni to cast their online votes.

 

1.4       Mr. Haris Siddiqui presented the flow chart of the election process and also the proposed election timeline.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director asked as to who will have access to the information about the voting.  Mr. Imran Batada replied that the Alumni Department will have the access right. The Dean & Director said that during three days of voting nobody should have access to the data on voting.  After the close of the voting, the information and complete control panel should be available with the Registrar. That information should remain anonymous; there should be time bound access. He asked as to what arrangement is proposed for those who do not have email or do not want to exercise their voting right through online system.  He suggested that for those who are not registered an advertisement should be placed in the newspapers as well as on IBA Website advising them to register and send their email ID.  A day should be reserved for those who wish to exercise their vote in person at IBA.

 

1.6       Mr. Imran Batada said that three days can be allowed for on-line voting, 4th day should be for physical voting from Karachi only and the result should be announced on the 5th day.

 

1.7       The Dean & Director asked if we should allow only those who are located in Karachi to contest or keep it wide open. It was suggested that since we are doing on-line and purpose of it is to involve all we should allow everyone to contest irrespective of their location.

 

1.8       The Registrar suggested to divide the flow chart into two: first for nominations, scrutiny and finalization of candidates and the second for the election process.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.9       The Committee approved the IBA Alumni on-line election system as presented along with the suggestions noted above and recommended to the Board for consideration.

 

1.10     Mr. Haris Siddiqui and Mr. Imran Batada would present the proposal to the Board for approval.

 

Item No. 2      To consider proposal to buy Sedona Software for AACSB Accreditation.

       

2.1       Dr. Amber Gul Rashid said that they were in the process of evaluating two products -“Sedona web” and “Digital Measures” - to streamline part of the reporting for AACSB accreditation.  As per the recommendations of ICT department, both are good products and can be useful for AACSB process. Cost wise "Sedona" is cheaper as compared to “Digital Measures”.  Recently, LUMS has also acquired Sedona. Both products would not be seamlessly integrated with the ERP, which means QEC Department has to work closely with ICT and has to take functional ownership of this product.

 

2.2       Dr. Amber Gul said that they have recommended Sedona system instead of IBA   Customized system for the following reasons:

 

o   it fulfills the requirements of AACSB for reporting purposes

o   it will cover the course outline details

o   data can be exported from ERP to Sedona

o   Sedona is used by many other HEIs

 

2.3       Dr. Amber Gul said that on the contrary IBA’s customized system will need to develop    many functionalities to fulfill AACSB requirements and this would increase cost and take          more time.

 

2.4       Mr. Imran Batada said that full customization is not possible in Sedona for MBA course outline; they have to export the data from ERP and import into Sedona which is very easy and would be done by IT department. He said that the Sedona System is charging US$5000 for universities over 100 full time faculty members. After discount of $1,125, the initial year’s license fee will be $3,875 USD.  In comparison to that Digital Measures are charging US$15,000 for more than 100 faculty members in the university.

 

2.5      Dr. Amber Gul Rashid said that they can use Sedona Systems for other programs also.  However, she said that if we get Sedona, key faculty members will have to be trained. In addition, significant effort will be needed to roll it out on the part of the QEC and IT departments.

 

2.6       The Dean & Director asked as to what amount has been allocated in the budget for AASCB. Mr. Imran Batada said that Rs.663,000/- has been provided for in the budget for AASCB.

 

2.7       To an observation from Mr. Abdul Wajed, the Dean & Director clarified that for archival purposes we should populate the LMS.

 

DECISION

       

2.8       The Committee approved the procurement of Sedona system.

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve standardization of certain items.

 

3.1      Mr. M. Sohail Khan, Manager Purchase & Stores proposed that in order to maintain inventory cost & supply and contain stock value, quality & delivery issue and to curtail undue taxes and commissions of stockiest, distributors and suppliers, it is prudent if we directly procure items such as laser papers, insect killer, detergents etc. from manufacturers / source. He said that if we standardize the mentioned items we can firstly cut down double tax being paid to manufacturers, stockist & suppliers; secondly receive original quality items and thirdly receive discount on manufacturing/ex-factory price.

 

3.2       Mr. M. Sohail Khan informed the Committee that IBA is using AA brand laser papers, Mortien brand insect killers, Surf/Glint & ANZO surface cleaner and MSN brand liquid hand wash. AA brand papers is being distributed by M/s Variety Papers; similarly Mortein is the product of Reckitt & Benckiser and detergents are the product of Lever Brothers, Colgate & Palmolive, ANZO and MSN and other renowned manufacturers and sole distributors in Pakistan. Thus, after standardization of items, we not only receive quality items but can also save undue taxes being paid to manufacturer/distributor & supplier & time and cost if we procure these items directly from source rather than through tender, RFQ, petty cash or other means.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director said that the EC has already decided on standardization of items; he wanted to know as to why it was presented again to the EC. He said that if through standardization we can save money we should do it. Mr. Jehanzeb said that in order to meet the SPPRA requirement, the Procurement Committee wants that the EC should give in writing that goods of those qualities intended to be procured are not available in the market except with the identified manufacturer / distributor / supplier. 

 

3.4       Mr. M. Sohail Khan presented the comparative statement of different items & identified available discount/saving if procured through manufacturer/producer/sole distributor in Pakistan. It was noted that:

 

a)      tender cost of AA brand Laser paper was 509/- while price of sole distributor would be 465/- i.e., saving of Rs.44/- per packet.               

b)      tender cost of Mortein spray was 425/- while price of producer would be 369/- i.e., saving of Rs.56/- per spray tin.               

c)      tender cost of Glint glass cleaner spray was Rs.188/- while price of producer would be Rs.3334.5/10ltr i.e.,  Rs.166.72 /- per 500 ml thus there is a saving of Rs.21.28/- per spray gun. Moreover, the company offered 10 empty spray guns free for reuse.               

d)     tender cost of Phenyl Liquid Finis & Phenyl Caroline FX was Rs.134/- & Rs.93/- per bottle respectively while ANZO one product i.e., Surface Cleaner would do the jobs of both chemicals and its consumption would be reduced by 30 to 40% of the total qty required by Phenyls. ANZO quality as acknowledged by Manager Maintenance is far better than the Phenyls.                

e)      tender cost of MSN Liquid Handwash was Rs.1100/- while price of manufacturer would be 850/- i.e., saving of Rs.250/- per can.

 

3.5       Mr. M. Sohail Khan informed the Committee that if we procure the standardized items directly from the manufacturers/producers/sole distributors, the saving would be over Rs.500,000/- in one go.

 

3.6      The Dean & Director said that for insect killer and other items the tender should be floated among the three leading manufacturers. Mr. M. Sohail Khan said that no manufacturer wants to enter into tender for such a small quantity because of various reasons.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.7       In view of the details and justifications provided by the Manager Procurement and Stores,            the Committee approved the standardization of the items listed at 3.4 above.
