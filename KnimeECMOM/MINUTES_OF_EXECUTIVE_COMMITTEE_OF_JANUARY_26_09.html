The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider comments and recommendations by Director Finance on Education Finance Program given by Dubai Islamic Bank Pakistan Limited.

1.1              The Dean & Director informed the Committee that he had asked seven banks whose chief executives are IBA alumni, to prepare Education Finance Program.  He said that Dubai Islamic Bank have presented this program. 

1.2              The Director Finance commenting on the Education Finance Program said that there are two options; one is that loan to be paid by the student himself, and the second option is that parents or guardians would pay the loan. He said that the loan will carry minimum mark up rate of 20% per annum because it is linked with the KIBOR rate which is very high these days.  There will be no financial or other risk on the part of IBA on these loans as IBA is not the guarantor, except that IBA will be liable to hold the issuance of degrees and transcripts for the relevant students till the time they get the NOC from the Bank. 

1.3              He said that in the first option where the student is to repay the loan, there is no restriction of city. However, in second option in which parents and guardians would repay the loan, the applicants should be a resident of either Karachi, Lahore, Rawalpindi/Islamabad or Faisalabad.

1.4              In the first option the minimum and maximum financing limit is Rs.100,000/- and Rs.500,000/- whereas in the second option Rs.500,000/- each for all applicants and Rs.1,000,000/- each for prime applicants. The minimum income requirement in first option is not applicable but for second option Rs.30,000/- per month for salaried parent / guardian and Rs.40,000/- per month for self-employed.     

1.5              In both the options, pricing would on floating rate.  Profit rate would be set with one year KIBOR as a benchmark. Rate would be KIBOR + Margin.

1.6              Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that we should limit our role as facilitator of loan.

1.7              The Dean & Director said that if a student of IBA who had availed loan facility while he was doing BBA, comes back for MBA, he / she will have to apply for restructuring of loan. The repayment of loan will have to be rescheduled.  He said that there must be a moratorium clause in the agreement for those IBA students who go for higher education.

1.8              The Dean & Director said that if the Executive Committee approves the Education Finance Program then the Director Finance will contact Dubai Islamic Bank Pakistan for finalization of the program.

1.9              The Dean & Director suggested to the Director Finance that once the agreement is finalized he should write letters to parents of the students that we are planning to offer Education Finance Program and get their feed back if the program is acceptable to them or not.   

1.10          After the agreement is signed, the details of the Program will be given in the Program Announcement, advertisement and the IBA Portal.

DECISION

1.11          The Committee agreed to the proposal of Education Finance Program as long as the role of IBA would be restricted to that of a facilitator and that IBA would hold degrees of those students who avail the loan facility till such time we receive an NOC from the Bank.

1.12          The Committee wanted that the facility be made available to the residents of any city of Pakistan rather than restricting it to only four cities of Pakistan.

1.13          The Director Finance will contact the concerned person at Dubai Islamic Bank Pakistan and finalize the agreement with them.

1.14          The Committee decided that once the agreement is finalized / signed, the information will be fully disseminated through Program Announcement, advertisement, and IBA Portal.

ACTION

1.15          The Director Finance will contact the concerned person at Dubai Islamic Bank Pakistan and finalize the agreement with them.  He would also ensure that the moratorium clause is inserted in the agreement.

1.16          The Director Finance would also write to the parents of the existing students informing them about the Education Finance Program and get their feedback.

1.17          The Registrar will disseminate the information after the agreement is signed.

Item No. 2      Brief by the Director Projects on Extension and Renovation of existing Admin. Block at the City Campus.

2.1              The Director Projects stated that he had discussed with Mr. Mobin Khalili the conceptual plan for extension and renovation of existing Admin Block at the City Campus and he has given some suggestions. The first one is that the Director’s Office including PS room, visitors’ room, meeting room and an attached bath should be located on the first floor facing the lawn. The new room for Controller of Examinations should have a room for PS and an attached bath. There should be separate offices for morning and evening programs staff. The conceptual plan does not show the rooms used as examination printing room on the ground floor and the back office where students’ academic records are compiled and kept. Large rooms are required for this purpose. Locations of faculty room and toilets on the ground floor may be swapped. Faculty room should have enough space to place planned fifteen computers in cubicles for the faculty to do their work. The Director I.T. can provide the exact requirement. Provision of a tea bar in the faculty room may also be considered. Toilets may be relocated from the first floor entrance.

2.2 The Dean & Director said that he has no objection to the relocation of his office as long as everybody can have easy access to him. He said that Program Office is a front office and he would like that Program Office, Director’s Office and Coordinator’s offices to be visible and easily accessible to the students and visitors.

2.3              The Director Projects said that his first priority is to provide decent accommodation to our faculty members. He said that the whole block for Program Offices and Program Coordinator will be redesigned but that would be done in the second phase.

DECISION

2.4              The Committee allowed the Director Projects to go ahead with his first phase plan.

Item No. 4      To consider placing information on CDs for Program Announcement.

4.1              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi showed the existing format of the program announcement placed on CDs to the EC members and invited their comments. He said that the final product for Program Announcement would also be placed on the CD.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee approved placing the information related to Program Announcement on the CDs.

Item No. 5      Visit of Website on IBA Projects.

5.1              The Director I.T. showed the website on IBA Projects to the EC members.

5.2              The Committee wanted to know the result of the competition for redesigning and developing IBA website. The Director I.T informed the Committee that the website competition has been conducted however, some additional assignments have been given to the students. He said that the website design given by the students is for external internet.  The existing website would be changed with the new one.

DECISION

5.3              The Committee approved the website on IBA projects. However, it decided that our website should be further improved so that the external users are aware of the IBA and the website should be continuously updated.

5.4       The Committee requested the Director Projects to assign some one from his department to provide up-date on projects for the IBA website after an interval of 10 to 15 days.

ACTION

5.5       The Director Projects would ensure that up-date on IBA Development Projects after an interval of 10 to 15 days is provided for the IBA Website.

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)  Offering Summer Program in morning hours.

6.1              It was suggested that we should not restrict our summer program for our students and for the people living and working in Karachi. We should invite people from outside Karachi also who may be provided hostel facilities because the IBA hostel is available during summer. Moreover, the summer program may be offered in day timings also for which the faculty members may be separately compensated.  

DECISION

6.2              The Committee agreed to the suggestion.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
