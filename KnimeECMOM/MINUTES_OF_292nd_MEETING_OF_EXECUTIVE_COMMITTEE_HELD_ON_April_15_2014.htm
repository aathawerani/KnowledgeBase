 Item No.1       Presentation on “Smart Card”.

1.1       The Registrar said that he has invited Mr. M. Shahid Mirza, Managing Director, SAZTEL to give a presentation to the EC on “Smart Card”.  He requested Mr. Shahid to make the presentation to the EC.

1.2       Mr. Shahid Mirza said that he would be giving a presentation on Integrated Institutional Card for IBA. Before the presentation he briefed about his company’s profile which according to him is the first IT networking company of Pakistan established in 1985. He said that the Integrated Institutional Card (IIC) may be needed to:

  • Stop unauthorized entry

  • Stop misuse of campus facilities

  • Reduce transaction time at library, cafeteria & stationary shop

  • Eliminate daily handling of cash associated risks

  • Integrate with the library system

 1.3       Mr. Shahid Mirza said that the IIC could be plastic card with a magnetic stripe or chip based, personalized with IBA logo etc.  Each card would be in an individual’s name, and each card would carry information related to the holder.  The card can be utilized for the following four purposes (four-in-one):

  • For Identification and Access Control:  The identification of the holder will be available on swipe of the card. (Optional interface to mechanical access control such as turnstile). The identification of the holder available in the form of Photograph or registration number on the front of the card. Maintenance of all entry & exit logs of each user, and secure entry to controlled areas.
     

  • Library Card:  Swipe of card displays the history and current status of borrowing of the holder if linked to the current library software (if possible), and can be used to deduct library fines on the spot.
     

  • eWallet: Money can be loaded into the card system. The balance is indicated on swiping the card. Amount can be charged securely at each outlet, thus reducing the balance. Paper receipt is issued for cash transactions. The charged amount is credited to the related outlet’s account, to be settled at designated intervals.

1.4       The Dean & Director asked as to what other options besides swipe were available and which could be cost effective. Mr. Shahid Mirza said that the options are of contact less, RFID, and GSM based. He said that magnetic stripe would be cost effective but there would be limited information as compared to Chip based card.

1.5       The Dean & Director asked as to what is the option available for loading balance in the eWallet. Mr. Shahid said that the balance can be loaded from the terminal device. The organization has to choose whether loading balance can be allowed from several places or it could be centralized.

1.6       The Head Librarian asked if EM and RFID system can be integrated into the system. Mr. Shahid Mirza confirmed that it can be. 

1.7       The following are the components of the system; the first four to be provided by the company and the last two are available at IBA:

  • Customized card for all card holders

  • Card swipe mechanism at each access control point

  • Card terminal at each payment point

  • Application software

  • Servers to run the application

  • Connectivity to all the above, primarily using the existing LAN network

1.8       The Dean & Director asked as to what is the cost of the terminal machine and whether enhancement and up-gradation of application software would be possible after installation. Mr. Shahid said that each terminal machine costs around Rs.30,000 and enhancement and up-gradation is covered in the maintenance agreement.  He said that if there is no customization required in the application software, the cost would be less than Rs.2 million, and the maintenance cost would have to be worked out.  The Dean & Director said that for the first one or two years the maintenance is usually covered by the company. Mr. Shahid said that that could be considered. He further said that roughly 10% of the cost would be required for maintenance. 

1.9       The card will cost Rs.60 each if it is magnetic stripe but the chip based card would cost somewhere between Rs.200 to Rs.300 each and if it is contact less then it would cost Rs.350 each. However, he said that RFID is costly.  The Access Control point would cost around Rs.10,000 to 15,000 each. The warranty for hardware will be for one year.   

1.10     The following are the benefits of the system:

  • A secure and instant means of identification

  • Aids in surveillance; maintains all entry/exit logs

  • Automatic access control. Controls unauthorized entry

  • Easy and tamper-proof library control

  • A secure and immediate payment system which eliminates cash handling and reduces transaction time on the campus outlets.

  • Maintains a positive cash flow for the institution

1.11     To a question as to what is the difference between magnetic and chip based cards, Mr. Shahid said that it is the amount of information.  He said that there is a security code and there will a pin for every card. The Dean & Director said that at this moment we may consider magnetic card and we can up-grade the card in the future. The acquired hardware and the software will remain the same in both the cases.  

1.12     Mr. Shahid Mirza said that their next step towards implementation is meetings with Heads of Security, Library, Administration, Finance, and IT to ascertain precise requirements and need for integration with existing system, if any.  This would enable costing of the solution. A demonstration of the system was given to the EC.

1.13     The Head Librarian said that we have to go for industry standards system, but since the system they are demonstrating is in-house developed there may be compatibility issue. Mr. Shahid said that all the equipments conform to the industry standards. He said that software is their own developed product. The terminals conform to those standards and the software has to run and interface with the terminal. To that extent he said that it is industry standardized. However, he said that he would get details and if needed may provide the details. To a question he said that vehicle ID has not been included.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that it should be compatible with the ERP. Mr. Shahid confirmed that it would be compatible with the ERP.

 DECISION & ACTION

 1.14     The Committee decided that IBA will call a meeting of all stake holders to finalize a solution that best suits the needs keeping in view this presentation.

 Item No. 2  To consider an Appeal from Mr. Shameem Ahmed (ex-Director HEC Projects) for Earned Leave from the date of his joining.  

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed in his email stated that the terms and conditions of letter of his appointment, dated February 16, 2008, specified that he was not "eligible for medical cover and leaves" during the six-months' appointment period. The settlement of his dues has therefore not included any earned leave for the six-month period.  However, he referred to the EC meeting held on June 8, 2009, wherein it was decided as a policy "that all employees will be provided medical, leaves and other associated perks from the date of joining". He mentioned that his interpretation of the decision was that it was applicable to him also and that he should be allowed Earned Leave facility from the date of his joining IBA. He requested the EC to validate his interpretation of the decision.  He further mentioned that during his six years' employment with IBA he or any of his family members were never hospitalized or claimed any other medical benefits except meager amounts for eye surgery (cataract).

2.2       It was decided that since leaves during the original six months’ appointment period were not allowed in his contract (letter of appointment), he cannot be entitled for Earned Leave for that period retrospectively.

DECISION & ACTION

2.3       The Committee asked Mr. Adnan Hameed to convey the decision of the EC to Mr. Shameem Ahmed that as per his appointment letter, he is not entitled to Earned Leave for six months.

 Item No. 3      To consider and approve proposed tariff for using IBA facilities.

 3.1       The Registrar stated that consequent to completion of various newly constructed facilities, the same are now attracting users from outside IBA. The latest being LUMS which had hired our facilities for running the US AID funded ASP  LUMS workshops;  likewise we have been approached by the Japanese Embassy for providing facilities for running once weekly Japanese language course. He said that on the advice of the Dean & Director, a survey was undertaken of similar facilities being provided on payment by HEJ and APWA, NED etc. He presented a draft of a presentation prepared for showcasing the new facilities and identifying tariff chargeable for individual premises to various organizations / persons who may make request for use of the same. 

 3.2       The Registrar said that the Director Projects has conveyed certain suggestions which have been incorporated.  The Registrar said that the rental idea was given by Mr. Dinshaw Avari that roughly one percent of the cost of the building should be used as tariff.  

 3.3       The Dean & Director said that since our facilities are ready and operational, we should highlight what facilities we have in our infrastructure. Mr. Imran Batada suggested to highlight internet bandwidth facilities also. The Registrar presented the proposed tariff for each facility. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that if our own students participate in any program at our premises organized by some other agencies we should charge subsidised rate for the facilities. The Registrar said that this is reflected in the proposal. He said that if for example we rent out our auditorium to someone and more than 50 per cent of the participants are IBA students then we would not charge provided the event is not ticket based. The Dean & Director suggested that there should be different classes of tariffs with eligibility criteria; (a) fully recoverable charges, (b) partial or discounted charges and (c) on pro rata basis.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that there should be per hour rate, standard hours rate plus additional hours rates.

 DECISION & ACTION

 3.4       The Committee appreciated and approved the idea of renting out the facilities. The Registrar would incorporate the suggestions given above.

 a)      Watchman’s Guard Tour Clock System.

 6.1       The Registrar said that in order to increase the efficiency of security staff, specially night shift guards, Amano watchman’s guard tour clock system has been procured and will be installed in both campuses shortly.  Mr. Khalid Rishi made a demonstration of the system. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that two sets would be required and each set would cost Rs.60,000/- There would be 15 keys with one set, and six months’ stationery (papers for printing the day to day status).  He said that the key is of special quality and one key is of Rs.3000/-. This would record the date, and time and in the morning it would be given to the supervisor who would print the status. He said that he would be monitoring it.
