Item No.1 Up-date on Cafeteria arrangements at both campuses.

                   

1.1    The Registrar said that in 2010 when it was decided to outsource the cafeteria, a contract was signed with Pakistan Beverages for City Campus and in 2012 another contract was signed with La Rosh for Main Campus.  After the termination of the contract with La Rosh for unsatisfactory performance the catering arrangement was temporarily given to the caterer of City Campus for the whole of 2013 and portion of 2014. The only difference in the two agreements was that at the City Campus Pakistan Beverages agreed to utilize the existing contractor but at Main Campus they have engaged a new firm i.e. Masters.  He said that in the agreement there is no mention of Aman Tower. However, he said that it was agreed that as and when required we may extend the scope of the operation. He said that Masters recently visited Aman Tower and they are willing to extend the services at Aman Tower. He said that their only concern was about the volume of business at Aman Tower. The Registrar said that he explained to them that they would have much more business at City Campus than at Main Campus because there will be training courses offered throughout the year by the Centres, the residential accommodation in the Tower.
 

1.2    The Dean & Director said that the distinguishing feature of Aman Tower is that in this building 24 hours service would be required because people will stay there at night too. He said that its services should be upto the standard of a 5-Star hotel because we will be charging the market rate from the guests living in the rooms. The guests who would be staying would be very senior executives.   He said that the catering services to be provided at the functions arranged by CEJ, CEE, ASP LUMS and functions held at JS Auditorium would generate additional business for the cafeteria contractor.  He suggested that these features should be included in the advertisement for caterer. The Registrar said that there would be separate enclosure for faculty, executives and staff. 
 

1.3    One of the faculty members observed that the Cafeteria at Aman-CED at Main Campus is usually not available due to its reservations for various events and that there was limited availability of dishes in the menu. The Dean & Director suggested that in the tender for the Aman Tower, cafeteria at the Aman-CED at the Main Campus should also be included.  This may attract competition as the volume of business would be high.

 

DECISIONS & ACTIONS

 

1.4    The Registrar would get a single advertisement placed for the award of catering services at Aman Tower and Aman-CED.  The ad would include the features noted above.

 

 

Item No.2 To consider the proposal of the expansion of Mosque and integration with the new prayer hall.

 

2.1    Dr. Nasir Touheed had made the following observations with regard to the facilities at the Prayer Hall:

 
 o    On Friday we have to pray outside the mosque in a temperature of forty degree.
o    Even if we pray inside the hall we still feel the hot temperature.
o   The Fans are not of any help in these summer months.

 

2.2    Dr. Nasir Touheed suggested to combine the new Mosque and the Old Mosque and also cover the area in between the two. This way we can build a Mosque of capacity of 450 with air-conditioning facility.

 

2.3    The Dean & Director asked Mr. Noshad of Icon about his opinion on the proposal to integrate the old mosque and the prayer hall and the area in between the two mosques.  Mr. Noshad suggested that courtyard can be integrated to increase the capacity.  Mr. Imran Mumtaz said that they can utilize the court yard structure instead of disturbing the existing prayer hall. He said that in the court yard alteration can be made. Mr. Mahmood Ali said that the flow of rainy water is towards that direction and there is a slope of 2 to 2.5 feet. Mr. Noshad said that underground storm water drainage system can be covered but there would be cost impact. Dr. Nasir Touheed said that if the court yard is also included, then there would be three prayer places. He said that as the prayer hall is open from all sides mosquitoes come in easily.  He suggested that there should be a boundary wall and the prayer hall should be given the look of a mosque.  Mr. Imran Khan suggested that two wash rooms should also be provided in the new design.

 

2.4    The Dean & Director said that if the old mosque is integrated with the Prayer Hall and the area between the two is integrated, its capacity would increase.  He said that his concern is the convenience of the Namazis. He also cautioned that the cost should not be more than its utilization because the use of mosque is time sensitive; the months when temperature is very high last 2 to 3 months only and the number of namazis increases during Ramazan and on Friday prayers only. He suggested to find a solution which is least cost but convenient to the Namazis. He said that initially the Prayer Hall was not intended for the Juma prayer but later he decided that Juma prayer should be offered there too.   

 

2.5    The Dean & Director said that the consensus was on joining the old and new mosques.  He requested Mr. Noshad, Mr. Imran Mumtaz, Mr. Imran Khan and Dr. Nasir Touheed to visit the site and find a practical solution.  He told Mr. Mahmood Ali that this project would be his responsibility.

 

2.6    Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested to provide some alternate area for prayer for non-Muslims. Dr. Nishat said that a room can be provided to them.  

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

2.7    Mr. Noshad, Mr. Imran Mumtaz, Mr. Imran Khan and Dr. Nasir Touheed would  visit the site and find a practical solution of integrating the old mosque and the prayer hall. 

 

2.8    Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects was made incharge of this project.

 

2.9    The Registrar would arrange a place for prayer for non-Muslims.

 

       

Item No.3 Fortnightly up-date on Boys’ Hostel, Block ‘B’.

       

3.1    Mr. Mahmood Ali reported that from Excavation to 2nd floor columns pouring of Zone 2 has been 100% complete till April 12, 2015.  He said that in zone 1 excavation to first floor beams and slab pouring has been 100% completed.  HVAC & Plumbing works, electrical works and finishing works in both zones are in progress.  He said that there was no issue in Block ‘B’ but on Block ‘C’ there is an issue because the area is confined. 

 

3.2    The Registrar said that he had met the Registrar, Karachi University for the area but they said that they had earmarked the space beyond which we cannot place our construction material. He suggested to work within the limit allowed by the Karachi University.  The Dean & Director said that Karachi University has given three months time.  He asked Mr. Mahmood Ali to ask the Contractor to complete the work which requires outside space in 3 months and not to violate it.

 

3.3    The Registrar suggested to include comments also in the progress report which Mr. Mahmood Ali agreed to do.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that they are preparing BoQ for the furniture and would give it to Mr. Abdul Rehman on April 22, 2015.

 

DECISION & ACTION:

 

3.4    The Committee noted the up-date as given by Mr. Mahmood Ali. 

 

3.5    Mr. Mahmood Ali would ask the Contractor to complete the work in 3 months time given by the Karachi University and not to go beyond the space allowed for keeping construction material.

 

3.6    The Contract for the furniture of the new block should be awarded immediately so that it is ready by the end of August.

         

 

Item No.4 Presentation on up-gradation of the existing Canteen at Main Campus.

         

        4.1    Mr. Imran Mumtaz said that before they prepare a presentation for the EC, he wanted to   know if the existing canteen is to be up-graded like the NBP Building because in that       case the cost estimate would be around Rs.4 to 5 million. The Dean & Director said   that we cannot spend so much money on the canteen renovation but would like that it      should not appear like an eye sore.  Mr. Imran Mumtaz said that there are two options;     either to follow the existing theme or just make a simple paint. The Dean & Director     suggested to match the paint with the color of other buildings.

 

DECISION

       

4.2    The existing canteen would be painted so that its color matches with the color of other         buildings.

        

4.3    Mr. Mahmood Ali would get the existing canteen at Main Campus painted.
