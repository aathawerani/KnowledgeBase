Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Escalation Claims by M/s. Technologist on Boys’ Hostel and City Campus Academic Block Renovation Projects.

 

Boys’ Hostel Project Main Campus

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the Boys’ Hostel Project was started in February 2008 and was completed in March 2010. Original completion period allocated was 8 months, whereas the project was finally completed in 27 months. The extension in time of completion was granted based on the genuineness of the reasons for delays as certified by the Architects (M/s. Icon) and their Consultants and Project Manager (M/s. Integ).  The first extension was approved by Audit & Finance Committee in its meeting held on March 9, 2009. He said that the A&FC was not approached after that as the project progress had been totally dependant on the availability of spaces and areas of work and full attention was concentrated on piecemeal work completion. He said that the cost at which the work was awarded to M/s. Technologist was Rs.70 Million (which was 1.145 % lower than the Engineers’ estimate) and the completion cost as certified by Project Managers is Rs.69.153 Million inclusive of all kinds of variations. The contractors, however, requested for compensation for escalated prices on materials used in construction and refurbishment works.  He said that they have filed the claims in June 2010, for Rs.7.8 Million, which is 11.27% above the cost of the project.

 

Renovation of Academic Block at City Campus

 

1.2       The Director Projects stated that the Renovation of Academic Block was started in February 2008 and was completed in May 2009. Originally the construction period allotted was 4 months whereas the project was completed in 17 months.  The extension in time of completion was granted based on the genuineness of the reasons for delays as certified by the Architects (M/s. Icon) & their Consultants and the Project Managers (M/s. Integ).  First extension was approved by Audit & Finance Committee in its meeting held on March 9, 2009. A&FC was not approached for further grant of extension because project progress had been totally dependant on the availability of spaces and areas of work and full attention was concentrated on piecemeal work completion. He said that the cost at which the work was awarded was Rs.47.5 Million (1.02 %  higher than  the Engineers’ estimate) and completion cost as certified by the Project Manager is Rs.43.678 Million including all kinds of variations.

 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that in March 2010, the Contractor requested for compensation for escalated prices of materials used in the project.  Later in June 2010, the Contractor submitted the details of the claim amounting to Rs.13.4 Million, which is 30.67% over the completed cost of the project.

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that both the claims were forwarded to the Project Managers, who asked for detailed documents from the Contractors along with the rate analysis of the items. All those documents were sent to the Project Managers. After scrutiny the Project Managers sent back the documents to IBA Office soliciting IBA’s advice as to categorization of items on which the escalation may be allowed.

 

1.5       The Director Projects said that there is no clause of escalation in the tender documents and it will become a point of sheer judgment to choose the items on which escalation is to be allowed. It needs a thorough study of escalation laws allowed by Pakistan Engineering Council and legality of escalation for which they need competent Consultants who have experienced contracts specialists. He further said that the Architects, their Consultants and the Project Managers are the organizations already involved in certifying bill payments and the time extensions. Repeatedly M/s. Integ had shown their inability to process the escalation based on the reason that this is not part of their responsibility.

 

1.6       The Director Projects proposed that as the Project Manager (M/s. Integ) are not interested to process the claim, the whole case may be sent to one of the following pre-qualified Consultants / Project Managers:

 

·         M/s. NESPAK

·         M/s. Consult-Tech International

·         M/s. AAA Associates

·         M/s. Q.S. Consultants

 

1.7       The Director Projects said that the above Consultants have experienced contracts cells and hoped that they would give a proper professional advice.  He said that a certain percentage of the claimed amount will be paid to the Consultant which would be deducted from the agreed claimed amount. He requested the Executive Committee to permit the processing of the claims through one of the above Consultants. He further requested the EC to recommend the extension of the time and completion cost of both projects to A&FC, as detailed above.

 

1.8       The Director proposed said that since M/s. Icon and M/s. Integ have done the Projects, they are in a better position to judge.  He also suggested to apply the Pakistan Engineering Council’s escalation formula.

 

1.9       The Director Projects said that they have not included the PEC formula in the Contract so they cannot apply it.  He further said that the contract was signed in 2007 when there was no escalation clause but now the escalation clause has been included in all contract agreements.

 

1.10     The Director asked the Director Finance if it can be checked by Pre-Audit department whether the claims for escalated prices are justifiable or not.  The Director Finance replied in the affirmative.

 

1.11     The Director Projects said that it is an extensive exercise. He said that the Consultants have record of the rates of all the material at different period.

 

DECISION

 

1.12     The Committee decided that an independent consultant be assigned this task and the fee should be in fixed amount rather than in percentage of the claimed amount.

 

ACTION

 

1.13     The Director Projects would obtain quotations from the four Consultants noted in 1.6 above and then he would assign the job to the lowest bidder.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the Promotion Rules for non-teaching management and non-management employees.

 

3.1       The Director H.R. said that Mr. Adnan Hameed, Assistant Manager H.R. has worked on the draft Promotion Rules for non-teaching management and non-management employees. She requested Mr. Adnan Hameed to present the Policy.

 

3.2       The Director said that they are creating two tracks of promotion; one is the seniority based promotions in which upon completing a certain time period an employee may be promoted to the next step. Another is promotion to vacant position through competition. For example if someone is in level 1 and level 3 position is advertised and the incumbent fulfils the criteria of level 3 he/she would be given the chance to compete with outside candidates.  He said that this he had tried while he was in State Bank of Pakistan and it proved to be a great catalyst for increase in productivity. Under this incentive system several persons prepare themselves and improve their skills to compete for the higher level job while only one person is promoted. The skills acquired or qualifications improved by other candidates contribute to higher productivity of the entire organization.  He said that we may induct alumni in the Interview for these positions.

 

3.3       Mr. Adnan Hameed, Assistant Manager H.R. described the purpose, scope, types of promotion, general guidelines on basis of promotion, general criteria for promotion, promotion ladder, promotion procedure etc. in detail.

 

3.4       The Director ICT pointed out that for IT staff new position of “specialist” was created but it has not been shown in the draft policy. He also suggested to include acquiring of relevant advanced level academic qualification in the explanation for “Demonstrated Potential” parameter.  During discussion on this matter, the Director IBA mentioned that IBA encourages its employees to acquire higher education.  On the request of the Director HR, he clarified that the financial assistance for higher education would only be provided to employees in case they get admission in IBA on merit, he further stated that educational assistance would not be provided to employees who are enrolled in any other institution.  The Director IBA also suggested to re-circulate IBA’s policy on fee concession among employees.

3.5       Referring to one of the eligibility criteria for promotion i.e. requiring at least one year’s service at IBA, Mr. Mobin Khalili said that it is against the principle of open merit. He said that if the internal candidate meets the requirements specified in the ad, he / she should be given the opportunity to compete with other candidates.

 

3.6       The Director agreed with Mr. Khalili and asked Mr. Adnan Hameed to remove such conditions which may be discriminatory as they want to provide equal employment opportunity to everyone. He also suggested to mention BPS grade for regular employees in the General Guidelines for Movement onto the next job band.

 

3.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that one of the parameters i.e. Exposure within IBA, requires working with different departments of IBA.  However, he said that in the past some employees were transferred to various departments mostly because of their inefficiency. For them the proposed parameter would be in their advantage.  

 

3.8       The Director H.R. said that they have given the least weightage to Exposure within IBA and the past performance of the employee would also be considered while considering him/her for promotion.

   

            DECISION

 

3.9       The Committee decided that after incorporating the following decisions, the Promotion Policy should be sent to all staff seeking their comments and after incorporating the reasonable suggestions it should be presented to the Selection Board:

 

·         The position of “Specialist” to be included in the corresponding job band / technical family.

·         The position of “Officer” in Job Band 1 should be renamed as “Senior Executive”, and the Senior Officer, and Officer in the Band Step should be renamed as “Senior Executive II, and Senior Executive I” respectively.

·         The positions of “Senior Executive, and Executive” in the Band Step of J3 to be renamed as “Executive II, and Executive I” respectively.  

·         Relevant Academic qualifications should also be included in the weightage on “Demonstrated Potential”.

·         The requirement of one year’s service and related conditions for internal candidate should be deleted.

·         Technical titles should also be mentioned in the General Guidelines for Movement onto the next job band.

           

            ACTION

 

3.10     The Director H.R. after incorporating the above decisions would send the Promotion Policy to all staff members for their comments and finally present it to the Selection Board.

b)     Approval for Internet and VPN layer 3 bandwidth.

 

4.16     The Director ICT reported that IBA is using two separate internet links at each campus from different internet service providers. These internet links are redundant to support failover internet strategy.  He said that at Main Campus they have two internet links, one internet of 35 Mbps has been taken from Internet Service Provider Wateen; and another internet link has been in operation which has been taken from HEC based PERN2 internet service. PERN2 was of 35 Mbps internet bandwidth, but now HEC has upgraded 35 Mbps internet link to three times and now it is 105 Mbps on same cost.

 

4.17     He further said that at City Campus they have two internet links, one internet of 35 Mbps has been taken from Internet Service Provider Wateen; another internet link of 6 Mbps is operational which has been taken from Transworld as failover mode.

 

4.18     The Director ICT suggested that since HEC has upgraded internet link from 35 Mbps to 105 Mbps on cost of 35 Mbps, the internet services by Wateen at Main Campus and Transworld internet services at City Campus be discontinued.  He further said that for a video conference meeting with HEC, HEC acknowledged that they have floated tender to provide fiber optic for internet at City Campus.  He said that once IBA upgrades HEC intranet link from 20 Mbps to 60 Mbps layer 3 VPN, they may discontinue internet services from Wateen at City campus as well. Both campuses will share internet bandwidth of 105 Mbps provided by HEC PERN2. Thus, there would be only one ISP for internet and layer VPN between the two campuses and they would have a total of 105 Mbps internet bandwidth and 60 Mbps Intranet bandwidth for campuses VPN connectivity.

4.19     The Director ICT further said that HEC has assured that they have back bone link from two international ISP (Transworld and PTCL), which will reduce the downtime and there would be no need for backup link.
