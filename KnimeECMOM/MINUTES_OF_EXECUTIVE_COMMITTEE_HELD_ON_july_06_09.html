The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider and approve cancellation of bids for renovation work of Auditorium at Main Campus. 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the Audit and Finance Committee already approved the award of work for detail designing, tender documents and top supervision to M/s ICON and detail supervision and project management work to M/s Integ for the renovation of Auditorium at Main Campus. He stated that after preparation of Tender Documents and drawing, tender documents were issued to the following three pre-qualified Contractors on May 02, 2009: 

1.      M/s. Abaseen Construction company

2.      M/s. A.S Enterprises

3.      M/s. Gulf Construction

1.2       The Director Projects stated that a technical proforma was also issued to Contractors along with tender documents as per instruction of BoG. The contractors were advised to submit the technical and financial proposals separately duly signed and sealed. The pre-bid meeting was held on May 13, 2009 in which the Contractors requested to extend the bid submission date which was extended from May 19, 2009 to May 22, 2009. All three Contractors submitted their bids on May 22, 2009. Technical proposal of all three bidders were opened on May 22, 2009 and sent to Project Manager and Consultants for their evaluation. The Project Manager and the Consultant submitted their reports on May 27, 2009. Based on the technical evaluation report the financial bids were opened on May 28, 2009 in the presence of Consultant, Project Manager, IBA official and representative of contractors. 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that the following were the standings of the contractors after opening of financial bids: 

1.  M/s. A.S Enterprises                      Rs.81,427,819             1st lowest

2.      M/s.  Abaseen Construction          Rs.93,146,420             2nd lowest 

3.      M/s. Gulf Construction.                Rs.97,025,652             Highest 

1.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the financial bids were sent to Consultant and Project Manager for their evaluation on May 28, 2009. The Project Manager and Consultant submitted their report on June 15, 2009. He said that when compared with the estimates of the year 2007, it was found that the lowest bidder was 58% higher than the 2007 estimate. Architects and consultants revised their estimates considering the deletion of the gallery which was in original design and addition of a few works such as extension of lobby and the fire suppression system. He said that the revised estimate amounted to Rs.76.340 Million. They however could not substantiate and explain the main variations which according to them were due to following factors: 

1.   Increase due to devaluation of Rupee                  Rs. 6.0 Million

      2.   Increase due to general escalation                        Rs. 2.5  Million

3.   Water and electricity expenditure of bidder  

      During execution                                                  Rs. 1.2 Million

4.      Day and night working of the contractor             Rs. 2.7 Million

5.      Design changes                                                     Rs. 1.6 Million 

1.5       The Director Projects observed that the amount mentioned at Sr.# 3 & 4 had no basis and relation to cost of works. Similarly no justification was provided for item # 5. He reported that in order to provide the estimates and the justifications of variations, the Architects took about 12 days, yet their report was found to lack substantial evidences and quantification of the high variation of about 61% over their estimate of 2007. He said that the fire fighting system was not included but the Consultants had put in without consulting them. 

1.6       The Director Projects presented the analysis of the overall bids and the estimate furnished by the Project Managers M/s Integ. 

1.7       The Director Projects presented the following conclusions: 

a)   The basic cost estimate of the Auditorium is very high. It needs to be reviewed viz-a-viz the design. Design may need some revisions and adjustments to reduce the cost

b)      As the estimate needs to be rationalized, the present bids cannot be compared with the present estimate, which is unrealistic.

c)      Changes in BOQ cannot be made now.

d)     Under the above circumstances, all bids will have to be cancelled and new bids be invited with revised BOQ and specifications.

1.8              On the basis of the conclusion, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the following: 

a)      Cancel all the bids

b)      To ask the Architects to rationalize the design and cost estimate

c)      To re-bid on the basis of new BOQ

1.9              The Director said that we have to understand the component in which there is cost escalation. He was of the opinion that placing another bid and cancelling this bid will further delay the work.  He said that as long as there is no difference in the BOQ, the Director Projects can compare the cost estimates. 

1.10          The Director Projects reported that they will have to change the BOQ.  The Registrar suggested that IBA has to give its own requirements and on the basis of that a fresh BOQ will have to be prepared. He further said that the Consultant being professionals should give proper advice and design options instead of being rigid. He said that they should be advised to review their design to meet IBAs budget Dr. Arshad Siddiqui agreed to the observations and recommendations of Director Projects and the Registrar. 

DECISION

1.11          The Committee approved the recommendations to cancel the bids.  

1.12          The Committee requested the Registrar to provide the requirements to the Director Projects which shall be communicated to the Architects.

1.13          The Director Projects will table the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the A&FC to float new tenders based on revised design by IBA and request them to cancel the present bids. 

ACTION 

1.14          The Registrar will provide the requirements to the Director Projects for preparing a fresh BOQ.  

1.15          The Director Projects will place the recommendation of the Executive Committee for cancellation of bids before the A&FC.

Item No. 4      To consider draft policy on Welfare Fund for IBA employees.

Purpose of the Welfare Fund:

4.1       The Director H.R. presenting the draft of the IBA employees welfare fund policy stated that the objective of the IBA Staff Welfare Fund is to help employees to cater to the following emergency needs:

a)      Medical expense (amount which is not covered by IBAs Medical Entitlement Policy).

b)      Natural disaster and other unforeseen catastrophic or tragic events.

c)      Marriage in the employees family.

d)     House repairs.

e)      Education of employees/ or their children.

Structure of the Welfare Fund:

4.2       She stated that the structure of the fund will be as under:

a)      IBA will contribute 0.15% - 0.20% of the total IBA revenue every year to this Fund.

b)      A separate bank account will be maintained and administered by Finance Department.

c)      The finances of the Fund shall not be used for purposes other than those listed above, nor be merged with other funds of the Institute.

d)     The Welfare Fund will be granted as an interest-free loan (refundable by the employee).

e)      The loan will be up to a maximum amount of Rs.10,000/-.

f)       Loan will be repaid in a maximum of 10 equal instalments, to be deducted from salary.

Eligibility: 

4.3         The following will be the eligibility for the Welfare Fund Loan: 

a)      Employee must have completed the probation period of 3 months in the capacity of a full-time employee (regular or contract).

b)      Employees monthly gross salary should not be more than Rs.25,000/-.

c)      Employee does not owe any previous loan to the Institute.

d)     Employee is not involved in any disciplinary misconduct, or violation of any rules and regulations of IBA.

4.4       The Director H.R. also presented the procedure for processing the application for both regular and contract employees.  

4.5       The Director Finance submitted that an amount of Rs.1 million (i.e. 0.20% of the total IBA revenue) has already been provided for in the Annual Budget of IBA 2009-10. He suggested that the total fund may be divided into 2 segments. 

a)         80% for Refundable loan

b)         20% Non Refundable grants/Aid

4.6       The Director Finance informed the Committee that IBA will contribute each year similar amount in the range of 0.20% of the total IBA revenue which will be approximately Rs.1 million yearly. He suggested that employees should also contribute in the Employees welfare Fund to develop healthy fund so that more employees can be benefited. The monthly contribution from employees may be @ 0.10% of the gross pay. Thus, the total annual contribution would be around Rs.200,000/-.

4.7       The Director Finance further suggested that the Welfare fund may be granted in any of the two options: 

a)         As an interest-free loan (refundable by the employee)

b)         As a non-refundable grant to the employee (for lower grade employee only) who suffer from natural disaster or of similar nature (e.g. earthquake, victim of terrorism etc)

4.8       The Director Finance also suggested that the loan ceiling should be up to three salaries of an individual employee which will be repaid in maximum of 12 to 18 equal installment to be deducted from salary.        

4.9       He also said that the total amount contributed by the employee during his / her tenure with IBA will be refunded to the employee at the time of leaving the institute.

 

4.10     Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the employees getting a gross salary of more than Rs.25,000/- may also be given the option of contributing to this fund so that they may make some monthly savings. However, such persons will not be the beneficiary of the Welfare Fund Loan.

 

4.11     The Director said that the procedure for disbursement of loan should be transparent and the employees should be made aware that it is in their own interest.

 

DECISION

 

4.12     The Committee approved the policy along with the modifications suggested by the Director Finance and Dr. Sayeed Ghani.

 

ACTION

 

4.13     The Director Finance will revise the draft policy and present it to the A&FC for approval.
