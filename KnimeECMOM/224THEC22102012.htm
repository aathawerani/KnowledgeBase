Item No. 2     Presentation by the Architects M/s. NBCL on the conceptual design of the pavilion of Cricket ground, Main Campus.  

2.1     The Director Projects said that he has invited the representatives of NBCL to present the conceptual design of the pavilion of cricket ground of UBL Sports Arena, at Main Campus as an alternate to the earlier design which was rejected due to high cost. Earlier the design was a part of the Master plan, which will now be replaced with the new design, estimated budget for which has been kept as Rs.10 million. 

2.2     Mr. Zayed presented the conceptual design of the pavilion of cricket ground.  He said that originally two pavilions were designed but later one was dropped because of budget constraint while the other one was converted into open seating platforms.  He said that the pavilion will have a seating capacity of 450 persons and there would be two boxes but there would be no AC at the moment. There would be medical room (physio) on both sides. 

2.3     To a question if there is any toilet facility for the students / visitors, Mr. Zayed said that the toilets are there for the players but there is no toilet for the students / spectators. Mr. Zayed was advised to make available toilet facilities for the students / visitors also.  It was suggested that one of the two medical rooms may be converted into toilet.  Mr. Zayed said that a toilet can be made on the back side of the store.  He said that they would look into it. 

2.4     The Director Projects said that there should be more than two showers.  However, Mr. Zafar Siddiqi said that two showers are enough because international level game is not to be played there.  The Dean & Director wanted to know if it would be international level ground. Mr. Zayed replied that it would be university level ground.      

2.5     Mr. Mehmood Bughio suggested that for announcement sound system should be provided.  The Director Projects said that for commentators box sound system is needed; one speaker would be required. He suggested that the commentators box should be ventilated but provision for AC should also be made.  He said that food will be served to the team in the dressing room, but there should be some place for keeping food and eatables.

2.6     Mr. Zayed said that they have proposed electronic score board but it is a concept, its actual cost is not known.  The Dean & Director said that if it is within the budget then fine otherwise we would go for manual score board.  

DECISION 

2.7     The Committee approved the conceptual design of the pavilion of cricket ground of UBL Sports Arena, at Main Campus along with the suggestions noted above. The Consultant would ensure that the cost does not exceed the budget.   

Item No. 3     Discussion and decision on the observations received on the prayer hall during walk through held on October 17, 2012.  

3.1     The Director Projects reported that as a result of walk through held on October 17, 2012, the following observations were received on the prayer hall: 

·         Capacity of females of 27 Nos. is too small, it should be increased.

·         There should be a separate partition between the male and female prayer hall.

·         Registrar pointed that the water body is being considered which will be a wastage of area as it is very difficult to maintain it. He further advised to remove one water body, out of two and convert it to the ladies prayer hall for its extension.

·         The ablution area of males and females should be separated from each other. In the drawings there are 02 Nos. ablution for females which are also in the open area and is quite visible for all.

·         The entrance of males and females should also be separate from each other.

·         The jaali placed as per the design should not be open and it should be covered with glass or any alternative arrangement. 

3.2     The Director H.R. said that the capacity of 27 females is sufficient. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that ablution area for the females should be covered so that they are not exposed and their privacy should be ensured. 

3.3     Mr. Zayed said that they may convert water body area into plantation area.  However, the Registrar observed that the plant will attract insects. Mr. Zayed said that they can place screen but that would add to the cost.  

DECISION 

3.4     The Committee decided that at females ablution area screen will be placed to ensure privacy, and the water body area would be converted into plantation area.  

ACTION 

3.5     The Director Projects would get the above decisions implemented through the Architects.   

Item No. 4     To consider and approve the extension of project management services of M/s. AAA Partnership on renovation of FCS Building, City Campus.  

4.1     The Director Projects said that the contract for the Project Management Services was awarded to M/s AAA Partnership on December 2010 after approval of IBA Authorities; initially the contract duration was 18 months for the complete renovation of FCS Building, City Campus which includes the one month post bid and one month after completion of project. However, he said that due to bifurcation of FCS building project into phases the contractor was awarded the job of Phase-I for the period of 12 months from June 16, 2011 at a cost of Rs.61,081,747/- Later on it was decided by the management to execute the renovation work of Phase-II for which  extension was given to contractor, and the project cost increased from Rs.61,081,747/- to Rs.70,103,968/-. Similarly the duration of project increased from 12 months to 22 months up till March 2013.  

4.2     As per contract of Project Manager, the services duration was 16+2 months, 16 months for Execution which started from June 16, 2011 and expired on October 16, 2012. He said that according to the original contract they have to pay the Project Management services fee @3.75% of awarded cost of Rs.61,081,747 for the period of 12 months as the contract of contractor M/s. Prefab was awarded for 12 months. 

Awarded cost                                                           Rs.61,081,747

Project Manager Fee @3.75%                                     Rs.  2,290,565 

4.3     The Director Projects reported that since the contract cost and duration of contractor has been revised the project Manager Fee and time duration also require revision. The following are the basis for revision: 

Revised Awarded Cost                                               Rs.73,043,097

Total Revised Project Manager Fee  @3.75%                 Rs.  2,739,116 

Cost Impact due to Revised Cost                                    Rs.      448, 551 

4.4     The Director Projects said that after October 2012 only the supervision charges will be paid to Project Managers in order to continue their services. He said that currently IBA is paying Rs.133,616/- per month but after negotiations with Project Manager they agreed to reduce their supervision charges to Rs.100,924/- per month for the extended period up till March 2012. So the cost impact due to extended period will be Rs.504,620/- and the total cost impact for both extended period and revised cost increase will be Rs.953,171/-                       

4.5     The Director Projects reported that the case was presented on October 19, 2012 to the Central Purchase Committee for approval. The CPC after reviewing all the terms and conditions and details approved the additional cost impact of Rs.953,171/-.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the above additional cost impact for the Project Management services of M/s AAA Partnership (Pvt.) Limited. 

4.6     The Director Projects said that he has asked M/s. AAA Partnership to complete the project by December 2012. The Dean & Director said that if they do not complete the project by December 2012 then penalty should be imposed on them.  He further said that if the Project Manager is saying that they would complete the project by December 2012 then why are they being given extension till March 2013.  The Director Projects replied that M/s. AAA Partnership have said that they would complete the project by March 2013 but the Project Department has written them that they would be penalized if they do not complete the project by December 2012. However, he said that two months time is taken after the completion of the project for rechecking and finalizing the bills so it would be completed by February 2013. The Dean & Director said that extension upto February 2013 and not till March 2013 be given. 

DECISION 

4.7     The Committee approved extension till February 2013 in project management services of M/s. AAA Partnership on renovation of FCS Building, City Campus along with its total financial impact of (Rs.953,171 -  Rs.100,924) Rs.852,247/- 

Item No. 5     Up-date by the Director ICT on CISCO embargo.  

5.1     The Director ICT reported that they had a very successful but very hectic visit to Dubai; to resolve the issue of embargo on CISCO. He said that it was discovered that the embargo was not on IBA it was on the fact that the equipment for IBA was being delivered to Dubai. This was the point of contention for EU. He said that if they get the equipment delivered directly to Karachi there would not be an embargo problem.  

5.2     The Director ICT said that they will have to look at the custom issues as the importer will be Technology Partner whereas the equipment will be coming in the name of IBA.  Also as to the payments related issues as the full payment is to be made by the Technology Partner.  He reported that AMFCO brought up the point that even though Customs duties exemption is allowed by Customs but Excise and Taxation Department does not accept these exemptions and as a result the Technology Partner had to pay approximately Rs.200,000/- as excise duty on COMSCOPE shipment.  

5.3     The Dean & Director said that IBA is exempted from Federal Government Excise duty but for provincial government we will have to pay the duty. He wanted to know as to when the equipment will be received at IBA.  The Director ICT said that within four weeks time the equipment will be available.  However, he said that he would give the exact date in the next meeting of EC. 

DECISION 

5.4     The Committee noted the up-date given by the Director ICT.  

Item No. 6     Any other item with the permission of the Chair. 

a)           Verification of experience certificate of students admitted into MBA Program. 

6.1     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the two years experience certificate submitted by the students admitted into MBA program needs to be checked and verified because some of them may be fictitious.     

6.2     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is a set procedure of document verification.  He said that experience certificate verification should be done by the Enrolment Section in the same way as is done for the educational certificates but the Admission Committee has taken a decision that experience certificates are checked at the time of interview therefore the Enrolment Section needs not to get it verified. The Dean & Director said that since Enrolment Section does verification of educational certificates it should also verify experience certificate.  But it should be done before we offer admission.  Mr. Mobin Khalili said that enrolment work starts after the admission is completed and Enrolment Forms are received from the Program Offices. Enrolment Sections job is to scrutinize the educational documents received along with enrolment forms to ensure that newly admitted students meet the admission criteria as per the approved policy. Their educational documents are also sent to respective boards and universities for verification of its authenticity. He further said that there is no separate Enrolment Section; the Degree Section is doing this voluntarily. He said that with only two staff members in the Degree Section preoccupied in their own work, it is not possible to provide assistance to the Testing Service or the Admission Committee during the admission process. 

6.3     The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to get experience certificates of all those who qualify in the test verified before calling them for interview.  He said that the experience certificates of those admitted into the new batch should also be verified.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he would help the Registrar in this regard. The Registrar said that he would need help of two persons for a short period for getting the experience certificates verified. 

DECISION 

6.4     The Committee decided that the experience certificates of those who qualify the test for MBA would be verified by the Registrars Office before the candidates are called for interview. 

6.5     The Registrar would get the experience certificates of those admitted into the MBA Program this year verified. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would help the Registrar in experience certificate verification process.       

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
