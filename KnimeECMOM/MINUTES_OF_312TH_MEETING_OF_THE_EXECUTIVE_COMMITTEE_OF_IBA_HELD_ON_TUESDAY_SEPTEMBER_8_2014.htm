The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve the Budget allocation for Architect’s Fee for designing tender documentation for the proposed Faculty Apartments at Staff Colony.

 

1.1       The Director Projects said that he had suggested in the 263rd meeting of Executive Committee held on September 2, 2013 that since the proposal for faculty apartments at Staff Town was approved by the Board, Mr. Shameem Ahmed be requested to get its designing and tender prepared so that as and when fund was available for this project the work could be started which would save at least 4 months time required to complete the designing and tender preparation.  The only cost involved at this stage would be the fee to the Architect.  The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee agreed to his proposal and decided that Mr. Shameem Ahmed be requested to get this preliminary work of designing and tender preparation of faculty apartments done. However, at the request of the Project Director (HEC Projects) the matter was kept in abeyance due to priority given to other projects.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that the Consultants (Firco) have now sent an offer to undertake work of the designing and tender documentation based on approval of their negotiated fee in 2013, according to which designing fee was 3.8% and detailed supervision as 3% of the cost of the Project. Total of 6.8% against their bid of 8.3% which was the lowest.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that the estimated cost of the project is Rs.131 million (Covered area 52,750 sft at Rs.2500/- per sft). As such total designing fee @ 3.8.% will be Rs.5.0 million while the fee for the present scope of work pertaining to detailed designing, tender documentation, preparation of architectural structure, electrical, and plumbing works including BOQ shall be paid at 70% of the total fee as Rs.3.5 million.

 

1.4       The Director Projects said that the above amount has not been allocated in the budget for the year 2014-15. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the following:

 

a)      Fee for designing, tender documentation to be provided in the current budget 2014-15 so that order could be placed to Architects.

b)      Allocation of additional Rs.3.5 million in the current budget.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director asked if we had signed any agreement with Firco for designing and tender documentation.  He said that they have to find out if EC authorized the Director HEC Projects that on the basis of the competition of designs, Firco be given contract to design then the EC may consider payment otherwise not.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.6       The Director Projects would check if the Director HEC Projects was authorized by EC that on the basis of the competition of designs, Firco be given contract to design the project and an agreement has been signed with Firco then the EC may consider payment otherwise not.

 

Item No. 2    To consider and approve Convocation Invitation Card Write Up and the Tag Line.

 

2.1       The Registrar presented the design, text and the minute-to-minute program of the proposed Invitation Card for the Convocation 2014. He asked if they could write on the card that the Honourable President of Pakistan will be on the Convocation.

 

2.2       The Dean & Director said that we should mention on the invitation card that the Honourable President will be on the Convocation. He suggested that all the donors should also be invited to the Convocation.

 

2.3       About the sequencing of the program, the Registrar said he has proposed that after the Valedictorian’s Speech, Doctoral Degree would be conferred on the Chief Guest.  However, the Committee decided that sequence should be as follows:

2.6       The Committee also decided that all the donors would be invited to the Convocation. 

 

2.7       The Committee approved the following tag line for Convocation 2014:

 

            “EXCELLENCE ENVISIONED: EXPLORING NEW HORIZONS”.

Item No.4       To consider and approve the recommendations of the Claim Settlement                 Committee on the claim submitted by M/s Consult – Tech International on three projects.

 

4.1       The Director Projects stated that M/s. Consul-Tech International had submitted claims regarding their payments for providing extended services beyond their contract period with HEC Projects Department to the tune of Rs.8.321 million in addition to the fee already paid to them on:

 

a)      New Boys Hostel

b)      Visiting Faculty Residences 

c)      New Girls Hostel

 

4.2       These claims were submitted while the Director HEC Projects was incharge but as he left on 28th February 2014, the matter remained unsettled.  The case was presented in the 305th EC meeting held on July 14, 2014. As per instruction of Executive Committee, a Claim Settlement Committee comprising the following was formed to deal with the Claim of Consultants:

 

1.      Mr. Shabih Hiader, Lecturer Economics and Finance

2.      Ms. Fuaaza Qazi, E&C Engineer

3.      Syed Jehanzeb, Assistant Manager, Projects (Finance)

 

4.3       The Claim Settlement Committee was to scrutinize the claim submitted by M/s. Consult-   Tech International (CTI) on the above mentioned three projects completed by HEC     Projects Department, for their additional services beyond the contract period.  The terms          of reference of the Committee were as follows:

 

· to Choose appropriate methodologies for claim substantiation or analysis.

· to identify if the claim has been lodged by CTI within its contract specified time.

· to study the steps taken by the Director HEC Projects in claim resolution through his communication with CTI

· to establishing contractual obligations of both CTI and Director HEC Projects

· to find if there is any counter claim filed by the Director HEC Projects during execution?

 

4.4       Mr. Jehanzeb reported that the Committee reviewed all the available documents and         contract agreement made with CTI for carrying out consultancy of HEC projects and   came up with the following conclusions:

 

o   It was found that contract agreement was made on percentage basis and the payment terms were on milestones achieved. Other Project Managers usually have payments terms on monthly basis, which they use as claims for additional fees.

o   The fee of Project Managers (3% of contract Value) was subject to cap of contract cost to the maximum of 10% of or the cost of completion whatsoever was lowest.

o   It was found that according to Contract agreement CTI has already been paid the fee as per the contract cost however their additional fee as per completion cost comes to Rs.778,918.95, whereas CTI is claiming an amount of Rs.8,321,905.00 million for their extended period.

o   After detailed discussion the Committee came to the conclusion that their claim for extended period is baseless however they may be paid for the Post construction and Pre-Construction period which were beyond their contract.

4.5       The Claim Settlement Committee requested the Executive Committee to allow Director    Projects to negotiate with CTI as per the recommended cost of Rs.2.39 million (working shown in Annexure-II).

4.6       Mr. Jehanzeb said that if we divide their claim of Rs.8.32 million by three it comes to Rs.2.7 million. The Dean & Director said that it is a logical approach because CTI was providing parallel services to the three projects with the same manpower.

DECISION

4.7      The Committee endorsed the recommendation of the Claim Settlement Committee and     requested the Director Projects and Mr. Jehanzeb to negotiate with the Contractors on Rs.2.39 million.  The   EC authorized the Director Projects to negotiate payment upto 1/3rd of their claim i.e. Rs.2.7 million.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
