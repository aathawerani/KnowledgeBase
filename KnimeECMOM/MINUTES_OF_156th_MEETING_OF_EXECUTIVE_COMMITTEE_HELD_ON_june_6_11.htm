MINUTES OF 156TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, JUNE 6, 2011

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                   Dean & Director

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the Selection of Contractor for Supply & Installation of Passenger Elevators for Administration Building, City Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee in its 124th Meeting held on October 18, 2010, approved the appointment of M/s. KP & Associates as Consultant for project management and supervision for the task of supply and installation of two passenger elevators for IBA buildings. At their request M/s. KP & Associates prepared the tender documents. Seven contractors who were pre-qualified for the supply and Installation of elevators, were sent invitation. Only the following four contractors collected the tender document on specified date and time which was from March 21 to April 04, 2011:

 

a.       M/s. Reliance Engineering Services.

b.      M/s. Islamabad Industrial and Trading.

c.       M/s. Merin (Private) Limited.

d.      M/s. Marketing Centre (Private) Limited.

 

1.2       The pre-bid meeting was held on April 11, 2011 and tenders were opened on April 25, 2011. The bids were submitted by only two bidders (S. No. a & b) on specified date and time. The bids were opened in front of IBA official’s contractor representatives and Consultant. The price quoted by them is mentioned below:

 

a)         M/s. Islamabad Industrial & Trading:             Rs.2,754,786/-.

b)         M/s. Reliance Engineering Services:               Rs.2,170,000/-.

 

1.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Consultants M/s KP & Associates recommended the acceptance of bid of lower tender i.e. M/s. Reliance Engineering Services on the basis of their past experience and market reputation. He reported that in order to further verify the consultant’s recommendation, a joint visit by IBA projects team along with Mr. Khalid Pervez of M/s. KP & Associates, Architects M/s. NBCL, representatives of M/s. Reliance Engineering Services and Project Managers for Aman projects was undertaken on May 21, 2011 to a hospital to inspect the lift installed there by Reliance Engineering Services. The staff’s feedback regarding operation and post installation maintenance performance was found to be satisfactory. A few salient features of the elevator whose price has been quoted by Reliance are mentioned hereunder:

 

·         Make:                    Suzho Fuji (Japan)

·         Capacity:               450 kg/6 persons

·         Speed:                   1.0 meter/second

·         Area:                     3’-4” x 3’-11”

·         Finish:                   Stainless steel with dull finish

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that the Technical Evaluation Committee as well as the Central Purchase Committee has approved M/s. Reliance Engineering Services being the lowest at a cost of Rs.2,170,000/- and recommended to the EC for approval.

 

1.5       On the basis of supplier’s past experience and especially the post maintenance record, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee that the consultant’s recommendation for award of work of supply and installation of passenger elevator in Administration Building, City Campus to the lowest bidder M/s. Reliance Engineering services be accepted at a total price of Rs.2,170,000/-.

 

1.6       The Dean & Director wanted to know elevators of which companies have a good reputation in Pakistan.

 

1.7       The Director Projects replied that elevators of Hyundai and Schindler elevators are good but they are very costly.

 

1.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to why only two out of 4 contractors submitted the bid.

 

1.9       The Director Projects replied that the contractors wanted 120 days instead of 60 days time.

 

1.10     The Dean & Director observed that if the building is to be ready in ten months, then they can allow 120 days.  He suggested that the process should have been started in advance.

 

1.11     The Director Projects said that he has started the process in advance but the preparation of tender documents and their evaluation took time.

 

DECISION

 

1.12     The Committee approved the selection of contractor M/s. Reliance Engineering Services for Supply & Installation of two passenger elevators for Administration Building, City Campus at a cost of Rs.2,170,000/-.

 

 

Item No. 2                  To consider and approve award of work for Renovation and Refurbishment of FCS Building Phase-I at City Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the detailed designs and tender documents were prepared by the Architects M/s. ICON for the renovation and refurbishment of FCS Building Phase-I. The detailed design was scrutinized by Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership and their suggestions and amendments were then incorporated by the Architects. After finalization of Tender documents the invitation of tenders were floated to the following contractors:

 

1.      M/s. Abaseen Construction Company (Pvt.) Ltd.

2.      M/s. Al Shafi Enterprises

3.      M/s. Prefab Systems

4.      M/s. Project Managers

5.      M/s. The Technologist

6.      M/s. Total Construction

7.      M/s. Principal Builders

8.      M/s. Paragon Constructors Pvt. Ltd.

9.      M/s. Izhar Construction Pvt. Ltd.

10.  M/s. Gulf Constructors Pvt. Ltd.

 

2.2       Out of the above 10 contractors only the following 6 contracting firms collected the tender documents:

 

1)      M/s. Abaseen Construction Company (Pvt.) Ltd.

2)      M/s. Al Shafi Enterprises

3)      M/s. Prefab Systems

4)      M/s. Project Managers

5)      M/s. The Technologist

6)      M/s. Total Construction.

 

2.3       The Director Projects reported that in order to clarify the contract documents a pre-bid meeting was held on March 19, 2011 with all the contractors who collected the documents. In response to the queries of contractors pre-bid meeting minutes and 2 addendums were floated to contractors along with the extension in time of bid submission from March 24, 2011 to April 05, 2011. Among six pre-qualified contractors following five contractors submitted their bids on 5th April 2011:

 

1)         M/s. Abaseen Construction Company (Pvt.) Ltd.

2)         M/s. Al Shafi Enterprises

3)         M/s. Prefab Systems

4)         M/s. Project Managers

5)         M/s. The Technologist

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that the Tenders were opened on the same day i.e. April 5, 2011 at 3:00 p.m in front of IBA officials, representative of Project Managers, representative of consultants and the representative of contractors. The tender documents along with the technical details were then sent to Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership in order to prepare evaluation report and scrutinize the bid documents. The Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership submitted their detailed report on May 21, 2011 which was also reviewed by the Project Department.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that the Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership in their report assessed M/s. Prefab Systems as  the lowest evaluated responsive bidder and recommended that the work be awarded to them at the cost of Rs.61,081,747/-. The case was presented in Technical Evaluation Committee meeting held on May 28, 2011 for their scrutiny. The Technical Evaluation Committee recommended the approval of award to the lowest bidder. The case was then presented to Central Purchase Committee for their scrutiny. After the detailed scrutiny the CPC found the process in order and recommended to the Executive committee that the work be awarded to the lowest bidder as recommended by the Project Managers and the Technical Evaluation Committee.

 

2.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to recommend to the A&FC the ward of work to the lowest responsive bidder based on the recommendations of Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership, the Technical Evaluation Committee and the Central Purchase Committee to award the work to the lowest responsive bidder. 

 

2.7       The Dean & Director pointed out that he had suggested to keep the cost at Rs.48 million whereas the lowest bid is for Rs.61 million.

 

2.8       The Director Projects replied that he has included the cost of data cabling and electrical cabling so that when the ground floor is shifted to the first floor, they may easily perform their work.  He further said that he would try to reduce the cost by Rs.5 to Rs.6 million by exercising value engineering measures.

 

2.9       The Dean & Director said that since it is a dedicated building of FCS, Dr. Sayeed Ghani should be the Task Manager and Mr. M. Akmal Khan should oversee and report to Dr. Sayeed Ghani.  He said that for every project there will be a task manager and the Project Department will be involved from the point of view of infrastructure development and the implementation will be done by the Task Manager. For FCS building Dr. Ghani will be the Task Manager and he is authorized to take decisions and solve all the problems. The Dean & Director further said that for Admin Building, the Registrar will be the Task Manager.

 

DECISION

 

2.10     The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the ward of work for Renovation and Refurbishment of FCS Building Phase-I at City Campus to the lowest responsive bidder M/s. Prefab Systems.

 

2.11     The Committee requested the Director Projects to exercise value engineering measures to bring the cost down to Rs.55.0 million without compromising on the quality.

 

ACTION

 

2.12     The Director Projects would present the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the A&FC for consideration and approval.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve award of work to the lowest bidder for Renovation and Refurbishment of Staff Colony of IBA.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that a detailed survey of staff colony was made by IBA Projects department and Maintenance department in order to make a detailed scope of work for renovation and refurbishment of staff colony houses.  Realizing the existing conditions of the houses it was decided to undertake renovation of one house. Detailed meetings were held with the Maintenance Department in order to determine the essential requirements for the house to make it liveable and safe. In first phase House # C-18 the area of which is 1586.73 sft. was selected and after detailed study only vital areas and elements were selected to remain within a budget of Rs...... The essential items selected for complete renovation are:

·         Replacement of complete electrical wiring, main supply and the distribution box

·         Rebuilding complete kitchen including floor and wall tiles, complete water and sewerage pipes, all fittings and fixtures and construction of new kitchen cabinets

·         Re building of 2 bathrooms by replacing  old  floor with new tiles, installation of tiles on walls, replacement of all water supply pipes, sewerage  pipes, drainage pipes and bathroom fixtures and fittings

·         Installation of tiles on all floors of the house.

·         Replacement of old windows with Aluminium windows

·         Repair/replacement of doors including door frames

·         Replacement of worn out grills

·         Repair and cleaning of underground water tank and overhead water tank.

·         Exterior and interior paint

Item No. 5      To consider and approve the extra fee for changing the design and drawings of ground, first and second floors of Aman – CED building due to changes.

5.1       The Director Projects reported that the following changes have been communicated to the Architects to meet the requirements as advised by Assistant Director CED (1 and 2) and duly approved by the Director CED. Similarly changes #3, #4 and #5 advised by Mr. Imran Khan for housing the SAP research facilities were communicated to the Architects. The SAP requirements were duly approved by the Director CED and Dr Saeed Ghani, Associate Dean FCS:

 

1)      Conversion of offices on first and second floors into seminar hall type class rooms similar to the one already designed at the ground floor.

 

2)      Conversion of small class rooms into semicircular tiered seating.

 

3)      Conversion of three break out rooms on the ground floor into SAP facilities as per their requirements

 

4)      Conversion of ground floor administration office into break out room to compensate for the loss of the break out rooms.

 

5)      Conversion of two offices on first floor into administration office.

 

5.2       The Director Projects reported that the building is under construction and majority of the work on the electrical wiring and conduiting is completed. The work in the areas under revision has been stopped. The Architects have asked for a total compensation of Rs. 250,000/- for their revisions and re-designing as well as those for the AC (air-conditioning) and MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) Consultants. Originally they had demanded Rs.250,000/-  for #1 and #2. After negotiations they have agreed to do the changes for SAP in the same amount.

 

5.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know if there was a big hall for 250 persons which may serve for multipurpose where they can arrange programs like H.R. Forum which they had to arrange outside IBA because of non-availability of space.

 

5.4       The Director Projects said that at Students’ Centre there is a possibility of arranging programs for 250 persons.

 

DECISION

 

5.5       The Committee approved the extra fee of Rs.250,000/- for changing the design and drawings of ground, first and second floors of Aman – CED building due to changes identified in 5.1 above.
