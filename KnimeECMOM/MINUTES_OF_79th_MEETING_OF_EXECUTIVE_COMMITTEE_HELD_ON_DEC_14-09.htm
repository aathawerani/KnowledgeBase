b)   Approval for the purchase of UPS in one phase.

 

5.4       The Director ICT in his note of December 12, 2009 addressed to the Executive Committee stated that UPS requirements have increased due the following reasons:

·         All buildings shall be ready in the limited time frame and they have to provide the backup power facility to the IBA’s users for the continuity of the operations.

·         Existing UPS’s have lived their lives and are creating extra load on electricity.

·         Data Centers need their UPS’s ASAP in place and operational.

·         If we go with the phase wise purchasing process we will be exposed with foreign currency escalation risk.

·         If this contract expires and they go with the re-bidding, they cannot get the high level of discounts and special prices for education. They would not be able to meet user requirement and needs, creating a bad  image on IBA and also creating hurdles for IBA to achieve targets.

·         Urgency for the project is shown very clearly. ICT Department of IBA is buying complete infrastructure that is very high end and requires backup power on different places at both campuses.

·         Also there was no commitment of purchasing in phases before the SBD was floated.

·         According to the discussion with Finance and Audit team, IBA cannot be exposed with any Foreign Currency escalation Risk.  The phase wise purchasing could expose IBA to the above mentioned risk. 

·         The expected budget utilization for the year 2009-10, will remain within the approved available budget. All the payments of purchase made by the end of June 2010 would not be 100 % due to the fact that IBA would be holding the 10% as per the payment terms.  This 10 % would cover the purchase of all UPS’s by June 2010.

 

5.5       The Director ICT requested the Executive Committee to approve the purchase of UPS’s in one phase to eliminate the risk of foreign currency escalation and chance of losing the special discounts that IBA is getting as education institute.

 

5.6       The Director said that that earlier the understanding was to place all orders but the delivery would be taken in two years time. He further said that IBA is not interested in the dollar fluctuations as the tender was in Pak Rupee so it should be in Pak Rupee. He said that the Director ICT had committed that he would by UPS from next year’s budget.

 

5.7       Mr. M. Faisal Zia who attended the meeting as Director ICT’s nominee, stated that the Technology Partners may cancel the tender. In such case IBA will have to pay extra and will not be getting the special price.  He said that as orders were placed late, the payment terms of 2009-10 will be postponed. He said that although the A&FC and the Board had approved the award of contracts to the Technology Partners in July, 2009, the contracts were not placed on time for reasons for compliance with the PPRA rules and thus there had been delay in the placement of contracts.  Consequently, the payment terms have also spilled over to 2010-11. The UPS supplier is not willing to honour the delivery for the second phase because of dollar – Pak rupee fluctuations. He proposed that IBA should purchase the UPS in one phase in order to avail special price.  He said that the savings because of deferred payments on other tenders will be utilized in purchasing the UPS.

 

5.8       The Director said that the Board had re-appropriated Rs.151 million for IT plan implementation.  The assumption was that the tenders will be executed by that time but due to compliance with PPRA rules, it was delayed for six months. As the renovation of the buildings is nearing completion and due to the requirements of backup power, all the UPS may be purchased in one phase.        

 

DECISION

 

5.9              The Committee approved the proposal for purchasing UPS in one phase instead of two phases for the reasons as explained above.
