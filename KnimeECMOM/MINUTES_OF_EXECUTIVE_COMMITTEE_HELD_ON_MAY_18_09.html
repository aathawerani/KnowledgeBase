MINUTES OF THE 48th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MAY 18, 2009

The 48th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 18, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean
  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN            Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                           Director I.T.
  9. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                           Director CED
  10. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                          Director H.R.

Mr. Farrukh Naseem, Director M/s. Prolink attended by special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Selection of Consulting Architect for Multi-storeyed Building Complex at IBA.

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on May 11, 2009 advised him to open the financial bids of both the Architects. Results were as follows:

-   M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Ltd        Rs.    5,000,000

         -   M/s. F.N.M.H                                                Rs.    1,600,000

1.2       The Director projects reported that the baseline estimates proposed by him was Rs.350,000/-  He stated that the Committee after due deliberation had advised him to review its estimate and negotiate with M/s. F.M.N.H. at the same time.

1.3       The Director Projects stated that in order to determine a realistic baseline, a table of fee paid (or being paid) to M/s. ICON for the same type of work was prepared. According to the table of fee the Architects (M/s. ICON) have charged on an average Rs.24/- per sft for conducting need analysis, development of conceptual plans, estimates and perspectives.

1.4       The Director Projects stated that the work at multi-storeyed Complex (MSC) is almost 40% of the total assignment as described above and as such the fee for the assignment of (MSC) should be about Rs.10/- per sft. or Rs.2,000,000. He said that the fee quoted by F.M.N.H. is near to what M/s. ICON is charging. However, he said that negotiations shall be conducted, on approval of the above baseline.

DECISION

1.5              The Director said that the task assigned to the Consulting Architect for the Multistoreyed Building Complex at IBA is very limited as it will liaise with the architect of the project to ensure user specifications and standards are being followed whereas M/s. Icon have to prepare the design, detailed drawings, tender documents and also supervise the implementation therefore the two assignments are not comparable. He asked the Director Projects and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to negotiate with M/s. FNMH.

ACTION

1.6              The Director Projects and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would negotiate with M/s. FNMH.

Item No. 2      To discuss emails from Director Projects and the Director I.T. on the subject “A Humble Request”.

2.1       The Dean & Director taking note of the email sent by the Director Projects to the Director Finance and the Head of Internal Audit and copy to the EC regarding sequential objections raised by the Finance and the Audit Departments which were also endorsed by the Director I.T. stated that the process should be simplified.  He said that as decided earlier there should be a dedicated full time person in Finance Department who should do accounting, process the projects related bills and inform the concerned if there is any deficiency in documentation and resolve that issue so that there is no delay in the processing of the bills. He said that he wants the Audit and Finance Departments to work in harmony with the Projects and I.T. Departments to produce results on time.  They should prepare check lists and hand them over to the projects and I.T. Departments so that the documentation they submit conforms to the requirements of this check list.  

2.2       The Director Projects reported that he had had a meeting with the Head of Internal Audit and the Director Finance and issues are being sorted out. He informed the Committee that the Head of Internal Audit was preparing a check list of required documents for payment.

2.3       The Director said that he wants all the payments to be pre-audited. He asked the Director Finance to strengthen and automate the process of pre-audit and maintain a time line for the process. 

DECISION

2.4              The Finance Department will assign a person in his department the responsibility of handling and resolving the project related payments and documentation issues and strengthen and automate the pre-audit process.

Item No. 3      Presentation by the representative of M/s. Prolink Consulting on H.R. Consultancy.

3.1              The Director H.R. stated that recently an advertisement was placed for getting some H.R. Consultancy done for job description, job analysis, salary survey. M/s. Prolink was selected for this job and it was also approved by the Executive Committee. She introduced Mr. Farrukh Naseem, Director M/s. Prolink and said that he would make a presentation to give a view of what this job involves.

3.2              Accordingly Mr. Farrukh Naseem gave a presentation on strengthening of H.R System which included job description, job evaluation, remuneration survey up-date, remuneration structure redesign, benchmarking of positions of evaluation etc.

3.3              The Committee thoroughly discussed the presentations, asked several questions, sought clarifications and made some suggestions.

DECISION

3.4              The number of positions to be benchmarked for evaluation be reduced from proposed 60 to 40 which should cover all critical positions.

3.5       The EC members will be requested to suggest changes in the initial list of 40 positions and Head of Departments will nominate individuals whose roles best represent the selected benchmark jobs. Director IBA will sign off the final list compiled by Director HR and prolink.

3.6       Teaching Fellow to be included as a benchmark position.

3.7       The Director IBA suggested that all directors be treated as one benchmark position. The Consultants were requested to consider this proposition within the framework of their job evaluation methodology.

3.8       The Consultants to identify which jobs could be given to students also on part-time basis.

3.9       The salary survey of 2007 should be updated but there was no need for a new survey.

3.10     The positions will be matched with the skills of the incumbents holding those positions.

3.11     The assignment will be completed by the Consultant in six weeks time provided the required information (Data Collection Questionnaires) is provided by the EC Members on time.

ACTION

3.12     The Director H.R. will ensure that the above decisions are incorporated / implemented on time.

Item No. 4      To consider email from a parent of a student regarding change in policy – admission test for MBA.

4.1       A parent of a student doing her BBA at IBA requested to review the admission policy for MBA. According to him if this rule is enforced it would have the following negative impact:

·         There may be a drop in students competing to get into IBA with the results that the intake would no longer be the best cadre.

·         The students knowing that they can also come in to join IBA at the Master level, would prefer to get admission at other lesser institutions where the workload is not very strict, have lower fee and more opportunities for getting scholarships/fee discounts.

·         This would be very unfair on the IBA, BBA students as they have been working hard for four years and have been regularly assessed by the IBA teachers.

·         The MBA intake would not be the best lot as any one being a bit lucky on the day of test and working out for six months at the well known preparatory centers could make their way in.

·         This policy is totally flawed as it discriminates its own students who must be given preference the same way US Universities give preference to their own graduates. He said that he did his Masters about 12 years back from a well known US University and when he applied to get admission into the doctorate program, they not only accepted his application, but exempted him from taking the GRE and gave him a scholarship.

4.2       He requested the Executive Committee not to implement this policy and if at all there is some reason for its implementation, there should be a system which should exempt a good student (GPA above 2.8) from taking the admission test.  He stated that he was speaking not only for himself but on behalf of numerous parents who hold similar view.

DECISION

4.3              The Committee referred the request to the Academic Board for consideration.

ACTION

4.4       The Secretary Academic Board would ensure that the request is included in the agenda item for Academic Board.  

Item No. 5      To consider request from Ms. Marzia Asad Ali for a semester break (Fall 2009 semester).

5.1       Ms. Marzia Asad Ali, a student of MBA-II requested for allowing her a semester break (Fall 2009) for personal reasons and that she would resume her one year remaining MBA in Spring 2010.  

DECISION

5.2              The Committee allowed a semester break (for Fall 2009) to Ms. Marzia Asad Ali.

ACTION                  

5.3              The Program Office will convey the decision of allowing a semester break to Marzia Asad Ali.

Item No. 6      To consider proposal from Standard Chartered Bank for installation of ATM Machine at IBA.

6.1              The Director Finance informed the Committee that he has received a proposal from Standard Chartered for installing an ATM at a convenient location inside the IBA Campus.  For this they have requested for:

a)       A room (preferably a dedicated one) with an air-conditioner where the ATM will be installed.

b)       Supply of main power and a backup power through generator at all times

c)       Roof top access for installation of dish antenna for wireless radio connectivity, and

d)      A telephone line which will serve as a helpline for customers.

6.2              The Director Projects said that in view of the construction and renovation work at both the campuses, it is not possible to spare a room for ATM.  He suggested that the installation of ATM be postponed for one year.

DECISION

6.3              The Committee agreed with the Director Projects and decided to postpone the installation of ATM for one year. 

6.4              The Committee further decided that Standard Chartered would be asked to pay the related cost to meet the requirements.

ACTION

6.5              The Director Finance will convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Standard Chartered.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
