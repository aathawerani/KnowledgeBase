Item No. 3      To consider note from General Manager (P&D) regarding selection of an Electrical Consultant.

3.1       The General Manager (P&D) in his note of November 19, 2008 stated that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on September 8, 2008 had approved pre-qualification of the first four of the following seven electrical Consultants who had applied for pre-qualification:

·         Jafri and Associates

·         S.K. Engineers

·         W.J. Associates

·         A.S. Consultants

·         Advance Engineering Associates

·         Associated Technical Consultants

·         Matrix Consultants

3.2       The General Manager stated in his note that the first four were accordingly issued ToR and were requested to submit their Technical and Financial Proposals. However, one namely M/s. Advance Engineering Associates who were not pre-qualified, requested consideration by IBA.  After discussion with the Director and the Director Projects it was decided that as M/s. Advance Engineering Associates were technically good they should be considered as pre-qualified and given an opportunity to compete. The General Manager (P&D) requested the Executive Committee to regularize his pre-qualification.

3.3       The General Manager (P&D) further reported that he has received Technical and Financial Proposals from all five pre-qualified Consultants. The Technical proposals have been opened and evaluated.

3.4       He submitted the four unopened financial proposals and recommended that the proposals submitted by M/.s. W.J. Associates and A.S. Consultants who have scored the highest rating obtaining 96 points each, be opened in presence of the Executive Committee members and an approval given for further discussion with them on the scope of services vis-à-vis the fee they have asked for before selecting  a Consultant on the basis of their technical and financial proposals as the discussion and its conclusions would form the basis of the legal agreement with the Consultant. .

3.5              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects clarified that functionally he is helping Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (P&D) as well as he is the member of the Executive Committee, as such he proposed that the matter should be open for discussion.

3.6              The Director Projects said that from the evaluation of technical proposals submitted by five pre-qualified Consultants who were issued the ToR, only three namely; M/s. A.S. Consultants, M/s. W.J. Associates, and M/s. Jafri & Associates have obtained 96, 96 and 92 points. The remaining two namely M/s. Advance Engineering Associates, and M/s. S.K. Engineers have got 66 and 40 points respectively. He wanted to know whether they should open the financial proposal of the three (having 92 and above scores) or two only with 96 points each. The Committee decided to open the Financial Proposals of the three.

3.7              To a question from a member as to why the Electrical Consultant is required and whether or not the Electrical Engineer of the IBA can do that job, the Director Projects replied that the Electrical Consultant will be required to do electrical audit. The entire building of the Main Campus and City Campus, Hostels, and staff town will be surveyed to determine present condition of the system, identify rectification and replacement works, work out load requirements vis-à-vis available load. The Consultant’s assignment will be completed in three phases. In the first phase they would be recommending certain works and requirements related to existing facilities.  In second phase they would prepare tender documents for rectification works and provide detailed supervision of rectification and replacement works. In third phase they would be required to study loads of proposed new buildings and extensions of old buildings, to develop overall electrical system of both campuses with recommendations for self-generation or otherwise. They will continue to work for a longer period however, the amount will remain fixed. He further clarified that the Electrical Engineers of IBA are employed for maintenance works and do not have the required qualification, experience and manpower to do this job.

3.8              The Director Projects opened the three Financial Proposals submitted by (1) M/s. A.S. Consultants, (2) M/s. W.J. Associates, and (3) M/s. Jafri & Associates before the Executive Committee.

DECISION

3.9       The Committee requested the Director Projects to study the documents submitted by the three Consultants mentioned in 3.8 above and present a comparative statement with recommendation in the next meeting of the Executive Committee, for award of work.

ACTION

3.10     The Director Projects will obtain the comparative statement from General Manager (P&D) and give his recommendation on the Financial Proposals submitted by the three Consultants in the next meeting of the Executive Committee.

Item No. 4      To consider note from the Registrar and the Chairman, Department of Management regarding equivalence of full time field experience and the five month Field Project for accepting IBA’s four year BBAs into the full time MBA Program.

4.1              The Registrar said that the issue is related to the admission in MBA Program of those who have done their 4-year BBA from IBA. He said that the requirement is that BBA students wishing to do MBA in the morning must do a 5-month project to be supervised by the IBA.  Such students get exemption of first semester, however, they have to pay the full fee before they go for the project. He said that many BBA students with one year’s or six months’ service have applied for MBA program.  For such students existing rule allows them for MBA (Evening Program) but many students are keen to join MBA in the morning hours after having done six months or more work in other organization. 

4.2       Mirza Sardar Husain said that if a student can be admitted to the Evening Program and our Morning and Evening programs of MBA are considered to be of the same level then why students may not be admitted to the Morning Program. He said that the rule is not clear. We need to go through a process of following the rule to equate 5 months project which is supervised, monitored and evaluated with six or more months’ experience to be allowed one semester’s exemption. Mirza Sardar said that we need to closely look at the format and see how we can use the same format in the Morning Program.  We will have to consider financial matter and how the credit would be entered into the transcript.  He said that with slight modifications in the requirements, this can be done.       

DECISION

4.3       The Committee referred the matter to the Academic Committee.

ACTION

4.4       Dr. Muhammad Nishat will discuss this matter in the Academic Committee and put up the decision before the Executive Committee expeditiously so that the students can be informed.

Item No. 6      To consider email of November 20, 2006 from Mr. Mobin Khalili regarding procurement of heavy duty photocopier machines and appointment of an Assistant Controller of Examinations.

6.1       Mr. Muhammad Asif Jaffer, faculty member in his email of November 15, 2008 pointed out that Faculty members use to photocopy quiz question papers (and sometimes exam papers as well) from the IBA commercial photocopier. The machines that these people have, save the image of the document in the memory so that copies can be taken out even after some time when the teacher is not there.

6.2      He suggested to arrange a separate photocopy machine for faculty that should be operated by faculty themselves and placed at some secure place. Faculty should be asked and trained to use the machine themselves and delete the image from the memory after taking the photocopies.  They will each have to have a password to operate the machine.

6.3              The Director in reply to the email from Mr. Muhammad Asif suggested that we should not use commercial photocopiers for quiz question papers.  All such work should be centralized and photocopied only at the Examination Department.

6.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili clarified that most of the faculty members use photocopier for quizzes for their own convenience as they reach just a few minutes before the start of classes and in a rush they get their quizzes photocopied from the shop. Some bring copies of their quizzes with them made from outside IBA. It is a matter of convenience for the faculty members and if a quiz is photocopied just before the start of a class the question of its leakage to students does not arise. Those faculty members who plan their quizzes in advance, give their quiz papers to the office for making multiple copies. He stated that this practice is going on smoothly for years and no problem has been faced.   

6.5       He further said that quizzes cannot be centralized like term and final exams which are scheduled and their dates are pre-determined. Faculty members are notified to submit their papers in advance. Quizzes are surprise exams, their dates are not determined and faculty members at both campuses decide when to give quizzes to their students.  It is their freedom and should not be taken away.

6.6       He said that involvement of photocopy shops in making copies of quiz papers at both campuses can be eliminated but for that the following arrangements are required.

a)      Procurement of 5 heavy duty photocopier machines: One each for Examination Department, Faculty Room City Campus, Program Office City Campus, Program Office CCS City Campus, and Faculty floor Main Campus, Program Office Main Campus. Faculty members according to their convenience would use services of these centres for making photocopies of their quizzes.

b)      Appointment of an Assistant Controller of Examinations, that has been approved, to assist the Controller of Examinations in managing examination matters at both campuses and also to work out the details for establishing a proper Examination Department which is non existent at the moment.

6.7              The Director H.R. suggested that we may approach companies like Jaffer Brothers who provide photocopying service.

6.8              The Controller of Examinations said that the teachers type their question papers on computer in faculty room. On more than one occasions, the paper was printed somewhere else but luckily it did not go to a wrong person.  He suggested that computers and printers meant for examination papers should not be connected through internet to avoid access to the data by students.

DECISION

6.9       The Committee approved the purchase of two photocopiers one each at the City Campus and the Main Campus and decided that all teachers will use only these designated photocopiers for quiz questions papers, mid-term and other surprise exams. They will type their question papers on computers that are not linked to internet.

6.10     The Committee requested Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to arrange one computer and one printer in both campuses which should be separate from internet connection.

6.11     The Committee further decided that faculty members would be assigned code no. for use of photocopier.

ACTION

6.12     The Director Finance would ensure that the funds are arranged for the purchase of two photocopiers by re-appropriation if needed.

6.13          Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to arrange one computer and one printer without any internet connection in both campuses.

6.14          All faculty members would be notified by the Controller of Examinations of the new arrangements once the photocopiers, computer and printer are put in place.

Item No. 7      To consider email from Mr. Mobin Khalili regarding re-hiring of Mr. Zahid Iqbal.

7.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili in his email of November 20, 2008 stated that re-hiring of Mr. Zahid Iqbal who retired as Admin. Officer CCS was approved by the Director and the Executive Committee in principle. He stated that the process needs to be expedited on priority basis as Mr. Noman Shah, Incharge of Program Office, City Campus, has suffered a stroke and will take months to fully recover and resume his duty. In his absence there is no person to attend duties assigned to him like scheduling of next semester classes, tabulation and finalization of semester final examination results, calculation of students’ CGPA, preparing and issuing transcripts to students etc.  

7.2       He said that if we do not take immediate action to arrange a replacement the functioning of the City Campus Program Office will be seriously hampered. Mr.Zahid Iqbal who has rich experience of running a program office needs to be hired immediately. He further stated that a sanctioned post of Incharge Enrolment & Degree Section is vacant for quite sometime. He suggested that Mr.Zahid Iqbal may be re-appointed on mentioned post on last salary drawn by him in IBA. Mr. Zahid Iqbal, he said, will be primarily responsible for the functioning of the City Campus Program Office but will also look after the Enrolment & Degree Section. He will be an able help during the examinations as well.

7.3       The Director H.R. said that the Executive Committee had earlier decided not to hire people who had retired. She suggested that instead of hiring a retired person we should promote a person who has started a career so that he can be groomed.  She said that there is a possibility of assigning one person from the Program Office so that the immediate problem is resolved and this would not set any precedence of re-hiring retired people.

7.4       The Controller of Examinations said that the job requirement which is of highly sensitive nature that we cannot assign to a new person but only to such person whose personal integrity is already tested.

7.5       The Director H.R. said that we have to clarify whether we would re-hire retired persons or not.  The Controller of Examinations pointed out that many persons who are recently appointed on senior positions have passed the retirement age. Therefore, a person who retired from IBA should not be disqualified on this basis if he is fit and competent to serve IBA further.

 7.6       The Director Finance said that if one has been our asset and if we can use him or her then what is the harm in rehiring.          

7.7       The Registrar said that Mr. Ayaz Ahmed is also doing a very good job and second in command in CCS Department. He suggested that Mr. Ayaz may be assigned the job proposed for Mr. Zahid Iqbal. 

7.8       The Controller of Examinations said that he wants a person of his confidence.

7.9       The Director H.R. said that we have to promote internally and develop successors but the problem is that we do not have a system. She further said that if we have to look at things at operational level rather than strategic level, we will not be able to develop a system because every situation is urgent.  She said that we have to find interim solution.

7.10    The Acting Director, Dr. Muhammad Nishat wanted to know if for some reason Mr. Zahid Iqbal does not join then what would be alternative that the Controller of Examinations would suggest. To this the Controller of Examinations said that he has given the solution and do not have any other recommendations.  

7.11          The Director H.R. proposed to examine the possibility of internal arrangement and explore other options but the Controller of Examinations did not want to discuss any further and left having informed the Chair that he had some other important assignments.

7.12          In context of the situation the Director Projects suggested that we should arrange a workshop on change management for our executives.

DECISION

7.13          The Committee decided that the Director H.R. would check with the Director if he has given approval for re-hiring of Mr. Zahid Iqbal and what would be the status of the earlier decision of not rehiring retired employees.

7.14          The Director H.R. Checked with the Director. The Director informed the Director H.R. that as the Controller of Examinations could not identify any other suitable candidates from within the IBA to fill in this position and as this was a highly sensitive position that very much depended on the trust of the Controller of Examinations, the Director suggested that a one-time exception be considered by the Executive Committee in this particular case. He, however, advised that Mr. Zahid Iqbal’s case should not form a precedent for the future and the earlier decision of Executive Committee not to rehire retired persons but promote qualified persons from within IBA will stand intact.

c)         Leave Encashment

8.7              The Registrar said that non-teaching employees are entitled to leave encashment for a maximum of 30 days at the end of the calendar year.  He said that the IBA had retained 15 days earned leave last year and proposed to increase it to 30 days for contract employees. He said that the purpose of retaining 15 days leave was to discourage employees leaving without any notice. He said that it would serve as a small deterrent.

8.8              The Director H.R. said that she is collecting data about the number of employees who have left during the last three years without serving notice period. In her opinion, if the number of such persons who left without notice is not significant, then it would be an unfriendly policy for the vast majority.

DECISION

8.9              The Committee authorized the Director H.R. to decide whether or not to retain additional 15 days Earned Leave of contract employees based on the data for three years.

d)         Earned Leave Balance

8.10          The H.R. Manager stated that the present practice is that we credit the earned leave of faculty and staff at the end of the year i.e. December 31. He proposed that instead of crediting the earned leave at the end of the year, we should credit the earned leave each month on prorata basis i.e. 2.5 days earned leave every month.

DECISION

8.11          The Committee agreed to the proposal of crediting earned leave every month for only those employees who wish to leave or wish to utilize their earned leave during the year.

ACTION

8.12          The H.R. Manager would communicate the decision to the Incharge, Leave Record for compliance.
