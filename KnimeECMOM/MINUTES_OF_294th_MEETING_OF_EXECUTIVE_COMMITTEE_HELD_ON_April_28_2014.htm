Item No.1       Customer Satisfaction Survey Report by AC Nielson.

 

1.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed introduced Ms. Jamila Kidwai of AC Nielson. Ms. Kidwai giving background of the Survey said that IBA wanted to understand where their performance levels currently stand in terms of their importance and how they have performed this year versus last year. Hence there was a need to conduct a Satisfaction Survey among the faculty, management and students. She said that students report was presented to the EC last year. She said that when the report was presented to the EC on July 8, 2013, the EC advised to re-submit the report after conducting the survey on the increased based of respondents.  She said that the report shows the findings of the satisfaction survey from the faculty and management of IBA.  She further said that the report also shows the comparison with the last wave that was conducted in 2012.

 

1.2       Ms. Jamila Kidwai briefed about the objectives, methodology, eQ Model, Key Performance Indicators. The factors used for evaluating the importance vs performance of Purchase Department, Security system at both the campuses, General Administration at both campuses, Transport, Faculty of Business Administration, Library at both campuses, Program offices, MBA and BBA, Human Resource Department, IT Maintenance and Customer Support, ERP / Campus Management System, Finance Department.

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the award of work for construction of Boys’ Hostel Blocks ‘B’ and ‘C’.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that an advertisement for the pre-qualification of contractors for construction of Boys’ Hostel Blocks ‘B’ and ‘C was published in daily newspapers on January 02, 2014. In response to the advertisement 33 contractors submitted their document and only the following 10 contractors met the evaluation criteria and stood qualified:

 

1. M/s Total Construction

2. M/s Aikes Pvt Ltd.

3. M/s Hasscon Services

4. M/s A.H Construction Pvt limited

5. M/s Mughals Constructor

6. M/s Mughals Pakistan

7. M/s Habib Rafiq

8. M/s Paragon Constructor

9. M/s Al-Shafi Enterprises

10.  M/s Banu Mukhtar Pvt Ltd.

2.4       After detailed scrutiny it was found that the 1st lowest M/s Hasscon is 9% higher than Engineering Estimate (Rs.193, 015,378) whereas the 2nd and 3rd lowest M/s AHCL and M/s Aikes are 10% and 14% higher than Engineering Estimate. The Director Projects presented the comparative statement.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on April 26, 2014 considered and approved  the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Hasscon at a cost of Rs.212,662,278/-. The Committee was also apprised of the comments received from the Architect Mr. Zayd Bilgrami who expressed his dissatisfaction on the performance of the Contractor and that the contract be awarded to the second highest bidder M/s AHCL. The Committee considered the observations as subjective and advised the Projects Department to obtain their objective evaluation. On query, the Director Projects briefed that once the contractor was prequalified based on his merit and that his bid was responsive, the lowest bid (which is 9% higher than the Engineer’s Estimate) cannot be rejected. 

 

2.6       The Director Projects said that he also apprised the Procurement Committee that the HEC department had not taken the approval of the prequalification of the contractors and presented the prequalification evaluation report to the Committee for consideration and approval. After detailed deliberation the Procurement Committee approved the prequalification of the contractors. He presented the Evaluation report.

 

2.7       Based on the recommendations of the Procurement Committee the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve:

 

(a) the prequalification of Contractors, and

(b) to recommend to the A&FC and to the Board of Governors to consider and approve the award of the contract of the construction of Boys’ Hostel (Blocks B and C) to M/s HASSCON – the lowest evaluated responsive bidder at a cost of Rs.212,662,278/-

 

2.8       The Registrar asked if HASSCON have worked for IBA.  He said that we should give weight to those who have worked for IBA and we are satisfied with their work. The Director Projects replied that HASSCON have prepared power hub at IBA. He said that they have prepared Dr. Syedana Bhurahuddin Department of Genetics Engineering Building at Karachi University, and EOBI building at Nursery, Karachi.  He said that he has rechecked their capability. They are both professionally and financially sound. He said that the project they have completed at IBA, there was no time over run, and no escalation and the project is acceptable.

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee approved the prequalification of contractors, and recommended to the Board of Governors to consider and approve the award of the contract of the construction of Boys’ Hostel (Blocks B and C) to M/s HASSCON – the lowest evaluated responsive bidder at a cost of Rs.212,662,278/-

 

ACTION

 

2.10     The Director Projects would present the recommendation of EC to the Board in its meeting scheduled for May 3, 2014.

 

Item No. 3      Programme Announcement 2014-15 – Presentation by the Registrar.

 

3.1       The Registrar presented overview of the Programme Announcement 2014-15.  He said that last year it was of 184 pages but this year it contains 240 pages because some additional courses descriptions, data and photographs, overseas Chapter of alumni, and features of new programs have been added into it. The cover page is also different from those of previous years. The infrastructure development, different activities, sports have been highlighted in the cover page. He said that till 2009 there was a page called Journey of Milestones which was later dropped but this year it has been reinstated.  He said that the font size in previous years used to be 8 which they have increased to 11.  It was suggested to use the font size of 10. He reported that the Programme Announcement has been vetted by different departments. Dr. Huma Baqai suggested to send the page on Journey of Milestones to Mirza Sardar Husain for vetting.

 

3.2       To a suggestion to mention the mission and vision statements, the Committee was told that the mission and vision statements were being developed under the guidance of Mr. Zaffar A. Khan and that can be included in the next year’s Programme Announcement.  It was also suggested that instead of hard copy, CDs of the Programme Announcement should be given to the potential students.  The Programme Announcement to be loaded on the Portal should have search option.

 

3.3       The Committee observed that some information is repeated in the MBA Program. It was suggested that the information already covered should not be repeated. 

 

3.4       The Registrar showed the facilities layout of Main Campus in 2013 and what was in 2009 which he proposed to include in the Programme Announcement.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee noted and approved the format of the Programme Announcement 2014-15 along with the comments / observations noted above.

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)       Request for the residence for technician/electrician inside Staff Town.

 

4.1       The Registrar reported that a servant quarter is available. The Manager Maintenance has requested that the maintenance facilities requirements have changed and they require a trained electrician available at the Staff Town. He said that although there are two applicants in queue who are senior to the applicant electrician, the vacant servant quarter, can be allotted to the electrician under clause 8(b) of the Accommodation Allotment Rule which says that an employee may be allotted an accommodation, subject that in the opinion of the allotment Committee and the Dean & Director, his/her stay in the Campus is essential in the interest of the Institute. 

 

4.2      The Committee was told that there is already an electrician living in the Staff Town.  The Registrar said that he may not be so competent to tackle all sorts of electrical problems.  The Committee advised the Registrar to train that electrician.

 

DECISION

 

4.3       The Committee decided that the House Allotment rule should strictly be followed.  

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
