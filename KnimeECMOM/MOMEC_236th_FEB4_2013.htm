Mirza Izhar Husain, Director CEE attended the meeting on special invitation.

The members welcomed Mr. Imran Abdul Rahman Batada to the Executive Committee in place of Dr. Arshad Siddiqi whose contract ended on January 31, 2013.

The following items were discussed:


Item No. 1 To consider a proposal on 360-Feedback.

1.1 Mirza Izhar Husain, Director CEE informed the Committee that they have developed a program / course by the name of Transformational Leadership Program (TLP). In this program they conduct 360o feedback for participants coming from corporate sectors. He said that in order to provide 360o feedback assessment they need professionals to help conduct the 360o exercise. He said that since the expected cost of this program is well over Rs.100,000/- therefore they need to float tender for this. He said that in the Transformational Leadership Program (TLP) which will be offered from February 18 to 21, 2013, the 360o Feedback is an integral part of the program. He further said that the 360o assessment is a sensitive and technical tool and it has been developed by Dr. Nasir Afghan and will be offered through his company; M/s. Design Concepts (Pvt.) Limited. He requested the Executive Committee to allow him to float the tender.

1.2 Mr. Zafar Siddiqui observed that there would be a conflict of interest if a faculty member takes part in the tender. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the issue is an internal one and it should not have come to the EC. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that TLP is a program that is done by Dr. Nasir Afghan and 360 feedback is a component of this program. He was of the view that there is no need to do tender and the Dean & Director is to decide how much is to be paid for 360o feedback to Dr. Nasir Afghan. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that Dr. Nasir Afghan may be remunerated for his services but we cannot involve his Company.

DECISION

1.3 The Committee decided that since the program will be conducted by Dr. Nasir Afghan, the rate will be decided and approved by the Dean & Director. As no external organization is to be involved in it, there is no need of any tender.
 


Item No. 2 Walk through of Students’ Centre.

2.1 Following observations were made by the EC members during their visit of the Students’ Centre:

i. Design of donor’s wall is to be provided by Architect.

ii. Door and electric switches on donor’s wall shall be shifted on other side.

iii. Two service windows are required in resource room instead of one.

iv. Soft board is required on outer wall of society offices.

v. Record cabinets are required in society office on both floors.

vi. Size of coffee shop’s counter is to be reduced.

vii. Low height partition is to be provided on hand wash area in dining room on ground floor.

viii. Provision of Multimedia with screen is to be provided in event hall on first floor.

ix. Event hall first floor needs to be separated with ground floor dining room to avoid noises from dining hall during programs in event hall.

x. Separate entrance is to be provided for gymnasium on 1st floor.

2.2 The Registrar stressed for providing false ceiling in Event Hall. However a Mockup with charcoal grey paint on event hall ceiling shall be prepared for review.

2.3 Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that a bigger space be allocated if possible for book store.


Item No. 3 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Up-date on formation of sub-committee on “Laying of Water Transmission Line”.

3.1 The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee had formed a sub-committee to analyse the issue of Laying of Water Transmission Line and was required to submit its report. However, he said that the meeting of the sub-committee could not be held as they did not get the minutes of CPC. He said that he was pursuing with the Chairman, CPC for the minutes but has not yet received it.

3.2 Dr. Sayeed Ghani requested Dr. Nasir Touheed to get the minutes of CPC, in which the subject matter was discussed, issued at the earliest.

b) Legal opinion on taxes.

3.3 Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that he has obtained legal opinion on deduction of taxes with particular reference to AMFCO, the contractor of Data Centre. He said that as per legal opinion the tax is to be deducted by IBA. However, he said that as per advice of the Dean & Director, IBA may consider this issue on merit provided a request is received from the Contractor after the completion of the project on due date.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
