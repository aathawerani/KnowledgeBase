MINUTES OF 217TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 03, 2012

 

The 217th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, September 3, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Societies Meeting Room, Adamjee Academic Centre, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

          1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                        Dean & Director
          2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                 Associate Dean, FBA
          3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                       Associate Dean, FCS
          4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN        Registrar
          5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                         Controller of Examinations
          6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz           Director Projects
          7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                       Director ICT
          8. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                   Director CED
          9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                  Director H.R.
        10. Mr. Moeid Sultan                        Acting Director Finance

The following items were discussed:   

Item No.1       To consider and approve the revised estimate (based on actual lowest bid) for construction of Stores/Workshops at City Campus due to increase in scope of work.  

1.1       The Director Projects said that the Executive Committee in its 212th meeting held on 23rd July 2012, had approved the estimate of Rs.1.0 million for the construction of stores / workshops at the City Campus. However, he said that while the tenders were being prepared the requisitions for construction of following additional works were received:

1. One Guard Room                                                              

2. One wash room

3. One room for the operators of the chillers, on the roof top of Admin Block        

4. Two fibre glass sheds

5. Mild Steel Staircase  

1.2       The Director Projects reported that the overall area for construction comes to 1956 sft after adding the above facilities, as detailed below:

1.      Guard  room                                                                             = 18’-0”x14’-0”   =   252

2.      Guard’s wash room                                                                  = 10’-0”x8’-0”     =     80

3.      Electrical room & workshop                                                      = 31’-0”x16’-0”   =   496

4.      Carpenter work shop                                                               = 30’-0”x 16’-0”  =   480

5.      Examination store                                                                    = 24’-0” x14’-0”  =   336

6.      Chiller room                                                                              = 12’-0”x12’-0”   =   144

7.      Shed over the Electric D.B. for chiller at Admin. Block Roof.      = 11’-0”x8’-0”      =    88

8.      Shed over water pumps for chiller at Admin. Block Roof.          = 10’-0”x 8’-0”     =    80

                        Total Area of all proposed facilities                                       =          1956  SFT. 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that the bids process was completed based on the above additional works as well as the original scope of work comprising construction of four stores/workshops and one Examination Store on the roof top of the Academic Block. He said that the lowest bid is of Rs.1,779,627/- and cost of the construction comes to Rs.909.82 per sft., which is well within the already approved cost of Rs.1000/- per sft. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the revised cost of Rs.1.8 million based on the lowest bid amount.

1.4       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why the Guard Room is needed there. Mr. Mobin Khalili said there already existed a Guard Room which was broken during the construction and it is needed again.

1.5       The Dean & Director wanted to know which of the facilities listed above was not included in the tender document. The Director Projects replied that Chiller operator room was not part of the tender and was later given as a variation order, but it was not accepted by the Administration Building Contractor as he had a dispute with the Project managers M/s INTEG.

1.6       The Registrar observed that no shelves have been provided in the Examination Room and suggested to make concrete shelves over there.  The Dean & Director wanted to know if they can use the shelves taken out from the Library. The Registrar replied that the shelves taken out from the Library would be used again in the Library.

1.7       The Director Projects said that he would get pre-cast shelves placed in the Examination Room.

DECISION

1.8       The Committee approved the revised cost of Rs.1.8 million based on the lowest bid amount for the work mentioned above.

ACTION

1.9       The Director Projects would get the additional amount re-appropriated.

2.5       The Director ICT said that in the budget of 2012-13, 150 PC’s were approved for ICT labs. Presently 50 PC’s are available in ICT inventory so they would have total 200 PC’s, 180 will be issued leaving 20 in stock. 2.6       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why we need additional PC’s while we have not added new labs. The Registrar said that two new labs have been added at Main Campus, and one at the 3rd floor of FCS Building, City Campus.

2.7       The Dean & Director said that when new classes are shifted to the Main Campus so why excess capacity is being made at the City Campus. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that excess capacity is needed because due to the shortage of computers, they have to arrange compute lab exams in three shifts at City Campus.

                                    Total additional PC’s required:                                   130 PC’s

2.9       Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that Rs.8.28 million has been kept in the budget 2012-13 for the purchase of PC’s.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that if discount is available on bulk purchase then the PCs should be purchased in one go, otherwise they should be purchased as and when they are needed.

DECISION

2.10     The Committee granted conceptual approval for the purchase of additional 130 PC’s.

ACTION 

2.11     The Director ICT would find out what discount is available on bulk purchase of PC’s.     

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
