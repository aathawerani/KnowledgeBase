Dr. M. Shahid Qureshi, Program Director CED attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1 Proposals from Dr. M. Shahid Qureshi for:

a) The purchase of Kiosks

1.1 Dr. Shahid Qureshi before presenting his proposal for the purchase of Kiosk, briefed the members about the activities going on at the Centre for Entrepreneurial Development in the areas of Collaboration / Partnership (national and international), Community Outreach Activities, Entrepreneurial Co-curricular Activities, IBA Incubator, Entrepreneurship Research, Faculty Development, and Entrepreneurship Teaching at IBA. He also highlighted major achievements of CED including the USASBE Outstanding Emerging Entrepreneurship Program Award. He said that there were 50 participants in the USASBE competition which were shortlisted to 4; IBA CED was one of the four. He said that Institute from India with 18 years standing got the first position. He was very optimistic that next year the chances are very high for IBA CED to be number one. The members congratulated Dr. Shahid Qureshi on this great achievement within a short span of three years of the inception of the IBA CED. Dr. Qureshi also apprised the members of the new Projects and the business activities of CED Incubatees.

1.2 Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that students have come up with an idea of plastic made Kiosks. He said that four 4 kiosks are required to accommodate businesses of FME Students because the current activity rooms are completely occupied and the demand of spaces is increasing. He presented the pictures of proposed kiosks which would cost around Rs.110,000 each. He said that the kiosks life may be up to four or five years and the cost may be recovered in two years time by renting them out at Rs.5000/- per month to the users.

1.3 Dr. Shahid Qureshi proposed that Kiosks can be placed on the green belts between the G&T Auditorium and Adamjee Academic Block, in the sunshade area & inside or outside the Alumni Resource Centre. The Dean & Director said that the Kiosks should be located at such a place that they are not needed to be further relocated. He asked the Director Projects to identify the location after seeing the Master Plan so that it may be integrated with the Master Plan and it should be lowest cost operation for which comparative bidding should be done.

1.4 Dr. Shahid Qureshi further suggested that the electricity to these Kiosks should be provided by IBA. The FME students will generally not be charged for the electricity expenses. Alternatively a nominal rent can be charged which will cover the electricity expenses. This will however depend if the business is making some adequate amount of sales or not. These Kiosks will serve as a display room/place, backed by the storage space allotted to occupants for keeping their inventory. The occupants will be given a S.O.P sheet to maintain their Kiosks and they will be penalized at the end of their contract/term in case of any damage done to the Kiosks. Another option is to collect a nominal caution money sum from the occupants before handing over the Kiosks.

1.5 The Registrar said that there are three aspects; physical purchase of Kiosks, its planting and running it. The Dean & Director suggested to connect food items with the canteen. He suggested to consult Mr. Zayad Bilgrami of NBCL so that its aesthetic is not affected. He suggested to involve Dr. Shahid Qureshi and the Registrar in the meeting with Mr. Zayad. The Dean & Director requested the Registrar to place the tender for the Kiosks.

DECISION

1.6 The Committee approved the proposal for purchase of four Kiosks for Rs.110,000 each.

ACTION

1.7 The Director Projects would arrange a meeting of Dr. Shahid Qureshi and the Registrar with Mr. Zayed of NBCL to finalize the design and integration of the Kiosks (including future requirements) with the Master Plan. They would also select the place where the Kiosks would be placed.

1.8 The Registrar would float tender for the purchase of four plastic Kiosks.


b) Request for Permission of Lockers at IBA

1.9 Dr. Shahid Qureshi reported that one of their students (member of FAKAT Team) has sought permission for placing student lockers at IBA under the name of FAKAT, a company that came into existence as part of their course. He clarified that there would be no financial implications on the part of IBA. The lockers would be available 24/7 to the students of Entrepreneurship course. He said that at LUMS and KSBL also lockers are provided to the students. He said that a unit of 12 lockers would require an investment of around Rs.21,000/- which can be recovered in 12 months time. The investment on lockers and its management will be done by the students themselves. The Dean & Director said that it is a completely new idea for IBA and we have to try and see if this is successful. He said that since it is on payment basis we have to see as to how many students would use it. He suggested to start with 12 lockers and see if there is demand for more lockers, then increase the number of lockers. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to consider providing such arrangement at City Campus too. The Dean & Director said that at Aman Tower also lockers facility can be provided.

DECISION

1.10 The Committee granted permission to the member of FAKAT Team for placing a unit of 12 lockers for the students of Entrepreneurship course.


c) FME Mentors

1.11 Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that FME is a heavy course and 40 students have launched their businesses. He said that TAs attached with teachers were mentoring the projects. However as every student wants personal mentoring and in view of the increase in business projects, senior students are engaged as mentors. He proposed that by reducing the number of TAs and replacing them with senior students, they can engage 8 to 10 mentors in place of one TA because the cost of one TA would be spread over several mentors. He suggested that senior students engaged as mentors may be given some incentives in the form of payment of @Rs.200/- per hour and a maximum of 5 hours in a week. The Dean & Director asked as to what they would be doing and what their terms of reference would be. Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that there is a complete document for accountability and it would be channelized through H.R. The Dean & Director said that as long as there is a saving on TAs he has no issue. Mr. Adnan Hameed suggested that total number of hours should not exceed 20 hours in a week.

DECISION

1.12 The Committee approved the proposal of Dr. Shahid Qureshi to engage senior students as mentors by reducing the number of TAs and providing the proposed incentives to the mentors.


Item No.2 To consider proposed changes in Staff Welfare Fund Policy.

2.1 Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the existing Staff Welfare Policy was approved in 2010 by the BoG and since then after a lapse of three years there was a need to update the same to account for changes due to the inflation and other reasons. He reported that the policy has been improved in the following areas:

• Scope has been broadened to cover few other areas for grants as well as the loan facility:

o Loans for Marriage will now also be given for the marriage of sister and sons
o Qarz-e-Hasna facility has been introduced for Higher studies of Self and immediate family members
o Loan facility of eligible employees will also be given for House Construction, Acquisition.

• Limits have been enhanced keeping in view the inflation factors and also to benefit few other categories of the employees:

o Eligibility to become a member of the fund will now be for employees having salary of Rs.50,000 (earlier it was Rs.40,000)
o Eligibility for Grants (medical and children education) have been increased from Rs.15,000 to Rs.20,000 per month standard gross salary.
o Medical Grant may be applied up to a maximum of Rs.25,000/- in a twelve month period
o A General Purpose Loan has been introduced to meet other emergency financial requirements.

• IBA will now contribute each year an amount maximum up to 0.4% of the total IBA students’ revenue (instead of 0.2% in current policy).

• It is estimated that an additional financial impact of Rs.500,000 will be incurred annually against the above mentioned changes which will be overcome from the interest earned on the fund.

2.2 The Dean & Director emphasized that IBA employees’ children getting admission in our program should be properly publicized so that the children of other employees of IBA are encouraged and motivated. He further said that policy related to Qarz-e-Hasna should also be publicized.

2.3 On the proposal of increasing the contribution from 0.2 percent to 0.4 percent of the revenue, the Dean & Director said that as the total revenue as well as the enrolment of students is increasing, the amount of 0.2 percent contribution would be much larger than past years. There is no need to increase the percentage of contribution. He suggested to reinvest the interest earned on the contributed amount.

2.4 Mr. Imran Batada asked if there can be any collaboration of IBA with good schools whereby children of IBA employees can also study in those schools such as Beacon House, City School and IBA can pay their fee. The Dean & Director appreciated the idea and said that he would look into it and talk to Beacon House, City School, and Generation School. He said that The Educators can also be considered. He said that we may expand the scope of our policy and add that if anybody gets admission in Beacon House, City School, or Generation School IBA will reimburse their fee as we do for higher education. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that in the category of school going children, the number is much higher for which the fund is not available. The Director Projects suggested that Zakat may also be given to this fund. The Dean & Director agreed with the Director Projects and said that people can be persuaded for giving Zakat to this fund.

DECISION

2.5 The Committee approved the proposed changes in Staff Welfare Fund Policy as presented by Mr. Moeid Sultan except the proposal of raising the contribution from 0.2 percent to 0.4 percent.

ACTION

2.6 Mr. Adnan Hameed would find out the age group of the low paid employees (around 150 in number), where their wards are studying, and what would be the financial implications if provided schooling at the proposed schools and present it to the EC for consideration.

2.7 The Director Finance would report the proposed changes in Staff Welfare Fund Policy to the A&FC for approval.

He advised the Director Projects that once the approval in writing is received then he (Director Projects) may develop implementation plan in that he can give his argument for deploying Projects staff instead of Project Manager.
