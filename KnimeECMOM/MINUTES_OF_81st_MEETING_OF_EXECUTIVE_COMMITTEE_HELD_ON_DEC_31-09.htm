Item No. 2      To consider and approve vetting of Structural Designs of Critical Portion on on-going projects at City Campus and Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that there have been numerous occasions when M/s. Icon and their Structural Engineer were pointed out unsafe design and construction method adopted by them on IBA Projects. He cited the following cases where timely intervention by IBA prevented the unsafe actions.

 

a)         Punching of holes in the structural beams were disallowed by Mr. Shameem Ahmed in the old structure of Academic Block, City Campus as it was against the engineering practice. (year 2008 )

 

b)         They were advised not to dismantle the roof over hangs on both sides of the Academic Block, City Campus as it would have rendered the roof structure unstable and unsafe for human occupancy. The matter was taken up to Audit & Finance Committee, who also approved not to dismantle the overhangs. (early 2009 )

 

c)         As a part of their design they have dismantled the inner slabs of over hang beams in spite of the fact that Director Projects advised them to avoid it. The Architects and Structural Engineer declared this action as safe and harmless.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that recently following major deviations have been noted which need to be addressed immediately:

 

a)         Director Projects very strongly advised the Architect M/s. ICON not to dismantle the First Floor Structure because it will destabilize the overall framed structure of the library. The Architect and apparently his structural consultant did not consider IBA’s recommendations. Architects’ latest plans show the dismantling of the structural beams and slabs: Structural Consultants advice is thus questionable.

b)         The elevated pathway in the Academic Block is a steel structure and is a combination of welded and bolted joints. During the month of November 2009, Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, who had the charge of Director Projects (in his absence) and Director ICT observed that there were no specifications or design of welded joints and asked for detailed design and recommended the testing of welding against defined criteria as the structural members were already welded at Site. There was an exchange of communication on this matter.  The Director Projects reported that on his return he had several meetings with the Architects and the Structural Designers, who finally agreed to furnish the design calculations. They have been told that IBA would get the design vetted for structural strength, stability and safety.

 

c)         While the structural foundations were being cast at site for the Canteen and Lounge Shed at City Campus, the Director Projects reported that he observed that the reinforcing steel was in excess of what was required. After detailed discussion, the Structural Engineer informed that the excess steel is there to take care for earthquake requirements and to withstand with gust of 80 miles (128 km/hr). This was totally an unconvincing statement as the structure is lightweight, single storied located and in the protection of CCS building. There is no question of an 80 mph wind gust in the area. He said that he had asked for detailed design calculations for vetting by experienced Structural Engineer.

 

d)         Based on the structural testing results the Structural Consultant had advised to construct 4th Floor on CCS Building within certain limitations. The Director Projects said that on one hand the same Structural Engineer is advising stability of a single storied shed to meet the design criteria for earthquake resistant structure and on the other hand he is advising to raise the height of a 20-25 year old building without analysis of the structure against the criteria of earthquake resistant structures. He said that the merit of the advice is questionable. The building thus needs to be structurally analyzed through computer modelling.

 

2.3       The Director Projects said that the Architect has suggested to build 4th Floor on top of existing Administration Block at Main Campus without any investigation. He said that earlier the idea of building 4th Floor was dropped on his advice. He further said that all renovation designs of the Architect do convey partial dismantling of structural members which need special designs and restoration techniques. He said that those are not being properly addressed although it is the prime responsibility of the Structural Engineer of the Architect to design safe and economical structure. He said that he feels that both the aspects are being ignored. He recommended that in order to safeguard the interest of IBA, it is essential that the following particular cases be given to professionally competent and experienced Structural Consultants for vetting:

 

1.                  Elevated pathway structure of Academic Block, City Campus.

2.                  Students Canteen and Shed, City Campus

3.                  CCS Building, City Campus

4.                  Library – dismantling of beams and slabs

5.                  Administration Block Main Campus, for construction of 4th Floor

6.                  Any case involving tampering with old structure

 

The suggested experts are:

 

1.                  M/s. Consultech International (Engineer Hafeez Khan)

2.                  M/s. Mushtaq & Bilal              (Engineer Mushtq Dawood

3.                  M/s. Alliance Consultants        (Engr. Siddiq Issa)

 

2.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the above and approve appointment of one of the above named Structural Experts for vetting of design of above mentioned buildings. All of the above consultants have 40 years of experience for such assignments. He clarified that competitive bidding is not applicable. In order to save time assignments can be divided amongst the Consultants.  He said that contractually IBA can ask the Architects to employ experienced Structural Consultants to avoid this kind of situation. He is getting highest fee amongst all the Architects. He can afford to employ a better consultant.

 

2.5              The Director said that they are non technical people so they are totally ignorant about it.  Moreover he said that the structural test was done through the government lab.

 

2.6              The Director Projects replied that in the test report they had not given the analysis but only the condition of the existing structure on the basis of spot tests. The structural consultant in his report has given a general statement that 4th floor can be built under limitations. He also has not analysed the behaviour of building structure under earthquake resistant structure. He further added that it is risky to build 4th floor on a building which has been designed for 3 storeys. The consultant is required to prove his point with structural analysis.

 

2.7              The Director Projects said that there is an intermediate floor supported by column-beam framed structure in the library of the Main Campus. He stated that he had asked the Architect not to dismantle that floor, but his latest drawings show that it has to be broken. He further added that he wanted that the structural consultant should prove that this is safe through proper structural analysis. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to allow him to get his analysis vetted by an expert.

 

2.8              The Registrar said that he had objected to that design. He said that the Architect is insisting to break it but he Director Projects is insisting not to break it.

 

2.9              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that Mr. Shahab Ghani and the Director Projects should decide on it instead of leaving it on to the Consultant only.

 

2.10          The Director said that it is the responsibility of the Architect and he is taking the responsibility of adding 4th floor on the existing Admin. Block of Main Campus as well as other decisions. He said that IBA cannot spend extra amounts on hiring experts. He advised the Director Projects to ask the Architects to get all such cases properly designed by his structural consultant who should take full responsibility of safety and soundness of the structures.

2.11          The Registrar suggested that the structural consultants should provide full proof supported by the calculations or otherwise such cases be got vetted by the Architect at his cost.

 

2.12          The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Registrar and advised Director Projects to convey this to the Architect M/s ICON.

 

 

DECISION

 

2.13          The Committee allowed the Director Projects to get the report vetted at the cost of the Architects. 

 

2.14          The Committee disapproved construction of fourth floor on the existing CCS building.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Benefit / compensation policy for employees who die during service at IBA.

             

4.1              Mr. Husain Kazani said that they have prepared a draft policy on benefit / compensation for employees who die during service at IBA.  He presented the draft policy which included the scope, responsibilities, entitlement, eligibility and procedure.  He said that in the proposed policy an employee who dies while in active service, his/her nominee will get an ex-gratia payment equivalent to six month’s gross salary (last drawn) or Rs.200,000/- whichever is higher.  He said that all full-time faculty and non-faculty including regular and contractual who would fulfil the following criteria will be eligible for the ex-gratia payment:

 

·         Employees who have completed at least ten years’ of continuous service with the institution.

·         Employee’s monthly gross salary should not be more than Rs.40,000-

·         Employee at the time of death should not be on Foreign Service Leave / Foreign Training / Study Leave and / or unpaid leave of absence for more than 6 months for reasons other than disability / sickness.

 

4.2              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that the third condition (employees on leave) should be excluded.  He also wanted to know the reason of the condition for ten years’ continuous service.

 

4.3              The Director suggested that instead of 10 years continuous service, the entitlement should be fixed at average length of continuous service of employees. He asked the Manager H.R. to find out the average length of service of employees of IBA.

 

4.4              Mr. M. Faisal Zia wanted to know as to why the contract employees have not been provided life insurance coverage.

 

4.5              The Director replied that General Life Insurance would be extended to all employees and they are working on it and expect to get it finalized in the next three months. . 

 

DECISION

 

4.6              The Committee decided that the Director H.R. will circulate the revised policy after making necessary amendments as suggested by the EC and will be considered approved if no further queries are raised.

 

ACTION

 

4.7              The Director H.R. would determine the average length of service of employees at IBA and incorporate that in the policy.

 

4.8              The Director H.R. would start the process of providing General Life Insurance cover to all contract employees.

c)         To review the project development and subsequent revisions and approve one of the final two plans.

 

4.13     The Director Projects reported that in order to meet urgent need of ICT Department, ‘Extension of Server Room’ located on 3rd Floor of Administration Block at Main Campus, planning was undertaken in June 2009. On approval of the planning by Director ICT of the plan dated 24th June 2009.  This plan of 3rd Floor was a revision of the plan submitted by the Architect on 29th May 2009. The tender documents were finalized and issued in August 2009. At the time of pre-bid meeting which was held on 5th September 2009, Director ICT was sent a letter for comments on 3rd September 2009. No comments were received

 

4.14     The Director Projects reported that on 2nd November 2009, Director Projects forwarded the overall plans of Ground to 4th Floor (received from Architect on 29th October 2009) to all concerned including Director ICT, seeking their comments and suggestions. This plan incorporated the revised plan of 24th June 2009, as mentioned above.  Director Projects had also mentioned that if any comments are not received within ten days, it will be construed that they are satisfied with the proposed plan. As the letter was meant for the overall floors, no revision was expected from Director ICT as the plans for server room on 3rd floor and the laboratory were already approved, for which tenders were already floated. The procurement process was completed with the final approval from Central Purchase Committee on October 31, 2009. On November 4, 2009, the ICT Department was requested to give possession of site to the Contractor for mobilization. In reply Director ICT asked the Project Engineer (Contracts & Planning) to discuss the plan of action with them. Engr. Abdul Rehman, Project Engineer (Contracts & Planning) handed over the drawings and Bills of Quantities to Mr. Abdul Qadir of ICT the same day. Director ICT held meetings with the Architect separately.

 

4.15     The Director further reported that in the meantime, a request was received from Co-ordinator Testing Services on 11th November 2009, asking for isolation of testing services. This request was sent to the Architect on 16th Nov. 2009. On 24th November 2009, line plans were sent by the Architects M/s. ICON to all concerned in IBA (including Executive Committee) informing that the layout of Administration Block at Main Campus was revised based on the feedback received from Testing Department and Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi, Director ICT” only. The Architect also requested to send their feedback if any and confirmation latest by Thursday 26th November so that they can proceed with the construction drawings of server room extension and overall design development of Administration Block as whole.

 

4.16     The Director Projects stated that the plans were totally different from those already approved and based on which tenders were floated. The Laboratory which had a capacity of 51 students was reduced to 30 and UPS & battery rooms were located on the ground floor. These plans did not incorporate the requirements of Testing Department. The Architect was informed the same day. The revision of Server Room was thus done by ICT Department after the process of tendering was completed and work order was issued based on the layouts already approved and signed by Director ICT. It was told that the need of revision arose because the new servers’ sizes and weights were not known at that time. The layouts differed totally with that approved earlier. Revised plans as of 24th November 2009 showed a completely new layout on 3rd Floor on the Server Room side.

 

4.17     The Director Projects further reported that on 5th December 2009, the Architects sent another revision of the layouts of 24th November 2009, totally different from those of 24th November 2009.  The layouts on both sides i.e. ICT side & Testing Department side were changed. On ICT side one room for MIS / IT Manager was added and electrical room was changed into store. There are no minutes of Director ICT’s meetings with the Architect.

 

4.18     The Director Projects reported that during all this, contractor’s work could not be started as the layouts were drastically changed. This was a work which has intense works of electrical and data wiring. All requirements need to be redesigned because of the layout changes. He said that the drawings of 5th December 2009 were sent to Director ICT and Testing Services Coordinator on 8th December 2009 for their consent. It was again mentioned in that letter that plans will stand accepted if no comments are received within 3 days. On 11th December 2009, a letter was received on email written by the Architect to Director ICT, Director Projects and Registrar with layouts of overall Administration Building. He informed that in the layouts he has incorporated all the requirements. He stated that the Director Projects had sent his comments / corrections on September 14, 2009 and Registrar on 24th November 2009. These layouts were totally different from those of 5th December 2009 plan. In this layout complete laboratory was removed and relocated on 4th Floor. The capacity was increased to 60. The Architect was told in June 2009 that there will be no construction on the roof of existing building. He did not consider it. He has now been asked to send proof of structural safety through his Structural Consultants. Only after that the construction on the 4th Floor will be considered. We may have to get it vetted too.

 

4.19     The Director Projects giving details of the present situation stated that:

a)   The Architect has been given a go ahead to develop the construction drawings of the 3rd Floor related to server area only and issue to the contractors

 

b)      The Architect has to give a structural safety certificate from the Consultants that Fourth Floor can be constructed. (That would be subject to vetting by Structural Specialists).

 

c)   There has been lot of changes in the scope of work of the Contractor who has already been awarded the work of Server Room extension.  Drawings of 11th December are totally different from those of 5th December 2009 and have nor elations with the drawings on which work has been awarded.

 

d)   Within a period of only 6 days (between 5th Dec 2009 and 11th Dec 2009), the planning of Server Room area has been drastically changed. .

 

e)   Architect may take another 3-4 weeks to re-design the services of this area provided a clear decision to adopt 5th December plan or 11th December plan is approved.

 

f)   It is only after the new designs, new BOQ and estimate are received that contractual status of awarded contract be decided. Till such time contractor will be kept on hold. It may be noted that mobilization advance was paid to the contractor          .

 

g)   Work of Testing Department side cannot be added to the existing contract as that contract is of a small portion on ICT side.

 

4.20     In view of the above events pertaining to multiple revisions of ICT and Testing Department areas on 3rd Floor, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to:

 

a)   Approve deviation from approved plan of 24th June 2009, as requested by ICT Department based on changes in server equipment requirements and associated services and equipments. The revision will involve dismantling of the Server Room completed last year at a cost of Rs.1.9 million.

 

b)      Approve one of the plans produced by Architect on 5th December 2009 and 11th December 2009. December 11th Plan will involve structural investigation of the building as well as the extra cost.

 

4.21     The Director said that 60 students capacity requirement is on the basis of class size.  He said that if it is the academic requirement then we have to issue variation order.

 

4.22     The Registrar suggested that only a room should be provided and networking will be done by the ICT people.

 

4.23     The Director wanted to know as to why the cost would be doubled when the third floor for testing and ICT (server lab) would be given to the same contractor and the 1st and 2nd floor will be given to the new contractor.

 

4.24     The Director Projects said that the scope of work has doubled so the value of the contract will be doubled.  He said that they have changed the plan and they have to give a go ahead to the Architect to give BOQ.

 

4.25     The Registrar said that if the volume increases then according to PPRA IBA will have to ask a rebate from the contractors.

 

4.26     The Director Projects pointed out that it is not only the matter of getting a rebate but it will impose contractual limitations as the bids were obtained on open competition basis.

 

DECISION

 

4.27     The Committee requested the Director Projects to work out what would be the cost due to increase in scope.

 

4.28     The Committee decided that the labs capacity should be increased from 40 to 60.

 

4.29     The Committee in principle approved deviation from approved plan of 24th June 2009, as requested by ICT Department based on changes in server equipment requirements and associated services and equipments. But the existing server room will not be dismantled, only expanded.

 

4.30     The Committee also approved the layout plan which incorporated construction of computer laboratory on the fourth floor. The Committee advised the Director Projects to ask the Architect that they should ask their structural consultants that the construction of 4th floor will be safe and give a proof that structurally the building had been analysed under code for earthquake resistant. If required consultants will have to get it vetted at his cost from an expert.
