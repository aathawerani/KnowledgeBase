MINUTES OF 153RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MAY 16, 2011

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      Status Reports from:

 

Director Finance:

 

1.1       Re-appropriation in the budget for new KESC Sub-station: The Director Finance reported that the bid cost is under Rs.2 million so they have approached the Director IBA for re-appropriation.

 

1.2       Up-date on the Task Force on student loan: The Director Finance reported that the HEC has finalized the Student Loan policy in coordination with State Bank of Pakistan whereby Commercial Banks would implement Student Loan Policy. He said that since all HEC projects are static at the moment, hence the final implementation is delayed.

                                                                                                 

1.3       Control on overtime: The Director Finance reported that overtime has been discussed with Registrar and he has started controlling the late sitting.

 

            The Registrar said that from one of the departments he received overtime along with bills for lunch. He clarified that those who are getting overtime are not entitled for the reimbursement of bills for the meals.     

 

1.4       Banking Services at IBA doorsteps: The Director Finance reported that the matter was pending with Askari Bank. 

 

 

 

            The Director Projects informed that the bank officers wanted to open up a mini branch within the main campus premises with the front of the bank having direct access from the road in front of the Campus.   He further informed that he has asked the Bank officers to provide the bank’s layout plan so that the Campus Architects could be asked to include the building in the external development of the campus.

 

            The Registrar said that opening of a branch facing the main road would require permission from the KU.

 

            The Committee decided that instead of allowing the bank to open a branch facing the main road, the bank should be allowed to set up a kiosk within the IBA premises. The Committee requested the Director Finance to convey the decision of the EC to the Bank.  

 

1.5       The Director Finance would ask those students who have not cleared their dues to clear them before the semester final exams: The Director Finance reported that two reminders were sent to students and considerable amount of dues has been recovered.  He said that the final list would be placed before the Final Examination.

 

Registrar:

 

1.6       The Registrar to install the Attendance Device at the City Campus: The Registrar reported that the device will be installed in the Academic Block on completion of demo for three weeks.

 

1.7       Physical fitness tests of security staff: the Registrar reported that route march was undertaken for the guards along with lecturers emphasising the need for them to be physically fit. However, he said that rigorous tests cannot be undertaken as most of the guards are heavily committed with their duties and are in age group where armed forces type physical fitness tests cannot be done by them. He said that he is planning route march for the security guards every semester.

 

1.8       The Registrar to negotiate with BBCL for reducing the charges for up-grading the documentary: The Registrar reported that the matter of up-grading of documentary is dead locked due to BBCL not agreeing to reduce the cost or share the stock shorts of the original documentary with IBA.

 

            Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he has invited quotations for the new documentary and if the quotation is of the same amount as demanded by BBCL for up-grading the documentary then they would go for the new documentary.

 

            Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that in the agreement it should be mentioned that stock shorts would be the IBA property.    

 

1.9       The Registrar to contact the Karachi University first and, if not satisfied, then the nearest quality hospital not on the IBA Panel for providing verification and referral services: The Registrar said that he was pursuing this.

 

1.10     Vacating of house at IBA Staff Town occupied by Mr. Mohammad Younus, ex-Daftary of IBA: The Registrar said that Mr. Younus has failed to respond to all initiatives taken by IBA including providing of extension in date and finally conveying IBA’s willingness to contribute some money on humanitarian ground from welfare fund.  He reported that they have now embarked on the legal track and a case was being filed by IBA’s legal advisor M/s. Mumtaz Associates against Mr. Younus for trespassing and illegal occupation of the IBA property.

 

The Registrar said that Dr. Talat Wizarat informed him that the daughter of Mr. Younus had asked her to arrange Rs.....lacs for them to get the house outside against their settlement in IBA. The Registrar enquired from the Finance and the Finance Department reported that the amount due towards Mr. Younus does not exceed Rs..... lacs. Meanwhile, he said that the water and electricity connections remain cut off to the house of Mr. Younus for the purpose of expediting his vacation of the house.

 

The Registrar said that Rao Nisar Ahmad Khan and Mr. Mohammad Naeem are living in the staff town beyond the allowed period of stay after their retirement.  He requested the Director Finance to see the issues involved in their cases and try to resolve them so that they may vacate the houses.

 

1.11     Lockers for MBA students: The Registrar reported that purchase and provisioning of lockers for all students is under consideration.  Wooden lockers available in the break out rooms continue to be used by the MBA students during their project sessions.

 

            Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that there are so many students and all of them may ask for the lockers.  He clarified that he was not against providing lockers but for MBA class only.    

 

DECISION

 

1.12     The Committee noted and approved the up-dates along with the suggestions noted above.

 

ACTION

 

1.13     The Director Finance would convey the decision of the EC to Askari Bank that instead of opening a branch facing the main road, the bank should set up a kiosk within the IBA premises.

 

1.14     The Director Finance would see the issues involved in the cases of Rao Nisar Ahmad Khan and Mr. Mohammad Naeem and try to resolve them so that they may vacate the houses.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve IBA External Development and Infrastructure works.

 

3.1       The Director Projects said that the work of Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of the External Development work at IBA Main Campus was awarded to M/s. NBCL in January 2011. He said that they had developed conceptual planning considering the requirements communicated to them through an RFP which were checked by the Engineers at the Projects Department and IBA Architects Ms. Sana Kashif. Several meetings were held with them and observations were communicated to them. They have incorporated the corrections communicated to them and have been invited to present their final design in the meeting. He requested the EC members to give their valuable advice according to their requirements and their vision as to how the Campus should look in the future. Based on the advice the Architects will give the campus a final shape.

 

3.2       The representatives of M/s. NBCL giving presentation on the concept design, said that they have divided the design into three zones; Ceremonial Zone, Education and Social Interaction Zone; and the Sports Zone.  About parking they said that it is not just in one zone; it has been distributed into various zones. They said that since there is a large cafeteria, the existing cafeteria may be converted into clinic or public safety department. They said that they have proposed a Monumentary Garden for each batch of graduates.

 

3.3       The Director H.R. said that there are 800+ IBA graduates and new batches would also be passing out so for such a large number of graduates, the monument wall will be too crowdy.

 

3.4       After the presentation by the representatives of M/s. NBCL, the representatives of M/s. S. Mehboob & Co., gave a presentation on the mechanical side of the external development plan which included fire alarm system, CCTV surveillance system, ICT network infrastructure and other mechanical services.

 

3.5       Explaining the features of fire alarm system, Mr. Maroof Mehboob said that they would be using Halon free material because it is environment friendly.

 

3.6       The Director Finance wanted to know if the fire alarm system would cover the whole area.  The Director Projects said that the arrangement for fire fighting will be independent but monitoring can be done from the Control Room.

 

3.7       Mr. Mehboob said that for sewage presently there is no infrastructure.  He presented the proposed sewerage system and the water supply scheme. He said that keeping in view the requirements for reserved water for fire, irrigation and other uses for three days, 120,000 gallons of water would be required to be stored which may be split at least in two tanks. He also presented details of the proposed water treatment plant.

 

3.8       Mr. Akeel Bilgrami observed that maintenance of water treatment plant involves a lot of problem. He said that sewage water can be used directly for irrigation and watering plants.

 

3.9       Mr. Mehboob said that maintenance of treatment plant is not so complex. Moreover, from health’s point of view untreated water is not recommended. However, he said that they may discuss and decide this.

 

3.10     The Registrar said that he has some observations to make. The first one is that the average consumption of water is about 30,000 gallons per week. This they can double keeping in view the future requirements but he said that the requirement calculated by the Consultants is very high. His second observation was with regard to the level of the ground and the proposed location of sewage deposit which according to him was not the right place. He also observed that they should prioritize what should be done first. He said that presently there is no proper septic tank.  Another observation was that space should be developed for movement of tankers.  He said that the above are basic problems which should be addressed first. He further said that because of the increasing number of graduating students the existing space of the area where convocation is held may not be sufficient so they may have to use football ground for such events. He suggested that Architects should review the placement of the pavilions considering the sun movement so that the sun light should not come directly on the face of the spectators.

 

3.11     The Director Projects said that he has received requirements from faculty and students for playing cricket at night for which infrastructure is needed.  The Registrar seconded the opinion and stated that the night light provision be made in the infrastructure.

 

3.12     The Registrar added that it should not be restricted to sports only; it may be used for other activities so it should also be taken on priority. The Registrar also suggested that at the entrance, space should be provided for placing banners for events and there should also be donors’ walls at the reception area.

 

3.13     The Director Projects informed that the project of water supply system is already under construction. Similarly the electrical infrastructure is also being designed by an independent consultant. He further informed that a meeting will be held to integrate planning and designing of all the utilities.

 

3.14   The Director Projects informed that he has received some comments from the faculty members too which are being forwarded to the Architects.

 

DECISION

 

3.15   The EC appreciated the work done by the Architects and approved the planning as proposed along with the comments communicated to them.

 

ACTION

 

3.16     The Architects will incorporate all the comments in their proposed plan and develop the final schematic drawings and submit to IBA for approval.

 

3.17     Director Projects will arrange a meeting with the Architects, their consultants, the water supply and sewerage consultants and the electrical infrastructure consultants to develop coordinated plans for utilities network.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
