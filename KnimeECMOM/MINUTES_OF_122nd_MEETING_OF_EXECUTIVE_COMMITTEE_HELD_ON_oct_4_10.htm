MINUTES OF THE 122nd MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 4, 2010

 

The 122nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 4, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Societies’ Meeting Room, Academic Block, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

4. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

6. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director H.R.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider EC decisions implementation Reports:

 

Status Report from the Registrar:

 

1.1       The Registrar to review the IBA agreement with PEPSI: The Registrar reported that PEPSI has installed the AC’s under the same contract. He also reported that they have dropped the condition that the duration of contract be extended to five years.  

 

1.2       Written approval for continuing with existing schedule of six working days: The Registrar reported that the Sindh Government is reverting its decision of six working days.

 

1.3       Revised draft on Honor Council: The Registrar reported that the honour pledge has been included in the Student Handbook along with information of empowerment of faculty members to take action against the students in accordance with the IBA Code Book.  

  

1.4       A different IBA ID Card for Faculty:  The Registrar reported that according to his observation only 20% faculty wears IBA ID Card. He said that until all the faculty members wear the IBA ID Card, it would be useless to have different card for them. He requested the Associate Deans to request their faculty to wear IBA ID Card.

 

1.5       Preparation of Maintenance Schedule: The Registrar reported that the preparation of Maintenance Schedule is in process. However, he said that because of new things the maintenance requirement has substantially changed.  He cited the example of the failure of energy savers in the Boys’ Hostel.

 

            The Director Projects said that he would check and find out the reasons for the energy savers getting fused.

 

 

DECISION

 

1.3       The up-dates given above were noted.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve ICT Help Desk Services:

 

2.1       The Director ICT presenting the detailed ICT Help Desk Services document stated that the purpose of this Help Desk Services (HDS) Document is to acquaint end users with the policies and the ICT HDS standards. He said that the items that have been included in this document are the hours of operation, contact methods, departmental goals and end-user responsibilities. It may be used as a reference when requesting technical assistance from ICT. He said that this document describes basic Services Level for IBA-wide ICT helpdesk function. The ICT helpdesk services and support will be provided for all software and hardware that has been approved, purchased and installed according to the IBA ICT Policies. The helpdesk is the point of contact for all computer hardware and software problems faced by the users. He said that the basic Helpdesk numbers for the two campuses are:

 

·         CITY CAMPUS:        1811

·         MAIN CAMPUS:        241

 

2.2       Referring to the areas which ICT Helpdesk Services would be covering, Dr. Sayeed Ghani pointed out that UPS has not been included into it.

 

2.3       The Director ICT said that when there will be computerized UPS, it will be in the domain of the ICT. However, he agreed to include UPS also.   

 

2.4       The Director Projects suggested to include multi-media and hardware in the list of ICT Helpdesk Services.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee approved the ICT Help Desk Services document incorporating the suggestions made in 2.3 and 2.4 above.

 

ACTION

 

2.6       The Director ICT would get the ICT Help Desk Services document disseminated after incorporating the suggestions noted in 2.3 and 2.4 above.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve award of work of tender drawings, tender documents including specifications, conditions of contract and BoQ of detailed supervision and Project Management of new Academic Block at IBA Main Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the RFP for the detailed Supervision and Project Management of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus was sent on 21st September, 2010 to all the eleven Pre-qualified Project Managers. Earlier they were sent RFP on 17th June, 2010 but it was cancelled by the Executive Committee in its 120th meeting dated 20th September, 2010 for the reason that the 80:20 rule for composite evaluation of Technical and Financial bids was not included in the RFP.  He said that the Project Managers have now been asked to submit their proposals i.e. Technical as well as Financial Proposal based on the two envelop as per the PPRA rules latest by 11th October, 2010.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To award contract to the lowest bidder for Bulk Water Supply Line and Reservoir Work.

 

5.1       The Director HEC Projects in his note stated that with the approval of the TEC, CPC, EC and the A&FC, M/s. NESPAK were appointed as consultant for the project of Bulk Water Supply Line and Reservoir Work who did the designing work, pre-qualification of specialized contractors, preparation of tender documents etc. The tenders were then issued to four pre-qualified contractors on July 28, 2010. Out of the 4, only three contractors participated in the bidding and the bids were opened on September 4, 2010. The Technical Evaluation Committee and the Central Purchase Committee reviewed the bids and approved award of the work to the lowest bidder namely; M/s. Samad Khan Babar & Co. at the bid price of Rs.36,764,101/- The Comparative Statement was also presented to the Executive Committee.

 

5.2       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia reported that water storage tank has also been included in the project.

  

DECISION

 

5.3       The Committee recommended to the A&FC for approval for award of contract to the lowest bidder i.e. M/s. Samad Khan Babar & Co. for Bulk Water Supply Line and Reservoir Work at the bid price of Rs.36,764,101/-

 

ACTION

5.4       The Director HEC Projects would present the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the A&FC for approval.
