The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 Provision of Furniture on hire to IBA Boys Hostel.

1.1 The Registrar reported that there is a shortage of accommodation in Boys’ Hostel; there are 45 students for whom no room is available in the Boys’ Hostel. He said that the gym and laundry which are not being used (as old block’s gym and laundry are being used), can be converted into dormitory but there is no bed/furniture. He said that there are two options; either to acquire the required bed and furniture on rent for six months or purchase them from the market.

1.2 The Dean & Director suggested that as furniture would be required for the new blocks of the Boys’ Hostel, we should purchase it according to the specifications given by the Consultant for one block. The Registrar said that such pieces of furniture are not readily available in the market; it would take time but we need them urgently.

1.3 Mr. M. Sohail Khan said that after much efforts, one of the furniture manufacturers agreed that they, after purchasing furniture pieces from different vendors, can provide on rent to IBA. The Registrar said that rent for furniture including bed, mattress (foam), Almirah, for 45 students for six months would cost around Rs.1.8 million. Mr. Jehanzeb said that they would have to hire the furniture for a year because construction of a block of the boys’ hostel would not be completed before a year. The Dean & Director said that if we rent the furniture for a year then the rate should further be reduced. Mr. M. Sohail Khan said that he has floated RFQ for five months rental. However, he informed the Committee that the one who has agreed to supply furniture on rent has asked for advance rent as in vogue for rental cases.

1.4 The Registrar said that one option is to temporarily shift the furniture and 30 beds from new Girls Hostel to the boys’ hostel, although it would be difficult to move from there. The Dean & Director appreciated the idea of the Registrar and suggested to move the furniture and 30 beds to the Boys’ Hostel and acquire the remaining beds and furniture on rent for a year. He further suggested that preference should be given to out of city students in providing accommodation at Boys’ Hostel.

1.5 The Dean & Director asked Mr. Adnan Hameed to request Mr. Mahmood Ali to prepare tender for the furniture including vanishing blind for the Boys’ Hostel. The delivery schedule can be staggered to suit our fundings.

DECISION & ACTION

1.6 The Committee approved the proposal of the Registrar to shift the furniture and 30 beds from new Girls Hostel to Boys’ Hostel and to acquire furniture and beds for the remaining students on rent for one year.

1.7 Mr. Adnan Hameed would request Mr. Mahmood Ali to prepare tender for the furniture including vanishing blind for the Boys’ Hostel.


Item No.2 Status Reports by:

REGISTRAR:

2.1 Highlights of the Library and the Automation System to be included in the IBA documentary: The Registrar reported that the existing documentary was being edited to include new developments at IBA. Library portion was pending owing to civil works not yet completed.

2.2 The Registrar to examine the possibility of getting empty water Tanker: The Registrar reported that the tanker owner was contacted but the response was not too encouraging. One owner quoted rates which were Rs.7000/- per day for 8 hours inclusive of holidays or Rs.1200/- per trip inclusive of all charges. Time of operation will be 4 pm onwards. One trip will take 2 hours so we will get 4 trips (20,000 gallons) in one day. Our current daily consumption of the main campus is 6/7 tankers (35000 gallons). He said that Karachi University will not allow tankers to operate in the morning; it can be operated after 4:00 p.m. but it does not suit to the tanker owners. The Dean & Director advised to use the tanker early morning before the traffic comes to the Karachi University. The Registrar said that he would explore it.

SYED JEHANZEB:

2.3 Mr. Jehanzeb to prepare cash flow forecasts for the Boys’ Hostel: Mr. Jehanzeb said that the payment of Rs.59.76 million has been made so far and the projected expense is upto Rs.40 million. So the total estimated expense is Rs.99.76 million against which Rs.30 million has been received from Government of Sindh. He said that Rs.52.5 million is expected to be released further by the Government of Sindh, making a total of Rs.82.5 million. Thus the short fall would be of Rs.17.26 million.

The Dean & Director hoped that the balance would be released in the fourth quarter. He suggested to do bridge financing for the balance amount. He said that Rs.150 million was approved by the Sindh Government after much effort but out of it Rs.50 million may lapse.

DECISION & ACTION

2.4 The Committee noted the up-dates as noted above.

2.5 The Registrar would check if the tanker can be available early morning before the traffic comes to the Karachi University.


Item No.3 To consider the proposal to allow prayer break 10 minutes after the Azaan for Maghrib prayer.

3.1 The Committee considered the proposal for allowing prayer break 10 minutes after the Azaan for Maghrib prayer.

DECISION & ACTION

3.2 As there was no issue on allowing prayer break 10 minutes after the Azaan for Maghrib, the Committee approved the proposal.

3.3 The Registrar would get the decision implemented.


Item No.4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Advance Payment to Ziafco.

4.1 Mr. Adnan Hameed said that M/s Ziafco have requested for 35% advance of the project cost of Rs.33 million.

DECISION

4.2 The Committee allowed 35% advance payment to M/s Ziafco against a bank guarantee.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
