MINUTES OF 277TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 23, 2013

The 277th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 23, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                       Associate Dean, FBA

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider a request from Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects regarding permission to buy desktop and laptop at book value.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed in his email stated that a desktop computer and a laptop have been in his use for about five years.  He requested the EC to consider allowing him to buy these at their book value or market price when he leaves IBA in February 2014.

 

1.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that computer/laptop as per policy can be given at the market assessed value and not at the book value. The Dean & Director said that only the laptop can be allowed at the market assessed value; both laptop and desktop cannot be allowed.  He wanted to know as to who would assess the market value. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that one person from Finance and one from ICT departments would assess the market price of the laptop.  

 

DECISION

 

1.3       The Committee allowed Mr. Shameem Ahmed to take the laptop only at the market assessed value when he leaves IBA in February 2014.

 

Item No.2       To consider proposal for compensation to staff for their involvement in invigilation duties, and charging examination fee from students.

 

2.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan referred to the EC decision of 267th meeting held on September 30, 2013 wherein it was decided that:

 

a)      Examination is an integral part of teaching, and faculty members have to perform invigilation for their papers for the whole duration of exam not just for 10 minutes.

b)      Except the Associate Deans, all faculty members including chairpersons, program directors, program coordinators have to perform invigilation duty.

c)      MS and PhD students would also be assigned invigilation duties.

 

2.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that in the same meeting of EC he was asked to examine the proposal of compensation to staff for their involvement in invigilation duty and charging examination fee from students in light of the practice followed in other universities and report to the EC for a final decision. 

 

2.3       In view of the above, Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that four different universities were contacted to know the status of Invigilation Remuneration and charging of exam fees. Following is the status:

2.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan made the following suggestions:

 

1.      The exam duty may be assigned first to the faculty (full time, visiting, TAs, RAs, MS and Phd Scholars) and to the staff of examination department. So the no. of invigilators required in a year could be minimized.

 

2.      Employees of other departments (i.e. other than examination department staff, faculty full time, faculty visiting) are not hired for examination / advocacy purpose and thus be paid for their added vigilant services.

 

 2.6      The Dean & Director observed that NED is paying Rs.300 per session for invigilation duty whereas Karachi University is paying Rs.600 to its BPS-1 to 16 employees. He wanted to know if Rs.600 is for per session or for the whole exam.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that it is for per session. However, he said that he would get it reconfirmed again.

 

2.7       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that 1627 invigilators (non faculty) in a year would be required. However, he said that the number of non-faculty invigilators can be reduced if we make invigilation compulsory for faculty. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that faculty, MS and Ph.D scholars are doing invigilation duty. He said that every faculty is doing one additional invigilation. To a question the Dean & Director clarified that graduate students serving as TA’s/RA’s and MS and Ph.D scholars would be remunerated for invigilation duty. However, faculty including visiting faculty would not be paid for this. He said that undergraduate TA’s/RA’s would not be involved in invigilation duty.

 

2.8       Mr. Adnan Hameed suggested to include program offices also along with examination department for compulsory examination duty. He said that payment against invigilation duties can affect employees’ preference, as performing paid invigilation duties during office hours is very likely to be preferred over the departmental responsibilities. Consequently more employees will be willing to participate in this activity; and in this situation assigning of invigilation duties will also become a challenge, as those employees who will not be assigned duties can complain that they are being discriminated. In addition, he said that paying remuneration to some employees and not paying to others (those who are on compulsory examination duty) for the same assignment may also be taken as discrimination. The Dean & Director agreed with Mr. Adnan Hameed and said that he does not want any discrimination. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there would not be much financial impact on including examination department also for the payment. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that during exam, normal work suffers because of the involvement of staff in exam duties. He suggested that if the staff members work for exam duty on payment, they should not use it as an excuse for not doing the job they have been hired for. They should put extra hours to clear their backlogs and for that they should not be entitled to overtime. Mr. Khalili clarified that from Program Offices and IT, all staff members are not involved in the examination duty at the same time; they are assigned duties on rotation. Moreover, he said that staff members are involved for 1½ hours in term exam and for 3 hours in final exam; not for the whole day. The HoDs should ensure that because of the additional work, their existing work does not suffer. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that they may deduct number of hours spent on invigilation duties on monthly basis from over time claims. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the effective date for payment of remuneration and charging examination fee from students will be after the approval of budget 2014-15 by BoG.

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee, subject to approval by the Board, approved the proposal of charging examination fee from students @Rs.1000 per student per regular semester, and payment of remuneration @Rs.400/- for midterm exam and Rs.800/- for final exam to the non-teaching staff involved in invigilation duty. Faculty members including visiting faculty would not be entitled to the remuneration on invigilation duties.

 

2.10     The Committee further decided that those staff members who get the remuneration for performing invigilation duties will not be entitled to receive overtime payment for the number of hours spent on invigilation duties in a month. The concerned department heads would ensure that the routine work assigned to their staff as normal duty is completed for the days devoted for invigilation duty. The staff receiving the payments can be asked by the heads of departments to stay 1½ hours and maximum three hours extra on the days they are invigilating midterm and final exams respectively.

 

ACTION

 

2.11     The Director Finance would include the proposed examination fee to be charged from students and payment to non-teaching staff on invigilation duty in the draft budget 2014-15 for approval by the Board.

 

Item No.3       Proposal for Retention of NESPAK for Sports Ground and the Library Projects.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the agenda paper has been initiated and was being presented based on the following:

 

·         Decisions of the Executive Committee taken in its 275th meeting held on December 2, 2013 on two cases pertaining to outstanding fee of Nespak on the sports ground project and the library project.

 

·         Decision of the Executive Committee taken in the above said meeting regarding extension of Nespak services on Library Project.

 

3.2       The Director Project said that as he was hospitalized since November 30, 2013, the decisions of the EC were communicated verbally to Mr. Abdul Mateen of M/s Nespak as soon as he was able to read the minutes. He said that the following was the response of Nespak:

 

·         They are ready to reduce the outstanding fee by 20% as was done by them on other IBA Projects, instead of 31%

·         They declined to outgo the fee of one month of the Sports Ground Project. However 20% reduction is acceptable to them.

·         They are not in favor of conclusion of their services after 31st December 2013 as most of the critical works on the MEP and Air-conditioning system are still far behind schedule and that IBA’s Project Department do not have Mechanical and HVAC staff to provide detailed supervision.

 

3.3       The Director Projects proposed the following two options for consideration by the EC:

 

OPTION  A.

 

Conclude the contract of Nespak on 31st December 2013 and depute the Electrical and Mechanical team of Maintenance department to provide detailed supervision of Installation, testing and commissioning of Electrical, Mechanical, air-conditioning, fire alarm system, fire fighting system etc including the checking and verifying all the relevant bills of the contractor.

 

(The MEP and Air-conditioning Consultants could have been asked to provide the detailed supervision but they do not have the capacity to provide the detailed supervision services.)

 

OPTION  B

 

Retain the services of Nespak till 28th February 2014 or completion of Project whichever is earlier. This is in the interest of the Project as explained in earlier paper to CPC and the Executive Committee. (NESPAK may be asked to give us only specifically required manpower instead of the whole team.)

 

3.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve one of the above options so that further actions could be taken.  He presented the letter from Nespak along with the list of remaining works.

 

3.5       The Dean & Director asked the reasons for the delay in completion of the project.  The Director Projects said that his assessment is that they have given quite good relaxation to the Contractor in terms of payment. He said that they have to tell NESPAK that they (Nespak) have to get the work completed by the Contractor by February 28, 2014 or earlier. The Registrar suggested to give time line for every work. The Dean & Director said that we have to use Nespak on sensitive items where we do not have our expertise. He suggested to get specific staff for such work. The Director Projects said that he has recommended the same in Option ‘B’ above. He said that he has invited Mr. Abdul Mateen of Nespak to answer any query from the EC.

 

3.6       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Abdul Mateen as to why he could not get the work completed by November 30, 2013.  Mr. Abdul Mateen said that he had been constantly reporting about the performance of the Contractor for the last six months but the Contractor is unable to do the work on time. He said that the nature of work left is very critical.  The Dean & Director said that he wants Nespak to supervise, test and certify HVAC work and for that provide expertise. He said that he is answerable to A&FC to which he has said that the project would be completed by November 30, 2013 but again he has to cut a sorry figure that instead of November 30, 2013 it would be completed by end of February 2014. Mr. Abdul Mateen apprehended that even in February 2014 the Contractor would not be able to complete the project because of financial crunch. He said that payment to sub-contractor for woodwork payment has been defaulted by the Contractor. The Dean & Director said that he had devised a mechanism of payment to sub-contractors through the payments made by IBA to the contractor. He said that as soon as the contractor makes payment to subcontractor the payment to contractor will be released. Mr. Mahmood of Projects Department confirmed that they are following the same. However, he said that the earlier balance of sub-contractor has not been cleared by the Contractor.  The Dean & Director said that it is not our responsibility to get the earlier balance of sub-contractor cleared. He suggested to make payment of Rs.850,000 to Woodwork from Rs.1 million available in the outstanding payment to be made by IBA to the Contractor. Mr. Abdul Mateen said that 80% of the payment has been made for HVAC. The testing and commissioning of HVAC has been left for which 20% has been retained.  The Dean & Director said that as soon as testing and commissioning is done, 20% would be released.  He asked the Director Projects to inform the sub-contractor of HVAC that we have to get this work done and as soon as Nespak certifies, we would release the 20%.  The Director Projects said that 5% will have to be retained for summer testing. The Dean & Director said that providing project management services one month after the extended period is the responsibility of Nespak. He said that by January 2014 the HVAC work should be completed. He said that he cannot justify any extension for the Consultant and Project Managers to the A&FC.

 

3.7       Mr. Abdul Mateen suggested to check the validity of performance bond and one month maintenance services with the Contractor and get it revalidated if required.  The Dean & Director asked the Director Finance and the Director Projects to look into the issue and get it resolved satisfactorily. He said that after testing and commissioning of HVAC if the contractor cannot carry out the work, we should consider terminating his contract and get the remaining work done by sub-contractors directly. He asked Mr. Mahmood and Mr. Jahanzeb to release 15% payment to the contractor after the commissioning, testing and certification of HVAC and retain 5% for summer testing.  He also requested Mr. Mehdi of Nespak to expedite verification. 

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee accepted the request of NESAPAK to reduce the outstanding fee by 20% as was done by them on other IBA Projects, instead of 31%.  The same 20% reduction would be applicable on the Project Management fee of Rs.187,671/- for one month for the Sports Ground Project.

 

3.9       The Committee approved Option ‘B’ i.e. “Retain the services of Nespak till 28th February 2014 or completion of Project whichever is earlier. NESPAK would give us only specifically required manpower instead of the whole team.

 

ACTION

 

3.10     The Director Projects would get the validity of performance bond checked and get it revalidated if required. 

 

3.11     Mr. Mahmood and Mr. Jahanzeb would release 15% payment to the HVAC sub-contractor and retain 5% for summer testing as soon as commissioning and testing of HVAC is done and NESPAK certifies it.
