Item No.3       To consider the proposal for utilization of Visiting Faculty Residence for          regular faculty.

 

3.1       The Registrar stated that IBA has recently commissioned an air-conditioned, fully furnished facility comparable to any 4 star hotel for accommodating visitors to IBA particularly members of foreign faculty / guest speakers. Referred to as the “Habibullah Visiting Faculty Residences” the premises is located in the IBA Staff Town.  It comprises 3 suites, 11 single rooms, a lounge cum dining hall and associated spaces including a kitchen, gym, business center, prayer hall etc. The gym and business centre are however yet to be equipped. The utilization of the premises is strictly on basis of payment except for guests. The current tariff rates are as under:

 

a)      Meal charges on actual

b)      Room charges for permanent / full-time faculty moving in / relocating from outside Karachi:

 

i)       First week No Charge

ii)      Second Week 25% per day of (Rs. 5,500/=) the approved room rate

iii)     Third Week 50% per day of (Rs. 5,500/=) the approved room rate

iv)     Fourth Week 75% per day of (Rs. 5,500/=) the approved room rate

v)      Fourth Week and beyond 100% / full room rent per day (Rs. 5,500/=), for a maximum period of
         stay of up to 45 days.

 

3.2       The Registrar presented details of the occupancy of the VFR since its inauguration in January 2014.  The Registrar said that presently the IBA is in the process of recruiting new faculty often from outside the city. These faculty members are provided complimentary accommodation for one week after which they are required to pay as per rates mentioned above. To avoid heavy payments of room charges, some of the faculty members intend utilizing the Boys’ Hostel where the rates are cheaper than the VFR. However, due to shortage of space, this accommodation cannot be provided except in the summer.

 

3.3       In the light of the above, the Registrar proposed that the 4 single rooms on the 2nd floor of the VFR may be exempted from the above tariff rates and be allowed to be used for accommodating faculty members who plan to stay for longer duration. Such faculty members will be provided the accommodation at Rs.2000/- per day or equivalent of house allowance which they receive as part of their remuneration. This is the practice being followed by HEC and HEJ.

           

3.4       The Dean & Director suggested to make categories; category one should be for the invited guests who would not pay anything. Another category is our new faculty members who are coming from outside Karachi/Pakistan who will be facilitated until they get their own home.

           

3.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that instead of per week rate, it should be per month rate, and instead of first week, it should be for the first month that there would be no charge. The Dean & Director said that we already have serious difficulties in recruiting teachers from outside Karachi and it is, therefore, necessary to provide some incentive to the new faculty who choose to teach at IBA.  He said that the room rate should be applicable for daily visitors but for full time faculty from outside Karachi some fixed rate should be charged.

 

3.6       The Registrar informed the Committee that Dr. & Mrs. Ahmed Junaid who have been staying in Room No.6/Suite C since July 8, 2014 have not been charged for 30 days after obtaining approval from the Dean & Director. He requested that as Dr. Junaid has not been able to find a suitable accommodation, he requested that he (Dr. Junaid) be given an extension of two more months to say free of cost.

 

DECISION

         

3.7       The Committee decided that the room rate @ Rs.5,500/- per day would be charged from daily visitors.  However, for the full time faculty from outside Karachi the following fixed charges would be applicable:

 

a.       First month No Charges

b.      Second month Rs.25,000/- for the month.

c.       Third month Rs.35,000/-

d.       Fourth month and onward Rs.45,000/- per month.

 

3.8       The Committee agreed to allow two months’ extension to Dr. Junaid to stay in the VFR   free of cost.

4.3       The Director Projects said that as per the comparative statement the lowest bidder M/s DR Construction is 11% higher than Engineering Estimate, the 2nd Lowest bidder M/s AH Construction is 33% higher than Engineering Estimate and the highest bidder M/s Total Construction is 66% higher than Engineering Estimate. The bids were scrutinized item wise and the following observations were made:

 

M/s DR construction

 

M/s DR Construction is the lowest bidder and they quoted 35% high in Civil works because they quoted 8% low in MEP works and 2% low in MS Stair. If we go over all, their bid is balance and rates are workable. However an assurance can be obtained from contractor for MEP and MS Stair items that they will provide the quality material and produce quality workmanship.

 

M/s AH Construction

 

M/s AH Construction are 2nd Lowest bidder. They quoted 71% higher than Engineering Estimate in Civil works 11% high in MEP works and 168% high in MS Stair item. Overall their bid is 56% higher than Engineering Estimate which is exorbitantly very high and not recommended for execution of works.

 

M/s Total Construction

 

M/s Total Construction are the highest bidder and they quoted 82% higher than Engineering Estimate. They are equal in MS Stair item with Engineering Estimate. Overall their bid is 67% higher than Engineering Estimate the rates are exorbitantly very high and not recommended for execution of works.
