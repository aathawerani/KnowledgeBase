MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON SEPTEMBER 1, 2008 (ELEVENTH MEETING)

The eleventh meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, September 1, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Chairman
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                          Professor
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                                Registrar
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr.  Ali Taha                                     Director Finance
  6. Mr. Moeid Sultan                                General Manager Finance & Accounts
  7. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                         H.R. Manager
  8. Mr. Fahad Javed                                (Asstt. Manager Finance by special invitation)
  9. Mr. Kamran Abdullah                           (Assistant Coordinator Testing by special invitation)

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Revision of Testing Service Remuneration

1.1       The General Manager Finance & Accounts presented the total budgetary impact based on last years test data. He said that with the proposed increase in testing service remuneration, the expenses will increase by 124%. He said that the presently 60% of the total revenue goes to expenses which with the revised rate would go to 80% of the total revenue. 

DECISION

1.2              The Committee was convinced that the revision was justified in view of the fact that the rate was last approved in 2001 and since then no change in the rate was done although the inflation has gone up by over 100 percent over this period.

1.3       The Committee approved the revised rate for IBA admission tests for 2008 onward and decided to report to the Board through A&FC.

1.4       The Committee decided that for tests other than IBA’s admission test, such as PSO, Khushhali Bank etc. the Testing Department would present an estimate of the total revenue and total expenditures for consideration by the Executive Committee. The IBA would approve only those tests which would generate some additional revenues for the IBA after deducting all the expenses.

ACTION

1.5       The Finance Department will arrange payments to the concerned faculty and staff on the approved revised rate of testing remuneration.

Item No. 2      To consider email from Mr. Jawad Tariq of Froebels (Pvt.) Limited offering the options to IBA to utilize their school premises during evening hours and during summer vacations.

2.1       Mr. Jawad Tariq, an IBA graduate working for M/s. Froebels (Pvt.) Limited offered the IBA to utilize their premises in Islamabad in evening hours and during summer vacations.

DECISION

2.2              The Committee considered the offer but regretted that the IBA did not have any plan at the moment to establish its chapter outside Karachi due to limited resources at its disposal.  However, the Committee agreed that for programs such as Executive Education or Entrepreneurial Development, if we needed their facilities, the IBA would utilize their facilities.

ACTION

2.3      The Registrar would send a reply to Mr. Jawad Tariq accordingly.

Item No. 3      To consider note from Dr. Nasir Touheed regarding the eligibility criteria for admission at IBA.

3.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed in his email stated that the eligibility criteria for admission to undergraduate programs as approved by the Academic Board and the Board of Governors was an average of  minimum 1”B” and 2 “Cs’.  He said that a student namely Ms. Mashaal Faseeh of BBA(MIS)-I obtained the grades of A, C and D but she was told that she failed to meet the eligibility criteria.  He said that the average of 1”B” and 2 “Cs’ is 10 points (4+3+3) and in her case the average is also 10 (5+3+2).  In his opinion asking her and other students with similar grades not to come to IBA is a violation of the decision made by the Academic Board and the Board of Governors of the IBA.  He requested the Director to reconsider the decision taken by the IBA and implement the rules approved by the Academic Board and the Board of Governors of the IBA.

3.2              It was pointed out that in the advertisement for admission it was clearly stated that minimum 1”B’ and 2”Cs” will be eligible for admission. There is no mention of averaging.  Had that been mentioned in the advertisement some other candidates with similar grades would have applied.  

DECISION:

3.3       The Committee referred the matter to the Academic Board for clarification on their decision of averaging.

3.4       The Committee further decided that in future announcement it should be mentioned that there would be no averaging for “A” Level grades.

ACTION

3.5       The Controller of Examinations would ensure that there is no mention of  averaging for “A” level grades in future program announcements and admission ads.

Item No. 4      To consider note from I.T. Manager regarding Microsoft Exchange Server 2007 Training.

4.1       The I.T. Manager in his note of August 26, 2008 stated that M/s. OnWire Education who are Microsoft Certified Partner for Learning Solutions (CPLS) were organizing a two months training to enhance knowledge and skills of IT staff on Planning, Implementing and Maintaining Microsoft Exchange Server 2007.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve nomination of Mr. Mansoor Ali, Network Manager, and Mr. Muhammad Asif Khan, Assistant Network Manager for this training. He stated that the training is vital for the smooth operations of IT/MIS infrastructure in IBA.  The training will enhance IBA IT Staff knowledge and will ensure more secure environment in IBA.  The duration of the course is two months; two classes per week. The fee is Rs.28,000/- per participant payable in advance.   

4.2              The Committee was informed that the Board has approved a policy whereby an IBA employee getting professional training arranged and paid by the IBA will have to sign one year’s service bond if the training cost is above Rs.10,000/-

4.3              The Director stated that we have to differentiate between job related training and career advancement training. He said that job related training will improve our productivity and efficiency and as such there is no need of any service bond.  He said that the Board will be requested to reconsider the policy.   

DECISION

4.4       The Committee agreed with the Director and approved the nomination of Mr. Mansoor Ali, Network Manager, and Mr. Muhammad Asif Khan, Assistant Network Manager for the Microsoft Exchange Server 2007 Training.

4.5       The Committee decided that the non-teaching staff will be provided job related training only.

4.6       The Committee further decided that those who are trained should train other staff members also.  Providing training would be in replacement of a service bond.

4.7       A policy for training and development of faculty and staff would be presented to the Board for approval.  

ACTION

4.8       Mr. Azam Sardar will arrange training of the two nominees with M/s. OnWire Education and ensure that the nominees after their training also provide training to other staff members.

4.9       The Director H.R. will prepare a training policy for faculty and staff for approval by the Board after the review by the Executive Committee.

Item No. 5      To consider email from Mr. Lutuf Husain, Assistant Librarian, regarding non-operation of Library Management System due to indifference of the Head Librarian.

5.1              The Committee considered the note from Mr. Lutuf Husain in which he had alleged that the Head Librarian was not interested in the operation of Library Management System and uses abusive language against some library staff. 

5.2              The Registrar said that there are two groups in the Library and a tussle is going on between the two groups.  He said that he had received complaints against the Head Librarian from some employees also. He said that the Head Librarian is short tempered.  He said that he had written a letter to him to respect others.  It worked for some time but again he has resorted to behaving unmannerly.    

5.3              The Director showed concern over LMS not being operated and everything being done manually.  

5.4              Dr. Mohammad Nishat suggested that the Library Committee may look into the complaints of non-operation of LMS.   

DECISION

5.5              The Committee referred the complaints to the Library Committee with a request to look into it and figure it out.

5.6              The Committee decided that the complaints against the Head Librarian will be handled by the Director H.R. when he / she joins the IBA.

ACTION

5.7              Dr. Mohammad Nishat will refer the issue to the Library Committee.

Item No. 6      To consider request from Mr. Fahad Hussain Korai

6.1       Mr. Fahad Hussain Karai, who was admitted into MBA program of the IBA stated that he has done three years BBA(Hons.) from PAF-KIET.  Before that he did his ‘A’ levels and “O” levels.  His “O” level’s period extended to three years and did his “A” level in two years.  Thus he spent 16 years in academics.  However, the IBA has calculated his academic tenure as 15 years as his 13 years of schooling has been considered as 12 years of schooling.  He requested to allow him to continue with his MBA program on the grounds that his academic tenure spreads over 16 years.

DECISION

6.2       The Committee felt that the short-coming should have been detected before the final list was prepared. 

6.3       The Committee considered Mr. Fahad Korai as not qualified for admission.

6.4       The Committee further decided that the Legal Advisor should be consulted on what ground he should be refused admission.

ACTION

6.5       The Registrar will seek legal advice from the lawyer on this issue.  Based on the legal advice, the applicant would be informed by the Registrar.

Item No. 7      To consider email from Mr. Waseem M. Arain regarding provision of essential utilities like tissue box and air-freshener to faculty members.

7.1       The Registrar said that there is a policy of getting visiting cards published for authorized personnel. Similarly IBA mugs are for giving away and for external use and not to be given to all staff.  Likewise, we need to provide tissue and air-freshener to limited individuals to avoid any possible misuse.

DECISION

7.2       The Committee decided that instead of giving air-freshener to every individual, it should be made responsibility of a peon at faculty floor / faculty offices to ensure that the rooms are sprayed air-freshener every morning and tissue boxes are also available.

7.3       The Committee further decided that there should be one person responsible for store items such as stationery and supplies at the IBA City Campus.  For computer peripherals and computer related items, a person from CCS should be made responsible to hold stocks and maintain records / accounts of such items; similarly for maintenance items a person from Project Department and for parts and accessories for transport a person from transport department should be made responsible to hold stocks and accounts of their respective store items.  Faculty members and heads of administrative department can fill in a requisition slip to obtain the requisite supplies from these stores. The Store Keeper will maintain a record of all the requisitions made.

ACTION

7.4       The Registrar will depute peon(s) who would ensure that the faculty rooms are sprayed air freshener and make tissue boxes available in each faculty room.

7.5       The Registrar and the Administrator, City Campus will assign a person the responsibility of holding stocks and maintaining records and accounts of store items related to stationery and supplies.  A sub-store will be set up at the City Campus with a store keeper incharge. 

Item No. 8      Any other item with the permission of the Chair

a)        Request from a student from Quetta for withdrawal from the program and refund of fee.

8.1       Referring to the decision of the Ninth meeting of the Executive Committee regarding refund of fee, the Director requested the Director Finance to inquire as to what is the procedure of refund of fee at Aga Khan University and other institutions.

8.2       The Committee decided that a Students’ Welfare Fund may be created by depositing unclaimed caution money, remaining instalments and donations from philanthropists.

8.3       The Director said that the student from Quetta who has requested for refund of fee due to weak financial position, should be refunded the fee from Students’ Welfare Fund.  

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
