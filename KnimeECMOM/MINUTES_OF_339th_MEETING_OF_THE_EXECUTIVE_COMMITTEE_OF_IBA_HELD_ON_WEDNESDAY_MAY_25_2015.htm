Item No.3       To consider proposal on Marketing function at IBA.

 

3.1       The Dean & Director said that the need for integration of marketing efforts arose because different units and departments are approaching external institutions and organizations in an uncoordinated manner. For example, Students societies of IBA approach an organization at the same time for sponsorship while others in IBA are asking for sale of corporate tables at fund-raising dinner and it creates confusion.  The students’ societies also do not have coordination among themselves. So far coordination and integration of marketing function is missing. He said that the basic idea is that every unit would deliver and execute but there would be a marketing plan for the whole institution. The marketing plan would spell out who to do; every single activity is to be documented and its planning, execution and responsibility to be fixed. The Marketing Manager / Head would be a facilitator, problem solver and coordinator. The activities would be carried out by each unit / centre at a decentralized level but their planning and monitoring would be integrated and centralised.   He said that he received a lot of feedback from those who are in advertising and PR agencies that our own brand manual is not being followed properly.  He said that there is no uniformity; no owner of brand manual and somebody has to manage it. He said that he has asked Ms. Maheen Ghouri to talk to stakeholders and put up a presentation on the marketing functions. 

 

3.2       Ms. Maheen Ghouri said that the recurrent theme of IBA is to continuously expand its programs, courses, and services so as to provide education, skills, and facilities to individuals and the community for academic, professional, and personal development. Currently, all Departments, Programs, and Centers at IBA have been marketing / promoting their own courses, services, and facilities. A few of the revenue-generating Centers and Programs have their own staff who are involved in the marketing and promotion activities.  She presented program specific marketing and promotion activities which are being carried out.

 

3.3       Ms. Maheen Ghouri emphasizing on the need for an integrated strategic marketing department, said that as of now, each department and center is individually managing its own marketing activities. Each develops its own marketing budget, based on the revenue it is generating, and works within that budget. As IBA is growing and expanding its programs and services every year, there is a need:

 

· to coordinate and oversee all marketing and promotional activities at IBA

· to develop new marketing plans / strategies for new programs and services

· to ensure an internal marketing / communications network to encourage internal communication between faculty, management, and staff

· to develop a centralized marketing social media page, where all centers and programs can market their courses and services on one single Facebook page

· to creating a marketing plan of action that is realistic, and will help improve overall IBA revenues

· to coordinate and leverage marketing activities University-wide

 

3.4       Ms. Ghouri said that there is a need to attract candidates from within the country and abroad to join its diverse programs and utilize its services, so as to promote IBA as one of the top schools in Pakistan.  The IBA Marketing Function / Department would add value to IBA as a whole, as well as help us in achieving the goal of being amongst the Top 100 Business Schools in the world. The objectives of the Marketing Function would come under the Head of Marketing, whose role and responsibilities would be as given below:

 

HEAD OF MARKETING - Role And Responsibility

 

PRIMARY ROLE

· Develop the Annual Marketing Plan for IBA

· Oversee and coordinate the development of the IBA Annual Report every year

· Developing and managing external affiliations and partnerships (networking) with other organizations, institutions, schools, etc to build IBA relationships and reputation in the community.

· Visits to external organizations and institutions for academic program presentations, Orientation Programs, inviting visitors to IBA for various forums, academic events

· To build the overall brand management policies for IBA

· Collaborating with all programs, centers, and departments at IBA from time to time to develop strategic marketing plans and communication campaigns for new programs and courses.

· Developing new marketing strategies/promotion campaigns with relevant departments to attract prospective students and candidates (social media, advertising, direct mailings, newsletters, etc)

· Develop and manage strategies to market professional development programs and activities.

· Monitoring and supervising the progress of various functions and departments, and implementing remedial action where necessary.

· Manage and supervise any other marketing/promotion activity at IBA.

 

COORDINATION

· Providing leadership for IBA marketing strategies for Admissions, New Programs, CED, CEE, Skill Development Center, CEJ, NTHP, Facilities & Sports Complex, Alumni Department, Student Societies/Clubs, and Data Center. These would include developing and assisting in strategies for web services, community affiliations and partnerships, and advertising new IBA products and services.

· Liaising with the Admissions, Testing, and Program Offices to assist them in the coordination of all marketing and promotional activities for New Admissions. These will include marketing/promotion of Open Houses and Orientation Programs across the country.

 

  • Developing and managing an internal marketing/communications network to encourage internal communication/partnerships between faculty, management, and staff, and to coordinate and leverage marketing activities University-wide.

 

OUTSOURCING FACILITIES

  • Developing and managing a strategy to successfully outsource IBAs facilities to external parties, organizations, institutions, etc, that is, outsourcing Auditoriums, IBA Sports Complex, and the Data Center.

 

COMMUNICATIONS & PUBLIC AFFAIRS

· Oversee and coordinate university level marketing and advertising based on the budget they have developed; assisting various programs and departments with developing marketing strategy and execution.

· Developing a centralized marketing social media page, where all centers and programs can market their courses and services on one single Facebook page.

· Reviewing policy and planning to establish strategic direction for web development.

· Working with the Communications department from time to time to promote and document new IBA products, services, programs, events, etc.

 

3.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani observed that there was no mention of the students societies activities and their role.  He wanted to know as to how the marketing department is to be related to the existing communication department and how the budget is to be allocated. Ms. Maheen Ghouri said that Communication and Media Department is going to work hand in hand with Marketing Department; it has to disseminate the information through print, advertising and video. The Dean & Director said that it would be the job of Ms. Maheen Ghouri to serve as a watch dog to make sure that there is a uniformity and consistency. He said that there would be a marketing plan and all expenses would flow out of the marketing plan and then it would be distributed. He clarified that at the evolutionary stage there would be no specific TOR for the Head of Marketing; it would be demand driven.  

 

3.6       Mr. Asad Ilyas suggested that each department should set a target and develop its KPI. The Dean & Director agreed that every department will have its own KPI. Ms. Maheen Ghouri will be monitoring them with the help of student interns.

 

3.7       Mr. Izhar Husain said that publicity and PR are more important and effective than advertisement. Central marketing function should identify all key “Branding Touch Points” and develop user friendly templates for the users. Blogs can also be used for public relation. He suggested that there should be a marketing committee or brand committee comprising internal and external members with acknowledged expertise in marketing and customer ascendency and they should give suggestions.  He also suggested that faculty members who do not have industry and business experience should be placed with different industrial or business sectors. Dr Ishrat Husain said the faculty has been already engaged with industry through “students’ projects”, which are being appreciated by the businesspersons. Mr. Izhar Hussain further said that the details of the projects done at IBA are not available on our website; they are not being used as marketing tool although many of them are very good.  He suggested that only the sectors on which projects have been done should be mentioned on the website. Dr. Qazi Masood updated that they have recently gathered information on projects done during last 4 to 5 years and will place them on the website. The Registrar reported that a letter from the Ministry of Defence has been received in which it is mentioned that the Army Chief has advised to contact IBA for marketing of defense products. The members were delighted to know about the confidence of the Army Chief in IBA.

 

3.8       The Dean & Director said that there is no body to monitor the contents of the website and up-date it. He said that the Head of Marketing has to monitor it and ensure that the stale content is weeded out continuously.  Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that our  website is 50% slower than international standard.

 

3.9       The Registrar said that it was assumed that IBA facilities are underutilized and we can market it.  He clarified that now the hostels, both boys and girls, seminar rooms they are all fully utilized.  He said that recently there has been a clash of programs of CEE. He said that everybody has a tendency to use the facilities of S-3 and assumes that the program would be held there but it is not practically possible. He said that the facilities that can be outsourced their details are available with him. The Dean & Director said that Ms. Maheen will coordinate with the Registrar and others in the allocation to external organizations.  Dr. Nasir Touheed said that class room allocation is not shown in ERP.  The Dean & Director asked Mr. Imran Batada to do that.  

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.10     The Committee approved the creation of Marketing Department and its functions suggested and noted above. Ms. Maheen Ghouri would serve as Head of Marketing.
