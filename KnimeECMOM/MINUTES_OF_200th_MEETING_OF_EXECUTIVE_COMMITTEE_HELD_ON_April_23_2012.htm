The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve revised scope of Admin Block Pergola.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the contract for the construction of pergola was awarded to M/s. Paramount Constructors through open bidding in February 2012.  He said that in March when one section was erected, it was noticed that the width of the pergola (5.0ft) did not cover the verandah. The Architects were informed of this and were advised to redesign the pergola to increase its width so that the ingress of rain water into the verandah could be controlled. At the same time Architects were also requested to provide cover on the Academic sides and the gaps of the columns.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that Architects produced various designs but were not accepted by projects department as the cost increase was high. Finally a design was selected and approved which included redesigning of supporting structure to have acceptable cost increase. The following are the details of the case.

  

§  The width of pergola has been enhanced by 1 ft-09 inches to further minimize the chances of rain water splashing across the corridor. The amended width is now 6.75 ft from the original 5.0 ft.

 

§  The length of pergola has increased by 48 ft due to addition in front of columns and sideways. The total length therefore is now 191 rft from the original 143 rft.

 

§  The supporting pipes and framing have been redesigned so as to economize the cost.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that after marathon meetings and negotiations with contractors in collaboration with Architects, the revised proposal was furnished according to which the increase in cost after all these modifications is Rs.75,795/- which is 15.14% of original work order of Rs.500,460/- dated January 19, 2012.  He said that the Contractor has been informed that approval would only be given for the amount of 15%, to which, he has agreed. The Director Projects said that since it is a BOQ based contract, efforts would be made to keep the cost within the increased amount. The new value of the contract shall be Rs.575,529/-

 

1.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to grant 15.0% increase in cost to expedite completion of work before monsoon.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee approved 15% increase in cost of Admin. Block Pergola at City Campus due to revised scope of work.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve award of work for procurement of furniture for new Boys’ Hostel at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that as planned, the furniture and fixtures were required to be procured on or close to completion of the civil work. He said that they issued Invitation for Bids letter dated March 29, 2012 to the following four pre-qualified furniture manufacturers and suppliers:

 

·         Furnishing Pavilion,

·         Interwood Mobel (Pvt.) Ltd.,

·         Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd.,

·         Unique Wood Working Company,

 

 2.2      Mr. Shameem reported that the tender documents were collected by all four of them. However, only two of them namely; Simab (SMC-PVT) Ltd., and Unique Wood Working Company submitted their bids which were opened on April 16, 2012, in the presence of the bidders’ representatives and other relevant persons. Mr. Shameem said that they received an extremely poor response to the enquiry letter and received two bids only; one of them is incomplete and therefore non-responsive. He presented a Comparative Statement of the bids according to which Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd. has quoted the lowest price, namely; Rs.5,946,634/-, while Unique Wood Working (Pvt.) Ltd. is the highest at a price of Rs.7,759,124/-.

 

2.3       Mr. Shameem reported that the bids were considered in the joint meeting of TEC and CPC held on April 19, 2012. The Committees recommended that the furniture be procured from the lowest bidder, namely, Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd. at the bid price of Rs.5,946,634/-.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest bidder namely; M/s. Simab (smc-pvt) Ltd.  

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee approve the award of work to the lowest bidder namely; M/s. Simab (smc-pvt) Ltd., at the bid price of Rs.5,946,634/-

Item No.4       Reporting Item:  Waste Water Disposal System at the Boys’ Hostel and Staff Colony.

 

4.1       Referring to the Executive Committee’s decision of April 9, 2012 on the waste water disposal system, Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the EC decision was considered in the joint meeting of TEC and CPC held on April 19, 2012. Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

            a)         Proposal for Connecting two Chillers together at Main Campus.

 

5.1       The Registrar said that the air-conditioning system for both the blocks at Main Campus is centralized and this is being served by two different chillers; 105 ton chiller is supporting Auditorium, and 135 ton chiller to Academic Block. He said that the problem is that although the two are together, they cannot support each other and if one goes out of order, we cannot divert to another one. However, he said that this is possible which would require a small expenditure. He said that Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance has been invited to explain as to how it can be done and at what cost.

 

5.2       Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed presented technical details through which the two chillers can be connected together and would support each other. However, he said that it would take seven days during which period the chillers should not be in use.  He said that the total cost that would be incurred would be around Rs.250,000/-

 

DECISION

 

5.3       The Committee appreciated and approved the proposed plan for connecting the two chillers together at a total cost of Rs.250,000/-

 

5.4       The Committee agreed with the Registrar that during August 21 to 28, 2012 this can be done as no activity is planned during this period.

 

ACTION

 

5.5       Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance would get the two chillers connected together during August 21 to 28, 2012.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
