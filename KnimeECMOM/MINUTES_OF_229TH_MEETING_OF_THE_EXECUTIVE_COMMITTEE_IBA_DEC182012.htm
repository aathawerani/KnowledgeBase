MINUTES OF 229TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON TUESDAY, DECEMBER 18, 2012

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                  Dean & Director

7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai            Director H.R.

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, Mr. Ishfaque Ahmed Lashari, Assistant Manager, CDC, and Mr. Mehmood Bughio, Senior Manager Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following also attend the meeting on special invitation to participate in the discussion of item 3 of the agenda:

a) Mr. Aman U. Saiyed    Program Director BBA

The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 Status Reports from:

Mr. Ishfaque Ahmed Lashari:

1.1 Tapping of Gulf and the Middle East Markets - Mr. Lashari to obtain data base of Pakistanis working in Saudi Arabia: Mr. Lashari stated that they have come to know that most of colleges in UAE and Saudi Arabia have counsellors who help students to get admissions in universities around the world. He said that he was trying to arrange counsellors list. He said that they have obtained information on schools in Saudi Arabia and UAE and now they may contact them for appointment. He presented a list of schools in different cities of Saudi Arabia and UAE.

The Dean & Director said that first we have to make a selection of schools. International schools along with British and Cambridge schools should be selected and they be sent details of our programs, requirements for admission to these programs, and the facilities available. Depending upon the response from the selected schools, we may proceed further.

The Registrar informed the Committee that two years back when they went to Pakistani schools in UAE, they were told that they have to get approval from the ministry first. Similarly when they sent Program Announcements to some schools, the material was confiscated and the schools asked them to follow the procedure.

The Dean & Director asked Mr. Lashari to write on his (Dean & Director) behalf to the embassies in Saudi Arabia and UAE informing them that we want to give an orientation to the students of the selected schools. He asked Mr. Lashari to approach Pakistani Professional Association to identify schools where Pakistani students are studying. He further asked Mr. Lashari to visit Dow University to find if they have any list of schools / colleges in Saudi Arabia and UAE from where students are coming to Dow.

The Registrar said that most of the queries related to admission are on the availability of hostel facility and security arrangement. He said that the new boys’ hostel has been fully occupied. The new Girls’ Hostel is not expected to be ready by the time the fall semester 2013 starts.

The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to ask the Consultant to prepare tender documents and detailed drawings of the remaining two blocks of Boys’ Hostel. He said that at this stage we should get at least one of the two blocks constructed. He wanted to know the completion date of Girls’ Hostel. Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that it would be ready by end of December 2013. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to give priority in construction work to the residential block of the Girls’ Hostel so that the students could be accommodated there for the Fall 2013 semester. He said that interim arrangements can be made in the VPR which would be ready by June 2013. The Registrar suggested that all furniture for Girls’ Hostel should be ordered which could be stored in one of the rooms of the Girls’ Hostel.

Director H.R.:

1.2 Action Plan of IBA Strategy Review 2012 – The Director H.R. to see as to how a tracking mechanism can be developed: The Director H.R. informed the Committee that all the 10 points are an on-going process and they are being implemented as an on-going process. However, she said that maintenance of data base of guest speakers is not possible as the teachers invite the guest speakers by themselves. The Dean & Director said that if it is not possible then it should be dropped.

1.3 The Director H.R. to request Ms. Maria Hasan to organize dramas at which outsiders may also be invited: The Director H.R. informed that according to outsiders are invited to the dramas and they pay for the tickets also.

1.4 The Director H.R. to get it checked through Audit that the staff of the Evening Shift at City Campus also performs duties for 41 hours in a week: The Director H.R. reported that details of employees’ attendance and overtime claims over the last 3 months have been provided to Audit Department for checking. She said that the Audit Department would be checking it across the board.

1.5 Announcement of the Foundation Program for the children of IBA employees (Upto BPS-16 and equivalent): The Director H.R. reported that the announcement was made in Urdu and circulated through email, notice boards and Current newsletter. Children of two employees Ms. Sadiqa’s daughter (Nida Aftab) and Mr. Bazar Khan’s son (Waqar Hussain) successfully qualified the assessments and have been enrolled in the foundation program. She said that this update would be shared with employees in the newsletter’s next issue.

1.6 Reward and recognition to Security Guards: The Director H.R. reported that the Security Guards were given cash rewards as per the enhanced limits. She said that the A&FC would be reported about the enhancement of amount in its forthcoming meeting.

DECISION

1.7 The Committee decided that brochures containing details of our programs offered, requirements for admission to these programs, and the facilities available be sent to the selected schools (International schools along with British and Cambridge schools) in Saudi Arabia and UAE. Depending upon the response from the selected schools, we may proceed further.

1.8 The Committee decided that Mr. Lashari on behalf of the Dean & Director would write to the embassies in Saudi Arabia and UAE informing them about the orientation programs to be organized for the students of the selected schools in these countries.

1.9 The Committee decided that the construction of 2nd block of Boys’ Hostel should be initiated.

ACTION

1.10 Mr. Ishfaque Lashari would approach Pakistani Professional Association to identify the schools where Pakistani students are studying. Mr. Lashari would visit Dow University to obtain list of schools / colleges in Saudi Arabia and UAE.

1.12 Mr. Shameem Ahmed would ask the contractor to give priority to the residential block of the Girls’ Hostel so that it is completed before September 2013.

Item No. 3 Discussion on “Leadership Through Character Building”.

3.1 The Director H.R. said that IBA has signed an MoU with SP Jain Institute of Management & Research. She said that they have shared with IBA their various academic initiatives, one of which is a focus on developing “Leadership Through Character Building” in their student body. She said that she would be presenting the salient features of this program and after reviewing the program, they will examine the possibility of IBA using some / all of the features.

3.2 The Director H.R. presented details of the programs offered, age group of the students, students strength, student – teacher ratio at SP JIMR. She said that the students are not given any financial aid although some of the students come of a very poor family. The day a student gets admission, he / she also gets students loan letter from the bank. She said that the first year MBA curriculum focuses on character building. She briefly explained the following five character building initiatives that are taken at SP JIMR:

ABHYUDAYA – Mentoring School Children
o Develop mentoring skills
o Concern for contribution
o Joy of giving
o Learning by doing

ADMAP
o Getting things done
o Team skills
o Influencing without power
o Interpersonal skills

Social Project
o Work in an unstructured environment
o Develop empathy and sensitivity
o Learn through hardships
o Respect for values

Gita Shibir
o Self Management and Development
o Value for values
o Management through Gita
o Success =f(IQ, EQ, SQ)

Personal Growth Lab
o Awareness of self
o Team Building
o Growth through self-discovery

3.3 The Dean & Director said that we have to see what we can easily adopt from it for our students and what element we can further add to our existing programs.

3.4 The Director H.R. then presented highlights of the SDP activities at IBA for BBA, BCS, and MBA programs from first year to fourth year of BBA, BCS and 1st to 4th semesters of MBA.

3.5 Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the Personal Effectiveness Course is offered in the fourth year - 7th semester. He was of the view that this course should be offered in the first or second year. The Dean & Director agreed with Dr. Ghani and said that Personal Effectiveness course should be offered either in the 2nd or 3rd year.

3.6 Mr. Aman U. Saiyed said that the MBA students come after two years experience so they must have gone through the exercise of mock interviews, CV writing workshops, career related workshops. Mirza Izhar Husain suggested that Leadership, Team Building and Strategy should be offered to the MBA students in their 3rd semester. The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. to conduct a web-based poll for the incoming classes asking their preferences for leadership, C.V. writing workshop, mock interviews, team building, career related workshop and arrange them accordingly.

DECISION & ACTION

3.7 The Director H.R. would get the suggestions noted in 3.5 and 3.6 above implemented.


Item No. 4 Up-date on kitchens in the Students’ Centre.

4.1 The Registrar said that originally the kitchen was planned in four portions. He was given the task to talk to students and get their feedback. He said that representatives of Ambala Food, Students Biryani, and KFC visited IBA. Ambala wanted bigger space whereas others did not find it economically viable to come to IBA. He said that he has provided details of the electrical point, the power (ampere) and related requirements vide his email of December 1, 2012 to the Projects Department. He said that he requested the Director Projects to prepare generic kitchen where there will be no major cooking on big burner; only the cooked food can be heated.

4.2 The Dean & Director said that majority of the students would like to have Pakistani cuisines such as Biryani. He wanted to know as to where it would be cooked. The Registrar said that the outlet in the corner can be combined with another one so that space is extended and food could be cooked there. The Dean & Director agreed that the bigger portion should be reserved for Pakistani food where biryani and other food stuff could be cooked.

4.3 Mr. Mehmood Bughio said some vendors may require gas and some may require electricity to cook/heat up their food. The Registrar said that electricity load of 35 amperes for each counter would be needed and a gas supply line would be needed for the burner. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi suggested that, if possible, the gallery on the rear of the kitchen should be terminated so that more space is available.

DECISION

4.4 The Committee decided that there would be three outlets; the bigger outlet combining the two existing ones will be reserved for Pakistani food which would also be cooked there.

ACTION

4.5 The Projects Department would arrange and provide the facilities details of which were already provided by the Registrar to the Director Projects.


Item No. 5 Presentation on “Performance Appraisal system for supervisory staff”.

5.1 The Director H.R. said that presently there are three different performance management systems; for faculty, for management staff, and for support staff. Now H.R. is proposing the fourth system for those who are in supervisory positions in BPS-12 to 15 or in IBA scale. She said that the performance components will remain the same; their job description will be used as a basis. She said that the idea is that for future pipeline the performance may be linked to career progression. The outcome will materialize in terms of career progression, and training. She clarified that there would be no monetary impact of it.

DECISION

5.2 The Committee noted and approved the fourth system of appraisal for supervisory positions.

ACTION
