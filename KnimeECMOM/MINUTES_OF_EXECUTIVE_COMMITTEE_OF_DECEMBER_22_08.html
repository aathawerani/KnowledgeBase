MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON DECEMBER 22, 2008 (TWENTY SEVENTH MEETING)

 

The 27th meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, December 22, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                        Professor
  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Chairman CS & MIS
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                              Registrar
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                     Director Projects
  6. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                          Director I.T.
  7. Mr. Shameem Ahmad                         G.M. (P&D) by special invitation

The following items were discussed:

 

Item No. 1      To consider and award the work to the lowest bidder for the work of providing security grills and allied works at the VPR and girls’ hostel No.B

1.1       The Director Projects in his note of December 20, 2008 stated that as per instructions of the Director IBA, regarding repairing works at V.P.R. and Girls Hostel situated in the Staff Town, sealed quotations were called from the following four pre-qualified Contractors:

1.  M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical and Mechanical Co.

2.  M/s. Kashif Iron Works

3.  M/s. Ghulam Jelani Associates

4.  M/s. Al-Asr Associates

1.2       The Director Projects said that the quotations were evaluated and the comparative statement was prepared. He said that M/s. Al-Asr Associates were found to be the lowest competitive bidder with a bid amount of Rs.95,156/- for the said project. He said that the rates were reasonable and workable and recommended to award the work to M/s. Al-Asr Associates. 

1.3       The Director Projects further said that the work is of urgent nature in response to the security needed. He further said that there would be no change in the façade by fixing of grills at the VPR and the girls’ hostel.     

DECISION

1.4       The Committee approved the recommendation of the Director Projects to award the work to M/s. Al-Asr Associates who have quoted the lowest rate of Rs.95,156/-

1.5       The Committee further decided that the Director Projects would ensure that the quality is maintained.

ACTION

1.6       The Director Projects would issue work order to M/s. Al-Asr Associates for the work mentioned above.

Item No. 2     To consider and award the work to the lowest bidder for the work of converting two garages into store.

2.1       The Director Projects stated that as per instructions of the Director IBA, regarding shifting of store situated on the first floor of the Administration block to the Garages situated on the Ground floor and to accommodate the store, two garages have to be altered and at the same time the boundary wall near the garages have to be secured against trespassing by installation of iron grill. He said that the workup for the same was done. The dimensions were taken of the garages and the existing store and the sketches were prepared. He said that the Bill of Quantities for the entire work was prepared and issued to the following pre-qualified Contractors on 15-12-2008:

1.  M/s. The Strength

2.  M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical and Mechanical Co.

3.  M/s. D.R. Construction Co.

4.  M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor

5.  M/s. Waqar & Co.   

2.2       He said that the sealed tenders were opened by the IBA (P.P.&D.) staff in the presence of the representatives of the concerned Contracting Firms on  December 19, 2008. Four Contractors participated in the tender opening and one Contractor namely M/s. D.R. Construction Co. did not submit their tender.

2.3       He said that the tenders were evaluated and the detailed comparative statement was prepared. M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor were found to be the lowest competitive bidder with a bid amount of Rs.226,860/- for the said project. He said that the rates were reasonable and workable and recommended to award the work to M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor. 

DECISION

2.4       The Committee approved the recommendation of Director Projects to award the work to M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor for converting two garages into a store at the bid amount of Rs.226,860/-.

2.5       The Committee requested the Director Projects to ensure that the store should be such that the items placed in the store remain safe and secured from rainy water i.e. there should be no leakage of water.

ACTION

2.6       The Director Projects would issue work order to M/s. Khalid Jamal Constructor for converting two garages into a store at the bid price of Rs.226,860/-

Item No. 3      To consider note from General Manager (P&D) regarding clearance of site for construction of multi-purpose building at the Main Campus.

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (P&D) in his note of December 19, 2008 stated that there were about 20 grownup short and tall trees at the site where the multi-purpose building was proposed to be built. The trees can possibly be saved by shifting them to another location in the Main Campus. He said that the lowest of the tree quotations for shifting the trees, was for Rs.90,000/-

3.2       He requested the Executive Committee to approve the cost if it is desirable to save the trees. Alternatively it may approve clearance of the site by cutting the trees.

DECISION

3.3       The Committee agreed in principle to shift the trees.  However, in order to know whether the shifted trees would survive, the Committee decided that Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia and Dr. Nishat would consult with the experts.  Based on the expert opinion, a final decision would be taken.

ACTION

3.4       Dr. Mohammad Nishat and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz would consult with the experts about the chances of survival of such shifted trees and report to the Committee.

Item No. 4      To consider note from General Manager (P&D) regarding selection and appointment of an Electrical Consultant.

4.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, General Manager (P&D) in his note of December 18, 2008 stated that as per directive of the Executive Committee they have prepared a comparative analysis of financial proposals submitted by the three electrical consultants namely; M/s. Jafri and Associates, M/s. W.J. Associates, and M/s. A.S. Consultants.  He also presented a brief comparison of phase-wise services offered by the three consultants.

4.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed further stated that instead of mixing up the two phases it is advisable that for the time being we go for rectification and revamping / strengthening of our existing electrical infrastructure at the two campuses. He stated that the financial proposal submitted for phase-1 by M/s. W.J. Associates appears to be the lowest and more reasonable. He recommended that M/s. W.J. Associates be hired as consultant for Phase-1 only for which they have quoted Rs.1,114,000/-.  Phase 2 may be postponed for the next year when, after completion of designs of various new buildings we will have a clear idea of our energy needs in the future.

4.3       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz stated that although M/s. Jafri & Associates have quoted the highest rate, they have offered to work on honorary. 

DECISION

4.4       The Committee approved the recommendation of Mr. Shameem Ahmad to hire M/s. W.J. Associates as Electrical Consultant for phase 1 at a cost of Rs.1,114,000/-

4.5       The Committee requested the General Manager (P&D) to include UPS requirement assessment in the TOR for M/s. W.J. Associates and to ensure that the work is completed on time.

ACTION

4.6       The General Manager (P&D) would complete the process of hiring M/s. W.J. Associates and would ensure that UPS requirement assessment is also included in the TOR.

Item No. 5      To consider and approve proposal for Training on PeopleSoft Human Capital Management submitted by M/s Techlogix.

5.1              Dr. Sayeed Ghani stated that in principle the training was already approved by the Executive Committee. He further said that now they are going to provide training on HCM for five days (half days sessions) for which M/s. Techlogix would charge Rs.100,000/- which would be credited back to IBA in case IBA chooses to award implementation of PeopleSoft Human Capital Management to Techlogix.

5.2       The participants would be served refreshments which would cost an additional Rs.5000/- 

DECISION

5.3       The Committee approved the proposal as mentioned in 5.1 and 5.2 above

ACTION

5.4       Mr. Imran Batada, Sr. MIS Manager would arrange training and refreshments as mentioned above.

Item No. 6      To consider email from Mr. Muhammad Shafi, Admin. Officer, Evening Program, regarding compensatory holiday in lieu of Sunday, December 7, 2008.

6.1       Mr. Muhammad Shafi in his email of December 20, 2008 stated that final examinations of Fall 2008 were scheduled from December 1 – 15, 2008. Due to law and order situation in the city, the Government of Sindh had declared holiday on December 1, 2008.  As such the examination of that day was held on Sunday, December 7, 2008. He suggested that in lieu of the duty of Sunday, December 1, 2008, a compensatory holiday may be given on Friday, December 26, 2008.

DECISION

6.2       The Committee decided that those who were involved in the examinations and had performed duties on December 7, 2008 will have holiday on Friday, December 26, 2008.

ACTION

6.3       The Registrar will convey the decision to all concerned.

Item No. 7      To consider request from Ms. Saira Qamar, an ex-student of IBA Class of 1994 for allowing her to appear in the Comprehensive Exam being held on December 24, 2008.

7.1       Ms. Saira Qamar in her application of December 20, 2008 stated that she was an ex-student of IBA class of 1994 and wishes to appear in the Comprehensive Examination being held on December 24, 2008.  She stated that she was informed that the time period for her eligibility for the exam has lapsed. She requested that she be allowed to take this examination. 

7.2       The Controller of Examination informed the Committee that Ms. Saira Qamar had taken one attempt out of three allowed attempts within 10 years time to clear the Comprehensive Examination. He further said that prior to 2004 there was no documented information on practiced 10-year time bar policy.  He said that Ms. Saira Qamar says that she was not aware of the condition to clear the Comprehensive Exam in 10 years time. 

DECISION

7.3       The Committee decided to allow all such students to clear the Comprehensive Exams in allowed three attempts. 

 7.4       The Committee further decided that the Controller of Examinations would prepare a policy on it and get the policy endorsed by the Academic Board.

ACTION

7.5       The Controller of Examinations will allow Ms. Saira Qamar to appear in the Comprehensive Examination on December 24, 2008 and get a policy on it endorsed by the Academic Board.

Item No. 8      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

            a)         Central locking system for IBA cars provided to officials

8.1       Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi proposed that all IBA cars provided to officials should have central locking system for security purpose. He said that it would cost around Rs.3,800/- (one time) for each car.

DECISION

8.2       The Committee agreed to the proposal.

ACTION

8.3       The Registrar will arrange to get central locking system for IBA cars provided to IBA officials.

b)         Design of furniture for classrooms / centres

8.4      Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that instead of procuring old-designed ready made furniture from market or copying others design of furniture, the IBA should get its classroom furniture designed keeping in view its own requirements.

DECISION

8.5       The Committee agreed to the proposal and decided to form a Design Committee for furniture comprising Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Director Projects, Controller of Examinations, Director I.T. and the Registrar.

c)         Progress on temporary accommodation.

8.6       The Committee showed its concern on the slow pace of work of temporary accommodation and requested the G.M. (P&D) to update the Committee on the progress.  

8.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, G.M. (P&D) said that the delay was due to certain reasons; the main being the procedure that they have to follow; the mistake in estimate and the Eid Holidays. He said that initially the deadline was end of January 2009 but it would now be completed by 15th of February 2009.  To a question he clarified that there is no arrangement for AC, so fans would be fixed.

DECISION

8.8       The Committee requested Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to help Mr. Shameem Ahmed in electrification and I.T. related work in the temporary accommodation. 

8.9              The Committee further decided to use the services of Electrical Consultant to be hired.

ACTION

8.10          The G.M. (P&D) would use the services of Electrical Consultant and seek help from Director I.T. in I.T. related work.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
