Item No.1       Status Reports from:

 

Mr. Sharaf Hussain / Dr. Sayeed Ghani:

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Batada and Mr. Sharaf Hussain would carry out a survey on making available centralized common services such as printing, fax machine and scanner on each floor and making a person responsible for such services: Mr. Sharaf Hussain reported that universities like LUMS, NUST, Bahria, and IQRA are using the centralized printing services because of the physical setup. Their faculty members sit in a hall and they have their cubicles but at IBA every faulty member has got a separate room. He said that they can arrange the printing facilities at corridors. He further reported that since Feburary 2016, ICT will implement an online system for toner issuance which would help them to determine the need of centralized printing services on the basis of six months data. To a question he clarified that the above mentioned universities do not charge from faculty for printing but students are given a quota.

 

1.2       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that limits may be set for the number of toners issued to faculty members beyond which faculty members may either be required to pay or to justify their issuance.   Regarding the students, Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the roaming profiles were tested last semester whose results have been determined to be satisfactory. He said that during Spring 2016 all students will be issued roaming profiles. Subsequently printing limits will be set for each student per academic year. He reported that for October 2014 – September 2015, the total pages printed from Lab printers were about 280,000; on average it comes to 75 pages per student. Based on this, he recommended that the limit of 100 pages (on grey scale) per year per student or 50 pages for semester be set. Students exceeding the limit will be blocked further printing unless they have specific approvals from concerned approving authority.

 

1.3       The Dean & Director said that if we are going to move towards a paperless system then we should gradually reduce printing; instead of 50, we should allow 25 pages per semester. This will give a strong signal forcing economy in usage. The teachers should also upload their course material. Dr. Nishat said that LMS usage will have to be increased. The Dean & Director suggested that the students should be communicated about the printing limit. Mr. Imran Batada said that students printing limit would be implemented from April 2016. Mr. Abdul Wajed suggested that faculty should also start taking paperless assignments.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that there should be an app through which faculty members may make corrections, give their remarks on the assignments submitted by the students. 

 

1.4       Conversion of Faculty Lounge into Students Lab: Mr. Sharaf Hussain reported that after surveying the Faculty Lounge of Faisal Bank Block, they have concluded that only 44 PCs can be accommodated in the new lab. He presented a rough sketch and measurements of the new computer lab.

 

1.5       Mr. Abdul Wajed said that some faculty members want to take on-line exam which is done by arranging temporary partition but in the proposed lab it cannot be done. He requested to include the feature of temporary partition in the design of the lab for the purpose of online exams. Mr. Sharaf Hussain said that provision for partition would be made in the furniture design. The Dean & Director suggested that it should be temporary partition not permanent. He also advised Mr. Abdul Wajed to encourage faculty members to take online exam. He suggested that everything should be automated gradually. He said that online exam system should be separate from everything else and its security should be more strengthened compared to other systems. He asked Mr. Imran Batada to design online exam system from scratch with built in security features in collaboration with the Controller of Examinations.     

 

Director Finance

 

1.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan to obtain estimate for the gym and also see if there is any space in FCS Building for changing room and report to the EC: Mr. Moeid said that for the estimate for gym he would need couple of weeks more. He wanted to know if the indoor games suggested to be accommodated in the existing canteen, can be shifted to Aman Tower because of shortage of space at the existing canteen. The Dean & Director said that at 11th floor temporary arrangement can be done. The Registrar said that the frequent shifting would be cumbersome and damage the equipments too. The Dean & Director asked if it can be arranged at FCS Building. The Registrar said that on the basement of FCS Building opposite ATM where cars are parked, space can be provided for the indoor games. He said that he along with Mr. Moeid would visit the site.  Mr. Abdul Wajed suggested to convert the opposite side into a store which is very much needed. He said that presently the store is on the roof and its passage is narrow. The Dean & Director said that the passage should be improved and widened if possible. The Registrar said that he would that.

 

1.7       Finance Department to send SMS alert if the fee is not received on due date and the second step should be to send SMS alert to the parents if the student does not respond: Mr. Moeid Sultan said that for SMS alert automation cannot be done but manually it can be done.  Mr. Imran Batada said that after due date if the student has not paid the fee, they can draw the list of such defaulters and send SMS in block to those on the list.

 

1.8     The Director Finance to work out the financial impact of merit based scholarship on the basis of the Deans’ Lists for year 2 to year 4:  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the EC had decided in its earlier meeting that top 10% students on merit list of entry test would get the scholarship in the first year only. In 2nd, 3rd and 4th year those who are on the Dean’s List would get scholarships equivalent to 50% applicable tuition fees. He said that earlier those who were maintaining a CGPA of 3.5 or above were given this scholarship in the 2nd, 3rd and 4th year. He said that for this year there would be overlap between the existing recipients and the new ones on the Dean’s List. This would require an additional budget allocation of around Rs.8. million. The Dean & Director said that it would be the transition period; from next year whosoever makes it to the Dean’s List in 2nd, 3rd and 4th year will get the merit scholarship. Mr. Moeid said that the stated policy would be applicable from Fall 2016; for academic year 2015-16 they have to absorb the remaining 76 students on the Dean’s List. The Dean & Director said that the Dean’s List is very stringent so anybody who qualifies on the Dean’s List is more deserving than those who have secured 3.5 CGPA.  He said that it should be announced that the criterion of 3.5 CGPA for award of merit scholarship in years 2 to 4 will not be applicable from Fall 2016 and only those regular full time program students who are on the Dean’s List would be eligible for it. 

 

DECISIONS & ACTIONS

 

1.9       The Executive Committee decided that in order to promote paperless system the printing should gradually be reduced. It was decided that:

 

a)       From April 2016 students would be allowed a quota of printing 25 (on grey scale) pages per semester.

b)       The teachers should also upload their course material and increase the usage of LMS.

c)      The students would be communicated by Lab Administrator about the printing limit of 25 pages w.e.f. April 2016.

 

1.10     Mr. Imran Batada would demonstrate online exams with built in security features to the Controller of Examinations.

 

1.11     Mr. Sharaf Hussain would ensure that temporary provision for partition is made in the furniture design for the Students Lab for online exam purposes.

 

1.12     The Registrar along with the Director Finance would visit the site at FCS building for Indoor game facilities.

 

1.13     The Registrar would get the passage to the top floor store improved.

 

1.14     The Committee decided that from next year i.e. Fall 2016 the merit scholarship would be awarded in years 2 to 4 to only those regular full time program students who make it to the Dean’s List. The merit scholarship will cover 50% of the applicable tuition fee; however, for the transition period of the academic year 2015-16, the 76 students who are not receiving the merit scholarship under the existing scheme but have become eligible under the new scheme would be absorbed.

 

1.15     The Finance Department would announce / communicate to the students that the criterion of maintaining 3.5 CGPA in the years 2 to 4 will no longer be applicable from Fall 2016. Only those regular full time program students who are on the Dean’s List will be entitled to merit Scholarship. 

 

1.16     Along with the above decisions, the up-dates given above were noted and approved.

Item No.4       To consider the following items proposed by Head of ICT:

 

a)      RFID Project.

 

4.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that after gathering requirements in coordination with all concerned including Security Head, they prepared and floated BoQ, took approval of the TEC and CPC and then placed purchase order.  He said that in the first phase they plan to issue cards for faculty and staff and then to the students.  He said that initially the readers would be placed on the gate. Each reader would cost around Rs.3000 to Rs.4000 but the exact amount would be ascertained. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that the reader has got two options: thumb and digits. If we do not use card then we may use thumb. Mr. Batada said that the thumb machine is integrated with ERP but when RFID card is introduced the thumb machine will no longer be used. The Dean & Director said that in transition period both systems should be operational. He further said that whatever the system is it should be multi-functional with compatibility features. He said that students attendance should be integrated with ERP module in second phase.  He said that the cards should allow only restricted access to users. He asked as to who would provide the access right. Mr. Batada said that the access right would be regulated by the Security Head. The Dean & Director said that it should be centralized with the Registrar.  He asked Mr. Batada to develop SOP along with design features. Mr. Batada said that he would work on the SOP along with design.   

 

4.2       Mr. Imran Batada presented some samples of the RFID card including the one used at Sukkur IBA. The Registrar suggested that the card should have the following fields:

 

-  Name

-  Designation

-  ERP Employee’s No.

-  Visual identity

-  CNIC No.

-  Issuing Authority (Office of the Registrar - No signature required )

-  Date of Issue

-  Date of Expiry

-  Address for recovery and tracks (no the personal address)

-  RFID Strips

 

4.3       The EC approved the sample that was presented with the Logo of Sindh Government excluding SAQS logo. Mr. Adnan Hameed inquired that whether an autonomous institution like IBA can use the Sindh Government Logo as KU and other institutions do not use it and whether prior permission from the government would be required in this case? In response, the Dean & Director clarified that we are the creation of the legislation of the Sindh Assembly and an instrument of Sindh Government; all government departments, and autonomous bodies use this logo, we can also use it on our ID Card. It was clarified that there would be no difference in the format and color of students, faculty and staff cards; in place of designation, program and class would be mentioned in students card. Mr. Abdul Wajed suggested that the color combination of the card should be such that its photo copy is legible.

 

4.4       Mr. Imran Batada suggested to divide the printing of cards in two phases; cards for staff and faculty should be printed at HR department and then the same printer should be moved to the Program office for printing students’ cards at program office. He said that ICT department would provide technical assistance.  The Registrar said that he was not in favour of assigning this task to HR or the Program Office; the cards should be printed from one place that is ICT department. However, the other members were of the view that the functional department should take the ownership but there should be strong supervision from ICT.

 

4.5       The Registrar suggested that they should ask Sukkur IBA as to what issues they faced in introducing and implementing the RFID cards.

 

4.6       The Dean & Director suggested that the Data Administrator should send the basic information to the individual faculty, staff and students for verification with a deadline and then the HR Department and Program office should print the card.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

4.7       The EC approved the sample of RFID Card that was presented with the logo of Sindh Government.

 

4.8       Mr. Imran Batada would get the agreed suggestions noted above incorporated and implemented including development of SOP.    

 

b)     Next step after ERP Implementation

 

4.9       Mr. Imran Batada said that they have implemented several systems (ERP, LMS, HRMS, Students Financials, SMS, Alumni etc.) in IBA. Now the major challenge is the utilization. He said that the systems are completely integrated with each other. If a proper entry is not being made its effect will appear in other modules such as enrolment numbers, registration numbers or in the financials. He said that to overcome this challenge, they have developed a Task Management System Application that will identify the task at the micro level. Tasking System provides a platform for managing and tracking tasks assigned to the employees. This Tasking system enables Managers to have detailed and up-to-date project schedule to direct team members accordingly and move the project forward. By utilizing this web based application, the team will know what they have to do, when it must be done and who needs to do it. This Tasking system also includes email alerts which are sent to the Managers/Supervisors as well as to task incharge when the tasks are created in the system. He said that the tasking system incorporates all the business processes that are being followed in IBA to accomplish routine tasks. This tasking system will benefit us in enforcing proper utilization of all the online systems (ERP, LMS, HRMS etc.) that are being developed and maintained by ICT team. Effective use of Tasking system will enable them in maintaining data accuracy and reliability and will also help in identifying and evaluating potential resources which can contribute in progress of IBA.

 

4.10     Mr. Imran Batada said that they have prepared the sample draft of Online Admission Business Process and incorporated the same in the Task Management System.  He said that this application will also be helpful to identify the job description of each and every individual hence the role of HR Department is pretty important as well. 

 

4.11     The Dean & Director said that how efficiently the systems are developed and resources used they would not be effective unless the functional departments as their guardians and custodians take the responsibility for data entry, validation, verification and up-dation. He said that only if the system has clearly defined responsibilities for each stage it would be robust. He suggested that the task completion tick off authority should be at one level up. Dr. Nishat suggested to incorporate alert in the system on the last date of completion. The Dean & Director suggested to give demonstration of the system and arrange workshop for the whole departments.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

4.12     The Committee appreciated the efforts of Head of ICT and advised to give demonstration of the system to all concerned and also arrange a workshop for them before implementing it.
