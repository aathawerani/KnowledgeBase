Dr. Nasir Touheed and Dr. Shakeel A. Khoja attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       To consider a proposal from Dr. Nasir Touheed on fee structure.

 

1.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed pointed out that currently the tuition fee of BBA is Rs.153,000/- whereas that of BS (Computer Science, Economics & Maths, Social & Liberal Arts) is Rs.117,000/-     He said that the Top 10% students of BS (CS, Eco Math, SSLA) are paying Rs.40,500/- more as compared to the top 10% students of BBA and BS A&F. He suggested that the top 10% students of BS (CS, Eco Math, SSLA) be given  a merit scholarship of Rs. 40, 500/- so that all top 10% students of all undergraduate programs will pay the same amount which is Rs 88,000/-

 

1.2       Dr. Sayeed Ghani agreed with Dr. Nasir Touheed and said that there would be a disparity between the top 10% students of BBA and BS (Computer Science, Economics & Maths, Social & Liberal Arts) if they are not considered for top 10% merit scholarship. The Dean & Director asked as to what would be the financial implication if 10% of the students of BS (CS, Eco Math, SSLA) are also allowed the merit scholarshipof Rs.40,500/- each. Dr. Nasir Touheed said that there could be 30 students in top 10%; thus the per annum financial impact would be Rs.2.4 million.

 

1.3       Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that the merit scholarships to top 10% students of BS (Computer Science, Economics & Maths, Social & Liberal Arts) should be given on the differential amount and not 50% of the tuition fee.  Dr. Shakeel Khoja suggested that in various universities fee structures of different programs are different but when we say 50% we should match it with the tuition fee. The Dean & Director clarified that in the transition period we will pay the differential amountbut when we fees of all undergraduate programs are equalized over the years, it would be 50% of the tuition fee for all undergraduate programs.  

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee decided that for 2015-16each of top 10% students of BS (CS, Eco Math, SSLA) would get the differential amount of merit scholarship which is currently Rs.40,500/- per semester.  Over the years when the fees of other BS programs are equal to the fee of BBA program, top 10% students from all undergraduate programs would get a merit scholarship of 50%.The additional expense will be covered from the additional revenue which will be generated from the increase in the budgeted number of student for 2015-16.

 

ACTION

 

1.5       The Director Finance would get the decision implemented from Fall 2015.

 

Item No.2       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         To approve merit scholarship to all those who are on Dean’s List.

 

2.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that the condition to continue to get Merit Scholarship for the top 10% students is to have and maintain a minimum CGPA of 3.5.  However, if a student (not in the top 10%) later improves his CGPA to 3.5 or above he / she cannot get the merit scholarship due to non-existence of slot.

 

2.2       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that in the first semester when the merit list is prepared, there are some components which are beyond the control of IBA. When the students complete their first semester only then we are in a position to gauge them again to find as to who are on the merit list.  He said that not all of the students who are on the Dean’s list get Merit Scholarship. He was of the view that those who come in Dean’s list should get some advantage and get the scholarships. Mr. Tanveer when asked confirmed that not all in Dean’s List get merit scholarships.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director agreed that all those who are in Dean’s list should automatically get the merit scholarship.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       It was decided that everybody on the Dean’s list would automatically be entitled to merit scholarship after adjusting for need based Scholarship received by the individual on the list. The Merit scholarships slots released yearly (due to 3.5 CGPA requirement) will be given first to those who are appearing on the Dean’s list. If there are any remaining vacant slots, i.e. after adjustment with the Dean’s list students, then such slots will be offered to other students (who are not on the Dean’s list) with CGPA 3.5 and above. The list would be reviewed once a year. The decision will be implemented from Fall 2016 onwards.

 

2.5       Those students who are on the merit scholarship but are not maintaining the CGPA will be given notice to improve their CGPA to a minimum of 3.5 failing which they would not be entitled to merit scholarship.

 

2.6       BS Students of (CS, Eco Math, SSLA) who are on the Dean’s list will get the scholarship of differential amount as decided in 1.4 above.

 

b)         Calculation of CGPA of the course result of which not announced on time.

 

2.7       Mr. Abdul Wajed sought clarification as to how the CGPA would be calculated if a student has done 24 courses but result of one course out of 24 was not announced for quite some time.

 

2.8       The Dean & Director said that the faculty members havewho have not posted the results on time have been penalized through salary deduction.  He clarified that if anybody has done 24 courses but the result of one course out of 24 has not been announced, the provisional CGPA should be calculated on 23 courses results of which have been announced. He said that the error factor in this case is 1/24 which is very insignificant and as such there would not be much distortion. The CGPA can be corrected in next semester when the actual result of the course is available. This would not delay the finalization of Dean’s List which is the case at present.

 

2.9       This formula should be applicable for all students and not just students with 24 courses. e.g. if a 1st semester student who has studied only 6 courses and result for one course is pending then chances of error are 1/6 and similarly 1/5, 1/10, 1/12 for 5, 10,12 courses respectively as students of 1st and 2nd semester of different programs have different course loads.

 

2.10     If the student whose result has not been posted till the announcement of Dean's List (with average marks) has obtained any scholarship, then it will not be reclaimed in case the student is found to be out of the Dean's List after posting of the result for that particular course. 

 

2.11     By the same token a student who could have been on the Dean's list but was not listed due to the usage of average marks (for marks not posted course) would be entitled to scholarship for the semester in which he/she was entitled but not listed on the Dean's List.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
