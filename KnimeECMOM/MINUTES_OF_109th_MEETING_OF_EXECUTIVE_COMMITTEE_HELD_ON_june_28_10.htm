MINUTES OF THE 109TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JUNE 28, 2010

 

The 109th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 28, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.  Dr. Ishrat Husain                                   Director

2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                            Associate Dean

3.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN               Registrar

4.  Mr. Ali Taha                                         Director Finance

5.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects

6.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                  Director ICT

7.  Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                              Director CED

8.  Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                              Director H.R.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Award of construction work of duct for Fiber Optic and other cables from data centre of Admin Block, City Campus to FCS Building.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that at the request of ICT Department for providing duct for fiber optic cable and other data cables from data centre at Admin Block to FCS building, a complete survey was done by Projects team at City Campus and jointly prepared a layout plan for the duct. He said that after detailed study, Projects team prepared a tender document. All pre-qualified contractors of tier #2 (pre-qualified in 2010) were invited to participate in tendering process. The invitation letters were sent to all contractors among which only four contractors collected the tender documents and submitted their bids on June 19, 2010. The following were the final bid prices of the contractors and their positions after arithmetical check:

 

M/s. The Technologist                         Rs.1,879,710/-             1st Lowest

M/s. Electro System                            Rs.1,927,675/-             2nd Lowest

M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.                   Rs.2,358,750/-             3rd Lowest

M/s. MIMA                                         Rs.3,013,825/-             Highest

 

1.2       The Director Projects stated that the Engineering estimate of the works was Rs.1,611,410/-, which was  prepared in house by Projects team at City Campus. M/s. The Technologist quoted 16.65% higher than the engineering estimate whereas the second & third lowest are 19.6% and 46.37% higher than engineering estimates therefore the rates quoted by M/s. The Technologist are competitive and workable. He requested the Executive Committee to give principle approval for the award of work to M/s. The Technologist at a cost of Rs.1,879,710/-  subject to final approval by the CPC. He said that if approved, the amount would be charged under budget head# 404.9

 

1.3       To a query, the Director Projects clarified that the bid price is for the civil work only; it neither includes cable nor the new buildings.

1.4       The Director asked the Director Projects to meet the requirements of the multi-storeyed building also. He further inquired about the laying of the fibre optic and other cables, and if M/s. The Technologist has the experience of laying duct.

 

1.5       The Director Projects and Director ICT explained that the ducting will be done in this tender till the data centre in the administration building. When the designing of the multi-storeyed will be completed the duct will be extended to, They confirmed that the FCS building is included in the loop.  He confirmed that M/s. The Technologist has the experience of laying duct. Director ICT informed that the cables will be laid by the ICT vendors.

 

1.6       The Director Projects further said that the existing Project Manager will be assigned this project at a lump sum amount instead of percentage as it is a small project.

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee in principle approved the proposal of award of work to M/s. The Technologist at a cost of Rs.1,879,710/-  subject to final approval by the CPC.

               

1.8       The Committee further decided that on execution of the Project, it would be monitored by the ICT staff to be nominated by the Director ICT.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve additional cost for furnishing canteen and lounge built at City Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that a complete furniture plan, air-conditioning requirements, details of canteen, lounge and kitchen were provided to the Registrar for onward transmission to the prospective sponsors for furnishing the canteen and lounge building. He said that with the efforts of the Registrar, PEPSI agreed to sponsor the furnishing and made a visit to the building. The PEPSI team required numerous changes in the Kitchen, storage, and service area layouts which were already built. They also wanted changes in finishing materials too. Some of the costs were borne by them whereas some costs associated with civil works and external works will have to be borne by IBA.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that for installation of six 2-ton split air conditioners PEPSI officials showed their ignorance that they were not informed about the requirement.  He said the ACs’ may have to be installed by IBA.       

 

2.3       The Director Projects stated that after detailed telephonic conversation by Mr. Umar Makhdoomi with Mr. Zafarul Islam of Integ, a tentative cost detail for these extra items were provided by Mr. Zafarul Islam which is as follows:

 

· Cost for 06 split ACs’ (one additional AC on Pepsi’s demand)

(2 Ton capacity each Mitsubishi brand) including installation,

wiring, piping etc.                                                             =    Rs. 500,000/-

 

 

· Cost for other additional items  which were not included in

the initial estimate e.g. gas connection, water connection,

electrical cable, exhaust/bracket fans etc.                             =      Rs. 500,000/-

(These belong to bulk utilities and are chargeable to external

development budget)

 

Grand Total                                                                       =   Rs.1,000,000/-

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that Integ is also of the view that there may be some saving also from the existing BOQ when a few of the original items would be expunged and replaced with new requirements. Hence the net cost impact because of the changes would be somewhat less than this amount. He requested the Executive Committee to approve in principle the above work. The Registrar IBA may review the IBA agreement with PEPSI and see if they can add the A.C. cost in their sponsoring amount.

 

2.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that PEPSI should sponsor the whole facilities. The Director also agreed with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee requested the Registrar to obtain details of the items PEPSI had agreed to provide and if the ACs’ are not included in the agreement then the Registrar would request PEPSI’s representative to sponsor that too.

 

2.7       The Committee agreed that IBA would bear the cost of around Rs.500,000/- for additional items such as electrical cable, gas connection etc.

 

ACTION

 

2.8       The Registrar would review the IBA agreement with PEPSI and approach the representative of PEPSI for AC’s.

 

 

Item No. 3      To resolve the matter of electrical load for staff colony.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the electrical problem at the IBA Staff Town was referred to the Projects Department on 21st June 2010. He also said that the Director IBA had advised that a system for improvement of the electrical supply as suggested by the Karachi University be verified by Projects Department.  He reported that they along with Electrical Consultant M/s. S.K. Consultants held a meeting with the University Engineer and the Electrical Engineer Karachi University on June 24. In this meeting the KU Engineers accepted that because of the extraordinary load at their sub station # 5 from where the supply is given to the IBA Staff Town, there is a serious problem of voltage drop. They advised that either IBA should get its own sub-station and get the required load from KESC or lay a high tension cable between an existing S/S # 4 to # 5 and a Low tension cable from S/S 5A to # 5. By this the supply will be improved as the supply from 4 and 5A will compensate for shortfall.  The Consultants agreed to this proposal, however they advised that a detailed survey was needed to establish the short and long term feasibility of this as it would involve heavy expenditure.  In a subsequent meeting with the Consultants, they informed that they have done reconnaissance survey  and estimated that the overall cost involved will be in a range of Rs.16 to Rs.17 Million. The 1250 Meter of high tension cable will alone cost Rs.12.0 Million.

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported that the Consultants have asked for a total lump sum fee of Rs.150,000/- for survey, designing, preparation of tender documents including drawings and specifications and top supervision. He requested the Executive Committee to approve in principle the following:

 

a)      To undertake the works at an estimated cost of about Rs.17.0 million

b)      To appoint M/s. S.K Consultants at a lump sum fee of Rs.150,000/-

c)      As the amount involved is high, to approve M/s Integ (who are already mobilized in Main Campus) as independent project managers on the job. Their fee will be on the lump sum basis (their proposal has been invited)

 

3.3       The Director Projects further said that the work is unforeseen, but it will have to be charged to External Development budget 2010-2011

 

3.4       The Director asked the Director Projects to apply for a sub-station. He said that he would speak to Mr. Waqar Siddique for the sub-station and additional load.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee agreed with the Director that the Director Projects should apply for a sub-station.

 

ACTION

 

3.6       The Director Projects would apply for a sub-station and the Director IBA would speak to Mr. Waqar Siddique on this.

 

 

Item No. 4      Conceptual approval  for launching of Alumni Chapter in Dubai.

 

4.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui stated that the Alumni relations department at IBA is in the process of establishing Alumni Chapters throughout the world in locations where the IBA alumni population is sufficiently dense. He reported that to commemorate their success and acknowledge their contributions, the IBA is holding Alumni Reunion Dinner and Dubai Alumni Chapter launch on July 19, 2010. He said that they have identified three hotels for holding the dinner and launch of alumni chapter at any of the three identified hotels.  He said that he is in the process of obtaining quotations from the three hotels.  He requested for conceptual approval for launching of Alumni Chapter in Dubai.

 

4.2       The Director suggested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to talk to Mr. Waqar Siddique regarding Alumni Chapter in Dubai.

 

DECISION

 

4.3       The Committee granted conceptual approval for launching of Alumni Chapter in Dubai.

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Copy of the approved IBA Budget 2010-11 to be provided to each department head.

 

5.1       The Director asked the Director Finance to provide hard copy of the approved budget 2010-11 along with allocations and the budgetary spending ceilings to each head of department.  He said that each department will be responsible to keep track of its budget.

5.2       The Director said that decision on project related manning requirements will be made by the Executive Committee. He made it clear that only faculty positions would be created. d)     Inauguration of IBA Main Campus Auditorium.

 

5.4       It was decided that the inauguration of the IBA Main Campus Auditorium will be done on September 14, 2010 by which date the Director Projects committed that the Auditorium would be ready.

     

DECISION

 

5.5       The Committee noted and approved the decisions mentioned in 5.1 to 5.4 above.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
