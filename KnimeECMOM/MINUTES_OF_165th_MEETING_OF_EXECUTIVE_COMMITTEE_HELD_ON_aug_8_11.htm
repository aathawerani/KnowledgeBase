Item No. 1      To consider and approve covering over elevated walkway of Academic Block at City Campus.  

 

1.1       The Director ICT reported that in the original design of the elevated walkway of the Academic Block at the City Campus, a semicircular canopy was proposed and the material was also procured, which is stored at the vendor’s warehouse and the vendor is claiming payment for that. He said that when the walkway was completed, it was decided not to have the canopy; however after about 15 months of completion the general feeling is that we should reconsider the canopy installation for the following reasons:

 

·         The metallic pillars to support the canopy have been erected and they look atrociously bad giving an incomplete look.

·         The walkway becomes blistering hot during the mid-days.

·         The walkway becomes difficult to walk upon during the rains; even slightest rain makes it slippery and dangerous.

·         The material is already procured as the vendor is claiming payment for that.

·         The overall building gives an incomplete look.

 

1.2       Considering the above mentioned points, the Director ICT requested the EC to reconsider the previous decision and approve the installation of the canopy.

 

1.3       The Committee was informed that the Director Projects has suggested that there is a new technology which is relatively better and cheaper too.

 

1.4       The Registrar suggested that the design of the canopy should be such that it does not have deposits.  

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee decided that Mr. Umar Makhdoomi would discuss the various options including the new technology with the vendor and bring a proposal to the EC for consideration.

 

ACTION

 

1.6       Mr. Umar Makhdumi would discuss this issue with the vendor to find out the best and cheapest option and present a proposal to the EC for consideration.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve matters pertaining to Architects M/s. Icon.

 

2.1       The Director Projects had requested the Executive Committee to consider the following issues for settlement of the cases of M/s. Icon:

 

a)         To waive off penalty on delays on the designs of the Administration Building Main Campus.

             

                 b)         To settle the matter of breach of contract by M/s. Icon

 

 

a)         To waive off penalty on delays on the designs of the Administration Building Main Campus.

 

2.2       Mr. Abdul Rahman, Project Engineer reported that they had informed M/s. Icon of the delays on the designs and documentation of the Administration Building Main Campus. They were informed through letters dated 2nd May 2001 & 28th June 2011 that a penalty as per clause 3.6. of the agreement would be imposed. In response to the letters M/s. Icon submitted their explanations which were analysed by the Projects Department. They informed that the changes were numerously made by the IBA and designs had to be revised several times. Further delays had occurred because of the scrutiny by IBA Project Managers, who also took considerable time and later on M/s. ICON had to incorporate many suggestions made by the Project Managers.  He said that M/s. Icon had further informed that the execution of projects assigned to them had been delayed without any fault of the Architects. They had not charged any extra amount on those projects and have been providing their services for the prolonged period such as on Boys’ Hostel, and City Campus. The other projects had also been completed beyond the completion dates during which period M/s. Icon have provided their services without any extra charges.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know the assessment of the Projects Department.

 

2.4       Mr. Abdul Rahman said that they were continuously getting input and the work was not stopped. He said that the Projects Department after due deliberations has reached the conclusion that the penalty may be waived off keeping in view the above facts.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee waived off the penalty considering the grounds and recommendations given by the Architect and the Projects Department. 

            b)         To settle the matter of breach of contract by M/s. Icon.

 

2.6       Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that a letter dated 27th April 2011 was sent to M/s. Icon asking them to explain the reasons within 30 days of receipt of the letter (as per agreement) on the prolonged absence of Principal Architects Mr. Hanif Daud who had gone to United States. M/s. Icon immediately explained their position on the matters. Mr. Hanif Daud also reached Karachi well in time and further explained the working of his office in his absence as well as his involvement on the projects.  He also confirmed appointment of a full time Sr. Quantity Surveyor and part-time Quantity Surveyors.

 

2.7       Mr. Abdul Rahman stated that they had asked the Project Managers about the performance on the projects, who confirmed that they did not experience any difficulty during the absence of Mr. Hanif Daud and that the Architects stationed at Karachi had provided responses in time. Based on the above facts, he requested the Executive Committee that the matters raised against M/s. Icon may be treated as withdrawn and resolved.

 

2.8       The Dean & Director wanted to know the up-date on the Admin. Block, Main Campus.

 

2.9       Mr. Abdul Rahman said that tender for the renovation of Admin. Block would be done next week and the construction/renovation would be completed in 24 months.

 

2.10     The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to make an Admin. Floors’ vacation plan in consultation with Mr. Abdul Rahman and Mr. Hanif Daud.

 

DECISON

 

2.11     The Committee approved the request to treat the letter issued to M/s. Icon on breach of contract withdrawn and the matter stands resolved.

 

ACTION

 

2.12.    The Director Projects would withdraw the letter issued to M/s. Icon on April 27, 2011.

 

2.13     The Registrar in consultation with Mr. Abdul Rahman and Mr. Hanif Daud would prepare a vacation plan of Admin. Floors of Main Campus for the renovation / construction of the Admin. Building.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider and approve proposal for honorarium for examination duties on Saturdays/Holidays/after office time.

 

4.1       Mr. Muhammad Saleem Umer, Program Director EMBA, stated that the following members of Executive MBA team were performing exam duties on Sundays/holidays or on weekdays after their usual office hours:

 

1.      Mr. Muhammad Munawar             Senior Program Executive

2.      Mr. Arshad Zia                          Office Assistant

3.      Ms. Bushra Bilal                         Office Assistant/Secretary.

 

He said that the three cannot claim any overtime as their salary has surpassed the upper limit; therefore, there is a need to consider a compensation method for them. He said that in a similar case, the Executive Committee in its 134th meeting held on December 30, 2010 approved a compensation of Rs.1,000/- per day for a staff member doing examination duty on Sundays. He reported that the matter was discussed with the Dean & Director and was also examined by the Director H.R. and their opinion is as under:

 

“The EC has approved payment of Rs.1000/- per day for 3 members of the examination department. Two are involved in night duty of confidential nature plus logistical arrangements; the 3rd, Mr. Rais A. Khan is paid Rs.1000/- per day for supervision of EMBA exams in case this takes place on Sunday. Mr. Saleem Umer’s request is similar to Mr. Rais Khan’s situation and can therefore, be accommodated in the same manner.”

           

4.2       Based on the above, Mr. M. Saleem Umer requested the Executive Committee to approve the following compensation for the above mentioned three staff members:

 

i)    For Sundays/public holidays @Rs.1000/- per day.

ii)   For invigilation duty after working hours @Rs.200 per hour (Maximum Rs.1000 per day).

 

4.3       The Dean & Director said that at Assistant level we should consider compensation / overtime but at Executive level we should not consider because the job we expect from an Executive is of different nature. He said that there should be a distinction among managers, executives and lower staff.

 

4.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqi suggested that Executives should be considered for rewards.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee approved the proposal for payment for working on Sundays/public holidays @Rs.1000/- per day to the Assistants only. The Committee also approved the payment for invigilation duty after working hours @Rs.200 per hour (Maximum Rs.1000 per day). The working hours will be calculated on actual invigilation hours.

 

 

b)     To consider request for rescheduling final examination from Syed Jahanzeb Shah, student of EMBA-CM-IV.  

               

4.6       Mr. M. Saleem Umer reported that he has received a request from Syed Jahanzeb Shah, student of EMBA-CM-IV for rescheduling his final examination for the reason that his wife will be going for a Caesarean section case in Aga Khan Hospital on August 11, 2011 and the date and time is coinciding with the final examination of Accounting for Business Decision. He said that the student has mentioned in his request that he lives independently and families of both (he and his wife) are not residents of Karachi so he has to be with his wife at the Hospital during the surgery.  He further stated that he tried his best to reschedule the surgery date but since the Operation Theatre and Surgeon schedules were already booked, they have no other choice. 

 

4.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that we should allow make-up on compassionate ground.

 

DECISION

 

4.8       The Committee decided that the request of Syed Jahanzeb Shah be considered in accordance with the decision of the 51st meeting of Academic Board held on June 18, 2010 which is reproduced below:

 

            The Academic Board however, decided that in case a student misses his / her exams on personal medical grounds, or other extraordinary unavoidable circumstances he / she may apply for ‘I’ (Incomplete) with all supporting documents including medical certificates through his / her respective program coordinator to the Executive Committee. If the Executive Committee is satisfied with the genuineness of the claim then it may award an ‘I’ instead of an ‘F’ in that course.  In that case the concerned student will have to appear in the examination of that course in the following semester. However, if the Executive Committee is not satisfied with the genuineness of the case, then ‘F’ will be awarded.  This policy will be applicable for all IBA programs. It was also clarified that the interim transcript would include all details (‘I’, ‘W’ or ‘F’) but the final transcript would show only the final results.

 

ACTION

 

4.9       Mr. Muhammad Saleem Umer would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Jahanzeb Shah.

4.13     The Director Finance would arrange to pay one month’s basic salary to IBA employees in BPS-1 to 15 or equivalent grade.

 

d)     Division of Maintenance functions into two; General Maintenance, and Technical Maintenance.

 

4.14     The Dean & Director said that the Director Projects has advised that Maintenance should be divided into two; General Maintenance, and Technical Maintenance including Security.  He requested the Registrar to look into it.
