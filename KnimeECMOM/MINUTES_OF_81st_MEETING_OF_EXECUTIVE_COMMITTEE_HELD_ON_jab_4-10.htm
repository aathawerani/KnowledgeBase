Item No. 4      To consider proposal from Director Finance on restructuring of fee schedule.

 

4.1       The Director Finance reported that IBA has been charging other charges as one time charge to students at the time of admission, together with the tuition fee. He said that the one time charges and some recurring charges have not been reviewed for past couple of years. He suggested that as the concessional fee policy has changed, there is a need to review the fee structure altogether.  He informed the Committee that under the current practice they have been charging following other charges:

                                                                                         Rs.

i)        Caution Money  (effective from 1994)        5,000/-  Refundable to students

ii)      Development charges (effective from 98)  25,000/- One time charge from

                                                                                                     Concessional program

                                                                                                students

iii)    Enrolment fee    (Revised in 2005)               2,000/-  One time charge

iv)    Transcript fee    (Revised in 2005)               1,000/-  One time charge

v)      Lab cum Library (Revised in 2005)              1,500/-  Recurring charge

   

4.2       The Director Finance further reported that IBA has been charging Rs.25,000/- as development charges since 1998 to all those students who had been enrolled under concessional programme. He said that from the current academic year 2008-09 concessional programme has been abandoned and IBA is offering Merit based (i.e. 50% of tuition fee) scholarship to top 10% new entrants on the basis of grades obtained in the admission test.

 

4.3       The Director Finance said that the Caution Money is due for refund one year after a student passes out. Refund process involves non productive clearance process in various departments of IBA. He said that in most of the educational institutions (including LUMS) there are no refundable charges.

 

4.4       The Director Finance made the following suggestion to streamline the fee structure:

 

·         IBA may charge one time admission fee of Rs.10,000/- (non-refundable).

·         IBA may increase Lab cum Library charges from Rs.1,500 to Rs.2,500/- per semester for all students. The rational for increase is that IBA is providing modern Library and I.T. equipped facilities to the students.

 

·         Caution money of Rs.5,000/- and Enrolment fee of Rs.2,000/- be removed.

The reason is that the charges are too low and have not been revised since 2005. Further, the Caution money and Enrolment fee are now suggested to be merged in one time charge under the head Admission Fee.

 

4.5       The Director Finance presenting the details of the net impact on revenues stated that in the year 2010-11, the proposed re-structuring in the fee schedule would result in an Increase of Rs.8.8 million in IBA total revenues based on 2,200 students (including 800 new entrants).

 

4.6                   The Director Finance sought approval of the Executive Committee for removal of Development Charges, Caution Money and Enrolment Fee and introduction of a new Admission Fee of Rs.10,000/- in replacement and enhancement of Lab-cum-Library fee from Rs.1,500/- to Rs.2,500/-

 

4.7       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to increase the Admission Test Fee from Rs.2,500/- to Rs.5,000/-.

 

4.8       The Registrar suggested that instead of Rs.5,000/- the Admission Test Fee should be fixed at Rs.3,500/-

 

DECISION

 

4.9       The Committee agreed with the suggestions of the Director Finance on restructuring of fee schedule as presented above.

 

4.10     The Committee recommended that the Admission Test Fee should be fixed at Rs.3,500/-

 

ACTION
