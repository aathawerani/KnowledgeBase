MINUTES OF 260TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, AUGUST 12, 2013

 

The 260th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 12, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                               Acting Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                         Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                            Registrar

4.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                                Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                                     Director Projects

6.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                               Acting Head of ICT

 

Dr. Nasir Touheed, Coordinator Testing Services, attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Review of Admissions Fall 2013.

              

1.1       The Registrar reported that on the status of admissions for Fall 2013 provided by him to the EC through email on August 3, 2013, the Dean & Director observed that the number of students admitted to programs other than BBA, MBA and MS is extremely high and out of range.  The Registrar said that the Dean & Director has stated that we should not have admissions in the spring semester for any of the undergraduate programs.  The Registrar said that he has invited Dr. Nasir Touheed, Coordinator Testing Services to present an analysis of admissions for Fall 2013.

 

1.2       Dr. Nasir Touheed presented a program-wise comparison of the total number of applicants, admissions offered, and students admitted in 2012 and expected numbers in 2013.  A comparison of the expected fresh intake in undergraduate programs for Fall 2013 as provided in the budget 2013-14 was also made with the actual intake. It was noticed that except for BS Accounting & Finance Program, admissions in other undergraduate programs were below the budgeted figure – approximately 612 students are expected to be admitted against the budgeted figure of 670.  It was also noticed that 265 students are expected to be admitted into the BBA program and 126 are expected to be admitted in the BS A&F Program although the budgeted figures were 350 and 60 respectively.

 

1.3       The members were satisfied as far as the number of total intake was concerned except the number of intake in BBA Program which is due to the introduction of new undergraduate programs of BS A&M, and BS SS.

 

1.4       The Committee also considered the observations of Mr. Ejaz Mian regarding absence from the list of candidates all government schools i.e. FA, FSC, FCOM etc.  It was thought that as most of the students with intermediate background cannot afford the admission test fee, they may not be appearing for the admission test.  It was suggested that to increase the pool of applicants from intermediate level, those intermediate students who cannot afford the admission test fee, may be allowed exemption from test fee after obtaining documentary evidence of their inability to pay the admission test fee. The Committee requested Mr. Imran Batada to find out the ratio of candidates with Intermediate background applied for and were offered admissions in undergraduate programs of IBA. The Committee decided that if the percentage of applicants with Intermediate level, is very low then exemptions from test fee may be considered and for which a mechanism would be developed.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee concluded that the number of students admitted in BBA program reduced because of introduction of new programs.

 

1.6       The Committee decided that except for the BS Computer Science, no fresh admission would be offered in Spring 2014. 

 

ACTION

 

1.7       Mr. Imran Batada would find out the ratio of candidates with Intermediate and ‘A’ level backgrounds applied for and were offered admissions in undergraduate programs of IBA and send it to the EC for information.  

 

Item No. 2      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Status Report from Director Projects:

 

2.1       Renovation and Refurbishment of existing Library – Project Management Fee: The Director Projects reported that the project was not complete as of 3rd August 2013.  He said that NESPAK is being conveyed the decision of the Executive Committee that if there is any residual work after August 3, 2013, the Director Projects would get it completed by M/s. NESPAK without making any additional payment.  He further said that the extension of the completion date was approved as November 30, 2013 by the A&FC.    

 

The Registrar observed that the project does not seem to be completed by November 30, 2013.  He said that the Convocation is planned in November 2013 and requested the Director Projects that at least the exterior of the Library should be completed by that time and the dump of waste material should be removed from around the library.   

 

2.2       Designing and supervision of work of supply and installation of elevator at FCS Building: The Director Projects reported that the Projects Department had decided to undertake the designing and procurement of the elevator of Admin. Building too on the same basis. He reported that tenders have been prepared and both the works would be advertised next week.

 

2.3       Collapse of Seminar Hall Ceiling of Aman CED Building – The Director Projects to get the reconstruction and ratification works as recommended by the Architect done through the contractor without making any additional payment: The Director Projects reported that the work has been completed, inspected by the Architects, and certified by them.  No cost has been paid.

 

2.4       Cracks in FCS Building: The Director Projects reported that preliminary report was sent to the EC on July 13, 2013. He said that details of the structures were sent to Mr. Mushtaque Dawood and a meeting was also held by Mr. Mushtaque Dawood with the structural designer and the Architect of the Building on August 2, 2013. The designer had promised to send his design analysis after Eid holidays. He recommended that as the matter is important, a detailed vetting of the structural design of FCS Building be conducted by the external expert Mr. Mushtaque Dawood. He reported that a letter has already been written to him asking for his technical and financial proposal.  He requested the EC to allow him to get the vetting of the whole FCS building if the report is not satisfactory.  

 

DECISION

2.5       The Committee noted the up-date and allowed him to get the vetting of the whole FCS Building by Mr. Mushtaque Dawood if the report is not satisfactory.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
