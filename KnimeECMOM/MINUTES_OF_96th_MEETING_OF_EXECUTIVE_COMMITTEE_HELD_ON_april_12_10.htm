Item No. 2      To consider and approve:

 

a)       Reward Policy &

b)       Grievances of Option ‘A’ employees’ on Earned Leave entitlement and other issues.

 

a)   Reward Policy:

 

2.1       The Director H.R. stated that IBA endeavors to inculcate a positive, productive and innovative organizational culture. With this objective they want to introduce an on-going program for Employee Recognition and Reward aimed at fostering a culture of acknowledgement and appreciation amongst employees for:

 

·         Introducing innovative professional practices,

·         Showing extra ordinary efforts for achievement of goals,

·         Enhancing IBA’s corporate image.

 

2.2       The Director H.R. said that the scope of the reward policy would cover all fulltime faculty and staff, including regular and contract. She proposed that the criteria for nominees must include one or more of the following:

 

·         Commitment

o   Outstanding skills or efforts / contributions above and beyond standard job requirements.

·         Professionalism / Competence

o   An excellent level of performance during an organizational emergency or period of high stress, or shown great ingenuity or perseverance to achieve customer satisfaction.

·         Creativity & Innovation

o   Suggestions leading to improvements in the work process, workflow, in customer service, or save significant money and time of the Institution.

·         Leadership and Excellence

o   Uphold the IBA Values, exhibit Role Model, Major extraordinary achievement and/or extraordinary contribution requiring maximum effort that significantly impacted customer service, productivity and IBA’s corporate image that is not likely to repeat itself.

2.3       The Director H.R. said that formally, the Employee Reward and Recognition Program would encompass following four types of recognition levels, based on the achievement of the individuals/teams:

 

·                     1st Level - An informal way of recognition, which includes simple Thank you notes / emails / letters.

·                     2nd Level -  It includes a Cash Award of Rs. 5,000/-, and a Recognition Certificate

·                     3rd Level - It includes a Cash Award of Rs. 15,000/-, a Recognition Certificate & Shield

·                     4th Level - It includes a Cash Award of Rs. 30,000/-, a Recognition Certificate & Shield

 

2.4       The Director H.R. also briefed about the procedure of recommending and approving the nominees for the award and the responsibilities of departmental / functional heads towards nomination for the award.

 

2.5              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested that the performance required for each level needs to be clearly defined.

 

DECISION

 

2.6              The Committee appreciated the proposal for rewarding / recognizing employees at IBA for their outstanding performance and approved the Reward Policy as presented by the Director H.R.

 

2.7              The Committee requested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and Dr. Nasir Afghan to suggest the performance required for each level to the Director H.R. for incorporating in the Reward Policy.

 

 

 

 

ACTION

 

2.8              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and Dr. Nasir Afghan would suggest the performance / achievements to be required for each level to the Director H.R. for incorporating in the Reward Policy.

Reinstatement of Leave Allowed to Government employees:

 

2.10     The Director H.R. stated that the existing IBA Leave rules came into effect with the approval of IBA Board of Governors in January 2004 whereby, IBA employees’ entitlement of 48 earned leaves was reduced to 30 earned leaves with provision of encashment of 30 days’ leaves. She said that they sought legal opinion from Mumtaz Associates and they were of the opinion that the IBA Board of Governors is empowered to reduce the Earned leaves from the 48 days prescribed in University of Karachi Leave Rules to 30 days as per its own rules. This action of the Board of Governors does not suffer from any legal infirmities.

 

2.11          The Director wanted to know as to what is the government’s current earned leave rule.

 

2.12          It was confirmed that according to government rules 48 days earned leaves are allowed. 

 

2.13          The Director said that they cannot withdraw the advantages available to Option “A’ employees. He said that in all fairness and equity they should restore the 48 days Earned leave to Option ‘A’ employees. He requested the Director H.R. to present the case before the Board through A&FC for reversal of decision of 30 days Earned Leave for Option ‘A’ employees.

 

DECISION

 

2.14          The Committee agreed with the Director that the Board should be requested to reverse its earlier decision on reduction of Earned Leave from 48 to 30 for Option ‘A’ employees.

 

ACTION

 

2.15          The Director H.R. would present the case to the Board through A&FC for reversal of the decision on Earned Leaves.

 

Double Overtime on Holidays:

 

2.16     The Director H.R. stated that she sought legal advice on the claim of double overtime on holidays. She said that the legal opinion is that there is no separate formula for computation of over time on holidays, standard over time rates will apply, however if Board of Governors had approved a different policy for over time on holidays only then would a different calculation apply.

 

DECISION

 

2.17     In light of the legal opinion stated above the Committee did not approve the requested double overtime on holidays.

Item No. 3      Presentation by Dr. Nasir Afghan on Branding MBA and the future outlook for MBAs.

 

3.1       Dr. Nasir Afghan shared with the EC members the information he gathered in a Conference held in Barcelona, Spain. This was focused on the impact of financial crises on MBA job market and the future outlook for MBAs.  He also made suggestions on the branding of post-experience MBA of IBA and the recruitment strategy which included the following:

 

·         Developing MBA program flyer and poster

·         Contacting Chamber of Commerce and Industries

·         Visiting and writing letters to companies

·         Newspaper ads for MBA program only

·         Sending Posters and letters to alumni, family businesses, engineering / manufacturing / service / financial sector

 

3.2              The Director said that we should think of a new program i.e. one year Master’s in Management if we do not get sufficient number of students for MBA program.  He also suggested that during interviews, candidates should be tested on IBA values as exercise of IBA values is very important. He also suggested that instead of contacting Chamber of Commerce & Industries, they should contact Overseas Chambers, American Business Council, Marketing Association of Pakistan, and Management Association of Pakistan, Pakistan- French, Pakistan-German, Pakistan-UK Business Forums.

 

3.3              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that the advertising agency should be approached and they would give options based on IBA’s requirements.

 

DECISION

 

3.4              The Committee approved the recruitment strategy as presented by Dr. Nasir Afghan and asked him to go ahead with his plan as he would be the focal person for the implementation of this plan. He was specifically requested to draft letters to the President, OCC&I and other organizations mentioned above on behalf of the Director IBA, get newspaper ad prepared through advertising agency and obtain mailing list from the Alumni Office of IBA.

 

ACTION

 

3.5              Dr. Nasir Afghan would implement the decisions as noted in 3.4 above.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Payment proposal for transition phase of ERP.

 

5.1       The Director ICT stated that the ERP implementation process at IBA has successfully entered into the UAT phase. He said that at the completion of this phase the next task would be to enter previous active students’ records to the new ERP system. For this purpose there is a need for extra human resources which includes ‘Data Entry Operators’ and ‘Verification staff’. He presented details of the estimated records to be entered and then verified.

 

5.2       The Director Projects stated that all IBA Program Offices had been handling this data previously and would also be using it in future so the best resource to perform the above mentioned task would be the members of Program Offices. However, he suggested that if they motivate them by giving a monetary incentive then they can be requested to put extra hours for the ‘Data Entry and Verification’ task. Based on the nature of work and the time estimated to perform this task, he suggested that each ‘Data Entry Operator’ and ‘Verification Staff’ be paid a flat rate of Rs.25 per student record.  The total estimated cost for completion of this task would be Rs.134,400/-. In order to further facilitate and accelerate the process, he proposed that if they announce an incentive of 20% extra payment on completion of the task 4 days in advance then the total estimated cost would be Rs.161,280/-. He also presented the break-up of the amount proposed to be paid.

 

DECISION

 

5.3              The Committee approved the payment proposal for transition phase of ERP as presented by the Director ICT.  However, the Committee decided that:

 

a)      No payment would be made on wrong entry of data

b)      People involved in data entry work would not be doubly compensated i.e. both overtime and per entry payment would not be allowed.

c)      Entry would be completed on time.

 

ACTION

 

5.4              The Director ICT would get the data entry job completed.

 

 

 

b)         Hardware for ERP Development over cloud computing.  

 

5.5              The Director ICT said that they are ready to start the implementation of ERP for the test run during the Summer Term. However since they have not established their Server Rooms, they have to go for Cloud Computing. He said that he has two offers:

1.     International company Redplaid. Cost of same solution is $10,499 per month.

2.     The ERP Consultant, Techlogix is providing the same solution via Ufone on month to month basis. Estimated Cost for this solution is $5100 per month.

 

5.6       The Director ICT recommended to get the Cloud Computer Solution from Techlogix since they are cost effective and they are guaranteeing the proper operation, and they would also get the benefit of one vendor for both services (Hardware & Software). He further said that if it is not done then they would have to shut down the ERP. He requested that the proposal may be approved for submission to the CPC.

 

DECISION

 

5.7       The Committee in principle agreed that the proposal may be taken to the Central Purchase Committee.

 

ACTION

 

5.8       The Director ICT would take the proposal to the Central Purchase Committee.
