Director Finance:

 

1.1       The Director Finance to pass on to the Associate Deans the list of those students who have two or more semesters’ dues so that they may ask those students to clear their dues: The Director Finance reported that the list of outstanding fee had been provided to Associated Deans in December 2011. However, he said that as a normal accounting practice they have also sent request letters to the concerned students.  He said that the Finance Department has evaluated the current fee receivable; provision for doubtful recoverable fee and the amount of old fee to be written off. He presented the statement showing the amount of fee receivable as on February 15, 2012. The net receivable as on February 15, 2012 is Rs.14,711,991/-

 

The Director Finance said that fee of some of the students who wait for scholarships of private donors and Sindh Government which are received late, appears as outstanding in the statement. 

 

The Director Finance said that he has also presented two statements; one to be treated as bad debts (Rs.544,771) and another for making provision for bad debts (Rs.3,932,121) which will be reviewed in June every year due to addition and deletion.  He said that in percentage term, the bad debts will be very very minimal.  He requested the EC to give approval for writing off the amount of Rs.544,771/-   He said that he had discussed his proposal with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to approach the employers of our alumni who have not cleared their dues for recovery of outstanding dues but Mr. Siddiqui did not agree because of the small amount involved. He sought permission for placing list of defaulters on the IBA Portal and notice boards with the amount recoverable from each alumnus.

 

The Committee did not agree on placing list of defaulters on the IBA          Portal. The Committee allowed to write off the amount of Rs.544,771/-              and to make provision for bad debts for Rs.3,932,121/-

Item No.2       To consider and approve Performance Evaluation Criteria for Architects and Consultants.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee had assigned the task of development of the Performance criteria to Mr Shameem Ahmad. Later on Projects Department was asked to assist Mr Shameem Ahmed. He said that the criteria for performance evaluation of Architects and Consultants have been developed and on the approval of the format, the criteria for Project Managers and Contractors will be developed on the approved format.  He said that for the first time they would be establishing this kind of criteria because it is not done in any institution in Pakistan. He said that they have taken guidance from the manual of Ministry of Construction in U.K. where it is extensively used. He further informed that he has used the same format as developed by HR department IBA under performance management system being implemented in IBA. He presented three different types of forms with information columns for name of the project, projects volume in rupee, total fee, and assignment duration.  He further said that the Projects Department asks them to provide Internal Control Manual and only those who have their manual are prequalified.

 

2.2       It was suggested to add a column of “delays” also in the form.

 

2.3       The Director Projects presented Form –A for Home Office Management and Controls, Form – B for Drawings and Documentation, Form – C for Top Supervision (Design & Site Coordination).

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that if the formats are approved in principle then they would develop it for contractors, project managers and architects.

 

2.5       The Dean & Director said that the performance criteria will be part of the new contract and if it is not in the existing contracts, then this will have to be incorporated.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee noted and in principle approved the formats for performance evaluation criteria as presented by the Director Projects.

 

 

Item No. 3      Presentation on Electrical Work at IBA Sports Arena.

 

3.1       The Director Projects said that Mr. Asad Ilyas has requested for night lighting arrangement on the sport grounds.  He said that the Projects Department is working on external development and if the EC decides that night lighting is to be arranged then they would have to lay conduits and underground pipes for cable and complete the underground works now.

 

3.2       The Registrar said that laying of cable is less expensive but night lighting itself is very costly. The estimated cost is Rs.32 million for light and fixture.  He suggested that only pipe and cabling should be done for the cricket ground only.  This cricket ground can be used for multipurpose; even the convocation can also be done there.

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that Mr. Asad Ilyas has said that he would try to find donor for the night lighting arrangements. He said that the estimated cost of underground pipe and cabling work would be Rs.677,000/-  He said that some civil work is also involved which would cost around Rs.300,000. So the total estimated cost would be around Rs.1 million. He said that he needs approval because the underground work will be done now for the cricket ground.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director said that technical advice is very necessary on sports ground.

 

3.5       The Director Projects said that the contractor has engaged a technical person and Mr. Asad Ilyas will also bring his own person for technical supervision of sports ground during the construction.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee approved the underground pipe, cabling and civil work for the cricket ground which is estimated to cost around Rs.1 million.

 

3.7       The Committee asked the Director Projects to send the estimate of Rs.32 million to Mr. Asad Ilyas for arranging donor.

 

ACTION

 

3.8       The Director Projects would get the underground work i.e. laying of pipe and cable and civil work for the cricket ground completed. 

 

3.9       The Director Projects would send the estimate of Rs.32 million to Mr. Asad Ilyas for arranging donor.
