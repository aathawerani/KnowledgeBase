MINUTES OF 166TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY,

AUGUST 15, 2011

 

The 166th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 15, 2011 at 9:00 a.m. at Societies’ Room, Academic Block, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean, BA

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

5.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

6.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                                   Director CED

7.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                               Director H.R.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      Approval of Siemens bid for the project “Construction of a new KESC Sub-station and relocation of four generators at IBA City Campus.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Executive Committee in its 160th meeting held on July 5, 2011 had approved the pre-qualification of the two contractors namely; (1) Siemens (Pakistan) Engg. Co. Limited and (2) AREVA T&D Pakistan (Pvt.) Limited for the project “Construction of a new KESC Sub-station and relocation of four generators at IBA City Campus”. He said that M/s. Siemens, over and above our standard terms and conditions, had imposed some new conditions which were sent to the Consultant and Project Managers for evaluation. The Consultant rejected the bid because of the conditions but the Project Manager suggested that if Siemens withdraw their conditions then they may be considered. He said that the Technical Evaluation Committee in its meeting held on August 3, 2011 recommended the award of the project “Construction of a new KESC Sub-station and relocation of four generators at IBA City Campus” to the lowest bidder, Siemens, at their bid price of Rs.44,072,765/- provided Siemens withdrew the additional technical and commercial terms and conditions that was attached with the bid. He said that the TEC’s recommendation was considered by CPC in its meetings held on August 4, 6 and 11, 2011. He reported that Siemens withdrew their conditions but there are two conditions which they insisted to retain.

 

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that as the generators are required to be relocated urgently due to FCS building project which has already started, and as the existing KESC sub-station is required to be shifted to its new location, so that the site be cleared for Aman Tower building, therefore keeping in view the emergency of starting this project without loss of time, the CPC in its final meeting held on August 11, 2011, has approved award of the work to Siemens at their bid price of Rs.44,072,765/-. CPC has also approved that the standard contract agreement may include the following clauses:

 

a)         Maximum Liability: Items 20, 21 and 47 of Appendix A to Bid (Special Stipulations) will be subject to the following interpretation, namely, “An Aggregate Limitation of Liability of Contractor for any and all claims for damages including LD and indemnification in any respect shall not exceed the Contract Price”. 

­

b)         Liability for Indirect or Consequential Damage: Neither party shall be liable to the other for any loss or profit, loss of use, loss of production, loss of contracts or for any other indirect or consequential damage.   

 

1.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know the break-up of Rs.44 million.

 

1.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed informed the Committee that there are 20% civil work involved in this project, the existing sub-station would be moved to the new place, the work also includes shifting, relocation and synchronization of the four generators, procurement of cables and equipments such as transformers, switch gears etc. All these are covered in Rs.44 million.

 

1.5       The Registrar presented a proposal received from the Maintenance Department in March 2011 regarding shifting of generators. It was seen that the entire proposal amounting to Rs.1.6 million presented by the Maintenance Department already formed part of the work being assigned to Siemens. The Registrar further stated that in his opinion the amount was less as it did not contain the entire work assigned to Siemens now. Mr. Shameem Ahmed confirmed that the work involving relocation of the four geneators was a part of the project now to be executed by Siemens.

 

1.6       The Dean & Director being out of country could not attend the meeting but through email he wanted to record his agreement to the recommendation made by CPC. He had mentioned in his email that Siemens is a world class company and they have shown flexibility by withdrawing other extraneous conditions. He was sure that Siemens would do a competent job of this shifting.

 

1.7       The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed to request Siemens that all work relating to shifting of generators be done on priority and a time line be given for that.

 

DECISION

 

1.8      The Executive Committee recommended the proposal to the A&FC to approve Siemens bid of Rs.44,072,765/- for the project “Construction of a new KESC Sub-station and relocation of four generators at IBA City Campus. The Committee also recommended to A&FC to approve to include in the contract agreement the two clauses noted in 1.2 (a) & (b) above.  

 

ACTION

 

1.9       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would send the proposal to the A&FC for approval through circular resolution.

 

1.10     Subject to approval by the A&FC/BoG, Mr. Shameem Ahmed would request Siemens to complete the work relating to shifting of generators on priority and to provide a time line for the completion of the project.

Item No.2       To consider and approve Financial Guidelines for Students.

 

2.1       The Director Finance reported that the Financial Guidelines for Students were circulated to Program Directors, Program Officers, and Executive Committee for their feedback. He said that minor changes were suggested by the Registrar which have been incorporated.  He further said that new fee structure has been included and bank addresses etc. have also been changed wherever applicable. He requested the EC to consider and approve the Financial Guidelines for Students.  

 

2.2       The Registrar suggested that the Financial Guidelines for Students should be part of the Student Handbook. The Committee agreed with the Registrar.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee approved the Financial Guidelines for Students as presented by the Director Finance.

 

ACTION

 

2.4       The Registrar would get the Financial Guidelines for Students included into the Student Handbook and have them printed for distribution among the students.

 

 

Item No. 3      Progress Report on Data Centre Projects.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that August 25, 2011 was the date for closing of tender but different vendors are requesting to extend the date because of Ramazan. He also reported that the proposal for appointment of M/s. H.K. Shah as Project Managers has been forwarded to the CPC for approval.  He reported that Icon has not yet sent the drawings.

 

DECISION

 

3.2       The Committee noted the up-date given by the Director ICT and advised him to send an email to M/s. Icon and copy to the EC asking Mr. Hanif Daud to send the required information.

               

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
