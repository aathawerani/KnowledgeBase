MINUTES OF THE 60th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON AUGUST 10, 2009

The 60th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 10, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat Associate Dean
2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean
3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN Registrar
4. Mr. Ali Taha Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Zafar A. Sidiqui Director CED
7. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi Director I.C.T
8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai Director H.R

The following items were discussed:

 Item No. 1      To examine proposal received from Mr. Kazim Saeed, son of Late Prof. Syed Mumtaz Saeed for establishing of a scholarship in the name of the Late Prof. Syed Mumtaz Saeed.

 

1.1       In his proposal Mr. Kazim Saeed stated that through the announcement of the IBA’s Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund on July 3, 2009, Dr. Ishrat Husain, Dean and Director of the IBA, has chosen to recognize Mr. Syed Mumtaz Saeed’s lifelong contributions to the development of high-quality executives in Pakistan in his roles as a distinguished IBA faculty member, as an author, and as a management development practitioner. Late Mr. Saeed maintained a long-standing association with the IBA as a faculty member from 1964 to 1967 and 1971 to 1977, as a member of the IBA’s Board of Governors (1974-1976), as a President of the IBA alumni association MBAians (1991-1992), and as a member of IBA’s Academic Board (2002-2008).  Mr. Kazim Saeed presented the following proposal for the IBA-Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund:

Objective:

a)      The IBA Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund’s objective shall be to support outstanding IBA students with demonstrated financial need. This shall be an endowment fund with a corpus (invested amount) of Rs.4 million. All contributions to the Fund will be invested and only the yield from investments will be used to award scholarships. This will allow the Rs.4 million to last and continue to provide funds for scholarships.

Selection:

b)      This Fund shall be administered as an organic addition to the IBA’s existing selection process for financial aid. A 3-member committee shall select beneficiaries of this Fund’s scholarships from among students proposed by the IBA Scholarship Committee. The Committee members shall be:

-          Chairman of the IBA Scholarship Committee (chair);

-          Mr. Inayat Din, veteran faculty member of the IBA, former chair of the IBA Scholarship Committee and a one-time colleague of Mr. Syed Mumtaz Saeed (as a representative of the academic community);

-          A nominee of Mr. Syed Mumtaz Saeed’s family and friends: Mr. Kazim Mumtaz Saeed, Energy Consultant to the World Bank and beneficiary of scholarships at Cornell University and Princeton University (as a representative of donors to the Fund).

Eligibility & Criteria

c)   Students of the IBA’s BBA program shall be eligible since the BBA program has the highest incidence of financially disadvantaged students among IBA programs. The selection criteria shall be two-fold: academic and financial. The academic criteria shall be:

-          For students just admitted to the IBA’s BBA program: a minimum of 3A’s in A-levels or over 75 percent marks in intermediate exams;

-          For students who have already completed one or more semester in the IBA’s BBA program: a GPA of 3.0 or above.

(The above criteria have been devised keeping in mind the weak preparation of students from financially weak backgrounds at the IBA).

The financial criteria shall be: the student’s family has an income of less than Rs.35,000 per month (about $5,000 per year) as determined by the IBA Scholarship Committee.

Fiduciary aspects and supervision:

d)         The Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund is among the first attempts of the IBA to raise funding from IBA alumni who may have a link with a former faculty member of the IBA. Many such alumni would be located abroad. To secure the confidence of such prospective donors, the IBA shall maintain a dedicated account for its Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund into which donors can make contributions. The IBA Director Finance, whose mandate includes the management of IBA’s endowments, shall maintain the Fund’s account and report periodically on its movements. The IBA Director Finance will employ judgment regarding the proportion of the Fund’s investment yield to be utilized for scholarships versus the proportion that is to be ploughed back into the Fund to counter the effects of inflation and / or falling interest rates.

 

Pledges to date:

 e)         It is imperative that the Fund’s account be established as soon as possible. Firm pledges for Rs.1.6 million have been secured within a month of Dr. Ishrat’s announcement of the Fund. An account has to be established for these pledges to be converted into actual contributions and for pledges to be sought farther afield.

           1.2       The Registrar said that the Director’s observation was that people pledge but do not commit. He said that the Director was in favour of creating an Endowment Fund rather than Scholarships Fund. 

 DECISION

 1.3              The Committee approved the proposal of Mr. Kazim Saeed for establishing of a scholarship fund in the name of the Late Prof. Syed Mumtaz Saeed along with the selection, eligibility and criteria as stated above.

 ACTION

 1.4              The Director Finance will initiate the process of opening a separate bank account in the name of “IBA-Syed Mumtaz Saeed Scholarship Fund”.

 1.5              The Registrar will intimate Mr. Kazim Saeed about the approval of his proposal by the Executive Committee.

  Item No. 2      To discuss the Draft Policy Guidelines against Sexual Harassment in Institutions of Higher Education and Learning received from HEC.

 2.1       The Registrar informed the Committee that the draft policy guidelines against sexual harassment in institutions of higher education and learning have been received from the Higher Education Commission for comments / inputs.  He said that in his opinion it is mainly based on an office environment rather than educational environment. He informed the Committee that he has suggested to the HEC that the policy should incorporate teacher student relationship and be more student specific. He said that excerpts of the draft policy guidelines received from the HEC has been included in the student handbook prepared by the IBA.

 2.2              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that a clause on Sexual Harassment should also be included in the IBA Code Book.

 DECISION

 2.3              The Committee decided that:

 a)            A clause on Sexual Harassment should be included in the students’ handbook as well as on the IBA Code Book.

b)            Penalty on Sexual Harassment should also be mentioned in the students’ handbook as well as on the IBA Code Book.

c)            Instead of oral reprimand as mentioned in the HEC draft policy, it should be documented.

 ACTION

 2.4              The Registrar will ensure that the decisions are incorporated in the students’ handbook as well as on the IBA Code Book.

  Item No. 3      To consider suggestions made by Mr. Muhammad Anwar regarding crossing the road between IBA and APWA Buildings.

 

3.1              Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Incharge, Affiliation Department of IBA in his email stated that due to shifting the City Campus offices to APWA Building, staff and students shall face many problems while crossing the Road between the two buildings. He suggested that on an urgent basis (1) Zebra Crossing Lines may be drawn between the two buildings. (2) With the help of Traffic Control Authority a sub-signal may be fixed in front of IBA and APWA and a security Guard may be assigned the duty to switch on red signal when students or staff members cross the road.

 DECISION

 3.2              The Committee requested the Registrar to liaise with the Cantonment Board for necessary arrangements.

  Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 a)       Up-date on the Pedestrian Bridge between the IBA City Campus building and the APWA building.

 

4.1              The Director Projects informed the Committee that the soil testing and the drawing for the Pedestrian Bridge have been completed.  However, the said that the APWA had some reservations with regard to the structure of the building and the use of the bridge by common public. He said that they have been assured that the structure of their building would not be disturbed and the bridge would not be used by common public. He said that they are satisfied with the assurance but they want approval of the Cantonment Board as the area does not come under the jurisdiction of the City District Government.

 4.2              The Registrar informed the Committee that he has sent a request to the Cantonment Board for approval and said that he was hopeful of getting approval from their end.

 DECISION

 4.3              Noted by the Committee.

b)       UPS requirements for IT Lab at APWA Building.

 4.4              The Director ICT said that they urgently need UPS for IT labs only at the APWA Building for uninterrupted functioning of the lab.

 4.5              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to why this requirement was not envisaged six months back

 4.6              The Registrar said that the APWA is a temporary arrangement and only most essential requirements will be provided. He said that power generator has already been arranged for the APWA Building. Now there is no need of UPS.

 4.7              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to make the labs in two classes at CCS building and shift those two classes at APWA Building.

  DECISION

 4.8              The Committee decided that the Registrar and the Director ICT will do the home work and come up with the solution to the EC.

 There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
