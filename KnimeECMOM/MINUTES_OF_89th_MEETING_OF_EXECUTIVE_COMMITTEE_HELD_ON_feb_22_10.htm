MINUTES OF THE 89TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, FEBRUARY 22, 2010

 

The 89th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, February 22, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN            Registrar

  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance

  5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                   Director Projects

  6. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                               Director CED

  7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                               Director ICT

  8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                           Director HR

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed attended the meeting by special invitation.

 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      EC decisions implementation status reports.

 

1.1              The EC decisions implementation status reports received from the following members were presented before the Executive Committee:

 

Report from Director ICT

 

1.2              Town Hall meetings on ICT and ERP: The Director ICT reported that although the presentations have been prepared, yet at the advice of the Students’ Consular it will be presented after a week as the students were busy in term assignments and final exams.

 

1.3              Presentation to Library Committee on SAKAI:  The Director ICT reported that he had a meeting with the Library Committee and discussed their requirements. The Library Committee would gather their requirements and provide them to ICT for the upgrade. Meanwhile he said that they had analyzed their LMS and found that it would be cost effective and easier to actually upgrade their LMS and then connect it to the ERP and transfer all data through the XL sheets. He said that SAKAI is not needed for Library.

 

1.4              Two laptops and one four-in-one machine for the Communication Department:  The Director ICT reported that the laptops and PC are expected to arrive by the mid of March 2010. He said that he could provide the old PC’s but they are not of good quality.

 

1.5              The Director H.R. requested to arrange a PC for the Head of Internal Audit who would be joining soon.  The Director ICT assured that he would arrange a PC for the Head of Internal Audit.

 

1.6              Written proposal from HEC for stoppage of facility in case PERN 2 is not procured from HEC: The Director ICT informed the Committee that letter from HEC has still not been received. However, he said that the HEC has informed him about a new development where they are going to provide the bandwidth at half the rates. He said that they have requested to wait till they finalize the details.  He said that Wateen will used as the primary link and the HEC could be used as the redundant backup or vice versa.

 

1.7              Signing of MoU along with TPA:  The Director ICT reported that out of 8 different vendors, M/s. Tanberg have signed the TPA and four have signed the MoU. He said that by the 15th of March others will also sign the MoU/TPA. 

 

The Director ICT reported that the delivery of IBA Server has not yet been confirmed because according to Intel, the chip will be available by the end of this quarter.  He further said that 4-CPU machine is not available but 2-CPU machine is available and they are considering of joining 2-CPU machine which should make no difference. He said that this exercise is underway to confirm that there would be no degradation on the performance if two 2-CPU machines are linked together.

 

The Director said that they are trying to get state-of-the-art stuff so why should they get 2-CPU Machine instead of 4-CPU machine.

 

The Director ICT said that availability of 4-CPU would take time as it would not be available before October this year.

 

The Director waned to know as to what would be the interim arrangement to which the Director ICT replied that they may use the 2-CPU machine as back up. He said that meanwhile he is talking to vendors to provide a 2-CPU machine for demo/test purpose. However, he said that they may have to rent the machine till they get their machines.

 

The Director showed his concern about the performance of the chip as it is taking so much time. He said that IBA should opt for tested and tried products and equipment as we cannot afford to take chances with the reliability of the system.

 

The Director ICT said that according to them it is super machine.  He said that by next month he would have a clear picture and will up-date the EC.

 

1.8       System outage and Solution: The Director ICT reported that the budget and reimbursement policy has been sent to Director H.R. by SM IT/IO for consideration and approval.

 

The Director advised the Director ICT to present the budget and reimbursement policy to the Executive Committee.

 

Commenting on power outage the Director said that there is none to switch on the generator on holidays and requested the Registrar to look into it.

 

The Director ICT suggested that the person who operates the generator should be given to ICT.

 

The Director H.R. said that the City Campus is under the administration of the Registrar. She said that if the functions are fragmented then it will not work.  She said that such work should be under the admin stream and suggested to revisit the structure of the admin.

 

The Committee decided that the Director H.R., the Controller of Examinations and the Registrar will sit together and resolve these issues.

 

1.9       The Director wanted to know as to what happened to the linkages of the telephone system of the two campuses.

 

1.10     The Director ICT replied that the telephones of the two campuses would be linked within six weeks time.

 

 

Report from Director Finance

 

1.11     The up-dated decision of the Task Force on student loan: The Director Finance reported that the Task Force has set the initial target of 10,000 students for financing at an average educational cost of Rs.50,000 per year per student which comes to Rs.500 million. To create a financing facility of Rs.500 million, a fund of Rs.5 billion is needed to be raised. He said that they have suggested various options for fund raising. They have also suggested to form a Fund called “Pakistan Education Fund” with a Board of Trustees.  He said that the proposal of the Task Force has been submitted to the Governor State Bank.  He said that after the comments from the Governor State Bank are received, they will have more clear picture about the Scheme.

 

1.12     Legal opinion on the legal standing of the MOU: The Director Finance reported that M/s. Mumtaz Associates have advised that MOU is a simple document of intent between the parties. MoU itself is not a legal document and not an enforceable document. However, MoU may be enforceable provided a separate contract / agreement is signed and the MoU becomes part of the agreement.

 

Report from the Registrar

 

1.13     Data on the uses of official calls on cell phones: The Registrar reported that he is working on it and it is in progress.

 

1.14     Maintenance Schedule:  The Registrar informed the Committee that he has prepared the periodic, regular and emergency schedule of maintenance.

 

The Director requested the Registrar to inform all concerned about the periodic, regular and emergency maintenance schedule.

 

1.15     Installation of cameras and walkthroughs:  The Registrar reported that the purchase order has been issued and by 30th March all cameras will be installed.

 

Report from Director H.R.

             

1.16     Provision for faculty capacity building activities in the budget:  The Director H.R. reported that when the work on budget commences, she would provide for the budget for faculty capacity building activities.

 

1.17     General Life Insurance Cover to all contract employees: The Director H.R. reported that they have received the quotations from different insurance companies for providing life insurance cover to the contract employees and they are in the process of comparing them.

 

1.18     Comparison between the IBA leave rules and the government leave rules:  The Director H.R. reported that they have provided all related documents and sought legal opinion from M/s. Mumtaz Associates on this issue.  She said that she would up-date the EC after the legal opinion is received.

 

Report from Controller of Examinations

 

1.19     Ms. Maheen Shaikh to explain her case in person to the Controller of Examinations: Mr. Mobin Khalili reported that a letter by the Registrar was issued and delivered to the student through the Program Office.  He stated that Ms. Maheen Shaikh failed to produce the required documents within the stipulated time period.  

 

DECISION

 

1.20     The up-dates from the respective members along with the observations were noted and approved. The Director suggested that where the actions are pending or not completed they should be reported in the next status report.

a)                  Clarification on the re-designation of program coordinators.
