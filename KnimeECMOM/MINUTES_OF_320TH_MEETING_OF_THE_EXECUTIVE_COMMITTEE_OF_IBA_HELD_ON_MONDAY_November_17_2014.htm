MINUTES OF 320TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 17, 2014

The 320th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 17, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FCS
3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
4. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects, and Mr. Shakil Anwer , Project Manager, Aman Tower attended the meeting on special invitation. The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       Up-date on Aman Tower.

 

1.1       The Director Projects said that there were two major parts in Aman Tower; (i) Core & Shell, and (ii) Interior Decoration.  He reported that the Civil & Architecture Works of Phase-I of Core & Shell were completed up-to 98% in October. He said that the Granite Fixing work on Podium columns resumed from September 6, 2014 and 25% fixing work was completed by end of October 2014.  The reason for the delay was that the granite supplied earlier was rejected and new granite had to be imported. He said that MEP Works Phase – I (Core & Shell) have been 92% executed against the target of 100%.  The Phase II of ID Works has been 70% executed against the target of 80% for October 2014. He said that the Phase III of Furniture Works will be completed by February 20, 2014 although some sets of furniture would be available by December 2014.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that there has been only 20% progress in Vertical Transport (Elevators) because Ziafco is not pursuing the contract professionally; pre-shipment inspection for elevators has not been arranged as yet as Chinese Government  declined to issue visas. He said that the Project Manager has issued notice for Liquidated Damage to M/s. Ziafco.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that the Gen set contract was in re-tendering phase because A&FC had rejected the Gen set contract. The re-tendering process will take two months. Another four months would be required for delivery and installation of gen-set.  He said that the building would not be fully functional without gen-set. 

 

1.4       The Director Projects identified the following hurdles in completion of the Project:

 

o   Slow rate of progress on external façade work due to insufficient manpower deployed by Paragon.

 

o   Delay in approval of Variation Order of diffusers is causing delay in placing order; hence will delay the false ceiling work.

 

o   Delay in approval of Stainless Steel Duct for kitchen exhaust on fifth floor is causing delay in ducting work.

 

o   Revision of light fixtures in seminar room ground floor and lift lobby will delay the delivery and installation of light fixtures.

 

o   Delay in shipment of fire water and plumbing pumps will affect the completion of HVAC system.

 

o   Delay in obtaining external connection of water, gas, and electricity will delay the electrical and plumbing works and will have its impact on building functionality.

 

o   Design of multi-media training centre is in process; half of fourth floor has been on hold since March 25, 2014.

 

1.5       The Director Projects said that due to delay in furniture, generator, elevator, and the kitchen, although the project would be completed by December 2014, the building may not be functional before March 2015.

 

1.6       The Director Projects said that the architects have changed the light fixturesin seminar room, ground floor, and lift lobby because of which there is much variance and approval has not yet been given. The approval forms part of other variation orders amounting to about 6.5% overruns approval of which is to be given by the IBA-Aman Committee which could not meet as all the members were not available in November.

 

1.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked about the cost impact. The Director Projects said that the major variation (Rs.4.5 million) was in fire alarm system because of suggested change from zoning to addressable system. He said that the estimated cost impact due to delay and the technical improvements is within the range of 6.5% which is under scrutiny by the Architects and Projects Managers.

 

1.8       The Director Projects informed that approval for water connection from KW&SB was expected in a week. For electricity he said that road cutting was involved and the K-Electric promised that as soon as approval from Cantonment Board is obtained by IBA, they would lay the cables.  He said that approval for road cutting was obtained from City Government but the Cantonment Board, through the efforts of  the  Registrar, has promised to give approval in a week or so.  He said that the water and electric work would be completed by December 2014.  For Gas line approvals for road cutting from both City Government and the Cantonment Board have been obtained by Project Department’s Engineer at the City Campus.

 

1.9       The Registrar suggested not to keep on hold half of the fourth floor because of design approval of multi-media training centre.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that for the multi-media training centre they have a grant agreement for three years.  The delay may result in lapse of grant. He requested to expedite the completion of the Centre. The Director Projects apprised the Executive Committee that all drawings and photographs were sent to the foreign consultants more than a month ago. They have recently (on November 14) sent some questions, replies of which would be sent to them on November 17.

 

DECISION

 

1.10     The Committee noted and approved the up-dates along with the suggestions noted above.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the constitution of a new ‘Claim Settlement of Committee’ for handling the case of M/s. Consult-Tech International.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that in its 312th meeting held on 12th September 2014, following decision was taken by the Executive Committee which was duly communicated to M/s. Consult-Tech International:

           

“The Committee endorsed the recommendation of the Claim Settlement     Committee and     requested the Director Projects and Mr. Jehanzeb to negotiate         with the Contractors on Rs.2.39 million.  The   EC authorized the Director     Projects to negotiate payment upto 1/3rd of their claim i.e. Rs.2.7 million.”

           

2.2       The Director Projects informed the EC that M/s. Consult-Tech International did not accept the decision and wanted to have a meeting with the ‘Claim Settlement Committee’.  The Director Projects reported that the two members out of three members of the Claim Settlement Committee had already left IBA, leaving only one member (Syed Jehanzeb) on the Committee. (Members who left IBA are Mr. Shabih Haider, Lecturer Economics and Finance and Ms. Fuaaza Qazi, HEC Engineer).

 

2.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to re-constitute the ‘Claim Settlement Committee’ so that the matter may be taken up with M/s. Consult-Tech International. He proposed that any two of the following persons may be selected in addition to Mr. S. Jehanzeb:

 

            -        Engr. Abdul Rehman, Sr. Manager Contracts (Projects Department)

            -        Capt. (Retd) Ahmed Zaheer, Registrar IBA

            -        One Faculty member

 

2.4       The Registrar showed his inability to be on the Committee. However, he said that he could nominate Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager (General Maintenance) on the Committee.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Executive Committee decided that since the recommendations of the Claim Settlement Committee were considered and approved by the EC, the Consult-Tech may meet the EC.  The Committee did not agree to the proposal of the Director Projects of re-constituting the Claim Settlement Committee for this purpose.

 

ACTION

 

2.6       The Director Projects would arrange a meeting of the Consult-Tech with the Executive Committee, if required.

 

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve the Award of Work ‘External Lighting at Main             Campus’.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that Tender for External Lighting at Main Campus was   floated in daily newspapers on September 19, 2014. The bids were invited on Single Stage two envelop bid process and the following contractor collected the Tender     Documents:

           

1.      M/s Sound Engineering

2.      M/s MS Engineering

3.      M/s Hilite Electric

4.      M/s Iqbal Engineering

5.      M/s Philips

6.      M/s Musheer and Sons

7.      M/s National Engineering

8.      M/s Future Technologies

9.      M/s MIMA

10.  M/s Electrix Traders

 

3.2       The Technical bids were opened in front of IBA officials and contractor representatives and then sent to Consultant M/s KP Associates for their scrutiny. M/s KP Associates submitted their report on October 28, 2014. The report was presented in Procurement Committee on November 01, 2014 for the permission of financial opening of the same contractors who were technically qualified. The financial bids of the following two qualified contractors were opened on November 7, 2014 after approval of Procurement Committee, in front of IBA officials and Contractor’s representatives. Following were the bid prices announced at the time of bid opening:

3.3       The financial bids were then sent to M/s KP Associates for their evaluation and recommendation of best evaluated lowest price contractor. M/s KP Associates submitted their report on November 14, 2014. As per their recommendation M/s NEC is the best     evaluated lowest price contractor.

           

3.4       The Director Projects reported that on the basis of the Consultant’s recommendation, IBA Projects Department recommended to the Procurement Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated contractor M/s NEC at a cost of Rs.16,291,610/-

           

3.5       The Director Projects reported that in its meeting held on November 15, 2014 the Procurement Committee considered the details of evaluation and after due scrutiny recommended the award to the lowest evaluated qualified contractor M/s. NEC at a cost of Rs.16,291,610/-  He informed the Committee that the Engineer’s Estimate was Rs.17 million.

           

3.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and recommend to the A&FC to approve the award of work to the successful bidder.

           

3.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked as to why only two contractors qualified.  The Director Projects replied that the technical submittals were given by the two contractors only; others did   not give the technical submittals.

           

3.8       The Registrar said that he was of the view that Philips standard would be higher than others. The Director Projects said that the component of bulb is only 5%.  He further said that they would follow the specifications given in the contract.   

 

3.9       Mr. Moeid Sultan referring to the Bid Evaluation Report wherein it has been implied that the rate quoted by NEC includes 17% GST, suggested to get it confirmed from NEC that Rs.16,291,610 is inclusive of 17% GST. (The Director Projects during the meeting obtained written confirmation from NEC that the rate quoted by them is inclusive of GST.)

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Committee recommended to the A&FC to consider and approve the Award of Work of  ‘External Lighting at Main Campus’ to the lowest evaluated qualified contractor M/s. National Engineering Co., at a cost of Rs.16,291,610/-.

 

ACTION

 

3.11     The Director Projects would present the recommendation of EC to the A&FC for consideration and approval. 

 

 

Item No.4       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)          Expeditious arrangement for making ready the route for convocation procession.

 

4.1       The Registrar showed some slides of the immediate proximity of Students Centre and the area from where the convocation procession is planned to pass.  He said that those areas needed immediate action of levelling the ground and placing pavers so that the work is completed before Convocation as there was no alternate arrangement available.

 

DECISION

 

4.2       The Committee realized the gravity and urgency of work and requested the Director Projects to get it completed before Convocation. The Director Projects assured that he would get it completed. 

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
