1.3       Commenting on the first question, the Dean & Director said that there are ten ways to establish linkages with the industry. One is to invite practitioners from industry to teach at IBA. Second is what our faculty members do i.e. invite guest speakers in their class, but it is not systematic; there is no data base. The third is the MBA project which is very well received by the industry as we are providing useful services to problem solving by the industry. The fourth is the Responsible Citizen Internship which is a compulsory internship of six weeks and some students continue to be involved in this voluntary work. Fifth is the mentoring, coaching, interviewing and students development program in which our alumni from industry have been involved. The sixth is our Centre for Executive Education which is attracting companies for customized training and also for open courses and they have a very large data base. The seventh is linkage with industry through some of our faculty members who are doing case studies. Eighth is consultancy and research in which so far we have not been able to establish sufficient linkages with the industry. He suggested that students’ projects may be included in the CBER website. Ninth is consistent interaction in form of focus groups with industry representatives which should be done electronically and manually.  He requested the Director ICT to ask Mr. Imran Batada to imbed this into the system. The tenth is the Corporate Internship.  He asked the Director H.R. to see how a tracking mechanism can be developed for all the ten approaches mentioned above.  In addition, we have a Corporate Leaders Advisory Board comprising CEOs of leading companies, the IBA Advisory Council, the Board of Trustees of the Friends of IBA Trust (FIBAT) and more recently the Advisory Board for the CED. Faculty of Computer Sciences organizes an Annual CEO Forum and the HR Department / Club hold the HR Forum annually.  These institutionalized mechanisms should be used to strengthen the linkages.    

1.4       It was suggested that faculty’s representation on the boards of various organizations would also help in establishing and strengthening linkages with industry.  The Dean & Director said that if a faculty member works on certain project which is relevant to that industry and it comes into the knowledge of that industry then the chances are there that this particular faculty may be taken on board of that industry. He said that IBA cannot do anything for this.

1.5       On the recommended actions for the second question, the Dean & Director said that it is not the Director Public Affairs alone responsible for all actions. He asked the Director H.R to mention the designation of responsible persons against every bullet point.  He said that for social media Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif, Dr. Erum Hafeez and Mr. Imran Batada are the team members.

1.6      Mr. Aman U. Saiyed suggested that our social media should depict the positive image about Karachi. The Dean & Director advised that the Citizens of Karachi should be exposed to IBA through activities such as sports, dramas, art shows etc. Ms. Maria Hasan, the Patron of Drama Society should be approached for organizing stage dramas.

1.7       On the third question, the Dean & Director asked the Associate Deans if all faculty members have been provided training. Dr. Muhammad Nishat reported that one round of training has been completed.

1.8       About the recommended action that “Return on training investment mechanism to be worked out”, Mr. Aman U. Saiyed wanted to know as to how return can be quantified.  Dr. Nishat said that improvement in quality of papers, adoption of new techniques in teaching and research etc. these may be the returns.  However, he agreed that these cannot be quantified.

1.9       On the fourth question “how to develop a research culture at IBA”, the Dean & Director said that this is a very good agenda and the Director CBER, and the two Associate Deans have to work on it.  Dr. Nishat said that if we are to strengthen CBER then we have to concentrate on contract research.

1.10     On the fifth question on “how to develop a culture of ownership built on shared values” the Dean & Director said that if each one of us practices our values then cultural change can take place very easily. He said that we talk of our values but our behaviour is totally different.

Item No. 5      To consider and approve the payment for consultancy services to FNMH in respect of Phase 2 of new Girls’ Hostel.

5.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that originally four blocks were conceived and planned for the new Girls’ Hostel. Accordingly, the Architect, M/s. FNMH completed all designing work and prepared all construction drawings and the tender documents. However, as the estimated cost was too high to be included in the budget, it was decided in a meeting held by the Dean & Director with the Consultant and the Project Manager to split the project into two phases and go for construction of phase 1 only which comprised two residential blocks, the front block and some reduced external work. He said that the lowest tendered cost on which the work has been awarded to the successful bidder and on which the Consultant’s 4% fee is payable for phase 1 is Rs.94,483,564/-  He said that since the Consultant completed all the designing wok etc. for the balance of the work also (Phase 2), he is entitled to be paid upto that stage as per our contract agreement with him. He said that the revised, reduced and agreed cost of the balance construction work is Rs.60,375,681/-       

5.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the bill from FNMH was forwarded to our Finance Department for payment to FNMH Architecture. However, the Finance Department has returned the bill with the observation that “the only cost approved by IBA is the percentage (4%) of the awarded construction cost. Any additional amount must be approved by EC and CPC”.  Accordingly, Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the EC to approve the payment to FNMH for the services they rendered as per agreement.

5.3       The Dean & Director said that whenever we go for other blocks, the design work has already been done and we will follow the design and the tender documents already prepared by the FNMH.  For this we will not have to engage an architect anew. He wanted to know on what amount the fee will be paid.

5.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that 4% fee was decided to be paid at various stages. The cost of Phase 1 is Rs.94 million and that of phase 2 is 60 million. On Rs.94 million full fee @4 percent will be paid and on Rs.60 million of phase 2, we have to pay @ 2 percent for preparing the design and tender documents for phase 2. In future when we award the contract for phase 2, we will have to pay the remaining 2% of Rs.60 million only for the supervision of the construction work.

DECISION

5.5       The Committee approved payment of full fee @4 percent of Rs.94 million to FNMH because they have prepared the design and tender documents and are providing supervision for phase 1. 

5.6       The Committee decided that for the services the Consultants have provided till date for phase 2 (preparing design and tender documents) IBA owes them 2% of Rs.60 million (cost of phase 2).  The Committee further decided that in future if we award the contract on the basis of the tender documents prepared by FNMH, and ask them (FNMH) to supervise the project, then IBA will pay only 2% of Rs.60 million to FNMH for the supervision of the construction work of phase 2. This understanding should be clearly recorded in the letter while releasing the 2% fee to FNMH.

5.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed was advised to obtain auto cad drawings of both the phases of girls’ hostel from the Consultant.
