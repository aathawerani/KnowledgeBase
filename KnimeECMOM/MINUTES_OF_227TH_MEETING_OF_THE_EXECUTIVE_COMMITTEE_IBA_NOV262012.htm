MINUTES OF OF 227TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 26, 2012



The 227th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 26, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:


1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                      Dean & Director
2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat               Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                     Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN      Registrar
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz         Director Projects
6. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                     Director ICT
7. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                 Director CED
8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                Director H.R.
9. Mr. Moeid Sultan                     Acting Director Finance


The following items were discussed:

Item No.1 To inspect and approve the seminar hall chairs for Academic Block.

1.1 The Director Projects requested to see the sample of the Seminar type chair selected by the Architects for two seminar halls of the Academic block. Accordingly, the members inspected the model chairs. The members felt that the chairs presented for inspection were not suitable for a seminar room where students will need the space to write and secondly they will be cramped up. The members then visited the seminar room of Tabba Academic Block, Main Campus where the table and chair type arrangement was put in place physically by temporarily moving them from the Aman CED seminar hall. There were five chairs behind the table.

DECISION

1.2 The Executive Committee approved the table and chair type arrangement as has been done for Aman-CED seminar hall. The Executive Committee decided to add one more row on the floor so that there may be twelve seminar desk with five seating capacity making the total capacity of (12 x 5) = 60.

1.3 The Executive Committee did not approve the chairs (Malaysian Make) shown by the Vendor as an alternate for the computer lab, MS / Ph.D office and Reception area. They emphasized that the chair should exactly be the same as mentioned in the BOQ.

Item No. 2 Presentation of the up-dated case of the additional cost of electrical infrastructure after the meeting with SMC, the Electrical Consultant of the Buildings by Mr. Khalid Pervez.

2.1 Mr. Khalid Pervez of KP Associates informed the Committee that a meeting was held with the Electrical Consultants SMC in the office of Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia wherein it was confirmed that the total increase in loads was 216 kW. It was also noted that the NBCL’s Consultants had used 74% Diversity Factors for Academic Block, 71% for AMAN-CED, and 81% for Students Centre.

2.2 Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the Consultants were of the opinion that these factors could be reduced only if IBA guarantees that there would never be full occupancy of the respective areas and facilities. It thus transpired that no reduction in building loads was possible. He said that he had applied 15% cushion for additional future needs of the buildings. However, the same was removed in line with the agreement reached in the EC meeting held on November 12, 2012. He reported that together with the above working an increase of 64 kW in the load of new Administration Building was also noted. He said that although the consultant had recommended 20% cushion for future needs, he (KPA) has curtailed it to 10%. This required increase in cable capacity which almost negated the cost reduction in cables which was achieved from elimination of future cushions of the three buildings of NBCL’s scope. He said that the following was the outcome as far as material cost of cables was concerned:

• Previous Cost of Cables: Rs. 23,231,295/-
• Revised Cost of Cables: Rs. 22,827,790/-
• Decrease in Cost of Cables: Rs. 403,505/-.

2.3 Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the overall reduction in the cost would be as under:

• Previous Total Cost of Works: Rs. 123,334,906/-.
• Revised Total Cost of Works: Rs. 122,931,401/-.

2.4 He said that if IBA gets 22% discount on cables, the revised total cost of work shall be Rs.117,909,287/-. He said that his professional opinion is that no further room of cost reduction is possible in the way of providing a safe and sound infrastructure network capable of serving this prestigious institution over the next many decades.

2.5 The Registrar suggested to provide the UPS at the Lab only and remove other UPS’s from offices of Academic Block so that the electrical load is further reduced. The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what would be the impact on the load factor as well as on cost saving if the number of UPS is reduced from four to one. He said that even in Students Centre UPS is not needed except one for the lab. He clarified that IBA has reached an understanding with KESC that there would be zero load shedding at IBA. He said that UPS would be needed in worst case scenario. The Registrar said that there are 18 offices in the Academic Block; most of these offices would be occupied by the faculty and they would be working on their own laptops so no UPS would be required for their offices. He said that at Aman-CED Building the offices have temporarily been shifted which will go back to the Admin Building. The Dean & Director said that at Admin Block we should have provision for full load but for other buildings the load factor is on the higher side as compared to the actual use; at Students Centre 81% load factor is high and could be brought down to 75% - 70%. He said that if order has been issued for the supply of UPS then it has to be cancelled so that the cost of UPS is saved.

DECISION

2.6 The Committee noted and approved the Revised Total Cost of Works as Rs.122,931,401/- with a provision of further reduction to Rs.117,909,287/- after discount on cables to be obtained from Pakistan Cables.

ACTION

2.7 The Committee decided that the Director Projects, in consultation with the Director ICT would cancel the Purchase Order for the UPS except for the labs.

2.8 The Director Projects would present the revised total cost of work to the A&FC as a reporting item.

Item No. 3 To inspect the mock-up rooms of the faculty offices at the third floor of Admin. Building.

3.1 The Committee inspected the mock-up rooms prepared for the faculty at the third floor of Admin. Building. The Dean & Director suggested that the size of the faculty offices should be the same as that of Aman-CED building.

3.2 The Committee enquired about the provision of shelves on the wall, to which the Architect assured that he has designed the selves in each room. The Committee was apprised that there are two sizes of faculty rooms available in the Admin Block. One size is almost equal to the room sizes of Aman CED which can accommodate two faculty members and also the same size rooms which are designated for single senior faculty members. The other smaller rooms are for one faculty member in each room.

DECISION

3.3 The Committee showed its satisfaction over the quality and design of mock-up rooms.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
