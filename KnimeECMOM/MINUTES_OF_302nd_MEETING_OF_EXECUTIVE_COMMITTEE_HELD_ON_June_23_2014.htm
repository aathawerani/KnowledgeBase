MINUTES OF 302ND MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, JUNE 23, 2014

The 302nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 23, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr. Shakeel Khoja Acting Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Syed Jehanzeb (nominee of Mr. Moeid Sultan)  

Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui, and Dr. M. Shahid Qureshi, Program Director CED attended the meeting on special invitation.

Before considering the agenda items of the meeting, the Executive Committee offered Fateha for Dr. Matin A. Khan, Professor Emeritus who expired on Sunday, June 22, 2014. The Committee offered heart-felt condolences to the bereaved family.


The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 Up-dated status of cost of construction of Clinic and solicitation of advice for further action.

1.1 The Director Projects stated that in its meeting held on 13th June 2014, the matter pertaining to the award of the contract for construction of Clinic to the lowest bidder at a cost of Rs.18,739,734/- was duly approved by the Procurement Committee as well as the Executive Committee. However, the Audit and Finance Committee in its meeting held on June 12, 2014 observed that the cost of construction is too high and advised the Director Projects to get the design further reviewed by the architects to bring down the cost of construction. He reported that the following is the status of the value engineering already done even after the bidding, for which revised bid process was completed.

Original Engineer’s Estimate                                                     Rs.25,838,135

Original Lowest Bid (M/s. Badar Engineering Works)                      Rs.24,413,027
(Bid opened on April 19, 2014)

Engineer’s Estimate after Value Engineering                                Rs.18,284,061

Lowest Bid (M/s. Hascon Engineering Services)                           Rs.18,739,734
(Revised Bid opened on May 24, 2014)

1.2 The Director Projects said that the Architects M/s NBCL were advised to review design and further reduce the cost. However, according to their advice they can only reduce the cost by Rs.0.8 million to Rs.0.9 million by revising the structure for a single story building instead of Ground +1. Also the building being at an important location it may not be feasible to compromise on its façade elements which will disturb the overall ambiance. He reported that on June 21 he talked to Mr. Zayed Bilgrami and convinced him to change the design so that it should be made simple. He said that NBCL will charge for the redesigning. He requested the Executive Committee to consider all these aspects and advise further course of action.

1.3 The Dean & Director said that for a small clinic we would not require Architect, he advised the Director Projects to get it designed by Ms. Sana Tanveer. However, the Director Projects reported that Ms. Sana Tanveer is not available after June 30, 2014. The Dean & Director suggested to reduce the size of the hall and the number of beds from 4 to two and accordingly give a new tender document to those pre-qualified bidders who had participated earlier.

1.4 The Registrar suggested that on the first floor of the existing cafeteria of staff there is a huge place on which doctor can sit. However, the Director Projects said that patients will have difficulty in getting to the first floor. The Registrar said that one large open space on the ground floor and one large open space on the first floor with one toilet should be enough. The Director Projects said that the lay out will be changed and for redesigning they have to pay some fee to NBCL. The Dean & Director said that NBCL have done so many projects of IBA. For this small work they should not demand any fee. The Director Projects said that he would talk to Mr. Zayd and would try to get the project completed within Rs.15 million (the amount committed by the donor).

DECISION & ACTION

1.5 The Director Projects in consultation with the Registrar would modify the tender documents so that the project is completed within Rs.15 million.


Item No. 2 To consider and approve IBA-CED and Deutsche Bank Greenhouse Initiative – Land Requirement.

2.1 Dr. Shahid Qureshi stated that as part of IBA CED’s Entrepreneurship Initiative two students Yousuf Motiwala (04278) and Ibrahim Petiwala (04247) of BBA- Entrepreneurship of the 6th Semester are working on a green house project using hydroponic method of farming. Hydroponics is a modern farming method where plants are grown without soil. This method has many benefits as compared to traditional means as both product quality and quantity are far better as compared to that from the traditional method; as well as need for a fertile land is eliminated so practically this method can be carried out on any piece of land. This method is already very popular in the developed counties specifically Australia and USA.

2.2 Dr. Shahid Qureshi reported that the students intend to start by growing tomatoes and chillies in the greenhouse and for this they have through IBA-CED already collaborated with the Deutsche Bank-Pakistan. This project has far-reaching benefits not just in terms of providing an entrepreneurship opportunity to the students of IBA but also the learning achieved through this project can be disseminated to the society at large as well, for the benefit for all. This is something that Deutsche Bank is also interested in. The students plan to start on 8000 square yards piece of land at North Nazimabad for which EC’s approval is requested.

2.3 The Registrar said that the concerned plot is about 8459 sq yds in size, it has a grilled perimeter and was acquired by IBA in late 90’s for purpose of developing a skill development center/classes. Presently, it is lying unused and has two IBA guards performing duty on 24 hour basis. He said that initially the students had asked for a place in the staff town but they were told that that was not possible (because of the past experience) and were offered to work at the plot in Boys’ Hostel. He said that behind the existing block they can provide 400 yards but the problem is that there is a mountain of soakage pit earth which cannot be used and has to be removed. Alternatively he had suggested the Nazimabad Plot which was given for skill development centre. The Dean & Director said that projects such as hydroponics are also a part of skill development. He said that there are many ways of entrepreneurship and skill development for Entrepreneurship courses. It is not confined to classroom teaching only. He said that we can have a classroom or couple of classrooms and start outreach programs for the intermediate students of government colleges of surrounding areas such as New Karachi, North Karachi, F.B Area, Nazimabad etc. He said that IBA can also start summer program for the catchment areas – students of Government schools, and colleges can be provided training there.

2.4 Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that the duration of the project of the students is of one year. He said that from next summer they can provide orientation course for students of those areas. Mr. Jahanzeb said that the plot is covered with bushes which have to be cleaned. The Registrar said that they would provide the space; cleaning and other related work will be the responsibility of the project sponsor. He said that a proper agreement will have to be signed with the sponsor.

DECISION & ACTION

2.5 The Committee approved the proposal of greenhouse project to be started at North Nazimabad Plot of IBA.

2.6 Dr. Shahid Qureshi, the Registrar and the Director Projects would sort out the details and requirements for classroom, toilet, furniture etc. for summer orientation to be started next year.

2.7 Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui was requested to find a donor for the classroom, toilets and other related facilities.

Item No.5 Additional requirements – Badminton court at City Campus.

5.1 The Director Projects reported that in accordance with the EC decision to see the possibility of converting portion of the existing cafeteria at City Campus into a badminton court by raising the roof of the required portion, he sought advice from Mr. Noshad of M/s. Icon. Mr. Noshad reviewed the drawing of existing facility and compared it with the requirement of a badminton court. His recommendation is as follows:

Size of court:
As per standard court size which is usually 20’-0”X 44’-0” and a minimum 5’-0” clearance on all side is required. Which makes it 30’-0” X 54’-0” whereas the required clear height must be min 15’-0” is required.

Here in the existing canteen the space is 24’-0” X 46’-0” and height varies from 10’-0” to 14’-0”. Therefore it is impossible to convert this space into badminton court by only raising the height of the building as the area is less to the usual size.

5.2 Mr. Noshad has suggested that this space should be used for other indoor games like board games, table tennis, snooker, fitness club etc.

5.3 The Dean & Director said that there are many other indoor sports facilities available at the Campus. Dr. Shakeel Khoja suggested to put some fitness / body building equipment such as weight lifting, bench press etc.

DECISION

5.4 The Committee noted the advice received from Mr. Noshad of Icon and decided that the portion of the existing canteen at City Campus would be equipped with some fitness / body building equipment.


Item No.6 To consider and approve the time extension for supply and installation of passenger elevators for Administration Building at Main Campus and FCS Building at City Campus.

6.1 The Director Projects reported that the contract for Supply and Installation of Passenger Elevators for Admin Building Main Campus and FCS Building City Campus was awarded to M/s. Reliance Engineering Services at a cost of Rs.7,070,000/- The project was awarded on January 27, 2014 and it was to be completed on May 31, 2014 i.e. in 120 days. However, due to the following reasons the project could not be completed on time and an approval for extension of 60 days i.e. upto July 31, 2014 is requested:

o Chinese New Year holidays for the month of February.
o Delay in pre-shipment inspection due to visa processing from embassy.
o Non availability of elevator shaft for layout works

6.2 The Director Projects said that based on the above facts for the major reasons for delay the Projects Department recommended the extension of time as stated above. He informed the Committee that in its meeting held on June 21, 2014 the Procurement Committee recommended the approval of extension in time up till July 31, 2014. Based on the recommendations of Project Department and Procurement Committee he requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till July 31, 2014 for both the buildings.

6.3 The Director Projects clarified that this extension of time shall be without reimbursement of the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall extend all insurances and guarantees, related to the Contract, accordingly. He further said that these Projects are done in house and there is no cost involvement of Architects/Consultants and Project Manager’s fees. To a question he said that the elevators have been received at IBA.

DECISION

6.4 The Committee approved the time extension till July 31, 2014 for supply and installation of passenger elevators for Administration Building at Main Campus and FCS Building at City Campus.


There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
