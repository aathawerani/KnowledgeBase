Item No.3       To consider and approve engaging NESPAK for designing and supervising the construction of the waste water disposal system for the boys’ hostel and staff colony.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects said that in view of the urgency of the two disposal systems, especially the Boys’ Hostel’s, which must be ready and in operation before the students move in, in August/September this year. He further said that the in-house expertise and resources with NESPAK for the job are incomparable with those of any other consultants who normally depend upon their external associates. For these reasons he requested the EC to approve engaging NESPAK for designing and supervising the construction of the disposal systems for the two locations at a lump sum fee of Rs. 500,000/-. He said that NESPAK is a government organization and, as such, engaging them in an emergency will not be a violation of any rules and regulations. He said that he consulted the Head of Internal Audit who advised to get it approved by CPC too.

 

3.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed informed the Committee that NESPAK has also asked for a direct cost of Rs.150,000/- for Soil Percolation Tests and Topographical Survey and requested the EC to consider and approve it.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to get the fees reduced as much as possible.

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee approved the proposal for engaging NESPAK for designing and supervising the construction of the waste water disposal system for the boys’ hostel and staff colony.  The Committee also approved the Soil Percolation Tests and Topographical Survey by M/s. NESPAK.

Item No.4       To consider and approve second level basement below MJS Auditorium at IBA City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects said that in the 196th Meeting of the Executive Committee held on March 26, 2012, a question was raised regarding the possibility of providing a 2nd level basement below the upcoming projects of Aman Tower & MJS Auditorium at IBA City Campus (although not recorded in Minutes). He reported that the Project Manager appointed for Aman Tower Project i.e. M/s. Halcrow, communicated about the technical non-viability of providing an additional level basement below Aman Tower. According to them due to satisfactory ongoing progress in the basement work of Aman Tower, any change in plan would entail extra cost and extra time on a substantial basis.  He said that this opinion was also ratified in a meeting held amongst the IBA team, representatives of MJS Foundation, project managers of Aman Tower and Architects of MJS Auditorium, convened at IBA City Campus on March 27, 2012. However, it was decided that as the contractor for Auditorium is not mobilized yet, possibility of a 2nd level basement be considered below the 1st level for Auditorium only.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that a tentative engineering estimate was worked out by consultants & project managers for providing an additional level of basement below Auditorium on the basis of existing soil conditions below ground and it amounted to Rs.99 million.

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that the preliminary estimated cost of “No Man’s Land”, a 24 ft strip between Auditorium and Tower, starting from basement to Ground Level, and currently under no one’s purview, has also been evaluated and it amounts to Rs.9.9 million. In the meeting held on March 27, 2012, it was agreed by the MJS Foundation that 36% of the cost would be borne by them and remaining 64% should be shared by the IBA / IBA Aman Committee. He said that this percentage ratio was initially proposed by M/s EA Consulting in a presentation before the Dean & Director of IBA on August 30, 2011, for sharing of combined basement, which was the case then.  However, a 2nd opinion for the requirement of strong secant piling below auditorium for 2nd basement can be obtained by carrying out a geo-technical investigation. It would then definitely be a probability that in case of much smaller size of structure of auditorium than the tower, the provision of secant piling is avoided which would result in drastic reduction of cost for the 2nd level basement below auditorium. If the secant piling, which was recommended for Aman Tower in case of two basements, can be averted for auditorium without compromising on the stability of underground structure, the approx. estimate would come down from Rs.99 million to Rs.65 million. He further said that to confirm about the same, a sub-soil/geo-technical investigation would have to be executed, the tentative cost for which would be around Rs. 150,000/-.

 

4.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to permit subsoil investigation below Auditorium so as to search for optimization of the cost of 2nd level basement.

 

4.5       The Dean & Director said that we do not have money and MJS Foundation has already paid Rs.140 million for the Auditorium.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee dropped the second basement plan.

 

4.7      The Committee approved the estimated cost of the area “no man’s land “in between Aman Tower and the Auditorium at Rs.9.9 million and its sharing ratio as 36% and 64 % to be borne by MJS Foundation and IBA respectively.

Item No.6       Presentation on RFID.

 

6.1       The Director ICT made a detailed presentation on Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) – a system that transmits the identity in the form of a unique serial number of an object or person wirelessly, using radio waves. He briefed EC on the uses of RFID, its basic operation, types of RFID tags (Active RFID / Passive RFID), Access Control System etc. He also presented details of total estimated cost of this project which is around Rs.2 million. The break-up of the total estimated cost is as under:

           

o   Software                     Rs.   340,000/-

o   Equipment                   Rs.1,065,000/-

o   Consumable Cost        Rs.   595,000/-

 

6.2       The Registrar suggested that we may initiate the RFID system as a test case at our Library.

 

6.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that RFID may be incorporated with ERP.

 

DECISION

Item No. 9      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Procurement of 12 licences of VMware vSphere 5.0 to implement disaster recovery system and to prevent data lost mishaps in future.

 

9.1       The Director ICT reported that a tender for servers and services was floated in the year 2010 to meet the IBA requirements for the deployment of ERP and ICT services. The server consolidation was a part of the project to save energy power and save physical space. VMware vSphere 4.0 was part of virtualization for servers and services and 28 licenses were procured in year 2010 for servers and services tender of which was awarded to INBOX Pvt. Ltd.  He said that the Disaster Recovery site construction was expectedly late therefore to reduce the support and operational cost they did not procure licenses and deployment cost for Disaster Recovery (DR) Site. He informed the Committee that the basic infrastructure for temporary DR site setup including UPS and passive cabling work at Main Campus is almost completed and will be operational within two months. We will gradually move our ICT center from Admin Block, third floor to the Ground Floor of MP building. The storage system, servers and services deployment at primary site is also in the final phase therefore it has become essential to make operational the DR site to plan and recover from any expected disaster in the future.  He said that the DR site would require 12 licenses for VMware vSphere system for server virtualization.

 

9.2       The Director ICT said that in the year 2010, VMware vSphere 4.0 was the latest version and now VMware is dealing with the newer version vSphere 5.0 which was launched in the last quarter of 2011. He requested the Executive Committee to give conceptual approval against the variation of procurement for 12 licenses of VMware vSphere 5.0 to implement disaster recovery system and to prevent data lost mishaps in future. The total cost of procurement and Support charges is US$69,669.60. The break-up is as under:

 

·         Procurement Charges   $ 33,171.36

·         Support Charges           $ 36,498.24 (patches, new version, support and updates)

·         Total:                             $ 69,669.60

 

9.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know if it has been provided for in the budget.  The Director ICT said that he would check it.

 

DECISION

 

9.4       The Committee accorded conceptual approval for the procurement of 12 licences along with its support.

 

ACTION

 

9.5       The Director ICT would follow the procurement procedure and present the proposal to TEC and CPC.
