Item No. 2      Observations from Dr. Kristiana Raube, of Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley, on her visit to IBA from February 20 – 24, 2011:

 

2.1       The Committee considered the following observations and suggestions made by Dr. Kristiana Raube of Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley who visited IBA from February 20 – 24, 2011:

 

·         Research Workshop - To create a “research culture” among the faculty, IBA should implement a weekly or bi-weekly faculty research workshop. During each workshop, a designated faculty member should lead the session and present their work in progress.  Discussion should be encouraged, which will help to foster an intellectual, research-oriented environment.

 

·         Business Cases – Business leaders were enthusiastic about the development of cases describing Pakistani business issues. They had specific suggestions for cases that focused on either business successes or failures, and were willing to open their organization to faculty research.  This should be pursued vigorously and quickly – not only will it create closer relationships with local business leaders, it also will result in better, less theoretical teaching.

 

·         Case E-Journal – Currently there is discussion among the IBA leadership to create an electronic journal of local business cases. This will provide a locus for faculty publications, as well as a source for real-world, local business cases for teaching. To assure a high quality journal, Dr. Ishrat should explore whether the International Advisory Board convened by GBSN would be willing to act as reviewers.

 

·         Experiential Learning – Alumni and business leaders are clamouring for opportunities to collaborate with IBA and students want to put their knowledge into practice.  This presents a perfect opportunity to implement more experiential learning.  With the implementation of the MBA Project this year, IBA has a good start, however, there are many more potential applications. For example, at both the MBA and BBA levels -- increased use of class projects, more class discussion and less lecturing, a longer internship requirement, and more guest lectures from local or international business leaders.

 

·         Marketing of IBA – During the meetings with alumni and business leaders, it was clear that many held outdated views of IBA and were not aware of the many new initiatives underway at the school.  To change the view of the school – and increase its prominence in Pakistan and the region – a concerted marketing effort should be implemented.

 

·         Review of BBA and MBA Changes– Given the inability of students to directly pursue the MBA (which most students had done in the past), IBA should monitor whether the BBA graduates are able to find meaningful employment. In addition, there has been a marked decrease in the number of women pursuing the MBA, which may be due to the new 2-year work requirement.  Since these changes are very new, the difficulties may resolve themselves. Nonetheless, IBA should commit to reviewing the changes annually to monitor their impact.

 

·         Career Counseling – With many new BBA graduates now seeking employment, IBA will need to market its students and work with local employers to assure they understand the BBA students’ capabilities.  In addition, more career counseling should be provided to the students pursuing the BS in computer science.   

 

2.2       Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that they hold Research Seminar but generally it is attended by the Ph.D students.  Outsiders are not allowed. 

 

2.3       Dr. Ishrat Husain suggested to invite faculty and Ph.D students from institutions like NED and AERC.  He suggested to prepare a list of interested faculty and they should be invited to Research workshops / seminars.

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee noted the observations and suggestions and expressed its pleasure over the concluding remarks of Dr. Kristiana in which she wrote, “IBA is in an excellent position with many plans underway that will move it in a positive direction for its students and faculty.”

Other academic matters included in the report should be discussed at the Monthly coordination meeting.
