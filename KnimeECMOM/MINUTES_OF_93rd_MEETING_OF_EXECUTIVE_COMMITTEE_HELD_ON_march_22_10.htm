  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider proposal from Director HEC Projects for Tree Plantation at Main Campus.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on March 8, 2010 approved a proposal for large-scale plantation of decorative and shady trees on the peripheries of Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel and the Staff Town and asked him to present the selection of trees and their plantation cost before the EC for approval. Accordingly, he presented the following details for consideration by the Executive Committee:

 

·         Number of trees to be planted      approx. 500

 

·         Decorative and shady trees           Amaltas, Gul Mohr, Bottle Palm, Neem, recommended                          Imlee, Pipal and colorful but thorny

                                                            Bougain Valleas climbing up on to the

                                                            boundary walls for safety and security.

 

·         Cost of the plants @ an average Rs.200/- each                Rs.100,000

                 

·         Digging of 3′x3′x3′ holes, @ an average Rs.350/- each        175,000

    

·         35 sweet earth dumpers @ Rs.4,500/- each                           157,000

                       

·         15 trucks of manure (cow dung) @Rs.3,500/- each                52,500

                 

·         Estimated labour cost for planting the trees                            20,000

                       

·         Initial watering by water tankers for the first six                  240,000

                  months, once every week, 120 tankers, @ Rs.2,000 each

                                                           

·         Transportation, Miscellaneous, etc.                                         25,000

 


 

                                                Total estimated cost    :                   Rs.  770,000      

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that his assumption is that all labour work, including digging of holes will be done by daily wagers who will be paid cash and that all purchases will be done piecemeal; each purchase not to amount to more than Rs.5,000/-.

 

1.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve the proposal and the estimated cost involved if it is adjudged as feasible and practicable.

 

DECISION

 

1.4              The Committee decided that initially a total of 200 trees would be planted out of which 50 trees would be planted by the alumni through Mr. Zafar Siddiqui in the next three to four months. The remaining 150 trees would be planted in the periphery through Mr. Shameem Ahmed.

 

1.5              The Committee also decided that treated water would be used for watering these plants.  

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider a request from Mr. Rais Ahmad Khan, Assistant Admin. Officer for including Radiotherapy in the agreement on panel hospitals and to allocate fund for dangerous diseases.

 

2.1       Mr. Rais Ahmed in his email stated that on 22nd January 2010 his wife was operated at Liaquat National Hospital Karachi due to acute Ca Breast. The Oncologist of LNH referred her for radiotherapy and advised that the hospitals were providing the facility for radiation:

 

·         Aga Khan Hospital & Medical University  (on IBA Panel)

·         Dr. Ziauddin Cancer Hospital & Medical University  (on IBA Panel)

·         Kiran Hospital Karachi (no IBA Panel)

 

2.2       Mr. Rais stated that he visited the Aga Khan and Dr. Ziauddin Cancer Hospital for the process of treatment as well as the expenses. The Aga Khan Hospital and Dr. Ziauddin Hospital informed him that approximate expenses would be Rs.200,000 to Rs.300,000 based on cycle of radiation (including CT scan + Planning + consultation + lab test) which he said was out of his capacity to pay. Consequently, he said that he met the Manager HR for approval of this huge amount   But the Manager H.R. informed him that radiation was not included in the agreement of LNH so it could not be paid / reimbursed to him by the IBA. The Manager H.R. advised him to arrange it by himself which was shocking for him as his take home salary is about Rs.23,000 per month.   

 

2.3       Mr. Rais requested that radiotherapy may be included in the agreement on panel hospitals so that treatment of the cancer could be completed in time. He also suggested that a separate fund may be allocated for these dangerous diseases if required immediately by a needy staff member. 

 

2.4       The Director suggested that there should be an insurance cover for such catastrophic diseases.  He asked the Director H.R. to talk to EFU, SLIC and NJI for coverage for catastrophic risk and bring the proposal to the Executive Committee.  

DECISION

 

2.5              The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Director for providing insurance cover for catastrophic risks.

 

ACTION

 

2.6              The Director H.R. would obtain proposals from EFU, SLIC and NJI for providing insurance coverage for catastrophic risks and present it to the EC for consideration.

 

 

Item No. 3      Results of “Voice of IBA”.

 

3.1       The Director H.R. while presenting the result of “Voice of IBA” stated that the completion of Voice of IBA 2010 was a fulfilment of one of the commitments made by IBA to its faculty and staff to increase engagement and participation of faculty and staff in issues related to the well being of employees and IBA’s interest. She said that this was the first time that such an exercise was formally conducted and it was conducted on an anonymous basis to ensure full confidentiality of all participating employees so that a true reflection of all employees is captured towards making IBA a top-tier institute. She said that the survey offers an opportunity to provide honest, open and confidential opinions about the Institute as a whole, its departments, and other various aspects related to work. She further said that the survey has been confidentially conducted and tabulated by an independent specialist H.R. Firm Access Consulting which undertook the assignment on a pro bono basis as part of its commitment to support IBA. She said that the survey results reflect all opinions, both positive and negative and the findings are presented with a view to bringing about positive change to the Institute.  She stated that a major limitation of the survey was that no comparative data was available from previous years nor any minimum standards were defined to make this assessment more comparable. However, she said that these findings can serve as a baseline for further comparison for future.

 

3.2              The Director H.R. said that the objectives of the Voice of IBA 2010 were to:

 

·         Document where employees feel positively about working at IBA as well as where they might not;

·         Identify which gaps cause any dissatisfaction – if any;

·         Prioritize areas for improvement;

·         Assess, overall, the current level of motivation;

·         Make the Institution a better place to work.

 

3.3              Along with other details the Director H.R. presented department-wise findings on various dimensions and sub-dimensions of opinions.

 

3.4              The Director asked the Director H.R. to share the findings systematically in faculty as well as management meetings and requested the Director H.R. to arrange meetings and share these findings and to document the feed back received in the meetings.  He said that for all negative responses they have to develop action plan (remedial) otherwise there would be a credibility gap. 

 

3.5              The Director also asked the Director H.R. to focus on the areas which are weak i.e. Policy, Pay and Reward, Work Environment and Role Clarity for faculty and Training & Development and Employment Security and other dimensions for other departments.  He concluded that the responses by the faculty were more guarded and cautious compared to those by the non-faculty.

 

3.6              The Director said that in future there should be only two major classifications of respondents; (a) faculty and (2) non-faculty so that the identity of the department is not disclosed and the respondents may feel comfortable in responding to the questions. He also suggested that the x axis of the bar chart should begin from zero instead of various positive numbers. He also asked the Director H.R. to have a survey of visiting and part-time faculty which should be more focused.

 

3.7              The Director Projects suggested that in order to increase employees’ satisfaction, IBA should offer outpatient reimbursement facility and loans for cars and motorcycles.

 

DECISION

 

3.8              The Committee thanked the Director H.R. for getting the survey done.

 

3.9              The Committee requested the Director H.R. to make an action plan with time lines based on the feedback and to widely disseminate the results of the survey and post them on the portal.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve strategy for marketing of BBA Graduates.

        

4.1              The Director H.R. said that the objective of the proposed strategy is to introduce BBA graduates who have completed 16 years of education and are prepared to join the Corporate World. She presented the following Action Plan for the marketing of BBA Graduates:

 

·         Distribution of Announcement Letter to 1000 employer data base

·         Individual visits by IBA top management

·         Involvement of Visiting Faculty

·         Involvement of IBA Alumnus

·         Touch Points 

 

4.2              The Director H.R. said that visits of five different organizations randomly selected have been assigned to each senior manager of IBA.  She further said that the Career Development Centre would request visiting faculty members and IBA Alumni to explore their networks and come up with just one feasible opportunity for BBA graduates through personal contacts, friends, colleagues, past employers etc.

 

DECISION

4.3              The Committee decided that the individual managers would decide about the organizations they would like to visit instead of assigning the organizations by CDC.

 

4.4              It was also decided that the Director would write to visiting faculty and alumni about the availability and marketing of BBA graduates. 

 

 

Item No. 5      Approval for Areas and Budget for Students’ Centre at IBA Main Campus.

 

5.1       The Director Projects reported that Request for Proposals (RFP’s) were issued to the six (06) Pre-Qualified firms of Architects on 25th June, 2009 for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of Student Centre at IBA Main Campus. The designs received from Architects on 5th December, 2009 were sent to Consulting Architect M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates for technical scrutiny on 9th December, 2009. The Consulting Architect M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates submitted their report on 12th January, 2010 after detailed scrutiny of designs. The report from Consulting Architect was discussed in the meeting of Technical Evaluation Committee on 25th January, 2010. The EC members had seen the designs submitted by the five (05) Architects and also the ‘Evaluation Report’ submitted by the Consulting Architect of IBA i.e. M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates. The Executive Committee had approved the top three (03) designs. The Technical Evaluation Committee also reviewed the Evaluation Report submitted by M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates. All the members who were present concurred with the recommendations of M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates and approved the best design as per their recommendations.

 

5.2       The Director Projects further reported that in continuation to the meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee dated 25th January, 2010, another meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee for the opening of Financial Proposals was held on 1st February, 2010.

 

5.3       The Director Projects stated that M/s. NBCL voluntarily submitted the revised rate of 4.0% of the total cost of the Project based on their existing rates of other buildings like Centre for Entrepreneurship Development, New Academic Block, New Boys Hostel and Visiting Faculty Hostel, IBA Main Campus. He said that as per the Comparative Statement, M/s. NBCL had a combined valuation of 92 points and stood 1st Rank. The Central Purchase Committee (CPC) approved M/s. NBCL at 4.0% as a Consulting Architect for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of Student Centre at IBA Main Campus. The Audit and Finance Committee (A&FC) also approved M/s. NBCL at 4.0% as a Consulting Architect for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of Student Centre at IBA Main Campus on 3rd March, 2010.

 

5.4              The Director Projects informed the Committee that as per the budget proposed, the estimated cost of the Project as of October, 2008 was taken as Rs.92.30 million including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure (excluding ICT equipments). The estimate was based on an area of 26,000 sq-ft @ Rs.3,150 per sq-ft for Civil, MEP & HVAC Works. However, he said that M/s. NBCL has given the estimated cost of construction of Rs.156.3 million @ Rs.4,115 per sq-ft for Civil, MEP & HVAC works for an area of around 38,000 sq-ft. According to NBCL, all essential requirements have been incorporated in the planning specially the amphitheatre capacity has been kept to accommodate 540 students. Their total cost comes to Rs.166.8 million including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure and their estimate seems to be on higher side.

 

5.5              Keeping in view inflation trend w.r.t 2008, the Director Projects said that the estimated cost shall be Rs.3,750 per sq-ft. As such cost of the Students’ Centre shall be based at Rs.153 million, if the estimated cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure is taken as the same as provided in the budget (excluding the cost of ICT equipment).  He requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the increase in area from 26,000 sq-ft to 38,000 sq-ft and the increase in estimated cost from Rs.92.3 million (including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure) to Rs.153 million (excluding the cost of ICT equipment).

 

DECISION

 

5.6              The Director said that he is not in favour of increasing the area and exceeding the budget. He asked the Director Projects to review the plan and resubmit before the Executive Committee providing justification for increase in the area. The Committee agreed with the Director.

 

ACTION

 

5.7              The Director Projects would review the plan and resubmit the proposal before the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 6      Approval of areas and budget for New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus.

 

6.1              The Director Projects reported that Request for Proposals (RFP’s) were issued to the six (06) Pre-Qualified firms of Architects on 19th May, 2009 for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus. The designs received from Architects on 7th July, 2009 were sent to Consulting Architect M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates for technical scrutiny on 13th July, 2009. The Consulting Architect M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates submitted their report on 28th July, 2009 after detail scrutiny of designs and then the report was sent to Technical Evaluation Committee. The members of the Executive Committee had also seen the designs submitted by the five (05) Architects and also the ‘Evaluation Report’ submitted by the Consulting Architect of IBA i.e. M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates. The Executive Committee had approved the top three (03) designs.

 

6.2              The Director Projects stated that the Technical Evaluation Committee in its meeting held on 10th August, 2009 reviewed the Evaluation Report submitted by M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates and all the members who were present concurred with the recommendations of M/s. Shahab Ghani & Associates and approved the best design as per their recommendations. In continuation to the meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee dated 10th August, 2009, another meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee for the opening of Financial Proposals was held on 26th August, 2009. M/s. NBCL voluntarily submitted the revised rate of 4.0% of the total cost of the Project based on their existing rates of other buildings of IBA Main Campus. He said that as per the Comparative Statement, M/s. NBCL had a combined valuation of 90 points and stood 1st Rank.

 

6.3              The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee (CPC) approved M/s. NBCL at 4.0% as a Consulting Architect for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus. The Audit and Finance Committee (A&FC) also approved M/s. NBCL at 4.0% as a Consulting Architect for the detailed Planning, Designing & Top Supervision of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus on 2nd October, 2009.

 

6.4              The Director Projects informed the Committee that as per the budget proposed, the estimated cost of the Project as of October, 2008 was taken as Rs.185 million including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure (excluding ICT equipments). The estimate was based on an area of 46,500 sq-ft @ Rs.3,500 per sq-ft for Civil, MEP & HVAC Works. He said that M/s. NBCL have given the estimated cost of construction of Rs.232.57 million @ Rs.4,115 per sq-ft for Civil, MEP & HVAC works for an area of around 56,519 sq-ft. According to NBCL, all essential requirements have been incorporated in the planning. Their total cost comes to Rs.254.57 million including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure and their estimate seems to be on higher side. He said that keeping in view inflation trend w.r.t 2008, the estimated cost shall be Rs.3,750 per sq-ft. As such cost of the New Academic Block shall be based on Rs.234 million, if the estimated cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure is taken as the same as provided in the budget (excluding the cost of ICT equipment).  

 

6.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the increase in area from 46,500 sq-ft to 56,519 sq-ft and the increase in estimated cost from Rs.185 million (including the cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT infrastructure) to Rs.234 Million (excluding the cost of ICT equipment).

 

6.6       The Director wanted to know as to what would be the construction cost.

             

6.7       The Director Projects stated that the cost breakdown for the building shall be:

 

a)         Cost to be borne by Tabba Group shall be Rs.120 million which will cover the cost of civil and architecture finishes for an area of 56,519 sft.

 

b)         Cost to be borne by IBA SHALL BE Rs.113 million which will cover the cost of HVAC, drainage, fire protection, water supply and electrical works.

c)         The total estimated cost shall be Rs.233.0 million which does not include cost of furniture, fixtures & ICT works and equipments

6.8       The Director IBA asked the Director Projects to provide drawings and all details of the New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus to Mr. M.A. Tabba. He further said that other work such as HVAC, furniture and ICT equipment would be arranged by IBA, while the Civil and related works as detailed above will be carried out by TABBA Group.

 

DECISION

 

6.9       The Committee decided that the Director Projects would provide drawings and all details related to new Academic Block at IBA Main Campus to Mr. M.A. Tabba of Tabba Foundation. 

 

6.10          The Registrar would request Mr. MA. Tabba for considering a higher allocation in view of the revised estimates as the building is being dedicated in his father’s name.

    

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
