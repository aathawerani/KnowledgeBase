Item No. 1      To consider note from Mirza Noman Shah regarding clearance for students.

 

1.1       Mirza Noman Shah in his email of February 25, 2010 reported that they have a procedure to issue a form to each student who cleared BBA or MBA for the transcript. The clearance is required from four departments i.e. Library, Hostel, Placement and Finance. All the departments, except library are located at the Main Campus so they have to dispatch the form as and when received to the Dispatch Section at the Main Campus. Thus the clearance from each department takes longer time and in some cases the form becomes traceless and they have to re-initiate the process.  He suggested that to overcome this problem one person of the respective department may be deputed at the City Campus or an official may be assigned the duty of getting clearance from various departments on daily basis.

 

1.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that the Program Offices should seek online clearance from concerned departments by emailing them on daily basis the names of students who apply for transcripts / degrees.

 

1.3              The Director agreed to the proposal of Mr. Khalili and said that an email be sent to the concerned departments asking them to nominate a person who would be responsible for providing clearance / responding to the emails. He further said that a time line for the Program Office and for all concerned department should be fixed for processing the applications which should not take more than three days. He further said that this would also serve as performance indicator.

 

DECISION

 

1.4              The Committee endorsed the proposal of Mr. Mobin Khalili for obtaining clearance through email. The turnaround time will be 24 hours for clearance by the respective departments.

 

ACTION

 

1.5              The Controller of Examinations would ask the heads of concerned departments to nominate a person from their respective department who would be responsible for providing clearance / responding to the emails related to clearance. The Controller of Examinations would convey the names of nominees to the Program Offices.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Project regarding tree plantation at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the Spring season was the right time to plant trees and suggested a large scale plantation of decorative and shady trees on the peripheries of the Main Campus, Boys’ Hostel, and the Staff Town.  He said that if planted now, these trees will become quite mature by the time the new Boys’ hostel, Visiting Faculty Hostel, New Girls’ Hostel and many new buildings at the Main Campus are completed in the next 2 to 3 years.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the project in principle.  He further said that recommendation for selection of trees, their quantities and the plantation cost will be put up for approval later. The funds may be made available through re-appropriation in the 2009-10 budget.

 

2.2              The Director said that IBA should have a landscaping consultant.  He advised to ensure that the proposed plantation of trees must fit into the landscaping of the Main Campus.

 

DECISION

 

2.3              The Committee in principle approved the project of plantation of trees at the Main Campus as proposed by Mr. Shameem Ahmed.

 

ACTION

 

2.4              Mr. Shameem Ahmed would present the selection of trees and their plantation cost before the EC for approval.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve Proposal for Group Life Insurance for Contract Employees.

 

4.1              Mr. Muhammad Munawar presented the Group Life Insurance Proposal for Contract Employees. He informed the Committee about the present Group Life Insurance coverage provided to regular employees and also presented proposed policy for contract employees with two options; Option 1 is the same as has been provided to Regular Employees and Option 2 with some added benefits to be applicable to both regular and contract employees.  He further clarified that under Option 1, all contractual faculty and non-faculty staff would be provided life insurance cover up-to the age of 60 and the policy will be in alignment with regular employees except post retirement coverage which is available to regular employees only. It would be a contributory scheme, mandatory for every eligible employee as a policy. Under Option 2, the following additional benefits are provided:

 

·         Accidental Death (coverage: double sum assured)

·         Permanent Disability (Natural and Accidental)

·         Temporary disability (Accidental)

 

4.2              Mr. Munawar said that proposals from the following three organizations were received and analysed:

 

i)                    State Life Insurance Corporation (SLIC)

ii)                  Eastern Federal Union (EFU)

iii)                New Jubilee Insurance (NJI)

 

4.3              Mr. Munawar also presented the amount of premium with and without additional benefits quoted by SLIC, EFU and NJI according to which the lowest per annum premium for contractual employees was quoted by EFU which was of Rs.382,699 and Rs.272,500 respectively and the second lowest quote was of SLIC which was Rs.456,658 and Rs.382,699 respectively. After deducting employees contribution, Rs.32,856/- would be payable annually by the IBA to SLIC and in case of EFU there would be a surplus of Rs.41,103 per annum (using employees base as on January 25, 2010)

 

4.4              Mr. Munawar also informed the Committee that SLIC also offers Profit Commission Rider according to which SLIC will reimburse to IBA certain percentage of the premium if the claim does not exceed the premium over every three years.

 

DECISION

 

4.5              The Committee decided that:

a)           The same coverage as is provided to regular employees be given to the Contract employees. 

b)          SLIC should be asked to charge the premium equivalent to the lowest quote.

c)           Employees should be asked if they would like the amount of coverage to be enhanced for the surplus amount or would like their premium to be reduced in that proportion.

d)          The Director H.R. and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would talk to EFU for providing the Profit Commission Rider Scheme which is provided by SLIC. If this is provided by EFU then the Director H.R. would decide about the organization from which the contract employees to be insured.

e)           The Director H.R. was requested to include compensation on permanent and partial disability in the IBA Welfare Scheme instead of getting this coverage through insurance company.

 

ACTION

 

4.6              The Director H.R. would take actions on the above decisions.

        

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
