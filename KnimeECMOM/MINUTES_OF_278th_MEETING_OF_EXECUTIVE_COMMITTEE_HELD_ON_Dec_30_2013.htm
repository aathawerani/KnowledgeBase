MINUTES OF 278TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

MONDAY, DECEMBER 30, 2013

 

The 278th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 30, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                       Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                             Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                          Registrar

5.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                               Controller of Examinations

6.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                                 Director Projects

7.      Mr. Adnan Hameed                                                           Head of H.R.

 

Syed Jahanzeb, Assistant Manager Finance (Projects) attended the meeting as nominee of Mr. Moeid Sultan. Mr. Khalid Javed Rishi, Manager Security attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Demonstration of locally made explosive detector.

 

1.1       Mr. Khalid Rishi, Manager Security, presented explosive detection history in detail, and made a demonstration of Explosive Detector – SNIFFEX Plus. He said that he had gathered the information for the presentation through various sources including Janes Defence Weekly, Israeli homeland security and Military College of Engineering, Risalpur which he had attended as officer BD Course. He said that the following are used in explosive detection.  He also gave a brief on the functioning of each detector:

 

·         Dogs

·         Honey Bees

·         Mechanical Scent Detection

·         Spectrometry

·         X-ray Machines

·         Neutron Activation

·         Silicon Nanowires for Trace Detection of Explosives

 

1.2       The Dean & Director asked as to which device is cheap and most effective, and what is his recommendation. Mr. Khalid Rishi replied that none of the above device is cheap. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that the device he thinks most effective would cost Rs.4 million. The Dean & Director said that we could not afford such an expensive device. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that the cheapest option is SNIFFEX Plus – ADE 651 which consists of a swivelling antenna mounted via a hinge to a plastic handgrip. He said that this is local made device and would cost around Rs.100,000/- It requires no battery or other power source; it is powered solely by the user’s static electricity. To use the device, the operator must walk for a few moments to charge it before holding it at right angles to the body.  He said that it is reported that ADE 651 was purchased for the use by the Airports Security Force. However, after the ADE 651 became the focus of controversy for its role in Iraq, concerns were raised in Pakistan about its employment as a bomb detector by the ASF.  He said that Pakistani scientists rejected the scientific basis on which the device was claimed to work.  He also referred to a report from US Navy which concludes that the Sniffex handheld explosives detector does not work. However, when Mr. Khalid Rishi made a demonstration of Sniffed handheld explosives detector, it worked well.  The Dean & Director with reference to the US Navy Report said that the requirement of US Navy is very stringent. He said that the threat perception to US is not comparable to IBA.  We just need a deterrent.

 

1.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the Sniffex handheld explosives detector could be more effective than the mirror being used. He suggested to acquire one set for the City Campus and see its performance.  Mr. Khalid Rishi said that in case of detection for any reason, the vehicle involved will have to be isolated. The members agreed that it has to be isolated. The Registrar said that there has to be a single operator because the device cannot be handled by everyone. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the guards should be trained for handling the Sniffex handheld explosives detector. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that two guards; one for morning and another for evening shifts should be trained for it.

 

1.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that hydraulics system is very much needed at the City Campus.  The three systems; hydraulic barrier, the explosive detector, and the boundary wall boulders, if installed at City Campus, would be enough. The Registrar said that traffic flow is blocked because of hydraulic system as it happens near Marriott Hotel. However, he said that he would see what can be done to stop blockage of vehicles.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee approved the procurement of one Sniffex handheld explosives detector for the City Campus and see its performance.    

 

ACTION

 

1.6       Mr. Khalid Rishi would procure the device following the laid down procedure.

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the new works at Administration Building, City Campus.

 

A)    Re-roofing of Administration Building:

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the water proofing, insulation and tiling works of the Admin. Building, City Campus were completed in September 2011. The roof had extensive pedestrian traffic more than expected. It was extensively used by the Maintenance Staff for the last 2½ years. In March 2012, work of Data Centre was commenced and in this connection heavy air-conditioning equipments and generator were installed on the roof by the ICT Contractors. Because of this the roof tiles have been damaged beyond repair. At many places the roof has been punctured as well. He said that Mr. Mobin Khalili, Controller of Examinations had pointed this out to Projects Department to look at it and provide corrective measures. He said that the roof was inspected by him as well as the Engineers. It was found that if the maintenance work would have been done in time the extent of problem would not have been multiplied. He presented the photographs which showed that the patch work would not be useful. He said that in his opinion the entire roof tiles may be disposed off along with insulation. The water proofing is repairable and there will be minimal expenses on water proofing. He said that the best solution is to install roof composite tiles as has been done on the FCS Building. The estimated amount shall be Rs.2.5 million. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the estimate so that the procurement process could be initiated.

 

2.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that at the initial stage when rainy water was seeping from the roof, he went to the roof to see the fault and found that loose tiles were placed on the roof.  He had at that time objected to this but he was told that it was as per design. 

        

2.3       The Dean & Director said that those who had designed the Admin. Building should take corrective measures because it is there responsibility. He asked Mr. Khalid Pervez to ask the Architect and the Contractor to replace the existing roof tiles with the roof composite tiles as suggested by him because it was due to their mistakes. The Dean & Director made it clear that he would not pay for that.

 

DECISION AND ACTION:

 

2.4       The Director Projects would ask the Architect and the Contractor to replace the existing roof tiles with the roof composite tiles because it was due to their fault in design.

 

B)    Addition of Pergola:

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that there have been certain areas which were left over in the original architectural design. From these left over areas water is ingressing in the veranda and sometimes in the rooms. It is highly necessary to cover the same areas with the kind of pergola as already installed. The estimated cost is approximately Rs.130,000/-  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the addition of pergola so that the work could be taken up.

 

2.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that it is a small patch. He wanted to know if it can be done internally. The Director Projects said that much work including welding is involved for which maintenance department does not have the resources.

 

DECISION

        

2.7       The Committee approved the cost estimate of Rs.130,000/- for addition of Pergola at the City Campus as proposed by the Director Projects.

                   

Item No.3       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Broken / damaged sewerage lines of IBA Staff Town.

    

3.1       Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager (General Maintenance & Security) presented the general lay out of Staff Town.  He said that the existing Karachi University line was of about 15” diameter and the new one is of 8” diameter.  The 18” line of Karachi University is hitting the 8” line.  He said that earlier it was agreed that a septic tank would be constructed which would be connected to the Visiting Faculty Residences. However, he said that this work has not been completed. Thus there is no connection of VFR with the sewerage line.  The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why the septic tank was not constructed.  Mr. Fahim Uddin said that it was a project of Mr. Shameem Ahmed and he can tell the reason. The Dean & Director said that the EC cannot take a decision without hearing Mr. Shameem Ahmed. He said that we have to take into account his view point also before making a decision.

 

DECISION

 

3.2       This item was deferred and would be discussed in the presence of Mr. Shameem Ahmed in the next EC meeting.

 

b)     List of pre-qualified contractors for small works.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director asked Syed Faheem Uddin, Manager General Maintenance & Security to prepare a list of pre-qualified contractors for small works.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
