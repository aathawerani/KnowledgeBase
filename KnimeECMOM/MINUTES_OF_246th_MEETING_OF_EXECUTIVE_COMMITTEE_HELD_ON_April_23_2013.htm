MINUTES OF 246TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

TUESDAY, APRIL 23, 2013

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                 Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                  Registrar

4.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                     Director Projects

5.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                             Director CED

6.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                            Director H.R.

7.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                 Acting Head of ICT

 

The following attended the meeting on special invitation:

1.      Dr. Nasir Touheed

2.      Mr. Jawad Khan

3.      Mr. Abdul Rahman

4.      Mr. Jahanzeb

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1 Status Report from Mr. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer.

 

1.1       Mr. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer to monitor the progress of the balance work of Computer Lab of Abdur Razzak Tabba Academic Block and report to the EC. Mr. Jawad provided the following up-date:

 

· Technology box Electrical wiring work of the modular lab furniture is not completed. So verification of wiring on UPS / generator backup or otherwise could not be verified.

 

· Air-conditioning is in operation but can’t be verified because the HVAC Person of the contractor is not available. Some lighting work is yet to be completed. Fire Alarm is also not operational.

 

· Sound system is not operative. Floor / Junction box has not been properly installed.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director was not satisfied with the report presented by Mr. Jawad. He said that the EC had assigned this task to Mr. Jawad for getting the work completed by April 20, 2013. So he was exercising the authority of the highest administrative body of IBA. He wanted to know as to who in the Projects Department was supposed to get the work done. The Director Projects replied that Mr. Sohail will coordinate with Mr. Jawad.  He further said that the work was delayed due to non-availability of cable which has since been procured by the contractor. He further added that the new item of full partition and the addition of more seats in the administration area will take some more time.

 

DECISION

 

1.3 Mr. Jawad Khan would coordinate with Mr. Sohail of Projects Department and get the work done and report to the EC.

   

 

Item No. 2 To consider and approve the amendment in contract for the project “Renovation and up-gradation of existing library” at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1 The Director Projects stated that the case for the Extension in time for the Project of renovation and Up-gradation of Exiting Library was presented in the Executive Committee meeting on March 25, 2013.  In this meeting the contractor had requested the Executive Committee that in order to expedite the work an advance of Rs.10 million may be given so that the project can be completed within the extended period. The Executive Committee  had decided to facilitate the Contractor through payment of extra secured advance of Rs.10 million on the explicit understanding and undertaking that the Contractor will complete the project by July 3, 2013 and they will be given one month’s grace period after that and the Contractor will not claim any additional cost including escalation. The Director Projects and NESPAK will complete the required contractual formalities.

 

2.2 The Director Projects reported that in this regard M/s NESPAK was asked to draft an amendment to contract/s.  Accordingly, NESPAK sent their draft to IBA in which they specified the following two major clauses which are as under:

 

a) Special Secured Advance Recovery

This special Secured Advance shall be recovered in three equal installments starting from the forthcoming Interim Payment submitted by the Contractor for the Month of April 2013.

 

b) Clause 60.2. Monthly Payment

After Para (b) Add, “A rebate of 1% (One Percent) will be deducted on the certified work done in all forthcoming payment applications from April 2013 and onwards submitted by the Contractor.”

 

2.3 The Director Projects reported that the Draft was reviewed by IBA Contracts Department and sent to Contractor for their agreement. The Contractor insisted that instead of making deduction (As per Clause ‘a’ above) from April 2013 it may be done from May 2013 and application of rebate should also be considered from May 2013.  However, M/s NESPAK stood firm on their recommendations and refused to make the change from April to May 2013. He said that the Projects Department advised NESPAK to submit the pros and cons for their recommendation and contractors opinion. M/s NESPAK via email dated April 18, 2013 explained the reasons for standing on April 2013 for recovery and rebate.

 

2.4 The Director Projects requested the Executive committee to consider the above case as it is authorized to take a decision to relax the condition of deduction.

 

2.5 The Dean & Director said that as client we can amend the contract if there were cogent reasons for doing so. It was not the prerogative of the consultant or Nespak as they do not have any stake in prolonged dispute with the contractor that will only further delay the completion of the project. He said that the decision was taken in the EC meeting on March 25 and this issue was brought to the EC again after a month.  He said that if we do not pay the advance money, the Contractor would not be able to complete the work by July 3, 2013. He advised the Director Projects to make amendments in the contract and get the advance payment released to the Contractor.  He apprehended that the project would not be completed by July 3, 2013 because the amended contract has not yet been signed. He said that he has to get approval for extension from A&FC so he wants a realistic date of project completion. He has to justify the reasons for the extension of time and the financial implications.

 

2.6 The Director Projects said that there would be no significant financial implications except the Projects Managers’ fee for the extended period. Mr. Mahmood Ali said that realistically the project may be completed by the end of September 2013. The Director Projects said that because of elections and Ramazan and Eid the projects may get delayed.  He suggested that we should extend the date till end of October 2013 and seek approval for extension from A&FC till November 30, 2013.

 

DECISION

2.7 The Committee decided that the Director Projects would get the contract amended to facilitate advance payment and recovery of advance payment in instalments from May 2013.

 

2.8 The Committee allowed extension in the completion of the project till October 30, 2013.

 

ACTION 

2.9 The Director Projects would get the above decisions implemented.  He would also prepare a paper giving justification for extension in the completion time of the project along with the financial implications, if any, for the approval of A&FC. 

 

Item No. 3 To consider the request of M/s ICON for review of their fee as intimated to them in reply to their fee proposal based on the decision of the Executive Committee held on April 15, 2013.

 

3.1 The Director Projects reported that the Architects (M/s ICON) of the Administration Building and the Library had asked for a total fee of Rs.2,300,000 (rebated) for the designing of the furniture of both the buildings. The Executive Committee had reduced the fee by 50%, to which Architects have not agreed. The Architects want to present their arguments in front of the Executive Committee.  He requested the Executive Committee to consider their viewpoint and decide the matter.

 

3.2 Mr. Naoshad, the representative of M/s. Icon said that the Director Projects had asked them to use furniture design/ shop drawing and specification of other project for the Library and admin building furniture. But he said that it is not possible as the concept design/theme and usage of all mentioned projects are different. He said that as the library is a very important specific type of project from architecture up to the details of furniture, it requires a specialized furniture design rather than copied.

 

3.3 The Dean & Director agreed that library is a specialized project so the EC rightly decided that there would be no cut of 50% in the quoted consultancy fee.  As such the original proposal of Rs.9,50,000/- is accepted.  However, for the Admin Building for which the design and specifications are already available, the EC had decided to cut the original quote to 50%.  The Registrar said that as in Aga Khan we should maintain the same specifications of furniture for all our buildings. He said that rooms may be of different sizes so they would have to make tables according to the room’s size but the specifications will be the same.

   

3.4 Mr. Naoshad said that they had given 7% discount on quoted fee of Rs.1.35 million for the Admin. Building furniture and had kept it at the lower side by bringing it down to Rs.1.255 million.

 

3.5 The Director Projects said that the Consultants have to do evaluation of the tender and will ensure quality of the furniture as per specifications. The Dean & Director said that the architects will only have to supervise the work and ensure quality which will be a routine matter in case of Admin. Building compared to Library where the nature of work is more sophisticated.

  

DECISION

3.6 The Committee decided, which was also accepted by the representative of Icon, that for the designing of Library furniture IBA will pay the fee of Rs.950,000/- and for the Admin Building Rs.700,000/- (Total Rs.950,000 + Rs.700,000 = Rs.1,650,000) to the Consultant.

 

Item No. 4 Correlation between SAT & IBA Test.

 

4.1 Mr. Imran Batada presented the three years statistics of the SAT and IBA Test Scores and their correlation.

 

4.2 Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that we should also find out the CGPA of those admitted through IBA test and those admitted on SAT score. The Dean & Director suggested to separately analyse the performance of those with ‘A’ level background and those coming with Intermediate level.

 

4.3 The Dean & Director observed that for IBA test, there are three cut offs bindings so there may not be one to one relationship between SAT and the final score of IBA.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that we can do it by comparing our own English and maths tests with SAT’s.   

 

4.4 Dr. Nasir Touheed said that IBA does not receive the SAT score directly.  He said that he picks the score provided by the students on-line. The Dean & Director said that this is contrary to the best practice where the admitting institution receives the scores directly. He suggested that we should become partner with the SAT and requested Dr. Nasir Touheed to get IBA enlisted into it.

 

4.5 The Dean & Director requested Dr. Nasir Touheed to consult outside IBA how to improve our entry test.  He advised Dr. Nasir Touheed to talk to Mr. Thomas Christie, Director, Aga Khan University Examination Board in this regard and also arrange his meeting with the internal examiners.

 

DECISION

4.6 The Committee noted the correlation between SAT and IBA test.

    

ACTION 

4.7 Dr. Nasir Touheed would explore the availability of recourses outside IBA that could be used to improve the quality of our entry test.  He would also talk to Mr. Thomas Christie, Director, Aga Khan University Examination Board in this regard. He would also arrange a meeting of the internal examiners with the Dean & Director.

 

4.8 Dr. Nasir Touheed would get IBA enlisted for the SAT scores.

 

4.9 Mr. Imran Batada would find out the CGPA of those admitted through IBA test and those admitted on SAT score. He would also analyse the performance of those IBA students who have ‘A’ level background and those with Intermediate level.

 

Item No. 5 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a) Approval for time extension of FCS building at IBA City Campus.

 

5.1 The Director Projects stated that the Renovation and Refurbishment of Existing FCS building was awarded to M/s Prefab Systems on 16, June 2011. Due to addition of Phase-II & Phase-III work the time of completion was extended up till March 31, 2013 after approval of IBA Authorities.  However, the Contractor could not complete the work on due date because of to last minute changes made by the FCS department, which involved preparation of revised details and drawings specially due to the changes in electrical and ICT works which had taken a lot of time.  As per the Consultant’s report following were the reasons for the delays:

 

· Design changes by the end user about 70% design changed on certain floors:

 

i. 1st revision in design of Ground Floor was made in October 2011

ii. 3rd but not last revision in design of Ground floor was made in August 2012.

iii. 1st revision in design of First floor was made in April 2012

iv. 3rd but not last revision in design of First floor was made in March 2013

v. 1st revision in design of 3rd  floor was made in November 2011

vi. 5th but not the last revision in design of 3rd floor in March 2013

 

5.2 The Director Projects said that on the basis of the above delays the Architect and Project Manager recommended 90 days further extension in order to complete the work i.e by 30th June 2013. He said that 50% building has been handed over to the users and those areas are fully functional. He said that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on Saturday April 20, 2013 had considered and recommended the extension to be approved by the Executive Committee. They advised that the design of the FCS be completely frozen to avoid further delays. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the time extension up to 30th June 2013, based on the facts as stated above.

 

5.3 The Dean & Director said that we should build in all the changes at the designing stage not at the construction phase. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said those are not last minute changes; in fact they were done much earlier.

 

5.4 The Dean & Director wanted to know whether there would be any financial implications due to extension in time. The Director Projects said that they would partially utilize the services of Project Managers for which he would submit a proposal separately in the next EC meeting.

 

DECISION

5.5 The Committee approved the extension in the completion time of FCS building till June 30, 2013.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
