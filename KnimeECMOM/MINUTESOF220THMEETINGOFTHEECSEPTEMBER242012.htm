MINUTES OF 220TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 24, 2012

1.       Dr. Ishrat Husain                                       Dean & Director

2.       Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                Associate Dean, FBA

5.       Mr. Mobin Khalili                                        Controller of Examinations

6.       Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                          Director Projects

7.       Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                      Director ICT

8.       Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                  Director CED

10.    Mr. Moeid Sultan                                       Acting Director Finance

 

Mr. Mehmood Bughio, Senior Manager Projects, and Mr. Adnan Hameed, H.R. Manager attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:   

Item No.1      To consider and approve the two Public Sound Systems proposed for Students Centre.

1.1     The Director Projects said that originally the Architects / Consultants had proposed a simple public address system (of Make M/s. Bosch) for the Alumni Student’s Centre at Main Campus in the Contract comprising the following:

1.    For the entire building at a total cost of Rs.182,200/-

a.    3 Watts Ceiling Mounted Speakers = 11 Nos.

                                                                          i.    Passage                                = 08 Nos.

                                                                          ii.    Lounge                                 = 02 Nos.

                                                                          iii.    Entrance Lobby                     = 01 No.

b.    60 Watts Microphone (01 No.), DVD Changer (01 No.) plus Wiring, Testing & Commissioning, etc. 

1.2     He said that in 201st Executive Committee meeting held on April 30, 2012 it was suggested by Mr. Zafar Ahmed Siddiqui to install central address system in the entire building including classrooms of the Students’ Centre. He said that the sound systems proposed by the Architects / Consultants are inadequate to meet the requirements. Other important areas, such as dining halls, lounges, indoor games, gymnasium (covering the entire building) should also be provided sound system. 

1.3     The Director Projects reported that a technical as well as financial proposal from M/s. Bose was obtained, who has suggested the following system. The sound system from the same company has already been installed in the Existing Auditorium, Aman CED Building and Tabba Academic Block. 

1.    For Ground and First Floor 

a.    Surface Mounted Speakers                 = 37 Nos. 

                                                             i.    Dinning                 = 16 Nos.

                                                             ii.    Corridors              = 08 Nos.

                                                             iii.    Play Area             = 04 Nos.

                                                             iv.    Outdoor              = 04 Nos.

                                                             v.    Cafeteria             = 03 Nos.

                                                             vi.    Sitting                = 02 Nos.

 b.    Business Music System (01 No.), Interface Volume Selector (02 Nos.), Goose Neck Microphone with Stand (01 No.), Single Disc DVD Player (01 No.) plus Wiring, Testing & Commissioning, etc.

Total Cost = Rs. 1,007,800/-

2.    For Lounge and Gymnasium

a.    Satellite and Flush Mounted Speakers   = 16 Nos. 

                                                                       i.    Gynasium-1        = 05 Nos.

                                                                       ii.    Gynasium-2       = 05 Nos.

                                                                       iii.    Lounge             = 06 Nos. 

b.    Business Music System (01 No.), Interface Volume Selector (03 Nos.), Single Disc DVD Player (01 No.) Plus Wiring, Testing & Commissioning, etc. 

Total Cost = Rs. 697,400/- 

For total of M/s. Bose Sound System             = Rs. 1,705,200/-

With Tax, OH and Profit                               = Rs. 2,187,090/- 

1.4     He said that if the Bose System is selected it will cost an extra Rs.2,004,890/-   

1.5     The Dean & Director wanted to know how often this public address system would be used in a year. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he had suggested for central address system but he said that since there is a huge cost difference (Rs.2,187,090 against Rs.182,200/-), it is not advisable to go for central address system.   

1.6     The Registrar suggested that the eight Ceiling Mounted Speakers proposed for the Passage should be placed at cafeteria, dining hall, and the gym with supporting equipments but the speakers should be of 20 Watts and instead of 3 Watts.  He said that Bose Sound System is very costly. He said that he had arranged on his own for the IT, two public address system at a cost of around Rs.40,000/- instead of over Rs.100,000/-     

DECISION 

1.7     The Committee did not approve the proposal to install central address system in the entire building including classrooms of the Students’ Centre. 

1.8     The Committee decided that the eight Ceiling Mounted Speakers proposed for the passage would be placed at the cafeteria, dining hall and the gym but those would be of 20 watts with supporting equipments. The Registrar would identify the areas where the speakers are to be placed.   

ACTION  

1.9     The Director Projects would get the above decisions implemented by ensuring that the total cost does not exceed Rs.182,200/- 

Item No. 2     First monthly progress report on Data Centre Project.  

2.1     The Director ICT reported that order for the bulk of the items of this project has been placed. Civil work is being done. The work is progressing as per schedule and the project would be completed by 31st January 2013 

DECISION 

2.2     The Committee noted the update. 

Item No. 3     Note from the Controller of Examinations on “Interference in other Departments’ Affairs”. 

3.1     With reference to the Agenda Item # 2 of the EC meeting held on September 17, 2012, Mr. Mobin Khalili objected to the inclusion of this item in the Agenda for the following reasons: 

1.    Coordinator Testing Service has developed some sort of fixation with the number of exam days and every now and then he brings it up at different forums. A number of times he brought up this “issue” in the EC and Academic Committee meetings insisting to cut down the exam days but his proposal was not approved. Then he got his “proposal for reducing the exam days” included in the agenda of the 56th Meeting of the Academic Board held on October 17, 2011. The Academic Board did not agree to his proposal of reducing the exam days. He said that once the proposal was turned down by the highest body only last year, there was no justification to include it again in the EC agenda. 

2.    The Admission Committee in its meetings every now and then discusses matters concerning the Examination Department. Every committee has a ToR and must operate within its boundaries.  

3.    Interference in other departments’ affairs must not be encouraged at the pretext of “freedom of open debates, discussions and discourse” otherwise it will lead to complete administrative chaos and anarchy.  

3.2     The Registrar said that the item was included in the agenda for EC meeting with his permission. He said that whenever there is an agenda item from other than EC members, the concerned person is invited to attend the meeting. He said that since he is not an academician, he took the advice of the Admission Committee and invited them to the meeting. 

3.3     The Dean & Director said that the Academic Board is a superior body which has already taken a decision on the issue of Examination Calendar. Thus there was no justification of bringing this issue before the Admission Committee and the EC again unless that there are new grounds on which it should be considered.  But before reopening this matter, the concerned department head should be consulted.  He said that the examination belongs to the Academic matter and it has to be from the Associate Deans but he did not hear about any problems in this regard from them. He said that the two Associate Deans also look after the Program Departments. Any conflict between Program Directors and Chairpersons are resolved in the Monthly Coordination meeting.  He suggested that the Controller of Examinations should also be invited to the Monthly Coordination meeting where he should raise relevant issues. He said that if the two Associate Deans feel any changes to be made in the examination system, then in consultation with the Controller of Examinations, they may discuss the changes in the Academic Committee.

3.4     The Registrar gave reasons for proposing the reduction in the number of days for exams by the Admission Committee.  However, he apologized for not consulting the concerned head of the department. 

DECISION 

3.5     The Committee decided that in future, any proposal before presenting it to the EC, Admission Committee or the Academic Board, should first be discussed/consulted with the concerned department head.          

3.5     The Controller of Examination would also be invited to the Monthly Coordination Meeting.

4.2     The Dean & Director said that as already decided in the EC, that we would do physical verification with the assistance of the staff of the Projects Department. If this is done, then the solution to staff’s difficulties can be found. However, he said that if H.R. is satisfied that with the proposed solutions, issues would be resolved then the EC should endorse these proposals. He clarified that he does not want the sanctity of the rule to be diluted but the procedure should be made as smooth as possible.  

4.3     Mr. Adnan proposed that the attestation done by the IBA Scale employees in Manager and above levels should also be accepted.  

DECISION 

4.4     The Committee in order to simplify the procedure, approved the solutions as proposed above by Mr. Adnan Hameed with regard to documentary requirements for House Ceiling Rental Allowance including attestation by the IBA Scale employees in Manager and above levels.         

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
