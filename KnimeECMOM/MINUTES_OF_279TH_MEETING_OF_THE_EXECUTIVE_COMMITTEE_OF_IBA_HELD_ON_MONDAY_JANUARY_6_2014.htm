MINUTES OF 279TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JANUARY 6, 2014

The 279th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, January 6, 2014 at 2:00 p.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

Mr. Shamem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, and Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager (General Maintenance & Security) attended the meeting on special invitation.


The following items were discussed:


Item No. 1(a) Time Bound Project – Projects Department.

1.1 Time extension of construction of Power Centre at Main Campus till December 30, 2013: The Director Projects reported that 98% work has been completed. He informed that the Contractor was working on final coat on exterior, MS Doors and punch list items. All the foundations of the Generators were ready and all transformers, HT & IV panels have been placed in position.

To a query the Director Projects said that the Power Center Project under discussion pertained to only the Power Center building and all the Civil works needed for cable trenches and the foundations of electrical equipment and Generator. The rest of the woks of installations of electrical equipment and the generators is the responsibility of the Electrical contractor of “Electrical Infrastructure Project. The Registrar explained that there would be two panels in the building that has been completed; on one panel generators would be placed and on the other KESC line would be fixed. The Director Projects informed the members that in March 2014 the power hub will start functioning and connection to all buildings will go through this power hub.

b) Status Reports from:

Projects Departments:

1.2 The Director Projects to request the MD Philips to audit our electrical facilities and see where we can have savings: The Director Projects regretted that he could not arrange the meeting because of being very busy. He further said that they have to coordinate with KESC also. He said that he would try to have a meeting with the MD Philips soon.

1.3 Supply and Installation of four passenger lifts and one Service Lift at Aman Tower: The Director Projects reported that order has been placed to M/s. Ziafco. The Dean & Director suggested to ask Ziafco to expedite the work so that the lifts are installed and become functional when the Aman Building is inaugurated.

1.4 Mr. Shameem Ahmed to arrange a meeting with the Registrar, the Director Projects, Mr. Jami Moiz and the NBCL and finalize the minor changes in the design of new Boys’ Hostel, Block ‘B’ & ‘C’. Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the meeting was arranged and minor changes have been incorporated in the design and sent to the Registrar for comments. The Registrar said that he has sent his comments to Mr. Jami Moiz.

1.5 Payment of additional fee of Rs.1 million to NESPAK for extended period of 5 months in respect of project “Construction of KESC sub-station and relocation of four generators at IBA, City Campus. Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that NESPAK has accepted a reduction of 20% in the project management fee for the extended period of five months.


1.6 The Director Projects to request MJS Foundation for fixing hydraulic barrier at the City Campus as required by the Cantonment Board: The Director Projects reported that the decisions have been communicated to the Architects and MJS Foundation. He said that the following day he would hold a meeting with Mr. Charania.

1.7 Lump sum fee proposed by NBCL for four projects at Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that NBCL have agreed to reduce their fee upto 5% and confirmed in writing.

1.8 The Director Projects to ask NBCL to give a coloured drawing with grooves and without grooves (silbatta) of the MP Building: The Director Projects stated that NBCL did not agree to change their design as according to them major part of groove plaster is matching with CED building and the cost difference between silbatta and groove plaster is minimal.

The Registrar said that NBCL has not addressed the AC issue of MP Building. The Director Projects said that completion of the project would take six to eight months’ time. The Dean & Director suggested not to include pavers in tender because that would be provided by IBA through donation from ENCRIVETE. Similarly, National Tiles should also not be included in the tender as we would be getting them as donation. He said that for City Campus also pavers will be supplied by donors; only fixing charges will paid to the Contractor.

1.9 The Director Projects to ask M/s. Icon to redo the design and engineering estimate of the three activities of the Master Plan of City Campus; road carpeting, external and parking lighting systems, and rain and storm water drain: The Director Projects reported that Icon is working on these. The revised design will be submitted after Mr. Noshad of Icon is back from leave on January 12, 2014.

Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Director Projects to ask M/s. Icon to include Sui Gas line also in the Master Plan for City Campus and also provide space for cables.

Head of H.R:

1.10 H.R. Department to enhance the entitlement limit for the mobile phone set: Mr. Adnan Hameed reported that in view of the special discount that IBA got due to bulk purchase of the cell phones, and the possible fluctuations in prices, the entitlement amount incorporated in rules is Rs.26,500/- instead of the purchase price of Rs.25,650/- In addition, the ‘Black Berry service” has been replaced with “GPRS”.

Head of ICT:

1.11 Lecture Recording System: Mr. Imran Batada reported that demonstration of Panopto Lecture recording system has been conducted for EC members, CEE, Dr. Khoja and for EMBA program from Argosy Enterprises (the sole distributor of Panopto in Pakistan). He further said that they have recorded a couple of sessions using the same software. Dr. Khoja’s session was also recorded and he was satisfied with the results of the software.

Mr. Batada said that KSBL has no lecture recording system. They are using their video conferencing equipment for recording lectures. Mr. Batada said that Panopto Software is fulfilling their requirement but it is licensed product and we have to pay licence cost on yearly basis. He said that ICT team and Dr. Khoja are exploring other open source solution(s) as well. However, he said that open source is not up-dated with the speed as others do. He said that a minimum of five licences will have to be taken. The Dean & Director asked as to what is the fee for five licences. Mr. Batada replied that around US$10,000 is the fee for five licences and the same amount is the annual recurring fee. He said that the equipment cost would be between Rs.3 to 4 lacs per licence. He said that there is a provision in the budget for such expenses.

The Registrar suggested to reserve one licence for Aman Tower. He further suggested that one class room at Aman Tower should have Electronic Board. Mr. Batada said that Panopto can be integrated to Smart Board/Electronic Board also. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Batada to make provision for the Electronic Board in the up-coming budget.

DECISIONS & ACTIONS

1.12 The Committee noted and approved the up-dates noted above.

1.13 The Director Projects would exclude ENCRIVETE pavers and National Tiles from tender as those would be provided in donation. Pavers for the City Campus also would not be included in the tender.

1.14 The Director Projects would ask M/s. Icon to include Sui Gas line also in the Master Plan for City Campus and to provide space for cables in the Master Plan.

1.15 The Committee approved the procurement of five licences of Panopto Lecture recording system and its related equipment from Argosy Enterprises (the sole distributor of Panopto in Pakistan).

1.16 Mr. Imran Batada would make provision for Smart Board/Electronic Board in the budget.

Item No.2 Broken / Damaged Sewerage lines of IBA Staff Town.

2.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed and Syed Fahim Uddin briefed the members about the issues involved in the repair of broken / damaged sewerage lines of IBA Staff Town. Various options were considered in light of the instruction of the Dean & Director that there should be a complete sewerage plan for the whole staff town including VFH and the Girls’ Hostel.

DECISION & ACTION

2.2 Due to non-availability of in-house expertise, the Committee requested the Director Projects to ask Engr. Mateen of M/s. NESPAK to suggest a solution to sewerage issue of IBA Staff Town on an urgent basis for consideration by EC.


Item No.3 Approval for award of work for elevators of FCS and Administration Buildings.

3.1 The Director Projects reported that Tender Notice for inviting the bids on the basis of single stage two envelops procedure was published in leading newspaper on September 15, 2013. The following three firms collected the tender documents and submitted their technical and financial proposals on the given date and time:

1. M/s Pakistan Elevator
2. M/s Ziafco Engineers
3. M/s Reliance Engineering

3.2 The Director Projects stated that a pre-bid meeting was arranged in order to clarify the queries of the bidders. At the request of bidders the tender opening date was changed from October 5, 2013 to October 12, 2013. The bidders were informed about the change of date through pre-bid meeting minutes. The technical proposals were opened in front of contractors’ representatives and IBA officials. The technical evaluation and scrutiny of documents were done by Projects Department. The Technical Bid of M/s Pakistan Elevator was rejected after approval of Central Purchase Committee. Among three firms the following two emerged as technically qualified for the subject work:

1. M/s Ziafco Engineers
2. M/s Reliance Engineering

3.3 After approval of Central Purchase Committee the financial bids were opened on December 24, 2013 in front of IBA officials and contractor representatives. The following were the quoted prices which were announced at the time of bid opening in front of all present:

1. M/s Ziafco Engineers Rs. 10,819,680/-
2. M/s Reliance Engineering Rs. 7,070,000/-

3.4 The Director Projects reported that detailed financial evaluation was done by the Projects Department in house and found the prices of M/s Reliance Engineering as the lowest responsive bidder. He said that the Projects Department recommended the award of work to M/s Reliance Engineering at a cost of Rs.7,070,000/- The case was presented in Procurement Committee for their recommendation and award of work to the lowest bidder M/s Reliance Engineering in its meeting held on 4th January 2014. The Procurement Committee recommended the approval of award of work to M/s Reliance Engineering at a cost of Rs.7,070,000/-

3.5 Based on the recommendations of Projects Department and the Procurement Committee, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated bidder at a cost of Rs.7,070,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Seventy thousand). He said that the estimated cost of both the elevators was Rs.10 million.

3.6 The Director Projects informed the EC that Suzuho Fuji China Elevators have already been installed at Administration Building (G+1), Students Center (G+2), Aman CED (G+2), and the Tabba Academic Building (G+3) through Competitive bidding by the same contractor. He reported that:

• The bid price of FCS elevator (G+3) for 6 passengers is Rs.3.2 million as compared to TABBA Academic Building for 10 persons G+3. The cost is Rs.3.8 million.

• The bid price of Admin building elevator (G+4 ) for 10-12 persons is Rs.3.8 million as compared to Tabba Academic Building 10 Persons (G+3). The cost is Rs.3.8 million.

3.7 The Director Projects reported that the entire process of preparation of tender documents and procurement was done in house. He said that the supervision will also be done in house.

DECISION

3.8 The Committee approved the award of work for elevators of FCS and Administration Buildings to the lowest responsive bidder M/s. Reliance Engineering at a cost of Rs.7,070,000/-
