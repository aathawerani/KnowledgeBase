Item No.1       To consider and approve the issuance of RFP to Engineering Consultants International (Pvt.) Limited (ECIL).

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that in pursuance of EC’s instructions given at its meeting held on October 8, 2012, the Request for Proposal (RFP) was issued to 11 pre-qualified architects/consulting engineers whose names and addresses were provided by the Projects Department. He reported that the list did not include the name of ECIL (Engineering Consultants International (Pvt.) Ltd. who recently designed soakage pits + septic tanks for IBA free of cost, most probably in the hope that sometime in the near future they will be able to get some business from IBA. Mr. Shameem Ahmed recommended that ECIL be given an opportunity to compete in the Faculty Apartments’ designing and execution work and he may be allowed to issue the RFP to ECIL, too.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that the prequalification was done through an open and transparent procedure under the procurement rules. If M/s. ECIL were not included in the pre-qualified list then they cannot be added later on even if they have rendered services to us free of cost.  We have to keep the sanctity of the prequalification process intact. He said that they may have to wait till we have the next round of prequalification process.  He wanted to know after how long the prequalification is done again.  Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that the Government Audit requires pre-qualification once a year but at IBA we pre-qualify for the projects. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that there is no project for future for which ECIL may be considered.

 

DECISION

 

1.3       The Committee did not approve the recommendation of Mr. Shameem Ahmed to allow him to issue RFP to ECIL.

Item No. 3      To consider a note from the Director HR on Customer Survey.

 

3.1       The Director H.R. said that HR is going to initiate the Customer Satisfaction Survey undertaken by AC Nielsen, next month. She said that AC Nielsen has suggested the following changes:

 

A) Feedback from students may not be sought with regard to the following service units since they have no / very little interaction with them.

 

·         Facilities Maintenance including Electrical Unit

·         IT Maintenance and Customer Support: Students only interact with the lab staff and that also very rarely

·         Finance Unit: this is separate from Financial Aid Unit which will be assessed separately.

 

B) If sample size increases, the cost will also increase. Till last year we had included MBA morning students, EMBA students and BBA students. This time we want to know if the MBA evening students should be included in the sample. This would increase the sample by at least 30 respondents. Also as we want to have a base of at least 30 respondents for all Service Units, there is a likelihood that the sample size will be more than last year.

 

C) Conducting the study on CAPI (Computer Aided Personal Interviewing Technique) will increase the timelines by at least 10 – 14 days i.e. the results would be shared in the beginning of February. Moreover, the results may vary due to the change in methodology.

 

The CAPI system, as told by Nielsen is more correct as it is not subject to human error to the same extent as their earlier PAPI system. It is the recommended best practice the world over. However, for “tracking studies” like IBA’s, it is left to the customer to decide if they want to switch from PAPI (person aided) to CAPI (computer aided) as there is bound to be some variation in the results due to reduction in margin of error. I would recommend that as we have 3 waves to go, we should switch to CAPI.

  

3.2       The Dean & Director said that he wants feedback from the students on the performance of all computer lab staff. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that students heavily rely on labs and their main complaints were on lab services. Dr. Shakeel Khoja suggested that helpdesk and Learning Management System (LMS - Sakai) for faculty should also be included.  It was agreed that lab services and I.T helpdesk services should be separately included in the survey.

 

3.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that facilities maintenance should also be included in the feedback to be sought from the students as there are many complaints on cleanliness of washroom, spray for mosquito etc. The Director H.R. said that it is not a survey of complaints but on interaction of students.  The Dean & Director suggested to get the questions changed; they be asked about their satisfaction level with the upkeep of classrooms, toilets, open spaces, and general facilities separately for City Campus and Main Campus. 

 

3.4       About the inclusion of Evening Program students in the survey, the Dean & Director said that evening program students do not have much interaction so there is no need to include them in the sample. He said that the minimum size of the sample for individual service unit to be valid would remain the same i.e. 30. He said that the response will be better because the questions would be rephrased.

 

3.5       The Director H.R. said that there are two interviewing techniques, Computer Aided Personal Interviewing Technique (CAPI), and Person Aided Interviewing Technique (PAPI).  She said that she would recommend CAPI because through this system human error is reduced.

 

3.6       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that if there is no cost impact, then we should go for CAPI.  The Dean & Director suggested that human intervention should be unbiased and this should be communicated to A.C. Nielsen. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to see the extent of variation on CAPI and PAPI.     The Committee finally agreed to CAPI system.

 

3.7       The Director H.R. said that in the first wave they did tender for the survey and then the Director Projects and the Head of Internal Audit had advised that the duration of the tender should be for two years. However, she said that for the next year, they would have to do a fresh tender.  She said that there is a possibility that some other organization may get the tender in that case the methodology and technique may be different and the comparison of result with the previous results may not be accurate. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that based on performance of the Consultant, renewal of contract can be done. The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. to consult Mr. Khalid Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit in this regard.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee decided that that lab services and I.T. helpdesk services should be separately included in the survey and Learning Management System (LMS - Sakai) for faculty should also be included.  Facilities maintenance will not be excluded from the survey but for this questions have to be changed and it would be separately surveyed for Main Campus and City Campus. 

 

3.9       The evening program students would not be included in the survey but the students of the morning program will be included in the survey.  The minimum size of the sample for each service unit would be 30 for validation purpose.

 

3.10     The Committee decided that CAPI system should be followed.

 

ACTION

 

3.11     The Director H.R. would get the above decisions implemented.  She would consult Mr. Khalid R. Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit about the renewal of contract.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider an email from an IBA Alumnus on Students Safety.

 

4.1       The Dean & Director said that the concern about extra classes is voiced by many parents/siblings/relatives/privately. He said that one such concern he has received in writing from an alumnus whose sister is currently studying at IBA. He has complained that every day his sister comes home asking to go to a certain event or take an extra compulsory class or participate in a compulsory seminar in IBA apart from regular classes, staying beyond 6.00 pm in the campus. On Friday 16th November, in the afternoon, there was a seminar being conducted by CDC as well as a compulsory extra class that she had to take. He had suggested that all non-educational events be postponed till the security situation stabilizes.

 

4.2       The Dean & Director said that there are two reasons for rescheduling of classes; unanticipated holiday, and another is law and order situation specially at City Campus due to which we have difficulty in mobility. He said that the rescheduling of classes will be approved by the concerned Associate Dean at the prior request of the teacher who will justify it. No faculty member on his/her own can reschedule classes unless they provide justification to the concerned Associate Dean and get approval.  He said that the request for classroom is made to the Registrar so the Registrar should entertain the request only if there is a written permission from the Associate Dean.  The email from the Associate Dean to the Registrar should also be copied to the Program Office and Program Director.

 

4.3       About the timing of activities, the Dean & Director said that he would ask Patrons of Societies to just allow the mega events while other events / activities should not go beyond 5:00 p.m.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee agreed with the remarks of the Dean & Director as noted in 4.3 and 4.4 above.

 

ACTION

 

4.5       The Associate Deans would ensure that no rescheduling of classes is done without their written permission.
