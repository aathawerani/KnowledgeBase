Item No.1      Status of time bound projects given by:

Director HEC Projects:

Director Projects:

The Dean & Director said that the inauguration of the CED Building will take place on November 20, 2012 and we would have some eminent international visitors. He asked the Director Projects to work with the architects / contractor  to phase out the External Development work in a way so as to  complete the pathway  leading to CED from Auditorium first before November 20, 2012. He said that material would be reused and only the labour cost will be involved which would be deducted from the External Development Work.

He asked the Director Projects to follow the drawings of External Development Project. The Director Projects said that a variation order will be issued to the existing contractor who is making sports ground. The Dean & Director said that if it is budget neutral, then he does not have any objection.

Director ICT Projects:

1.4      Procurement of Active Components:  The Director ICT reported that ICT Department initiated the CISCO active equipment tenders for the following buildings in May 2012:

·         New CED Building & Boys Hostel

·         New Academic Tabba Block

He said that after the opening of the bids the tender was awarded to M/s. AMFCO and the delivery time of the equipment was July 18, 2012. But even after various reminders to M/s. AMFCO regarding the supply of CISCO equipment they could not deliver due to the ban on CISCO equipment by the Netherlands Government. He said that when they asked from CISCO, they replied that the export ban for was for Asia Asia Region, which has still not been lifted. The delivery of the equipment is therefore delayed. He said that as per EC’s instruction the CED building has been energized by using the backup components.  However, the new Boys Hostel could not be energized due to the unavailability of the parts. AMFCO and CISCO still do not have any idea as to when will the ban be lifted.  He said that the alternate is to use HP, Juniper or any other brand but none of them would be compatible with our CISCO equipment. Another option is that we can get CISCO equipment from those who bring from other channels but they do not give any guarantee. He requested the EC to consider this situation and extend the completion time period till the equipment is arranged by the vendor. 

The Dean & Director asked the Director ICT to arrange his meeting with the head of AMFCO and the Country Manager of CISCO. 

DECISION

1.5     The Committee noted the up-date along with the observations noted above.

ACTION

1.6     The Director Projects would follow the drawings of External Development Project for the Main Campus and get the pathway completed by issuing a variation order to the existing contractor who is making sports ground. The equivalent amount would be deducted from the contract awarded for External Development. He would also ensure that the work is completed without incurring additional expenditure.

1.7     The Director ICT would arrange a meeting of the Dean & Director with the head of AMFCO and the Country Manager of CISCO.

Item No. 2     To consider a note from Dr. Nasir Touheed regarding “Semester Calendar”.

2.1     Dr. Nasir Touheed said that at IBA we have the longest duration of a semester in the world. He said that at LUMS the first class of the semester started on August 29, 2012 and the last exam would be held on December 22, 2012 whereas at IBA the classes started on September 03, 2012 and the last exam would be held on January 17, 2013. So our duration is three weeks longer as compared to LUMS.  He further said that at LUMS only 14 working days are spent on exams but at IBA 22 working days are spent on exams. He proposed that if we don’t want to reduce the instruction days we can at least reduce the duration of exams by one week which is doable. He said that Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif has developed a software through which duration of exams can be reduced without any clash of exams.

2.2     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that last year the academic cycle has been reset and streamlined.  Moreover, he said that such a proposal was earlier presented in the Academic Board also in October 2011 but was not approved.  He further said that if the duration is reduced, students will have to take three exams in a day which cannot be possible for the students.

2.3     The Dean & Director requested Dr. Nasir Touheed to give him a demonstration of the software.

DECISION

2.4     The Committee decided that as the Academic Board had considered but not approved the proposal, there were no cogent reasons for re-opening the issue at this juncture.

ACTION

2.5     Dr. Nasir Touheed would request Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif to give a demonstration of the software.

a)   Foundation Program for the Children of IBA employees (Up to BPS-15 and equivalent).

4.1     The Director H.R. said that recently the IBA Karachi, in collaboration with the Community Development Program (CDP), Planning & Development Department, Government of Sindh has launched a 6-months Foundation Program. This program aims at targeting talented and deserving students of Sindh region with an objective to identify the meritorious-cum-needy students from less advantaged districts of Sindh province, to prepare them for entrance test and interviews for the undergraduate programs offered in Pakistan. This program will commence from December 2012. Considering the usefulness of this program, she proposed that:

·         This program be offered to children of IBA employees (up to BPS-15 and equivalent) who meet the eligibility criteria and clear the assessment test & interview for the Foundation Program.

·         Those who successfully complete the IBA Foundation Program and clear the IBA admission process be given fully funded scholarships for the duration of their study at IBA.

4.2     She hoped that this initiative will motivate the children of the lower grade employees of IBA to develop themselves by getting a First Class Educational opportunity which will serve to uplift them and their families. She presented the Eligibility Criteria, Features and Facilities and relevant details of this Foundation Program.

DECISION

4.3     The Committee appreciated and approved the proposal as presented by the Director H.R.

ACTION

4.4     The Director H.R. would present the proposal before the A&FC for approval.

b)   Elaboration / Changes in the Attendance and Flexible Timing Policy.

4.5     The Director H.R. said that IBA follows a 41-hour work week for non-teaching staff and offers flexible timings which are as under:

 

          Monday to Thursday, and Saturday:          8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. (inclusive of lunch    

                                                                    break)

          Fridays:   8:30 a.m. to 12 noon.

           Employees sign and record ‘time in’ as soon as they enter into the work place and ‘time out’ at the time of leaving the work place, daily on the Attendance Register maintained at the Reception, which is then handed over to Incharge Leave Record after the 15 minute grace period from the start of the standard work day at 8:30 a.m. A 15-minute grace period is allowed to accommodate an occasional delay in reporting to work.

 4.6     She said that the changes / elaborations proposed in the procedure are:

           “IBA will follow a 41-hour work week for all non-teaching staff based at either of the campuses. This requirement is equally applicable on employees working in any general or department specific work shifts / duty roaster etc. The standard official timing for general shift (Standard working hours) will be:

           Monday to Thursday, and Saturday:          8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. (inclusive of 30

                                                                     minutes lunch break)

          Fridays:   8:30 a.m. to 12 noon.

           Departmental heads depending on the nature of their departmental functions can set-up variable timings for their staff members; however, this information has to be communicated to Administration Department.

           Employees will mark their attendance (time in) as soon as they enter into their duty campus and ‘time out’ at the time of leaving the campus, on the Attendance Machine placed at the respective campuses.

           A 15 minutes grace period is allowed to accommodate any occasional delay due to unforeseen reasons in reporting to work for employees following the standard general shift timings only; this facility will not be available to employees availing flexi-time option. Departmental heads must ensure that employees do not assume this facility as part of their working hours and should monitor and control its exercise on routine basis.”   

4.7     Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to who monitors the attendance and takes actions.  He further said that there is no identification of the Campus from where one is recording his/her attendance.  He suggested that the Leave Incharge should send a monthly report to the concerned department; the concerned Line Manager may discuss with the HOD and take action.

4.8     Mr. Adnan Hameed, H.R. Manager said that the working hours of evening shift of three major departments at City Campus i.e. Program Office, Examination and ICT need to be redefined.

4.9     Mr. Mobin Khalili informed the members of the reasons due to which the evening program office hours were not changed. However, he said that he would look into it.

DECISION

4.10    The Committee noted and approved the Elaboration / Changes in the Attendance and Flexible Timing Policy as noted in 4.6 above.

4.11    The Committee also decided that the Leave Incharge would be asked to send a monthly report to the concerned Line Manager who would discuss the report with the HOD and take action accordingly.

ACTION

4.12    The Registrar would instruct the Leave Incharge to send a monthly attendance report to the concerned line managers for discussion with the HOD and appropriate action.

4.13    The Program Directors, Director ICT and the Controller of Examinations would ensure that their staff of the Evening Shift at City Campus also perform duties for 41 hours in a week.

 

c)   To consider request from Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Senior Manager Examinations, for treating his retirement in BPS-19.

4.14    Mr. Adnan Hameed, Manager H.R. reported that Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Senior Manager Examinations, who retired in January 2012 in BPS-18 has requested that he be retired in BPS-19. He said that Mr. Anwar in his application has requested that since IBA has adopted many rules from Karachi University so the decision of the Syndicate of Karachi University should be adopted in IBA also.  The Karachi University allows up-gradation six months before retirement. Mr. Anwar has also referred the case of Mr. Shameem Akhtar who was up-graded before retirement.  However, Mr. Adnan clarified that Mr. Shameem Akhtar’s case was processed six months before his retirement and his promotion / up-gradation was overdue; he was last promoted in 1985 whereas Mr. Anwar was promoted in 2008. He said that Mr. Muhammad Anwar applied after retirement.  Moreover, the referred policy is not a government standard rule, it is the policy made by the Karachi University.  He said that if the Karachi University rule is adopted then Mr. Anwar has to be promoted from back date with the approval of the BoG.

4.15    Mr. Mobin Khalili said that for regular employees the Karachi University is the benchmark and they should see if the Karachi University rule is applicable on merit.   The Dean & Director clarified that Karachi University rules have been made reference points not the benchmark.

DECISION

4.16    The Director H.R. would see if the case of Mr. Muhammad Anwar merits for back-dated promotion/up-gradation.
