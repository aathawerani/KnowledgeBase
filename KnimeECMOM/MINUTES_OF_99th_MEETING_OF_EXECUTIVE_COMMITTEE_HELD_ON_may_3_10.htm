MINUTES OF THE 99TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MAY 3, 2010

 

The 99th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 3, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider suggestions from Director H.R. on the disciplinary action related to untoward incident at Hawksbay.

 

1.1       The Director H.R referring to the decision of the Disciplinary Committee on the untoward incident at Hawksbay, raised several questions which included:

 

  • What such severe humiliation of these young adults will accomplish?

  • Should we be trying to improve and better the moral fiber of the young men and women under our charge or should we be rejecting them and encouraging their further downfall by condemning them in this extreme manner?

  • Is it not possible to take away some of their privileges instead and allowing them to continue towards their degree with a compassion that just might reverse their negative tendencies?

  • Should we not be doing activities to raise awareness amongst students about the detrimental effects of smoking, drinking, drugs by calling in professional who can give the students facts?

 

1.2       The Director H.R. further stated that since she is working on the strategy Implementation plan, she observed that the Disciplinary Committee is under Academic Management led by the Dean IBA and the 2 Associate Deans. However, the Committee itself is manned by none of them and is led by the Registrar. In her opinion it is the future of the young generation that is at stake and those who are the most troubled, those who make the most wrong turns, need our attention the most, rather than being dealt with an “off with their heads” approach. It is a point to ponder upon.

 

1.3              The Director said that so far he has not interfered in the Disciplinary Committee. On receiving the above observations from the Director H.R. he said that he thought it appropriate to have an open debate and discussion on this issue in the EC meeting.

 

1.4              The Director ICT talked about the attendance and dressing code of students. 

 

1.5              Mr. Mobin Khalili commenting on the first point said that the Disciplinary Committee did not humiliate the students involved rather they were recommended punishment according to the laid down procedure and it was known to everyone.  He further said that it is the deterrence that they want to achieve. He reported that very few such cases in the last 20 years have happened in IBA and credit goes to the strict disciplinary policy.  

 

1.6              Further commenting on the suggestion by the Director H.R. that we should try to improve and reform the moral fibre of young men and women under our control, Mr. Khalili said that “Reform” theory of Crime & Punishment has been rejected by thinkers; it is “Deterrence” (the fear of punishment) theory which has been proved most effective in controlling crimes in a society provided it is implemented without any fear or discrimination. The Disciplinary Committee has been precisely practicing it in IBA; hence the reason of very few cases of cheating or breach of discipline over so many years.

 

1.7              Commenting on the observations of Director H.R. on the formation of the Disciplinary Committee, Mr. Khalili said that he has no objection to the suggestion to re-form the committee and offered to voluntarily withdraw from the Committee so that people with better insight could be inducted in the Committee as suggested by the Director H.R.  

 

1.8              The Director said that it should not be taken personal. He said that they are discussing what their policy should be. He said that according to Director H.R’s understanding the Committee should be such but he said that they may decide that it should continue.

 

1.9              The Registrar said that they ensure that there is no discrimination against any student and in all the three cases for which they have announced punishment, the parents had come and expressed satisfaction that they have not been given any harsh punishment. He suggested that faculty members should check that students do not bring eatables in the classrooms and are properly dressed.  He suggested that Associate Deans should also be included in the Disciplinary Committee. However, the Director did not agree to his proposal of including Associate Deans in the Disciplinary Committee.

 

1.10          Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that faculty has to be more proactive and there should be one or two senior faculty members who should be looking at Campus discipline and should have authority to enforce discipline even before a breach of discipline takes place.

 

1.11          The Director said that they are trying to nurture certain values; it is not the reform of the individual but it is the impact on others. He said that he wants IBA students to be an epitome of good behaviour. He said that they are trying to protect IBA students from indulging in malpractices and he is totally satisfied with the punishment given by the Discipline Committee as they are upto the mark.

 

1.12          The Director Projects suggested that there should be a Campus Manager to take care of such incidence.

 

1.13          Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested to involve students also in the maintenance of discipline which was accepted with appreciation.  

 

DECISION

 

1.14          The Committee decided that three faculty members for each campus to be nominated by the Associate Deans would act as discipline enforcer and they would also be delegated some authority. (Proper title to be given).

 

1.15          The Committee requested the Registrar to prepare terms of reference in consultation with Associate Deans for “discipline enforcers” and suggest a panel of faculty members who would act in this capacity. An appropriate name may be chosen for this assignment.

Item No. 3      To consider proposal for renewal of Microsoft Campus and School Agreement.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that IBA has contracted with Microsoft for full range of their software which is renewable on an annual basis. He said that the previous year contract expired on March 31, 2010 and the grace period for the contract was April 30, 2010.  He said that they have started negotiations with Microsoft regarding the price after including the new requirements on February 24, 2010. He reported that Microsoft has given the following two options to IBA:   

a)      Renew the current Microsoft Campus and School Agreement (CASA) with Standard CAL after incorporating additional requirements, OR

 

b)      Go for Microsoft Campus and School Agreement (CASA) with Enterprise CAL.

 

3.2              The Director ICT stated that the benefit of Enterprise CAL is that they are switching from McAfee AntiVirus to Microsoft ForeFront suite which is bundled with Microsoft Campus and School Agreement and they would not need to renew the AntiVirus every year which costs around Rs.750,000/-  Thus they would have a saving of Rs.750,000/- if they go with Microsoft Campus and School Agreement with Enterprise CAL.

3.4              The Director ICT reported that the PCs procured under current project / agreement with New Horizon Technologies (NHT) were procured without Operating System (O/S) because the prices offered by NHT for O/S starter were much higher than the anticipated price they would get under MS-CASA. He said that the requirement of Microsoft regarding original software is that every PC should have at least Starter Edition of Operating System (Windows). As the Starter Editions were not included in the procurement of desktops, they are also included in the current licensing. He said that the price of Starter Edition would be payable only once. 3.6              The Director ICT replied that it is not part of that agreement.

ACTION

Item No. 4      To consider and approve additional cost of Administration Building at City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that an additional area of 5,000 Sq-ft was approved for the Administration Building at City Campus in the 79th meeting of the Executive Committee held on 14th December, 2009. Moreover, the case was also forwarded to the Audit & Finance Committee in its meeting held on 19th December, 2009 to accommodate essential requirements of ICT Department as well as to provide two prayer halls one each for gents and ladies. The area is an extension of the under construction work. The structure is monolithic with the structure under construction as well as the services such as electrical, ICT data network and water supply. He said that it is not possible to tender this portion and to award it to another contractor. Moreover the present contractor has already been engaged based on open competition. It was thus necessary that the same contactor at site should continue to work on this additional area.

 

4.2       The Director Projects reported that the construction drawings and BOQ of the extensions were furnished to the Contractor who was asked to submit the cost of this portion. The BOQ contained the items already existing in the BOQ of awarded contract where the existing rates were applicable. However, a number of new items had to be included in the BOQ; rate analysis of each item was obtained on 20th January, 2010.

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that initially the contractor submitted the revised project cost of Rs.84.03 million on 27th January, 2010 to the Architects and Project Managers which was scrutinized by them. This included revised prices of Civil, ID, Plumbing, Electrical and HVAC works on the revised BOQ issued by Architect on 20th January, 2010. The price also included a premium of 12% on the contract BOQ rates for hold-up and 10% increase in fuel prices as well as 15% increase in material prices. After detailed discussions held subsequently with the contractor, they were asked to review their rates for new items and submit rate analysis, withdraw their claim for premium on contract rates and review the identified corrections in some items of revised BOQ. The contractor submitted rate analysis of major items of the revised BOQ and final price after review and correction on 9th March, 2010. The quoted price of Rs.84.03 million was scrutinized by Architect, their consultants and finally by M/s. Integ, the Project Managers who have recommended an amount of Rs.82.13 million. The Director Projects further reported that M/s. Integ have also recommended an additional provision of Rs.6.0 million for revisions expected as most of the Architectural, structural and Electrical layouts still need revision.

 

4.4.      The Director Projects informed the Committee that ICT has asked for a modern Data Centre in Admin Building. He said that the Civil, Electrical and HVAC works for this Data Centre is being finalized. The design criteria for HVAC works of Data Centre has been approved by IBA on 27th April, 2010. The Electrical and Civil works including provision of raised floors remained to be finalized. He said that on the basis of the discussion with the Consultants, a provisional amount of Rs.20 million is being included. Thus the total Project cost comes to Rs.82.13 million + Rs.6.0 million+ Rs.20.0 million = Rs.108.13 million.

 

4.5       The Director Projects stated that the current cost of the project is Rs.62.46 million. He requested the Executive Committee to accord its approval in principle to increase the existing project cost from Rs.62.46 to Rs.108.13 million including Rs.20 million for the special utilities for the Data Centre. The contractor already working on the building is engaged in the construction of structure and related works of the extended area. The final cost of this extension will be submitted to the Audit and Finance Committee for approval as per the PP&P of IBA.

 

4.6              The Director emphasized upon the Central Purchase Committee to exercise due diligence in evaluation of the proposals presented to the CPC.

 

4.7              The Registrar informed the Committee that, if needed, CPC forms a Technical Evaluation Committee which is generally not a part of the CPC. He suggested to make a technical evaluation of the requirement of special utilities separately.

 

DECISION

 

4.8       The Committee requested Dr. Sayeed Ghani to hold a meeting with the Director ICT, the Director Projects and look into the requirements of additional Rs.20 million for special utilities for the Data Centre.

 

ACTION

 

4.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani to hold a meeting with the Director ICT, and the Director Projects and look into the requirements of additional Rs.20 million for special utilities for the Data Centre and report to the EC.
