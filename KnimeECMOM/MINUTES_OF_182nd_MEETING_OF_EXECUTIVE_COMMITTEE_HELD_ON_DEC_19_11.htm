MINUTES OF 182ND MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

MONDAY, DECEMBER 19, 2011

 

The 182nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 19, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                  Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                 Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                           Associate Dean, FBA

4.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                  Registrar

5.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                   Controller of Examinations

6.      Mr. Ali Taha                                                        Director Finance

7.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                       Director Projects

8.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                                 Director ICT

9.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                             Director CED

10.     Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                            Director H.R.

 

Mr. Ishfaq Lashari, Assistant Manager, CDC attended the meeting to present up-date on the activities of Career Development Centre.

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

  

Item No. 1      To consider and approve SOP for Transport Department.

1.1       The Registrar said that the SOP for Transport Department has already been included in the Code Book provided to the EC members last week. The SOP includes requisitioning of vehicles, operation of transport, repair/maintenance of vehicles, vehicle security and documentation, payment of charges for transport facilities for the students, responsibilities and duties of transport staff, use of pool vehicles, etc.

1.2       The Dean & Director said that SOPs should flow from policies and plans that ought to be discussed first in the Executive Committee and then in the A&FC.  He said that we have to consider various options as far as transport uses are concerned.  He said that we have to see how are we capturing the growth of students in providing transport facilities to them. Further he said that we are starting BS(Mathematics & Economics) at the Main Campus and when the Academic Block is ready, most of the classes will be held at the Main Campus so we have to see if we will have to increase the shuttle service or will the present fleet be enough. He said that he wants to ensure convenience to the students in moving from their homes and in between campuses. He clarified that SOPs are issued after the approval of the policy. He advised the Registrar to make a proposal on what will be the transport policy for different purposes; existing availability, utilization and future need. He said that prior discussion of where we are and where we want to go is very necessary before we approve the policy and procedures.

  

DECISION

 1.3       The Committee agreed with the Dean & Director and requested the Registrar to prepare and present the Transport Policy to the EC.

ACTION

1.4       The Registrar would prepare and present Transport Policy to the Executive Committee for consideration.

 

Item No.2       Up-date on Career Development Centre Activities.

2.1       Mr. Ishfaq Lashari, Assistant Manager, CDC presented up-date on Employment Survey, Responsible Citizen Internships, Faculty Advisors, Personal Counselling, Career Counselling, Developmental Coaching, and way forward.  He reported that according to the employment survey till December 19, 2011, out of 290 BBA graduates, 191 have been employed; 40 are unemployed, 13 have gone for higher education, and 46 did not respond. The average salary of BBA is Rs.36,700/- per month. He clarified that the actual number of unemployed may be less than 40 but since they are in search of better offer through CDC, they do not disclose their employment lest CDC may exclude their names from unemployed ones.   He reported that out of 15 students of BS, 13 are employed and only two are unemployed. Their average salary is Rs.44,300/- per month.

2.2       Mr. Lashari presenting the survey result of MBA students, said that out of 220 MBAs, 150 are employed, 42 are unemployed, 10 are not interested, and 18 did not respond. Their average salary is Rs.53,500/- per month.

2.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know if they have any comparison of what BS from FAST and other institutions is getting.  He advised to find out the market absorptions details of BS from other institutions.  He also suggested to keep a track of IBA students on what their existing average salary is and what will be after three years; what is its growth rate.

2.4       Mr. Lashari said that out of 282 BBA/BS students, 189 did their Responsible Citizen Internships at 11 major organizations such as Saylani Trust, Zindagi Trust, Darul Sukun, Fatimid Foundation, The Garage School etc.

2.5       The Dean & Director said that if the students do not do Responsible Citizen Internships, they would not get degree.  He advised to keep track of RCIs.

2.6       Mr. Lashari informed the Committee that eight sessions of Developmental Coaching were held wherein 21 MBA, and 357 BBA/BS students participated and they gave a very positive feedback on this. He said that detailed results were compiled on each competency i.e. Listening Behaviours, Oral Communication Behaviours, Team Work, Leadership, Decision-making, and Ethics.  He presented the ratings of students in each behaviour. 

2.7       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Lashari to coordinate with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui for seeking alumni’s help in Developmental Coaching.  He also requested Dr. Muhammad Nishat to nominate a faculty member who may serve as Coordinator for Personal Effectiveness program for students arranged through CDC.

 

DECISION

2.8       The Committee recorded its appreciation for the work CDC is doing. 

ACTION 

2.9       Mr. Ishfaq Lashari would get the suggestions noted above (2.3, 2.5, and 2.7) implemented. 

 

Item No. 3      To consider proposal for participation in “Dawn Education Expo 2012”.

3.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui informed the Committee of the Dawn Education Expo 2012 that is being held in three main cities of Pakistan i.e. Islamabad, Lahore and Karachi in February 2012. He said that the minimum package is Rs.285,000/-for Karachi only and may be the same for other cities. So in total it would be around Rs.800,000 plus the charges of backdrop etc to be prepared by the agency.  He suggested that the people who would be seated in the stall should be very knowledgeable.

 

3.2       The Director Finance said that last time it was told that the structure can be re-used so if it is reused then there would be lesser cost on backdrop.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed with the Director Finance.

 

3.3       The Registrar said that participation in Dawn Education Expo 2012 in three cities including airfare, stall, backdrop, brochures etc. would cost around Rs.1.5 million and the total budget for Open House is of rupees one million only.  He wanted to know the names of the Pakistani universities / institutes participating in the Expo in order to decide whether IBA should participate into it or not.   

 

3.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that we should ask the students appearing in IBA’s admission as to where they got the information about IBA’s programs from.  This may give an idea about the effectiveness of participating in the Expo and our Open Houses.

 

3.5       The Dean & Director sought advice from the EC whether IBA should participate in the Dawn Education Expo or not.  Majority of the members were in favour of participating in the Expo provided our competitors had their stalls there.

 

DECISION

3.6       The Committee deferred this item and requested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to obtain the list of the institutions attending the Expo and present it to the EC for a final decision.

ACTION

3.7       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would obtain list of the institutions which are participating in the Dawn Education Expo 2012 and present it to the EC.

 

Item No.4       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 4.1       The Director ICT referring to the cost estimate said that in the previous EC Meeting in which H.K. Shah had given presentation, the EC wanted to know if the ICT Part cost of Rs.22.3 million was included in the original tender or not. He said that he got the previous design checked and did not find those items in the previous design. He also said that the cost of electrical work outside Data Centre including 250 KVA Diesel Generator, Electrical Cable, and Phase Sequence Panel which comes to Rs.11.61 million should not be included in the Data Centre Project and should be handled separately.

 4.2       The Dean & Director wanted to know if the electrical work outside data centre is included in the External Development work of City Campus.

 4.3       The Director Projects replied that it is not included but added that he will get that included.

4.4       The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to discuss this issue with the Director ICT and finalize it.

4.5       The Director Projects said that H.K. Shah had given an estimate of Rs.81 million out of which Rs.22.3 million is the ICT Part which was suggested to be checked in the last EC meeting if they were included in the original tender. He said that the finding is that it was not included.  He wanted to know as to why it is necessary to incur this additional cost.

4.6       The Director ICT said that these are very important ICT parts without which they cannot meet the standards required for Tier 3. The details are being prepared by HK Shah, which will be presented to the Executive Committee.

4.7       The Director Projects said that in the last EC meeting H.K. Shah had shown a figure of Rs.15 million as cost of the civil works of the ICT Data Centre.  He said that the amount shown as part of BoQ of Civil Works for Data Centre is Rs.5.10 million and not Rs.15 million.  He said that the figure of Rs.15 million quoted by Mr. Makhdoomi was a total expected savings of the Admin Building cost including the civil BoQ cost of Data Centre as Rs.5.10 million.  He informed the Committee that as of today the total savings expected from Admin. Building is Rs.7.7 million which may be applied towards civil work for Data Centre.

4.8       The Director ICT said that the tender originally given for data centre has already expired so another tender has to be given and it would take around 69 days on going through the approval process and related work due to which the project may be delayed for two months. He suggested that we may take the Main and City campuses projects together and issue the tender for the two projects together so that H.K. Shah may complete the Main Campus homework at the same time.

4.9       The Director Projects said that he has prepared a letter asking the 18 bidders to withdraw their tender and get another tender. He clarified that there was no need to send the tender to newspapers as per the SPPRA. However, he said that if we are doing tender for both campuses then it has to be re-advertised because earlier it was for City Campus only.

4.10     The Dean & Director said that we will go for the ongoing City Campus Data Centre to save time. For the Main Campus tender will be issued separately. He asked the Director Projects to issue the letters to those contractors who had bought the tenders based on ICON design, to return those and obtain the new tender documents prepared by HK Shah. He then advised the Director ICT to immediately send the new tender documents for City Campus to the Director Projects for issuance and the Tender Notice for Data centre Main Campus to the Manager Communication for placement in the newspapers.  He asked the Director ICT that in order to save time he should seek approval from relevant committees / authority through circulation.    

 

DECISION

4.11     The Committee agreed with the Dean & Director that the tender documents for the City Campus data centre be issued immediately and tender for the Main Campus data centre be issued separately.

ACTION

4.12     The Director Projects and the Director ICT would finalize the inclusion of the electrical supply work outside data centre in the external development works so that the cost estimate for Data Centre at the City Campus is properly worked out.

4.13     The Director ICT would send on December 20, 2011 the tender documents for City Campus to the Director Projects for issuance and the Tender Notice for the data centre of Main Campus to the Manager Communication for placement in the newspapers.  In order to save time, if required, he would seek approval from relevant committees / authority through circular resolution.

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      ASCB Accreditation.

5.1       The Dean & Director informed the Committee that LUMS is going to ASCB Accreditation instead of EQUIS and requested Dr. Muhammad Nishat to see the procedure of accreditation by ASCB.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
