Item No. 1      To consider and approve report of the sub-committee constituted by the Executive Committee on the extension of time period and escalation in the cost of laying of water supply system and ancillary works at Main Campus.

 

1.1       At the outset the Director Projects stated that the sub-committee has authorized him to present the report to the Executive Committee on its behalf. He informed that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on 28th January 2013, had constituted a sub-committee comprising Dr. Nasir Touheed, Chairman, CPC, Mr. Moeid Sultan, Director Finance, and Mr. Abdul Mateen, Advisor NESPAK and himself as Convenor to analyze the case pertaining to the extension of time period and the cost escalation of the Contract of the Water Supply System at Main Campus, which was presented to the Executive Committee by the Director HEC Projects and M/s. NESPAK for consideration and approval of the Executive Committee.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that in order to carry out the assignment given to the Sub-Committee, a number of meetings of the sub-committee were held and all required documents were obtained from M/s. NESPAK (Project Manager of the Project ) and the Director HEC Projects. The copies of the documents were received progressively and the last document was received on17th April 2013.

 

1.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the sub-committee has gone through voluminous reports, detailed correspondence exchanged by M/s. NESPAK, minutes of CPC meeting, minutes of the Executive Committee and the weekly project progress review meetings held by M/s. NESPAK and IBA on the projects. Additional information was also obtained from M/s. NESPAK on the cost data on various aspects of the project. After thorough analysis based on the discussion held in the meetings of the Sub-Committee, the findings, conclusion and recommendations were formulated which were given in detailed report.

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that after summarizing the findings, it was concluded that:

 

·         The project had faced difficulties right from the beginning and the circumstances were beyond the control of the Project Manager as well as the Director HEC Projects for certain period when the approval from Karachi University for the right of way to lay down the pipeline was not available.

 

·         Secondly, the design had to be changed due to non-availability of the required water head from Karachi Water & Sewerage Board Bulk Water Supply Line and the encroachment found in the ROW approved by Karachi University. (KW&SB had committed 20 feet head of water at their 64 inch dia trunk main but on actual testing the water head was found to be 8ft only).

 

·         After the approval received by IBA and after re-designing of the intake line from Bulk Water Supply of Karachi Water & Sewerage Board, both M/s. NESPAK and the Director HEC Projects gave priority to the continuation of the pending works. During the holdup periods and the execution phases they did not consider the contractual obligations pertaining to the delays of the Project and the cost overrun and did not move the cases for the approvals of the competent authorities on time.

 

·         The project commenced on January 2011 and the delays in the time schedule and cost overrun was submitted to IBA Management (first to the CPC and then to the EC) as late as January 2013. The documents show that M/s. NESPAK were fully aware of the delays and cost impact but they did not receive any response from the Director HEC Projects in this regard even after writing letter after letter.  Documents are silent pertaining to actions which were necessarily to be taken by the Director HEC Projects.

 

1.5       Based on the information and data available from the furnished documents and as per thorough analysis of the facts, the Committee came to the following conclusion and recommendations:

 

Ø  Documents are silent on the contractual actions which were necessarily to be taken by the office of Director HEC Projects. They were responsible for timely actions to apprise IBA management on the delays and cost overruns and soliciting the management approvals in time. It is now IBA’s management discretion to fix the responsibility for poor contract management and at the same time accord its post facto approvals to complete the remaining works and to save the project from a major legal disaster.

 

 

 

Ø  Extension of 316 days in contract execution period w.e.f November 1, 2011, and additional 90 days for completion of remaining works.

 

Ø  Additional cost of Rs.39.57 million be approved after authentication by the office of Director HEC Projects, as the major costs have already been incurred. Contractually the work has to be awarded as continuation of existing contract through an amendment. The additional works are in fact the continuation of the existing contract and if contracted out to another contractor, it would have cost more, as the circumstances remained uncertain.

 

Ø  Increase in NESPAK’s Consultancy Fee (Design & Construction supervision) due to revision of contract price and extended time period may also be taken in to account at this stage while the Project cost is being revised. This needs to be scrutinized and authenticated by the office of the Director HEC Projects.

 

Ø  According to Nespak's calculations the loss incurred by IBA has mainly been due to Karachi University (KU Rs.2.0 million and KWSB Rs.16.0 million) to the tune of Rs.18.0 million.  IBA has to claim this amount from both the organizations.

 

Ø  According to M/s. NESPAK cost calculations if KESC cable was laid separately it would have cost Rs.18.00 million to KESC Contractor.

 

Ø  Similarly M/s. NESPAK cost shows that Rs.18.0 million would have been incurred by WATEEN if they had to lay the Fiber cable in a separate trench (these figures could not be verified by the members of the Committee other than NESPAK’s representative). KESC and Wateen, instead of digging their own trench utilized the trench under this project.

 

Ø  Office of the Director HEC Projects need to take immediate action to recover the costs as detailed above From Karachi University, KESC, KW&SB and Wateen.

 

1.6       The Director Projects presented the following financial status of the project as per NESPAK’s presentation:

 

a) Contract price at the time of award of contract                           Rs.36,764,101/-

b) Summary of cost as per BoQ                                                     Rs.28,703,040/-

c) Increase in cost of BoQ item due to revision of rates

after re-commencement of work from 20-6-2012                              Rs.  8,370,086/-

d) Total amount of Variation Orders                                                Rs.39,262,537/-

e) Final Project Cost                                                                    Rs.76,335,644/-

f) Increase in project cost                                                            Rs.39,571,563/-

g) Percentage increase                                                                107.64%

   

1.7       The Dean & Director wanted to know the amount which IBA has to incur due to circumstances beyond our control such as approval from Karachi University, change of diameter by KW&SB etc. and the amount involved because of our not taking action on time.  Mr. Abdul Mateen, Advisor NESPAK replied that IBA has to incur additional Rs.2 million because of late approval by Karachi University, Rs.16 million because of change of diameter by KW&SB. KESC and Wateen had to spend Rs.18 million for separately laying the cable/optic fibre which they did not because they laid their cable/optic fibre in the trench provided by IBA. He said that at this stage they are considering the project cost and at a later stage they would consider the supervision fee.

 

1.8       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the escalation is quite high and if there exists any provision for this in the budget.  The Dean & Director said that we will take it to the next year’s budget. Mr. Abdul Mateen, Advisor NESPAK clarified that it was not escalation rather additional work which was not anticipated.

 

1.9       The Dean & Director said that he feels at ease because the delay in completion of the project and increase in cost is mostly due to late approval by Karachi University, re-routing of line and misrepresentation by the KW&SB. He said that the hurdles should have been brought to his knowledge at every stage. However, he said that we will claim the amounts from KESC, KW&SB and Wateen.

 

1.10     Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know if there is any break-up of payment made to KESC.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that all payments are made in advance and there is no breakup.  Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that he had asked for break up from KESC but they replied that they do not have any break-up.

 

DECISION

 

1.11     The Committee recommended the case to the A&FC/BoG for approval.

 

1.12     The Committee decided to claim the amounts outlined in para 1.5 above from KESC, KW&SB, and Wateen.

 

ACTION

 

1.13     The Director Projects would prepare a note for the A&FC for approval for the additional amount and extension in completion time of the project based on the sub-committee report and the decisions taken by the Executive Committee. The note is to be divided into two parts; increase in cost due to inaction on our part, and factors which were beyond the control of IBA and the Project Managers.

 

1.14     Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects would file claims against KW&SB, KESC and Wateen for the amount spent due to their misrepresentation and saving in laying the cable /optic fiber.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve proposal on Library Automation.

 

2.1       Mr. Mohammad Anwar, Head Librarian said that to meet the evolving needs of users in the rapidly changing research and learning environments and to achieve greater efficiency and effectiveness libraries are increasingly looking for new paradigms to deliver improved and completely new services to the uses. New physical and technological infrastructure at IBA motivates a rethink incorporating cutting edge concepts and industry trends in managing the library and disseminating information.

 

2.2       Mr. Anwar said that IBA library started automating its operations in 2004 using LMS developed by Tower Technologies – a Lahore based firm. The system probably was selected keeping in view the library automation needs of that time which has drastically been changed now due to rapid development in information and communication technologies. He said that there is a very basic requirement of an integrated client / server, industry standard library automation systems. The following are the key benefits of this system:

 

v  Obtain increased operational efficiencies, effectiveness, and staff productivity.

 

v  High level user satisfaction and empowerment with improved quality, speed and effectiveness of services.

 

v  Improve access to resources with the integration of variety of tools.

 

v  Improve access to remote users and other stakeholders.

 

v  Provide new services which are not possible with existing system by facilitating wider access to information.

 

v  Improved management of physical resources.

 

v  Will provide strong foundation towards the development of new level of services.

 

2.3      The Dean & Director wanted to know as to how much interaction they had with the users in proposing this system. Mr. Anwar replied that users are the major driving force and it is to enhance our service to them. We have to make the existing system compliant with the industry standard.

  

2.4      Mr. Anwar said that there are two options available; (i) Proprietary or Off-the-Shelf Systems, and (ii) Open Source Systems.  Following are the features of these systems:

 

Proprietary or Off-the-Shelf Systems:

 

· For large, medium to small size libraries

· Specialized and dedicated research and development teams

· Cost of these systems range from $25,000 to $100,000

· Charge annual maintenance fee + heavy site licensing fee based on concurrent users.

· Implementation success rate is high.

 

Open Source Systems:

 

· Work with the concept of community contribution.

· Free with no initial and annual site licensing fee.

· Emerged as low cost solutions for Academic Institution – large number of educational institutions are migrating to open source systems.

· High risk in implementation success (but can be avoided by choosing experienced implementation partner).

· No specialized R&D, majorly depend on community contributions.

 

2.5       Mr. Anwar suggested that we opt for Open Source Systems. He said that there are two Integrated Library Systems, Koha, and Evergreen but he would recommend Koha. The Dean & Director wanted to know if our library support staff will be able to handle this system. Mr. Anwar said that the library support staff would be provided training for which they would charge $2,500/-

 

2.6       Mr. Imran Batada wanted to know if the proposed system will have integration with the ERP system. Mr. Anwar replied that the system is stand alone. Mr. Imran Batada said that he would not recommend to go for cloud computing because at IBA they have their own cloud computing system with ample capacity. He further suggested that since our stakeholders are faculty members, they should be involved and given a demonstration of the system.

 

2.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the Company is based in USA and it becomes difficult to remotely control the system.  He suggested that they should have some representatives in Pakistan. Mr. Anwar said that as it is open source not proprietary product, the technical issues can easily be resolved on line.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee in principle approved the library automation proposal as presented by Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Head Librarian.

 

ACTION

 

2.9       Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Head Librarian, and Mr. Imran Batada, Acting Head of ICT would coordinate with each other in making the system integrated with ERP.

 

2.10     Mr. Muhammad Anwar would arrange a demonstration of the system for the faculty.

 

2.11     Mr. Moeid Sultan would make provision in the budget for the Library Automation.
