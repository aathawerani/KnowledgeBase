MINUTES OF 306TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, JULY 21, 2014

The 306th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 21, 2014 at 9:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FBA
2. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
3. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
4. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
5. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
6. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance, and Mr. M. Sohail Khan, Manager Purchase & Stores attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No.1       To consider and grant conceptual approval to initiate tender process of Operation and Maintenance.

1.1              The Registrar said that new facilities at both Campuses of IBA are in final stage of construction and shall be handed over to Maintenance Department in next two – three months.  He said that the buildings have been designed and constructed as state of art, not only in sense of architecture but also in sense of Electro-Mechanical facility installed inside building, like:

· Gas fired Absorption chillers

· Water cooled electric chiller

· Air cooled chiller (Auditorium)

· High speed elevators,

· BMS control system,

· Air Handling Units

· Modern Bus Riser system

· Complete separate power setup including Transformer, switch gear and

· Latest Sound system for Auditorium

1.2     Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed said that they have developed the strategy and plan for Operation and Maintenance of these new facilities after they are handed over from Projects Department to Operation & Maintenance Department.  He said that the main and critical stage will be the equipment testing and commissioning phase.  He said that Maintenance team must be deputed before that phase.  He said that challenges for planning of high rise building operation and maintenance strategy are different as compared to horizontally spread buildings/facilities.

1.3     In pursuance of IBA service outsourcing policy, and successful implementation of this policy in existing IBA buildings, Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed said that the Technical Maintenance Department is going to initiate the tender process for the Operation and Maintenance of these Facilities. Tender documents for complete operation and maintenance services of Air conditioning and Electrical facilities including BMS, Elevators, Generators and switch gear system have been developed. 1.4       Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to give conceptual approval to proceed the tender process as per SPPRA rules and regulation. 

 

1.5       The Registrar said that the Dean & Director had asked him to find out how buildings similar to Aman Tower are being maintained and how many manpower is required. He said that while making a list of O&M staff required for Aman Tower and MJS Auditorium, they studied the requirements of 12 storyed Faysal Bank  building which is comparable to our Aman Tower.  Faysal Bank has 30 technical staff for  the building. However, for Aman Tower they have proposed 17 staff and for MJS Auditorium 5 staff for eleven different functions/fields. The Registrar said that Aman Tower has got residential facilities also for which some staff would also be required in shifts. Mr. Mushtaque said that at the Main Campus services of four buildings (a) Adamjee Academic Block, (b) G&T Auditorium, (c) New Library Building, (d) New Admin Building will be outsourced through one contractor for which on an average 5 staff per building would be required.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know as to what is the estimated financial impact. Mr. Mushtaque said that the financial impact of the required outsourced staff is around Rs.3.5 lacs per month for the Main Campus and around Rs.300,000 for the Aman Tower and MJS Auditorium which has been provided for in the budget.

 

1.6       The Director Projects said that for the elevators at Aman Tower, Academic Building, and FCS they would hire trained staff through the elevator companies for which they have signed contract for one year which may be extended upto three years as per A&FC advice. Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed said that he has proposed the Lift Operators. The Registrar said that in order to reduce dependency on the supplier / manufacturer of elevators the staff should be available during the commissioning phase so that they may know all the technical details. He suggested that the technical staff should be engaged by September 2014.

 

1.7       The Registrar presenting the Campus Statistics said that there were in total 29 buildings in 2008 and presently there are 47 buildings (including Main and City Campuses, Boys Hostel, Staff Town, APWA Building, and North Nazimabad Plot).  The covered area at Main Campus increased from 169573 in 2008 to 425320 sft in 2014.  Similarly at the City Campus the covered area increased from 55632 in 2008 to 259493 sft in 2014.  The total number of students, faculty and staff in 2008 was 2243 which increased to 3390 in 2014.  The generator power at Main Campus in 2008 was 650 KW which increased to 3000 KW and at City Campus it increased from 400 KW to 2000 KW.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.8       The Committee granted conceptual approval for the initiation of tender process of Operation and Maintenance as detailed above.
