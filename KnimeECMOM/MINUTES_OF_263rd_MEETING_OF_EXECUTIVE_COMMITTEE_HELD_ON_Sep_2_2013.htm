MINUTES OF 263RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 2, 2013

 

The 263rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, September 2, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                        Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                              Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                           Registrar

5.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                                    Director Projects

6.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                                          Director H.R.

7.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                               Acting Head of ICT

 

Dr. Nasir Touheed, Coordinator Testing Services, attended the meeting on special invitation. 

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Discussion on Strategy Development for 2014/15 – 2019/20.

 

1.1       The Director H.R. said that to prepare the strategy for 2014/15 – 2019/20 there may be three options:

 

i) Through small group of faculty, management, students and alumni

ii) Executive Committee and Program Directors

iii) Volunteers

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that we have to see how to develop the strategy and through which methodology agree on; what are the future challenges, and where we want to take IBA in the next 5 years. 

 

1.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the key issue is the involvement of faculty. About the HEC ranking, he suggested that besides the emphasis on publication on ISI indexed journal, local industry related research should also be encouraged. Dr. Nishat said that maybe we are missing the HEC recognized journals while targeting to get publication in ISI Indexed journal. The Dean & Director suggested that while giving KPI, the Associate Deans should give target of publication in HEC recognized and top ISI Index journal to faculty. He said that our ranking is based on ISI Indexed journals. It was also suggested to segment faculty into research and non-research.

 

1.4       The Registrar said that the continuity and viability of the two programs namely BS Social Sciences, and BS Accounting & Finance may be a possible challenge. He also proposed to establish feeder colleges. The Dean & Director said that the viability of the Social Sciences programs will be known after four years, and for Accounting & Finance, after seven years when they become Chartered Accountant. He hoped that the later program would be very successful. He said that instead of feeder colleges, we should strengthen our Summer School.

 

1.5       Dr. Nishat said that unless a faculty thinks of his personal development, institutional development will not take place. He said that people get involved in other matters rather than personal development. Dr. Nishat said that there is a need of coordination and integration as people work in isolation. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that we should have a group of faculty members who may become a model for others. He said that the seniors should guide and support the youngsters in research and publication work and co-auther with them. About the incentive for research and publications, the Dean & Director said that if the research work of a faculty member is published in top ISI Index journal, he / she will get Rs.500,000/- as reward. The reward is recommended by the Research and Publication Committee. The Director H.R. was suggested to announce the best teacher’s award and its criteria in consultation with the Associate Deans.

 

1.6       About the profit centres, the Committee felt that the heads of respective profit centres should be invited to brief the EC about the strategy they want to adopt for their respective profit centres for the next five years. The Registrar suggested that the head of Resource Mobilization should continue to hold his office as he is doing an excellent job towards fund raising.

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee concluded the discussions and agreed on the following key points:

 

· There is a need of cultural change – much emphasis is to be given on creating research culture.

· There should be some mechanism to ensure on-campus availability of faculty at least for 30 to 40 hours.  Associate Deans would come up with a proposal.

· Self-development of faculty should match institutional development.

· For strategy development a small group of faculty, and management should be formed.  Students and alumni can be included at a later stage.

 

1.8       The Committee decided that in order to find out the long and short term plans of profit centres, the heads of these centres should be requested to brief the EC about the future plans for their respective centres.

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the time extension of Prayer Hall at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects stated that the first extension in the construction of the ‘Prayer Hall’ was approved by the Executive Committee in its meeting held on 10th June 2013. The completion date was fixed as 30th June 2013. Subsequently, a delay of two and a half months occurred due to non-availability of material necessary for change in the floor layout as well as the re-working of various areas.  He said that the ‘Prayer Hall’ will now be substantially completed by 15th September 2013.

 

2.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that in its meeting held on August 31, 2013, the Central Purchase Committee recommended the approval of the extension in time up till September 15, 2013.  Based on the recommendations of Project Department and the Central Purchase Committee, he requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in completion of time up till September 15, 2013. He clarified that there is no additional cost impact because of extension.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee approved the extension in the completion time of Prayer Hall till September 15, 2013.

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the Admissions Schedules of Spring and Fall 2014.

 

3.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed, Coordinator Testing Services presented the Admissions Schedules of Spring and Fall 2014 for undergraduate and graduate programs.  He said that the Entry Test for BS Computer Science, and Social Sciences, and for MBA, MS & PhD (Computer Science/Economics) for Spring Semester Admissions 2014 will be held on November 17, 2013. The Entry Test for EMBA for Spring Semester 2014 Admissions will be held on December 8, 2013.  The entry test for Fall Semester Admissions 2014 for MBA will be held on May 25, 2014, for EMBA June 8, 2014, and for MS & PhD (Computer Science/Economics / Mathematics) the Entry Test would be held on July 20, 2014. The Classes will commence from August 25, 2014. However, the classes of EMBA will commence from September 6, 2014.   

 

3.2       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that there would be two rounds of admissions for undergraduate programs for Fall Semester 2014. However, for BS-CS and BS Economics & Mathematics there would be only one round of admission. The admissions test for BBA, BS Accounting & Finance, and BS Social Sciences will be held on March 9 and June 22, 2014 while for BS Computer Science and BS Economics & Mathematic the admission test will be held on July 20, 2014.

 

3.3       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that the advertisement in the dailies about admissions would appear on September 29, 2013.  The Information Session (Open House) in Karachi will be conducted on October 6, 2013 and in Lahore, Islamabad, and Multan it will be arranged during October 25 to 28, 2013. Dr. Nasir Touheed reported that he had discussed it with Dr. Nasir Afghan and he has also agreed to the proposed schedule for Information Session and advertisements.  

 

3.4       Dr. Nasir Touheed requested the Director H.R. to arrange Student Development Program (SDP) on 4/5 Saturdays instead of one go. He said that second half of Fridays can also used for this purpose. The proposed dates for SDP are August 30, September 6, 13, 20, and 20, 2014. The Director H.R said that she would look into it.

 

3.5       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that classes will commence from August 25 instead of 1st week of September which has already been mentioned in the Program Announcement 2013-14.  He said that the reason for holding the classes a week earlier is to overcome the shortage of lectures the students going to foreign universities under exchange program had to face.  Their exams also had to be arranged earlier. He said that by August 13 ‘A’ level results are usually announced.  The intermediate results are not announced even in the first week of September.

 

3.6       The Registrar suggested that interviews and group discussions of the successful candidates of round one should be done along with the successful candidates of round two.  Dr. Nasir Touheed suggested that interviews and group discussions should be held separately for each round of candidates and the successful candidates be issued admission letter because the students would not wait without an admission offer letter and may move to other institution.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee noted and approved the admissions schedules for Spring and Fall 2014 as presented by Dr. Nasir Touheed.

 

3.8       The Committee decided that Open House which has been renamed as Information Session will be held on October 6 at Karachi and in other cities i.e. Lahore, Islamabad, and Multan it would be held during October 25 to 28, 2013.

 

3.9       The Committee noted and approved the change in the commencement date of classes which is now August 25, 2014.

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a) Designing and preparation of tender for the faculty apartments.

 

4.1       The Director Projects suggested that since the proposal for faculty apartments at Staff Town has been approved by the Board, Mr. Shameem Ahmed be requested to get its designing and tender prepared so that as and when fund is available for this project the work can be started which would save at least 4 months time required to complete the designing and tender preparation.  He said that the only cost involved at this stage would be the fee to the Architect.

 

DECISION

 

4.2       The Committee agreed with the Director Projects and decided that Mr. Shameem Ahmed be requested to get this preliminary work of designing and tender preparation of faculty apartments done.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
