Item No. 1      Budget 2009-10 – Status report from Director Finance.

1.1              The Director Finance reported that almost 90% of the budget exercise has been completed. Some clarifications were presently in hand on disbursement schedules for projects, insurance coverage for the IBA assets, medical insurance and allocation of budget for HR Training and Development.  He said that the existing risk coverage of the IBA property is based on lower value of the property and proposed that the value of property should be enhanced and accordingly the risk coverage should be enhanced. He informed that insurance premium for medical coverage is higher than the actual cost on providing medical facilities.

1.2       The Director Projects suggested that there should be a separate section in the Finance Department for the projects so that bills and other project related issues are settled quickly.

DECISION

1.3       The Director advised the Director Finance to finalize and present the draft budget in the next EC meeting.

1.4              The Director requested the Director H.R. to look into the work flow and in consultation with the Director Finance depute a dedicated staff in the Finance Department for capital projects.

1.5              Regarding health insurance coverage, the Director said that if the medical reimbursement is cheaper than the health insurance premium, then we should continue medical reimbursement policy rather than going for health insurance. He advised the Director Finance to provide for reimbursement for medical facilities in the budget.

1.6              Regarding comprehensive insurance for IBA property, the Director said that first we should get a survey through insurance people of the whole building to determine the replacement cost then we can decide what are to be covered. He said that the first process should be to appoint an Insurance Surveyor.  He advised the Director Projects to provide rough figure for the comprehensive insurance of the IBA property

ACTION

1.7              The Director Finance will present the draft budget in the next EC meeting.

1.8              The Director Projects will provide figures for the comprehensive insurance of IBA property and get a survey of the IBA property done to determine the replacement cost.

1.9              The Director H.R. in consultation with Director Finance will depute a person to look after the capital projects in the Finance Department.

Item No. 3      To discuss IBA Panel Review Report.

3.1       Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that the members of the External Review Panel visited IBA twice and held meetings with the faculty, Executive Committee and other stakeholders and on that basis they have given us a report which has been circulated to the members Executive Committee, Academic Committee and Department chairs. He said that the bottom line of the report is that we should mainly focus on our MBA program.  He said that the review panel wants our MBA program to be well defined and should be independent of the BBA program. The MBA program should be more structured and the main hardcore MBA faculty should be allocated to MBA program. We should focus all our efforts in the first instance to restructure the MBA program, provide best infrastructure facilities, best faculty and most of resources to this program.

 

3.2       The Director said that we were going in a different direction but the ERP suggests that we should treat BBA and MBA as separate programs and there should be no linkage between the two. He said that in his opinion there is a lot of merit in the report and it is always good for experienced people to come from outside and make a review.  He said that one of the feed back they gave is that there is no ownership of the whole change management. He said that he has an open door policy. Everybody can come to him, or send email and he wondered as to why people did not convey their misgivings about the change process openly.

 

3.3              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi suggested that we can take our final year students to organizations such as SMEDA to find out whether it can be turned around or not.  Similarly, he said that if we induct students in our MBA program with 2-3 years experience they would be more mature.  Moreover they would value the time when then come from the industry.

 

3.4              Mirza Sardar Husain agreed that we should first focus on our MBA program in the areas of Finance, Marketing and MIS and later on other programs.  We may also allow bi-major. He also suggested that we should look at the total package which means infrastructure, computer facilities, cafeteria etc. He said that we should not repeat the mistake of overlapping of courses. We need to convince the students that if there is any over lapping, then there will be increased level of difficulty for courses offered to MBA students.

 

3.5              The Director said that there appears to be a need to have a Program Director instead of Program Coordinator who has to be very dynamic and high calibre person because the success of the revised MBA program will rest squarely on the Program Director.

 

3.6              Dr. Muhammad Iqbal suggested that the teaching load of the faculty should be reduced so that faculty members may have time to consult more books and journals for a course.  He also suggested that the seating arrangements for the students should be ‘U’ shaped. 

 

3.7              The Director requested the members present to think of and plan for what we have to do for the classes beginning September 2009. Shuttle services between the two campuses, redesigning of the MBA program in the way in which the ERP has suggested by a complete rehash of the curriculum, evoking of interest in the potential students, changes in physical infrastructure are some of the issues which have to be addressed before this deadline.

 

3.8              The Director advised the Director Projects to make available two fully equipped classrooms at each campus dedicated and available only for the MBA class.  The Director advised Director I.T. to create a video network between the two campuses including video conferencing facilities in which a teacher at Main Campus can lecture the section of the City Campus and vice-versa.

 

3.9              The Director requested the Registrar to examine how the two campuses can be connected through a dedicated shuttle bus service for transportation of students and faculty between the two campuses. 

 

3.10          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to what will be the differentiation in our MBA program and others MBA program. He said that people with three years experience would only come for the IBA’s  MBA program if it is so good otherwise they would go to other institutions.

 

3.11          Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that he has identified courses and teachers and has also identified gaps.

 

3.12          A clarification was sought whether Evening Program should have the same MBA or should it be given a separate name such as Executive MBA.  Mr. Zaheeruddin said that the general perception about the Executive MBA is that it is superior to the normal MBA program. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that 5 years experience conditions may be imposed on Executive MBA whereas three years experience should be required for morning MBA.

 

3.13          The Director requested the Director H.R. to enquire from H.R. heads of various organizations employing IBA graduates if there would be any limitations of age limit in job market as a result of 2 to 3 years job experience before getting admission into MBA Program.

 

3.14          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that we should start PR activities for the new MBA program. Articles should be published on the necessity of two years experience. Pre-marketing of the new program is required.

DECISION

3.15          The members present endorsed the recommendations of the ERP and agreed that we should have a completely different MBA program than offered now. The contents of the proposed MBA program will be independent of the BBA program and cater to the market demand. Our best faculty would be devoted to MBA program. Our pedagogy would be a mixture of classroom lectures, case studies, and problem solving activities so that it may be more attractive.  Testing and assessment would also be structured differently from the current practice and those applicable to BBA program.

 

3.16     There will be a requirement of minimum two years experience for admission to MBA program but the Director H.R. will give her feed back after getting in touch with the top 10 employers and enquiring whether our graduates will face any limitations of age in job market as a result of this program.

 

3.17     The program will start from September 2009.  The Open House, the admission advertisements and the program announcement will reflect that we have new rejuvenated MBA program.

 

3.18     There will be three streams of specialization in the program; Marketing, Finance, and MIS and a bi-major also.

 

3.19     We will develop a marketing strategy to familiarize all concerned about the new MBA program. A full time Program Director will be appointed for this program. (The Director requested the members present to help him, Dr. Sayeed Ghani and Dr. Muhammad Nishat in finding the right person – ( an experienced person who commands respect of everybody, has problem solving approach, can coordinate with faculty, should have empathy with the students and knows what is happening outside).

 

3.20     The Director Projects will make sure that there will be two dedicated rooms fully equipped with furniture, multimedia and video conferencing facilities available on each campus for the MBA program. The Director I.T. will help in making I.T related facilities available.

 

3.21     The Program Design Group formed by the Director will frequently meet to finalize the program.

c)         Reorganization of Student Societies at IBA

5.13          In compliance with the decision of the Executive Committee, the Registrar gave a presentation on the re-organization of student societies at IBA. The registrar stated that the presentation was based on the contents of the memo issued by the Director on proposed re-organization of the student bodies at IBA. The presentation included a brief about the existing student bodies, rationale behind the re-organization, underlying principles guiding re-organization, composition and terms of reference of the IBA Student Council (ISC), transition arrangement for the academic year 2009-10, composition and terms of reference of the societies, aim of the societies, and guiding rules for student bodies.  

5.14          Giving rationale behind the re-organization, the Registrar stated that as the IBA is restructuring itself, co-curricula, extra-curricula and service oriented activities of students have to be realigned too in the light of the changed circumstances.  Anomalies such as the following are to be eliminated:

·         Fragmentation between Main Campus, City Campus, Morning, Evening, Executive, MIS programs.

·         Tendency amongst students of simply going through the ritual of padding the CVs with positions occupied in the societies, rather than availing the opportunity to develop self talent and improve organizing / teamwork skills.

·         Instances of overlap in dates and over activities on functions.

·         Difficulties encountered by students in one campus of IBA to participate in co / extra curricular activities conducted by the other campus.

·         Overload of admin and support facilities.

·         Instance of lack of financial control.

5.15          The Registrar presented the following to be the underlying principles guiding re-organization:

·         One single brand i.e. IBA rather than campus or program discriminated

·         Optimum utilization of state-of-the-art facilities

·         A seamless IBA campus with equal opportunities for all students to nurture and display their real talent in various fields such as sports, drama, music, art etc.

·         Increase the number of student societies and realign the focus of the student societies to make them more talent specific, service oriented and management efficient

·         Reorganizing the existing three independent student bodies and merging them into one body, {the IBA Student Council (ISC)}, centrally elected, structured and empowered to represent all communities and effectively coordinate, implement the agenda of the 27 independent student societies.  

5.16          Giving details of the composition and terms of reference of the ISC, the Registrar said that the office bearers of the ISC will be elected through a general poll conducted annually simultaneously at the two campuses in which all IBA students will cast their vote. The office bearers of ISC will consist of:

·         The Dean and Director will be President of the Council

·         Vice President

·         General Secretary

·         Treasurer

·         Six members Executive Committee – three from the MBA and three from the BBA/BS programs.

·         Student Counsellors will be the Adviser to the ISC and all the Societies.

5.17          The principal functions of the ISC will include the following:

·         Organize all social functions such as annual dinners, picnics, excursions, social get together

·         Allocate the budgets for the societies

·         Guide, help, supervise and facilitate the activities of all the student societies.

5.18          The Registrar gave details of the transition arrangement for the academic year 2009-10, said that there will be two coordinators elected by the Main Campus and City Campus students separately to serve as the office bearers of the ISC.  The Vice President, General Secretary, and Treasurer will be elected by all IBA students in 2009-10 only. One Campus Coordinator and two members of Executive Committee for each Campus would be elected by the students of the respective campuses for 2009-10 only.

5.19          The election of the following seven office bearers of the societies will be open to the active members of each society who will enrol themselves in the first week of September. The elections of the office bearers of each society will be held under the Chairmanship of the Patron:

·         Manager

·         Two Coordinators

·         Treasurer

·         Three members of Executive Committee

·         One faculty member …. as Patron

 

5.20          The principal functions of the Societies will include:

·         Organizing a Fresher Fair at the beginning of the semester in September, at which each society shall set up stalls / booths for a week and carryout a campaign to enrol members to their societies.

 

·         Regulating the expenditure of the budgeted amounts and ensuring financial control.

 

·         Guiding, helping, supervising and facilitating the activities of the student of the Societies.

5.21          The Registrar presented the following as the Rules governing the Student Bodies:

·         Membership of these societies will be open to every student enrolled at IBA.

·         Each student can be active member of maximum three societies

·         The tenure of the office bearers will be for one year

·         All candidates will have to abide by a Code of Conduct in which expenditures on election campaigns will be discouraged

·         Student office bearers will be responsible for planning, organizing and managing all the events of their societies

·         If Patron feels that any event proposed by the office bearers may tarnish the image of IBA or create security problems he/she will bring this to the notice of the Dean & Director

·         The office bearers of each society will prepare their annual work plan and the budget associated with it under the guidance of the Patron

·         Each society is expected to organize at lest one mega event.

·         Each society will prepare a Calendar of Events for the whole year that will be submitted to the ISC which will ensure that there is no overlap or duplication

·         Joint events by different societies would be encouraged and promoted

·         General Manager Finance will act as the financial advisor to the ISC and all the societies, and will advise and guide them in their financial matters according to the procedures laid down. He will get the expenses audited the same year.

DECISION

5.22          The Committee appreciated the Registrar for making a detailed and comprehensive presentation on the Reorganization of Student Societies and requested him to email the presentation to the Executive Committee as well as to the faculty.

ACTION

5.23          The Registrar would email the presentation on the Reorganization of Student Societies to the Executive Committee and to the faculty for their feedback.
