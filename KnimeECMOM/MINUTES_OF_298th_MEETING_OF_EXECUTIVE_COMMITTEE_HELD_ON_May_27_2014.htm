MINUTES OF 298TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
TUESDAY, MAY 27, 2014

 

The 298th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Tuesday, May 27, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FCS
3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
4. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of H.R


Mr. Mohammad Anwar, Head Librarian attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No.1 To consider and approve the agreement of M/s. Icon for preparation of design, tender documents, engineers’ estimate and detailed supervision of external development works at City Campus.

1.1 The Director Projects reported that in the year 2012, the Executive Committee desired that the Master Plan of the City Campus be prepared by M/s. ICON as they had renovated and provided their top supervision on the following projects at City Campus:

• Academic Block
• FCS Building
• Administration Building
• Canteen and Lounge for students

1.2 In its meeting held on October 8, 2012, M/s. ICON presented the conceptual Master Plan for consideration of Executive Committee. As desired by the Executive Committee, the revised Master Plan was presented by M/s. ICON to EC in its 275th Meeting held on 25th November 2013. The Executive Committee approved the revised Master Plan after recording many comments of Executive Committee which are to be incorporated in the plan. The Executive Committee in principle had appointed the Consultant M/s. ICON based on their expertise and their work done at the City Campus and that they had already done major works and development of conceptual and schematic design of the Master Plan. The Director Projects said that it is necessary that their appointment be regularized along with their agreement since it is based on direct contracting principles.

1.3 The Director Projects said that M/s. ICON were requested to submit their agreement on this work. They had submitted the same vide their letter dated 3rd September 2013. They had demanded for a lump sum fee of Rs.3,156,000/- (including taxes), for designing, preparation of tender documents and top Supervision. The estimated cost of the External Development project is about Rs.50 million. The percentage M/s Icon are demanding comes to about 6.0% (including taxes). He said that up till now we have been paying them a fee of 5.85% on the projects for their services comprising the designing, tender documentation and top supervision. This project being a smaller one, fee was negotiated with them for carrying out, in addition to their services, the detailed supervision also within the same fee M/s. Icon have agreed to this arrangement. That would dispense with the need for project managers and result in saving of the extra cost that we would have paid to external Project Managers (which have been employed on other projects) to the tune of at least 3%. As such the fee payable comes to 3.0% (instead of 5.85% which they have been charging us) for the designing, tender documentation, top supervision and 3% for detailed supervision.

1.4 The Director Projects said that the above arrangement is universally accepted wherein the Architects carry out the detailed supervision and Construction Management. He said that he has provided three annexures; Annexure I provides their main offer whereas Annexure II provides the details of the detailed supervision services to be provided to IBA. Annexure III provides the extension letter of the services agreement of ICON on 3 Projects on the same terms and conditions which were duly approved by the Audit and Finance Committee. He said that the assignment under consideration is not the extension but a new form of contract wherein we are asking the Architects to provide Detailed Supervision of the Project. He said that if it is treated as a repeat order the percent increase is 6% over the fee of Rs.47 million paid to them on the project cost of Rs.808 million which is well within 15%. He provided details of the projects of IBA done by M/s ICON. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the appointment of Architects M/s. ICON and their fee structure as above.

1.5 The Dean & Director wanted to know if the external development work / Master Planning of City Campus is included in the original agreement with ICON. The Director Projects after getting it verified from his department confirmed that the scope of work as specified in the original agreement included external development work at City Campus.

1.6 The Dean & Director suggested to limit the fee to 5.85% and split into two components; half of 5.85% for detailed supervision as we are not going to hire Project Manager, and half for supervision including designing, preparation of tender documents.

DECISION & ACTION

1.7 The Committee approved the extension in agreement of M/s. ICON for preparation of design, tender documents, engineer’s estimate and detailed supervision of external development works at City Campus. The 5.85% fee to be divided into two components; half of 5.85% for detailed supervision, and half for preparation of design, tender documents, engineer’s estimate etc.


Item No.2 To consider and approve the cost and Time line of Technology Centre.

2.1 The Director Projects reported that on May 19, 2014, a detailed inspection of Ground Floor of Multi-purpose Building was conducted by him, his team, the Head of ICT and his team in order to finalize the planning of ‘Technology Centre’ on the ground floor of Multipurpose Building. He said that the earlier planning from the Registrar had to be reviewed and adjusted to accommodate the server room. He said that the Head of ICT agreed that the design prepared by M/s. NESPAK had to be implemented in order to have proper server room and its services like cooling equipment, fire alarm system, fire suppression system and electrical system.

2.2 The Director Projects further informed the Committee that the basic planning of civil and interior work on the areas other than the server room on the ground floor has been done. This work can be combined with the civil work of the interior of the 1st floor. However, the server room work being a speciality job is to be tendered separately to speciality contractors on turnkey basis. This will require a period of six months, which will include tendering process and import of cooling equipment. As such the ground floor will be ready by end November 2014.

2.3 The Director Projects reported that the cost of the server room is estimated at Rs.10 million and the estimate for the interior civil works for Ground Floor and First Floor is being prepared. He said that as the First Floor and Ground is fully occupied they need to create alternate space for occupants to move partially either to other places or wait for the permanent relocation of occupants to Administration Building in September 2014. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the cost estimate of Server Room and its construction time line.

2.4 The Dean & Director said that the estimate of Rs.10 million is too high as we are not setting up the technology centre de novo. We are simply moving the servers to the ground floor main hall. The Registrar also said that all the equipments, hardware and power supply are in place and the cooling equipment, fire alarm system, fire suppression system and electrical system should not require such a large amount. Mr. Imran Batada said that the major expenditure is on electrical items. He said that he wanted to place tender for electrical work and cooling system separately so that the work can be done at a minimum cost but he said that he has no expertise in these fields. However, he said that as they have got good exposure they may take care of networking. He said that he had initially budgeted Rs.5 million for such work to be carried out on turn key basis. However, he said that to let out and supervise other works he would need cooperation from Admin Department. The Registrar while assuring full cooperation from the Admin suggested that Mr. Mushtaque can be involved in the electrical work. He suggested that for cooling they can procure four floor standing ACs. He said that for cooling, fire detection and fire suppression Rs.5 million should suffice. He advised to issue tender for each work separately in coordination with Mr. Mushtaque.

2.5 The Director Projects presented the layout of the ground floor of the MP Building (which is to be named as NBP Technology Centre). He said that the server room, battery room, power centre all will be accommodated on the ground floor and the general store will occupy the space where ICT procurement staff is currently sitting. The ICT procurement staff will be moved to the first floor. The Director Projects said that they will be able to make two floors of the Admin Building operational by July 15, 2014 when some offices of MP Building can be shifted. The Registrar suggested that first the program office should be shifted to the Admin. Building when the floor is ready. The wall in the Computer lab of MP Building should be removed. Simultaneously new computer desks and chairs should be ordered for the 20 computer stations to be added to the existing labs.

2.6 The Registrar said that they had physically visited and checked the size of the existing store and realized that they would require more space. The Registrar said that the space allocated for storage of spares, and offices for store staff was insufficient. He proposed that the existing store rooms along the boundary wall and south of the under-construction workshop block may be retained. The Director Projects said that he had visited the site and found that this was possible but involved re-routing of the bulk water line and risk involved when rocks will be cut just next to the building which is not a framed structure. Works to strengthen the existing room as well as improve their facade to match it with surroundings will be required.

DECISION

2.7 The Committee approved Rs.5 million for the cooling equipment, fire alarm system, fire suppression system, electrical system, IP Phone and internet work and other allied work at the Technology Centre.

ACTION

2.8 The Head of ICT in coordination with Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed would issue tenders for each work separately and ensure that the work is completed and the Technology Centre is ready by end of November 2014.


Item No. 3 To review the conceptual design of the Gateway to IBA Main Campus.

3.1 The Director Projects presented the design of the Gateway to IBA Main Campus as prepared by NBCL. According to the design the exit and entrance were at the same place. The Dean & Director said that in order to maintain smooth flow of traffic, the exit and entrance should be from Gate #1 and Gate # 4 respectively. He asked the Director Projects to have a meeting with Mr. Zayed and explain our requirement and location.


Item No. 4 Institutional Pay Contract with Harvard Business School Publications for Case Method Teaching for MBA Program.

4.1 Mr. Mohammad Anwar said that as there is shift in pedagogy from lecture-base to case method in MBA. He said that the HBSP – IBA Institutional Pay Contract, will ensure the provision of Cases to the faculty and will have following benefits:

• Globally acclaimed pedagogy adopted by top business schools.
• IBA will join the group of HBS Partner Institutes.
• Access to network support for training, development, and capacity building.
• Access to robust course and teaching contents including teaching notes.
• Distinctive positioning as to the peer Institutions.
• Will enhance MBA program competitiveness and thus of the graduates.
• Pay the way to international accreditations – AACSB
• Shun the use of illegal and unethical teaching contents.

4.2 Mr. Anwar said that there are two modules; Standard Course Pack, and Extended Course Pack. Standard Course pack includes unlimited access to the following content at a fixed fee:

• HBS Cases, HBP Brief Cases and Case notes
• HBR and newsletter articles
• Digitized HBS Press book chapters
• Multimedia cases and video cases
• Case Analysis Coach (a comprehensive, concise student tutorial for analysing, discussing and writing about cases)

The Extended Course Pack includes the content covered in Standard Course Pack. In addition, available partner schools’ case collections, articles and book chapters will also be available.

4.3 Mr. Anwar said that 18 online simulations and seven online courses are also available in the areas of Finance, Marketing, Operations & Service Management, Organizational Behavior, and Strategy. Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked Mr. Anwar to give a proposal as to which simulations we should acquire. He also wanted to know as to how many simulations faculty members are using currently. 4.4 Mr. Anwar said that if they go for Extended License @$89 per student per year for 350 MBA students, it would cost US$31,150/- He said that if this amount is charged to the students, it would cost Rs.9000/- per student/year. Mr. Anwar said that in last two years they purchased 324 cases from different sources for which they had to pay US$12,000. The Dean & Director said that the number of students in MBA program is less than 350; it may be 180. He said that initially we can provide this facility for the MBA Morning Program only. He said that first year IBA will pay the license fee and next year we may consider whether to charge the students depending upon its effectiveness and attraction. He further said that since all teachers are not teaching case studies, we do not need all the cases. Mr. Anwar said that the Program Coordinator can design the curriculum in a way that case studies are also covered.

4.5 The Registrar said that at LUMS there is an LCD which displays the services and journals that are available in their library. He suggested that similar type of LCD displaying the relevant information should be placed in our new library. The Committee appreciated the idea of the Registrar.

4.6 Mr. Mohammad Anwar said that the following may be the challenges on shifting from text book and lecture based method to case method teaching:

• Understanding the pedagogical shift from textbook to case method teaching.
• Adoption / Curriculum Development / Training
• Ownership of the key stakeholders (faculty and students).
• Overall course-load, as case method is requires more rigor than textbook (mainly based on lectures)
• Physical facilities pertinent to case method teaching
• Financial or budgetary provisions

4.7 The Dean & Director said that the License fee will be paid from the Library Budget. He said that Rs.10 million has been allocated for the Library out of which the License fee should be paid. He asked the Head Librarian to prioritise the access by including value resources like HBP Cases and unsubscribing less used resources. He said that the Program Director MBA will have to work hard on shifting from lecture/textbook based teaching to case method teaching. He suggested that Dr. Nishat and Dr. Nasir Afghan should give a presentation to the MBA faculty to motivate them to incorporate Case Method.

DECISION & ACTION

4.8 The Committee approved the Institutional Pay Contract with Harvard Business School Publications for Case Method Teaching for MBA Morning Program.

4.9 Mr. Muhammad Anwar will give presentation to MBA core group about the scope and modalities of IBA-HBP partnership. Dr. Mohammad Nishat and Dr. Nasir Afghan would discuss with the faculty in faculty meetings for the revision of course contents of MBA program accordingly to incorporate Case Method Teaching.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
