MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MARCH 9, 2009 (THIRTY EIGHTH MEETING)

 

The 38th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 9, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean
  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                    Director Projects
  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                         Director I.T.
  9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                        Director H.R.
  10. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                             Director CED

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Budget 2009-10 – Status report from Director Finance.

1.1              The Director Finance presented the status report on the budget formats submitted to Finance Department till March 7, 2009. He stated that the budget formats from the office of Associate Deans, Internal Audit Department and Centre for Executive Education have not yet been received.

1.2              Dr. Muhammad Nishat stated that he has presented the library budget and also submitted estimate for faculty training and development. He said that at this stage they cannot give exact number of faculty to be provided training and development; they can only provide estimates for faculty training and development.  

DECISIONS

1.3              The Director clarified that once the budget is submitted by a department, it should not be considered as final because it would go through various iteration processes. He said that the budget for faculty training and development will be on aggregate and not on individual’s name. The Director requested the Director Finance to include the sources of funding in the budget i.e. HEC funding, IBA’s own resources and other donors disbursements such as Aman Foundation.

1.4              Regarding Internal Audit Department, the Director said that he would not like to expand Internal Audit Department too much. But he said that the Internal Audit Department may engage Consultancy Services for specific assignments for which inhouse capacity is not available.

1.5              The Director said that Mirza Izhar Hussain, Director CEE has not yet prepared business plan for his Centre. The Director requested Director Finance to coordinate with Mirza Izhar Hussain.

Item No. 2      To consider emails of February 25 from Mr. Mobin Khalili on Absence Report on 1st Term Exam.

2.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili in his e-mail of February 25, 2009 attached a list of administrative staff of various departments who did not report to the invigilation duties assigned to them during the First Term exam at the Main Campus from February 12 to 18, 2009.  He stated in his email that all staff members and their respective heads were informed well in advance about the invigilation duties assigned to them. He suggested that those staff members who do not take such an important assignment seriously should be warned and informed that the invigilation duties to the administrative staff are assigned in accordance with a policy decision by the Executive Committee and non-compliance will lead to disciplinary action.

2.2       The Director wanted to know the names of those who did not perform their invigilation duties second time. Mr. Khalili said that he would provide the names of those who absented themselves second time.

2.3       The Director further said that there should be a system of obtaining signature from every individual who is assigned invigilation duty. Mr. Khalili confirmed that signature of individuals is obtained. The Director also suggested that notice of invigilation duty should be sent through email to all concerned with request for acknowledgment of receipt of emails.

2.4       The Director requested the Registrar to get a survey of all the staff to know if they all have access to IBA email.

DECISION

2.5       The Committee decided that the heads of the departments would ensure that the staff members of their respective departments who are assigned invigilation duties report for invigilation duties and do not remain absent without valid authorization and advance notice.

ACTION

2.6       The Controller of Examinations would present the names of those staff members who did not perform invigilation duties second time to the Registrar for calling explanation and issuing warning to them.

2.7       The Registrar will get a survey of the accessibility of IBA emails to all staff.

Item No. 3      To consider email of March 4, 2009 from Mr. Mobin Khalili on the subject of Computers and Printers for faculty to type and print exam papers.

3.1       The Controller of Examinations in his email of February 17, 2009 to Dr. Arshad Siddiqi, referred to the EC decision for arranging computers and printers at both campuses for the faculty to exclusively use for the typing and printing of question papers. He stated that he had pointed out to the EC the importance and urgency of this arrangement on the basis of few incidents that came to his knowledge when question papers were typed by certain teachers on the computers in the City Campus faculty room but the print out of those papers popped out from the printers located somewhere else. He said that such incidents happened again. He said that such incidents could prove catastrophic for the credibility of their examination system. He suggested that in addition to separate computers and printers for faculty for typing and printing of question papers, the faculty room computers should not be linked with any other printers except those that are in the faculty room for the sake of confidentiality.

3.2       The Director I.T. said that the computer in the faculty room is not attached with other printers. He also said that some PCs in faculty room have been upgraded with 1GB RAMs and four more will be upgraded as soon as they get the RAMs. 

3.3       The Director appreciated that Dr. Arshad Siddiqi has upgraded the RAMs of the PCs in faculty room and four more would be up-graded.

DECISION

3.4       The Committee advised Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to arrange to procure heavy duty printer for faculty room. In the meantime, the Committee requested Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to ensure that the PCs in the faculty room are connected with the Printer placed in the faculty room only and disconnected from all other printers. 

ACTION

3.5              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi is to ensure that the PCs in the faculty room are connected with the printers placed in the faculty room and they have no connection with other printers.

 

Item No. 4      To consider proposal from Director Projects for inclusion of Registrar and G.M. (P&D) on the Furniture Committee.

4.1       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects stated that besides him, there were three more members on the Furniture Committee namely; (1) Dr. Sayeed Ghani, (2) Mr. Mobin Khalili, and (3) Dr. Arshad Siddiqi. However, Dr. Sayeed Ghani withdrew from this Committee. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia proposed that Capt. (R.) Ahmed Zaheer, Registrar, and Mr. Shameem Ahmed, G.M. (P&D) should also be included in the Furniture Committee. He also suggested that there should be a quorum of three members because all members are unable to meet at one time.

DECISION

4.2       The Executive Committee approved the proposal of Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz for inclusion of the Registrar and the G.M.(P&D) on the Furniture Committee and also agreed to fix a quorum of three members for the meeting.

ACTION

4.3       The Director Projects would issue a notification of inclusion of Registrar and the G.M. (P&D) on the Furniture Committee.

Item No. 5      To consider email from Dr. Awais Ahmed Tipu regarding earned leave.

5.1       Dr. Awais Ahmed Tipu who served as faculty member of the IBA till August 7, 2008 claimed that according to 2.1(c) of the IBA Leave Rule which says that, “The Director and the teaching staff, i.e. staff engaged in the work of teaching, will not be entitled to encash annual leave(s) unless the IBA engages them in some mandatory activity during the semester break”,  he is entitled to encashment of Earned Leave as he was assigned a course to teach during Summer 2008.

DECISION

5.2       The Committee noted that as Dr. Awais Tipu was assigned a course in Summer 2008, he could not avail his Earned Leave and continued teaching till the completion of three months’ notice period on August 7, 2008. The Committee allowed Dr. Awais Tipu the encashment of his Earned Leave which was reported to be 59 days.

ACTION

5.3       The Director Finance will arrange encashment of Earned Leave of Dr. Awais Ahmed Tipu.

Item No. 6      Status Report from Director Projects on Physical Infrastructure Projects under implementation.

6.1       The Director Projects presented status report on physical infrastructure projects under implementation which included Centre for Entrepreneurship Development and Incubation Centre, Existing Academic Block of Main Campus and City Campus, New Boys Hostel at Main Campus, Visiting Faculty Hostel at IBA Staff Town, Multipurpose Building, External Development (Main Campus), Master Plan of City Campus, Student Centre, Auditorium Main Campus.

6.2       The Director Projects also briefed the Committee of the progress of on-going projects and works which included renovation/refurbishment works at City Campus, renovation/refurbishment works at the Boys’ Hostel, Main Campus,  Lab 16 and Server Room, Wooden partition Offices at the ground floor, Administration, Main Campus, Soil Testing, Water & Sewerage Lines at the Main Campus, 500 KVA Generator for the City Campus, appointment of Electrical Consultant, Girls’ Hostel Extension, Video Conference Room at the City Campus, Renting of APWA premises, Collaboration with Pakistan Medical Association for use of their open areas.  The Director Projects also briefed on the status of property ownership matter being handled by GM(P&D) with the Military Estate Office.

6.3       The Director Projects informed that the renovation of the existing boys’ hostel including kitchen, gym, laundry, and dining hall will be completed by June 30, 2009.

6.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the construction work of multi-purpose building has been started and it would be complete by the end of September 2009.

6.5       The Director advised the Director Projects that he should simultaneously plan for the IT facilities, electrical supply system, furniture and the space to be given to library in the multipurpose building so that the building becomes fully functional when the construction work is complete.

6.6       Regarding renovation of the auditorium the Director Projects said that M/s. Icon were advised to submit detailed drawings and tender documents in February 2009. He said that the documents were expected to be received now in March 2009. All tender drawing and documents have been approved as per the original concept already approved by the Board. He said that he has informed M/s. Icon that construction of gallery in the auditorium is not technically feasible.

6.7       The Director advised the Director Projects not to make payment to M/s. Icon for the drawings if they do not give us detailed drawings. He asked the Director Projects to replace the existing facilities in the auditorium such as false ceiling, chairs and commissioning of central air-conditioning system in phase 1 and to carry out the construction of exterior works in phase 2.

6.8       The Director Projects said that the renovation and replacement work of phase 1 at the IBA Main Campus Auditorium would be completed by the end of December 2009.

6.9       The Registrar said that he would get the existing chairs from the Auditorium of Main Campus removed by March 30, 2009.

6.10     Regarding renting of APWA premises, the Director Projects suggested that we should sign three years agreement with APWA with the provision of extension of contract for another one year.  The members agreed to his proposal.

6.11     To a question whether we should shift UGL or not to APWA premises, the Director Projects said that he would consult M/s. ICON to determine whether UGL and Research Centre will be disturbed by the planned construction work.  He said that the Server Room will not be affected as it would be isolated.

DECISION

6.12     The status report as presented by the Director Projects was noted. The Committee also agreed to the comments / observations made by the Director. The following deadlines were given to Director Projects:

i)         Renovation of the existing Boys’ Hostel to be completed by June 30, 2009.

ii)        Multipurpose building fully functional and operational by September 30, 2009

iii)       Main Campus auditorium preferable date September 1, 2009 but can be extended by one month.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)         Management of the donation made by Aman Foundation to IBA in the IBA Budget.

7.1              The Director said that there are two major segments for which Aman Foundation has made the donation; one is I.T. and the other pertains to Projects (multi-storeyed building and Centre for Entrepreneurship Development). He said that he has asked the Director I.T. to give details of the expenditures made on I.T. related equipments for reimbursement by Aman Foundation. For clarity, he said that all expenses related to above segments will be out of the IBA budget but the source of funding will be Aman Foundation. The execution will be done by the Project Committee.

7.2              The Director Projects suggested that if the building is constructed by Aman Foundation and handed over to IBA as a gift, it would facilitate fast decision making otherwise the building will be delayed in following the prevailing procedures.

7.3              Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the Aman Foundation may get the building projects completed through their Contractor and handover the completed projects to us on turn key basis as a gift.

DECISION

7.4              The matter was discussed in detail. The Director said that we have to find a methodology by which at the end of the day the Centre is transferred to us as a gift by Aman Foundation. Meanwhile the Director Projects is to provide estimate of multi-storeyed building to Director Finance for inclusion in the budget.

7.5              The Director asked the Director I.T. to include expenditures related to ERP, video-conferencing facilities for both the auditoria, linking the City and Main Campuses as a virtual network and technology requirements for the next three to four years in the budget to be charged to Aman Foundation. Video conferencing facilities should be made available in all the four classrooms allocated to MBA program at the City and Main Campuses.

7.6              The Director also suggested that the Director I.T. should talk to “Wateen” for making our projects I.T. enabled through broad band wireless connectivity and their optic fibre pipe.    

b)   Observations of Director H.R. on the workload of Director Projects

7.8       The Director H.R. observed that the Director Projects has to take double load because of two campuses.  It requires more time to do things.

7.9       The Director Projects said that the distribution of the total load in percentage is 39% for new projects which will be his responsibility plus 46% of the existing projects whereas Mr. Shameem Ahmed will have 17% of the work load.  He said that he will have a senior Project Manager and his team for the two Aman Foundation funded buildings whose salaries and benefits would be charged to Aman Foundation.

DECISIONS

7.10     The Director appreciated the efforts the Director Projects has made on making a complete review of all the on-going and proposed projects and compiling projects related documents. He advised the Director Projects that whenever he feels that he is overloaded he should come to the Director and talk to him. He asked the Director Projects to discuss with Aman Foundation regarding the team he would require for execution of the project.

7.11     The Director also appreciated the initiatives being taken by the Registrar in getting the auditorium works completed in time.

c)   Reorganization of Student Societies     

7.12          The Director said that students’ societies such as BASC, BITS, ESF would be redundant because of the flexible policy of taking classes at both the campuses in which a student may take one class in the Main Campus and another class at the City Campus. He proposed that from next year we should have one IBA wide elected students’ Society only instead of three. The Society will have elected office bearers. He said that some students came to him with transition plan suggesting that one main society be formed after one year but in the meanwhile two societies be formed; one for Main Campus and another for City Campus or there should be one Society but it should have its chapters at two campuses.

7.13          The Director suggested that under the Main Society, there would be 24 to 27 clubs. If anybody who is interested in Finance will become member of the Finance Club and those who are interested in H.R. will become members of H.R. Club and son on.  So membership will be based on professional interest. Another club will be based on talent such as photography, literary activities, sports, drama, art etc. The third will be service oriented like IQRA, Go Green, community welfare. The Patron of the societies / clubs will be the faculty members. Office bearers will be elected from amongst the members. Interested candidates will give their plan and through secret ballot elections of Vice President, General Secretary, Treasurer will be held. The societies will be given budget; some of the amount will be given to larger societies and some to smaller societies.

7.14          The Registrar suggested that the societies grouped under these main headings namely: Co-curricular Society, Extra-curricular society, Organizing Committee/Society etc. He said that he would present the structure of the proposed societies in the next meeting.

7.15          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that one society with two chapters should be introduced from Fall Semester starting from September this year so that required changes can be made in the subsequent semesters.

DECISION

7.16          The Committee agreed to the proposal of making one society instead of campus and program based societies.  The Committee requested the Registrar to present the proposed structure of the society before the Executive Committee as soon as possible.

ACTION

7.17          The Registrar would present the proposed structure of the society before the Executive Committee.

d)   Procurement of BlackBerry handsets with service and Mobilink Infinity Internet Access.

7.18          The Senior Manager I.T. through Director I.T. presented a proposal for providing six BlackBerry handsets to I.T. department so that complaints of the users can be addressed to in minimum possible time.  He also suggested that to provide 24x7 IT support services, the IT department should also be provided an official internet connection so that they could be connected anytime or can remotely support service or perform troubleshooting on servers from home.

7.19          The Director asked the Director I.T. if he was convinced that providing BlackBerry handsets will make a material difference to the standards of service.  The Director I.T. said that by providing BlackBerry we will be able to see the response time i.e. in how much time the problem was resolved.

7.20          The Director H.R. said that those who are on sensitive assignments will have to be available all the time. Moreover there are others who need to be provided such facility. She suggested that instead of BlackBerry which is costly, we can provide cell phones.

7.21          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is a problem in BlackBerry. It takes time for replacement if there is any fault.

7.22          The Director I.T. said that a provision of immediate replacement could be included in the agreement.

7.23          It was also suggested that like I.T. personnel, Maintenance and Security coordinators should also be provided cell phones.

DECISION

7.24          The Committee decided that persons in critical positions such as I.T., Maintenance, Security would be provided cell phones. The Director H.R. would suggest the amount for reimbursement for call charges for such personnel.

7.25          The Dean & Director authorized the directors that if these cell phones are required to be given to the IBA officials during the course of work which is necessary for the smooth functioning of the organization, the directors can do so. There is no need to bring such matters to the Executive Committee for approval.

7.26          The Director asked the concerned departments to place the cell numbers of the personnel to be provided IBA cell phones on the IBA portal as well as on the Notice Boards.   

e)   Group emails for students.

7.27          The Director said that group email for students is not available.  He said that there should be group email program-wise and for all students.  All IBA students should have an IBA email account.  He asked the Director I.T. to involve program offices to screen out which students do not have IBA email account.

DECISION

7.28          The Director I.T. will get a group email account prepared for all students and another program-wise group email for students.

f)       Miscellaneous issues.

7.29          The Director advised the Director I.T. to arrange a small ceremony for giving lap tops to the faculty when the lap tops are received.

7.30          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the PCs and printers of the Program Office of the Main Campus are not connected with the Main UPS.  Similarly, the PCs and printers at the Evening Program office are not linked with the main UPS.

7.31          The Director I.T. assured that he would get the PCs and printers at Main Campus as well as Evening Program office connected to the main UPS.
