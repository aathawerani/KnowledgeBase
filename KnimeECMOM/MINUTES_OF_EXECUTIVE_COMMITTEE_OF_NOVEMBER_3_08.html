MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON NOVEMBER 3, 2008 (TWENTIETH MEETING)

 

The twentieth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, November 3, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Chairman, CS& MIS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  6. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                          Director H.R.
  7. Mr. Shameem Ahmad                          General Manager, Planning  & Development

Mr. Imran Khan, Faculty Member and Project Manager, ERP Academic Alliance attended by special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider email from Director and reply from the Controller of Examinations regarding students’ allowed absences.

1.1       The Director in his email of October 16, 2008 stated that while we have reduced the number of absences from six to four we are at the same time encouraging our students to participate more actively and compete at national level of activities such as sports, mock UN sessions, debates, conferences and  seminars. He said that these extra-curricular activities are essential for all- round and balanced development of personality and building of self confidence among our students. He reported that inquiries from other prestigious universities indicate that they allow more absences than we do. He further stated that as the students face surprise quizzes that count towards the final grade they do not have the incentive to absent themselves from the classes unnecessarily. To strike a balance he proposed that we should raise the number of authorized absences from four to six.

1.2              In reply the Controller of Examinations stated in his email that the number of absences allowed to students has not been reduced. As a policy students have always been allowed four absences in 28 teaching sessions of 75 minutes each and six absences are allowed in 42 sessions of 50 minutes each. He said that there has not been any change in the policy.  He was of the opinion that allowing 6 absences in 28 session courses will mean that students can miss 22% of classes which he thought to be a bit high.  He further wanted to know if that would be allowed to all students or only to those who would be officially participating at national level of activities.

DECISION

1.3              The Committee decided to allow a maximum of six absences for 75 minutes sessions and 9 absences for 50 minutes sessions only to those students who officially represent the IBA at national or international level activities and have prior approval of the Director.  However, it would not be applicable to evening program students. This change will become effective immediately.

1.4              The Committee further decided that availing six or nine absences would not be a right of the students and has nothing to do with emergencies.  For all other students, the existing practice of class absences will be followed.

ACTION

1.5              The Registrar will ensure that this policy is notified immediately among all the students, program staff and the faculty and is included in the next Program Announcement.

1.6              The Program offices should inform all the students while the Student Counsellors should notify to office bearers of all societies for wider dissemination.

Item No. 2     Presentation on IBA ERP Academic Alliance (EAA) with SAP and Oracle.

2.1              Mr. Imran Khan, faculty member and Project Manager, ERP Academic Alliance presented the concept paper on IBA ERP Academic Alliance with SAP and Oracle.  The concept paper included the benefits of ERP Academic Alliance Project to the IBA, curriculum and faculty training, project phases, project cost (excluding cost of faculty training, cost of ERP Lab, infrastructural cost and administrative staff cost which have to be determined).  He suggested to do it as a pilot project and see the result.   

2.2              The Committee in principle agreed that the IBA ERP Academic Alliance with SAP and Oracle would be beneficial for the students and faculty of the IBA as well as the ultimate employer of IBA graduates. However, the Committee wanted to know the cost of faculty training and other expenses which had not been worked out. 

2.3       The Director said that there may be two options; to have a consortium with other universities in Pakistan so that offered cost is reduced; another option is to go for pilot project but for that we need to know the cost both for training and maintenance.  If the pilot succeeds we will make a full course.

DECISION

2.4       The Committee requested Mr. Imran Khan to obtain training cost and email to the Executive Committee for its approval.

2.5       The Committee wanted to know the details of all costs related to the project before signing the agreement.

ACTION

2.6       Mr. Imran Khan will obtain the cost of training and details of other related costs and send it to the Executive Committee for approval.

2.7       The Committee further decided that Mr. Imran Khan will contact other universities in Pakistan for forming a consortium for this project.   

Item No. 3      To consider note from Registrar regarding Gold Medals for Convocation 2008.

3.1              The Registrar in his note of October 31 stated that M/s. H. Aftab Associates created some funds to be utilized for instituting a Gold Medal for the overall best student in Marketing Research. He stated that since the said Gold Medal would be of real gold worth Rs.35,000 to Rs.40,000/-, it raises a question regarding the more demanding Gold Medals such as the overall best MBA and BBA Medals. Until last convocation these were only gold plated medals valued at less than Rs.1000/- each. The IBA needs to question / amend its policy about the value of these Gold Medals. He said that at present there is no separate budget for six such Gold Medals.

3.2              The Director said that the donor i.e. M/s. H. Aftab Associates want the gold medal to be given to the student who gets the highest score in MBR and no other course.

DECISION

3.3              The Committee decided that we should check what Aga Khan University and other universities do. If they give gold plated medals then we should continue that tradition and if they give real gold then IBA should also do that.

3.4              The Committee further decided that if it is to be real Gold Medal, then the cost would be covered from the Convocation Budget.

3.5              The Committee decided that this year M/s. H. Aftab Gold Medal for Marketing Research should be given to the student who scores the highest marks in MBR only. The decision taken in the nineteenth meeting of the Executive Committee with regard to M/s. H. Aftab Gold Medal for Marketing Research would be applicable from next year.

ACTION

3.6              Mr. Ali Taha, Director Finance will obtain information from the Aga Khan University and other universities regarding Gold Medal and inform the Committee accordingly.

3.7              The Registrar will ensure that this year the Marketing Research Gold Medal sponsored by M/s. H. Aftab Associates is given to the student who scores the highest marks in MBR.

Item No. 4      To consider request from Ms. Aniqa shah for reduced course load.

4.1              Ms. Aniqa Shah, faculty member, requested that she be allowed reduced course load (two courses) at half the salary for the Spring semester 2009 as she has to look after her four months old baby.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee decided that instead of reduced course load, she may continue teaching as part time faculty.

4.3              The Committee further decided that the Director H.R. would frame a policy on it.

ACTION

4.4              Dr. Sayeed Ghani would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Ms. Aniqa Shah.

4.5              The Director H.R. would frame a policy on reduced course load.

 

Item No.  5     To consider proposal from General Manager (Planning & Development) regarding extension of Girls’ Hostel at Staff town.

5.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (Planning & Development) stated that on the Registrar’s instructions, and with a view to determining the feasibility of creating some additional accommodation, his Department surveyed the open space available at the present Girls’ Hostel.  They found that enough space is available for construction of another 10 to 13 rooms, common wash rooms and an independent dining room. With double occupancy the new facility would accommodate 20 to 26 students. He stated that the estimated total cost for about 3000 sq. feet covered area amounts to Rs.4.5 million which includes the cost of necessary furniture as well. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the proposed expenditure which is not included in the 2008-09 Budget.

5.2              The Committee wanted to know if the cost of internet connectivity was included in the estimate. To this Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied in the negative.

DECISION

5.3              The Committee suggested that Rs.4.5 million may be re-allocated from the budget for Boys’ Hostel to the Girls’ Hostel expansion.

ACTION

5.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed will prepare a paper for the A&FC for reallocation of budget and would also include internet connectivity cost in the estimated expenditure.

5.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed will obtain internet connectivity cost from Mr. Azam Sardar.

Item No. 6      To consider request for and a policy on reimbursement for mobile uses for official purposes.

6.1       Assistant to the Chairperson Mr. Ali Zaheer stated that he has been sending mobile text messages to the faculty members of the departments through mobile for the last two months.  This is done because most of the times information is urgent and has to be forwarded within less time among faculty members and making phone calls and emailing does not get the satisfactory results.  He said that it costs him Rs.250/- per month and requested that he be allowed an allowance of Rs.250/- per month w.e.f. October 1, 2008.

DECISION

6.2              The Committee allowed an allowance of Rs.250/- per month for sending official mobile text messages to concerned persons w.e.f. October 1, 2008 to Mr. Ali Zaheer, Assistant to the Chairperson and Ms. Ghulam Fatima, Faculty Coordinator.

ACTION

6.3              The Registrar will convey the decision to the concerned individuals with advice to Finance Department to include the approved allowance in the salary of Mr. Ali Zaheer and Ms. Ghulam Fatima.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      Finance Coordinator

7.1              The Director Finance stated that there is a need to have a representative of Finance Department available at the City Campus.

DECISION

7.2              The Committee requested the Director Finance to work out the timing and job description for the Finance Coordinator.

b)     Structural Testing of CCS Building at City Campus.

7.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the structural testing of CCS Building at City Campus has not been done so far. He said that for structural testing additional fund is required.

DECISION

7.4       The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed to get the structural testing of CCS Building and the cafeteria of City Campus along with soil testing done as soon as possible.

7.5       The Committee further decided the fund may be reallocated from Refurbishment Fund.

ACTION

7.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed will get the structural testing of CCS Building and City Campus Cafeteria and soil testing at the City Campus done.

c)      Purchase of a Car for and payment of salary to the Coordinator, MBA(Financial Services).

7.7              The Manager H.R. stated that there is no provision in the budget for car, salary and other related expenses for Coordinator MBA(Financial Services). 

7.8              The Director said that like CEE, CED. the MBA(Financial Services) will be self sustaining cost centres. They will be managed by generating revenues and endowment funds.  They will not be a liability on the IBA budget.    

DECISION

7.9              The Director requested the Director Finance to treat the expenses as advance and get them reimbursed from cost centres. The Committee agreed with the Director.

d)     Donation for Earthquake victims of Balochistan.

7.10          The Director H.R. suggested that the employees be requested to voluntarily donate one day’s salary for earthquake victims of Balochistan.

DECISION

7.11          The Committee agreed to the suggestion and decided that the Director H.R. would issue an appeal for voluntary donation of one day’s salary for the earthquake victims.  It was further decided that as the Government has not yet created any fund, the amount so collected would be handed over to Edhi or other trustworthy organization for onward distribution.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
