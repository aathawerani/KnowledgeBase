MINUTES OF THE 50th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON JUNE 1, 2009 

The 50th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 1, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                     Director

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                    Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN             Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                   Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                           Director Finance

  6. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                              Director CED

  7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                     Director Projects

  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                              Director I.C.T

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Implementation Reports / Up-date on the Executive Committee decisions.

 

Up-date from Director Projects

 

1.1              Pre-qualification of Contractors:  The Director Projects reported that tender documents of five projects were issued from April 10 to May 28, 2009.

 

1.2              Washrooms and Pantry for ground floor temporary offices: The Director Projects reported that the cost as per the bids was much higher, therefore, as advised by the Director that instead of building the washrooms and pantry, requisition has been sent to the Registrar to buy one refrigerator, one microwave oven and one tea station.

 

1.3              Request to Tapal / Brooke Bond for installing tea dispensers:  The Director Projects reported that both the companies were approached. But they have discontinued this operation. He said that the dispensers will have to be purchased from the market.

 

1.4              Construction of facilities in the multipurpose building:  The Director Projects reported that four classrooms are being built on the roof of multipurpose building. These classrooms will finally be converted into stores later on. He said that new location will have to be decided with the assistance of the Architect.

 

1.5              Offices for maintenance staff at APWA: The Director Projects reported that he is arranging a room at APWA for maintenance staff.

 

1.6              Visit to HEJ Institute to study system of power generation:  The Director Projects reported that as HEJ engineers were not available, he approached the Chief Engineer of Karachi University who informed him that they plan to construct power house (gas based) to generate 7 MW of electricity. The project has not been started because of non-availability of funds. When asked about the sharing of power generation, he replied that it will have to be sorted out at the highest level.

 

The Director said that he had discussed it with the Vice Chancellor and the VC had advised to arrange with HEJ. He asked the Director Projects to get the facts from HEJ. He further said that if the arrangement with HEJ is cost effective then there is no need to go for our own co-generation system.

  

1.7              Claim from Insurance Company of the amount spent on boundary wall: The Director Projects reported that they are pursuing the Insurance Company and hope to get 60% of the cost.

 

1.8              Temporary Sheds for the students at the City Campus: The Director Projects reported that Rs.2 million has been provided as provisional sum to complete the work. He further said that the Architect is designing the shed which will then be tendered for construction.

 

The Registrar suggested that shade of fibre glass should be arranged at the Main Campus where students can sit.

 

It was decided that the Registrar will identify the place and the Director Projects will arrange temporary shades at the Main Campus.  

 

1.9              Revisit of the manpower requirement: The Director Projects reported that he revisited the manning requirements and is unable to reduce it further.  He further said that it may be applicable to the Maintenance Department which will be given under Registrars control.

 

The Director said that the Maintenance Department will be shifted to the Registrars office w.e.f. July 1, 2009. He requested the Director H.R. to issue a notification for shifting of Maintenance Department from Projects Department to Registrars Office w.e.f. July 1, 2009.

 

1.10          Issuance of letters to the three Architects for the Aman Foundation buildings: The Director Projects reported that the letters to the three Architects were sent after the approval of the Director IBA. He said that M/s. HFA regretted to participate. M/s. NBCL and M/s. FNMH quoted Rs.5 million and Rs.1.5 million respectively. These were rejected by the EC. It was decided to invite proposal from Mr. Shahab Ghani. His reply is awaited. The baseline estimate is Rs.0.35 million.

 

1.11          Assigning garbage removal work to pre-qualified contractors:  The Director Projects reported that the garbage removal work was tendered and the work has been completed.

 

1.12          Construction of pantry toilets and security check post at Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that the pantry and washroom work was cancelled. However, the work of security post improvement has been re-tendered as it was originally a part of whole tender.

1.13          Report on technical and economic viability of the AC Plant: The Director Projects reported that the air-conditioning consultants already engaged have been asked to arrange inspection and provide us with their technical advice.  He said that the report will be ready in a weeks time.    

 

Up-date from the Director ICT

 

1.14          Tender for Technology Partnership Agreement and Long Term Agreement: The Director ICT reported that tenders were floated and the Technology Partners have been selected.

 

1.15          Expansion of AVAYA Communication Manager: The Director ICT reported that the BOM has been completed and the tender will be floated in one week. He further said that the issue has been resolved and they will go with the AVAYA Solution as there has been no response from Cisco and Wateen. He said that the AVAYA set up will have video, data and voice facilities. He clarified that if something goes wrong with fibre optic link, then radio link will be available so there would be no disruption.

 

1.16          Purchase of lap tops:  The Director ICT reported that the purchase has been done.

 

1.17          Policy on depreciation and donation of computers and laptops: The Director ICT informed the Committee that the policy is being prepared. However, he said that the lap top issue needs further decision as this is not going to be useful for schools and the existing faculty want to hold on to them.

 

The Director said that we will make pilot experiment.

 

1.18          Recommendations on the ERP Issue: The Director Projects said that they have to cancel the tender for ERP because the cost of ERP and the training cost are beyond the budgeted cost. He said that the HEC had sent a letter in January 2008 stating that they were going to buy licence at reduced price but the letter did not reach IBA. He said that had they got that letter, they would not have tendered for ERP. He said that the HEC prices are lower than all others.

 

1.19          The Director said that the issue should first be taken to the Purchase Committee and then the recommendation of the Purchase Committee should be sent to the A&FC. 

 

1.20          The Director clarified that instead of separate departmental Purchase Committees, there would be one centralised Purchase Committee.  He also made it clear that tendering is the responsibility of the Directors and not of the Purchase Committee.

 

1.21          Revisit of Manpower Requirements: The Director ICT reported that due to extensive work of TPA, LTA and ERP this has been delayed.

 

1.22          Nomination on the ERP Implementation Team: The Director ICT reported that it has been delayed till a decision is taken on the ERP.

 

Up-date from the Registrar

 

1.23          Shuttle Services between the two campuses: The Registrar reported that quotations have been obtained for 26 seater passenger coach. The matter will be taken up in the next A&FC meeting for approval.

 

1.24          Medical Facilities with Karachi University Clinic: The Registrar reported that he had a meeting with the medical officer of Karachi University. He said that they would charge Rs.2,000/- per student per month for providing medical facilities at their clinic.

 

The Committee considered the fee very high and thus did not approve. The arrangements with the Patel Hospital near IBA will be made use of.

 

1.25          Security Arrangements at City Campus: The Registrar informed the Committee that the barriers have been installed at the City Campus. Height of compound wall is being raised and the Security Coordinator for City Campus joined on May 31, 2009.  SOPs, Charter of Duties for the security coordinators have been prepared. A new Security firm has been engaged on expiry of contract of M/s. Hifazat Security Services.

 

Up-date from the Controller of Examinations

 

1.26          Request for make up examination due to performance of UMRA: The Controller of Examinations stated that the decision of the EC was conveyed to the student through the Program Office.

 

1.27          Rescheduling of final exams for Mr. Ateeb Iftikhar: The Controller of Examinations stated that the student appeared in exams on scheduled dates as the seminar which he was to attend was rescheduled and its dates did not clash with the exam dates.

 

1.28          Request of Mr. Mohammad Omair Azhar for a semester break: The Controller of Examinations reported that the Program Office has been instructed to convey the decision.

 

Up-date from the Director Finance

 

1.29          Agreement on Student Loan: The Director Finance informed the Committee that the role of Dubai Islamic Bank was to design and structure finance facility to be offered by any interested bank in Pakistan. He said that DIB have clarified that they have not planned any such credit facility. DIB is not to sign the agreement. The loan product developed by DIB is for other banks for their review and adoption.

 

The Director agreed that DIB cannot offer the loan product they have designed because they are very small. He said that it is our job to market the product to other banks. He requested the Director Finance to send a letter signed by him (Dean & Director) to the Deputy Governor (Banking), State Bank of Pakistan for introducing and implementing the Student Loan scheme.

 

Up-date from the Director H.R.

1.30          Tuition waiver in addition to T.A. Ship at LUMS:  The Director H.R. informed the Committee that T.As are not given tuition waiver at LUMS.

 

1.31          Line of control between the Director Projects and the General Manager (P&D): The Director H.R. stated that she would discuss this with stakeholders.

 

The Committee decided that in order to meet the HEC requirement, the GM (P&D) be designated as Project Director for HEC Projects.

 

1.32          Travelling and Accommodation Policy: The Director H.R informed the Committee that the Travelling and Accommodation Policy has been sent to the Head of Internal Audit for review.  

 

DECISION

 

1.33          The up-dates presented above were noted by the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider request from Mr. Hizabbar Husain, Head Messenger for promoting him from BPS-5 to BPS-6 and rehiring him on contract after his retirement in June this year.

 

2.1       Mr. Hizabbar Husain, Head Messenger at the City Campus, stated in his application that after rendering almost 40 years of service, he would be retiring in June this year. He stated that after all these years he has not been able to save enough for the education and marriages of his children. He requested that he should be hired on contract for one year after June 2009 and promoted to BPS-6 for the remaining length of his regular service. He also attached along with his application favourable comments / recommendations from faculty members about him

 

DECISION

 

2.2              In view of the endorsements by so many faculty members, the Executive Committee approved hiring Mr. Hizabbar Husain on contract for one year with no further extension.

 

ACTION

 

2.3              The Director H.R. will appoint Mr. Hizabbar Husain on contract for one year after his retirement.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider application from Mr. Muhammad Ameen for facilitating him during entry test and other examinations during the program as he is suffering from a severe eye disease.

 

3.1       The Registrar stated that the application of Mr. Muhammad Ameen was presented before the 35th meeting of the Executive Committee. The Committee had referred the application to the Admission Committee.  The applicant had stated that he was a student of class XII and was interested to take admission in IBA under the graduate program but he was suffering from a severe eye disease Macular Degeneration of both eyes and has a poor vision (at only inches for reading and writing) and cannot be corrected with glasses.  He wanted to know if IBA would facilitate him during the entry test and other examinations during the program.

 

3.2       The Registrar informed the Committee that the Admission Committee considered the application of Mr. Muhammad Ameen but regretted to accede to his request as it is not only the admission test, but if selected, special arrangements will have to be made for him throughout his stay at IBA for quizzes, home assignments, tests etc. which is practically not possible.

 

DECISION

 

3.3              The Committee decided that it would not be possible to make special arrangement for Mr. Muhammad Ameen for the admission test, quizzes, tests, home assignments etc for a period of four years.

 

ACTION

 

3.4              The Registrar will convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Muhammad Ameen.

 

  

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

            a)   Resource Mobilization Strategy for IBA and Implementation Plan.

 

4.1              The Director informed the Committee that Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui and he himself attended a Conference which was organized by HEC-USAID. In that Conference he presented a paper on Resource Mobilization Strategy for IBA and Implementation Plan. (Copy was already circulated among the EC members).  He said that the important aspect of this paper is para 3 in which he has mentioned that the A&FC will oversee and monitor the resource mobilization efforts of IBA and report to the Board. The friends of IBA Committee will do fund raising campaign. He said that different kinds of donations and contributions are being received. He informed the Committee that he has organized them into five; (i) Endowment Fund, (ii) Alumni Fund, (iii) Development Fund, (iv) Faculty Development Fund, and (v) Scholarship Fund. 

 

4.2              The Director said that there will be a Board of Trustees which will be administering the Endowment Fund. The Board of Trustees will consist of all those who have contributed or donated or pledged a minimum of Rs.30 million to IBA in cash or kind. The Director informed the Committee that on-line donation system has recently become available. He said that a separate Alumni Fund will be established in which all regular and one-off donations made by the Alumni from all over the world will be deposited.  Giving details of the Faculty Development Fund, he said that the fund will be maintained mainly in foreign exchange and the proceeds will be used to finance the scholarships, living expenses, travel, tuition fees, and research of IBA faculty proceeding abroad for higher degree or non-degree courses, short term assignments, post doctoral fellowships, research collaboration, etc.

 

4.3              The Director said that we would have to give preference to our donors in all our functions; they would be provided special place.

 

DECISION

 

4.4              The Committee appreciated the Resource Mobilization Strategy for IBA prepared by the Director IBA and recommended it to the A&FC for approval.

 

b)    Rs.5 million given to IBA Fund by the United Bank Limited

 

4.5              The Director Finance reported that the United Bank Limited had given Rs.5 million to the IBA Fund. However, they have not specified whether it is for Scholarship or for any other purpose.

 

DECISION

 

4.6              The Director suggested that this fund may be given to Development Fund with the permission of the UBL. 

 

ACTION

 

4.7              The Director Finance would obtain approval from the UBL for transferring this amount to Development Fund of IBA and report to the EC.

 

c)      Warehouse Requirements

 

4.8              The Committee decided that the ICT Department, Manager Maintenance, the City Campus Coordinator and the Registrar will identify their storage and inventory space requirements and send them to the Director Projects for arranging for temporary warehouse / storage facility.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
