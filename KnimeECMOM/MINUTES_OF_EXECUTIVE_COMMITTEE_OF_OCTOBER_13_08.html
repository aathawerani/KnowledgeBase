MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON OCTOBER 13, 2008 (SEVENTEENTH MEETING)

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                               Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor

  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  8. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                         H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider note from General Manager (Planning & Development) for Renovation/Refurbishment of the Auditorium at the City Campus.

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager Planning & Development in his note of October 11, 2008 stated that on the request from the Registrar he had obtained cost estimate for the renovation / refurbishment of the City Campus Auditorium including water proofing, false ceiling, replacement of all lights and seats and air-conditioning. The estimated cost for the above mentioned work was Rs.20.301 million which included Rs.560,925/- for lighting.

1.2       He stated that as the existing lighting system in the Auditorium needs to be taken care of urgently, he requested the Executive Committee to approve the replacement of the existing lighting system at a cost of upto Rs.560,925/-

1.3              The Director agreed that the lighting system in the Auditorium of the City Campus should be replaced as some speakers / visitors had also pointed out to the inadequate lighting system.

DECISION

1.4     The Committee approved the replacement of lighting system at the Auditorium of the IBA City Campus at an estimated cost of Rs.560,925/-

1.5     The Committee further decided that Energy Savers may be used for lighting purposes.

ACTION

1.6    The General Manager Planning and Development get the lighting system replaced as soon as possible.

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Mr. Muhammad Shafi, Admin. Officer (Evening Program) regarding selection of courses by students.

2.1              The Director in his email to concerned persons / offices had suggested that each student should be allowed to sit in and audit different sections of the same course during the first week of each semester and then be asked to finally choose the section which he / she would like to attend for the rest of the semester. He also suggested that no class should have a size larger than 30-35 and if the number of candidates exceeds the class size, the Program Coordinator of each program will allocate the spillovers in an equitable manner so that all class sections have similar numbers. This according to him will minimise all the petitions received for transfer from one section to another during the semester and also provide an indicator of each teacher’s capability.

2.2              In reply to the email Mr. Muhammad Shafi, Admin. Officer clarified that in the past the students used to attend different sections of the same course and even different electives and then finally chose the electives / sections. But it often created a mess because of the fact that in a classroom of 50, 100 or more students were there in the first class. This situation has improved because of offering more than one sections of elective courses with a maximum limit of 45 students.  He suggested that the names of the teachers are also made known to the students before registration so that they may take the courses with the teachers of their choice.

2.3              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that there is an option available in the CMS for making changes in the elective courses but for regular courses they do not have option to change.

DECISION

2.4       The Committee decided that Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Mr. Mobin Khalili, two department chairs and one program officer will propose to the Executive Committee how to implement the selection of courses including needed modification in CMS along with time line.

Item No. 3      To consider application from Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin Rajput faculty member and Ph.D student for allowing her to attend Asian Semantic Web School at Kasetsart University, Bangkok.

3.1              Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin Rajput in her application stated that she was pursuing Ph.D studies at the IBA in the area of Semantic Web technologies. She had applied to Asian Semantic Web School to be held at the Kasetsart University, Bangkok during December 1-6, 2008 and has been admitted. She stated that the school would immensely help her in further expanding her knowledge and providing an opportunity to interact with world class experts in the area of semantic web.  She requested that the IBA should cover the school fee/registration, travelling and daily allowance which amount to Rs.123,550/-  

DECISION

3.2       The Committee approved the participation of Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin to the Semantic Web School at Kasetsart University, Bangkok.  The Committee also approved the payment of Registration fee of 500 Euro, Daily Allowance of $40/day for seven days and air fare for travelling.

3.3       The Committee decided that first Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin should apply to HEC for travel grant and if the HEC does not pay, she will be paid from the IBA’s Faculty Development Fund.

ACTION

3.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani will convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Ms. Quratulain Nizamuddin.

Item No. 4      To consider application from Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager for fee waiver for EMBA program

4.1              Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager stated that he successfully cleared Admission Test and interview for the Executive MBA program of the IBA and has been offered admission in the same program.  He requested that since he has been serving at the IBA since May 2003, he be allowed fee waiver for the program.

DECISION

The Committee allowed fee waiver to Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager against service bond as per rule.

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)  Security Arrangements

6.1       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects, stated that he had analyzed the existing security arrangements.  There are 24 guards at the Main Campus and 10 at the City Campus; six outsiders and four internal.  He said that there is a need for more guards at the Main Campus because of the large area of staff town. He said that there is a need to enhance lighting arrangements at night.  He also said that a prefabricated room will be installed at the City Campus to receive visitors.  He said that for fire prevention and fire fighting arrangements, a survey is needed.

DECISION

6.2       The Committee requested the Director Projects to place advertisement for prequalification of Consultant for fire fighting and fire prevention.

b)   Disposal of Junk material

6.3              Mr. Mobin Khalili informed the Committee that he had provided the list of junk material at the City Campus to the Registrar. He said that some material are re-usable.

DECISION

6.4              The Committee decided that junk material at both the campuses including reusable material should be disposed off in one month’s time.

6.5              The Committee also decided that we should prequalify for junk disposal and place an advertisement for each disposal. 

ACTION

6.6              The Registrar will dispose off junk material of both the campuses and place an advertisement for each disposal.

c)       Completion of four years bachelors program in less than four years.

6.7              The Controller of Examinations said that after scrutiny it was found that three students admitted in the MBA Program have completed the four years bachelors in 3 ½  years – less than four years time. s

DECISION

6.8              The Committee decided that as the program is of four years, we should give the students credit for completing studies in less than four years time.

d)      Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (Planning & Development) to act as Acting Director Projects in the absence of Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

6.9              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz informed the Committee that he was going on leave for one month from October 19, 2008 and in his absence Mr. Shameem Ahmad would look after the Projects related job.

DECISION

7.0              The Committee noted the leave of absence of Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz and decided that Mr. Shameem Ahmad would be the Acting Director Projects in the absence of Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia.  s

ACTION

7.1       The Registrar will issue a notification to this effect.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
