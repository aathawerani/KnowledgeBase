MINUTES OF THE 108TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JUNE 21, 2010

 

The 108th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 21, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Associate Dean

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                        Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN           Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                     Director Finance

  6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                  Director Projects

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Approval for procurement of OPNET Simulator.

 

1.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that in Telecommunication Research Laboratory (TRL) M/s. Faisal Iradat, Waseem Arain and Raza Abedi are working under his supervision. Their work consists of extensive network simulations. Only this year the group managed to publish five articles in international conferences including two in international journals so far.  He said that at this stage, it is critical that they buy the next generation of simulator i.e., OPNET to continue their research and Ph.D. work further.  He informed the Committee that the software has already been provided for in the budget 2010-2011.  He informed the Committee that after negotiations with OPNET for many weeks, OPNET have shown willingness to offer their products to IBA under their University Program on 50% discounts. It would now cost IBA US$16,500. He said that OPNET deals with universities through their website because it is only available to the qualified (approved) universities.  He said that the proposal has already been approved by the Central Purchase Committee. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the procurement of OPNET Simulator.

 

DECISION

 

1.2       The Executive Committee granted conceptual approval for the procurement of OPNET Simulator at a cost of US$16,500/-

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve concept paper for Alumni website.

 

2.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented the following justifications for an alumni website:

 

·         The IBA alumni department has alumni records from 1957-2009.

·         The alumni department aims to have the updated records of all alumni from 1957-2009. To enable strong and consistent communication with maximum alumni, an engaging and interactive website is needed. 

·         The website has to serve two primary functions:

o   Enable a two way communication between the alumni and department

o   Allow the alumni to register themselves on the alumni database (through the website) and update their profile details time to time

 

·         Lack of security

Anybody can view any one’s profile details. Many alumni have complained regarding the lack of data privacy that this system prevents.

 

·         Fake updates

There is no verification system for the alumni who register/update their profile. Anyone can update any one’s profile.

 

·         Difficulty of registering/updating

The system is not user-friendly and many users fail to register themselves.

 

2.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui also presented the following justifications for outsourcing:

 

·         The website needs to be made on an urgent basis. This requires total attention of the website development team on it.

 

·         The IBA IT department is currently busy with ERP so they are not able to give enough time to the website. The database structure proposed by the IT department is also not automated and simple for the users. Aesthetically, it is not very welcoming and self-explanatory.

2.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui also briefed the EC about the utility of alumni website to the alumni and to IBA.  He said that Rs.800,000/- has been allocated in the budget for this purpose.

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that for outsourcing they would have to float tender.

 

2.5       The Registrar suggested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to see the alumni websites of other universities and show the one he likes to the EC.

 

2.6       It was suggested and agreed that the proposed alumni website and data base should be named as “Alumni Information System”.

 

DECISION

           

2.7       The Committee granted conceptual approval for alumni website which is to be renamed as “Alumni Information System”.

 

 

Item No. 3      Proposals for (a) Establishment of ERP Help Desk,  and (b) Overtime facility to ERP Staff.

 

Establishment of ERP Help Desk

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Batada, Senior Manager IS stated that they were at the final implementation stage of the ERP. At this stage it is very crucial to have some man power for the people having problems in the ERP System during and after the office hours.  For this trouble-shooting task he proposed an ERP Helpdesk whose members would also have a dedicated SIM with the limit of Rs.2000/- each and EVO devices so that people may contact them at any time for the ERP problems. If any users like students/faculty member face any problem after the office hours then they may contact the designated staff member for immediate solutions.  He suggested the following ERP Helpdesk team members:

 

1.      Moin Uddin – Team Lead ERP

2.      Danish Khan – Senior Web Developer

3.      Summaiya Loya – Software Developer

4.      Khurram Wali Khan – SQA Engineer

 

3.2       The Registrar suggested that instead of four persons, one person should be deputed to the Help Desk.  He also wanted to know the statistical data as to how many calls are received after office hours. 

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee approved the proposal of establishing ERP Help Desk to be manned with two persons.

 

3.4       The Committee decided that the persons to be deputed to the ERP Help Desk would maintain a log of the complaints received which would be monitored by the Director ICT.

 

ACTION:

 

3.5       Mr. Imran Batada would get the above decisions implemented.

 

Overtime Facility for ERP Staff

 

3.6       Mr. Batada said that the IS Staff is playing a vital role in ERP Implementation. They have performed and fulfilled all the deadlines during the implementation and now they are about to launch the new ERP System.  He said that some of his team members are playing pivotal role and working after the office hours also.  In order to run the smooth running of the System they are doing late sittings almost on daily basis. He recommended overtime facility to the following staff:

 

1.      Moin Uddin – Team Lead ERP

2.      Danish Khan – Senior Web Developer

 

3.7       The Director H.R. suggested that instead of overtime or honorarium, they may be considered for reward for outstanding work when the project is complete.  Another option that she suggested was to present a proposal for remuneration for the job requiring late sittings and charging the expenses to ERP project after obtaining approval from the EC.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee requested the Director ICT to present a proposal for remuneration for ERP related jobs requiring late sittings and present it to the EC for approval.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       The Director ICT would present the proposal for remuneration for ERP related jobs requiring late sittings to the EC for consideration.

Item No. 6      Request from Ms. Sana Irfan Patel of BBA-8 for make-up examinations.

 

6.1       Ms. Sana Irfan Patel in her application stated that she missed the Islamic Finance (Elective) Final exam held on June 7, 2010 as she got confused with the exam date; mistaking the exam for June 9, 2010.  She requested the Executive Committee to allow her to retake the exam as she was due to get married soon after the exam and would be shifting to the US thereafter. She further stated in her application that she was in her last semester at IBA and had had a stable academic record.

 

DECISION

 

6.2       The Committee considered the request of Ms. Sana Irfan Patel but regretted as there is no provision in the existing policy for make-up examination.

 

6.3       The Committee decided to give Ms. Sana Patel the two options; she may do the elective course from IBA in summer within the allowed time limit of five years, or she may take an elective course from any top 50 universities and get the credit transferred.  In such case she will have to suggest the courses out of which the Academic Committee will allow a course for credit transfer.

 

ACTION

 

6.4       The Controller of Examinations would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Ms. Sana Irfan Patel.

 

 

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)        Absences of faculty and staff from invigilation duties:

 

7.1       The Controller of Examinations reported that there is an increasing tendency of absenteeism from invigilation duties on part of the faculty and staff.  He presented details of the faculty and staff who did not perform their assigned invigilation duties during the final examinations. He said that during semester final exams (June 5-16) he faced serious problems in arranging required number of invigilators at the Main Campus; faculty members openly objected to performing extra invigilation duties; staff were assigned invigilation duties and intimated in advance by the Registrar Office but they did not turn up on different pretexts, some of them flatly refused to come. He suggested to take measures to check this tendency and motivate faculty and staff to take exams seriously.  He said that the only way to motivate the staff is to pay them for invigilation duties at a reasonable rate. He suggested the following rates which will not have any big financial implications but will greatly improve the quality of examination supervision:

 

1.      Term Exams :                 Rs.300/= per invigilation per person

2.      Final  Exams :                Rs.600/=  “       “             “      “

3.      Centre Incharge                         Rs.1000/= per day

4.      Asstt. Centre Incharge Rs.500/= per day

 

7.2       The Controller of Examinations requested the Associate Deans to handle the faculty who do not perform invigilation duties.

 

DECISION

 

7.3       The Committee requested the Controller of Examinations to resubmit his proposal with actual financial implications.

 

7.4       The Committee decided that an email from the Dean & Director to all faculty may be issued for taking the invigilation duties seriously.

 

ACTION

 

7.5       The Controller of Examinations would resubmit the proposal with actual financial implications.

 

 

b)     Review of EC decision of make-up exams.

 

7.6       The Controller of Examinations referred to the EC decision (June 7, 2010) for allowing make up exams for a maximum of three days in case of death of mother or father of a student.  He suggested that such decisions should be regularized by the Academic Board.

 

DECISION

 

7.7       The Committee decided to request the Academic Board to regularize the decision of the Executive Committee for allowing make up exam to a student whose father died a day ahead of final exam and the student had to take the dead body of his father to interior of Sindh for burial.

 

7.8       The Committee further decided that in future Examination Department’s comments would be included on such cases before presenting to and discussing in the Executive Committee.

 

ACTION

 

7.9       The Registrar would make the request on behalf of the Executive Committee to the Academic Board for regularization of the decision.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
