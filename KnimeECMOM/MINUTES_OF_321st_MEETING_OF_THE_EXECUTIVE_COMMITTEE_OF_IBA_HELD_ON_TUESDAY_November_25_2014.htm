MINUTES OF 321ST MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON TUESDAY, NOVEMBER 25, 2014

The 321st meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Tuesday, November 25, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Board Room, 3rd floor, Fouji Foundation Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
5. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

Before discussing the agenda item, the Dean & Director took up-date from the Registrar on the Convocation arrangements.  The Registrar reported that invitations to the guests have been sent on Saturday, November 22; the brochure is ready, medals and shields have been made, gowns have been ordered, and catering arrangements have been finalized.  He said that everything would be in place in the evening of December 4, 2014. 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       Presentation on Implementation of Business Intelligence System.

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Batada reported that they have successfully implemented the Business Intelligence System which is also connected with ERP.  He said that initially, they were waiting for HEC to provide the solution, however, since they did not get a timely solution, they have done this entire project in-house. He said that the Business Intelligence will be useful for all the top management of IBA.  It will provide high level information graphically. It also has features of comparing trends over different semesters/years.  He said that the access has been provided to the top management but he suggested to include Associate Deans, Chairpersons, Program Directors, Registrars, Director Finance, and Head of H.R. He said that as Business Intelligence Licence is issued on named user, they have to purchase 15 to 20 named licences.  He said that the per annum cost of each licence is between US$1000 to US$1500 and the recurring expense is 22% of the licence fee. However, he said that they would try to get further discount and obtain licence after the system is fully implemented.  He made a presentation highlighting the functions and features of the system. The following comments / suggestions were made by the Chairman and the EC members:

-  The Dean & Director suggested to include total number of students in “Students Enrolment” and then show its distribution program-wise, gender-wise, and campus wise.

-  The Dean & Director suggested that there should be faculty-wise, institution-wise, and department-wise grade analysis as well as grade averages for students each semester.

-  Faculty time table should also include their office hours.

 

-  The Dean & Director suggested to juxtapose the grades given by a faculty in courses during the semester with the faculty evaluation.

 

-  The Dean & Director suggested that the statistics of new students intake should show the students coming from Karachi, and outside Karachi, and their gender, as their trend would be helpful in planning hostel accommodations.

 

-  Mr. Adnan Hameed was asked to assign a person to regularly up-date the data on the ERP relating to faculty training and development and the same data can be used in Calcom.

 

-  The Dean & Director said that the units earned by faculty needs to be verified.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani briefed about the methods of verification that has been adopted and being done by a student who has been assigned this job. The Dean & Director suggested to divide the faculty in chunks and use the process of elimination for verification purposes so that the job is made easy.

 

-  The Dean & Director said that for budget purposes they have done trend analysis and when they prepare budget they need to look at the tends in internally generated revenues, student fees, employment cost, operational revenues, operational expenditure, etc.  He suggested that such information should also be included. Mr. Imran Batada said that when the GL is implemented those graphs would also be included in the system.

 

-  The Dean & Director observed that faculty recruitment status has not been included.  He suggested that recruitment status of teaching and non-teaching staff should be included. Dr. Nishat added that Teaching Assistants’ information should also be included.

 

-  The Dean & Director further said that complete information about faculty i.e. total number of faculty, PhD, non-PhD, foreign qualified local degree holders, and the faculty members pursuing higher studies should also be included.         

 

-  The Registrar suggested that the programs offered at Main Campus and City Campus should separately be shown so that he may plan room allocation accordingly.  He further suggested that graphs showing award of scholarships i.e. full scholarships, half scholarships should also be included.  The information would be provided by Mr. Tanveer Ahmed of Finance Department.

 

-  Dr. Nishat suggested that external partnership data should be included.  Ms. Shehreena would provide the required data.

 

-  The Dean & Director said that a number of programs are arranged at CEE and profit centres the details of which should also be included.

 

-  It was pointed out that events organized by Students Societies are not part of ERP; they should also be made part of ERP and future events booking would be done through ERP.  The Dean & Director said that he would send a note to all patrons in this regard.

 

-  Dr. Nishat suggested that extra classes rescheduled on Sundays or holidays should also be included in the ERP.

 

DECISIONS & ACTION:

 

1.2       The Committee highly appreciated the efforts of Mr. Imran Batada and his team in implementing the Business Intelligence system at IBA.

 

1.3       The Head of ICT would incorporate into the system the suggestions noted above.

 

1.4       The Head of ICT would make a presentation of the BI System in the Program Directors, and Chairpersons' meeting and then in the faculty meeting.

 

Item No.2       Proposal for Pizza Hut outlet at Students Centre.

 

2.1       The Registrar said that since January 2013 they have been trying to get contractors for IBA cafeteria.  They approached some multi-nationals also but could not succeed. He said that last month the EC allowed Hobnob to operate for 40 days at the Students Centre which they are doing. 

 

2.2       The Registrar reported that consequent to the success of trial run opportunity afforded to M/s. Hobnob to run an outlet in the premises of the Students Centre, they have been approached by Pizza Hut for permission to execute a similar arrangement, aimed at exploring the financial viability of running such an outlet in IBA.  He said that Pizza Hut has also sent a presentation for the EC.  He said that they asked Pizza Hut to operate in CED also but they said that as they would be having one oven, they would not be able to cater for the CED. The Registrar requested the EC to give conceptual approval for allowing Pizza Hut to operate at the Students Centre on the same terms and conditions as those of Hobnob. He further said that M/s Burger King had also approached for an outlet but they have not sent their proposal as yet.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that if M/s. Burger King come up with their proposal we should consider and allow them also to operate.  

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee allowed Pizza Hut to operate at the Students Centre on the same terms and conditions as are applicable for Hobnob.

 

Item No.3       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

        

a)      Permission for a Souvenir shop to IBA – CED student.

           

3.1       The Registrar said that a student of IBA has approached him for permission to set up a souvenir shop at IBA.  The Registrar said that earlier also a number of students had done it but they could not maintain the quality standard. He said that he asked the student to make the product of quality standard and give a sample of the material he wanted to place on the shop for the approval of the EC. Accordingly, they have sent the sample of the product and the banner displaying the product.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director suggested that they should design T-Shirts also.

 

DECISION

         

3.3       The Committee approved the sample provided by the student and requested the Registrar             to allow the student to place his product at the Souvenir Shop.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
