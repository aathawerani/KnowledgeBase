MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON DECEMBER 15, 2008 (TWENTY SIXTH MEETING)

 

The 26th meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, December 15, 2008 at 2:30 p.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                        Professor
  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Chairman CS & MIS
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                              Registrar
  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                     Director Finance
  5. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                     Director Projects
  6. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                          Director I.T.
  7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director H.R.
  8. Dr. Nasir Touheed                             Professor (by special invitation)
  9. Mr. Imran Khan                                 Faculty Member (by special invitation)

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider email of November 27, 2008 from Director H.R. regarding Teaching Assistants rate at LUMS.

1.1       With reference to the decision of the Executive Committee of November 24, 2008 in which the Director H.R. was requested to obtain information as to how many hours in a week are fixed for the students to work at LUMS, the Director H.R. obtained the following information through informal sources at LUMS:

a)      There is no hourly rate for Teaching Assistants

b)      A lump sum of Rs.15,000/- per quarter (10 weeks) is given to students performing this duty.

c)      Six hours per week is required

d)     Job duties include:

-          Handling of student queries / complaints

-          Conduct of special tutorial classes in subjects where people have difficulties

-          Quiz making (some teachers) and quiz checking

-          Administrative duties assigned by teacher

-          Handing out of corrected exam papers

1.2              The Director H.R. further stated in her note that according to the above information, the hourly rate works out to be Rs.250/- per hour which is more than our present rate of Rs.200/- per hour.

1.3              A member on the Committee observed that for the students to be selected as Teaching Assistant is a mater of prestige because this is not available to everyone.  Therefore, the student selected for Teaching Assistantship should not see the remuneration only.

DECISION

1.4       The Committee decided that the Academic Committee along with chairpersons will decide the terms of reference for Teaching Assistants to be engaged for four months only during a regular semester.

1.5       The Committee further decided that the candidates for Teaching Assistant should be selected by inviting applications from the interested students and be selected on the basis of the courses done and their grades on the courses.

ACTION

1.6       Dr. Nishat will ensure that the terms of reference for Teaching Assistants are decided in the Academic Committee.

Item No. 2     To consider request from Mirza Noman Shah regarding leave policy.

2.1              The item was not discussed but another note from the Registrar related to Mirza Noman Shah was considered under item No.3. 

Item No. 3      To consider note from Registrar regarding Medical Leave Policy.

3.1              The Registrar in his note of December 15, 2008 stated that Mr. Mirza Noman Shah, Incharge Program Office at City Campus had to resume his duty after having a heart attack, violating the instructions of the consulting doctor because he had no leave balance in his account and he could not stay at home for rest on leave without pay.

3.2              The Registrar stated that in most public sector / semi-autonomous organizations, people suffering from long, serious ailments are allowed one month leave on full average pay and another one month leave on half average pay after the expiry of the medical / earned leaves.  He stated that IBA has also precedents of granting such facility to some employees. He suggested that such a facility may be included, in case of need, in our leave policy.  He also suggested that a special fund may be created (e.g. out of the unclaimed caution money of old graduates) from which seriously ill employees may be given up to one months salary if he / she has consumed all the medical leaves allowed.

3.3              The Director Finance stated that no unclaimed caution money amount was available. However, he said that he would check from which account such payments could be made.

DECISION

3.4              The Committee decided that as a special case Mirza Noman Shah be given one months full pay and another months half pay if needed.  The Committee decided that it would not be used as a precedent and will be a one time payment only.

ACTION

3.5              The Director Finance will see from which account the payment could be made and arrange to pay one months full pay to Mirza Noman Shah and if needed (supported by medical advice) release half pay for the second month.

Item No. 4      To consider request from Dr. Awais Ahmed Tipu for leave encashment.

4.1       Dr. Awais Tipu in his note of December 6, 2008 requested the EC to reconsider his case of Leave Encashment. He stated that he served formal resignation and completed the three months notice period. During his stay in IBA, he earned 53 days leave. He stated that he was assigned to teach a course in summer semester during the notice period. Therefore, he was unable to avail the earned leave. He referred to the Leave Encashment Policy of IBA wherein faculty members can encash the earned leave if they are given the mandatory assignment to complete during the notice period. He stated that this provision makes him eligible for leave encashment as he was assigned a summer course to teach during his notice period.  He said that it was a genuine concern of an ex-faculty member who served IBA wholeheartedly for two years. 

DECISION

4.2              The Committee decided that under the existing rule the claim is not valid.

Item No. 5      To consider revised Concept Paper on the IBA ERP Academic Alliance with SAP and Oracle.

5.1              With reference to the decision of the Executive Committee of November 3, 2008 wherein Mr. Imran Khan, faculty member was requested to obtain training cost and details of all costs related to the project before signing the agreement, and email to the Executive Committee, he presented the required details for consideration.

5.2              Mr. Imran presented the faculty training cost The Oracle Academy: Advance Computer Science Curriculum which was US$2,187.5 after 50% off.  He also presented various options for faculty training. The recommended option for the IBA was Option # 4 which provided in-house training by local SAP Certified Trainer to 5 to 10 faculty members @ Rs.2000 per hour. Thus the  cost under Option # 4 was Rs.280,000/-

5.3              Mr. Imran also presented the project cost which was 5000 Euro per annum for University Competency Centre fee for SAP UAP, six Desktop PCs (for 2009-10 implementation) and Rs.20,000 for two USB Harddisk.

DECISION

5.4              The Committee approved faculty training cost and the project cost as mentioned in 5.2 and 5.3 above.

5.5              The Committee requested the Director Finance to study the agreement before it is signed.

5.6              The Committee further decided that pilot faculty for this program would be given reduction of one course load so that they may devote proper time to the training.

ACTION

5.7              Dr. Sayeed Ghani in coordination with Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi and Mr. Imran Khan will implement the decision. 

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      To consider e-mail from Dr. Zeenat Ismail regarding purchase of new Cars

6.1              Dr. Zeenat Ismail stated in her note of December 13, 2008 that a new elective course would be started in the Social Science department from Spring Semester 2009.  She stated that they have a contract with the Chinese teachers that the IBA would send a car for their pick and drop three times a week.  She further stated that at the moment the cars available at the City Campus are in quite a bad shape and do not match IBAs image.  She requested the Committee to approve new cars for the City Campus.

6.2              The Registrar informed the Committee that he has sent a proposal for purchase of two cars.

DECISION

6.3              The Director Finance was requested to see if we can outsource this arrangement.

b)     Installation of Anti-theft Device in the IBA Vehicles

6.4              The Director I.T. suggested that anti-theft device should be installed in IBA vehicles.

DECISION

6.5              The Committee requested the Director Finance to check with the Insurance Company what would be the cost if tracker is installed in IBAs vehicles insured with National Insurance Corporation.

c)      To consider note from Registrar regarding Rules for non-clearance of Remedial Courses.

6.6              The Registrar in his note of December 15, 2008 stated that at the end of the first semester some students had failed either in Maths or English courses being offered as remedial subjects. Since they are non-credit courses needed to be cleared as they become pre-requisites for other subsequent credit courses, he suggested that some rules should be established to guide the Program Offices and the Administration regarding the status as well as the courses to be offered to such students in the forthcoming semester.

DECISION

6.7              The Committee decided that since we have to build the students to a certain level, failed students may be offered a repeat course in Summer for Maths and English. 

6.8              The Committee further decided that remedial course of Maths is pre-requisite for Business Maths. If a student fails in remedial course, he / she will have to repeat the remedial course(s).

6.9              The Committee further decided that Dr. Muhammad Nishat will inform the Academic Committee of the decision of the Executive Committee.

ACTION

6.10          The Registrar will communicate the decision to all program offices.

d)     Invigilation by teachers in other teachers papers.

6.11          The Registrar pointed out that some teachers invigilating other teachers exam papers do not pay much interest in the exam and read magazines, journals or books during the exams.

DECISION

6.12          The Committee decided that a reminder from the Registrar should be served that teachers are not allowed to read books, magazines, journals during the invigilation.

ACTION

6.13          The Registrar will issue a reminder to all faculty that they are not allowed to read books, magazines, or journals during the invigilation.

e)      To consider resignation tendered by Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager

6.14          Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi informed the Committee that Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager, has tendered his resignation and has got a lucrative offer from an IPP. He suggested that he be retained at the IBA and be promoted as Senior Manager I.T.  He further said that Mr. Azam Sardar was willing to stay at IBA if he is given a substantial increase in his salary.

6.15          Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that both Mr. Azam Sardar and Mr. Imran Batada are good people and we should pay something which is market competitive.  He further said that their replacement cost would be far more than what we would pay to them.

6.16          Dr. Sayeed Ghani further said that everything is on the memory of Mr. Azam Sardar and not in the system. 

6.17          The Registrar said that Mr. Azam Sardar should be requested to put the details in black and white as documentation is necessary.

DECISION

6.18          The Committee decided that his salary would be reviewed at the time of increment which would be market competitive.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
