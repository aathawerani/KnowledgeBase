Item No. 1      To consider and approve fee concession policy for IBA employees and their eligible dependent.

 

1.1              The Director Finance presenting the fee concession policy for IBA employees and their eligible dependent stated that the purpose of this policy is to support and encourage low paid employees, their spouse and dependent child for improving their qualification and skills.  He said that all full time employees in service (i.e. both on regular and contract basis), their eligible dependent (spouse or a child) having obtained admission into the IBA would be covered under this policy. He clarified that no part-time / daily wages staff or visiting faculty would be covered under this policy. He also presented the proposed fee concession schedule under various salary ranges of the IBA employees. He presented the following eligibility criteria for Fee Concession:

 

·         Employees who have completed at-least one year of continuous service with the Institution. The concession will only be allowed to employees for semesters occurring after completion of one year of service.

·         Employee/Spouse/Dependent Child who has obtained admission into the IBA as per specified admission procedures.

·         Unmarried daughter till she gets independent either by way of marriage or employment.

·         Unmarried son up to the age of 25 years who is financially dependent on employee and not gainfully employed.

·         This facility is only for one child or a spouse or for the employee himself.

 

1.2              The Director Finance also proposed the following General Rules for Fee Concession:

 

(a)    If the employee decides to leave the IBA, the benefit will be immediately cancelled with effect from his / her leaving the institution and the fee will then be recovered from the employee.

 

(b)   In case the benefited individual leaves the study before its successful completion, the entire fee waiver will be held cancelled and will be recovered from employee.

 

(c)    In case the benefited individual fails in any of the courses, the fee for reappearing in that particular course will be borne by the employee.

 

(d)    If an employee avails this facility for himself/herself, he / she has to fill-in a 3 years service bond for pursuing BBA/MBA program. For Certification Courses, requirement of bond is not applicable.

 

1.3              It was proposed that “the recovery of fee from the employee” from 1.2(a) of the General Rule be deleted as the fee concession was allowed against the services rendered by an employee. Similarly, it was proposed that the condition of 3 years service bond should be removed because an employee becomes eligible for this benefit after one year’s of service and if he / she leaves IBA, the benefit would be discontinued. It was also suggested that the restriction of allowing the fee concession benefit to only one child during the entire service should be removed. 

 

DECISION

 

1.4              The Committee requested the Director Finance to email the fee concession policy again to the EC for their comments, if any, after incorporating the following changes:

 

(a)    The 3 years service bond requirement to be dropped.

(b)   The benefit should not be restricted to one child only during the entire service – it should be open.

(c)    Increase each of the proposed monthly gross pay range by 50%

(d)   No fee to be charged for short courses if recommended by the Head of Department.

 

ACTION

 

1.5              The Director Finance will email the revised draft fee concession policy to the EC for their comments and after incorporating the comments, if any, send the Policy to the A&FC for consideration.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider note from Director ICT on system outage and solution.

 

2.1       The Director ICT reported that there was a serge from the service provider, affecting the Server failure on Friday night (January 15, 2010).  The existing HP server which is at the end of life became unstable and support from principal, enhancement or improvement was not possible as a result network monitoring system was unable to send email alert.

 

2.2       The Director ICT further reported that the hardware failure caused continuous rebooting of server which caused corruption in all users email boxes data at email sever. All corrupted data was ultimately repaired successfully by IT team. He said that the new infrastructure that will be implemented will have effective safeguards and advanced technology to firstly prevent the outages and secondly the recovery would not take as much time due to Disaster Recovery procedures.  He reported that the existing data which was corrupted was successfully recovered and repaired by IT team with zero data loss.  He said that in situations where the prime server becomes unstable, recovery of 100% data is very difficult, but the IT team achieved a big milestone by their expertise, devotion and by spending two days and nights to solve the issue. He said that since they do not have additional disk-drives, the data could not be shifted from the unstable server, as it would have corrupted the data beyond repair. Therefore lots of interruptions of hanging and restarting of server machine were faced. The team had to be physically present as they did not have the means of communication to handle the situation remotely, meanwhile the situation got worsened. Ultimately when the server did not stabilize, another server was borrowed from the vendor and the entire system was recreated on the new server, which itself is a very time consuming process.

 

2.3       Suggesting preventive measures the Director ICT said that the IT team must be equipped with proper means of communication like mobile phones with email and EVO Broadband internet connection to be procured from Communication Budget and the bills to be paid from ERP Project Budget. 

 

2.4       The Director ICT also proposed that the team which worked for almost 48 non-stop hours should be compensated for their efforts. Paying them through the Overtime mechanism would not work as there is a limit of the over times. He proposed that a letter of appreciation with a token amount of money be given to the IT team members. He said that Mobile Phone and PTCL EVO Internet connection were also put forward previously and were approved in Executive Committee but somehow were not captured in the minutes of the meeting.

 

2.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed that letter of appreciation be given at a suitable occasion but he did not agree on paying some token money because he said that it was their job which they had to do.  He also said that for managers it is a basic requirement of the job to have mobile phones.  

 

2.6       The Director Projects suggested that at the time of final evaluation they should be compensated for such job.

 

2.7       The Registrar said that they have to be very careful in payment of compensation.  He reported that this year 25% more overtime would be paid compared with last year’s overtime payment. He reported that in Army commendation certificate is given to best performers and at the end of service the certifications are monetized. 

 

2.8       The Director ICT suggested that some bonuses should be given to the IT team members.

2.9       The Director H.R. and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui did not agree to this proposal.

c)         Honorarium for IBA faculty conducting in-house Faculty Development Program.

 

3.16     Dr. Muhammad Nishat reported that Mr. Ahmad Raza, faculty member, has held a workshop for two days for IBA faculty members on Multivariate Statistical Analysis. He said that the attendance was very broad based and large and this was a demand driven workshop for which excellent feedback from the participants was received. He said that the Dean & Director suggested that IBA should award an honorarium to Mr. Ahmad Raza for the services he has rendered over and above his duties. He said that the Director had witnessed the voluminous background reference material that Mr. Raza had compiled for the participants of the workshop and he has suggested that if we can pay outside trainers  thousands of dollars we should also compensate albeit modestly our own faculty members. To avoid any possible misuse and control this activity there should be some criteria such as demand for the workshop, minimum number of participants enrolled, approval based on the rationale and justification and the volume of work both for the preparation and delivery etc.

 

3.17     Dr. Nishat said that the Dean & Director had asked him and Dr. Ghani to suggest a sum in consultation with HR and Finance to be paid to him under the Faculty Development program.

 

3.18     Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that he had recommended something in the range of Rs. 2500 – Rs.5000 per hour for such sessions, depending on the details of the workshop and the faculty conducting the sessions.

 

3.19     Dr. Muhammad Nishat agreed with Dr. Ghani. However, he added that IBA used to give Rs.5000 per hour to faculty (irrespective of their seniority) for such workshop conducted for FBR and other clients. He recommended for Rs.5000 per hour in this case.

 

3.20     The Director H.R. informed the Committee that they were making a policy on it which would be presented to the Board through EC & A&FC for approval.  In the case of Mr. Raza she agreed to the proposed amount of Rs.5000/- per hour.

 

DECISION

 

3.21     The Committee approved the honorarium @Rs.5,000/- per hour for Mr. Ahmed Raza for conducting workshop on Multivariate Statistical Analysis.

 

ACTION

 

3.22     The Director H.R. would arrange to provide the number of hours and other details to the Finance Department for payment of honorarium to Mr. Ahmed Raza.
