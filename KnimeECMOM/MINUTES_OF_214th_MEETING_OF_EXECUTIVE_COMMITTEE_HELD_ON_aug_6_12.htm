Item No.1       To consider a note from Syed Sultan Raza, retired faculty member, for enhancement of letter grade of Mr. Abdul Rafai.

 

1.1       Mr. Sultan Raza, retired faculty member of IBA, in his email addressed to the EC reported that one of his students Mr. Abdul Rafai of BBA MIS as per ERP Record has scored 92.45 in his Entrepreneurship course 10251 in spring semester 2012 and is losing his ‘A’ grade by 0.05 Marks.  He mentioned that on going through the case of Mr. Rafai, he noted that all students of the class were given 3 extra marks with the exception of Mr Rafai who has been given a lower increase probably 0.50 i.e 2.50 less than his class mates which is a mistake on his part and he admits the mistake.  He mentioned that as per SOP mistakes in grade register are corrected by / under vigilance of the Controller of Examination and the Associate Dean. 

 

1.2       Mr. Sultan Raza reported in his note that he approached the Examination Department for rectification of this mistake but the Controller of Examinations vide his email dated 30th June  regretted citing reasons of absence of authority.  He was advised by the Controller of Examinations to approach the Dean or the Associate Dean for this purpose. He mentioned that he has already declined many requests for grade enhancement from many students from three Courses which he had taught as they did not qualify on merit but Mr Abdul Rafai case is on pure merit. He did not agree to the suggestion of the Controller of Examinations, because according to him acceptance of this request by Dean or Associate Dean would tantamount to grant of UNEARNED additional marks to a student which is not the actual case in point.  It is correction of mistake pointed out by student, admitted by the course instructor and duly supported by record.  Hence it calls for treatment due as prescribed in SOP.  His request to the EC was to:

 

a)         Allow improvement of grades to the distressed student.

 

b)         Use its Collective wisdom to guide him and all other faculty members in such matters for future.

 

1.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is a SOP for rectifying an error but this is not an error; it is a second thought for which Mr. Sultan Raza should talk to the Dean or the Associate Dean.

 

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee requested Dr. Mohammad Nishat to discuss the case of Mr. Abdul Rafai with Mr. Sultan Raza and if there is a legitimate mistake then necessary corrections should be made in the grade.

 

ACTION

 

1.5       Dr. Muhammad Nishat would discuss the case of Mr. Abdul Rafai with Mr. Sultan Raza and if he finds legitimate mistake then he would allow necessary corrections to be made in the grade.

 

 

Item No. 2      Presentation on Social Media.

 

2.1       Dr. Erum Hafeez, Manager Communication giving presentation on the use of social media said that more than 20 million Pakistani NETIZENS are online today and the population is growing at a rapid pace because it is fast, saves time, is convenient and cost effective.  She said that it is more popular among male (75%) and among the youngsters below the age of 24 (70%).  She presented evidence of world student usage of social networks.  She presented the number of tweets and followers at IBA in May 2012 and July 2012 which increased from 122 to 195 and from 34 to 76 respectively. In comparison she presented the figures of tweets and followers of LUMS and KSBL which were a little higher than IBA but the figures of Stanford were in thousands, 5596 tweets and 78935 followers! She presented the nature of content and videos on You Tube of IBA, and LUMS, KSBL, and Stanford in comparison. She also presented a comparison of IBA on facebook with LUMS, KSBL, and Stanford; the number of likes, and talking about of each institution. She observed that there are untailored information about events and programs of IBA on facebook. She presented a summary highlighting what is being done and what needs to be done:

 

S.No.

What is being done

What has to be done

1.

Only Program and event announcements

A new initiative: Open office hours

Relevant presentations, interesting articles and creative status up-dates

2.

Up-till now news and event information was provided on voluntary basis

All sorts of information should be shared in real time.

3.

Slow response factor and limited interaction

Proper official replies should be given to queries; need to be timely and short.

Interactive activities like polls to enhance engagement with the community.

4.

Things like photos and YouTube links are mentioned on the facebook page.

Moreover video lectures, career counselling and topical sessions should be posted.

5.

The page does not contain anything that attracts foreigners towards an enriching & secure environment at IBA.

The page should contain elements for awareness of the society e.g. videos made by students about secure campus at IBA.

6.

Monitoring is an issue:  Dedicated staff member is required to monitor, address the queries and remove the garbage messages / videos.

One central authority assigned to check for violation of policy and take proper action against it. (Should be well-versed with facebook rules and regulations.)

 

2.2       The Dean & Director observed that students have not been consulted in this process. He said that students are very smart in the use of these social media and they can help us in this whole exercise. He said that Media Communication Society should be assigned the responsibility of coordination with the 27 students’ societies. He said that Social Media Committee headed by Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif, and Dr. Erum Hafeez, and Mr. Imran Batada  being its members will decide what is to be uploaded and what not.

 

2.3       Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif presented the proposed IBA Social Media Strategy.  He said that the Social Media Strategy is based on four key design principles. These principles will enable IBA to Listen, Engage, Interact, and Inform prospective students and other stakeholders such as faculty, staff, current students, alumni and society at large. He explained the goals, target audience, content, roles, responsibilities, measurement and assessment for IBA’s Social Media Strategy. In his view content is the single most important driver for the success of social media. IBA needs to produce a constant stream of high quality content for its social channels. The opportunities for producing content already exist at IBA. There are twenty seven student societies that conduct events and activities on a regular basis both at local and national levels. Other important events include the Director’s speeches, Guest Speaker visits, Convocations, Conferences, Reunions, etc. He said that IBA’s Social Media Strategy recommends video recording all important public events and activities, especially the Director’s speeches at Orientation sessions, Societies’ Mega Events, Distinguished Guest visits and other such events.   

 

2.4       Talking about the Roles and Responsibilities, Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif suggested that the responsibility of producing content for social media will be distributed among the 21 service units, with lead being taken by the Program Offices, and the Communications Department. Each Program Director should spend an hour or two live on facebook each month answering queries in real time. Similarly each Society Manager will spend an hour or two talking about his/her Society and life at IBA in general. Each Departmental head should also be available online at specific times and the Dean & Director may also maintain some presence on Social Media.

 

2.5       Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif suggested that we may use Twitter for information to students.  The Dean & Director agreed and asked Mr. Imran Batada to inform all the students that we will be conveying information through Twitter.  

 

2.6       Mr. Imran Batada said that there is none to respond to facebook queries.  The Dean & Director said that new Program Managers will be engaged for responding to the questions and for the questions they have no knowledge, they would refer them to some other person who has the information.  He said that we should have shared resources because dedicated resource does not have complete information. The Dean & Director asked Ms. Saadia Baig, Executive Communication to see the pending questions to find out which areas they are mostly related to so that the relevant person(s) can also be included in the team. He said that Ms. Mahwish But can also be assigned this responsibility to respond to questions in regard to admissions. However, he said that first we have to know the nature of questions and then they should be referred to the relevant persons or teams who can respond to these questions.

 

2.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to provide in-house training particularly to our junior staff how to use facebook.  The Dean & Director agreed with Dr. Ghani and requested Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif to arrange an in-house training for the staff on how to use facebook.  He asked Dr. Ghani and Dr. Asif to arrange a competition to make our website more attractive.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee thanked Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif, Dr. Erum Hafeez, Mr. Imran Batada and all others who worked and prepared the IBA Social Media Strategy.

 

2.9       The Committee approved the IBA Social Media Strategy as presented along with the suggestions noted above.

 

2.10     The EC empowered the Social Media Committee headed by Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif, and Dr. Erum Hafeez, and Mr. Imran Batada being its members, to go ahead with the implementation of the Strategy.

 

ACTION

 

2.11     Te Social Media Committee will arrange the responses to the questions / comments received through facebook, twitter. The Committee will also ask the Program Directors / Program Managers, Chairpersons to have live interactive sessions.

 

2.12     Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif will arrange an in-house training for the staff on how to use facebook.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve procurement of Active Components of FCS Block.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that the “FCS Block”, at City Campus is nearing completion and would be handed over by May 2013. The passive work related to ICT infrastructure in the buildings was already a part of the Building Contractor’s BoQ; however the active Components (i.e. switches, Wi-Fi Access Points, Multimedia Projectors etc.) will have to be procured separately. He presented the BoQ, with estimated price of the Active Components and informed the Committee that the procurement of these components is included in the 2012-13 budgets. He requested the EC to grant conceptual approval for procurement of the equipment, as mentioned in the BoQ, for the TEC and CPC to go through their processing, so that the equipment could be procured by the time the building is ready to be handed over and they may be able to “ICT enable” the buildings before it is occupied.

 

3.2       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what was the justification of coming again to the EC for conceptual approval of an on-going project that has already been approved by A&FC. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that it is in accordance with the Transaction Authority and Policy of IBA.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director said that it is not in the TAP to seek conceptual approval at multiple stages. The conceptual approval is sought at the project inception stage only. Thereafter the detailed project document consisting of various components is prepared and submitted to the appropriate forum for its approval. Tenders are then floated by the respective departments for their components. He said that once the project is approved by the Board, its sub-components should not be presented to the EC for approval once again unless the estimated amount exceeds the budgeted amount or its configuration is being changed.  Approval for the budgeted amount will be given according to the TAP. He further said that for the implementation purpose different components of the projects such as civil work, IT, furniture and fixture may be allocated budget under separate heads and the responsibility of each component assigned to different departments. For civil work, the Director Projects is responsible, for IT, the Director ICT, and for Furniture and Fixture the Registrar is responsible. He requested Dr. Sayeed Ghani, since he is the owner of FCS Block, to make sure that all requirements are included in the project and have been approved by the concerned authorities to make the building operational. 

 

DECISION

 

3.4       The Committee noted the clarification on TAP as given by the Dean & Director at 3.3 above.

 

3.5       The Committee advised the Director ICT that instead of seeking conceptual approval of cost estimate of the sub-components, he should seek approval for re-appropriation of budget if its cost exceeds the approved budget.

 

ACTION

 

3.6       The clarification given at 3.3 above would be communicated to the Head of Internal Audit for his information.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the conceptual design of Faculty Apartments.

 

4.1      Ms. Sana Tanveer, Assistant Architect (HEC Projects) presented floor plans of 3 blocks of proposed faculty apartments for conceptual approval. In the proposed Block 1, with covered area of 1420 sqft, ground floor plan had two bedrooms with drawing/dining whereas the top floor of the same block had 3 beds with drawing /dining. In Block 2, having covered area of 1520 sqft, she presented the layout of two bed room apartments with drawing /dining. In Block 3, with a covered area of 3200 sqft she presented the layout of three bed room, separate dining and living room, courtyard, kitchen with attached utility and a servant room. The bedrooms had attached bath / dress room. The orientation of Block 3 was presented with two options; one in ‘L’ shape, and the other one in horizontal alignment.    

                                        

4.2      The following comments were made on the layout for Block 1 (ground floor) of the proposed faculty apartment:

 

o   Landing space in the staircase should be reduced.

o   The bathroom should be in-between the two bed rooms. Also, wind direction should be considered.

o   Location of entrance door should be changed.

o   Layout of block 1 with two bed room apartment was accepted as one type (two bed rooms) on ground floor, and the 3 bedrooms layout on top floor seemed cramped hence, the idea of 3 bed rooms on top floor was dropped.

 

4.3       On the proposed layout for Block 3, the Director H.R. observed that kitchen has been placed first and then drawing which is not liked by most of the women, the kitchen should be away from the entrance and the drawing and dining should be at one place. She suggested that we should also request our Consultant Najmi Bilgrami to review the layout.

 

4.4       The Director Projects said that he had earlier requested M/s ICON to submit their design for this project and it was handed over to Ms. Sana Tanveer. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that he would talk to Mr. Mukhtar of FNMH to get his critical analysis for this design.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee asked Ms. Sana Tanveer to get the layout reviewed by the Consultant, after incorporating the suggestions noted above and present the modified layout again to the EC.
