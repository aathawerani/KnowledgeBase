The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1 To consider and approve the continuation – extension of NBCL consultancy services for Phase 2 of the new Boys’ Hostel, Block B & C.

1.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that following the EC's instructions he and Mr. Rehanul Ambia had detailed discussion with NBCL and requested them to reduce their consultancy fee from 4% to 2% for the phase 2 of the new Boys’ Hostel, Block B & C. He said that they told NBCL that:

(a) No major change in the structural design is involved,
(b) There is hardly any change in the MEP design,
(c) There is no HVAC design involved,
(d) Specifications need not be prepared from scratch, and
(e) No mock-ups required

In turn, NBCL argued that:

(a) Their own associates involved in this Consultancy assignment take a fixed fee of 2% of the cost of construction,
(b) The project is not a complete repeat. There will be over a 100 drawings to be made which entail 2 months of man-hours of draftsmen, architects and principals,
(c) Site coordination (top supervision) will continue for a period of 18 months.
(d) For all of the above reasons they cannot do the project financially for anything less than 3.5% of the cost of construction.


1.2 Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that their final meeting with NBCL was held on February 28, 2013 in which, after much persuasion, NBCL agreed to reduce the fee from the original 4% to 3%. He said that they are of the view that 3% is a reasonable figure. He requested EC to approve it.

1.3 Mr. Shameem Ahmed brought to the knowledge of the EC the following points which have resulted as a part of their discussion and negotiations with NBCL’s Mr. Zayd Bilgrami.

(a) The combined estimated covered (built up) area of Blocks B & C is 63,000 sq. ft.

(b) At the rate of Rs.3,000/- per sq. ft., the total estimated cost amounts to Rs.189 million.

(c) However, it has been agreed that for the purpose of renewing our Contract Agreement with NBCL for Blocks B & C, the notional cost of the project will be taken as Rs.120 million and payments for NBCL’s services until they submit an Engineer’s Estimate will be made accordingly.

(d) On approval of the Engineer’s Estimate by IBA, the notional cost will be revised and all payments made upto that stage will be adjusted accordingly.

(e) Once the project has been tendered and the awarded cost determined, all payments made or to be made to NBCL will be revised and adjusted as per the awarded cost. However, the final fee payable to NBCL will be capped at 3% of 110% of the awarded cost in case the cost of the project increases for any reason.

(f) The project will be tendered as a whole for both Blocks B & C, and if the project is held up or postponed for any reason, NBCL will be entitled to consultancy fee upto that stage according to the approved Engineer’s Cost Estimate or the Awarded Cost, whichever is less.

1.4 The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why the total estimated cost is Rs.189 million while the existing block was completed at Rs.147 million. Mr. Shameem Ahmed clarified that its area is more than that of the completed one, and in three years time the cost has also gone up.

1.5 The Dean & Director wanted to know the current occupancy status of the Boys’ hostels and the projected requirements. The Registrar said that as the new NTHP batch would be coming before the passed out batch leaves the hostel, they have to find and arrange accommodation for the NTHP students. The Registrar proposed that the lab on the third floor of the new Boys’ Hostel which has a capacity of 30 students can be used as dormitory for the NTHP students.

1.6 The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to get the height of the boundary wall of the Boys’ Hostel raised on priority and then start the external development work. He asked the Director Projects to visit Salman Dawood School of Business of LUMS and take Mr. Hasnain also along with him.

DECISION

1.7 The Committee approved the continuation – extension of NBCL consultancy services for Phase 2 of the new Boys’ Hostel, Block B & C at 3% fee of the awarded cost.

ACTION

1.8 Mr. Shameem Ahmed would get the height of the boundary wall of the Boys’ Hostel raised at the earliest.

1.9 The Director Projects and Architect of Aman Tower Mr. Hasnain Lotia will visit SDSB at LUMS.

Item No. 4 To consider and approve the Final Evaluation Report on Faculty Apartment at IBA Staff Town submitted by the sub-committee formed by the EC.

4.1 The Director Projects reported that the Executive Committee in its 238th meeting held on February 18, 2013 formed a Sub-Committee of three persons comprising Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Mr. Javed Islam of HEC Projects, and Ms. Sana Tanveer to evaluate the technical proposals including the designs submitted by the competitors and recommend ranking in order of 1st, 2nd and 3rd. He said that in compliance the evaluation was done by the three members committee and following methodology was adopted:

 Technical capability was carried out by all the three members independently based on the documentation submitted by the seven competitors and established criteria already presented to the EC.

 Total of 60 marks were assigned for the Technical Capability evaluation

 Evaluation details for assigning marks have been documented.

 The three evaluation reports were combined and average rating was obtained.

 The evaluation of the designs were also carried out in the same manner that committee carried and assigned them 40 marks. The resultant marks were combined with the technical capability marks resulting in the combined evaluation out of 100 total marks.

4.2 The Director Projects said that from these criteria the following competitors emerged as successful competitors in the order given below:

o M/s. NESPAK 96.3%
o M/s. FIRCO 91.6%
o M/s. CTI 90.3%
o M/s. EA Consulting 81.0%

4.3 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to witness the designs of the above competitors and select three of them so that their financial proposals could be opened and final evaluation for the successful candidate may be carried based on the SPPRA criteria 80:20 (80 technical) (20 financial). The successful candidate will then be awarded the job.

4.4 The Dean & Director inquired that Meinhardt is an internationally reputed firm but in the evaluation it has got low marks - 72.6 %. He wanted to know the reasons for low scoring. The Director Projects replied that Meinhardt did not give any design and they do not have on-ground capacity for project management in Karachi. Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know if the evaluation criteria were defined in the RFQ. The Director Projects confirmed that they were included in the RFQ and these have also been placed on the website of the SPPRA. Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to how they were prequalified if they do not meet the criteria in the evaluation. The Director Projects replied that for every competition the technical capability for that particular project is scrutinized independently depending on the nature of the work in a competitive mode. He said that if they have worked with us we get to know their strengths and weakness.

Design Presentations:

4.5 The four consultants separately presented their designs for the two and three bed room Faculty Apartments.


DECISION

4.6 After witnessing the designs presented by the four consultants, the Committee selected the following:

1. M/s. FIRCO (First choice)
2. M/s. NESPAK (Second choice)
3. M/s. CTI (Third choice)

ACTION

4.7 The Director Projects would open the financial proposals of the above three consultants.


Item No. 5 To consider and approve the Extension of Time of existing library.

5.1 The Director Projects requested extension of time in the completion of the renovation and refurbishment of existing library. He said that the project as per contract was to be completed on January 31, 2013 but due to various reasons it got delayed. He said that the contractor claimed 267 days for Extension in time but after detailed study by M/s NESPAK and IBA project department it was recommended that 157 days are justified. He said that the case was presented to the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on 16th March 2013. After due deliberations, the CPC has recommended that the extension be granted to the contractor till 3rd July 2013. The CPC advised the Projects Department to review the present state of the progress and work out a realistic schedule as in their opinion the extended period appears to be unrealistic.

5.2 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the extension up to 3rd July 2013. He informed the Committee that the Project Managers have been advised to monitor the work on daily basis and ensure that no further extension is demanded except on extraordinary circumstances.

5.3 The Dean & Director observed that the contractor is demanding 267 days extension whereas the recommendation is for 157 days. He said that if we allow 157 days extension, the contractor may come up again for further extension. The Director Projects said that the contractors have agreed to finish the project in 157 days i.e. by July 3, 2013. The Dean & Director wanted to know the financial implications due to extension in the completion time. The Director Projects said that we have to pay the project management fee for the extended period. However, he said that he has not worked out the amount that is to be paid. The Dean & Director said that the case should be presented with complete details including financial implications and the commitment of the contractor to complete the project by July 3, 2013.

DECISION

5.4 This item was deferred.



ACTION

5.5 The Director Projects would present the case again with complete details including financial implications. He would also invite the Contractor to the meeting.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
