The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       Food Issue at IBA Main Campus Cafeteria:

 

1.1       Mr. Fawad Mahdi, Manager CED reported via an email to the Dean & Director the problem he had personally faced after consuming the food from the Main Campus Cafeteria and made some observations and suggestions with regard to the hygiene and food quality at IBA Main Campus Cafeteria.  The Dean & Director directed to discuss this issue in the EC. 

 

1.2       The Registrar said that although he had already taken the requisite steps to avoid recurrence of such incidents. He added however that the item reported as stale by the CED staff was incidentally also consumed in a full meal by himself – as the same was offered to him for sampling – and that he (Registrar) did not find it to be stale. He then requested the EC members to advise him as to how he can improve quality and variety of food available.

 

1.3       The Registrar gave a brief overview of the events which have taken place since June 2012 to date in respect of ‘outsourcing’ of the cafeteria facilities. He informed that the services had been tendered thrice since that date. The first tender led to outsourcing of the cafeteria facilities to Messrs. La Rosh which had had a good catering record of convocations and other events. They operated during Spring 2013. The contract was terminated due poor quality of food and general state of dissatisfaction among students, faculty, staff and the event holders who are to be satisfied. He said that it was easy to satisfy students but it was very difficult to satisfy lower staff, who were at the fore front claiming that the prices charged by La Rosh were on higher side and quality of food lacked in variety / quality. He said that in Fall 2013 the service was re-tendered but received poor response as bidders expressed reservation on operability on account of:

  • Non-availability of gas

  • Loss of business due to various food kiosks operated by the EDP program which were selling lower standard, albeit cheaper food.

  • IBA unwillingness to allow the facility to be open to KU students

1.4       Due to non-finalizing of vendor the services given on interim basis to the canteen operator at City Campus to provide service at Main Campus too. However, he had some compulsions, availability of gas being one of them, due to which he could not improve on variety / quality of the food. He was bringing cooked food from outside.  He said that in the presence of Kiosks the contractor would not be able to compete. He further said that the hygienic condition at kiosks is also questionable and they are offering far from cleaner food and charging for it but students do not want to pay.

 

1.5       Mr. Imran Batada said that God forbid if anything goes wrong with the food served at     Kiosks who would be responsible.  Mr. Moeid asked if there is any check list. The Registrar said that they have developed a SOP. Mr. Moeid said that if the SOP is not followed, the kiosks should be closed and there should be some specified time period for allowing the kiosk to be operated.

 

1.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani referred to the quality of food and hygienic condition at the cafeteria of Aga Khan. He asked if their system could be followed at IBA. He said that they substitute food on daily basis. The Registrar said that they cannot compare with Aga Khan Cafeteria for various reasons.  He said that to follow their system, three cold rooms, and one huge store to put fresh and dried    provisions would be needed which they cannot provide due to space constraints. Also the meals at the AKU canteen are a byproduct of the main cooking which is done 3 times a day in large scale for over 4000 patients everyday

 

1.7       The Registrar said that the services of the existing contractor of canteen were hired in January in accordance with a duly completed tender process. They are generally observed to be delivering good service; they also arranged two events in which the quality of food was good. However, the complaints are on the high price charged by them.  Also apart from one or two complaints including the one by Mr. Fawad, generally most persons are happy with the service. However, the Registrar said that he undertook to call the vendor personally and advise him to be careful. He suggested that the existing contractor be allowed to continue and advised to improve the hygienic condition and quality of food.  Mr. Fawad Mahdi suggested that fresh food should be supplied by the contractor and the menu should be changed every day. Ms. Shabana said that menu is changed but the students want Biryani to be served every day. The Registrar said that we will nevertheless advise him to rotate the menu. However, any major improvement in variety of food / fresh cooking on site is not possible until we have the gas, till such time we have to accept food cooked outside and lesser variety / fresh items.

 

1.8       Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that quality of good and hygiene condition should regularly be checked. The Registrar said that Admin conducts walkthrough inspections and inspects facilities on a check list – the checked items include availability of filtered water, cleanliness, storage and serving of food – general cleanliness etc. However; he said that they would become more vigilant and check the cleanliness and quality of food.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee after thoroughly discussing the issue advised as under:

 

a) The Registrar would advise the existing contractor to improve the quality of food and pay more attention to hygiene and increase the variety of items available.

b) The menu for food items should not be repeated daily; some items are to be added and price be reduced if possible to make the items more in reach of particularly the lower staff.

c) Mr. Fawad Mahdi and Ms. Shabana would prepare a sample menu and give it to the operator.

d) The operation of kiosks with regard to quality of food and hygiene would be monitored by the Administration. The kiosk operators would have to follow the SOP prepared by the Administration. Ms. Shabana will send the checklist to Mr. Fawad so that he will forward that checklist to the kiosk operators.

e) The CED would allot the kiosk for a specified time period after which it has to be vacated.

f) Arrangements for making available the gas are to be expedited by the projects dept.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
