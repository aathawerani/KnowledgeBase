The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       Academic Calendar 2015-16

 

1.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that the academic calendar was earlier prepared by the Controller of Examinations and then by the Registrar and himself, although this should have been prepared by the Academic Committee. He said that the reason for revising the academic calendar is that the current semester will end on January 6, 2015 and the schools and colleges are already closed. Therefore, the children of faculty members are on leave so the faculty members want to go on leave but during this period we have exams. He said that some faculty members have gone on leave. The Dean & Director said that none of the faculty members has complained that due to exam they cannot avail the leave. The Registrar said that the main idea was that the academic semester should be finished within the calendar year i.e. by December 31. The Dean & Director said that as we have to provide some predictability to the students, faculty and parents about the Academic Calendar, we cannot go on experimenting every year. The Registrar said that they have been compressing the semester date because of which the exam department is in great pressure. He said that the EMBA activities have also been compressed. He said that the convocation is held in the first week of December and when the exam is on the students will become disinterested in the convocation due to exam. The Dean & Director said that there must be some certainty about the academic calendar. He agreed that the academic calendar should be prepared by Academic Committee.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.2       The proposal of Dr. Nasir Touheed to revise the academic calendar was not accepted.  However, the Committee decided that in future the Academic Calendar would be prepared by the Academic Committee.

 

Item No.2       Construction of outer boundary wall at Main Campus.

 

2.1       Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager General Maintenance reported that north west outer boundary wall towards unhabituated KU land was constructed long ago.  The wall panels on most of the places are without solid base and panels are not strengthened  / joined together through columns thus prone to fall by vibrations of heavy vehicles operating inside and also through heavy push or pull effects.  In recent past about 6 panels fell down during convocation 2014, which were constructed / repaired by project department through their contractor. On the morning of January 1, 2015 when heavy gusting wind / dust storm blew, the wall panel on north-west side fell down. He suggested that due to prevailing Security Environment and threats to Educational Institutions all such panels of boundary walls (approximately 2000 x 8 r/ft) be constructed with proper plinth and column joints between various wall panels.

 

2.2       Syed Fahim Uddin said that one solution  is to add columns in between the walls joint but it would be very costly solution; it would cost around Rs.2000 per rft.  The Dean & Director said that he wants a permanent solution from security as well as from aesthetic point of view. The Registrar said that walls towards AERC do not need anything to do but the walls towards Commerce Department to Students Centre need urgent repair. He further suggested that a watch tower should also be constructed and the wall to this side should be strengthened; its total length is approximately 1500 rft.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director inquired if the construction of wall was included in the external development work and was a part of Master Plan.  Mr. Mahmood Ali reported that it is included in the Phase 2 of the Master Plan.  He further said that the architects have not given any design for that yet. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Mahmood Ali if a new wall should be raised or the existing wall can be improved. Mr. Mahmood Ali said that the plinth has been eroded and the blocks have become very old. Further the plinth beam is without foundation and placed on the surface of the ground. He said that it has to be rebuilt. He said that if pillar is added, other walls would be affected due to vibration. The Registrar suggested that if they add 12 pillars to the wall towards the Commerce Department and the Students Centre, it would temporarily work and the cost would be around Rs.50,000 to Rs.60,000/-

 

2.4       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Mahmood Ali to ask Mr. Shabih of NBCL to prepare the design; we may complete the wall in phases but under a common architectural design. He thought that as a temporary measure the solution suggested by the Registrar appeared feasible.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee decided that a temporary wall with minor work should be raised. In the meantime Mr. Mahmood Ali would ask Mr. Shabih of NBCL to prepare a design for the boundary wall after which advertisement should be placed and the short-listing of petty contractor for a period of two years completed (if the validity of earlier short listed petty contractors expired) as the Maintenance Department would also need the services of petty contractors.

 

Item No.3       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Dismantling of overhead bridge between APWA and IBA and to be used for       watch tower at Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Registrar said that APWA wants that the bridge connecting APWA and IBA to be disconnected since IBA would no longer use the APWA building. He said that as the bridge was gifted to IBA, its steel can be used for watch tower.  The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Registrar and said that with the steel of the bridge two watch towers can be erected at the Main Campus. The steel of the bridge can be used for other purposes too. He asked the Registrar to get the bridge dismantled by petty contractors.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
