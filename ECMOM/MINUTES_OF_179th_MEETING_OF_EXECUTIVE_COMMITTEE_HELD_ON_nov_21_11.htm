Item No.2       To consider and approve Alternate Parking Space at City Campus.

 

2.1       The Registrar reported that apropos the update forwarded last week to the EC, the following developments have taken place during the current week:

 

a)         Compound of Disused Building adjacent to IBA: Mr. Sheikh Nazaz has contacted the owner in Australia and communicated our offer of Rs.90,000/= per month as parking charges for 30 vehicles. The owner has informed that he is unwilling at this rate. He has demanded Rs.300,000/= per month for what he claims is a forty crore worth plot. He, however, is willing to make necessary arrangements including clearing of all debris and material, so that the parking is doubled (30 to 60 vehicles) by including the parking space on other side of the building too. Six months advance and minimum one year agreement would be required.

 

b)         Vacant Plot behind OMI Building: This is a new find (size about 1500 sq yards) which has been identified through the courtesy of Director HR. It has space for about 30 cars. It belongs to an ex IT firm once working on ‘Project Mobile Eye’. The plot is approachable through a rather narrow and congested lane about 100 metre from IBA between two buildings adjacent to OMI building. Rental is not known but is envisaged to be similar to the parking plots of cinema halls (Rs.1800/= approx) per vehicle per month.  Presently the plot is strewn with concrete debris ex demolished building which stood at that location. This debris will have to be cleared by us.

 

2.2       The Registrar said that after visiting both plots and examining the pros and cons he is in favor of the plot adjacent to IBA for the following reasons:

 

a)      More number of vehicles can be parked (up to 60).

b)      Entry and exit of vehicles is clear and direct from the main road.

c)      Immediate proximity makes the premise easier to guard and more secure compared to a detached locations.

d)     Accessibility and Parking of IBA Buses is possible during night hours.

e)      For faculty and students it would be a preferred location as it is adjacent to the campus.

f)       If approved, all we have to do is to break the common wall in between and provide for a gate.

g)      Provision of power lighting will be easier and this is a must for any parking space.

h)      The venue is easier from direction pointing view for visitors.

i)        The owner is willing to clear the debris and shrubbery.

 

2.3       The Registrar, however, apprehended that there might be litigation case with Taxation Department but he would take this issue at the time of signing the agreement. He requested that the above proposal may please be examined and decided upon at an early date as construction of the Aman Building is scheduled to commence next week.

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that the Contractors’ labours may be allowed to stay in the adjacent plot and the Contractor may be charged some amount for that.

 

2.5       The Registrar said that if we say that we will provide accommodation to the labours also they may charge more.

 

2.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that we may charge a nominal fee from the students for providing parking space.

 

2.7       The Director H.R. suggested to negotiate with them as they are demanding Rs.300,000/- per month against our understanding of Rs.90,000/- per month.

 

2.8       The Registrar said that the owner is in Australia but his Attorney is in Lahore and he is communicating with the Attorney. However, he said that he has not yet mentioned any amount in the communication. He presented the following text of the email he had sent to the Attorney:

 

“Apropos our telecons during last week and the week prior to that; I am pleased to more formally apprise you that the Institute of Business Administration, Karachi is about to commence the construction  of a multi-storied building in the parking lot of its Saddar campus which is located at the M.R. Kayani Road, opposite the Headquarters Coastguard.

 

As discussed, we are interested in acquiring on a monthly rental basis, the vacant space in your disused property of MA Jinnah Road which lies adjacent to our campus. It is our intention to hire these premises from you for a duration of up to 18 months to facilitate the parking of about 50 vehicles belonging to our faculty, staff and students.

 

We would be deeply obliged if you could identify the rental that you would charge for the said facility required by your neighbour on a temporary basis. We are willing to enter into a formal agreement with you for the provision of the said facility.”

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee noted the progress and agreed that the plot adjacent to IBA is more suitable to IBA for parking of vehicles.
