MINUTES OF THE  41st MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON APRIL 6, 2009

The 41st meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, April 6, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Dean & Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                          Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                Associate Dean
  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN            Registrar
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  6. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  7. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                            Director I.T.
  8. Ayesha A. Menai                                 Director H.R.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Budget 2009-10 – (first draft)

1.1              As the draft budget 2009-10 was not ready, the item was deferred. It was decided to hold a special meeting of Executive Committee on Wednesday, April 8, 2009 at 9:00 a.m.

Item No. 2      Decisions Implementation Report (31st to 39th EC meetings)

Status Report from Director Projects:

2.1       Visit to HEJ Institute to study the HEC and Karachi University’s joint system of power generation and co-generation: The Director Projects stated that he could not visit HEJ. However, he promised that he would visit HEJ this week.

2.2       Estimates for boundary wall at the Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that the estimates were presented and approved in the EC and tenders have also been issued and awarded to the lowest bidder. Work has also commenced and at the same time loss claim has been lodged with the Insurance Company. He said that the claim amount of the boundary wall would be processed by the Insurance Company after the report of the Insurance Surveyors.

2.3       Up-gradation of Main Campus Auditorium:  The Director Projects reported that the architect has to submit the detailed drawings and they have been sent reminder too on April 2, 2009.

            The Director wanted to know as to what is the role of architect in the auditorium.  The Director Projects said that the architect has to give complete design including acoustic design, seating arrangements, false ceiling, lighting, electrical and mechanical drawings. He apprised that existing rotten false ceiling, doors and windows are to be removed for which the tendering was done and that demolition and cleaning work will be commenced in a day or two. He said that work without any design drawings cannot be done. The Registrar said that we want to make the auditorium operational before the Fall Semester.

The Director said that if a person drags the feet and slows down work, we should give him notice.  He advised the Director Projects to send an email to the architect stating that we would terminate the contract if they do not respond on time.

2.4       Proposal for construction of rooms for storing disposable items: The Director Projects reported that he would undertake the assignment this week.

2.5              Comprehensive insurance of IBA property: The Director Projects reported that he provided the information to Director Finance and said that he has done the valuation on the basis of cost of reinstatement of existing buildings and infrastructure as in present condition. Accordingly the total cost comes to Rs.355,000,000/-. He said that the policy with NIC will thus be taken on the basis of reinstatement value as per the insurance norm. He also said that he would get the survey done after buildings are fully renovated.

2.6              Rooms for MBA Program: The Director Projects assured that the two dedicated rooms fully equipped with furniture, multimedia, video conference facility would be available by 1st September 2009 on both the campuses. 

The Director I.T. requested that he should be provided drawings so that he may start working on the areas that he has to do. The Director Projects promised that he would send the drawings as soon as he receives them from the Architect.

The Director requested the Director Projects and the Director I.T. to coordinate and ensure that the two fully equipped rooms are ready and all the facilities are available for MBA program.

2.7              The Registrar suggested that we should install video conferencing facility in one room at each campus and another should be provided for auditorium when it is ready.

The Committee decided that the existing video conferencing room at the City Campus shall be used for video conferencing at the City Campus while the auditorium at the Main Campus will be provided with video conferencing facility for Main Campus. 

Status Report from Director I.T.

2.8              Expansion of AVAYA Communication Manager: Dr. Arshad Siddiqi reported that other options are still being evaluated. He said that since now we are going with LTA and PTA agreements, the purchase of AVAYA would depend on these; if they have AVAYA then we will have to go for AVAYA. He said that if we place AVAYA we cannot arrange Video Conferencing on that.  He said that it can only work on audio but for video, another equipment would be needed.

The Director said that we should have full information to take a decision and after taking a decision we should go ahead with that to avoid delays in execution. The Director I.T. promised that he would resolve the issue in the next meeting of Executive Committee.

2.9              PCs in the faculty room having no connection with other printers: Dr. Arshad Siddiqi confirmed that all PCs in the faculty lounges were configured for local printer. However, he said that the faculty members have configured different printers in their personal profiles which will have to be deleted. For this each faculty must be present to enter his / her profile. He said that the issue was discussed by the Assistant Manager Network with the Controller of Examinations; his view was that it will not be possible for the faculty to be present and that the issue should be left as it is. The Director advised Dr. Arshad Siddiqi to brief the faculty about configuration of printers in the faculty meeting scheduled on April 10, 2009.

Status Report from Director H.R.

2.10          Earned Leave: The Director H.R. stated that she has collected the data.

Muhammad Nishat and Nasir Touheed.  She said that the three Consultants have different approaches. However, M/s. N.H.R. seem appropriate and they would charge Rs.85,000/- per training day.

He also said that we would keep a waiting list of candidates from this time.

Item No. 3      To review and approve the initial list of requirement of spaces to be given to three Architects (M/s. Habib Fida Ali, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative, and M/s. FMNH) to obtain their technical and financial proposals for conducting users need analysis, design development, space planning, development of specifications, preliminary drawings and preliminary estimates.

3.1              The Director Projects presented a list of requirements for the multi-storeyed complex at City Campus compiled by him based on responses from majority of the users. He tabled a flow chart showing responses of IBA and the Project Committee. The flow chart showed that IBA will appoint the Architect who will prepare the design of the building. The Director Projects further stated that three out of seven pre-qualified Architectural and Engineering firms are recommended to be given the Request for Proposal for the buildings. These are; (1) M/s. Habib Fida Ali, (2) M/s. FMNH, and (3) M/s. NBC (Pvt.) Ltd. The remaining four firms namely (1) M/s. ACE Arts, (2) M/s. MMP, (3) M/s. Icon, and (4) M/s. Zaheeruddin are not recommended because the first two are Lahore based while the third one is already too busy with IBA and they may not meet deadlines. The fourth one is recommended to be dropped as their last proposal was technically deficient.

3.2              The Dean & Director inquired as to why IBA should spend money on the development of detailed designs and drawings for multi-purpose building which is the responsibility of Aman Foundation. He said that we should only inform them about our requirements. Aman Foundation will engage their own architects and they will prepare detailed drawings and designs. The Director said that he does not want to spend any money on design development etc. out of own resources.

3.3              The Director also wanted to know as to who would determine the need of classrooms in each campus.  The Committee discussed this point and advised that the Associate Deans and Programme Coordinators may be requested to provide the necessary requirements.

3.4              The Committee discussed the matter pertaining to construction of classrooms / seminar rooms and break out rooms at the Main Campus, Admin Block.  The Committee also discussed about the place to be provided to the students when the construction is in progress as well as when the ground floor open space will not be available after completion of the construction.

3.5              The Director Projects informed the Committee that a temporary shed would be constructed for the students.

DECISION

3.6              The Committee agreed with the Director that the IBA should not spend money on the detailed design and drawings of multi-storeyed building at the City Campus to be funded by Aman Foundation.

3.7              The Committee decided that the need of classrooms in each campus would be decided jointly by the Program Coordinators and the Program Offices. The Committee further decided that the formulation of time table, allocation of classrooms, teaching methodology will be under the Associate Deans.

3.8              The Committee requested the Director Projects to erect temporary sheds for the students when the construction work at Academic Block of Main Campus is started.

3.9              The Committee also decided that the requirement list shall be issued to three Architects namely (1) M/s. Habib Fida Ali, (2) M/s. FMNH, and (3) NBC (Pvt.) Ltd.  The Committee decided that the services scope will not include the designing.

ACTION

3.10          The Director Projects would get the draft letter approved by the Director before issuance of letter to the three Architects.

Item No. 4                  To consider note from Director Public Affairs on invitation of bids for IBA Video Documentary.

4.1              The Director Public Affairs in his note stated that as per instructions of the Director IBA regarding production of IBA Video Documentary and event management of ICICT conference, detailed brief was sent to the following four pre-qualified media agencies of IBA and they were invited to send their technical and financial proposals by March 3, 2009:

1.      BBCL (Pvt.) Ltd

2.      Channel 7 Communications (Pvt.) Ltd.

3.      Mass Advertising (Pvt.) Ltd

4.      Midas International (Pvt.) Ltd

4.2       The sealed tenders were opened on March 12, 2009 by the Director Public Affairs, Mr. Zafar Siddiqui in the presence of Dr. Wasim A Khan, Mr. Jami Moiz and Ms. Ms.Erum Hafeez. Three agencies participated in the bidding process while one agency namely Midas International (Pvt) Ltd did not submit tender documents. The tenders were evaluated and a comparative statement was prepared after meeting with the representatives of the concerned media agencies on 20th March, 2009 regarding the technical details of the documentary.

4.3       The Director Public Affairs stated that M/s. BBCL (Pvt.) Ltd were found to be the lowest competitive bidder with a bid amount of Rs.650,000/- for the IBA Video Documentary of 10 to 12 minutes duration. The rates submitted by the agency were found to be reasonable and the agency is deemed to be technically sound. Based on the above-mentioned facts, he recommended that M/s. BBCL (Pvt.) Ltd should be awarded the IBA Video Documentary Project.

DECISION

4.4       The Committee approved the recommendation to award the Video Documentary Project to M/s. BBCL at a cost of Rs.650,000/- and the cost would be charged to advertisement project.

4.5       The Committee further decided that ex-post facto approval would be obtained from the A&FC for the Video Documentary Project.

ACTION

4.6       The Director Public Affairs would ensure that ex-post facto approval from the A&FC is obtained.

6.1       The Senior Manager I.T. in his email of April 4, 2009 stated that to provide laptops to faculty so that they can do research, prepare lectures or presentations either being in the office or on the move, quotations were sought from the companies already qualified in the category of “Supply, installation, maintenance and repair of I.T. equipment”. Following seven (07) companies responded to the query:

1.      Mushko Electronics (Pvt.) Ltd.

2.      Inbox Business Technologies (Pvt.) Ltd.

3.      Jaffer Brothers (Pvt.) Ltd.

4.      Gerry’s SoftLogic (Pvt.) Ltd.

5.      O&A Business Machines (Pvt.) Ltd.

6.      Viper Technology (Pvt.) Ltd.

7.      Ora-Tech Systems (Pvt.) Ltd.

6.2       The Senior Manager I.T. stated that quotes from O&A were found to be the lowest among all participants. Item No. 7      To consider suggestions from Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Incharge Affiliation Department on the sale of IBA old vehicles.
