Item No. 2     To consider and approve the project management services for the project Development of Electrical Infrastructure for the new Boys Hostel, Staff Colony and new Girls Hostel.  

2.1     Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Projects Department has entered into a Contract Agreement with NESPAK for detailed supervision and project management services for the following projects:

(i)           External Development & Infrastructure Works including Sewerage Treatment Plant,

(ii)          Sports Ground & Ancillary Works,

(iii)         Electrical Infrastructure System (at the Main Campus) 

2.2     He informed the Committee that the total estimated tendered cost of the above-mentioned three projects is Rs.273,459,151/- while the project management fee agreed with NESPAK is 2.95% of the total completed cost of the aforesaid projects.  He said that the Contract Agreement, under its clause 6.6, provides for Additional Services in case the client wishes to obtain project management services outside the scope of services already agreed upon. He proposed that instead of pre-qualifying and hiring another project manager for the Electrical Infrastructure for the New Boys Hostel, Staff Colony and New Girls Hostel (which process will take several months to complete) IBA may let NESPAK manage this project too as an Additional Service on the same percentage fee and on the same terms and conditions as already agreed upon by our Projects Department.  He reported that the proposal was presented to and approved by the CPC. 

2.3     Mr. Shameem Ahmed informed the Committee that the lowest tendered cost of the project Development of Electrical Infrastructure for the new Boys Hostel, Staff Colony and new Girls Hostel is Rs.29,710,800/-.  At the rate of 2.95% the project management fee payable to NESPAK works out to Rs.0.876 million which is about 11% of the fee otherwise payable to NESPAK. Thus this is within 15% allowed under SPPRA rules. 

2.4     The Dean & Director wanted to know as to who is the main incharge of infrastructure development.  Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia said that he is incharge of infrastructure development but for Boys Hostel, Staff Colony and Girls Hostel Mr. Shameem Ahmed is responsible.    

DECISION 

2.5     The Committee approved to award the project management services for the project Development of Electrical Infrastructure for the new Boys Hostel, Staff Colony and new Girls Hostel to M/s. NESPAK as additional services which are within 15% of the total project allowed under SPPRA Rules. 

Item No. 3     To consider and approve the award of Interior Work Package to M/s. Total Construction already working at Alumni Students Centre, Main Campus.  

3.1     The Director Projects reported that the design of interior works package of Alumni Students Centre, Main Campus was given to the Architects, M/s. NBCL as the interior works formed part of the overall theme of the building.  In the meantime the overall Architectural works were continued. The final approval of the Interior design was accorded by the Executive Committee on 1st October 2012 in its 221st meeting with certain amendments.  In order to maintain the flow of the works and as the Architectural and Interior works were interconnected in the same spaces, the Architects also recommended through their letter dated 31st October, 2012 that the interior works be made as part of the existing contract since M/s. Total Construction are already working on those items which are in the BOQ and have to be integrated with the interior works.  He said that it will be very difficult to bring in another contractor to work in the same spaces where M/s. Total Construction are already working. He said that the Architects and the Project Managers have assured that M/s. Total Construction have the required capabilities and resources to undertake the interior works and co-ordinate with the ongoing works.  In this regard the contractors will be asked to depute an experienced supervisor to closely supervise the finishing works.  

3.2     The Director Projects reported that in order to dovetail the Interior Works, complete BOQ was prepared by the Architects based on the approved interior design and was given to M/s. Total Construction who submitted their bid in sealed envelope to IBA. The rates were checked by the Architects, the Project Managers and IBA and found reasonable.  The Amount quoted by M/s total Construction against the Interior BOQ is Rs.8,923,800/- 

3.3     The Director Projects said that after detailed scrutiny it was found that most of the items can be merged into original BOQ which amounted to Rs.5,095,285/- therefore this amount has been deducted from the tender for interior BOQ.  He said that the Architects were further asked to reduce the remaining cost of Rs.3,828,515 for Interior Works. After value Engineering the Architects reduced the cost of Rs.3,828,515/- to Rs.1,696,965/-. Therefore the additional payable amount for Interior Works is Rs.1,696,965/-. He provided breakup of the same. 

3.4     The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the above and approve in principle the estimate and adding the interior works to the existing contract. He said that if approved in principle by the EC, the process for approval of the TEC and CPC would be initiated.  He said that the available budget for interior and furniture is Rs.31 million, whereas the provision made in the budget year 2012-13 has been kept as Rs.10 million. 

3.5     To a query on value engineering the Director Projects clarified that the false ceiling has been removed, and tiles which are less costly but equally good would be used. He said that some adjustments have been made in the BoQ but they would remain within the BoQ. 

DECISION 

3.6     The Committee agreed in principle to add the interior work to the existing contract of M/s Total Construction at the estimated cost of Rs.1,696,965/- for the interior works. 

ACTION 

3.7     The Director Projects would present the proposal to the TEC and CPC for approval.

Item No. 6     To consider and approve the additional amount as the cost differential due to change in the design of Main Campus Electrical Infrastructure.  

6.1     The Director Projects said that after value engineering the final cost of the project was concluded as Rs.109,988,984/-  Accordingly contract was awarded and the work was taken up and completed on the temporary connection to the Aman-CED Building as part of overall BOQ and  some additional approved cost under separate head. Subsequently there has been changes in the main power supply mode from KESC because of which the design layout had to be changed as well as many components of the system were revised. He presented a report from the Consultant in this regard. He said that he has invited Mr. Khalid Pervez of M/s. KP Associates to explain the technical details. He further said that based on the recommendations of the Consultant a summary of cost differences due to change in the design has been prepared which is based on the revised BOQ #1 and BOQ#2.  

6.2     The Dean & Director said that earlier it was told that there would be a central hub and the two sources; existing supply from KESC and the new supply; would be merged into the central hub. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there was a misunderstanding that the existing KESC connection and new connection would be merged but these cannot be merged. The Dean & Director wanted to know as to why it cannot be merged. Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that KESC sub-station it is not fully dedicated to IBA as two other institutions of Karachi University are also getting supply from there.  Mr. Khalid Pervez said that as a precautionary measure, when they did value engineering they checked again with building consultant if there was any change in the demand. He said that the Consultant intimated in writing that there was a demand of additional 539 KW for CED, Academic Block, Student Centre and for UPS.     

6.3     The Dean & Director said that the additional demand has to be justified. Moreover, he said that KESC has put IBA on zero load shedding. He wanted to know as to what will be the difference between the design and maximum demand ignoring the additional demand.   Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there is an increase of 39% in the total load.  He said that design is based on 80% maximum load utilization of the connected load.  The Dean & Director said that if we reduce the maximum utilization load from 80% to 70% the additional projected demand of 39% would no longer be valid. Mr. Khalid Pervez agreed with the Dean & Director. To a question Mr. Khalid Pervez said that if in future there is a substantial increase in load, then we may change the circuit breaker.    

6.4     Mr. Khalid Pervez said that due to continuity of existing KESC substation, the two 750 KVA transformers, related 11 KV switchgear and MV cable were eliminated. This necessitated addition of LV cables to bring power of standby generators from power centre to the electric room where distribution of 400 KW LV power of KESC was planned.  

6.5     To give a specific example, the Dean & Director said that the initial load for the Academic Block as reflected in the BoQ was 506 KW but now it has been raised to 715 KW, which is not needed because 506 KW will meet the requirements. Mr. Khalid Pervez requested to give mandate of maximum demand.  The Dean & Director said that as a client he thinks that the load utilization included in the original BoQ is O.K. However, if technically the electrical consultants want to review then they may do so.  The Director Projects said that in the work order of Rs.109 million it was 500 KW but later after re-calculating the load factors, NBCL gave new loads of 715 KV so cables were procured for 715 KV. He said that they would see if cable of 550 KV or 715 KV is needed. The Registrar said that the original plan was of 500 KV but because of generator of 750 KV, cable was procured of 750 KV load. The Dean & Director said that the additional cable from City Campus was also placed here. The Director Projects said that it was connected with multipurpose building to energise UPS and this cable cannot be used here. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the cable which is needed is different in size and length from the one brought from City Campus. 

DECISION 

6.6     The Committee requested the Director Projects to have a meeting with the Consultant, Mr. Khalid Pervez and SMC, the designers of the electrical system of the three buildings and review the technical details of the energy requirements of all the three new buildings and prepare a realistic estimate of energy demand from KESC source.  

Item No. 7     To consider and approve cancellation of the proposed kiosk in the courtyard of new Academic Block, Main Campus.     

7.1     The Director Projects reported that during the walk through of New Academic Block, a suggestion was made by the Registrar IBA to construct a Kiosk as a reception/way finding etc.  In response, the Architects did some initial work and found that the cost was coming out as high as Rs.1.0 million because a new structure had to be built requiring dismantling of already constructed RCC planter at that particular location and raising the structure from foundation level. It would also need electrical and ICT systems for which already laid floors will have to be cut and repaired. He said that the Architects have recommended alternate proposal comprising hanging way finders in connecting corridors positioned in such a way that it will catch users eye when seen from distant entry point. This will be economical and will not disturb the character of the courtyard having trees, benches and sitting areas.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the cancellation of construction of kiosk and approve the alternate proposal recommended by the Architects. 

DECISION 

7.2     The Committee inspected the proposed site for the kiosk and approved the central planter of the courtyard at the new Academic Block to be converted into the reception counter.
