Item No. 3      Appointment of a Project Manager for the project “Construction of a new KESC Sub-station and relocation of four generators at the City Campus”.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the proposal for appointment of a Project Manager for the project “Construction of a new KESC sub-station and relocation of four generators at the City Campus was considered in the Central Purchase Committee. It was mentioned in the proposal presented to the CPC that the office of Mr. Shameem Ahmed invited technical and financial proposals from eight pre-qualified project management individuals/firms. Four of them responded and their technical evaluation was considered by the TEC in its meeting held on January 1, 2011. The TEC did not approve the technical evaluation and decided that the four applicants be asked to revise their technical and financial proposals. The revised technical and financial proposals were received and presented to the TEC. As decided by TEC, a negotiation meeting with KP & Associates was held on February 7, 2011 as a result of which the applicant reduced his fee from Rs.2.825 million to Rs.2.4 million. A meeting was held with NESPAK too on February 10, 2011 with the objective of encouraging them to review and improve their manning schedule which they accepted to do vide their letter of February 15, 2011. The results of the two negotiations were considered by the Technical Evaluation Committee in its meeting held on February 19, 2011. 

 

3.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the TEC recommended to hire M/s.NESPAK as Project Manager for the project at a professional fee of 3½% of the total cost of the project. The CPC has endorsed the recommendation of the TEC that NESPAK be appointed as Project Manager for the project at a professional fee of 3½% of the total cost of the project. Thus the fee would be around Rs.1.5 million.

 

3.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed also informed the Committee that a few days ago Karachi Cantonment Board has approved IBA’s Building Plans for construction of the New Auditorium and Multi-Storey Building at the City Campus. He said that an early construction of the new KESC sub-station would facilitate start of the auditorium and multi-storey building project. 

 

3.4       The Director observed that the KESC sub-station is a small project for which Project Manager is not needed. He said that Mr. Jawwad can do this job.

 

3.5       The Director Projects clarified that it is the decision of the A&FC and the Board that project management should not be with the Architect, it should be separate. He said that the construction of a sub-station and relocation of generators is highly technical work in which the cabling will have to be redone, and the synchronization of all the four generator sets will have to be done. He further said that the equipments are highly costly and they should be efficiently installed. The KESC will only provide high tension line and shift the old connection to the new sub-station. He said that the synchronization of the four generators, their dismantling and re-fixing will be the responsibility of the Project Managers.  He said that the Project Manager would also provide assistance during the defect liability period.

 

3.6       The Registrar suggested to take advice from the Consultant if a Project Manager is needed for this job or not.

 

3.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the nature of the job is such that the Consultant cannot supervise the installation and it is also written in the contract that the Consultant will not be providing detailed supervision.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee approved the recommendation for appointing M/s.  NESPAK as Project Manager for the above mentioned project at a professional fee of 3½% of the total cost of the Project.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would obtain a letter from the Consultant stating that a detailed supervision is required for this project which they cannot provide.  Mr. Shameem Ahmed would circulate this letter to the EC for their information.

Item No. 6      To consider email from a faculty member regarding Bone Marrow Recruitment Drive.

 

6.1       The Director said that he received an email regarding bone marrow recruitment drive which can be started at IBA through Community Welfare Society of IBA. The email states that the global registry of bone marrow donors has 14 million people on it, of which less than 1% are South Asians. The bone marrow compatibility test is conducted through a simple cheek swab test - a sample of saliva is taken from one’s mouth - it is free (as all costs would be funded by the family of the affected child), painless and takes 30 seconds. Gender is not relevant, but South Asian ethnicity is critical. In most cases, once someone has been identified as a match, the process is as simple as a blood transfusion and the donor can leave a couple of hours after donating. Finally, there is no commitment, donors have the right to opt out at any time should they not want to continue with the actual donation after the match.

 

6.2       The parents of the affected child are now looking to start bone marrow tests in Pakistan at major educational institutions. As there are no adequate testing facilities in the country so samples collected will be brought back to labs in the US to be analyzed - again this will be funded by the family of the affected child. This will create a global database of Pakistani donors, thereby helping not only the affected child but others that, over time, may find themselves in such unfortunate situation.

 

6.3       The Registrar informed the Committee that students have also spoken to him about this and he has suggested to them that a leaflet with contact person should be placed on the Notice Boards and to observe the response in a week or two. The future action will be dependent on the response received. 

 

DECISION

 

6.4       The Committee agreed to the suggestion made by the Registrar.

 

 

Item No. 7      Report on Open Houses.

 

7.1       The Registrar presented details of the number of visitors (1121 in 2009), (1095 in 2010) (and 1981 in 2011), and the cities and places open houses were arranged at.  He said that the actual number of visitors is more than the registered one because some of the visitors could not fill in the form. He said that open houses in Peshawar and Quetta had to be postponed due to cancellation of flights.

 

7.2       The Registrar submitted the following conclusions and recommendations based on the open houses arranged in 2009, 2010 and 2011:

 

·         The idea to drop the hotels as venues and instead visit the Colleges and Schools individually though good in terms of guaranteeing an audience – posed problems for those candidates who were not studying in the visited colleges. Therefore, a more cost effective central venue be considered in addition to the college visits.

 

·         Government Colleges are not appropriate as venues for IBA Open House as they are deprived of many facilities and the focus of students is limited. Army Public Schools and Cadet Colleges are recommended to be included. Secondly, on a comparative basis, amongst the schools visited; the student lot fielded by Beacon House Schools was observed to be more keen and focused compared to the City School students.

 

·         Larkana be included as a location.

 

·         Conducting of separate open houses at both campuses of IBA may be continued. However, a gap of one week be given in the date of the two open houses so that it suits the convenience of the prospective candidates and their parents.

 

·         The visit to Gulf area was restricted to UAE as Saudi Arabia Schools had longer vacations in that time frame. The help provided by the Embassy staff was quite good. As many as 550 students participated in the open house at Sharjah (300) and Abu Dhabi (250). However, for various socio-economic reasons the number of prospective candidates for admission in IBA from the Pakistan Schools is limited – therefore there is a need to include various international schools in the area. These schools have a large number of Pakistani students studying in them; but local regulations prevent them from allowing enrolment activities by foreign institutions. The only solution for this is to consider holding of an open house session in a hotel and well publicize it in advance so that adequate audience is available. Dubai Avari has proposed to allow IBA to use their facilities for this purpose at subsidized rates for the said purpose. As per information gathered in UAE, IBA has better prospects in Saudi Arabia as that country does not have as many Business Schools as UAE. It is therefore proposed that a visit be undertaken to Saudi Arabia soonest preferably in March 2011.

 

·         In overseas visits more involvement of the Alumni is recommended – particularly in so far as using the open house visits is concerned for marketing of IBA’s MBA Executive programs is concerned.

 

·         The mechanism of booking of sessions with the individual schools, receiving their confirmations and providing particulars of the same to the concerned faculty members needs to be improved / streamlined. Concerned faculty members need to be kept in loop from the beginning and CC in all messages to him to avoid any confusion.    

 

·         The IBA Documentary should be updated to include the infrastructural developments at both campuses. The Registrar informed the Committee that BBCL who have produced the original documentary have asked for Rs.350,000/- for up-grading the documentary.

 

·         In addition to carrying various Promotional materials, program announcement copies, Souvenirs must be provided and carried for all principals and student counselors that are visited.

 

·         The Principals of Beacon House Multan and Pakistan Schools at UAE have volunteered to field their premises for conducting entrance test and interviews if the situation so develops. This may be considered especially in view of the feedback from students in UAE and from Saudi students in their emails – where they have requested for at least one exam centre in UAE so that their travel expenses can be saved.

 

DECISIONS:

 

7.3       The Committee approved the recommendations given at 7.2 above.  The Committee further decided that:

 

a)      The postponed open houses in Quetta and Peshawar should be re-arranged.

b)      Open Houses in Chitral and Northern Areas should also be arranged.

c)      Every month one good college in Karachi should be visited for which the Registrar to make a plan.

d)     For open houses outside Karachi, central places like Defence College, Army Public Schools, Cadet Colleges, Hyderabad Public Schools may be considered.

e)      The Registrar was advised to negotiate with BBCL for reducing the charges for up-grading the documentary.

f)       Dr. Nasir Touheed and Dr. Nasir Afghan will visit Saudi Arabia in March 2011.
