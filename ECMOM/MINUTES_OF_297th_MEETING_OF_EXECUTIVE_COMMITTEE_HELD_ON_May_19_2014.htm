MINUTES OF 297TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, MAY 19, 2014

 

The 297th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 19, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                             Associate Dean, FCS

2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat                                       Associate Dean, FBA

3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                              Registrar

4. Mr. Moeid Sultan                                              Director Finance

5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                  Director Projects

6. Mr. Imran Batada                                              Head of ICT Department

7. Mr. Adnan Hameed                                            Head of H.R

 

Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects, and Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve the Time Extension of Manufacturing & Supply of Furniture for Administration Building at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the contract for manufacturing and supply of furniture for Admin Building was awarded to M/s. Simab (SMC-PVT) Limited on January 18, 2014.  As per contract the project was to be completed in three months i.e. by April 28, 2014 at a cost of Rs.14,286,098/-  However, an extension in completion time for 4.5 months i.e. Upto September 15, 2014 is required for the following reasons: 

 

· The main reason for delay is the site i.e. Administration Building which is in unfinished / incomplete state. The main contractor is still working at site (Admin. Building) as the extension of time already given to them up-till September 15, 2014.

 

· No proper covered space is available to store finished furniture to avoid damage during construction activities and weather. Contractor was advised to schedule the supply of furniture items according to completion of finish space by main contractor so that furniture does not get damaged.

 

· Procedural delay in approval of mock-up / shop drawings from the IBA.

 

· Frequent revision in the layout of all floors and specially first floor. Due to this manufacturing activities were on hold till the finalization of the layout.

 

1.2       Based on the above facts for the major reasons for delay and as per recommendation of Project Architect the extension in time required is up till September 15, 2014. The Director Projects said that the Project Department also recommends the extension of time as stated above because the supply of furniture is interlinked with main contractor’s works.

 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 17th May 2014 scrutinized the case and considering the reasons as valid, and approved the extension in time.  The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till September 15, 2014.  He clarified that this extension of time would be without reimbursement of the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. There would be no escalation. The Contractor shall extend all insurances and guarantees related to the Contract accordingly.

 

1.4       The Registrar said that during the semester when the students are here, it becomes very difficult to shift the offices.  He asked the Director Projects to obtain and send to him a delivery schedule immediately which was agreed by the Director Projects.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee for the reasons mentioned above approved the extension in time up till September 15, 2014 for Manufacturing & Supply of Furniture for Administration Building at Main Campus and recommended to the A&FC for approval.

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the Time Extension of Electrical Infrastructure Works.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the project of Electrical Infrastructure works was awarded to M/s. National Engineering Company on July 10, 2012. Due to value engineering the project was on hold and commenced on December 15, 2012.  It was to be completed in six months i.e. by June 14, 2013. The Project cost after value engineering was Rs.109,988,984/-  For the reasons mentioned below an extension 8.5 months i.e. till February 2014 in completion of the project was given.  However, as the project could not be completed during the extended period for the mentioned below, another extension till May 30, 2014 is requested. The first Extension of time given to the contractor up to February 15, 2014 was due to the following major reasons:

 

Design Review

                         

Due to design review and observation of M/s. NESPAK on design all the technical submittals were kept on hold by the Consultants (M/s. KP Associates).  After detailed review meetings by IBA with NESPAK and Consultants, a consensus had to be developed wherein it was decided that the design by KP Associates was their responsibility and that NESPAK’s observations were kept on record. KP Associates confirmed that their design philosophy will remain unchanged. In these meetings opinions of IBA Maintenance Department were also solicited who agreed to the design of KP Associates. Due to this hold up, 45 valuable days were lost.

 

Delay due to Construction of Power Centre

 

As the Power Centre construction was dependant on finalization of the Electrical Infrastructure Work, its work had to remain on hold for 182 days. Due to this reason the LV2 room and sub-station building were also not ready and contractor was asked not to bring the equipment.

 

Major reasons for the delay and the justification for further extension in time:

            

Delay in approval of Centralized Fire Alarm System.

 

· Initially, at design stage Consultant M/s KP&A considered the addressable fire alarm panels which could be hooked with the central fire alarm system of the Existing / under construction buildings of IBA.

 

· Contractor M/s NEC submitted the revised submittal of centralized fire alarm system because of the different fire alarm systems (addressable & conventional) installed in the existing buildings of IBA to the Consultant M/s KP&A for review and approval on November 05, 2013 with breakup of additional cost of Rs.2.2 million. After that Consultant M/s KP&A granted the technical approval of submittal on January 13, 2014, which was fully integrated and intelligent centralized fire alarm system.

 

· The Director Projects advised the CEO of M/s KP&A for a presentation to IBA’s Executive Committee in order to convey the importance of centralized fire alarm system and justification of additional cost involved in this account.

 

· The Executive Committee advised the Consultant for some other alternate with lesser expensive scheme. Consultant M/s KP&A suggested another alternate option to get only identification of the buildings if any fire incident has occurred. This scheme was accepted by the IBA’s Executive Committee and IBA gave the financial approval of centralized fire alarm system on April 27, 2014. Based on revised system a saving of approximately Rs.1.6 million was achieved.

 

2.2       The Director Projects said that based on the above facts for the major reason for delay and as per recommendation of Project Managers M/s. Nespak the second extension required is up till May 30, 2014. He said that the Project Department also recommends the extension of time as stated above. He reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on May 17, 2014 scrutinized the case and approved the extension in time up to May 30, 2014. He requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the extension in time up to May 30, 2014.

 

2.3       The Director Projects clarified that the extension of time shall be without reimbursement of any escalation claim, the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall have to extend all insurances and guarantees related to the Contract accordingly.

 

2.4       Mr. Moeid Sultan asked if there would be any increase in the project cost or we have to pay fee to the Project Manager for the extended period.  The Director Projects said that as they have worked in the previous project of Nespak, that out of their claim of Rs.12 million they settled it at Rs.7 million and for that they developed a logic. The same logic they would apply in this project.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that external development work, HVAC, Power Centre, Sports arena have been awarded as whole package which are to be completed in parallel.  He further said that as per agreement there is a cap of a maximum increase of 10% in case of delays.

 

2.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani pointed out that the value of contract (as per letter of award) as mentioned in the attachment is Rs.137.54 million whereas in the note the cost of the project is Rs.109 million. The Director Projects said that the cost of the project was Rs.127 million which after value engineering was reduced to Rs.109 million.  He said that there was an estimated cost of Rs.17 million for street lighting included in Rs.127 million which was segregated and he was advised to talk to Philips in this regard. However, he said that he would check as to why Rs.137 million has been mentioned in the note.  He further said that the cost of the project will increase from Rs.109 million because of the additional cables.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Executive Committee approved the extension in time up to May 30, 2014 for the completion of Electrical Infrastructure Works and recommended to the A&FC/BoG for approval.

 

2.7       The Committee further decided that the Project Management fee of Nespak should be kept as low as possible.

 

ACTION

 

2.8       The Director Projects would present the recommendation of EC to the A&FC/BoG for approval.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)       Bursting of bulk water supply pipeline at Boys Hostel.

 

4.1       The Registrar reported that we have a big reservoir of a 100,000 gallons capacity and an overhead tank of 50,000 gallons.  As the overhead tank is already on the height, there is much pressure on the pipe line.  He reported that on Friday night the line got burst because of pressure and there was no way to control the wastage of water. He said that there should have been pressure check valves at least at three locations between the water tank and the boys hostel but there is no such valve.

 

4.2       Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed said that at the request of the Director Projects, the contractor has started to restore the pipeline but it would take two days time.  He further said that in the meanwhile they have temporarily managed to supply water to the staff town and girls’ hostel.

 

4.3       The Registrar emphasized to undertake the work on emergency and provide for the pressure valve at the earliest.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee requested the Director Projects to get the restoration of burst pipe done and pressure reducing valves placed in emergency in order to ensure proper supply of water and to the staff town, girls hostel and boys hostel and to control wastage of water.  

                                

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
