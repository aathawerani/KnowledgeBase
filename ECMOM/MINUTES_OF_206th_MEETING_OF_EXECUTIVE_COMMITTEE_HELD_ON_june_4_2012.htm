Item No.1       To consider and approve additional implementation cost of SAKAI 2.8.

 

1.1       The Director ICT said that they had to delay implementation of SAKAI due to not having the in-house data center ready. He said that initially they had started the activity with SAKAI 2.5 however recently new version 2.8 has been launched with the additional features mentioned below:

 

·         Improved web browser compatibility. Sakai 2.8 supports the latest versions of web browsers, and has made its WYSIWYG text edit compatible with the Safari web browser on the MacOS X platform.

·         Performance increases. Tools that require looking up and accessing user details will experience a speed increase. Tests with the Site Info tool have shown improvements of up to 300%.

·         A collapsible navigation menu. Individual users can collapse the left-hand navigation menu of the current site, leaving more room for the main content. Approximately 130 pixels of horizontal space will be saved.

·         Functionality improvements to various tools.

·         Announcements - Users can subscribe to a site's public announcements via an RSS feed.

·         Forums - Users can opt to "watch" forums, allowing them to be notified when a new message is posted or when a thread receives a new message. Forums can now have an open and close date.

·         Polling - Polls can be made available for the public, allowing anonymous voting.

·         Schedules - Site maintainers can set the default "view" for their site (Options: By Day, By Week, By Month, By Year, List of Events)

·         Test and Quizzes - The Responds assessment management tool can be used for all question types.

·         Wiki - Comments can now be hidden. Date format now reflects user's chosen locale.

·         Addition of a new tool, Profile2. This replaces the existing "Profile" tool, and provides advanced social networking capabilities to all users. Users can search for and make "connections" to other users within Sakai, send private messages and upload images to their personal picture gallery. A "kudos" ratings mechanism is used to measure profile completion, and linkage to twitter allows automatic status updates to users' Twitter accounts. Enhanced privacy settings are available to protect your personal information.

 

Additional Cost

 

1.2       The Director ICT informed the Committee that Techlogix has quoted additional implementation cost of US$2753.00 for this update.  He requested the Committee to approve the addition implementation cost so that they can proceed further. He reported that they have planned to start training session of SAKAI in the upcoming Summer 2012 semester.

 

1.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui observed that by the time the data centre is ready, it is quite possible that a new up-graded version higher than 2.8 might be introduced then again they would have to up-grade it. He suggested not to go for up-gradation it at this stage.

 

1.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani also agreed with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and suggested that when the SAKAI is on full-fledged operation after the data centre is ready, then if needed, we may consider for up-graded version.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee did not approve implementation of new version of SAKAI 2.8 at this stage.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the redesigning and rebidding of External Development works to meet budget limit.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the amount allocated in the budget for External Development Works was Rs.160 million and the bid received for this project is of Rs.140 million but due to financial constraint, the Dean & Director suggested to reduce it to Rs.100 million. However, he said that if they reduce the works to the limit of Rs.100 million that would mean exceeding the allowable limit of 15% reduction which is permissible under SPPRA. He said that in order to bring the cost to Rs.100 million, further value engineering of at least 10 to 20 major items will have to be done, which will involve major changes in the scope of works, specifications and quantities. This, he said, cannot be done as the bids have been opened and evaluated.

 

2.2       Based on these reasons mentioned above, the Director Projects said that all the bids will have to be cancelled and new scope of works, new specifications, revised drawings and the BOQ will have to be issued to all the bidders. Architects and Consultants will take at least 3 weeks. NESPAK will take another two weeks to review.  Sealed bids will be invited from the same bidder for which at least 3 weeks will have to be given to the bidders by including pre-bid meeting. The evaluation and approval procedure may take another 3-4 weeks. The project will thus be delayed by 12 weeks from today. He said that in order to save money the above procedure is to be adopted.

 

2.3       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the cancellation of the bids and re-issuance of new scope of work, specification, new drawings, revised BOQ and the new time lime, so that Architects may be asked to start preparation of documents.

 

2.4       The Registrar suggested that instead of cancelling the bids, the Director Projects should cancel some of the items from the bids to bring the cost at Rs.100 million.  He further said that the Solar lights should not be approved as it did not work at IBA and its cost is also very high.

 

2.5       The Director Projects replied that according to SPPRA they would have to cancel the bid. He clarified that they were not considering solar lights.

 

2.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to try to reduce the time for completion of the process from 12 weeks to 10 weeks.

 

2.7       The Director Projects said that the architects may demand for re-designing work but he would try to get it done free of cost or at the maximum payment of Rs.100,000 to Rs.150,000/-.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee approved the redesigning and rebidding of External Development works to meet budget limit. The Director Projects would try to complete the process in 10 weeks time.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Measures to reduce hissing sound at the Video Conferencing Room, Main Campus.

 

4.1       Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that there is hissing sound at the Video Conferencing Room at Main Campus which interferes in the proceedings at video conference and needs to be reduced.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that the room was not designed to be used as Video Conferencing Room but when the construction of ducts was completed, then it was requested to install the Video Conferencing equipments.  He said that the hissing sound is created because the mikes are placed near luminaries due to which the air sound is there.  The mikes being sensitive, also catch the sound of the fan units of the central AC plant. He said that this can be remodelled for which they will have to strip off complete ceiling and redo the designing of the ducting system which would be costly and take about two months time.

 

4.3       The Registrar suggested installing two floor mounted ACs of 4 ton each which would be switched on during video conference and central AC would be switched off to reduce noise level. He further added that this would serve as back up units in case the central AC fails.      

 

4.4       The Director Projects agreed that the proposal of the Registrar was better because it would act as standby system in case of downtime of the main A.C. system.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

4.5       The Registrar would get two floor mounted ACs of 4 ton each installed at the Video Conferencing Room at Main Campus in order to reduce the hissing sound.

 

 

b)   Note of appreciation for the Registrar.

 

4.6       The Committee recorded its appreciation for the wonderful job that the Registrar and his team has done on bringing the Program Announcement on time.
