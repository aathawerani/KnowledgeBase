MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON SEPTEMBER 22, 2008 (FOURTEENTH MEETING)

The fourteenth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, September 22, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                          Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                Chairman, CS & MIS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                                Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  8. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                         H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider Evaluation Report prepared by Director Projects for prequalification of Architects and Engineering Consultants for HEC-funded Projects.

1.1       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects stated that in response to the advertisement for prequalification, 30 companies submitted their documents. He said that for the ease of evaluation the companies were classified into categories and were properly evaluated for prequalification by assigning marks to various factors such as location of the company, type of company, professional certificates, key technical persons, financial position, overall experience, projects completed since 2001, projects in hand etc.

1.2              The Director Projects also presented the Project Implementation Strategy with two options (1) Either to engage Architectural and Engineering firm which may be given entire responsibility of Architectural and Engineering Designs, detailed supervision and project management, or (2) to Engage Architects having competent associates / sub-consultants. He also mentioned the advantages and disadvantages under each option.

1.3              Based on the implementation strategy and requirement of the project, he recommended to pre-qualify the following seven consultants who may be issued the request for proposal (RFP) asking them for their technical and financial proposal:

a)      Architectural and Engineering Firms

i)          ACE Architectural & Town Planning Services (Pvt.) Ltd.

ii)         Zaheeruddin Consultants (Pvt.) Limited

iii)        MM Pakistan (Pvt.) Limited

b)      Architects having associates / sub-consultants  

i)                    Habib Fida Ali

ii)                  Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Ltd.

iii)                FNMH Architecture

iv)                ICON

1.4              The Director said that he had a meeting with the Director USAID and had assured him that the Centre for Entrepreneurial Development would be started in 2008-09. The Director said that we should start the construction of Centre for Entrepreneurial Development immediately after going through the required process.

DECISION

1.5              The Committee approved the recommendation of Director Projects for prequalifying the seven Consultants listed in 1.3 above.

1.6       The Committee further decided that instead of selecting and assigning the work to only one prequalified consultant, more than one prequalified consultants be selected and they be assigned different projects.

ACTION

1.7       The Director Projects will obtain letter of interest from the seven prequalified consultants and issue Request for Proposal to them.    

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Network Manager for hiring I.T. Support Staff.

2.1              The Network Manager in his request stated that IT infrastructure at IBA has been expanding continuously for the last few years. Internet facility was provided to Boys Hostel more than two years ago.  Now the facility has been extended to Staff Town and Girls Hostel.

2.2              To provide continuous and trouble free services to the end users and for monitoring and troubleshooting the network and related services, he proposed that there should be dedicated technical support persons available.  He further stated that hostels and staff residences are the places where I.T. services are required round the clock. He proposed that three I.T. Support Staff for the Evening Shift at Boys Hostel and two for Morning and Evening shifts for Girls Hostel be appointed.

DECISION

2.3              The Committee decided that in the morning shift, the I.T. Support Staff posted at Boys Hostel may also move to the Girls Hostel if any problem related to I.T. arises in the Girls Hostel.  For this he may be provided a bicycle.

2.4              The Committee further decided that instead of hiring permanent staff, students should be hired for such jobs.

ACTION

2.5              The Registrar will make a bicycle available at the Boys hostel for moving from boys hostel to girls hostel if needed.

2.6              The I.T. Manager will hire capable students to solve such problems as and when needed.   

Item No. 3      To consider e-mail from Ms. Maheen Ghauri, Faculty Member regarding a request from a potential candidate from Peshawar for second admission test.

3.1       Ms. Maheen Ghauri, faculty member of the IBA forwarded an email from Mr. Asad Ali, a potential candidate for IBAs BBA program from Peshawar requesting that instead of one admission test, the IBA should conduct two admission tests; one in February/March and the second in June so that those candidates whose semester ends in December may also apply for the IBA instead of trying other alternatives.

DECISION

3.2              The matter was deferred and no action was taken.  However, it was decided that the IBA should develop a question bank so that in future, the IBA may have the facility of offering admission tests on two different times.

ACTION

3.3              Dr. Nasir Touheed would start developing question bank.

Item No. 4      To consider request from Ms. Kiran Shahzad, a transfer student from LUMS to IBA regarding attendance in classes.

4.1              Ms. Kiran Shahzad, a transfer student from LUMS to IBA stated that she has maintained a 100% attendance at the IBA.  However, due to delay in the transfer of her file from the Main Campus to the City Campus, she was not able to attend her classes on August 1, 2008.  As a result her attendance fell short due to a change in her courses for adjustment of pre-requisites and time clash. She requested that she be given full attendance as she had been attending classes and did not expect any changes in her courses.

4.2              The Program Office of the City Campus confirmed that Ms. Kiran Shahzad joined IBA on August 4, 2008.

DECISION

4.3              The Committee decided that the absences of Ms. Kiran Shahzad be counted from the date she joined the IBA i.e. August 4, 2008. The Committee further decided that the classes she missed due to transfer process may be ignored.

ACTION

4.4              The Controller of Examinations will convey the decision to concerned program office.

4.5              The Students Counsellor would guide Ms. Kiran Shahzad not to add further absences.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
