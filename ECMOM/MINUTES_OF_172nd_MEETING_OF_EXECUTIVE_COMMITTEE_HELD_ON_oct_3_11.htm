a)      PERN2 Bandwidth at City Campus

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that initially HEC provided 8 Mbps at $360/Mbps. Item No. 6      To consider and approve the Allotment of Accommodation and House Rent Policy for IBA Staff Town.

         

6.1       The Director H.R. presented the draft Allotment of Accommodation & House Rent Rules for IBA Staff Town.  Defining the purpose and scope of the proposed policy, the Director H.R. said that the purpose of these rules is to facilitate smooth operations related to IBA Staff Town by outlining the procedures pertaining to allotment and cancellation of accommodation, and defining house rent and other related charges in this regard. These rules will be applicable to full time (Option B and Contractual) employees of IBA currently residing or are interested in residing in IBA Staff Town. Same rules will be applicable to Option A employees, except the House Rent rates prescribed in this document. House Rent of Option A employees will be charged as per guidelines of the government.  The Policy covered the Eligibility Criteria, Priorities, Residence Categories, Allotment Committee, Procedures for Applications Submissions and Review, Guidelines on Procedure for Allotment, Status of Allottee, Responsibilities of the Allottee, Retention Period after Resignation / Retirement, etc., House Rent & Other Charges, Cancellation of Allotted Accommodation, Occupancy during study leave/deputation, Interchange of Accommodation, and Declaration.

 

6.2       On the proposed Priorities through which employees joining IBA from abroad will be preferred over those joining IBA from within Pakistan, the Registrar suggested to remove this clause because this may create resentment among the existing employees on the waiting roster.

 

6.3       The Dean & Director said that he wants to attract the best people from all over the world and one of the attractions for the potential faculty is the housing facility. Our competitors provide such a facility that tilts the candidate’s choice in their favour and IBA suffers. The Director H.R. added that the specific query from the potential faculty was about the availability of housing facility.

 

6.4       The Registrar suggested that suits in the Visiting Faculty Residence where the potential faculty can be provided accommodation should also be included in the ambit of the policy. The Committee agreed with the Registrar.

 

6.5       The Director Projects suggested that instead of renovating the old houses they should be demolished and vertical housing (G+3) should be constructed in their place and the amount of Rs.20 million allocated for renovation of those houses may be used for construction of apartments.

 

6.6       The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Director Projects and requested him to get preliminary exercise done.  However, the Registrar suggested that the houses already decided to be renovated and the residents of which have also been informed about renovation, should be renovated.

 

DECISION

 

6.7       The Committee recommended the Allotment of Accommodation & House Rent Rules for IBA Staff Town as presented by the Director H.R. with the following modifications:

 

a)      Residences to be categorized on types A, B, C, D & E rather than area.

b)      The second sentence from para 7(e) i.e. “The allotment and control of IBA Staff Town accommodation shall be in the discretion of the Dean & Director IBA……” should be deleted.

c)      Only IBA employees will be allotted the houses. However, those IBA employees who have been provided residence in IBA Staff Town and have already provided accommodation to their private employee in a category E house, will have the customary right but for future it will not be applicable and for future cases para 8(i) of this rule will be applicable.

 

ACTION

 

6.8       The Director H.R. after incorporating the above recommendations would present the Allotment of Accommodation & House Rent Rules for IBA Staff Town to the A&FC.

 

6.9       The Director Projects would get preliminary exercise done for the construction of G+3 apartments to accommodate the faculty members living in Category A and B houses and future hires.
