Item No. 4      Proposal for Financial Assistance.

 

4.1       The Dean & Director said that according to the existing policy the top 10 percent students on the basis of admission test score continue to receive merit scholarship (50% concession) as long as they maintain CGPA of 3.5. However, others who were not included in the original merit list but have improve their CGPAs to 3.5 or above in subsequent semester(s) are not eligible for merit scholarship.  He wanted to know if the merit scholarships should remain confined to this original close group or opened up to recognize others who have done well subsequently.

 

4.2       The Registrar said that earlier top 50% were given concession.  Now he said that they have started a new way of recognizing students through Dean’s List.

 

4.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that top 10% students should be identified every academic year. He said that there is no replacement of the student who is dropped from the list of top 10% because of his CGPA falling below 3.5.  He was of the opinion that it should not be exclusive group rather it should be inclusive group.

 

4.4       The Dean & Director suggested to rename the Need-based Scholarship as Financial Aid to differentiate between the merit based scholarship and need based financial aid.  

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee recommended that top 10% students would be identified every academic year from the merit list and they would be given the scholarship i.e. 50% concession in their fee. 

 

4.6       The Committee decided that before reporting the change in the existing policy to the A&FC, input from the faculty on the proposed changes should also be obtained.

 

ACTION

 

4.7       The Registrar would send the proposed changes in the policy of Scholarship to all faculty for their input/comments.

a)      To consider request for release of 10% payment to UNISYS.

 

9.1       The Director ICT reported that M/s UNISYS had delivered the Storage devices to IBA on April 26, 2009. The payment terms were 60% payment at the time of delivery, 30% at the Provisional Acceptance Test (PAT) – Normally after 2-3 months once the configurations are done and tested, and remaining 10% at the Final Acceptance Test (FAT) – Normally after one year of PAT. However, in IBA’s case due to the non-availability of the Data Centers, he said that they could not follow the usual practice and the Storage devices could not be installed as per the plan. He said that to cover IBA’s interests, they signed an LOU with UNISYS that FAT shall be PAT an FAT shall be conducted once the storage is installed; this point is also mentioned in LOU (point No.4). UNISYS was required to fill in a 10% Performance Bond also which they complied with.

 

9.2       The Director ICT said that the matter was presented in the CPC which has suggested that:

 

·         Security Deposit may be released subject to furnishing Bank Guarantee of ‘A’ rated bank only for a period approved by the EC.

·         Holistic timeline and schedule of work to complete this project should be provided.

 

9.3       The Director ICT requested the EC to release the 10% payment to Unisys as LOU has been signed and they have the 10% Performance Bond for three years.
