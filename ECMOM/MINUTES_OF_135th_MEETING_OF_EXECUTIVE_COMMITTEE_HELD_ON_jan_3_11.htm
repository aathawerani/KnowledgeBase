Item No. 3      To consider and approve construction of New Boys’ Hostel: Revised Cost / Tender price of Phase 1 and Award  of Contract to the Lowest Bidder.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects said that the revised estimated cost for the Construction of New Boys’ Hostel indicated in the 2010-11 Budget is Rs.152 million, while the expenditure budgeted for the year is Rs.43.177 million. He said that this may exceed by about Rs.20 million. So a re-appropriation will be needed.

 

3.2       Mr. Shameem said that competitive bids for the construction work were invited from pre-qualified contractors in October 2010. In all five contractors participated in the bidding. He presented a Comparative Statement of the bids received and opened on November 10, 2010.  He said that as per the Comparative Statement M/s. Mughals Constructors have quoted the lowest price i.e. Rs.137,444,411/-, while M/s. Abaseen Construction are the second lowest at the quoted price of Rs.140,812,110/-. He said that the latter have subsequently offered a discount of 3.5% voluntarily and brought their price down to Rs. 135,883,242/-. He said that under the applicable rules the subsequent offer cannot be entertained. It may, however, be useful for getting some possible discount from the lowest bidder.

 

3.3       Mr. Shameem said that the five bids were evaluated by the Consultant, M/s. NBCL, as well as by the Project Manager, Consult-Tech International. He said that their comprehensive Evaluation Reports are available for study by members of the Executive Committee if they so desire. However, for the convenience of EC members, he presented copies of the relevant pages.  He said that both have recommended that the contract be awarded to the lowest bidder, namely, M/s. Mughal Constructors, at the corrected bid price of Rs.137,630,817/-. He further said that NBCL’s observations that “ (a) a list of manufacturers that form the basis of the bid should be provided to ensure that there are no deviations from approved brands, specifically for MEP Works, and  (b) a compliance statement to tender drawings, BoQ and specifications should be provided” will be taken care of by Consult-Tech International who is responsible for managing the project.

 

3.4       Mr. Shameem requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of contract to M/s. Mughal Constructors at the bid price of Rs.137,630,817/-  He also requested for re-appropriation in the 2010-11 Budget.

 

3.5       The Director observed that it is the responsibility of the Director Projects or whosoever is presenting the proposal to ensure that all the process is complete and is in accordance with the PPRA rules. He said that the procedure should be such that the department submitting the proposal should first present it to the Technical Evaluation Committee which would evaluate the proposal on technical ground whether it meets all the requirements or not and then present to the Central Purchase Committee to see if PPRA rules, evaluation criteria, transparency, have been followed or not and then to the Executive Committee which would recommend to the A&FC. He clarified that if it is mentioned in the manual that the TEC is a sub-body of the CPC then CPC can guide the TEC.

 

3.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that all the procedures have been followed and he takes full responsibility.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee approved the Revised Cost / Tender price of Phase 1 and Award  of Contract to the Lowest Bidder i.e. M/s. Mughal Constructors at a bid price of Rs.137,630,817/-

 

3.8       The Committee felt and decided accordingly that there was no need for re-appropriation as requested by Mr. Shameem Ahmed.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would present the recommendation of the EC to the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve Code of Conduct for use of ICT resources.

 

4.1       The Committee considered the Code of Conduct for use of ICT resources and made the following observations:

 

·         J3 from 5.2(c) to be deleted and it should be cross referenced to 11.1.

·         At 11.1 the “Scope” should be replaced with “Eligibility”.

·         The Director ICT was advised to consult the Registrar about the authorization to J1 & J2 staff

·         There is no replacement or disposal policy.

·         The name and designation of the person(s) to be contacted for help desk services should be mentioned in the policy.

·         The Users’ Manual should be separate from the Policy. 

·         Routine maintenance, back up to be covered in users’ manual and it hard copy of the manual should be available to everyone.

 

DECISON

 

4.2       The Director ICT after incorporating the above suggestions would send the draft Code of Conduct to the Registrar, the Director H.R. and Dr. Muhammad Nishat for their feedback and then present the final version to the EC.
