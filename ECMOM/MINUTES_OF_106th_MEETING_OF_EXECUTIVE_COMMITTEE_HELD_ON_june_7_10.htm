MINUTES OF THE 106TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JUNE 7, 2010

 

The 106th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 7, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                     Associate Dean

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                           Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                  Director Finance

  5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz               Director Projects

  6. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                           Director ICT

  7. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                           Director CED

  8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                       Director H.R.

 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve list of institutions for Computer donations.

 

1.1       The Director ICT presented a list of 21 institutions which he recommended for the donation of used IBA Computers.  He presented complete detail of the institutions which included contact person, date of establishment, number of students, existing computer set up, number of available PC’s, and requirement for PC’s. 

 

DECISION

 

1.2       The Committee approved the recommended list of institutions along with their requirements for PC’s except Zafar & Atia Foundation for which the Committee decided that initially 50 PC’s against the demand for 100 PC’s be donated and if needed, additional PC’s may be given at a later stage.

 

1.3       The Committee asked the Director ICT to obtain Tax Exemption Certificate from the institutions which are to be donated the PC’s.

 

ACTION

 

1.4       The Director ICT would obtain Tax Exemption Certificate from the institutions which would be donated the PC’s.

 

 

Item No. 2      Internal Audit Charter

 

2.1       Mr. Khalid Rashid Jhumra, Head of Internal Audit presenting the Internal Audit Charter before the Executive Committee said that the Charter has been prepared to serve as a guide in the performance of internal audit functions. He explained the Purpose, Role, Authority, Reporting procedure, Independence, Scope, Responsibility, Audit Planning, Quality Assurance, Standards of Audit Practice and Periodic Assessment given in the Charter.

 

2.2       The Director ICT commenting on the Role of Internal Audit suggested that instead of referring to the rules, the Audit Department should also explain and guide on issues referred to the Audit Department. 

 

2.3       The Head of Internal Audit agreed with the Director ICT that the Audit Department would also explain and guide on issues referred to them.  

 

2.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to include Audit of Information System in addition to audit of IT which he said is different from the Information System.

 

2.5       Mr. Khalid Jhumra clarified that audit of Information System is also included in the Audit Plan.

 

2.6       The Director Projects suggested that the draft of various agreements to be signed should also be vetted / scrutinized by the Head of Internal Audit so that the rules and policies are fully implemented.

 

2.7       The Head of Internal audit clarified that IAS is already providing consultancy services to vet rules / policies and advised the Director Projects to send a copy of draft agreement for review and comments.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee noted the presentation on Internal Audit Charter as presented by the Head of Internal Audit.

 

 

Item No. 3      Summer Term Rollout.

 

3.1       The Director ICT stated that the term activation for all the students is the mandatory step for Summer Term Enrolment.  He apprehended that due to the program offices staff being very busy in examination activity, it may be not be possible to complete the task on time which would derail the whole process of ERP implementation. To avoid further delay which they cannot afford under any circumstances, the Director ICT informed the Committee that he had a meeting with the Acting Dean and Director, and Associate Dean in which it was decided that the overtime to be paid to the IBA Staff who perform this task including ICT Staff and the program offices staff who want to perform this task during and after the examination duty.  However to make sure about the accuracy of the data, verification of all the data would still be the responsibility of program offices. He further said that in the same meeting it was also decided that the overtime shall be paid using the same scheme as used by the Testing Services.  He said that the total payment including payment for refreshments would be around Rs.125,000/- which could be charged to the ERP Project.

 

DECISION

 

3.2       The Committee considering the urgency of the job, endorsed the decision of the Acing Dean & Director and the Associate Dean that the staff to be involved in the data entry / verification job would be paid overtime using the payment scheme that is used in Testing Services and the payments would be charged to ERP Project.

 

           

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  To consider note from the Controller of Examinations on the request of Mr. Ammad Aleem, student of MBA Program for make-up exam.

 

4.1       The Controller of Examinations in his note of June 4, 2010 stated that Mr. Ammad Aleem, a student of MBA Program (Main Campus) did not appear in the semester final exams of 5 subjects in Fall Semester 2009 which was his first semester. As per the existing policy he should have been dropped from the program. However the Director MBA Program requested the Executive Committee in its meeting on April 12, 2010 to allow him make up exams on medical ground. The Director IBA commented that they cannot deviate from the existing policy.

 

4.2       The Director MBA Program made a proposal to the Academic Board and the BoG suggesting changes in the existing rules for make-up exams. The BoG in its last meeting held on May 8, 2010 did not approve the suggested changes and referred it back to the Academic Board. Meanwhile Ammad Aleem was allowed by the Director MBA Program to continue in the 2nd semester (Spring Semester 2010). The semester final exams would be commencing on 5th June 2010.

 

4.3       The Controller of Examinations in his note raised the question if they can allow Ammad Aleem to appear in the semester final exams.  Changes in the existing make-up exam rules recommended by the Director MBA Program were not approved by the BoG; it has been referred back to the Academic Board which will take some time to meet and further deliberate on the recommendations. Meanwhile semester final exams will be over.  His point of concern was that if the Academic Board finally turns down the recommended changes and Ammad Aleem is dropped from the program after the announcement of semester final results, IBA will be in a very awkward position. And if the student goes to the Court, it will be very difficult to justify before the Court that if the student was a drop-out case how he was allowed to continue for two semesters.

 

4.4       He requested the Executive Committee to pay attention to this serious situation and take a prompt decision.  

 

4.5       Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested that Mr. Ammad should be allowed to sit in the Semester Final Examinations but his result should be withheld till the decision is taken by the Academic Board and endorsed by the Board of Governors of IBA.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee allowed Mr. Ammad Aleem to appear in the Spring Semester 2010 Final Examinations against an undertaking to be given by Mr. Ammad Aleem that he would not hold IBA responsible for withholding his results pending decision of the Academic Board and ratification by the BoG. Further, if the revised make-up policy is not approved by the Academic Board and the Board of Governors of IBA, his standing in IBA as a student of MBA program would be decided as per the existing grading and evaluation rules and the Spring Semester 2010 Final Examination he has been allowed will stand ‘null and void”.

 

ACTION

 

4.7       The Registrar would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Ammad Aleem.

 

 

b)                 Make-up exam for a student whose father died the preceding day of the start of Semester Final Exams.

 

4.8       The Registrar reported that the father of a student was sick and died a day earlier of the start of the semester final exam and he had to take the dead body to interior of Sindh for burial.  He said that it would not be possible for him to come back and take exam the very next day.  He suggested that for such cases make up exams for first two days be allowed.

 

DECISION

 

4.9       The Committee decided that in such cases (death of mother / father) make up exams for a maximum of three days will be allowed.     

 

c)                  Offering open-ended contract.

 

4.10     The Director H.R. reported that the contract of three faculty members are to be renewed and after renewal it would be open-ended contract as it used to be in the past.

 

4.11     It was pointed out that for employees already retired at the age of superannuation, there are certain conditions for re-hiring for specified period.

 

DECISION

 

4.12     The Committee noted the renewal of contract on open-ended basis. The Committee advised the Director H.R. to check the rules for re-hiring retired employees.

 

d)                 Tender for creating Alumni website through outsourcing.

 

4.13     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui requested the EC to allow to float tender for creating Alumni Website through outsourcing.

 

DECISION

 

4.14     The Committee approved the concept of tendering for alumni website.

 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
