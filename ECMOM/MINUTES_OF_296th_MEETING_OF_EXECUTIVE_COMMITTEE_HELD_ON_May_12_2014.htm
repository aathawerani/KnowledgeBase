 1.2          The Dean & Director said that the MP Building is to be named as “NBP Technology Centre”.  All IT equipments including servers and racks will be placed in the hall of this building. It should be neatly organized so that it may appear like a standard Technology Centre. The offices of IT support and programs would be moved to where presently HR and Internal Audit offices are as the latter two offices would be shifted to Admin Building when ready. To the requirement of a store in the MP Building by the Registrar, the Dean & Director asked the Registrar, the Director Projects and the Head of ICT to resolve the spacing issue.  He advised the Director Projects to keep more open space in the hall, and configure it without incurring additional expense.

 1.3          The Dean & Director observed that for completion of Clinic and Transport Shed one year’s time is too long.  He said that these are minor works. He wanted that these to be completed before December 2014.  The Registrar said that at least the ground work (paver and road) should be completed and the Transport shed area should be cordoned off. However, he said that since the clinic will be front facing it would not look nice in an incomplete state. He said that the internal roads should be completed before the Convocation. About the placement of Kiosk, the Dean & Director said that if the kiosk does not fit into the plan, it should be placed somewhere else.  He said that we should adhere to the plan as too many adhoc changes will affect the final form, shape and configuration of the campus.

            Director Projects reiterated that the clinic and the Transport Facilities Buildings are not minor works and need to be carefully built. Five months’ period is too short specially under the uncertain conditions in Karachi. He said that we may cordon off the buildings with good quality barriers.

 1.4          About the Court Yard, the Dean & Director said that he had talked to Ms. Adeela Tarek and she has given go ahead. About the paving, the Dean & Director said that this is to be taken on high priority. The Registrar added that there is a need of a ring road too on priority.  The Dean & Director advised to make parking lot simple and complete it as soon as possible. The Dean & Director advised that the Main Lawn should be restored in its original shape.

 1.5          The Director Projects said that the work on Entry-Exit gates would be started in phase 2.  The Dean & Director said that it should be done in phase 1 as the first impression for any visitor is formed when they enter the main gate. He wanted to know as to how much money would be required for the Entry-Exit gates.  The Director Projects said that he will ask the Architects to undertake the design and estimation work and that he may be able to give a budgeted figure next week. The Dean and Director said that at present we may not replace the boundary wall but will have to make it presentable.

 1.6          The Registrar said that only Security barriers would be completed in phase 2.  He reported that cameras and equipments have already been approved. 

 1.7          The Director Projects said that he is constantly in touch with the GM Sui Gas. He said that as soon as they get the 300 meter pipe, the gas connection would be available and the internal gas line would also be provided at the Main Campus as part of the external development on going works.

 DECISION

 1.8          The Committee noted the monthly progress report along with the observations noted above.

Item No. 2      Presentation on renovation of old mosque at Main Campus.

 2.1          Mr. Noshad of M/s ICON  said that the Director Projects had asked them to re-design the old mosque (to be used by females) in such a way that it becomes part of the Campus.  He said that there were three major challenges; it had to be related to the campus orientation, at a minimum cost, while retaining privacy.   He said that all these aspects were kept in view.  He said that two walls, one towards auditorium and another towards library would be raised. The wall would work as divider. The ablution area, washroom and everything would be covered. He said that the pathways along the auditorium may have to be reduced from 10 feet to 8 feet. He said that since the mosque would be used by females, the Mehrab would not be required but he said that it will remain part of the Mosque.  He said that in order to beautify the wall, some Islamic calligraphy may be inscribed on it. He said that the cost estimate is around Rs.2 million which includes change of flooring, walls, court yard, windows and everything.

2.2          The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to show the presentation to Mr. Mohammad Asif and to the Iqra Society and incorporate their suggestions.  

2.3          The Registrar suggested that the outside work may be started immediately but the inside work of the mosque should be completed during the semester break. He said that the outside work should be given the highest priority. Mr. Noshad confirmed that they would do accordingly.  The Registrar asked for time line for the walls and internal work to which Mr. Noshad said that he would let him know.

2.4          The Dean & Director said that they may give this work on turn-key basis for which they have to find a donor.  He asked the Director Projects to request Mr. Aamir Abbasi to donate additional Rs.2 million for the renovation of this mosque and the whole complex will be after the name of late Pervez Abbasi.

DECISION & ACTION

2.5          The Committee noted and approved the renovation plan of the old mosque subject to the incorporation of any suggestions / amendments to be proposed by Mr. Mohammad Asif and Iqra Society.

2.6          The Director Projects would approach Mr. Aamir Abbasi for an additional donation of Rs.2 million for the renovation of the old mosque.

2.7          The Director Projects would obtain time line for completion of this project from Mr. Noshad and would send it to the EC for information.

Item No. 3           Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

Issues regarding assignment of Mr. Shahrukh Abbas.

3.1          Dr. Mohammad Nishat presented the case of Syed Shahrukh Abbas, student of BBA-II (06947). Mr. Shahrukh in his email to Dr. Framji Minwala and others stated that he took the course “English Grammar and Composition” from Dr. Framji in the previous semester i.e. Fall 2013. He submitted each and every assignment. However, when the results were uploaded he came to know that two of his assignments marks were not uploaded.

3.2          Dr. Minwala vide his email of April 9, 2014 accepted the narrative of Mr. Shahrukh. He accepted that it was his fault not that of Shahrukh. He stated in his email that when he uploaded marks of the final essay on the evening of April 7, he discovered that he did not have this assignment from three students. He contacted all three students via email and Shahrukh was the only one who responded and presented the assignment to him in person the very next day. However, as he had already posted the marks for his section, he could not upload the mark for his submission. He stated that he read the essay and marked it and requested to Dr. Shahid Mir, Chairman, Department of Management to add this mark (7.5 out of 10) and adjust the final grade of Mr. Shahrukh accordingly.  Dr. Shahid Mir approved the correction and asked Ms. Ghulam Fatima to up-date Mr. Shahrukh’s marks on the ERP.

DECISION

3.3          The Committee noted that it was an omission from the faculty side and not the fault of the student.  The Committee decided that the marks of the assignment not entered earlier due to omission, should be included in the final grade and entered into ERP.

He said that they can build a workshop with store over the underground water tank along with MP Building out of External Development Fund.

Review of draft IBA Budget 2014-15

3.6          Mr. Fahad Jawed presented the highlights of draft IBA Budget 2014-15.  The following comments / observations were made on the draft budget:

The justification for increase in the budget for Security from Rs.7 million to Rs.12 million.  The Registrar said that the number of security guards has increased and due to new company the rate will also increase. He said that for Aman Tower also they would require security guards. It was decided to trim to Rs.10 million.

Why Rs.2.3 million has been budgeted for the APWA Building which would be required for six months only while Rs.2.9 million was budgeted for 2013-14. The amount was reduced to Rs.1.5 million.

Rs.3.5 million budgeted for outsourcing services was reduced to Rs.1.5 million and shown under the head Maintenance of Facilities.

The Dean & Director advised to reduce the number of 349 desk top PCs requested by ICT. He said that for the licences and software packages he fully endorsed the requirements but for hardware the requirement has to be reviewed. The Registrar said the offices that would be vacated after the shifting of the staff to the Admin Building will have to be equipped with PCs, IP Phone etc. for which they require PCs and IPs. The Dean & Director said that there is no reason for duplicating facilities for one person. He said that the equipments in use in the existing offices should be moved to the Admin Building and offices to be vacated at Tabba or Aman-CED Building need not be equipped with desk top, IP Phone etc. He suggested that there should be critical minimum equipment available in stock which ICT department should be able to supply on demand and if the stock is depleted, new orders can be placed. Similarly, he said that the number of multimedia should be reduced from 45 to 20.  The Dean & Director said that he believes in portable facilities rather than fixed ones.

3.7          The Dean & Director said that our financial model is based on the principle that the operating revenues should either be equal to operating expenses (minus depreciation) or it should have the surplus.  He said that in 2008 when our strategy was made, we had 1800 students. This year when all facilities have been prepared, we will have 3400 students; thus adding 1600 students to our revenue stream. He said that those who criticise infrastructure development should be made to realize that this has enabled us to enhance the revenue through increased enrolment. In absence of these facilities and simply by increasing the fees, we could not even pay the salaries of our faculty and staff.

DECISION & ACTION

3.8          Mr. Fahad Javed would incorporate the suggested changes in the draft IBA Budget 2014-15. The hardware requirements of IT would be incorporated after the Head of ICT reviews the hardware requirements and gets them approved by the Dean & Director.                   

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
