MINUTES OF THE 57th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY JULY 20, 2009 

The 57th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 20, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Associate Dean
2. Mirza Sardar Husain Associate Dean (Acting)
3. Dr. Wasim A Khan Associate Dean (Acting)
4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN Registrar
5. Mr. Ali Taha Director Finance
6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
7. Mr. Zafar A. Sidiqui Director CED
8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi Director I.C.T
9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai Director H.R

Ms. Tahseen Jafary, Management Associate attended by special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider proposal from Mr. S.M. Qamar Abbas Rizvi, Security Coordinator regarding Strength of IBA Security.

1.1              Mr. Qamar Abbas Rizvi, Security Coordinator in his proposal stated that presently 29 guards are on the strength of IBA Security. Many of them are over aged, medically unfit / physically very weak and not enthusiastic to perform Security duties in professional manner. He also mentioned in his note that the daily wages guard Mr. M. Yameen is not available to Security Department as his service is being borrowed for some other purpose and contract guard Mr. Ismail is habitual late comer / absentee. 

1.2       Mr. Rizvi further mentioned in his note that massive construction work is in hand by PP&D therefore, large number of contractual labour is being engaged in Main Campus premises which required close monitoring.

1.3       Mr. Rizvi stated that at City Campus APWA building hired by IBA for its utilization as Library / Auditorium / Class rooms etc. needs additional security measures.  

1.4       Mr. Rizvi stated that Security allocated with 01x vehicle (Suzuki high roof) is without driver. He also stated that he has been allowed an office along with I.T facility however no PC Operator is available.

1.5      Mr. Rizvi suggested that in view of the security situation in the country in first Phase security guards at Girls hostel / Staff colony and Boys’ hostel need to be increased.  He further suggested that:       

a)   03x capable Male/Female Security Guards/Lady searchers with multiple experience may be inducted in IBA Security on Contract. 

b)      Security Guards Mr. Yameen and Mr. Ismail may be adjusted to some other department from security as they are mostly not available or consider themselves relinquished from Strength of Security.

1.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that first we should identify our need for security guards at both campuses, staff town and the boys’ and girls’ hostels before deciding to hire additional guards.

1.7              The Director said that he is not very happy with the daily wages security guards as there may not be so much commitment and loyalty from their side.  He said that either we should have our own regular security guards or we should outsource it.  He said that we will have to make cost benefit analysis of these two options. In cases such as staff town and hostels, our own guards whom we know for their character may be deployed while the campuses where professional security is required, outsourcing may be considered.  

DECISION

1.8              The Director said that as the Registrar is supervising the Security Coordinator, he should draw a security plan, human resources, equipment and physical infrastructure and deployment.  He further said that daily wage security guards should be redeployed and in future no hiring be made on contract, regular or daily wages for security. He requested the Registrar to find out as to how many of the regular security guards are retiring in the next one or two years.

ACTION

1.9       The Registrar will present a security plan to the Executive Committee within one month.

Item No. 2      To consider and approve draft Standard Operating Procedure prepared by H.R. Department on:

a)       Earned Leave

b)       Sick Leave

c)       Casual Leave

d)      Maternity Leave

e)      Study Leave

f)      Conference Leave

g)      Sabbatical Leave

h)      Student Recruitment – Internship, Part-time Employment and Teaching / Research Assignments.

 2.1              Ms. Tahseen Jafary, Management Associate HR presented highlights of the draft Standard Operating Procedure of the above listed leaves for faculty and staff. 

 2.2              The Director wanted to know if the leave rules of government educational institutions such as NED and Karachi University have been consulted or not.  The reply was in the negative however, they assured that they would consult the government rules.

 2.3              It was pointed out that internationally faculty members are hired for nine months while paid in 12 equal instalments. If they work beyond that period they are paid for that. 

 2.4              The Director said that he wants to encourage faculty members to use summer for research, assignments, professional development and other productive work. 

 2.5              Mirza Sardar Hussain suggested that there should be a uniform policy regarding provident fund, leave encashment and gratuity of all the employees; teaching and non-teaching. The existing leave balance (maximum 180 days) of teaching and non-teaching employees may be encashed and new rules should apply across the board. He also suggested that option may be given to all types of employees. However, the Director said that he would not like to open old cases.

 2.6              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that there should be provision for allowing advance leave to be adjusted from the leaves to be earned.  It was clarified by the Director H.R. that due to Audit objection un-earned leave cannot be allowed. 

 DECISION

 2.7              The Committee recorded its appreciation for Ms. Ayesha Menai, and Ms. Tahseen Jafary for preparing SOPs and asked them to prepare a memo for the A&FC for approval of the SOPs after incorporating the following amendments:

 

a)              Instead of department head, immediate supervisor will approve or disapprove the leave application and the department head would be kept informed.  The information about leave would be provided to all concerned.

 

b)              Till the time a deferred benefit scheme is offered by IBA, both the teaching and non-teaching staff can accumulate unlimited number of Earned Leave.  Also, non-teaching staff can continue to encash up to 30 days of accumulated leave per year, but teaching staff is not allowed any encashment, unless engaged in any mandatory activity during the semester break (like teaching a course). However, if IBA introduces a Deferred Benefit Scheme, accumulation of a maximum of 30 days Earned Leave will be allowed and it will be applicable to non-teaching staff only. Teaching staff will not be allowed any accumulation. At the time of introduction of such a deferred benefit scheme, the existing staff will be given an option of either continuing with the unlimited accumulation of Earned Leave and the related annual leave encashment, or opting for the Deferred Benefit Scheme, in which case they will only be allowed to accumulate Earned Leave up to 30 days. If non-teaching staff opt for the deferred benefit scheme, they will not be allowed any leave encashment, except for their current and previous year’s accumulated leave balance, at the time of leaving IBA. Teaching staff will not be allowed any leave encashment. For future employment the new policy (with Deferred benefit Scheme) will be applicable, and the new employees will not be given the choice as will be extended to existing employees.       

 c)              Medical Certificate from Registered Medical Practitioner will be required for 3 or more days’ sick leave.

d)              Medical Certificate will be required with Maternity Leave application.

e)              The word “Foreign” was deleted from the (Foreign Training) of the Study Leave as training may be arranged within the country as well.

f)               The requirement of 3 years continuous employment with IBA was reduced to 2 years for Study Leave.

g)              Instead of full salary for first year and half salary in the second year, full salary upto a maximum of four years be paid during study leave.

h)              Only that period during which the faculty awarded the Study Leave is physically away from IBA will be counted as Study Leave.

i)                Faculty going to other institutions under faculty exchange program will also be treated as on Study Leave.

j)                Enhancement of professional qualifications / experience should be linked with promotion policy.

k)              The current practice of recovering paid amount with interest on breach of contract was dropped. In such cases only the amount paid would be recovered.  However, this would be applicable with prospective effect.

l)                Faculty members availing Conference Leave will be required to present paper and disseminate their findings with the faculty and students of IBA at a seminar to be organized by our Centre for Research upon the return of the faculty member.

m)            The proposed Compensatory Leave rule was not approved. However, it was suggested that a Reward and Recognition scheme for extra-ordinary performance may be developed.

n)              For Sabbatical Leave, letter from the sponsor university will be required. 

o)              Internship at IBA be restricted for IBA students only. Students exchange with LUMS and other reputed foreign universities will be encouraged.    

p)              A group of faculty members teaching the same subject in different sections may also be given Teaching Assistant.

q)              Students of MS program should also be considered for part-time employment.

 ACTION

 2.8       The Director H.R. and Ms. Tahseen Jafary will prepare a memo with a comparative chart; present and proposed, highlighting the changes only, and full justification for new policies for A&FC’s approval.  

 Item No. 3      Suggestions of Executive Committee on the contents of appointment letters issued to teaching and non-teaching staff.

 3.1              The Director H.R. informed the Committee that the Board of Governors of IBA had asked her to get feed back from the faculty on the contract employment issued to faculty and staff. She presented the existing as well as proposed letter format for feedback from the EC members.

 3.2              The Registrar suggested that the designation and equivalent grade should also be mentioned in the appointment letter issued to faculty members.

 3.3              The Director H.R. said that they are working on the criteria and equivalent grade for faculty members. She said that for the time being the designation of “Full time faculty” is being used instead of a clear designation.

 3.4              It was suggested that the job responsibilities should be attached with the letter.

 3.5              The Committee approved the format of the proposed contract letter for faculty and staff.

  Item No. 5      To consider email form Mr. Mobin Khalili suggesting to preserve the structural history of IBA.

 

5.1      Mr. Mobin Khalili in his email suggested that in order to preserve the structural history of IBA we should arrange models/replicas of old buildings of both campuses before these are demolished. These models/replicas should be according to scale and of very high quality. Two models/replicas of each campus should be prepared to be displayed prominently at both campuses.

5.2      Mr. Khalili also suggested that we should also have aerial view colour photographs of both campuses. Enlarged photographs of old buildings can be displayed in new buildings. Quality videos should also be made of old buildings before these are demolished.

DECISION

5.3     The Director agreed to the suggestion. However, he said that it will be simple clay model of the old buildings without involving huge expenses.

ACTION

5.4       The Director Projects will arrange to get model of the existing buildings prepared.
