Item No. 1 To consider and approve the extension of time and escalation in the cost of the project “Laying of Water Transmission Line”.

1.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the project of Laying of Water Transmission Line was conceived and approved in 2008, which included:

(a) taking an independent water connection for IBA direct from Karachi Water and Sewerage Board. (This was necessitated due to acute shortage of water at the Main Campus, especially in the summer when the University reduced the water supply to IBA).

(b) construction of underground and overhead tanks of 100,000 and 50,000 gallons capacity respectively and their ancillary requirements at the Staff Colony, and

(c) laying of underground water transmission pipelines from the KW&SB main trunk line (located within the University premises) upto the underground water tank in the Staff Colony and from the overhead tank there upto the Main Campus and Boys’ Hostels.

1.2 On approval of the new water connection by KW&SB, NESPAK was hired by IBA as a consultant-cum-project manager. NESPAK was required to do all designing, prepare tender documents and, on award of the contract, provide detailed supervision and project-management services. After following all prescribed procedure the work was awarded to the successful (lowest and responsive) bidder, Samad Khan Babar & Co., on 20-10-2010, at the bid price of Rs.36,764,101/- The contractor commenced execution of the work on January 1, 2011 and was required to complete it in 10 months on October 31, 2011. However, the project execution according to the original design encountered unusual difficulties and obstacles of various nature right from the very beginning. These included lack of adequate pressure at water head in the KW&SB’s main trunk line, strong resistance by encroachers to laying the intake water pipeline along the University-approved right-of-way (Raah-e-Salim), non-availability of the corridor originally approved by the University for laying the pipeline from the Staff Colony to Boys’ Hostels, re-routing the pipeline (in consultation with University’s Master Planner, ECIL) and its delayed approval by the University, etc.

1.3 Mr. Shameem Ahmed further said that in addition to what has been stated above, as electrical power for CED building was urgently needed, a then-and-there decision had to be made to lay the KESC HT cable in the same trench that had been dug for laying the water pipeline from the Staff Colony to Boys’ Hostel. Besides, almost simultaneously, the ICT department approached and asked them to let their contractor Wateen to lay fiber optic cable in the same trench from the Staff Colony to Boys’ Hostel. The fiber optic cable laying had also been earlier delayed unduly due to non-availability of permission from the University. He said that they had no choice but to allow it in IBA’s interest. He said that they faced extreme pressure from HEJ and the University to fill the dugout trench and re-carpet and restore the road to its original condition. As a result of these unforeseen developments, not only the project completion got delayed inordinately, but its cost also has escalated. It has doubled from Rs.36.764 million to Rs.76.336 million. The project is no longer the same as was designed originally and for which tenders were invited.

1.4 Mr. Shameem said that complete details in this regard have been prepared by the project manager, NESPAK and they at his request, have agreed to make a presentation of the same before the EC. What is requested through the presentation is an approval of Extension of Time and the escalation in the cost of the project.

1.5 Mr. Shameem reported that the case was presented to the CPC in its meeting held on January 12, 2013. The CPC referred the case to the EC.

1.6 The Dean & Director said that the Director HEC Projects did not tell him during this period that he had got any problem in the execution of this project. He said that it is difficult to raise extra money to finance the additional cost.

1.7 Engr. Abdul Mateen of NESPAK said that the project was conceived in 2008 and started towards end of 2010. He said that as the project execution proceeded, it started running into serious problems. He said that the original design was based on at 20 feet pressure head at the connection point which was also confirmed by KW&SB but when they actually carried out the testing they found that the Water Board had provided pressure of 8 feet instead of 20 feet. As 8 feet pressure cannot work for a 2500 feet pipeline they had to switch to gravity flow. For this purpose the minimum depth of the trench required was 12 feet which they had to redesign. He said that after laying 550 feet line the work had to be stopped for the following reasons:

• The service corridor provided for laying of transmission line at Raah-e-Saleem had already been occupied by 36” dia water line, and 4” dia gas line. These two were so closely located to each other that it was difficult to lay 6” dia water line in the same corridor due to space constraints.

• Along the provided alignment, a number of encroachments are located i.e. houses and boundary wall and other structure. As the encroachers refused to remove their structures, the laying of pipe line along the approved alignment / corridor at 11 feet depth was no longer possible.

• Work on water supply line from overhead water tank to IBA Boys’ Hostel could not be started and remained held-up for six months because the service corridor having only 3 feet width provided by Karachi University for laying of transmission line at Road had already been fully occupied with existing services i.e. KESC sub-station, electric poles, optical fibre, manholes & other structures. Due to this laying of pipe line in the allocated service corridor at 5 to 6 feet depth was not possible.

1.8 Engr. Abdul Mateen said that it was suggested to find an alternate route and it took six months to find a different route but it was also saddled with encroachments. Then they proposed to lay the pipeline in the centre of the road with the coordination of the Karachi University. This decision was confirmed and approved. He said that they have placed three utilities in the same trench; water pipeline, optical fiber and the KESC high tension cable which was not originally planned but which was needed urgently. He also said that because of this there has been considerable saving for IBA.

1.9 The Dean & Director wanted to know as to how much has been saved for the three jobs done together. The Director ICT said that for Optic Fiber they had Rs.1 million.

1.10 Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that for high tension line they have given Rs.66 million to KESC and the KESC assigned this job to their sub-contractors to lay the pipeline. The Dean & Director said that KESC used our trench and we paid the whole amount to KESC. He said that it was the responsibility of KESC to dig the trench but it was provided by IBA. He asked Mr. Moeid Sultan to see as to what provisions exist in the original contract with KESC in this regard.

1.11 Engr. Abdul Mateen said that 1800 feet line is held up and out of that 550 feet have been completed. He said that now they would take it diagonally. The Dean & Director said that the recurring cost for repairs will have to be paid by IBA if dig up the road for one reason or the other.

1.12 Engr. Abdul Mateen said that due to change in design five variation orders have been placed. He requested the EC to approve the extra/additional work amounting to Rs.39.27 million.

1.13 The Dean & Director said that he has no problem in extending the time but he wanted to know as to who authrorized the expenditure above Rs.36 million without the approval o the necessary bodies. Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that he had no realization that the cost was escalating so much. He further said that the five variation orders are additional items and they are not included in the original BoQ. The justification for these orders has been provided by the Project Manager i.e. NESPAK.

DECISION

1.14 The Committee constituted a sub-committee comprising the Director Projects, the Director Finance and the Chairman CPC. The Committee would thoroughly analyze the case and give its recommendation to the EC in its next meeting. The Director Projects who would be the convenor of this sub-committee will also invite Engr. Abdul Mateen of NESPAK to the meeting of the sub-committee.

The Bid Documents and Technical details of the contractors were sent both to M/s NBCL and M/s NESPAK for their scrutiny and Evaluation. M/s NESPAK and M/s NBCL submitted their reports. As per their reports they corrected the arithmetical errors of M/s. SMK Construction which was Rs.23,070,532/- and not Rs.25,070,532/-. The rest were correctly calculated.

2.2 Based on the above rates and given details both Project Manager M/s. NESPAK and Project Architect M/s. NBCL recommended M/s. Hasscon Services as the lowest evaluated bidders and recommended IBA to award the work at a cost of Rs.15,052,000/- The case was presented in TEC on January 23, 2013 which recommended the award of work to the lowest bidder M/s. NBCL and the Technical Evaluation Committee the case was presented to the Central Purchase Committee on 26th January 2013. The Central Purchase Committee, after due scrutiny, recommended the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s. Hasscon Services at a cost of Rs.15,052,000/-

2.3 The Director Projects reported that there were two technical observations raised by the members of the Technical Evaluation Committee as well as the CPC regarding provision of Fire suppression system and the surveillance system. He said that the fire alarm system provision is in the contract of electrical infrastructure whereas the provision for conducting of surveillance cameras is already a part of contract. He further said that the Consultants have been asked to design the fire suppression system and let them know the cost. That can be taken care of independently. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder as detailed above. He said that Rs.16 million is available in the budget for this under the head of External Development Fund. He said that the Power Centre Building would be constructed behind the AMAN-CED Building.

DECISION

2.4 The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of construction of Power Centre Building to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s. Hasscon Services at a cost of Rs.15,052,000/-.

Item No. 3 To consider and approve the continuation of NBCL Consultancy Services for Phase 2 of the New Boys Hostel.

3.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that IBA hired Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Ltd. (NBCL) for providing consultancy services for construction of New Boys’ Hostel, Phase 1, comprising the Front Block and Block A, at a fee of 4% of the total value of the project (approx. He suggested that we can ask them to reduce the fee. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed with the Director Projects and advised Mr. Shameem Ahmed to convince BBCL to agree at 2%. The Dean & Director asked Engr. Rehanul Ambia to talk to NBCL in this regard.

3.6 The Committee decided that the Director Projects would talk to NBCL to reduce the fee for the B & C blocks of the new Boys’ Hostel.


Item No. 4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Revision in Completion date of Tier III Data Centre at IBA City Campus.

4.1 The Director ICT said that he has invited Mr. Waseem of H.K. Shah to present the request for revision in completion date of Tier III Data Centre at IBA City Campus.

4.2 Mr. Waseem said that during the presentation made by Mr. Maroof, CEO of AMFCO, the Executive Committee was informed that the completion of data centre will take 170 days after the signing of the contract. At that time the signing of the contract was expected to be July 28, 2012 and the expected project completion date was January 31, 2013. However, the Executive Committee decided to conduct a value engineering exercise to save the cost. The value engineering meant to restart the search of products with comparable specifications and features. This also necessitated to redo the contracts and other documentations. They had to make new connections with the different product vendors which took substantial time as they had to evaluate these new products before submitting them to IBA for approval. Ultimately the new contract was signed off on September 26, 2012. He said that the new completion date by adding 170 days comes out to be March 27, 2013. He assured that they would try their best to meet the 170 days time limit. However, he said that since they would be dealing with new vendors and new products, the approval of these products by IBA authorities might cause some delays in ordering these products which may cause additional days in delivery of these products and completion of the Data Centre. He requested the EC to approve the revised completion date of March 27, 2013. He confirmed that there is no cost escalation.

4.3 The Dean & Director said that H.K. Shah should have done the work in parallel in good faith so that the time could have been saved. He asked Mr. Waseem to prepare Critical Path Analysis (CPA) and give it to Mr. Mansoor. Mr. Mansoor said that four submittals which are minor in nature have not yet been received from H.K. Shah. Mr. Waseem assured that they would send them in the next three to four days.

4.4 The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what are the common elements in the data centres of the two campuses. He suggested to design the data centre of the Main Campus in such a way that essential parts are fully redundant and compliant with tier III and the rest can be kept at Tier II. He said that security of servers should be fully reliant because we want to provide services to small banks which cannot afford to have their own data centre. These prospective clients would be more concerned about the security and confidentiality of the data.

4.5 Mr. Imran Batada said that the Contractors’ understanding is that the project cost will be paid to AMFCO and the taxes will have to be paid by IBA. Mr. Moeid Sultan said that income tax will be withheld according to contract. Mr. Waseem said that the Contractor is referring to clause 4.1 of the contract according to which IBA will bear all the taxes. The Dean & Director said that on imported items there are sales tax and central excise duties and import duty but income tax is something which we are bound to deduct and withhold. He said that income tax is a part of the profit which the contractor has to pay and for other taxes IBA has the exemption. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Waseem to convince AMFCO that we want to be fair but we have to follow legal obligations.

4.6 The Registrar said that servers and others equipments will come in sealed units and for the Air-conditioners it is pre-requisite that they be up. He wanted to know the date when the AC will be on. He said that the size of the equipment should be checked and ensured that it passes through the doors or windows and if not then doors or windows can be enlarged. He informed the Committee that on the third floor where computer lab is located, the racks were to be placed but due to its size it was taken in by breaking the window. Mr. Waseem said that the Air-conditioner will be activated before March 2013. The Registrar asked to fix the date. Mr. Waseem said that he would inform the exact date.

4.7 To a question from the Director Projects, whether pre-shipment inspection of the equipment has been done, Mr. Waseem said that it has not been done because of various reasons; time and money being the main factors but he confirmed that factory tested certificate for the equipments will be obtained.

DECISION

4.8 The Committee approved the revised completion date of March 27, 2013 of the data centre at the City Campus.

ACTION

4.9 Mr. Waseem would provide CPA to Mr. Mansoor Ali, and the date to the Registrar on which the AC’ would be activated.

4.10 The clauses on tax exemption will be referred by the Director Finance for legal opinion.
