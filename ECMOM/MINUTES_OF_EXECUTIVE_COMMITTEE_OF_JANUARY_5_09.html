1.14          Regarding payment of fees through online payment, the Director Finance was advised to fully disseminate the information by placing it on the IBA Portal, Notice Boards and informing the Program Offices and the parents of the students.

1.15          Regarding recovery of fee, the Dean & Director stated that the figure of receivables provided by Finance Department was not correct. He said that the fee of students whose scholarship has not yet been decided has also been shown as receivable.  He requested the Registrar to finalize the scholarship so that fees of such students may not be shown in the receivables.

1.16          The Director Finance reported that IBA has not yet received Scholarship amount from HBL given in the name of Dr. Ishrat Husain.  The Director Finance should contact HBL to obtain the amount.

1.17          Regarding payment of one months full pay to Mirza Noman Shah, the Dean & Director said that we should look after our employees.  He said that Mirza Noman Shah came to office despite Doctors advice. He asked the Director Finance to create a Welfare Fund for staff and students out of the unclaimed deposits and refunds. He assured that if needed, he can arrange money from philanthropists for Welfare Fund.

1.18          The Committee recommended that the Board of Governors be moved to approve the creation of such a Fund and that outsiders / philanthropists may also be permitted to donate to the IBA Welfare Fund for staff and students. The Director Finance will prepare a paper for the BoG meeting.

1.19          Regarding purchase of new cars and the option of outsourcing transportation services for the Chinese faculty, the Dean & Director said that we may outsource the transportation arrangement. However, since the car for Director Alumni is available, it may be sent to the City Campus for the Chinese faculty.  

1.20          He also said that we should dispose off fully depreciated cars and get new ones in replacement.  He asked the Registrar to make a policy that vehicles after specified period should be disposed off through a procedure approved by A&FC and new vehicles in place of old ones may be purchased.

1.21          The Dean & Director opined that there is no need of Tracker as the IBA vehicles are fully insured. However, he said that if the rebate on insurance premium due to Tracker is significant and equals payments for trackers, then it could be justified to have a Tracker.

Item No. 4      To consider note from Registrar regarding Diesel / Lubricants for Generator 75 KVA installed at IBA Boys Hostel.

4.1              The Registrar informed the Committee that the 75 KVA generator installed at the IBA Boys Hostel has been commissioned.  He stated that the valuable addition of facility for hostel residents raises the question about its operative expenses as well as maintenance charges.  He recommended that IBA look after the maintenance expenses of its facility and the running charges pertaining to diesel and lubricants be charged to the users i.e. the hostelites.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee decided that along with maintenance expenses, the running charges pertaining to diesel and lubricants would also be borne by the IBA.

ACTION

4.3              The Registrar would instruct concerned person to arrange diesel and lubricants for the generator of IBA Boys Hostel.

Item No. 5      To consider application from Mr. Waqar Ahmed for Study Break.

5.1              Mr. Waqar Ahmad, a student of MBA (City Campus) stated that he has completed three semesters with good grades and now getting into the final semester. He stated that before getting into IBA, he had appeared in CSS Examinations which he qualified and was allocated in Foreign Service of Pakistan.  He further stated that he has been called for the Common Training Program commencing from January 15, 2009 and after that he would have to go through Specialized Training program related to Foreign Service of Pakistan.  He requested for one year study break so that he may be able to successfully complete his probation period of training under Federal Government of Pakistan and later on rejoin IBA to complete his final semester.

DECISION

5.2              The Committee granted study break for one year to Mr. Waqar Ahmed from January to December 2009.

ACTION

5.3              The Registrar will convey the decision of the Executive Committee regarding approval of study break for one year to Mr. Waqar Ahmad. He would also convey the decision to the concerned Program Office.

Item No. 11    Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)         Emergent repairs of broken chairs of City Campus Auditorium.

11.1     The Director Projects in his note of January 3, 2009 stated that he was informed by the Director H.R. that a few companies from the market were to hold recruiting sessions of IBA graduates on January 12, 2009 and that those companies have demanded that the seats which are totally broken should be fixed. He stated that he personally inspected the seats for assessment of immediate repairs. He said that although the seats were totally worn out and in a very bad shape, yet some repair work would make them useable for the period till such time the Auditorium is fully renovated.

11.2          The Director Projects stated that sealed quotations were invited from the experienced contractors who were explained the job and the site in his presence. The sealed quotations were received on January 3, 2009 and it was found that M/s. VIP Workers are the lowest bidder who agreed to repair 250 chairs at a cost of Rs.42,500/-

11.3          He requested the Committee to approve the award of work to M/s. VIP Workers.

DECISION

11.4          The Committee approved the award of work to M/s. VIP Workers for repair of 250 chairs at a cost of Rs.42,500/-

ACTION

11.5          The Director Projects would issue Work Order to M/s. VIP Workers.

b)        Generator Operators for both the Campuses

11.6          The Director I.T. informed the Committee that the generator at City Campus gave trouble again due to which the Server was down. It was pointed out that the problem usually occurs during night or holidays when no body is readily available to operate the generator.

11.7          The Registrar suggested that there is a need to have a Generator Operator during night shift for both the campuses.

11.8          The Director Projects said that he has included generator operators in the proposed organogram.

DECISION

11.9          The Dean & Director said that if the position of operator has been included in the organogram and we seriously need such persons for essential services, we should hire the person without waiting for approval of the organogram.

11.10      The Dean & Director requested the Director Projects and the Director I.T. to develop a SOP for the operation and maintenance of generators and provide that to concerned persons.

11.11      The Director also said that old generators which are unhealthy should be overhauled or if needed those should be replaced with new ones.

ACTION

11.12      The Director Projects in consultation with Director I.T. would develop a SOP for generator.

11.13      The Director Projects would also provide job description and job specifications for Generator Operator to the Director H.R. for recruitment.

c)         Lap Top for Faculty

11.14      The Director I.T. informed the Committee that nine sets of lap top have been procured and 17 more are in the process of procurement. He said that some faculty members are demanding lap top but there is no provision of lap top in the budget for them.

DECISION

11.15      The Director said that every faculty member should have a lap top.  He also advised the Director I.T. to obtain a choice from all faculty members whether they want to keep lap top or P.C.  He further advised the Director I.T. to pool all the requisitions and make bulk purchases and provide them to the faculty on first-come-first serve basis.

ACTION

11.16      The Director I.T. would obtain requisition and choice of PC or Lap Top and accordingly order for bulk purchases.
