MINUTES OF THE 132ND MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 13, 2010

 

The 132nd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 13, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                           Associate Dean

2.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN              Registrar

3.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                   Controller of Examinations

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                        Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                     Director Projects

6.      Mr. M. Faisal Zia, nominee of                  Director ICT

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider EC decisions implementation reports:

 

Up-date from Director HEC Projects:

 

1.1       Bids for the construction of a new KESC sub-station and relocation of the four generators at the City Campus:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed, the Director HEC Projects reported that competitive bids were opened on December 4, 2010. These will be presented before the A&FC in its forthcoming meeting.

 

1.2       Construction of a new Boys’ Hostel: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the bids received have been evaluated by the consultant and are now under scrutiny by the project manager. Their evaluation and recommendation for award of the contract will be put up for approvals by reconstituted TEC, CPC, EXCO, A&FC and finally by BOG. Actual construction work will start in about 3 weeks after the final approval; most probably in February 2011.

 

1.3       New Girls’ Hostel: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the designing work was progressing speedily and hopefully it would be presented before the Executive Committee for approval in February 2011.

 

1.4       PC-1 for new Girls’ Hostel for submission to P&D Department of Sindh Government:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the draft of PC-1 was prepared and put up for approval.  The Director IBA has given his guidelines which will be followed to finalize the document and it would be ready by the end of this month.

 

1.5       Girls’ Hostel Extension: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the last date the contractor has given for completion of the construction work is December 15, 2010. He said that grills and some additional furniture are being ordered. The hostel will be ready for occupation in January 2011.

1.6       New sub-station for KESC’s approval:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the two sub-stations are to be built by IBA; one in Staff Colony and the other in Boys’ Hostel.  He said that the sub-stations will be constructed when the load is approved which is expected in January or February 2011 and it would be part of the external development work being done by Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

 

1.7       Visiting Faculty Hostel: The design of the Visiting Faculty Hostel has been completed. The consultant (NBCL) has promised to submit draft tender documents this week. These will be scrutinized by the Project Manager. The tenders would be floated in early February 2011.

 

1.8       Relocation of four generators at the City Campus: Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the bids received on December 4, 2010 were under evaluation by the Consultant. He said that as an independent project manager needs to be appointed too, the actual construction work is likely to start in February, 2011. This may coincide with ground-breaking for the Multi-Storey Building at the City Campus.

 

1.9       Mutation of the Main Campus Land in IBA’s Name: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that there is no further progress and no further progress in sight as he said that they are not getting the needed cooperation from the University of Karachi.

 

Up-date from Director Projects:

 

1.10     The Director Projects to arrange storage facilities at Multipurpose Building when it is vacated and / or include storage areas in new Academic Block and CED: The Director Projects stated that for MP Building, the advice has been noted.  He said that Architects have been advised for creation of storage spaces in both the buildings.

 

1.11     Space for Data Centre at City Campus:  The Director Projects reported that the Data Centre will be completed within 10 days. But he said that now it is not needed by ICT anymore.

 

1.12     Mr. M. Faisal Zia (followed by Dr. Arshad Siddiqi on phone) said that they needed the temporary data centre.

 

            The Committee decided that Dr. Arshad Siddiqi, Mr. M. Faisal Zia, and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz under the chairmanship of the Registrar should hold a meeting the next day i.e. December 15 and resolve this issue. They all agreed to meet on December 15 at 9:00 a.m. at the Main Campus.         

 

1.13     Insurance Claim: The Director Projects reported that Mr. Sanaullah Khan of NIC has gone to perform Haj and has not yet returned.  He said that there is little possibility of getting the claim as it is not covered in the policy.

 

1.14     Departmental Manual:  The Director Projects said that they would endeavour to complete the Departmental Manual by the end of December 2010.

 

1.15     External Development Design around Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus:  The Director Projects reported that after opening of the bids on November 4 and going through the approval cycles of EC and CPC the work was awarded to M/s. The Technologist on November 11, 2010.  It has been 50% complete. Completion date is 31st January 2011.

 

1.16     Water Proofing of the Roofs and Covering of Walking Passage:  The Director Projects said that no solution has yet been finalized.  However, he said that they have installed one aluminium door covering entire opening in front of the VC Room.

 

            The Registrar suggested to fix doors at each opening.

 

1.17     Furniture and wooden partitioning work at Centre for Business & Economic Research:  The Director Projects reported that at the last minute the location of the research room was changed so they had to re-do the complete exercise of layouts. He said that the work is now 40% complete and should be 100% complete in 15 days i.e. by 27th December 2010.

 

1.18     Data Centre Civil Works at IBA Main Campus: The Director Projects said that they could not overcome the hindrances as some rooms are still occupied.  He said that it became Project Department’s responsibility to get these vacated by creating spaces.  He further said that they have found one room in the auditorium for shifting of books.  He said that in a meeting with ICT Department, it was agreed that the Projects Department can construct temporary storage provided the ICT Department raises a requisition. He said that the requisition is awaited. He further said that there is a deviation from original plan initiated by ICT Department for which drawings are awaited from the Architects. He said that while the date of 9th December could not be achieved, no completion date can be given keeping in view the above mentioned constraints.

 

            The Registrar said that the lab and the books kept by the Librarian should remain there.  He said that the Societies have no room to meet and proposed to give them room at auditorium.

 

            Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that they would construct a pump room for water projects.  He suggested that on the roof of the Pump Room, store and furniture may be placed.  However, for that he said that the foundation should be strong enough to bear the load of one more floor.

 

Dr. Mohammad Nishat requested the Registrar and the Director Projects to sit and resolve the issue.  He also requested Mr. M. Faisal Zia to raise the requisition for temporary storage for ICT Department. 

 

1.19     Designing and development of Sports Ground: The Director Projects reported that RFPs were floated to pre-qualified Architects. Their Technical and Financial Proposals have been received and are under evaluation.

 

DECISION

 

1.20     The Committee noted and approved the status reports along with comments noted above.

 

 

Item No. 2      To reassess the request of Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair for building up a Scientific Computing Infrastructure at IBA.

 

2.1       The Committee reviewed the earlier decision of the EC wherein Dr. Hisham had submitted a proposal for a computer for research and he was advised by the EC to approach HEC for funding for acquiring Intel Xeon Computer for research work. The Committee reviewed its earlier decision in light of the fact that IBA has already submitted a proposal of Rs.700 million to the HEC, so making another request for merely Rs.1.5 million would not be reasonable.

 

DECISION

 

2.2       The Committee decided to arrange the required machine from IBA’s own fund by re-appropriating the amount, if needed.

 

ACTION

 

2.3       The Director ICT would arrange the required machine by re-appropriating the amount, if so required.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve a proposal for Prayer Hall at IBA Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the Projects Department had planned the commencement of the construction of Prayer Hall at Main Campus in January, 2011 and completion by December, 2011. He said that the representatives of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Limited had presented the conceptual design of the new prayer hall to the Executive Committee in its 100th meeting held on 10th May, 2010. M/s. NBCL has designed the Project free of cost. He said that they received the tender documents on 6th December, 2010 and on completion of the procurement process the construction may commence in January, 2011. The cost estimate of Rs......... Million has been received by M/s. NBCL on 4th December, 2010 for a covered area of 3,300 Sq-ft and landscape area of 4,360 Sq-ft. Provision in the Budget was made for Rs......Million at which time the conceptual design was not even initiated. He requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the above cost estimate based on the approved design.

 

3.2       The Registrar said that there is no need to break the existing prayer hall.  He suggested that the plan for construction of a new prayer hall be postponed.

           

3.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the existing prayer hall is serving the purpose.  He suggested that minor renovation may be done to match it with other buildings.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that the existing prayer hall does not fit into the Master Plan and it has to be vacated.

3.5       It was suggested that if IBA finds a donor for the proposed prayer hall, then it may go for it. It was also suggested that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami may be given a certificate recognizing that he prepared the design for the prayer hall free of cost.

 

            DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee decided that the proposed prayer hall may be constructed if a donor is available for funding this project.

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Proposal for a panel of doctors for evaluation of medical reports presented by students.

 

4.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that requests for make-up examinations on medical ground are increasing. He said that he has received many more applications after the three applications already presented to the EC in its last meeting.  He said that the rule is to allow make-up in a very serious condition only and not for minor illness.  He proposed that there should be a doctor on the panel to evaluate the medical reports to be submitted by the students and on the basis of the recommendation of the doctor make-up may be allowed.

 

4.2       The Registrar suggested that the Controller of Examinations should write a note which should be issued by the Dean & Director advising the students not to mis-use this facility.

 

DECISION

 

4.3       The Committee advised Mr. Mobin Khalili to send a formal proposal to all members of the Executive Committee for consideration.

 

4.4       The Committee allowed the Controller of Examinations to take action on remaining applications for make-up examinations on merit as per IBA Policy.

 

            There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
