The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve KPIs.

 

1.1       The Dean & Director said that he felt that after these few years experience with Performance appraisal process we should reflect and see what we have learnt. It is clear that we have to make a distinction between the service units and other departments where the interaction takes place between the students, faculty or alumni or outsiders with service units and parts of the departments the AC Nielsen type of surveys should be useful. These surveys should be selective, and not be carried out across the board. He said that in some case there will be a mix of the survey results as well as the views of the supervisors. He said that he has invited Mr. Zaffar A. Khan, who is a very experienced person and is the team leader for Strategy 2020, to help us think through this process.  He said that every department has to be treated in a different way rather than uniform way. He invited comments from the participants for making a more rational and relevant system.

 

1.2       Mr. Aman U. Saiyed said that AC Nielson has a standard set of questionnaire which they put before every one.  The Dean & Director said that the questions are given by the IBA Administration to AC Nielsen. He said that the questions can be modified.  Mr. Aman U. Saiyed suggested that for the survey the number of persons that comes in contact with a particular department should be considered rather than the number of persons in a department.  The Dean & Director agreed. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that every department has some deliverables.  There should be clarity on what are those deliverables. He said that the deliverables should include element of challenge too. The group should feel that those deliverables tie in with the overall institutional goals.  At the end of the year when the performance evaluation is done the element of surprise will be reduced if the supervisors have met three or four times with the staff to review the process. He said that this process has to be institutionalized although it would take time. He said that the goals have to be smartly specified and the KPIs should be in a standardised format. He recommended to arrange short training so that everybody is on the same page. He said that there should be three columns: deliverables, KPIs and final assessment.   KPI should be measurable and quantifiable against the benchmark.

 

1.3       The Committee then considered the KPIs presented by the following HoDs:

 

Dr. M. Shahid Qureshi, Program Director CED:

 

1.4       Dr. M. Shahid Qureshi presented the suggested KPIs for Centre for Entrepreneurial Development. He presented activity-wise KPIs for CED which were (a) Strategic Activities, (b) IBA Student Related activities, (c) non-student activities, (d) incubation activities, (e) Outreach activities, (f) Research related activities.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director suggested that the process should not be included in the KPI. He said that the Deliverables for KPI should be:

 

o   Women entrepreneurships

o   Youth Entrepreneurship

o   Agricultural development

o   Number of courses to deliver

o   Fund raising.

 

1.6       The KPI should suggest:

 

o   The number of students to be increased from say 50 to 60,

o   New Programs or training

o   Research and case studies (how many research and publications and how many case studies as compared to last year)

o   Incubation and Start up (How many incubation and start ups)

o   Financial objectives and their KPIs  

 

1.7       The Dean & Director said that CED would not be required to be surveyed by AC Nielsen. The evaluation would only be done internally for the courses attended by the participants at the end of the course.

 

Dr. Qazi Masood Ahmed, Director, Centre for Business & Economic Research (CBER):

 

1.8       Dr. Qazi Masood Ahmed said that they have three deliverables; (1) Contract Research, (2) In-house research,  and (3) Research Dissemination, which are in progress. He said that they have got two contract research projects. One is CCI Project which will be completed in October 2014 but it has been agreed that this project will be extended for three years.  Another project is with World Bank – Sindh Government and IBA to start a project on Fiscal Reforms Unit at Sindh Government from October 2014 initially for one year.

 

1.9       The Dean & Director suggested to divide Contract Research into two:  (a) On-going project, and (b) New Research Contracts.  He said that Contract Research, In-House Research, and dissemination are the three deliverables of CBER. In-house research includes research grants, conferences and, dissemination includes the number of seminars, conferences and workshops conducted and guest speakers invited. He suggested to internally keep record of ISI Journal, and HEC recognized journals published by CBER. The Dean & Director said that CBER would not be surveyed by AC Nielsen. The evaluation would only be done internally for the courses by the participants at the end of the course.   

 

1.10     Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested that instead of writing “In progress” mention the milestone achieved. 

 

Mr. Izhar Husain Mirza, Director Centre for Executive Education (CEE):

 

1.11     Mr. Izhar Husain Mirza presented 12 KPIs for CEE. The Dean & Director suggested that first the deliverables should be identified.  He said that the following four could be the deliverables of CEE:

 

            Open courses

            Customized courses

            Collaborative courses with foreign institutions

            Net Income earned during the year

 

1.12     The Dean & Director said that in Open Courses, if the old course is substituted by new course because of market demand that would be differentiation from those which are repetitive.  The number of participants will be the KPI.  For customize courses, other than the existing clients, the new clients brought in and the number of courses and participants will be the KPI.  The third KPI would be number of collaborative courses to be offered with ISB, or SP Jain etc. and the targeted number of participants.  There would be target for each program such as advance management program, executive development program etc. The fourth would be the target of net profit and what is achieved.

 

Dr. Nasir Touheed, Head of Testing Services:

 

1.13     Dr. Nasir Touheed presented six KPIs for the Testing Department.  The Dean & Director said that there are four major deliverables for Testing Department:

 

1)       In-house test which they have to do twice a year (except EMBA) on time and number of candidates – increasing or decreasing.

2)      External Client, PSO, PIA, etc. – if it is increasing or decreasing.

3)      Quality and innovation.  If the contents, new questions are brought every year or not, and if there are elements of surprise or not.

4)      Net Revenue generated.

 

1.14     Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested to include some challenge, innovation through technology.  Dr. Nasir Touheed said that they are moving to on-line test. The Dean & Director suggested that they should see what is happening at CAT, SAT, GMAT and absorb new ideas and new ways of doing things. He said that some psychological tests should also be done. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that in quality minimization of errors should be one of the KPIs. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested that some quantification such as the number of tests, number of candidates and revenue generated should be included in the KPIs.  The Dean & Director said that the Testing Department will not be surveyed by AC Nielson, internal team will give its feedback.

 

Dr. Zeenat Ismail, Coordinator, NTHP, STHP Foundation Programs:

 

1.15     Dr. Zeenat Ismail presented the proposed KPIs of NTHP/STHP programs along with their means of verifications. 

 

1.16     The Dean & Director said that the deliverables for NTHP/STHP Programs would be (a) the number of students brought in and the number of students admitted, (b) new programs introduced and how many participated / enrolled in that program, (c) financing (if NTHP financing is solid or not).  The goal to increase the number of participants every year. 

 

1.17     The Dean & Director suggested to include in success indicators those students who were trained at IBA and went somewhere else.  He said that as there would be hard numbers, it would not require survey. The management team would review it and will see if the target was achieved or not.

 

Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Associate Dean, FCS:

 

1.18     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that he has divided the KPIs of FCS into seven sub-sections.  The Dean & Director said that the deliverables for FCS are: (a) Faculty Publications and Research; (b) Faculty Performance; and (c) Faculty Development.  He said that on these deliverables KPIs are to be developed.  He said that the supporting information such as the number of students currently enrolled, and the number of students targeted to be enrolled should be for their own record but for the management review just take a few most important and critical indicators. He said that the target should be to increase the existing percentage of research & publications. He suggested that instead of the number of CEE sessions conducted in collaboration by FCS faculty, it should be the number of students placed in new IT firms for final job or for internship that would be one of the success factors.  The Dean & Director said that it is said that IBA has got both Computer Science and Business Administration students and faculty but they are not doing any IT start ups in entrepreneurship.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that there were many students who were engaged in setting up their own enterprises.  The Dean & Director said that it needs to be publicized.  A joint goal of CDC and FCS should be the new start ups in a year and success of the older start ups.

 

Dr. Muhammad Nishat, Associate Dean, FBA:

 

1.19     The Dean & Director said that the deliverables for FBA would be the same as that of FCS.  He suggested that along with the targeted number of publications it should also be mentioned that out x number of publications, so many publications will be in the ISI journal. The number of new faculty to be recruited, curriculum, new courses introduced in Management, Finance, Marketing will be the deliverables. He suggested to separately indicate the average faculty evaluation target for full time and visiting faculty under External Partnership Program, the number of students benefited from this program and the faculty collaborated with other faculty would be the KPIs.

 

Mr. Muhammad Saleem Umer, Program Director EMBA:

 

1.20     Mr. M. Saleem Umer proposed the following five deliverables, their yardstick and verification source:

 

a)      Program Marketing

b)      Program Management

c)      Quality Assurance

d)     Facilitation

e)      Net profit

 

1.21     The Dean & Director suggested that there should be three major deliverables; (a) Program Management including marketing and facilitation, (b) Quality Assurance, (c) Net profit.  He said that the stakeholders response will be covered in the survey by AC Nielson and the students evaluation will go into Quality Assurance.  The customer service should be included in the Survey on the EMBA Program Office.

 

Mr. Aman U. Saiyed, Program Director BBA:

 

1.22     Mr. Aman U. Saiyed said that he has identified two deliverables for the department: (1) Service to Students and Faculty, and the Capacity building of the staff of the undergraduate program office. The first should be assessed through students and faculty survey conducted by AC Nielsen and cover the following:

 

a)      Delay in circulating the next semester’s schedule of classes and faculty

b)      Number of complaints received about students not being listened to while they register for the various courses

c)      Number of complaints received about students not getting registered in the courses of their choice.

d)     Faculty not contacted about their scheduled classes and timings

e)      Complaints from students about not being directed to the right administration office when they come to the program office looking for help

 

1.23     Mr. Aman U. Saiyed said that there is a great need to train staff in their interpersonal skills and English writing and communication courses.

 

1.24     The Dean & Director said that Training of staff is not the KPI; it is an integral part of staff development. He said that the increase in students’ satisfaction level will be the KPI. The survey by AC Nielsen will reveal the satisfaction level, and the current year’s survey will be the benchmark for next year.

 

Mr. Muhammad Anwar, Head Librarian:

 

1.25     Mr. Muhammad Anwar said that he has identified certain objectives and each objective has been linked to the KPI. He said that he has suggested the KPIs, Operational Definition, method and the target for each KPI. He said that he has also identified the functions which are to be surveyed by AC Nielsen and through other methods.

 

1.26     The Dean & Director suggested to put all those activities which are to be surveyed by AC Nielsen together.  He further suggested to refine the KPIs and present the broader picture of the KPIs for management review; other details should be for internal monitoring purposes. He said that the contents of the survey should reflect what you want. He said that innovation and challenges should be separately included in the deliverables.

 

1.27     Mr. Muhammad Anwar said that one of their goals is to introduce cutting edge technological and physical facilities & learning spaces. However, he said that as they are in the transition phase, and the physical facilities are not yet ready, they cannot implement it at this stage.

 

1.28     Mr. Zaffar A. Khan asked if there is a track (count) of the usage of library by faculty and students.  He suggested that one of the targets should be to increase the usage of library. 

 

Mr. Adnan Hameed, Head of H.R.

 

1.29     Mr. Adnan Hameed while presenting the proposed KPIs of HR department said that he had divided the KPIs into three parts and has also indicated the sources of data.  The Dean & Director said that recruitment, training, compensation and benefits administration are the deliverables for the H.R. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested to focus on organizational development which should include organizational culture, communication and interaction and the overall work environment. The Dean & Director said that for their own department the H.R. can have more detailed sub-goals and KPIs but for Management purpose, they have the following deliverables:

 

1)      organizational development,

2)      faculty recruitment, training, development and retention

3)      compensation and benefits administration

4)      development and training of managerial pool

 

1.30     The Dean & Director suggested to develop the KPIs for the above four deliverables.

 

Mr. Imran Batada, Head of ICT:

 

1.31     Mr. Imran Batada presented detailed strategic priorities and aims of ICT and KPIs.  He also presented the objectives and performance measures.

 

1.32     The Dean & Director appreciated the effort in preparing very detailed objectives and KPIs. However, he suggested that the details may be useful for their internal usage.  For the Management HCM, Financials, BIs, help desk services, maintenance, software up-gradation and marketing of data centre would be the deliverables of ICT. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan suggested to prioritise the deliverables and KPIs because some may be done this year, and some in subsequent years. 

 

Mr. Moeid Sultan, Director Finance:

 

1.33     Mr. Moeid Sultan said that they have several sub-functions such as revenue, payables, Budget, Financial Aids, Students Societies, payrolls and the users of all those functions have increased. The Dean & Director said that the deliverables of Finance Department are to bring about system efficiency in revenue collection and management. He said that the EC is not interested in processes. The processes and other details can be useful for internal monitoring but for management purposes it has to be aligned with the objectives of the whole institution.  He said that the strategy of the institution is that we must have a sustainable financial model in which most of our operational expenditure is financed from internally generated revenue. He said that the management will see how the revenue is increasing, expenditure is controlled and savings are done. These should be measurable and quantifiable. He said that for transport and maintenance you have to reduce the cost.  Mr. Moeid Sultan said that they can make efforts for reducing the cost of transport and maintenance but they cannot directly control the expenditure.  The Dean & Director said that curtailing maintenance and transport cost would be the joint target of finance and maintenance / transport department. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan said that the primary responsibility for reducing the maintenance or transport cost would be of the department concerned but the finance department can flag the issue.

 

1.34     The Dean & Director suggested to collapse and prioritise what the Finance Department wants to do and set quantifiable targets.  He said that where students interaction is involved it would be surveyed by AC Nielsen. He further said that the visiting faculty would also be part of the customer satisfaction survey.     

 

DECISION

 

1.35     The Dean & Director thanked Mr. Zaffar A. Khan for his valuable comments.

 

1.36     The Committee decided that in light of the feedback noted above the KPIs of each department have to be recast in the form of deliverables, KPIs and Assessment methods.  The KPIs should be quantifiable / measurable and the benchmark should of the last year.

 

Item No. 2      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)            HEC Ranking

 

2.1       Dr. Amber Gul Rashid said that they send a standard KPI form to HEC every quarter on the basis of which they give a score to every QEC.  In the last round, IBA received ‘Y’ but now she hoped to get ‘W’ or ‘X’ as they have completed 10 assessments. She said that there are three main points; QEC Secretariat Establishment, Implementation Status of SA Mechanism, Empowering the QEC.  If people working in the QEC are on additional charge, they give lesser marks.  She said that they were lagging in self-assessment report but now they have managed it and hoped to get better scorecard. 

 

2.2      Dr. Amber stated that the HEC was asking for surveys of our graduating students, alumni, employers, etc. She said that HEC requires program wise survey of employers i.e. BBA, BS etc. which IBA currently did using dropdown menu on the same form and other such things.  She said that generally IBA faculty cannot be HEC approved supervisors unless their degrees are attested by HEC.  She asked if the PhD faculty members have to get their degrees attested by HEC themselves or IBA can help to get their degrees attested by HEC. The Dean & Director said that the faculty members themselves have to get their degrees attested by HEC. Dr. Amber Gul proposed that instead of individual faculty, a person (or she herself) with authorization letter from faculty members could go and get the degrees verified by HEC. The Committee appreciated the solution presented by Dr. Amber Gul Rashid.  

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
