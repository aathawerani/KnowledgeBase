Item No. 1      Demonstration on on-line Testing System.

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Batada demonstrated the features and functioning of on-line Testing System that is meant primarily, in the first stage, for the candidates from outside Pakistan.  He said that the demonstration of the system was earlier given to the Admission Committee and the Admission Committee is satisfied with the system.  To a question he clarified that the complete right of access to the system will rest with the Testing Department not with the ICT. The Testing Department can manage, review, and restrict the system.  

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that in Testing Department Dr. Nasir Touheed will be the only custodian and user of the system. The Dean & Director asked Dr. Nasir Touheed to keep control on the system, and not pass on the access privileges to any other person. The ICT Department will only provide technical help when needed. The Dean & Director said that in the first phase on-line test will be conducted for candidates staying outside Pakistan and then in the second phase it will be for the candidates stationed outside Karachi.  He said that at Karachi it cannot be done immediately because of large pool of applicants. We have to make preparatory arrangements before introducing it at Karachi. He said that the sequence of questions and the possible answers should be shuffled for each candidate and for candidates appearing in second batch and the whole set of questions should be different from the previous tests.

 

1.3       Dr. Hisham asked if, due to any disturbance in the network, the timer in the system would be affected.  Mr. Imran Batada agreed that the timer would be affected in case there is some disturbance in the network.  He said that there has to be robust internet connection.  He said that if, for example, five minutes of a candidate is lost due to the fault at internet connection, then the Coordinator Testing Services who has access to the system can enable the system and allow another 5 minutes that was lost.  The Coordinator Testing Services may switch to manual system also if there is any major problem with the internet connection.

 

1.4       Dr. Nasir Afghan wanted to know the measures taken for the security of the system and to safeguard the system from hacking.  Mr. Imran Batada said that they have tried to make the system as much secure as could be possible. He suggested that the system should be operated from the laptop of the Coordinator Testing Services only so that the possibility of hacking is further reduced.

 

1.5       To a query if the questions could be cut and pasted, Mr. Batada confirmed that it can be done but if the operating system is disabled it would not be possible. He said that permission from the organization / institution that would be providing the facility of conducting on-line test will have to be obtained for disabling the system or making changes to suit our requirements.

 

1.6       It was clarified that only the total number of right and wrong answers would be communicated to the candidates after they have taken the test.  The final result would be communicated after the cut-off points are finalized. At this stage the admission test fee would be the same for candidates appearing from outside Pakistan as would be charged from candidates within Pakistan.

1.7       The Committee noted and approved the on-line testing service as presented by Mr. Imran Batada.

 

1.8       The Committee decided that the right of access to the on-line Testing Service will be given to the Coordinator Testing Services only. The host institution where the test will be conducted would be requested to disable the Operating System temporarily for the duration of the tests.

Item No. 4      To consider the reports of the Architects and other experts on the collapse of seminar hall ceiling of Aman CED Building, and to decide future course of action.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the collapsed portion of false ceiling was immediately inspected by the Projects Department, Architects and the contractors M/s. Total Construction early morning of 29th May 2013 as soon as it was brought to the notice of Projects Department. Subsequently along with NBCL, NESPAK, INTEG, Total Construction & Contractors, and the Projects Departments themselves were also requested to inspect and submit their independent reports on the causes of failure of false ceiling. In this regard their reports were received, summaries of each report have been reproduced below. The Director Projects reported that he has invited Mr. Zayd Bilgrami of NBCL to present his report on the collapse of Gypsum Ceiling of seminar hall of Aman CED Building. 

 

4.2       Mr. Zayd Bilgrami stated that on 29th May 2013, NBCL was informed by IBA that the Gypsum Ceiling had collapsed the previous night in the 2nd Floor Seminar Room of the Aman CED Building. NBCL was requested to visit the site, make technical investigations and present its findings. He said that Aman-CED site was visited the same day by Ar. Shabih Haider (Project Architect, NBCL) who took photographs of the collapsed ceiling. The following day, i.e. 30th May 2013, the site was visited by him (Ar. Zayd Bilgrami, Principal, NBCL) and Ar. Shabih Haider along with the Contractors, Total Construction, the Project Managers, Halcrow & Partners and the representatives of IBA Projects Department.

 

4.3       Mr. Zayd reported that it was witnessed that the entire left side panel of the 1/2”gypsum ceiling constituting about 1/3rd the area of the Seminar Hall had collapsed, bringing down with it A/C diffusers, plenums, light fixtures, etc., as also the adjoining metal Dampa ceiling. It was also seen that the Aluminium hangers, framing and screws had not failed and were still intact. The following day they were informed that on the previous night, that is, the 30th May, another portion of the middle section of the ceiling had collapsed. This was part of the intact ceiling which was temporarily propped up the previous day by vertical posts and wooden planks. NBCL team made further site visits and arranged two independent specialist third party visits, one of a very senior architect, Ar. Yadullah Haider, Principal Architect at Meindhardt and the other, an Independent ceiling contractor, Thermec.

 

4.4       Mr. Zayd said that their initial site visits and the visits of the experts were made to investigate the following:

 

· Quality of material used versus what was specified

· Whether the fixing details were followed as per standard practice and as indicated in Architectural Drawings issued to the Contractor

· Possible cause of ceiling failure

 

Quality of Material Used:

 

4.5       It was confirmed from examination that the Gypsum sheets are Elephant brand make from Thailand as specified by NBCL and considered to be one of the best available in the market. The framing material, i.e., galvanized metal threaded suspension rods with heavy duty level adjusters, aluminium frame channels, ‘Hilti’ bolts and screws were all found to be as specified by us.

 

Fixing Details:

 

4.6       The framing and suspension rods were spaced as per standards and in accordance with the architectural drawings provided to the Contractor. All the framing material was perfectly installed as per standards and in accordance with the architectural drawings provided to the Contractor.

 

4.7       The only divergence from their drawings seemed to be at the convergence of the gypsum ceiling and the 4 feet wide strip of Dampa ceiling. Instead of hanging the Dampa ceiling independently, this was clipped on to the gypsum ceiling. However, the weight of the Dampa ceiling panels is negligible and cannot be the cause of collapse of an entire section of the gypsum ceiling.

 

Possible Cause of Ceiling Failure:

 

4.8       The cause of failure was extremely puzzling for them, or the experts who visited the site as they have never witnessed a gypsum ceiling collapse in this fashion where the framing system, the bolts, the rods, channels and screws were all intact.

 

4.9       Since no one else had ever witnessed such an event and the causes were very difficult to determine, online research was conducted to see case studies around the world where this might have happened. Two such cases were found.

 

4.10     Upon reviewing all of the feedback and studying the site conditions, NBCL were unable to come up with any logical reason for the ceiling collapse. However, they said that during discussions the following possibilities of ceiling failure were discussed and discarded:

 

4.11     Incorrect fixing of screws: The reason the entire ceiling collapsed at once is that each sheet was tied to the next with drywall tape and joint compound. When the first section fell, it dragged its neighbors down as well.

 

4.12     Another possibility of failure was related to the non-conforming of details at the junction of the gypsum ceiling and Dampa ceiling. The Dampa ceiling has been clipped to the Gypsum ceiling area which was cantilevered.

 

Summary:

 

4.13     Mr. Zayd said that the above were different possible scenarios for the ceiling collapse as per their findings. However, the most probable cause remains that it was forcefully tampered with on two separate occasions, i.e., on the night of 28th May and the night of 30th May, causing portions of the gypsum ceiling and Dampa ceiling in the Seminar Room on the 2nd floor to collapse.

 

Corrective Measures:

 

4.14     Mr. Zayd apprehended that the same detail of unsupported Dampa ceiling exists in all seminar rooms. He said that although this does not seem to be the cause of the collapse, they recommended that the ceiling boards adjoining the joints should be carefully removed to provide independent supports at the edges of the Dampa ceiling and also the edges of the gypsum ceiling and this work should be carried out by certified gypsum contractors (Namely Thermec as mentioned in BOQ).

 

4.15     Mr. Zayd said that if IBA wants to be extra cautious, then they can also replace the entire ceiling with simple Dampa and make the light coves in MDF, framing. NBCL will issue the drawing and along with Contractor and the Project Managers, over see its installation in detail. Mr. Zayd assured that their office is open to lend any form of support required by IBA in rectifying the damage.

 

REPORT FROM NESPAK

 

4.16     Mr. Abdul Mateen, General Manager of M/s. NESPAK visited the site of broken false ceiling along with his team of expert engineers on 31st May 2013. Their observations and comments were as under:

 

Observations

 

a) The hanging system for holding such a heavy load of false ceiling, lights, sound & other electric items does not seems to be properly designed and approved by the Structural Engineer/ recommended agency.

 

b) The holding wires are, neither located at proper/equal spaces nor vertically fixed from the roof concrete at many places.

 

c) At all ends, false ceiling seems to be simply supported directly on the wooden decorative support which is about 5 ft. away from the last hanger, having no holding support in between 5 ft. unsupported span.

 

d) There are evidences which suggest that there have been deliberate tempering of the false ceiling especially on the night of 30thth June. This should be explored further.

 

REPORT FROM M/S. INTEG

 

4.17     Mr. Zafarul Islam of M/s. Integ visited the site on 29th May 203. After conducting his physical inspection of the site, he was of the opinion that:

 

· All the screws and hangers are fixed at their places only Gypsum Sheets have fallen down.

 

· It is likely that someone has tried to carry out maintenance work or etc. work over the Gypsum ceiling, which causes abnormal direct force on Gypsum Ceiling instead of GI framed structure.

 

REPORT FROM SR. MANAGER (PROJECTS) IBA

 

4.18     Engr. Muhammad Rehan Sheikh is of the view that ceiling failure could have occurred because:

 

· The fasteners were correctly spaced, but over-driven

 

· The fasteners were incorrectly spaced

 

· Adhesive was used between the ceiling joists and drywall, but failed to bond correctly and because the fasteners were spaced farther apart in that installation approach, there were not enough of them to hold up the ceiling when the glue failed

 

· Any combination of the above might have come into play in the ceiling failure

 

· The reason for the entire ceiling collapsed at once might be that each sheet was tied to the next with drywall tape and join compound. When the first section fell, it dragged its neighbors down as well.

 

REPORT FROM TOTAL CONSTRUCTION & CONTRACTORS

 

· Perchance somebody tried to climb or intend to maintain any item above ceiling. The ramification which resulted was: a force sufficient enough to produce a “punching shear”, causing the ceiling to give way. 

 

· The temperature of the area might have been a contributing factor, as it may have dampen the atmosphere due to which sheets were to come out from screws. As witnessed the entire suspension was firmed in its position, with none of the hangers abandoning their posts. This implies that the structure could more than provide for the sheets. The distribution of the suspension was ascertained by the construction manager during the course of execution.

 

· The material used for suspension are Hilti bolts, galvanized threaded rods, heavy duty level adjusters clips and heavy duty channel distribution. Gypsum sheets are Elephant brand made in Thailand but normal not moisture resistant as specified.

 

· It has also been observed that all the HVAC and other devices were resting on gypsum sheets without any hanging supports, causing extra load on sheets, as written earlier that the suspension is on its position and sheets have come down. 

 

· Another deduction, maintenance staff that is using access through demountable ceiling, does not seem fully trained, causing panels’ and suspension damage.

 

· Negative pressure has also been observed in that particular area that might loosen the screws during this time span.

 

PROJECTS DEPARTMENT FINDINGS

 

· It is an unusual incident very rarely happened in practice.

 

· Weight of the Dampa ceiling should not have been taken on the plenum of gypsum ceiling and it should have its own supporting hangers.

 

· It is unlikely that false ceiling could have fallen until forced down. The pattern of collapse of a portion on the night of 28th -29th 2013 and the pattern of collapse of another portion between the night of 29th& 30th May clearly shows that the ceiling had been deliberately torn down manually in between the struts. Question arises as to why rest of portions did not fall when the room remained under the charge of Administration Staff for another 3 to 4 days.

 

CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

 

1. A fully impartial committee should investigate as to how the ceiling was deliberately brought down especially between the night of 29th& 30th May. CCTV footage be checked.

 

2. The entire ceiling of the S-5 be replaced by Dampa Ceiling.

 

3. Regarding two other Seminar Halls corrective measures be taken to strengthen the Dampa ceilings where its weight should altogether be taken to hangers to make it complete independent of the gypsum.

 

4. Weights of all lights and ducts be taken out of the gypsum ceiling.

 

5. Close examination of over driven screws be done. If signs of failure are seen then the gypsum ceilings be changed into Dampa ceiling.

 

6. Responsibility of the event be fixed after the inquiry as suggested in #1 is completed.

 

7. Architects to submit the estimates of the loss and the amount for re- construction and rectification works.

 

4.19     The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to decide as to how the extra expenses be met.
