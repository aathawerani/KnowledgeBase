Item No. 3      To consider and approve proposal for Cisco & Hardware Lab Equipment.

 

Hardware lab:

 

3.1       The Director ICT said that the Faculty of Computer Science is working to meet the technology demands of new advancement in technology. Therefore FCS is moving towards industry based education and building an Industry based Hardware Lab which will incorporate technology needs of industry across all over the globe.  He informed that the budgetary amount of Rs.6.3 million was approved in principle by the Dean & Director last year, but the project could not be presented for the approval in the EC meanwhile the budgetary cost increased during one year period. He requested to approve the purchase of equipment to build hardware lab and also approve the revised budgetary amount of Rs.7.5 million for the said purpose.

 

3.2       Mr. Raza Abdi, faculty member, said that they want two different labs; hardware and Cisco labs.  He said that they need networking lab because they are offering BS, MS and Ph.D but for them they do not have any lab. Similarly they do not have any lab to conduct research in engineering area.  Right now they have only general purpose lab.

 

3.3       The Registrar wanted to know as to how quality of instruction would improve through the requested lab.

 

3.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the major shortcoming of BS program is that they do not have any hardware lab.  He said that because of non-availability of hardware lab, the program received a negative comment (despite getting the highest rating) in the last NCEAC accreditation, and with the next round of accreditation this year may not get the highest rating if the lab is not approved.

 

3.5       Mr. Raza Abdi said that there are 13 items in the BOQ and open tender would be issued for the hardware lab.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee approved the purchase of equipment to build hardware lab and also approve the revised budgetary amount of Rs.7.5 million for the said purpose.

 

ACTION

 

3.7       The Director ICT would arrange the procurement of the equipment following the required procedure.

 

CISCO Lab:

 

3.8       The Director ICT said that there were some misunderstandings between FCS and ICT Departments regarding the equipment requirement by the FCS, which delayed the procurement of the required equipment. He said that the EC had asked the two departments to get together and come up with the final figures. He said that the budgetary prices were calculated based on price structure of early 2010 which has changed due to PKR devaluation and Cisco price revision (owing to phasing out the proposed equipment and offering newer models with enhanced features with a price increase).  He requested the EC to approve the following:

 

o   Purchasing of CISCO equipment for FCS Networking Lab.

o   A Budget for 2011/12 of Rs.3.7 million for the above project.

o   Attached BOQ for Tendering.

 

3.9       Mr. M. Faisal Zia said that they had planned that they would make Cisco Academic Lab through Cisco. He said that Cisco’s requirement is that outside students should also be allowed but they (IBA) decided that they would not go for that and did not accept the Academic Alliance with Cisco. So now IBA would get 60% discount instead of 75% which was conditional to Academic Alliance with Cisco and allowing outside students also.

 

3.10     The Director ICT said that they would negotiate with Cisco and try to get 75% discount instead of 60%.

 

3.11     Dr. Sayeed Ghani He said that the proposal will go to the CPC and then will be resubmitted to the EC.  At this stage principle approval is needed from the EC.

 

3.12     The Director Finance wanted to know if it was possible to talk to other vendors so that they may get further discount.

 

3.13     Mr. Faisal Zia said that there are many vendors but the principal is one. Cisco does not sell directly, they sell through vendors.  He said that if approved now, the process would take another six months to finalize.

 

DECISION

 

3.14     The Committee granted principle approval for:

 

o   purchasing of CISCO equipment for FCS Networking Lab.

o   a Budget for 2011/12 of Rs.3.7 million for the above project.

o   BOQ for Tendering.

 

ACTION

 

3.15     The Director ICT would present the proposal to the CPC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 4      Report on Aman Tower.

4.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that while discussing the proposal to approve award of the Interior Design of Aman Tower to M/S. EA Consulting, the EC in its 149th meeting had requested him to prepare a comparative statement of the scope of work as given in the original contract of the Aman Tower and what they were asking for so that duplication of payment could be avoided.   He said that he studied the scope of services required from the architectural and engineering consultant EA Consulting (Pvt.) Ltd. which involves only the architectural and engineering design of the building as a whole, providing top supervision during the construction phase and ensuring quality as per given specifications. It does not include any Interior Design (ID) work and, as such, there will be no duplication of payments if designing of the required ID work is added as a separate scope of services required from the Consultant.  He was of the view that assigning both engineering and ID work to the same consultant will facilitate speedy progress and completion of the building. He said that he also obtained and studied the Scope of ID services required from the consultant.  He said that on the Director Projects’ request, it was prepared by the Aman Tower’s Project Manager, Halcrow. He said that the scope of ID services is quite comprehensive and includes all aspects of ID work likely to be needed in the building.  He said that there was no duplication with the scope of services required from the architectural and engineering consultant EA Consulting.  He presented details of scopes of work of both the parties. He said that since there was no duplication of work there would be no duplication of any payment as well. However, he suggested that in order to guarantee it further, an additional clause may be inserted in the Consultant’s Contract Agreement stipulating that under no interpretation of the agreement the consultant will be entitled to a payment under both of the Scopes of Services.

 

4.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed recommended that the work of interior decoration of Aman Tower be awarded to EA Consulting (Pvt.) Ltd., for which an approval in principle has already been given by the Executive Committee. 

 

4.3       Mr. Rehanul Ambia said that the Consultant would work at 2.5% fee instead of 4%. Thus they would have a saving of around Rs.3 million.   

 

4.4       The Director Finance wanted to know if the floors of Aman Tower have been reduced from 14 to 12.

 

4.5       The Director Projects clarified that it would be Ground plus 12, the total area will remain the same but the cost will be reduced and BoQ will be changed.

 

4.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that at the top floor provision for helipad should be made.

 

DECISION

 

4.7       In view of the report from Mr. Shameem Ahmed that there would be no duplication of payment, the Committee approved the recommendation of awarding the work of interior designing of Aman Tower to EA Consulting (Pvt.) Ltd.
