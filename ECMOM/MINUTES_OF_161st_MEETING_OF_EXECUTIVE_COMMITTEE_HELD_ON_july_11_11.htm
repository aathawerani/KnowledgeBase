Item No. 1      Policy on purchase of IBA Laptop by the faculty and staff.

 

1.1       The Director ICT reported that one of the faculty members is leaving IBA after serving IBA for five years. He said that the faculty member has made a request that he may be allowed to purchase the Toshiba L-300 laptop that he has been using for about two years. However, the Director ICT said that there is no such policy. He said that when they replaced the old PCs they did not let IBA staff have them; instead IBA donated the better PCs to schools and then distributed the rest in three categories to IBA staff through balloting. He said that to let the outgoing faculty member purchase the PC / Laptop would require the policy change which would then be applicable to all faculty and staff. He also said that Dr. Sayeed Ghani had suggested that this issue should be presented before the EC for further action. He said that the laptop replacement period is after three years.

 

1.2       The Director Finance clarified that three years time period is not for the replacement but for it is for depreciation of PCs and laptops. 

 

1.3       The Dean & Director agreed with the Director Finance and said that if the PC / Laptop is working then why should it be replaced.  For IBA vehicle also he said that, if their repair and maintenance cost becomes very high then they are replaced; they are not replaced automatically. He said that the policy should be to maximize the utilization of scarce resources.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee decided that the following will be the policy with regard to allowing the purchase of IBA’s PC/Laptop already given to faculty and staff:

 

a)      The concerned faculty / staff must have satisfactorily served IBA for at least 5 years before he/she leaves IBA.

b)      He/she will have to pay the residual value of the instrument.

 

1.5       The request of the concerned faculty (Mr. Toshio Fujita) will be considered in light of the above policy.

 

ACTION

 

1.6       The Director Finance would convey the residual value of the Laptop to Mr. Fujita for payment.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Approval for the selection of vendor for the Adult Population Survey.

 

4.1       Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Assistant Professor and Associate Director, CED stated that the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) Adult Population Survey (APS) is a study of individual attitudes and activities related to entrepreneurship and conducted cross-nationally on an annual basis. The results are unique in their focus on the individual-level and their use of a consistent set of standards for data collection that allows cross-national comparisons. The focus on individual-level behaviour and attitudes mandates that each national team collects data from a representative sample of the adult population of their country using standard instruments and procedures. APS is the primary research tool of GEM; that will be used by IBA CED to build up their resources and targets (especially seminar, conferences and curriculum). IBA in cooperation with GEM and USAID conducted the 2010 APS entrepreneurship survey to assess the role of entrepreneurship in Pakistan’s national economic growth. The survey has put Pakistan on the map of nations conducting the Global Entrepreneurship Survey. To have a longitudinal view of the entrepreneurial climate, the second cycle of the GEM Pakistan (2011 APS Survey) is envisaged. USAID has agreed to take care of the GEM participation fee of £7,500 (sterling) to be submitted directly to the Consortium. IBA will finance the Adult Population Survey.

            Dr. Qureshi reported that a tender was floated in the national newspapers of Pakistan to hire the services of a vendor to carry out the Adult Population Survey throughout the country. The participants were asked to submit Expression of Interest along with Technical and Financial proposals. GEM Standard RFP was circulated among the Vendors as per request. The following firms participated and submitted their proposals:

 

1.      Oasis International                 

2.      SEBCON

3.      National Rural Support Program

 

An Evaluation Committee comprising the following assessed the vendor proposals:

 

1.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui, Director CED, IBA

2.      Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Associate Director, CED

3.      Dr. Shahid Mir, Chairperson Management, IBA

4.      Mr. Sarfraz A Mian, Principal Investigator , GEM

5.      Mirza Izhar Hussain, CEE, IBA

6.      Mr. Jami Moiz, Assistant Professor, IBA

 

4.2       Dr. Qureshi reported that M/s. Oasis International, and M/s. SEBCON qualified technically and thus participated in the Financial Evaluation. M/s. National Rural Support Programme were not considered as they were technically disqualified. Following are the costs quoted by the Technically Qualified Bidders:

 

Oasis International                  Rs.1,900,000

SEBCON                                Rs.4,933,784

Based on the above quotations OASIS International was the Lowest Evaluated Bidder. Dr. Qureshi requested the EC to approve the award of the project to OASIS International at a Cost of Rs.1,900,000/-.

 

4.3       The Director Finance suggested that since the job has been tendered, as per TAP it will have to go to the TEC and CPC.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee granted conceptual approval for the Adult Population Survey (APS) and requested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to consult the Audit, and Finance and if they advise that approval of the TEC and CPC is needed, he should complete the procedure.
