The following items were discussed: 

Item No. 1     To discuss and approve the draft Procurement Policy and Procedure.

 

1.1              Mr. Moiz Hassanali, Head of Internal Audit stated that he has presented the version 2.1 of the draft policy after discussing the draft with the members Executive Committee. He said that the purpose of the Procurement Policy and Procedure is to ensure that financial integrity is assured by prescribing and following a range of appropriate methods for purchasing and procurement, including, where applicable, a competitive tendering and contracting process.  He said that the two main principles that apply are; the requirement for impartiality and fairness across all stages of the tendering and purchasing process, and the necessity for obtaining the best possible value for money.  He further stated that timely and transparent contract implementation is a key to the success of procurement and will only be achieved by making available on IBA website and at PPRA and SPPRA all tenders notices, evaluation reports, award decisions, etc. for general public information.

 

1.2              Mr. Moiz Hassanali gave a detailed presentation covering purpose, policy, public procurement in Pakistan, scope, and procedures supplemented by attachments. During the presentation and discussions, the following observations were made:

 

a)            The Director ICT suggested that as they are working with Technology Partners, they should also be mentioned with vendors.

b)            The Standard Bidding Documents should once be vetted by the legal advisor and approved by the Central Purchase Committee (CPC).  Later only the changes (additions / deletions) in the Standard Bidding Documents should be presented to the CPC for approval.

c)            On emergency and subject to consensus of CPC Chairman the approval from CPC through email / circular resolution can be obtained.

d)           The formation of the Central Purchase Committee is the prerogative of the Director IBA.

e)            If Standard Bidding Document requires the threshold for technical score of 60% for selection of bidder instead of 75% proposed in the document that must be approved by the CPC on case by case basis according to its  justification.

f)             The evaluation report of a project will be sent to the CPC and then according to TAP, A&FC or the BoG.  There is no need to come back again to CPC

g)            The Central Purchase Committee is not required to seek justification for a project approved by the Competent Authority. However, the CPC should be provided complete documentation and approval from the competent authority.

 

DECISION

 

1.3              The Committee appreciated Mr. Moiz Hassanali and his team for preparing a comprehensive Procurement Policy and Procedure.

 

1.4              The Committee approved the Policy along with the suggestions made in 1.2 (a to g) above and recommended to A&FC for approval.

 

1.5              The Committee decided that once the Policy is approved by the A&FC/Board, it would be placed on the IBA website as well on the Portal.

 

ACTION

 

1.6              Mr. Moiz Hassanali would present the Procurement Policy and Procedure to the A&FC for approval.

 

   

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects and Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Project Director HEC Projects for providing basic necessities to IBA consultants during site supervision.

 

2.1              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz stated that in all kinds of contracts with contractors amenities such as vehicles for engineers of owners, site offices furnished with furniture and phone, computers and printers are made part of the contract as the responsibility of contractors. These are also prescribed by Pakistan Engineering Council. The purpose is to save the owner from the hassle of arranging vehicles, fuel, and site offices including furniture etc. during the period of construction and then disposal of the same. He said that the engineers are using their private vehicles and their own fuel for which a small amount form part of their salary package. The fuel is just enough for them to commute between their home and IBA offices. No allowance is being paid to them to commute between Main Campus and City Campus. He further said that the travel will increase as the work on new Boys Hostel, New Academic Block, Centre for Entrepreneurship, Multi-storied Building, and water supply project will commence. He said that provision of vehicles to engineers will enhance their efficiency as they can travel to site at any time. He requested the Executive Committee to recommend inclusion of only necessary site requirements in the contract conditions for approval by A&FC.

 

2.2              The Director wanted to know as to what is mentioned in the contract to which the Director Projects replied that it was not mentioned in the contract but said that generally such amenities are provided. He further stated that this matter is brought here for approval of Executive Committee so that it could be included in the future contracts.

 

2.3              The Director said that IBA is paying the fee to cover all these expenses. He said that if we provide other facilities then we have to cut down the fees.

 

DECISION

 

2.4              The Committee did not approve the recommendation made by the Director Projects as the requested facilities were not provided earlier in the contract. 

 

 

Item No. 3      Appointment of Electrical Consultant on Emergent Basis.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the first phase of Academic Block was completed on time i.e. August 30, 2009. However, he stated that between 28th & 29th August, Karachi University Electrical side experienced constant breakdown when chillers were being tested using the power from Karachi University. He said that Karachi University was approached who showed their inability to provide a higher rated breaker for the reasons that power mains from their grid will not support the required load. Secondly they refused to provide any extra power with the reason that their overhead system did not have the capacity.

 

3.2       In order to meet the requirements to run the chillers, the Director Projects reported that one 250 KVA Generator set was brought in by the Contractor which was operated from 31st August 2009. For this an extra item for rental, operational and maintenance cost, was issued to the contractor, which is still being run.

 

3.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that efforts for enhancement of loads were continued during past six weeks by the Director  HEC Projects and with the support of Director IBA, the KESC has assured on 19th September 2009 that extra load would be sanctioned. He said that in the meantime, numerous meetings were held by IBA and the Electrical Consultants M/s. S. K. Engineers who are already working with IBA on almost all the projects under renovation, with Karachi University. The Karachi University intimated that they are going to change their electrical network from existing overhead lines to the underground cable network. They also warned that they may dis-continue power supply to IBA at any time to work on change over of the system.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that it was thus necessary for them to design and lay down their underground cable system which according to Karachi University directives would be as follows:

 

               a)      Cable from Commerce Building Sub-Station to the Feeding Pillar to be designed and erected in front of gate 4 of IBA.

 

      b)      Designing & installation of LT Panels within IBA boundary walls.

 

      c)     Connection of existing two cables from Academic Block to the LT panels

 

      d)      Connection between Feeding pillar and LT Panels.

 

      e)     Laying  cables from Feeding Pillar to Boys Hostel to cater the requirements of existing  Boys Hostel and New Boys Hostel.

 

3.5       The Director Projects informed the Committee that at present Rs.150,000/- is being spent per week to provide energy to run the chillers at the Academic Block. It is highly necessary that the above work should be completed on emergent basis so that the power to the Academic Block as well as to the Boys Hostel is ensured. He further said that if we do not act now there will be no electricity available from Karachi University

 

3.6       The Director Projects reported that as there was no time to invite various consultants to prepare the proposal for the above work, they contacted M/s. S. K. Engineers who is already working in the entire network of the building and the campus. M/s. S.K. Engineers have submitted their proposal to carry out the above works which included designing and detailed supervision and have quoted a lump sum fee of Rs.600,000/-  for the above job. He said that after detailed negotiations by him and the Project Director  HEC Projects, they have reduced their fees to Rs.300,000/-. The total cost of the work shall be about Rs.20 million and the percentage comes to 1.5%, which is very reasonable. He presented a copy of the proposal received from M/s. S.K. Consultant.

 

3.7       The Director Projects reported that the following steps were taken well before the completion of Phase-I of the Academic Block at Main Campus:

 

a)   Laying of 240 mm Cable from existing Sub-Station to Academic Block Main Campus.

 

b)   Laying of 75mm Cable to Multi-Purpose Building from the existing Sub Station

 

      c)   Fabrication and installation of new LT Panels to cater for the connection from Sub-Station to the Multi-purpose Building and Academic Block, Provision provided for Administration building

 

 

      d)  Application to KESC to enhance the load from existing 110 kW to 400 kW

 

e)   Replacement of two old cables of 75 mm and 95 mm connected from Academic Block Main Campus to overhead line of Karachi University .

 

f)         Procurement and installation of 250 KVA standby generator set (bids have been received  Consultants will submit their evaluation report this week).

 

3.8       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the following:

 

a)   Appointment of M/s. S. K. Engineers as the Consultants for the above work at a total fee of Rs.300,000/- .

 

b)      To approve in principle the expenditure of laying of underground cable system at a cost of Rs.20 million approximately. This amount is being provided in the budget under appropriation exercise.

 

3.9              The Director wanted to know if the Consultants have endorsed the proposed underground cable system. 

 

3.10          Mr. Shameem Ahmed clarified that after obtaining technical information from M/s. S.K. Engineers the proposal has been prepared. He further said that competitive bids have not been taken. However, he said that the execution of the work would be done on the basis of competitive bids.

 

 

DECISION

 

3.11          The Committee approved the proposal subject to the endorsement by the Central Purchase Committee that the work is of urgent nature.

 

ACTION

 

3.12          The Director Projects would take the matter to the Central Purchase Committee for endorsement.
