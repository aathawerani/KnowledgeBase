MINUTES OF THE 56th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON JULY 13, 2009  

The 56th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 13, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Dr. Wasim A Khan                            Associate Dean (Acting)

  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                              Controller of Examinations

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance

  7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                Director Projects

  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                         Director I.C.T

Dr. Nasir Touheed and Ms. Nida Aslam Khan attended by special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider request from Ms. Faiza Izhar for reimbursement of admission fee and a note from the Director Finance on this issue.

 

1.1              Ms. Faiza Izhar in her email stated that in response to her request for reimbursement of fee taken in advance in lieu of Summer 2009, Fall 2009 semesters and the entire admission fee, she received the cheque of Rs.61,550/- only which is the sum paid in advance for the above mentioned semesters and not the admission fee and caution money. She stated that according to the policy of IBA, in such admission termination or null or void admissions IBA is liable to return entire admission fee to the candidate. She stated that the entire fee incurred totalled up to Rs.105,509/-, which in her opinion is not valid to be paid for any certificate course. She requested for payment of all her dues as mentioned above.

 

1.2       The Director Finance giving detail about the issue stated that Ms. Faiza Izhar was given admission on the basis of her 14 years of education + 3 years Advance Diploma from APTECH. Her credentials from APATECH were sent to HEC for equivalency but HEC has disqualified this institute as degree awarding institution.  And hence no credit was allowed to her for Diploma from APTECH and virtually she has not been able to meet 16 years of education as required by IBA for admission in MBA. 

     

1.3       The Director Finance reported that IBA has already refunded Rs.66,550/- i.e. Fee for Fall 2009 and Summer 2009 plus Caution money of Rs.5,000/-.  He said that now she is claiming an amount of Rs.100,509/- He stated that as per IBA practice if a student attends classes then he/she is not entitled to get the refund for those semesters. He said that not only she has attended the full semesters (Fall 2008 and Spring 2009) but she has been issued provisional transcript for passing relevant subjects after studying those two semesters.  

 

DECISION

 

1.4              The Committee decided that Ms. Faiza Izhar be given two options; either to surrender the certificates she has already been issued or she should surrender the certificates in order to get the fee refunded. In case she wants to retain the certificates, the fee charged for certificate courses would be adjusted from the fees she has already paid.

 

ACTION

 

1.5              Dr. Wasim A. Khan will talk to Ms. Faiza Izhar and inform the Director Finance accordingly.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider recommendations from Dr. Muhammad Nishat on the absences accumulated by the BBA students Mr. Haseeb Ahmed and Ms. Farzana Siddique Narejo.

 

2.1       Dr. Muhammad Nishat reported that two students of BBA VIII/I, of Main Campus namely; Haseeb Ahmed and Farzana Sidiqque Narejo registered in ‘Entrepreneurship’ course during Spring Semester 2009, with Mr. Sultan Raza, Faculty member, Department of Management. He said that apart from other examinations (term tests, Final exam and quizzes) students were also asked to write a business plan of 10 marks in a group of 4 students. During assessment of term report the faculty member identified that many business plans were plagiarized (detected through computer package VIPER and Plagiarismdetect). After the matter was reported to Dean and Director, a three member committee (Dr. Wasim A. Khan, Dr. Qazi Massod, and Dr. Hemen Das Lohano) was formulated to assess the whole plagiarizing issue. The committee sent their report to Mr. Sultan Raza on June 17, 2009 which included the following:

 

1)         The reliability of anti-plagiarism software Viper and Plagiarismdetect is doubtful

2)         As agreed by the committee and yourself the student groups have filed the revised reports

3)         The students submitted the report that carries only 10% marks

4)         Message to the students has been clearly conveyed and properly understood

 

It is therefore recommended that students are marked for remaining 10 Marks”

 

2.2       Dr. Nishat said that due to whatever reason may be, the results of the above two students (along with other students involved in plagiarizing activity) were declared on 7th July 2009 and they got ‘F”.  He informed the Committee that it was their last semester for graduation and they wanted to register for summer program to complete the degree.

 

2.3       Dr. Nishat informed the Committee that after discussion with Dean and Director, and Associate Dean CCS, he allowed the two students to register for the ‘Entrepreneurial Management’, a senior level course offered in Summer as it was lapse on part of our faculty to announce the result in time and there was no fault of the students.  However, he said that by the time their results were announced and they were allowed to register in Entrepreneurial Management, four sessions were already held which they could not attend.  As the absences were accumulated for no fault of theirs, he requested the Executive Committee to condone their four absences.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee condoned the four absences accumulated by Mr. Haseeb Ahmed and Farzana Sidiqque Narejo as it was because of the delay in announcement of results for a variety of reasons for which students cannot be penalized.

 

2.5              The Office of the Associate Dean would convey the decision to the Program Office for necessary action.

 

 

Item No. 3      Report on admissions by Dr. Nasir Touheed.

 

3.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed presented report on admissions for undergraduate programs with basic statistics.  He informed the Committee that 2340 forms were issued for BBA/BS programs out of which 2307 appeared in test in five cities including Karachi.  445 were called for interview and 351 were selected.  He presented the cut off point and said that the English essay portion of only those candidates who achieved the cut off points was evaluated. Giving details of final merit list, he said that the 445 students who were called for interviews went through structured interviews and the final merit was determined by taking 80% of the test and 20% of the interviews marks. He informed the Committee that two students were selected on the basis of SAT score. He also presented the criteria for the choice of Campus and the BBA- Breakup of 351 successful candidates. He said that top 35 students from the merit list were to be awarded merit scholarship.

 

3.2              In regard to BS admissions Dr. Nasir Touheed informed the Committee that in the first stage 32 candidates were selected on the basis of their 1st choice who met the cut off for BS admissions. He said that out of 83 candidates who could not qualify for BBA program, 52 candidates were selected for second round of interviews on the basis of their 2nd choice. He reported that the marks of Motivation to pursue BS degree were doubled (from 10 to 20) in the interview process. He informed the Committee that 14 candidates are in the waiting lists as they satisfy the revised cut off.  He said that the Admission Committee decided to lower the total cut off point so that the required number of students are available. He clarified that although the total cut off was lowered, individual cut off points remained unchanged. He also suggested that Merit Scholarship to top six students be given. He informed the Committee that because of BS, they cannot prepare waiting list for BBA He said that the 80% and 20% weightage formula used for BBA was not used for the BS program.

 

3.3              Giving details of EMBA in Banking and Financial Services Programs, Dr. Nasir Touheed said that 52 candidates appeared in the test out of which 37 were called for interview.  He said that interview results have not yet been received.     

 

3.4              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that for the sake of transparency 2nd lists by lowering the cut off points for BS program should not have been prepared.

 

3.5              The Registrar said that we can lower the cut off point if we do not achieve the given target. He said that by doing this they are not compromising on the standard.

 

3.6       The Director said that he wants to give a separate identity to Computer Science faculty from Business Administration faculty and develop a new brand for the faculty of Computer Sciences. He said that BS as a new product be launched and for that Advisory Committee from among the major employers should be formed and it should be explored as to what type of product is required. Moreover, we will have to do marketing for the BS program.  He said that if the Associate Dean, Faculty of Computer Sciences goes to Karachi Grammar or Beacon House, he will present his own program. Similarly the Associate Dean of Faculty of Business Administration will make a presentation about his own faculty programs.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       In view of the fact that very few students had applied for BS program the Committee decided to revisit the advertisement for BS program and make it more attractive. 

 

3.8       The Committee also decided that advertisement for BBA and BS programs would be separately placed and the test would also be held separately.

 

3.9       The Committee noted that out of 60, 3 was the cut off point for General Knowledge and decided that for future admission tests, there would be no General Knowledge part.

 

3.10     The Director requested the Registrar to review the point buses requirements in view of campuses choice given to the students. He hoped that lesser number of point buses would be required because of this and the savings on this account could be used for inter campus shuttle services.

 

3.11     The Committee requested Dr. Nasir Touheed to circulate the following data to the Executive Committee:

 

a)       How many students from 3rd stage have made it to the final selection of 60 students for BS program.

b)      If the  admission criteria is adjusted to 80% and 20% weightage for BS program, what impact would it have had on the final list.

c)       What are the percentage marks obtained by the top six candidates of the BS programs who may be considered for merit scholarship.

 

 

ACTION

 

3.12     The Admission Committee will implement the decisions recorded in 3.7 to 3.9 above.

 

3.13          The Registrar will review the point buses requirements for students.

 

3.14          Dr. Nasir Touheed will arrange to provide the information requested in 3.11 above.

Item No. 5      Presentation by M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Limited on the final designs (architectural, structural, MEP and HVAC) and the time line for the project

 

5.1              Mr. Zayd Bilgrami, the representative of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Limited (NBCL) presented the final designs of the Centre for Entrepreneurship and Incubation Centre and the site plan. He also presented the following time line for the project:

 

·         Design Development                         July 20, 2009

·         BOQ, Tender Documents      August 30, 2009

·         Working drawings                 On Award of Contract

·         Ground breaking                    Upon mobilization of Contractor

5.3              In reply to a query regarding master planning of Main Campus, Mr. Zayd Bilgrami stated that he had studied the Master Plan in detail and found that the placement of new buildings is not providing proper connections with one another.  He further stated that the Master Plan needs a lot of improvement in traffic flow both vehicular and pedestrian.

 

5.4              While discussing the location of the Centre for Entrepreneurship, the Registrar proposed that the Centre be relocated at a better site preferably on the rear to save the beautiful lawn of the Main Campus as it has been a real open space for all the building occupants. This way the open space could be saved. The members agreed with the Registrar. Mr. Zayd Bilgrami also agreed to his proposal.

 

5.5              Mr. Zayd Bilgrami was requested to have a meeting with M/s. Icon and Mr. Shahab Ghani to decide and finalize the re-location of the Centre of Entrepreneurship. They were also requested to study the façade development of existing buildings done by M/s. Icon on both the Campuses and ensure that the buildings have a homogeneity of façade elements, colour, texture and material.

 

5.6              The Director said that he is very much concerned that the projects are completed on time and in all this exercise the time schedule is not disturbed.

 

DECISION

 

5.7              The Committee decided that M/s. NBCL will have a meeting with M/s. Icon in the presence of Architect Shahab Ghani to improve the Master Plan and find out a proper location of Centre for Entrepreneurship so that the central lawn space remains free.

 

5.8              The Committee decided that in order to ensure that the colour, material and texture of the buildings on both the campuses are the same, representatives of M/s. Icon, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami and Mr. Shahab Ghani as referee should sit together and agree on the above aspects.

 

ACTION

 

5.9              The Director Projects will arrange a meeting of M/s. Icon, M/s. Najmi Bilgrami and Mr. Shahab Ghani.

Item No. 7      To consider request from Senior Manager IT (I&O) for increase in overtime limit.

 

7.1              The Senior Manager IT(I&O) stated in his note that due to a major overhaul in the ICT infrastructure, ICT staff will have to spend long hours beyond duty time for working on the projects till such time the projects are finally finished. He reported that the overtime accumulated by any individual ICT staff would easily go beyond 30 hours which is the maximum limit for pre-approved overtime hours. Further he stated that there is a restriction that people drawing gross salary of more than Rs.25,000/- are not allowed to claim overtime. Due to this he stated that most of the people who are getting over Rs.25,000/- per month and have to stay back after duty hours are reluctant to work for extra hours because they would get no benefit out of it.

 

7.2              He requested the Executive Committee to increase the limit of salary from Rs.25,000/- per month to at least Rs.30,000/- per month for claiming overtime. He also requested the EC to increase the maximum permissible limit of pre-approved overtime hours to the discretion of Director ICT on project wise basis, a maximum limit may be considered as up to 100 hours per month.

 

DECISION

 

7.3              The Director said that people have made this as a part of their regular earnings. He said that if there are justifications for overtime above 30 hours that must be made by the person certifying the overtime.

 

7.4       The Committee did not approve the request of the Senior Manager IT.   

 

 

 

 

Item No. 8      To consider email from Controller of Examinations on Master Plan of IBA.

 

8.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili in his email commented on the presentation made by M/s. Icon on the proposed structural Master Plan of IBA. He said that from the presentation his feeling was that as if we are going to destroy the structural history of IBA and on its ruins are trying to build structures that might be ten times more grand than the existing one. He wondered as to where will be that IBA which was constructed in 1965 as the oldest business school outside the North America. Where will be those class room blocks, corridors, libraries, auditoriums, that magnificent sprawling lawn (main campus) that witnessed the training of best business managers who proved their worth in the top national and multinational business firms ever since 1958?  All these will be gone forever according to the proposed master plan and will become part of reminiscence.

 

8.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili accepted that we are far behind in infrastructure and related facilities. But he said that standard norm world over is to preserve the structural history as heritage and add on new structural facilities that blend with the old buildings. He asked if it would not have been better if we had done the same; preserving the historical buildings by renovating it and constructing new buildings symmetrically blending with the old ones. He requested for a rethinking on adopting the new construction plan for protecting our more than half a century physical presence.

 

DECISION

 

8.3              The Director said that in principle he agrees with Mr. Mobin Khalili and has the same feelings as those of Mr. Khalili and he also believes in the heritage building but he said that there is a shortage of space and he has no choice.  He said that if IBA could get the Income Tax Building adjacent to the City Campus, it would have been possible to preserve the existing buildings.
