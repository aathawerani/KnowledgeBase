MINUTES OF 245TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, APRIL 15, 2013

 

The 245th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, April 15, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                            Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                  Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                   Registrar

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                    Controller of Examinations

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                      Director Projects
6.      Mr. Zaffar A. Siddiquiai                                           Director CED

7.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                              Director H.R.

8.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                   Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, and Mr. Zafar Chaudhry, Assistant Manager Finance attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the consultancy proposal for furniture design of Library & Administration Building at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects said that the Executive Committee had advised the Project Department to take up the procurement process and execution of furniture works. In this regard project department decided to prepare the design of furniture of Existing Library and Administration Building at IBA Main Campus.

1.2       The Director Projects said that M/s ICON and its co-consultants are working as consultant on Renovation of Existing Library and Administration Building. The design of the fixed furniture (wall cabinets etc.) is already in the scope of the Architects’ original agreement as such it is in the interest of the project to engage the Architect to maintain the same theme of the furniture colour, texture and design as an extension of the existing works. As per SPPRA Rule 16(b)-iv, he said that they obtained the proposal of M/s ICON for the design and supervision of Furniture for above two buildings. He reported that M/s ICON quoted an amount of Rs.950,000/- for the furniture design of Existing Library (Area 27000 SFT) and Rs.1,350,000/- for the Existing Administration Building (Area 63,000 SFT).

1.3       The Director Projects said that this being a feasible proposal, recommendations were solicited from the CPC in its meeting held on 13th April 2013.  After detailed deliberation the CPC recommended the proposal with the advice that the Architects be asked to reduce his fee on the Library Building. The Director Projects said that the Architects could not be approached as their office was closed on Saturday, April 13, the meeting was scheduled on April 15, 2013.

1.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the recommendations of the   Central Purchase Committee and   approve the award of work to M/s. ICON for furniture Design and supervision of Existing Library and Administration Building at IBA Main Campus.

1.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to as to why we will be paying to M/s. Icon to design the furniture while we already have the designs of furniture for the completed buildings. The Registrar said that the projects department has complete specifications for the furniture.  He said that the size of furniture may be different but the material will be the same. He requested the Director Projects to provide the specifications to him so that he may get it done. However, he clarified that since the design of furniture for Library is to be different, he would not be involved in the arrangement of furniture for the Library. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that for the office staff of the Library it will be same; the only difference will be in the racks.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that for Library architects should be involved but not for the Admin. Building. The Director Projects said that prequalification, tendering, tender evaluation, mock-up, factory inspection will have to be done by the Architects. The Registrar said that for tendering and monitoring he does not have the staff.

1.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to further negotiate with the Architects to reduce the fee.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the fee quoted for the library and the admin building should be reduced to half.

DECISION

1.7       The Committee requested the Director Projects to negotiate with M/s. Icon to reduce the fee by 50% for the designs of furniture for both the buildings; Library and Admin. 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the time extension of construction work for Student Centre at Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the Student Centre as per approval was to be completed in April 2013. However, due to the following changes suggested by the EC on its visit of the Students Centre on February 4, 2013, the project would require additional two months i.e. June 30, 2013:

 

a)      Design of Donor’s Wall

b)      Additional Glass wall and Door in event hall

c)      Cabinets in Society room

d)     Notice boards in society room

e)      Low height partitioning wall in hand washing area

 

2.2       The Director Projects further said that on March 27, 2013 the Dean & Director visited the site and proposed the following changes:

a)      Removal of planters from dining hall

b)      Allocation of executive dining area in coffee shop

c)      Conversion of society office to book shop

d)     Roof garden terracing

e)      Protective fence on slop

2.3       The Director Projects said that based on the above facts all these items will be reviewed and drawings will be prepared. After approval of drawing and V.O orders the work will be executed but in order to cut short the process all work done parallel however as per the recommendations of Project Manager the time required is up till June 30, 2013.

2.4       The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on April 13, 2013, recommended the extension in time of completion based on the reasons explained to them. They advised that the extension case for the services of the Project Managers may also be submitted for approval. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the Extension in time up till June 30, 2013.

DECISION

2.5       The Committee approved the time extension of construction work for Student Centre at Main Campus till June 30, 2013.

 

Item No. 3      The problem of sewerage water flooding some important areas of staff colony.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that there is no proper arrangement in IBA Staff Colony for disposal of sewerage water. The old septic tank has lived its life; the waste water is pumped and discharged away from the septic tank. Same operation is carried out near the mosque in the Staff Colony. Not only that, our Maintenance Department has to take care of the sewerage water coming from the University sewerage line that passes through the Staff Colony and has no outlet outside the colony.  Mr. Shameem said that in view of the problem they had planned construction of two septic tanks + soakage pits at two different locations in the Staff Colony. Though the work is in progress, it will take about 4 months for completion.

3.2       Mr. Shameem reported that the condition has drastically worsened during the last 4 weeks, so much so that entire areas surrounding the mosque and the under-construction Visiting Faculty Hostel area flooded with the sewerage water, adversely affecting the construction work at the Visiting Faculty Hostel as well as the excavation being carried out for the new septic tank + soakage pit. Besides, the residents’ complaints of extremely unhygienic conditions and great inconvenience have also piled up. 

3.3       Mr. Shameem said that in order to at least have the KU sewerage water stopped from flowing into the Staff Colony, they approached the KU Engineer. On their request, he even visited the Staff Colony and saw for himself the dirty condition there. But in his meeting the KU engineer regretted his inability to be of any help to IBA, due to paucity of funds. Mr. Shameem said that as it is not in IBA’s interest to raise any dispute with the University, they have no choice but to take care of the situation themselves.

3.4       Mr. Shameem reported that the case was presented to the CPC.  The CPC considered it in its meeting held on April 13, 2013.  The recommendations of the CPC are as follows:

1.      CPC recommends the issue to be of Emergent nature, but as it is not clear who caused the issue & also who should resolve it, EC should be taken under confidence before moving forward on the project.

2.      When the work plan is finalized it would be forwarded to CPC for approval, ideally through circulation due to the emergent nature of the work.

3.      After the issue is resolved, it is important to ascertain who caused the issue. The guilty party should reimburse IBA with the cost incurred to resolve the matter.

3.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the EC to approve resolving the issue as an emergency, with an estimated expenditure of Rs. 500,000/-

3.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that first the Maintenance Department should inspect and if they think it is beyond their scope then it should come in the EC.  Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that it has been done so and the work is to be done by the Maintenance Department but he said that he has taken the initiative because Maintenance Department does not have the approval authority beyond Rs.25,000/-  He clarified that he does not have any clear idea as to what is to be done exactly to resolve this issue.

3.7       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to get it assessed what the problems are and what are their solutions along with their estimated cost.

DECISION

3.8       The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed to submit a work plan to the EC after getting it assessed what is needed to be done and at what cost.

 

Item No. 4      ; Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) To consider and approve Students Fee Collection Procedure and Write Offs.     

4.1       Mr. Zafar Chaudary, Assistant Manager Finance presented the Students’ Fee Collection Procedure and Write Offs on behalf of the Director Finance.   The members observed that since the paper was sent to the EC very late, they could not go through it.  However, the following suggestions were made:

a)      The Committee did not agree to placing list of fee defaulters on the notice boards or the IBA Portal.

b)      Fee defaulters shall be allowed to sit in the exam but their results should be withheld.

c)      Transcripts of fee defaulters should be released with the approval of the Dean & Director.

d)     It was suggested to block the enrolment of fee defaulters at the initial stage (before the start of the semester) so that the student may find a solution or approach the management if there is any genuine reason.     

 

e)      Publication of names and details of fee defaulters in the newspapers was not approved. Similarly, the proposal to send letter to employers of fee defaulters was not accepted.

DECISON

4.2       Mr. Zafar Chaudhry, after incorporating the above changes/suggestions would send the proposal again to the EC for their further comments, if any, through email. The final draft would be presented by the Director Finance to the A&FC for approval.

b) Request of a student to be heard by the Discipline Committee.    

4.3       The Registrar reported that a student was given punishment by the Discipline Committee.  However, the student has requested that he should have been heard by the Discipline Committee.  The Registrar said that Ms. Mhereen Nazar has advised that in this case the EC should direct the Discipline Committee to call the student and listen to him what he has to say.

DECISION

4.4       The EC requested the Discipline Committee to hear the student.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
