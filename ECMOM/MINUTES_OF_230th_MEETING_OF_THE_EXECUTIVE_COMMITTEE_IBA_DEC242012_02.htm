MINUTES OF 230TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 24, 2012


The 230th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 24, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                  Dean & Director
2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat           Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr.Sayeed Ghani                  Associate Dean, FCS
4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                   Controller of Examinations
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz     Director Projects
6. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui             Director CED
7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                 Director ICT
8. Mr. Moeid Sultan                  Acting Director Finance

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 To consider and approve the award of work to the lowest bidder for supply and installation of Sewage Treatment Plant.

1.1 The Director Projects reported that an advertisement was floated in May 2012 in order to pre-qualify the specialty contractors for the sewage treatment plant. After detail scrutiny and approval of IBA authorities the following contractors were pre-qualified and tenders for supply and installation of Sewage treatment plant were floated to the following contractors:

1. M/s Eco West International (Pvt.) Ltd.
2. M/s Samad Khan Babar & Co & So-Safe Water Technology
3. M/S PEPAK-KG(JV)
4. M/s RM Gulistan Engineer & Contractor
5. M/s KSB Pumps Co. Ltd.

1.2 All the five contractors collected the bid and except M/s Eco West International all submitted the bids on specified date and time. The bids were opened in front of Contractor’s representatives, Architect’s representative, Project Manager’s representative and IBA officials. The bids announced at the time of bidding after rebate given by the contractors were as follows:

1. M/s. Samad Khan Babar & Co. & So-Safe Water Technology Rs.28,830,820
2. M/s. PEPAK-KG(JV) Rs.30,210,637
3. M/s. RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractor Rs.19,966,182
4. M/s. KSB Pumps Rs.29,750,000

1.3 The Director Projects informed the Committee that the bids documents as received from the contractors were sent to M/s. NESPAK and M/s. NBCL for their scrutiny and evaluation on October 11, 2012. M/s NESPAK and M/s NBCL submitted their reports on November 26, 2012. In their reports M/s. NESPAK and M/s. NBCL both recommended the award of work to M/s. RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractors at a cost of Rs.20,866,109/- The Director Projects clarified that as per the Evaluation Report of M/s. NBCL the final price of the lowest bidder is Rs.20,508,005/- In their report they wrongly adjusted the O&M cost therefore there is a difference of Rs.358,104/- between NESPAK and NBCL reports. He said that they have taken the price of the lowest bidder from NESPAK’s report which is Rs.20,866,109/-.

1.4 The Director Projects reported that the case was presented in Technical Evaluation Committee and the Central Purchase Committee meetings held on Saturday December 08, 2012 and Thursday, December 20, 2012 respectively. Both the TEC and the CPC recommended the approval for award of work to the lowest evaluated bidder.

1.5 Based on the recommendations of Architect, Project Managers, Technical Evaluation Committee and Central Purchase Committee, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve of award of work to the lowest Evaluated bidder M/s. RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractors at a cost of Rs.20,866,109/- for the supply and installation of Sewage treatment plant at IBA Main Campus.

1.6 Dr. Nishat observed that the bid prices of three contractors are almost consistent but the bid price of M/s. RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractors is almost 50% low. The Director Projects said that M/s. KSB Pumps have quoted Rs.5 million more than the estimate for the Pump. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the KSB Pumps’ STP is a compact unit and its quality is better. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there should be a comparison of the items and price quoted by the contractors so that the EC may know the difference of price and quality. The Dean & Director said that because of low price the equipment should not be of inferior quality which may give trouble at a later stage. The Director Projects said that more than the quality of equipment, it requires proper maintenance. He said that similar pump at Sindh Club was closed probably due to non-maintenance.

1.7 Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that if the TEC and CPC had done due diligence then the questions raised in the EC would have been raised and answered in TEC and CPC.

DECISION

1.8 The Committee deferred this item and requested the Director Projects to invite NESPAK and SMC to satisfy the EC that there would be no compromise on the quality of equipment and work if the contract is awarded to the lowest bidder.

ACTION

1.9 The Director Projects would invite NESPAK and SMC to brief the EC. The Director Projects would also obtain details as to why the pump at Sindh Club was closed.


Item No.2 To consider and approve the procurement of furniture for New Girls' Hostel, Phase 1 – STHP.

2.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that bids for the furniture for new Girls’ Hostel (Phase 1 – STHP) for which the funds have been provided by Government of Sindh, under the PC-1, were invited from the following four pre-qualified manufacturers / suppliers:

1. M/s. Inspire Interior
2. M/s. SIMAB (smc-pvt) Ltd.
3. M/s. Versant
4. M/s. Zunn Furniture (Pvt.) Ltd.

2.2 He said that one of the aforesaid prospective bidders, namely, M/s. Zunn Furniture (Pvt.) Ltd. declined to participate in the bidding, through their e-mail of December 08, 2012 because the term of payment was not acceptable to them. Rest of them submitted their bids which were opened on December 17, 2012, in the presence of two of the bidders’ representatives. He presented Comparative Statement of the bids. The bid prices quoted by them were as follow:

1. M/s. Inspire Interior : Rs. 6,494,000
2. M/s. SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd. : 6,229,437
3. M/s. Versant : 11,579,584

2.3 Mr. Shameem informed the Committee that TEC considered this proposal in its meeting held on December 19, 2012 and recommended that the contract be awarded to the lowest bidder, namely, SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd. at the bid price of Rs.6,229,437/- He said that the proposal has also been considered and recommended by CPC that the contract for the furniture be awarded to the lowest bidder.

2.4 The Dean & Director said that we have acquired furniture for boys’ and girls’ hostels in earlier periods. He said that there should be a comparison of the cost of furniture procured earlier and the price quoted this time. Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the specifications for the furniture for boys’ hotel were different so the cost was also high. He said that he has repeated the specifications of the furniture of old girls’ hostel and the price is almost the same.

DECISION

2.5 The Committee approved the procurement of furniture for new Girls’ Hostel (Phase 1 –STHP) from the lowest bidder namely M/s. SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd. at the bid price of Rs.6,229,437/-


Item No.3 To consider and approve the additional fee to M/s. KP & Associates for re-designing, re-engineering & designing and supervision for temporary electrical supply for Aman-CED Building

3.1 The Director Projects said that M/s. KP & Associates were requested to provide IBA value engineering of their design based on which the cost of the work as per the lowest bid was received for Rs.147 million. At the same time they had to re-design the entire system due to change in the mode of supply from KESC. This involved complete revision of the Electrical Infrastructure drawings. They were also requested to provide their design for the temporary electric supply for Aman-CED and New Academic Block. He said that since all these work fall in purview of additional work, KP Associates were asked to submit their fee based on actual man-hour in preparing the design and supervision. Accordingly, they claimed fixed fee of Rs.400,000/- However, when they were requested to reduce the amount, they reduced the amount by 10%. The Director Projects requested the EC to approve payment of Rs.360,000/- to M/s. KP Associates as the work done by them was to meet emergent timeline without which the power supply to Aman-CED and New Academic Block would have not been possible.

3.2 The Dean & Director said that M/s KP Associates have been very cooperative and they did the additional work at our request and they came up with temporary solution for the Aman-CED building to make it functional and save lot of costs on diesel for the generator that would have otherwise been used.

DECISION

3.3 The Committee approved the additional fee of Rs.360,000/- to M/s. KP & Associates for re-designing, re-engineering & designing and supervision for temporary electrical supply for Aman-CED Building.


Item No.4 Request from Mr. Shahid Ilyas, a student of EMBA for Make-up Examination

4.1 Mr. Shahid Ilyas, student of EMBA, through his email to the Controller of Examinations, reported that he could not appear for SHRM paper held on Friday, December 14, 2012 from 6:30 to 9:30 p.m. as he was stuck in a huge traffic jam around IBA City Campus. He had reported that he was coming from SITE and tried to reach IBA from three different routes but couldn't get there. He was finally stuck up at road from Lasbela to Zoo and was stranded there till 10:30 p.m. (at this time the situation of city also turned bad). He reported that he reached home at 11:00 p.m. He also mentioned in his email that Mr. Arshad Zia had called him at 8:16 p.m. and offered him to come even late for the paper, but he (Shahid) did not have a chance to get his car through and park somewhere like petrol pump. Mr. Shahid requested to consider this as a special case and allow him for the Re-paper in due course so that his grades do not deteriorate.

4.2 Mr. Mobin Khalili said that since it was the final exam, as per rule, make-up is not allowed. However, he said that Mr. Shahid Ilyas may be given an ‘I’ in that course.

DECISION

4.3 The Committee decided to give ‘I’ (Incomplete) to Mr. Shahid Ilyas on SHRM (Strategic Human Resource Management).

Item No.5 Up-date on Time Bound Projects from the Director ICT.

5.1 Cisco Active components: The Director ICT reported that he has been informed by Mr. Maroof Ayub – CEO, AMFCO, that the consignment would reach Karachi Port on the evening of December 24, 2012 and will be cleared by 27th if 25th and 26th are not public holidays, otherwise it will be cleared by 31st December 2012.

b) Kitchen at Students’ Centre.

7.5 The Director Projects said that he was not present in the last EC meeting where it was reported that 35 ampere electrical load for each outlet would be required. He said that according to the Consultant, 35 ampere would be insufficient. He further said that the existing gas pressure will not be sufficient; it will have to be increased but it may take time. Till such time the gas from SSGC is available, gas cylinder will have to be arranged.
