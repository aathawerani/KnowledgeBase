Item No. 1 To consider the proposal for cost effective increase in capacity of Main Campus mosque with simultaneous implementation of Islamic principle of segregation.

 

1.1       Mr. S. Irfan Nabi, Faculty member proposed that the existing mosque at Main Campus be turned into exclusively women section. He suggested that the existing partition between the two halls be removed to provide more space. Similarly the ablution sections be combined. The existing courtyard may be adjusted to allow movement space between library and this extended women’s section. Item No. 2 Approval for Centre for Entrepreneurial Development Accelerator Program (CEDAP).

 

2.1       Mr. Imran Khan, giving background said that CED Accelerator Program (CEDAP), a venture support initiative by the CED, aims to create an environment in which entrepreneurial ventures can flourish and help stimulate Pakistan’s economy at large. The program will facilitate start-up ventures to grow fast by providing necessary mentoring from established entrepreneurs with vested interest in the success of the ventures, seed funding, infrastructure, environment and access to IBA network. The entrepreneurs will go through a rich experience of starting and launching a new venture. He said that CEDAP provides a community and educational support to strengthen the entrepreneurial abilities and improve the chances of success of young entrepreneurs – with their current and future ventures. This is accomplished with a collaborative effort – the CEDAP ecosystem – consisting of CEDAP participants, leaders, mentors, and invited guests. CEDAP ecosystem engages and inspires its participant, to allow them to make meaningful progress with their venture, and to help them become a more experienced, knowledgeable and confident entrepreneur.  

 

2.2       Mr. Imran reported that the four ventures selected for the 2013 session of the program are the following:

 

1.      Goomsa is a web-based venture that provides an online community through which people can discover, share and interact with local businesses like restaurants, salons, shopping centers and doctors among others as well as enable businesses to attract new customers and solidify relationships with existing ones by replying to their reviews, offering loyalty-based deals and discounts and showcase their products.

 

2.      Baker Street is an upbeat and friendly home-based bakery that offers fresh cakes, gourmet cookies, brownies and a lot more. The unique feature about the products is that they contain high quality ingredients like premium cocoa beans and dairy butter, feature unusual textures and natural flavors.

 

3.      Ignite is a psychometric consultancy venture in Pakistan designed to cater to the counseling needs of Pakistani youth. Ignite aims to streamline the career choices of youth with their potential and strengths.

 

4.      Silver Sages Studios is a web-based virtual home system that provides a one-stop solution for home, office, kitchen designing, furnishing and décor.

 

2.3       Mr. Imran said that these ventures are mentored by some of the leading Pakistani entrepreneurs. He provided details of Seed Funding and the progress up-date of these ventures. He said that the CED Accelerator Program spans over a year and consists of three phases:

 

· Phase-1 (Spring 2013)

o Workshops, progress presentations and meeting with the mentors and other experts each month to execute and review the plan of action

 

· Phase-2 (Summer 2013)

o Weekly workshops, progress presentations, guest speaker sessions and venture refinement meetings to meet venture growth targets

o Launch of the business (if not done previously)

o A demo day at the end of the summer term during which the ventures will pitch to investors and general public

 

· Phase-3 (Fall, 2013)

o Follow up meetings with the mentors and presentations each month to discuss the position and progress of the ventures

 

2.4       Mr. Imran Khan presented details of estimated expenditure of Rs.2.95 million to cover seed funding @Rs.200,000 for four ventures, office space, honorarium/ remuneration for workshop facilitators, experts, executive assistants, lunch & learn sessions etc.

 

2.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know as to what will be the payback period of the financing to be provided by IBA.  Mr. Imran replied that the payback period will start three years after graduation of the student.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee approved the CEDAP proposal as well as budget estimates as presented by Mr. Imran Khan.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the proposal on Replacement of Laptops.

 

4.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that in the EC meeting held on May 20, 2013 he had presented a proposal for laptop distribution.  He said that the purpose of this proposal was to cut-off investment which IBA is doing on purchase of laptops. He said that the proposal was that the price of laptop be deducted from the salary of faculty / staff in three years’ instalments. After three years, the ownership of the laptop would be transferred to laptop user.  He said that following two options were proposed:

 

Option 1         Full amount deduction:

 

Full amount of the laptop would be deducted from the salary of faculty / staff in 36 instalments.

 

Option 2         50% amount deduction:

 

Half amount of the laptop would be deducted from the salary of faculty / staff in 36 instalments. In this case IBA will bear rest of the cost.

 

            Financial Implications under Option 1:

                           

Investment

Rs.3,025,000

Annual Return

Rs.1,008,330

ROI/Year

     33.3%

 

(Amount to be deducted per month = Rs.1,681/-)

 

Financial Implications under Option 2:

              

Investment

Rs.3,025,000

Annual Return

Rs.   504,162

ROI/Year

      16.6%

           

(Amount to be deducted per month = Rs.840/-)

 

4.2       Mr. Imran Batada said that the following would be the benefits to IBA and the end user:

 

10.  Benefits to IBA

11.  Benefit to End User

·         IBA will recover its full / half investment & reduce its capital expenditure.

·         End user would use this hardware with extreme care which will reduce overall operation cost.

·         IBA would not maintain any End of life laptop inventory for disposal.

·         Faculty / staff would get ownership of laptop on easy instalments without any interest being charged or would get laptop in 50% of its price, if option 2 is opted.

12.      

·         End user will get exclusive warranty / support during the three years period.

 

Rules:

 

1.      These laptops would be primarily used for the business purpose of IBA.

2.      Only one laptop will be issued.

3.      In case of snatching / stolen / damage, temporary hardware would be provided to the faculty / staff by ICT department, subject to the availability of the desktop / laptop in the inventory pool of  ICT and after the insurance claim, replacement would be provided.

4.      In case of stolen machine, the user must initiate and submit FIR to Manager Customer Support.

5.      During three years, ICT Department will be responsible for the hardware / software maintenance of the laptops.

6.      When a faculty/ staff resigns, the outstanding amount of computer will be deducted from his/her final pay. If insufficient funds are available in his/her pay, an invoice will be issued to the employee by the Finance Department.

7.      The faculty / staff will be responsible of the laptop and its accessories.

 

Software Installed on Laptops:

 

Software that IBA has purchased and is the part of its Enterprise Agreement would not be provided to the end user after three years. Only that software which is the part of laptop package and there is no separate identifiable cost, is to be handed over to employee after three years.

 

4.3       Mr. Imran Batada said that the EC in that meeting had asked him to discuss the proposal with the Associate Deans and after incorporating their suggestions, present the revised proposal to the EC. Accordingly, he said that he discussed his proposal with the Associate Deans who were of the opinion that IBA should continue to provide the laptops to our faculty, since it is useful for both teaching and research purposes.  They had also suggested that the laptops may be replaced in subsequent years instead of one go in order to balance the capex budget.  

 

4.4       Mr. Imran Batada reported that when he conveyed the recommendations of the Associate Deans to the EC, the following comments were received from Mr. Mobin Khalili, and Ms. Ayesha Menai respectively:

 

Mr. Mobin Khalili:

“Considering the financial crunch faced by IBA, I disagree with the recommendation as a member of EC. There is no need to replace laptops that are in good working condition. Only damaged / faulty laptops may be replaced.”

 

Ms. Ayesha Menai:

“I agree that for a public sector academic institution where funds will always be precious, laptop replacement should be only on need basis. Faculty and staff alike should be made aware of the need for frugality when times demand it. Given the technology up-gradation initiatives of the last 4-5 years, the impression that luxuries like laptops in addition to latest desktops, are in abundant supply is simply not sustainable. In the EC meeting where this issue was discussed, Dr Nishat's advice was sensible that instead of making a very strict policy, we should evaluate each case and based on genuine need, ICT Head should be empowered to make a judgment call.”

 

4.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan said Sukkur IBA provides loan to its faculty for the purchase of laptop.  The Director H.R. said that Sukkur IBA provides loan of Rs.60,000/- to its faculty for the purchase of laptop. Sukkur IBA itself does not purchase the laptop nor does it provide repair maintenance facilities.

 

4.6       The Dean & Director said that if we provide loan for the purchase of laptop instead of giving laptop, there may not be standard laptop and there is a possibility of installing pirated windows. He said that he took the initiative of providing free laptops to incentivize faculty members to use them for instructional purpose. As that promotional objective has been achieved we have to shift the gear. He said that Option 2 i.e 50% amount deduction seems appropriate as it combines the contribution of IBA towards the cost of laptop and the contribution of the individual faculty member. As the ownership will revert to the individual it is inevitable that care and maintenance will be better.  He said that after the warranty period maintenance will be the responsibility of the owner of the laptop. The Committee members agreed that Option 2 was a preferred option.

 

4.7       The Director Projects suggested to get compulsory insurance cover for the laptops the premium for which is very nominal. Mr. Imran Batada said that they already get insurance cover for IT equipments.

 

4.8       The Committee was informed by Mr. Imran Batada that as many as 66 staff members have been provided both laptops as well as desktops at the recommendations of their HoDs. The Dean & Director expressed his surprise at this wasteful expenditure. He said that non-faculty will get either the laptop or the desktop. He said that exceptional cases for laptop with justification will be approved by the Dean & Director.  He asked Mr. Imran Batada to retrieve the laptops given to the non-faculty except the HoDs.  

 

DECSION

 

4.9       The Committee approved Option 2 i.e. half amount of the laptop would be deducted from the salary of faculty / HOD and staff (in exceptional cases) in 36 instalments. In this case IBA will bear rest of the cost.

 

4.10     The Committee decided that as a policy non-faculty will not be given laptop.  Laptops would be provided only to the faculty and HoDs and those considered justifiable by the Dean & Director.  A faculty / HOD holding more than one positions will be given one laptop only. 

 

4.11     The Committee further decided that if a fulltime faculty member or HOD wants to retain the IBA laptop after its useful life, he/she may retain it after paying residual market value to be assessed by the ICT department. In such cases IBA will not be providing any maintenance service.

 

ACTION

 

4.12     Mr. Imran Batada would start retrieving the laptops issued to non-teaching staff.

5.1       The Registrar said that the inauguration at the Main Campus of a full fledged ‘Student Centre’ is around the corner and scheduled to take place in August time frame. A cafeteria with semblance of a ‘food court’ forms part of the new premises.  Presently, two cafeterias at Main Campus and one cafeteria at the City Campus are operational and administered by the Manager Admin at the respective Campus. He presented draft of the proposed Cafeteria Committee consisting of the terms of reference, composition and responsibilities of the proposed Cafeteria Committee.

 

Objective: The Cafeteria Committee will supervise and ensure efficient management of the IBA Karachi cafeterias towards providing hygienic, healthy and tasteful foods for use of students, faculty and staff members.

 

Terms of Reference and Composition:

 

·         The Cafeteria Committee is accountable through its chairperson to the Executive Committee.  It proposes major decisions on the operation of the cafeteria and overall administration of the parties to whom the cafeteria services are outsourced. These include long and short-term goals, policies, quality of services, hygiene and equipment. Recommendations are reported to the sponsoring body prior to adoption. The Committee not only allows the workload to be shared as a team, but also provides a wider forum so that different views and ideas can be considered.

 

·         The cafeteria committee members will liaise directly with cafeteria staff and volunteers on matters relating to the day-to-day management of the cafeteria as per respective responsibilities and report matters of importance to the chairperson.

10.    One member of the staff from main campus…..…………………..     Member

11.    Manager Administration (both campuses) as ex-officio members

 

Role and Responsibilities of Members:

The main role of the cafeteria committee is cafeteria management and smooth daily operations of cafeteria. It will involve reviewing and planning healthy menus, monitoring and inspecting hygiene standards and general up keep of the cafeteria premises.

 

Monitoring and monthly MIS:

 

· Cafeteria committee will assemble at 5 pm on every second Friday of the month to meet with cafeteria operators to finalize coming week menu, the same will be made available to all in the institute on notice board.

 

· Will act on findings of review done on daily basis on all parameters by all members.

 

· Will act on complaints and intimate the improvements on the same to complainants.

 

Selection of Cafeteria Committee members:

 

It will be a voluntary committee, volunteers will be selected and names will be proposed by the following persons:

c. Staff rep by Manager HR.

 

Duration of Cafeteria Committee (CC):

 

It shall be functional for 2 semesters. There will be two CC in a year. Once worked in CC will not be working in CC in subsequent semester; however can apply for next to next semester.

 

Rewards and Recognition:

 

Best Cafeteria Committee will be judged on these criteria’s

 

· Observance of Cleanliness and Hygiene Standards

· Presentation of cafeteria and meal/ refreshment items

a)      Revised TOR of the Admission Committee.

 

9.1       The Director H.R. reported that the Registrar in his capacity as Chairman, Admission Committee has prepared the draft of the Revised TOR of the Admission Committee which is as follows:

 

Objectives / Scope

 

The objective of the Admission Committee is to ensure that the tenets of the IBA Karachi admission policy are stringently followed by all concerned and that all activities relating to the admission of candidates is executed in a planned and organized manner. These activities would include holding of open houses / information sessions, the announcement of admission schedule, registration of candidates for the aptitude tests, issue of admit cards, conduct of aptitude test, conduct of group discussion and interview sessions, followed by issue of admission offer letters to the successful candidates and other matters related to admissions at IBA.

 

Functions of the Committee

 

The terms of reference of the Committee will be:

 

- To formulate, review and update admission policies of all programs of IBA and present them as proposals / recommendations in front of the required forums for approval.

- To monitor rules and regulations relating to admission to various programs.

- To review, process and respond to all queries on Admission related matters.

- To administer and support the activities of the Admission Office.

- To plan and execute the holding of all open houses, information session and road shows related activity.

- To liaise with the testing services department and coordinate all activities relating to physical holding of aptitude tests, group discussions and interview sessions.

- To periodically review and upgrade the quality, rigor and relevance of the aptitude tests.

- To respond to queries relating to eligibility of candidates and issues relating equivalence / transfer of credits.

 

Composition of the Committee

 

The holders of the under mentioned offices shall constitute the Admission Committee:

 

- Registrar (Ex officio Chairman)

- Head of Testing Services Department

- Program Director MBA Program

- Program Director BBA Program

- Program Director Computer Sciences Program

- Any faculty member co-opted  by the Chairman for professional advice

 

Quorum for the Committee

 

The quorum for the meeting will be one half of the total number of members, a fraction being counted as one.

 

Coordination

 

The Committee will meet at 10:30 a.m. on the last Friday of every month or as and when required to respond to admission related queries.

 

All business of the Committee shall be reported as minutes of meeting to the Dean and Director, IBA Karachi for approval, before further processing.
