Item No. 1      Vacation of 1st floor of existing Administration Block, City Campus to be handed over to Contractor.

 

1.1       As Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia could not attend the meeting due to indisposition, Mr. Umer Makhdumi, Project Engineer, presented the case. He stated that as per M/s. Integ’s letter of December 07, 2010, extension of time was recommended for the project of refurbishment of Administration Block City Campus till May 31, 2011. However, he said that the various administrative constraints that cropped up during the last several weeks have hindered the handing over of 1st floor of existing Admin Block to the contractor and thus have the propensity of further delaying the project. He reported that in total six rooms in all on the 1st floor, four on one side (including Telecommunication lab) and two on the other, are still occupied by faculty and staff and should be re-located on an urgent basis. He suggested that the seven rooms of phase-1 of Admin Block, first floor which were handed over to admin staff on January 07, 2011, can be used to accommodate some of the staff of these six rooms. After the shifting of staff, the necessary utilities e.g. electrical and I.T. connections of all the 1st floor rooms along with cables will have to be disconnected and re-routed, plus shifting of furniture and other equipment, a process which also will be time consuming. He further reported that even if all the six rooms are fully vacated, the ICT Data Center on 1st floor, which is expected to be shifted on Ground Floor faculty room (handed over to management on January 06, 2011) will remain on hold for around 3-4 months as their scope of work and BOQ are still not ready. The portion of Data Center can thus extend beyond the stipulated date as its completion is linked with shifting of ICT staff to the Ground Floor faculty room.

 

Mr. Makhdumi informed the Committee that on January 22, 2011, the ICT Department installed a 30-KVA capacity UPS and battery in the old Degree Section on 1st floor, the area which was used as a passage to access phase-1. Therefore, this is also one of the factors which can contribute towards possible delay. He said that all these issues were communicated to stakeholders through mails and face to face meetings but the projects department is still unable to hand over 1st floor admin block to contractors.

 

1.2       Mr. Makhdumi further said that on the Ground Floor, a telephone DB is functional in the old telephone operator’s room which is proving to be a hiccup in demolition of wall. However, he said that the ICT has assured vide their message dated January 17, 2011 that they will remove the same along with related cables and wiring by January 30, 2011. He sought support of the Executive Committee in making the 1st floor of existing Admin Block available to the Projects Department (minus data center and lab-16) with all utilities disconnected so that the same can be handed over to contractors without any delay.

 

DECISIONS

 

1.3       The Director expressed his displeasure over the way the vacation of the 1st floor was being handled. He said that he wants the whole 1st floor vacated in order to get the refurbishment work completed in time. After hearing the view points of various members, he requested Mr. Mobin Khalili to visit the places along with Mr. Makhdumi after the meeting and then make a plan as to who would be shifted where and to communicate them in writing that they have to shift at such and such place on such and such date. He asked Mr. Khalili to report to him in the evening on the action they take.  

 

1.4       On the issue of shifting the DB from the old Telephone Operator’s Room, the Director ICT said that the DB can immediately be shifted but that would result in total disconnection from outside world. He requested for ten days time for properly shifting the DB from the Telephone Operator’s Room.  The Committee allowed 10 days time i.e. February 10, 2011 for shifting the DB.  

 

1.5       Regarding non-availability of the scope of work and BoQ for the ICT Data Center the Director requested the Director ICT to ask Mr. M. Faisal Zia to provide the scope of work and the BoQ and if there is any problem then he should inform the Director.

 

1.6       The Director clarified that the electrical outlets and cabling work is the responsibility of the Projects Department and other work including data nodes is the responsibility of the ICT Department.

 

1.7       Mr. Makhdumi assured that the cabling work at the Research Centre will be completed and the Centre will be fully complete by February 15, 2011.

 

1.8       The Director requested Mr. Mobin Khalili to monitor the work and if there is any problem then he should resolve it so that the Centre is fully functional by February 15, 2011.

 

1.9       Mr. Makhdumi and the Director ICT assured that by the end of February the Data Centre would be shifted and completed. The EC allowed the shifting and completion of Data Centre by the end of February 2011.  

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve estimate for tendering process for supplying and laying of UPS cables and electrical distribution board in Academic Block, City Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Makhdumi reported that in the Architectural plan for Academic Block, dated May 05, 2010, one Battery Room and one UPS Room were shown to cater for one UPS of Academic Block only. Thereafter, the ICT Department conveyed the requirement about incorporating one additional UPS in the Academic Block UPS Room to energize the Admin Block as well due to limited availability of space in Admin Block. So there would now be two UPS in Academic Block UPS Room; one each for Academic and Admin Blocks.  He stated that on August, 2010, the ICT provided the requirements to Projects Department which were forwarded to electrical consultant and on September 01, 2010, an initial/tentative BOQ and cable route plan for the said tasks were received from Electrical Consultant which were shared with ICT as well as the Electrical Department of City Campus.

  

2.2      Mr. Makhdumi said that in a meeting with projects team, ICT, electrical consultant and electrical department, it was decided that the incoming UPS cables for both the UPS will be connected from the electrical room of Academic Block. He said that as the task of supplying and connecting the UPS is within the purview of ICT, they informed in a meeting held during mid November, 2010, that their contractor would ensure supply and laying of DC cable, connecting UPS and battery bank, to the tune of only 10-feet. Hence the inclusion of extra length of DC cable had to be included in the electrical consultant’s scope of work. After receiving all necessary information from IBA stakeholders, the electrical consultant sent an engineering estimate for the whole task in the first week of January, 2011, which was reviewed and further corrected by PP&D and Project Managers. He said that the tentative estimate for cabling, conduiting, DB etc. is of Rs.3 million. 

 

2.3       Mr. Zafar-ul-Islam said that if it is kept in the existing place, then no additional cost would be incurred.

 

2.4       The Registrar considered it a very costly solution and suggested that after the central UPS system, many UPS placed in various offices may be spared and can be used as back up.

 

2.5       The Committee agreed with the Registrar that there was no need to incur additional cost.

 

2.6       Mr. Zafar-ul-Islam said that there are three scopes of work which include the total amount; first one is to energize the UPS in Academic and Admin Buildings, second is to supply the power from Academic Building to Admin Building, and the third is the DB.  However, he said that if the Admin Building is dropped, even then the cost of DB and cables to Academic Building will remain there.

 

DECISON

 

2.7       The Committee asked Mr. Makhdumi to present revised proposal in a comparative form for both the options – centralized and de-centralized.

 

ACTION

 

2.8       Mr. Umer Makhdumi would present a revised proposal in a comparative form for both the centralized and de-centralized options.

Item No. 4      To consider and recommend to the Board Retirement Policy for Teaching and Non-teaching Management and Non-Management Employees.

 

4.1       The Director H.R. presented the Retirement Policy for Teaching and Non-Teaching Management & Non-Management employees. She defined the purpose, scope, procedure, post-retirement employment procedure for faculty, non-teaching management and non-management staff.

 

4.2       Dr. Muhammad Nishat wanted to know if gratuity which is generally allowed in every university, has been allowed or not for those who would be re-hired after retirement.  The Director H.R. replied that gratuity has not been allowed to those who would be rehired after retirement.

 

4.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to which sources have been consulted in preparing this policy. He suggested that there should be no contradiction form the standard practice. 

 

4.4       The Director said that there was no source but it was a proposal. He said that generally teachers do not have retirement age but they are proposing 3 years extension at a time after retirement upto the age of 70 years. However, he said that if IBA gets enough younger qualified faculty, then senior teachers’ rehiring period may be reduced. He wanted to discuss each proposal; for faculty, management and non-management staff.

 

4.5       The Registrar wanted to know the rationale of 3 years extension at a time for faculty members.    

 

4.6       The Director H.R. said that it is a normal practice but she said that it can be revisited if needed.

 

4.7       The Registrar said that some faculty members were interested to hold the position of Registrar.  He suggested that there should be a position under the Registrar for faculty so that the concerned faculty member may be considered for the position of Registrar when needed.

 

4.8       The Director did not agree with the Registrar. However, he said that the best person whosoever it may be either from inside or from outside would be appointed.

 

4.9       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the employees can also take voluntary retirement at 25 years of service besides retirement at the age of 60.

 

4.10     The Director agreed with Mr. Khalili and said that they should not deviate from the government policy. He suggested that Karachi University may be made as a reference.

 

4.11     The Director H.R. stated that post-retirement employment contract of non-teaching non-management staff is not permissible under the proposed policy. However, if such a contract is awarded to a non-management staff with the special approval of the Dean & Director, IBA, the duration of the contract can only be for 1 year, with no further extension.

 

4.12     The Director advised to add “through the Executive Committee” in the above para (4.11) after the word “Dean & Director, IBA”. 

 

DECISION

 

4.13     The Committee recommended the Retirement Policy for Teaching & Non-teaching Management and non-management staff to the Selection Board for approval.

 

ACTION

 

4.14     The Director H.R. after incorporating the suggestions noted in 4.9, 4.10 & 4.12 above, present the policy to the Selection Board.

  

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Decision on contract award amount of IBA new Academic Block.

 

5.1       The Director Projects in his note stated that during the last three days a situation has arisen in case of the award of the contract for the works of New Academic Block.

 

5.2       The Director Projects referred to the minutes of the revised EC minutes of the 17th January 2011 which were revised by the Manager Corporate Affairs with the consent of Dean and Director IBA, in view of the observation from one member of the A&FC duly endorsed by the Chairman of A&FC. The Director Projects mentioned in his note that originally the Projects Department had obtained details of the duty and taxes amount from the contractor in pursuance of the decision taken by the EC in its earlier meeting. In the meeting held on 17th January, EC was conveyed the decision of the A&FC which had agreed to a deduction of Rs.6.5 million from the bid amount with the condition that contractor be asked to give details of Rs.3.0 million and the details of forms to be issued to the Customs by IBA. The amount of Rs.6.5 million was based on the calculations provided by the contractor which was duly checked and verified by IBA Finance department. But under the revised minutes a deduction of Rs.9.4 million is to be made from the bid amount, instead of Rs.6.5 million. The contractor was asked to accept this condition but he declined. He even declined to the deduction of Rs.6.5 million. Instead, he has informed that he would withdraw his offer of Rs.9.5 million rebate in case of IBA importing the equipment. He now requested that he may be issued the work order at the bid price of Rs.202 million and that the amount of exemption of duty and taxes (calculated at the time of release of equipment) be deducted from his bill.  The Director Projects also attached the recommendation of the Project Managers.

 

5.3       The representative of the Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership said that the Contractor’s apprehension is that if the exemption facility is withdrawn then how will they get the money so they want that there should be no mention of deduction in the contract agreement. He suggested that the deduction clause may be included in the Letter of Intent.

 

5.4       The Director suggested to draft the Letter of Intent (LOI) cross referencing the agreement and to send it to the IBA’s Legal Advisor for vetting and then it should be sent to the A&FC for approval.

 

5.5       The Director ICT suggested that it should also be written in the LOI that if the tax exemption facility is withdrawn then the Contractor will be entitled to receive that amount from IBA.

 

DECISION

 

5.6       The Committee decided that draft LOI with deduction clause be first sent to the IBA’s Legal Advisor for vetting and then to the A&FC for approval.

 

ACTION

 

5.7       Mr. Abdur Rahman would prepare the draft LOI inserting the deduction clause and the proposal of the Director ICT noted in 5.5 above and send it to the Legal Advisor of IBA for vetting and then to the A&FC for consideration.       

 

b)         To consider note from Director CED for payment of balance amount of US$ 58,580.52 for IBA CED Faculty Training to Babson College, USA.

 

5.8       The Director CED reported that three faculty members of IBA attended Faculty Training Program at Babson College, USA during Fall Semester 2010.  He said that the training was initiated in collaboration with USAID under capacity building program for IBA – Center for Entrepreneurship Development.  The IBA entered into an affiliation agreement with Babson College. Under this agreement the cost for establishment of IBA Entrepreneurship Center / faculty training at Babson College was estimated at US$1 million which was expected to be incurred in a span of three years. The cost was also anticipated to be financed by USAID under the IBA CED Project.  Unfortunately, during the middle of the said training, the USAID stopped its direct funding to Pakistani universities. The efforts from IBA, Karachi as well as from Babson College are underway for revival of this funding relationship. He reported that so far for this training, USAID had financed only a portion of the training project worth $128,191.24 i.e. from August 2010 till October 22, 2010. Now they have received an invoice of $58,580.52 from Babson College for the remaining period of the training i.e. from October 23, 2010 to December 2010.

 

5.9       The Director CED requested the Executive Committee to approve the balance payment of US$58,580.52 to Babson College at the prevailing foreign exchange rates.  He proposed that this amount may be charged to Affiliation & Certifications – CED Budget as an IBA expense.

 

5.10     The Director said that the intermediary’s contract with the USAID has expired and although they are taking it up with USAID, there is not a very hopeful situation. He suggested that the amount which comes to around Rs.5 million may be paid from the budget for Faculty Training and Development.

 

DECISION

 

5.11     The Committee approved the payment of US$58,580.52 to Babson College from the budget for Faculty Training & Development.

 

ACTION

 

5.12     The Director Finance would arrange payment of US$58,580.52 to Babson College from the budget for Faculty Training and Development.  

 

c)      Number of changes to be allowed in the ad design.

 

5.13     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that they give IBA’s ads for design to the two approved ad agencies for which IBA does not pay to them because they get commission on the ad IBA places.  He said that the Advertising agencies are often required to change the design of an ad several times.  This involves cost on the part of the agencies which IBA does not pay. He requested the EC to decide the number of changes to be allowed in the design.

 

DECISION

 

5.14     The Committee decided that a maximum of three changes be allowed in the draft design of an ad.

 

5.15     The Committee also decided that the agency with which the satisfaction level is very high should be given highly visible ads and the ads which do not require aesthetic look should be given to another agency.

 

d)     Complaint from some female students:

 

5.16     Mr. Mobin Khalili referred to the complaints from some female students about the mis-use of their contact numbers and photographs provided in the GD.

 

DECISION

 

5.17     The Committee requested the Director H.R. to given an option to the graduating students on whether they would like their photographs, email, contact numbers to be provided in the G.D. or not. 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
