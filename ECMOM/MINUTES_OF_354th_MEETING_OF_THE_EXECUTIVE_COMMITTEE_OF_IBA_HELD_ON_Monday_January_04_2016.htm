The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       Presentation on IBA International Entrepreneurship Summer School.

 

1.1              Dr. Najam Akber Anjum said that they were planning to start IBA International Entrepreneurship Summer School in August 2016. The duration of the program would be 17 days (from August 9 to 25, 2016). He said that the inspiration for this program came from the program held at Summer University 2015 in Thailand which was also attended by IBA students. He reported that IBA students made a survey to know if the participants were interested in coming to Pakistan if an Entrepreneurship Summer School was offered at IBA and the response was very positive. Most of the foreign students wanted to experience the culture of Pakistan as well as study of entrepreneurship at IBA. He said that they have prepared three week plan to offer 3 credit hours course covering 42 lectures.  Two weeks would be for intensive course work and one week would be for company visits, and touring Lahore, Islamabad, and Murree. The cost of the program would be 1000 Euro = Rs.110,500. He said that for those Pakistanis who do not want to go on Pakistan tour will be charged Rs.70,000. However, he said that if there are more sponsors, the charges could be reduced.  He reported that there is a commitment of sponsorship of Rs.500,000 so far. He said that the potential sponsors / partners could be PTDC, Board of Investment, PIA and some industrial partners.

 

1.2              Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that if we want to reach global countries, we have to offer such kind of programs, it could help IBA in becoming among the top 100 business schools, may earn international recognition, and an opportunity to enter into international market.

 

1.3       Dr. Najam said that they would require support from ICT, and administration but the biggest challenge was the security arrangement for the visiting students.    

 

1.4       Syed Jehanzeb asked if faculty cost is included in the budget.  Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that those costs would be covered from sponsorships. The Dean & Director said that the budget should be integrated one; what is all in all cost, and from where these costs would be met.  Dr. Shahid Qureshi said that most of the faculty will deliver lectures voluntarily. Dr. Shahid Qureshi confirmed that faculty would be available for the program. The Dean & Director said that even then their travel, food, and accommodation expenses will have to be paid.  He said that if the proposed cut off point of 40 participants is not met then what would be the contingent plan; will the program be cancelled in that event? Dr. Imran Khan said that if they get 15 to 20 students from outside Pakistan, and the remaining from Pakistan they can run the program otherwise it would be aborted. Mr. Fawad Mahdi suggested that if 50% of the class is from overseas the program can be feasible.

 

1.5       The Registrar said that since the fall session would start from August 17 there could be issues of availability of accommodation at hostel and the faculty to teach. He suggested to prepare a brochure for the program and circulate it among the prospective students. He further suggested to prepare a time line. The Dean & Director said that since it would be executive education, arrangements should be made at the City Campus and the 32 rooms at Aman Tower be blocked for this program.

 

1.6       The Registrar showed his concern about the security arrangement and said that he cannot provide armed guard outside Sindh province because arms licences are issued province-wise. For Lahore and Islamabad armed guards will have to be hired separately.  

 

1.7       To a query from Dr. Sayeed Ghani about the credit hours, it was reported by Dr. Qureshi that they would provide complete course outline for the 3 credit hours course.  Mr. Abdul Wajed said that for ECTS (European Credit Transfer System) they have to do three credit hours mapping and submit to the Academic Board for approval.

 

1.8       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Fawad Mahdi to rework the budget which should be all inclusive.

 

DECISIONS & ACTIONS

 

1.9       The Executive Committee gave conceptual approval for 17 days IBA International Entrepreneurship Summer School to be started from August 9, 2016.

 

1.10     Mr. Fawad Mahdi would rework the budget including all income and expenditures.

 

1.11     Dr. Shahid Qureshi would present the mapping of three credit hours and present it to the Academic Board for approval.

b)                 Deferred Promotion Case of Ms. Shehreena Amin.

 

2.3       Mr. Adnan Hameed reported that the promotion case of Mr. Shehreena Amin was considered in the 340th EC meeting held on June 15, 2015. The EC at that time had decided that the promotion case of Ms. Shehreena Amin would be reconsidered by the EC after six months.

2.6       The Head of HR would present the case of promotion of Ms. Shehreena Amin before the Selection Board for approval.
