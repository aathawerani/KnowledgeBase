Item No. 1      To consider email from Dr. Nasir Touheed regarding Earned  Leave as well as his application for encashment for Earned Leaves.

 

1.1              Dr. Nasir Touheed had applied for encashment of 90 days Earned Leave on the ground of having been given the assignment in the summers of 2007 and 2008 and from May 18, 2009 to August 29, 2009 for his active involvement in the BBA admission related activities in the capacity of Coordinator Testing Department. In his application he had referred to the following para from the Leave Rules:

 

“Teaching staff will not be able to encash their leave or obtain any benefit in place thereof, unless the IBA engages them in some mandatory activity during the semester break”.    

 

1.2              Later, an email from the H.R. Department was sent on January 19, 2010 to all full time faculty and all IBA Staff trying to bring clarity in the clause of leave encashment for teaching staff and to correctly reflect the existing practice added the following in the above quoted para from leave rule:

 

“However, the payment of leave encashment for that particular year in which he / she has been engaged in some mandatory activity during the semester break will be made only at the time of retirement or resignation / separation from service, subject to availability of leave balance.”

1.3              Dr. Nasir Touheed objected to this addition in the existing leave rule saying that it has not been approved by the Board which is the competent authority to make changes in the rules.

 

1.4              The Director H.R. said that it was their interpretation which was based on the past practice as only three faculty members were allowed encashment and that too at the time of retirement / leaving IBA.

 

1.5       The Director said that if any payment is made to the teacher for an assignment / taking classes during the summer then it cannot be a mandatory assignment and in such cases leave encashment will not be allowed.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee decided that the clause pertaining to encashment of earned leave will remain unchanged. The Committee further decided that all those faculty members who have mandatory assignment during the semester break will be entitled to encashment of earned leave as allowed in the existing leave rules.

 

1.7       The Committee requested the Director H.R. to withdraw the email of January 19, 2010 sent to all full time faculty and all IBA staff from her department. 

 

ACTION

 

1.8       The Director H.R. will process the application of Dr. Nasir Touheed for encashment of earned leave in accordance with the approved Leave Rule and would withdraw the email of January 19, 2010.

Item No. 7      Presentation on Process Engineering to Restructure the Program Offices.

 

7.1              Mr. Shahid Zaki, Director EMBA program referring to the EC decision to separate all examination related processes from the Program Offices and the examination related processes to be placed under the Examination Department said that they undertook a Process Re-engineering Study. He presented details of the current operational boundaries of the Program Offices at IBA, and areas of the function heads (Associate Deans, Registrar, Controller of Examinations). He proposed that with the growth in the number of students overtime, there is a need for all functions, programs, and departments, specially those that interface with students, parents, and faculty, to work with increasing efficiency and promptness, with clear accountabilities and well defined terms of reference.

 

7.2              Mr. Shahid Zaki said that he conducted a Process Re-engineering Study to establish clarity regarding (1) back office and front office processes done by the Program Offices, (2) time required to perform tasks and workloads, and (3) optimal manpower needs.  He said that the Evening Program Office at the City Campus was selected for the purpose of detailed study. However, he said that the results incorporate verification from Main Campus, (Day Program Office) and have been audited by Program Coordinator, MBA Evening.  He proposed that the following three Program Offices be maintained at IBA and each Program Office to be headed by a Program Director who will be ultimately be responsible for program delivery with the support of the Program Offices:

 

·         Program Office MBA

·         Program Office BBA

·         Program Office FCS

 

7.3              Mr. Shahid Zaki gave a detailed presentation on the objectives, methodology and results of the Process Re-engineering Study.  He said that the objectives of the study were to:

 

·         Map the existing process

·         Calculate cycle time

·         Determine the capacity required

·         Under occupation / over-occupation if any

·         Study the various tasks

·         Re-organize if required to optimize process time

·         Calculate process time

·         Calculate availability of resources

·         Suggest improvement areas on the basis of study

 

7.4              Mr. Shahid Zaki presented the proposed process flow of Evening Program Office, proposed exam office work flow, capacity requirement of different offices, Program Office Loads, Required head count in program office on basis of peak load, and identified problem areas in program office. He also presented the following recommendations for consideration and approval by the Executive Committee:

 

·         IT equipment like computers, scanners should be provided to enhance work potential.

·         Training programs should be instigated for staff and officers to facilitate them in their routine tasks.

·         Such training should constitute use of MS Outlook, MS Word, Advanced features of MS Excel, Document Versioning, as well as Communication and Writing Skills.

·         The office layout should be restructured to facilitate public dealing in an easy manner with least disturbance for other staff members. A front office for public dealing and back office for other operations is required to enhance the quality of service to students and faculty members.

·         Similarly standard job/query processing times should be estimated for routine tasks with students such as request for transcript etc.

·         Automation and Group Collaboration tools should be introduced to increase throughput.

·         A similar detailed exercise would be carried out to estimate the efficiencies and workloads for all program offices in the future.

 

7.5              The Director said that for examination and grading parallel system will continue until such time it is established that the ERP is fool proof.  He said that when the ERP is fully functional and reliable then they will go back to process re-engineering. He also said that EMBA should not be included with EPO.

 

7.6              The Director said that there may be two models; one is where MBA and EMBA may be taken as one joint program whether it is in the evening or morning.  Similarly at the BBA program office there would be one main office and a subsidiary office. Another model what is already there is that Examination Department has been stripped.  For example any student who wants transcript, his / her request will be passed on to the Examination Department through the front office and the back office will process that request. Front and back offices in that model may have to be placed. He said that the stakeholders have to consult which is the model that they want to adopt for the future and then any decision would be taken.    

 

7.7              The Director said that the Program Office will serve as a front office interacting between the students and the rest of the IBA. He said that the existing work load will remain as it is and the course allocation will be the responsibility of the chairpersons. The Controller of Examinations will be given dedicated staff who would report to the Controller of Examinations.  He said that of the existing staff working simultaneously for the Program Office and the Examination Department, half of the staff will be given to program offices and half will work for the Examination Department. The objective and evaluation of the program office staff will be done by the Director MBA Program.

 

7.8              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is a close interlink between the Program Office and Examination because Program Officer works as examination incharge also.

 

7.9              The Director said that such coordination will continue and all the four Program Coordinators will help during the examinations to the Examination Department.

 

7.10          Dr. Nasir Afghan said that all students’ record would be maintained at the Main Campus so that they may have a consolidated record. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that respective program offices maintain the students’ record.        

 

7.11          The Director said that the existing system of maintenance of students’ record will continue till ERP is implemented.

 

7.12          The Director said that there should be a uniform designation such as Director instead of Coordinator.

 

DECISION

 

7.13          The Committee noted and approved the recommendations made by Mr. Shahid Zaki in 7.4 above along with the observations made above.

 

ACTION

 

7.14          Mr. Shahid Zaki will prepare an implementation plan to be effective from Fall 2010 in consultation with the stakeholders i.e. Program Directors, Program Coordinators, Director H.R, Controller of Examinations.

 

7.15          Mr. Shahid Zaki will provide physical lay out to the Director Projects after discussing with the Program Directors and the Controller of Examinations.

 

 

Item No. 8      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Legal standing of MoU

 

8.1              The Director Finance taking advantage of the presence of Barrister Ali Mumtaz Shaikh of M/s. Mumtaz & Associates requested him to let the Executive Committee know the legal standing of MoU.

 

8.2              Barrister Shaikh explained that MoU is a basic understanding; it is not binding on the party other than understanding. The whole purpose of MoU is that preliminary investigations and research will be done and if the right results are obtained then a contract will be made. If one is satisfied with those understandings then one may go for a proper agreement.

 

8.3              The Director ICT wanted to know if the MoU becomes part of the agreement will it then be binding or not.

8.4              Barrister Shaikh said that if those understandings become part of the agreement then those will be legally binding. He said that MoU itself has no legal standing.
