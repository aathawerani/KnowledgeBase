Item No.3       To consider and approve the revised cost estimates of Electrical Infrastructure for the Boys' Hostel, Girls Hostel and Staff Colony.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects said that he has invited Mr. Khalid Pervez, Electrical Consultant of KP Associates to present to the EC the revised cost estimate for electrical power infrastructure development of IBA Boys Hostel, Girls Hostel, and Staff Colony in light of the guidelines given by the EC in its meeting held on April 2, 2012.   

 

3.2       Mr. Khalid Pervez presented the following technical and cost summary of IBA Boys Hostel, Girls Hostel, and Staff Colony.  He informed the Committee that the revised estimate incorporates use of existing cables which are laid underground and would be excavated out and the cost of excavation is also included. He further said that the estimate also includes cost of civil construction of the Electrical Building which would house transformer, main LV switchboard, 400 kVA generating set, a store and a roof for staff:

         

 

3.3       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there were some major equipments to be procured for which advance payment has to be made.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director said that we do not have any policy of up-front payment.

 

3.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmad said that mobilization advance upto 15% can be given, and 70% payment as secured advance, can be made on receipt of equipment in IBA premises and custody.

 

3.6       The Director Finance added that mobilization advance can be made against a bank guarantee.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee approved the cost estimate of Rs.36.25 million for the electrical power infrastructure development of IBA Boys Hostel, Girls Hostel, and Staff Colony

 

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve draft IBA rules regarding house rental ceiling allowance.

 

4.1       The Director H.R. said that in 2011 the Grievance Committee had recommended some benefits to the Option A employees.  House rental ceiling allowance was one of them. She said that the BoG had approved the introduction of House Rental Ceiling Allowance for those Option A employees of IBA who do not opt for the proposed Uniform IBA Scale and advised to develop a policy in this regard. However, she said that since majority of the employees have now opted the BPS scale, the proposed policy will be applicable to all full time employees of IBA working on BPS Pay Scale and meeting the conditions outlined in the policy and fulfilling the requirements for applying for this allowance. 

 

4.2       Mr. Adnan Hameed presented the scope of the policy, its eligibility; entitlements of residence categories & rental ceiling allowance; responsibilities of employee, H.R. Department, and Finance Department; Procedure for Applications Submissions and Review; method of payment; and General Guidelines. He also presented the financial impact on the implementation of this policy.

 

4.3       The Dean & Director said that those who have taken loan against Provident Fund for construction/repair and maintenance of their houses will not be eligible for the house rental ceiling allowance as they own their own houses.  He asked the Director H.R. to work out the financial impact by excluding those persons who have availed loan against Provident Fund for construction / repair and maintenance of their house.

 

4.4       The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to outsource the positions such as security guard, gardener, janitorial staff etc. as and when they are vacated due to retirement or their leaving the job.

 

4.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that to implement the policy of House Rental Ceiling Allowance, the control mechanism has to be very strict to check the false claim.   

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee recommended the House Rental Ceiling Allowance Policy to the A&FC for approval.

 

ACTION

 

4.7       The Director H.R. would present the House Rental Ceiling Allowance Policy to the A&FC by incorporating the suggestions noted in 4.3 above.

 

 

Item No.5       Approval for Services of Rental Generators at City Campus to facilitate transition of utilities and services from old KESC substation to new, and synchronization of electric supplies from newly established generator farm at City Campus. 

                       

5.1       The Registrar said that he has received a request from Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance for acquiring two diesel generators on rent; one of 500 kVA and another of 100 kVA for around two months on urgent basis so that the shifting of KESC sub-station, and synchronization of generators at City Campus can be completed without any power interruption.   

 

5.2       Mr. Mushtaque said that shifting of KESC sub-station would take 3 weeks time and then synchronization of generators which is a very slow moving process, would take another 4 weeks.  He said that he had discussed this with Mr. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer who has also agreed that one generator of 500 kVA is required so that during the unavailability or prime (KESC) source and back up (generators) during the relocation of the KESC sub-station and DG sets for synchronization, the existing system may be operated without affecting the end users.

 

5.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that Siemens, who are working on this project, had verbally said that the project would be completed by 10th of June but he said that it would take time. 

 

5.4       The Dean & Director said that shifting of generators should not take more than one week.  He said that KESC would be requested not to do load shedding during the shifting of generator.  He further said that he has been given assurance by KESC that there would be no loading shedding for IBA.

 

5.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that no load shedding for IBA would be possible when we have a separate feeder. Currently it may not be possible.

 

5.6       The Registrar said that if we get KESC supply by the end of June, 2012 then we can defer the shifting of generators.

 

DECISION

 

5.7       The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Mr. Mohammad Jawad Khan, and Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed to discuss this with KESC and let the EC know what to do.
