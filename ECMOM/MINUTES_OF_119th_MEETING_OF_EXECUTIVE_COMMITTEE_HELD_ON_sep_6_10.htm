Item No. 2      To consider suggestions from Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair, Assistant Professor, for logistical improvement at IBA.

 

2.1       Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair said that as stated by the Director in FCS Faculty meeting that he would like to have suggestions about international best practices regarding processes at IBA, he has suggested a few logistical changes which are aimed at providing more facilities and more access to faculty and researchers at IBA. Before suggesting changes, he illustrated the following small logistic issues:


ISSUES:

 

·         Clean drinking water.

·         Absence of coffee / tea machines.

·         Printing of documents at Ms. Fatima's office.

·         Requisition for stationery.

 

2.2       Dr. Hisham explained all the issues in detail and also proposed the following solutions to those issues:


The first step is to install "hierarchical locks" on all doors on the faculty floors. This will allow each floor member to access different rooms according to their access-privilege and still carry a single key. Allow a large office to Ms. Fatima with a store of necessities on shelves from where they can be picked up by members who need them.  All members should be able to access Fatima's office, and vice versa. This will eliminate the unnecessary delays in the procurement of stationery items. Designate another large room as pantry-cum-reprography and endow this room with two gray-scale printers, a color-printer, a Xerox copier, a water-boiler (for tea / coffee) or a coffee-machine (which also dispenses tea), a fridge, a microwave, a clean water dispenser, and a basin with a hot/cold tap. All floor-members should be able to access this room with their own keys. They can send off their print-jobs to a printer in this room and can fetch it from there.  They can store their edibles in the fridge, use the microwave and the coffee-machine etc at their convenience, at the time best suited to them. Off office-hour access exclusion will then no more be a problem either for stationery, for printing or simply for having a drink. He said that this is the prevalent access model at the Delft Univ. of Technology, and is much simpler than the prevalent system at IBA.

 

2.3       The Director appreciating the suggestions made by Dr. Hisham suggested that there should be two resource centres for the faculty; one at the Main Campus and another at the City Campus.

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that out of four rooms allocated for the Program Office, one room may be allocated for Resource Centre.

 

2.5       Dr. Hisham suggested that one security panel should be fixed and the faculty should be given access code.

 

2.6       The Director ICT said that access code can be given through RFID Card for which it is necessary that everyone has the RFID Card.

 

2.7       The Registrar said that they should resolve the problem in the existing situation as they are constrained of space. He said that they could place racks in Ms. Ghulam Fatima’s room and on that racks they can stack stationery items. Similar arrangement would also be made at the City Campus. He requested the Director ICT to provide a new printer because the printer currently installed at Gulam Fatima’s office is irreparable. He said that arrangements for tea and coffee making machine will be made at faculty lounge and one clean water dispenser would also be arranged for both campuses. He said that when the RFID Card is available to everyone then they may reconsider how to resolve those issues.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Registrar and requested him to implement it as soon as possible. The Committee also thanked Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair for the suggestion and requested him to keep on sending good suggestions / feedback.
