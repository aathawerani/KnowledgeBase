MINUTES OF 268TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 7, 2013

The 268th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 7, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                    Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                          Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                       Registrar

4.      Ayesha A. Menai                                                           Director H.R.

5.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                          Acting Head of ICT

 

The following attended the meeting as nominees of (1) Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz, and (2) Mr. Moeid Sultan who could not attend the meeting due to illness/participation in Strategy 2020 meeting:

 

1)      Abdul Rahman, Senior Manager Contracts.

3)      Mr. Adnan Hameed, Manager H.R. on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the furniture contractor for supply and fixing of furniture at Library Building, Main Campus.

 

1.1       Mr. Abdul Rahman stated that invitation of bids was sent to the following pre-qualified contractor/Firms:

 

1.      M/s SIMAB (Pvt.) Ltd.

M/s Workman Furniture did not collect the bid documents. A pre-bid meeting was held on September 24, 2013 in order to clarify the queries of the bidders. The bids were opened on September 28, 2013 in front of bidders’ representative, IBA officials and Consultant’s representatives. 1.5       After comparison of rates with the engineering estimate the consultant found M/s Sheetal as lowest evaluated bidder. The Engineering Estimate for the said work is Rs.21,826,350. The 1st lowest responsive bidder M/s Sheetal is lower than Engineering Estimate and the second lowest responsive bidder M/s Habib Sons is 37% higher than Engineering Estimate. M/s ICON recommended the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Sheetal at a cost of Rs.21,201,352/-. 

 

1.6       Based on the recommendations of consultant Ms/ ICON, Projects Department, and CPC  in its meeting held on October 5, 2013, Mr. Abdul Rahman requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Sheetal at a cost of Rs.21,201,352/-.

 

1.7       The Registrar observed that unlike Habib Sons, and Simab who have worked satisfactorily for IBA,  Sheetal is new for IBA. He said that if we are to award the contract to Sheetal we should visit their workshop in order to be satisfied with their quality of work and on time delivery of orders. Mr. Abdul Rehman replied that our Architects and our Engineer Mr. Mahmood Ali already visited their workshop at the time of pre-qualification. The Registrar wanted to see the profile of M/s. Sheetal. Dr. Sayeed Ghani observed that because of insufficient earnest money, we are rejecting other lowest bidders which would cost IBA additional around Rs.3 to Rs.4 million (Versant Interior quoted Rs.17 million and Simab quoted Rs.18.4 million).  He wanted to know if there is any provision in SPPRA through which the lowest bidders rejected due to insufficient earnest money can also be considered.  He also wanted to know if we can retender. Engr. Abdul Rehman replied that a grace of 10% of earnest money is allowed but the submitted bid securities were less than the allowed limit. Mr. Jahanzeb also confirmed that SPPRA allows up to 10% variation in earnest money which can be deposited within 24 hours but in these two cases the shortage is more than 10%. He said that re-tender can be done. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that retendering will take time which may further delay the completion of the project. The Registrar was of the view that it would take another 3 to 4 months when the furniture would be required to be fixed at the Library. The Registrar wanted to know if Sheetal has worked for and supplied furniture for any library. Mr. Abdul Rahman replied that none of the prequalified firms have worked for the library. Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked if they can give voluntary discount of around Rs.3 million without compromising on the quality. Mr. Abdul Rahman said that SPPRA does not allow negotiations but he said that he will discuss with the Director Projects and would see if they can give any discount. The Director H.R. said that if Sheetal has provided furniture at CBM then CBM can be visited to assess the quality of their work.

 

DECISION

 

1.8       The Committee authorized the Registrar to see the profile of Sheetal, and if he is satisfied he would approve the award of contract to Sheetal and report to the EC.

 

ACTION

 

1.9       Mr. Abdul Rahman would provide profile of M/s. Sheetal to the Registrar and talk about discount.

2.7       The Committee also approved additional four gardeners who would take care of the horticulture at Sports Ground, Aman-CED, Tabba Building, Students Centre, and Prayer Hall. 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a) Migration of email exchange server to Office 365.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that the EC in its 272nd meeting held on August 30, 2013 had in principle approved the migration of email exchange server to Office 365 and had instructed to involve Computer Science faculty in the test run and obtain their feedback. Mr. Batada reported that the feedback received is very positive. He said that he needed permission for starting the migration process to Office 365.

 

3.2       Mr. Imran Batada further said that Office 365 does not provide blackberry services for Pakistan and Asia. He said that EC members blackberry sets will have to be replaced otherwise they will not be able to get the services free of cost. He said that EC members can be provided Smart phone which is better than blackberry.  He said that it would cost around Rs.25,000/- per set. He said that due to migration to Office 365 there would be a saving of around Rs.20 million and the email storage space which is currently 5 to 8 GB would also be enhanced to 25 GB. It was pointed out that besides EC members, other IBA officials have also been provided blackberry. The Registrar suggested to get a list of persons who have been provided blackberry sets.    

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee approved the migration process to Office 365.

 

3.4       The Committee in principle agreed to the replacement of blackberry set with Smart Phone. However, Mr. Batada would seek approval of the Dean & Director on the financial implications on replacement of blackberry with Smart phone.

 

 

b) Mock-up of the ring road within the Main Campus premises.

 

3.5       The Committee witnessed the mock up of the ring road to be built within the Main Campus premises. It was noted that the existing canteen will not be demolished because of the ring road.  The Registrar observed that there should not be an island in between the roads.  Mr. Abdul Rahman would take care of the observation of the Registrar. 

 

c) Clarification on conveying EC decisions / issuing notices to faculty.

 

3.5       The Registrar said that the Secretary Executive Committee has been given the responsibility of conveying to faculty members EC decision regarding late submission of results. He said that ideally it should be communicated through the office of Faculty Coordinator or Associate Deans. The Director H.R. said that all faculty related decisions / announcements should go through Associate Dean’s office. The Associate Deans agreed and said that they have been doing so.   

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
