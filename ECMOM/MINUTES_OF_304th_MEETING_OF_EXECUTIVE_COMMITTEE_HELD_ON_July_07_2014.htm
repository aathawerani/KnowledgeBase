Item No. 2      To consider and approve the proposed revision in Eid Advance Limit.  

2.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed stated that the Manager Internal Audit has suggested to increase the limit of Eid Advance. The EID advance loan is provided to staff in the month of Ramadan which is deducted from the salary in six equal monthly instalments. As per practice and since a very long time the limit for the advance to contract employees is Rs.25,000/- The status of contract employees has been converted in the IBA pay scale. He had suggested that an appropriate revision should be approved (around Rs.40,000); or the employees in IBA pay scales may be given the EID advance as provided to BPS option B/A employees.

2.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan referred to the approved Financial Policies and Procedures according to which employees shall be entitled to have one salary as advance during the year on Eid-ul-Fitr/Eid-ul-Azha / Christmas/Easter / Holi/Devali and shall be recovered in 6 monthly instalments.  The Dean & Director said that the advance should be given upto BPS-16 or equivalent grade employees as it is for the low paid employees.  He suggested to give the same limit of advance to IBA scale employees as are given to BPS employees.  He clarified that the Projects Department employees are contractual employees; they are here for a limited time period. As soon as the project is completed, the positions would be abolished.  He said that the Projects Department staff of upto equivalent grade 16 can be given advance provided the advanced loan is recovered in six months period or the end of contract whichever is earlier.

DECISION

2.3       The Committee decided that employees upto BPS-16 or equivalent grade will be given an advance of one gross salary to be recovered in six monthly instalments.

2.4       The Committee further decided that contractual employees (Projects Department) of upto equivalent grade 16 will also be entitled to the loan of one gross salary to be recovered in six monthly instalments or earlier in case their contractual period of service is less than six months.       

            (Later at the request of the Director Finance and the facts and figures presented by him, the Dean & Director very kindly allowed for those BPS-17 and above or equivalent grade employees who wish to avail of the advance to smooth their cash flow situation.)

Item No.4       To consider and approve the Time Extension of External Development and     Infrastructure Works at Main Campus.

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the contract for External Development and Infrastructure Works at Main Campus was awarded to M/s Venture Crest at a cost of Rs.84,753,139/- The project started in January 1, 2013 and was to be completed in 18 months by June 20, 2014 but it was delayed. Due to slow progress of contractor, different options were discussed with contractor in order to expedite the work after approval of Executive committee such as:

- Termination of contract
- Completion of work from other contractor on risk and cost of contractor
- Extension of contract upon revision of guarantees and warrantees.

4.2       The contractor was given the above three options. The contractor agreed that they will complete the work in three months from July to September 30, 2014. The contractor was asked to provide the undertaking for the completion of work in said duration which they did.  The Project Manager M/s NESAPK submitted their recommendations for extension of time on their request.

4.3       Based on the recommendations of Project Manager and request of contractor, the IBA Projects Department recommended the extension of time for the subject work up-till September 30, 2014 to Procurement Committee for approval.  In its meeting held on 5th June 2014, the Procurement Committee approved the extension based on the merit of the process. The Committee commented that no escalation should be admissible to the contractor during the extended period.

4.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the extension in the contract period till 30th September 2014.

4.5       The Dean & Director asked as to what would be the recourse if the contractor does not complete the project in extended three months time. The Director Projects said that there would be a penalty – which would be deducted from their payment.

4.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan asked as to what will be the financial impact. The Director Projects said that Nespak would charge 80% of their fee not 100%.  The Dean & Director said that if the construction is slowed down, the supervision of Project Manager is correspondingly reduced, then why should we pay them. The Director Projects said that the staff of Project Manager remain there and they do not remove them from the site. He said that they pay only the supervision fee not the whole fee.

DECISION

4.7       The Committee approved the extension till 30th September 2014 in the External Development and Infrastructure Works at Main Campus awarded to Venture Crest. 

4.8       The Director Projects would report the financial implications in the next EC meeting.
