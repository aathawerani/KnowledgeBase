The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve the additional fee for re-designing of two buildings                        of NBCL.

 

1.1       The Director Projects stated that because of the cancellation of bids of clinic the architects were asked by IBA to reduce the size of the building due to budget constraints. Similarly, the Transport facilities building had to be re-designed to change its Architectural features and the size also to cut the cost.  He reported that the Architects are doing the job and have demanded extra fee for re doing the job. On Clinic they had completed three stages of work and for re-doing that work they have demanded a fee of Rs.250,000/-  On the Transport building they have demanded a fee of Rs.125,000/-

           

1.2       The Director Projects said that according to Projects department calculations the Architects are entitled for Rs.192,520/- for re-designing the clinic and Rs.40,000/- for re-designing Transport Building.  He presented the basis of calculation.
 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that after lengthy negotiations M/s NBCL have finally agreed to accept the reduced fees as calculated by IBA and requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the following additional fee to NBCL for re- designing and re-doing the work:

a)      Clinic.                                Rs.192,520/-
b)      Transport Building                Rs.  40,000/-

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee approve the payment of additional fee of Rs.192,500/- for re-designing Clinic, and Rs.40,000/- for redesigning Transport Building to NBCL.

 

Item No.2       To consider request from M/s. Consult-Tech International to review the decision of Claim Settlement Committee.

 

2.1       The Director Projects stated that the proposal to reconstitute the Claim Settlement  Committee was presented in its 320th meeting of Executive Committee held on Monday, November 17, 2014. The Executive Committee then decided that since the recommendations of the Claim Settlement Committee were considered and approved by the EC, the Consult-Tech may meet the EC.  The Committee did not agree to the proposal of the Director Projects of re-constituting the Claim Settlement Committee for this purpose. Accordingly, the Director Projects said that he has invited Mr. Kamran Ahmad, Managing Partner, Consult-Tech International to meet the Executive Committee and present his case as he had not accepted the decision of the Claim Settlement Committee and refused further negotiations.

 

2.2       Mr. Kamran Ahmed said that there were three projects; (a) new Boys Hostel, (b) new Girls Hostel, and (c) Visiting Faculty Hostel.  The contracts for all the three projects were separately signed with common full-time staffing in site and key technical staff support from office. Each of the project man-month shared 1/3rd of the staffing cost at each of the sites. The cost of the Resident Engineer, site engineer/quantity surveyor and other technical key staff was 1/3rd recovered from each of the projects. He said that it was also incorporated in the contracts that the project might start one after the another though each of the projects time line was bounded by specific period.   

 

2.3       The Director Projects observed that on the basis of the argument made by Mr. Kamran Ahmed, they are in fact demanding 9% fee. He clarified that the agreed fee was on percentage basis; not on man-month basis.  He said that the costing and allocation of salary is their (CTI’s) internal matter.  He further said that although CTI had asked for extension, the Director HEC Projects had not approved any extension and even then IBA processed their case and came to the fair conclusion.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director said that they at IBA are not interested how CTI spreads the cost; there is an agreement on 3% of fee for each project awarded to CTI. He said that if they have taken the contract at 3% of fee, it means it is fully loaded; it is not that they are taking 1/3rd for payment of salary from each of the three projects.  He said that cost allocation was their (CTI’s) internal affair.     

 

DECISION

 

2.5       As Mr. Kamran Ahmed was neither convinced nor he could convince the EC members on his view point, the discussion and representation remained inconclusive and the decision taken earlier by the EC on this issue in its 312th meeting held on September 8, 2014 remained unchanged. 

 

Item No.3       Reconsideration of the proposal for “Standardization of Tissue Papers and     Mosquito Spray”.

 

3.1       The Manager Purchase in his email stated that he had apprised the decision of the Executive Committee regarding the standardization of insect killer spray and tissue papers to the Dean & Director who advised him to resubmit the case in the EC.  The proposal was earlier discussed in 319th meeting of EC wherein this item was deferred for the time being and decided to continue the existing practice.

 

3.2       The proposal from the Manager Purchase was that in order to manage inventory cost & supply and contain the value of money, quality & delivery and to curtail undue commissions and taxes of distributors, stockiest & suppliers, instead of procuring through tenders or RFQ, they had approached the manufacturers of renowned brand of tissue products & fly insect killer (FIK) i.e., M/s Packages Ltd., producer of Rose Petal brand of tissue papers; M/s SC Johnsons through their Authorized Distributor i.e., M/s NPD Karachi, the producer of Baygon FIK and M/s Reckitt & Benckiser, the producer of Mortein FIK respectively.

 

3.3       The Manager Purchase had reported that the producers/manufacturers of the respective products had agreed to supply the required items on ex-factory price and allowed further discount, albeit the quantity required at IBA for the year was much less than their minimum supply quantity but they have been convinced to share for propagation of quality & merit based education in Pakistan. 3.5       Mr. Syed Jehanzeb said that usually large suppliers do not participate in small tenders because of complications. Middlemen participate in such tenders but they charge 50 to 80% more.  He said that the Procurement Committee had suggested to standardize some items and if they are proprietary items then direct contracting can be done. However, he said that the tissue paper and the mosquito spray are small items and there is less cost saving on these items.  He further said that there was no basis for standardization and direct purchase. 

 

3.6       The Registrar agreed with Mr. Jehanzeb and added that tissue paper and mosquito spray are not purchased in large quantity hence there may not be much saving.  He said that these items may be purchased on RFQ basis.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       In view of the explanations given by the Registrar and Mr. Syed Jehanzeb, the Committee decided to continue to purchase such small items through RFQ.

 

 

Item No.4       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Umrah Scheme.

             

4.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that under the approved Umrah Scheme for full time employees of IBA, there is a flexibility for female employees (who have a requirement for travelling with a “Mehram”), that they will be required to bear expenses of their “Mehram” on their own.  However, for the purpose of facilitating the female employees, and reducing the financial burden of Mehram’s travelling, they will be allowed to select an economical package.  In such cases the amount set for the standard Umrah package will be paid by IBA directly to the travel agent and the difference will be paid by the female employee.

 

4.2       Mr. Adnan Hameed proposed that the same facility be allowed to male employees if they want to take their wife, mother, or sister to perform Umrah. He clarified that there would be no additional financial liability on IBA.

   

DECISION

           

4.3       The Committee approved the proposal of Mr. Adnan Hameed.


b)     Satisfaction over the progress related to Convocation arrangements.

 

4.4       The Dean & Director expressed satisfaction over the progress on convocation related arrangements and thanked the Registrar, the Director Projects and their team for their efforts.  

 

 

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
