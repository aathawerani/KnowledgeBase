MINUTES OF THE 131ST MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 6, 2010

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

6.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

7.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                                    Director CED

8.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                               Director H.R.

 

Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the proposal from Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair, Assistant Professor FCS, regarding Building up a Scientific Computing Infrastructure at IBA.

 

1.1       Dr. Hisham Bin Zubair, Assistant Professor, reported that his research requires a computing machine but currently at IBA there is no hardware infrastructure to cater for the requirements of computational problems. He proposed at this initial stage the acquisition of just one standard computational machine in order to facilitate research and development of computational science at IBA. The proposed machine is an Intel Xeon computer with around 12 MB cache, 64 GB RAM, and an 8-core processor.

 

1.2       Dr. Hisham said that he in collaboration with research partners in Antwerp, Belgium, is conducting a research for optimizing shifts for the multigrid Helmholtz preconditioner.  So far he has used Belgian resources over a static IP for this purpose, however, this process is often too slow to be useful for calibrating performance of different computational experiments.  He requested that acquisition of one good computational machine be promptly carried out at IBA to facilitate research in this avenue. He said that roughly the initial cost for this set-up would be around Rs.1.5 million.

 

1.3       The Registrar wanted to know if it would be a one time cost or would require frequent technical up-grading.

 

1.4       Dr. Hisham clarified that it would be useful for 3 years after that some technical up-grading would be required.

 

1.5       The Director Projects wanted to know as to what would be the benefit to IBA by acquiring this machine.

 

1.6       Dr. Hisham said that it would not be used directly in teaching at IBA but would be helpful in supervising Ph.d students of Space & Planetary Astrophysics.

 

1.7       The Director Finance wanted to know whether the existing facilities and the facilities in the pipeline would meet the requirements. He also wanted to know if the required machine would be beneficial for other researchers at IBA.

 

1.8       Dr. Hisham said that the existing facilities would not meet the requirements. 

 

1.9       The Director ICT suggested to request for funding to HEC through PC-I.  He also suggested to combine his research with that being done by Dr. Wasim A. Khan.

 

1.10     The Registrar requested Dr. Hisham to give a brief on this project for using it into promotional material.

 

DECISION

 

1.11     The Committee in principle approved the proposal for acquiring Intel Xeon Computer and suggested to write to HEC for funding.

 

ACTION

 

1.12     Dr. Hisham would prepare PC-1 for the required computer and send it to the HEC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider requests for make up examinations from the following students:

 

a)      Mr. Suhaib Jalis Ahmad, student of BBA-7

b)     Ms. Sehrish Yunus, student of BBA-III

c)      Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem of BBA-V

 

Mr. Suhaib Jalis Ahmad of BBA-7

 

2.1       Ms. Nida Farooq, student of BBA-7 on behalf of Mr. Suhaib Jalis Ahmad of BBA-7 reported that the mother of Mr. Suhaib Jalis Ahmad passed away on November 30, 2010, therefore he was unable to appear in the paper of Managerial Accounting scheduled for December 2, and requested to shift it to December 9, 2010.  She stated in the application that Mr. Suhaib would take all other exams as per schedule.

 

2.2       The Controller of Examinations, giving his remarks on the application stated that date of Managerial Accounting exam cannot be changed. However, the Executive Committee may consider allowing make-up exam keeping in view personal tragedy of the student.

 

Ms. Sehrish Yunus of BBA-III

 

2.3       Ms. Rukhsana Yunus, mother of Ms. Sehrish Yunus of BBA-III in her application addressed to the Controller of Examinations stated that Ms. Sehrish had Gastro Intestinal infection on December 3 and was taken to the Hospital. Consequently, she could not take the paper of Accounting on December 3. She attached Medical Certificate from Ashfaq Memorial Hospital and requested for make-up exam for the course.

 

2.4       The Controller of Examinations forwarded the application of Ms. Rukhsana Yunus to the Executive Committee to consider approving make-up exam of the 2nd term paper that Ms. Sehrish Yunus missed due to hospitalization.

 

Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem of BBA-V

 

2.5       Ms. Shazmah Nadeem, mother of Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem of BBA-V reported that Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem had very high fever and severe chest infection due to which she was having difficulty in breathing. She was on a wheel chair and was diagnosed with limb girdle muscular dystrophy.  She stated that due to her disease and the muscle weakness in her entire body, it was extremely risky for her to over exert herself. She requested to defer the exams of Ms. Mahnoor and take the exams when she gets better.  She stated that she would be missing the following papers:

 

o   Statistical Inference    taught by Mr. Yaseen Menai

o   Development Economics taught by Dr. Mahnaz Fatima

o   Principle of Microeconomics taught by Mr. Danish Pirzada

o   Production and Operation Management taught by Dr. Rameez Khalid

o   Small Business Management taught by Mr. Sultan Raza

 

2.6       The Controller of Examinations in his email commented that the health condition of Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem as described by her mother appeared to be serious which made a student eligible for make-up exam as per IBA Policy subject to approval of the Executive Committee. He further suggested that the lady may be requested to furnish medical documents for consideration in the next EC meeting.

 

2.7       The Registrar reported that the required medical documents have been received.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee allowed make-up examinations to all the three students named above.  

  

            ACTION

 

2.9       The Controller of Examinations would arrange make up exams for the courses missed by the three students namely; Mr. Suhaib Jalis Ahmad, student of BBA-7, Ms. Sehrish Yunus, student of BBA-III and Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem of BBA-V. 

           

2.10     The Program Office would convey the above decision to the concerned students.

 

            There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
