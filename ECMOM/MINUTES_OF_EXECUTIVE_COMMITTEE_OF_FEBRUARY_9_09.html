MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON FEBRUARY 9, 2009 (THIRTY FOURTH MEETING)

The 34th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, February 9, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  6. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  7. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                           Director I.T.
  8. Ms. Ayesha Menai                              Director H.R.
  9. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                               Director CED

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Report on visit to LUMS by Dr. Sayeed Ghani, Dr. Muhammad Nishat, Dr. Arshad Siddiqi and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

1.1       In the light of report regarding academics at LUMS presented by Dr. Sayeed Ghani and others, the Committee considered the following:

a)      The BBA VI and MBA-III semester students should be allowed an option to opt for courses which will determine the area of specialization. All BBA and MBA students of both campuses as well as BBA(MIS) students will be given degrees of BBA and MBA respectively. Those graduating with BBA(MIS) or MBA(MIS) will have this mentioned in their transcript as well as in their CVs. 

b)      Computer literacy course be offered for all students on the basis of tests on similar pattern as remedial courses for English and Mathematics. On the basis of similar tests at the time of entry, the level of students’ knowledge of computer and economics will also be assessed to give them exemption from basic courses.

c)   Because of the “A’ level students having their examinations till end of June, the starting time of Summer, Fall and Spring semesters will move by one month i.e. the Fall Semester will start in September instead of August.

d)   The Academic Committee will explore the possibility of a common testing for IBA and LUMS. This year the previous admission process of test, interviews, group discussions will continue. However, the test will be redesigned with the objective of enhancing its quality.

e)   Dr. Sayeed Ghani informed the Committee that teaching load of faculty members at LUMS was four courses per year along with research and case studies. The Academic Committee of IBA was requested to look into the proportionate course work reduction for faculty interested in research.  

f)   Students hand book related to students conduct was considered a useful material to be given to the students.

1.2       Dr. Arshad Siddiqi who had an interaction with the I.T. Director of LUMS suggested the following:

a)      As the LUMS had not involved the ultimate users during the implementation of the ERP, they had faced some difficulties.  The IBA administration felt quite relieved that the IBA Implementation Committee and ERP managers and the involvement of ultimate users along with the computer systems such as CMS, FMS are already available therefore, implementation should not be a big issue.

b)   Dr. Arshad Siddiqi also mentioned that LUMS has 14 to 15 servers and several back up servers and therefore to be able to perform better, IBA needs to have much larger space as well as more servers of greater capacity. 

c)      The Director referred to the pledge made by Mr. Arif Naqvi of Abraaj Capital regarding IT equipment and servers. 

d)     Talking of the Video Conference facility the Director proposed that intra campus and inter-university level use of this facility should be our goal.  For this he asked the Director I.T. to add the video conference facility at the Main Campus also.

DECISION

1.3              The Committee decided to adopt the suggestions given above.

ACTION

1.4              The Academic Committee, Associate Deans and the Director I.T. will ensure implementation of the above suggestions.

Item No. 3      To consider note from Director I.T. regarding pre-qualification of vendors for construction of Data Center.

3.1       The Director I.T. stated that in response to the advertisement for pre-qualification of contractors for construction of Data Center and allied works including civil, electrical, data HVAC and fire prevention systems, 30 vendors participated in the pre-qualification.  The minimum criteria for eligibility as published in the newspapers were:

a)      Have completed at lest three similar projects during last five years costing minimum    Rs.1 million each.

b)       Must be registered with Income Tax and Sales Tax departments.

        c)    Should not be in litigation or blacklisted by any client, government, semi-government department or autonomous body.

3.2       The Director I.T. said that after evaluating each and every application they have short-listed the following four companies and requested the Executive Committee to approve the pre-qualification of the four vendors:

i)          M/s. Siemens (Pakistan)

ii)         M/s. Inbox Business Technology (Pvt.) Limited

iii)        M/s. Mushko Electronics (Pvt.) Limited

iv)        M/s. IBM Italia S.p.a.

3.3       The Director wanted to know the reason for Mushko’s obtaining third highest point in the revised list whereas in the previous evaluation they had been disqualified.

3.4       The Director I.T. explained that against the previous evaluation, the revised evaluation included 10 points for clientele list and 10 points for previous working relations with IBA. He said that this was done to make the evaluation more objective. On this basis Mushko got additional marks and therefore it had been included in the list of recommended companies.  

3.5       The Registrar pointed out that because of the sophistication level of I.T. equipment it may become difficult for one or two of the four companies to meet the specifications and therefore for the sake of safety and from audit point of view we should increase the number of companies to more than four.  He also suggested that to increase the number of companies obtaining more than 85% should be included in the pre-qualified list.  The Committee did not object to this suggestion.

DECISION

3.6       The Committee approved the pre-qualification of the four vendors listed in 3.2 above.

Item No. 5      Suggestions for Medical Treatment.

5.1       The Director Finance presented a proposal for emergency treatment for students. He suggested that in case a student is admitted in the Hospital in emergency, the expenses will be borne by the IBA for treatment only.  The student will pay all expenses relating to in-patient treatment (hospitalization). He informed the Committee that OMI Hospital will offer 10% discount to IBA on emergency treatment / tests only and would require security deposit of Rs.100,000/-

5.2       The Director said that IBA should have good quality first aid boxes at both the campuses for emergency befalling in students, faculty or staff.  He said that the IBA may sign an agreement with OMI for the City Campus personnel and with Patel Hospital for the Main Campus and hostel students and personnel.

DECISION

5.3       The Committee agreed that the IBA should sign an agreement with OMI Hospital for any emergency treatment of personnel / students from the City Campus. The Committee requested the Director Finance to negotiate with Patel Hospital for emergency treatment.

5.4       The Registrar will arrange good quality first aid boxes at both the campuses.

Item No. 6      Medical Insurance for Students – Takaful Pakistan Limited.

6.1       The Director Finance presented a proposal of medical insurance for students given by Takaful Pakistan Limited.

6.2       The Registrar mentioned that in the past medical insurance was arranged for IBA employees but for relatively poor medical services given to the employees the IBA had to pay a higher cost.

6.3       The Director suggested that in the light of the decision of the Board of Governors of IBA, the IBA should explore the possibility of obtaining health insurance.

Item No. 7      Fixed Asset Register – outsourcing to Consultant.

7.1       In compliance with the decision of A&FC, a proposal from the Director Finance regarding maintaining Fixed Assets Register was presented by the Director Finance along with necessary categories of assets, purpose to develop fixed assets register, responsibility of IBA assets, scope of work and the quotations received from consultants.  He also presented the rates quoted by three consultants out of which M/s. Anjum Asim Shahid Rahman had quoted the lowest rate i.e. Rs.500,000/- to complete the work in 11 weeks time. He recommended to outsource the assignment of development of fixed assets register to M/s. Anjum Asim Shahid at a quoted fee of Rs.500,000/-

DECISION

7.2       The Committee approved the recommendation of the Director Finance to outsource the assignment of development of fixed assets register to M/s. Anjum Asim Shahid at a quoted fee of Rs.500,000/-

7.3       The Committee also decided that a bar code for each asset will be developed along with a non-computer readable number.

ACTION

7.4       The Director Finance will issue the work order to M/s. Anjum Asim Shahid for development of fixed assets register.

Item No. 8      Recovery of fee from Mr. Azam Sardar, ex-IT Manager.

8.1       The Director Finance wanted to know whether the IBA should recover fees for EMBA from Mr. Azam Sardar, ex I.T Manager who had resigned and left the IBA and is also not continuing his MBA at IBA.

DECISION

8.2      The Committee did not approve the recovery of fee from Mr. Azam Sardar.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
