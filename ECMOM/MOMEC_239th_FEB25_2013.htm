The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1 To consider and approve Procurement of Furniture for the Visiting Faculty Hostel.

1.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that through their letter of November 28, 2012, tenders were invited from the following four pre-qualified furniture manufacturers:

1. Zunn Furniture
2. Inspire Interior
3. Versant
4. SIMAB (smc-pvt) Ltd.

1.2 He said that though all four had obtained the tender documents, only the following two participated in the bidding. The prices quoted by them are mentioned against their names:

(a) Inspire Interiors : Rs. 4,149,000/-.
(b) SIMAB (smc-pvt.) Ltd. : Rs. 6,085,215/-

1.3 Mr. Shameem Ahmed presented a copy of the Comparative Statement, prepared on the date of opening.

1.4 As M/s. Inspire Interiors are the lowest of the two, Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the EC to allow placing an order to M/s. Inspire Interiors, at the bid price of Rs.4,149,000/- He reported that the TEC and the CPC have recommended to the lowest bidder M/s. Inspire Interiors.

1.5 The Director Projects wanted to know as to what was the engineers estimate. Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that there was no such estimate. The Dean & Director wanted to know about the quality of work of Inspire Interiors to which Mr. Shameem Ahmed explained his satisfaction. The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to visit the factory of Inspire Interiors and verify the quality of work independently.

1.6 Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to why those who have worked with IBA such as Habib & Sons have not applied again. The Director Projects replied that Habib & Sons has applied for Students’ Centre. The Dean & Director said that Mr. Shameem should have clubbed the requirements of both the Girls’ Hostel and Visiting Faculty Hostel. The larger tender amount would have attracted large companies to participate. The Dean & Director wanted to know for which facilities the furniture is yet to be procured. The Director Projects said that the furniture for Admin. Building and the Library at the Main Campus would be arranged by the Registrar. The Director Projects said that for Aman Tower the furniture would be arranged separately. The Registrar said that he was to arrange furniture for classrooms and the seminar rooms only not for the Admin Building and the Library. The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to club the furniture requirements of Admin Building and the Library and handle the procurement.

DECISION

1.7 The Committee approved awarding the contract for procurement of furniture for Visiting Faculty Hostel to the lowest bidder, namely, Inspire Interior, at the bid price of Rs.4,149,000/-.

ACTION

1.8 The Director Projects would club the furniture requirements of Admin Building and the Library and handle the procurement.

EMBA Program:

2.4 There were recommendations for two different positions at EMBA Program. The first was for up-gradation of the position from Senior Executive (EMBA Program) BPS-16 or equivalent grade to Assistant Manager BPS-17 or equivalent. Before presenting the justifications, Mr. M. Saleem Umer described in detail the various activities the EMBA Program office perform.

2.5 The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what are the differences between the functions of EMBA program as enumerated by Mr. M. Saleem Umer and those of other programs. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that more or less the functions of various program offices are the same except that EMBA program works on Saturdays and Sundays. So the EMBA program receives questions papers too. The Dean & Director wanted to know if result default rate is less in Mr. Saleem Umer’s Program, Mr. Mobin Khalili replied in the negative.

2.6 Mr. Saleem Umer stated that with the increase in number of students, the day to day operational activities of EMBA have increased and due to the flat structure minor operational matters are referred to the Program Director. He said that this up-gradation will address the urgent need of a focal person in the program office who will handle day to day operation and take instant decisions.

2.7 The Dean & Director said that Program Office is the front office where there is no hierarchy; anybody available in the office should handle the inquiries. In front office it is a team work and there should be a team leader. He advised to empower the front line persons.

2.8 Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested that Program Manager’s position should replace Senior Executive position. The Director H.R. said that in transition we can appoint an Assistant Manager.

2.9 The Dean & Director said that the basic difference between the EMBA program and other programs is that for EMBA there is a full time Program Director which is not the case in other programs. He said that ultimately the plan is that we will not have full time program director; we will have faculty members who will be part-time program directors. He said that at this stage we do not fill in the position of Manager EMBA Program but when we make the change from full time to part time program director then we will take a decision about the Program Manager. Mr. Saleem Umer reported that he has taken a course this semester so the part time element will kick in and at an appropriate time he would exit as a full time Program Director. He said that they have to groom someone as the future Program Manager.

2.10 For the second recommendation; Executive (EMBA Program) of BPS-14 or equivalent to Sr. Executive (EMBA Program) of BPS-15 or equivalent, Mr. Adnan Hameed said that BPS employees have to serve for a certain period before moving to the next grade, the incumbent was appointed in BPS grades w.e.f. April 1, 2012. As per rules, next grade will be due in five years time.

DECISION

2.11 In light of the discussions noted in 2.9 above, the Committee decided to up-grade the position of Senior Executive to Program Manager of BPS-17 or equivalent and the up-gradation of the position of Executive to Senior Executive. In lieu of the up-gradation of the position of Senior Executive to Program Manager, one position of Executive will be surrendered.

Item No. 4 To consider and approve extension in the completion time of the New Academic Block, (Abdul Razzak Tabba Building) Main Campus.

4.1 The Director Projects reported that the construction of New Academic Block (Abdul Razzak Tabba Building) was awarded to M/s AHCL in February 2011 and commencement of work started in March 2011. He said that according to the condition of contracts the completion date for the subject project was August 31, 2012. However, for a number of reasons the Executive Committee was requested to extend the project up till December 16, 2012. The Executive Committee in its meeting held on August 27, 2012 approved the extension of time for the project up till December 16, 2012. He presented the overview of the Project and said that the project could not be completed in accordance with extended time table due to the following reasons:

Overview of the Project:

o Total Plot Area (sft) 44,600
o Total Covered Area (sft) 48,000 (Ground + 2)
o Contract Awarded Cost Rs.202,385,590/-
o Value of Variation Order Rs. 10,956,865/-
o Value of ID Works Rs. 4,679,818/-
o Value of additional works Rs. 21,907,818/-
o Value of walk through works Rs. 2,995,531/-
o Total value of work Rs.220,301,679/-
(i.e. 8.85 % of Rs.202,385,590)

Reasons for time extension:

o Due to design changes, quality improvements and revisions in drawings by Architects and IBA. He said that since the delays in various activities noted in his presentation have been in parallel and overlapping therefore the day time has been taken as 65 days.

o Delays due to Contractor (AHCL) which included Carpet issue, delivery of tiles, delivery of external pavers, delay in submission of rates of walk through items works, delay in delivery of lift, delay in delivery of UPS, completion of major punch list items. Overall accumulated delays = 45 days

o Due to Client (IBA) which included late approval of carpet sheet / tile rate, curtain wall openable window design, approval of sill-batta, Coping cost, design approval of pavers, computer room seating arrangement approval. Overall accumulated delays = 20 days
o Due to unforeseen incidents including political / religious parties strike calls, rainy days, CNG issues, trucks strike due to which material could not be made available. Overall accumulated delays = 53 days.

4.2 The Director Projects requested for an extension of 100 days (March 31, 2013) so that they may also complete the ID works, additional items, and punch list major works. He said that the Central Purchase Committee has approved this case in its meeting held on Saturday, February 23, 2013.

4.3 The Dean & Director wanted to know if there was any penalty clause for the delay on the part of the architect. The Director Projects said that the penalty clause was removed because the Architects did not agree to sign it. He admitted that it was his mistake that this clause was not included in the RFP. However, he said that even if they put this clause in the RFP the Consultants insist up removing this clause. He said that he would recheck the RFPs of the projects to see if this clause of penalty is there or not.

4.4 The Director Projects said that the cost overrun of the project is by Rs.18 million i.e. 8.85% of the project. The Dean & Director said that he would request Tabba for additional Rs.18 million.

DECISION

4.5 The Committee approved the extension of 100 days (March 31, 2013) in the completion of the project (Abdul Razzak Tabba Building) so that they may also complete the ID works, additional items, and punch list major works.

ACTION

4.6 The Director Projects would recheck the RFPs of the projects to see if the penalty clause is included there or not.

4.7 The Dean & Director would request Tabba Foundation for additional Rs.70 million as the final cost of completion is Rs.220 million.
