MINUTES OF THE 105TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MAY 31, 2010

 

The 105th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 31, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                           Associate Dean

  2. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

  3. Mr. Ali Taha                                  Director Finance

  4. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz               Director Projects

  5. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                           Director ICT

  6. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                           Director CED

  7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                       Director H.R.

 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve Policy for ICT Equipment Issuance.

 

1.1       The Director ICT presented the draft ICT Equipment Issuance policy. The equipments included laptop, PC, 4-in-one-printer, printer, scanner, blackberry, EVO, wireless collar mike, and slide changer.  He also identified the equipments entitlement against each position. The positions were categorized into two; Faculty / teaching Staff, and Management Staff.  He suggested that both laptop and PC would not be provided to all except the Associate Deans, and the Dean & Director.

 

1.2       The Director H.R. suggested that the lap top should not be taken back from those who have already been issued and are not entitled to it according to the proposed policy.  However, they should be given the choice of keeping either the lap top or the PC.

 

1.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani agreed with the Director H.R. and said that the proposed policy would be applicable from the date it is approved and not retrospectively.

 

1.4       To a question, the Director ICT clarified that the positions of Registrar and Controller of Examinations are covered under “Directors”.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee approved the ICT Equipment Issuance Policy. 

 

ACTION

 

1.6       The Director ICT would present the policy before the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 2      Data Storage Payment - Unisys Letter of Understanding.

 

2.1       The Director ICT informed the Committee of the Data Storage Equipment that has reached IBA premises on May 24, 2010. He said that according to the contract 60% payment to M/S Unisys is due. He said that this payment is due upon delivery but line by line items in the delivery Challan according to agreed BoQ need to be checked. He said that they had a discussion with Head of Internal Audit for guidance who suggested to seek advice from EC regarding the following concerns:

a)      Confirm whether the equipment at this stage will be checked at the component level or not. (It is to be noted that for component level checking the packing needs to be opened for all boxes and once the factory packing is opened the same cannot be re-packed with same level of robustness and security.)

 

b)      M/s. Unisys have agreed that upon IBA’s request they will transport the equipment from University Campus to City Campus without any charges to IBA and Unisys accepts the responsibility of the safety of the equipment during the transportation of equipment from University Campus to City Campus. This is only true if the original factory packing of the equipment remains intact and is not removed.

 

2.2       The Director ICT presented the following additional information for consideration:

 

·         Warranty period has already started as equipment has been delivered.

 

·         Government Auditors may issue a Para if equipment is lying idle for a long period.

 

·         A further 30% payment will be due within 2 months of delivery.  IBA should make further 30% payment after successful completion of Provisional Acceptance Test which can be conducted only after data centres are ready

 

·         IBA is exposed to greater risk now since they are in possession of highly valuable equipment for which they have received a huge amount of discount. Therefore, he suggested that they should take all possible steps for safe keeping of the equipment and should not do anything which will violate terms of the contract.  Any violation of contract terms will result in a loss to IBA.  Since Unisys will not accept responsibility if boxes are opened by IBA, they must keep the original factory packing intact until data storage centers are ready for installation of equipment.

 

2.3       The Director ICT further stated that since Unisys has already delivered the equipment, IBA has to pay the 60% as per the contract; however, since ICT is not opening the packing to check the line wise BoQ, ICT needs decision from EC regarding the payment of 60 % to M/S Unisys now and do the line-wise BoQ check when they open the packing. He said that the Director had suggested to keep it in escrow account but he said that M/s. Unisys do not agree to escrow account.  He also presented the copy of the Letter of Understanding which IBA is to sign with M/s. Unisys.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee requested the Director Finance to send the draft of the Letter of Understanding to the legal advisor for advice.

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Approval of Water Supply and Sewerage line for Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the new chiller has a cooling tower for which the one time water requirement is about 800 gallons and makeup water requirement is 3800 gallons per day. He said that the consultants designed the water supply system consisting of underground tank and near the chiller room and a water supply line from admin block to underground tank. This water tank will be sufficient to serve the second chiller for Auditorium. He reported that as no sewerage system existed in the vicinity of Academic Block & Auditorium, a septic tank has also been designed for immediate construction as the sewerage is overflowing on the ground. The tender documents were prepared by M/s. ICON along with design and drawings.  Letter of invitation was sent to pre-qualified contractors but only the following four contractors collected the tender documents:

 

1.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmad & Co. (Pvt.) Ltd.

2.      M/s. Electro Systems (Pvt.) Ltd.

3.      M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical & Mechanical Co. (Pvt) Ltd.

4.      M/s. Technologist.

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported that M/s. Technologist did not submit their bid. The bids received from the first three contractors were opened on May 17, 2010 in front of IBA officials and contractor representative. The following were the prices quoted by the contractors:

 

1.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmad & Co. (Pvt.) Ltd.                                   Rs. 5,225,007/-

2.      M/s. Electro Systems (Pvt.) Ltd.                                               Rs  3,038,616/-

3.      M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical & Mechanical Co. (Pvt.) Ltd.          Rs. 2,994,002/-

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that all the bids were sent to M/s. INTEG for evaluation and scrutiny who submitted their report on May 25, 2010.  After scrutiny there was arithmetical correction in bid price of M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical & Mechanical Co. (Pvt) Ltd. The detail comparative statement with itemized rates was prepared of the contractors bid. The following was the corrected bid price:

 

1.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmad & Co. (Pvt.) Ltd.                                       Rs.5,225,007             Highest

2.      M/s. Electro Systems (Pvt.) Ltd.                                                   Rs.3,038,616              Lowest

3.      M/s. B.G. Civil, Electrical & Mechanical Co.(Pvt.) Ltd.      Rs.3,162,002     2nd Lowest

 

3.4       Based on the recommendation of M/s. INTEG Project Managers and comparative statement, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to give conceptual approval and refer the case to CPC.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee granted conceptual approval for water supply and sewerage line for Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus and referred the proposal to the Central Purchase Committee (CPC).

 

ACTION

 

3.6       The Director Projects would present the proposal for water supply and sewerage line for Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus to the Central Purchase Committee (CPC).

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
