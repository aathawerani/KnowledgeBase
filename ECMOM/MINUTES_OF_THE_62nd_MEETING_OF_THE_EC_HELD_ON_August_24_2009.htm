The following items were discussed:

 

Item No. 1      Recruitment Status

 

1.1              The Committee was informed that out of 69 approved positions, 16 positions have been filled up; the rest are in process.  The Committee was also provided department-wise recruitment status.

 

1.2              The Director made the following directives:

 

a)        Associate Deans and concerned department chair’s recommendations are compulsory for selection of faculty.

b)        The chairman of the teaching department will have to ensure that as a result of appointment of a full time faculty in a department, two visiting / part-time faculty teaching in that department are disengaged so that the teaching strength does not exceed the approved strength.

c)        The Director H.R. will ascertain the total strength of faculty each department in coordination with the Associate Deans.

d)       The Director H.R. to ensure that until two visiting faculty members of a department are disengaged, appointment letter to full time faculty for that department is not issued.

e)        The Director ICT was advised to outsource the jobs as much as possible.

f)         Projects related positions will be terminated with the completion of the project.

g)        Relating to Facilities Management, the Director asked the Registrar to see if we are optimally utilizing our work force or not.

h)        Daily wage workers should he hired on need basis and they should not be made permanent.

i)          More weightage should be given to the hiring manager in the selection of a candidate as he / she has to get the work done by the person appointed.

1.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that a new position that of a Project Manager has been approved by the Director IBA for Aman Foundation project. He said that his services will also be utilized on other projects too. He reported that his appointment has been made and will be joining IBA in the second week of September 2009.

 

1.4       The Director Projects further informed that the Director IBA  had also advised to convert the position of Purchase Officer at Facilities Management Department into Procurement Manager, who should be placed under Director Projects. Secondly he had approved an additional position of a quantity surveyor.

 

1.5       The Director Projects informed that he has sent the revised organogram to the Director HR along with the job descriptions. He requested that the overall organizational structure of the project department be amended accordingly.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee noted the recruitment status and the directives given by the Director for compliance as mentioned in 1.2 above.

 

1.7       The Associate Deans, department chairs, Director H.R., Director ICT and the Registrar would ensure compliance of the above directives.

 

ACTION

 

1.8       The Director HR will amend the organizational structure of Projects’ Department

Item No. 3      To discuss and decide changes in renovation of façade of Academic Block, City Campus and the façade of Library at Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that recently a number of Executive Committee members had shown their concerns on the extensive changes in the facades of the two buildings. He said that during a meeting with the Arabtec Architects and IBA Consulting Architect on the new Multi-storeyed building, they also advised not to break the front corridor of Academic Block. Similarly the Dean and Director advised M/s. ICON not to change the facade of the existing library at the Main Campus during his visit to the site of Academic Block at Main Campus.

 

3.2       The Director Projects said that the original designs had been approved by the Audit and Finance Committee based on which the tenders for the renovation of Academic Block were floated. The work on the building has commenced. Similarly the Architect has completed the renovation drawings of the library based on the approved designs. He said that the changes in the approved designs will mean reversing the decision to maintain the existing facades instead of demolition of old facades and construction of new facades. The Architect will have to restrict to uplifting of the existing facades only.

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that if the Architect has to be advised to change the designs now, we will have to do so immediately and as previous designs were approved by the A&FC we will have to go to A&FC for the approval of the revised designs.

 

3.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to discuss and    decide, so that he could take further actions in this regard.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee decided that the Director Projects would get new drawings of the renovation of façade of the Academic Block while preserving the existing corridor.

 

3.6       The Committee further decided that the Director Projects would inform the Architect that:

 

·         The Library façade is not to be changed. It is to be improved as it is.

·         The walk-in arcade is not to be broken

·         The walkway of Academic Block at City Campus should not be broken

 

3.7       ACTION

 

3.8       The Director Projects to obtain new drawings of the renovation of façade of Academic Block at City Campus and the façade of the library at the Main Campus and get them approved by the Executive Committee.

 

3.9              The Director Projects would convey the decision to the Architect as stated in 3.6 above.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Additional requirements for EMBA (Banking & Financial Services) room.

 

7.1              The Director Projects reported that last week Mr. Saleem Umer had given him a list of requirements for EMBA classroom and syndicate rooms at APWA Building. He said that most of the items were purchased items which have been forwarded to the Registrar while many can be taken care by issuing variation orders to ongoing contractors.

 

7.2              The Director Projects reported that as decision for false ceiling was taken at late stage, installation of air conditioners was kept in abeyance.  Site conditions now dictate that wall mounted split units cannot be installed. Cassette type air-conditioners are not available. He said that the most technically viable solution is to install two 4-ton capacity stand alone cabinet type air-conditioners which are easily re-locatable and can be used anywhere else.  The per unit cost is Rs.135,000/- plus installation.

 

7.3              The Director Projects requested the Committee to approve procurement of two air-conditioners at about Rs.300,000/- (including installation).

 

7.4              The Director said that the EMBA (Banking and Financial Services) is a profit centre. The students are paying Rs.300,000 each and we have to take care of them.  He said that the money spent will be reimbursed by EMBA Program to the IBA.

 

DECISION

 

7.5              The Committee approved procurement of two air-conditioners at about Rs.300,000/- including installation cost for the EMBA classroom.

 

ACTION

 

7.6              The Director Projects would ensure procurement and installation of the two air-conditioners.

 

b)     To consider letter received from the Secretariat of Governor Sindh for allowing the students of 2008 batch to continue their studies at IBA on improvement of their grades in “A” level examinations.

 

7.7              The Director informed the Committee that he has received a directive from the Honourable Governor of Sindh on the appeal made by two of our students of 2008 batch who were dropped as they could not meet the eligibility criteria in “A” level examination. He said that the Honourable Governor of Sindh / Patron of IBA has accepted the appeal of M/s. Umair Misbah and Namreen Akhtar and other students of Batch 2008 (if any) aggrieved on similar basis and allow their admissions being genuine and hardship case as one time relief which will not be quoted as a precedence.

 

7.8              The Director said that under the law the Governor can give directive to the Board of Governors and the Academic Board. But he said that as it may become a thorny issue and may be a serious reputational risk on the institution he wanted to discuss this in the EC meeting.

 

7.9              Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that in the past we had given a chance to improve grades in “A” level examination but later this policy was discontinued.

 

7.10          The Registrar observed that allowing improvement of grades in “A“ level examination to the students of 2008 batch only would create heart burning among the incoming students of 2009.  He suggested that the policy should be changed and improvement should be allowed across-the-board for 2008 as well as 2009 batch.

 

DECISION

 

7.11          The Executive Committee after detailed discussion on this issue decided that rather than accommodating the students of 2008 batch only by implementing the directive of the Honourable Governor of Sindh / Patron of IBA, the students of 2009 batch should also be allowed to improve their grades.  If they meet the eligibility criteria they should also be allowed to continue their studies at IBA. The Executive Committee also felt that the policy should be changed for the future students also and improvement should be allowed within specified time period.

 

7.12          The Committee Recommended to the Academic Board to reinstate the pre-2007 policy of allowing improvement in “A” level grades across-the-board.

 

c)      Formation of Central Purchase Committee.

 

7.13          The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee has not yet been formed.

 

7.14          The Director clarified that until a Central Purchase Committee is formed, the existing Purchase Committee will continue to perform the functions of CPC. 

 

7.15          Relating to the functions of the CPC, the Director clarified that all purchases of Rs.300,000 and above will have to be approved by the CPC. Board approval does not mean that tender has been approved. The rules and procedures and the powers delegated to various authorities will have to be followed.

 

7.16          The Director also clarified that documentation is the job of each director instead of the Director Finance only.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
