MINUTES OF 186TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

TUESDAY, JANUARY 17, 2012

 

The 186th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Tuesday, January 17, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                         Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                               Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                Registrar

5.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                 Controller of Examinations

6.      Mr. Ali Taha                                                      Director Finance

7.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                   Director Projects

8.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                           Director H.R.

 

The following attended the meeting on special invitation:

 

1)      Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects.

2)      Mr. M. Saleem Umer, Program Director, EMBA

3)      Mirza Izhar Husain, Director CEE

4)      Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif, Program Director CS

5)      Mr. Aman U. Saiyed, Program Director, BBA

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

  

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve Key Performance Indicators for the following:

 

Faculty of Business Administration – Dr, Muhammad Nishat:

 

1.1       Dr. Muhammad Nishat presented the KPIs of FBA which included Faculty Publication & Research, Faculty Performance, Curriculum, Faculty Development, Linkages / Accreditation / MoUs / Student Exchange / Collaborations.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that Faculty Publications & Research will be monitored by Dr. Nishat and it will be facilitated and implemented by the Centre for Business & Economic Research headed by Dr. Qazi Masood. He suggested to include working papers and case studies in the KPI of Faculty Publication & Research.

 

1.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to whose responsibility it will be for assuring quality on teaching related matters such as semester scheduling. He said that some teachers are having classes once a week or on Sundays for evening classes. He said that if once it is decided that the classes will be held twice a week then it should be adhered to. He also mentioned of late coming and early walk off by teachers, course outline coverage, quality of question papers (sometimes 3 hours exam is finished in just 40 minutes).

 

1.4       The Dean & Director said that the program offices will see if the classes are held according to schedule, and on time and if not they would report to the program directors who would get them reinforced.

 

1.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the Academic Committee is going to audit if the course outline is being followed or not.

 

1.6       The Dean & Director said that if the Controller of Examinations has any complaint about exams he would report to the Associate Deans and they would resolve it. He said that scheduling problem has considerably been reduced. Sundays should be used for making up classes cancelled due to unforeseen reasons but should not form part of the regular schedule.

 

1.7       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that some regular faculty members are also taking classes on Sundays or once a week.

 

1.8       The Dean & Director said that such cases should be discouraged.  He asked the Associate Deans to get it audited as to how three hours papers is completed in 40 minutes and also to see if it was exceptional or it is a tendency. He said that the Chairman Academic Committee would monitor unit systems verification and validation.

 

Faculty of Computer Science – Dr. Sayeed Ghani:

 

1.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the objective of his KPI is that it should be quantifiable.  He said that the KPIs of FCS and FBA are almost similar. He said that he has also included CEE/Industry Collaborations – number of CEE sessions conducted by FCS faculty and number of on-going industry collaborative projects. However he said that he is also involved in the Academic Committee being its Chairman. He suggested that the KPIs on students (program wise and induction wise statistics: BS(CS), MS(CS), and Phd(CS) programs should be delegated to the Program Directors.

 

1.10     The Dean & Director said that the purpose of KPIs is to convert our weaknesses into strength. He agreed with Dr. Sayeed Ghani that the students statistics be included in Program Directors’ objectives.  He said that the KPIs of the Associate Deans of FCS and FBA should be the same. He advised to include entrepreneurship also in the KPI.

 

Director CEE, Mirza Izhar Husain:

 

1.11     Mirza Izhar Husain presented the KPIs of Centre for Executive Education which included the following:

 

·         Percentage increase in number of courses in Open Programs

·         Percentage increase of attendees in open programs

·         Percentage increase in client specific courses

·         Percentage increase in number of attendees in Client Specific Programs

·         Courses delivered by internal facilitators Vs courses delivered by external facilitators

·         Cumulative satisfaction index

·         No. of dissatisfied feedback client HR

·         No. Of courses with foreign institutions / business schools

·         Actual Vs Budgeted Revenue

·         Actual Vs Budgeted net income

 

1.12     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that there is mention of percentage and number. He suggested that there should be some measurement on quality also.

 

1.13     Mirza Izhar said that he would include percentage of middle management and top management participants in the KPI.

 

MBA Program:

 

1.14     The KPIs of MBA Program Office also included Task, and Required Skills. The KPIs included:

 

·         Efficiently and smoothly handling all queries

·         Process all requests within the given time frame

·         Timely gathering all course allocations from chairpersons

·         Optimized semester schedule before the deadline

·         Offer courses accurately on ERP before the deadline

·         Timely announcement of information

·         Uploading all course outlines and having presentations before the registration phase starts

·         Up-dated and informative MBA Website

 

1.15     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that although the KPIs are in the right direction, they are too vague and cannot be measured. He also wanted to know if a building is under or over utilized then whose KPI it would be to improve the utilization of the facilities.

 

1.16     The Registrar said that the Program Directors should increase the inductees. However, the Dean & Director said that Program Directors have nothing to do with the number of applicants and selection. Their responsibilities begin when they are handed over the batch of finally enrolled students.

 

BBA Program:

 

1.17     Mr. Aman U. Saiyed presented the KPIs of his BBA Program which included the following:

 

·         Establish open communication lines between the chairpersons and the program offices to quickly respond to changes in the program as they are made by the chairpersons.

·         Train the staff in the program office by rotating them so that they cover for each other.

·         Add and train at least one additional person in the Main campus to cover for the additional work to be done for BS and Economics departments.

·         Get the teachers to prepare course outlines and have them available at the program office for students to consult.

·         Further streamline the registration process thereby making it smooth and easy for students.

 

1.18     Mr. Aman Saiyed said that for KPIs given at first, fourth and fifth points user feedback is needed to measure the performance. This may require a survey to be conducted by MIS among the chairpersons, teachers and students to find out how good or bad the two program offices are performing.

 

1.19     The Dean & Director said that adding and training one additional person for the additional work to be done for BS and Economics departments is not a KPI. He said that because of construction work, future enrolment will be done at the Main Campus and the program offices should also be at the Main Campus.

 

1.20     The Registrar suggested that a person from the lab can be deputed to the program office.  The Committee agreed with him.

 

1.21     The Dean & Director suggested to merge the KPIs of program offices with those of Associate Deans but with clear lines of responsibility for KPIs assigned to the Program offices.  

 

1.22     The Dean & Director said that the Academic Plans should be finalized by June 30 every year and the Associate Deans will be responsible for preparing these plans and presenting them as part of the Annual Budget process. They have to prepare the Academic Plan in coordination with Program Directors and chairpersons.

 

1.23     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that in Academic Calendar we do not have cushions.  He suggested that there should a cushion of at least 4 days in the semester.

 

1.24     The Registrar said that he will prepare the Academic Calendar in conjunction with Mr. Mobin Khalili and would incorporate the suggestion.

 

FCS Program – Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif:

 

1.25     Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif presented the KPIs of FCS Program which included the following:

 

·         Student Enrolment

·         Mean Starting Salary of the graduates

·         Number of PhD produced

·         Number of Research Publications

·         Number of International Collaborations

·         Participation in International Competitions

 

1.26     The Dean & Director said that the goals presented by Dr. Asif will become part of the overall KPI of FCS program which will be internally divided. The Program Director will be accountable for results assigned to the Program office. He asked Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif to sit down with Dr. Ghani and delineate the goals assigned to him.

 

EMBA Program – Mr. M. Saleem Umer:

 

1.27     Mr. M. Saleem Umer, Program Director EMBA presented the Vision, Mission, and Key Performance Areas along with the Key Performance Indicators of the EMBA Program. The following are the KPIs of EMBA Program:

 

·         Weighted Average Faculty Evaluation Rating in each trimester

·         Number of new courses offered in each trimester

·         Average number of participants admitted in each trimester

·         Number of organizations represented per year

·         Amount of program fee collection per year

·         Average amount of fee receivables per year

·         Average number of EMBA staff Vs. Participants enrolment per year

·         Placement of EMBA participants after graduation

·         Promotions

·         New jobs of higher responsibilities

·         Average salary before and after graduation

 

1.28     The Dean & Director said that the “Average amount of fee receivables per year” will be KPI of Finance Department and not of Program Director. He said that HBL was reporting that they were approached by EMBA, Alumni, and CEE directors separately.  He suggested that instead of approaching individually, different departments and centres of IBA should go together and keep each other informed of the plan.

 

Registrar Office – Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer PN

 

1.29     The Registrar presented details of the KPIs achieved during 2010-2011, and those established for 2011-12.  He said that for the current year most of the work was done on documentation.  This included revision and up-dating of IBA Code Book, preparation of SOPs, updating and re-issue of student handbook 2011, program announcement and publicity brochures, preparation of allotment & licensee fee rules of IBA Staff Town. He said that they have prepared a check list for all activities.  He said that the program announcement in the last three years has changed drastically.  He said that the General Administration, which is day to day administration, is the biggest task for the next year. This includes take over and furnishing the office spaces in (a) renovated FCS block, (b) newly constructed Academic Block at Main Campus, (c) newly constructed CED block, (d) and under renovation Admin Block. This also includes vacation of un-authorized accommodation in IBA Staff Town. 

 

1.30     The Dean & Director asked the Registrar and the Director Projects to do an exercise to determine the space availability when all the facilities are completed and become operational e.g. how many classrooms there would be with seating capacity in each classroom and compare it with existing capacity and find out the excess capacity and plan our admissions accordingly to utilize excess capacity likely to become available.

 

1.31     The Director Projects reported that he has prepared details of the existing capacity and the capacity to be available in future for both campuses and would send it to the Dean & Director for his perusal.

 

1.32     The Registrar reported that the vacated houses of Mr. Fareed Ahmed and Mr. Shameem Akhtar are being floated under the new rules governing licensing of IBA Staff Town accommodation.  Giving details of the efforts on vacating the houses from Mr. Mohammad Younus, Mr. Mohammad Naeem, and Mr. Rao Nisar Ahmed, the Registrar said that three hearings have taken place in case of the house occupied by Mr. Mohammad Younus and after six hearings the court may decide to vacate the house.  About Mr. Mohammad Naeem he said that Mr. Naeem is not responding to the mails.  He said that he had a meeting with Rao Nisar who said that his move-over was not accommodated.

 

1.33     The Registrar said that under Academic Support they have the target to increase the number of overseas open house including one in Europe, and to increase the number of campus visits by students from colleges/schools in Karachi.

 

1.34     The Dean & Director asked the Director Finance to work out the financial impact of the arrangements of settlement with Mr. Rao Nisar and take it to the Board for approval. He said that if Mr. Rao Nisar does not agree then we would go to the court.

 

1.35     The Director Finance suggested to include vehicles and transport related issues in the KPIs.

 

1.36     The Dean & Director appreciated the initiative of inviting students of schools/colleges to visit IBA.  He suggested that the Admissions office should keep track on the number of students coming from Saudi Arabia and UAE and inform them that our admission process has started.

 

 

Projects Department – Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz:

 

1.37     The Director Projects made a detailed presentation on the Key Performance Indicators for Projects Department with respect to the detailed paper submitted to the Executive Committee. According to the report, he informed that there will be seven KPIs  for the Projects Department which are as under:

           

            1.         Cost                                                   2.         Schedule

            3.         Resources                                            4.         Customer Satisfaction

            5.         Quality                                                6.         Safety

            7.         Risk

 

1.38     He then presented the details of the KPI No. 2  “Schedule “  in which he provided the details in the  format already approved by the Dean & Director IBA . The format included the Projects completed in the year 2008-2009, 2009-2010 and 2010-2011. The next format included the Projects completed in the year 2010-2011, 20011-2012 and projects to be completed in 2012-13 and ongoing project of 2012-2013 and the projects expected to be completed in 2013-2014.

 

1.39     While reviewing the performance against the KPIs, he informed that the projects which have been completed until now remain within the standard variation of 5%, the resources have been judicially applied and the customers have expressed their satisfaction with the overall quality achieved in the building. Highlighting the importance of factor of safety at the site on the City Campus and on the Main Campus, he informed that there has been no accident at the site or injury or damage to the property during the construction operations during the past three and a half year. He further informed all health and safety standards are being followed at sites. Project department had sent four Contractors’ Safety Inspectors for training in the  concerned education department of NED.

 

1.40     The Dean & Director suggested that Projects Department should monitor all KPIs internally while the details of projects completed in the particular year should be shown along with those under implementation in the current year with start and completion dates. He said that this will also help in reporting to the A&FC.

 

Finance Department – Mr. Ali Taha:

 

1.41     The Director Finance reported that he has divided the KPIs into seven areas; HR Development of finance officials, financial reporting, budget, income, expenses including payroll, other recurring expenses, and capital expenditure.

 

1.42     The Dean & Director suggested to keep the Payroll for monitoring purpose but not for KPI as it is an internal matter. He suggested to add satisfying audit paras of both government and external audits as a KPI.

 

Human Resource Department – Ms. Ayesha A. Minai:

 

1.43     The Director H.R. presented the KPIs of HR Department which included Recruitment, Performance, Training, Compensation, and Work Planning.

 

1.44     The Dean & Director said that Recruitment is one of the major activities of HR. He suggested to mention the names of the various positions advertised, the number of candidates interviewed for each position, and the number of positions that were filled.  He also suggested to monitor non-faculty training.

 

DECISION

 

1.45     The Committee approved the KPIs along with the observations noted above.

 

ACTION

 

1.46     The revised KPIs in light of the EC’ observations should be sent by each head of the department to the Dean and Director.

 

1.47     The H.R. Department would conduct a user Survey every year in which feedback on some of the KPIs would be solicited.

 

1.48     The Associate Deans would coordinate and prepare the Academic Plans for their respective faculties that should be finalized by June 30.  The process should coincide with the Budget Cycle as the annual budgetary exercise would be based upon these Academic Plans. Associate Deans and Director Finance should therefore work together.

 

 

Item No.2       Presentation by M/s. KP Associates on final up-date on the electrical infrastructure at Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects said that he has invited Mr. Khalid Pervez of M/s. KP & Associates to give final update on Electrical Infrastructure at Main Campus and the technical and financial justification for not using solar street lights.

 

2.2       Mr. Khalid Pervez presented technical and financial analysis of the solar v/s conventional system of lighting and recommended that the solar system is technically and financially not viable.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee decided to go for conventional method of street lighting.

 

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve recommendations for position up-gradation.

 

3.1       This item was deferred.

 

 

Item No.4       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

4.1       This item was deferred.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
