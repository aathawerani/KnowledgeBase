Option 2         50% amount deduction:

Financial Implications under Option 2:

                           

Investment

Rs.3,025,000

Annual Return

Rs.   504,162

ROI/Year

      16.6%

           

(Amount to be deducted per month = Rs.840/-)

 

3.2       Mr. Imran Batada said that the following will be the benefits to IBA and the end user:

 

7.      Benefits to IBA

8.      Benefit to End User

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the TOR for the proposed Appellate Body of IBA.

 

4.1       The Registrar presented the background leading to the proposal of the BoG to put up a concept paper on having an Appellate Body for IBA.  He briefed the participants on the paper and informed that on the whole it was a useful exercise as it had provided an opportunity to review the overall procedure and documentation pertaining to handling of students related disciplinary cases at IBA; a corollary of the same he stated was the systematic documenting and listing of the SOP already followed by the discipline committee for handling disciplinary cases. 

 

4.2       The Committee members were of the opinion that there was no need for having an Appellate Body as such. The Dean & Director as the Chief Executive of IBA should perform that role.  However, in this regard some members quoted the example of LUMS and some other institutions where the Executive Head of the institute was guided in the Appellate role by a 2-member committee comprising one member each from faculty and staff.  It was suggested that similar committee to be named as the “Ethics and Values Committee”; which though not a supra body over the Discipline Committee, should have the responsibility for preserving and maintaining the value and culture of the institution so far as it related to addressing any grievance of the student body. 

 

4.3       The members of the Committee recommended to the Board that a Value and Ethics Committee be formed which will report directly to the Dean & Director with prime function of assisting him in his responsibilities as an Appellate.  However, it would not be necessary for the Dean & Director to consult or refer Disciplinary Cases to such a Committee on a routine basis.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The recommendation of the EC members was immediately communicated to the Dean & Director as he was not present in the meeting. However, the Dean & Director did not agree with the EC members as he does not have the authority to over rule the decision of the full Board which is the highest decision making body of IBA. All that the Board had asked him was to draft the TORs, the procedure and modalities of the Appellate Committee. This was the task entrusted to the Registrar and he has to comply with that. He said that the debate on the pros and cons of an Appellate Body was held by the BOG members and all the arguments were taken into consideration. It is not befitting for him to tell them that we do not believe in forming an Appellate Committee but would like to form another committee reporting to the Dean and Director. He further said that as he is party to the decisions of the DC it is only appropriate and in the interest of fair play and justice that another committee should hear those aggrieved by his decisions.

 

4.5       The EC appreciated the work of the Registrar and the Disciplinary Committee in the formulation of the SOP for handling day-to-day disciplinary cases and felt that it was a useful addition to the documentation process. 

 

ACTION

 

4.6       The Registrar would incorporate the SOP for handling day-to-day disciplinary cases into IBA Code Book and get it disseminated among all concerned.

 

4.7       The Registrar would present the draft TOR for Appellate Body in the BoG.

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and approve the designing and supervision of the work of supply and installation of elevator at FCS Building at City Campus.

 

5.1       The Director Projects stated that after approval of the work of supply and Installation of Ground + 3 Elevator at FCS Building, the Architect / Consultants of the FCS building were asked to quote their fee for providing their services for designing, tender documentation and supervision of the works. They had quoted a lump sum Fee of Rs.850,000/-

 

5.2       The Director Projects said that earlier, the Projects department has been involved in providing scrutiny of tender documents, procurement process and  top supervision of such works at the administration building City Campus, Aman CED, New Academic Block and Students Center where such works were part of the Consultants scope. He said that Engr. Rehan Sheikh, Senior Projects Manager has also very rich experience of such works. He said that the Projects Department after due deliberation came to the conclusion that having such experience and based on historical data in the Projects Department, it can handle this work departmentally and can save Rs.850,000/- for IBA plus the Project Managers fee. This would mean a total saving of Rs.1,150,000/-

 

5.3       He said that an internal Project team would be formed which would be headed by Engr. Rehan Sheikh. Following will be the responsibilities of the team members: 

 

· Rehan Sheikh and his team at City Campus: Preparation of Technical specifications, Technical and Financial Evaluation, inspection (pre-shipment and post-shipment), supervision of installation, testing and commissioning and verification of invoices and payments at Site.

 

· Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed and M. Jawad Khan: They would provide full assistance in detail supervision to Mr. Rehan Sheikh and his team.

 

· Mr. Abdul Rehman:  Preparation of BOQ, method of payment, general and special conditions of contract, procurement management including prequalification (pre bid, post bid), Contract management during execution, post completion project closures, obtaining approvals of Committees at all stages. Mr. Hakim and Mr. Tariq, Quantity Surveyor shall provide full assistance.

 

· Hakimuddin Khambaty: Bill checking, processing & variations (If any).

 

· Responsibility of successful project completion shall be of the Director Projects.

 

5.4       The Director Projects said that this arrangement is a deviation from the approved system of Project implementation and as such approval of the Executive Committee is required for getting the work done without Project Management. He said that the estimated cost of the project is very small i.e. around Rs.5.0 million.

 

DECISION

 

5.5       The Committee approved the proposal of the Director Projects to design and supervise the work of supply and installation of elevator at FCS Building at City Campus by the team of Projects Department instead of hiring Designing and Project Management firm.
