Item No.1       To consider and approve the award of work to the lowest responsive bidder for demolition work at the City Campus.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that KESC plans to remove its electrical material and equipment from the existing sub-station (located at the south-east corner of IBA City Campus) and shift the same to the newly-constructed sub-station at the north-east corner of the Campus. The existing sub-station, along with some other temporary site offices, is then required to be demolished, to make the space available for the New Auditorium. He reported that bids were invited from experienced contractors to demolish the vacated sub-station building. However, on Director Projects’ request, the scope of demolition has been expanded to include the 100 kVA generator room, the 132 and 135 kVA generator pads and the adjacent temporary site offices. This will clear the site completely for the auditorium work. He presented a Comparative Statement of the bids received from which it appeared that DCT Construction is the lowest responsive bidder, quoting a bid price of Rs.140,000/-

 

1.2      Mr. Shameed Ahmed reported that the bid was considered and approved in the joint TEC+CPC meeting to award the work to the lowest responsive bidder, namely, DCT Construction, at the bid price of Rs.140,000/-. He requested the EC to approve the recommendations of TEC and CPC.

 

1.3       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed as to why he did not include demolition work while he was seeking approval for KESC project.

 

1.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that demolition is to be done separately because the tender’s validity is 3 months and by that time the demolition could not be done.

 

1.5       To a query, Mr. Shameem Ahmed clarified that the contractor (DCT Construction) would take the material in addition to Rs.140,000/-

 

1.6       The Director Projects said that they are re-using the saria of the Library at Main Campus and due to which the construction cost will be reduced to that extent by the Contractor.  He also said that he had shifted 3 trucks of Saria obtained from the demolition of library and auditorium at City Campus to the Main Campus and it was probably auctioned by the Registrar.

 

1.7       The Registrar said that he does not remember to have auctioned three trucks of saria; he only remembers of furniture and fixtures that were auctioned.  However, he said that he would check it and let the EC know about it.

 

1.8       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that due to demolition of sub-station, if the boundary wall is affected it should be reconstructed.  He also wanted to know as to why the saria of City Campus was auctioned and not re-used.

 

1.9       The Director Projects said that he would take care of the boundary wall.  About the re-use of Saria of City Campus he said that it was not of such diameter that it could be reused and it was plain Saria as compared to the Main Campus which was helical. He further said that the demolition at City Campus was done by Arab Tec and the scrap including saria would have been lifted by them. However, he said that he would check what was in the contract.

 

DECISION

 

1.10     The Committee approved the award of work to the lowest responsive bidder namely, DCT Construction, at the bid price of Rs.140,000/- for demolition work at the City Campus.

 

ACTION

 

1.11     The Director HEC Projects would award the work to the lowest responsive bidder as noted above.

 

1.12     The Director Projects would see the contract of Arab Tec about the treatment of scrap including saria of the demolished buildings and report to EC.

Item No. 5      To consider and approve the change of  color of washed Terrazo of FCS Building facade.

 

5.1       The Director Projects reported that in view of the repeated complaints about the existing façade color of the buildings designed by ICON and on the advice of FCS Management, various options were considered for FCS building façade. The options included application of paints at three sides and Silbatta stone (as done in buildings designed by NBCL), or marble/granite or Alucobond (Aluminium cladding as in case of some portions of Aman Tower), only on the front. Cost of all options was found to be very high. Overall variation fell in the range of Rs.2 million to Rs.3 million. It was therefore considered advisable to explore some variation in the color of the washed terrazzo which was already part of BOQ.  He said that after lots of mock ups, one having Maroonish shade was approved by the FCS Management.
He presented the color perspectives which the Architect had developed on the advice of the FCS Management. He requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve this change of color for FCS façade

 

5.2       The Dean & Director said that there should be symmetry.  The proposed maroonish shade is quite different from the color of other buildings.

 

5.3       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that the existing washed terrazzo has got cracks and because of pollution it is getting dirty.

 

5.4       The Director H.R. suggested that the front of the building should have Silbatta stone and three sides should be coloured.

 

5.5       Dr. Shakeel Khoja suggested that on the entrance marble should be placed but its cost should not be increased.

 

DECISION

 

5.6       The Committee did not approve the proposed washed terrazo in light maroonish color, and decided that the three sides should be coloured (whitish grey) and the front side should be fixed with silbatta stone.

 

 

Item No. 6      To consider and approve appointment of Nucon for maintenance operation and maintenance of A.C. System at Administration Block, City Campus.

 

6.1       The Director Projects reported that the chiller at the Administration Block, City Campus is in operation since February 24, 2012 and the one-month test run is going on since May 24, 2012.  He said that the HVAC Consultant during his visit to site on June 05, 2012, expressed his dissatisfaction over the technical expertise and proficiency of chiller operators regarding routine maintenance of sensitive equipment.  On June 06, 2012, a team of Nucon, the dealer in Pakistan for M/s Carrier Corporation, France, who have manufactured the chiller, visited the site and their viewpoints were no different from that of the consultant. He said that the consultants and suppliers are of the opinion that the operators deployed at present are only capable of daily switching on and off of the chiller and seriously lack in the equipment knowledge and maintenance requirements of chiller and AHUs.

 

6.2       Considering the critical nature of the equipment, the Director Projects said that it is a cogent requirement that a proper HVAC service company be appointed to ensure proper maintenance of all the related equipment on a periodic basis e.g. condensers, FCUs, filter etc. M/s Nucon are the sole agent of Carrier equipment. It is necessary that during the critical phase they should be appointed as the operation and maintenance contractors before any loss occurs in the hands of un-trained personnel.  He said that the emergency alarm raised by HVAC Consultant makes it all the more necessary that a competent team be selected at this stage to avert any untoward incident in the future. We have to ensure that there is no breakdown of costly equipment.

 

6.3       He said that as the HVAC consultant and the Manager Maintenance IBA have opined that there can be no better firm than M/s Nucon to conduct and manage the maintenance of chiller. Being the dealer for the Supplier, they are fully competent as well as the only source to take on the responsibility to carry out this task in an efficient and effective way. It can also be recalled that they were instrumental in replacing the condensers in January 2012, thus enabling the contractor to commence the chiller operation before Summer.

 

6.4       The Director Projects reported that after comprehensive negotiations with M/s Nucon, their detailed quotation for a one-month test run amounting to Rs.200,000/- was received for consideration of the Technical Evaluation Committee and the Central Purchase Committee. In the combined meeting of Technical Evaluation Committee-Projects and Central Purchase Committee held on June 21, 2012, the approval was accorded for the subject quotation in view of urgency and sensitivity of the matter. Moreover, the Committees decided that as IBA’s Maintenance team is in process of finalizing a proper maintenance contract for subject chiller, a two month maintenance operation would definitely be required at this stage to ensure safety of equipment until and unless a proper Maintenance Contractor is appointed, a fact which was also endorsed by the Manager Maintenance IBA during the meeting. The Director Projects informed that the TEC and CPC advised that as it would now be a two-month task, the monthly quoted rate should be re-negotiated with the vendor to slash it further. The Maintenance team, meanwhile, has to ensure finalizing and selecting the proper maintenance contractor in two months’ time i.e. by August 25, 2012, to take over the HVAC system after successful completion of this special two-month maintenance period.

 

6.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the issuance of Work Order to M/s. Nucon for this two months’ maintenance operation of Admin Block City Campus HVAC System.

 

DECISION

 

6.6       The Committee approved the issuance of Work Order to M/s Nucon for two months’ maintenance operation of Admin Block, City Campus HVAC System.

 

ACTION

 

6.7       The Maintenance Manager has to ensure that the proper maintenance contractor is selected by August 25, 2012. All steps necessary to meet this deadline should be initiated immediately.
