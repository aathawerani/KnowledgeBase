Item No.1 To consider and approve changes in the Transaction Authority Policy and Procedure.

1.1 Mr. Fahad Rahman stated that some amendments in the Transaction Authority Policy and Procedures had become necessary for the sake of conformity with the SPPRA rules and the internal restructuring i.e. change in designations and abolition of certain positions; Director H.R., Director ICT for example. Limits have also been revised according to SPPRA rules. Relevant changes were discussed and cleared with concerned persons, and the suggestions given by directors/EC members have been incorporated in the revised documents. He said that as suggested managers have been empowered and some approvals which were earlier given at the Director’s level can now be given at the manager’s level.

1.2 The Dean & Director said that they were following the SPPRA rules but it was not documented as the Manual still showed the PPRA Rules. Now the shift from PPRA to SPPRA has been codified. He said that the Transaction Authority belongs to us but for the process we have to follow the SPPRA rules. He said that he had suggested that powers be delegated to senior managers and managers so that their grooming can also take place and they can exercise decision making ability. Those at the higher level will oversee if their managers are working properly or not. He asked Mr. Fahad Rahman to highlight the major changes that have been proposed in the draft.

1.3 Mr. Fahad Rahman highlighted the changes made in accordance with the SPPRA rules. The following comments / observations were made by the members:

  • On the maximum limit for RFQ i.e. three quotations for standard specification purchases which was (as per PPRA) Rs.500,000 has been reduced to Rs.100,000/- the Dean & Director said that keeping in view the inflation the SPPRA may be asked to enhance the limit.

  • The Dean & Director clarified that the Resource Mobilization will be with the Finance Department.

  • Since we are empowering our managers they and the building incharge should also be provided petty cash.

  • As activities keep on changing, therefore, the definitions should be more generic / more encompassing. There should be generic responsibilities rather than specific. There should be some language which allows the future centres also to be able to operate as it is a forward looking document.

  • The Dean & Director said that final approval on RFQ for Student Societies is the responsibility of the Finance Department, not of the Students’ Counselor as proposed.

  • On Facilities management and related matters, the Director Projects suggested that along with renovation and repairs of existing buildings, additional work (addition of a bath room, under-ground water tank for example) should be included.

  • The Dean & Director asked the Registrar and the Head of H.R to incorporate the changes in the Code Book too. He asked the Registrar to anticipate the changes / additions in view of his visit to LUMS and get them incorporated in the TAP. He asked Mr. Fahad Rahman to discuss with the Registrar to get some new ideas.

  • The Registrar suggested to enhance the printing limit from Rs.50,000 to Rs.200,000.

  • Mr. Fahad Rahman reported that for pick / drop of faculty at airport at late night, drivers usually take the vehicle to their home from where they go to the airport. He said that now they have suggested to avail Metro Cab facilities during 8:00 p.m to 8:00 a.m.

  • The Dean & Director observed that there is no mention of the sports equipment; their maintenance, purchase, and serving refreshment when there is any match or event. To an observation the Dean & Director clarified that students will have to bring in their own table tennis and badminton rackets; IBA will provide ball and shuttle cock.

  • The Dean & Director said that when the TAP is finalized he would like to have a meeting with all concerned.

DECISION & ACTION

1.4 The Committee approved the comments / observations made above and asked the Head of Internal Audit to incorporate those suggestions in the Policy and present it to the A&FC in its next meeting.

1.5 The Registrar and the Head of H.R. would incorporate the changes in the TAP (when finalized) in the Code Book.


Item No.2 To consider and approve cost overrun on the New Girls Hostel Projects and cost of additional works on External Development Works.

2.1 The Director Projects reported that the project was awarded at a cost of Rs.94.483 million and has been completed at a cost of Rs.107.396 million. The reasons of cost over run of Rs.12.913 million (a variation of 13.66 % of contract amount) are as follows:

a) MS Tubular Sun Shade Louvers:
MS Tubular sun shade Louvers also shown in tender drawing for block -3 and 4 like Front block of the hostel but not taken in construction drawings and BOQ due to cost involvement. During construction on architect’s suggestions, management allowed to carry out this work for architectural look, this work is an additional work.

b) Supply of Split ACs and Kitchen Equipments:
These are owner furnished material not included in BOQ. At the time of completion of respective building, management has decided that above said work are necessary; therefore the said work was incorporated in the scope of contractor through a Variation Order.

2.2 The Director Projects reported that the following additional works pertaining to “External Development and Landscaping Work” had to be completed which were not part of the original Bill of Quantity work for the buildings and could only be taken up after the completion of building. These works were not incorporated in the contract of M/s AH Construction. At the time of completion of respective building, management decided that these works are necessary for providing proper access and the outlook of the building. Because of the following reasons the said works had to be done through the contractor already working on the building through variation order:

  • Additional time involved in going for tendering process
  • The works which were the extension of the existing building works and as such sewerage lines and water supply lines were needed to make it livable.
  • Landscape works had to be integrated with the on-going works in the exterior of the building
  • Kitchen equipment, ICT Passive works and air-conditioning units and miscellaneous works were required.
  • Economy of rates as the contractor was already mobilized

2.3 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to recommend to the Audit and Finance Committee to approve: (a) the variation of 13.66% on the main contract which is within 15% and (b) the additional cost of Rs.11.107 million incurred on the additional works for external development. This will be financed out of the budget amount earmarked for External Development of Staff Colony.

DECISION

2.4 The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve (a) the variation of 13.66% on the main contract for new Girls’ Hostel which is within 15% and (b) the additional cost of Rs.11.107 million incurred on the additional works for external development which is to be financed out of the budget amount earmarked for External Development of Staff Colony.

ACTION

2.5 The Director Projects would present the recommendation of the EC to the A&FC for consideration and approval.


Item No. 3 To consider and approve the cost over run on the Visiting Faculty Hostel Projects.

3.1 The Director Projects reported that the Visiting Faculty Hostel Project was awarded at the cost of Rs.88.132 million and has been completed at a cost of Rs.98.102 million, the reasons of cost over run of Rs.9.77 million (a variation of 11.3%) are due to quantities variation and some additional works in the building.

3.2 The Director Projects said that the following additional works pertaining to “External Development and Landscaping Works” and “sewerage Line works” had to be constructed which were not included in the Bill of Quantity work awarded for the building construction and could be taken up after the completion of building:

a) External Work and Landscaping around Building:
External Development and landscaping work were shown in the drawings but were not included in the BOQ and the contract awarded to M/s Al-Shafi Enterprises. At the time of completion of the building, it was observed that the front area needed proper access (road), parking area and the landscaping, without which VFH could not be made functional. As the time of inauguration was approaching fast, it was decided to assign this additional work in November 2013 to the existing contractor M/s Al Shafi who was already mobilized and could work on the emergent need of IBA. The inauguration was held on 6th January 2014 by which time all the required work were completed.

b) Sewerage work around Building:

Sewerage work was also necessary for building sewerage system. It was also not included in the existing BOQ.

c) Internal Sewerage 18” dia:

Existing damaged 8” dia sewerage line in the Staff Colony had to be replaced with 18” dia pipes. Although this work was not part of building contact it had to be done as Karachi University sewerage water had been overflowing in front of the building. As per instruction of management this work was added in M/s Al Shafi scope by Variation Order.

d) Water Supply Line:
This work was necessary for the supply of water to the VFR.

e) Sewerage Line External Connection:
Changing of sewerage line was not envisaged earlier. However, while executing the internal sewerage it became apparent that it had to be connected to Karachi University System. This work was included in the scope of M/s Al Shfi Enterprises through a Variation Order.

f) Gas Line:
To supply the Gas to the Hostel laying of Gas line has been added in the scope of M/s Al Shafi Enterprises through a Variation Order.

3.3 At the time of completion of the building, management decided that the above works were necessary for providing proper access and to make the building functional. The said works had to be awarded to the Contractor on site M/s. Al Shafi Enterprises for the following reasons:

  • Time involved in new tendering process.
  • These works were the natural extension of the existing building works and without sewerage lines, water supply lines and Gas line, the residents could not use the facility.
  • Landscaping works had to be integrated with ongoing work in the exterior of building
  • Economy of rates as the contractor was already mobilized.

3.4 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to recommend to the Audit and Finance Committee, to approve (a) the variation of 11.3% on the main contract which is within 15% and (b) Rs.12.665 million incurred on the additional works done which were not part of the main contract including the exterior works of the building without which building could not be made functional.

DECISION

3.5 The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve (a) the variation of 11.3% on the main contract which is within 15% and (b) award of the additional work of Rs.12.665 million on External Development for making the building accessible and functional to M.s Al-Shafi Enterprises.

ACTION

3.6 The Director Projects would present the recommendations to the A&FC for consideration and approval.

Item No.6 To consider and approve the Amendment in Contract of M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises to make advance payment against guarantee.

6.1 The Director Projects reported that the contract for the Administration Building, Main Campus was awarded to M/s Al Shafi Enterprises in March 2012. The original contract duration for the said project was 18 months. Due to genuine reasons the project extension was granted to contractor till March 15, 2014, and currently the extension is being processed for completion by June 30th 2014 because of last minute layout changes and hold up on the fourth floor. The contractor so far completed the work as per given schedule and assured that they will complete the job within the given time. The Mobilization advance given to contractor has been recovered fully from their running bills till date.

6.2 In order to expedite the work and timely completion of the project, the contractor requested for an advance amounting to Rs.30 million against the insurance guarantee to meet the cash flow requirement of the project. The contractor’s request was sent to M/s NESPAK (Project Manager) for scrutiny and to determine the genuineness of cash flow requirement. M/s NESPAK have submitted their recommendation of Rs.20 million in order to facilitate the cash flow of the project. IBA projects department scrutinized the case and based on the recommendation of M/s NESPAK, recommends that Rs.20 million may be released as an advance against the insurance guarantee as the given mobilization advance has already been recovered fully.

6.3 The Director Projects informed the Committee that the contractor has billed an amount of Rs.81 million out of Rs.171 million therefore billing of about 90 million is still remaining out of which the secured advance of Rs.20 million shall be easily deducted from the upcoming bills of the contractor. The Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 29th March 2014 has checked the details and has recommended to the Executive Committee to approve the secured advance and the necessary amendment in contract. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to allow the amendment in contract so that secured advance of Rs.20 million may be released to contractor.

DECISION

6.4 The Committee approved the recommendations for the release of Rs.20 million to M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises as an advance against the insurance guarantee by making amendment in the contract.
