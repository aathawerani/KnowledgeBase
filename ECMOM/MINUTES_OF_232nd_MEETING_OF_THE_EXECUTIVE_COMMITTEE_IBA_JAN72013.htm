The following items were discussed:
 

Item No. 1 To consider and approve the Maintenance Manual.

1.1 The Registrar said that in order to facilitate maintenance activities at IBA, Maintenance Department (headed by the Registrar) has been bifurcated into two major divisions; (a) Technical Maintenance, and (b) General Maintenance. Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed is responsible for Technical Maintenance, while Syed Faheemuddin is responsible for General Maintenance. He said that Mr. Mushtaque will present the draft of the Technical Maintenance Manual.

1.2 Mr. Mushtaque said that the purpose of the Maintenance Manual for Technical Division is to provide an update of acceptable and effective maintenance and operations management “best” practices and current standards for IBA facilities. It is intended to provide a comprehensive framework for delivering beneficial and cost effective services at each Campus of IBA. The procedures manual will provide IBA Facilities Maintenance Staff with a set of clearly defined, yet flexible guidelines that are intended to complement sound facilities management practices already in use and offer new ones where necessary. He said that the proposed maintenance manual is just a guideline and shall be modified with times adherent with the requirements.

1.3 Mr. Mushtaque presented (a) Goals and Objectives of the manual, (b) organizational chart for Technical Maintenance Division, (c) Physical Scope of Technical Maintenance Division, (d) Functional Processes, (e) Operational Philosophy, (f) Work Plan for Technical Maintenance Division, and (g) facilities matrix for IBA Buildings.

1.4 It was pointed out that some actions are required to be taken in emergency including procurement of parts and equipments. But by following the set procedure, the work may be delayed. The Dean & Director said that the Manager Technical Maintenance will have delegated powers to exercise in emergency under the Transaction Authority Manual. He asked Mr. Mushtaque to do forecasting and by following the laid down procedure procure the equipment that may be replaced during the year. He said that for unanticipated break down the Manager Technical Maintenance will have the emergency power.

1.5 The Dean & Director suggested to do matching of the scope of Technical Maintenance Division with the organizational chart. He said that the responsibility for in-house nd outsourcing should be clearly defined. The Director Projects suggested to include STP (Sewage Treatment Plan) in the scope of work.

1.6 Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that there should be a user committee that may give regular feedback. The Dean & Director said that we get survey conducted by AC Nielsen and there are help desks at both the campuses. He asked Mr. Mushtaque to disseminate the contact numbers of help desks and other relevant information.

1.7 The Registrar asked Mr. Mushtaque to name the department which would be responsible for procurement of material along with the procedure in the Technical Maintenance Work Plan. The Dean & Director said that the department responsible for fire protection should be mentioned and the concerned staff should be trained. He asked the Registrar to invite Civil Defense people to inspect our facilities and tell us what else is needed for fire prevention and fire fighting at both the Campuses, the hostel and the staff colony. He said that the person responsible for Public Address System at the auditorium should also be identified given proper instructions.

1.8 The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to review the proposed flow chart of the Technical Maintenance Division and give his comments, if any, to Mr. Mushtaque.

DECISION

1.9 The Committee recorded its appreciation for the Registrar and Mr. Mushtaque for preparing the manual and recommended the manual to the A&FC for approval.

ACTION

1.10 Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed would incorporate the suggestions noted above and the suggestions, if any, to be given by the Director Projects after reviewing the flow chart and present the Maintenance Manual for Technical Division to the A&FC for approval.

1.11 The Registrar would invite Civil Defense people to inspect our facilities and tell us what else is needed for fire prevention and fire fighting at both the Campuses, the hostel and the staff colony.

Item No. 4 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Up-dated cost of Electrical Infrastructure at IBA Main Campus.

4.1 The Director Projects said that in its 227th Meeting held on 26th November 2012, the Consultants of Electrical Infrastructure Development at Main Campus, M/s. KP & Associates presented the following cost scenario pertaining to cost of cables based on their final design:

Previous Cost of cables Rs.23,231,295/-
Revised Cost of Cables Rs.22,827,790/-
Decreased in cost of Cables Rs. 403,505/-

Thus the overall reduction in the cost could be as under:

Previous Total Cost of Works Rs.123,344,906/-
Revised Total Cost of Works Rs.122,931,401/-

4.2 The Director Projects said that Mr. Khalid Pervez had further said that if IBA gets 22% discount on cables, the revised total cost of work would be Rs.117,909,287/-. The Committee had noted and approved the Revised Total Cost of Works at Rs.122,931,401/- with a provision of further reduction to Rs.117, 909,287/- after discount on cables to be obtained from Pakistan Cables.

4.3 The Director Projects reported that subsequently they approached Pakistan Cables for giving the rebate to IBA, to which they explained that they offer rebate up to 22% only on the small size wires and not the large size cables. In this regard meetings were held with Mr. Fahd Chiony and communications were also exchanged by IBA as well as KPA. As a result they offered a rebate of 4% and then another 2% on further negotiations. Based on 6% rebate cable cost came upto Rs.16,650,061/- (excluding contractor’s O.H. & GST).

4.4 The Director Projects said that they obtained prices of the same cables from their competitors M/s. Newage who submitted the cost as Rs.15,978,553/-. When Pak Cables were again approached, they agreed to match the Newage cost as such the cost of cable came as Rs.20,324,718/- after additions of overhead and profit of 20% and 6% GST. He said that the difference of the cost of cables as estimated by M/s. KP & Associates and as offered by Pak Cables came as follows:

Rs.22,827,790/- (KPA) including OH, Profit and GST
Rs.20,324,718/- (based on discounted price of Pakistan Cables
Including OH, Profit and GST
Rs. 2,503,072/- Difference

Based on above the contract cost shall be as follows:

Rs. 122,931,401/- (KPA)
Rs. 2,503,072/- discount from cable costs as above
Rs. 120,428,329/-

4.5 The Director Projects in conclusion reported that the above cost of contract is based on 11% discount and not 22% as anticipated. He requested the Dean & Director to talk to Mr. Chinoy for further discount.

4.6 The Dean & Director said that when such items are presented, there should also be presented along with this a running picture of the budget (what was allocated in the budget, what has been spent, and what is left).

4.7 The Dean & Director said that he would talk to Mr. Fahad Chinoy for further discount.

DECISION

4.8 The Committee noted and approved the up-date.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
