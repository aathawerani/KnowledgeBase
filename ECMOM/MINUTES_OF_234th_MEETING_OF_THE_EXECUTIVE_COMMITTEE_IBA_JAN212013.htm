MINUTES OF 234TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JANUARY 21, 2013


The 234th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, January 21, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                       Dean & Director
2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                 Associate Dean FBA
3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                      Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer,            PN Registrar
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz           Director Projects
6. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                  Director CED
7. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                      Director ICT
8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                 Director H.R.
9. Mr. Moeid Sultan                      Acting Director Finance

The following attended the meeting on special invitation:

1. Major (R) Qamar Abbas Rizvi,             Security Coordinator
2. Mr. Jawad Khan,                             Electrical Engineer
3. Mr. Shameem Ahmed,                      Director HEC Projects
4. Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed,                    Manager Operations & Maintenance.

The following items were discussed:


Item No. 1 To consider and approve proposal for CCTV Cameras for IBA Boys Hostel.

1.1 Major (R) Qamar Abbas, Security Coordinator presented the proposal for procurement of Electronic (CCTV) Surveillance System at Boys’ Hostel of IBA. He said that a total of sixteen cameras would be installed at the Boys’ Hostel.

1.2 The Dean & Director said that sixteen cameras have been installed at the Main Campus. He wanted to know as to why similar number is required for Boys’ Hostel. The Registrar clarified that they would use room specific cameras at the Boys’ Hostel.

1.3 The Director HR showed her concern over the growing stealing problem over there. The Director ICT wanted to know if there is a dedicated person on surveillance for 24 hours. The Security Coordinator replied in the negative.

1.4 The Director Projects inquired about response procedures & reaction time of security guards in case some unusual movement is observed by CCTV operator. It was suggested that an SOP be developed on method of raising alarm and responding to activity detected by the CCTV cameras.

1.5 The Dean & Director emphasized on the need to economize on human resources, deploy them judicially and ensure that the information gathered by the systems installed at different locations should be capable of being viewed at a central hub.

DECISION

1.6 The Executive Committee appreciated the performance / effective utilization of existing Electronic (CCTV) Surveillance System.

1.7 The Executive Committee in principle approved the procurement and installation of Electronic (CCTV) Surveillance System for IBA Boys’ Hostel.

ACTION

1.8 The Registrar would review the requirements of cameras and reduce the total number of cameras to 12 instead of 16. Procurement of the system would take place as proposed by security coordinator in his presentation as per IBA purchase and procurements procedures in vogue.

1.9 The Security Coordinator in consultation with the Registrar would develop a SOP on method of raising alarm and responding to activity detected by the CCTV cameras.


Item No. 2 To consider and approve the implementation of the Synchronization Scheme.

2.1 The Director Projects informed the Committee that about three weeks ago it was observed by him that the work on the LV Room at City Campus was left incomplete by M/s. Siemens because of a deficiency in the design done by M/s. EA Consulting. This was confirmed by Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects and Incharge of KESC Sub-station and re-location of gen-sets at City Campus as well as by Mr. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer. He further informed that he had advised Mr. Jawad Khan to undertake the completion of work on emergency basis so that power be made available to FCS Building which was near completion. He said that at the same time he requested Maintenance Manager Mr. Mushtaq Ahmad to prepare and present the case to the Executive Committee for approval of the emergency work after taking quotations from the Electrical Contractors working at the City Campus with the assistance of Mr. Jawad Khan.

2.2 Mr. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer and Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed, Maintenance Manager presented the technical details of the case which is summarized below:

• Mr. Jawad Khan informed that he had pointed out this deficiency in the start of the project to Mr. Shameem Ahmed. He said that after continuous follow up the Consultant admitted his mistake. Accordingly new system design was prepared by him and was forwarded for implementation.

• Nothing happened in spite of continuous follow up. Now LV distribution system is not energized as yet. Old system is providing the services. New system is to be energized after the completion of the balanced work. Scope of services was briefly presented to the EC by Mr. Jawad.

2.3 Mr. Jawad Khan further informed that the balance work consists of provision for the additional outlets for the load supply. For this the following three options were considered:

Option # 1: Modification of the existing panels and shifting of old cables to the new panel.

Option # 2: A new panel containing the required breakers to be placed at suitable place and hooked up with the existing system and shifting of old cables to the new panel.

Option # 3: To dismantle the existing panel and make a new panel altogether and shifting of old cables to the new panel.

2.4 Mr. Jawad Khan reported that after detailed deliberations, it was agreed that Option # 2 was the best option keeping in view the time factor, convenience and integrity of the existing system. For this purpose emergency quotations were obtained with the assistance of the Projects Departments from the contractors already working on IBA Projects. Some parameters like timeframe needed to be confirmed from contractors. He also said that the cost difference, between the quotations and if the normal procedure was followed, was minor.

2.5 Mr. Jawad said that the estimated cost based on the project contractors’ quotation is around Rs.1.8 million. He requested the Executive Committee to approve in principle the cost estimate.

DECISION

2.6 The Executive Committee approved in principle the cost estimate of Rs.1.8 for Option # 2 as stated above.

2.7 The Committee further decided that the work would be on permanent basis, without any changes in the future. However, arrangement must be made on temporary basis for supply of Power to classes in FCS Block by keeping off the air conditioning of the FCS Block. This AC supply is to be shifted to new panel thus making possible operation of the essential section of new electrical system of FCS Extension Projects.

ACTION

2.8 Mr. Jawad Khan would proceed with the case as per the laid down procedures.


Item No. 3 To consider an application and medical certificate from Ms. Asawera Khan a
Student of BBA-III for make-up exam”

3.1 Ms. Asawera Khan, a student of BBA-III in her application reported that she missed the second hourly exam of Socio-philosophy of Islam and Statistical Inference on November 29 and 30, 2012 as she had had a severe attack of chest infection and acute asthma and was under observation at Liaquat National Hospital overnight and was recommended two days of complete bed rest. She requested for the make-up examination for the two courses that she missed.

3.2 The Registrar said that he checked it with the LNH and the LNH in writing have confirmed that Ms. Asawera Khan was presented in Accident & Emergency Department of LNH on November 29, 2012 with complaint of shortness of breath, and was diagnosed as a case of acute exacerbation of asthma.

DECISION

3.3 The Committee allowed make-up examinations to Ms. Asawera Khan for Socio-Philosophy of Islam, and Statistical Inference that she missed in the second hourlies.

ACTION

3.4 The Registrar would convey the decision of the EC to Ms. Asawera Khan.

3.5 The Controller of Examinations would arrange make-up examinations for the above two courses for Ms. Asawera Khan.


There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
