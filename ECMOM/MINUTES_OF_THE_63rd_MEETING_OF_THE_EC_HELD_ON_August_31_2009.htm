MINUTES OF THE 63RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON AUGUST 31, 2009

The 63rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 31, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Director
2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat Associate Dean
3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean
4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN Registrar
5. Mr. Mobin Khalili Controller of Examinations
6. Mr. Ali Taha Director Finance
7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
8. Mr. Zafar A. Sidiqui Director CED
9. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi Director I.C.T
10. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai Director HR

Before the commencement of the meeting the Director congratulated the Registrar and his entire team for organizing the wonderful orientation. He said that it was superb job done by him and his team. He also congratulated the Director Projects and his team for making classrooms ready and the Hostel functional. 

The following items were discussed: 

Item No. 1      Approval for publishing of the advertisement for pre-qualification of.  

i)        Contractors for Civil / Electrical / Mechanical / Interior Decoration  

ii)       Architects for Designing and Top Supervision of new Buildings and Renovation Works  

iii)      Engineering Consultants for the Designing & Supervision of Infra-  Structure and External Development Works for Main Campus, City Campus, Boys’ Hostel and Staff Colony.

          iv)        Landscape Architects – Architects who are specialized in Landscape

Design and have expertise in horticulture.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that pre-qualification of contractors was done in October 2008. During the tendering process for nearly one year a few of the pre-qualified contractors did not respond to their invitation to collect bids and a few contractors did not participate in many tenders issued to them. Because of this their list narrowed down.  Moreover, the pre-qualification is one year old and as such as per PPRA rule it is essential to have fresh pre-qualification

 

1.2       The Director Projects further informed that similarly almost one year has passed after the pre-qualification of the Architects. He informed the Committee that they are holding competition for new buildings, during this process one Architect M/s. Fida Habib Ali have refused to take part in competition leaving five Architects to participate in the design competition. He said that in the past 3 competitions three Architects are consistently securing least marks and could not produce worthwhile designs. He sought approval to pre-qualify more Architects to broaden the base and to have healthy competition.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that infra-structure and external development works are of totally different nature from the building works and involve designs of roads, earthwork, surface drainage, electrical and related works. He said that it is necessary that the consultants with expertise in the design and construction of external development including roads, paths, utility corridors, streets lighting, boundary walls and related works may be pre-qualified now as the construction works on the infra-structure development and external development would be required to be commenced as soon as the Master Plan is ready.

 

1.4       The Director Projects further stated that landscape designing is a specialized field which needs expertise in horticulture too. IBA has large areas to be properly landscaped at the Main Campus, City Campus and the Staff Colony. He said that it is essential that we have experienced landscape Architects for designing and supervision of these works.

 

1.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the publishing of the advertisement for the pre-qualification of contractors and consultants as explained above.

 

1.6      Explaining the tendering process based on advertisements the Director Projects said that practically every time the advertisements for the works are not placed in the newspapers once the contractors are pre-qualified. He said that the interpretation of the Head Internal Audit that everytime an advertisement in the newspapers is to be placed for works above Rs.500,000/- is not relevant here because the very purpose of PPRA regulations pertaining to prequalification of contractors for works is to hold competition amongst the pre-qualified contractors who emerge successful after passing through the prequalification criteria .

 

1.7      The Director advised the Director Projects to contact any implementing agency for interpretation so that the objections of the Head of Internal Audit could be examined in the light of the actual practices. He was of the view that once prequalification was done there would be no need to place a generalized advertisement.

 

1.8      The Director Projects reported that he has talked to the people in NED and they have told that they do not advertise in cases where the prequalification has been done.

 

1.9      The Director said he needed documentary evidence so that he could address the objection of the audit. He said that he had written to the Chairman PPRA seeking clarification on this issue.

 

1.10    The Director Projects said that the architects do not design parking, roads, paths etc. These have to be specially designed. 

 

1.11    The Director said that those are small projects for which independent architects and top supervision are not required. He advised the Director Projects not to increase the work at the Projects Department of dealing with so many agencies.

 

1.12    The Director Projects suggested that the services of this nature can be included in  the advertisement for prequalification of Architects that they should have these specialities with them or they can associate with other experts. He also suggested that similar works can also be assigned to the pre-qualified firms of Project Managers majority of whom are Engineering Consultants

 

 

DECISION

 

1.13    The Director Projects would seek interpretation of PPRA rule regarding re-advertising and would also obtain documentary evidence from NED or any other government organization on the basis of which the Director would deal with the objection of the Head of Internal Audit.

 

1.14    The Director Projects would merge the advertisement requirements of the external development, infrastructure development and landscaping  in the advertisement to be placed for prequalification of Architects if needed services of pre-qualified Consultants be utilized on competitive basis as per the approved procurement policy.

 

 

Item No. 2      Medical facilities outside Karachi.

 

2.1      The Director H.R. reported that they have received an application from Mr. Asjad Asad stating that his wife is still in Lahore and cannot travel to Karachi because of her condition and requested that he may be allowed reimbursement of medical expenses of his wife.  The Director H.R. said that she has also received similar application from Mr. Musa Ali whose wife is in Skardu. 

 

2.2            The Director H.R. reported that according to the existing policy medical facilities are available in the approved panel of hospitals in Karachi only. She said that 196 dependents of our existing employees live outside Karachi. She said that if the medical facility is extended beyond approved hospitals in Karachi, there would be roughly an additional expense of Rs.800,000/- per annum on this account.

 

2.3            The Director said that the IBA is a national institution and we employ people from all over the country and we should treat all our employees equal.

 

 

DECISION 

 

2.4            The Committee allowed reimbursement of medical bills to Mr. Asjad Asad and Mr. Musa Ali at Liaquat National Hospital’s benchmark.

 

2.5            The Committee also decided to extend medical facilities (hospitalization) outside Karachi as well. However, the reimbursement of the medical bills would be according to the Liaquat National Hospital benchmark. The H.R. department would verify the bill to the extent possible. 

 

2.6            The Committee further decided that the Director H.R. would develop a policy on providing medical facilities to dependents. 

 

ACTION

 

2.7            The Director H.R. would forward the bills submitted by Mr. Asjad Asad and Mr. Musa Ali to Finance Department for reimbursement according LNH benchmark.

 

2.8            The Director H.R. would develop a policy on providing medical facilities to dependents.

 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)  Selection of the best design for new academic block at Main Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that in its meeting held on 10th August 2009, the Evaluation Committee considered the Evaluation Report of the Consulting Architect and examined all the design drawings and presentations. The ratings of the Consultants based on technical criteria were considered and approved by the Committee. It was decided to open the financial proposals of the following top 3 designers

                                                                                                                         S.No. Name of Designers                                                                Marks

              1.       M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt) Limited                     89%

              2.       M/s. FNMH                                                                               64%

  3.       M/s. ICON                                                                                 53%

 

3.2       The Director Projects presented the following summary of the financial proposals for the scope of services defined in RFP:

 

·         M/s. NBCL     4.5%    or lump sum Rs.11.25 million for an area of 50,000 sft.

 

·         M/s. FNMH    4.20% or lump sum Rs. 9.5 million for maximum of 65000 sft.

 

·         M/s. ICON      5.85%   (Did not quote the maximum fee)

 

3.3       The Director Projects reported that the Evaluation Committee decided to invite top two Architects separately on August 26, 2009 to negotiate their fee proposals as per the procedure laid down in RFP and which conformed to PPRA.  M/s. NBCL agreed to reduce their fee to 4.00% and the maximum limit to Rs.9.00 million for 50,000 sft, whichever  being less. M/s. FNMH were invited and were conveyed the observations of the Committee on their design. However, they did not agree to reduce their fee proposal of 4.2% and the maximum ceiling of 9.5 million for 60,000 sft..

 

3.4       The Director Projects  informed the Committee that the Evaluation Committee discussed the matter in detail and based on technical and financial merit, recommended that M/s. NBCL be appointed as the Architect for New Academic Block. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the evaluation process and appointment of the Architect M/s. NBCL for the New Academic Block

 

3.5              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui commenting on the evaluation said that for him it appears to be biased.  He said that in every parameter M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have been evaluated as the best.

 

3.6              The Director said that even a perception should not exist that we are not allowing anybody else to work for IBA in this field. He said that we should remain completely neutral and objective in evaluation. He therefore asked those members of the Executive Committee who are also on the Evaluation Committee to give their justification.

 

3.7              The Director Projects clarified that it was a unanimous agreement of the Evaluation Committee. He said that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have matched the façade of the Academic Block with the Centre for Entrepreneurship.

 

3.8              The Registrar said that the design of the Academic Block is similar to the Centre for Entrepreneurship. The alignment and space planning are good, courtyard is remotely located. He also said that he had pointed out that there was no storage space available and there was no pantry for faculty. 

 

3.9              The Director ICT said that the wind direction, light and placement of classes and offices have been adequately taken care of by M/s. Najmi Bilgrami. He said that the buildings should give an image of a business school and not of a hospital and all buildings must have resemblance.  On these bases M/s. Najmi Bilgrami were found to be the best.

 

3.10          The Registrar proposed that the three areas in which M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have got more marks than others can be checked to ascertain the validity of the evaluation.

 

3.11          The Director Projects said that the working capacity which is not included in the 12 areas of evaluation should also be considered.  He said that M/s. FNMH have got only a small office. M/s. Icon have also got a small office whereas M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have got eight architects and their office is four times bigger than others.  They have very senior architects in their team.

 

3.12          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the evaluation should be justifiable and verifiable and it should be documented. The Director Projects reported that it is completely documented.

 

3.13          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to how others were pre-qualified when it was known that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami were superior. He said that the starting point may be wrong.  We are not comparing apple to apple. On the set criteria for every project M/s. Najmi Bilgrami will be better than others 

 

3.14          The Director asked the Director Projects that only the firms which are at the same level of competence should be prequalified. He said that if M/s. Najmi Bilgrami have got 8 Architects but if they are all busy on different projects then nobody will be available to IBA.  He said that we have to protect our interest and to see how many architects are available to IBA. He asked the Director Projects to see the availability and utilization of the architects rather than the number of architects at the time of tendering

 

3.15          The Director Projects clarified that the details of those Architects and support staff is always demanded in RFP. The Architects do furnish details of the Architects who are dedicated to the project.

 

DECISION

 

3.16          The Executive Committee , after hearing the rationale and justification of the members of the Evaluation Committee present at the meeting, endorsed the evaluation process and the recommendation for appointment of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Limited for the New Academic Block at Main Campus.

 

3.17          The Committee decided that for future projects the Director Projects would check the quality of human resources and their availability to IBA.

 

3.18          The Committee decided to broaden the Evaluation Committee by co-opting the client department heads.

 

ACTION

 

3.19          The Director Projects would present the recommendation of the Executive Committee for appointment of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Limited for the new academic block at Main Campus to the A&FC.

 

 

b)   Renovation works at the Administration Building, Main Campus.  

 

3.20          The Director IBA discussed the matter pertaining to the renovation works at the Administration Building Main Campus. He emphasized the need of a proper shifting plan and undertaking of construction work floor wise.

 

3.21          The Director Projects informed the Committee that the entire space of the Multipurpose Building is required by the library and as such there will be no space available to the offices from Admin building to be shifted to Multipurpose Building.

 

3.22          The Director advised to construct offices on the first floor of the Multipurpose Building. He advised that the cubicles should be made of wood partitions with minimum of expenditure. He said that there is no need to build bath rooms on that floor. He also said that the library should not be shifted unless all the rooms are ready in the Academic Block.

 

3.23          The Director Projects said that outer walls and the roof will have to be of cement concrete. He felt that cubicles and partitions in the hall can be dispensed with if the staff of PP&D Dept is shifted to the first floor of the MP building as they will sit in the open hall. To this the Director agreed.

 

DECISION

 

3.24          The Registrar will finalize the planning of administration building Main Campus in consultation with the Director HR and the Architect. Major stake holders will also be consulted.

 

3.25          The Director Projects would present a proposal for the construction of offices at the first floor of the Multipurpose Building for approval by the Executive Committee.


There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
