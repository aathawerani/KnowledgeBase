Item No. 2      To consider the suggestion of individual counselling.

 

2.1       Mr. Nooruddin Muzzaffar in his email addressed to Dr. Ishrat Husain stated that after meeting a group of IBA students in the morning that had gone to his company for a case study assignment for one of their courses, he realized that there could be individual counselling idea implemented in the university. He further suggested that the counsellors need not be twenty, thirty or forty years elder to the student community but can also be competent, well-rounded, senior students or relatively recent graduates of the institution or older graduates of IBA too. He stated that over the years, he has noticed IBA graduates consistently perform a terrific job in certain areas such as meeting deadlines, setting priorities, brainstorming ideas, confidence, etc. but have lacking in several other areas, such as the firm grip of the various economic and business sectors and their business models, understanding of debt markets, good analytical understanding of insurance, modaraba, and banking sectors. He stated that with individual counselling as a concerted effort of the student and the institution both, more graduates would be groomed better based on their own peculiarities and strengths and the market repute of IBA would benefit as well.

 

2.2       The Director appreciating the suggestion of Mr. Nooruddin Muzzaffar said that IBA should have some part-time counsellor.

 

2.3              The Director H.R. said that at LUMS also there is a part-time students’ counsellor. 

 

2.4              Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested that Prof. Dr. Rizvi who is the Chairman, Department of Psychology at Karachi University and also practices at Ibn-e-Sina Hospital may be considered for part-time students’ counsellor.

 

2.5              The Director asked the Director H.R. to talk to AKU also.  He said that a psychiatrist working for the educational institution may be engaged as students’ counsellor. 

 

DECISION

 

2.6              The Committee decided that a person who has experience and has high success rate may be engaged on part-time basis as students’ counsellor and may be consulted as and when needed.

 

ACTION

 

2.7              The Director H.R. would identify a suitable person for engaging him/her as students’ counsellor on part time basis.

Item No. 4      To consider proposal for two advance increments to Option ‘A’ employees in BPS-1 to 15.

 

4.1       The Director H.R. stated that in recognition of staff performance for the year ended 2009, IBA is awarding annual increments to all option ‘B’ and contract employees at an average increment of 14%, which includes 8% inflation subsidiary and 6% merit increase. She said that the increment matrix is being implemented in-line with their efforts to create high performance culture at IBA and they are certain that it will help collectively achieve the Institution’s objectives. However, option “A” Regular Employees’ increments are governed by Government rules which are not sufficient to subsidize the general inflation rate in the country.  She further said that their understanding is that the revision in salary for Option ‘A’ employees will be based on the announcement by the GoP. In the meantime, she recommended that all Option ‘A’ regular employees who are on government BPS scales be given two advance increments to help ease cost of living increases. She said that this action will result in an additional cost of Rs.47,270/- per month or Rs.567,240/- annually or 0.4% of the non-faculty salary bill.

 

4.2              The Director H.R. stated that in the journey of becoming a world class business school, the entire staff including Option “A” regular employees are contributing at different levels and this small gesture of awarding two advance increments will motivate them to continue their work with more zeal and devotion.  She presented a detailed working of financial impact for two advance increments to option ‘A’ regular employees in grade 15 & below and grade 16 & above.

 

4.3              The Director said that this proposal was presented to the BoG and the BoG wanted that this should be recommended by a committee so he decided to present it before the Executive Committee for BPS-1 to 15 and he would take the recommendation to the Selection Board for BPS-16 and above.

 

DECISION

 

4.4              The Committee approved the recommendation of giving two advance increments to Option ‘A’ employees.

7.5              The Director ICT stated that one time device cost would be Rs.44,995/- and monthly recurring cost would be Rs.27,500/-  and both the cost would be charged to ERP project.

7.6              The Director ICT also presented the following general rules for this policy:

 

i)          Employees are encouraged to use the most cost-effective means of communication e.g. when in office, to use land line telephone instead of mobile phone etc.

 

ii)         Payments / reimbursements will be made on the basis of actual bill in accordance with the approved limit. No additional payments will be made in case of bills below the approved limit.

 

iii)        Any billing in excess of the monthly/annual limit will be deducted from the respective employee’s salary at the end of fiscal year or as soon as the annual limit is exhausted or as per contract terms. In case of separation, limit will be calculated on pro-rata basis based on annual limit and excess use (if any) will be deducted from final settlement or employee will be requested for legitimate payment.

 

iv)        IBA reserves the right to scrutinize an individual’s itemized billing statement for the purpose of determining the frequency, duration, timings and cost of phone calls made for official business.

 

v)         The cell phone is company property and will be covered under general insurance policy.

 

vi)        It is the employee’s responsibility to protect the cell phone from any possible loss and /or damage. However in case of theft/loss/damage of Cell Phone / SIM Card, the ICT department must be immediately informed for replacement and fulfilment of warranty / insurance claim and other formalities.

 

vii)       The ICT department shall then arrange for the replacement of set and SIM card as per the entitlement & eligibility criteria.

 

viii)      In case an employee entitled to a mobile set, leaves the organization, he /she shall have to return the mobile phone set to the IBA or pay through cash / final settlement amount equivalent to the book value in order to retain the set.

 

DECISION

 

7.7              After a detailed discussions the Committee approved the policy presented by the Director ICT by incorporating the following decisions:

 

i)        IBA will not provide the mobile phone / blackberry to the ICT staff recommended above. (employees would use their own mobile set)

 

ii)   The maximum reimbursement limit will be Rs.3000/- against the proposed limit of Rs.5000/-  There would be no change in the proposed limit of Rs.2000/- and Rs.1500/-
