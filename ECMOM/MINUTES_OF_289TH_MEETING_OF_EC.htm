The following items were discussed:

Item No.1 Presentation by Registrar on his visit to LUMS.

1.1 The Registrar said that at the instruction of the Dean & Director he visited LUMS and stayed at their Rausing Centre on March 13 and 14, 2014. He said that the purpose of his visit was to meet and network with the persons responsible for General Administration & Security functions, and to visit the facilities with specific focus on the following areas:

  • Rausing Centre for Executive Development
  • Pepsi Dining Hall Operations
  • House Keeping in general
  • Administration and Operation of the Hostels and Student Centre

1.2 The Registrar stated that by staying there he got the opportunity of knowing about their day to day administration and operations of the Rausing Centre, House keeping, and the Dining Hall facilities. He presented the layout of facilities on LUMS according to which the facilities are situated on a 100 acre plot with roads on all four sides. Out of 100 acres, 60 acres have been allocated for instructional facilities, 20 acres for residential facilities, 15 acres for extra-curricular, technical and sports facilities, and 5 acres are unutilized. The total capacity of Boys’ Hostel is 1200 studens and for girls 600 students. However, at the moment there are 250 girls staying in the girls’ hostel. IBA has a total area of 72 acres which is divided into five different locations; Main Campus (31 acres), City Campus (3.43 acres), Staff Colony (35 acres), Boys Hostel (1.10 acres), and Nazimabad Plot (1.7 acres).

1.3 The Registrar said that on his visit to LUMS facilities, he got the following observations and impressions:

General:

  • Being located in the safe environs of the DHA and having all facilities within one boundary makes the administration and security of the LUMS campus easier to manage in comparison to IBA’s dispersed facilities with campuses located in congested and sensitive environments.

  • The architectural design and materials used in the IBA buildings, interiors and furnishings present a modern look comparable to International Standards; the same therefore gives all our buildings a distinct edge over the LUMS buildings which lack in ambiance, aesthetics and décor and interiors.

  • With residential, instructional and extra-curricular facilities being co-located within one boundary for both faculty and students, the LUMS campus remains more populated round the clock. This facilitates profitable management of the catering and food vending facilities on a round the clock basis including providing three times meals for the hostel residents in the executive dining center supporting the REDC.

  • The overall capacity of LUMS hostels is several fold that of IBA – albeit at the cost of reduced privacy for hostélites as the bulk of the accommodation in the hostels is on twin sharing basis. In IBA we have single rooms in the Boys’ Hostel. The common use facilities at LUMS Hostels are not of the same standard as at IBA.

  • A lot of spaces at LUMS are open hall type allowing them to be configured for multi use; for instance the foyers become assembly areas, play courts and even lecture theatre. Also some of the lecture / discussion rooms have removable partitions.

  • Every building has a lounge and a rest room set aside for faculty use.

  • The outdoor sport facilities at IBA are better than those at LUMS except for squash courts, which may be provided at IBA with the boys hostels.

  • The campus is plagued with power problems and has as many as 10 interruptions a day.

Security:

  • LUMS manages its own security with 50 persons operating in 8 hour shifts. An 8 feet high, concertina fitted perimeter wall surrounds the campus. All buildings are equipped with electronic access control systems fitted at the main entrance. A 100 camera CCTV network with control room at the gate backs the security effort.

Catering & Food Vending:

  • The 3 floor PEPSI Dining Center (PDC) forms the nucleus of all catering and food vending activity at LUMS. It has a 24 hour running sprawling kitchen supported by large stores stocking dry and fresh provisions. Operated by LUMS it has a food and beverage manager who heads 56 employees comprising store persons, cooks, dish washers, waiters and shift supervisors. The first and second floor of the PDC are available to franchises for running their kiosks. Presently, Pizza Hut, KFC and two more local vendors maintain their staff – business is varied / so except on week ends.

  • The Rausing Executive Development Centre (REDC) has a refreshment bar. All eatables are provided by the PDC. Waiters for the PDC are employees of the REDC.

  • The Salman Dawood School of Business (SDSB) and Syed Babar Ali School of Engineering (SBASE) have small café’s of the size of our CED café which are operated by private parties and serve only snacks and refreshments from 8 am to 5 pm.

  • The Maratab Ali Student Centre (MASC) serves only beverages made available by PDC.

Housekeeping & Laundry Services:

  • Housekeeping & Laundry services are outsourced. They comprise janitorial services and house / room boys and have about 80 persons each operating in 2 shifts. (At IBA there are 56 persons for both the campuses.)

  • Material for housekeeping is provided by LUMS and is purchased in bulk.

  • The janitorial work force is seldom visible as the bulk of their work is done during silent hours and breaks.

  • The hostels, REDC and PDC have a running contract with Tip Top dry cleaners for laundry, of linen, drapery and carpets. Also Tip Top provides for washing of clothes of the students in the hostels on reduced payment.

1.4 The Registrar made visual presentation of the Guest Room, Writing Corner, Pepsi Dining Hall, Seminar Rooms at LUMS as well as at IBA. He also showed visuals of Access Point/Cash counter, Food Counter in Pepsi Dining Hall, Front Office, Garbage Disposal arrangement, Donated benches etc. He said that there is very effective use of walkie talkie by Security Staff at LUMS. Students use pre-paid Smart Card for using canteen; they cannot enter into cafeteria without making payment at the counter either through smart card of cash payment.

1.5 Addressing the Buildings Incharge the Dean & Director said that the buildings are now ready but they are to be maintained in the best possible way. We should learn from others too. He said that day to day operation is better known to incharge of respective buildings. He said that we should follow the best practices and see what further improvement can be made. He asked them to give their comments / suggestions.

1.6 Mr. Abdul Karim Modi suggested that there should be a separate budget for maintenance for each building. He suggested that spare parts should be in ready stock so that time is saved in repair and maintenance as and when needed. Mr. Ayatullah suggested that Petty Cash should also be provided to the building incharge for meeting urgent need related to repair and maintenance. The Dean & Director said that Mr. Mushtaq will ensure availability of the spare parts that would be required in emergency. He said that there would be a separate budget for maintenance of each building and petty cash would also be provided. However, he said that the donors who are trustees of the Endowment Fund would have to be convinced for providing fund for the maintenance of buildings. The funds would be managed by the Board of Trustees and we have to go to the Board of Trustees for approval of the Maintenance & Operations budget.

1.7 The Dean & Director said that after 2014 when all the facilities including Aman Tower are ready, the Maintenance Department will have to be re-organized. He asked the Registrar and the Director Projects to assess what should be the structure of our maintenance department, what is to be outsourced, what is to be kept in-house. A Maintenance Manual has to be prepared including the schedule for periodic, routine, and emergency maintenance along with chart of responsibilities.

1.8 Mr. Abdul Karim Modi reported that there has been seepage on the ground and first floors of Aman-CED building but the cause could not be detected. The Director Projects said that water pipe line was broken which has been replaced. Mr. Modi said that the seepage is at places not at one place. The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to investigate and fix the problem.

1.9 Mr. Abdul Karim Modi pointed out that the glasses of the buildings specially of the upper portion are not being properly cleaned by the outsourced firm. Mr. Faheem said that due to building structure scaffolding cannot be used as its stand requires 4 to 5 feet space which is not available due to keyari or water body. He said that the same problem is being faced at VPR. He said that there is a company which makes adjustable stand for scaffolding and our outsourced company – Master Multitech - is in contact with them. The Director projects said that there is an electric operated scaffold with hydraulic system available in the market. He suggested to procure that for IBA. The Dean & Director said that the IBA should have its own equipment provided it is economical.

1.10 Based on his visit and observations at LUMS the Registrar made the following recommendations to the EC for consideration:

  • Owing to limited number of clients and dispersed location of IBA facilities, outsourcing of food & beverage should continue.
  • For reasons of convenience of participants it is recommended that the City Campus be made the venue for all executive training classes including EMBA.
  • Smart cards be introduced for all members of the IBA community.
  • Building access system be introduced.
  • The shuttle service between the campuses be improved.
  • A separate Rest room is to be provided for Faculty at the Adamjee Building.

1.11 The Dean & Director suggested that on completion of the Aman Tower, IBA should float a consolidated tender for food and beverages for all the buildings, boys’ and girls’ hostels, VPR, Main Campus and City Campus so that on the basis of larger volume of business, good vendors at reasonable price may compete. The Director Projects said that the kitchen at Aman Tower is not so big and therefore, the existing cafeteria at the City Campus can be used as kitchen.

1.12 The Registrar said that there are safes in all the rooms at Rausing Centre in which people may keep their laptop and personal belongings. The Dean & Director said that at Aman Tower Guest Rooms we can provide the safes and should also have a counter. He said that our alumni can be asked to sponsor small items too like benches and they can sponsor even the existing benches.

1.13 The Dean & Director said that on completion of Aman Tower they will have to revisit the locations of campuses for different program offerings. He said that FCS and Executive Education may be located at City Campus.

1.14 It was agreed that IBA should also have Smart Card for our students and staff and the Building Access system should be introduced. The suggestion of providing a rest room and a wash room next to the faculty lounge at Adamjee Academic Centre was also appreciated. In regard to squash courts some suitable space has to be identified in the future.

1.15 The cleaning and mopping should be done before and after the working hours so that the janitorial staff are less visible during working hours.

1.16 The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to visit Aga Khan University also. The Director Projects suggested to include electrical, civil and mechanical people in the team for visit to AKU.

DECISION

1.17 The Committee approved the recommendations along with suggestions noted above.

ACTION

1.18 The Registrar and the Director Projects to assess what should be the structure of our maintenance department after 2014, what is to be outsourced, what is to be kept in-house, prepare Maintenance Manual indicating the schedule – periodic, routine, and emergency along with chart of responsibilities. The Director Projects will assist in the preparation of Maintenance Manual also.

1.19 The Director Projects would investigate the seepage issue at Aman-CED Building and get it fixed.

1.20 The Director Projects would suggest the economically viable equipment / scaffolding for cleaning of glasses of all IBA buildings.

1.21 The Director Projects would suggest space for two squash courts along with estimated expenditure. He would also arrange to provide Rest Room at Adamjee Building.

1.22 The Registrar would arrange and provide safes at Guest Rooms at Aman Tower.

1.23 The Alumni office would advise IBA alumni to sponsor small items too like benches and they can sponsor even the existing benches.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
