Item No. 2      To consider and approve the proposal for change in Policy – Benefit / Compensation in case an employee dies in Institution’s Service.

 

2.1       The Director H.R. reported that the HR Department received a request from the widow of late Mr. Lutuf Hussain for financial assistance on the basis of a notification of Finance Department, Government of Sindh (No.FD(SR-III) 3/84-2004) dated 13th June, 2005. The Director H.R. reported that the HR Department checked the validity of the notification and its applicability on the applicant through IBA’s legal advisor.

 

2.2       The Director H.R. stated that the notification provides guidelines of the government for providing financial assistance to the dependents of the deceased employees who die during their service. The Director H.R. said that since these guidelines were not available earlier, Regular Option “A” employees were also included in IBA’s policy on “Benefit/Compensation In Case an Employee Dies In Institution’s Service”.  According to this policy, employee’s dependents are entitled for an ex-gratia amount equal to six (06) last drawn gross salaries of employee or Rs.200,000/- whichever is higher.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve a proposal for overtime for ERP ICT Staff on late sitting.

 

4.1       The Director ICT stated that in the 108TH Executive Committee Meeting held on June 21, 2010, an Overtime Facility for ERP ICT Staff was discussed and the Committee requested the Director ICT to present a proposal for remuneration for ERP related jobs requiring late sittings and present it to the EC for approval. The Director ICT stated that the reasons for this incentive are as follows:

 

·         When a semester starts, the work load becomes very heavy to be handled during day.

·         A lot of configuration is made in the ERP System. This task is done on group-wise basis.

·         ICT staff is required to be available for 24X7 for covering the entire week.

·         The support staff is also on call from their homes through telephones, mobile phones and emails.

·         The ICT Systems have to be available on 24X7 basis and the staff is on call 24X7 basis.

·         The ERP users even require the support over the weekend thus the services must be available on Sundays.

 

4.2       The Director ICT requested that a budget of Rs.50,000 per month or Rs. 600,000 per annum be approved for the ICT staff involved in the ERP project and is sitting late for ERP Project.

4.3       The Director observed that every staff of ICT cannot be available for 24x7.  However, he said that for ERP implementation the persons who were required late sittings should be identified with justification. He said that the payment for late sitting would be time bound – from March 2010 till September 2010.  The identified persons would be given a one-time honorarium which would be covered under Reward and Recognition Policy.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee agreed with the Director and decided that a one-time honorarium to be covered under “Reward and Recognition Policy” be given to the identified and justified ICT staff for their involvement in ERP implementation.

 

ACTION

 

4.5       The Director ICT would identify the ICT staff who were involved in          ERP implementation and are justified for the honorarium.

Item No. 6      To consider and approve appointment of Project Managers for detailed supervision and Project Management of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus.

AAA Partnership as a successful Project Manager at the rate of 3.50% of the total cost of the Project. The case was submitted to the EC in its 118th meeting dated 30th August, 2010 for final selection of the successful Project Manager. b)      The number of courses allowed in a semester.

 

7.8       The Director wanted to know if there is any restriction on the number of courses a student can take in a semester.

 

7.9       Dr. Muhammad Nishat replied that they do not have a policy of restricting the number of courses in a semester. He said that the normal work load is six courses per semester.

 

7.10     To a question from the Director Finance the Director clarified that if a student takes less than six courses in a semester, he / she will have to pay the fee for the six courses i.e. semester fee.  However, if a student takes more than six courses, he / she will have to pay for the additional course(s) because the total fee is divided into four/eight semesters.  

 

7.11     Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know if a student can complete his degree program in less than the specified time period.

 

7.12     The Director said that it can be done. He cited his own example where he completed the course work in less than the specified period.

 

DECISION

 

7.13     The Committee requested Dr. Sayeed Ghani to take this issue to the Academic Committee for consideration and recommendation to the Academic Board.

 

 

c)      Introduction of Compulsory Internship towards social services.

 

7.14     The Director said that 80 to 85% of the students come from privileged classes; they have not seen poverty. For them he wanted to introduce compulsory internship with organizations such as Layton Rahimtoola Benevolent Trust, Marie Adelaide Leprosy Centre, Sindh Rural Support Program etc. and this would be part of their education process.

 

DECISION

 

7.15     The Committee agreed with the Director and requested Dr. Sayeed Ghani to consider this proposal in the Academic Committee.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
