MINUTES OF THE 113TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JULY 26, 2010

 

The 113th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 26, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.  Dr. Ishrat Husain                                    Director

2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

3.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

4.  Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

5.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

6.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

7.  Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                               Director H.R.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Project, Mr. Hanif Daud of M/s. Icon and Mr. Qaiser Imam of M/s. Integ attended the meeting by special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve purchase of Generator of 250 KVA for prime use for electrical system of Auditorium at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the applications for the additional load and the new sub-station for Karachi University are in the process of obtaining sanction from Karachi Electric Supply Company. He stated that there is power available for the lighting load of the auditorium but there is a requirement of 250 KVA to run the chiller and air-conditioning unit which is at present not available. Till such time new sub-station is constructed the generator will be used for Auditorium whenever required. This may be used as standby arrangement for Auditorium lighting load and for extended load of first Floor of Multi-purpose building as the load on currently available gen-set has increased by 20%. He further said that the data centre would also require heavy load. The estimate of the cost of gen-set is around Rs.4.5 million whereas the amount available in the captive generation this year including fee of Consultants is Rs.2.5 million. There is a shortfall of Rs.2.00 million which could be met from the available budget of Auditorium.

 

1.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the purchase of 250 KVA generator in principle so that tendering process can be started for this. The said generator is to be commissioned before 12th September 2010.

 

1.3       The Director said that the generators work till 2:30 p.m. at the Main Campus as there is no class after 2:30 p.m.  Moreover, he said that generators are stand-by arrangements, they are needed only when the KESC supply is not available. He said that they should give first priority to data centre, classrooms and academic block. He suggested to manage the load on the existing three generators.

 

1.4       The Director Projects said that the 150 KVA generator is actually giving output of 80 KVA.

 

1.5       The Registrar suggested to get the generator of 150 KVA located behind the cafeteria block overhauled so that it may work at its actual capacity.  He further requested that provision be made for the wiring of the auditorium block to allow for an externally hired generator to be connected up on important occasions as back up.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee did not approve the purchase of 250 KVA generator and asked the Director Projects to manage the load on the existing three generators.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Director Projects would get the 150 KVA generator overhauled and manage the load on the existing three generators. He would also make provision for the wiring of the auditorium block to enable the connection of externally hired generator on important occasions as back up.

Item No. 3      Outsourcing of help desk people – details about the cost on outsourcing and saving on head count.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that they obtained the annual cost of the ICT staff from the Finance Department which is in the range of Rs.3.5 million to Rs.4 million. He said that they also sought proposals from the following bidders:

 

            Name of Organization                Quoted Amount

 

            M/s. MIT                                 Rs.15 million

            M/s. Inbox                               Rs.18 million 

 

3.2       The Director ICT stated that by outsourcing they would be spending three to four times more than what is being spent at the moment. He recommended that:

 

a)      IBA should not outsource the lab staff

b)      they should deploy the CRM system

c)      train the staff at the level of other service providers

d)     provide service incentives against very strict performance goals.

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee endorsed the recommendations of the Director ICT.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve change of the design and cost of Pergola for the Academic Block at City Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects stated that in the original design approved by the Audit & Finance Committee, the Academic Block at City Campus had two pergolas (sheds), one in the front side (OMI side) and other on the back side (parking side). He said that because of the difficulties in construction of the prescribed system of pergola and the variation of quantities and cost pointed out by the Contractor, the construction of pergola was delayed.  It was discovered that the calculations of the quantities made by the Quantity Surveyor of M/s. Icon were wrong and the contractor asked for an increase of Rs.2.27 million over and above Rs.1.52 million, as provided in the BOQ.

 

4.2       The Director Projects stated that the Project Manager and M/s. ICON worked out on various alternates in order to keep the cost within the contracted amount. He presented the following recommendations of the Project Manager and the Architects:

 

a)      The area of proposed aluminium pergola is to be reduced by deleting the pergola from the façade facing the parking court yard. As per the opinion of the Architect the pergola is no more needed there.

 

b)      The aluminium section is to be changed to mild steel structure with polycarbonate Sheet as per original BOQ.

 

c)      The pergola will now be provided only on elevated platform, entrances and staircase on the front side (OMI side).

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that under the changed design and the deletion of pergola on the rear side the amount comes to Rs.2,583,000/- based on the original design whereas the cost of the alternate design for pergola on the front side (OMI side) only would be Rs.1,836,450/-.

                    

4.4       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the change in the design and the revised cost so that the Contractor M/s. Abaseen Construction Co. may be given go ahead to execute the work. The specialist for the pergola was identified by the Architects as M/s. Abaseen had been unsuccessful in finding another specialist for the same work.

 

4.5       The Director ICT opined that there was no need of pergola as there are generally no classes during the rainy season and when there is scorching heat during June / July.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Executive Committee agreed with the Director ICT and decided not to go for pergola. The Committee further decided that to give green effect, plants / creepers would be placed on the facade.   

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and approve external Development design around Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus.

           

5.1       Mr. Hanif Daud of M/s. Icon presented the external development design around Auditorium and Academic Block at Main Campus.  He said that the existing lawn would be preserved but some plants at the Multi-purpose building will have to be relocated.  He also presented the proposed path way and the pond in the ground.

 

5.2       The Director said that the proposed path way in the ground is redundant and is not needed.

 

5.3       The Registrar suggested not to disturb the existing set up including the pond on the ground.

 

DECISION

 

5.4       After physical inspection of the site, the Committee decided that:

 

a)      The proposed path way in the ground is to be retained.

b)      The pond should be shifted as proposed.

c)      The Consultant would ensure that the services line i.e. electrical, water and sewerage etc. along with pavements and right of way would be established and would not be interfered later. 

 

ACTION

 

5.5       The Director Project would ensure implementation of the above decisions.

 

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      Installation of bio-metric system for marking attendance of IBA employees.

 

6.1       Mr. Abdul Karim Modi, Incharge Leave Record, suggested to instal bio-metric system for marking attendance of IBA employees.

 

6.2       The Director ICT said that RFID is better than bio-metric system and RFID would be more suitable for marking attendance of IBA employees.

 

6.3       The Director said that they should also consider the cost and the implementation time required for installation of bio-metric system or the RFID.

 

6.4       The Registrar said that bio-metric system can be installed at both the campuses at a cost of around Rs.600,000/-

 

DECISION

 

6.5       The Committee requested the Registrar and the Director ICT to present details including cost and time period for implementation of the system of the bio-metric system of attendance and the RFID to the Executive Committee.  

 

b)     Regular meetings of employees with respective department heads.

 

6.6       The Director asked the department heads to have regular meetings with their staff and report to the EC what their issues are.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
