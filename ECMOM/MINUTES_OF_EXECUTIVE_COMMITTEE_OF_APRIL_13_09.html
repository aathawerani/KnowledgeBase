MINUTES OF THE  43rd MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON APRIL 13, 2009

The 43rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, April 13, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                  Dean & Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                            Associate Dean
  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN              Registrar
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                   Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                        Director Finance
  6. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                        Director Projects
  7. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                             Director I.T.
  8. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                             Director CED

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Status of Vacant positions

1.1              As the Director H.R. could not attend the meeting, Mr. Muhammad Munawar, Sr. H.R. Executive presented the status of vacant positions. The Director said that the response against the ad for faculty positions placed in London Economist is not very encouraging.

DECISIONS

1.2              The Committee decided to freeze the position of Executive Secretary at CEE and the positions of Assistants in Associate Deans’ Offices. On the advice of the Controller of Examinations, the Committee also decided to freeze the position of Assistants for the Examinations Department till such time the office space is made available.

1.3              In coordination with the Director H.R. the Associate Deans were requested to depute the four surplus staff to be identified by the Librarian at some other departments of IBA.

1.4              The Director said that instead of two positions; one of Store Officer and Accounts Officer at PP&FM Department, there should be one position responsible for store and accounts. The Committee agreed with the Director.

1.5              The Director asked the Director Projects to revisit the number of positions and reduce it to a minimum.

1.6              While discussing positions in I.T. department, the Director said that because of ERP, the head count must be reduced. He advised the Director I.T. to structure his set up in such a way where existing employees may be restructured in his department. He asked the Director I.T. to revisit and reduce the manpower requirement.

ACTION

1.7              The Director Projects and the Director I.T. would revisit their manpower requirements and give the minimum requirement to H.R. Department.

1.8              Mr. Munawar will prepare a revised status report based on the revised requirements to be submitted by the Director Projects and the Director I.T.  He would separately show the status of vacancies and new positions; project related and permanent positions.    

1.9              Mr. Munawar was also asked to prepare a list of students engaged at IBA against various positions and to remove such positions from the pay roll.

Item No. 2      Pre-qualification of Contractors for Maintenance Works.

2.1       The Director Projects reported that an advertisement for pre-qualification of contractors was posted in local newspapers on 09 February 2009 for maintenance works at IBA. The applicants were to be PEC registered of category C-6. The last date for receipt of applications was 19 February 2009. A total of 41 contractors applied for prequalification in following disciplines:

A.    Civil Works, including wood work, paint , polishing and steel works

B.     Mechanical Electrical and Plumbing (MEP)

C.     Air conditioning (window and split)

D.     Signage

E.     Water proofing.

F.     Horticulture

G.    Garbage Removal Services

H.    Cleaning of underground tank and overhead water tanks

I.     Fumigation and Rodent Control

J.     Floor cleaning and polishing

K.    Carpet & upholstery cleaners

L.    Building Maintenance & General order supplier.

2.2       The Director Projects stated that the applications were divided under the following two categories:

a)      Works which required PEC registration

·         Civil Works, including wood work, paint , polishing and steel works

·         Mechanical Electrical and Plumbing (MEP)

·         Air conditioning (window and split)

·         Building Maintenance & General order supplier

 

b)      Works which did not require PEC registration

·         Signage

·         Water proofing.

·         Horticulture

·         Garbage Removal Services

·         Cleaning of underground tank and overhead water tanks

·         Fumigation and Rodent Control

·         Floor cleaning and polishing

·         Carpet & upholstery cleaners

2.3       The Director Projects stated that keeping in view the nature of work it was decided to qualify contractors for maintenance work who had scored 65 or above out of 100. 

2.4       He further reported that out of 41 applicants 27 were those whose selection was based on PEC registration of Category C-6.  Eighteen applicants who had scored 65 and above have been short-listed for prequalification of maintenance work in IBA. He said that fourteen applicants were those whose selection was not based on PEC Registration. Ten applicants who had scored 65 and above have been short-listed for prequalification of maintenance works in IBA. Two applicants were suppliers, they were also considered for prequalification. He presented the detailed list of applicants.

2.5              The Registrar said that the Contractors should be financially sound. He also said that maintenance work requires quick response and it should be a part of the contract.

2.6              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that we need a contractor for garbage removal. The Director Projects said that he would contact some contractors who are pre-qualified for other work and they would be assigned the work of garbage removal. He said that for garbage removal, PEC registration is not required.

DECISION

2.7              The Committee approved the prequalification of contractors for maintenance works as recommended by the Director Projects.

ACTION

2.8       The Director Projects would contact some contractors pre-qualified for other work and assign them the garbage removal work.

Item No. 3      To approve changes suggested to be made by the Director Projects in the documents being issued to Contractors for bidding purposes.

3.1       The Director Projects recommended the following changes to be made in documents being issued to Contractors for bidding purposes. He said that these changes are recommended in the light of Pakistan Engineering Council’s standard form of bidding documents (Civil Works), harmonized with PPRA rules. He stated that Pakistan Engineering Council permits mobilization advance against insurance bond from an insurance company acceptable to the owner. He quoted Clause 60.11 which reads as under:

Quote  

60.11  Financial Assistance to Contractor :

Financial assistance shall be made available to the Contractor by the Employer by adopting any one of the following three alternatives :-

(Appropriate alternative only to be retained)

Alternative One : Mobilization Advance :

a)                  An interest-free Mobilization Advance up to 15% of the Contract Price

stated in the Letter of Acceptance shall be paid by the Employer to the Contractor in two equal parts upon submission by the Contractor of a Mobilization Advance Guarantee/Bond for the full amount of the Advance in the specified form from a Scheduled Bank in Pakistan or an insurance company acceptable to the Employer :

1)         First part within 14 days after signing of the Contract Agreement or date of receipt of Engineer’s Notice to commence, whichever is earlier ; and

2)         Second part within 42 days from the date of payment of the first part, subject to the satisfaction of the Engineer as to the state of mobilization of the Contractor.       

b)                  This Advance shall be recovered in equal instalments; first instalment at the expiry of third month after the date of payment of first part of Advance and the last instalment two months before the date of completion of the Works as per Clause 43 hereof.

                                                                        Unquote”

3.2              The Director Projects said that up till now IBA tender documents included condition of bond to be from a scheduled bank, because of which Contractors did not ask for advances as they could not get the bank guarantee and as such progress suffered. He requested the Executive Committee to approve this change and allow bonds from following insurance companies :

-          Adamjee Insurance Company

-          New Jubilee Insurance Company

-    Eastern Federal Union Insurance Company 

DECISION

3.3              The Executive Committee approved the recommendation of the Director Projects for changes in the documents being issued to Contractors for bidding purposes as they are allowed by PEC and harmonized with PPRA rules.

ACTION

3.4              The Director Projects would include the changes in the documents to be issued to Contractors for bidding purposes.

Item No. 4                  To consider financial bids from the Consultants for H.R. Consultancy Services.

4.1       The Director H.R. could not attend the meeting as she was to take her father to the hospital. However, since the matter needed to be finalized as early as possible and the financial bids were with her, she had requested that the Executive Committee may form a sub-committee to finalise this issue.

DECISION

4.2       The Executive Committee formed a sub-committee comprising Mr. Ali Taha, Director Finance, Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui, Director CED, and Ms. Ayesha A. Menai, Director H.R. who would open the financial bids and after evaluating the financial and technical bids submit their recommendations to the Executive Committee for approval.

ACTION

4.3              The Director H.R. would arrange a meeting of the sub-committee and present the recommendation of the sub-committee in the next meeting of the Executive Committee.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

            a)         To consider proposal for reimbursement for the hospitalization of the dependents of the employees living in far flung areas of the country and reimbursement for the therapy at home.

5.1              The Director H.R. in her note stated that there is no medical reimbursement policy for the dependents of the employees living in far flung areas of the country. Similarly, there is no provision for reimbursement for therapy given at home. She suggested that a policy should be approved whereby in such cases reimbursement may be made subject to production of the original documents and their verification.

DECISION

5.2              The Committee decided that there is no need to make a policy.  Such cases including hardship cases would be decided on case to case basis by the Executive Committee.  The Committee further decided that the hardship cases would be supported through welfare fund.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
