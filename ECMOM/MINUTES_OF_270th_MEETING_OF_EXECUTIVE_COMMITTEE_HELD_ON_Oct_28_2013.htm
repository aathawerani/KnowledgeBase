MINUTES OF 270TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 28, 2013

 

The 270th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 28, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                             Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                          Registrar

4.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                                 Director Projects

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                              Director Finance

6.      Ayesha A. Menai                                                              Director H.R.

7.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                             Acting Head of ICT

 

Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve supply and installation of vertical transportation for Aman-Tower at IBA City Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that the elevators required for the Aman Tower was part of the electrical package of shell and core construction works. After detailed deliberation, IBA Projects Department proposed that the elevator package should be tendered separately which would save IBA a 30% overhead and profit of the general contractor as well as the double taxation problem will also be solved. The Designers and the Project Managers consented to this proposal and hence the Elevator portion was deleted from the Electrical package for the Aman Tower.

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that Tender advertisement for the Supply and Installation of vertical transportation was published in newspapers on June 2, 2013 and bids were invited as per single stage two envelop procurement procedure. In response to the advertisement following companies collected the tender documents

 

1.      M/s Reliance Engineers

2.      M/s Capital Strategies Group

3.      M/s Ziafco Engineers

4.      M/s Islamabad Industrial Trading Corporation

From above four only following three companies submitted their documents and participated in bidding process:

 

1.      M/s Reliance Engineers

2.      M/s Islamabad Industrial trading corporation

3.      M/s Ziafco Engineers

 

1.3       The technical proposal of the companies was opened in front of their representatives on July 24, 2013. The Director Project formed an evaluation committee to scrutinize the technical proposal of the contractors. The Committee comprised the following three members:

 

1.      Engr. Rehan-Ul-Ambia Riaz,                  Director Projects

2.      Engr. Muhammad Rehan Shaikh,           Senior Manager Project City Campus

3.      Engr. Abdul Rehman,                             Senior Manager Projects (Contracts)

 

After detailed scrutiny of technical proposal the Committee recommended all three firms as qualified for financial bid participation. Approval of the same was obtained from CPC in its meeting held on September 14, 2013.

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that a technical comparison matrix was developed by M/s EA Consulting. The technical comparison was sent to following technical persons in order to verify the technical comparison of the bidders and recommendation of most technically qualified bidder:

 

a.       Manager Maintenance, IBA

b.      Principal Engineer, EA consulting

c.       MEP Supervisor, M/s Halcrow

 

All above three recommended M/s Ziafco as most technically qualified bidder for the subject work.

 

1.5       After approval of Central Purchase Committee of IBA the financial bids were opened in front of representative of three bidders, representatives of M/s Halcrow (Project Managers), representative of IBA Finance Department, representative of IBA Admin Department and representative of projects department. The quoted prices of the bidders were announced as follows:

 

1.      M/s Reliance Engineers                                         Rs.32,470,000/-

2.      M/s Ziafco Engineers                                            Rs.33,146,870/-

3.      M/s Islamabad Industrial Trading Co.                  Rs.45,843,629/-

 

1.6       The Director Projects reported that the bids were sent to EA Consulting for their Evaluation and Scrutiny. M/s EA Consulting evaluated and sent their report. As per their Evaluation they rejected the bid of M/s Reliance Engineering on following ground:

 

1.      Putting up a circumstance that their bid is based on the Dollar exchange through which their bid amount may be increased with the passage of time.

2.      They have quoted different capacity elevator as compared to the BOQ.

 

1.7       After Scrutiny and Arithmetic corrections by EA Consulting following were the standings of the contractors:

 

1.      M/s ZIAFCO Engineers               Rs.33,146,870/- 1st lowest

2.      M/s Reliance Engineers                 Rs.33,340,000/- Rejected due to conditional bid                       

3.      M/s Islamabad Industrial Trading Co.Rs.45,843,629/- 2nd Lowest

 

1.8       The Director Projects informed the EC that based on the recommendations of EA Consulting as per their detailed Evaluation report and technical comparison, M/s Halcrow, Projects Department and Manager Maintenance IBA, that the work may be awarded  to M/s Ziafco Engineers being the lowest evaluated responsive bidder at a cost of Rs.33,146,870/-, the matter was submitted to the Central Purchase Committee for their scrutiny and  approval. In its meeting held on Saturday, October 26, 2013, the CPC also recommended the award of the work of supply and installation of 4 passenger elevators and one service elevator to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Ziafco at Rs.33,146,870/-.  The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve the award on the basis of the recommendations of the Central Purchase Committee. Upon approval the matter will be sent to the Audit and Finance Committee for final approval.

 

1.9       The Dean & Director said that Reliance Engineers have worked with IBA previously too and have provided satisfactory services and the difference in the pricing is too insignificant. He wanted to know if Reliance was asked to withdraw this condition. The Director Projects said that reliance was not asked so. He further said that on the body of the tender Reliance have given a large elevator with exhaust fan at the same price which goes in favour of Reliance. The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to ask Reliance to withdraw their conditions and comply with the tender requirements of submitting unconditional bid.  To ascertain the technical feasibility of the bids he invited the Technical Maintenance Manager Mr. Mushtaq to the meeting.

 

1.10     The Dean & Director asked Mr. Mushtaq if the recommended elevators are reliable. Mr. Mushtaq replied that Chinese goods are generally not so reliable.  He suggested to use Korean made to avoid risk. He said that normally in high rise buildings Chinese elevators are not recommended. He said that the plant of SIGMA elevator offered by Ziafco is located at both China and Korea but the plant they have offered is made in China. The Director Projects said that the motor which is the main thing is coming from Korean and the cable is coming from China. The Director Projects said that the contractor may be asked to supply the motor being a major moving part from Korea.  The Dean & Director said that he would have no problem if the Motor and other moving parts are from Korea and assembled in China but this has to be assured. He said that if Mr. Mushtaq is satisfied about the critical components then it is alright. Mr. Mushtaq said that in the profile of Reliance they do not have any high rise building where they have installed elevators whereas Ziafco has got that. Moreover, the backup services of Reliance is also limited. The Dean & Director said that in such case we should go for Ziafco and talk to them to ensure that their critical components are Korean made. The Director Projects said that he would arrange a presentation by Ziafco on the equipments they would be providing. The Dean & Director asked as to who would inspect the equipments.  The Director Projects replied that one representative from the Consultants and our Manager O&M IBA would go for the pre shipment inspection as well as to witness the operation of the elevator on the test tower at the manufacturer뭩 factory in China.  Mr. Mushtaque informed that the cost difference between Korean and Chinese goods is about 30 to 35%.

 

1.11     Mr. Mushtaq suggested to use LED lamps instead of conventional lamps at Aman Tower.  The Dean & Director agreed and asked the Director Projects to talk to the MD Philips to audit our electrical facilities and see where we can have savings.  He said that the MD Philips is a member of IBA Corporate Advisory Board and has offered this service to IBA in a meeting.  

 

DECISION

 

1.12     The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of work for supply and installation of 4 passenger elevators and 1 service elevator to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Ziafco at Rs.33,146,870/-.

 

ACTION

 

1.13     The Director Projects would ask the contractors to ensure that the critical components related to elevators should be Korean made.

 

1.14     The Director Projects would request the MD Philips to audit our electrical facilities and see where we can have savings.      

 

Item No.2       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Cloud Computing for Disaster Recovery Site.

 

2.1       Mr. Imran Batada reported that since all ICT Services are running from the newly established Tier III Compliance Data Center, they would like to initiate the process of the Disaster Recovery Site as well. He requested the EC to give conceptual approval for Cloud Computing instead of building the Data Center. He informed the EC that this is already in practice in Pakistan in major banks / organizations such as KESC, NBL, NIB, Engro, AKU, and Bank Alfalah etc. They all have outsourced their data center(s) or DR site(s).

 

2.2       Mr. Batada informed the EC that there are numerous business advantages that are linked to outsourcing. IBA can proactively monitor and manage their IT architecture and attain maximum financial and operational efficiencies. The features and benefits also include:

 

Cost advantage

Scalability in IT operations to fulfil the growing business demand

Business continuity

Accountability through SLAs

 

2.3       Mr. Batada requested the EC to grant conceptual approval for outsourcing DR site. He said that all our equipments have completed 3 years and the SLA of Storage and Servers are very costly. He said that they would evaluate if we take it from Cloud provider will it be cost effective or not. For the equipment that has been purchased, he said that he has plan to use for outsourcing services which IBA will provide in the future. He said that at the moment they are providing full racks and they can also provide storage and servers.

 

2.4       The Registrar wanted to know as to how timely activation would be done because one never knows when the disaster may strike.  Mr. Batada replied that if the primary side for any reason is down, the secondary side would immediately be activated.

 

2.5       The Dean & Director said that faculty of Computer Science should be advising us on these technical issues. He said that there should be more interaction between IT faculty and ICT department. This would provide an opportunity of practical hands on training to them. Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked Mr. Batada to get this proposal vetted from IT faculty and get their comments.

 

2.6       The Dean & Director asked if Mr. Batada if he had talked about sub-contracting and prepared brochure. Mr. Batada replied in the affirmative.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee granted conceptual approval for outsourcing Disaster Recovery site.

 

b)         To consider application from Mr. Ali Akber, student of BB-7 for grant of two extra absences due to his eye surgery.

 

2.8       The Registrar said that Mr. Ali Akber, student of BBA-7 has stated that he had to undergo an eye surgery on October 12, 2013. Mr. Ali Akber had stated in his application that the nature of the injury to his eye was such that the operation was impending and could not be put off to a more academically viable time. He further stated that the post-op recovery is intensive and he has been advised complete bed rest for at least two weeks and conforming to certain postures in order to facilitate the recovery.  It is difficult for him to read or write, as well as commute every day to the university.  The procedure was done under general anaesthesia and requires utmost care and caution even post operation. The risk of infection while travelling to and from the university and exposure to the crowded university environment is also a concern for him. Mr. Ali Akber has requested for grant of two extra absences in his courses this semester. The Registrar said that Mr. Ali Akber has attached Medical Certificate from Hashmanis Hospital.

 

2.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that if we have the precedent then we can allow extra absences on such ground.  The Dean & Director said that it is not a matter of precedent, the EC can decide such cases on case to case basis. He said that if we examine the cases and get convinced we can allow the absences. The Director Projects suggested to get more information from Hashmanis on the nature of case. He said that Hashmanis is a big hospital and enjoys good reputation.

 

DECISION

 

2.10     The Committee authorized the Registrar to get more information from Hashmanis on the nature of operation and if it was a major eye operation which required complete bed rest, then the Registrar can allow two extra absences to Mr. Ali Akber in the courses he has taken this semester. 

 

2.11     During the course of the meeting, the Registrar got it confirmed from the Hospital and reported to the EC that Mr. Ali Akber had undergone a major eye surgery.  Accordingly, Mr. Ali Akber was allowed two extra absences in the courses he has taken this semester.

 

c)         Principal and Satellite offices for faculty at both the campuses. 

 

2.12     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that faculty of the same department generally do not meet and interact with one another. He suggested to provide seats to the faculty of the same department at the same place.

 

2.13     The Dean & Director agreed with Dr. Ghani and said that he does not want one department divided into two campuses. He said that every faculty member should have one principal office either at City Campus or Main Campus and then they can have a satellite office which they can share with other faculty members. Satellite office will have 5 to 6 tables and it would not be designated; any faculty member can sit. He said that faculty members can be provided space at five buildings. He proposed that at the Main Campus at Admin Building, Tabba Block, and Aman-CED faculty members of two departments on each building can be provided space and the residual can be placed at other places. At FCS Building at City Campus the Computer Science faculty would be accommodated. There would be a common room or lounge where faculty members can meet and take tea. There should be a tea break of 30 minutes for the faculty and it should be synchronized with the time table.  The Dean & Director advised the Registrar to think about it and ask the faculty members to let him know their preference of the campus for principal office and satellite office which can be implemented when the Admin. Building at the Main Campus is ready.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
