MINUTES OF 305TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD
ON MONDAY, JULY 14, 2014

The 305th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 14, 2014 at 9:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
2. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
3. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
4. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
5. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
6. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

The following items were discussed:

Item No.1 To consider and approve the cost impact of Project Management Fee for External Development Works at IBA Main Campus.

1.1 The Director Projects said that in the last EC meeting while approving extension for three months to the External Development Works at IBA Main Campus, the EC had asked to submit the cost impact of Project Management Fee for the extended period.

1.2 The Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s NESPAK were awarded the work for Project Management Services of External Development works at IBA Main Campus @2.95 % of the contract value. According to the contract agreement the following works were included in the scope of NESPAK and the cost of Project was fixed as Rs.115 million:

      • External Development works
      • Sewage Treatment Plant

1.3 The Director Projects reported that as per contract the total project management fee was Rs.3.3925 million and according to the contract 70% of the fee (Rs.2,374,740) was being paid in equal monthly installments during the execution period of 18 months. The per month instalment is Rs.131,390/- The Project Managers fee cost impact for extended three months is (3 months x Rs.131,390 per month) = Rs.394,170/- He said that as agreed by M/s NESPAK, they would give 20% discount on additional fee i.e. Rs.78,834. Thus the total impact of Project Management Fee for 3 months would be Rs.315,336/- He requested the Executive Committee to approve the cost impact and the extension of 3 months to Nespak.

DECISION

1.4 The Executive Committee approved the extension of three months to M/s Nespak. However, in view of the fact that M/s Nespak are working on so many projects of IBA, the Executive Committee requested the Director Projects to get waiver of the Project Management fee of Rs.315,336/- from M/s NESPAK.


Item No. 2 To consider and advise treatment of claim submitted by M/s. Consult-Tech International on three projects completed by HEC Projects Department.

2.1 The Director Projects reported that the following three projects were undertaken and completed under the supervision of the HEC Projects Department:

      1. Visiting Faculty Hostel       2. New Boys Hostel       3. New Girls Hostel

2.2 The Director Projects reported that M/s. Consult-Tech International (CTI) have claimed the following amount against each of the project for their extended services beyond the contract period in addition to amount as per contract:

      Visiting Faculty Hostel                                          Rs.3,102,081/-
      New Boys Hostel                                                 Rs.2,664,660/-
      New Girls Hostel                                                  Rs.3,211,173/-
                                                     Total                 Rs.8,977,914/-

According to the Consultant the above amount is after reduction of 22%.

2.3 The Director Projects stated that the claim of M/s. CTI had come under dispute while the Director HEC Projects was in office and when he left on 28th February 2014, the matter remained unresolved. Delays on each project claimed by CTI are as follow:

      Project                                          Pre-Construction                        Construction

      Visiting Faculty Hostel                      5.9 months                                Rs.13.37 million
      New Boys Hostel                             5.87    “                                    Rs. 5.60 million
      New Girls Hostel                              11.23  “                                    Rs. 8.03 million

2.4 The Director Projects said that the amount appears exorbitantly high and it would be appropriate to form a Claim Settlement Committee of Finance Department to study the details of the claim and see if the claim is justified or not. He said that the fee of M/s. CTI as per contract is as follows:

      Visiting Faculty Hostel                      Rs. 2,834,506/-
      New Boys Hostel                             Rs.4,270,269/-
      New Girls Hostel                              Rs.3,136,246/-
                                            Total       Rs.10,241,021/-

2.5 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to advise further action in the matter. The Registrar suggested to settle the claim by giving Rs.1.5 million as suggested by Mr. Shameem Ahmed. The Director Projects informed the Committee that the Consultants have not accepted this offer and the matter is under dispute since the days the Director HEC Projects was in office. He further informed that he had also argued with the Consultants but they remained firm on their claim. The present calculation has been submitted by them in response to his request which is 22% lower than the original claim. The Director Projects stated that this claim is still very high and needs to be properly scrutinized by an independent committee. The Executive Committee then should consider the payment on the recommendation of the proposed Claim Settlement Committee. Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that Mr. Syed Jehanzeb and a person from Projects Department should be included in the Claim Settlement Committee. He also suggested that the TOR of the Committee by given by the Director Projects.

DECISION

2.6 The Committee formed a Claim Settlement Committee comprising:

      1. Mr. Shabeeh Haider               Convenor
      2. Mr. Fauza Qazi
      3. Syed Jehanzeb

The Committee will review the claim made by M/s. Consult-Tech International (CTI) and submit its report in two weeks time.

ACTION

2.7 The Director Projects would provide all the related documents to the Claim Settlement Committee and the ToR.

Item No.3 To consider applications from students (a) Abdullah Farooqui, and (b) Arooj Minhas.

3.1 The Registrar reported that Mr. Abdullah Farooqui, student of BBA-2 in his application of June 12, 2014 stated that due to Relapse Typhoid he had been suffering from very high fever and as such could not take Principles of Management exam on June 10, 2014. He requested to take his Principles of Management exam after a few days. He had submitted medical certificate advising him complete best rest.

3.2 The Registrar reported that the Controller of Examinations has noted in his application that only in serious medical condition that requires hospitalization, an ‘I’ (Incomplete) grade is considered for missing the final. The student missed the final of “Principles of Management” due to Relapse Typhoid but it is not a hospitalization case.

3.3 Similarly, the Registrar said that another student Ms. Arooj A. Minhas of MBA-II has submitted an application in which she has stated that that she was suffering from Typhoid and high fever due to which she could not appear in the paper of Financial Modelling. In her case also Mr. Khalili has remarked that it is not a hospitalization case and an ‘I’ (Incomplete) grade in final is considered only in very serious medical cases which require hospitalization.

DECISION

3.4 The Committee authorized the Registrar to thoroughly examine both the cases and take a decision accordingly.

Item No.5 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

                  a) Request from Ms. Shahrukh Samad Siddiqui for waiver of fourth absence.

5.1 Ms. Shahrukh Samad Siddiqui in her application requested to waive off her absence incurred on August 16, 2013 in Principles of Accounting – II taken during Summer Semester 2013. She had stated that the fourth absence was caused due to the accident the medical certificate of which had been verified by IBA. She had requested that the waiver of fourth absence would allow her to complete the course she has been allowed and could complete her graduation on time.

5.2 Dr. Nishat said that the request of Ms. Shahrukh Samad for ‘I’ (incomplete) due to head injury and hospitalization was considered and approved in the 301st meeting. He said that out of the five courses in which she was allowed ‘I’ by the EC, there is only one course i.e. Principles of Accounting – II in which there is an additional absence. The request for waiver of fourth absence in that course is in continuation of the earlier decision.

DECISION

5.3 The Committee allowed fourth absence to Ms. Shahrukh Samad Siddiqui which she incurred in Principles of Accounting – II in Summer 2013 (August 16, 2013) due to accident.
