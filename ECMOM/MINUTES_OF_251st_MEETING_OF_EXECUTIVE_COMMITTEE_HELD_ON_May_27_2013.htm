Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, Mr. M. Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer, and Mr. M. Asad Ilyas, Chairperson, Department of Accounting & Law attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the payment of Mobilization and Secured advance against material at site to DCT Construction.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that his proposals for Construction of Septic Tanks & Soak Pits for New Boys Hostel, Visiting Faculty Hostel, New Girls Hostel and laying of Water supply & Sewerage line in New Boys Hostel, and approval of their cost estimates were approved by the Executive Committee in its meetings held on January 07, 2013.  He said that the Contractor DCT Construction has applied and requested for Mobilization and secure advance against material at site.  However, due to absence of Mobilization and Security advance clauses in the ‘Contract for smaller works’, Contractor is facing financial problems and is unable to procure sufficient material to boost up site progress.

 

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that as construction of Visiting Faculty Hostel & two Blocks of New Girls Hostel is nearly at completion stage, Septic Tanks have to be ready before that.Considering this CPC was requested to approve the Mobilization and Secure advance against insurance guarantees for the material at site. He said that the proposal was considered by CPC in its meeting held on May 18, 2013. CPC approved payment of 70% secured advance, and a mobilization advance of 10% against Bank Guarantee. Mr Shameem said that since a Bank Guarantee is as costly as the Mobilization Advance itself, why a contractor would want to have a Mobilization Advance against a Bank Guarantee. He said that we want to facilitate the Contractor rather than creating hindrances.  He said that both CPC and EC have recently approved payment of a Mobilization Advance to the furniture manufacturer, SIMAB, against an Insurance Guarantee.

 

1.3       The Dean & Director agreed that we should have a consistent policy instead of differentiated policy. He said that if Insurance Guarantees had been accepted why a Bank Guarantee is being stressed upon for which the vendor has to provide equivalent amount of funds as collateral. He advised that as per IBA policy on other projects Insurance should be from ‘A’ rated companies like Adamjee, New Jubilee and EFU. 

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee approved payment of Mobilization and Secured advance to DCT Construction against insurance guarantee from any of the three insurance companies mentioned at 1.3 above.

 

 

Item No. 2      Status Reports from:

 

Mr. M. Jawad Khan:

           

2.1       Requirement of UPS at Aman Tower: Mr. M. Jawad Khan reported that as directed by the Executive Committee, Aman Tower UPS Load reworking was carried out by the Projects and the ICT Departments. Data was analyzed & hence found that instead of 05 big UPS’s, only two small UPS’s of 60 KVA can cater the revised load. He presented the data sheets & comparison to the EC.  

 

2.2       UPS System at City Campus: Mr. Jawad said that following the directive of EC to study the IBA City Campus UPS System, he carried out a detailed study and observed that all three UPS’s were operating at stand alone with no back up & at low efficiency. He proposed that one UPS from the existing system can be spared to Aman Tower hence reducing the cost 5 times. He said that for this provision some modifications and up-gradation work are to be carried out.  He said that he would work out the actual engineering estimate and present it to the EC for approval.

 

Dr. Sayeed Ghani:

 

2.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani to nominate a coordinator for data-base up-date: Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that a part-time student who is Academic Committee Secretary has also been appointed as the coordinator EOS.

 

2.4       The Associate Deans would notify the faculty members about the penalty for late submission of exam results: Dr. Sayeed Ghani informed the Committee that all faculty members would be notified this week that Rs.5000/- will be imposed henceforth. However, he said that some details need to be discussed and clarified regarding the duration after which the fine will be applicable. The proposal is that grades must be posted in ERP within a duration which is calculated as follows: 10 days after the last final exam, or 1 day for grading of 10 scripts, whichever period is longer.

 

Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that if a teacher does not submit result even after the fine then what is the alternative. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested and it was also seconded by Mr. Asad Ilyas that before the decision is imposed, a meeting with faculty should be held in this regard. Mr. Asad Ilyas suggested not to schedule faculty meetings soon after the exams which demand time of the faculty because of which they are not able to check the papers and submit results on time. He said that submission of results should be given first priority.

 

Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the amount of fine should be increased by Rs.5000/- after every 3 days’ of non-submission of results.  However, if there is any problem in entry of results in ERP but the result has physically been presented on time then it would be acceptable and the concerned faculty would be exempted from the fine.

 

Mr. Mobin Khalili said that some visiting faculty come to teach at IBA out of their passion for teaching and not for money.  For them such messages will have a negative impact.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee noted and appreciated that instead of 05 big UPS’s, only two small UPS’s of 60 KVA would cater the revised load of Aman Tower.

 

2.6       The Committee in principle approved the proposal of sparing one UPS from the existing system at City Campus to Aman Tower and advised Mr. Jawad Khan to present the details of the amendments required and its cost within 15 days for approval.

 

ACTION

 

2.7       Mr. Jawad Khan would present the engineering estimate for modification / up-gradation of the UPS system in the EC meeting.

 

2.8       The Associate Deans will have a meeting with full time faculty and a separate meeting with visiting faculty and discuss the issue of late submission of results and the imposition of penalty. The penalty on late submission of results would be effective from the Fall 2013 semester.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the Sports Arena Business Proposal.

 

4.1       Mr. Asad Ilyas in compliance with the earlier suggestion of the Executive Committee presented a business proposal for the Sports Arena. He presented year-wise estimated revenue and expenses over four years period. According to his estimate the expected net earnings in the second year would be Rs.6,107,375/- and at fourth year it would be Rs.16,453,381. However, he clarified that in his calculation he has not included the following:

   

· Cost of Electricity charges on hourly basis

· Cost of Security charges per event

· Revenue from Sports Society

· Sponsorships for IBA teams

· Revenue from night matches.

 

4.2       Mr. Asad Ilyas said that the expenses would remain there and it is upto the management to decide if the expenses are to be met by generating revenue or through IBA funding.

 

4.3       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that what he could understand is that the flood lighting would be at the cost of pavilion. In his opinion pavilion is necessary and it should not be replaced with flood lighting. Mr. Asad Ilyas said that the sequence is changed; first flood lighting would be arranged and then in subsequent years through the revenue generated the pavilion would be built. He said that sheds can be arranged under which people may sit.  Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to where the players would go for change of dress, and for use of washrooms/toilets, if the pavilion is not there. Mr. Asad Ilyas suggested that the gym at the Students’ Centre can be used. The Registrar suggested to arrange separate toilet for males and females and changing room at the pavilion area which was agreed by the EC members. The Registrar requested the Director Projects to provide him the design of the two rooms (which would include separate washrooms for males and females and the changing rooms) and he would get them constructed.  He further requested the Director Projects to provide drainage line near the two rooms to be constructed.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee decided that after incorporating the electricity charges (rate to be provided by the Director Projects to Mr. Asad Ilyas to re-calculate the estimated expenses and inform the EC through email) if there is a net profit, then we would go for the flood lighting. In such case the Director Projects would try to complete flood lighting for cricket ground instead of pavilion, two rooms including separate toilets, changing rooms, washrooms, sheds etc within the budget of Rs.10 million.  

 

ACTION

 

4.5       Subject to the favourable balance (net profit) the Registrar would get two rooms constructed at the pavilion area which would include separate toilets for males and females, and changing rooms.

 

4.6       The Director Projects would provide the design of the shade over the existing seating area and the two rooms with Wash rooms to the Registrar.

5.3       Mr. Batada said that usually labs are used for conducting classes (like classes of programming and networking), therefore students complain about the non-availability of lab. However, he hoped that this issue would be resolved after the new labs are constructed. He said that the strength of students will increase in future (computer science as well as in other disciplines) then again further labs would be needed to maintain computer-student ratio according to HEC criteria.

 

5.4       The Registrar suggested that the lab incharge should be very competent and strong man.  The Dean & Director agreed with the Registrar and said that he wants quality people in the labs because it is academic part not IT part.  He suggested to involve Mr. Sharaf Hussain in the development of computer labs.

 

5.5       The Registrar said that there are 20 lab stations at the Students Centre for which 20 PC’s would be required.  Mr. Batada confirmed that computers are available. 5.7       The Committee appreciated the proposal prepared by Mr. Sharaf Hussain and Mr. Imran Batada for new labs.

ACTION

5.10     Mr. Imran Batada would provide 20 PC’s for the lab stations at Students Centre.

 

 

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair:

 

a)      Misuse of printers and stationery items.

 

6.1       It was reported that at faculty lounge printer and stationary items are being misused. The Dean & Director suggested that there should be a centralised resource centre where all these services should be available against a requisition. He suggested that program offices can be asked to do this service. He said that it would serve two purposes; discourage printing, and avoid misuse of papers and toners. He said that if there is any problem in obtaining requisition form then there should be some system of keeping track of the usage of stationery items. Similarly, colour printer should be centralized; one at Main Campus and one at City Campus.

 

6.2       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Batada to let him know the names of those faculty members who are not using SAKAI.

 

DECISION

 

6.3       The Committee decided to keep track of the usage of stationery items specially the printing papers, so that in case of excess use, justification can be obtained from the concerned person.

 

ACTION

 

6.4       Mr. Imran Batada would devise a tracking mechanism and brief the EC about its functioning.

 

6.5       Mr. Imran Batada would convey the names of those faculty members who are not using SAKAI.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
