Item No.2       To consider and approve Trust Deed for IBA employees Provident Fund.

 

2.1       The Director Finance presented the following highlights of the IBA employees Provident Fund Rules as vetted by the legal advisor:

 

·         The institute is empowered to appoint new or additional trustees, and to remove any trustee at the Institute’s discretion.

 

·         There will be a minimum three Trustees and not more than six.

 

·         IBA will appoint the first Trustees.

 

·         The trustees shall have administration and control over the fund, responsible for the safe custody and investments of its properties and assets.

 

·         The trustees shall not be entitled to any remuneration.

 

·         To invest the fund’s monies.

 

·         Operate and maintain bank account.

 

·         To frame rules for the operation and administration of the fund.

 

·         The Meeting shall be convened by the Chairman.

·         All eligible employees of IBA are entitled to become member of the fund. (Employees above 60 will not be eligible to become member.)

 

·         The management of the Fund and the control of its Funds shall be vested in not less than three and not more than six Trustees who will undertake such management without remuneration. 

 

·         Every member during the period of his membership shall make a monthly contribution to the fund at the rate given hereunder:

 

1.      Government based pay scale employees   @ 12% of basic pay

2.      IBA Based pay scale employees               @ 5% of standard Gross pay

 

·         At the end of each month, IBA shall contribute to the fund for IBA based pay scale employees, a sum equal to the aggregate contribution to the members who are under IBA based pay scale.

·         The money payable to any member from the fund under shall be paid within three months of claim.

 

·         The trustees shall open and maintain a bank account for the fund which shall be operated jointly by any two trustees.

 

·         The Accounts of the Fund shall be made upto the 30th day of June of each year and audited by the external auditors.

 

·         The expenses incurred on the Fund shall be borne by the income generated from the fund. IBA shall not borne any expenses.

 

·         The trustee will make the payment in the event if the employee leaves IBA or to his nominee in case of death.

 

·         Applicable income taxes shall be deducted by the trustees on making payments

 

·         The trustees are empowered to make investments in Government Securities and Commercial Banks.

 

·         Withdrawals would be allowed as per income tax rules 103, 104 and 105 of Income Tax ordinance 2002.

 

2.2       Dr. Muhammad Nishat wanted to know if a person who after retirement continues to serve, will be allowed to contribute or not.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that it will have to be checked if a retired employee wants to make voluntary contribution will that be able or not.

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee noted and approved the Eligible IBA Employees Provident Fund Rules.

 

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve extension time of Contractor for renovation and refurbishment work and extension time of Project Manager Services of Administration Building at City Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the contractor was given the extension up till July 2011 in order to complete the extended work (area increased by 5400Sft). The work was delayed due to the following reasons which have been substantiated and duly verified by the Consultant, Project Manager and IBA Project Manager Engr. Umar M. Makhdoomi. He said that the detailed reasons for delays are provided in Project Manager’s (M/s INTEG ) letter summary of which is as under:

 

·       Server room on 1st floor was completely handed over in May 2011

 

·       After shifting of PABX from ground floor in April 2011 the area was handed over to contractor

 

·         Due to change in furniture layout of telecommunication lab, related work of data and electrical points were redesigned and the entire work had to be stopped in the room. Moreover, the color scheme for wall was also changed by end user in October 2011.

 

·         Additional points for UPS had to be provided in all seven faculty rooms on 1st floor during end September 2011 which were not in the original drawing, to cater for two faculty members in each room in place of one.

 

·        Additional work for Public address system had to be incorporated

 

·        The laying of UPS cable was delayed as shut down for the same could not be provided up till October 2011. Furthermore, administrative procedures were involved in arranging rental generator to ensure uninterrupted working of temporary data center during the 36-hour shut down;

 

·       Testing commissioning of chiller could not be done due to non availability of power load

 

·        Law and order situation affected the progress of work in July and August 2011 as a substantial number of labor force was living in the worst-affected areas and thus could not reach the work site for some days.

 

·        Heavy rains affected the work Progress in September 2011.

 

3.2       For closure of contract the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the above facts and approve the extension time of contract up till October 31, 2011 so that the Projects Department can issue the substantial completion certificate to the contractor.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what was the original date of completion of this project.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that the original contract was awarded in October 2009 which was to be completed in 12 months but due to increase in covered area the date was extended till July 2011 but it was completed in October 2011 for various reasons.

 

3.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that since the work has been completed in October 2011 the EC may give post facto approval for extension.

 

3.6       The Dean & Director said that it not only includes extension in completion date but also the financial implications because we have to pay to the Project Manager also.

 

3.7       The Director Projects said that Project Management services was provided by the M/s INTEG. He said that the contractual and financial closure of the Project take another two months in order to check the final bill of the contractor, Punch list items, measurements and left over rectification works. He said that the Project Management services charges for additional two months will be paid as per existing contract. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time of M/s INTEG from July 2011 to October 31, 2011 and February 2012 to March 2012. The total extension required for M/s INTEG is 6 Months and the net cost impact will be Rs.130,135 x 6 months = Rs.780,810/-. He said that they have now included in the agreement that two months before the start and two months after the completion of the project the Project Managers will have to provide services.

 

3.8       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the punch list has not been provided. He asked if the things which were to be redone have been completed or not.

 

3.9       The Director Projects said that the Contractor is bound to do that and is doing that.

 

DECISION

 

3.10     The Committee approved extension in time for Project Manager Services for Admin. Building, City Campus.

 

3.11     The Committee asked the Director Projects to negotiate with M/s Integ and reduce the amount of Rs.780,810 to the extent possible.
