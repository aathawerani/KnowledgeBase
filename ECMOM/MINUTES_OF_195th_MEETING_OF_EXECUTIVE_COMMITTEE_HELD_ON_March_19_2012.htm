MINUTES OF 195TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON

MONDAY, MARCH 19, 2012

 

The 195th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 19, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Societies Meeting Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                         Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                               Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                 Controller of Examinations

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                                      Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                   Director Projects

6.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                               Director ICT

7.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                           Director CED

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       Conceptual approval for procurement of Active Components of Razzak Tabba Academic Building (new Academic Building).

 

1.1       The Director ICT said that Razzak Tabba Academic Building (the new Academic Block) at Main Campus is nearing completion and shall be handed over to IBA by September 2012. The passive work related to ICT infrastructure in the buildings was already a part of BoQ. However, the active Components i.e. switches, IP Phones, Wi-Fi Access Points, Multimedia Projectors etc. are to be procured separately. He presented the BoQ with estimated price of the Active Components.

 

1.2       The Director ICT said that Rs.10 million was provided for in the 2011-12 budget. However, since this building will be completed after June 2012, the budget was reallocated for the Aman CED Building ICT active components, which is nearing completion. He requested the EC to grant conceptual approval for the procurement of equipment; as per the BoQ so that the equipment be procured by the time the building is ready to be handed over to make it “ICT enable”.  He clarified that the proposal would be presented and approved by the TEC and CPC.  He further said that he would give the time line for commission and installation of active components in the next EC meeting.

 

DECISION

 

1.3       The Committee granted conceptual approval for procurement of Active Components for Razzak Tabba Academic Building (new Academic Building).

 

ACTION

 

1.4       The Director ICT would present the proposal for procurement of Active Components along with BoQ to the TEC and CPC for approval.

 

1.5       The Director ICT would present the time line for procurement and fixation of Active Components in the next EC meeting.

Versant was rejected at the time of bid opening as they did not submit the earnest money pay order and instead of Pay Order they submitted the insurance bond. 2.4       The Director Projects reported that Architects M/s. NBCL had recommended the award of work to the second highest bidder M/s. Interwood Mobel at Rs.18.394 million, which was1% lower than the Architects’ estimate and 35% higher than the bid of lowest bidder M/s. Habib & Sons at Rs.13.60 million. He said that the Architects had based their recommendations on their apprehension about the quality of furniture to be produced by M/s. Habib & Sons as they had not visited their workshop and showroom.

 

2.5       The Director Projects informed the Committee that he, along with Engr. Abdul Rehman of Projects Department, visited the Workshop and Showroom of M/s. Habib & Sons on 8th and 9th March 2012 and found them to be of adequate and good standard. The only difference between M/s. Habib & Sons and M/s. Interwood Mobel was that of the “brand” name.

 

2.6       The Director Projects reported that the case was presented in the combined meeting of TEC, and CPC on March 13, 2012. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, Registrar IBA and Mr. Zafar Ahmed Siddiqui, Director CED were also invited to oversee the proceedings and provide their valued input in the decision making. M/s. Zayd and Ahsan, and Ms. Saadia, the Architects of NBCL were also present. After detailed discussions and deliberations both the Committees recommended to award the work to the lowest responsive bidder M/s Habib & Sons at a cost of Rs.13,600,971/-. This was also agreed by the Director CED, the Registrar and the Architects.

 

2.7       The Director Projects reported that based on the recommendations of CPC, TEC, the Registrar and the Director CED, and in anticipation of the approval of the EC, they have issued the work order to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s. Habib & Sons at a cost of Rs.13,600,971/- on March 15, 2012 as waiting for the EC meeting would have further delayed the procurement process.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee endorsed the award of work order to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s. Habib & Sons at a cost of Rs.13,600,971/- for supply and fixing of furniture at Aman CED Building, Main Campus.

 

 

Item No.3       Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that in the weekly meeting to monitor the progress of IBA Data Centre held on March 14, 2012 the following two points were discussed:

 

·         M/s. H.K Shah informed that there were lot of typo or calculation mistakes in the two bids received and suggested that IBA should call the lowest bidder informally and sort out all concerns before issuing any formal request.

 

·         The data centre of Main Campus was discussed in context of Tier 3 and Tier 2 scope and costing. It was decided that the structural layout of the two tiers will remain same so that in future any up-gradation or expansion can be done easily and swiftly.  In light of the decision, HK Shah revised the layouts for the Main Campus and re-submitted it to IBA ICT for final approval so that once approved, work can be done on the financial aspect of both tier 2 and 3 designs.

 

3.2       The Director ICT said that the data centre at the Main Campus will be the Disaster Recovery Centre for which tier 2 will sufficiently meet the requirements.

 

DECISION

 

3.3       The Committee noted the up-date and asked the Director ICT to present a comprehensive note with corrected figures of the bid for the approval of the award of work to the lowest responsive bidder for the data centre at City Campus. The Committee also decided that the Director ICT may ask the Consultant to come and give a presentation to the EC if he so desires.

 

ACTION

 

3.4       The Director ICT would present a comprehensive note with corrected figures of the bid to the EC for consideration and recommendation to the A&FC/BoG for approval of the award of work to the lowest responsive bidder for the data centre at City Campus.

 

 

Item No.4       Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Cloud Service Agreement.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that they have deployed all our ERP Services to Cloud since our Data Center is not ready yet. The Executive Committee had given an extension in cloud computing agreement in its meeting on November 21, 2011, for four and a half months i.e. till March 15, 2012. The existing contract has expired on March 15, 2011. He requested the EC to extend the contract of Cloud Computing till the completion of the IBA City Campus Data Center. He said that the charges for PeopleSoft Campus Solution Hardware Provisioning & Hosting are $5,319.00 per month. He reported that the agreement can be terminated by either party at a fifteen (15) days’ notice.

  

DECISION

 

4.2       The EC considering that the Data Centre at the City Campus would be ready by June 30, 2012, allowed extension in Cloud Computing agreement upto 4 months w.e.f. March 16, 2012.

 

b)     Request for a make-up exam.

 

4.3       The Executive Committee considered the application of Ms. Sadia Shamim, a BBA-IV student who could not appear in the exam of MIS course held on March 13, 2012 due to the sudden demise of her grandmother the previous day of the exam and her (grandmother’s) burial on the exam day i.e. March 13, 2012. 

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee considered the application of Ms. Sadia very sympathetically and decided that as the rule does not permit make-up exam under the stated situation, Ms. Sadia may withdraw from that course before the second term exam.  

 

ACTION

 

4.5       The Program Office would convey the decision of EC to Ms. Sadia Shamim.

 

 

c)      Inclusion of Synergy (advertising agency) in the short-listed agencies.

 

4.6       The Director CED reported that they had short listed three advertising agencies and it was approved by the EC. However, after sometime, one of the agencies was not responding to /participating in IBA’s ad works. He reported that they have now included Synergy in its place. Now there are three short listed agencies namely; (1) Argus, (2) Orient, and (3) Synergy.      

 

DECISION

 

4.7       The Committee noted the inclusion of Synergy (ad agency) in the approved list of short listed ad agencies.

 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
