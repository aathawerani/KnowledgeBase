1.2       Admin Building: The Director Projects said that there has been slippage on floor-wise completion date of Admin Building. However, he said that by November 30, 2014 the Admin. Building would be completed. With reference to the email from Dr. Nasir Touheed wherein he had asked for the implementation of penalty clause in case of delay in completion of project, the Director Projects said that he has written to the Project Manager to suggest what should be the contractual remedy.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that the Head of Internal Audit has suggested some changes in his department’s offices as according to him the office space is insufficient. The Committee decided that at this stage no change can be made.

 

1.4       The Director Projects said that the Manager Purchase has requested that his Department should be attached to the Stores which is housed at NBP Technology Centre. He said that the space allocated for Purchase Department at Admin Building would be available.  The Registrar said that he would utilize that space.  However, the Director Projects requested to allow Projects Department to utilize that space for the time being.  The Registrar agreed.  

 

1.5       NBP Technology Centre: The Director Projects reported that for the over-run cost due to new ACs, the A&FC wanted some explanation which was provided. However, the A&FC was not satisfied and a meeting of A&FC is scheduled on October 24, 2014 to discuss this issue.

 

1.6       Mr. Imran Batada reported that for the Data Centre at NBP Technology Centre, there was no response on the first bid. On the second bid there was only one bidder namely Silicon which offered a bid of Rs.22 million whereas the estimate was of Rs.10 million.  He said that in consultation with Mr. Mushtaque they did value engineering and informed Silicon that they have the budget of Rs.10 million and asked as to what they could do.  He hoped that the response from Silicon would be received by next week after which he would present the case to the EC.

 

1.7       The Director Projects said that there is an issue of power and cable for the Data Centre. The Registrar said that the data centre is already in operation.  He said that the data centre at Main Campus will be very different in terms of environment, air-conditioning but as far as equipment is concerned, he said that no server or switches would be required and there was no need to move the equipment; the only thing that was needed was to improve its environment. He was not in favour of spending Rs.10 or Rs.15 million.  Mr. Imran Batada said that they have to ensure that the risk of any mishap is minimised for which investing Rs.10 to Rs.15 million on a project of over Rs.70 million is not unreasonable. The Committee decided that this matter would be discussed in the next meeting while considering the response from Silicon.

 

1.8       Transport Shed (transport facilities building): The Director Projects said that they have suggested to the Architect not to provide shed at the washing area. The Registrar said that shed was needed but it could be of low cost. 

 

1.9       Main Lawn: The Registrar suggested that the paver around the Main Lawn should be placed. Mr. Mahmood Ali said that the pavers are available and they would get them placed.  

 

DECISION

 

1.10     The Committee noted and approved the up-date on the Monthly Progress along with the suggestions noted above.

 

Item No. 2      Up-date on Aman Tower – September 2014.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that 97% of the Civil & Architecture Works of Phase-I (Core & Shell) were executed in September 2014 while 90% MEP Works Phase-I (core & Shell) were completed during the same period.  The Internal Decoration works of Phase-II were 65% complete against the target of 70% for September 2014. Approval for furniture works has been obtained from A&FC and the work has been awarded. It would be completed in 5½ months by February 20, 2015. He said that as advised by A&FC Tender for Gen-set has been cancelled and is being retendered.  Kitchen contract has been approved and awarded. Duration of contract is four months.

 

2.2       The Director Projects said that they are facing the following hurdles in the completion of the project:

o   Delay in arrival of lifts

o   Delay in approval of 12th floor Electrical Drawing

o   Delay in shipment of HVAC Pumps

o   Design of multi-media training Centre is in process; half of fourth floor has been on hold by IBA since March 25, 2014.

o   Delay in obtaining external connection of water, gas, electricity.

o   Delays in payment to the contractors.

 

2.3       The Director Projects hoped that all those hurdles will be overcome by end of November 2014.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee noted the up-date on Aman Tower.

 

Item No.3       To discuss and decide about the issuance of work order to Firco who won the             competition in 2013 for design of faculty apartment.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that in 312th meeting of Executive Committee held on 8th September 2014, the matter pertaining to issuance of the work order to the successful firm of Architects was submitted along with the request for allocation of funds for the architects’ fee for faculty apartment. The Executive Committee had asked for the approval of the design competition and the status of any agreement if signed with M/s. FIRCO.

 

3.2       The Director Projects reported that the documents in this regard were searched from the record of HEC Projects’ Department as the competition was held by that Department in February 2013.  It was found from the record that although the evaluation process was completed and that the lowest evaluated technically and financially qualified Consultants were declared as M/s. FIRCO, no further action was taken by HEC Projects’ Department. There was no approval from either Procurement Committee or the Executive Committee for award of work.

 

3.3       The Director Projects said that as per SPPRA Rule No.38 the work order should have been issued to the successful firm within validity period. This was not done and the matter is now time-barred.  No work order can be issued as it violates the SPPRA rule. He said that the whole process of the competition will have to be initiated again and the previous competition will have to be declared null and void.  He requested the Executive Committee to consider and advise further action.

 

3.4       The Registrar asked if any payment was involved in the design of faculty apartment.  The Director Projects replied that no payment was involved as it was a conceptual design.

 

DECISION

 

3.5       The Committee decided that since it is a time barred case, no action is required.

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve the submission of Schematic layout drawing for        conversion of existing canteen and lounge into Gymnasium at City Campus. 

 

4.1       The Director Projects said that as desired by the Executive Committee, they had asked M/s. ICON, the appointed Consultant of the External Development Work of City Campus, for preparation of layout for gymnasium to be built in place of the existing Canteen and Lounge at the City Campus. The Director Projects presented the layout prepared by the Consultant.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that in the earlier meeting the Executive Committee had decided that such layout will be reviewed and finalized by the Registrar IBA and whenever needed the gymnasium will be built by IBA itself.

 

DECISION

 

4.3       The Registrar said that the whole canteen cannot be converted into gymnasium.  He said that some portion would be utilized for Staff canteen. He said that he would use the design and get the gymnasium built internally.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
