Item No.1       To consider and approve time extension to M/s. Badar Engineering for completion of NBP Technology Centre façade face lifting work at Main Campus.

 

1.1              Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi reported that the Executive Committee in its 310th meeting held on August 26, 2014 approved extension of time to M/s Badar Engineering for completion of NBP Technology Centre façade face lifting work till December 26, 2014. The case was submitted for approval of A&FC on September 1, 2014 but the A&FC considered it and approved the additional work on October 24, 2014 by that time the work was on hold. After that contractor was given go-ahead for work, contractor team for band plaster which is a specialized job was engaged somewhere else. The contractor again mobilized old team to start work in one month time. Additional work approval for 4 months already given by EC from August 26, 2014 to December 26, 2014. The work was resumed in November 2014.

 

1.2       Mr. Mahmood Ali reported that the Contractor almost completed their work except final coat of paint and sealer on band plaster due to working of other internal finishing work         and external development work in progress around the building. The internal finishes    work was delayed due to late shifting of various departments in Admin Building, ICT             department shifted on first floor in end of April 2015. Internal finishes of these areas are       in progress and near to completion.  

 

1.3       Mr. Amin Ashraf said that the case was presented in the Procurement Committee which recommended for extension in time upto 31st March 2015 for work on NBP Technology Center (done by M/s Badar Engineering).

 

1.4       Based on the recommendations of Project Department and Procurement Committee Mr.   Amin Ashraf requested the Executive Committee to approve the Extension in time up     till March 31, 2015.

 

1.5       Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi clarified that this extension of time would be without    reimbursement of the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional       overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall have to   extend all insurances and guarantees, related to the Contract, accordingly.

 

1.6       Mr. Mahmood Ali suggested that since the final coat of paint and sealer on band   plaster work is in progress, and the punch list items are being handled, the extension may be     granted till June 30, 2015.

 

1.7       To a query from EC member, Mr. Mahmood Ali clarified that there is no supervision cost involved because it is being done in-house and also there is no cost escalation. He further          said that the delay was due to internal issues not because of the contractor. 

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.8       The Committee approved the time extension to M/s. Badar Engineering till June 30, 2015.

 

1.9       Mr. Amin Ashraf Qureshi would report to the A&FC about the time extension to M/s Badar Engineering along with the final cost of completion. 

 

Item No.2       Presentation on GPS Tracking of IBA vehicles.

 

2.1       The Registrar said that he had been facing a problem of more fuel consumption than actual running of the vehicles. He said that Mr. Khalid Javed Rishi recently attended a workshop where he also came to know about the fuel monitoring solution  a system through which time check, location check and fuel consumption level of the vehicle is possible. The solution is available with Tracking World  a device partner of Telenor. He said that Mr. Sohail Ghouri of Tracking World and the representative of Telenor have been invited to make a presentation on the features available in the system.

 

2.2       Mr. Sohail Ghouri said that there are two types of monitoring; fuel level monitoring and fuel consumption. The system can generate a report as well as can send a SMS providing information on when re-fuelling was done  the time, from which station it was done, how much fuel was filled and how much fuel was consumed. He said that a fuel level sensor is placed on fuel tank which records the above noted information. He said that GPS device connected with Telenor makes recording and pushes data on the server. It also tells about the speed of the vehicle, its location, time, and fuel availability.  He said that there is a system of CCTV also through which the boarding and de-boarding of persons is monitored.

 

2.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked if the system can be customized.  Mr. Ghouri confirmed that the system is customizable. Mr. Imran Batada asked if the information can be shifted to IBAs server. Mr. Ghouri confirmed that the data can be pushed to IBAs server too.

 

2.4       Mr. Ghouri said that they have drivers tracking option also.  The representative of Telenor briefed about the features of RFID based biometric system of attendance. However, Mr. Imran Batada said that the tender has already been opened and its results are published on SPPRA (as per the process). Shortly, it would be awarded to the lowest technically qualified bidder.

 

2.5       Mr. Ghouri said that the monthly charges for fuel monitoring solution is Rs.1000 per vehicle and the following are the one time costs for the device:

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee liked the fuel monitoring solution and asked Mr. Ghouri of Tracking World to install the device in one of the IBA vehicles for trial. Mr. Ghouri agreed to provide and install the device free of cost for 10 days for demonstration purpose.
