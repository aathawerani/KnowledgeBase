MINUTES OF THE 118TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, AUGUST 30, 2010

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve Renovation and Refurbishment of existing FCS Building at IBA City Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects stated that for the Renovation and Refurbishment of existing FCS Building at City Campus M/s. ICON being the Architect for this work initially submitted the cost estimates in October, 2008 for Ground + 04 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 10,050 Sq-ft which came to Rs. 42.835 Million. The estimated cost was purely based on the refurbishment works and no new extension work was included. Later on, the Executive Committee in its 81st meeting held on 31st December, 2009 decided not to construct 4th Floor because of technical reasons concerned to the structural safety. The same decision was conveyed to M/s. ICON on 6th January, 2010. The second cost estimate was submitted by M/s. ICON in May, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 28,620 Sq-ft which comes to Rs.110.675 Million. The above cost was based on both the refurbishment works as well as new extension works. Major errors were found in the same estimates which were then communicated to M/s. ICON. The third cost estimate was submitted by M/s. ICON in June, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors (including 02 Nos. lifts) for an area of 32,719 Sq-ft which came to Rs. 101.472 Million. The case was taken to the Executive Committee for approval in its 107th meeting held on 14th June, 2010. The Committee did not approve the cost estimate of the building as submitted by M/s. ICON and advised that the renovation cost of the old area be restricted to Rs.2,000/- per Sq-ft and the new area construction should be restricted to a maximum of Rs.3,600/- per Sq-ft. The same decision was conveyed to M/s. ICON on 28th June, 2010. Finally, the fourth cost estimate was received from M/s. ICON in July, 2010 for Ground + 03 Floors which includes the refurbishment as well as new construction with the following three (03) options:

 

OPTION NO. 1:

Total Estimated Cost of G + 03 Floors         = Rs.46.528 Million.

Refurbishment area                                  = 10,982 Sq-ft

New construction area                             = 13,700 Sq-ft

Total area                                                        = 24,682 Sq-ft

 

(In Option No.1, the refurbishment of Ground Floor measuring an area of 3,422 Sq-ft is not included; whereas the refurbishment of 1st, 2nd & 3rd Floors is included. The new extension of all Ground +03 Floors is also included).

 

OPTION NO. 2:

 

Total Estimated Cost of G + 03 Floors         = Rs.52.630 Million

Refurbishment area                                  = 13,009 Sq-ft

New construction area                             = 13,700 Sq-ft

Total area                                                        = 26,709 Sq-ft

 

(In Option No.2, the complete refurbishment as well as the new extension of Ground + 03 Floors is included).

 

OPTION NO. 3:

 

Total Estimated Cost of G + 03 Floors         = Rs.47.415 Million.

Refurbishment area                                  = 13,009 Sq-ft

New construction area                              = 13,700 Sq-ft

Total area                                                        = 26,709 Sq-ft

 

(In Option No.3, the complete refurbishment of Ground + 03 Floors is included; whereas the 2nd Floor structure is included).

 

1.2       The Director Projects also presented the breakup of the cost estimates of the above three (03) options.

 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that Associate Dean, FCS has shown his interest in Option No.2 as top priority, however Option No.3 may be considered as an alternate in view of budget constraints. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider and approve one of the above cost estimates among the three (03) options.

 

1.4       The Director said that they should see the total space requirements at the City Campus for the next 10 years and also see how much the existing facilities are catering for and what the new buildings would be providing for. He also wanted to know the cost of the extension of the two wings only in the FCS Building.

 

1.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that he needed two labs (hardware and teaching labs).

 

1.6       The Director Projects said that if the old structure is renovated, it would be completed within Rs.25 million and if two labs are added, then the total cost would be around Rs.40 million.   

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee decided that the ground floor offices, floors and external facade be changed on the basis of the original drawing and extension on the 3rd floor be made for adding two labs.  The Committee requested the Director Projects to complete all the work within Rs.40 million without compromising on the quality and the facade should match the other two buildings i.e. Academic and Admin Buildings at the City Campus.

 

           

Item No. 2      To consider and approve appointment of Project Managers for detailed supervision and Project Management of new Academic Block at IBA Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects stated that matter with regard to appointment of Project Managers for detailed supervision and Project Management of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus along with a Comparative Statement was discussed in the EC in its 112th meeting held on 19th July, 2010.  The Committee in that meeting had observed that the composite bid evaluation method was not prescribed in RFP as well as in the pre-bid meeting as such the decision on this basis may not be acceptable to the bidders.  The Committee thus had referred the proposal to the TEC for re-evaluation of the proposal both on technical and financial grounds.  Accordingly, the complete case was submitted to the TEC for final selection of the successful Project Manager and recommendation to the Executive Committee.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that the TEC in its meeting held on July 28, 2010 decided to invite M/s. EA Consulting (Private) Limited & M/s. AAA Partnership as they had secured the maximum technical score of 92 Points & 89 Points respectively and asked them to provide their best financial rates. M/s. EA Consulting (Private) Limited has given the rate of 3.90% of the total cost of the Project; whereas M/s. AAA Partnership has given 3.80% of the total cost of the Project. The Director Projects informed the Technical Evaluation Committee that M/s. AAA Partnership has employed a highly experienced Construction Manager and added him in his team because of which their technical capabilities has increased.  Secondly, they had made a better presentation than M/s. EA Consulting (Private) Limited when both were invited for technical qualification meeting.

 

2.3       Based on the above, the TEC in its meeting held on 16th August, 2010 decided to contact M/s. AAA Partnership and find out if they were ready to do the job at 3.50%; otherwise they may be awarded the job as per their existing rate of 3.80%.  M/s. AAA Partnership agreed to do the job and sent their revised offer letter at 3.50% of the total cost of the Project or Rs.7 (Seven) Million lump sum.  The Director Projects informed the Committee that the CPC in its meeting held on August 27, 2010 has approved the final selection of M/s. AAA Partnership as a successful Project Manager at the rate of 3.50% of the total cost of the Project.

 

2.4       The Director observed that if they are not considering M/s. EA Consulting, they should give cogent reasons for not considering them.  Similarly if they are not satisfied with M/s. Integ it should be documented.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee advised the Director Projects to send the proposal (a self-contained paper) for appointment of Project Managers to the Head of Internal Audit for his advice.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and recommend to the A&FC:

 

a)      The proposal for Deferred Benefits.

 

3.1       Mr. Adnan Hamid, Assistant Manager H.R. presented the proposal for deferred benefits for IBA Contract employees. This included (a) Provident Fund, and (b) Gratuity Scheme (unfunded). He said that in the proposed Provident Fund both employee and employer would contribute to the fund on monthly basis. 

 

3.2       Mr. Adnan Hamid presented the eligibility criteria and the proposed date of effectiveness of the unfunded Gratuity Scheme i.e. October 1, 2010.  He clarified that any service prior to the proposed effective date would be considered as qualifying period and employees would be placed into their respective level accordingly. The gratuity of the employees who join IBA after the proposed date of effectiveness would be calculated from their date of joining.  He also presented the table showing the Level, Service Length, and the Gratuity Rate (percentage of last drawn gross salary).

 

3.3       The Director Finance reported that currently only the regular employees are contributing towards the Provident Fund; there is no contribution from IBA.

 

3.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there would be a discrimination with the Option ‘A’ employees by allowing Gratuity and contribution to the Provident Fund from the IBA fund which is not available to regular employees.

 

3.5       The Director clarified that there would be no discrimination as the Option “A” employees would be getting pension also.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee decided that the effective date of the unfunded Gratuity Scheme would be January 1, 2011.

 

3.7       The Committee recommended the proposal for Provident Fund and the unfunded Gratuity Scheme to the A&FC/BoG for approval.

 

ACTION

 

3.8       The Director H.R would present the proposal for Provident Fund and the Gratuity Scheme to the A&FC for consideration.

c)      Reversal of 48 Annual Earned Leaves for regular Option ‘A’ employees.

 

3.13     The Director H.R. stated that the Government rules allow employees 48 days Earned Leave per year without provision of encashment except at the time of retirement. However, Karachi University and NED University offer 48 days leave and allow encashment of 30 days Earned Leave.  She said that the IBA employees had this benefit until December 2003 when the Board reduced their earned leave to 30 (all encashable).  She recommended that in view of the fact that this benefit is available in other institutions and was also available to IBA regular employees in the past, the regular Option ‘A’ employees may be given 48 days earned leave of which 30 days leave would be encashable in a year. She said that there would be no financial impact of this change.

DECISION

 

3.14     The Committee agreed to the proposal for allowing 48 days earned leave in a year out of which 30 to be encashable for Option “A” employees and recommended to the A&FC / Board for approval. 

 

ACTION

3.15     The Director H.R. would present the recommendation to the A&FC for consideration.

 

 

d)     Proposed Amendments in Staff Welfare Fund.

 

3.16     The Director H.R. suggested the following amendments in the Staff Welfare Fund:

 

·         Fully funded scholarship will be offered to children of IBA employees in grades 1-5 or equivalent grades who obtain admission in top professional education institutions e.g. Aga Khan University, LUMS etc.

·         Minimum salary requirement for Staff Welfare Fund membership increased to Rs.40,000/- (Previously it was Rs.25,000/-)

·         Requirement of two guarantors in case a contract employee applies for loan (previously the requirement was of one guarantor)

 

3.17     The Director said that the requirement of two guarantors would be for the initial years and once the amount of deferred benefits are equal to the amount of loan then no guarantor would be required.

 

DECISION

 

3.18     The Committee recommended the proposed amendments to the A&FC for consideration.

 

ACTION

 

3.19     The Director H.R. would present the proposed amendment in the Staff Welfare Fund to the A&FC for consideration.

 

 

Item No. 4      Approval for purchase of Server for the ERP Project.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that they had acquired a Nehalem based Dell demo Server from their vendor for UAT services which has been used by ICT/IS Department and is being used extensively by the ERP team for Testing for Fall Semester Registration Process, and Development of reports for registration and Financial System.  He said that since the Dell was on demo for a limited period the vendor has been requesting them to return the system. Now after continuous requests, the Server has to be returned back to the vendor.

 

4.2       The Director ICT further said that the existing Server machines with the ICT department do not have enough capacity to run the ERP services, therefore there is a dire need to acquire one more machine. He said that there are two ways of handing this need:

 

a)      Either through outright purchase of the Dell Nehalem Server, or

b)      Acquire one additional server from the cloud network of Ufone.

The Financial Module includes generation of vouchers and payment of fees which should be performed before the start of the semester so that students should be able to pay their fees through the ERP System. He said that to initiate this activity they need to have one system either on Cloud or in their premises with the specifications below so that they can perform test run and start the registration of students on time:
