MINUTES OF 178TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 14, 2011

 

The 178th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 14, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at Conference Room, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                         Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Dr. Wasim A. Khan                                             Acting Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                Registrar

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                                      Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                     Director Projects

6.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi represented by Mr. M. Faisal Zia, S.M. IT I&O                                              

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

  

Item No. 1      To consider a proposal from the Director ICT for the fixation of marble tiles on external walls of Lab 16 at 1st floor, Admin. Building, City Campus.

 

1.1       The Director ICT had sent an email to Executive Committee along with pictures of the external walls at the entrance of Lab 16, in the Admin Building 1st Floor at the City Campus. The walls were painted a few weeks ago but since the students, while congregating in front of the lab before the lab period, lean against the wall and place one of their shoes on the wall, thus leaving their shoe marks giving very dirty and unkempt look. He said that if the walls are painted and repainted or cleaned they will be dirtied by the students immediately. He suggested that at least three feet high matching tiles of marble may be fixed on the walls near all labs.

 

1.2       The Registrar said that if tiles are fixed, it would stand out differently from other walls.  He said that the Maintenance Manager had suggested placing Planters over there but he said that its maintenance would be difficult and people may use it for garbage disposal and spitting in it.

 

1.3       The Director Projects said that washable pint is the best solution as it could be cleaned. Installation of tiles will mean lot of wet work and removal of existing plaster.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee agreed with the Director Projects that instead of fixing tiles, the wall be painted.

 

 

Item No. 2      Presentation of monthly progress report of IBA Current and Up-coming Projects and the HEC Projects.

 

2.1       The Director Projects and the Director HEC Projects made presentations of the following current and upcoming projects:

 

2.2       FCS Building, City Campus: The Director Projects reported that the project started as per schedule on July 1, 2011 and its completion date is July 1, 2012.  He said that due to debris on roof, non-shifting of ground floor staff and non-shifting of 500 KVA Genset, the project is delayed by a month. The date for shifting has been fixed as November 15, 2011 by the EC.  He said that the ground floor is not yet available due to which further delay is expected.

 

2.3       Administration Building, City Campus: The Director Projects said that the project started in February 2010 which was to be started in October 2009.  Its completion date was October 2011 against the scheduled date of July 2011. He said that all rooms of ground floor and first floor were handed over except Telecommunication Lab as it got delayed due to change in layout and paint .  The faculty rooms are fully ready. Few finishing items have been left by the Contractor. A notice has been given to the Contractor to start the work by 3rd November 2011 and finish it by November 20, 2011 failing which IBA will get the remaining work done at Contactor’s risk and cost.

 

Dr. Wasim A. Khan said that the Computer Lab is not yet complete because on the drawings wall cabinets have been shown but they have not been mounted. 

 

The Director Projects said that wall mounted cabinets have not been approved.

 

Dr. Wasim A. Khan said that they are using a small room of ICT Department. He said that if they vacate the room and move to lab, they would have no space to fix things for which they need cabinets. He said that the drawings prepared by Project Engineer which included wall mounted cabinets have been signed and approved and it should be ensured that it is done accordingly.

 

The Director Projects said that wall mounted cabinets are not in original drawing.  However, he said that if clear requirements with the requisition are received the work can be done by the Projects Department.  

 

Dr. Wasim said that another issue is that there are many FCS faculty who are not moving to the Lab because its chiller is not working.  The Registrar said that by Thursday, November 17, 2011 it will start working.

 

2.4       Aman Tower, City Campus: The Director Projects said that the scheduled date was October 16, 2011 and project’s completion date is April 2014. He said that Letter of Intent for civil works has been issued to M/s. Paragon and the work will start from November 21, 2011. He said that although the construction period is 30 months, they would try to complete it in 22 months if they are also qualified to do the work of MEP ( Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing and HVAC ).

 

2.5       Auditorium, City Campus: The Director Projects said that the scope of work finalized as the basements for Auditorium and Aman Tower will be constructed by the respective contractors.  He said that M/s. MJS have engaged M/s. E.A. Consulting and have also agreed to engage Halcrow for Project Management. The start and finish dates will be decided in consultation with MJS Foundation.

 

2.6       ICT Data Centre, City Campus: The Director Projects said that the completion date of Data Centre, City Campus is end of February, 2012. He said that the complete responsibility of making the Data Centre functional now lies with M/s. H.K. Shah. The projects team will assist in monitoring of civil works. In future the progress will be reported by the ICT Department.

 

Mr.Faisal Zia said that ICT is already sharing the updates/reports with EC on weekly basis.  

 

The Director Projects said that a correction is needed in the minutes of 173rd meeting of EC held on October 10, 2011 (Item 3.4) wherein it was recorded that HK Shah will not be charging any fee for Project Management. He said that H.K. Shah will not be charging any fee for Civil work but for the Project Management he will charge the agreed fee.

 

2.7       Relocation of Generators and Construction of New KESC Sub-Station, City Campus:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that the scheduled start date was September 2011 but Siemens took two months to start the work so it started in November 4, 2011 and would be completed in March 2012 instead of January 2012.

 

2.8       New 2000 KW Connection, City Campus: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that basically the new 2000 KW connection is for the Aman Tower + Auditorium and will be pursued on completion of the basement of the building.

 

2.9       Extension of Load from 374 KW to 850 KW, City Campus: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that full payment has been made to KESC and IBA will get the connection on completion of the new sub-station within next three to four months.

 

Mr. Faisal Zia said that they would require 250 KW for the data centre at the City Campus.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that ICT never gave their requirements for Data Centre and so it was not included in the requirement for additional load.

 

The Director Projects said that since one basement at Aman Tower has been proposed to be reduced, the need for power will accordingly be reduced so the requirement of data centre could be met from the power that is saved due to reduction of basement in Aman Tower. When asked about the decision for placement of generators in the basement, the Director Projects said that he would find out a solution after talking to the Consultant Architect.

 

2.10     Library, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that the scheduled start date was April 2011 but it was revised and started in October 2011. The revised completion date is January 2013 instead of August 2012. He said that the delay was due to revision of designs, incorporation of suggestions made by the new Librarian and value re-engineering as the bid price was higher than the original budget.

 

2.11     Administration Building, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that the renovation of Administration Building, Main Campus is four months behind schedule. It was scheduled to start in May 2011 and finished in December 2012. There were a lot of mistakes in tender documents. The reviews were held 3 times by M/s. NESPAK as every time Architects / Consultants left observations unattended. He reported that the shifting plan has been finalized with the assistance of Registrar.  The shifting plan of testing department involves lots of civil and electrical works, which are being tendered and will be completed before the main contractor walks in. The new dates will be intimated as soon as the tender evaluation reports are received

 

2.12     ICT Data Centre, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that his department is only coordinating the civil works of the data centre at Main Campus.

 

2.13     Electrical Infrastructure System, Main Campus: The date for the start of the project was July 2010 and completion date is December 2013.

 

2.14     Sports Grounds & Ancillary Works, Main Campus: The date for the start of the project was May 2011 but it was delayed for four months due to late submission of tender documents by the Architects. The completion date is May 2012. Tender has been done and its evaluation will be completed in the 2nd week of November 2011.

 

2.15     New Academic Block, Main Campus: The project was started in May 2011 instead of January 2011 and it will be completed in September 2012 instead of July 2012. The structure work of 2nd floor is complete and the electrical conduiting of first floor is also complete.  HVAC equipment is in ordering stage.

 

2.16     Students’ Centre, Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that the projects started 3 months late in September 2011 and it would be completed in October 2012.  The delay occurred at the evaluation stage as the submittals by the Contractors were inadequate. However, he said that efforts would be made to catch up the delays.

 

2.17     External Development & Infrastructure Works, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that the scheduled start date was July 2010 and its completion date was December 2013. The project has been divided into two phases; External Development, and Infrastructure Works.  The delay occurred due to change in electrical infrastructure systems as per the decision of the Executive Committee.

 

2.18     Water Supply System to the Cooling Tower, Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that the work has been completed as per schedule in November 2011.

 

2.19     Prayer Hall, Main Campus: The date for the start of the project was September 2011 and its completion is August 2012. He said that tender has been received and is being evaluated.  The project will be awarded in the next three weeks.

 

2.20     Aman CED Building, Main Campus: The project started as per schedule in October 2011 and will be completed in April 2012. Structure work, rough plaster, HVAC Piping and electrical conducting completed. Facade work also started.

 

2.21     New Boys Hostel, Main Campus: The project started as per schedule in April 2011.  It would be completed in end of March 2012. He said that the project is six weeks behind schedule and it may be ready in Fall 2012. Work expedited on finishing works like block masonry, drainage and fire fighting works.    

 

2.22     Visiting Faculty Hostel, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that the scheduled start date of the project is December 2011 and its completion date is March 2013. He said that contract has been awarded to the lowest bidder after the approval of the BoG.

 

2.23     New Girls Hostel, Main Campus: The Director Projects said that tender documents would be issued for the new Girls’ Hostel in December 2011.  He said that within next three months they will hopefully start the project.

 

2.24     Construction of KESC sub-station at Boys and Girls hostels, Main Campus: The project was started in May 2011 and completed in August 2011.

 

2.25     Laying of Water and Sewerage Lines, Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that the project started as per schedule in January 2011 and was to be completed in October 2011 but has been delayed due to obstruction/encroachment in approved R.O.W from Karachi University.  Applied for change of route but no response has yet been received from Karachi University.

 

2.26     Three new power connections, for Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that three new power connections for Main Campus have been approved by KESC.  M/s. KP Associates are working on the cost estimates.

 

2.27     PC-1 for Sindh Talent Hunt Program: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that PC-1 has been approved and release of first instalment of Rs.10 million is expected in December this year.

 

2.28     Revised PC-1 of the mega project “Strengthening of IBA”:  The Director Projects reported that PC-1 has approved in CDWP meeting held on October 21, 2011.

2.29     The Committee noted the monthly progress report of IBA Current and Up-coming Projects and the HEC Projects.

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Update on Alternate Parking Space at City Campus.

 

4.1       The Registrar presented the following up-date on the alternate parking space at City Campus:

 

a)   Cnema Hall Parking: Available for Parking of 50 vehicles only during Forenoon Hours (up to 1 p.m. maximum) Rs.30/- per car per day. Vehicle owners will have to walk about 200 metres to the campus from the Prince Cinema.

 

b)      Saddar Parking Plaza: Complete basement floor available for booking parking up to 150 vehicles, monthly rent Rs.2000/- per vehicle. Hourly Shuttle service will have to be arranged to ferry vehicle owners from and to the plaza.

 

c)      Compound of Disused Adjacent Building: Attorney Mr. Sheikh Nazaz contacted in Lahore; parking space for up to 30 vehicles can be made available after spending about 10 - 15000 rupees to clear trees, debris etc. The Attorney wanted us to make an offer – we have offered him Rs.3000/- per vehicle per month – a max of 30 vehicles will be parkable at any given time. An agreement of tresspass rights to IBA will be signed by attorney – after he gets clearance from the owner who is in Australia. Minimum one year advance will be required.

 

 

DECISION

 

4.2       The Committee noted the up-date and fond option (c) as the most suitable option.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
