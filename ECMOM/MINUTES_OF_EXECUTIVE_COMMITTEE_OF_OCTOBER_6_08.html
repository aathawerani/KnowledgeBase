MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON OCTOBER 6, 2008 (SIXTEENTH MEETING)

The sixteenth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, October 6, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Professor
  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Chairman, CS & MIS
  3. Mr. Inayat N. Din                             Registrar
  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance
  5. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                    Director Projects
  6. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                       H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider the revised quotation submitted by BBCL for holding IBA dinner to welcome Dr. Ishrat Husain and to bid farewell to Mr. Danishmand on October 17, 2008.

1.1       M/s. BBCL presented the revised quotation for making arrangements for the dinner at Marriott Hotel, Karachi. The arrangements would include making computer generated 400 invitation cards with envelopes, Sound Systems, Multimedia Projections with screens, Ambience, and Effects Lights for event coverage. The also quoted their Professional Fee. The total cost quoted by them was Rs.261,000/- The quotation did not include venue rental and dinner.

DECISION

1.2              The Committee approved the revised quotation submitted by M/s. BBCL. The Committee also decided that Event Coverage including video coverage and still prints for which M/s. BBCL had quoted Rs.50,000/- would be arranged by the IBA.  Thus the total cost would be 211,000/-

ACTION

1.3              The Director Finance would issue Work Order to M/s. BBCL accordingly.

Item No. 2      To consider note from General Manager (Planning & Development regarding Soil Investigation at the two campuses.

2.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (Planning & Development) in his note of September 30, 2008 stated that in April this year they had advertised for procuring Soil Investigation and Destructive and Non-Destructive Testing Services.  However, there was no response to the advertisement. Thereafter, in May 2008 they wrote direct to six of the companies requesting them to send their quotations for the above mentioned job.  Only three of them namely: (1) Soil Testing Services, (2) Council for Works and Housing Research, and (3) Consolidated Engineering Services (Pvt.) Limited submitted heir quotations.  He presented an analysis and comparison of prices quoted by them.

2.2              He stated that execution of two projects namely; the low-cost multi-purpose building and Entrepreneurship Center needs to be undertaken urgently. Soil investigation results are required for both of them as well as for other developments projects.  He suggested that the soil investigation work should start without loss of time.

2.3              He stated that according to the comparative analysis M/s. Consolidated Engineering Services (Pvt.) have quoted the lowest rate.  He also mentioned that M/s. Consolidated Engineering are one of the top most reputable foundation engineering and soil investigation firms. He recommended that M/s. Consolidated Engineering be approved and he be allowed to issue Work Order.  He further stated that the scope of soil investigation to be carried out would be determined by them in consultation with consulting architect and engineer and its cost would not go beyond Rs.1.2 million which is the amount approved in this year’s budget.

DECISION

2.4       The Committee approved the recommendation of General Manager (Planning & Development) to issue Work Order to M/s. Consolidated Engineering Services (Pvt.) Limited.

ACTION

2.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed will issue Work Order to M/s. Consolidated Engineering Services (Pvt.) Limited.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve draft agreement with M/s. Pakistan Mobile Communications Limited for BlackBerry Enterprise Server for IBA Community.

3.1              Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager presented the draft agreement for availing PMCL’s service for consideration by the Executive Committee. Mr. Azam Sardar confirmed that there would be no liability on the part of the IBA on availing PMCL’s service.

DECISION

3.2       The Committee approved the draft agreement. However, the Committee decided that the draft should also be vetted by our Legal Advisor.

3.3       The Committee further decided that the IBA would bear the recurring cost of the 10 hand sets to be provided by PMCL to the IBA.

3.4       The Committee also decided that the IBA will get insurance cover for theft / snatching for the ten sets to be provided by PMCL.

ACTION

3.5       The Registrar will send the draft agreement to the Legal Advisor for proper vetting.

3.6       The Director Finance will get the ten sets to be provided by PMCL ensured against theft / snatching.

Item No. 4      To consider proposal from Mr. Amir Rizvi, faculty member, regarding GITEX Visit in Dubai for IBA CCS Faculty members.

4.1              Mr. Amir Rizvi, faculty member, proposed a 5 day visit to GITEX Dubai for IBA CCS faculty members. In his note Mr. Rizvi stated that the GITEX visit will be extremely beneficial for the CCS faculty members as that would provide the faculty members a chance to know about the latest trends in technology and in businesses, attend plenty of free seminars and interact with high profile IT professionals, all of this would later help IBA faculty members to conduct and transfer a quality education to IBA students. He said that the GITEX would commence from the 19th of October and will be open till 23rd of October. During this period there would be no classes.  He also presented the budget for this program.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee felt that participation in programs such as GITEX would be beneficial for practitioners and not for academicians and researchers. Moreover the fund allocated in the budget is basically for encouraging research and teaching and not for trade exhibition type of exposure.  

4.3       The Committee did not approve the proposal.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
