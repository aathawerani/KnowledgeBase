The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      Approval of Brand Manual.

 

1.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui made a presentation on the importance of Brand Manual and its need for IBA.  He said that a brand can be an identification or a mark that differentiates one business from another (through a name or a logo). It symbolises how people think about your business and ensures that brand and its integral elements are not left to the whim and fancy of personal tastes. The Brand Manual is a document designed to protect the integrity of the visual presentation of a brand. It defines how a brand is visually presented in communication and business materials. Giving detailed explanation of the logo and its usage, he listed the do’s and don’ts in Master Logo.  He also emphasized on the palette of colours for print, and for web, consistency in colour and font size.

 

1.2       In support of the need for a Brand Manual at IBA, Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented the following justifications:

 

o   Make sure literature reflects the IBA vision.

o   Brand Guidelines are designed to ensure that IBA is represented clearly and consistently.

o   Provide professionally written guideline that reflects the brand’s identity, Persona and Values.

o   Ensure that brand and its integral elements are not left to the whim and fancy of personal tastes.

o   Serve as a tool to understand the correct use of the valuable IBA brand.

o   Guidelines should be followed by all those preparing communication materials inside and outside the IBA.

o   Ensure that each piece of collateral look comes from the same Institution.

1.3       Dr. Nasir Afghan wanted to know if IBA can use the SAQS Accreditation along with IBA logo.

 

1.4       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that it cannot be used because IBA may keep on getting some other recognition and accreditation which will not be possible to place.

 

1.5       The Registrar said that it may be displayed on IBA Website and convocation brochures.

 

1.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui sought approval from EC for an estimated charges of Rs.200,000/- for preparation of IBA Brand Manual which would also include signage.

 

1.7       Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested that the Communication Department should help in marketing the products. He further said that like other universities which have marketing department, at IBA, by adding one or two marketing people, the Communication Department can be renamed as Communication and Marketing Department.

 

1.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the Communication Department is not concerned with the marketing. However, he agreed that there should be a marketing committee at IBA. He said that IBA has got a lot of marketing faculty who can give ideas and the marketing tools needed to reach the end beneficiaries because the faculty knows the program, and the end beneficiaries.

 

1.9       The Dean & Director said that we do not have marketing plan for every product. However, he said that IBA launched BBA as a terminal degree and each one of the Directors had to go and talk to the employers to make them aware that now we have a completely different product.  H.R. Forum was also arranged and the program was also presented in the Corporate Leaders Advisory Board. He said that no marketing manager in the world can substitute for the collective judgement of the faculty and management.  He agreed with Mr. Zafar Siddiqui that a committee can be formed which can identify the tools needed to reach the end users and then the strategy may be designed accordingly.

 

1.10     The Director H.R. said that the branding initiative should help in the implementation of the strategy of IBA.

 

DECISION

 

1.11     The Committee agreed to the concept of IBA Brand Manual as presented by Mr. Zafar Siddiqui and allowed him to go ahead with the branding plans and get the IBA Brand Manual prepared.

 

1.12     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui will take the lead in forming a Committee for marketing IBA products from time to time.

 

 

Item No. 2      Presentation on MBA Program 2012.

 

2.1       Dr. Nasir Afghan presented the admission and competitive analysis of IBA’s MBA Program of last 3 years (2009, 2010 & 2011), and future options which included MBA Program Marketing, Hiring Marketing Manager, Action Plan. He presented the following year-wise figures of the students applied for and offered admissions in MBA program:

2.2       The Dean & Director said that the falling number of applicants is mainly due to the tightened admission criteria – percentage raised from 50 to 60 in graduation, 16 years of education, and two years post graduation experience.

 

2.3       Dr. Nasir Afghan presented the result of the survey that was done to ascertain the number of BBA 2010 batch interested in joining MBA. According to this out of 181 students contacted, 58 students i.e. 32% showed their interest to join MBA program after their BBA.

 

2.4       The Registrar suggested that in order to attract those who are already working, IBA should give some incentives to them to come and join MBA Program.

 

2.5       The Dean & Director said that those who gain admissions after two years experience may be exempted from the first semester project that was mandatory for all students coming through the BBA route. This will save their one semester and money.  He said that this option may be examined.

 

2.6       Dr. Nasir Afghan said that Karachi School of Business and Leadership has required 21 months and have given preference to two years experience. He further said that HEC has also allowed that students who have done 4 years BBA may complete MBA in 18 months.  He presented the following four options for consideration:

 

            Option # 1:      Marketing Plan of Existing MBA

            Option # 2:      Two years work experience (Preferred)

            Option # 3:      Start 18 months MBA Program

            Option # 4:      Work Experience Preferred (point system for work experience)

            Option # 5       Combination of Options 3 and 4.

 

2.7       The Dean & Director said that BBAs with two years’ experience can complete their requirements in 18 months after beign exempted from the 1st semester project but the rigour and eligibility criteria for admissions to MBA will remain the same.

 

2.8       Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that there may be many reasons for lesser enrolment in MBA program; economic condition is one of them. He agreed that two years experience should not be waived but 18 months program for the BBAs with two years experience may be offered. He was not in favour of Options 2, 4 & 5 noted at 2.6 above.

2.9       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed with Dr. Nishat and suggested that companies may be asked to send their middle management people to IBA’s MBA program.

 

2.10     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that they should wait for at least one year. If the situation remains the same in 2012 then we may revisit the decision and may give preference to two years experience rather than making it mandatory.

 

2.11     The Director ICT was also in favour of waiting for a year.

 

2.12     The Dean & Director said that the majority is of the view that if the population of MBA students does not grow in 2013 then we may introduce Option # 2 of two years’ preferred experience.

 

2.13     The Director H.R. said that if we want to be among the top 100 business schools, our strategy has to be to get the best so should be the quality of output, degree of internationalization and research output.

 

2.14     The Dean & Director said that we do not have market data except the salary of the last three batches.  He said that ranking indicators include average age of the students, pre MBA and post MBA salaries, salaries after three years, what proportion of MBA class gets jobs after three months of graduation.

 

2.15     It was agreed that for 2012 there will be a modification in the admission criteria according to which all BBA students who have completed 16 years of education and two years of experience can appear at IBA admission test for MBA program.  They will be allowed to complete MBA in 18 months including summer and will be required to do 66 credit hours courses. If the evidence from the market shows that there is no perceptible increase in the intake in 2012 we can go to Option # 2 of two years experience preferred and point system for work experience starting in the year 2013.

 

2.16     Dr. Nasir Afghan said that there will be two MBA classes; one through BBA and another having different disciplines which will create confusion.

 

2.17     The Dean & Director clarified that there will be no two MBA classes.  He said that six credit hours exemption will be given to students coming through BBA. He further said that BBA students will not be required to do internship in Summer. The Associate Dean and the Academic Committee would recommend waiver of six credits to the Academic Board.

 

2.18     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that 66 credit hours will be for the students with BBA and others coming from other fields will have to do two additional courses of 3 credit hours each i.e. 72 credit hours as is at present.

 

2.19     Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested to hire MBA Program Marketing Manager and said that it is necessary to have a contact person to coordinate.

 

2.20     The Dean & Director said that CDC can be used for this till February because their peak work load starts from February.   

DECISION

 

2.21     The Committee decided that that for 2012 there will be a modification in the admission criteria according to which all BBA students who have completed 16 years of education and two years of experience after clearing IBA admission test can do 66 credit hours courses to complete their MBA requirement. If the enrolment in 2012 shows that there is no perceptible increase in the intake then we can go to Option # 2 with two years experience preferred and point system for work experience starting in the year 2013.vidence from the market shows that there is no much difference then IBA can go to Option # 2 of two years experience preferred and point system for work experience.

 

2.22     The Committee decided that a Committee comprising the Director H.R., Dr. Nasir Afghan and Mr. Jami Moiz headed by Mr. Zafar Siddiqui would discuss how to project the MBA Program and reach the target audience. Some students who can be helpful in social interaction can also be included in the committee.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve two ERP related items: (a) Cloud Computing Hosting Charges for Peak Time Period, and (b) Post Production of an ERP System – 2011.

 

a) Cloud Computing Hosting Charges for Peak Time Period.

 

3.1       The Director ICT said that they are using peak time hardware during the time of registration. He said that M/s. 3.3       The Director ICT informed the Committee that the Post Production Support cost of ERP system was discussed in the EC meetings held on January 10, 2011 and May 9, 2011 for the amount of Rs.750,000/- per year, which is through an agreement of HEC and Techlogix. He said that EC had approved the payment of first quarter 2011.

Item No. 4      To consider a request from Ms. Sara Pervez, student of BBA-5, for leave of absence on medical grounds.

 

4.1       The Registrar reported that he has received a request from Ms. Sara Pervez, BBA-5 student for grant of leave of absence on medical grounds. He said that in her application Ms. Sara Pervez has stated that she is suffering from viral hepatitis and the Doctor has advised her for complete bed rest for at least a week. She has requestrf for sick leave for a week and to allow her to continue her courses after her recovery.  The Registrar said that he had consulted the Controller of Examination who has said that if Ms. Sara is short of absences then she cannot be allowed the leave.  The Registrar reported that Ms. Sara has already availed 3 absences and only one is remaining.

 

DECISION

 

4.2       After due consideration of the facts and the rules governing students attendance the Committee did not approve the request of Ms. Sara Pervez for one week leave on medical ground. Under the circumstances as per policy she has two options namely: (1) Drop the Semester and avoid being marked 'F' in any subject; (2) Continue with the semester and avail the facility of 'make up examination' in respect of the subjects in which she is not short of attendance, while accepting 'F' in the subjects in which she does not meet the attendance requirements. 

 

ACTION

 

4.3       The Registrar would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Ms. Sara Pervez.

Item No. 7      Presentation by M/s. NBCL on conceptual Interior design of Aman –CED building.

 

7.1       Mr. Zayed Bilgrami presenting the Conceptual Interior Design for Aman CED said that the philosophy was to keep the basics in mine i.e. color, character, and textures. He said that each floor will be different in color. The floors of entire building are tiled except the seminar room which will have carpet tiles. He also presented the design of the furniture.

 

7.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that it has already been decided that all the floors will be of the same color, character and texture.

 

7.3       The Registrar suggested to try to have lesser dark floor.  He also suggested to make available pin up board with the wall. He suggested that furniture in student lounge should be more robust so that it can be easily maintained.

 

7.4       The Director ICT said that the buildings will be RFID based. He said that the cabling and everything for that has to be done.

 

7.5       The Dean & Director said that IBA will provide branding manual to NBCL for guidance. He said that the Centre does not need to be extremely sophisticated as some persons for whom short courses will be offered will come from diverse backgrounds.  He also said that they have to keep the Centre very modest. He also said that since the CED activities will be sporadic he does not want central air-conditioner in CED. He said that we had taken the decision that none of IBA buildings will have central air-conditioning system.

 

7.6       The Registrar said that he too was not in favor of central air-conditioning; he gave the example of the Academic Block at Main Campus where a lot of difficulty was being experienced in operating and maintaining of the chiller fed AC System. This was due to the fact that many a times only one or two rooms required AC and total cooling requirement was of say 5 tons but whole 60 -120 ton plant had to be run – causing waste of energy, higher electricity bill and requirement for heavier maintenance.

 

7.7       Mr. Bilgrami said that they have designed the Aman CED Building for Central Air-conditioner with variable features. He said that the package unit is one of the most energy efficient systems and it runs according to the need. 

    

7.8       The Dean & Director wanted to know about the Incubation Centre.  Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he would create that facility in the new building.

 

DECISION

 

7.9       The Committee thanked Mr. Bilgrami and his team for the presentation and approved the conceptual interior design of Aman CED with the following to be incorporated by NBCL in their design:

 

·         Specifications of signage (branding manual) would be provided by IBA and NBCL would incorporate it in their design.

·         The dark tile of the corridor will be replaced with a lighter shade for easy maintenance.

·         Provision will be made for emergency lighting in the Seminar Room.

·         Provision will be made for pin-up boards in Classrooms.

·         Furniture in Student Lounge would be more robust.

 

 

Item No. 8      Presentation on the Electrical Infrastructure system related to exclusion of full back up power to Central AC system by KP Associates.

 

8.1       Mr. Khalid Pervez of KP Associates made a presentation showing the cost difference between full back up and partial back up stand-by generation.   

 

8.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that in the last EC meeting it was discussed that if we have 100% and 70% back up, the cost difference will of Rs.3 million only. 

 

8.3       The Dean & Director said that the entire installed capacity of stand-by generation of 1250 KVA should be used as back up to KESC supply. We will enter into agreement with KESC that IBA will be exempt from load shedding. In case there is a power break down we may allocate the power to most priority places and shut off the least priority places. He said that Admin Block is not on priority. He said that his first priority is that data centre and all the services and equipments associated with it including UPS and labs should not be affected at all. The second priority is Academic Block where classes are held and third priority is the Library.

 

8.4       Mr. Bilgrami said that for the new CED building they have provided for generators.

 

8.5       The Dean & Director said that the Aman CED generator will be part of the centralized pool and to the extent the stand-by generating capacity would be augmented.   He said that as and when demand increases we can add generators to the central hub.

 

8.6       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that his proposal was of a hub to provide light and AC but for disconnecting AC he will have to divide it further. He said that he will have to see the design and drawings of the building prepared by the Electrical Consultant to find out if it is possible or not to disconnect AC by further dividing the DB. He will get back with his proposal to EC.

 

8.7       The Dean & Director said that the financial constraints do not allow investment in unutilized spare capacity. He said that as and when funds become available and additional stand by generator is needed, we will add to the central hub.

DECISION

 

8.8       The Committee decided that the generator budgeted for Aman CED will be placed in the Central Hub and as and when funds are available and additional stand by generator is needed, it will be arranged accordingly.

 

ACTION

 

8.9       Mr. Khalid Pervez will rationalize the power supply according to the set priority (mentioned in 8.3 above) and see the drawings prepared by the Electrical Consultant to find if ACs from non-essential areas can be disconnected from the distribution boxes in case of KESC’s power break down.      

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
