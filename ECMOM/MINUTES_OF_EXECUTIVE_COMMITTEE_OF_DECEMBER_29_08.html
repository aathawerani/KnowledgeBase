MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON DECEMBER 29, 2008 (TWENTY EIGHTH MEETING)

The 28th meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, December 29, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                          Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                Chairman CS & MIS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                                Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                            Director I.T.
  9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                           Director H.R.
  10. Mr. Shameem Ahmad                           G.M. (P&D) by special invitation

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider email from Director Projects regarding removal of smaller trees.

1.1       The Director Projects stated that in the last meeting of the Executive Committee it was decided that he should take expert advice on removing the smaller trees. Accordingly he said that he contacted the Consultant who advised him that it was not the right time to take out the trees and replant them at other place because the replanted trees would not survive. The Director Projects further said that Mr. Tofiq Pasha Mooraj, an expert in horticulture, has offered that he could develop a good size orchard in IBA at the same cost i.e. Rs.90,000/- whenever required.  He further clarified that no extra payment would be required for cutting the trees. The charges of cutting the trees would be covered in Rs.90,000/-

1.2              The Director Projects said that he would invite Mr. Mooraj to advise how to fit the green belt/orchard in the Master Plan.

1.3       The Director Projects further said that a proper landscape would be developed for which a landscape architect would be engaged because Mr. Hanif Daud is not expert in landscaping.

DECISION

1.4       The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Director Projects and decided that small trees should be removed and 100 trees be planted location for which has to be decided.

ACTION

1.5              The Director Projects will decide the location for the green belt / orchard which fits into the Master Plan and get 100 trees planted by Mr. Tofiq Pasha Mooraj.

Item No. 2     Organization structure oversight  following up (HR, Controller of Examinations, Associate Deans are to present their relevant organization structures for review.)

2.1       The Director H.R. presented revised organogram of H.R. Department. She stated that placement and career counselling will also be a part of the H.R. Department.

2.2              Dr. Muhammad Nishat and Dr. Sayeed Ghani presented the organogram of Associate Deans Offices.  Dr. Nishat said that in the organogram they have suggested four program coordinators out of which one will be responsible for programs run in the Evening and will be reporting to both the Associate Deans. The Associate Dean of Computer Sciences will have one Program Coordinator for BCS/BS/BBA(MIS) and MBA(MIS). The responsibilities of Program Coordinator and those of Program Incharge will be clearly defined. The Program Coordinator will inform what curriculum has to be followed, what is the sequencing that needs to be followed and he will also ascertain whether pre-requisites have been fulfilled before enrolling in the curse. The Program Incharge will partially be reporting to the Controller of Examinations also.

2.3              The Director said that Program Offices are front offices for IBA.  All the program offices will be interacting with the persons coming in.  Program offices should report to the Program Coordinator for their respective programs and for the academic records, transcripts, grading, semester examinations they will be responsible to the Controller of Examinations.  The Program Coordinator will oversee and ensure consistency across the programs. Program Coordinators will be reporting to Associate Deans who will be providing guidance to Program Coordinators.  

2.4              It was suggested that the issues of the Main Campus Library should be handled by Dr. M. Nishat and that of City Campus Library be taken care of by Dr. Sayeed Ghani.  H.R. will make posting and transfers at the request of Associate Deans.  For administrative issues Librarian will report to the Associate Deans.  The Committee asked the Associate Deans to upgrade the quality of services at the Library by posting only qualified and competent staff.

2.5              It was also suggested that the Registrar and City Campus Coordinator should be included in the Library Committee.

2.6              Students Affairs will consist of the Students Counsellor and Assistant Student Counsellors. Both of them will report to the Director but will also have a close liaison with the Associate Deans.

2.7              The Chairpersons will be evaluated by a committee comprising Associate Deans, Dean & Director, and the Director H.R.

2.8              The management and implementation of all academic programs would now be the responsibility of the Associate Deans, Chairpersons and the faculty committees.

DECISION

2.9       The Committee approved the revised organogram of H.R. department as well as the organogram of Associate Deans with the amendments mentioned above.        

2.10     The Committee requested the Controller of Examinations to provide the organization chart of Examination Department to Director H.R.

2.11          The Committee agreed to the suggestions given in 2-3 to 2.8 above.

2.12          The Committee further decided that the Controller of Examinations will also be the Coordinator for City Campus and the Registrar will be the focal person / Coordinator for the Main Campus.

ACTION

2.13          The Controller of Examinations will present organization chart of Examination Department to the Director H.R.

2.14          The Director H.R. will present the revised organogram before the A&FC for approval.

2.15          The Associate Deans in consultation with the Director H.R. will prepare job descriptions for Program Coordinators and Program Incharges. They will also select the Program Coordinators from among the existing faculty members through an open and transparent process.

Item No. 3      To review the policy on earned leave encashment at resignation of faculty.

3.1       The Director H.R. said that unlike non-teaching staff, teaching staff is not eligible for earned leave encashment.  She requested to Committee to review the IBA Leave Rule and make recommendations of amendments in the leave rule to the Board.  

DECISION

3.2       The Committee advised the Director H.R. to revisit the Leave Rule and present the draft revised Leave Rule before the Executive Committee for consideration and recommendation to the A&FC.

3.3            The Director H.R. was further advised to see what are the best practices particularly the practice in AKU, LUMS.

3.4            The Director H.R. was also advised to make the earned leave as an incentive for the faculty for using it for sabbatical leave.

ACTION

3.5            The Director H.R. will study the best practices with regard to earned leaves particularly the practice at AKU and LUMS and would present new leave rules.

Item No. 4      To consider email of December 24, 2008 from the Registrar regarding transfer cases of students.

4.1              The Registrar in his email of December 24, 2008 stated that several students wish to change their campus of study for their own personal reasons.  He stated that earlier the IBA had the policy of accommodating a few such cases. However, in the Fall 2008 no student was given the facility to change the campus. He requested the Executive Committee to give its opinion in the case of new applicants. He further stated that the fee structure as well as other terms and conditions will stay intact as per the earlier practice.

DECISION

4.2              The Committee decided that from Fall Semester 2009 the students will be given the choice of campus.  The Committee further decided that both new and old students will have the option on first come first serve basis.

4.3              The Committee further decided to increase the class timings at the Main Campus.

Item No. 5      To consider request from Mr. Waqas Anwaar Abbasi, a student of BBA-IV (City Campus) for transfer to Main Campus due to health problem.

5.1       Mr. Waqar Anwaar Abbasi, a student of BBA-IV, City Campus stated that due to a congenital disorder his kidneys were operated upon eleven times since he was a child. He further stated that since the disorder he suffers from is very rare, the doctors in Pakistan are unable to treat it. He stated that he has to wait for a foreign doctor to arrive from UK on his regular tours to Sindh Institute of Urology and Transplantation for his operations.

5.2            He stated that because of the complexity of his medical condition, the procedures performed on him are very complicated and requires a lot of post operative care. He further stated that his family is based in Islamabad and he resides in the hostel.  The distance that he needs to travel to get from the hostel to City Campus is injurious to his health specially post his operations that occur quite frequently.  He requested to transfer him to University Campus so that he may continue his medical treatment along with his studies.

DECISION

5.3            The Committee decided that since Mr. Waqas Anwaar Abbasi is getting his treatment through Sindh Institute of Urology and Transplantation (SIUT) headed by Dr. Adeeb-ul- Hasan Rizvi, we should consider it as a genuine case and allowed his transfer to the Main Campus.

ACTION

5.4            The Registrar will communicate the decision of allowing transfer of Mr. Waqas Anwaar Abbasi from City Campus to Main Campus to Mr. Waqas Abbasi as well as concerned program offices.

Item No. 6      To consider email from Mr. Khusrow Uzair, Visiting Faculty regarding delay in monthly payment of remuneration.

6.1              Mr. Khusrow Uzair, Visiting Faculty at the IBA in his email of December 19, 2008 stated that monthly payments to the visiting faculty at IBA are done every month after inordinate delays. He stated that up-till December 19, he did not receive the payment.

6.2              The Director Finance said that the attendance record of the visiting faculty is received late due to which the payment is delayed.

DECISION

6.3              The Committee decided that respective heads of Program Offices will send a certificate before 5th of each month to the Finance Department about the number of sessions taken by a visiting / part-time faculty. The Finance Department will honour that certificate.  The Committee further decided that if Attendance Register is required, that would be sent later to the Finance Department but for that, payment would not be delayed.

ACTION

6.4              The Registrar would communicate the decision to heads of all program offices and to the Finance Department.

Item No. 7      To consider note of December 26, 2008 from General Manager (P&D) and Director Projects regarding procurement of Suzuki Bolan Hi-Roof for PP&D Department.

7.1       The Director Projects and the G.M. (P&D) in their combined note of December 20, 2008 addressed to Purchase Officer stated that the description as Suzuki Carry Pickup in the budget approved for their department is an error. They needed a high roof van which could be used for transportation of staff as well as small volume goods. The funds allocated were Rs.500,000/-

7.2              The Purchase Officer observed that the budget for vehicle of PP&D is allocated for Pick-up version.  He further observed that the Purchase Office had placed order for three units of Hi-Roof vans @Rs.483,000/- and later on M/s. Suzuki raised its price to Rs.518,000/- each  In his opinion Hi-Roof with AC will be of higher value than Rs.518,000/- while budget allocation for pick up is Rs.500,000/-

7.3              In the same note the Registrar observed that any error in the budget description should first be corrected by the competent authority.

7.4              The Director Projects and G.M. (P&D) with reference to the observation made by the Registrar, requested the Executive Committee to approve correction of the error and enhance the allocation from Rs.500,000/- to Rs.575,000/- for Suzuki Bolan Hi-Roof CNG with AC for PP&D Department.

7.5       The Registrar said that the Purchase Order for the three Suzuki Hi-roof vans has already been issued and their delivery is due in the first 10 days of January 2009.

DECISION

7.6       The Director said that if for I.T. department purchase order has been issued for Suzuki Hi-roof then the same should be issued for the PP&D department.

7.7       The Director further said that there is no need of a Suzuki Hi-roof for Library. Whenever the library needs a vehicle for official purpose, they would be arranged a vehicle from the pool.

Item No. 8      To consider proposal from Director Projects for a car for Maintenance Manager.

8.1              The Director Projects in his note stated that during the past three months, interviews were held for the positions of (1) Maintenance Manager (2) Project Engineer (Planning & Contracts Management), and (3) Project Engineer (Execution).  All of them are contract positions.  He said that all the selected candidates demanded a car as part of their packages.

8.2       He said that the job in reality needed a car for the engineer to commute between two campuses, boys hostel and the staff colony.  The nature of job dictates that the engineer and Maintenance Manager have to remain mobile all the time and also they should be able to attend to emergencies to off times too.  

8.3       He further said that at present none of the maintenance in-charge and skilled worker has any logistic support to enable them move and work efficiently.  He said that the logistics are being worked out and details will be submitted in the Maintenance Management Strategy and System the guidelines of which are being prepared by Director Projects and would be submitted on January 15, 2009.

DECISION

8.4       The Director said that for all practical purposes including pick and drop cars will be made available to Maintenance Manager and Project Engineers but like Directors, they would not be provide cars.

8.5       The Committee agreed with the Director and requested the Registrar to see if one of the ordered Suzuki Hi-roofs (originally intended for the Library) can be changed to Cultus so that the Managers could be provided pick and drop and for movement for official purposes.

ACTION

8.6       The Registrar will check if the Purchase Order can be changed from Suzuki Hi-roofs to Cultus.

Item No. 9      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

            a)         Resignation of Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager.

9.1       The Director I.T. informed the Committee that Mr. Azam Sardar, Manager I.T. had taken leave for two days and then three days sick leave but after the expiry of leave he did not join. He further said that Mr. Azam Sardar had tendered his resignation which he (the Director I.T) has accepted. 

DECISION

9.2       The Committee felt annoyed at the irresponsible behaviour of Mr. Azam Sardar and decided to accept his resignation and requested the Director H.R. to advertise for the position of Sr. Manager I.T.

ACTION

9.3       The Director I.T. would forward the resignation of Mr. Azam Sardar to the Director H.R.

9.4       The Director H.R. would advertise for the position of Sr. Manager I.T. after receiving job specifications for the position from Director I.T.

b)         Duties and responsibilities of Director Project and General Manager (P&D).

9.5       The Director Projects said that there was a confusion that Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (P&D) reports to Director and not to Director Projects. He further said that the available maintenance staff has not been formally transferred to his department.

9.6       The Director said that the Director Projects is the head of the department. Mr. Shameem Ahmad will be reporting to Director Projects for every project except the HEC Projects.  He further clarified that Mr. Shameem Ahmad is not responsible for maintenance work.

DECISION

9.7       The Committee requested the Director H.R. to issue a notification of transfer of available repair and maintenance staff including electricians, plumbers and carpenters from Admin to PP&D Department.

9.8       The Director H.R. was also requested to make a chart of HEC and other projects assigned to Mr. Shameem Ahmad in consultation with Mr. Shameem Ahmad and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

ACTION

9.9       The Director H.R. would issue a notification of transfer of available repair and maintenance staff including electricians, plumbers and carpenters, janitors, gardeners, sanitary workers from Admin. to PP&D Department and to make a chart of HEC and other projects assigned to Mr. Shameem Ahmad in consultation with Mr. Shameem Ahmad and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

c)         Reporting Items  Donations of US$10 million from AMAN Foundation.

9.10     The Director informed the Committee that AMAN Foundation will be donating US$10 million to IBA out of which they want us to build multi-storeyed building at the City Campus. He said that they want us to sign an MOU as soon as possible. He said that we have to first identify the location of the multi-storeyed building, and then clarification from Cantonment Board as to how many floors we can go on.  He said that AMAN Foundation would also be financing for our ERP and infrastructure cost as they are keen on technology.

9.11     The Director requested the Director Projects to work on the planning of multi-storeyed building in consultation with the Director CEE, Program Coordinator for MBA(Financial Services), City Campus Coordinator, Chairman, CCS and others on apriority basis.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
