Item No. 1      To consider and approve new electrical connections for the Main Campus, Staff Colony and the Boys’ Hostel.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that the applications for the following three HT (High Tension) connections are under active consideration of KESC. He informed the Committee that on their request, KESC has agreed to provide High Tension connection from Ghulistan-e-Jauhar and have also given preliminary cost estimates for IBA’s budgetary purpose:

 

                                                              Power                        Estimated

Connection Location                         Required                         Cost__

b)      Staff Colony                         660 kW                   Rs. ...... million

 

c)      Boys’ Hostel                          750 kW                   Rs. ..... million

 

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that KESC’s earlier surveys had indicated that while new Switch Rooms / Sub-Stations were required at the Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel’s areas, the existing Sub-Station at the  Main Campus would be used for the new connection required there. Accordingly, the Executive Committee in its meeting held on August 23, 2010, approved the proposed locations of the new Sub-Stations at the Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel’s area and approved the cost estimate of Rs...... million for the Sub-Stations in its meeting held on February 7, 2011.

 

1.3       However, Mr. Shameem reported that the most recent surveys conducted by KESC on March 24 and 28, 2011, in respect of the connection required for the Main Campus have resulted in their demand of a  new Switch Room / Sub-Station at the Main Campus too. Its most desirable location was also discussed with the KESC surveyors on the spot.  He further said that the matter was also discussed with the newly-appointed electrical consultant Mr. Khalid Pervaiz of KP & Associates who agrees that the existing Sub-Station be left as it is in order to protect the investment IBA made when the load was extended from 110 kW to 400 kW last year.

 

1.4       Mr. Shameem said that the enhanced load of 2,500 kW (including the existing 400 kW) is required for the newly-renovated Auditorium and the upcoming new buildings like Centre for Entrepreneurship Development, New Academic Block, Students’ Center, etc.

 

1.5       Mr. Shameem reported that the KESC has suggested that the new Switch Room / Sub-Station of 16' x 26' size be preferably located along the main road and as close as possible to IBA’s Gate No.4. He presented the site plan of the Main Campus Master Plan in which the proposed location for the new switch room/sub-station was identified.  

 

1.6       Mr. Shameem requested the Executive Committee to approve the proposed location with necessary adjustments, if any, in the Master Plan.  He further said that as stated above, the Executive Committee has already approved an estimated cost of Rs...... million for construction of two new Switch Rooms/Sub-Stations at the Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostels’ areas.  He requested the EC to approve another one million rupees for the third Sub-Station.

 

1.7       The Director said that the entry point should aesthetically look nice and it should not be affected by the construction of a new sub-station around it.

 

1.8       The Director Projects said that Gate # 4 is for the entry of buses; entry for students is from another side.

 

1.9       The Director ICT suggested that they should also have power management system. To this the Director Projects reported that they have appointed an electrical consultant who is working on it and has also included in its Master Utility Plan. He said that this would be merged with external development plan so that in future they do not have any problem.

 

1.10     The Director wanted to know if alternate route is also included in the cost estimate of Rs....... Mr. Shameem replied that he hoped so but was not sure. Mr. Shameem said that he has requested for the approval of the location only of the third sub-station and an additional approval of Rs..... for construction of sub-station. He said that the total estimated cost of the construction of three sub-stations would be around Rs...... million.

 

1.11     The Director wanted to know as to why the requirements are presented on incremental basis while he had asked to put all requirements together. He wanted to know as to what are the advantages of the proposed new sub-station and what is wrong with the existing sub-station.  

 

1.12     Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that everything of the existing sub-station will have to be replaced so its cost would also increase.

 

1.13     The Director Finance said that an estimate of Rs......million was given for KESC facilities.  He wanted to know if Rs.......million and the additional Rs..... would be part of that estimate.

 

1.14     Mr. Shameem said that it would be part of that estimate.

 

1.15     The Director said that the estimated cost of completion and the budget provision are two different concepts and if no provision has been made in the budget then it will have to be re-appropriated through A&FC.

 

1.16     Mr. Shameem said that he would resolve this issue in consultation with the Director Finance.

 

1.17     The Director asked Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia and Mr. Shameem Ahmed to make a realistic assessment in respect of utilization of the budget by June 30, 2011 and make a proposal for re-appropriation within the budget. He suggested that all re-appropriations indicating savings on certain projects out of their existing allocations and the increased allocations to others should be presented in a consolidated manner to the A&FC.

Item No. 5      To consider and approve extension in time for the preparation of Projects Department Manual.

 

5.1       The Director Projects stated that in the earlier EC meeting he had reported that the preliminary draft of the Department Manual was 80% complete. He said that at the time of finalization it was discovered that most of the material provided and written by various engineers of Project Team was not upto the mark. He said that the same was being re-drafted and re-written.  So the Manual would be late because of the following other major reasons:

 

·         During the past two months, the Projects Department had to work on six projects simultaneously which were under the process of procurement and final stages of award. 

 

·         Scrutiny of tender documents and drawing of Library, FCS Building and Administration Building by Project Managers revealed major mistakes. It took considerable time to get those mistakes accepted and corrected by M/s. ICON.

 

·         IBA recently accepted to follow the SPPRA Regulations according to which the PP&P Manual would be revised. He said that he intends to include the revision in the manual.

 

·         After  the induction of Project Managers  on IBA Projects, he intends  to formulate and  rationalize   standard bid documents based on the results of  scrutiny of the documents  by  four Projects Managers (M/s. NESPAK,  M/s. CTI, M/s. AAA Partnership and M/s. Integ). He said that some portions of SBD have to be included in the Manual.

 

5.2       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to allow another three months to complete a good standard document.

 

5.3       The Director said that issue of non-availability of manuals was discussed in the A&FC meeting on April 4, 2011 and Mr. Sohail Wajahat said that the manuals were available on the net.  The Director asked the Director Projects to check it on the internet.

 

DECISION

 

5.4       The Committee allowed extension of another three months for completing the manual of the Projects Department.

Other than that he said that he also received the CV of an experienced ERP consultant who would like to provide his services as visiting faculty at IBA. He reported that he has set-up a meeting with him in coming week. He further said that he was planning to call him for faculty presentation and after that he would decide.
