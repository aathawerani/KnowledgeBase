MINUTES OF 243RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON TUESDAY, APRIL 2, 2013

The following items were discussed:

1.8       The Director Projects to recheck the RFPs of the projects to see if the penalty clause is included there or not. The Director Projects reported that the RFP did not include the penalty clause as fee is based on the percentage basis of the cost of project. Furthermore, no extra payment to Architects were made due to extension of the project.

 

The Dean & Director said that it is not correct to say that the Architects do not get any extra payment. He said that if the completion cost of a project increases they get their fee on the basis of the final completion cost. He said that Finance Department should ensure that there is a penalty clause in the future contract agreements and if it is missing then it should be pointed out to the Projects Department.

 

 

MR. M. JAWAD KHAN:

 

1.9       The implementation of the Synchronization Scheme: Mr. Jawad Khan reported that panel has been delivered and they are waiting for ICT to confirm shutdown of Data Centre to hook up the panel with existing system. They are also waiting for Siemens reply for commissioning of the ATS Panel. As soon as both confirmations are received, panel installation, commissioning, cable shifting, ATS Test run will be done in shut down.

 

Mr. Jawad presented the plan and scope of work for installation of the new additional LV (Low Voltage) Panel in LV Room at IBA City Campus. He said that he had requested the ICT department to allow two days’ shut down so that the data centre cable can be shifted. However, due to some practical difficulties the shut down cannot be done. Mr. Imran Batada explained that if they shut down the Data Centre, it would be for 48 hours during which all operations would come to a halt.  Mr. Jawad suggested that if they get a generator on rent for two days, the work would very smoothly be completed without any distraction.

 

The Committee approved acquiring a generator on rent for two days to complete the hook up of the panel with the existing system. Mr. Imran Batada would coordinate with Mr. Jawad Khan.

1.10     The Committee noted and approved the up-dates as noted above.

 

ACTION

 

1.11     Mr. M. Jawad Khan would obtain a generator on rent for two days to complete the hook up of the panel with the existing system.

Item No. 3      UAE Trip Report.

 

3.1       The Registrar presented report of UAE trip that he and Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif had made from March 21 – 23, 2013. He said that the purpose of the trip was to participate in an Education Expo organized by Pakistan Association of Dubai in collaboration with Study in Pakistan Program, a project of the NGO M/S Head On International, (pvt) Ltd who were chief promoters of Education EXPO on the occasion of Pakistan Day – March the 23rd. The trip also included meetings with Pakistan’s Consul General in Dubai, Vice Chancellors of various universities, Principals of Pakistani schools and professional academies in UAE, as well as investor groups interested in attracting Pakistani institutes of higher education for setting up campuses in UAE. He said that the trip was very useful; the Consul General of Pakistan in Dubai was personally involved in the activities.  He said that a place has been identified that can be used for conducting our admission test.  The only thing that IBA is to do is to do on-line testing.  He suggested IBA will be better off with on-line testing.

 

3.2       Mr. Imran Batada said that they are prepared to conduct on-line testing.  About the data bank of the test material, he said that he discussed it with Dr. Nasir Touheed who said that we cannot repeat old questions. The Dean & Director said that we can repeat the questions from the data bank. The Dean & Director wanted to know the number of schools in UAE interested in our programs so that we may know the pool of potential applicants.    

 

3.3       The Registrar informed the Committee that his team also met Mr. Danish Kazi who is a prominent IBA Alumnus settled in UAE and is presently coordinating the work of the IBA Alumni Chapter in UAE. The Registrar said that Mr. Kazi informed them that an MOU has been drafted in which the Pakistan Association Dubai will partner with IBA Karachi for promoting IBA presence in UAE and serve as a platform for IBA to launch its programs in Dubai in the foreseeable future. The MOU was to be signed on March 23, 2013 but could not be done as Mr. Waqar Siddique was not available in Dubai.  He said that Mr. Waqar and Mr. Irfan suggested IBA Alumni Chapter to become part of the Pakistan Association of Dubai.

 

3.4       The Registrar said that he would recommend that we should advertise an admission test in the local papers of UAE. He said that similar efforts will have to be made in Saudi Arabia. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that since June is very close we have to work on this proposal now onwards.

3.5       The Dean & Director said that his concern is that we should pool our resources and IRC, Alumni and the Registrar have to work together in order to make a big impact.

 

3.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that we can have a correlation between the SAT and IBA test results and set SAT score according to our test. The Dean & Director said that in foreign universities they have a minimum SAT score which we are following. He said that we are going to be a global business school and ought to follow the norms of global business schools.  He asked Mr. Imran Batada to find correlation between SAT and IBA test results of those who have given SAT and have also appeared in IBA test. He further asked Mr. Batada to see the performance of those admitted with 2’C’s and 1’B’ in the IBA examinations including mid-term, and final compared with 2’B’s and 1’C’ and see their scores in 1st semester. He said that this may provide an evidence that we have accepted 2’C’s and 1’B’ but their performance may not have any significant difference.

 

3.7       Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know as to what fee will be charged for the admission test from students outside Pakistan. The Dean & Director said that the same fee will be charged as we have to penetrate the market.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee noted the report on the UAE trip made by the Registrar and Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif and took the following decisions:

 

a)      IBA will be represented by the alumni chapter in UAE.

b)      An advertisement will be placed in the local newspapers in UAE announcing the IBA admission test.

c)      The same admission test fee would be charged from the candidates from outside Pakistan as is charged within Pakistan.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       The Registrar would place an advertisement in the local newspapers of UAE announcing about the holding of IBA admission test.

 

3.10     Mr. Imran Batada to study correlation between SAT and IBA test results of those who have given SAT and have also appeared in IBA test.

 

3.11     Mr. Batada to examine the data of results of those admitted with 2’C’s and 1’B’ in the IBA examinations including mid-term, and final compared with 2’B’s and 1’C’ and see their scores in 1st semester.    

     

Item No. 4      To consider and approve the proposal for time extension for Data Centre Tier III Project.

 

4.1       Mr. Imran Batada said that as per the contract agreement, the Data Centre Project was to be completed by March 27, 2013 but due to various reasons it would be delayed.  He said that he has invited the Contractor and the Project Manager to present this case for extension in project completion date.

 

4.2       Mr. Maroof, the CEO of AMFCO (Contractor) presented the status of the IBA Data Centre Tier 3 Project. He presented details of the completed tasks, in progress/pending tasks, delays, challenges, and the project plan. He said that the civil work, and some electrical works including conduiting have been completed – a fact which was confirmed by the representative of H.K. Shah present in the meeting. Mr. Maroof said that the wiring work which include lighting wiring, power sockets, power distribution cables, CCTV, Door access, fire suppression and detection and emergency shutdown are pending due to delay in custom clearance. He said that the Cooling System was also started late due to delayed custom clearance. He said that there is a delay of 60 days; 14 days on cable custom clearance, 16 days on strikes & riots, and 30 days on late approvals.

 

4.3       The Dean & Director said that if there was some issue with the Custom clearance he should have been informed so that he could take up the matter with the Customer authorities. He wanted to know what was still pending for clearance from Customs. Mr. Maroof said that the genset, fire suppression & detection, and the Electrical Distribution Board have been ordered late due to late approval, so these will be landing after 40 days.  The Dean & Director asked Mr. Maroof to let him know if there was any issue in the Clearance by Customs.

 

4.4       The representative of H.K. Shah said that they need 30 days to complete the wiring and then additional 30 days to patch up and to provide connectivity with DB.  He said that if everything goes smoothly, the project would be completed in 60 days. However, he suggested to give 15 days for buffer period so in total 75 days extension would be required. He said that he has not included holidays and Sundays in 60 days. He said that if the Contractors follow the schedule they have presented, the Contractors can complete the task in 45 days and in the rest 15 days they can spend in energization.

 

4.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested to prepare Gantt Chart and monitor the progress accordingly.

 

4.6       The Dean & Director asked if the cable for any reason becomes non-functional will there be any back up. Mr. Maroof confirmed that every cable that has been imported is 30% in excess to the actual requirement and can be used.

 

4.7       The Dean & Director said that we may allow extension of 75 days but if they exceed 75 days IBA will impose penalty clause. He said that this will be part of the approval letter.

 

DECISION

 

4.8       The Committee allowed extension from March 28, 2013 for 75 days in the completion of Data Centre Tier III Project in all respect. The Committee further decided that if the Contractors exceed the allowed 75 days, then penalty clause would be invoked.

 

ACTION

 

4.9       Mr. Imran Batada would issue approval letter for extension of 75 days after March 27, 2013 for the completion of the project in all respect.  He would also make penalty clause part of the extension letter.
