MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON SEPTEMBER 29, 2008 (FIFTEENTH MEETING)

The fifteenth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, September 29, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                               Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Chairman, CS & MIS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                      Director Projects
  8. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                         H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider proposal from Controller of Examinations for compensation for faculty and staff for additional duties during examinations.

1.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili in his note stated that the IBA conducts four term, one mid-term and three semester final examinations in one academic year in which thousands of students appear. The exams carry heavy load of sensitive nature of work like typing of questions papers, printing of papers, seating arrangements, logistics, invigilation etc. for which only trustworthy staff is engaged who have to work many extra hours on exam days to meet the dead lines. Due to a very large number of courses offered each semester, additional invigilation duties are assigned to the faculty and experienced staff members to invigilate the exams of these courses. Vigilance Teams consisting of senior faculty members and staff are also formed to visit different centres at both campuses during exams.  He presented details and volume of the work related to exams. He stated that the staff involved in the preparation and conduct of exams work for extra hours in addition to their normal duty hours, do not get any compensation except the over time. The over time rate does not compensate their hard work during examinations.

1.2              He further stated that the response of faculty members to extra invigilation duties assigned to them during exams is poor. He said that faculty members are not interested to perform extra invigilation, and departments other than Program Offices are not willing to release some of their staff members for invigilation. This non cooperation is due to the fact that there is no compensation for performing extra invigilation duties. He recommended compensation for faculty and staff involved in invigilation and other duties.

DECISION

1.3              The Committee decided that invigilation is a part of the responsibilities of teachers. It is not an extra duty.

1.4              The Committee further decided that the Controller of Examinations will prepare a roster of duty for involving staff in invigilation duties and present to the Executive Committee for approval.

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Dr. Sayeed Ghani regarding Competition for Redesigning of IBA Portal.

2.1              Referring to the decision of the Executive Committee of September 8, 2008, to arrange a competition for redesigning of the IBA Portal and IBA Website, Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that Mr. Imran Batada, Sr. Manager MIS had been assigned the responsibility for arranging the design competition and Mr. Mohsin Naqvi has been asked to conduct the user survey.  He said that the competition is recommended to be done in two phases; first for the IBA Portal and subsequently for the IBA Website. He stated that a detailed plan for the first phase has been made by Mr. Imran Batada specifying the rules and regulations, guidelines for design and a registration form for the interested students to fill.  He recommended that a cash prize of Rs.50,000/- be allocated for the winner(s) of the IBA portal design competition. The prize will be given only after the entire project is completed by the winning team. The competition will be started on 15th October, 2008.  He said that a similar activity will be subsequently performed for the IBA website.

DECISION

2.2              The Committee approved the proposal and recommendation of Dr. Sayeed Ghani as stated in 2.1 above.

ACTION

2.3              Dr. Sayeed Ghani will convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Imran Batada for arranging the design competition.

Item No. 3      To consider Legal Opinion on the requirements for admission into MBA Program.

3.1              The Registrar said that according to the decision of the Executive Committee on the case of Mr. Fahad Hussain Korai, he has obtained legal opinion on determining the period of O and A level education.  He said that as per the legal advice 11 years period is required for O level and two years for A level. Thus the student, according to the Legal Advisor, meets 16 years requirement by including his three years BBA studies.  As such his admission to MBA program should not be cancelled.

3.2              The Committee observed that the HEC treats A level equivalent to Intermediate for admission purposes.

DECISION

3.3       The Committee decided to obtain clarification from HEC if the A level is equivalent to 12 years of education or 13 years of education.

3.4       The Committee further decided that in future candidates with four years bachelors would be eligible to apply for MBA.

3.5              Mr. Fahad H. Korai, as per existing rules, will be dropped from IBA roll.

ACTION

3.6       The Controller of Examinations would obtain clarification from HEC as to what is the period of study counted for O and A level studies.

3.7       The Registrar will ensure that the condition of four years bachelors is included in the eligibility criteria for admission to MBA programs.

Item No. 4      Progress and Schedule Highlights from Director Projects.

4.1              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz presented progress and schedule highlights till September 27, 2008.  He presented details of the new projects including renovation, refurbishment of existing buildings and construction of new buildings. He also presented details of the progress of works in hand.

4.2              The Director Projects also presented an outline of suggested strategy of Project implementation through freshly prequalified Architectural and Engineering consultants.

4.3              The Director Projects informed the Committee that the government duties on imported tiles and other items have been increased by 30%.  He suggested to change the tiles at the Boys Hostel.

4.4              The Director informed the Committee that the BoG has approved two new buildings; a Girls Hostel and an International Hostel.  There will no concession in the fee for International Hostel and full fee would be charged.

DECISION

4.5              The Committee noted the progress details provided by the Director Projects.

4.6              The Committee decided that if the government duty on tiles and other items has been increased and the Executive Committee approves that, it should be reported to the A&FC.

Item No. 5      Status of Vacant Positions

5.1              The Manager H.R. presented the status of vacant positions.  He said that the selected candidate for Junior Architect position did not join and another ad had been placed for this position.  He also said that candidates for Finance Executive, Payroll Officer, GL Officer and Finance Assistant have been interviewed by the Director Finance and the General Manager Finance and applications for Personal Assistant and Financial Aid Assistant from interested students have been forwarded to Finance Department for further processing.  He said that Stenographers for Director Projects and the Registrar have been selected.  Further short listing would be done to find a Stenographer for the Controller of Examinations.  

5.2              The Director Projects said that there is no need to hire Junior Architect as they may get the work done from any of the Consultants.  

DECISION

5.3              The Committee noted the up-date on vacant positions and requested the H.R. Manager to arrange a Stenographer for the Controller of Examination as soon as possible.

Item No. 6      Security Arrangements at the IBA City Campus

DECISIONS

6.1              The Committee authorized Mr. Mobin Khalili, Controller of Examinations and Administrator City Campus to take over the entire security administration of City Campus.  He will notify the students to have their car stickers prominently displayed and if they do not do so their cars would not be allowed to enter into the City Campus. The Committee further decided that Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia would help Mr. Mobin Khalili in designing the security arrangements.

6.2              The Committee further decided that the short-listed candidates to be provided by the GHQ would be interviewed by Mr. Mobin Khalili and Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz.

6.3              The Committee also decided that IBAs regular Security Guards would be posted at the City Campus and the Security Guards hired from Security Agencies would be posted at the Main Campus. 

ACTIONS

6.4              The Controller of Examinations and Administrator City Campus and the Director Projects will implement the decision taken at 6.1 above.

6.5              The Registrar will arrange transfer of IBAs regular Security Guards at the City Campus and deployment of Agencys Security Guards at the Main Campus.

Item No. 7      Leave to Faculty

DECISIONS

7.1              The Committee authorized concerned chairpersons to grant leave to its faculty.  However, leave to department chairs and senior faculty would be granted by the Director.

7.2              The Committee further decided that the approved leave to a faculty member during Summer will have to be availed by the concerned faculty member unless the faculty member is called back by the concerned Department Chairperson.

ACTION

7.3              The Registrar will notify to all concerned about the decisions in 71 and 7.2. above.

Item No. 8      Miscellaneous Decisions

8.1              The Committee decided that all the Directors at the IBA would be members of the Executive Committee.

8.2              The Committee decided to advertise for Director Alumni, Public Affairs & Resource Mobilization and for Assistant H.R. Manager who should preferably be an MBA.

8.3              The Director Projects would prepare SOP and TOR for Maintenance Manager.

8.4              The Internal Committee for interviewing and recommending candidates for various positions would consist of concerned Director, Head of H.R., and a member of the Executive Committee.

8.5              The Committee recorded its appreciation for the work Dr. Wasim A. Khan, HEC Professor at IBA, is doing for hiring foreign faculty at the IBA.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
