MINUTES OF 147TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MARCH 28, 2011

 

The 147th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 28, 2011 at 9:30 a.m. at IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                      Dean & Director

2.      Dr.Sayeed Ghani                                      Associate Dean

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                  Registrar

4.      Mr. Ali Taha                                            Director Finance

5.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                         Director Projects

6.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                                 Director CED

7.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                     Director ICT

8.      Ms. Ayesha A. Minai                                  Director H.R.

 

Mr. Imran Khan attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve proposal for renewal of SAP connection with UCC Queensland University.

 

1.1       Mr. Imran Khan, faculty member, presented the SAP renewal schedule which included renewal fees for SAP AIS module and core module. He said that at the advice of Dr. Nasir Touheed, they are not continuing with SAP ABAP the current year so they would not be required to make payment for ABAP.  He said that the total amount that is required to be paid is $AUD5,700 for Economy Plus package.

 

1.2       The Director wanted to know the status of utilization of the SAP so far so that the Committee may decide on its cost and benefit.

 

1.3       Mr. Imran Khan informed the Committee that 50+ students were enrolled in SAP classes. He said that only one teacher namely Mr. Mohammad Asif is teaching this course.

 

1.4       The Director observed that if more students are enrolled into this course then it may be viable. He said that for only 50 students it is not justified. Mr. Imran Khan hoped that this semester more students would enrol in this course.

 

1.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that Mr. Ghias Khan, who is an IBA alumnus and is an expert in this field can be contacted as besides Microsoft, they have licences for SAP and ERP.  Mr. Imran Khan agreed to see Mr. Ghias Khan.

 

1.6       The Director said that Mr. Muhammad Asif would be going abroad for his Masters.  He wanted to know as to who would be teaching this course when Mr. Asif goes abroad.

 

1.7       Mr. Imran Khan replied that if Mr. Muhammad Asif leaves this semester, then they would be getting it renewed only for the period Mr. Asif is available.

 

1.8       The Director asked Mr. Imran Khan to see if visiting faculty can teach this course.

 

1.9       Mr. Imran Khan said that he would see if regular or visiting faculty members are interested to teach this course and based on his findings, he would present his proposal again to the EC.

 

DECISION

 

1.10     The Committee decided that Mr. Imran Khan would see if regular or visiting members are interested to teach this course and on the basis of his findings, he would re-submit his proposal to the EC.

Item No. 4      To consider suggestions regarding two additional Video Conferencing facilities - one for each campus.

 

4.1       The Registrar said that it was decided that there would be one Video Conference capable room in each of the academic block and the respective campus auditoriums to be equipped / configured to beam lectures on as required basis. However, he said that for future it was intended to provide for one VC capability either in the under construction New Academic Block or CED Block at the Main Campus and the Multi Storied building in the City Campus.  He said that in view of this he feels that there is no requirement of an additional Classical VC room in the academic blocks in the two campuses. What is required is to have a facility which can utilize the elaborate infrastructure available in the existing VC rooms to beam lecture held in the auditorium to another location. He said that there is no need to make the auditorium Video Conferencing Capable in the classical sense, the VC facility in the Lecture Hall being used is perfectly ok. The Director also presented the statistics of the uses of the VCR.  He said that IBA has 102 MB available out of that 12 MB goes to the VCR and they have some dedicated equipments.  He said that there are four essential requirements and all of them are placed in the VCR.  He said that the only thing that they require is two high quality cameras – one at the front and one at the rear. So four cameras and some cables will solve the problem.  He further suggested that M-1 should be avoided from regular classes. 

 

4.2       The Director said that he does not want the rooms to remain underutilized. However, he said that his only concern is that the equipments should not be damaged.

 

4.3       The Registrar said that the equipments are being damaged because of rat coming from the ducting at both campuses.

 

4.4       The Director asked the Director Projects to help the Registrar in blocking the entry of rats through the duct and other places.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee decided that cables between the M-1 and Auditorium be laid and two cameras along with a display screen and T.V. set be installed in the auditorium of IBA Main Campus. 

 

4.6       The Committee decided that at the City Campus instead of APWA building, the CEE will be connected with Video Conferencing facility by installing the two cameras, display screen, and T.V. sets at CEE.  

 

4.7       The Committee further decided that the cost to be incurred for these arrangements at City Campus will be charged to the CEE budget.

 

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Request for organizing Imam Husain Day at IBA.

 

5.1       The Registrar reported that he has received an email from a respected faculty member requesting him to take the matter of organizing Imam Husain (R.A.) Day at IBA to the Executive Committee for consideration and approval.

 

DECISION

 

5.2       The Committee considered the request and decided that instead of institutionalizing any day or event, it should be left at the discretion of the students’ societies who may organize such a day as a part of their annual calendar of activities.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
