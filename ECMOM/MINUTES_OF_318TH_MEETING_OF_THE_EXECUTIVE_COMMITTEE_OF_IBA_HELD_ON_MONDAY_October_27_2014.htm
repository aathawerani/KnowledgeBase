MINUTES OF 318TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 27, 2014

The 318th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 27, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Board Room, Third floor, Fouji Foundation Building (Admin. Building), IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
4. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No.1       Issue of make-up examinations.

1.1       The Registrar reported that in accordance with the decision of the Executive Committee of October 20, 2014, on the request from two students for make-up examination he conveyed the decision of the Executive Committee to the concerned students and teacher the same day. However, in response to the email, Mr. Aman U. Saiyed made his observation that make-up exam is something that is to be handled between the teacher and the student and that he has taken make-up exams before without Executive Committee getting involved. He had observed that the Executive Committee should not have been involved in this matter if he was willing to give his time to prepare the make-up exam, conduct it and grade.

1.2       The Registrar reported that he sent the policy of ‘Make up Exams” to Mr. Aman U. Saiyed. The policy authorizes the Executive Committee to consider allowing make-up exam in term exams only under Extraordinary Circumstances.  At this Mr. Aman U. Saiyed requested the Executive Committee to review the case as he had already taken a make-up exam and has also graded it. The Registrar reported that in response to the email from Mr. Aman U. Saiyed, the Controller of Examinations sent the following rejoinder:

“For maintaining discipline and avoiding administrative anarchy an organization is, and   should be, governed by certain policies and procedures. It cannot be, and should not be, left to individual’ personal fancies.

Policy regarding re-take of missed exams is clearly laid down in the Program Announcement which Mr. Aman U. Saied should have checked rather than taking a decision at his own which he   is not authorized to. A teacher can take a make-up exam only when it is approved and the decision is communicated to him/her. If Mr. Aman   did not agree with the decision of the EC, he could have requested for a review but  arranging a make-up exam at his will is totally unacceptable.  A clear message should go to him that he CANNOT take decisions at his own regarding matters which do not come in his purview.”

1.3        The Dean & Director said that the teachers say that it is their privilege to take the make-  up if they want to.  He said that he did not want to give an impression that the Executive Committee  was usurping their rights. He suggested that the academic matters should not be brought to the   EC, they should be referred to the Academic Committee. The Academic Board be requested to transfer the powers related to academic matters delegated to the EC to the Academic Committee. The Academic    Committee may invite the Controller of Examinations for his comments on such matters.

1.4       The Dean & Director suggested that as Mr. Aman U. Saiyed has already taken the make-  up exam and graded the paper, the EC should, as an exceptional case, honour his action.

DECISION

1.5       The Committee decided to honour, as an exceptional case, the action taken by Mr. Aman U. Saiyed with regard to make up examination for two students namely; Ms. Safa Habib of BBA-5 and Syed Anns Ali of BS CS-1.  In future, the policy guidelines should be followed and this case would not be cited as a precedent.

1.6       The Committee further decided that in future all academic matters including make-up examination be referred to the Academic Committee directly by the concerned faculty for consideration. In case of emergency approval of the Academic Committee may be sought through circulation.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Academic Board would be requested to transfer the powers on academic matters including make-up examinations delegated to EC, to the Academic Committee.  

 

 

Item No. 2      Clarification on Invigilation Remuneration.

 

2.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that with regard to invigilation remuneration as decided, the hours required for invigilation duty are to be adjusted from monthly overtime. However, the Examination Department clarified later that the adjustment should be done with daily overtime. Further, there were resentments from non-management staff on it. Their contention was that the overtime of non-management staff is deducted for the hours put in to the invigilation duty during office hours but for the management staff there is no such adjustment as they are not entitled for overtime.  He said that he was trying to clarify that the management staff, in addition to normal working hours, are more frequently required to work beyond office hours and sometimes they take the assignment to their home as well in order to meet the deadline. Mr. Adnan Hameed asked as to what would be the penalty if the management staff who does invigilation duty during normal office hours but does not put extra hours. The Dean & Director said that it would be the responsibility of the department head to make sure that the management staff, who have been assigned invigilation duty during normal office hours, complete such additional hours in a month. The Dean & Director said that it is also the responsibility of the HOD to ensure equitable distribution of invigilation duties.  Mr. Adnan Hameed suggested capping the maximum number of invigilation duties that can be performed by an individual.

 

2.2       The Director Projects said that the work of his department suffers due to the invigilation duties assigned to the Projects staff.  The Dean & Director agreed that it would be unfair on his part to hold the Director Projects responsible for projects related work and at the same time ask him to assign his staff for invigilation duty.  He asked the Registrar not to assign invigilation duties to Projects staff until the project is completed.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee decided that it would be the responsibility of the department head to make sure that the management staff assigned invigilation duty during normal office hours work for additional hours and the HODs would also ensure equitable distribution of duty. 

 

2.4      The Committee decided that the Project Department’s staff would not be assigned invigilation duty till such time the projects are completed.

 

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
