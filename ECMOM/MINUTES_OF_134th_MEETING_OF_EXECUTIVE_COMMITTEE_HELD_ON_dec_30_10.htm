Item No. 1      EC decisions Implementation Reports from (a) Director ICT, (b) Director H.R, (c) Director Finance.

 

UP-DATE FROM DIRECTOR ICT:

He further said that the pre-qualified electrical consultants have been asked to provide details of the data centres that they have prepared in the last five years.

            The Director said that the data centre is very sensitive and complicated and for that if they do not have the competence then they should bring in outside expert.  The Director Projects said that they are doing this.

 

1.4       ERP Password:  The Director ICT reported that in accordance with EC decision separate passwords have been issued to Teaching Assistants.

 

            The Director said that the Teaching Assistants may enter the grades but the grades will have to be verified by the concerned teachers.  He asked the Associate Deans to prepare a SOP making faculty members responsible for verification of the grades before sending it to the ERP and once it is sent to ERP nobody can change the grade.

Item No. 2      Presentations by:

 

a)         M/s. A.C. Neilson on Performance Evaluation and Satisfaction at IBA.

 

2.1       Ms. Jamila Kidwai of M/s. A.C. Neilson presented the findings of the survey they carried out at the request of IBA on Performance Evaluation and Satisfaction of various service units of IBA. She described the methodology adopted and the size of the sample which represented students, faculty and management. She presented the segment-wise findings i.e. students, faculty and management.

 

2.2       The Director advised to present the findings for the whole institution by merging the samples of the three i.e. students, faculty and management into two segments – students and faculty and staff together as the sample size of the two sub-samples is too small to draw meaningful inferences. He said that he was interested in knowing the overall satisfaction level. He said that the findings will become the benchmark for future improvement as it would give a baseline for comparison. He said that the strong points will have to be included in marketing / advertising and the weaknesses will have to be overcome.    

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee requested Ms. Jamila Kidwai of M/s. A.C. Neilson to revise the presentation in light of the observations made by the EC members.  The revised findings would be presented to the BoG.

 

ACTION

 

2.4       The Director H.R. would arrange presentation of the findings on Performance Evaluation and Satisfaction to all the service units and then to the IBA Board of Governors meeting.

 

b)         Presentation by Mr. Imran Zawaar on Career Development Centre.

 

2.5       The Director H.R. said that Mr. Imran Zawaar has tendered his resignation and is leaving for Australia for higher studies. She requested Mr. Zawaar to brief the EC on the goals achieved by the CDC during 2009-2010 specially with regard to placement of IBA graduates.

 

2.6       Mr. Imran Zawaar presented the statistics of the BBA and MBA students passed out in 2009-10 and their absorption in the job market. He also presented the average salary figures the BBA including BS and MBA batches have been hired at.

 

2.7       Mr. Imran Zawaar informed the EC members that CDC has arranged Career Fair on February 19, 2011 and they have planned an H.R. Forum in mid-March 2011. He said that the Graduate Directory would be launched through ERP but hard copies would also be available. He said that the CDC website which would be within the Alumni website is in process. 

 

2.8       The Committee congratulated Mr. Zawaar on getting scholarship for higher studies and wished him success in his future endeavour.         

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee noted the achievements of CDC for 2009-10 and decided that:

 

a)      In future statistics of passed out BS students should be presented separately.

b)      The CDC website should be within the alumni website and for budgetary purpose, if needed, re-appropriation may be done.

     

ACTION

 

2.10     The Director H.R. would ensure that the above decisions are implemented.

Item No. 4      To consider and approved options for SAKAI implementation.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that the ICT department is ready to implement SAKAI. However there are two options and some additional costs are also involved. He said that the first option is to implement SAKAI immediately by getting additional resources over cloud but this would cost about $7,000 – $8,000 per month additional. He said that the another option is to delay the implementation till ICT centres are ready which would mean that SAKAI would probably be available in the Fall Semester but this would save about $56,000 - $64,000.

 

4.2       The Director wanted to know as to what would be implications if they delay the implementation.

 

4.3       The Director ICT replied that Learning System Module will not be available to the faculty and staff.

 

4.4       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that LMS may be implemented on local server till such time the centres are ready.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee decided to delay the implementation till ICT centres are ready.  In the meantime the LMS may be implemented on local server.

Item No. 6      To consider and approve appointment of contractors for new Academic Block at IBA Main Campus.

 

6.1       The Director Projects reported that the tender documents for the appointment of Contractors for the Construction of New Academic Block at IBA Main Campus were sent to the eight Pre-qualified Contractors, out of which four responded. All the four bids were sent to the Architect / Engineering Consultant M/s. Najmi & Bilgrami Collaborative (Private) Limited as well as the Project Managers M/s. AAA Partnership (Private) Limited for their evaluation. As per the Tender Evaluation Report of the Project Managers, M/s. A. H. Construction (Private) Limited are the lowest evaluated responsive bidder.

 

6.2       The Director Projects stated that the TEC members raised an observations pertaining to the bid clarification of M/s A.H. Construction (Private) Limited, which states as under:

 

“We have offered a discount of Rs 9,400,000/- on HVAC work item no. A-G of Package Unit in case IBA will directly pay the import duties, excise etc.”

 

6.3       The Director Projects reported that M/s. A.H. Construction (Private) Limited have withdrawn their clarification as IBA cannot import these units.  Further, they will provide separately the breakdown of the items at the time of import and clearance.

 

6.4       Based on the recommendation of Project Managers and TEC and subject to approval by the CPC, the Director Projects requested the EC to recommend the award of work to M/s. A. H. Construction (Private) Limited at a total cost of Pak Rs.202,385,590/-.

 

6.5       The Director wanted to know the amount of rebate.  He advised the Director Projects to obtain the price of HVAC and then see the Controller of Valuation and obtain the amount of rebates.

6.6       This item was deferred and would be re-submitted by the Director Projects to the EC after obtaining all the details.
