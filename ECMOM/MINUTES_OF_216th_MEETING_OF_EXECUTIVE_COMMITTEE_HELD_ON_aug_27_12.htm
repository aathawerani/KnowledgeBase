Item No.1       To consider and approve the extension in the completion time of the new Academic Block, IBA Main Campus.  

1.1       The Director Projects reported that he has invited Mr. S. Nafees Hassan, Director Projects, AAA Partnership and his team, Mr. Abdul Haleem, Managing Director, AHCL, and Mr. Ahsan of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami to apprise the EC of the reasons for the delays in the completion of the project on scheduled time and to seek approval for extension in the completion time.

1.2       Mr. Nafees Hassan of AAA Partnership first presented the following overview of the project:

    Total covered area                                 53,957  Sq.ft
Date of Commencement                          March 2011
Planned date of Completion                     September 2012
Contract Awarded Cost                          Rs.202,385,590/-
Work done up-till July 30, 2012                74%

1.3       Mr. Nafees Hassan said that the project has been delayed and it cannot be completed on the planned date of September 2012 for the following reasons:

    1. Late approval of Curtain wall
2. Late approval of tiles due to Interior Decoration package work
3. Late approval of lighting fixtures due to false ceiling work of Interior Decoration Package
4. Overall structural work delayed
5. Late decision and approval of complete electrical system causing delay in finalization of distribution boards (DBs)
6. Electrical Cables / wiring base line completed in June 2012 whereas the completion date was March 15, 2012 but it was affected by the above delay.
7. Delayed delivery of package units (delivered on May 15 instead of April 15, 2012)
8. Delivery of Transfer Pump was to be made on May 30, 2012 but it was delivered on July 2012.
9. Delayed verification of running bills by Project Managers (13 days impact)
10. Changes made by IBA on 2nd floor of MS/PhD offices.
11. Additional items added to the project in late June 2012
12. Addition of Sound System

1.4       Mr. Nafees explained the reasons in detail for the delays in each activity which may result in the total delay of 92 days. He said that subject to the conditions that all drawings are frozen and all approvals are made by Architect and IBA by August 31, 2012, the project including the Internal Development work and additions items, would be completed with a delay of 77 days on December 16, 2012.  He said that by 15th November 2012, furniture can be placed in the offices.

1.5       The Dean & Director observed that in some of the activities specially the first three one, as reported by Mr. Nafees, there have been delays because of late approval by the Consultant/Architect.  Mr. Ahsan of NBCL explained the reasons for the delay in giving approval.

1.6       The Dean & Director further remarked that when there is a delay for any reason, the work force is subsequently increased to make up the delay. He said that there is a risk factor in every contract which is identified in advance and there is a provision for mitigation in the planning.

1.7       The Director Projects said that at the time of conceptual designing of the Academic Block, he had pointed out that because of glasses there would be too much heat. He has, therefore suggested that there should be some openings for cross ventilation.  He said that he has issued the order to the lowest bidder for the supply of furniture.  He said that it would take two months time so by mid of November 2012 the furniture would be available. To a question, Mr. Ahsan of NBCL confirmed that the lowest bidder has met our requirements and specifications for furniture.

1.8       The Registrar said that the furniture manufacturer has limited space at their workshop to keep all the furniture so they would be delivering the furniture as and when it is ready. He requested Mr. Nafees to provide at least eight or nine rooms where all electrical and necessary work has been completed to store the furniture temporarily. Mr. Nafees agreed to provide the requested rooms by October 15, 2012.

1.9       The Registrar suggested that the immediate surroundings of the building should be taken care of before the final finish of the building. The Director Projects said that although the work on Master Planning would start in December, he would take temporary measures before that to take care of the surroundings.

DECISION 

1.10     In view of the reasons explained by the Contractor for the delays, the Committee extended the completion time of the new Academic Block till December 16, 2012.

ACTION 

1.11     M/s. AAA Partnership would complete the project in all respect by December 16, 2012 and prior to that by October 15, 2012 they would provide at least eight or nine rooms where all electrical and necessary work has been completed, to the Registrar to place the furniture.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the power Supply needed urgently for the new Boys Hostel.

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that the New Boys Hostel has been substantially completed and its ground floor (36 rooms), is being readied to accommodate new-coming students by the end of the next week. However, he said that the power supply needed for the hostel is an urgent issue. The electrical infrastructure proposed for the hostel is at the tendering stage at the moment and will take about 5 months to complete. Until then we can depend upon power supply from the Universitys KESC Sub-Station No.3, through the cable that was laid in 2010. It runs upto the New Boys Hostels eastern boundary wall where a 400 Ampere circuit breaker is already installed. He said that they have managed to have this power supply switched on; an energy meter is, however, needed which they would arrange. 

3.2       He said that the immediate issue is that of taking the power supply from the boundary wall to the electrical room in the New Boys Hostel, for which an electrical cable, about 150 ft. in length and 240mm2, is required urgently. Buying a new cable will cost about Rs.2 million and the supplier will take about 3 to 5 weeks to deliver. An alternative is to extract the cable that has become redundant and lay buried underground at the City Campus. Its length is about 150 meters, part of which can be utilized for the New Boys Hostel and the rest elsewhere in the Main Campus. He reported that as this alternative appeared to be the most feasible and economical they have obtained the following three quotations for extraction/retrieval and transportation of the cable from City Campus:

1.     DCT Construction                :           Rs.182,700.00
     2.     Mohammad Saeed & Co.       :           Rs.172,608.00
     3.     Agha Associates                 :           Rs.212,788.00

3.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that about 30 hosteliers who would be coming on August 28, 2012 can only be accommodated in the hostel if there is power supply in the hostel. In view of urgency of the matter Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the EC to approve the awarding of the work to the lowest bidder, namely, Mohammad Saeed & Co., at the quoted price of Rs.172,608/-. He said that the quoted price also includes loading, unloading and transportation cost of the cable from City Campus to the Main Campus. He said that due to urgency, they are not adopting the required procedure of tendering. 

3.4       The Dean & Director was annoyed at not synchronizing the availability of power with the completion of the civil works by the Director HEC Projects keeping in view the target date of completion i.e. September 2012.

DECISION

3.5       In view of the urgency, the Committee approved the award of work of extraction/retrieval and transportation of the cable from City Campus to the Main Campus (Boys Hostel) to the lowest bidder; M/s. Mohammad Saeed & Co. at the quoted price of Rs.172,608/-

3.6       The extra length of cable not needed in the Boys Hostel would be given to the Registrar for connecting the multi-purpose building to the CED.

Item No. 6      To consider and approve the removal of Staircase Tower from Academic Block, City Campus.

6.1       The Director Projects reported that in order to provide working space for the Auditorium construction and to leave a space of about 5-6 feet between the two buildings, it was found necessary to shift the existing staircase tower on the southern side of the Academic Building, City Campus. In this regard, he reported that first a meeting was held with the Architects of Aman Tower and the Auditorium in order to modify the drawings. In this meeting various options were considered but the Architects of Auditorium advised that any modification in their design would reduce the number of seats by 40. Secondly, the curvature of the ramp could not be changed which is serving the basement from southern side. He said that because of the ground conditions and that the Aman-Tower structure is already built up to ground floor level, the only option available was to relocate the staircase tower.

6.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the situation was brought to the knowledge of the Dean & Director IBA on 16th August 2012 who inspected the site and advised to work out the options which should possibly save the removal of the staircase tower. Accordingly, another meeting was held on 18th August 2012 at the site after re-verification of the ground co-ordinates and it was concluded that there was no other way except to remove and re-locate the staircase tower.

6.3       He further reported that on 23rd August 2012, the Architects of the Building M/s. ICON were invited to suggest any technical and cost effective measures. After considering all the pros and cons, it was advised by M/s. ICON that the staircase tower as a whole can be removed and its re-location will not be needed. Instead a spiral staircase will be installed in the corner which will be sufficient. M/s. ICON further confirmed that this will not affect the architectural aesthetics and functionality of the building. There will be 6-8 feet passage available in between Academic Block and the Auditorium, which is highly desirable. In this way, IBA will have to pay for the dismantling, rigging and transportation to the Main Campus for storage of the steel structure either for disposal according to the disposal procedure or to be utilized elsewhere. He said that an estimate of recoverable cost is being worked out. He hoped that the sale of the staircase can pay for the dismantling, removal and construction cost and possibly the cost of spiral staircase.

6.4       The Director Projects suggested that as the Contractor of MSJ Foundation is moving to the site within 2-3 days, this work is to be done on emergency basis.  He said that the Projects Department has invited quotations for this work from the open market as there is no time for tendering process.

6.5       The Registrar suggested that the area of dais can be reduced instead of comprising on the seat by modifying the design.  However, the Director Projects said that there is not much space to reduce.

6.6       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that if we decide to remove the stair case tower, then it should immediately be disposed off by going through the disposal procedure.  The Dean & Director agreed with Mr. Khalili and said that instead of transporting it to the Main Campus, it should be disposed off from the City Campus.  He asked the Director Projects to place tender for disposal of stair case tower on website of SPPRA and IBA and dispose it off immediately following the laid down process.

6.7       Mr. Khalili further suggested that the walls of Auditorium facing the Video Conference Room should be straight and aesthetically good looking. 

DECISION

6.8       The Committee approved the removal of Staircase Tower from Academic Block, City Campus.

ACTION

6.9       The Director Projects would arrange to dispose off the dismantled Staircase Tower by following the laid down disposal procedure.  He would also ensure that there is adequate light in between the Auditorium and the Academic Block and the wall of the auditorium facing the Academic Block remains aesthetically good looking.

 

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair. 

a)         Status of Fall 2012 admissions of under-graduate and graduate programs.

7.1       Dr. Nasir Touheed, Coordinator Testing Services presented the status of fall 2012 admissions of under-graduate and graduate programs as on August 25, 2012.  He presented program-wise number of candidates applied, appeared in the written test (including SAT), invited for interview, offered admission, number of offers that could not be delivered, grades below the requirement, number of successful candidates whose results have not yet been announced, choice of Main and City Campuses. He wanted to know if other institutions start their admissions process at the same time or early.  Dr. Mohammad Nishat said that some parents come to him and say that their wards earlier got admission in other institution where they have already deposited the fee which is non-refundable and on getting admission at IBA also they cannot afford to pay the fee again.

Future enrolment will be done at the Main Campus and as such the program offices should also be at the Main Campus. However, he said that some of the students had agreed to come to the City Campus and not at the Main Campus.

7.5       The Dean & Director said that there is a seamless boundary between the two campuses; classes can be offered at either campus.  He said that three foundation courses be offered at the Main Campus and three foundation courses be offered at the City Campus.  He suggested to start this from the first semester so that there may be mobility between the two campuses. He further said that there should be separate time tables for graduate and undergraduate programs without mentioning the campus.

7.6       Dr. Nasir Touheed said that in the existing arrangement where time tables for each campus is separate, if there is any disturbance in the city, the classes are rescheduled but in the proposed arrangement it cannot be done.
