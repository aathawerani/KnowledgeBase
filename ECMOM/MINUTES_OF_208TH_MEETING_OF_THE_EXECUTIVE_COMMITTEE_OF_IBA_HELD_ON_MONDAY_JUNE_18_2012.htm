Item No.1      To consider and approve the award of Interior Work Package to M/s. A.H. Construction (Pvt.) Limited already working at Abdul Razzak Tabba Academic Block, Main Campus.

 

1.1     The Director Projects reported that the design of interior works package of Abdul Razzak Tabba Academic Block, Main Campus was given to the Architects, M/s. NBCL as the interior works formed part of the overall theme of the building. Accordingly the overall Architectural works were continued following an approved theme. The majority of the architectural works have been completed.

 

1.2     The Director Projects said that in order to maintain a flow of the works, the Architects have recommended that the interior works be made as part of the existing contract since M/s. A. H. Construction (Private) Limited (AHCL) are already working on those items which are in the BOQ and have to be integrated with the interior works. He said that it would be very difficult to bring in another contractor to work in the same spaces where AHCL is already working. The Architects and the Project Managers have assured that AHCL has the required resources to undertake the interior works and co-ordinate with the ongoing works. In this regard the contractors will be asked to depute an experienced supervisor to closely supervise the finishing work.

 

1.3       The Director Projects reported that in order to dovetail the interior works, complete BOQ is being prepared by the Architects based on the approved interior design and will be given to M/s. A. H. Construction (Private) Limited and sealed bid will be obtained. The single contractor has to be selected as another contractor cannot work on the same works which are in progress.

Item No. 2       To consider and approve the Oracle Licensing for ERP Solution.

 

2.1       The Director ICT reported that the contract of Oracle Support for ERP expired on February 22, 2012 and they would like to renew the same for one year. He said that Techlogix has quoted Rs.1,038,480 for one year’s support. The support includes online help from Oracle as well as downloading of latest patches/versions.

 

2.2       The Director ICT said that they have also contacted Oracle directly for the same support and they (Oracle) are willing to provide the same level of support for Rs.915,716.24 (plus applicable tax).

 

2.3       The Director ICT said that he would recommend to go directly with Oracle since they have given lower price.  He requested the EC to approve the contract for support directly from Oracle.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director said that as long as the Director ICT is satisfied that there would be no change in the level and quality of service, he has no objection but the renewal date should be June 18, 2012.

 

2.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know if there is any chance of reducing the number of licences.

 

2.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that the applicable tax should be included in the quoted rate because government auditors object to it.

 

2.7       The Director ICT said that he would look into it.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee approved the Oracle Licensing for ERP Solution with Oracle w.e.f. June 18, 2012.

 

Item No. 3       Presentation on Customer Satisfaction Survey 2011 by AC Nielson.

3.4       Ms. Tahmina said that the e     Q Index and Vulnerable Share are well within the APIMEF standard and Pakistani Norm but the commitment share has to be increased.

 

3.5       She presented service unit wise students’ evaluation according to which Financial Aid services showed a bit improvement over the previous year; but the hostels’ evaluation slightly slid. There is much improvement in library and a very significant improvement in maintenance services.  Students Affairs activity related services, CDC, CMS, FMS, and LMS, and Program Offices showed some improvement but the services of CS program showed a bit decline.  For the EMBA Program due to non-availability of adequate sample the result was not reliable.   

 

3.6       The Dean & Director congratulated the Registrar and the Librarian for significant improvement in Maintenance Services, and Library services respectively. He asked the Registrar and the Librarian to share the findings with their respective unit staff. However, he was concerned about the Hostel Services because with the improvement of facilities, the evaluation should have been better than last year.  He asked the Registrar to randomly ask the students staying in hostel what problems they were facing and then take action to remedy them.  It was also felt that since it is a combined report of both the boys’ and girls hostels, therefore, the evaluation might have gone down as girls’ hostel does not have as much facilities as does the boys’ hostel. It was suggested that the CDC should be separately evaluated and students affairs should be named as Event Management.  The survey for EMBA program should be covered over the weekend so that a representative sample of students is drawn.

 

3.7       Presenting the findings about service units as evaluated by faculty and management she said that the overall performance has increased and Finance Department has done the best among all the units and it has got zero Vulnerable Share. She said that the program offices especially the BBA program office needs to improve its interaction with faculty members.  Maintenance / help desk including electrical unit and ICT Maintenance unit need to improve their responsiveness and professional competence to resolve issues.  City Campus library needs some attention to be paid in order to improve its performance.      

 

3.8       The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. that she should share the Survey Report with the Academic Board, Selection Board, A&FC, BoG, and Corporate Advisory Board.

 

3.9       The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. to arrange his meeting with the service units heads and make a presentation of the survey report of service units in his meeting with the unit heads.

ACTION

3.12     The Registrar would randomly interview the students staying in hostel to know what their problems are.

 

3.13     For future the Director H.R. would get the CDC and Event Management separately evaluated. (The students’ affairs to be named as Event Management.)

 

3.14     The Director H.R. would include in the performance objectives of respective unit heads the critical areas identified in the Survey Report.

Item No. 5       To consider and approve the Horizontal Thrust Boring for laying KESC cables.

 

5.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that on approval by KESC of IBA’s application for a new 3.2 MW HT connection for the Main Campus (including the Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel) and on our payment of the cost involved (Rs.54,901,675/-)  to KESC, the utility company has started the cable laying work from its Gulistan-e-Jauhar grid to our premises (via the University premises). The work is required to be completed by June 30, 2012, as per permission given by KMC for road-digging. He said that while all road-digging and refilling for laying the cable is KESC’s responsibility, KMC does not allow to cut any roads for laying the cable. Instead, the cable is to be laid under roads by horizontal thrust boring which is IBA’s responsibility. The thrust boring is, however, to be done by KESC pre-qualified contractors only. Accordingly, he said that they invited quotations from KESC-prequalified contractors as per IBA advertisement published in dailies Jang and The News on May 29, 2012. In all four contractors submitted their quotations which were opened on June 12, 2012. The rates quoted by the four interested bidders are as follow:

 

·         Danish & Brothers                      :           Rs. 3,000/ per meter

·         Shafiq Ahmed Boring Wala           :           Rs. 4,000/- per meter

·         Hasan Khan Contractor               :           Rs. 3,500/- per meter

·         Al-Fatah International                :           Rs. 3,200 per meter

 

5.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that as Danish & Brothers have quoted the lowest rate, namely, Rs.3,000/- per meter, the TEC and CPC have recommended to award the contract to Danish & Brothers @ Rs.3,000/- per meter.  He said that the measurement of the 18 roads under which the horizontal boring is required to be done (along the 6 KM route) was taken by engineer Mr. Nazir Ali Naeem. The total width amounts to approximately 280 meter, on the basis of which the total cost involved comes to approx. Rs. 840,000/-.  He said that the actual payment will be made as per measurement and certification by KESC.  He requested the EC to approve the same.

 

DECISION

 

5.3       The Committee approve the Horizontal Thrust Boring for laying KESC cables to the lowest bidder M/s. Danish & Brothers.

Item No. 7       To consider a request from Ms. Sadia Ali, student of EMBA (PS-V) for make up of Mid Term Exam.

 

7.1       Ms. Sadia Ali, student of EMBA (PS-V) in her email addressed to the Program Director, reported that due to the sad demise of her grandfather on June 2, 2012 she was unable to appear in the paper of Advance Corporate Finance on June 3, 2012. She had also attached Death Certificates issued by the Hospital and the City Government.  She requested to allow her to appear for the make-up of Mid-Term Exam considering the circumstances which were beyond her control.

 

7.2       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that since there is no provision in the rules for make-up of missed exam under the reported circumstances, the EC may take a formal decision.

 

7.3       The Dean & Director said that we should have some sympathy for such cases and bring such cases into the purview of the existing policy that on the death of grandfather / grandmother of a student make-up exam be allowed to that student. He requested Dr. Sayeed Ghani to present the revised policy to the Academic Board through Academic Committee.

 

DECISION

 

7.4       The Committee allowed make up examination to Ms. Sadia Ali.

 

ACTION

 

7.5       The Controller of Examination would arrange make up examination for Ms. Sadia Ali for the paper of Advance Corporate Finance.

 

7.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani would present a modified make-up examination policy allowing make up examination for the student who misses the exam due the death of his/her grandfather / grandmother.  He would also obtain post facto approval from the Academic Board for Ms. Sadia Ali.
