The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1      To consider reward case of Mr. Abdul Khaliq.

 

1.1     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that Mr. Abdul Khaliq who is a Messenger at QEC was nominated for cash award of Rs.20,000 but Grievance Committee had objected to it on the ground that he was getting Rs.7000/- as QEC allowance. Dr. Amber Gul in her email has said that the policy should be implemented from next year because it is already approved and it will de-motivate the employee. Further Mr. Khaliq is not entitled to overtime because of this allowance. Dr. Sayeed Ghani asked if there was any such case which was not allowed. Mr. Adnan Hameed said that there are four such cases. Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that if we reconsider the policy we will have to pay to other four also and it would open a Pandora box.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.2     The EC decided that since a policy decision on the basis of the recommendation of GC has already been taken by the EC, such requests cannot be re-considered.  However, for next year if the recommendation is forwarded from the concerned HoD, EC would consider it on its merit.

 

1.3     Mr. Adnan Hameed would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Dr. Amber Gul Rashid.

 

Item No.2      To consider Revised Financial Policies and Procedures and its financial impact.

 

2.1     Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the Grievance Committee had recommended certain changes in the Financial Policies and Procedures with regard to Staff Welfare Fund and the EC had asked for its financial impact.

 

2.2     Mr. Moeid said that he discussed the recommended changes with Chairman, GC, Dr. Nasir Touheed. Mr. Moeid said that if the changes suggested by the GC are accepted, there would be an additional financial impact of around Rs.2.7 million. He said that the GC has recommended to include loans facility for purchase of house and vehicle/car also and to increase the membership entitlement limit of employees drawing gross salary of Rs.70,000 from the existing limited of Rs.50,000/- However, after discussion they have agreed at Rs.60,000/-  Maximum loan limit has been agreed to remain at its existing limit of 3 Gross Salary to be recovered in 18 months’ time period which was recommended at 4 Gross Salary to be recovered in 24 months.

 

2.3     Application for Wedding Loans as per policy is required 2 months before the wedding. However, GC had recommended that it should be one month but finally they agreed at 2 months’ notice.  He said that GC had suggested to increase the limit from 1.5 Gross Salary to 2 Gross Salaries of General Purpose Loan (to meet other emergency financial requirements) and to include loan for brothers and sisters marriage also. Mr. Moeid said that they agreed at two Gross Salary. However, when it was explained to them that for the General Purpose loan they do not need to mention the reason, they were satisfied.

 

2.4     Mr. Moeid said that on Grant against Medical Expenses for self and immediate family members, GC has recommended that it should be extended to employees upto BPS-15 or equivalent grade (currently it is for BPS-1 to 11 or equivalent grades) and the limit be reduced from Rs.25,000 to Rs.20,000/-

 

2.5     The Dean & Director said that considering the frequency of extra-ordinary medical expenses, natural disaster and other unforeseen catastrophic or tragic event  which is very few, it should be extended from BPS-11 to BPS-15 or equivalent grades while the limit should remain unchanged i.e. Rs.25,000/- For grant against school/college education of children, the Dean & Director said that schooling is to be encouraged and the grant will be given upto BPS-12 or equivalent but considering the inflation, the limit should be enhanced upto Rs.24,000/- He said that we are giving this as an incentive and the grant would be reimbursed on production of proper evidence.  

 

2.6     Mr. Moeid said that it has been suggested by GC to increase employees’ contribution to the Welfare Fund from 2 percent to 5%.  However, he said that most of the employees are not in favour of increasing their contribution. He said that it is optional. The Chairman suggested that employees should be encouraged to save as much as they can and they be given profit too on their investment.

 

2.7     Mr. Moeid said that it has also been recommended that IBA’s contribution to the fund be increased from existing 0.3 percent of the total revenue generated through students’ fees to 0.6 percent.  Mr. Moeid said that he explained to them that even at 0.3 percent the amount of contribution is already increasing due to increase in the amount collected on account of fees and the rising number of students.

 

2.8     Mr. Moeid said that GC has also recommended to increase the salary range for educational scholarship. For employees getting a salary of up to Rs.30,000, 100% scholarship is approved while they want the salary limit to be revised i.e. from Rs.30,000 to Rs.45,000/- and so on. The Committee agreed to increase the limit from Rs.30,000 to Rs.40,000 for 100% scholarship and in that proportion limit be increased for 75% percent and 50% scholarships.

 

DECISION & ACTION          

 

2.9     The Committee approved the changes as suggested by the Director Finance and the Dean & Director in the Financial Policies and Procedures.

 

2.10    The Director Finance would present the revised Financial Policies and Procedures to the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No.3      To consider and approve mapping of titles i.e. use of government / BPS designations for BPS employees.

 

3.1     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that based on the recommendation of the GC regarding the use of government / BPS designations for BPS employees the Dean & Director IBA advised HR to develop a mapping of government and IBA Scale titles. He presented a chart showing IBA Job Band titles, IBA Job Band, Equivalent BPS Grades and BPS titles. To a question Mr. Adnan clarified that the titles have been derived from ESTA Code and the HEC guidelines. He said that it would be notified and for future mapping the same guidelines would be used. He further said that the IBA Scale titles would be used but on backend BPS title would be used for implementation of any government notification. Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that before notifying, GC’s comments should be taken, Mr. Adnan Hameed said that he would send this mapping document to GC for their review.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.2     The Committee approved the mapping of titles as presented by Mr. Adnan Hameed.

 

3.3     Mr. Adnan Hameed, after consulting with GC, would notify the titles.  

 

 

Item No.4      To consider proposed Activity Based Rewards.

 

4.1     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that they have received two proposals for Activity Based Rewards. One is for the Planning & Execution of Contingency Water Supply for Aman Tower, and another proposal is from CEE.

 

DECISION

 

4.2     The Dean & Director suggested to bring all such proposals together in a year and not in isolation. He said that we should have a comprehensive review of all rewards where quality is ensured. He said that although he has a great appreciation for those who have been recommended yet presenting cases in piece meal is not a good idea as it does not provide a complete comparative picture.

 

4.3     The Committee approved cash rewards of Rs.20,000/- each for the recommended staff members. With regard to CEE rewards, Mr. Adnan was advised to share the recommendation table with Director Finance for verification.

  

Item No.5      Proposal for Exam Invigilation Remuneration for Support Staff.

 

5.1     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the support staff including Office Attendants, Security Guards and Sanitary Workers perform dedicated duties during exams, however they are not entitled for Exam Invigilation remuneration and they are only entitled for overtime after duty time. He said that the proposal is that Exam Invigilation remuneration may also be paid to these support staff members at a lower rates compared to clerical staff. The proposed rate for exam invigilation remuneration for the support staff is @Rs.300/- per exam duty for mid-term and @Rs.600/- per duty for final exam. They will not be entitled for Over Time equal to the duration of their total exam duties performed during the day.

 

5.2     The proposed rates for third party staff is Rs.200/- & Rs.400/- per duty with no overtime for the duration they perform exam duty.  Mr. Adnan Hameed also suggested that:

 

 -  Number of duties per employee should be capped.

 - HOD should be consulted before assigning exam duties to their departmental staff (Ref: EC decision of 27 October, 2014)

- Staff members who are not eligible for Overtime should mainly be assigned duties after office hours. 

In case, they perform duties they should either complete office hours or necessary adjustments should be made in their exam remuneration.

 

5.3     It was suggested that support staff should be given an option either to take overtime or invigilation remuneration. Managerial staff of BPS-17 or above or its equivalent grades should not be eligible for exam duty except staff of Examination Department.

 

5.4     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that Office Attendants and Security Guards are also assigned responsibilities during exams. He presented lists of their responsibilities during exams. He also wanted clarification about the Sanitary Workers who perform dedicated duties during the exam but are not directly related to exam duties. The Dean & Director suggested that on the basis of exam related responsibilities the Office Attendants and Security Guards should be given the option either to take overtime or invigilation remuneration.  The Sanitary Worker will be entitled for the over time only.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

5.5     The Committee approved the suggestions noted above.

 

5.6     On capping of number of duties per employee, Mr. Abdul Wajed would do the working.

 

 

Item No.6      To consider and approve changes suggested by the Grievance Committee in the Medical Policy.

 

6.1     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the Grievance Committee has suggested the following changes in Medical Policy:

Ø  Inclusion of retired employees and pensioners along-with the spouse under medical coverage

Ø  Constitution of a committee that will monitor usage of medical facility and will also facilitate employees during hospitalization as and when needed.

Ø  Coverage of OPD including those expenses incurred during pregnancy.

Ø  Room Entitlements for Hospitalization

Ø  Coverage of pre & post hospitalization expenses upto 30 days

Ø  Spouse nomination should be for one year (in case of more than one wife).

Ø  Divorced or widowed daughters should also be included

Ø  Mentally retarded / (physically disabled) children with no age restriction  

6.2     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the GC has also recommended the following:

•       Infant vaccination/Hepatitis/Radiotherapy/Chemotherapy/Dialysis/ Physiotherapy/Thalassemia. 

•       Treatment in an emergency ward not resulting in hospitalization

•       Dental treatment  

•       CT Scan, MRI, ETT/ECHO, Thallium Test, Nuclear Lab Test 

•       Deduction Schedule to apply in case the difference of LNH and other hospitals is more than 30%

•       Add new hospitals

6.3     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that considering the increase in medical expenses, it is not recommended that the retired employees for included for medical facilities. He said that the proposal of constituting a committee to perform monitoring visits regarding appropriate use of this facility as well as providing any required facilitation to employees admitted in hospital has been accepted. For the room entitlement he said that they have used health insurance model which is as under:

Grade 16 – 19

Job Band 2 - 5

Grade 9 – 15

Job Band J2 (III) - 1

Grade 1 – 8

Job Band J1 – J2 (ii)

Rs.12,000

Rs.7,500

Rs.3,000

6.4     Mr. Adnan Hameed made the following suggestions:

o    Pre & Post hospitalization expenses should be covered upto 15 days, however, specialized investigations (MRI, CT Scan, Angiography etc.) should be covered even in OPD settings.

o    Cancer treatment will be covered including both chemotherapy and radiotherapy or any other type of cancer treatment. Kidney Dialysis; Hepatitis B & C medication and vaccination will also be covered in OPD setting.

o    Essential medication of dread diseases will be covered even in case of non-hospitalization for employees in BPS Grades 1-11 & Job Band J1 & J2.

o    Accidental emergencies and medical emergencies treated in panel hospitals will be covered, the treatment should be given in emergency ward; however, the pre & post hospitalization expenses reimbursement will not be allowed in such cases as well as in specialized investigations.

o    New Hospitals will be included in IBA Panel.

o    Deduction of expenses in any hospital other than LNH or a non-panel hospital will be done in case the difference of expenses is more than 30% of the LNH estimate.

o    Deduction will be made in maximum 24 monthly installments. However, on exceptional cases the period can be extended.

6.5     On nomination of spouse in case of more than one wives, he said that they have allowed one change during the employment.  Dental treatment is normally not covered and is not recommended. Similarly, coverage of divorced / widow daughters are not recommended for medical coverage; however, unmarried daughters with no age limit will continue to be covered throughout employment life.

 

6.6     To a question Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the room rates have been derived from the rates set in Health Insurance, LNH benchmark hospital and the expected increase in room charges.

 

6.7     The Dean & Director said that the OPD would be provided at the Campus clinic and would be available for self and dependents.  Essential medication would be allowed only on the recommendation of an approved medical practitioner referred by our panel hospital.

 

DECISION

 

6.8     The Committee asked Mr. Adnan Hameed to present this to the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No.7      To consider and approve proposed policy framework for engaging IBA non-teaching staff in assignments not directly related to their job.

 

7.1     This item was deferred. The Head of HR would circulate the proposal for consideration in the next EC meeting.

 

Item No.8      Saturdays to be observed as Holidays.

 

8.1     The Dean & Director said that the Grievance Committee wants that on Friday the office timing should be for full day and on Saturday, as per government notification, IBA should be off. However, due to various programs including EMBA classes which are held on Saturdays and Sundays, staff of Program Office, Building Incharge and concerned staff will be required to come on Saturdays.  He said that Saturdays would be off day from the end of the current semester. He said that we had taken exemption from the Governor’s House for keeping IBA open on Saturdays but at the recommendation of the GC we would follow the government notification. He advised the Registrar to issue notification. He said that whosoever the Dean & Director IBA would be, will observe 5 days a week for IBA.

 

8.2     Mr. Abdul Wajed said that during exams he would need his staff on Saturdays too. The Dean & Director said that they can be called on exam days. He clarified that classes and exams will be held as usual on Saturdays too.

8.3     Mr. Adnan Hameed said that as per rule, those who are not entitled to overtime would be provided compensatory leave for working on off days.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

8.4     The Registrar would issue a notification for observing Saturdays as Holidays with effect from the first Saturday after the end of the current semester.

    

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
