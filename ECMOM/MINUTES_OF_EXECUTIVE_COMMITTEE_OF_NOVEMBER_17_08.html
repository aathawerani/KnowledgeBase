Item No. 1      To consider note from General Manager (P&D) regarding Procurement and Installation of Generators at the City Campus.

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmad, General Manager (P&D) in his note of November 15, 2008 stated that they are in the process of re-tendering for procurement of a 500 KVA generator for the City Campus.  However, the Electrical Engineer of IBA has suggested that in view of our total power requirements and the deteriorating condition of the two existing generators (100 KVA and 150 KVA) we install two 500 KVA generators instead of one.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve procurement of two 500 KVA generators for which funds have already been approved in the 2008-09 budget.

The Director Projects reported that we are in the process of hiring Electrical Consultant who will determine the total load and present his report for consideration.

DECISION

1.2              The Committee in principle approved the procurement of two 500 KVA generators. However, the Committee decided that the second generator would be procured and installed only after the Electrical Consultant justifies the need for the second generator on the basis of the actual load.

1.3              The Committee requested the Director Projects to let the Committee know as to when the Electrical Consultant would be hired.

1.4              The Committee requested the Director Projects to ensure that all the air-conditioners are commissioned in both the campuses by March 2009.

1.5              The Director Projects was requested to contact KESC for additional connected load.

1.6              The Director Projects was advised to ask the Electrical Consultant to make provision for electrical load for future ERP requirements also.

ACTION

1.7              The Director Projects will arrange to procure one 500 KVA generator and up-date the Executive Committee on the hiring of Electrical Consultant and contact KESC for obtaining additional connected load for the City Campus.

1.8       The Director I.T. will give electrical load requirements for IT Centre and ERP to the Electrical Consultant.

Item No. 3      To consider proposal from Director H.R. for inclusion of probationary period in the employment contract.

3.1       The Director H.R. stated that one of the terms and conditions of employment of non-teaching contract employees is to serve one month’s advance notice from either side or salary in lieu thereof if either the IBA does not want to retain that employee or the employee does not want to continue his / her services at the IBA. However, there is no Probationary Period.  In the absence of probationary period it sometimes becomes difficult for both the IBA as well as for the new employee to discontinue services.     

3.2       The Director H.R. further stated that in case an employee finds it difficult to adjust in IBA or the IBA finds a new recruit not up to the mark, the employee or the IBA cannot discontinue the services without fulfilling the conditions. In such cases either the employee leaves without fulfilling the condition or serves the notice period half-heartedly. Similarly, the IBA has to retain unwanted employee for a month or pay one month’s extra salary.

3.3       She suggested that in order to make it easier for both the employee and the IBA, we should include probation period of one month for employees hired for six months or a maximum of one year; and three months’ probation period for employees hired beyond one year. While on probation, the contract can be terminated without any notice period from either side.

DECISION

3.4       The Committee agreed with Director H.R. and approved the proposal presented in 3.3 above.

3.5       The Committee decided that the probation period as suggested in 3.3 above would be applicable to faculty also.

ACTION

3.6       The Director H.R. will ensure that for all appointments in future, the probation clause is included.

b)                To consider note from Director Finance regarding concessional fee programs.

6.4       The Director Finance said that there are two different types of tuition fee structures prevailing in the IBA both for BBA & MBA students.

i)  One is full fee program

ii) The other one is on concessional fee basis

6.5       He said that concessional fee is granted to those top 50% students who pass the IBA entrance test with higher grades. These 50% students also include those who have strong financial background and belong to comparatively richer class (69% of students inducted in 2008-09 semester belong to income group of Rs.300,000 and above). They may not deserve concession and instead the concessional fee be offered to poor and needy students. He said that this practice is contrary to our scholarship criteria where financial aid is to be provided to those who are deserving and very needy.

6.6       He further said that the IBA is being funded from HEC to cover the concessional program for 50% top students. In view of the current scenario he apprehended that HEC grants in future years will not be available and precisely will be zero in the academic year 2012-13. On the basis of HEC funded grants which is going to reduce every year effective from current academic year 2008-09, the total cost of concessional students i-e tuition fees and other charges will be out of IBA funds.

6.7       He clarified that at present 4 batches of concessional program students are studying and the last batch will pass out in the Spring semester 2012.

6.8       He suggested that effective from academic year 2009-10 we should revamp the concessional fee program, otherwise the IBA will be incurring great cost.

6.9       The Director said that personally he was not satisfied with the existing arrangement of scholarship and would suggest to divide the scholarships into two types; one is to give merit based scholarship to top 10% of the successful candidates of each program (except Evening Program) instead of 50%. These top 10% students will pay the concessional fee. They may take pride in having been awarded merit scholarship. The second type of financial assistance would be need based scholarship which would be available to any student who fulfils the scholarship criteria. In addition to financial assistance, students can obtain loans and Qarz-e-Hasna from banks for which the IBA will facilitate the students.

6.10     The Director said that if we allow concessional fee to only top 10% successful candidates, we will have an additional revenue of around Rs.13 million by not allowing concessional fee to 40% students who will now pay full fee on the basis of current fee structure. The working is as under:

            A. Under the Proposed Plan

       10% of total intake of 400 students = 40 x Rs.80,000                 =  Rs. 3.2 m

                 90% of total intake of 400 students = 360x Rs.160,000            =  Rs.57.6 m

                 Total Revenue                                                                  =  Rs.60.8 m

            B.  Under Existing Policy

                 200 students (50% of 400 students) to pay half of Rs.160,000 = Rs.16.00 m

                 Remaining 200 (50% of 400 students) to pay full fee               = Rs.32.00 m

                 Total Revenue                                                                  = Rs.48.00 m

       Additional Revenue {A – B (Rs.60.8 million – Rs.48.00 million)} =

       Rs.12.8 million. Say  =  Rs.13.00 million.

6.11     The Director proposed that the students enjoying concessional fee will have to maintain a minimum CGPA of 3.5. 

DECISION

6.12          The Committee agreed to the proposal of the Director as mentioned in 6.9 – 6.11 and recommended to the Audit & Finance Committee for consideration.

6.13          The Committee further decided that the students will be allowed to be admitted to either campus of their choice since their fee structure will be the same on first come first serve basis.

ACTION

6.14          The Director Finance will send the recommendation of the Executive Committee to the Audit & Finance for consideration.
