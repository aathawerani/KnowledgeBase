Item No.1       To consider and approve waste water disposal system for the boys’ hostel and staff colony.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that in view of recurring water shortage at the Main Campus, a project for getting an independent water supply line and laying IBA’s separate sewerage lines was conceived and approved in 2008. The consultant, NESPAK, initially estimated the total cost as Rs.40 to 45 million. However, on completion of the required survey and design, the estimated cost rose to Rs.117 million. It was therefore decided that the project be implemented in phases, the Phase 1 being getting an independent water connection from KW&SB, construction of underground and overhead water tanks at the Staff Colony, and laying water transmission pipelines from the intake point to the Boys’ Hostel, via the Staff Colony and Main Campus. Unfortunately, due to various reasons (notably the law and order situation in the city last year and lack of cooperation from the University), the construction work of the project started in January 2011, is still incomplete.

 

1.2       With regard to sewerage lines, Mr. Shameem said that the NESPAK design was based on the University’s Master Plan that envisaged construction of a main trunk line to which IBA’s sewerage lines were to be connected. However, the construction of the University trunk line was found to be dependent upon HEC funds which were not released to the University. In the meantime, some of the IBA projects in hand have either been completed or are nearing completion. At the Main Campus, the Adamjee Academic Centre and Gani &Tayub Auditorium have been completed while Aman CED, Razzak Tabba (New Academic Block) and the Students’ Center will be completed this year. Sewerage lines for the area are a part of the External Development work the execution of which is being supervised by the Director Projects, Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz. Therefore, his request to the Executive Committee for consideration and approval is related with the Boys’ Hostels area and the Staff Colony only.

 

1.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed suggested that as the New Boys’ Hostel is nearing completion and will be ready for accommodating students in August/September this year, it is important that an action be taken to arrange disposal of the waste water that will be generated. A similar arrangement is required also at the Staff Colony where the Visiting Faculty Hostel is under construction and work on the New Girls’ Hostel will start within 6 to 8 weeks. He reported that the matter was discussed first with NBCL’s associate (SMC) and then with NESPAK who have a team that specializes in designing waste water disposal and treatment systems. The preliminary proposal they have submitted gives three options which, briefly described, are as follows:

1.4       Based on NESPAK’s three options, Mr. Shameem Ahmed presented details of the estimated populations and the daily generation of waste water. He requested the Executive Committee to consider and decide about the most acceptable option. He said that the life of each option may be taken as 5 to 10 years.  However, his recommendation was as follows:

 

Boys’ Hostels:             Option 1, provided soil percolation test confirms that the soil is suitable for soakage pits. Otherwise Option 2.

 

Staff Colony:              All  three options  are  technically  feasible.  But Option 3 appears to  be  the  best  if  we  can  afford to       spend Rs.9.6 million.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director said that we should see what is the least cost temporary solution because ultimately the waste water will go to the trunk line of Karachi University. He further said that if the matter is not resolved at the Registrars’ level, then he would talk to the new Vice Chancellor of Karachi University.

 

1.6       The Registrar said that Option 3 is very costly solution and Option 2 is also not a better choice because transporting out the effluent would be very cumbersome.

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee approved Option 1 for both Boys’ Hostels and Staff Colony.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and recommend to A&FC the award of contract for construction of New Girls’ Hostel to the lowest bidder.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the following eight contractors were prequalified for the project of New Girls’ Hostel:

 

1.      M/s. Mughals Constructors (Pvt.)

2.      M/s. DR Construction

3.      M/s. Qavi Engineers (Pvt.) Ltd.

4.      M/s. AH Construction (Pvt.) Ltd.,

5.      M/s. Buildfast Countrywide (Pvt.) Ltd.

6.      M/s. Total Construction

7.      M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises

8.      M/s. Mecatech (Pvt.) Ltd.

 

2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed informed the Committee that one of the pre-qualified contractors namely, M/s. Mecatech (Pvt.) Ltd., expressed its inability to participate in the bid. Tender documents were purchased by all others. However, only four of them submitted their bids which were opened on March 13, 2012, in the presence of the bidders’ representatives and other relevant persons. He presented the Comparative Statement of the bids according to which M/s. AH Construction (Pvt.) Ltd. have quoted the lowest price, namely, Rs.94,483,564/-, while M/s. D.R Construction is the second lowest at a price of Rs.101,324,187/-.   

 

2.3      Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the four bids received were evaluated by our project manager Consult-Tech International. The project manager has recommended that the contract may be awarded to the lowest bidder, namely, M/s. A.H. Construction (Pvt.) Ltd.  He said that an Evaluation Report from the Consultant, FNMH Architecture, is awaited.

 

2.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the proposal was considered by the Technical Evaluation Committee in its meeting held on March 28, 2012, and by the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on March 29, 2012. Both have recommended that the contract be awarded to the lowest bidder, namely, AH Construction (Pvt.) Ltd., at the quoted price of Rs.94,483,564/-. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the same for consideration by the Audit & Finance Committee in its meeting scheduled for April 7, 2012.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee endorsed the recommendations of the TEC and CPC and recommended to the A&FC that the contract be awarded to the lowest bidder, namely, M/s AH Construction (Pvt.) Ltd., at the quoted price of Rs.94,483,564/-.

 

ACTION

 

2.6       Mr. Shmeem Ahmed would present the recommendation of the EC to the A&FC for consideration and recommendation to the Board for approval.

 

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve the cost estimates for construction of electrical infrastructure at the Boys’ Hostel and staff colony.

 

3.1      Mr. Khalid Pervez of KP & Associates made a presentation on electrical power infrastructure for Boys’ Hostel, and the Staff Colony (including the New Girls’ Hostel, Visiting Faculty Hostel and the proposed Faculty Apartments).  He highlighted the design basis of these projects and their estimated costs. 

 

Design Basis of Boys’ Hostel and its estimated costs:

 

·         Main Power to Block ‘A’ (new Boys’ Hostel), and existing boys’ hostel.

·         100% Emergency power to block ‘A’ only.

·         Space provision for 11 kVA – panel of future blocks

·         Maximum demand 396 KW

·         Maximum emergency load 290 KW

·         Capacity of DG set 400 KVA

·         Cost estimate: Rs.16.254 million which includes all sub-station equipment, cables etc. but excludes civil construction.

 

3.2      Mr. Khalid Pervez suggested slight expansion in the KESC sub-station room to accommodate panel of future blocks which may cost an additional amount of around Rs.4 to 5 lacs.

 

3.3       The Dean & Director said that since we have an assurance from KESC that there would be no load shedding for KU and IBA, there is no need to make provision for 100% emergency power for boys’ hostel.  However, he said that priorities will have to be determined in advance as to which areas are to be provided full power in case of power break down; the ACs which are on the dining hall and lounge will have to be switched off in case of power break down. He said that a parallel system should be used at the minimum as a back-up. He said that the estimated amount of Rs.5 million for generator should be reduced as much as possible as we do not want 100% back up. He further said that superfluous items which are not needed immediately should be removed.

 

3.4       The Director Projects wanted to know as to what would happen to the recently laid new cables of Rs.19 million in the existing Boys’ Hostel.

 

3.5       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that it would become redundant.  He requested the Director Projects to provide him details of the cables so that it could be used at new Boys’ Hostel or at Main Campus.

 

Design Basis of the Staff Colony (including the New Girls’ Hostel, Visiting Faculty Hostel and the proposed Faculty Apartments) and its estimated cost:

  

·         Power to New Girls’ Hostel

·         Power to new Water Project

·         Power to Staff Colony

·         Power to network for Visiting Faculty Hostel and the proposed Faculty Apartments

·         Refurbishment of street lighting poles and extension up to Visiting Faculty Hostel

·         Emergency Power to:

o   New Girls’ Hostel – partly

o   Visiting Faculty Hostel - 100%

o   Proposed Faculty Apartments – partly

o   Street lighting – 100%

 

·         Total Electrical Load 831 KW

·         Maximum Demand 537 KW

·         Maximum Emergency Load 302 KW

·         Capacity on DG Set 450 to 500 KVA

 

·         Total Cost Estimate Rs.25.939 (includes all sub-station equipment, cables, poles etc., but excludes civil construction).

 

3.6      The Dean & Director suggested that instead of emergency power UPS at new Girls’ Hostel be provided. He further said that there should be no emergency street lighting.  When there is normal power supply then the street light will be on and if there is no normal power supply then there would be no street lights. However, on main gate there should be emergency light attached with UPS.  For Visiting Faculty Hostel he said that there should be partial emergency power which should not cover ACs. The Dean & Director advised to use UPS wherever possible instead of gen set. He also suggested use of solar panels for external lighting.

 

3.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that we have an approval of 400 KVA gen set for the new Girls’ Hostel.  The Dean & Director said that this gen set can be used for emergency power supply.  He suggested to remove Rs.8 million from the estimated cost of 500 KVA gen set. He said that if in the approved 400 KVA gen set, street light is covered then fine, otherwise there would be no emergency power supply for street light.      

 

DECISION

 

3.8      The Committee requested Mr. Khalid Pervez to present the revised cost estimate to the EC in the light of the suggestions noted above.

 

 

Item No.4       Conceptual approval for additional CCTV cameras at City Campus.

 

4.1       Mr. S. Fahim Uddin, Manager General Maintenance/Security said that in September 2009 they were asked to install CCTV camera at both the campuses. He said that 16 cameras in each Campus were installed because beyond 16 another DVR is required. He said that after over two years it started giving problem. Out of 16, two cameras have become non-operational and DVR has also become non-operational.  He further said that because of expansion at the City Campus, there is a need to increase the number of CCTV cameras. He said that the CCTV camera of better quality (made in Taiwan) can be available at around Rs.11,000/- each and the DVR would cost around Rs.60,000/-

 

4.2       The Director ICT said that he would make a presentation to the EC next week about the IP based camera.  He clarified that the existing sets cannot work with IP but they can be used separately for Boys’ Hostel or Girls’ Hostel if the DVR is repaired. 

 

DECISION

 

4.3       The Committee allowed the purchase of two CCTV cameras and one DVR for the City Campus in replacement of the none-functional ones.

 

 

Item No. 5      Combined cost of both phases of FCS Building, City Campus.

 

5.1       The Director Projects stated that in its 188th Meeting held on 29th November 2011, the Executive Committee had approved the merger of Phase-II of the FCS Building and its award of work to the existing contractor already working on Phase-1. The EC had also decided that as per SPPRA we can go for additional work upto 15% of the total cost and give the work to the same contractor. However, the HVAC, water proofing and purchase and installation of any equipment could be given out under a separate tender because we cannot get these jobs done through civil contractor but by specialists. By issuing separate tender for such jobs/items, the cost of additional civil work can be restricted to 15% of the total cost. The saving through value engineering in Phase I can also be utilized on Phase-2. 

 

5.2       The Director Projects presented the following details for the information of the Executive Committee:

 

Approved Awarded Cost for Phase-I after 0.33% rebate                          Rs. 61,081,747

Approved variation 15%                                                                    Rs.   9,162,262

Total amount                                                                                  Rs.70, 244, 009

 

5.3       The Director Projects reported that the final calculations submitted by the Architects/ Consultants and checked by Project managers and Projects department are as follows:

                                                           

Contract cost after value Engineering for Phase-I                                   Rs.49, 600,796

(excluding Cost of HVAC but including cost of water proofing)

 

After 0.33% rebate    (Original bid rebate)                                             Rs.49, 437,113

Cost of Phase-II after value Engineering                                                Rs.21, 078,504

(excluding cost of HVAC)

Total Cost after value Engineering                                                        Rs.70,515,617

 

HVAC works cost Phase-I                                                                    Rs.5, 230,350

HVAC works cost Phase-II                                                                   Rs.3, 374,510

 

5.4       The Director Projects reported that after addition of Phase-II cost, the total cost increased to 15.44% from the original bid price.  He said that the HVAC works will be done through separate Tender the total estimated cost of HVAC works will be Rs.8,604,860.  In nut shell the total cost of FCS building including HVAC works becomes Rs.79,120,477 for Phase-I and Phase-II.  However, he said that they are trying to do further value engineering so that the total cost comes down from Rs.79 million to Rs.70 million.

 

5.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know if the cost of some additional work that was suggested has been covered in this total cost.

 

5.6       The Director Projects said that he would cover the cost of additional work in the total cost.  He said that the project would be completed by February 2013.

 

DECISION

 

5.7       The Committee noted the up-date and appreciated the efforts of the Director Projects in bringing down the total cost through value engineering.
