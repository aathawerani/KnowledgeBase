MINUTES OF THE 58th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON JULY 27, 2009

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                             Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                      Associate Dean
  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN         Registrar
  4. Mr. Ali Taha                                   Director Finance
  5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                Director Projects
  6. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                        Director I.C.T
  7. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                       Director H.R.

 Mr. Sohail Khan (Electrical Consultant) and Mr. Faisal Zia (Manger IT) attended by special invitation.

 The following items were discussed:

  Item No. 1      To consider proposal regarding publication of Annual Reports.

            1.1        Dr. Nishat informed the Committee members that the soft copy of the Annual Reports for year 2006-7 and 2007-8 were ready for printing. Data compilation was in progress in respect of the 2008-9 report and the same would be ready for printing shortly.    

 1.2       The Registrar informed that he had obtained quotations for the printing of the document from the market. The lowest quote received for printing of 500 copies was for Rs. 170/- per copy.  The issue of work order was pending mainly for clarification on the number of copies that were required to be printed. 

      1.3       The Director informed that the Annual Report of any institution was an important academic document and was required to be provided to the Board of Governors, donors, Government and civic agencies. The report should cover the period of one academic session i.e. from July to July each year. He emphasized that there should be consistency of information between the information printed in Program Announcement and the Annual Report; furthermore, the contents should be factual and an apt portrayal of the efforts of the Academia as well as the management. About 500 copies must be printed annually of the same; his preference would be that IBA develop in-house facility for desktop printing of bulk items. In this regard, he directed the GM (IT) to examine the feasibility of bulk printing in IBA.

 DECISION

The following decisions were made:

 a.       The Annual Report was to be responsible for all activates related to the compilation of data, and formatting and publication of the Annual Report. Relevant portions of the information contained in the Director’s monthly report to the Board of Governors was to be incorporated in the Annual Report.

 

b.       The Annual Report was to be published and distributed by the last week of August each year.

 

c.       The IBA website and portal were to host information on the Annual Report.

 

d.      Arrangements are to be made to get 500 copies each of the 2006-7 and 2007-8 reports to be published from the market this year and distributed by the 30th of August. A copy each is to be given to all faculty members and a suitable number of copies is to be retained in each of the two IBA libraries.  

 ACTION

 1.4              Dr. Nishat is to include in the terms of reference of the “Academic Committee” the responsibility for preparation of the Annual Report.

 

1.5              The Registrar is to arrange printing of the Annual Reports of 2006-7, 2007-8 as per existing sample.

 

 Item No. 2      To consider the request made by the Project Director for provision of a 250 Kva diesel generator for the under-renovation Academic Block at Main Campus as requested by the electrical consultant SK Engineers .    

 

2.1        The Director Projects informed that upon calculation of the electrical load of the under-renovation Academic Block, it was evident that an independent diesel generator set was required for meeting the standby power requirements of the Water Chilling unit installed for air-conditioning of the Academic Block. He emphasized that the continued availability of air-conditioning in the block was essential since there were no other arrangements for ventilation  

 

2.2       Electrical Consultants, M/s. S. K. Consultants represented by Mr. Suhail Khan explained the detailed reasons for their recommendations for purchase of standby generation. They informed the Executive Committee that from the study of existing electrical system and availability of power from KESC they had concluded that the existing prime power of 110 KW from KESC when enhanced to 500 KW can meet the increased demand of Academic Block (220 KW) along with the existing loads of Administration Building, Multipurpose Building and auxiliaries. However, when all the buildings are completed the prime power requirement from KESC will be to the tune of about 2.8 MW.  In order to meet today’s requirement, IBA needs to apply for load enhancement from KESC for 110 KW to 500 KW as the 500 KVA transformer is already installed and then to 2.8 MW ultimately.

 

2.3       Discussing the need of standby generation, he explained that the existing 250 KVA diesel generator which is catering for Administration Building and 150 KVA standby generator will not be sufficient to cater for entire standby requirement of Academic Block.

 

2.4       Director IBA suggested that a combination be devised by means of which loads be managed in a manner wherein the load requirement of chiller at Academic Block be cut off. To this, the consultants replied that the Architectural requirements have no provision for fans and ventilators and as such we have to have a dependable standby generation.

 DECISION

 The Executive Committee after detailed discussion concluded that there is a need of standby generation and that the consultants should submit a detailed report along with the line diagrams to support this necessity. Meanwhile, owing to the shortage of time and requirement to make the academic block at main campus available for academic session commencing August 31st 2009, it was decided that the necessary action be initiated for procurement of the required generator.

 ACTION

 Director Projects, Project Director HEC & Director Finance should work out if the amount of Rs.4.0 million as intimated by the consultants is available or there will be a need for re-appropriation of funds. The case is to be put up at the next A&FC meeting too for the requisite approval.
