1.1       The Director Projects reported that a meeting of the Technical Evaluation Committee was held on July 14, 2010 just after the meeting of Central Purchase Committee was over to discuss the above mentioned agenda item. The Director Projects briefed about the methodology for scoring of the Project Managers.  He said that out of six Project Managers, five Project Managers submitted their Technical Proposals. Their names along with points they scored out of 100 points are mentioned below:

 

a) M/s. Consult-Tech                                 86 Points         

b) M/s. AAA Partnership                              89 Points

c) M/s. INTEG                                           84 Points

d) M/s. EA Consulting                                 92 Points

e) M/s. Noor Durrani                                   82 Points                     

 

1.2       The Director Projects reported that after the Technical Evaluation, the Financial Proposals were opened in the presence of the Technical Evaluation Committee. The representatives of M/s. Consult-Tech, M/s. INTEG, M/s. EA Consulting & M/s. Noor Durrani Associates participated, whereas the representative of M/s. AAA Partnership did not participate in the opening of the Financial Proposal. The rates quoted by the five Project Managers are mentioned as under:

 

a) M/s. Consult-Tech                     3.72% of the total cost of the Project   

b) M/s. AAA Partnership                  4.50% of the total cost of the Project    

c) M/s. INTEG                               2.85% of the total cost of the Project

d) M/s. EA Consulting                     4.50% of the total cost of the Project

e) M/s. Noor Durrani                       5.67% of the total cost of the Project

 

1.3       The Director Projects presented a comparative statement for final selection of the successful Project Manager. He said that as per the Comparative Statement, M/s. INTEG has a combined valuation of 87 points and obtained 1st Rank. He said that 80% weightage was given to Technical Evaluation and 20% weightage was given to Financial Evaluation. He further said that there was a difference of only one (01) point between the next rank i.e. M/s. EA Consulting (Private) Limited who had secured eight (08) points more than M/s. INTEG in technical evaluation. He recommended that M/s. INTEG be considered for award of work only if they give an undertaking that they would provide experienced and professional engineers deployed at the site and their head office after approval of IBA.

 

1.4       The Director wanted to know if the weightage criteria were mentioned in the RFP. The Director Projects replied that it was not mentioned in the RFP but this was in the knowledge of the prospective bidders.  The Director asked the Director Projects that in future he should include the criteria in the RFP.

 

1.5       The Director said that the Financial Evaluation score has not been mentioned in the memorandum presented by the Director Projects. He instructed that instead of attachments, the memorandum should be self-contained and the statement should logically flow.  He also said that the proposed undertaking is not required to be obtained. He further said that it is the responsibility of the Technical Evaluation Committee to thoroughly study the Procurement Manual. 

 

1.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed observed that for just one point they are not considering the second lowest bidder i.e. M/s. EA Consulting.  

 

DECISION

 

1.7       The Committee referred the proposal for appointment of Project Managers for detailed Supervision and Project Management of new Academic Block at Main Campus to the Technical Evaluation Committee who will thoroughly evaluate the proposal both technical and financial in light of Procurement Manual.

 

ACTION

 

1.8       The Director Projects would present the proposal to the Technical Evaluation Committee for its thorough evaluation of both Technical and Financial proposals and then re-submit the proposal to the EC along with recommendation of the TEC.            

 

 

Item No. 2      Relocation of 4 generators at the City Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects stated that relocation of the four generators is a part of the project for shifting of the existing KESC Sub-station to the north-east corner in the Campus in order to make the space available for construction of the new Auditorium. He reported that a meeting of all available stakeholders was held at the City Campus on April 24, 2010 in which it was decided that the four generators would be relocated close to and in tandem with the proposed new sub-station in the north-east corner along the OMI Road.  However, he said that subsequently the following objections were raised:

 

a)      Placing all generators near the Admin. Block will create unbearable noise pollution, causing serious distress to the people working in the building, and

 

b)      Installation of the generators at the proposed location would create vibrations and electromagnetic flux which may cause damaging effects on the new data centre equipment in the Admin. Block.

 

2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that various alternatives were considered again; one of them was to place the generators in an L-shape in the corner behind PMA premises and the open plot in the South. He said that this option was still open and could be considered by the Executive Committee as it is close to Electrical Room and he as well as Mr. Jawad, Electrical Engineer recommends that.

 

2.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed further stated that in order to sort out the issue, he and Mr. Rehanul Ambia had a meeting with Mr. Husnain Lotia of EA Consulting on June 23, 2010.  Mr. Lotia proposed that the 4 generators be placed on a raised steel platform by the PMA wall. This would be a temporary solution.  The generators can be permanently placed at the Aman Tower basement along with other standby generators.  He also presented the drawing of the proposed location.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       After a detailed discussion the Committee agreed to the proposal of Mr. Shameem Ahmed of temporarily placing the four generators in the corner behind PMA premises and the open plot (Income Tax Building) in the South. Finally when the multi-storied building is ready, the generators would be placed in the basement of that building.   

 

           

ACTION

 

2.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would ask the Consultant to finalize the design and tender documents for temporarily relocating the four generators and making provision for the four generators in the basement of the multi-storied building.
