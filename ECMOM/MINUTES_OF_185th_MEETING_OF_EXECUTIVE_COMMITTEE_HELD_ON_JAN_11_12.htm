Item No.1      EC decisions implementation report submitted by Projects and HEC Projects Departments.

 

1.1      Cabling work at Admin. Block, City Campus and electrical connectivity in seven rooms to be completed by October 5, 2011:  It was reported that the work has been completed.

 

1.2      The Director Projects to try to reduce the completion period of Admin. Block, Main Campus: The Committee was informed that because of the handover of building in portions and demolition works to be done at night, the reduction in duration of completion time is not possible.

 

1.3      Establishing Performance Indictors for the Consultants / Contractors for renewal of their contract: The Committee was informed that the performance indicators for the Architects and Consultants and have been completed while the work on the development of the indicators for Project Managers and the contractors is underweay.  Comprehensive report shall be presented to the EC on January 23, 2012.

 

1.4      The Director Projects to get preliminary exercise done for G+3 apartments at IBA Staff Town to accommodate faculty members living in category A, B houses and future hires: It was reported that the criteria are being developed which will be presented in the next EC meeting.

 

1.5      Re-appropriation of Rs.3 million from the budget 2011-12 for the pergola on the elevated pathway of Admin Block, City Campus:  It was reported that the work had been awarded at Rs.3.73 million and re-appropriation will be done after the completion of the work.

 

1.6      Options on making provision for 12 single rooms for MS and Ph.D students either at the Visiting Faculty Residence or at the Boys’ Hostel: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the entire Visiting Faculty Residence is one single block containing 14 rooms altogether. Therefore, no required provision can be made for MS and PhD students over there. However, in the new Boys’ Hostel two separate portions containing 24 rooms (8 on each floor) located to the staircase may be designated as MS & PhD students’ Hostel (12 males and 12 females).

 

          The Dean & Director said that the HEC project approved by CDWP has two parts; Boys’ Hostel, and MS & PhD hostel.  He said that the allocations approved by HEC for the two projects should be aggregated. He asked Mr. Jahanzeb, Project Accountant to ensure that we claim the aggregate amount from HEC.

 

1.7      Sui Gas Connection for IBA: Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that preliminary enquiries have been made and contacts established in SSGC. Formal applications will be submitted by the end of January 2012 on final determination of gas requirement for chillers required at the City Campus.

 

          The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to expedite the process.

 

1.8      The work for HVAC, water proofing, and purchase and installation of any equipment for FCS block be given under a separate tender:  It was reported that the details were under preparation.  Work will be dovetailed with the overall schedule.

Item No.3      To consider and approve the revised Organ gram of Facilities Management Department.

 

3.1     The Registrar said that the Facilities Management Department under the Registrar’s office has been divided into two; (a) General Maintenance, and (b) Technical Maintenance (Operation & Maintenance). Syed Fahimuddin, Manager General Maintenance will be responsible for the general maintenance, and Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed, Manager Technical Maintenance will look after the technical maintenance but they will work together and report to the Registrar.  He presented the proposed job description of each position, the proposed areas of responsibility of both the positions, and the proposed distribution of staff. 

 

3.2     The Registrar said that since increase in headcount is not allowed, the shortages of personnel be met through outsourced contract employees as already being done in case of Generator Operator, ACs and Chillers technicians.

 

3.3     Manager Geneneral Maintenance / Security presented the details about work load of House Keeping Responsibilities viz a viz Area to be managed on daily basis versus available manpower.  The Dean and Director appreciated the data provided by the Manager Gen Maintenance / Security and said that such details are being presented to him first time and therefore desired to have a separate meeting with Registrar and Manager General Maintenance to discuss the case of additional requirement of outsourced manpower. The Dean & Director also said that there are three types of maintenance, routine, periodic, and emergency. If routine and periodic maintenance are done properly, then emergency maintenance will hardly be needed. He said that he is satisfied with the efficient and hard work of Mr. Rizwan Rizvi at City Campus but he needs similar person for the Main Campus with a problem solving approach. This person must be moving around and anticipating and resolving the problems. He said that the ideal arrangement is that one staff member is made incharge of the upkeep and maintenance of each building. He suggested outsourcing the janitorial work as much as possible but we should have strict supervision and quality control on them.

 

           DECISION

 

3.4     The Committee approved the revised organogram of Facilities Management Department as presented by the Registrar.

 

            Item No. 4  Presentation on “Brand Manual”.

 

4.1    The Director CED said that the logo and text used in visiting cards, letterheads, and envelopes at IBA have variations in color, font, dimensions, and placement. He presented the three options including those in use for each item and sought advice which should be taken as standard one. He also presented options for signage for building/offices. He said that whichever option is approved, and made a standard, the person getting the material printed should ensure that it is exactly the same.

 

           DECISION

 

4.2    The Committee considered the design of the existing visiting card (Option 1) as the best one.  However, the Committee did not agree to the suggestion of writing qualifications on the visiting card which was proposed by an EC member.

 

4.3     The Committee approved Option 2 for the IBA Letterhead, and Option 2 for the Envelope. For door signs, the Committee decided that the title will be with the same crimson color as that of IBA logo.  The placement of building signage will be as per the requirement of the donors.

 

 

             Item No. 5 Specimen presentation on Open House 2012 for approval.

 

5.1    The Registrar presented the specimen of the presentation on Open House 2012 for comments / observations / approval. The presentation consisted the Heritage, Vision, SAQS Accreditation, Key Strengths, Eligibility Criteria, Admission Test Composition, Exemption from Admission Test, Schedule of Admissions Fall 2012, Programs offered at FBA and FCS, Scholarship Fund, Student Life & Diversions, Academic Logistics, Student Services, and Fee Structure.

 

          DECISION:

 

5.2     The Committee decided to make the following changes in the presentation:

 

1.    Minimum International Baccalaureate score requirement would be 25 as approved in the 56th meeting of the Academic Board held on October 17, 2011.

2.    In the Eligibility Criteria, for BBA, the words “three principal subjects” be included after ‘A’ levels as has been mentioned for BS programs.   

3.  There would be under-graduate and graduate programs.  The prefix “Post” to be deleted from graduate programs.

4.    Ph.D (Economics) should also be included in Doctoral Programs offered at IBA.

5.   The Registrar, and the Coordinator Testing Services would review the Admission Test Composition, and the criteria for Exemption from Admission Test as according to Dr. Nasir Touheed the GMAT and not the GRE is required for MBA test. Similarly SAT-1 is required for BBA and SAT II is required for BS programs. 

6.    Along with the Fee Structure the word “existing” should be added and on the footnote a sentence should be added reading “Fee Structure is subject to change”.

7.    The word “mentor” from Student Services should be replaced with “Faculty Advisor”.

 

 

Item No. 6  To consider and approve revision of PhD Students’ stipend.

 

6.1     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the PhD students in the FCS are currently paid a stipend of Rs.33,000/- per month. He said that this stipend has not been revised for the last two and half years (since 2009). He requested that the stipend be revised to Rs.45,000/- starting from Spring 2012, to account for inflation for this period.  He further suggested that in future the inflationary impact should be included in the stipend.

 

6.2     The Dean & Director said that our Ph.D students should be encouraged to go to other universities/institutions for jobs after completion of their PhD in the interest of their professional growth because they have not been tried out in some other environment. Moreover, it will also be a testimony to the rigor of our PhD program if our students are in high demand.

 

 DECISION

 

6.3     The Committee approved the revision of PhD students’ stipend from Rs.33,000/- to Rs.45,000/- per month to be effective from Spring 2012.
