Item No. 2      To consider and approve the standardization of Tissue Papers and                   Mosquito spray.

 

2.1       The Manager Purchase stated that he had proposed, which has also been recommended by the Registrar, that in order to manage inventory cost & supply and contain the value of money, quality & delivery and to curtail undue commissions and taxes of distributors, stockiest & suppliers, instead of procuring through tenders or RFQ, they have approached the manufacturers of renowned brand of tissue products & fly insect killer (FIK) i.e., M/s Packages Ltd., producer of Rose Petal brand of tissue papers; M/s SC Johnsons through their Authorized Distributor i.e., M/s NPD Karachi, the producer of Baygon FIK and M/s Reckitt & Benckiser, the producer of Mortein FIK respectively.

Item No. 3   Clarifications on List of approved and pre-qualified manufacturers and suppliers.

 

3.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan referred to the EC decision of 303rd meeting held on June 30, 2014 wherein it was decided that:

 

“the Director Projects would send a note to the Manager Purchase stating that the cables used in IBA Projects including External Development project were purchased from Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables & Universal.  These four companies (Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables & Universal) were pre-qualified and selected by the Consultants. For repair and maintenance, cables from the same companies should be used.  Similarly the Director Projects would send the specifications / identification of paint manufacturers given by the Consultants.”

 

Accordingly, the Director Projects sent the list of approved manufacturers of various items including paints, as already prequalified by the Consultant, to the Manager Purchase.  Those were ICI, Berger, Nippon Paint, Deluxe, Robbialac.  (The first three were the manufacturers and the last two were the brand names).  

 

3.2      Mr. M. Sohail Khan said that Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager (General Maintenance) had given the requirement of Berger paint along with the color code for painting the wall and general work. He said that in the past they had painted walls of old Boys’ Hostels with BUXLY paints, which is the subsidiary of BERGER and is lower in cost. However the Manager Maintenance was not satisfied due to the difference in shade/hue. Due to this reason this time he approached to the BERGER, under the rule of Direct Contracting, since the boundary walls and other old infrastructure have been painted with BERGER product and specifically required by the Manager Maintenance. He said that Berger Paints have asked for 100% advance and have made it clear that they would neither provide any bank guarantee nor would they pay the stamp duty.  As the amount was over Rs.100,000/- as per TAP the case was presented to the Procurement Committee.

 

3.3      Mr. Moeid Sultan said that the observations of some members of the Procurement Committee was that we should standardize one on technical ground; not more than one where there is a much variance in quality.  For paint the PC was of the view that it was not a single source item.  Had there been a single source, IBA could have gone for one manufacturer.

 

3.4      The Registrar said that he was asked on October 15, 2014 to get the boundary wall painted because the proposed gates would not be ready by the time of convocation. He volunteered to do that.  He said that he faces great difficulty in getting things done on time due to such obstacles.

 

3.5       Mr. M. Sohail Khan referred to the Direct Contracting rule of SPPRA which reads as under:

 

"Direct Contracting – This method means procurement from a single source without competition and shall only be applicable under any of the following conditions:

 

(i) Standardization of equipment or spare parts, to be compatible with the existing equipment.

 

(ii) Where a change of supplier would oblige the procuring agency to acquire material having different technical specifications or characteristics and would result in incompatibility or disproportionate technical difficulties in operation and maintenance.

  

3.6       Mr. Moeid Sultan asked if EC wants to go for direct contracting or to obtain quotations from all the pre-qualified manufacturers. However, he said that in case of direct contracting it has to be justified that company x is the single source. Mr. Imran Batada suggested to obtain quotations from prequalified companies.  Dr. Nishat also suggested to obtain quotations for the technical product i.e. weather matte.  Dr. Nishat further said that where compatibility is an issue, direct contracting should be applicable.   

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

3.7      The Committee asked the Manager Purchase to obtain quotations from the pre-qualified paint manufacturers for the technical product i.e. weather matte / weather shield etc. for the general work. 

 

3.8     Due to urgency of the work, the Committee approved 100% advance payment to the lowest acceptable bidder, and waiver of stamp duty and bank / insurance guarantee by the manufacturer.
