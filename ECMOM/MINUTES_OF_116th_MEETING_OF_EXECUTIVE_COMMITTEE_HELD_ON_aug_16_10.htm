MINUTES OF THE 116TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, AUGUST 16, 2010

 

The 116th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 16, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

2.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

3.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

4.  Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

5.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

6.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider monthly progress report upto July 2010.

 

1.1       The Director Projects presented monthly progress upto July 2010 of 42 projects.  He reported that out of 42, 15 projects have been completed, 11 are in construction stage, 14 are in pipeline and two are in initial stages. These included renovation as well as new projects at both the campuses. He reported that the renovation of the existing Administration Block (Phase I) of the City Campus would be completed by September 30, 2010.

 

1.2       The Director ICT pointed out that all the glasses in the both rooms are rusted. The Registrar said that the exhaust fans are too small to serve the purpose.

 

1.3       The Director Projects assured that he would handle those problems.

  

1.4       The Director Projects reported that the final planning for FCS Building has been done. However, he said that there are two different cost estimates; one is of Rs.46 million and another is of Rs.52 million whereas the Dean & Director wants this project to be completed within Rs.40 million.

  

1.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that ideally he would want the project of Rs.52 million as it covers the whole ground floor whereas the estimate of Rs.46 million does not cover the whole ground floor.

 

1.6       It was decided that this issue would be presented again in the EC in the presence of the Dean & Director.

 

1.7       The Director Projects reported that they have pre-qualified Consultant for Master Planning of External Development and they would be sending RFP to them which would include utility master plan as well as landscaping.

 

1.8       The Director Projects stated that he would make a presentation for the Sports Ground before the EC in the presence of Mr. Asad Ilyas who is currently on leave.

 

1.9       The Director Projects reported that the new Prayer Hall is a part of Master Plan Development.

 

1.10     The Registrar suggested that if the Prayer Hall is not being constructed free of cost then it should not be constructed. The Director Projects confirmed that its construction would not be free of cost; only the Architect and Consultants will not charge their fees.

 

1.11     The Director Projects reported that 90% physical work of the 1st floor of Multipurpose Building has been completed.

 

1.12     The Director ICT pointed out that there is no fire escape route on the first floor of the multipurpose building.  The Director Projects assured that it would be provided.

 

1.13     The Director Projects reported that 50% physical work for water supply line from Admin Block to Academic Block and Auditorium, Main Campus has been completed. The planned completion date is September 2010.

 

1.14     The Registrar apprehended that with the current pace of work, the job may not be completed by September 2010.  He suggested to accelerate the work.

 

DECISION

 

1.15     The Monthly Progress Report for July 2010 along with the observations made above was noted and approved.

 

           

Item No. 2      To consider memorandum from Secretariat of Governor Sindh regarding contribution to Floor Relief Funds.

 

2.1       The Director Finance stated that they have received a circular from the Secretariat of Governor Sindh addressed to all public sector universities/institutes in Sindh requiring employees of public sector universities/institutes to contribute the following amount to the Flood Relief Fund:

 

(i)                Employees in BPS-17 – 22                 Five days’ pay

(ii)               Employees in BPS-1 – 16                   One day’s pay

 

2.2       The Director Finance further stated that in the said circular it was requested that the amount so calculated may be intimated to the Secretariat for procurement of relief goods for the Flood affectees of Sindh. The procurement and distribution of relief goods would be made in transparent manner through the Provisional Disaster Management Agencies. The Universities/Institutes will be required to make payment directly to the suppliers after the instructions are issued by the Secretariat.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Committee requested the Director Finance to obtain details as to what NED and Karachi University are doing in response to this Circular. 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
