MINUTES OF 168TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY,

AUGUST 29, 2011

 

The 168th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 29, 2011 at 9:00 a.m. at Societies’ Meeting Room, Academic Block, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                  Dean & Director
2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                           Associate Dean, BA
3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                 Associate Dean, FCS
4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN              Registrar
5.      Mr. Ali Taha                                        Director Finance
6.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                 Director ICT
7.      Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                                 Director CED
8.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                             Director H.R.

 

The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve variation order of Wateen Optical Fiber Project.

 

1.1       The Director ICT reported that this item was presented in the EC meeting of July 5, 2011 but was deferred as the Registrar had referred to the roll of Optical Fiber cable lying behind the Academic Block of Main Campus. The Director ICT clarified that the referred cable roll has actually been supplied by the vendor as part of the contract and it is already included in the calculation of total requirement. Therefore the variation in length of Optical Fiber Cable is 1069 meter in addition to the earlier estimates.  He also referred to the EC minutes of July 5, 2011 wherein the Director Projects requested Director ICT to give his requirements. He reported that all the requirements have already been sent across to the consultant for external development. He pointed out that that only conduits are part of the Master Planning. He said that according to the new plan all electric work will be behind the Academic Block as such all other equipments, CCTV etc. will have to be shifted to that place.

1.2       The Registrar said that they can reduce the cost if instead of taking all into a loop, only one loop at Students’ Centre is taken.

1.3       The Director ICT said that since they are relocating the Data Centre from Admin Building to the Students Centre, so taking all into a loop is needed. He further said that it would guarantee that all systems are run in parallel.

DECISION

1.4       The Committee approved the variation order for 1069 meter Optical Fiber Cable.  The Committee also advised the Director ICT to carry out this project in coordination with the Projects Department.

Item No.2       To consider and approve the installation of UPS.

2.1       The item was to be presented by Mr. Muhammad Jawad Khan, Electrical Engineer. But, as he could not attend the meeting, this item was deferred.

2.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to bring this item in the next EC meeting with complete information on the number of UPS available, and where they are needed.

2.3       The Registrar suggested not to have Data Centre at Boys’ Hostel because most of the students use their lap top.  He suggested to keep two UPS in reserve for back up.

2.4       The Dean & Director wanted to know the life of UPS.

2.5       The Director ICT said that if the UPS is not energized, then it will last up to seven years.

2.6       The Dean & Director said that it should be kept as it is and, if needed, can be used for Data Centre back up.

DECISION

2.7       The Committee deferred this item. The Director ICT was requested to present the proposal again along with the details of the UPS available, and the places they are needed to be installed.

Item No. 3      Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

3.1       The Director ICT said that last week there has been much progress on the data centre project; LoA has been issued to M/s. H.K. Shah who will send design of City Campus and get approval from IBA by August 30, 2011. He said that he would obtain bid for two campuses so that he may get maximum discount on infrastructure equipments. He said that temporary data centre at the Main Campus is not needed because it is working as a backup from the City Campus; at Main Campus it is for the disaster recovery. He said that the systems which are operational at one campus, their backup will be available on the other campus.

DECISION

3.2       The Committee noted the up-date on the Data Centre Project.

Item No. 4      To consider  request from Mr. Shah Mohammad Azhar, a student of MS(Economics), for retake examination.

 

4.1       Mr. Shah Mohammad Azhar, a student of MS(Economics) reported in his application that due to serious indisposition he was admitted into a hospital and thus could not sit in the paper of Micro Economics Theory held on June 14, 2011.  He requested for a retake in this subject.

DECISION

4.2       The Committee requested the Registrar to get the statement verified. He was authorized to allow the student for retake if the statement is found correct.

ACTION

4.3       The Registrar would get the statement of Mr. Shah Mohammad Azhar verified and on the basis of that he would process the application of Mr. Shah M. Azhar.

Item No. 5      To consider proposal for signing Oracle SAIP License for SAKAI.

5.1       The Director ICT reported that they have been using Oracle Software though signing Generic License; after the implementation of ERP, they are using Oracle Data Bases through license agreement which was signed by HEC. Now he said that they need to use Oracle SAIP (Student Administration Integration Pack) for using SAKAI. So once again they have to sign a generic international license.  He informed the Committee that Oracle has sent them their Generic International License which is signed by everyone across the world for SAIP. He said that this is the same practice as signing the license while purchasing the software over Internet. There is no possibility of making any changes in the license. He reported that they have just purchased Photo Shop through the same process.

5.2       The Director ICT said that this time the license of SAIP for Sakai was sent to lawyers, who made some changes; however, Oracle has informed that the license cannot be changed as it is the International License. If we want to use SAIP we must sign it. He said that he had gone through the license sent by Oracle and has not found any thing in it that may cause any harm to IBA, thus in his judgment, IBA should go ahead and sign the license agreement to start using SAIP Oracle Software for SAKAI. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the signing of the License.

5.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to who sent the Licence to the Lawyer.  The Director ICT said that he did not send it to the Lawyer.

5.4       The Dean & Director clarified that legal matter is with the Administration not with finance or any other department.

DECISION

5.5       The Committee approved the signing of the Oracle SAIP License for SAKAI.

ACTION

5.6       As soon as the software is obtained the Director ICT would arrange training for faculty and all concerned on Oracle SAIP.

 

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)                  Cut down in operational expenses.

6.1       The Dean & Director said that because of cut down in the intake of MBAs in the morning program, they have to cut down the operational expenses as the tuition fee is one of the major sources of IBA’s revenues.  He asked the Director HR to make justification in hiring and see which positions can be frozen.  He also asked the Director Finance to issue a circular for all department heads advising them to cut down their operational expenses.

b)                 Request for a break out room at both the campuses for FME Project.

6.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui requested for a break out room at both the campuses for the FME project of BBA(Entrepreneurship) class.

DECISION

6.3       The Registrar was requested to allocate a break out room at both the campuses for the FME Project.

             

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
