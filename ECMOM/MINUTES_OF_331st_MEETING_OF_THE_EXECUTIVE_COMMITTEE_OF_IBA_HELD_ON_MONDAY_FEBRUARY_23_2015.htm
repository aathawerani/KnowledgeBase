The following items were discussed:

Item No.1 Status Reports from:

Registrar:

1.1 Shed for the motorcycle stand: The Registrar reported that two stands are planned; one each behind Fauji Foundation Building and one behind Power Center Building. As the work forms a part of the external development work the shed design has been forwarded by Projects Department to the Architect.

1.2 Dismantling of overhead bridge between APWA and IBA and to be used for watch tower at Main Campus: The Registrar said that he had earlier presented the proposal to the EC under the impression that the bridge was gifted to IBA by the then Mayor, Karachi Metropolitan but in fact it is the property of Cantonment Board and the Cantonment Board would decide whether the bridge is to be dismantled or relocated.

1.3 The Registrar to invite Pizza Hut and show them the kitchen at Aman Tower and get their requirements incorporated in the kitchen: The Registrar said that the requirements were earlier incorporated; now it is the part of physical component. He said that they were awaiting cafeteria floor to take shape and observe operation of the facility at Main Campus. He said that the Pizza Hut at Main Campus is on three months’ trial period and they are operating like a kiosk. He said that if they find it feasible then they would be provided space at Aman Tower.

1.4 The Registrar to provide a team of house keeping to Mr. Noshad for getting the Library cleaned: The Registrar said that the work at Library is still on and Mr. Noshad has not yet requested for the team. He expected the team would be required by mid of March, 2015. He said that there is a standard system that once the project is fully completed, it is handed over neat and clean but in our projects the Maintenance Staff is requested to clear the debris and clean the building. The Registrar felt that buildings, maintenance and security should only be taken over when they are properly handed over and are ready for being occupied. Presently, in his opinion the contractor was saving money in cleaning and site clearance and getting it done from maintenance, just because we wanted to him to expedite the handing over.

Mr. Imran Batada agreed that no project has to date been formally delivered. He said that if there is any issue, it is difficult to know as to who would solve that issue. He said that recently there was an issue with ICT nod which took 10 days to resolve, because projects had not yet completed tagging of the wiring such as in case of Fauji Building.

1.5 The Registrar to check if the Water Tanker can be available early morning before the traffic comes to the Karachi University: The Registrar reported that the tanker operator who had agreed to provide the service is reluctant as it was not practicable due to non availability of hydrant or pumping facility / arrangement at the bulk water supply for the gravity filling of tanker, which is the correct method of filling the tankers. Tanker owner has expressed inability for suction by tanker of the water from tank as it would take sufficient time also he has no pumps available. Direct flow from the main line is not possible as there are no valves and the pressure is too high. Therefore, substantial civil and plumbing works required for installation of hydrant including valves and supports which would require an estimated amount of Rs.399,000/-. He said that if the pump is used, it would take three hours to fill a tanker and seven round trips in a day are not possible in the agreed rental time.

Dr. Sayeed Ghani felt that if Rs.300,000 to Rs.400,000 is required for the civil and plumbing for the installation of hydrant; we should go ahead with it as it would give saving in long term. The Registrar said that he would find out the details of the cost involved and how much savings could be made and report to the EC.

Head of H.R.

Dr. Nishat asked if it is possible that the TAs enter their assignment and the concerned faculty member verifies it. Dr. Sayeed Ghani also suggested that concerned faculty should verify the assignment completed by the TA, but the faculty member should not enter the details. Item No.2 To consider and approve the award of work for supply and installation of 500 KVA Generator at Aman Tower, IBA City Campus.

2.1 Mr. Abdul Rahman reported that an advertisement for the Supply and Installation of 500 KVA generator at Amn Tower, IBA City Campus was floated in daily newspapers onNovember 23, 2014. The following 13 bidders collected the bid within the specified date and time:

     1. M/s Afzal Motors
     2. M/s MIA Energy
     3. M/s Sound Engineering
     4. M/s Greesham Eastern
     5. M/s Orient Energy
     6. M/s SM Jaffer & Co.
     7. M/s World Wide Equipment
     8. M/s Multiline Engineers
     9. M/s Generator X power System
     10. M/s Jaffer Brothers
     11. M/s Synergy Corporation
     12. M/s Greaves Pakistan
     13. M/s Power Zone Engineering

2.2 Mr. Abdul Rahman informed the Committee that only the following four bidders submitted their bids within the specified date and time. The Technical bids were opened in front of IBA officials and Contractor’s representatives on January 24, 2015:

     1. M/s Greesham Eastern
     2. M/s Orient Energy
     3. M/s SM Jaffer
     4. M/s World Wide Equipment

The Technical bids were then sent to M/s EA Consulting for their evaluation and scrutiny of technical proposals who submitted their report on Friday, February 13, 2015. As per their evaluation bid of M/s. Greesham was not responsive and their technical bid was rejected. The following three bidders stood technically qualified:

     1. M/s Orient Energy
     2. M/s SM Jaffer & Co.
     3. M/s World Wide Equipment

2.3 Mr. Abdul Rahman said that after approval of Procurement Committee on February 20, 2015 the financial bids were opened on the same day in the presence of contractor’s representatives and IBA officials. He said that a brief and initial scrutiny and evaluation was done by him. He presented comparative sheet for the information of EC members. He said that as per the initial scrutiny cost wise the lowest bidder is M/s World Wide Equipment whose bid is lower by Rs.2,303,223/- from the second lowest bidder. But their delivery time for the Genset is 20 weeks (5 Months approx.). In terms of cost, M/s SM Jaffer are the second lowest bidder but they can deliver the equipment from their ready stock. M/s. Orient Energy are the highest among three but their delivery of equipment is also from ready stock. The fuel consumption rate of the gen set offered by S.M. Jaffer is also lower than the gensets offered by other two bidders. The spare parts cost of M/s. S.M. Jaffer is also very low as compared to the other two suppliers.

2.4 Mr. Abdul Rahman said that the proposal was presented in Procurement Committee meeting held on February 21, 2015. The Procurement Committee recommended the award of work to M/s World Wide Equipment at a cost of Rs.13,077,027/- being the lowest bidder.

DECISION& ACTION

2.5 After due deliberation and considering the overall time line status of the project, the Executive Committee observed that the 2nd Lowest bidder M/s. S.M. Jaffer was the ‘Most Responsive Bidder’ and therefore recommended the 2nd lowest bidder M/s. S.M. Jaffer at a bid price of Rs.15,380,250/- to the A&FC for approval on the following grounds:

a) Quicker Delivery Time:
M/s. S.M. Jaffer have the ready stock whereas the lowest bidder M/s. World Wide Equipment would take 20 weeks time. (The Aman Tower is planned to be inaugurated in April 2015 for which the building has to be energized before that).

b) Better Assembler / Origin country:
S.M. Jaffer have offered F.G. Willson (UK), whereas World Wide have offered Countryman (Singapore).

c) Lower Fuel Consumption Rate:
On 100% load fuel consumption offered by SM Jaffer is 98.5 Liters/Hr
On 100% load fuel consumption offered by M/s World Wide Equipment is 103 Liters/Hr

d) More Hours on Warranty:
S.M. Jaffer                          1 year or 2000 hours
World Wide Equipment              1 year or 1200 hours

e) Cheaper Cost of Spare Parts:
S.M. Jaffer                          Rs.541,991
World Wide Equipment           Rs.612,027

2.6 Mr. Abdul Rahman would present the recommendation of EC to the A&FC for consideration.

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
