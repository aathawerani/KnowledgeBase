MINUTES OF THE 70TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 19, 2009  

The 70th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 19, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

1.   Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director

2.   Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

3.   Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean

4.   Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

5.   Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

6.   Mr. Ali Taha                                        Director Finance

7.   Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                Director Projects

8.   Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

 

Dr. Nasir Afghan, Program Director (MBA) and Mr. Imran Abdul Rahman Batada, Sr. Manager I.S attended by special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     To consider and approve Code of Professional Conduct of Projects Department.

 

1.1       The Director Projects presenting the Code of Professional Conduct of Projects Department stated that the purpose of this code is to set basic guidelines and standards to govern the conduct and behaviour of all Engineers and technicians in fulfilling their responsibilities at work and in dealing with their colleagues and external bodies such as Architects, Consultants, Project Managers, Contractors and Civic Authorities. Professionalism, honesty, ethics, transparency, financial integrity, patience, engineering skills, and legal compliance have to be the main ingredients of the professional conduct of each and every member of the Engineering Department. Vision and purpose of this professional conduct is therefore the “commitment” of a member of project department to do what is right and honourable. He stated that in developing the Code for IBA Engineers and technical staff, codes have been referred to as developed by various professional institutions.  He presented the list of such institutions.

 

1.2       The Director Projects said that all engineers and technical staff will be governed by IBA Code Book and the Procurement Policy and Procedures (PP&P) already in practice. He stated that following are the core values of the code:

 

-   Responsibility

-   Respect

            -   Fairness

-   Honesty

 

 

1.3              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that as he had suggested for I.T. department, the Code of Professional Conduct proposed by the Projects Department should be named as Policies and Procedures.

 

1.4              The Director agreeing with Mr. Khalili said that the code of conduct is an institution wide product and is the responsibility of the HR Department. He advised the Director Projects to make the proposed code a manual of Projects Department and include “Economy” also in the core values.

 

1.5              Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia replied that he had prepared the draft Code of Professional Conduct in consultation with the Director H.R.  He agreed that he would transform this in form of a manual of Projects Department and also include “Economy” in the core values.

 

1.6       The Director said that he is more interested in the practice of these policies and procedures rather than just framing them. He said that we have to prove ourselves as role model for our staff.

 

1.7       The Director Projects said that engineers need to be provided training in other fields as they have exposures in their professional field only.

 

1.8              Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested the Director Projects to identify the type of training needed for the engineers in his Department.

 

1.9              The Director asked the Director H.R. to help the Director Projects in identifying the type of training and arranging training for the engineers and other staff in Projects Department.

 

DECISION

 

1.10          The Code of Professional Conduct presented by the Director Projects would be named as Policies and Procedures and will be used as a manual for Projects Department.

 

1.11          The Director H.R and the Director Projects would identify the type of training needed for the engineers and other staff in Projects Department and accordingly arrange training for them.

 

ACTION

 

1.12          The Director Projects would rename the Code of Professional Conduct as Policies and Procedures and include “Economy” as one of the core values and use it as a manual for his Department.

 

1.13          The Director Projects and the Director H.R. would identify the training need and accordingly arrange training for the engineering and other staff in Projects Department.

 

 

 

Item No. 2      To discuss and approve restructuring of Program Offices.

2.1       Dr. Nasir Afghan, Director Programs (MBA) in his email addressed to the Director H.R. and the Controller of Examinations on the subject of Restructuring of Program Offices made the following suggestions:

 

·         2010 MBA program will be based on One IBA philosophy.

·         All MBA students will be managed by one MBA Program Office (front end process and back end processes)

·         MBA students will not be differentiated between campuses and not be called as MBA Direct or MBA through BBA.

·         Back end functions such as course registration, GPA calculations, will be treated as shared processes till MBA Program Office has all the resources and trained staff.

·         Examination process and relevant records will always be with the Controller of Examinations.

 

2.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili and Dr. Nasir Afghan had agreed: in principle  on the following course of action for the future:

 

·         Mr. Noman Shah will be placed at Main Campus as MBA Program Incharge to facilitate and assist Dr. Nasir Afghan.

·         As a replacement of Mr. Noman Shah at the City Campus, Mr. Akmal will be given dual charge of City Campus Program Office in addition to CCS program Office.

·         Mr. Manoj and Mr. Akmal will continue dealing with BBA and MBA students and will be responsible for all back end processes of these students.

·         Mr. Noman Shah will work in close coordination with the Program Officers of both campuses.

·         Program Offices will continue to report to the Controller of Examinations for students’ academic record, transcripts, degrees etc. and will assist him in scheduling and conduct of examinations as approved in the Organogram.

 

2.3              Director H.R. however was of the view that “The organization structure charts are a visual representation of the way the department will be organized. People have as yet not been plugged in.  That will be done as per agreement (what has been summarized above) once there is clarity and agreement on structure.  The process of reorganization of Program Offices has been divided into 2 phases, spanning a period of approximately 1 year. This is to make sure that undue disturbance is minimized as well as to prepare the ground for a smooth transition which was your primary concern.  My understanding is that this initiative is necessary to align the structure of the Program Offices with our strategy of One IBA.”

 

2.4              The Director H.R. presented the agreed structure for the Program Office at present and 1 year from now for approval by the Director. However, the Director wanted to discuss the proposed structure in the Executive Committee in which Dr. Nasir Afghan was also invited to participate.

 

 

2.5              Commenting of the structure the Director said that he does not want expansion in head count to achieve a budget neutral outcome.  He said that one option is either to surrender lower positions and substitute it with higher position (good quality people).  He also said that they may think of outsourcing some back end positions.

 

2.6              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the structure presented by Dr. Nasir Afghan and the Director H.R. had some additions to the headcount. He suggested that the existing structure should continue and there is no need of any addition.

 

2.7              Dr. Nasir Afghan disagreeing with Mr. Khalili said that he wants one MBA Office with a sub office at the City Campus  which will be managed by MBA office at Main Campus. He said that the Main Campus  Program Office will be  the  hub and  the  centre point from where instructions and information will flow.

 

2.8              The Director said that after ERP the required number of persons ought to be reduced instead of being increased.

 

2.9              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that ERP will not reduce their work as they are already working on CMS.  He said that the back office is doing extremely confidential work.

 

2.10          The Director suggested to centralize the work of transcript, grades, degrees, and all examination related work and the trusted people should be posted for such work in the Examination Department. Program office as the Front office will entertain all the requests and applications for Examination Department related work and pass on to that Department for further processing. Once the deadline is met the output from the Examination Department will be delivered to the respective Program offices for onward  transmission to the students who had originated the request.

 

2.11          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the persons are of Examination Department working in the Program Offices to facilitate students of each Campus.

 

2.12          Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested that the Program Offices’ existing structure should be stripped and all examination related work should be controlled by Controller of Examinations.

 

2.13          The Director wanted to as to how many dedicated people are needed to work on that so that they may be transferred to the Examination Department and in the Program Office their duties will be to work as front office.

 

2.14          Dr. Nasir Afghan suggested that we should increase IT usage instead of manual work.

 

2.15          Mr. Mobin Khalili said that although the academic data will be generated by ERP he is keeping manual system also as a back up.  He said that the manual system was working very effectively and he would continue to have manual system in parallel with the ERP for as long as he is 100 % sure that ERP is working well.

 

DECISION

 

2.16          The Committee decided that the back office staff will be transferred to Examination Department and the Program Office will have 2 to 3 qualified and courteous staff with helpful and problem solving attitude. Students’ assistance may also be availed by the Program Offices on temporary basis  during the peak periods of work .

 

2.17          The Academic Records will be centralized in the Examination Department.  The request for services will be originated from the respective Program Offices.

 

2.18          The Director H.R. would provide details of the persons in Program Offices who can be transferred to Examination Department.

 

2.19          The Director Projects will arrange to make two separate Program Offices; one for MBA and another for BBA for which he would coordinate with the Program Coordinators and the Controller of Examinations.

 

ACTION

 

2.20          The Director H.R., the Controller of Examinations and the Director Programs will implement the decisions noted above.

 

Item No. 3      Proposal for formation of ERP Committees.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Batada, Senior Manager IS requested the Executive Committee to approve the establishment of a Steering Committee in ERP implementation and the establishment of ERP Implementation Team for Campus Solution.  He proposed that the Steering Committee should consist of the following:

 

i)                    Director IBA

ii)                  Associate Dean(s)

iii)                Director ICT

iv)                Director Finance

v)                  Controller of Examinations

vi)                Senior Manager IS

vii)              Mr. Salman Akhtar (from Techlogix)

 

3.2       Mr. Imran Batada also recommended the following members on the ERP Implementation Team for Campus Solution:

 

i)          Director ICT

ii)                  Controller of Examinations

iii)                Program Coordinators

iv)        Two Members from Finance Department for the Implementation of Students Financials

v)                  Testing Coordinator

vi)                Senior Manager IS

vii)       Project Manager (Techlogix)

 

3.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as to what would be the functions of Steering Committee and of the ERP Implementation Team.  He said that the Program Offices will be implementing the Campus Solution but they have not been included in the ERP Implementation Team.

 

3.4              Mr. Imran Batada said that the Steering Committee will have three major roles; Decision Making, Business Process Engineering, and overseeing, monitoring and supervising the progress, and the Implementation Team will closely work on software and the recommendation of the Implementation Team will be approved by the Steering Committee. He clarified that this is only for the CMS so no HR people have been included into it.  He agreed that Program Offices would be included in the Implementation Team.

 

3.5              Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that we should have many more members on the Steering Committee as we start other modules besides CMS.

 

DECISION

 

3.6              The Committee approved the establishment of the proposed Steering Committee and the ERP Implementation Team for Campus Solutions. The Committee also decided that Program Coordinators, Registrar and Dr. Nasir Touheed will also be members of the Steering Committee while Program officers in the Implementation Committee.

 

ACTION

 

3.7              Mr. Imran Batada would suggest terms of reference of the Steering Committee and of the Implementation Team.

 

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Request from Ms. Samreen Tariq, student of MBA-I for allowing make up examination.

 

4.1       Ms. Samreen Tariq, a student of MBA-I wrote to the Director MBA Program that because of her father’s critical condition at the hospital, she had to be at the hospital with her father and thus could not appear for mid-term examinations on October 9 and 10, 2009.  She requested for make up examinations. 

 

4.2       Dr. Nasir Afghan, Director MBA Program as a special case allowed make up examinations and requested the Controller of Examinations to arrange for her make up examinations for the missed midterm exams.

 

4.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili referred the case to the Executive Committee as according to Program Announcement 2009-10 make-up examination is not allowed for missing term or semester final exams for any reason.  He clarified that make up examination is allowed on medical ground if the student himself / herself is hospitalized.

 

4.4       Dr. Nasir Afghan said that IBA allows one exam exemption to Evening Program. He suggested that there should be one policy for day as well as evening program students.  He further said that in every university there is a make up examination policy.

 

4.5       The Director said that the make up of evening cohort is totally different from the morning batch and therefore they enjoy more flexibility.  So the evening policy cannot be applied to morning program students.  He said that the make up examination policy can be re-visited.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee decided to honour the commitment made by Dr. Nasir Afghan and allowed make up examination for the missed mid-term examination papers to Ms. Samreen Tariq as an exception case.

 

4.7       Dr. Nasir Afghan was advised to present a proposal for make up examinations for consideration by the Executive Committee and the Academic Board.

 

ACTION

 

4.8       The Controller of Examinations would arrange make up examinations for Ms. Samreen Tariq for the missed mid-term examination papers.

 

4.9       Dr. Nasir Afghan would present a proposal for make up examinations for consideration by the Executive Committee and the Academic Board.

 

 

b)         Scheduling of classes.

 

4.10     With regard to the availability of option in ERP for scheduling classes, Dr. Nasir Afghan said that scheduling can better be done in Excel as doing this through ERP may be costly.  He suggested that we should do the cost – benefit analysis for the two options.

 

DECISION

 

4.11     Dr. Sayeed Ghani was requested to assign this project to Dr. Sajjad Haider to find a solution for easy scheduling of classes.

 

 

c)                  Digitalization of examination related records.

 

4.12     The Director requested Mr. Imran Batada to start digitalizing all the examination related records with the assistance of the Controller of Examinations. He said that there must be a system in which responsibilities for data entry, data verification and data validation are clearly spelled out. He said that while data verification would be done by the immediate supervisor of the data entry person data validation should be done by some senior persons. He said that Mr. Mobin Khalili should validate the data.

 

4.13     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that the data is being entered on the CMS but the problem is that one data entry operator is doing data entry as well as program office work.

 

4.14     The Director asked Mr. Imran Batada to depute additional manpower for data entry and give time line for completion.

 

d)                 Approval of Final draft of Staff Welfare Rules and Pre-employment Medical Exam Rules.

 

4.15          The Director H.R. stated that she had sent to the Executive Committee the final draft of Staff Welfare Rules and Pre-employment Medical Exam Rules for consideration and approval.

 

DECISION

 

4.16          The draft was not discussed in the meeting. However, the Committee decided that individual members of the Executive Committee would send their approval of the draft rules to the Director H.R.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
