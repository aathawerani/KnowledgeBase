MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 16, 2013

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                                Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                                         Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                               Associate Dean, FCS

5.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                                     Director Projects

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Conceptual approval for the Electronic Surveillance Cameras at Students’ Centre.

 

1.1       Mr. Khalid Javed Rishi, Manager Security, stated that the newly constructed Students’ Centre is the most popular location for students, faculty and staff alike. He suggested that this asset should be protected against any sort of misuse.  He said that in universities CCTVs are used for policing campus activities and serve as a deterrent by keeping the students in check and under control through electronic surveillance which targets:

 

·        Entry / exit check points

·        Aids in detention & investigation of theft / acts of misconduct

·        Promotes timely action / response against acts of misdemeanour

·        Serves as deterrent against acts of pilferage / theft

·        Assists in record keeping

·        Reduces need to physically deploy persons and helps in distant monitoring.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to how long the record can be kept and what is the system of storage and its retrieval.  Mr. Khalid Rishi said that in the existing system they have the recording capacity of two weeks and they can go for another two weeks so in total one month’s recording capacity is available. The Registrar said that if any incident takes place, it is preserved separately. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that to enhance the capacity to 120 days, a storage capacity of three terra bites per month would be required. The Dean & Director wanted to know if it can be integrated with our server.  Mr. Imran Batada said that it can be integrated to our server but that can be done when they move to the cloud.  The Dean & Director said that our servers will be redundant when we migrate to the cloud. Mr. Imran Batada said that by March 2014, there would be nothing on the server. He said that storage server would be provided from the City Campus.

 

1.3       Mr. Khalid Rishi presented the following cost estimate for installation of equipments:

 

Ø  12 x fixed IP cameras (2 MP) @ Rs.20,000/- cam             =          Rs.240,000/-

Ø  NVR (16 channel) or PC Core I3                                    =          Rs.  60,000/-

Ø  3 TB Hard disk                                                           =          Rs.  16,000/-

Ø  1 x 32” Samsung LCD                                                  =          Rs.  30,000/-

Ø  Cabling, Switches & Installation                                     =          Rs.  60,000/-

                                                                                             ----------------

            Total Expenditure                                               =          Rs.406,000/-

 

1.4       The Dean & Director said that there is no need to spend money on cable and switches since it is already provided in the original design. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that cables for seven points have been provided. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Rishi to start with seven cameras.  The Registrar said that if IT provides Computer, there would be an additional saving of Rs.60,000/- as there would be no need to get NVR or PC Core I3. Mr. Imran Batada said that he would provide PC’s.  Mr. Imran Batada further said that he can provide 3 TB Hard disk when they move to cloud.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director said that seven cameras were allocated in the original design.  He wanted to know as to why five additional cameras have been proposed. The Registrar said that at least 45% of the total areas should be visible without scrolling. The Dean & Director suggested to start with 7 cameras, and change the location to get maximum coverage if needed.  He said that there would be Incharge of Students’ Centre and his staff who would also be patrolling the building and facilities.  Similarly, in Computer Lab there is no need of CCTV as there would be a lab attendant present. The Registrar said that at Students Centre it would be unattended computer lab.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani recommended that in un-attended lab, there should be CCTV.

 

1.6       Mr. Khalid Rishi presented a comparison between Analog Camera and IP Camera according to which there is much advantage in going to IP Camera.  The Dean & Director wanted to know about the budgetary allocation to which Mr. Moeid replied that it would be covered from the budget of Students’ Centre.

 

1.7       The Registrar said that if conceptual approval is given by the EC, it would be taken to the CPC and on CPC’s approval it would be published in the newspapers. The process will take 3 months time.

 

1.8       The Director Projects suggested that cameras for administration building and Library should also be included in order to save time in the procurement process. The Dean & Director agreed and asked the Registrar to take the proposal of 12 cameras for Students Centre plus the cameras required for library and admin building to the CPC and accordingly tender and evaluation should be completed.  He said that as we stored servers, IP Cameras can also be stored till such time the Library and Admin Building is completed. The bulk purchase order may fetch us better unit price.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Executive Committee granted conceptual approval for the purchase and installation of 12 CCTV cameras at Students Centre.

 

1.10     The Registrar was authorized to proceed with the acquisition of IP cameras for three premises i.e. Students’ Centre, Library, and Admin Building through a single tender document.

 

ACTION

 

1.11     The Registrar would determine the need of IP cameras for Library and Admin Building and include them in the tender document.

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve the Terms of Reference for Strategy 2020 Steering Group.

 

2.1       The Director H.R. said that in the last EC meeting she was asked to prepare ToR for Strategy 2020 Steering Group.  Accordingly, she said that she has prepared the same. She said that the following is suggested to be the objective and scope of work of the Steering Group:

OBJECTIVES FOR THE STEERING GROUP

· To review and analyze the current scenario in terms of achievements, gaps, internal & external environment and major issues and opportunities that require high priority attention by Management.

· To facilitate debate and discussion by all stakeholders on the status of IBA’s achievements over the last 5 years, and possible future direction.

· To formulate options, and a unified business strategy and plan for sustaining and enhancing academic and support operations of IBA in the next 5 years.

SCOPE OF WORK

 

1.      Perform a Situation Analysis of the Institute’s Internal Environment (i.e. current mission & vision, strategic objectives/pillars, strategies and results, and major strengths & weaknesses), and External Environment (Social, Technological, Economic and Political), to identify 2-5 major critical issues.

2.      Undertake Stakeholder Analysis with focus on Faculty, Students, Alumni, to collect feedback on IBA’s performance, perception and accomplishment of stated plans.

3.       Based on the feedback from points 1 & 2, identify the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities and Threats ( SWOT), that will form the basis of what IBA chooses to do in the future.

4.      Produce a clear set of recommendations with supporting justifications that revise as necessary, Mission and Objectives, and supply the Strategies to accomplish them.

5.      Identify appropriate implementation approaches (inclusive of possible revenue streams, matching income and expenditure) to determine suitability of recommended strategy.

6.      Formulate an Action Plan to make the new strategy operational and effective in achieving the organization's objectives.

7.      This exercise will not include IBA’s Profit Centers. However, the Profit Centers of IBA will provide the Steering Group with their individual Strategy Plans so that revenue streams may be determined. 

MEMBERSHIP & TIMELINE

 

2.2       The Director H.R. said that the Steering Group, selected by the EC, of not more than 9 members of the faculty and management staff, chaired by the Dean & Director IBA, be appointed. The deliverable required from the Steering Group will be in the form of a report to be presented to the Board of Governors by June 2014. The report will include:

 

·        A section highlighting the findings of the SWOT analysis

·        A section capturing the major issues highlighted by the key stakeholders

·        A section that spells out the recommended strategy, resource requirements, and action plan specifying how this will be done

2.3       The Director H.R. said that the members of the Steering Group should possess the following experience & qualifications:

 

1.      Experience in strategy formulation

2.      Experience in evaluation and re-engineering of processes, organizational audit and restructuring customer driven entities

3.      Demonstrated knowledge and experience in using participatory approaches

4.      Good interpersonal skills and ability to negotiate shared positions

5.      Postgraduate qualifications

 

2.4       The Dean & Director did not agree with the Director H.R. that he should lead the Steering Group.  He said that as the recommendation would come to the EC, therefore, EC members including Dean & Director should be at arms’ length. He said that when the steering group completes the objective and scope of work, then the EC will look at it. He said that when he prepared the first strategy he knew that he would be implementing that strategy but the strategy to be prepared would be implemented by someone else so the organization and the faculty should own it.  He said that he would persuade Mr. Zaffar A. Khan to lead the team because he had taken a lot of interest in strategy review of IBA conducted earlier and his experience in preparing the corporate strategy.

 

2.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that the Steering Group should be asked to up-date the EC every fortnightly to see which direction they are heading to. He proposed that in faculty meeting members they should be asked either to nominate someone or volunteer themselves if they are interested.  The Registrar suggested that the two Associate Deans should have a luncheon meeting with their respective faculty and request them to help him out by volunteering themselves or nominating their colleagues.

 

2.6       The Dean & Director said that he has a faculty meeting on 21st September in which he would ask them if they would like to participate in strategy formulation exercise they may volunteer themselves or in case they are not interested they may suggest the names of their colleagues for the Steering Group.  Prior to that meeting he said that he would also send an email to all full time faculty.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Executive Committee decided to nominate eight members from among Full time faculty to form the Steering Group. The Dean & Director through an email to all full time faculty would send the TOR and ask them if they are interested to be part of the Steering Group of eight. In case they are unable to volunteer themselves, they may suggest names of the colleagues for the Steering Group. Mr. Zaffar A. Khan would lead the Steering Group.

 

Item No. 3      Up-date on the IBA Strategy Review 2013 – recommended Action Plan.

 

3.1       The Director H.R. said that as a result of the IBA Strategy Implementation Review held on July 18-19, 2012 at the Arabian Sea Country Club, Karachi, the following five critical elements were identified:

 

o   Lack of marketing and branding

o   Systems and procedures need to be simplified and refined

o   Internal management lacks coordination and there are silos in departments

o   Weakening of commitment to teaching and students – (Dilution of ownership of IBA values)

o   Re-evaluating options of resetting the goals as per the security situation obtaining in Karachi.

 

3.2       The Director H.R. said that the above five critical elements were turned into the following five questions with recommended actions and assigned responsibility. She said that she has prepared six monthly progress report (January to June 2013) on the recommendations.

 

1)      How do we develop linkages with the industry for research, student development and social impact?

2)      Given limited resources, how do we market the IBA to potential students and faculty?

3)      How do we hire and train our faculty / staff so that they are best in the world?

4)      How do we develop a research culture at IBA?

5)      How do we develop a culture of ownership built on shared values?

 

3.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to where we are lagging behind so that remedial actions can be taken. The Director H.R. said that we are still lagging behind in research and regular seminars are not being held.  The Dean & Director said that the best research award process has been completed.  He said that the research award criteria is publication in top ISI Indexed journal. The Director H.R. said that until the criteria is disseminated, nobody would know about it. Dr. Nishat said that there is Impact Factor in ISI Indexed journal based on which reward can be given.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that Dr. Hisham bin Zubair is working on developing research evaluation criteria and he has divided it into four quartiles which can be the basis of future evaluation.

 

3.4       The Dean & Director wanted to know as to who is looking after the social media campaign after Dr. Zaheeruddin Asif.  Mr. Imran Batada said that there is much progress on social media campaign and now the postings have been decentralized. He said that the hits on our facebook have significantly increased. The Dean & Director said that somebody should monitor the postings.   

 

3.5       The Dean & Director opined that we should intensify our efforts to attract students from intermediate stream. He therefore, requested the Registrar to give presentations on IBA’s programs to intermediate colleges such as St. Joseph, St. Patricks, Adamjee, Commecs etc.

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee noted with satisfaction the six monthly progress report on the Strategy Implementation Review.

 

Item No.4       Status Report by Associate Deans – Imposition of fine on late submission of results.

 

4.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that on June 11, 2013 an e-mail was sent by the Dean & Director seeking feedback from all faculty members on the imposition of the fine. In response to the e-mail, 13 faculty members gave responses.  Of these 9 were in favour and 4 opposed to imposition of a fine. Of those in favour, majority preferred a flexible deadline catering to issues such as number of copies, number of exams and duration since last exam, versus some preferred a universal fixed deadline.

 

4.2       Based on the above, Dr. Sayeed Ghani proposed that if a fine is to be imposed the following may be decided:

 

a)      Whether a universal fixed deadline be given or it should be different for each exam and faculty member, based on factors such as how many exams and copies are to be graded and to allow sufficient duration after completion of each exam.

 

b)      Fine amount and recurrence to be decided.

 

4.3       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that once the above is decided, the details of the calculation may be left for a subsequent meeting, and will be worked out in collaboration with MIS dept. to ensure that the deadline can be calculated in an automated manner, based on the above factors.

 

4.4       The Dean & Director said that universal fixed deadline will be more appropriate because in that management and monitoring would be easy.  He suggested that 15 days time after last exam be given for submission and posting of results in ERP.  Rs. 5000/- is to be fined for delay in posting of results with the amount of fine increasing by Rs. 5000/- after every 3 days.

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee decided that a universal fixed deadline i.e. 15 days after last exam would be given to the faculty for submission and posting of results in ERP.  A fine of Rs.5000/- would be imposed on not posting the results within 15 days and an additional fine of Rs.5000/- after every three days would be imposed.

 

ACTION

 

4.6       Mr. Imran Batada would develop a system of keeping track of deadline, calculation of fine and intimation to finance department for necessary action.

 

4.7       The Secretary, Executive Committee would disseminate this decision of the Executive Committee through an email to all the faculty members and the Exam. Department. 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
