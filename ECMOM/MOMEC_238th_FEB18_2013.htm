Item No. 5 To consider and approve the appointment of a consultant / consultant-cum-project manager for Faculty Apartment at IBA Staff Town.

5.1 Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on 8th October 2012 had approved the design layout for two types of faculty apartment that were prepared by HEC-Projects department. The EC had also given a go ahead for hiring of a consulting engineer cum project manager by inviting technical and financial proposals from an existing list of pre-qualified architectural and engineering consultants. The list was provided by project department.

5.2 Mr. Shameem said that the project scope is such that IBA needs to hire the firm which can prepare the final design based on the proposed preliminary design. This includes architectural design, civil engineering, structural engineering, MEP design and tender drawings and documents. With a view to improving the proposed preliminary design, the prequalified consultants were asked to submit their design based on the guidelines given in the RFP.

5.3 Mr. Shameem said that the technical proposals were opened in the presence of the representatives of participating firms. Total nine proposals were received. The technical proposals were opened and thoroughly reviewed. The evaluation criteria have been separated into two categories. The firms offering architectural services only, namely NBCL and FNMH, were evaluated separately. Whereas the firms with architectural and project management facilities all under one roof have been evaluated together. He said that depending on the decision of EC, the consultants will be asked to make a final design presentation.

5.4 Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that all nine applicants have obtained 80% or more marks and are therefore recommended for pre-qualification at this preliminary stage. He said that the proposal was considered by TEC-PROJ in its meeting held on February 8, 2013 and it recommended the preliminary evaluation of the pre-qualification of consultant / consultants-cum-project managers for the Faculty Apartments at IBA Staff Town to the Central Purchase Committee. The CPC considered the matter in its meeting and decided that in view of SPPRA Rule 66(7) the Technical Proposal received from the two architects, namely, NBCL and FNMH, cannot be entertained. The aforesaid rule stipulates that evaluation of technical proposals should conform to the criteria given in the RFP that allows only architectural and engineering consultants-cum-project managers to submit their technical and financial proposals; it excludes those who do not have all facilities under one roof. The CPC therefore approved the evaluation of technical proposals of only the 7 applicants.

5.5 Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve the CPC’s recommendation so that they may ask the seven applicants to present their preliminary designs before the EC and then they may open the financial proposals of those who get higher marks after combining out of 20 marks to be given by the EC to out of 80 marks. The Committee noted that 20 marks for the design are too less and advised that logical division of evaluation criteria and assigned marks should have been done.

5.6 Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve the CPC’s recommendation so that they may ask the seven applicants to present their preliminary designs before the EC and then they may open the financial proposals of those who get higher marks after combining out of 20 marks to be given by the EC to out of 80 marks.

5.7 To a question as to who has evaluated the technical proposal, Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied that it has been evaluated by Ms. Sana Tanveer, Assistant Architect. The Dean & Director said that instead of single person’s evaluation, it should have been evaluated by a committee as is done by the Projects Department.

DECISION

5.8 The EC formed a committee of three comprising Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Mr. Javed Islam of HEC Projects, and Ms. Sana Tanveer to evaluate the technical proposals including the designs submitted by the competitors and recommend to the EC as to who is number one, two or three in the ranking.

a) To consider and approve the Award of Furniture Works for the Students’ Center at IBA Main Campus.

6.1 The Director Projects reported that invitations for collection of Tender documents for furniture works for the Students’ Centre at IBA Main Campus were sent to the following pre-qualified contractors:

1. M/s Habib Sons
2. M/s Versant
3. M/s Simab
4. M/s Interwood
5. M/s Turnkey

Out of the above five pre-qualified contractors, only three of them collected the tender documents; M/s Interwood and M/s Turnkey regretted.

6.2 The Director Projects reported that procurement process of Single stage two envelop was adopted for this work. The technical bid was opened in front of Architect, IBA and Contractors’ representatives. The Technical bids were then sent to the Architect for their evaluation. The Architect sent their report based on the evaluation criteria given in Tender documents. As per their report all three bidders M/s Habib Sons, M/s Versant and M/s Simab are technically qualified. He said that the Technical Evaluation report was presented in TEC. The TEC approved the opening of bids via circulation. In this meeting The TEC recommended to open the bids of all qualified bidders.

6.3 The Financial bids were opened on the given date as mentioned in bid opening statement in the presence of Project Architect M/s NBCL, IBA and contractors’ representatives. The following were the quoted prices announced at the time of bid opening.

1. M/s Versant Rs.8,116,700/-
2. M/s Habib Sons Rs.7,446,574/-
3. M/s SIMAB Rs.6,638,639/-

6.4 The Director Projects reported that M/s SIMAB did not submit the Earnest money bond therefore their bid was rejected as per SPPRA rules. The Financial bids were then sent to Project Architect for their Evaluation. The Project Architect submitted their Evaluation report and recommended to award the furniture works to the Lowest Evaluated Bidder M/s Habib Sons at a cost of Rs.7,446,574/- as M/s SIMAB bid was rejected at the time of bid opening.

6.5 The Case was presented in TEC Meeting held on February 8, 2013. TEC recommended the award of work to the lowest Evaluated Bidder M/s Habib Sons at a cost of Rs.7,446,574/- Based on the recommendations of Project Architect M/s NBCL, TEC-Project, and the Central Purchase Committee, the Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder at a cost of Rs.7,446,574/-

6.6 The Registrar said that the quality of the furniture of Habib Sons is good. He further said that they have provided furniture for CED on time.

DECISION

6.7 The Committee approved the award of furniture work to the lowest evaluated responsive bidder M/s Habib Sons at a cost of Rs.7,446,574/-
