MINUTES OF 218TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 10, 2012

The 218th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, September 10, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                         Associate Dean, FBA
2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                               Associate Dean, FCS
3.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                Registrar
4.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                              Director ICT
5.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                          Director H.R.
6.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                               Acting Director Finance


Syed Muhammad Qamar Abbas Rizvi, Security Coordinator, Main Campus, attended the meeting on special invitation.


The following items were discussed:   
 

Item No.1       Approval for augmentation of Security Arrangements.

1.1       The Registrar reported that recently there have been incidents of theft of a PC from the Girls Hostel and a PC from the Lab for which the Director ICT has been requested to conduct an inquiry. He said that the Dean & Director had asked him to let him know the number of PCs provided to the Girls Hostel. He said that according to the record there were six PCs installed at the Girls’ Hostel and it was also confirmed by the Lady Warden but later she reported that there were only five PCs and she could not tell since when one PC was missing.  He said that after the data centre becomes operational, the moment any PC or IT equipment is removed, it would immediately be alarmed by the system but presently there is no such arrangement. He said that there has been increase in the number of buildings and other facilities but the security has not been increased in that proportion.   

1.2       Dr. Muhammad Nishat showed his concern over the attitude of Security Guards performing duty at IBA Staff Town. He mentioned of continuous use of cell phone either by IBA or hired Security. However, he appreciated the security arrangement and checking drills / procedures at Main Campus. 

1.3       The Registrar informed the Committee that Dr. Shoaib Zaidi, Dean Electronics Department, NED University appreciated and expressed satisfaction over the security arrangements and use of CCTV Surveillance System at IBA.  He said that Dr. Shoaib Zaidi has been assigned on behalf of the Sindh Governor’s House, to suggest and coordinate security arrangements including availability of electronics’ surveillance system at NED, Karachi University, and IBA under one command and control mechanism.

1.4       Syed Muhammad Qamar Abbas Rizvi, Security Coordinator presented a summary of posted / hired security guards at IBA Main Campus; and their shift & post wise deployment. He also briefed the members about the problems he was facing in Girls’ Hostel and made the following suggestions to overcome those problems:

a)      Hostel Warden be shifted immediately to Girls Hostel ‘A’, adjacent to Staff Town Entry Gate for better command & Control. He said that since majority of the girls are staying in Girls Hostel “A”, where PCs are also placed, the Lady Warden should also be staying there. 

b)      Intercom facility between hostel warden office / room and Security\check post (at Staff Town entry gate) to be arranged.

c)      Up-dated list of all inventory held on charge of Girls’ Hostel be prepared and maintained and audited on regular basis.

d)     Person to be nominated as Inventory Incharge, will work under close consultation with Hostel Warden & Hostel Superintendent.

e)      All precious / costly items like computes, TV, Wi-Fi devices etc. to be checked by the responsible person on daily basis.

f)       Movement of all inventory from girls’ hostel to be made on proper gate pass duly signed by the Hostel Warden or any other dedicated authority.

g)      Any gift or new / used items given to hostel staff as charity by the students must be recorded and moved on gate pass.

h)      Lost / breakage to be noted and reported immediately to the Hostel Superintendent, Manager Administration with information to the Registrar by the Hostel Warden.

i)        Responsibility for lost / breakages to be ascertained and damages to be recovered from defaulters.

j)        Physical presence of Hostel Warden is necessary in the hostel. However, in absence, person to be nominated to take charge.

k)      Facility-wise (Girls Hostels –A, & B, VPR) caretakers to be nominated.

l)        Auxiliary staff like cooks, cleaning staff, ayahs / maid, service unit staff etc. to be strictly monitored.

m)    Students Hostel Committee to be selected, CR to be nominated and they be educated to be more responsible, caring and watchful about their personal belongings as well as the property of the Institute.

n)      Visitor rooms facility for students’ guests to be made available at Girls’ Hostel ‘A’.

o)      IBA Girls’ Hostel rules and regulations to be spelled out to its occupants in clear words for compliance.

p)      Karachi University or other reputable institutions which are maintaining Girls’ Hostel facilities may be approached for consultation and formulation of better SOPs in same regards.

q)      Only at key points security cameras may be installed considering and respecting privacy of the occupants.

r)       Duties of Security Staff towards Girls’ Hostel and its occupants to be specified in clear terms.    

1.5       Mr. Qamar Rizvi then presented details along with financial impact for hiring four additional Security Guards and two lady searchers. Two private security guards; one for day and another for night shift were requested for each AMAN-CED Building, and IBA Boys Hostel (New). Two Lady Searchers, one for day and one for night shift were requested for Girls’ Hostel. The Financial Impact @Rs.11,920 per month per security guard/lady searcher worked out to Rs.71,520/- = Rs.858,240/- per year.

1.6       About the shifting of the Lady Warden from VPR to Girls’ Hostel ‘A’, the Registrar said that there are in total 45 girl students who are staying at three different locations at Staff Town; 26 are living in Girls’ Hostel ‘A’; 12 in ‘B’ where the lady warden is also staying, and 7 at ‘C”.  He requested the Director H.R to discuss with the Lady Warden to know the reason for which she is not moving from VPR to Girls’ Hostel ‘A’.

DECISION:

 1.7       The Committee considered the suggestions made by the Security Coordinator and decided that:

a)      The Director H.R. would discuss with the Lady Warden to know the issues involved in shifting of the Lady Warden from VPR to Girls’ Hostel ‘A’.

b)      IP Phone would be arranged by the Director ICT to connect the hostel warden office / room with Security\check post (at Staff Town entry gate) and other offices. The Security Coordinator would email the requirement for IP phone to the Director ICT.

c)      The Lady Warden would maintain list of all the inventories including TV, fridge, oven, AC’s etc. and report to the Administration immediately if there is anything abnormal. She would check on daily basis the PC’s, Wi Fi devices and IT equipments and report the status to the IT Department on weekly basis. The IT Department would check the PC’s and IT related equipments on monthly basis. 

d)     One senior girl would be made Proctor for each Girls’ Hostel who would be reporting to the Lady Warden.

e)      The Security staff deputed at IBA Staff Town may be assigned their duties and responsibilities towards Girls’ Hostel by the Girls’ Hostel Superintendent and copy of Rules & Regulations pertaining to IBA Girls’ Hostel be supplied by the Girls’ Hostel Superintendent to the Security Office.

1.8       The Committee allowed to hire the services of four additional security guards (two for AMAN – CED Building, and two for new Boys’ Hostel) through outsourcing to meet the requirements which have cropped up because of the new buildings.

ACTION

1.9       The Director H.R. would discuss with the Lady Warden to know the issues involved in shifting of her (the Lady Warden) from VPR to Girls’ Hostel ‘A’ and get the issue, if any, resolved.

1.10     The Security Coordinator would make a request to the Director ICT for arranging IP Phone facility at Lady Warden’s office.

1.11     The Director H.R. would get the decisions noted at (c) and (d) above included in the Job Descriptions of the Lady Warden and also convey her for compliance.

1.12     The Director ICT would get the physical verification of PC’s and other IT related equipments at Girls’ Hostel on monthly basis through deputed IT staff.           

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
