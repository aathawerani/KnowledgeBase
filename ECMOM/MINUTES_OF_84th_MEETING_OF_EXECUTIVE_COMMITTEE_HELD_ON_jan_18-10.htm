MINUTES OF THE 84TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, JANUARY 18, 2010

 

The 84th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, January 18, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

  8. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

  9. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                         Director CED

  10. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Request for waiver of EMBA fee.

 

1.1              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi informed the Committee that his son Rizwan Arshad Siddiqi has been admitted to the Executive MBA program.  He stated that his son has done his BIT from Michigan University and according to Mr. Shahid Zaki he had scored the highest marks in the admission test of IBA. He said that as per the policy of IBA, one son of IBA employee who has completed one year of employment, is entitled to free education at IBA. He said that he has completed over one year of service at IBA and requested the facility of fee waiver for his son.

 

1.2              The Director said that fee waiver and scholarships should be on need basis.  Furthermore, he said that there is no scholarship for EMBA program. He said that the EMBA program is for those who think they can invest in themselves or they are sponsored by their organizations. He said that the lower staff may be provided this facility but not the members of the Executive Committee. He requested the Director Finance to revise the fee waiver policy and present it to the A&FC for consideration.

 

DECISION

 

1.3              The Committee agreed with the Director and did not approve the request for fee waiver.

 

ACTION

 

1.4              The Director Finance would present a revised fee waiver policy for IBA employees for consideration by the A&FC.

 

Item No. 2      Presentations by Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui, Director CED on:

 

a)                  Business Plan Competition

b)                  Methodology for Implementation of Communication Strategy

2.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented the Aims & Objectives, Scope, Modalities, Financials, and Summary of the proposed IBA-Business Plan Competition. The aims and objectives included the following:

 

·         Building IBA-CED Brand

·         Establishing IBA-BPC as a role model for other institutes in Pakistan

·         Integrating learning of all areas of Business: Hands on Training on Business Plan, Executive Summary, Elevator Pitch, and Presentation

·         Enhancing Collaboration with industry and other universities

·         Establishing BPC as viable business projects

·         Establishing a self-sustaining process

 

2.2              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the scope of IBA-BPC would be to arrange trainers, mentors/coaches, graders, domain experts and judges; general sponsorships, media and seed/feasibility funding; collaboration with other universities and industry.  He said that the IBA and 3-4 technical universities from a selected list would be the participating institutes.

 

2.3              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the modalities would consist of four rounds after the initiation of Project, formation of committees and preparation of the project plan.  Round 1 will be Selection of up to 100 teams; Round 2 will involve submission of Executive Summary and Elevator Pitch, selection of up to 20 top teams. At Round 3 up to 10 finalist teams would be selected who would be provided mentoring / coaching for Business Plan and draft Presentation. Round 4 will be the judging, selection of top 3 teams, and awarding the winner. He also presented the tentative schedule of the modalities and the estimated financial inflow and outflow. 

 

2.4              The Registrar suggested that the proposed IBA Business Plan Competition should be given some other brand name.  To this Mr. Zafar Siddiqui agreed and said that he would look into it.

 

2.5              The Director suggested to enlist Institute of Computer and Emerging Sciences in the suggested participating technical universities / institutions.

 

DECISION

 

2.6              The Committee granted conceptual approval to the proposal and requested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to email the presentation on IBA-BPC to the Executive Committee so that if the members have any comments or ideas they may send them to Mr. Zafar Siddiqui. 

 

Item No. 3      Methodology for Implementation of Communication Strategy.

 

3.1              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui made a presentation on the methodology for implementation of communication strategy.  He briefed on the Objectives, the ideal situation for IBA, the gap, and the action needed. He described the current communication route and the proposed communication route and the strategy. He said that BBCL will provide the following:

·         Integrated approach

·         Refreshing concepts

·         Impact making brand positioning

·         Focused and persuasive ATL /BTL

·         Cost effective solutions for consistency and higher reach

 

3.2              Giving details of Brand Immersion Program, Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that this phase will focus to define the new strategic positioning hence requiring a meeting with heads of the core departments at IBA in presence of Corporate Communication representatives to have a clear insight on the following

 

·         Core Values

·         Refreshed Vision

·         Mission, Challenges

·         Structure

·         USPs

 

3.3              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui proposed to include the following in the Strategy:

 

·         A ‘refreshed vision’ with the rationale ‘change is the only constant’

·         Shift from a bureaucratic to an objective-oriented approach

·         Pro-active stakeholders management

·         Centralized brand image

·         Strategic Media Planning

·         Website transformation and up-gradation

·         Event management Support

 

3.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented the brand driver components, Brand Manual – Development Timeline and the Communication Vehicles which included Media (print, electronic, online), Brand Activation at Convocation/Education Expos/ Conferences/other activities, Transformed Website, Outdoor (vehicle branding, directional signs, signage), Newsletter and other marketing collaterals, Event/Activities by alumni. He also presented Retainer Arrangement Package for creative services.

 

3.5              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui provided details of the current agency (BBCL) engagement and proposed agency engagement.  He also presented the proposal of IBA Cell at BBCL, Terms and conditions and Action Plan.

 

3.6              To a question Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that the Agency (BBCL) is wiling to give new idea but it is afraid because of rejection of the idea.  He said that he has asked the agency to give three options and one of the three options should be radical.

 

3.7              The Registrar said that there is a big confusion in the minds of the advertising agency.  He said that IBA is a service rendering organization and not a product manufacturing unit as such IBA ads have to be different from say for example; fast food restaurant. He further said that they have never discouraged any new idea but the advertising agency usually gives three identical options.

 

3.8              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that when we advertise our regular program our approach should be different from what we adopt for our Executive Program.  He wanted to know if we have done any research to know where we stand – number one or number two.

 

3.9              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that he has not done this type of research but he said that the employers have their own perceptions.

 

3.10          Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that they need to have accurate and comprehensive data regarding IBA graduates based on regular market surveys. This would enable them to improve their decision making process.

 

3.11          Commenting on the proposed communication route, Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that there is a need to streamline the communication. He suggested that the ads / information should be sent to the Agency (BBCL) through the Communication Department. 

 

3.12          The Director said that the Communication Department should add value to it and play the role of coach, guide and mentor.

 

3.13          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that they have formed an Advertising Committee comprising Mr. Jami Moiz, Ms. Erum Hafeez and the stakeholder.

 

3.14          The Director advised to include H.R. representative also in the Committee.

 

3.15          The Director H.R. suggested to make the core value alive and to ensure that everybody lives in accordance with the core value.

 

3.16          The Director said that the practice of each value may be different for each individual.  He said that there should be a strong feedback mechanism.   

 

3.17           Commenting on the Communication Vehicle, the Director said that instead of outside, we should first strengthen our internal communication.

 

3.18          Giving details of the proposed Agency Engagement Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that they get a commission of 15% but if they are appointed on retainership then the commission percentage would be reduced.

 

3.19          The Director said that for engaging the agency on retainership, we will have to give advertisement in the newspapers.

 

3.20          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui assured that proper procedure will be followed for appointment of the agency on retainership.

 

3.21          The Director suggested to present the Communication Strategy and all strategies to the Board through A&FC. He requested Mr. Zafar Siddiqui to present the strategy to the A&FC in its forthcoming meeting. 

 

DECISION

 

3.22          The Committee granted conceptual approval to the proposed Methodology for Implementation of Communication Strategy.

 

ACTION

3.23          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui, Director CED will ensure that the proper procedure is followed for the appointment of the agency on retainership.

 

3.24          Mr. Siddiqui would present the Communication Strategy to the Board through A&FC.

 

 

Item No. 4      Approval of Stall Designs for Dawn Education Expo 2010.

 

4.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented the two different options of the proposed IBA stall designs for Dawn Education Expo 2010 to be held in Karachi, Lahore and Islamabad.

 

4.2              The Director Projects suggested that instead of sofas, there should be round table.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that working tables with high stools would be more appropriate.

 

4.3              It was also suggested that the tower should be moved backward in order to get more space.

 

4.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that BBCL is asking Rs.700,000/- for making the stalls with sofas, chairs, tower, plazma T.Vs. and carpet. This would include transportation charges, installation and dismantling of the towers, rents for sofas, chairs, plazma TVs etc. and it would be their (BBCL’s) responsibility to install and dismantle the tower and fix other items at three cities. He said that IBA has paid Rs.300,000/- to Dawn separately. He said that the payments can be made from the Advertisement Budget.

 

DECISION

 

4.5              The Committee approved the Stall Design (Option 2) with modifications suggested above and the total charges of Rs.700,000/- for the stall to be placed at three cities.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
