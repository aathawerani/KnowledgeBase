Item No. 2      To consider an email from Mr. Mobin Khalili regarding construction of Pergola.

 

2.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili in his email to the Director Projects reported that the two-day heavy rain exposed the designing and construction flaws in the Academic Block of the City Campus.  He stated that:

 

a)       Due to poor water proofing water is dripping in few rooms spoiling false-ceilings and the furniture.

b)      Walking passage and passages leading to classrooms on the first floor are not only at the same level but the slope is towards the classrooms. As a result rain water is flowing and entering into the classrooms freely.

c)      In the absence of a proper sheltering cover people going to ground floor and first floor classrooms are badly exposed to rain, sun and cold winds. When the Mid-term exam started on July 27 at 6:00 p.m. heavy rain was pouring down. Faculty, students and invigilators were completely drenched when they entered the classrooms.  Attendants posted outside the classrooms had to perform duty under pouring rain.

 

2.2       Mr. Khalili suggested that the walking passages and corridors of the ground and first floors essentially needed proper shelter.

 

2.3       The Director Projects in his email replied that observations (a) & (b) were brought to the knowledge of the Architects and that the levels could not be lowered except that the pergola sections could be extended over that area on the entrances to the classrooms on first floor.  He reported that the Architect has been asked to provide a feasible solution to this.

 

2.4       In reply to observation (c) the Director Projects stated that the situation where classrooms are held during summer and in the evening was not discussed when the EC members took a decision against the construction of the Pergola.  He requested the Executive Committee to review the decision made in the last meeting considering the facts pointed out by Mr. Khalili.

 

2.5       The Director said that in the previous meeting the Executive Committee had a detailed discussion on this subject and it was observed that in summer classes are very few when the rainy season starts so the proposal of pergola was not justifiable on cost and benefit analysis. However, he agreed that the pedestrian bridge should be covered and it should be ensured that the slope should not go towards classrooms so that rainy water may not enter into classrooms.

 

2.6       The Director Projects said that he would manage it.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee requested the Director Projects to get the walking passage covered and proper water proofing of the roofs and report to the EC.

Item No. 5      To consider the cases of employees who have attained the age of superannuation.

           

5.1       The Director H.R. stated that as a follow up to the approved policy by the BoG, H.R. Department has prepared a list of non-teaching employees who have attained the age of superannuation.  She stated that the list contains details regarding employees date of joining, date of birth, their previous status with IBA i.e. before retirement and the validity of their current contracts.  She said that she presented the status before the Executive Committee on the basis of the BoG approved policy on Conditions for Service under which non-teaching employees retire from IBA services upon attaining the age of superannuation i.e. 60 years.  She sought advice of the Executive Committee on continuation or conclusion of the employees presented in the list.

 

5.2       The Director said that on the basis of suitability of the employee (physical fitness and usefulness to the organization) employees who have attained the age of superannuation should be terminated by giving a decent notice. He further said that exceptional cases, where retention of a particular person is necessary in the interest of the organization, may be taken to the Board for approval. He emphasized on the succession planning and advised the department heads that the individuals who could be the right persons as replacement must be trained for the positions to be given in future.

 

DECISION

 

5.3       The Committee considered the list of the non-teaching employees who have reached or crossed the age of superannuation and advised the Director H.R. on case to case basis taking into account the physical fitness and usefulness of the individual to the organization.  

 

ACTION

 

5.4       The Director H.R. would request the Department Heads to start succession planning and arrange on-the-job training of the would-be successors.
