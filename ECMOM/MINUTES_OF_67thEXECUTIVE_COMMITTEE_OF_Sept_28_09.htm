Item No. 4      To consider and approve recommendations relating to Service Bond and Declaration of Assets for Ph.D Scholarship.

 

4.1       The Director H.R. informed the Committee that:

 

a)      Mr. Rashid Aziz Farooqui has signed 3 years service bond for doing Ph.D at IBA, whereas M/s. Faisal Iradat & Waseem Arain have signed 5 years service bond for doing Ph.D at IBA. Three faculty members namely Mr. Imran Khan, Ms. Quratulain Rajput, and Syed Irfan Nabi who are doing Ph.D at IBA have not signed any Service Bond.

 

b)      Existing Surety Bond contains the clause “Relative/ Kinship” which is related to the eligibility of a surety that creates difficulty for scholars to find a non-relative/non-kinship surety who can afford to pay the penalty.

 

c)      Surety has been taken without any amount of declaration of assets

 

4.2              The Director H.R. stated that the recommendations of the Associate Deans are to reduce the service bond period from 5 to 3 years for all local scholars. Existing local scholars bond will be redone for three years.

 

4.3              The Director H.R. suggested that the clause of Relative / Kinship may be eliminated so that Scholars can easily arrange their sureties. She also suggested that the surety with amount of declaration of assets should be provided by the scholar in-line with the investment by IBA in order to ensure his / her ability to pay the penalty in case of breach of agreement

 

4.4      The Director H.R. also requested the Executive Committee to decide the amount of declaration of assets (value of property) by the guarantor in case of overseas PhD Scholarship. She informed the Committee that faculty members who are being hired at IBA for overseas PhD scholarship program need to provide surety with declaration of assets of Rs.10 million. However an average amount of funding per scholar for 4 years is around Rs.14 million (25000 GBP X PKR141). She said that as per HEC terms and conditions for their scholarship program available on HEC website, the surety declaration of assets amount is Rs.6 million.

 

4.5      The Director H.R. also requested the Executive Committee to decide the amount of penalty in case of breach of agreement.

 

4.6       The Director H.R. said that she had discussed with the Associate Deans and they have suggested that the total amount funded by IBA is recoverable in case of breach of agreement however this amount shall be proportionate to the period not served at the IBA (She informed the Committee that the proportionate term is mentioned in the service bond of IBA Scholars but not mentioned in the Overseas Scholars service bond).

 

 

DECISION

 

4.7              The Committee decided that:

 

a)        The service bond period would be 5 years for scholars doing Ph.D. abroad and three years for scholars (both new and existing) doing Ph.D at IBA, LUMS and any other institution within Pakistan subsequently approved by the Executive Committee.

b)       The amount of declared assets for surety shall be in-line with the investment by IBA e.g. if the total funding is of Rs.10 million, the value of property as surety should be equal to that amount. However, if it is purely HEC funding, then all HEC conditions will have to be fulfilled.

c)        The total amount funded by IBA is recoverable without interest in case of breach of agreement however this amount shall be proportionate to the period not served at the IBA

d)       The Board will be requested to waive the conditions that only relatives can be the guarantor.

e)        The bond for existing scholars would be revised to reflect the above changes.

 

ACTION

 

4.8              The Director H.R. would place the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the Board for approval.
