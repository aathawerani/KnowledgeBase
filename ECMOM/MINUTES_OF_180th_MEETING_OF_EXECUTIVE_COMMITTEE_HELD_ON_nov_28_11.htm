Item No. 1      To consider request from Security Staff for revision of Sunday Allowance.

 

1.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed, Manager H.R. reported that they have received a request from IBA Security Guards for revision of their Sunday Allowance which is paid to them in lieu of their duty on Sundays.  He provided the following relevant details for consideration of the Executive Committee:

 

·         Total number of Security Guards in IBA is 20; two (02) of these guards will be retiring in December 2011.

·         Current rate of Sunday Allowance is Rs.1,500/- per month, which was last revised in 2009.

·         Security Guards work 7 days a week without any weekly off day.

·         Security Guards do not get holidays on religious occasions and gazetted holidays. In lieu of these holidays they get 13 earned leaves in the annual leave balance.

·         The overtime earned by Security Guards is comparatively less than the overtime earned by other entitled staff members working in equivalent BPS grades 1-5.  It is because Security Guards work in 3 shifts in a day, and overtime is only earned in case a replacement is required in the next duty shift.

 

1.2       Mr. Adnan said that considering the above facts, the HR department recommends an increase in the Sunday Allowance of Security Guards. The current annual expense on this allowance is Rs.360,000/-. He presented the following three  options with their financial impact for consideration:

1.3       The Director Projects said that according to labour law, one cannot work seven days a week.  He suggested that the roster should be made in such a way that security guards get one day’s off in a week.

 

1.4       Major (R) Qamar Abbas, Security Coordinator, said that he can prepare the roster as suggested by the Director Projects. He proposed that Sunday Allowance should be given to only those who perform on Sundays.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee noted that the Security Guards besides Casual, Medical and Earned Leaves, are also getting additional earned leaves for performing duties on gazetted holidays. So on an average they accumulate 4 days earned leave in a month which they may avail as and when needed in addition to the 10 days casual leaves and 10 days sick leaves. In such a situation if they perform seven days a week, it is neither inhuman nor against the law.

 

1.6       The Committee approved Option #1 i.e. Rs.2000/- per month per IBA Security Guard as Sunday Allowance.

 

1.7       The Committee asked the Security Coordinator to arrange fitness / medical test of the IBA Security Guards.     

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve Performance Management Training.

 

2.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the Director H.R. has suggested that since the year end is fast approaching the following trainings need to be held:

 

Performance Management Training for Faculty: A number of new faculty members have come on board. Also some members have taken on new roles. Additionally, the new unit system has been implemented and people will benefit from a review before appraisal. Moreover, it is important to provide people the opportunity to refresh their memories so that they can participate more effectively in the performance management process.

 

Performance Management Training for Management Staff: There are many new team members who need to be introduced to the system at IBA so that they know what to expect. There are also people who may want to refresh their memory and practice some of the major areas where they feel help is needed e.g how to conduct the appraisal interview, how to write objectives, how to give and receive feedback etc.

 

Additionally, during the break between the Fall and Spring Semesters, we want to have a team meeting (all full time faculty and Management staff) just like the one conducted last year. The purpose is to review progress on strategy and set future direction. Further, to provide an opportunity to align the entire team and network in a relaxed environment, get to know people and develop rapport.

 

2.2       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that conceptual agreement of the EC is required to move ahead with these projects as they will have to be tendered, go through TEC and CPC, which takes a long time.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know if the cost of the training will be on the basis of per participant or will it be a fixed amount. Mr. Adnan replied that it would be a fixed amount.  The Dean & Director asked Mr. Adnan to mention in the tender that the rate be quoted on per participant basis.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director further said that the unit system can be better explained by the Associate Deans as the outside agency may not be much familiar with this system. . He also said that the training on Performance Management should be provided to new entrants only.

 

DECISION

 

2.5       The Committee granted conceptual approval for Performance Management Training to faculty and management staff who are new and have not undergone such training before at IBA.

 

ACTION

 

2.6       The Director H.R. would obtain rates for conducting Performance Management Training on per participant basis. 

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve the cost for refurbishment of Phase – 2  of FCS Block at IBA City Campus.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the tenders for Phase-1 for refurbishment of FCS Block at IBA City Campus were opened on April 05, 2011 and after following all necessary procedures, the work at site commenced w.e.f. July 01, 2011. The tender was awarded to the lowest tenderer at the quoted cost of Rs.61 million, but has been curtailed after value engineering to Rs.55.69 million (the quotation from contractor awaited).

 

3.2       The Director Projects said that as per various meetings and initial plans, renovation of Phase-2 primarily includes Robotics Lab, Associate Dean’s Office, faculty offices, faculty lounge, meeting room etc. He reported that the initial engineering estimate for Phase-2, amounting to Rs.33,069,280/- reached IBA on April 11, 2011. After detailed meeting among FCS faculty, Projects team and Architects on April 19, 2011, the scope of work was further reduced as refurbishment of corridors had already been taken care of in Phase-1 and it was decided that only one Robotics Lab would be constructed in Phase-2 (Hardware & Networking Lab now included in Phase-1). As a result, a reduced engineering estimate amounting to Rs.26,349,720/- reached IBA.

 

3.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the A&FC, in its meeting held on June 08, 2011, instructed him to bring down the estimated cost of Phase-2 to around 15% of awarded cost of Phase-1 which would make it Rs.9.15 million; a directive which was passed on to ICON vide Director Project’s letter dated July 21, 2011.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that after a myriad of meetings and brainstorming sessions, many finishing items were amended for value engineering and the revised estimate of Rs.19,746,400/- finally reached IBA during end October, 2011. He said that considering the covered area of 8,760 sft and cost/sq.ft of Rs.2,254.16/-, the furnished amount is pretty reasonable. This cost happens to be 35.45% of Rs.55,698,982/- which is the cost of Phase-1 after Value Engineering.

 

3.5       The Director Projects said that at this stage, it would be plausible to extend the contract of existing contractor to carry out the tasks of Phase-2 as a special case because these works form a natural extension for the already mobilized contractor. Moreover, if the Committee decides on tendering, it has all the probability that higher cost will be quoted by different contractors due to changing market conditions, and also the additional mobilization costs involved in deployment of new contractor at an under-construction.

 

3.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the final engineering estimate of Rs.19.7 million for Phase-2 and to accord the task of refurbishment of Phase-2 of FCS Block at IBA City Campus to existing Phase-1 contractor i.e. M/s. Prefab Systems so that Architects can be given the necessary go-ahead for preparation of working drawings and BOQ.

 

3.7       The Dean & Director said that as per SPPRA we can go for additional work upto 15% of the total cost and give the work to the same contractor. However, he said that HVAC, water proofing and purchase and installation of any equipment can be given out under a separate tender because we cannot get these jobs done through civil contractor but by specialists. By issuing separate tender for such jobs/items, the cost of additional civil work can be restricted to 15% of the total cost. He further said that the saving through value engineering in Phase I can also be utilized on Phase-2.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Dean & Director as noted in 3.7 above.

 

ACTION

 

3.9       The Director Projects would issue separate tender for HVAC, Split ACs/equipments and ensure that Phase-2 is completed within 15% of the total cost of Phase-I and report to the A&FC.

 

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve the cost for Academic Block Pergola and for tendering process for Admin Block pergola at IBA City Campus.

           

4.1       The Director Projects reported that in order to economize the Academic Block, City Campus project, the plan for Academic Block pergola canopy and that of Administration Building was shelved by IBA management thereby saving the cost. In the aftermath of recent rains, particularly after the rough spells of September 06, 10 and 13, 2011, the necessity of providing and installing of pergola structures on both buildings  was strongly felt. The Executive Committee, in its 169th Meeting held on September 12, 2011, approved in principle both proposals for installing pergola canopies so as to off-set the effect of rains in future. He said that the task for Academic Block pergola would be undertaken by the contractor who had finished the Academic Block project at City Campus (as a supplementary part of the main academic block project) and likewise the Admin Block pergola would fall in the purview of existing contractor of Admin Block.

 

4.2       The Director Projects said that detailed meetings and negotiations were then held amongst Project Managers Integrated Engineering Services, Architects ICON, IBA projects team and contractors Abaseen for submission of quotation for Academic Block pergola, thereby having considered different material alternatives, rates etc. to arrive at an optimal solution. The contractor eventually submitted a quotation and after project manager’s recommendation, the different cost details for Academic Block pergola are mentioned as under:

 

·         Total awarded cost for Academic Block project at City Campus:     Rs.59.43 million

·         Total actual cost for Academic Block after completion:                 Rs.57.58 million

·         Saving achieved:                                                                   Rs.  1.85 million

·         Contractor’s quoted cost for academic pergola:                           Rs. 3.7375 million

 (6.3% of awarded cost)

·         Additional amount required after considering the saving:                Rs.1.8875 million

 (3.18% of awarded cost)

 

4.3       The Director Projects reported that for Admin Block Pergola, Architects ICON have furnished an engineering estimate of Rs.465,380 (0.0056% of the total awarded project cost of Rs.81.7 million) and existing contractors were accordingly instructed to carry out the job as an extra item.

 

4.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that in spite of continuous follow-up with the contractors, they expressed their inability to carry out the work as they have deactivated their site team to a considerable extent after substantially handing over the Admin Block, City Campus. In a meeting held among Projects team, Integ and ICON on November 15, 2011, it was decided that there was no other alternative but to go for tendering for initiating the task of pergola canopy work for Admin Block, City Campus. He reported that the tender documents for subject project have reached final stages of completion in this regard.

 

4.5       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of task of Academic Block Pergola canopy to contractor Abaseen at a cost of Rs.3,737,500/- as a supplementary part of Main Academic Block project. The earlier savings from the Academic Block should be utilized to defray part of this cost.

 

4.6       The Director Projects further requested the Executive Committee to allow tendering phase for commencing Admin Block task at City Campus.

 

DECISION

 

4.7       The Committee approved the award of task of Academic Block pergola canopy to M/s. Abaseen at a cost of Rs.3,737,500/-

 

4.8       The Committee allowed to float tender for pergola canopy work at Admin Block, City Campus.

a)      Space for Parking of vehicles of students, faculty and staff at City Campus -  Up-date:

 

6.1       The Registrar gave up-date on the efforts he has made on arrangements for parking of vehicles of the faculty, students, and staff of IBA City Campus.

 

6.2       The Dean & Director said that we have no obligation to provide parking facilities to our students. When we had the space we provided this facility but due to construction work the existing parking space will not be available so we have explored alternate arrangements but for that the users will have to pay the charges.  IBA cannot pay Rs.3.6 million annually for two years for providing parking facilities.  

 

6.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that there are students of two shifts so the charges which have been worked out to be Rs.5000/- per vehicle per month (Rs.300,000 /60 vehicles) can be divided into two.  Thus the user will have to pay Rs.2,500/- per month.

 

6.4       The Director Projects said that the Contractors M/s. Paragon also want some space for their labours.  If possible, space for 20 vehicles can be provided to them against payments and the remaining space for 40 vehicles can be used for parking of vehicles.

 

6.5       The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to send an email to all students informing them that there will be dislocation and distraction of vehicles because of the construction work of multi-storeyed building and Auditorium at the City Campus for the next 24 months. In order to accommodate vehicles near the City Campus, we explored various options and the best option is the utilization of the premises adjacent to IBA which can be made available on payment of rent.      The Dean & Director said that the students be informed that they have three options:

           

(a)        Students may ask their driver to leave after dropping them at the campus and then come back and pick them up.

(b)        Students may use any suitable parking place at their own discretion.

(c)        Pay for the parking of their vehicles (around Rs.3000/- per month for one year which the owner of the adjacent plot is asking for).

 

6.6       The Dean & Director further said that the students may be asked if they are interested in making use of this facility {option (c)}. They may also be told that there is a space to accommodate 60 vehicles only; so we will entertain requests on first-cum-first-serve basis.) 

 

6.7       The Registrar said that so far he was exploring all these options with the notion that the rent / charges would be paid by IBA.  He said that he would send the email to the students as advised by the Dean & Director. He added that another option is the vacant plot behind OMI Building which we can only identify to the students and they may use it on payment of the charges.

 

DECISION

 

6.8       The Registrar would send an email to IBA students giving them the three options noted in 6.5 above.
