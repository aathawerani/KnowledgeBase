Item No. 1      Presentation on IBA’s entry into Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM).

 

1.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui reported that Pakistan’s flag has been included and is also appearing along with flags of other member countries of Global Entrepreneurship Monitor.  He reported that the name of IBA, Karachi and the names of the GEM Pakistan team members are listed in every GEM Global Report. He said that IBA has been given access by GEM to all printed material which is a big achievement.  He said that IBA, Karachi may use the GEM logo on its website and all related material, printed and electronic.    

 

1.2       Giving background of GEM, Mr. Zafar Siddiqui informed the members that GEM was started as a joint venture between Babson College and London Business School with 10 counties and now it has 56 countries. He said that to-date it has completed 11 cycles of surveys and scholars and researchers from more than 70 countries have taken part in it. GEM is the only measure of entrepreneurial activity based on interviews with individuals and has world’s richest database on entrepreneurship.  GEM is regularly cited in prominent international publications. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui presented some samples of GEM influence i.e. the GEM reports are used in many departments of China’s government, in Chile, Brazil, Croatia, United States.   

 

1.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui informed the members that GEM’s empirical research consists of two major parts: (1) Adult Population Survey (APS), and (2) National Expert Survey (NES).  The APS is a survey of at least 2000 adults in each nation to gauge respondents’ involvement in and attitude towards entrepreneurship. He presented details of Data Sources for APS. He said that they at IBA would deal with APS.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The members noted the inclusion of the name of IBA and Pakistan’s flag in the list and flags of other 55 member countries.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve pre-qualification of advertising agencies for 2010-2013.

 

2.1       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui reported that as per the IBA & PPRA procurement policy, pre-qualification of Advertising Agencies was done in the 1st quarter of the year 2009. Since one and a half year had passed new applications were invited through advertisement published in Daily Dawn and The Express on Sunday, July 11, 2010.  In response to the ad, 21 applications were received within due date. The Advertising Agencies were asked to submit their company profiles & other relevant documents considering the evaluation criteria that were shared with all of them. Initially, received applications were thoroughly studied to check if the agencies had submitted all the required documents. Detailed evaluation was then carried out under the following criteria for which marks were allocated as mentioned below:

 

Relevance of Current Campaigns & Clients                                                   10

List of Clients & Campaigns in last five years                                               10

Overall experience in the field                                                                   15

Technical Expertise including Permanent Employees &

   Services offered                                                                                  24

Latest Equipment & Facilities                                                                     15

Type of Agency (Partnership/Proprietorship/Int. Alliances) & Location 10

Accreditation                                                                                          10

Extra Supportive information/ Testimonials                                                    6

                        Total                                                                                                    100

b)     To consider and approve eligibility of an employee for annual increment who gets salary raise at the time of contract renewal.

 

3.8       Mr. Adnan Hameed, Assistant Manager H.R. stated that in continuation of the memo from the HR department to the EC dated October 23, 2010, regarding eligibility of faculty on study leave and confirmed employees with less than 6 months service, for annual increment the following was submitted for consideration and approval by the EC:

 

·         Generally IBA contract employees are given salary increments at the time of annual performance appraisals; however, in special cases a salary raise may be given to a contract employee at the time of their contract renewal with the special approval of the Dean and Director IBA.

 

·         With the objective to maintain fairness and equity among employees and to discourage employees’ expectations for salary raise in the middle of the year, it is proposed that:

 

i)        In case an employee is given a salary raise at the time of contract renewal before 30th September of the assessment year; s/he would be evaluated as per normal practice. However, s/he would be entitled for a prorated increment which will be based on the remaining number of months in the assessment year.

 

    ii)     In case an employee is given a salary raise at the time of contract renewal on or after October 1st, the employee would be evaluated as per normal practice, however, s/he would not be eligible for any increment during the annual performance appraisal.

 

DECISION

 

3.9       The Committee recommended the proposal to the A&FC for consideration.

 

ACTION

 

3.10     The Director H.R. would get the above proposal incorporated into the proposal of October 23 approved in the 125th meeting of EC and present it to the A&FC.
