  1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Chairman CS & MIS

The following items were discussed: 

Item No. 1     To consider proposal from Director HEC Projects regarding selection of M/s. NESPAK as IBA’s Consultant for the Water & Sewerage Lines Project for the Main and City Campuses. 

1.1     Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects giving background for the need of separate water and sewerage lines stated that so far water is being supplied by the Karachi University. In view of water shortage for boys’ hostel and the staff town and the construction of visiting faculty residence, girls’ hostel and new boys’ hostel in future it was felt that IBA should also have a separate water line to meet the growing requirements. Accordingly Karachi Water & Sewerage Board was approached and on the advice of the KW&SB a request for an NOC was made to the Karachi University. The Karachi University granted the NOC with the condition that the route for the water and sewerage lines would be set by the Karachi University. On submission of the NOC, the KW&SB approved 3” diameter pipe line from the main siphon.  He stated that around 50,000 gallons of water would be supplied daily through this pipeline. He reported that an underground tank with 100,000 gallons capacity and an overhead tank of 50,000 gallons would be constructed.  

1.2     Mr. Shameem Ahmed further reported that they advertised for pre-qualification of consultant for this project. The RFPs to pre-qualified consultants were issued and the proposals received were evaluated by the Technical Evaluation Committee which comprised Director Finance, Director ICT, Director Projects and Director HEC Projects. (He reported that the Registrar is also on the Evaluation Committee but could not attend the meeting due to pre-occupation.). He reported that M/s. NESPAK scored the highest point.  However, they quoted their fee at 17% of the total cost of the project.

1.3     Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that following the decision taken by the Technical Evaluation
         Committee in its meeting held on August 24, 2009, preliminary negotiations with NESPAK were
         held on September 2, 2009. Thereafter NESPAK responded by submitting their revised
         consultancy fee. With a view to persuading NESPAK to bring their fee down to about 6%,
         another, final negotiation meeting was held at their office on September 8, 2009. He reported
         that after negotiation by the Technical Evaluation Committee M/s. NESPAK have agreed at 6½%
         consultancy fee which includes preparation of design, bidding documents and evaluation of bids
        (2.75%) and detailed supervision during the construction phase (3.75%) plus lump sum of
        Rs.500,000 for feasibility study for waste water treatment plants. According to Mr. Shameem
        Ahmed the fee quoted by M/s. NESPAK is quite reasonable in view of the fact that for other
        projects IBA has been paying 5.85% for designing and top supervision, plus 2.50% for project
        management. He requested the Executive Committee to approve selection of M/s. NESPAK as the
        Consultant at a fee of 6.50% of the total cost of the project, plus Rs. 500,000 for feasibility
        study for waste water treatment plants.

1.4    To a question Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that Rs.30 million has been provided for in the budget but the cost may exceed and it would be around Rs.40 to 50 million. He said that the location of the underground and overhead tanks at the staff town would be decided based on the proposals to be given by the Consultant. 

1.5     The Registrar wanted a time lime for completion of the Project. He also observed that there is no project cost of which has not escalated.  The Director HEC Projects stated that at this stage they require approval for appointment of NESPAK as consultant only and not re-appropriation of budget.  

1.6      The Director Finance wanted to know if there was any HEC contribution for this project to which Mr. Shameem Ahmed replied in the negative but added that when they get the cost estimate they may request the HEC for funding

1.7     Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know the details of technical and financial proposals submitted by the four Consultants.

1.8     Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that M/s. MM Pakistan had quoted a total, lump sum fee of Rs.42.34 million which was exorbitant and out of all proportions vis-à-vis the estimated cost of the project (Rs.40 to 50 million).  NESPAK being one of the two Consultants technically ranked as No.1 quoted a fee of 16.96% of the total cost of the project while M/s. Techno-Consult ranked technically as No.2 quoted 5.6% (Rs.2.8 million) for designing and 3% for detailed supervision, M/s. Shahzad Associates technically ranked as No.3, quoted 1.55% for designing and 1.3% for detailed supervision.

DECISION

1.9     The Committee approved the Selection of M/s. NESPAK as Consultant for Water & Sewerage Lines Projects for the Main and City Campuses at a fee of 6.5% which also includes detailed supervision during the construction phase plus Rs.500,000/- for feasibility study for waste water treatment plants.

ACTION

1.10    The Director HEC Projects would present the recommendation of the Executive Committee
 before the A&FC for approval. 

Item No. 2      To consider proposal from Mr. Imran Batada, Patron of Web Society regarding launching of web radio.  

2.1      Mr. Imran Batada in his proposal stated that IBA Web Society is one of the unique ideas that came up from elite minds of IBA students. He informed the Committee that the following are Web Society 2009-10 projects: 

i)   Web Radio

ii)   Web TV

iii)  Social Networking Portal (among Pakistani Universities)

2.2      Mr. Batada informed the Committee that currently Web Radio project is in a state of execution. He said that this project would revolutionize the young minds of IBA in terms of management, strategy planning, marketing and would also intensify their entrepreneurship skills.  He also stated that the Web Society has planned for Inter University Collaboration which would provide a platform through which young minds can execute their thinking in a timely manner and the Web Society would act as execution channel and provide them with all the guidance and support. 

2.3     Mr. Batada informed the Committee that since the inception of the Web Radio idea, it has left a huge impact around IBA.  Students are becoming actively involved in the project. This project is providing them with the on-campus opportunity to earn and learn in fast paced environment. He stated that students from a similar project at SZABIST are having a countless experience and on an average every student is getting an amount of Rs.15,000 to Rs.20,000 as a part of SZAB FM. He said that the students of IBA are passionate about the launch of the IBA Web Radio as this would be the first of its kind in the history of IBA. Furthermore the project management of IBA Web Radio is purely handled by the students so providing another opportunity intensify their professional set of skills while learning at IBA. 

2.4     Mr. Batada stated that the project funding channels would be (1) IBA’s fund for Societies, and (2) Funding from Sponsors. He further stated that initially IBA’s allocated fund for societies would act as launching pad for the IBA Web Radio project.  Later on the funds raised from the sponsors would be required to run the project activities smoothly.  Sponsors will get huge benefit by funding the project. They will be having their commercials in between radio scheduled programs, e-advertisement by means of IBA Web Radio’s website, and means of marketing the sponsor’s brand, along with that IBA students can even host shows with sponsor’s representatives as guests, disseminating their ideas / product offerings through the IBA Web Radio platform. 

2.5     Mr. Batada suggested that the revenue generated through the activities of IBA Web Radio could be distributed between IBA and the students working in the project with a greater chunk forwarded to the students. 

2.6     To a query about the cost of equipments to be needed for this project, Mr. Batada clarified that the equipments are not so costly and may be arranged through sponsorship or through the Society’s budget. He said that for Web T.V., studio and equipments would be required but they are working on it and it is not at the launching stage. He said that the Radio at SZABIST is frequency based for which licence is required. Mr. Batada was advised to initiate the process of obtaining licence. 

2.7     The Director Projects suggested that there should be some code of conduct for live transmissions. He wanted to know as to who would be overseeing the program and making sure that indecent dressing and language are not used.   

2.8     It was suggested that an undertaking from the students participating in the live transmission be obtained to the effect that they would follow the code of conduct.  

2.9     Dr. Sayeed Ghani wanted to know as to what extent the Patron of the Web Society would be involved in such activities and if he would be able to find time to monitor the activities. It was suggested that somebody else would have to be assigned this responsibility as it would not be possible for Mr. Batada to find time for such activities particularly when the ERP project would be installed. 

2.10    The Registrar suggested that the Project should be run for three months as pilot project and Mr. Imran Batada should administer the project and see how much time is needed.  

DECISION 

2.11     The Committee approved the launching of the Web Radio project.  

2.12     The Committee asked Mr. Imran Batada to present a code of conduct, proposal for staffing, sponsorship, and commercials for consideration and approval by the Executive Committee and to apply for licence.  

ACTION 

2.13    Mr. Imran Batada would present a code of conduct for Web Society and a proposal for staffing, sponsorship and commercials for consideration and approval by the Executive Committee and would apply for Web T.V. licence.

b)       To consider note of September 14, 2009 from Director ICT for Approval for the agreement with Techlogix. 

3.6     The Director ICT referring to the EC / BoG decisions for the ERP Implementation at IBA, Karachi, regarding deployment of Campus Management System in both the campuses by directly contracting with HEC stated that when they contacted HEC for contract agreement for the implementation of ERP, the HEC informed that they have signed the contract agreement with M/s. Techlogix for the implementation of PeopleSoft Campus Management Solution and all universities which have acquired ERP through HEC have signed contracts with M/s. Techlogix.

3.7     The Director ICT stated that the HEC had mentioned that the IBA should tender for the implementer. However, he suggested that since M/s. Techlogix is the only vendor who has implemented PeopleSoft in all universities through HEC and no other PeopleSoft vendor has the experience of implementing Campus Management System in Pakistan, IBA should also contract with M/s. Techlogix as the single source vendor. He requested the Executive Committee to allow the Single Source Contracting with M/s. Techlogix for the PeopleSoft Enterprise package.

3.8     The Registrar stated that a lot of problems arose in the ERP project because of the urgency to utilize the money by June 30, 2009 and due to this urgency proper procedures could not be fully followed.  He said that now we are trying to salvage them.

3.9     The Director ICT clarified that they followed the procedure but unofficially. He said that he got the price for up-to June 30, 2009 with 50% to 70% discount but they are still willing to give at the same price. He said that due to increasing dollar exchange rate, the IBA will have to pay extra cost if it is re-tendered and may lose around Rs.200 million because of price escalation. He said that as M/s. Techlogix have done the implementation of PeopleSoft Campus Management Solutions for the HEC they become a single source.  Further he said that M/s. Techlogix have done it for 8 universities in Pakistan and no other organization has got the experience of implementing PeopleSoft Campus Management System.

DECISION 

3.10    The Committee approved the recommendation of the Director ICT to treat M/s. Technogix as the single source on the justifications given by the Director ICT in 3.9 above and recommended for approval by the A&FC.

ACTION

3.11    The Director ICT would place the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the A&FC for approval.  

c)  To consider note from Director ICT on pre-qualification of vendors for Technology Partner Agreement.  

3.12    The Director ICT reported that IBA had published advertisement in leading newspapers in May 2009 for pre-qualification of Technology Partner Agreement.  Forty nine vendors participated in the pre-qualification and 24 were short listed who were also approved by the Executive Committee.  However, he said that due to human error one envelop from a vendor was missed and later when evaluated they scored as per requirement and qualified thus the number of short listed vendors increased from 24 to 25.  He said that all tenders were shared with them but they did not win any tender. 

3.13    The Director ICT reported that the Purchase Committee has required that M/s. Oratech may be qualified by the EC as had been required by the A&FC. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the pre-qualification of the 25th vendor (M/s. Oratech).

3.14    The Director Projects suggested that the note should have come up through the Evaluation Committee. The Director ICT clarified that M/s. Oratech are not number one. However, he said that if number one backs out then M/s. Oratech will be presented through Evaluation Committee.

DECISION 

3.15    The Committee approved the recommendation of inclusion of M/s. Oratech among the pre-qualified vendors as the 25th vendor in the short-listing and recommended to the A&FC for approval.

ACTION

3.16    The Director ICT would present the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the A&FC for approval for inclusion of M/s. Oratech. 

d)   Approval for selecting M/s. Commtel Systems as a single source for implementing the solution of Internet Security Services (ISS). 

3.17    The Director ICT informed the Committee that IBM is one of the Technology Partners of IBA. He said that IBM has got two agents (1) M/s. Commtel Systems, and (2) M/s. Net Sol.  He further said that IBM did not bid for ISS as they do not have the set up and capacity to implement this solution in Pakistan. However, they advised to issue the contract and accordingly pay to M/s. Net Sol for ISS. The Director ICT stated that as M/s. Net Sol are not among the Technology Partners of IBA, they cannot be given the contract. However, he informed the Committee that M/s. Commtel Systems, who are one of the Technology Partners of IBA, have agreed to implement the system at the same price as quoted by M/s. Net Sol. He further said that the work will be done by M/s. Net Sol as a sub-contractor of M/s. Commtel Systems. The Director ICT requested the Executive Committee to approve the selection of M/s. Commtel Systems as a single source for implementing the solution of ISS. 

3.18    The Director Projects observed that as the first vendor was out therefore, the second remains there and stands a successful bidder based on technical reason. He said that technically M/s. Commtel systems are now the only bidder and as such this is not the case of single source.

DECISION 

3.19    The Committee in view of the justification given by the Director ICT, approved the selection of M/s. Commtel Systems as a single source for implementing the solution of Internet Security Services (ISS). 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
