MINUTES OF THE 76th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 23, 2009 

 

The 76th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 23, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.   Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean (Acting Director)

2.   Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

3.   Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN        Registrar

4.   Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

5.   Mr. Ali Taha                                        Director Finance

6.   Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

7.   Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                              Director CED

8.   Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

9.   Mr. Shameem Ahmed                         Director HEC Projects

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     To consider an email of November 18, 2009 from the Controller of Examinations regarding conduct of exams.

 

1.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili, Controller of Examinations, through his email of November18, 2009 addressed to the Executive Committee recorded his protest to the way Dr. Nasir Afghan was interfering in the conduct of scheduled exams and demanding serious deviations from the standard examination practices of IBA.

 

1.2       Mr. Khalili reported that Dr. Afghan is teaching a course to the Evening students in the current semester. On the day of the Ist term exam (Oct.8th ) he (Dr. Afghan) informed him that his was a Take-Home exam. He distributed the question papers to the students in the classroom at the scheduled time, the students took the paper with them, solved it at home and submitted it to the office the next morning. He stated that since this type of exam is not in practice in IBA therefore he requested Dr. Afghan to get its formal approval from the competent authority i.e. the Academic Board. He agreed and assured him that he would get it done. He reported that the formal approval was still awaited.

 

1.3       Mr. Khalili further said that the Examination Schedule for the 2nd Term was issued and notified to all on 7th November. Dr. Afghan’s Evening paper was scheduled on 19th November at 6 pm. On 17th November Dr. Afghan sent an email to Mr. Shafi (Evening Program Officer) informing him that his 2nd Term is a take-home exam and asked him to distribute his question papers to his students on 18th Nov. at 6 p.m. which the students will submit back on 19th Nov. at 11 AM.

 

1.4       Mr. Khalili said that Mr. Shafi has nothing to do in the conduct of exams. Moreover, the exam is scheduled on the Evening of 19th November which was known to all students as well as the faculty members well in advance. He said that he contacted Dr. Afghan on phone to talk about it but he refused to talk on the subject and simply said that he would decide how and when to conduct his exams.

 

1.5       Mr. Khalili said that with this type of attitude and serious deviation from standard practices the message will go to the student that examination schedules issued by the Controller of Examinations have no sanctity and that any faculty member can ignore it and can conduct the exam as and when he likes. He said that if this is the message we want to give to the students’ community then there is no need for an Examination Department or a Controller of Examinations in IBA. The new management should adopt a clear policy. He said that if the new management feels that conduct of examination should be at the will of faculty members, he has no problem, but it has to be approved by the Boards. Till then, he said that any interference in the affairs and conduct of exams from any quarter is not acceptable.

 

1.6              Mr. Khalili said that the IBA’s history on exam is that the time table that is issued is followed. He narrated many instances from the past when the city was crippled due to curfews and riots but exams were held dot on time and date as per the exam schedule.  He said that it was for the first time that a teacher has violated it and refused to follow the time table.

 

1.7              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that he got a call from Mr. Abdul Wajid on this issue and he asked Dr. Nasir Afghan but he said that the matter has already been resolved and the papers have already been distributed.

 

1.8              Mr. Khalili said that it was a misstatement on the part of Dr. Afghan. He further said that the matter was not resolved as he as the Controller of Examinations did not agree to release the question papers to the students a day in advance of the scheduled exam date and time.

 

1.9              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that there is no issue in terms of take home exam as it is already coming to the Academic Board. He said that he and Dr. Nishat had given approval for take home exam. He further said that Dr. Nasir Afghan should not have made the statement that he would decide when to take the exam. He said that we are going through a process of change and we have to take care that when the changes come in we should guide the people about the IBA system and make sure that they follow these policies.

 

1.10          Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that when a senior position person said that he would take approval for take home exam, he should have taken the approval before the second mid-term.

 

1.11          The Registrar suggested that the Committee should give Dr. Nasir Afghan an opportunity to explain this issue.  He further observed that procedurally it was wrong but the intention is good for the sake of change for improvement.

 

1.12          Mr. Khalili did not agree with the Registrar and said that he has no problem if an existing policy is changed by the competent authority i.e. Academic Board and the BOG. But till that time an existing policy must be followed by everyone in letter and spirit.

 

1.13          The Director ICT agreed with the Registrar that Dr. Nasir Afghan should be asked to explain to the Executive Committee.

 

1.14          The Registrar said that it is the unanimous opinion that in examination system no one should interfere but he said that in the existing system there will be a change and it should be accommodated. However, he said that Dr. Nasir Afghan should be asked as to why he did not take approval.

 

 

DECISION

 

1.15          The Committee expressed its concern on the violation of the policy and for not taking approval from the Academic Board for take home examination.    

 

1.16          The Committee further decided that the exams related matters should be allowed to be handled by the Examination Department.

 

1.17          The decision of the Academic Board on this issue would be communicated to the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 2      To discuss and decide location of Auditorium at IBA City Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Director Projects Mr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz before leaving for Hajj, had handed over to him two options presented by M/s. Arabtec; (1)  One Tower, total 75,000 sqft, and (2) Two Towers, total 132,000 sqft. and had said that Option 2 had been accepted. He said that Option 2 would comprise Aman Foundation Tower A (78,881 sqft.) and other donor’s Tower B (53,351 sq.ft) including auditorium.

 

2.2              Mr. Shameem said that the Director got a donor for the Auditorium at the City Campus and he (the Director) asked Mr. Shameem to arrange the visit of the new donor and to show them the proposed auditorium. He said that the proposed auditorium was not acceptable to the new donor because they also wanted their names to be prominently displayed. The observation given by the new donor was conveyed to M/s Arabtec as well as to the Dean & Director. He said that the Dean & Director asked him to meet Mr. Ahsan Jamil and Mr. Ali which he did. He said that the objective of the meeting was to change the location of the building.

 

2.3              Mr. Shameem said that the agreement has not yet been signed with the Arabtec and the Aman Foundation because IBA has not been able to provide design brief to them as yet.

 

2.4              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that there is a Project Committee and everything has to be approved by the Project Committee. He said that there should not be any problem in signing the contract

2.5              The Registrar said that two towers were already there but the only thing was that there was no fund for the auditorium and the auditorium was to be built in one of the towers.

 

2.6              Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that now the situation has changed and there is no merit in having two towers.

 

DECISION

 

2.7              The Committee decided that there would be one tower only with monolithic foundation with the proposed auditorium in the provided space.

 

2.8              The Committee also decided to sign the agreement. 

 

 

Item No. 3      Reporting Item:

 

3.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmed presented the email received from Mr. Shahab Ghani, consulting architect in which Mr. Ghani had stated that Mr. Bilgrami concurs with his (Mr. Shahab Ghani’s) suggestion of locating the overhead tanks at RHS Corner (Right Hand Side) of the boundary.  The reason was that it is 7 to 8 feet higher in elevation from LHS, the main water connection is closer (therefore economical in cost) and it is away from all the projected developments being planned and will not be a hindrance for future projects.

 

DECISION

 

3.2              Noted and approved by the Executive Committee.

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)         Request from a student for fee transfer to the next semester.

 

4.1       The Director Finance reported that a student has requested that his fee be transferred to the next semester as he has withdrawn from the semester due to illness.  He said that the precedent is that the fee is not carried forward and to do that the policy has to be changed if the Committee agrees.

 

DECISION

 

4.2       The Committee decided that the fee cannot be carried forward.

 

ACTION

 

4.3       The Director Finance would convey the decision to the concerned student.

                 

b)     Request for make-up examination.

 

4.4       Mr. Mobin Khalili reported that a student namely; Mr. Sheharyar Siddiq has requested for make up examination as he could not appear in the exam due to high fever (malaria).

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee did not approve the request for make-up examination due to high fever as it is allowed only in exceptional cases such as hospitalization, chicken pox, accident and not on high fever.

 

ACTION

 

4.6       The Controller of Examination would convey the decision to the student and would also notify the policy for make up examinations.

 

 

b)                   Requests / cases to be sent to the EC through concerned departments with comments.

 

4.7       Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that requests / cases from individuals should be made through concerned department and the department concerned should give its comments on the requests / cases for consideration by the Executive Committee. He mentioned a recent case when on the request of a main campus student make up exams were approved by the EC but the case or the decision was not referred to the Examination Department or the Program Office. As a result the makeup exams were not held according to the laid down procedure for makeup exams and within the specified time.

 

DECISION

 

4.8       The Committee agreed to the suggestion that requests / cases should be made through concerned department and the department concerned should give its comments on such requests / cases.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
