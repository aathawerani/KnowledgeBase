Item No. 2      To consider and approve amendment in the existing Merit Scholarship Policy.

 

Existing Merit Scholarships Policy:

 

2.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that according to existing Merit Scholarship Policy, the Merit scholarships are extended to candidates who have scored high enough for their names to fall in the top 10% of the list of candidates appearing in aptitude test and thereafter qualifying to be admitted to the BBA & MBA Morning Programs. The list of these candidates is published at the culmination of the admission process i.e. qualifying aptitude test, group discussion, and interview and providing proof of having met the minimum academic eligibility standards for the concerned program. These candidates are automatically awarded a 50% reduction in their tuition fees.

 

2.2       It will, however be obligatory / incumbent on such candidates to maintain a CGPA of 3.5 on completion of every two semesters each year. Inability to maintain the said CGPA will render the concerned merit scholarship holder ineligible for continuing of the merit scholarship in the remaining semesters.

 

2.3       In the event of a student losing his standing for merit scholarship, and regaining the required CGPA in any subsequent semester; the student will again become eligible for merit scholarship in the next semester but will not be entitled to claim the same for the preceding semester.

 

Proposed Amendment in the existing Merit Scholarship Policy:

 

2.4       Mr. Moeid Sultan proposed that the existing scholarship should continue as mentioned above, i.e. the top 10 percent on the basis of admission test score continue to receive merit scholarship (50% concession of tuition fee) as long as they maintain minimum CGPA 3.5 in the subsequent years of their degree program at IBA. Those who fail to maintain minimum CGPA of 3.5 at the end of the Academic year will become ineligible for further continuation of Merit scholarship. The number of scholarship slots released as a result of this discontinuation will be made available to the toppers (maintaining highest CGPA) in the dean’s honor list for that particular academic year. The new merit scholars would also be required to maintain minimum CGPA of 3.5 at each completed year for continuation of Merit Scholarship, i.e. other rules for continuation / discontinuation of merit scholarship, as mentioned in existing policy, will also apply to these new scholars.

 

2.5       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that according to the existing policy, students of subsidised programs such as BS(CS), BS (Economics & Maths) are not entitled to Merit Scholarships. He suggested to clearly mention this in the policy.  The Committee agreed with Dr. Shakeel Khoja.

 

2.6       The Dean & Director said that it should be publicised that if a student fails to maintain a minimum of 3.5 CGPA, then he/she would be excluded from the Merit Scholarships list. Students from Dean’s List having 3.5 CGPA (in standing order) would be accommodated against the vacant slot.  If there is no student with CGPA of 3.5 or above in the Dean’s List, then the slot will remain vacant.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee approved the amendment in the Merit Scholarship Policy as proposed by Mr. Moeid Sultan.

  

ACTION

 

2.8       Mr. Moeid Sultan would take action on the suggestions noted in 2.5 and 2.6 above.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve selection of vendor for GEM Adult Population Survey 2012.

 

3.1       The Committee considered the note from Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Associate Director, CED. In his note Dr. Qureshi stated that Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) Adult Population Survey (APS) is a study of individual attitudes and activities related to entrepreneurship and conducted cross-nationally on an annual basis. The results are unique in their focus on the individual-level and their use of a consistent set of standards for data collection that allows cross-national comparisons. The focus on individual-level behavior and attitudes mandates that each national team collects data from a representative sample of the adult population of their country using standard instruments and procedures. APS is the primary research tool of GEM; that will be used by IBA CED to build up their resources and targets (especially seminar, conferences and curriculum). IBA in cooperation with GEM and USAID conducted the 2010 APS entrepreneurship survey to assess the role of entrepreneurship in Pakistan’s national economic growth. The survey has put Pakistan on the map of nations conducting the Global Entrepreneurship Survey. To have a longitudinal view of the entrepreneurial climate, the third cycle of the GEM Pakistan (2012 APS Survey) is envisaged. IBA along with a consortium of Pakistani universities will finance the Adult Population Survey.

 

3.2       Dr. Shahid Qureshi reported in his note that a tender was floated in the national newspapers of Pakistan to hire the services of a vendor to carry out the Adult Population Survey throughout the country. The participants were asked to submit Expression of Interest along with Technical and Financial proposals. GEM Standard RFP was circulated among the Vendors as per request. The following firms participated and submitted their proposals:

 

1.      Oasis Insights

2.      Grant Thornton                      

 

3.3       An evaluation committee assessed the vendors’ proposals. The committee issued its report on 2nd July, 2012. Committee members included:

 

1.      Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Associate Director, CED, IBA, CO Principal Investigator, GEM

2.      Dr. Sarfraz A. Mian, Principal Investigator, GEM

3.      Jami Moiz, Assistant Professor, IBA

4.      Dr. Azam Ali, Assistant Professor, IBA

5.      Syed Jehanzeb Khan, Assistant Manager Finance

 

3.4       Oasis Insights is the only company which qualified technically and thus participated in the Financial Evaluation.  Grant Thornton was not considered as they were technically disqualified. Oasis is the only Technically Qualified Bidder with a quotation of Rs.1,700,000/-. Based on the fact that OASIS Insights (Pvt) Ltd is the only Technically Qualified Bidder, it was requested by Dr. Shahid Qureshi to approve the award of the project to OASIS Insights at a cost of Rs.1,700,000/-.  The Committee was informed that approval for the subject project has been obtained from the CPC. 

 

DECISION

 

3.5       On the basis of the approval of CPC, the Executive Committee approved the award of the project “GEM Adult Population Survey 2012” to Oasis Insights at a cost of Rs.1,700,000/-.

 

 

Item No. 4      Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

4.1       The Director ICT reported that he had a meeting with AMFCO and M/s HK Shah on July 13, 2012.  He said that due to value engineering, AMFCO is facing payment terms problems with the new vendors who are asking for 50% advance against insurance guarantee. He said that he has invited Mr. Maroof Ayub, CEO of AMFCO to make a presentation to the Executive Committee about the timelines and the implementation schedule of the IBA Data Center ICT Equipment importation and installation.  He said that he has also invited representative of H.K. Shah.

 

4.2       Mr. Maroof Ayub, CEO of AMFCO made a presentation to the EC which included introduction of AMFCO, Group Overview, Group Domains, their technology partners, IBA Data Centre project time lines, challenges, and requested payment terms.  While giving details of the Project time lines, he said that there are two major factors; electrical products & their peripherals, and the cooling systems which may take long time.  He said that Crac units have to be ordered and it would take 10 to 12 weeks after the acceptance of the order and then it would take another 20 to 25 days in reaching the consignment from Singapore to Karachi. So in total it would take 170 days for the Data Centre to be operational.  He further said that at the time of the bid the exchange rate was a little bit under Rs.90 for one US dollar but now it is around Rs.95.00.  He said that they have to make 50% advance payment to the vendors against insurance/bank guarantee along with order.    

 

4.3       The Dean & Director wanted to know the total foreign exchange components of the bid amount.  Mr. Maroof replied that it is over 80%.   

 

4.4       The Director Projects said that 30% mobilization advance is given against insurance / bank guarantee. He wanted to know if technical submittals have been approved by the Project Manager. To this Mr. Maroof replied that it would be approved by the Project Manager in 10 days time.

 

4.5       After extensive discussion it was agreed that in view of the changed circumstances 30% mobilization advance against insurance / bank guarantee be given to AMFCO and then as equipments are received, payments against the vouchers can be made.  The Dean & Director said that we have to facilitate the work so that we do not face difficulties in the future because of further possible depreciation of rupee. By making up-front payment we are in fact hedging against foreign exchange fluctuations.  He said that there are two variables which we have to keep in mind; we have done value engineering which was not the case while giving original tender, and we have got a high component of foreign exchange due to our preference for direct imports (as IBA will be saving import duty payments because of the exemption it enjoys) and that has the repercussion on exchange rate. He said that to lock in today’s foreign exchange rate, the Contractor has to remit the payment now.

 

4.6       The Director Projects said that a letter from the Project Manager has to be issued to IBA with firm recommendations on the agreed terms and conditions of payment which is 30% mobilization advance to be released against insurance / bank guarantee, 40% on delivery of equipments (70% secured advance minus 30% mobilization advance), 20% on running bills, and 10% to be retained for 12 months after acceptance.  Mr. Waseem of H.K. Shah said that he would issue the required letter to IBA.

 

DECISION

 

4.7       The Committee approved the City Campus Data Centre completion timeline which is 170 days beginning from July 17, 2012 and requested AMFCO to place order for the equipments and start working on the project.

 

4.8       The Committee also approved the payment schedule which is as under:

 

-          30% mobilization advance to be released against insurance / bank guarantee,

-          40% on delivery of equipments (70% secured advance minus 30% mobilization advance),

-          20% on running bills, and

-          10% to be retained for 12 months after acceptance.

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Request for re-take of Midterm Exam.

 

6.1       Mr. Muhammad Saleem Umer, Program Director EMBA reported that during midterm exam of June 9, 2012, invigilators observed that a few examinees were carrying cell phones in their pockets contrary to the guidance and instructions given. They re-announced the rule and accordingly six participants delivered their phones to the concerned staff. Subsequently, these participants were asked to handover their copies as their paper had been cancelled.

 

6.2       Mr. Saleem Umer requested that these participants may be reprimanded but forgiven for this act and be allowed to re-take this exam in lieu of cancelled paper in the final exam which is to be held next week. He referred to the decision of 57th meeting of Academic Board wherein it was decided, “in such cases (bringing mobile phones in the examination room) ‘I’ (Incomplete) should be awarded in the relevant course. The concerned student whose final examination has been cancelled for disciplinary action will have to appear in the examination of that course in the following semester without attending classes provided the attendance of that student was complete in the semester in which the paper of the student was cancelled.”

 

6.3       Dr. Shakeel Khoja said that allowing re-take to the students of EMBA in the same semester would be injustice to the students of other programs who have been given ‘I’ on similar violation case and they have to appear for the cancelled paper in the next semester. He suggested that mobile phone jammers may be installed around the examination rooms

 

DECISION

 

6.4       The Committee agreed that as per Academic Board’s decision, ‘I” (Incomplete) may be allowed. However, for consistency in the policy the Committee decided that the students of EMBA will also have to appear in the cancelled paper in the next semester.
