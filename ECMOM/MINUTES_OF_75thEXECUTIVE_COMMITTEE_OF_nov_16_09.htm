MINUTES OF THE 75th MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 16, 2009

 

 

The 75th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 16, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present: 

  1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean (Acting Director)

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  6. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

  7. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                         Director CED

  8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

  9. Mr. Shameem Ahmed                         Director HEC Projects

 

Mr. Shahab Ghani of M/s. Shabab Ghani & Associates attended by special invitation.

 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     To consider note from Director HEC Projects regarding location of underground and overhead water tanks at IBA Staff Town.

 

1.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects informed the Committee that in order to identify the exact point of KW&SB bulk water pipeline (passing through the University of Karachi) at which IBA will be given a 3″ main connection, a joint survey of the area was carried by IBA’s Consultant M/s. NESPAK and KW&SB’s Executive Engineer concerned on November 5, 2009. He reported that in this regard they got the needed cooperation from the University as well. As a result of the survey, the tee-off point was determined and agreed upon. Accordingly, IBA will be given a connection from KW&SB’s filtered-water pipeline of 64″ dia, instead of from 84″ dia pipeline that carries chlorinated but unfiltered water.

 

1.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that M/s. NESPAK have now proposed the most logical and economical straight-line route for laying the pipeline from the KW&SB tee-off point, upto the IBA Staff Colony where it is planned to build the underground and overhead water tanks. He said that the route is yet to be approved by the University but hoped that there would be no problem in this regard.

 

1.3       According to the NESPAK proposal the pipeline will enter the IBA Staff Colony through its southern boundary wall. The underground and overhead tanks may be located anywhere along the wall as well as at the (Right Hand Side) RHS or (Left Hand Side) LHS corner. He recommended that the Executive Committee may consider approving the location at the RHS corner for the following reasons:

            (a)        It will be more economical as the main pipeline will be terminated at                       the shortest possible distance,

 

            (b)        The construction work, which will continue for about 8 months, will                       be far away from the residential areas and therefore will not create

                        any physical inconvenience for the faculty and staff, and

 

(c)        In case IBA’s New Girls’ Hostel and Visiting Faculty Hostel are located in the area close to the LHS boundary wall, the overhead water tank will not mar their elevations.

 

1.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested for an early approval as he would have to submit the plans to the University as soon as possible.

 

1.5              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui observed that the Architects should suggest where to make the overhead and the underground water tanks.

 

1.6              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that when we are going to build girls’ hostel and other buildings at the Staff Town, those should be synchronization with the Master Plan.

 

1.7              The Registrar said that the Left Hand Side (LHS) Corner seems most suitable for the overhead water tank. He further said that in his opinion there is no need to link the overhead water tank with the Master Plan.

 

1.8              Mr. Najmi Bilgrami agreed to the proposal of the Registrar that the overhead tank be constructed on LHS corner.

 

 

DECISION

 

1.9              The Committee requested the Architects and Mr. Shahab Ghani to survey the site and accordingly suggest the location for the overhead and underground tanks. The location to be suggested by the Architects would be acceptable to the Executive Committee.

 

ACTION

 

1.10          Mr. Shameem Ahmed would submit the plan to the Karachi University on the basis of the suggestions to be given by the Architects and Mr. Shahab Ghani on the location of the overhead and underground tanks.

 

 

Item No. 2      Review and Selection of a design for the proposed Visiting Faculty  Hostel at IBA Staff Town.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects informed the Committee that detailed Terms of Reference were issued to and Technical & Financial Proposals invited from the following six pre-qualified architectural and engineering consultants on August 29, 2009:

                        1.         M/s. FNMH Architecture

                        2.         M/s. M.M. Pakistan (Pvt.) Ltd

                        3.         M/s. ACE Architectural & Town Planning

                        4.         M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative

                        5.         M/s. ICON

                        6.         M/s. Zaheeruddin Consultants

 

2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed also presented a copy of the “Preliminary Ideas for Designing the Building and its Ancillary Facilities”, which was a part of the TOR.  He said that Topographical Survey and Service Drawings of the Staff Town were also provided to all architects earlier. He said that two of the above-mentioned six consultants, namely, M/s. FNMH and M/s. ACE did not participate in the design competition. Technical Proposals received from the other four participants were forwarded to the consulting architect Mr. Shahab Ghani Khan on October 19, 2009 for evaluation from an architectural perspective, keeping in view IBA’s needs which were communicated to the competing architects through TOR.

 

2.3       Mr. Shameem stated that they have now received an evaluation report from Mr. Shahab Ghani on the Technical Proposals submitted by the four competing architects. He presented the most relevant pages and said that the full text of the report is available on demand by any member of the Executive Committee. He reported that Mr. Shahab Ghani has recommended selection of Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt.) Ltd. as our consultant for the project.

 

2.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that before his departure for Hajj Engr. Rehan A Riaz, Director Projects also evaluated the competing designs and gave his “confidential” comments to him according to which M/s. Icon got 85 on a scale of 1-100 and M/s. NBCL got 75.  The other two namely MMP and ZCL got 40- and 30 respectively.

                                               

2.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive committee to review the two preliminary, schematic designs prepared by M/s. NBCL and M/s. Icon and give its decision.

 

2.6       Mr. Ahsan Najmi of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami first presented the conceptual design of the Visiting Faculty Hostel at IBA Staff Town. The design was for ground plus two floors under the total covered area of 20,378 sft with provision for further extension depending on the foundation.

 

2.7       After the presentation of Mr. Ahsan Najmi, Mr. Hanif Daud of M/s. Icon also presented the conceptual design of the Visiting Faculty Hostel prepared by them.  They had also prepared the design for G+2 with the total covered area of 26,350 sft.

 

2.8       After the presentation when the two architects, left, the Committee discussed the preliminary schematic designs presented by the two.

 

2.9              The Registrar observed that M/s. NBCL is following a theme in all his designs which is lacking from the designs presented by M/s. ICON.

2.10          Mr. Shahab Ghani observed that every time M/s. Icon present the Master Plan differently.  He suggested that it has to be frozen somewhere.  He also said that there is no provision for elevation and emergency exit in both the designs  

 

2.11          The Committee also considered the points score on design reviewed by Shahab Ghani & Associates. According to the criteria M/s. ICON got 63 points whereas M/s. NBCL got 85 points.

 

 

DECISION

 

2.12          The Committee unanimously approved the preliminary schematic design presented by M/s. NBCL

 

 

Item No. 3      Installation of surveillance security cameras and walkthrough gates.

 

3.1              Major Qamar Rizvi (R), Security Coordinator, Main Campus, stated that in view of the worsening security environments and increasing incidences of terrorist attacks; directives were received from higher authorities for improvement of existing security system at the IBA campuses.  He also informed the Committee that a meeting in this regard was held at Governor’s House on October 21, 2009 which was also attended by the Dean & Director IBA. During the meeting the educational institutions were directed to anticipate ongoing security situation and prepare themselves through incorporating under vehicle mirrors, metal detectors, erection of barriers etc. proactively and install security surveillance cameras and walk through gates at selected locations. He stated that a survey has been carried out to estimate the requirements in respect of two campuses of IBA.  He presented the estimate of requirements along with availability and cost of material in respect of the installation of the security cameras and walk-through gate for approval of the Executive Committee.

 

3.2              Major (R) Qamar Rizvi stated that 16 cameras would be placed at different places at the Main Campus (he also indicated the locations where the cameras have been planned to be installed).

 

3.3              Dr. Sayeed Ghani observed that the main focus is on inside premises but there is a lot of concern outside the premises too.

 

3.4              Mr. Mobin Khalili asked the Security Coordinators to ensure that no sensitive area is left uncovered.

 

3.5              Major (R.) Qamar Rizvi clarified that two cameras will be making coverage of the outside area. He said that at certain locations there would be fixed cameras and at some there would be moving cameras.

 

3.6              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested to fix one stationary camera in each class room.  However, the Committee felt that this will be considered at a later stage. 

 

3.7              The Registrar said that under the proposed system 16 CCTVs can be installed but if we go for more than 16 we would have to install more than one system. He said that all 16 cameras will be recording at the same time 

 

3.8              Major (R) Qamar Rizvi said that 7 and 30 days records saving options are available.  He suggested to have records saved for 30 days instead of 7 days as the price difference between the two options is of around Rs.10,000/- only.            

 

3.9              Major (R) Qamar Rizvi informed the Committee that approximately Rs.0.9 million would be incurred on installation of security surveillance system in IBA Main Campus.

 

3.10          Capt. (R) M. Faisal Rizvi, Security Coordinator, IBA City Campus also presented the proposed lay out of CCTV; 12 for City Campus main buildings and 5 for APWA. He said that three cameras will be covering the road as well as walls.

 

3.11          Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that instead of erecting poles for installing cameras, trees may be used at the City Campus for such purposes. 

 

3.12          Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that in view of the high security risk of the City Campus due to its location at least one hydraulic barrier at the Exit Gate, if not at all gates, should be installed as it is more vulnerable as compared to other gates. He wanted to know as to what would be the cost of hydraulic barrier at City Campus to which Capt. (R.) Faisal Rizvi replied that a 2.8’ hydraulic barrier would cost around Rs.600,000/-

 

3.13          It was suggested that one more camera be added or relocated to cover the Aga Khan III road. It was also suggested that certain cameras be relocated and moved a little ahead so that wider area may be covered.

 

 

DECISION

 

3.14          The Committee approved the concept of security surveillance cameras and walk through gates for both the campuses as presented by the Security Coordinators.

 

3.15          The Committee also decided that in addition to five cameras for APWA, one more camera at the top floor of APWA building will be installed to cover Aga Khan III Road.

 

3.16          The Committee agreed to the suggestion of Mr. Mobin Khalili for installing a hydraulic barrier at the exit gate at the City Campus.

 

ACTION

 

3.17          The Security Coordinators will take necessary actions to finalize the installation of the proposed security surveillance cameras, walk through gates and one hydraulic barrier at the City Campus.

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

   

a)      Warm Water Problem at the Boys’ and Girls Hostels.

 

4.1              Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects informed the Committee that the Boys’ Hostel Manager Mr. Ahmed Mustafa reported to him that day that getting warm water in the hostel’s bathrooms is becoming a problem.  He said that the problem was assessed by him and the Maintenance Manager on the spot. He said that an additional water heater for the kitchen and replacement of two old geysers may solve the problem. Similarly, two additional water heaters (geysers) are needed at the Girls’ Hostel too.

 

4.2              Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve an estimated expenditure of Rs.75,000/- and to advise the Finance Department to provide the required funds urgently.  He said that the installation work will be done by IBA Plumbers.

 

4.3              The Registrar said that he had ordered the Purchase Officer to get the quotation and said that the estimated cost is within the approval limit of Mr. Shameem Ahmed. 

 

DECISION

 

4.4              The Committee approved the purchase of three geysers – one additional and two as replacement.

 

b)     Additional Security Guards for City Campus.

 

4.5              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that two more security guards are needed at the City Campus.

 

4.6              Mr. Muhammad Faisal Rizvi reported that two guards have been deputed at APWA and two guards have been placed at the construction site.  He said that at least two additional guards for day shift are needed.

 

DECISION

 

4.7              The Committee authorized the hiring of two additional guards for the City Campus through outsourcing.

 

ACTION

 

4.8              The Director Finance will check the availability of the budget and inform the Registrar accordingly for hiring two additional guards.

 

c)      To consider suggestion from Mr. Azmat Masood, father of a student.

 

4.9              The Registrar said that Mr. Azmat Masood, father of a student has sent an email stating that as per IBA Policy, the full time day students of IBA are allowed a maximum of 4 absences from a class in the semester and slight relaxation in the policy is for students participating in out of city sports events and are allowed six absences.  Bu for students who are genuinely sick, there is no relaxation.  Such students have to attend the classes at a risk of their health and without grasping anything, or drop the class to lose valuable time and money. He suggested to allow a few extra days of absence to the students who are down with typhoid, measles, chicken pox etc and for the sake of all other students of the class who may be affected by these ill students.

 

4.10          It was clarified that the two absences are not allowed for sports events outside city but on an official representation of IBA and these two additional absences are allowed by the Director only.

 

DECISION

 

4.11          The Committee considered the application sympathetically but due to the fact that the sickness may prolong for an indefinite period - a week or two or more, decided that a line for allowing maximum sick leave cannot be drawn. Thus the Committee decided that the existing policy of allowing four absences in a semester will continue. However, the students may apply for withdrawal from the course / courses and the Executive Committee will approve withdrawal after assessing the justification for withdrawal.

 

ACTION

 

4.12          The Registrar will convey the decision of the Executive Committee to Mr. Azmat Masood.

 

 

d)     Condoning absences on account of “A” level examinations for improvement of grade(s).

 

4.13          The Registrar said that the Board has allowed improvement of grades in “A” level examinations to those students who could not meet the eligibility criteria for admissions.  He said that all such students who have to appear in the “A” level exams are marked absent on that day at IBA.  He suggested that absences due to “A” level exams should be condoned.

 

DECISION

 

4.14     The Committee decided that the concerned student would be required to submit a copy of date sheet and other relevant document as a proof of his / her appearing in the “A” level examination on the basis of which the concerned program office would condone the absence(s) for the particular day(s). However, the class teacher will mark the student absent for that day.

 

ACTION

 

4.15     The Registrar would convey the decision to the Program Offices for compliance.

 

 

e)      Fee Receivable from students.

 

4.16     The Director Finance presented a list of those students who have not paid their dues and would be conferred the degrees in the coming convocation.  He also presented the specimen letter to be addressed to the students asking them to settle their dues before convocation.  He sought advice of the Executive Committee so that he may send the letters to the students before the invitation for convocation is sent to them.  He informed the Committee that last year they had followed the same practice and received significant outstanding dues from the students.

 

4.17     The Registrar informed the Committee that the Dean and Director through his email suggested that the Finance Department should do exactly what they did last year.

 

DECISION

 

4.18     The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Dean & Director and requested the Director Finance to do what they had done last year and send letters for recovery of fee to the students under his signature.

 

ACTION

 

4.19     The Director Finance would send letters to the students from whom the fee is receivable as he had done last year.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
