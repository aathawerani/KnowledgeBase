The following agenda items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve Revision in Financial Policies:

 

a)      Student Bodies Fund - Policy and Procedures:

 

1.1       Mr. Moeid Sultan, General Manager Finance said that some clarifications and a few changes in the existing policy are needed which have been proposed for consideration and approval by the EC.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director wanted to know what has been the impact after the implementation of this policy and if the objectives of the policy and procedures have been achieved or not.  He further said that if the objectives have been achieved then the Committee may fine tune the policy.

 

1.3       Mr. Moeid and Mr. Zafar Siddiqui confirmed that the objectives, particularly probity in societies’ financial matters, are being fully achieved.

 

1.4       However, the Registrar said that he has the following reservations:

 

·         The students arrange sponsorships for their events but this becomes an obligation for IBA. Moreover, they spend a lot of time arranging conferences; thus wasting their precious time.

·         Some events are arranged during class timings due to which the classes are disturbed.

·         Much effort is needed in security arrangements.

 

1.5       The Dean & Director said that students at all good universities seek sponsorship and it does not become an obligation on the institution. He said that students’ efforts and time required for arranging such events are part of their grooming and confidence building.  They have to learn how to strive a balance between studies and extra-curricular activities. However, he agreed that events should not disturb the classes. He said that the Registrar has the authority not to allow such activities which disturb the classes.

 

1.6       The Director H.R. said that the Patrons should be actively involved in societies’ activities and their guidance would be highly valuable.

 

1.7       Mr. Moeid Sultan highlighted the proposed additions in Objective, Calendar of Events, Financial Proposal, Sponsorships Management, Ticket Selling, Incurrence of Expenditure, procedures for payment of Expenses more than Rs.5,000/-, Petty Cash Expenses, Payment Mechanism, Closure of the Event, and Finance / Audit.

 

1.8       The Dean & Director clarified that if the Patron of the Society certifies that certain petty expenditures, which are not compliant with this policy, are genuine then such expenditures should be accepted for payment. He instructed to advertise for pre-qualification of vendors to supply services such as hotel, catering, printing, backdrop & sound system, event management firms, etc. for the functions / events of IBA. Student Societies will also then enter into contract with these prequalified vendors.

 

DECISION

 

1.9       The Committee recommended the additions/changes in the Student Bodies Fund Policy and Procedures as presented by Mr. Moeid Sultan to the A&FC for approval.

 

1.10     The Committee requested the Registrar to advertise for prequalification of vendors for various activities / events including convocation, orientation, seminars, conferences, workshops, lectures and events arranged by the IBA and Societies as well. The prequalified vendors would be requested to quote the prices for the various activities and the lowest responsive bidder would be awarded the job.

 

ACTION

 

1.11     The Registrar would advertise for prequalification of vendors for various services to be supplied at functions / events of IBA.

 

1.12     Mr. Moeid Sultan, in consultation with the Students’ Counsellor and  would arrange to obtain feedback from the faculty, administration, students and some randomly selected sponsors on the professionalism exhibited by each society.

 

b)     Financial Policies.

 

1.13     The Director Finance presented the proposed revision / additions/deletions in the existing Financial Policies which covered:

 

·         Maintenance of Accounts

·         Charge of fee, its payment and fee refund policy

·         Payroll Policy

·         Fee concession for IBA employees and eligible dependents

·         Car Entitlement Policy

·         Staff Provident Fund

·         Creditors/Payments to Third Parties

·         Review of basic pay-scales.

 

 1.14    The Director Finance said that in the existing policy it is mentioned that “no money shall be received in cash”, and the same has been retained in the proposed policy; the only addition is of “promptly deposited” for cheques/demand drafts/pay orders received by the Finance Department.

 

1.15     The Dean & Director observed that in exceptional cases, for example, if the donors are giving money in cash then we cannot refuse. He suggested to reword the sentences to read as, “No money shall be received in cash as a matter of routine. Cheques/Demand Drafts/Pay Orders/cash, if any, received by the Finance Department should also be promptly deposited into the IBA Bank account.”

 

1.16     The Committee agreed with the Dean & Director and suggested to mention time limit for deposit of cash and other instruments.

 

1.17     The Committee decided to replace the words, “future teaching periods”, with the words, “future semester”.

 

1.18     The Committee decided to reword Item C of the Fee Concession for IBA employees and eligible dependents policy as under:

 

            “In addition to the above, all IBA’s additional charges (admission fee, lab cum library charges and student activity charges) for the duration of the program are also 100% exempt for the employee/his/her spouse and eligible dependents if the employee’s monthly gross pay is Rs.25,000/- or less excluding overtime and any other additional benefits and allowances.”

           

1.19     The Dean & Director asked the Director H.R. to issue a notification on his behalf requesting the IBA employees to intimate the H.R. Department if any eligible dependent of the IBA employee has secured admission in Aga Khan University or GIK Institute or other similar institutions so that IBA may pay the fee for such students.

 

1.20     The Committee decided to withdraw the option of purchasing the car after it has completed a maximum useful life by the person who has been allotted the car.

 

1.21     On the basic pay scales, the Dean & Director said that recently the BPS has been revised.  He suggested to include the revised BPS instead of the pay scale of 2008.

 

DECISION

 

1.22     The Committee recommended the proposed additions/deletions/amendments in the Financial Policies along with the decisions noted above to the A&FC for approval.

 

ACTION

 

1.23     The Director Finance would present the revised Financial Policy to the A&FC for approval.

 

1.24     The Director H.R. on behalf of the Dean & Director would issue a notification requesting the IBA employees to intimate the H.R. Department if any eligible dependent of the IBA employee has secured admission in Aga Khan University or GIK Institute or other similar institutions so that IBA may pay the fee for such students.         

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve additional amount incurred on refurbishment of Centre of Executive Education and construction of emergency staircase.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that after the approval of CPC, tender was awarded to lowest bidder M/s. SIMAB Traders for the renovation and refurbishment of Centre of Executive Education at a cost of Rs.2,392,232/- on August 12, 2010. Initially the contract duration was 45 days but due to increase in scope of work the whole work was completed in January 2011. Similarly, due to change in scope of work the cost increased from Rs.2,392,232 to Rs.3,753,092.  He said that the total cost of the project as per original contract was Rs.2,392,232/- The amount has risen due to increase in scope of work to the extent of Rs.1,360,823.  He presented the following break-down of the increase in scope:

 

1.      Qualitative change                                                  Rs.   63,525/- Floor Carpet

2.      Extra item/Additional work                                        Rs.   484,147/- Extra item

3.      Revised Design of Stair from1st floor to roof top           Rs.   813,151/-

Total                                                                                               Rs.1,360,823/-

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that the Central Purchase Committee has recommended the approval of the additional amount and the time duration of contract in its meeting held on July 22, 2011. Based on the recommendations of Central Purchase Committee, he requested the Executive Committee to approve the additional amount of Rs.1,360,823 and the time extension from October 2, 2010 to January 31, 2011.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that Mirza Izhar Husain being the head of the CEE, is the client and he should have been here to confirm that he supports those increases in scope. He further said that this change in the scope should have been approved before giving out the variation orders to the contractor.  He wanted to know as to who asked them and authorized them to revise the stair case design. He said that if the client had asked them to revise the design then the Director Projects should have presented it to the EC for approval.

 

2.4       The Director Projects said that when they opened the ceiling of the CEE, it was discovered that almost all the wirings were burnt or broken which had to be replaced.

 

2.5       The Dean & Director said that as Director Projects he could order modification in the work order as the need arises due to unforeseen demands during the process of renovation as he did for the CEE but he said that he cannot make substantial changes in the design.

2.6       The Director Projects said that the height of staircase in the original BoQ was kept to touch the level of Emergency Exit door. It was later decided that it should be extended upwards to the roof level. The reason was that there was no other way of access to the academic block roof for maintenance / electrical / admin teams to carry out their routine day-to-day tasks. The Director Projects said that he authorized this change in the height of the staircase hoping to get ex-post facto approval from the EC.

 

2.7       The Dean & Director said that there are two ways of getting things done; when one comes across unanticipated situation and if the Director of the Department is convinced then variation order may be issued and reported to the EC or whosoever has the power to approve it.  The second situation is that when one is making changes of a substantive nature such as revised design, the owner of the building will make a request which is to be presented to the EC for approval before the variation order is issued.

 

2.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that there is only one exit and entry door at the CEE. He suggested to provide another door.

 

DECISION

 

2.9       The Committee approved the additional amount of Rs.1,360,823/- and the extension in time duration of contract from October 2, 2010 to January 31, 2011.

Item No. 4      To consider and approve award of work for the construction of Student Centre at IBA Main Campus.

 

4.1       The Director Projects reported that the Tender Documents for the construction of Student Centre at IBA Main Campus were sent to the ten Pre-qualified Contractors, out of which the following five responded:

 

a.      M/s. Paragon Constructors.

b.      M/s. Principal Builders.

c.      M/s. Total Construction.

d.      M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises.

e.      M/s. Project Managers

 

4.2       The Director Projects reported that the bids were opened in front of the representatives of Contractors, Project Managers, and Project Architect and representatives from Projects Department of IBA. Following were the quoted prices of the contractors:

 

a)  M/s. Paragon Constructors           Rs.184,296,444/-              

b)  M/s. Principal Builder                   Rs.169,863,328/-

c)  M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises              Rs.163,926,442/-

d)  M/s. Total Construction                Rs.152,894,003/-

e)  Project Managers                         Rs.140,694,420/-

 

4.3       The Director Projects stated that the bids and all the relevant documents were sent to Project Architects M/s. NBCL and   Project Managers M/s. Integ for detailed evaluation and scrutiny. As per the evaluation report of M/s. NBCL, they recommended the 2nd lower bidder (M/s.Total Construction) for the subject work whereas the Project Manager M/s. Integ recommended the 1st lowest bidder M/s. Project Managers Constructor Fabricators.

 

4.4       The Director Projects informed the Committee that in order to get the technical clarifications from both the bidders, a meeting was held with Project Architects, Project Managers, IBA’s Project Department representative and the 1st and the 2nd lowest bidders. Following the technical clarification meeting, both the bidders were asked to submit their technical specifications of HVAC equipments and the core Project Team details. Both the Contractors submitted their documents to Consultants and Project Manager. The Project Architects/ Consultants and Project Manager submitted their supplementary evaluation report in response to the submission of documents on July 13, 2011. In their Supplementary Reports, the Project Architects /Consultants again recommended the 2nd lowest bidder M/s. Total Construction and M/s. Integ again recommended the 1st Lowest Bidder M/s. Projects Managers.

 

4.5       The Director Projects reported that in order to finalize the contractor for award of work a meeting was held in Project Architects /Consultant Office between IBA’s representatives, Project Architects/Consultant and Project Managers on July 14, 2011. M/s. NBCL, the Project Architects in response to the above meeting, sent a  letter dated 15th July 2011 and again recommended the 2nd lowest bidder. They have given following five reasons based on which they have concluded that M/s. Total Construction is technically and financially the lowest responsive bidder:

 

·         The pre-qualification of Contractors were entirely done by IBA prior to their designing of the Students Centre and thus M/s. NBCL were not part of the prequalification process. Having had unhappy experience in the past with M/s. Project Managers and having confirmed this with other consultants and seeing the quality of their work recently delivered to IBA, they (NBCL) confidently say that they (Project Managers) are incapable of handling the Students Centre Project. As the Architects of this Project they know the complexity of the design, and they strongly feel that if this project is awarded to M/s. Projects Managers, it will not be constructed to their standards and create problems for IBA and the teams involved.

 

·         The Bid submitted by M/s. Projects is 6% lower than the Engineer’s Estimate in which the margin of overheads, taxes and profit was kept as low as 20%. Any bid below the Engineer’s Estimate creates serious doubts in minds as to the financial workability of this project.

 

·         Further to this is the fact that M/s. Project Managers are subcontracting most of the construction jobs to others, including the project management. The fact that it is being outsourced and their bid is so low, creates further doubts in their ability to complete the project as per drawings and specifications.

 

·         The HVAC bid submitted by M/s. Project Managers is 35% lower than the Engineer’s Estimate. This again is a major cause for concern and raises questions as to how they could deliver the projects.

·         Following the meeting with M/s. Total  Construction, they have on July 11th 2011, submitted a letter stating that they would be using Kaaf Engineering as HVAC Sub-Contractors and using SMK Equipment which is as per Specifications. They have fulfilled all the requirement of MEP and are adhering to all requirements of the Tender Documents. In addition, they (NBCL) have had a good experience with them in the past on other projects and are fully satisfied with the progress and quality work they are doing on the current CED building on the IBA campus.

 

4.6       The Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s. Total Construction also submitted a post bid letter verifying their HVAC Sub-Contractor and voluntarily offered a discount of Rs.5,000,000/- on their quoted price. He said that if they consider the voluntary discount offered by M/s. Total Construction in the interest of IBA, their total bid price comes to Rs.147,476,213/- from Rs.152,476,213/-

 

4.7       The Director Projects said that the TEC and the CPC both have scrutinized all the details and recommended to award the work to the technically and financially evaluated lowest responsive bidder M/s. Total Construction at a cost of Rs.147,476,213/- Based on above facts Project Architects and Project Managers reports, he requested the Executive Committee to recommend the award of work to M/s. Total Construction against a total price of Rs.147,476,213/- as they are technically and financially the lowest evaluated  responsive bidder.

 

4.8       The Director Projects said that Rs.134 million has been provided in the budget for this project. He further said that Rs.134 million includes Rs.14 million for the cost of gen set also.

 

4.9       The Dean & Director said that the gen set should be excluded from this tender as a gen set will be available under the external development project. He said that he has divided the Centre into several units and each unit is to be sold for Rs.5 million to the alumni. He said that the intermediary owner of the Students Centre is Mr. Zafar Siddiqui who should be consulted by the Projects Department during the execution of the project. 

 

DECISION

 

4.10     The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of work to M/s. Total Construction against a total price of Rs.147,476,213/- as they are technically and financially the lowest evaluated responsive bidder.

 

ACTION

 

4.11     The Director Projects would present the proposal to the A&FC for consideration and approval.

Item No. 6      To consider and award the Project Management and Consultancy Services for City and Main Campuses to M/s. H.K. Shah.

 

6.1      The Director ICT in his note of July 19, 2011 stated that the procurement process of the data center construction at City Campus was in advanced stage. This required services of Project Manager to be hired. As M/s. Integ were already providing services to IBA on construction of Data Center Civil Works at City Campus, they were asked to give their proposal for carrying out the Project Management of Data Center at City Campus with special provision that they will provide the experts with experience in the Data Center construction. They submitted the bid and are asking for a fee of 3.5% of the project value.

 

6.2      The Director ICT further reported in his note that M/s. H.K Shah who are the only Project Manager meeting the Selection criteria and are technically qualified experts, were also invited to send their offer to undertake the project management services, as they had offered their services earlier in response to the RFP issued to them. Their Services also included Design Validation, Consultancy and Project Management. They had also committed themselves to help IBA in the International Certification if and when IBA decided to do so. M/s H.K Shah offered to provide Design Validation and Project Management Services at 2.5% of the cost of project per Data Center. The following will be the scope for each campus:

 

Scope of City Campus                                                           

 

·         Scrutinize the SBD.

·         Respond to all vendor queries and Issue addendums or corrections.

·         Evaluation of Bids, design validation and Project Management.

 

Scope of Main Campus

 

·         Design of the Data Center, prepare tender document.

·         Evaluation of Bids, design validation and Project Management.

 

6.3      The Director ICT reported in his note that M/s. H.K Shah’s original bid was considered and processed through open competitive process. The project is already delayed and there is an emergent condition to engage a Project Manager before completion of tendering process.  He requested the EC that M/s H.K Shah be considered for award of work as they are lower than M/s. Integ and technically have much more experience and expertise along with certification of Uptime.

 

6.4       The Dean & Director said that last time they had decided to do the work internally but it seems that this cannot be done satisfactorily as he has experienced delays in last few months. He said that they had given the work of Design Supervision of Data Centre to ICON at 5.8% which they will continue to do. He said that Integ is demanding 3.5% for project management whereas M/s H.K Shah will be doing this at 2.5% and they would do project management, validation of the design and also supervise and scrutinize the SBD.

 

6.5       The Director Projects said that at the Main Campus the design of data centre is being done by M/s. Icon.

 

6.6       The Dean & Director said that the scope of work to M/s. H.K. Shah should comprise (a) whether the designs are valid or not, (b) evaluation of bids received, and (c) project management of two data centres. He asked the Director Project to inform M/s. Icon that they have not done the work of data centres to the satisfaction of the Executive Committee as is evident from the prolonged delays. Therefore, they would be paid at a reduced rate i.e. at 3% rather than 5.8% and the amount so saved would then be paid to M/s. H.K Shah for Design Validation and project management. In this way there would be no additional liability on IBA. The Committee agreed with the Dean & Director.

 

DECISON

 

6.7       The Committee approved the award of the Project Management and Consultancy Services for City and Main Campuses Data Centres to M/s. H.K. Shah at a total fee of 2.5% of the project cost.

 

ACTION

 

6.8       The Director ICT would award the project to M/s. H.K. Shah.

 

6.9       The Director Projects would convey the decision of the Executive Committee to M/s. Icon that since they have not done the project to the satisfaction of the Executive Committee therefore, the Committee has decided that Icon would be paid at a much reduced rate i.e. at 3% rather than 5.8%.

   

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
