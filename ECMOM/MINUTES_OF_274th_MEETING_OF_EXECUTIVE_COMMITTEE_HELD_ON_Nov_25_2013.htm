MINUTES OF 274TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, NOVEMBER 25, 2013

 

The 274th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, November 25, 2013 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                              Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Mohammad Nishat                                                       Associate Dean, FBA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                             Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                          Registrar

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                              Director Finance

6.      Mr. Imran Batada                                                             Acting Head of ICT

7.      Mr. Adnan Hameed                                                           Manager H.R.

 

Mr. Muhammad Rehan Shaikh, Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Managers Projects, and Mr. Noshad Rizvi of Icon attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the minutes of Special Committee meeting on review of Aman Tower’s Interior and Space Data:

 

1.1       Mr. Rehan Shaikh presented the case as Engr. Rehanul Ambia could not attend the meeting due to indisposition.  Mr. Shaikh said that there are some recommendations of the Special Committee on Aman Tower which require consideration and approval of the EC.

 

1.2       The Dean & Director at the very outset clarified that if money is involved on the review of Aman Tower’s Interior and Space Data then the        EC cannot entertain the proposal as we have already exceeded our allocation from the IBA’s own budget. He said that an additional amount of Rs.200 million has already been provided by Aman Foundation.  He said that the Interior of Aman Tower including security has been discussed after every three or six months. He said that if the recommendations can be implemented from the allocated budget or from its saving then he has no problem.  He said that the proposed CCTV, walkthrough gates, handbag/cargo scanner, and speed barriers can be done from Security budget, if available.  To a question Mr. Rehan Shaikh clarified that conduits for all CCTV have been provided but not for the walk through gates. The Registrar suggested to put plain 2” or 3” diameter PVC pipe for walkthrough conduit which would not cost much. The Registrar said that probably Mr. Mobin Khalili had wanted a hydraulic barrier which is very expensive – it would cost around Rs.20 million. He suggested to get its pit and civil work completed and as and when fund is available the hydraulic barrier would be fixed. The Registrar said that it is the instruction from the Cantonment Board to place hydraulic barrier. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Rehan Shaikh to request Mahwish Jahangir Siddiqui Foundation to place a hydraulic barrier and tell them that we may not get building completion certificate unless we fix the hydraulic barrier. 

 

Besides point No.5 of the minutes discussed above, Mr. Rehan Shaikh presented the following points from the minutes of Special Committee meeting for consideration and approval:

 

1.3       Point #6 of the minutes -  The provision of Access System for different floors should also be considered so that the movement of individuals may be regulated to their relevant areas/floors.

 

Decision of EC: The EC did not approve the recommendation.

 

1.4       Point #9 of the minutes: The suggestion to carve out more space for parking.  Mr. Rehan Shaikh informed that there is space for parking 35 cars in addition to 70 cars space in the basement.

 

Decision: The basement will be common for all. The Dean & Director said that the option for second basement was discussed thoroughly and ruled out.

 

1.5       Point # 10 – Security reception counter at the basement for the visitors. 

 

Decision:  Security Reception at the basement is not required as security check will be done at the barrier.  Visitors would be instructed to first go to the ground floor where there would be security reception counter.

 

1.6       Point # 18 – Tablet chairs should be provided in the classrooms instead of fixed tables and chairs. 

 

Decision: The Registrar said that the tablet chairs at the Main Campus are slightly bigger. He said that for Aman Tower they would ask for a bit smaller tablet chair. The Dean & Director said that if the same specs furniture has been used for undergraduate programs at Tabba, Adamjee, Aman-CED, and FCS buildings then the same should be repeated for Aman Tower so that it can easily be replaced if needed.

 

1.7       Point #20 – No steps are required in the classrooms of third and fourth floor and at least one computer lab be provided on each floor.

 

Decision: The Dean & Director said that if there are no steps and there is cost saving he has no objection. Mr. Rehan Shaikh agreed that there would be cost saving. 

 

1.8       Point #21 – High quality modular type furniture to be provided in all four CEE’s classrooms at 3rd and 4th floors similar to existing CEE furniture.

 

Decision:  The Committee agreed that the furniture in all four CEE classrooms should be similar to the existing furniture at CEE.

 

1.9       Point # 22 – Not to give partition between classes at 3rd and 4th floors as it would not serve the purpose.  Mr. Rehan Shaikh said that partitioning between classes would involve additional cost still it would not solve the problem because there would be pillar in between which would hinder the black board’s visibility.

 

Decision: The Dean & Director suggested that all seminar rooms should have a horse-shoe seating arrangement which is necessary for case study discussions. Mr. Rehan Shaikh said that there is a slight curve. However, he said that he would recheck that.

 

1.10     Point # 26 – All floors specially residential area of CEE should be provided with Access System so that irrelevant persons may not trespass.

 

Decision: The Committee did not agree.

 

1.11     Point # 27 – Prayer area, space for indoor games may be created at 11th floor leaving adequate space for holding lunch, dinner etc. 

 

Decision: The Dean & Director said that there should be no enclosure. The space be left open so that it may be used flexibly for different activities such as indoor sports, prayer, or any event.

 

1.12     Point # 28 – Aisle should be provided in the centre of both seminar rooms for unhindered movement of participants.

 

Decision: The Committee decided that as horse-shoe seating arrangement is to be made, the suggestion of providing aisle may be reconsidered.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.13     The Director Projects would take care of the decisions noted above for each point.

 

1.14     The Director Projects would request MJS Foundation for fixing hydraulic barrier at the City Campus as required by the Cantonment Board.

2.7       Mr. Mahmood Ali to a query replied that drawing and BoQ have been approved and NBCL have promised to submit these next week.  He said that their target is to start the project in January 2014 and it would take six months time to complete. About the facade of the multipurpose building Mr. Mahmood Ali said that NBCL would cover the ACs in fencing and it would not be visible.  The Registrar said that Silbatta would be more suitable for the multipurpose building.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that overall silbatta would not match with the building.  The Dean & Director asked if we use silbatta only what would be the cost.  Mr. Ali replied that if we use silbatta around 30% of the cost would be reduced. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Mahmood Ali to ask NBCL to give a coloured picture with grooves and without grooves (silbatta) of the MP Building attaching the pictures of admin building and CED building and present it to the EC. If it is woven with the existing building we will be able to save some money.

2.8       The Committee decided that the lump sum fee proposed by NBCL is too high. The Director Projects would convince NBCL to charge a fee that should not be more than 4 or 5 percent of the estimated cost of these projects mentioned in para 2.2 above.

 

2.9       The Director Projects would ask NBCL to give a coloured drawing with grooves and without grooves (silbatta) of the MP Building attaching the drawing with the picture of admin building and CED building and present it to the EC.

 

Item No.3       To consider and approve the top supervision fee for extended period of electrical infrastructure for M/s. KP Associates.

 

3.1       Mr. Mahmood Ali reported that the Central Purchase Committee and the Executive Committee approved the extension of the Project’s contractor for 8.5 months from June 14, 2013 in their meetings held on October 26, 2013 and September 30, 2013 respectively. The time for completion of works has also been extended up till February 28, 2014 for Contractor. Therefore the services for detailed supervision of designer of the project will also be required up till the completion date.

 

3.2       He said that the Services of M/s KP Associates also have to be extended along with contractor’s extension and the remuneration for additional months put in by M/s. KP Associates will have to be provided. In this regard the details of fee are as under:

 

As per the contract per month remuneration of Project Management Firm is    Rs.274,972/- Therefore the total Fee up till Extended Period (8.5 months) =   8.5 X Rs.274,972 = Rs.2,337,262/-

 

3.3       The Committee was informed that a meeting was held by the Dean & Director on 3rd October 2013 with Mr Khalid Pervez, Managing Director of KP Associates. In response to the Dean and Director’s advice that they were not doing any work during the months the NESPAK and KP Associates were having differences on the detailed design, they ought to reduce the fee proportionately, Mr. Khalid Pervez offered a discount of 30% of their total fee. After discount the total fee shall be Rs.1,636,433/- The per month fee shall be Rs.192,521/-

 

3.4       Mr. Mahmood Ali informed the members that the Central Purchase Committee in its meeting held on November 16, 2013 re-scrutinized the case and after finding it in order recommended it to the Executive Committee for consideration and approval.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the Project Management Fee of Rs.192,521/- per month till February 28, 2014. The total fee shall be Rs.1,636,433/-  He clarified that this cost is only for the extended period other terms and conditions of the contract will remain unchanged.

 

3.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan wanted to know if the changes carried out were of technical nature.  Mr. Mahmood Ali replied that the changes were technical in nature and were resolved in three or four meetings.  Mr. Moeid further said that the design prepared by KP Associates was challenged by Nespak. In such case he said that we should not pay the fee for the extended period. The Dean & Director said that the Project Manager (Nespak) who is working on IBA’s behalf said that the design of KP Associates was not acceptable to them. However, KP Associates said that they would implement their own design and not the design proposed by NESPAK.  Later KP Associates convinced Nespak that their (KPA’s) design was the best.  

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee approved the Project Management Fee @ Rs.192,521/- per month for 8.5 months i.e. till February 28, 2014. The total fee shall be Rs.1,636,433/-

 

Item No.4       To consider and approve the revised Master Plan of the City Campus prepared by M/s. Icon.

 

4.1       Mr. Noshad of Icon said that the Master Plan of City Campus was presented by them in the 222nd Executive Meeting held on October 8, 2012.  The revised plan includes the comments and observations as pointed out by the Executive Committee in that meeting.  He said that the revised plan also includes a proposal for additional parking space as suggested by the Dean & Director IBA. He said that the existing arrangement has a parking capacity of around 65 to 70 vehicles whereas the revised proposal provides space for parking of 88 to 90 vehicles. He said that the total parking capacity at the City Campus will be for 160 cars (80 cars at parking lot, 10 in FCS, 70 in Aman Tower & Auditorium).  He said that another proposal is of Parking Plaza through which the parking space can be enhanced to 237 cars and 25 motor bikes. The engineering estimate for this proposal is Rs.45 million but it was not considered viable as increasing space for only a few additional vehicles would require around Rs.45 million. 

 

4.2       Mr. Noshad said that they would also improve the landscape area. The courtyard between Aman Tower and Academic Block will be paved with benches under tree shades. The green area and the existing palm tree will remain there in the courtyard in front of FCS with well planned interactive seating arrangement. The facade of store and temporary structure will be up-lifted and converted into clinic/first aid room, and maintenance room. 

 

4.3       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Rehan Shaikh if there is any lounge in the Aman Tower for the students as it is in the Students’ Centre.  Mr. Shaikh replied that there is no lounge. It was suggested that some space should be found for students’ sitting areas from the existing spaces without any additional modifications or expenditures.

 

4.4       The Registrar suggested that at Aman Tower parking should be released much before the commissioning of the Tower.  The Dean & Director said that equitable space for parking should be available at both the campuses.

 

4.5       Mr. Noshad presented activity-wise engineering estimate the grand total of which was Rs.54.46 million. The break down is as follows:

 

·         Rs.16 million for the carpeted road.

·         Rs.17.73 million for external and parking lighting systems.

·         Rs.5.6 million for rain and storm water drain.

 

4.6       The Dean & Director suggested that external lighting design may be prepared but the work will be done when we get money. He said that we should use LED for lighting. The Registrar said that in the existing structure there are eight lighting points which can be used. The fixtures can be changed but no additional work is to be done on external lighting.  The Dean & Director said that Rs.6 million for rain and storm water drain appears excessive. Mr. Shaikh said that there does not exist any rain and storm water drain system in the Campus. He said that with rain water drain system, the life of the road would be increased.  The Registrar said that so far he has never seen City Campus flooded with rain water. He said that there is no need to make a proper water drain system; only a pit at the FCS block would be enough.

 

4.7       The Dean & Director said that courtyard, parking, external roads these are necessary for the students but the cost for road carpeting Rs.16 million should be cut down to a more reasonable and affordable estimate. He suggested to make pedestrian pavement and if needed in emergency vehicles can pass through it. Mr. Noshad said that he would redo the costing for road. The Dean & Director suggested to use existing infrastructure for lighting to the maximum extent. 

 

4.8       The Registrar said that the existing cafeteria shed can be used at the cricket ground at Main Campus as pavilion area.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that it can be done.

 

4.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that in the recent rain, water entered into FCS Building.  He suggested that it should be looked into.  The Dean & Director said that for protecting FCS storm water drainage is needed.

 

DECISION

 

4.10     The Committee approved the revised Master Plan of the City Campus along with the comments / observations noted above.

 

4.11     The estimates for carpeted road, external and parking lighting system, and rain and storm water drain need to be revised.

 

ACTION

 

4.12     The Director Projects would ask M/s. Icon to redo the design and engineering estimate of the three activities; Road carpeting, External and Parking Lighting Systems, and Rain and Storm Water Drain in light of the suggestions noted above.          

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
