MINUTES OF THE 117TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, AUGUST 23, 2010

 

The 117th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 23, 2010 at 9:00 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.  Dr. Ishrat Husain                                    Director

2.  Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean

3.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean

4.  Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

5.  Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

6.  Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

7.  Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                   Director ICT

8.  Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                               Director CED

9.  Mr. Shameem Ahmed                               Director HEC Projects (by special invitation)

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No. 1      To consider and grant principle approval of Data Centre Procurement process.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that in its meetings held on July 5 and July 12, 2010 the Executive Committee approved to relocate data centre to Multipurpose building ground floor after coordination with ICT department.  He said that after going through the complete requirement of ICT department for data centre the consultant M/s. ICON was asked to design the data centre. The detail drawing & Bill of Quantity were prepared by the Architect M/s. ICON and these were sent to ICT department on August 13, 2010 for their review and approval. The Engineering Estimate prepared by Architect is of Rs. 4.5 Million (Excluding furniture) for all the civil works of Data centre at ground floor of Multipurpose Building.

 

1.2       The Director Projects said that the work cannot be given to the same contractor who has completed the work of first floor and ground floor of the building and as per PPRA rules the tenders will have to be floated to the pre-qualified contractors for the small civil works up to 10 million. He requested the Executive Committee to approve in principle to issue the tenders and to approve the Architect M/s. ICON and Project Manager M/s. INTEG for this project as both were involved in construction of ground floor. The proposal for their fee will be obtained from them which will be on lump sum basis.

 

1.3       The Director said that the engineering estimate of Rs.4.5 million is exorbitant to the needs of the Data Centre and includes other superfluous items.  He said that only the Data Centre requirements should be fulfilled and that he would not like to spend so much money on other ancillary work.  He asked the Director Projects and the Director ICT to decide what are essentially required for the Data Centre only and present the final recommendation.

 

DECISION

1.4       The Committee decided that the Director Projects and the Director ICT would decide what are essentially required for the Data Centre and present their recommendation to the Director for his approval.

 

           

Item No. 2      To consider and approve location of two new KESC sub-stations at the Main Campus.

 

2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects stated that they had applied to KESC for the following new power connections:

 

a)      Main Campus (existing buildings             2,500 kW

         and the buildings coming up in               (an extension from the

         the next 2 to 3 years)                          present 400 kW to 2500 kW)

 

b)      Staff Colony                                             660 kW

c)      Boys’ Hostel                                             750 kW

 

2.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that KESC has already carried out the required survey of the demands which are at the approval stage at KESC Head Office. He said that it may take 6 to 12 months for actual connections to be made available to IBA.

 

2.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed further reported that for providing the Main Campus High Tension connection KESC has advised that the existing sub-station would serve the purpose. However, for the Staff Colony and Boys’ Hostel High Tension connections KESC’s requirement is that IBA should build and provide to KESC two new sub-stations at the two premises. He said that the possible locations of the two required sub-stations were discussed with the KESC surveyor during the survey.  He said that the recommended location in the Staff Colony is almost in front of the University sub-station No.5; just across the road which may facilitate cable laying for new connection.

 

2.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the ideal location for building a sub-station at the Boys’ Hostel premises would be the north-east corner because of its closeness to the University’s sub-station No.4.  However, he said that it was not desirable as it would mar the architectural look of the present as well as the New Boys’ Hostel.

 

2.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to consider and, if found OK, approve the two recommended locations which he presented in the site plans.

 

2.7       The Director said that there should be three parameters; minimum expenditure, convenience (from users point of view), and technical soundness.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee approved the recommended locations of sub-station for Staff Colony i.e. in front of the University sub-station No.5, and North East Corner of the Boys’ Hostel for Boys’ Hostel.

 

Item No. 3      Site clearance at the Staff Colony.

 

3.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that the IBA’s plans for expanding physical facilities include the construction of the following at the Staff Colony:

 

a)       Large underground and over-head water tanks and a pump room as a part of long-term water supply system.

b)      New Girls’ Hostel: Designing work in progress.

c)      A new KESC sub-station

 

3.2       Mr. Shameem reported that the sites of the above projects are presently covered by thick and densely populated wild bushes, plants and trees. He suggested that since the rainy reason is almost over, they should have the site cleared of the wild bushes so that it could be made available for starting the construction work. He informed the Committee that an enquiry letter has been issued to 18 pre-qualified petty contractors inviting them to submit their quotations on the basis of a lump sum amount for the work. He also presented the scope of work which included posting of at least five labourers with strong clubs in their hands to hunt and kill the snakes and other reptiles, if any, before they reach the residential area.

 

3.3       The Director said that the people living in the Staff town should be safe and secured from the snakes and harmful reptiles that may disperse towards Staff Town as a result of uprooting of the bushes. 

 

3.4       The Registrar suggested to dig a pit of 2 to 3 feet in order to ward off the snakes and other reptiles from the Staff Town.  He also suggested that the site should be cleaned when the project is handed over to the Contractor.

 

3.5       The Director Projects suggested that old used GI sheets can be embedded into the ground and supported by the steel pipes in order to stop snakes and other reptiles move towards staff town.

 

3.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that precautionary measures have been taken, as a part of the scope of the work, to prevent the snakes and other reptiles, if any, from reaching the residential area.

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee noted and approved the site clearance of the Staff Colony and also the preventive and precautionary measures planned.  The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed to implement the measures effectively.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve Contribution to Flood Relief Fund.

 

4.1     The Director Finance reported that they have received a letter from Governor House for contribution by the employees of IBA for flood victims.  As per the directives, the deduction should be:

a)      Employees in BPS-1 to 16            One day pay

b)      Employees in BPS-17 to 22          Five days’ pay

 

4.2       The Director Finance stated that the said notification was brought to the notice of the Executive Committee in its last meeting and the EC had instructed to find out the implementations in NED and Karachi University.  The findings were as under:

 

                                                                     NED                   Karachi Univ.

a)      Employees under Govt. Pay Scale            Basic Pay             Gross Pay

b)      Employees on contractual basis              No deduction         No deduction

 

4.3       The Director Finance said that prior to this notification they had taken option from IBA employees and a number of them opted not to contribute.  He sought advice from the EC on deduction for contract based employees, and deduction for Option ‘B’ employees and from those who opted not to contribute.

 

4.4       Some members of the EC reported that they have already donated to the organizations who they thought were trustworthy.

 

4.5       The Director said that this is an institutional effort and the staff should contribute to this fund irrespective of their status whether they are contractual employees or option ‘B’ employees.

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee decided that the employees in BPS-1 to 16 or equivalent grade (contractual employees) would contribute one day’s pay and the employees in BPS-17 and above or equivalent grades (contractual employees) would contribute 5 days’ pay to the Flood Relief Fund.

 

4.7       The Committee further decided that the Finance Department would make contribution of five days’ pay of BPS-17 and above or equivalent grade employees from the IBA fund and recover the amount from the employees over five months period @ one day’s pay per month.

 

  

Item No. 5      Construction of Offices for IBA Consultants and Project Managers.

 

5.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects reported that as per terms and conditions of their contract agreements with NESPAK and Consult-Tech International, IBA is to provide reasonably furnished office for their resident engineers.  He said that offices would also be required for other project manager(s) whose selection and appointment is in process.  He stated that in anticipation of this need they budgeted an estimated cost of Rs.6 million, about 90% of which can be invested in the civil work and the rest on a few computers, some modest quality office furniture and other essential items.

 

5.2       Mr. Shameem said that for the next 2 to 3 years they would need and use the building for providing the required office accommodation to IBA’s consultants and project managers. The budgeted cost is enough for construction of the facility having a covered area of 2000 to 2500 sq.ft. on the ground floor. However, it may be designed in such a way as to make it useful for some other purposes as well after all projects related work has been completed and the Projects Department wound up. He suggested that the space can be used as a central record room for storing hard copies / files, documents etc., which are required under law to be preserved for a specified number of years. The space can also be used as a surplus store of discarded, unserviceable goods pending their final disposal or it may be used as a central transit warehouse.  He presented the proposed location for construction of the building and requested the EC to approve the proposed location or give another decision in this regard.   

 

5.3       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to how the proposed building would fit into the master plan and how its facade would be.  He suggested to arrange offices in the Staff Town or in the Hostel rather going for construction of another building.

 

5.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that there is no master plan of Staff Town. He said that M/s. Icon had prepared the master plan of the existing Staff Town only.

 

DECISION

 

5.5       The Committee decided that as the Girls’ Hostel will be ready by September, 2010, and the girls living in the VPR will move to the Girls’ Hostel, offices for Consultants and Project Managers can be arranged in the VPR.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
