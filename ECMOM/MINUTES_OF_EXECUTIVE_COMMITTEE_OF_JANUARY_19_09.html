The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider and approve the IBA Strategy Map.

Item No. 3      Selection of ERP Vendor.

3.1       The email from the Director I.T. regarding selection of ERP Vendor was discussed. It was mentioned in the email that the IBA published pre-qualification of Contractors for ERP Project including Campus Management, HR & Financials on July 23, 2008. In response to this 22 vendors applied.  The minimum criteria for the vendors as per the advertisement included that:

i)                    They must have been in similar business for the last three years

ii)                  They must have completed at least two successful contracts involving supply, installation and maintenance support for Campus Management, HR and Financials in any organization during the last three years,

iii)                The must have proven experience in providing ERP support services for at least three years.

3.2       After evaluating each and every application following three firms were short-listed by the Director I.T.

i)      M/s. Techlogix

ii)      M/s. Siemens

iii)     M/s. Synergy Computers

3.3       The short listing was done on the basis of the following:

i)                    PeopleSoft (Implementing Company is Techlogix, PeopleSoft campus solutions is one of the best solutions and is being used by over 200 universities all over the world).  HEC also selected PeopleSoft.

ii)                  SAP (Implementing Company is Siemens). Siemens have completed several ERP based projects in Pakistan and it is providing complete ERP Solution.

iii)                Banner (Implementing Company is Synergy). Banner solution also used in several universities. However, they have not done any implementation in Pakistan.

3.4       The Director I.T. requested the Committee to approve the short-listing of ERP Vendors.

DECISION

3.5       The Committee approved the short-listing of ERP Vendors as suggested by the Director I.T.

3.6              The Committee further decided that the short-listing of ERP Vendors will be reported to the A&FC.

Item No. 4      To consider email from Dr. Zeenat Ismail regarding policy on external paid assignments during office hours of faculty / IBA staff.

4.1              Dr. Zeenat Ismail in her email of December 31, 2008 wanted to know if a full time faculty member is allowed to do paid private practice during or even after office hours.  She requested that the policy on this should be notified. 

DECISION

4.2              The Director clarified that as far as consultancy services are concerned, teachers after fulfilling their obligations to IBA in terms of teaching, research, committee assignments, and counselling can undertake consultancy services but they will have to pay 30% of the consultancy fee to IBA in accordance with the decision of the Board of Governors of IBA.

4.3              Cases of external paid assignments for faculty would be decided on case to case basis.

 

Item No. 5      To consider note from the Registrar regarding Scholarship to students having CGPA below 2.8.

5.1              The Registrar in his note of January 17, 2009 stated that the Scholarship Committee is facing a dilemma regarding eleven students who were also awarded scholarship but now their GPAs have fallen below the required 2.8 CGPA. He stated that the need level though demands the continuity of financial assistance, yet their performance is unsatisfactory. 

5.2              The Dean & Director said that the scholarship is on the basis of need so that a needy student coming to IBA could continue his education. He said that for merit-based scholarships we had decided that below 3.5 GPA we will not give scholarship. He said that we cannot apply the criteria of need-based scholarship on merit based scholarship though such applicants should be encouraged to keep improving their CGPAs. He said that the need based scholarship would be stopped only when a student is dropped.   

DECISION

5.3              The Committee decided to rename the Scholarship as Need-Based Scholarship and it would continue to be paid as long as a student is not dropped due to low GPA.

5.4              The Committee further decided that the Financial Aid Officer should write to the students whose GPA goes down to improve thier GPA.

ACTION

5.5              The Scholarship committee would rename the scholarship as “Need-based Scholarship”.

5.6              The Financial Aid Officer would write to the students whose GPA goes down for improvement of GPA.

 

Item No. 6      To consider note from Mr. Muhammad Asif, faculty member, on the application of Mr. Faizul Hasan Farooqui relating to low GPA.

6.1              Mr. Muhammad Asif in his email stated that the admission in IBA of Mr. Faizul Hasan Farooqi is at stake due to short fall of 0.025GPA in BBA final results. Mr. Farooqui did his BBA from COMMECS with a CGPA of 2.47. Mr. Asif stated that he is mentor of Mr. Faizul Hasan Farooqui who approached him to bring his case for consideration.

6.2       Mr. Asif stated that we give admission on 2.50 GPA to the students of the institutes affiliated with the IBA which corresponds to 80% marks. For those who are from CBM, this corresponds to 64% marks. For Bahria University students, this corresponds to around 66% marks. He suggested to change our admission criteria and to fix percentage as benchmark rather than GPA because of different versions of GPA in different universities. According to him we are admitting students of average calibre (64%) from CBM but refusing those of high calibre (79%) from other universities).

6.3       Mr. Farooqui stated that according to his understanding IBA enrolled BBA students are exempted from MBA entrance test and 2.5 CGPA criteria. He requested to consider his case on same footings though he had gone through all the admission test procedures again for MBA admission.

6.4       The Controller of Examinations clarified that we have control over 40% marks only. The rest is graded by the affiliated institutes over which the IBA has no control.  

6.5       The Dean & Director said that we cannot follow other institutions and do not want to know what their courses are and what their grading plans are.  

DECISION

6.6       The Committee did not approve the request of Mr. Faizul Hasan Farooqi.   

ACTION

6.7              The Registrar would convey the decision to Mr. Faizul Hasan Farooqi and to Mr. Muhammad Asif, faculty member. He would also inform the concerned program office about the decision.

Item No. 7      To consider email from Ms. Bushra Haqqi, a student of MBA(MIS) regarding recognition of non-credit courses taken as electives.

7.1       Ms. Bushra Haqqi, a student of MBA(MIS) stated in her email to the Executive Committee that many of her classmates and she herself  had taken non-credit courses as electives in the four-year BBA (MIS) Programme. She said that the non-credit courses were removed from the list of electives stated in respective profiles in the Graduate Directory for the Year 2007. Neither were they mentioned on the transcripts.

7.2       She stated that she took Advertising as a non-credit course in the Spring Semester 2007 and obtained 90% marks in it securing a grade of A-. Although Advertising was a non-credit course, she attended all the classes and appeared in all three hourly examinations.

7.3       She said that as Advertising is an important elective and she had worked hard throughout the semester for it, so she requested that it be given recognition in the transcript as well as in the Graduate Directory 2009. She further stated that the Graduate Directories printed prior to and in the Year 2006 had all the electives including the non-credit courses mentioned in the profiles of students.

DECISION

7.4              The Committee allowed to include non-credit courses in the Graduate Directory as well as Transcripts.

ACTION

7.5              The Registrar would inform the Program Offices, Examination Department, Placement Office and the concerned students about inclusion of non-credit courses in the Graduate Directory and Transcripts.

Item No. 8      To consider email from students of MBA(MIS) IV regarding payment of additional fee for Fall Semester 2007.

8.1       Four of the students of MBA(MIS) IV stated in their email that on January 14, 2009, they received a letter dated December 31, 2008 from the Director Finance. In that letter each of them was required to pay Rs.44,673/-  more for the first semester of the MBA(MIS), Fall Semester 2007, which they spent on the 5-month projects. 

8.2       They stated that the previous Dean and Director, Mr. Danishmand, had announced that all BBA students coming back for the MBA were to receive a concession in fee if their CGPAs were in the top 50% of their batch. This promulgated policy was followed for the first batch of students who had graduated from a 4-year BBA in 2006. The students had received an official notice from the then Alumni Affairs and Placement Manager, Mr. Imran-ul-Haq, under the directives of Mr. Danishmand confirming that those whose CGPAs of BBA were among the top 50% were qualified to pay concessional fee. Accordingly, the returnees from the BBA batch of 2006, including those from BBA-MIS, who were qualified for the concession paid concessional fee for all four semesters of their MBA.

8.3       These applicants also stated that the CCS Programme Office called them to collect concessional fee vouchers and informed them that they had to pay Rs.30,963/- for the first semester and Rs.33,709/- for the second semester of MBA-MIS. The Placement Office also confirmed that the amount of fee required to be paid for Fall Semester 2007 was Rs.30,963/-. Accordingly, they all paid the concessional fee of Rs.30,963/- for the first semester.

8.4       They further stated that although the letter states that they were given several reminders regarding the payment of additional fee, the letter itself was the first notification that they received of this payment. They stated that if they were to pay the additional Rs.44,673/- for the Fall Semester 2007, they would be paying above the concessional rate they were awarded due to their high academic performance in BBA(MIS). They further stated that the MBA-MIS Programme is and has always been subsidized. Their MBA-MIS counterparts who have not done their bachelors from IBA, have paid the concessional fee for all four semesters of their MBA-MIS.

8.5              The Director Finance stated that when they collect fees from the students, their ranking is not determined due to non-availability of results of some of the courses.

8.6              A member wanted to know the time limit within which an IBA’s BBA graduate can join MBA program.

DECISION

8.7       The Committee decided that if the results are not announced by the time the fees are collected, students may be allowed to pay the fees in two instalments and if they qualify for concessional fee, the fee should be adjusted.

8.8       The Committee requested the Director Finance to check if they are eligible for 50% concessional fee on the basis of their ranking and charge the fee accordingly.

8.9              Regarding time limit i.e. years of experience after which an IBA graduate can join MBA program, the Committee referred this issue to the Admission Committee to recommend the time limit to the Academic Board.

ACTION

8.10          The Director Finance will check the eligibility of the applicants for the concessional fee and if they are eligible for concessional fee, he would charge accordingly.

8.11          Dr. Muhammad Nishat will discuss in the Admission Committee the issue of maximum length of experience after which an IBA graduate can join MBA program and put the recommendation before the Academic Board for approval.

8.12     The Director H.R. will notify about the formation of Admission Committee.

Item No. 10    Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)         To consider request from Dr. Huma Baqai for permission to work in the media as anchorperson for current affairs show.

10.1     Dr. Huma Naz Baqai, requested for permission to work in the media as an anchorperson for current affairs show.  She assured that her commitment in the media would not in any way hamper her performance at IBA.  She also stated that she teaches Media Studies and Speech Communication at IBA. Her practical experience with the media compliments her teaching.

10.2          The Director said that she told him that at the time of her appointment at IBA she had informed the ex-Director about her activity as anchorperson and he had allowed her to work in the media as anchorperson. Moreover, she is teaching the course on Media Studies at IBA for which her working as anchorperson would be a plus point.  The Director further said that Dr. Huma Baqai has promised that classes and other assignments would not suffer because of her involvement in the media.

DECISION

10.3     The Committee approved the request of Dr. Huma Baqai to work in the media as an anchorperson provided her classes and other assignments are not disturbed.  

10.4     The Committee further decided that permission to Dr. Huma Baqai will not set a precedent as the ground on which she has been allowed is justified.

ACTION

10.5     The Director H.R. will communicate the decision to Dr. Huma Baqai.

b)         Meeting of the Dean & Director with the Vice Chancellor, Karachi University, and the Members, External Review Panel - Up-date to the E.C.

10.6     The Dean & Director informed the Committee that he had a meeting with the Vice Chancellor, University of Karachi and discussed issues related to title of land, girls’ hostel, independent water sewerage line, dispensary, sports facilities and power supply.

10.7     The Director informed the Committee that the Vice Chancellor, University of Karachi was saying that there is resentment among the staff and faculty in the Karachi University over the possession of land beyond the allotted limit by constructing a boundary wall by the IBA.

10.8     The Director said that he requested the Vice Chancellor to help us in finding out the title of City Campus. He said that the Vice Chancellor very kindly assured that he would try to find out.

10.9     The Registrar said that the boundary wall of IBA was constructed in mid 80s’ when the IBA was a part of the University of Karachi so we have not illegally grabbed the land.

10.10   The Director said that he discussed about an NOC for an independent water and sewerage line. The Vice Chancellor informed him that they have prepared a Master Plan for Water Sewerage line and he advised to contact the person of the Karachi University who has prepared the Master Plan for water and sewerage line.

10.11   The Director said that the University of Karachi is constructing a Girls’ Hostel for M.Phil and Ph.D students but that would be ready in next 5 to 6 months only then they would be able to know how much space could be spared for the girls students of IBA. Till such time we will have to wait.  He said that we have to announce in the Program Announcement as well as in the Open House sessions that girls’ hostel facilities for 25 students are available. We can rely on the Karachi University’s Girls Hostel for Fall semester 2009.

10.12   The Director said that the Vice Chancellor has allowed IBA to use their ground for sports activities. However, they will have to be informed in advance if we arrange any tournament. 

10.13   Regarding power supply by KESC, the Director informed the Committee that the Karachi University along with NED is planning to have their own power generation plant. He said that the IBA can also participate on cost sharing basis. He said that the HEJ Institute of Chemistry has already started this. Their cost of power has come down very significantly.

10.14   The Director informed the Committee that he met Dr. Salman Shah and Dr. Syed Zahoor Hasan who advised him to do two or three things immediately. One is replacement of furniture of classrooms. (The Director said that he has seen the HEC project which was approved in 2004-05 to replace the furniture but nothing has been done so far. There is a balance in that head also. Now we have to ask for extension as it was 12 months’ project).

10.15   The Director Projects said that the Committee constituted by the Executive Committee has selected two or three designs of chairs.  He requested to allow five months time for replacement of the furniture.

10.16   The Director said that another observation from Dr. Salman Shah and Dr. Zahoor was that the IBA is not doing enough for the existing faculty.  The existing faculty should be sent for short courses or to institutions where case studies are prepared and also used as a teaching technique. He requested the Director H.R. to design some programs for young faculty in consultation with Associate Deans.

10.17   The Director said that Dr. Zahoor Hasan and Dr. Salman Shah observed that some fresh IBA graduates are on the faculty. They advised that instead of fresh graduates, persons with three to four years experience be preferred. They have emphasized to improve faculty.

10.18   The Director said that they also said that teachers’ work load is heavy at IBA. They said that there are so many product offerings due to which course load has increased. He said that there is a need to rationalize the number of product offerings. He requested the Associate Deans to consider offering general BBA and MBA with concentration on MIS in the final two semesters by choosing elective courses.

DECISIONS

10.19   The Committee requested the General Manager (P&D) to contact the persons at the Karachi University who has prepared Master Plan for Water & Sewerage line and find out the solution of IBA’s independent water and sewerage line.

10.20   The Director Projects was requested to visit HEJ along with his Electrical Engineer to see their power generation system and to obtain relevant details including cost.  He was also advised to make a comparison of Co-generation and Karachi University’s joint system of power generation and present it to the A&FC for consideration.

10.21   The Associate Deans would discuss with the faculty and students about offering major in MIS in the last two semesters instead of offering BS, BBA(MIS) and MBA(MIS) and present their proposal to the EC.

10.22   The Director H.R. will provide training for structured interview to faculty for interviewing students who clear the admission test.  She would schedule the training in March.  The Director H.R. would also design some programs for young faculty in consultation with Associate Deans

ACTIONS

10.23   Mr. Shameem Ahmed will contact the person at Karachi University who has prepared Master Plan for Water & Sewerage Lines.

10.24   The Director Projects would present a comparison after studying the HEJ, Karachi University’s joint system of power generation and the co-generation.

10.25   The Associate Deans would present a proposal to the EC on offering major in MIS in the last two semesters instead of offering BS, BBA(MIS) and MBA(MIS) and present their proposal to the EC.

10.26   The Director H.R. will provide training to faculty for structured interview in March 2009.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
