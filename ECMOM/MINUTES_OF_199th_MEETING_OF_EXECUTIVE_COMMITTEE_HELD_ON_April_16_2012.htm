Mr. Abdul Rahman, and Mr. Mehmood Bughio, Project Engineers attended the meeting on special meeting as Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia had to go to the physician in emergency.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve 60% payment to HK Shah for Consultancy.

 

1.1       The Director ICT said that in the previous EC meeting held on April 2, 2012, a request for the payment of H. K Shah’s Consultancy and Project Management Fee was made. According to the agreement, M/s H.K Shah has submitted the invoice of Rs.825,000/- being the 60% of the total project fee of Rs.13,75,000 which is 2.5% Consultancy fee of Rs.55 million.

 

1.2       However, The Director ICT said that the EC had recommended that since the 2.5% was agreed upon the estimated cost of Rs.20 million, and now the expected cost is Rs.55.83 million, therefore, the Consultancy Fee should be renegotiated. The Director ICT reported that he tried to renegotiate however, the contention of M/s. H.K. Shah is that the estimate of Rs.20 million was for a non-Tier III data center and there is a lot more work involved in a Tier III Data Center and the 2.5% fee is not even covering their charges. However since they want to work for IBA they would still complete the project even at loss with 2.5% Consultancy Charges. 

 

1.3       The Director ICT requested the EC to approve the release of Rs.825,000/- (Rupees Eight Hundred and Twenty Five Thousand) payment as the 60% of the total project fee assuming that the project cost will be Rs.55.83 million.

 

1.4       The Director Projects said that the A&FC has decided that the project cost be capped to the tune of plus minus 10% so that the fee is paid as percentage of that value.  He suggested that in this case also the fee should be frozen at the project cost of Rs.55.83 million.

 

1.5       The Director ICT said that he would convey the decision of the A&FC to M/s H.K. Shah and ensure compliance.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee approved the payment of Rs.825,000/- being 60% of the total consultancy fee to M/s. H.K. Shah.

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Director ICT would ensure that the consultancy fee is paid at the capped price of the project which is Rs.55.83 million.  

 

 

Item No.2       Progress Report on Data Centre.

 

2.1       The Director ICT reported that in compliance with the directive of the A&FC he has asked H.K. Shah to give a report on the following:

 

o   Segregation of dollar based items and rupees based items.

o   The calculation of imported item and their calculations as done by HK Shah to arrive at Rs.55.83 million and what AMFCO has done.

2.3       The Director ICT said that the project cannot be completed by June 30, 2012 and it would get delayed.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee noted the up-date on the Data Centre.

 

           

Item No.3       Presentations by the Architects M/s. NBCL on:

 

a)                  The designing and fabrication of the furniture for Aman CED Building.

 

3.1       Mr. Ahsan Najmi, representative of M/s. NBCL said that M/s Habib & Sons were given the contract by IBA for supplying furniture for classrooms, offices, and seminar rooms. He said that they (NBCL) were to approve the shop drawing as a Consultant.  He reported that the first submittal of the shop drawing by M/s Habib & Sons was inadequate and therefore, it was rejected. Later they submitted the second shop drawing which was relatively better in certain items but there were still some ambiguity. He said that they had provided the observations to Habib & Son item wise.

 

3.2       Mr. Aqeel Bilgrami said that there are a lot of mock ups which have not yet been produced. He said that Habib & Sons have submitted the revised shop drawings two days ago which they would review and give approval in batches so that the work goes on without break. Even then he said that the work will be delayed.

 

3.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know as why Habib & Sons were recommended if they are not expert enough or fully equipped.

 

3.4       Mr. Ahsan Najmi said that Habib & Sons’ excellence is in steel furniture.  They have latest equipments too but innovativeness is missing. He said that in his opinion they never prepared the shop drawings.  As far as on time delivery is concerned, they had agreed that they would follow the schedule but they have missed all deadlines due to revision in shop drawings. 

 

3.5       Mr. Aqeel Bilgrami said that if they prepare their mock up according to our requirement then it would not take time.

 

3.6       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that according to schedule, the ground floor of the Aman CED building was to be completed by mid of April 2012 which got delayed.  He wanted to know as to how much further delay there would be.

 

3.7       Mr. Ahsan Najmi said that at the moment they cannot give definite date. However, next week they would have clear picture as to when this would be completed and how much delay there would be.    

 

3.8       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui appreciated that NBCL is trying to get the work completed on time at the same time he requested Mr. Aqeel Bilgrami to put in a lot more efforts so that the job is completed as soon as possible.

 

DECISION

 

3.9       The Committee noted the update on the designing and fabrication of the furniture for Aman CED Building.  

 

ACTION

 

3.10     M/s. NBCL would inform the EC about the completion date and the exact number of days for which the project would be delayed because of non-delivery of furniture by M/s. Habib Sons.

 

 

b)                 Interior concept and schematic drawing of the Razzak Tabba Academic Building (new Academic Block) at Main Campus.

 

3.11     Mr. Ahsan presented the interior schematic design and drawing of the Razzak Tabba Academic Building at Main Campus.  He said that they simplified the design in order to make it cost effective.

Item No.4      Presentation on the progress status of Aman CED Building by Project Managers M/s. Halcrow.

 

4.1       Mr. Aamir Shabbir of Halcrow gave a presentation on the progress made so far towards the construction of Aman CED Building, the delays and their causes, measures adopted for coping with the delays, and the revised targets.  He said that as per schedule the ground floor, first floor, and the second floor of Aman CED Building were to be completed in mid of April, May, and June 2012 respectively.  However, because of certain reasons which included the law and order situation in the city, delay in procurement of electrical related items, etc. the work got delayed.  He said that now the ground floor would be completed in end of April 2012. Similarly, the first and second floors would be completed towards the end of May and June 2012 respectively. He said that the inspection and testing of lift generator has already been done and it would be made available by April 20, 2012.

 

4.2       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know the status of IT room.  To this Mr. Aamir confirmed that IT room of first floor has already been given to ICT department.

 

4.3       The Registrar wanted to know as to what has been done towards external development work around the Aman CED Building.  Further, he said that there is a forecast that the first monsoon rain will start from May 7, 2012 so he suggested that the on-going work on the roof top should be completed before that.

 

4.4       Mr. Aamir said that the external work around the building is in progress and this would also be completed by April 30, 2012.  He also confirmed that the electrical and air-conditioning work on the roof top would also be completed by April 30, 2012.

 

4.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui requested Mr. Aamir Shabbir that they should stick to the revised deadlines. 

 

DECISION

 

4.6       The Committee noted the progress up-date on the Aman CED Building and approved the revised deadline which is as under:

 

·         Completion of Ground Floor        -           April 30, 2012 (including pedestrian path

way access to the building and work on the

roof top).

·         Completion of First Floor             -          May 30, 2012

·         Completion of Second Floor        -          June 30, 2012

           

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

            a)         Urgent AC requirement for Library.

 

5.1       The Registrar said that IBA always provides an ideal academic environment to their customer i.e. student to enhance their educational activities. In view of architectural deficiency of proper air ventilation and air circulation inside library, mechanical cooling inside library is required to keep inside atmosphere at standard level defined for library i.e. Temperature 22 C – 25 C , Humidity 45 % - 65 %.  

 

5.2       The Registrar reported that at the request of Mr. Anwar (Head Librarian & Incharge Research Data Centre), cooling load estimation was carried out by the Operation and Maintenance Department and after calculation it was concluded that 12 USRT is required to keep the atmospheric parameters at comfort zone. Currently, three old split ACs of capacity 1.5 USRT each have been installed at Library, but they are not enough to overcome the required cooling demand. Compressors of these installed ACs are also running continuously without any rest time, which is again not recommended.

 

5.3       The Registrar said that the existing (temporary) library at Main Campus will be operative at-least for further 1½ years, so installation of 3 ACs of 2 USRT each is necessary because of the warm / humid atmosphere and the safety of existing air-conditioning units installed at Library. He requested the EC to approve procurement and installation of another 3 split ACs of 2 USRT capacity each.  

 

DECISION

 

5.4       The Committee approved the procurement and installation of three split ACs of 2 USRT capacity each for the (Temporary) Library at the Main Campus.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
