1.2       Library: The Director Projects reported that almost all the work of Library is complete except the testing and commissioning of the plant. He said that he has invited the consultant and the supplier to resolve this issue and speed up the work.

 

1.3       NBP Technology Centre (internal Works): The Dean & Director suggested that the offices of HR, Internal Audit, EMBA and Programme offices be shifted from NBP Technology Centre to the Fauji Foundation Building so that the NBP Technology Centre internal work could be completed expeditiously. He said that as 1st, 2nd and 3rd floors of Fauji Foundation Building are ready, the offices at NBP Technology Centre could temporarily be accommodated there.  He further suggested that the Finance Department should be moved from Aman-CED Building to Fauji Foundation Building within 15 days.

 

1.4       Monument Garden: The Registrar said that he had seen in such monument gardens abroad that on top of the monument there is a disc for lighting purpose. The Director Projects said that lighting arrangement has been made on the bottom of the pillar.  He said that if there is no proper light upto the top of the pillar then they may consider placing disc. The Registrar said that the light will not remain on everyday; it will be lit only on some events and occasions.  The Dean & Director said that alternatively lightings can be arranged at the monument garden whenever there is any big event.

 

1.5       Internal Roads: The Director Projects said that completion of road upto Fauji Foundation Building will take time for the reason that on the way there is an old underground tank and the security office which have to be demolished.

 

The Dean & Director asked as to when the road from Students Centre, to Aman-CED and NBP Technology Centre would be ready. The Director Projects said that the ground has to be excavated from Students Centre to NBP Technology Centre and then road is to be built as per the approved section.  The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to instruct the contractor to start the work on priority.  He said that he would visit it after a month.  

 

1.6       Water Supply: The Dean & Director asked as to what was the issue in getting connected to the water supply line.  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that the pump room was not ready. The Registrar said that they can temporarily connect the line with the existing underground tank behind the Fauji Foundation Building but it would cost around Rs.150,000/-  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that they have to break the Security offices to get the line connected. He said that the pump will be available in six weeks time after which it will take another 2 to 2.5 months to make it functional.  The Director Finance said that they are spending around Rs.500,000/- per month on water being supplied by tankers. Mr. Mahmood Ali suggested that the tanker can take the water from IBAs overhead tank. In this way there may be some savings on payment for water. The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to talk to the tanker owner if they can fill their tanker from IBAs overhead tank and supply water to IBA so that there may be some saving.

 

1.7       Internal Gas Line (Main Campus):  The Director Projects reported that the estimate from SSGC has been received which is Rs.500,000/-  The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to finalize the payment and get the work started immediately. 

 

1.8       New Boys Hostel (Blocks B & C):  The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to start the tendering process of interior decoration work as well as procurement of furniture parallel to the construction of Boys Hostel in order to timely utilize the Sindh Government Grant. He asked Mr. Jehanzeb and Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects to prepare cash flow forecasts.

 

The Dean & Director said that Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects would be responsible for the new Boys Hostel (Blocks B & C).

 

1.9       Irrigation System:  Mr. Mahmood Ali said that the irrigation system around the sport arena will be functional by January 15, 2015.

 

1.10     Transport Shed (Transport facilities building): The Director Projects said that the design, drawing and estimate for Transport Shed would be submitted by the Architect by December 20, 2014. The Dean & Director said that the requirement for transport shed is very simple and a very sophisticated building is not required for this purpose.

 

1.11     Clinic: The Director Projects said that the design for clinic has been received along with its estimate which is Rs.9 million. He said that it would be taken to the A&FC for consideration and approval. 

 

The Registrar suggested that the Clinic should be shifted behind the Students Centre. He said that there would be some advantages in shifting the clinic such as centralized parking, the space where the clinic was proposed earlier could be used for various purposes.

 

The Dean & Director agreed that the Clinic should be shifted to the vacant plot behind the Students Centre. However, he suggested to convert the space, suggested earlier for the Clinic, into green spot. The Registrar observed that the vehicles could not pass if space was converted into green spot. The Dean & Director after hearing the view points of the members said that he did not want to deviate from the Master Plan. He therefore, requested the Director Projects to discuss it with Mr. Zayd Bilgrami of NBCL. He said that there were two options; either to use this space for parking of vehicles, or to use it for green spot which may also be discussed with Mr. Zayd.

 

DECISION & ACTION

 

1.12     The Committee noted and approved the up-date on the Monthly Progress along with the suggestions noted above.

 

1.13     The Registrar would get the offices of HR, Internal Audit, EMBA and Programme offices temporarily shifted from NBP Technology Centre to the Fauji Foundation Building.

 

1.14     The Director Finance would get the Finance Department shifted from Aman-CED Building to Fauji Foundation Building within 15 days.

 

1.15     The Director Projects would instruct the contractor to start and complete the work for road from Students Centre, to Aman-CED and NBP Technology Centre on priority basis.

 

1.16     The Registrar would talk to the tanker owner if they can fill their tanker with IBAs overhead tank and supply water to IBA at comparatively lesser rate.

 

1.17     Mr. Jehanzeb would work with Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects and prepare cash flow forecasts for the Boys Hostel.

 

1.18     The Director Projects would get the Clinic constructed behind the Students Centre. He would discuss with Mr. Zayed of NBCL as to which option would be suitable; either to use the space (earlier suggested for Clinic) for parking of vehicles, or to use it for green spot.

 

Item No.2       Aman Tower Project - Status Report.

 

2.1       The Dean & Director asked Mr. Najmul Islam of Halcrow about the progress at Aman Tower.  Mr. Islam reported that there has been 98% progress in November 2014 on Civil & Architecture Works Phase I (Core and Shell); Phase-II ID works were 78% completed against the target of 90%.  He said that of the MEP Works of ID Package, 90% HVAC works have been completed.  However, he said that by end of January 2015 it would be 100% complete including its testing and commissioning. He said that there had been only 70% progress in UPS work due to revision by IBA on its rating and numbers but now this issue has been resolved.

 

2.2       Mr. Najmul Islam reported that there has been only 20% progress against the target of 100% in Vertical Transport (Elevators).  The Director Projects said that they were told at the last moment that the Chinese Government had refused visa to the inspection team but now they hope to get the visa in a day or so.  He said that the pre-shipment inspection would be completed by December 15, 2014 and the lifts would arrive by January 15, 2015. Installation and testing of lift would be completed by March 15, 2015.

 

2.3       The Director Projects said that the gen-set contract was in tendering phase and it would take two months time to complete the tendering process and another four months would be required for delivery and installation of gen set. The Dean & Director said that the delay in installation of gen set should not cause any problem in operating the building as the gen set would be placed on the basement of the building.  He suggested that the observations given earlier by A&FC should be fully addressed at the time of the technical evaluation of the bid.

 

2.4       The Dean & Director said that he wanted the Library at APWA building to be shifted to the Aman Tower because the contract with APWA was ending on December 31, 2014.  Mr. Najmul Islam said that there were two items; furniture and carpet flooring; which will be completed by January 15, 2015. The Dean & Director suggested to complete the fixing of furniture and carpet flooring work on priority basis so that the APWA building could be vacated on time.  He asked the Registrar to seek extension for retaining the APWA building upto January 31, 2015. He asked Mr. Shakil Anwer to make sure that the furniture, carpets and shelves are in functioning order by January 31, 2015.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that approval from Cantonment Board for road cutting for power has been obtained on December 4, 2014. He hoped that the power and gas will be available by the end of December 2014.  For water connection they hope to get approval from KW&SB by the end of December 2014.  Mr. Najmul Islam reported that they are exploring the possibility of connecting sewage line on the manhole already built for JS Auditorium. Thus they will not have to do road cutting. The Committee appreciated the alternate arrangement suggested by Mr. Najmul Islam.

 

2.6       Mr. Najmul Islam said that optic fibre and ICT connectivity to the building was being provided in coordination with Mr. Mansoor of ICT.  The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shakil Anwer to coordinate with ICT staff and get the fibre, ICT and internet connectivity completed by January 31, 2015.

 

2.7       Mr. Najmul Islam said that in the residential block of Aman Tower, they were asked to change the AC system from chiller to Split AC.  However, he said that if they go for split AC, the whole system will have to be changed and it would take another two months and would also entail additional cost. The Dean & Director suggested to see if there could be any alternate of not using the chiller when the air-conditioning was required for only a few rooms. However, he made it clear that he would not like to spend additional cost and time on it. The Director Projects explained the technical issues involved in it. The matter was finally dropped and it was decided to continue with the approved and designed arrangements for the chiller based Air-conditioning system.

 

2.8       Mr. Najmul Islam said that the design of multimedia training centre was in process but half of the fourth floor has been on hold since March 25, 2014.  The Director Projects reported that he was waiting for the reply from Mr. Jeffrey Prah, the designer of Centre of Journalism.  The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to talk to Mr. Jeffrey on skype, answer to his queries and get it resolved and give go ahead to the Contractor.

 

2.9       The Director Projects said that there have been variations of 5.5% over the tender cost in electrical and mechanical work which they will be presenting to Aman Committee.  Mr.   Najmul Islam presented the details of cost variation. 

 

As the figures of cost escalation given by the Project Manager and those given by theProjects Department did not match each other, the Dean & Director asked Mr. Jehanzeb to sit down with Mr. Najmul Islam of Halcrow and IBA Projects Department and review the details of variation and its associated cost and to see if it could further be reduced.   

 

DECISION

 

2.10     The up-dates given by the Project Manager of Halcrow and the Director Projects of IBA were noted and approved.

 

ACTION

 

2.11     The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to seek extension for retaining the APWA building upto January 31, 2015.

 

2.12     The Dean & Director asked Mr. Shakil Anwer to make sure that the furniture, carpets and shelves are in functioning at Aman Tower Library order by January 31, 2015. He was further asked to coordinate with ICT staff and get the fibre, ICT and internet connectivity completed by January 31, 2015.

 

2.13     The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to talk to Mr. Jeffrey and get the designing issue of Centre of Excellence in Journalism resolved and give go ahead to the Contractor.

 

2.14     Mr. Jehanzeb to sit down with the Project Manager of Halcrow and IBA Projects Department and check the calculation and the bases for escalation.

 

There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
