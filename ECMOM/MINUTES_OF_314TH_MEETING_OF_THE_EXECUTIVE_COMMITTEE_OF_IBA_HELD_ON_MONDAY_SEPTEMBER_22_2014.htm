The following items were discussed:

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve lump sum fee for designing and top supervision of    three gates at Main Campus.

 

1.1       The Director Projects reported that in its meeting held on 30th June 2014, the Executive Committee had approved the conceptual plan of the main gates for IBA Main Campus presented by M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative (Pvt) Limited, the Architects who had prepared the Master Plan of Main Campus. They were given go ahead to develop schematic and detailed designs so that construction of the gates could  be taken on top priority and two gates be constructed before the Convocation being held on 6th December 2014. However, later M/s. NBCL were asked to submit a detailed design of only one gate.

 

1.2       The Director Projects informed that M/s. NBCL had demanded a fixed fee of Rs.600,000/- (for designing Rs.350,000/- + Rs.250,000/- for   top supervision) of all the three gates. He said that earlier they were awarded the job to design and top supervision of the following projects at 5% of the completion cost:

1.      Transport Facilities Building
2.      Clinic
3.      Multi-purpose Building Façade Renovation

1.3       The Director Projects said that he had requested M/s. NBCL to charge the fee for the gates at 5% but they did not accept the request and have insisted that they must be paid a fixed fee of Rs.600,000/-  for the designing and top supervision of the three gates. The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider the award of the designing and top supervision to M/s. NBCL at a fixed fee of Rs.600,000/- which comes to 6% of the total estimated cost of Rs.10.00 million of 3 gates.  He reported that they have submitted detailed design, BOQ and construction design for one gate on 16th September 2014.

1.4      To a question, the Director Projects clarified that they are not hiring Project Manager for the gates. The Projects Department will take care of it and NBCL will assist them. The Director Projects said that the gates and boundary walls are included in the design and their estimated cost is Rs.10 million.   He said that the demanded fee of Rs.600,000 by NBCL works out to 6% of the estimated project cost. The Dean & Director said that once we go for the construction of boundary wall, we would use their drawings which now belong to us. The Committee felt further haggling for Rs.100,000/- reduction in fees may cause further delay in the completion of the gates by December 6, 2014.

 

DECISION

 

1.5       The Committee approved a lump sum fee of Rs.600,000/- for NBCL for designing and top supervision of three gates at Main Campus including their boundary walls.

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the award of work for manufacturing, supply and   fixing of kitchen equipment.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that advertisement was floated in daily newspapers: Dawn, The Express and The Aas for invitation of sealed bids for manufacturing, supply and fixing of kitchen equipment in response to which the following contractors collected the tender documents:

1.      M/s Anis and Brothers
2.      M/s Ari (Pvt.) Ltd. formerly Prestige Kitchen
3.      M/s Gujrat Steel

2.2       A pre-bid meeting was held on August 28, 2014 on the request of contractors in order to clarify the queries regarding the equipment. The two contractors submitted their bids along with proof of their experience.  M/s Gujrat Steel failed to provide any evidence of their experience hence their bid was not accepted.

 

2.3       The bids of two contractors M/s Anis and Brothers and M/s ARI were opened on September 3, 2014 in front of IBA officials and Contractors representatives. The following were the quoted prices announced at the time of bid opening:

1.      M/s ARI (Pvt.) Ltd                       Rs.3,763,100/- without GST
2.      M/s Anis and Brothers                  Rs.5,583,637/- without GST

2.4       The Director Projects reported that after arithmetical check no error was found hence their final bid prices stand the same as quoted.  He said that based on earlier experience of kitchen installations in IBA, the budgeted amount for the Kitchen was kept as Rs.4 million. As per the price M/s ARI (Pvt) Ltd. stand the lowest bidder.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 20th September 2014 considered and approved the award of work for manufacturing, supply and fixing of kitchen equipment to M/s. ARI (Pvt.) Limited at a cost of Rs.3,763,100/- based on the competitive bidding.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest bidder M/s ARI (Pvt.) Ltd. at a cost of Rs.3,763,100/- (without GST).

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee approved the award of work for manufacturing, supply and fixing of kitchen equipment to the lowest bidder M/s ARI (Pvt.) Ltd. at a cost of Rs.3,763,100/- (without GST).

Item No.4       To consider and approve encashment of balance Earned for retired BPS         Scale employees. 

 

4.1       Mr. Adnan Hameed said that in January 2004 the Leave Rules approved by the Board were implemented. According to this policy the Earned Leave was reduced from 48 to 30 days.  However, in September 2010 the Earned Leaves of the BPS employees were restored and subsequently the balance leaves accumulated since 2004 were also credited. He said that now the proposal is for the encashment of balance earned leaves to retired employees who served during this period, these are 20 in number (some of them have died). The financial impact is estimated to be around Rs.1.5 million. He said that there were only four employees who would be entitled for 365 days leave encashment.

 

4.2       Mr. Moeid Sultan said that in 2005 when the Option letter was given to BPS employees, they were asked if they needed BPS based increment or IBA related salary and increment.  He said that the option did not include other terms and conditions such as leave encashment. Dr. Nishat said that salary includes all the benefits.

 

4.3       It was pointed out that the Leave Rule was implemented in 2004 whereas the Option was given in November 2005 till such time all the employees were in BPS system. As such the Leave Rule is not linked to the Option Letter. Mr. Adnan Hameed said that the balance leave for this period (January 2004 till November 2005), can be given to Option B employees but not after their terms and conditions were changed.  The Dean & Director agreed with Mr. Adnan Hameed.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Committee approved encashment of balance Earned leaves for those BPS employees who retired during January 2004  September 2010).
