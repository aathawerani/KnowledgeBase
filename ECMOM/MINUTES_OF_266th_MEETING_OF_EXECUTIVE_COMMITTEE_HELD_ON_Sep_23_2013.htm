MINUTES OF 266TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 23, 2013

The 266th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, September 23, 2013 at 3:30 p.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                                     Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                                     Associate Dean, FCS

3.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                                  Registrar

4.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                                       Controller of Examinations

5.      Engr. Rehanul Ambia Riaz                                           Director Projects

6       Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                      Director Finance

7.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                                                 Director H.R.

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, and Mr. Zayed Bilgrami of NBCL attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:   

 

Item No. 1      Presentations on conceptual designs of (a) Clinic, (b) Staff Canteen, and (c) Face lifting of Multipurpose Building by the Architects M/s. NBCL.

 

• Design of Clinic

 

1.1       Mr. Zayd Bilgrami representing Architectural firm M/s Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative Private Limited presented the conceptual design of Clinic at the Main Campus.  He said that the design has been developed on the guidelines provided by IBA. Director Projects confirmed that he himself and Mr. Shameem Ahmed had studied the Karachi University clinic and developed the requirements for IBA. He informed that four different zones will be connected together with the Clinic.  There would be a male and a female day care, a separate room for family examination, and an OPD Section. The total covered area is 2700 sft and its total cost of construction is estimated to be at around Rs.10 million.  The per square foot cost of construction would be around Rs.4,000/-

 

1.2       The Dean & Director said that the per square foot cost of construction is very high. He said that we do not require fancy structure, it should be simple. Mr. Zayed said that the cost includes interior furniture, bed, cabinets, tables etc.

 

1.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that the clinic should easily be accessible from staff town, and hostel too. He also suggested that the Aman Foundation Ambulance should be available at the Main Campus instead of City Campus. Mr. Zayd said that there exists a wide road for vehicles to reach to the clinic.

 

DECISION

 

1.4       The Committee in principle approved the design of the clinic as presented by Mr. Zayd Bilgrami. The Committee requested Mr. Zayd to make the design simple so that it may not be so costly.

 

ACTION

 

1.5       The Registrar would request Aman Foundation to make Aman-Foundation Ambulance available at Main Campus instead of City Campus.

 

• Staff Canteen.

 

1.6       Mr. Zayed Bilgrami presented the site plan of Staff Canteen at IBA Main Campus. He said that the proposed canteen will have the seating capacity of 96 persons whereas the existing canteen has the capacity of 55.  The covered area is 1500 sft and its per sft cost is Rs.3,780/-

 

1.7       The Dean & Director said that the seating capacity should be around 55 to 60.  He asked Mr. Zayed to make it simple so that the cost of construction should not be more than Rs.1500/- per sft. And its covered area should be 1200 to 1300 sft.  He asked Mr. Zayed to revisit the Staff canteen plan in the light of the guidelines provided to him.

 

DECISION

 

1.8       The Committee decided that Mr. Zayed Bilgrami would revisit the Staff Canteen design and try to bring its estimated cost of construction at Rs.1500 per sft.

 

• Face lifting of Multipurpose Building.

 

1.9       The Dean & Director giving background said that National Bank has given a donation against which the Multipurpose building has been dedicated after NBP. He said that earlier it was thought that the data centre would be at the Multipurpose Building so the building was to be named as NBP Technology Centre but when it was decided that the data centre will be at the City Campus and our backup will be on cloud it was decided to use this building as second Administration Building. Hence it would be called NBP Administration Building.  He said that it was thought that the facade of the building should be developed and its external configuration should be redone.

 

1.10     Mr. Zayed Bilgrami presented the view of the multipurpose building after its face lifting and improvement of facade.  He said that they have worked for the face lifting of the building only. The interior work is to be done by the Director Projects.  To a question Mr. Bilgrami confirmed that the facade would be developed from all sides.  He said that the total estimated cost for face lifting would be around Rs.2.5 million.  The Registrar said that the front side of the facade (which was not shown to the EC as it was not prepared) should also be shown to the EC. Mr. Zayd Bilgrami said that he would send the design of the front side of the facade to the EC. 

 

1.11     The Director Projects said that in the interior floor tiling would be done and on the ground floor a central store is to be made for which he has received the requirements.  The Registrar said that he needs simple store not the hi fi one and added that he would take care of the central store.  Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the entrance to this building is very narrow.  It should be made wide.  The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to see how the entrance can be made better. The Dean & Director further said that the Registrar has conveyed the requirements related to interior work which should be complied with. 

 

1.12     Mr. Mobin Khalili Informed that as there are still 7 months remaining to complete the Administration Building, he had advised the Director Projects to renovate the existing offices of the contractors in front of the MP Building so that  the Main Campus Examination Office which is  in a very bad shape could be shifted to these offices. He further said that these offices should be made presentable as the Students and Parents coming to the offices should not form a bad impression of IBA. The Director Projects informed that the work on renovation has been commenced. Two rooms will be properly converted into offices whereas two rooms will be renovated to accommodate the stores. He said that the existing store under the Examination Department needs to be demolished for external development works. He said that he would look into it. 

 

1.13     The Dean & Director wanted to know as to how much time and cost it would take to complete the renovation of the contractor’s offices. The Director Projects informed that it will take at least 3-4 weeks and will cost up to Rs.500,000/-  

 

1.14.    Regarding the Facade work of MP Building the Dean and Director inquired about the time it would take to complete the work. The Director Projects said that after the approval of the design and procurement process, it would take at least 3 months time to complete the project.  He requested the EC to give in principle approval for appointment of NBCL for this project.

 

DECISION

 

1.15.    The Executive Committee approved the design of the facade of Multipurpose building as presented by the Consultant, NBCL.

 

1.16     The Committee in principle approved the appointment of NBCL as Consultant for the Multipurpose Building face lifting, Canteen and the Clinic. 

 

1.17.    The Committee approved the renovation of the existing contractor’s offices in front of the MP building within the cost limit of Rs.500,000/-

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve time extension of renovation and up-gradation of existing Administration Building at Main Campus.

 

2.1       The Director Projects said that the renovation and up-gradation of existing Administration Building at Main Campus started in March 2012 and was to be completed in 18 months time i.e. by September 2013. However, due to the following reasons the completion of the project has been delayed for six months and now it is expected to be completed by March 14, 2014.

 

Taking Over the Portions of IBA Admin Block

 

The major reason of delay is partial handing over of floors. It was difficult to proceed the work as per schedule. When contractor started the work various departments were working on different floors and were disturbed due to noise. To carry on construction activities in day time was very difficult. The shifting of the departments to other buildings of the Campus, therefore, took some time and hence delayed the handing over to the Contractor.   

 

On-Hold Areas

 

On hold areas were another major reason for delay. The demolishing of existing overhead tank is still on hold as water is supplied from this source to other buildings on the campus. Till construction of new overhead tank the existing structure cannot be demolished affecting the Southern façade work and balance work of 4th floor. The back side facade work is also affected due to working of electrical infrastructure contractor. The ground floor occupied by Library was handed over to the contractors as late as June 2013. (8 months late than planned)

 

Delays due to change in location of Lift well foundation.

 

During excavation existing footing of foundation was encountered due to this width and location of lift well was revised.

 

Hard Rock Excavation

 

On finding hard rock the design of footing was revised and pace of excavation slowed down as excavation was performed manually without using mechanical equipment in order to noise control and to avoid disturbance in existing operational library at ground floor.

 

Late Approval of Shop Drawings / Status for Mockup Room by the Architect.

 

Revised Drawings

 

The furniture as well as electrical and HVAC drawings were also revised due to the changes proposed by the users. The latest changes have been accommodated during September 2013.

 

Law & Order Situation

 

The continuous law and order situation and shut down of CNG caused non availability of public transport which affected arrival of labour at site and procurement of material.

 

2.2       The Director Projects reported that in its meeting held on September 21, 2013 the Central Purchase Committee recommended the approval of the extension in time up till March 14, 2014. Based on the recommendations of Project Department, Project Manager M/s NESPAK and Central Purchase Committee, he requested the Executive Committee to approve the extension in time up till March 14, 2014. He clarified that this was based on the assumption that no more changes would be communicated to them except those which may require technical improvements by ICT and maintenance department.

 

2.3       The Director Projects said that the extension of time should be without reimbursement of the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall have to extend all insurances and guarantees, related to the Contract, accordingly.

 

DECISION

 

2.4       The Committee approved the extension in completion time for renovation and up-gradation of existing Administration Building at Main Campus till March 14, 2014 without reimbursement of the costs of extended preliminaries, cost of operations, additional overheads or any other cost whatsoever in this respect. The Contractor shall have to extend all insurances and guarantees, related to the Contract, accordingly.

 

ACTION

 

2.5.      The Director Projects would extend the contract period subject to the conditions noted at 2.4 above.

Item No.4       External Development of Visiting Faculty Hostel.

 

4.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the External Development of the Visiting Faculty Hostel was not a part of the Consultant NBCL’s design of the Visiting Faculty Hostel. Also, in order to keep the cost down and within budgetary limits, it was not envisaged to be included in the construction contract awarded to Al-Shafi enterprises at the lowest bid price of Rs.88,131,975/- He said that since the project is nearing completion, it is important that in order to give its surroundings a pleasant and friendly look, the external development work is undertaken on a priority. He said that a sum of Rs.8 million was allocated in the budget 2013-14 for this purpose.

 

4.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the required external development was initially designed by NBCL in April 2013.  The Engineer’s cost estimate at that time was Rs.5.558 million. He said that in August 2013 at their invitation, the present VFH Contractor Al-Shafi Enterprises quoted Rs.7.877 million for the External Development Work. He said that he had had a meeting with the consultant and contractor and as an outcome of the meeting, the Consultant revised the BoQ while the contractor submitted a revised / reduced quotation amounting to Rs.6.117 million. However, on their request the Contractor allowed a special rebate / discount of 10% which brings the quoted price to Rs.5.505 million. 

 

4.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that he had requested the Central Purchase Committee to approve awarding the work to the present contractor Al-Shafi Enterprises as a variation order of Rs.5.505 million for the following reasons:

 

• The external development work may be made a part of the existing work of the building as bringing in a new contractor will create a class between the two contractors mobilization and demobilization of a new contractor for the small work is not advisable.

• The urgency of the work demands that we avoid an altogether new tendering process which may take several months to materialize.

• The additional work is just about 6% of the original contract which is much below the permissible limit of 15%.

 

4.4       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the request was presented in the CPC meeting on September 7, 2013 and the CPC has recommended awarding the work to the present contractor as a Variation Order for Rs.5.505 million.  He said that the CPC has also recommended extension of time for completion of Visiting Faculty Hostel upto December 31, 2013 for the following reasons:

 

• The miscellaneous Sewerage Work is still in progress.  It may take about two months to be completed.

• As water and gas supplies to the VFH are yet to be arranged, the final testing of plumbing and kitchen work will take time.

• Similarly, power supply to the VFH has also been delayed. At the last moment KESC has refused to energize the power connection to Boys’ Hostels and Staff Colony due to non-installation of PFIPs at the Main Campus. (Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that he is taking up the matter at the highest level of KESC but until they get the power supply, the electrical installations at the VFH cannot be tested).

 

DECISION

 

4.5       The Committee approved the recommendations of the CPC for (a) extension of time for completion of Visiting Faculty Hostel upto December 31, 2013, and (b) award of the external development work of Visiting Faculty Hostel to the present contractor M/s. Al-Shafi Enterprises as a Variation Order for Rs.5.505 million.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
