Item No. 3 To consider and approve the Terms of Reference for Publication and Research Committee of Centre for Business and Economics Research.

3.1 The Director H.R. proposed the following terms of reference for the Publication and Research Committee of Centre for Business and Economic Research (CBER). She said that the activities would be managed by Publication and Research Committee (PRC) which is headed by the Director Research and comprises distinguished academicians and practitioners of business field.

• To assist in developing the research culture at IBA.
• To provide input to the development of IBA research priorities.
• To be responsible directly or indirectly for all core and contract research activities at IBA
• To assist in searching for good researchers in the relevant field of business
• To assist in reviewing and assessing the Faculty’s request for financial support for research projects.
• To establish collaborative research projects with international and national institutions of repute.
• To assist in generation of external research fund for the research activities at the Centre.
• To solicit research ideas and funding from the industry and corporate sectors for core and contract research.
• To establish collaborative research projects with international and national institutions of repute.
• To assist in development, negotiation and implementation of contract research proposals
• To be responsible for smooth running and ensuring good quality of core and contract research of IBA.
• To organize research seminars workshops/conferences.
• To supervise the initiative of the Working Papers/ Policy Paper series.
• Regularly publishing and updating the quality of IBA Business Review.
• To assist in publishing IBA research in the high quality outlets.
• To develop a team for the dissemination of research through conferences and seminars.

3.2 The Director H.R. suggested that a bicameral sub-committee of the RPC should act as the Research Evaluation Committee (REC) to separately evaluate research proposals submitted by the Faculty of Business Administration and the Faculty of Computer Science. So there will be a Business-REC and a CS-REC. Each REC will have a total of 5 members, with 2 members common to both, 2 members from the concerned faculty, and 1 member to be chosen from outside IBA based on the research to be evaluated. She said that the basic TOR of the REC will be as follows:

• To review and approve research proposals from full time faculty members for the purpose of allocating research units.
• To manage and make recommendations regarding any issues pertaining to allocation of research units that may arise from time to time.

3.3 The suggested composition of the Research and Publication Committee is as under:

Dr. Qazi Masood Ahmed (Chairperson and Convener)
Prof. Dr. Mahnaz Fatima
Dr. Ahmed Ali Shah
Dr. Waseem A Khan
Dr. Nasir Tauheed
Dr. Ijaz A. Mian
Dr. Zeenat Ismail
Associate Dean, FBA (Ex-officio)
Associate Dean, FCS (Ex-officio)
Editor of IBA Business Review (Ex-officio)

3.4 The Director suggested that there should be two committees; one for the FBA and another for the FCS and these should be with the Centre for Business and Economics Research. However, he invited suggestions from the EC members.

3.5 Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that the faculty members suggested above are reasonable and he was in favour of one Research & Publication Committee.

3.6 Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested that since the work is being broadened and the FCS is to grow, there should be two committees. However, he suggested that initially they may start with one committee but as the demand increases, it should be separated into two committees.

3.7 The Director said that for faculty’s units conversion, one committee will work but for research and publication two committees will be appropriate.

3.8 Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that students’ research has not been incorporated and it needs to be thoroughly looked into.

3.9 Mr. Zafar Siddiqui was in favour of two separate committees.

3.10 The Director ICT suggested that there should be three committees; one for research evaluation, one for Research & Publication for FCS and one for FBA

DECISION

3.11 The Committee decided that there would be two Research and Publication Committees; one for FBA and one for FCS.

3.12 The Committee also decided that there would be a single Research Evaluation Committee having a total of 5 members, 2 members from each faculty, and 1 member to be chosen from outside IBA based on the research to be evaluated. The TOR of would basically be:

• To review and approve research proposals from full time faculty members for the purpose of allocating research units.
• To manage and make recommendations regarding any issues pertaining to allocation of research units that may arise from time to time.
3.13 The Committee decided that Dr. Qazi Masood Ahmed, Dr. Muhammad Nishat and the Director H.R. will revisit the composition and TORs of Research and Publication Committees and present them to the EC in two weeks time.

b) Approval for Project Manager for Data Centre Design Validation.
5.15 The Director ICT stated that since IBA is following the strategy of being a World Class University and they are developing a world Class ICT infrastructure and Data Centers, it is prudent that they must try to get the accreditations of the Data center design. He said that there are various agencies like Data Center Validation Services that help in getting the Data Centers meet the international standards. He further said that the goal of the Data Center Design Validation Services (DCDV) is to validate the design of a new data center and evaluate it for compliance to International Standards and Norms such as TIA-942, SS507, ISO-24762 and others. The outcome of their evaluation is a report which would indicate the level of compliance and what potentially needs to be addressed to ensure for a successful and smooth audit that leads to certification. He said that their requirement is to follow multiple standards including TIA 942, SS507 and ISO 24762 which verifies architectural, electrical, telecommunication, mechanical, maintenance and documentation aspects of data center. The awarded vendor should be adroit in mentioned skills to execute building data center project in proper way. This vetted data center design in light of TIA 942 standard design will enable to reduce the operational expenses. Following data center standard, IBA will have energy efficient data center which would also save operation cost leading to Green Computing. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the hiring the Project Manager Firm. He also specified the qualifications required for the Project Manager firms.

DECISION

5.16 The Committee did not approve the proposal.
