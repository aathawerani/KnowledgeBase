Item No. 2      Approval for award of work to M/s. DWP for extra items.

 

2.1       The Director Projects reported that the Renovation and Refurbishment work of existing Academic Block (Ground Floor) at City Campus was awarded to M/s. Abaseen Construction Company Limited after the approval of BOG in its meeting held on July 25, 2009 (A&FC minutes of July 11, 2009) at a cost of Rs.57,579,296/.  The execution work started on August 4, 2009 during the execution the ICT department raised their requirement for such equipments and cables which were not included in the BOQ at the time of tendering, giving the reason that they are changing their complete infrastructure and the brands which were quoted by the contractor are technically not acceptable by them. He said that the ICT department handed over a BOQ of 13 items, the BOQ was sent to Consultant and the Project Manager to scrutinize and analyze. The project Manager M/s INTEG asked for the quotation from the Contractor M/s ACCL who submitted their quotation of Rs.7.5 million for the extra items. The quotation was sent to ICT department for scrutinizing and rate analysis. After complete analysis ICT Department responded that the rates quoted by M/s ACCL are very high.

 

2.2       The Director Projects stated that a meeting was held among Projects Department, ICT Department, Project Manager M/s INTEG and the consultant M/s SK Engineers on December 24, 2009. In the meeting all the items were discussed individually and after joint agreement it was decided that ICT department will obtain the quotation from their approved vendor M/s DWP who is the technology partner of IBA and will give special rates to institutions.

 

2.3       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the ICT Department obtained a quotation of Rs.3,528,727.70/- in which M/s DWP offered a discount of Rs.530,727.70/- after the discount the final amount became Rs.2,998,000/-. On January 07, 2010, the same was sent to Project Manager M/s. INTEG who sent the same quotation to M/s. ACCL to issue the Purchase Order to M/s DWP.

 

2.4       The Director Projects stated that some clarifications were required from M/s DWP in this regard in a meeting that was held between M/s DWP, M/s IES (Electrical Sub contractor of M/s Abaseen) IBA Projects Department, and M/s INETG on January 15, 2010.  M/s DWP informed that they would deliver the local components within 10 days after receipt of Purchase Order whereas the imported components will take 4 to 6 weeks for the delivery  and they will send a revised quotation mentioning the manufacture and model of the equipment.

 

2.5       The Director Projects reported that M/s DWP furnished their revised quotation of Rs.3,091,840.98 after discount on January 25, 2010 which was Rs.93,840 higher than the quotation submitted by them on January 07, 2010.  He said that M/s DWP were asked to give the reason for increase in amount but they failed to give a valid reason for increase of amount .The ICT negotiated and obtained the quotation of same amount form M/s DWP. The quotation was forwarded to M/s INTEG, the Project Manager who forwarded the same quotation to M/s ACCL to issue the P.O to M/s DWP. M/s ACCL submitted their quotation on January 29, 2010 to M/s INTEG for this extra item after loading 22% on account of profit and overheads. After which the amount becomes Rs.3,670,451/- which is Rs.672,451/- higher than the rates quoted by M/s DWP.

 

2.6       The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to give approval for issuing a separate work order to M/s DWP who is approved ICT vendor for the subject work. He clarified that as per Procurement Procedure & Policy they can give a contract directly to a contractor who is technically qualified as per their specifications and no other vendor has the same capability.  He also requested the Executive Committee to approve in principle the award of work to M/s DWP at an amount of Rs.2,998,000/-

 

2.7       The Director Projects reported that the budget is available under budget head # 401.1.2 “Furniture Fixtures of class rooms including ground floor and IT Equipment”. The budget provision is Rs.10.0 million out of which Rs.2.16 million has already been committed for Furniture and a variation order for ICT cabling of approximately Rs.1.9 million is under process. The balance amount available is Rs.5.94 million. He said that this will then be submitted to CPC for final approval. He further said that because of 60% discount to educational institutions and 20% savings on taxes they will have a saving of Rs.3 million on awarding the work to M/s. DWP.

 

DECISION

 

2.8       After a detailed discussion, the Executive Committee granted conceptual approval for the award of work to M/s. DWP.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve draft rule for payment of honorarium to eligible teachers / trainers.

 

3.1       The Director H.R. presenting the draft rule for payment of honorarium to eligible teachers / trainers stated that IBA endeavours to encourage specialized development training programs for faculty & students through distinguished and eminent teachers / trainers well-known in their respective fields from within and outside Pakistan. She said that the purpose of the policy is to outline the rules and regulations governing payment of Honorarium to eligible teachers / trainers. She presented the scope, eligibility criteria and honorarium limits.

 

Eligibility Criteria

 

3.2       The Director H.R. clarified that honorarium will be paid to only those outstanding people / trainers who are eminent in their fields, who contribute towards the rigorous professional development of IBA faculty and students. The key eligibility criteria for awarding honorarium would be as follows:

 

a)      The individual’s background (experience and qualifications).

b)      The amount of effort entailed in preparation and delivery of the activity offered.

c)      The individual must be eminent in their field, with a minimum of 5 years of professional experience in the relevant field in a leading position.

d)     Demand for the workshop / program.

e)      Minimum number of participants enrolled.

 

Honorarium LIMITS

 

3.3       The Director H.R. proposed the following scale for determining honorarium:

 

Type of Teacher / Trainer

Min Rate / Hour (taxable)

Max Rate / Hour (taxable)

External (Coming in from abroad)

US$ 500

US$ 5000

External/Internal

(Local from the Market or from within IBA)

PKR 2,500

PKR 8,000

 

RESPONSIBILITIES:

 

a)      Associate Deans / Director IBA will request the Trainer for facilitation.

b)      Associate Deans / Director IBA will recommend an Honorarium rate in accordance with the Scale, for the trainer and Director IBA will approve it.

c)      Assistant Manager External Linkages will facilitate the required administrative support.

d)     Finance will make the payment of Honorarium.

 

PROCEDURE:

 

a)      Workshop/Training requirement with appropriate rationale and suggested distinguished trainer(s) will be sent to Associate Deans and Director IBA for consideration.

b)      Director IBA’s approval will be required.

c)      Associate Deans / Director IBA will request the distinguished trainer for facilitation.

d)     Associate Deans will recommend the rate of Honoraria to be given to the trainer and Director IBA will approve it.

e)      Honoraria payment will be made after the successful completion of workshop/training sessions.

f)       Assistant Manager External Linkages will request finance for payment processing by providing all the necessary documents with approvals.

g)   Finance will ensure timely payment to the Trainer.

 

3.4       The Registrar suggested that the minimum number of participants should be quantified. However, the Committee decided that at this stage instead of fixing a minimum number it should be open because some subjects such as I.T may not be of interest to majority of the faculty members.

 

3.5       It was also suggested that the minimum per hour rate of remuneration be increased from Rs.2,500 to Rs.5,000/-

 

DECISION

 

3.6       The Committee recommended the rule for payment of honorarium to eligible teachers / trainers as presented by the Director H.R. to the Audit & Finance Committee for approval with the following changes:

 

a)      The minimum per hour rate of remuneration will be increased from Rs.2,500 to Rs.5,000/-

b)      The rate will apply on the duration for which the program is conducted.

c)   The rates will be revisited after two years.

 

ACTION

 

3.7       The Director H.R. will present the rule for payment of honorarium to eligible teachers / trainers after incorporating the above suggestions to the Audit & Finance Committee for approval.

b)        Proposal for streamlining of Teaching Assistant’s Remunerations.

 

4.6              The Director H.R. reported that during Fall Semester 2009, four teaching assistants were hired who are paid according to the following rates for a maximum of 80 hours per month:

 

·         Students of BBA/BS-III to BBA/BS-VIII          Rs.20,000/- per month

·         Students of MBA-III-IV                                      Rs.25,000/- per month

 

4.7              The Director H.R. said that when H.R. analyzed their working details at the end of the semester, they found that all of them were underutilized.  She presented an analysis of the hours put in by these RA’s.  She said that the new policy for Teaching Assistants is in progress and its finalization may take substantial time of the current semester. She proposed that until the new policy is revised and approved, teaching assistants may be paid according to the following rates:

 

·         Students of BBA/BS-III to BBA/BS-VIII          Rs.200/- per hour

·         Students of MBA- III - IV                                   Rs.250/- per hour

 

4.8              The Registrar suggested that instead of BBA/BS-III to BBA/BS-VIII students, BBA/BS-V to VIII students should be made eligible for Teaching Assistantship.

 

DECISION

 

4.9              The Committee approved the proposal of the Director H.R. for the current semester by incorporating the suggestion of the Registrar as given in 4.9 above.

 

4.10          The Committee also decided that the Academic Committee will make a policy on Teaching Assistantship and its remuneration for next semester on the basis of the note received from Mr. Ahsan Kirmani.

 

ACTION

 

4.11          The Director H.R. will get the decision implemented on the revised remuneration policy for Teaching Assistants.

 

4.12          Dr. Muhammad Nishat will get a revised policy on Teaching Assistants and their remuneration framed in the Academic Committee on the basis of the note received from Mr. Ahsan Kirmani and present it before the Executive Committee.
