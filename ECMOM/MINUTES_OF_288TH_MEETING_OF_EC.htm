Item No.1 Follow-up presentation on Employees’ Data regarding promotions.

1.1 Mr. Adnan Hameed said that in the last EC meeting the members were briefed on the three types of promotion namely; (i) Promotion through appointment, (ii) Promotion through up-gradation of position, and (iii) Seniority based promotion; the third one is applicable for non-management staff. However, he said that in the last meeting, the EC felt that there are some people on senior positions who have not been promoted for a long period of time. The EC had also decided to review and revise the third pillar of promotion to allow seniority cum merit based promotion for BPS-16 and above and equivalent employees and present it to the Selection Board and BoG for approval.

1.2 The Dean & Director said that we have to be consistent with and follow what the government rules of promotion are and the same rules should apply for BPS as well as Job Band employees in the equivalent scale. He wanted to know as to what is the minimum service length required under government rules for promotion from BPS-17 to 18 and onward. Mr. Moeid Sultan reported that the HEC Promotion Policy requires five years of experience for promotion to the next grade.

1.3 Mr. Adnan Hameed presented details of the employees for consideration for promotion from BPS-17 to 18 / Job Band 3 to Job Band 4, and BPS-16 to 17 / Job Band 2 to Job Band 3. The Committee, in addition to the data provided by H.R., referred to the minutes of the Selection Board in order to reconfirm the date of last promotion. The Committee also considered the Job Analysis carried out in 2011 and approved by the Selection Board / Board of Governors, and the annual performance appraisal.

1.4 Out of the list of 17 employees in BPS-17 / Job Band 3, the Committee, on the basis of length of service in the existing grade/Job Band, above average reports at Annual Performance Evaluations, and educational qualifications found two of them – Mr. M. Akmal Khan, and Mr. Abdul Khalid eligible for consideration.

1.5 Similarly, for promotion from BPS-16 / Job Band 2 to BPS-17 / Job Band 3, two employees – Mr. Khurram Khalid, and Mr. Ayaz Ahmed met the eligibility criteria. However, Mr. Adnan Hameed reported that Mr. Khurram Khalid’s case was already being separately considered for promotion (as a member of the Audit Department). Mr. Ayaz had previously served in different departments and his performance was above average.

1.6 For promotion from the position of Senior Executive Job Band 1 to Assistant Manager, Job Band 2; three employees: Syed Sohail uddin, Syed Akbar Hussain Kazmi, and Ms. Ghulam Fatima met the eligibility criteria and were found suitable for promotion.

1.7 The Dean & Director sought members’ comments / assessment on the potential of each of the employees who were meeting the eligibility criteria.

To a question he said that there had been a delay of seven months however, he clarified that is no financial impact involved in it.

Item No.3 To consider and approve the extension of time and revision in cost for Electrical Power Infrastructure Development Work at IBA Staff Town, New Girls’ and Boys’ Hostels.

3.1 The Director Projects reported that the work was awarded to contractor M/s Iqbal Engineering House through letter of Acceptance / Award No. IBA-MC/HEC-Projs/231/0529/2012 dated January 07, 2013. As per the contract the date of commencement was established as January 23, 2013 and the whole work was to be completed within 150 calendar days i.e. up-to June 21, 2013. But the work could not be completed in due time because of various reasons. He informed the EC that the Contractor M/s Iqbal Engineering House had submitted the reasons for delays vide his letter dated July 23, 2013, which were also examined in detail by the Project Manager NESPAK and the Project Manager recommended 82 days of time extension to the contractor i.e. upto September 11, 2013. The time extension recommended by Nespak is mentioned as following:

     • Occasional Hold-ups due to Law and Order Situation                       Recommended 09 days
     • Delay Due to General Election in the Country                                 Recommended 02 days
     • Change in Design (Manufacturing of LT Panels)                              Recommended 36 days
     • Removal of Existing Cable                                                           Recommended 10 days
     • Additional Scope of Work (Owner Supplied Item)                            Recommended 25 days
     • Energization from KESC (Procedural Delays)                                   Recommended 21 days
                                                                                           
                                                                                      Total: 103 Days Recommendation

3.2 The Director Projects reported that the time extension was recommended by Nespak on August 21, 2013 before the actual completion of work. At that time the anticipation was that the remaining work would be completed smoothly without any major obstruction and therefore IBA accordingly provided the plan to KESC to Energize Boys’ Hostel on August 20, 2013 & Staff Colony on August 27, 2013 but KESC energized both the schemes on September 11, 2013. The delay occurred because KESC rejected initially to energize the IBA schemes because IBA didn’t fulfil the PFIP installation requirement as per approved load at the Main Campus. IBA had committed earlier to do that. Later on with the intervention of IBA higher management the matter was resolved amicably with KESC/K-Electric. This whole exercise delayed the process and the construction schedule was disturbed badly. The remaining work i.e. dismantling of cable in KU’s premises could only be executed after the energization of Boys’ Hostel scheme. Thereafter, the cable could be laid in the staff town. Due to this reason the sub-project got further delayed by 21 days and was completed on September 30, 2013.

3.3 The Director Projects reported that the project also faced the issue of cost overrun as the approved quantities in BOQ didn’t cover the whole requirement of work and additional quantities had to be procured adding 12.26% to the cost. Besides the quantities variation in the project, some additional works were also performed by the contractor which amounted to 7.16% of the project tender cost; these works became necessary due to the actual site conditions that required viability, functionality and protection of the project.

3.4 The Director Projects further reported that another issue faced was the shortage of cables of 240 sq mm and 95 sq mm. The matter was referred to CPC in its meeting held on June 08, 2013. The CPC approved the request and decided that the requirement was genuine and direct contracting could be done. As per the decision of CPC the contractor procured the cables at the discounted rates which were offered by the Manufacturer to IBA. The cable was procured through the existing contractor but at a discounted rate negotiated by IBA considering the urgency of the matter and to save time on other activities of the project which could suffer badly if required cables were not procured on time. The overall cost impact of cable procurement with respect to tender cost is 11.85%.

3.5 The Director Projects reported that the above case was submitted to Procurement Committee in its meeting held on March 15, 2014. The Procurement Committee recommended the Extension of Time and the cost overrun up to 15% to the EC for approval. For the cost overrun exceeding 15% limits, the Procurement Committee recommended that the case of cost overrun should be revalidated by the Competent Authority of IBA i.e. Audit & Finance Committee, duly recommended by the Executive Committee.

3.6 The Dean & Director said that the variation order arising out of the items that were not included in the original BoQ should be treated separately. He said that the additional work carried for with justifiable reasons cannot be labelled as cost overrun. He said that the cost of work mentioned in the original BoQ did not exceed the 15% limit. However, during the implementation of the project, it was discovered that there was a shortage of cable because the old dug out cable was not of the dimension which was needed to link with the feeder. We did not have any drawings available of the old cable lines. The procurement of new cable of required dimension was therefore inevitable. Because of the urgency and in order to avoid the delay the work was given to the existing contractor. This was an unanticipated development and the approval for procurement of cable in emergency was obtained from CPC. He said that according to the original BoQ the variation is within 15% and the additional unanticipated work which had to be procured in emergency was part of External Development Work not of the Hostel project itself. He suggested to separate the unanticipated External Development work from the Hostel projects.

DECISION

3.7 The Committee endorsed the recommendation of Project Manager NESPAK for allowing 82 days time extension i.e. upto September 11, 2013 to the Contractor.

3.8 The Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the variation orders which are within the limit of 15%.

3.9 The Committee also recommended to the A&FC to approve the additional unanticipated work carried out in emergency as part of the External Development Work in the staff colony.

ACTION

3.10 The Director Projects would present the recommendations of Procurement Committee and the Executive Committee on the variation orders and the additional unanticipated work done in emergency with complete justifications to the A&FC for approval.

Item No. 5 Progress Report on Aman Tower.

5.1 The Director Projects presented the summary of the project and the progress of the project as of March 17, 2014. He presented the current status of Phase I, II & III and the cost involved in each activity. He said that initially the tender cost quoted by Contractors for Internal Development package was Rs.333 million which, because of reengineering exercise, was reduced to Rs.219 million and is expected to be reduced further. He said that Phase I works were scheduled to be completed by March 31, 2014 but for various reasons, it got delayed by one month and will now be completed by end of April 2014. He confirmed that the delay will not cause any day in the final completion as they will be dovetailed with the interior design works (Phase II) and will be completed within the scheduled time for Interior Development Works. The Dean & Director asked as to when all the work of Phase-I will be completed. The Director Projects said that all the work in Phase-I (civil and MEP) will be completed by June 24, 2014.

5.2 The Dean & Director asked as to why so many standby generators have been proposed while there is an assurance from KESC that IBA would be exempt from load shedding and we already have a 500 KVA and three 125 KVA generators as stand-by at the City Campus. The Registrar said that if there would be any breakdown that would be for a very short period for which one generator could be sufficient. The Dean & Director said that there is a 500 KVA generator which can be dedicated to Aman Tower. He asked the Director Projects to remove the gen set from the tender documents in process. The Director Projects said that the tender will have to be cancelled. The Dean & Director said that this will save Rs.40 million which was being spent on new generators that overlapped with the existing one. Mr. Mushtaq said that he has three generators of 125 KVA each and the another one has become very old. The Dean & Director said that the old one can be replaced with 250 KVA generator in the next budget. He asked Mr. Mushtaq to dedicate a 500 KVA generator to Aman Tower for meeting essential requirements in case of power breakdown. He asked him to do zoning and set priority for the Aman Tower as he has done for the Main Campus. Mr. Mushtaq said that there is no need to move the generator; Aman Tower would be connected through cable. However, he said that he would check the essential load requirements and inform the EC.

5.3 The Director Projects said that as there would be saving, they may go for the bridge to connect the two buildings. The Registrar said that its estimated cost is Rs.200 million. He said that there is no need of a bridge; stair cases will be sufficient. The Dean & Director agreed that stair cases will serve the purpose and a bridge is not required.

5.4 The Director Projects said that furniture design has been completed by EA Consulting and is under review at IBA. He said that the furniture work will start from May 2014 and the target dates are set as follows:

a) Phase – I All furniture except residences by end of October 14, 2014
b) Phase – II Residences furniture by December 31, 2014.

5.5 The Dean & Director advised the Director Projects to share the design of furniture with the clients; Director CEE, Registrar, Associate Deans, Controller of Examinations, Head Librarian and expedite the tendering process.

DECISION

5.6 The Committee noted the status on Aman Tower and decided that there was no need of spending Rs.40 million on stand-by generator and the existing 500 KVA generator would be dedicated to Aman Tower.

ACTION

5.7 The Director Projects would cancel tender for standby generators, and would expedite the tendering process for furniture.

5.8 Mr. Mushtaq Ahmed would study and examine the essential load requirements of electricity at Aman Tower and present his report to the EC.
