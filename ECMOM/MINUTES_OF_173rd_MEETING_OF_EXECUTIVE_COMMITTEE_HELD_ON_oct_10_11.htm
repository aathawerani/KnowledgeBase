Item No. 4      Presentation by M/s. KP Associates on the design of Main Campus Infrastructure.

 

4.1       The Director Projects said that electrical infrastructure design of Main Campus is being done by M/s. KP Associates while M/s. NBCL are doing the overall external development of the entire campus.  He said that M/s. KP Associates had presented a concept of central hub but when the Executive Committee was informed that the full back up power generation was part and parcel of the original buildings, they suggested that it should be on a central place.  On that basis KPA had made presentation but the Dean & Director had said that no full back up will be given to any building and advised to remove all generators and work out the amount to be saved by removing all generators.  He said that NBCL who have designed the building say that the building infrastructure is built on Central Air-conditioning system and at this stage it is not advisable to remove them. He said that he had a joint meeting with SMC, Electrical Consultant, and tried to resolve that problem.  He said that the consensus was that the provision for full back up power should be there so that there will be little affect on IBA building in case of KESC power outage. Mr. Khalid Pervez of M/s. KP Associates then made a presentation.

 

4.2       Mr. Khalid Pervez said that if IBA is exempt from normal load shedding even then under the circumstances we should have some provision to feed essential power requirements of the buildings.  He said that buildings have already been designed for centrally air-conditioned.  He further said that the cost differential in both cases – full back up and partial back up - is not much. 

 

4.3       Mr. Zayed of NBCL said that the students centre, Aman CED, and new Academic Buildings have been designed as centrally air-conditioned buildings which at this stage they cannot reverse.  He clarified that in the proposed centrally air-conditioned system which is most energy efficient, one can switch the air-condition for every room and can control the temperature too according to one’s own requirement.  He further said that individual A/Cs consume more energy than package units and extra overhead on running on full back up is negligible.  The Capital cost is already provided for in the budget, only the operational cost is involved which is far less in case of centrally air-conditioner than split units.  Although central air-conditioner systems are capital intensive, their operational and maintenance costs are less. He further said that the split AC does not have air change system but the Central Air-conditioner has air change system.  Central air-conditioner can be operated partially too.

 

4.4       Mr. Khalid Pervez presented details of total power requirements including 15% future addition; total requirements with all A/C systems, with essential A/Cs and non-essential A/Cs.  He also presented the following details of the capacity of required DG sets and the estimated cost:

 

            With 100% back up                5 DG sets of 750 KVA           =  Rs.49.5 million*      

            With partial back up                4 DG sets of 600 KVA          =  Rs.26.0 million*

                                                              Cost differential:         =  Rs.23.5 million

 

            *This does not include Capital cost of Rs.28 million for DG sets of new Academic Block and Students Centre.

 

4.5       Mr. Khalid Pervez further said that they have not included the cable cost which will remain the same in both cases; partial or full back up.

           

4.6       Mr. Zayed of NBCL said that out of Rs.49.5 million Rs.20 million has already been included against the cost of generators for new Academic Block and Students Centre. So now the cost differential is of Rs.3.5 million only.

 

4.7       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui said that for just Rs.3.5   million cost differential we should not leave the three buildings on partial back up. The Committee agreed with him.

 

4.8       Mr. Khalid Pervez presented the working of additional cost of DG sets with respect to KESC tariff for two hour KESC outage/day which is as under:

 

            100% back up                         Rs.556,048/-  per month

            Partial back up                        Rs.464,886/-  per month

             Differential                             Rs.  91,162/-  per month

           

Mr. Khalid PerveZ said that the differential expressed as a percentage of IBA monthly KESC bill of Rs.1,211,033/- comes out to just 8%. In view of the very small additional operating cost burden in providing 100% back up, he recommended to adopt 100% back up.

 

4.9       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that internal people should also verify the facts and figures presented by Mr. Khalid Pervez.

 

DECISION

 

4.10     The Committee decided that Mr. Sohail and Mr. Mohammad Jawad will check the calculation of loads on 100% back up and partial back up and the figures of electrical bills and convey to Mr. Khalid Pervez.

 

ACTION

 

4.11     Mr. Khalid Pervez will give a presentation again to the EC basd on the verified loads and figures.

 

4.12     The Director Projects would ask Mr. Sohail and Mr. Jawad to check and verify the figures presented by Mr. Khalid Pervez.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
