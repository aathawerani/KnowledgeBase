MINUTES OF THE 90TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MARCH 1, 2010

 

The 90th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, March 1, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

  8. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                             Director CED

  9. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                             Director ICT

  10. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director HR

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed attended the meeting by special invitation.

 

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      EC decisions implementation status report from Director Projects.

 

1.1       The Director Projects to convey the decision of the selection of grey coloured wash terrazzo: The Director Projects reported that he conveyed the decision about the selection of grey coloured wash terrazzo.  He reported that façade on Academic Block is 50% complete.  He said that the EC in its previous meeting decided not to use washed terrazzo anymore. The EC had also decided that façade work mock ups be prepared through the Architects to be witnessed by EC by March 31, 2010. He said that he had conveyed the decision to the Architect.   

 

1.2       Town Hall Meeting:  The Director Projects reported that a Town Hall Meeting was scheduled for 1st February 2010 but as the data from Icon was not received, so it will have to be rescheduled.

 

1.3              Vetting of Structural Designs: The Director Projects reported that according to the Vetting Engineer engaged by the Architects M/s ICON the structural designs of the under construction elevated pathway in front of existing Academic Block at the City Campus has been designed  as per American Code of Structure and as such the structure is safe. He further stated that the same vetting Engineer namely Mr Kamaran Dehlvi had informed that the CCS building (now FCS building) was designed by him as ground plus four storied building. He had advised that the fourth floor on the FCS building can safely be built.

 

Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that in order to have a centralized building where all computer sciences facilities are located, adding 4th floor on the FCS building will not be meeting the basic requirements. He said that at present there is insufficient provision for various required labs for FCS. He suggested that instead of adding 4th floor, two wings in the FCS building may be added so that it may cater to the need of the FCS for the next 10 to 20 years.  He said that the ground floor of the proposed three-floor two wings may be used for parking purposes.   

  

The Director Projects agreed to the proposal of Dr. Sayeed Ghani and said that the two wings can be added within the available budget.

 

1.4              Director Projects to work out the cost due to increase in scope:  The Director Projects reported that the scope of work given to the contractors on which the bidding process was completed in October 2009, has now been totally changed because of shifting of the server room (now called as ICT equipment room). He said that in accordance with PPRA regulations new scope of work cannot be issued to the contractor who won the contract. He said that the contract will have to be cancelled. He said that in a meeting held with the Architects, Projects Managers, M/s Icons structural engineer, it was decided to cancel the contract.

 

1.5              Labs Capacity to be increased from 40 to 60:  The Director Projects reported that the labs capacity has been increased from 40 to 60.

 

1.6              Changes in server equipment requirements and associated services and equipments:  The Director Projects reported that due to weight problem (there would be 8 servers; each weighing about 1 ton) the total plan has been changed and now there is a swap between the third floor where the server and other IT equipments were originally planned and the ground floor. He said that a tender will be issued on the basis of revised plan.

 

The Director requested the Director Projects to do user need analysis before issuing tender documents and also said that all users signature should be obtained before the tender is issued. 

 

1.7              Two faculty rooms with furniture to be ready by January 25, 2010:  The Director Projects reported that one faculty room has been handed over.  The other room which is students lounge will be ready by March 20, 2010.

 

1.8              Insurance Claim:   The Director Projects reported that he had two meetings with the Deputy Manager, NIC and as s result of these meetings the file was recovered which has been submitted to the Zonal Manager. He said that he would schedule a meeting with him on his first availability.

 

1.9              NBC to prepare a drawing on scale with completion dates of the projects assigned to them:  The Director Projects reported that drawings have been received but they have to rework on the completion dates.

 

The Committee requested the Director Projects to email the drawings to the Executive Committee.

 

1.10          Departmental Manual: The Director Projects reported that because of other priority work the manual could not be completed.  He said that he would need at least 6 more weeks to complete the manual.

 

1.11          Clay models of the buildings: The Director Projects reported that the Architects sent back the work order as the terms of payment were not acceptable to them.  He said that they have given them another work order and have asked them to send time line.   

 

1.12          The clay models already designed should be placed at the Main Campus: The Director Projects reported that because of other priority, work on overall model could not be initiated.

 

1.13     Extension of existing server room at City campus Admin Building: The Director Projects reported that due to heavy weight of the servers, it is being shifted to the ground floor.

 

DECISION

 

1.14     The Committee noted the up-date given by the Director Projects.

 

1.15     The Committee decided to cancel the contract of extension of server room on the 3rd floor of existing block at Main Campus given to M/s B.G Construction.

 

1.16     The Committee approved the extension of FCS building by adding two wings in lieu of adding 4th floor on the FCS Building.

 

1.17     The Committee decided that the Director Projects would do user need analysis before issuing tender documents and obtain signatures of all users signature before he issues the tenders in future.

 

1.18     The Committee requested the Director Projects to email the drawings to the EC

 

ACTION

 

1.19     The Director Projects would initiate the process of extension of FCS building by adding two wings.

 

1.20     The Director Projects would do user need analysis before issuing tender documents and obtain signatures of all users signature before issuing the tender.

 

1.21     The Director Projects would email the drawings of the Projects given by NBC to the EC.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider email from Mr. Suleman Ghazi requesting the EC not to mark absent to those students who are called for interview for internship during class timing.

 

2.1              Mr. Suleman Ghazi, a student of IBA requested the EC not to mark them absent when they go for the interview during the time their classes are conducted because they would be going to more than 4 organizations to interviews for internship.  He requested that the students called for interview during class timings be marked present.

 

2.2              It was pointed out that 15% absences have been allowed for such purposes which the students should avail.

 

2.3              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui suggested that such requests should be decided on case to case basis and at the recommendation of the CDC, the Associate Dean may exercise his judgement. However, the CDC should send the recommendation along with complete information about the time, date, and organization where the student would be going for interview.

 

DECISION

 

2.4              The Committee agreed to the suggestion of Mr. Zafar Siddiqui as stated in 2.3 above.  

 

ACTION

 

2.5              The Coordinator CDC would convey the decision of the EC to Mr. Suleman Ghazi and refer such requests in future to the Associate Dean with complete information.

 

 

Item No. 3      To approve the cancellation of contract for extension of server room at third floor of Admin. Building, Main Campus.

 

3.1       This item was already discussed and decided under items 1.4 and 1.15.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve conditions of service for IBA employees.

 

4.1              The Director H.R. presented the proposed conditions of service for IBA employees.

 

4.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili pointed out that re-employment has not been covered in the proposed conditions.

 

4.3              The Director H.R. said that non faculty staff (contract or regular) will not be re-hired after the age of superannuation. From the point of view of building a strong institution, it was important to induct, groom and prepare young talent to carry IBAs mission forward rather than to re-hire retired people.

 

4.4              The Director clarified that they had decided that they would not go for re-employment for non-teaching positions because young people for such positions are easily available.  However, he said that under very exceptional circumstances and on technical ground requests for re-employment may be considered. This would be an exception to the policy.

 

4.5              The Director requested the EC and specially the Controller of Examinations to do succession planning for those who are going to retire in the near future.  He said that it is the responsibility of the head of respective departments to ensure adequate succession planning for their departments.

 

DECISION

 

4.6              The Committee approved the conditions of service for IBA employees as presented by the Director H.R.

 

ACTION

 

4.7       The Director H.R. would present the policy before the A&FC for approval.

Item No. 6      To consider proposal from the Director Finance for Standard Fee Schedule.

 

6.1       The Director Finance stated that concessional fee structure has been abolished for new students effective from July 2009 but those students who were enrolled under concessional fee programme before July 2009 will continue to pay concessional fee till completion of their respective programs i.e. by Spring 2012. He further stated that as per record the last batch of concessional program of regular (MORNING) students will pass out in 2012.  He presented the following details of students under concessional fee structure:

 

             Program             Enrolment    Program          No. of            Final/Pass  

                                        Semester       Duration        students       Out semester

 

         BBA                     Fall 2008       4 Years             333               Spring 2012

         MBA Morning     Fall 2008       2 Years             103               Spring 2010

         MBA Evening      Fall 2008       3 Years             198               Spring 2011

 

6.2    To streamline the fee structure across IBA, the Director Finance suggested that they should allow these students to complete their courses within stipulated time. In particular the evening students should be allowed to complete their courses by Spring 2011, as normally evening student takes more than 5 years to complete evening program. He said that if the above suggestion is considered by Executive Committee then they would notify to all concessional students to clear their courses within the stipulated time.  He further said that if the remaining courses are not cleared within stipulated time, the standard fee shall be applicable afterwards. Thus in future IBA will have one STANDARD FEE SCHEDULE for all IBA students i.e. Morning, Evening, City Campus or Main Campus.

 

DECISION

 

6.3              The Committee approved the suggestion of the Director Finance and decided to allow one years grace period to the evening program students by allowing them to complete their courses by Spring 2012.

 

ACTION

 

6.4       The Director Finance would present the proposed changes in the concessional fee program and introduction of Standard Fee Schedule for all IBA students to the Board through A&FC. 

 

Item No. 7      Any other matter with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Policy on Maintenance of Accounts

 

7.1              The Director Finance presented a policy governing maintenance of books of accounts including bank accounts for the purpose of carrying out IBA operations. He stated that the policy has been prepared to ensure that the Institute maintains adequate financial controls over its financial and accounting functions through the creation of a formal process for establishing and maintaining Institute accounts as per International Accounting Standards as applicable in Pakistan.

 

7.2              The Director Finance also proposed in the policy that a bank account owned in the name of IBA may only be opened, maintained or closed with written approval of the official authorized by the BoG i.e. Dean & Director, IBA. He also proposed that separate bank accounts to fulfil specialized banking or operating needs for institutional or departmental operating purposes shall also be authorized to the Dean & Director, IBA.  

 

7.3              The Director suggested to include in the policy that the Dean & Director will report regularly to the Board about all the new accounts opened in the name of IBA.

 

DECISION

 

7.4              The Committee approved the Policy on Maintenance of Accounts as presented by the Director Finance with the addition of 7.3 above.

 

ACTION

 

7.5              The Director Finance will present the policy on Maintenance of Accounts to the A&FC for approval.

d)     Renovation & Refurbishment of existing Library at IBA Main Campus  Final approval of Areas and Budget.

 

7.14     The Director Projects reported that the work for the Detailed Planning & Designing for the Renovation and Refurbishment of existing Library at IBA Main Campus was awarded to M/s. ICON by Audit & Finance Committee in its meeting held on July 19, 2007 and the conceptual layout plan was approved by the A&FC in its meeting held on January 26, 2008.  He said that as per the overall master schedule prepared for all the projects at IBA, renovation and          refurbishment of Library should have commenced in October 2009. Subsequently this was delayed as Multi-purpose building, which was constructed solely for the purpose of shifting of the library, was occupied and used for the classes. Lately classes were moved to the Academic Block at Main Campus. However, area was then allocated to Programme Office and ICT Personnel. In the mean time, the original plan approved by the Audit & Finance Committee was reviewed by the Head Librarian and Dr. Ahmed Ali Shah, Chairman Library Committee who made certain changes in the interior planning of the library and requested the Architect to include stack areas for the books.  This was done by the Architects and the final plan was approved by Head Librarian in December 2009. The approved plan was sent to the Architect for finalization of schematic drawings, who submitted the same in 1st week of February 2010, wherein he provided the floor-wise area only.  After checking these drawings, observations of the Projects Office were sent on 15th Feb.2010 requesting the Architect to correct the drawings and resubmit those drawings.

 

7.15     The Director Projects stated that an in-house analysis of the area was done.  He presented details of the analysis. He said that according to the analysis the total built up area after renovation & refurbishment comes to 27,000 sft. whereas the present area is 12,338 sft. He said that n the post renovation, the area of the front arcade and back pond has also been included whereas in the pre-renovation area portion of arcade in front of Library is included.

 

7.16     The Director Projects said that taking off the two areas, effective gross area of library shall be 22,263 sft as against the present effective gross area of 11,138 sft. He said that the reading halls and periodical areas provided on the ground floor and first floor will be about 13,000 sft as against the present area of about 8,000 sft. As such there will be an increase of 5000 sft of space as desired by the Head Librarian. However, the stack area for the books both combined at ground and first floor will be 2065 sft as against the present stack area of 255 sft.

 

7.17     The Director Projects stated that the library building of LUMS at Lahore has been designed for 75,000 sft. And presently they are using 27,000 sft of space and the rest of the area is being utilized for other purposes.

 

7.18     The Director observed that because of digital library he does not think so much area is needed.

 

7.19     The Director Projects reported that the Librarian and the Chairman, Library Committee have recommended to include stack areas for the books.

 

DECISION

 

7.20     The Committee in principle approved the recommendation of the Director Projects to increase the construction area to 27,000 sft as against the existing area of approx. 12'500 sft.

 

7.21     The Committee further decided that the cost of the project should remain within +15% of the already approved budget of Rs.60.0 million excluding furniture cost and the ICT equipment cost.

 

7.22     The Committee requested the Director Projects to ask the Architects to use local materials / tiles to keep the cost within budget.
