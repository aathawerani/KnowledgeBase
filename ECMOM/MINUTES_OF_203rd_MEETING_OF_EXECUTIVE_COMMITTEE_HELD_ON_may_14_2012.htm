The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       Status Reports from:

 

Associate Dean, FBA:

 

1.1       Accreditation by AACSB: Dr. Nishat reported that he has sent application for membership but no reply has been received as yet. He said that Ms. Shehreena is following it up but currently she is on leave.

 

1.2       Associate Dean to get it audited as to how three hours papers is completed in 40 minutes: Dr. Nishat said that no further information was received from Mr. Khalili about this case he could not work on it.

 

            Mr. Mobin Khalili said that it was a paper of history and was taught by a teacher coming from Karachi University.

 

1.3       Proposal for MS leading to Ph.d in Economics and also Morning MS Program with teaching and research assistant stipends: Dr. Nishat reported that they have discussed this in the Academic Committee.

 

 

            The Dean & Director said that we have to use the MS students instead of BBA and MBA students (who have heavy workload) for Teaching Assistant and Research Assistant for both FCS and FBA. 

 

            The Dean & Director suggested to start one, and two credit hour courses. He said that we can bring in teachers who are experts in their respective fields but cannot come for a longer period of time.

 

1.4       Number of Term exams: Dr. Nishat said that the cases of taking only one term exam would be taken in the next Academic Board through Academic Committee.

 

1.5       Password passed on to T.A. by Faculty: Dr. Nishat said that in EMBA some faculty members have reportedly passed on their password to TA.

 

            The Dean & Director said that entry and posting of grades should be done by the faculty member only and not by the staff, teaching assistant or any other person.  He said that the program office staff can help the faculty but cannot do it by himself or herself.

 

1.6       Associate Deans to actively involve the Program Directors in the presentation by recruiters and they should be made responsible for bringing students to such events: Dr. Nishat said that they would coordinate through Faculty Coordinator, Ms. Ghulam Fatima.

 

            The Dean & Director said that the recruiters have reported that the students do not turn up on the scheduled time and keep them waiting. He said that Program Directors have to ensure punctuality by the students. He further said that monthly schedule should be sent to the faculty members by CDC and faculty members be requested to block the days on which recruiters would be coming to give presentation.  CDC should also inform the Office of Associate Deans about the events. He further said that for major recruiters programs the Program Directors should also be present.  He asked the Director H.R. to convey the above decision to the CDC.

 

1.7       Split Ph.D program: Dr. Nishat said that a report from Dr. Nasser Ali Khan is due on Split Ph.D program.

 

The Dean & Director said that Dr. Nasser Ali Khan from HEC has stated that he has sent the report to Dr. Nishat.  On this Dr. Nishat said that he would check it.

 

Associate Dean, FCS:

 

1.8       Number of Term exams: Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that Technopreneurship, which has already been approved by Academic Committee, and Web Based Application Development will be reported to Academic Board.

 

1.9       Password passed on to T.A. by Faculty: Dr. Sayeed Ghani reported that this was further investigated and it was found that no FCS faculty were sharing their passwords with TAs.

 

1.10     Associate Deans to actively involve the Program Directors in the presentation by recruiters and they should be made responsible for bringing students to such events on time: Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that he has sent an e-mail to the Program Directors regarding this.

 

DECISION

 

1.11     The Committee noted and approved the up-dates.

           

ACTION

 

1.12     The Associate Deans would present a proposal to the Academic Board for starting one, and two credit hour courses.

 

 

Item No.2       To consider and approve demolition work at the City Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Scope of Work and the proposed Terms and Conditions for the bidders for the demolition of the old (not in use) building of KESC sub-station, a generator room and some temporary site offices, all located at the City Campus have been approved by TEC-PROJ and CPC. He said that that originally they had planned demolition of the existing sub-station only. However, on Director Projects’ request, the scope of demolition has been expanded to include the 100 kVA generator room, the 132 and 135 kVA generator pads and the adjacent temporary site offices. This will clear the site completely for the auditorium work.  He requested the Executive Committee to approve the demolition work and its Terms and Conditions.

 

DECISION

 

2.2       The Committee approved the Scope of Work and the proposed Terms and Conditions for the bidders for the demolition of the old (not in use) building of KESC sub-station, a generator room including the 100 kVA generator room, the 132 and 135 kVA generator pads and the adjacent temporary site offices all located at the City Campus.
