MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON OCTOBER 27, 2008 (NINETEENTH MEETING)

  1. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Chairman, CS& MIS
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                          Professor

  6. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                         H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      To consider email from Director and reply from the Controller of Examinations regarding students’ allowed absences.

1.1       As the Controller of Examinations could not attend the meeting, the item was deferred to the next meeting of the Executive Committee.

Item No. 2      To consider suggestion from Syed Sultan Raza, Faculty Member regarding Guest Speaker Session.

2.1              Mr. Sultan Raza in his email of October 16, 2008 stated that the current semester schedule provides exactly 28 teaching sessions for Monday and Thursday set of days; 30 for Tuesday and Friday; and Wednesday and Saturday sets of days.  He stated that there is no provision on Monday & Thursday set of days for “force majure” cancellation of any teaching session. Shortage of class room and class clashes, due to electives, is likely to cause unnecessary inconvenience to students at the eleventh hour. He proposed that all faculty members should be fore-warned and advised to hold an extra session, for all classes scheduled on Mondays & Thursdays, on Tuesdays when classes are mostly off after 1:15 p.m. Students attending such extra class may be exempted from compulsory attendance in “Distinguished Lecture Series”.  He stated that he had scheduled a combined Guest Speaker session of both sections of BBA-7 S.B.M. class in Room # 8-9 on Tuesday, October 30, 2008 from 1:30 p.m. to 2:45 p.m.  He requested that the above mentioned classes be exempted from any compulsory attendance in any other activity to be scheduled on October 30, 2008. 

DECISION

2.2              The Committee approved the request of Mr. Sultan Raza for exemption of students of BBA-7 (both sections) from compulsory attendance on any other activity scheduled on October 30, 2008.

2.3              The Committee decided that Students Counsellor would ensure that there is adequate number of students available in the Distinguished Lecture series after consulting the time table for the day.

2.4              The Committee further decided that the Program Offices would be instructed to accommodate the Distinguished Lecture Series timing and date in their respective time tables for Spring Semester 2009, preferably on Fridays when all classes end in the first half of the day.

ACTION

2.5              The Registrar will inform the Program Offices and Students Counsellor of the decisions recorded in 2.2, 2.3 and 2.4 above.

Item No. 4      To consider email of October 24, 2008 from Registrar regarding eligibility for awarding degrees in Convocation 2008.

4.1              The Registrar in his email of October 24, 2008 stated that the program offices have submitted lists of the graduates on whom the degrees will be conferred in the Convocation 2008 scheduled on November 29, 2008. He said that those who have a course deficiency or failure in comprehensive exams would obviously not be included in the final list to be printed in the form of the booklet. However, he wanted clarification about those students who have certain dues outstanding against them. Some of them were on partial scholarship whereas others were not awarded any scholarships. He requested the Executive Committee to decide whether a person is eligible to have his / her degree conferred in the forthcoming convocation even when he / she cannot obtain final clearance.

4.2              The Director’s view was that in the first instance we should contact such students or their parents requesting them to clear the outstanding dues as the degree of their child may be withheld. If this does not work we should let them attend the Convocation and get conferment but the actual degree would be withheld until they clear the dues. Nevertheless the Committee realized that many students get proper jobs on the basis of transcripts and do not care for the degrees. This, as such, may not be sufficient motivator.

4.3              The Registrar further said that the Program Offices have been requested to make final list because the list is to be sent for printing but he has not yet received the list and is getting late.

4.4              The Registrar further suggested that we should take stringent action against those graduates who are working but not paying the dues and we should be lenient for those who are studying and are not able to pay.

DECISION

4.5       The Committee agreed to the views expressed by the Director in his email and decided that the Director Finance would issue letters to all those who are to clear the dues.

4.6       The Committee further decided that from next year those graduates who are to clear dues will not be conferred degrees until they clear their dues.

4.7       The Committee requested the Director Finance to send reminders / update of dues status to the concerned students / graduates on quarterly basis.

Item No.  5     To consider email of October 25, 2008 from the Registrar on the subject of “Opinion on the Proposal of FBR”.

5.1       The Registrar stated that Ms. Azra Mujtaba, Chief of HRM Wing of Federal Board of Revenue requested that the classes for the new entrants to the IBA should commence from last week of March instead of the beginning of January. This, she said, was necessitated because of the training activities of their newly recruited officers in the academy followed by some experience in their respective institutions till the end of February. He requested the Executive Committee to consider her request.

DECISION

5.2              The Committee decided that Ms. Azra Mujtaba, Chief of HRM Wing of FBR be informed that the proposed date for commencement of classes do not fit in our academic plan and to request them to change their own schedule if possible.

ACTION

5.3              The Registrar will communicate the decision to Ms. Azra Mujtaba, Chief of HRM Wing of FBR.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      Gold Medal Award for Marketing Research

7.1              Dr. Mohammad Nishat presented the recommendation of the Academic Committee for the award of Gold Medal in Marketing Research sponsored by M/s. H. Aftab Associates. He said that in order to formulate / suggest a policy / criteria for award of Gold Medal to students of Marketing Research at IBA, the Academic Committee discussed the status of Marketing Research courses with Chairperson Marketing Department. After discussion the Academic Committee recommended that:

i)        The only course which could fulfil the Marketing Research curriculum requirements for Gold medal award is “Advanced Marketing Research for 2007 academic year.

ii)   The Gold Medal should be given on the basis of highest marks in course work examination as well as the marks in research report. The research report should be mandatory for this course. Evaluation should be done by a panel on the basis of research report.  However, as research reports are not available for year 2007 for panel assessment, total marks in the Advanced Marketing Research may be used as a basis for Gold Medal Award.

7.2              Dr. Mohammad Nishat also mentioned that the members of the Academic Committee had reservations about the limited number of courses offered in Marketing Research and their coverage to boost Marketing Research.

DECISION

7.3              The Committee decided that a students who secures the highest marks in the Advance Marketing Research will be entitled to Marketing Gold provided he / she has a minimum CGPA of 3, has not received a minimum “C” grade in any course and has obtained a minimum “A-“ in Marketing 400, and Marketing Practices in Pakistan (519).

7.4              The Committee further decided that if a student who has highest grade / marks in Advanced Marketing Research but does not meet the eligibility criteria mentioned in 7.3 above, will not get Gold Medal. 

a)       Monthly Progress Report in Brief for October 2008 presented by Acting Director Projects.

7.5              The report included status of 500 KVA Generator for City Campus for which it was stated that re-tendering was to be done. 

7.6              Regarding Video Conference Room at the City Campus the Acting Director Projects stated that the HEC contractors are not responding. It would also save costs related to chowkidars, cooks, sanitary workers etc.  He said that the estimated cost for 10 rooms would be around Rs.4 to Rs.4.5 million.  However, he said that this is not budgeted.

7.11          The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmad to submit a proposal for addition of 10 rooms in existing Girls Hostel (A) in the next meeting of the Audit & Finance Committee of the IBA.
