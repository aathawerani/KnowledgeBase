MINUTES OF THE SPECIAL MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON WEDNESDAY, OCTOBER 5, 2011

 

A special meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Wednesday, October 5, 2011 at 11:00 a.m. at Conference Room, IBA Main Campus to discuss the recommendations of the Technical Committee which was assigned the following tasks by the Executive Committee:

 

·         Review recommendations of the Grievance Committee and assess the financial and related implications of their implementation.

·         Evaluate possible options for introducing a “One Staff Concept” for IBA employees by dissolving different categories including Options A, B, and Contract.

           

The following were present in the EC meeting:

 

1.      Dr. Ishrat Husain                                   Dean & Director

2.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                             Associate Dean, BA

3.      Dr. Sayeed Ghani                                   Associate Dean, FCS

4.      Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN                Registrar

5.      Mr. Mobin Khalili                                     Controller of Examinations

6.      Mr. Ali Taha                                          Director Finance

7.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                       Director Projects

8.      Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                               Director CED

9.      Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                               Director H.R.

 

 

Mr. Moeid Sultan, General Manager Finance, and Mr. Adnan Hameed, Manager H.R. attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

1.1       The Director H.R. before presenting the findings and recommendations of the Technical Committee, thanked the Technical Committee for working out the financial implications of the benefits recommended by the GC for the IBA employees and for the recommendations including two options on Uniform IBA Scale. The Director H.R. presented side by side the recommendations of the Grievance Committee on the charter of demands submitted by the employees and the recommendations of the Technical Committee.  Following is the summary of the presentation/recommendations:

 

1.2       House Rental Ceiling:  House Rental Ceiling may be given to Option ‘A’ employees as per their BPS grade entitlement. A formal policy has to be developed.  Estimated yearly financial impact is of Rs.2.5 million. Approval from A&FC and BoG would be required before implementation.

 

1.3       Discrepancies in IBA Medical Policy – Deduction Schedule:  Deduction schedule for different hospitals to be removed.  

 

1.4       OPD for in-service and retired employees: Emergency medical treatment reimbursement to be allowed to employees sustaining injury / accident during office hours or while on official duty.

 

1.5       Loan Schemes (House Loan, Motorcycle Loan, Car Loan: Motorcycle loan may be provided to Security Guards.  Formal policy document to be developed and approved by competent authorities.

 

1.6       Loan for Employees Housing Schemes: Application for land for Employees Housing Scheme has been submitted by IBA to the Governor House.

 

1.7       Overtime – different formula for Option ‘A’ and Contract employees: Overtime of Option ‘A’ employees may be calculated on their Gross Salaries.  Estimated yearly financial impact of this change is Rs.568,369/- Approval of the A&FC/BoG to be obtained.

 

1.8       Promotion Policy: Grievance of Option ‘A’ employees: Grievances of Option ‘A’ employees upgraded on the basis of seniority were reviewed and necessary adjustments made to streamline these grades.

 

            Mr. Mobin Khalili observed that there is a difference between promotion and up-gradation.  GC is recommending promotion of those employees who have for long not been promoted.

 

            The Dean & Director said that the Promotion Committee would now examine the cases which are fit for promotion.

 

1.9       Proposed Job Bands & Pay Structure Linked with BPS System: Position classifications to be expanded with requisite widening of the job scales in line with the recommendations of the Grievance Committee.

 

1.10     Reimbursement of expenses in case of Chronic Diseases: Not recommended by the Technical Committee.  Low income employees to be provided this facility from Welfare Fund.

 

            Mr. Mobin Khalili insisted that instead of paying from welfare fund for chronic diseases, reimbursement may be allowed because there may be very few employees who may have chronic diseases but for them this may be a great relief.

 

            Decision on this issue was deferred. The Director H.R. would get a survey conducted to assess the number of employees suffering from life threatening chronic diseases.

 

 1.11    Enhancement of Medical Allowance: Option ‘A’ employees are already getting minimum medical allowance of Rs.2,000/- per month plus an additional Medical Supplement by IBA. No further enhancement was recommended.

 

1.12     Medical coverage after retirement: Government has announced special increase in the pension of retired employees to compensate for medical treatment after retirement.

 

1.13     Eligibility for Medical Benefit: No change should be made in the existing medical policy.  

 

1.14     Retirement Policy: No change in the proposed Retirement Rules.

 

1.15     The Director H.R. presented two options towards proposed Uniform IBA Scale for consideration by the Executive Committee. Under Option 1 of Uniform IBA Scale following actions were suggested to be taken:

 

·         The term “Contract Employees” to be removed for all staff and employees will be referred as “IBA Regular Employees”.

·         Annual renewal of contract would be removed

·         Employees in Projects and ICT departments and other temporary positions to remain on contract.

·         Option ‘A’ employees will be given an option to opt for Uniform IBA Scale. It will be explained that the IBA scales are higher than those of Government and are likely to remain favourable in the future. The Government is facing serious financial difficulties which is not the case for IBA.

·         Contract, Option ‘B’ and those Option ‘A’ employees who opt for this offer will also be assigned Job Band titles as per their equivalent BPS grade or evaluated whichever is higher.

·         All government related benefits of Option ‘A’ employees will freeze as of the effective date of announcement of this scheme.  However, they would be entitled for pension commutation and pension on retirement based on the frozen basic pay.

·         Option ‘A’ employees opting for this option, will be entitled to (a) Annual performance increments based on their gross pay, and (b) Provident Fund membership @5% of gross pay, with matching employer contribution.

·         All IBA rules and regulations will be applicable to these employees

·         Option ‘A’ employees not opting for this option will continue to avail Government announced increments and benefits (including proposed House Rental Ceiling) and will be allowed to retain their job titles.

·         The pay scale of Option ‘B’ and contract employees will remain unchanged.

 

1.16     The Director H.R. said that under the proposed Option 1, there would be no financial impact except Rs.1.3 million per year which is subject to all 81 Option ‘A’ employees availing the proposed Option 1.

             

1.17     The Director H.R. then presented the main features of the proposed Option 2 which is BPS based option. This included the following:

 

·         All employees will be “Regular IBA Employees” with no condition of annual contract renewal.

·         Employees will be placed in BPS grades and designated in equivalent Job Bands.

·         Contract employees will also be assigned equivalent BPS grade or evaluated whichever is higher and designated in equivalent job bands.

·         Employees will have two salaries components – government pay, and professional supplement.

·         Employees will be given annual salary raises equal to government announced increases

·         They will be given pension benefits on retirement and Provident Fund according to the government rules.

·         Frozen Professional Supplement of Option ‘A’ employees will unfreeze.

·         The total financial impact of this arrangement will increase the annual payroll by approximately Rs.16.77 million.

 

1.18     The Director H.R. said that under the proposed Option 2 the confusion on government rules and IBA rules applicability will persist. Moreover, reversing the recently launched PF scheme of dual contribution will be legally difficult to withdraw.

 

1.19     The Dean & Director said that we must have one employment policy. Option ‘A’ employees should be given a chance to absorb in the new IBA Scale in which their increments will be performance based and deferred benefits, which are quite substantive compared to government scheme, will also be applicable.  He said that for contract employees uncertainty about job security is the major concern which will be removed and they will get deferred benefits also.

 

1.20     Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know if the proposed IBA Scale would be acceptable to the masses.

 

1.21     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that he cannot give different recommendations in GC and EC. He said that the Grievance Committee had recommended that the contract employees be regularized, the professional supplement of Option ‘A’ employees should be unfrozen, and the new Provident Fund Scheme should be abolished.

 

1.22     The Dean & Director said that the situation has to be examined on ground reality.  Salaries of Option “A” employees have been increased by 50%. The interpretation of Grievance Committee report is that sense of job insecurity should be removed and retirement benefits should be given.

 

1.23     Mr. Mobin Khalili said that he has not withdrawn from his original recommendation of the Grievance Committee. The EC can accept them or make some other recommendations.

 

1.24     The Dean & Director said that the proposed IBA Scale would redress most of the grievances of IBA employees.  He clarified that persons in IT and Projects departments will remain on contract because in 2014 when the projects are completed the projects will be closed. He agreed that efficient workers will be absorbed in the Maintenance Department.

 

DECISION

 

1.25     The Executive Committee endorsed the recommendations noted above at 1.2 to 1.14 and also endorsed the proposed Option 1 of the Uniform IBA Scale and recommended to the A&FC for consideration and approval.  The recommendations were based on the analysis that Option 1 is beneficial for the employees as well as the organization and has lesser financial impact. Mr. Mobin Khalili recorded his note of dissent. 

 

1.26     The Executive Committee once again recorded its appreciation for the report prepared by the Grievance Committee under the chairmanship of Mr. Mobin Khalili.  The Committee also thanked the Technical Committee for the review and assessment of financial and related implications on the implementation of the GC report. 

 

ACTION

 

1.27     The Director H.R. would present the recommendations of the EC to the A&FC for consideration / approval.

 

1.28     The Director H.R. would get a survey conducted to assess the number of employees suffering from life threatening chronic diseases. Based on the findings she will give her recommendations to the EC on reimbursement of expenses in case of chronic diseases.      

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
