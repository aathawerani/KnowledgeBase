MINUTES OF 303RD MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, JUNE 30, 2014

The 303rd meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, June 30, 2014 at 9:00 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
3. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
4. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
5. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz Director Projects
6. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
7. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

Mr. M. Sohail Khan, Manager Purchase & Stores, Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed, Manager Operations & Maintenance, Mr. Mahmood Ali, Senior Manager Projects, and Mr. Khalid Rishi, Manager Security attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 Recommendation for amendment in Item No.1 of the 284th meeting of EC held on February 17, 2014.

1.1 Mr. M. Sohail Khan, Manager Purchase & Stores referred to item # 1: ‘To Consider & Approve the Standardization of Electrical Items for Maintenance Department’ discussed in 284th meeting of Executive Committee wherein it was decided vide Point # 1.8 to prequalify the four cable manufacturer i.e., Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables & Universal and issue tender. He submitted that they have to adopt prequalification process as per SPPRA if they consider the decision. Hence not only the identified four manufacturers, other manufacturers i.e., Million Cables, HM Cables etc would also apply and they would be compelled to prequalify them. While Manager Operations & Maintenance intends to select only Pakistan Cable as standard for IBA use due to quality and standardization reason. Mr. Sohail Khan suggested to amend the decision of 284th meeting of EXCO vide Point #1.8 and instead of using the words of “prequalify & tender” just use the text of Point # 1.7 i.e. “The Committee approved the proposal of Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed for brand names of .......”.

1.2 Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed said that the EC has recommended to prequalify Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables, and Universal but the Purchase Department has difficulty because there is a complete process of prequalification which they have to follow. The Director Projects said that the cables that have been used in IBA projects are mostly from the above mentioned four manufacturers. The Dean & Director said that for repair and maintenance we will use the same cable which has been installed in our premises and therefore it is logical to prequalify only the above four companies. The maintenance staff have the responsibility to follow the same pattern. If the cable of Pakistan Cables is to be replaced it is to be replaced with Pakistan Cable. The Director Projects clarified that the four cable manufacturers have been pre-qualified and selected through the Consultants on product merit.

1.3 The Dean & Director said that as long as those companies which have been screened by experts / consultants are pre-qualified through an open, competitive process there should not be any problem. The Director Projects said that he can give in writing that the cables used in IBA Projects including External Development were based on Consultants specifications. The Consultants had prequalified the four and the existing network of cables depends on these four companies and their replacement should be from them not from others.

1.4 Mr. M. Sohail Khan requested that similar specifications / identification of manufacturers should be made for paints. The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to provide the same.

DECISION

1.5 It was decided that the Director Projects would send a note to the Manager Purchase stating that the cables used in IBA Projects including External Development project were purchased from Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables & Universal. These four companies (Pakistan Cables, Newage, Pioneer Cables & Universal) were pre-qualified and selected by the Consultants. For repair and maintenance, cables from the same companies should be used. Similarly the Director Projects would send the specifications / identification of paint manufacturers given by the Consultants.


Item No. 2 To consider and approve the provision of wire fencing around bulk water supply and bulk power supply at the IBA.

2.1 The Registrar said that the staff town is spread over 14 acres, and it has a perimeter which runs around staff town which is almost 8 KM long. He said that the bulk water supply and bulk power supply are just within the boundary wall of IBA Staff Town. He suggested that in order to provide security, barbed wire be placed around the wall of bulk water supply and bulk power supply. The total length is 550 rft for the bulk water supply and 310 rft for bulk power supply. The Director Projects said that the existing wall does not have foundation. Mr. Mahmood Ali suggested that they have to erect a column in between the wall at certain intervals. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that there would be block masonary so that the wall is raised upto 8 feet and then a 2 feet barbed wire should be placed. The Registrar said that although he has not obtained the estimate, the rough idea is that it would cost around Rs.1 million.

2.2 The Dean & Director said that we have to protect the key installations points and for night one dedicated guard should be deputed for bulk power supply and bulk water power installations. He said that we have to protect all the installations. He inquired if City Campus, and Boys Hostel installations are secured. Mr. Khalid Rishi confirmed that they were secure. The Dean & Director advised to make a drill with staff and students for evacuation from the buildings in emergency.

DECISION & ACTION

2.3 The Committee approved the proposal of wire fencing around bulk water supply and bulk power supply. The Registrar would obtain estimate for the proposed wire fencing and get it approved by the Dean & Director.

Item No.4 To consider and approve the advance payment to M/s. Sheetal for material purchase and extension of time.

4.1 The Director Projects reported that the supply and fixing of furniture work was awarded to M/s Sheetal on November 13, 2013 at a cost of Rs.21,201,352. The total time duration for the subject work was 03 months as per the schedule of the completion of building works. Unfortunately, the building works could not be completed as per schedule, however the furniture vendor completed the 35% manufacturing since eight months ago and due to space limitation could not deliver the manufactured furniture in IBA premises. The Inspection of completed furniture was done jointly by IBA engineer and Architect at their workshop. He reported that the furniture vendor completed the work as per their time schedule except 400 chairs among which 200 were not finalized. The approval of reading chair was given by the head librarian on June 20, 2014.

4.2 The Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s Sheetal vide their letter claimed the overhead and profit of about Rs.4.5 million due to delay in receiving the completed furniture and delay in approval. They also requested to release an amount of Rs.4 million in order to further purchase of material. IBA Projects Department negotiated with the contractor in order to withdraw their claim against the release of an amount of Rs.4 million after submission of Insurance guarantee of an equal amount from their project cost.

4.3 The Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 28th June 2014 considered and approved the extension in time up till September 30, 2014 (7.5 Months) based on the merit of the case. The Committee also recommended the special advance to be paid subject to the amount to be decided by the Executive Committee against insurance guarantee.

4.4 The Director Projects presented the following summarized reasons for delay:

  • Incomplete / unfinished site.

  • No covered space was available to store finished furniture properly to avoid damage.
    The site was not handed over to M/s Sheetal for marking, layout and assembling of the onsite furniture including Electrical and data point.

  • Contractor was advised to schedule the supply of furniture according to completion of finish space so that furniture does not get damaged. Due to non-completion of the library building, area was not available.

  • Procedural delay in approval of mock up / shop drawings from the Architect and IBA.
    Revision in the Furniture design and due to this manufacturing activity was on hold.

4.5 The Dean & Director said that our objective is to get the work done and get the library ready in all respect. He said that we cannot penalize other sub-contractors because of the negligence and non-payment by the main contractor.

DECISION

4.6 The Committee approved Rs.4 million as advance payment to M/s. Sheetal for material purchase against insurance guarantee and allowed extension of time up till September 30, 2014 (7.5 Months).


Item No.5 To consider cancellation of bids for construction of clinic building.

5.1 The Director Projects reported that an advertisement for the pre-qualification of contractors for above subject works was published in daily newspapers on November 05, 2013. In response to the advertisement 20 contractors submitted their document and only 11 contractors met the evaluation criteria and stood qualified. Bid Invitation letters were sent to all qualified contractors.

5.2 The Bids were opened in front of all contractor representative and IBA officials. The amount of lowest Bidder was Rs.24,413,027/- which was higher than the budgeted amount. In order to reduce the project cost, Architect was advised to do value engineering to meet the budgeted cost. After value engineering Architect prepared revised BOQ with the engineering estimates of Rs.18.27 million.

5.3 Revised Bid Invitation letters were sent to those bidders who have participated in first bidding. M/s. Banu Mukhtar Contracting regretted to participate. The revised Bids were opened in front of all bidders representative and IBA officials. After detailed scrutiny it was found that 1st lowest M/s Hasscon is 11% lower than Engineering Estimate (Rs.18,272,101)

5.4 The Director Projects stated that the request for approval of award of work was submitted to all IBA Committees i.e PC, EC and A&FC. Two committees (PC and EC) approved the award of work to the lowest bidder. However the Audit and Finance Committee rejected the bids advising that the cost of construction was too high and that the clinic should not be that large. They further advised that the clinic be re-designed as a simple building with simple fittings and fixtures to bring down the cost. The Director Projects said that under this advice they need to go for re-designing of the building and will have to go for fresh tendering as it is not possible to make amendments in the existing plan. He reported that the Procurement Committee in its meeting held on 28th June 2014 approved the cancellation of the bids based on above reasons. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the cancellation of the bids of the Clinic.

5.5 The Director Projects said that the tender and redesigning will take time and it would not be completed by December 2014. The Dean & Director suggested to start the process of redesigning and tendering but the construction of clinic should start after Convocation as he did not want any site showing an incomplete structure.

DECISION

5.6 The Committee approved the cancellation of bids for construction of clinic building.
