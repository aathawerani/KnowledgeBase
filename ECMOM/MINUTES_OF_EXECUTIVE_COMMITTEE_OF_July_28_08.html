MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON JULY 28, 2008 (SIXTH MEETING)

The sixth meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, July 28, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Professor
  2. Mr. Inayat N. Din                             Registrar
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Chairman, CS & MIS
  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations
  5. Mr. Moeid Sultan                              Acting Head of Finance & Accounts
  6. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                       H.R. Manager

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Proposal from students on IBA Orientation Week

1.1       A proposal from four students of BBA-V-I regarding Orientation Week was presented to the Executive Committee through Director IBA for consideration. The students who had submitted the proposal were also invited to present their proposal to the Executive Committee which they did.

1.2       The purpose of the proposed Orientation Week, according to the students was more than just an ice-breaking ceremony. It was meant to inform new students what IBA is and what makes it more special than all the other business institutions. Providing practical information, showing fresh entrants what they can expect in their courses and assignments, introducing them to their teachers and administrators on an informative note; introduction to the societies, hostel, and miscellaneous events would be part of the Orientation Week.

DECISION

1.3       The Executive Committee appreciated the idea of Orientation Week. However, the Committee suggested these students to involve Students Counsellor and student bodies such as BASC, BITS, ESF etc. for the sake of perpetuity even after these self-motivated students were to move out of IBA. They were also suggested to consider a trade-off between the additional information suggested to be imparted to new comers and the costs. The final recommendation should come through the Students Counsellor and it would be made a part of the responsibilities of student bodies.

Item No. 2      Request for Transfer from City Campus to Main Campus

2.1       Mr. Nadeem Yahya, father of Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem who was offered admission in BBA at City Campus requested for transfer of her daughter to the IBA Main Campus as she being a handicapped only operates on a wheelchair. He agreed to pay the City Campus fee for his daughter and not to apply for scholarship throughout her stay at the Main Campus. 

DECISION:

2.2       The Committee decided that although Ms. Mahnoor Nadeem does not meet the IBA Main Campus cut off point, she may be allowed transfer from City Campus to Main Campus as a special case purely on humanitarian ground as there is no ramp at the IBA City Campus to move on wheel chair and this will not be quoted as a precedent.

Item No. 3      To consider requests from Mr. Ali Raza Khan and Ms. Aasma Habib Shaikh for transfer from MBA-MIS (Evening) to MBA-MIS(Morning) program.

3.1       The requests from Mr. Ali Raza Khan and Ms. Aasma Habib Shaikh who were offered admission in MBA-MIS(Evening) Program (which was their first choice) for transfer to MBA-MIS(Morning) Program was considered purely on merit as they met the cut off point of this program.

DECISION:

3.2       The Committee approved the requests of Mr. Ali Raza Khan and Ms. Aasma Habib Shaikh for transfer from MBA-MIS(Evening) to MBA-MIS(Morning) program on the basis of (1) the high cut-off score and (2) the fct that they were not employed at present.

Item No. 4      To consider note from Dr. Muhammad Nishat regarding request for transfer of Ms. Kiran Shahzad from LUMS to IBA starting Fall 2008.

4.1       Dr. Mohammad Nishat stated that Ms. Kiran Shahzad is a student of B.Sc (Hons.) in Accounting and Finance at LUMS and has applied for transfer to the BBA program of the IBA. He said that the transfer from LUMS to IBA is in accordance with the transfer policy of the Academic Board of the IBA. He said that out of 47 required courses for BBA, Ms. Kiran Shahzad has completed only 16 courses for which she could get credit, subject to approval by the Academic Board as per rule. He stated that she has completed four courses from BBA-I, five courses from BBA-III, and two courses from BBA-V.  He suggested that she may be allowed to join BBA-V where she may choose courses from BBA-I and BBA-III whichever may be possible and we may allow relaxation on sequencing of courses except the prerequisites. He said that she may take courses of BBA-II and BBA-IV in Summer semester, Evening or in subsequent semesters.

4.2              It was pointed out that the IBA students join 5th semester after completing 24 courses whereas Ms. Kiran Shahzad has completed only 16 courses which are not sufficient to complete 3rd semester.

DECISION

4.3       The Committee recommended to the Academic Board that Ms. Kiran Shahzad be allowed to take a maximum of six courses available to complete the course requirements of BBA-I, III & V following IBA rules for pre-requisites.  The actual semester placement will be decided in Spring semester 2008.

4.4       The Committee further decided that instead of entering her semester work into CMS which at this stage is not possible, a “Transfer Account” may be created or the record should be maintained manually till the semester she would be enrolled in would be decided.

4.5       The Committee also decided that full fee would be charged in transfer cases.

ACTION

4.6       The H.R. Manager would place the recommendation of the Executive Committee before the Academic Board for consideration / approval.

4.7       Subject to approval by the Academic Board, Dr. Sayeed Ghani would instruct concerned person to create a Transfer Account for such cases.

Item No. 5      Any other item.

a)        To consider the case of students transferred from one Campus to the other with reference to their ineligibility to apply for financial assistance in case of any change in their family financial status.

5.1       Mr. Inayat N. Din referred to the case of Mr. Zaid Bin Shahid who is unable to pay the fee due to financial crises.

5.2       Mr. Inayat N. Din said that since an earlier decision of the Academic Board disallows all transfer cases to apply for any financial assistance during their period of study at IBA, the Executive Committee considering the spirit of such decision to be discouraging such transfers, may recommend to the Academic Board to allow such students the permission to apply for Financial Assistance if there is serious change in the family’s financial strength.

DECISION

5.3       The Committee agreed with Mr. Inayat N. Din and recommended to the Academic Board to allow such students the permission to apply for Financial Assistance if there is serious change in the family’s financial strength.

ACTION

5.4       The Manager H.R. will place the recommendation of the Executive Committee to the Academic Board.

b)        Payment to faculty involved in paper setting, conducting and grading of papers for English and Maths to be given to new comers to determine the need of remedial courses.

5.5       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that as decided in the Academic Board, tests of English and Maths of three hours duration each will be given to all the incoming students on July 30 and 31, 2008 respectively. He said that all the arrangements for the test, grading of papers and determining need of remedial course(s) are to be made in a limited time for which the faculty members involved need to be compensated for the extra effort and additional work.      

DECISION

5.6       The Committee decided that faculty members would be compensated in accordance with the rates applicable for paper setting, invigilation and grading of papers of affiliated institutes.

5.7            The Committee also considered the extra efforts put in by the support staff and the program offices and recommended additional motivation for them as well.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
