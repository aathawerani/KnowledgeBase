MINUTES OF THE 68TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, OCTOBER 5, 2009 

The 68th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, October 5, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                         Director

2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                Associate Dean

3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate        Dean

4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer       PN Registrar

5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                       Controller of Examinations

6. Mr. Ali Taha                               Director Finance

7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz         Director Projects

8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                 Director I.C.T

9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                 Director HR

 

Before discussing the agenda items, the Director thanked the Registrar and his team for very decently organizing the IBA Program at HEJ.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1     To consider and approve award of work for construction of students’ canteen and lounge at City Campus.

 

1.1                The Director Projects reported that the Audit & Finance Committee has already approved the award of work for detail designing, tender documents and top supervision of students’ canteen and lounge at City Campus to M/s ICON and detail supervision and project management work to M/s Integ. He said that the detail design and tender documents were completed in April 2009. After preparation of Tender Documents and drawing, tender documents were issued to the following five pre-qualified contractors on May 2, 2009:

 

1.      M/s. Project Managers Constructor

2.      M/s.  SteelMan

3.      M/s.  Prefab Systems

4.      M/s.  Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.

5.      M/s.  Al-Shafi Enterprise.

1.2       The Director Projects informed the Committee that the bids were opened on June 2, 2009, and the lowest bidder quoted an amount of Rs.8,752,782. The recommendations were presented to Executive Committee in its meeting held on June 29, 2009 to award the work to the lowest bidder. The Executive Committee decided to advise the Architects to review the design in order to reduce the cost and bring it up to Rs.5.0 million for the student canteen and lounge. Accordingly, he said that the redesigning process was initiated and revised design and tender documents were completed in August 2009 by the Architect. In the new tenders air-conditioning was deleted too. The revised estimate amounted to Rs.5.36 million. He said that invitations for collection of Tender documents were sent to following seven pre-qualified contractors:

 

1.      M/s. Prefab Systems

2.      M/s. Abaseen Construction Co.

3.      M/s. A.H Construction Company.

4.      M/s. Gulf Construction Company

5.      M/s. Ziauddin Ahmed & Co.

6.      M/s. Al-Shafi

7.      M/s. Project Managers, Engineers, Fabricator & Constructor.

 

1.3       Five contractors collected the tender documents but only following three bidders submitted their bid on September 19, 2009.

 

1.      M/s. Prefab Systems

2.      M/s. Abaseen Construction Company

3.      M/s. A.H Construction Company.

 

1.4       The Director Projects reported that the bids were opened on same day i.e. on September 19, 2009 in front of IBA, INTEG officials and contractor representatives and the following were the standings of the contractors:

 

1.      M/s. Prefab Systems                                 Rs.5,831,687               1st Lowest

2.      M/s. Abaseen Construction Company      Rs.6,796,552               2nd lowest

3.      M/s. A.H Construction                             Rs.7,328,839               Highest

 

1.5       The Director Projects said that the Architect’s estimate is Rs.5.3 million. The lowest bid is 9.4% higher than the Architects’ estimate.  He said that the bids were sent to Project Manager M/s. INETG for scrutiny of Bids on September 25, 2009. Project Manager M/s INETG sent their report on September 30, 2009 after detail scrutiny of bids. The Director Projects also presented the Report from Project Manager M/s. Integ.

 

1.6      Based on the recommendation of Project Manager M/s INETG, the Director Projects requested to Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest bidder M/s Prefab Systems.

 

1.7              To a question about the time line, the Director Projects informed the Committee that M/s. Prefab Systems have given four months time to complete the project.

 

1.8              The Registrar suggested that the construction material should be placed in such a way that the parking space is not further congested.  He suggested that some space for faculty parking should also be marked.

 

DECISION

 

1.9              The Committee approved the recommendation to award the work of construction of students’ canteen and lounge at City Campus to the lowest bidder namely M/s. Prefab Systems.

 

1.10          The Committee requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, to request the PMA to provide parking space to IBA.

 

ACTION

 

1.11          The Director Projects would request Mr. Shameem Ahmed to request the Pakistan Medical Association (PMA) to provide parking space for IBA faculty and students.   

 

1.12          Mr. Mobin Khalili would arrange parking space for IBA faculty.

 

 

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the ICT Code of Conduct.

 

2.1              The Director ICT introducing the IBA ICT Code of Conduct said that the document is prepared to give an idea of what is expected from IT Infrastructure Department of IBA and what is required from the users in return to ICT department of IBA.

 

2.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that instead of naming it as “ICT Code of Conduct” it should be named as “ICT Policies and Procedures”.  He further suggested that a one or two pager information on the ICT Policies and Procedures should be prepared for faculty, students and staff.

 

2.3              The Director agreed with Mr. Mobin Khalili that a one or two pager handout should be provided.  He also suggested that we should have a workshop for the users for their training on IT.  He requested the Director ICT to prepare a training calendar for the users. Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that there should be a continuous training.

 

2.4              The Director ICT said that they have asked the Technology Partners to provide training to the users which they (the Technology Partners) have agreed to do. 

 

2.5              The Director H.R. advised to inform what changes the Director ICT is making in the proposed policy and circulate to all concerned.

 

2.6              The Director Projects observed that there was no mention of hardware, multimedia and video conferencing in the proposed policy.  To this, the Director ICT said that hardware has no concern with the users.  However, he said that he would include multimedia and video conferencing in the policy document.

 

 

DECISION

 

2.7              The Committee requested the Director ICT to present a one or two pager information highlighting the salient features of the policy for approval by the Executive Committee.

 

 

 

 

 

Item No. 3      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Installation of Split AC or Central AC

 

3.1              With reference to installation of Air-conditioners in the new projects it was suggested that instead of Central AC, Split ACs should be installed because they are relatively cheap and can be localised.

 

3.2              The Director said that in a compact building central AC will be economical but where arrangements are scattered, Split ACs will be more economical.

 

3.3              The Director IBA said that we should experiment and install the chiller we have ordered for the Academic Block. He said that if during six months it works trouble free and from users point of view there is no problem then we can go for the second chiller. He further said that if chiller is not recommended, we can go for package A.C.

 

3.4       The Director Projects informed that the package system as installed in the Academic Block and CCS building at the City Campus is working satisfactorily. He said that there have been problems at the Academic Block which had been addressed. Dr Sayeed Ghani informed that the A.C system at CCS building is all right.

 

3.5       The Director Projects further informed that the Air conditioning of Auditorium being a large space has been designed based on central A.C system as the room for Auditorium Chiller has already been built. Both the chillers will thus supplement each other and act as standby for each other.

 

3.6       The Director said that we should get the best solution which is economical and efficient. He said that they have to take decision for air-conditioning in the new Academic Block, Students Centre, and the Library.

 

3.7       The Committee decided that the Director Projects will consult with the Architects and the HVAC consultant and conduct the feasibility of using split units at the Library and the Admin Block as an alternate to Chiller based centralized air-conditioning system.

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Director Projects will consult with the HVAC consultant about the feasibility of using split type system as an alternate to Chiller based Central AC system.

 

3.9       The experience of the chiller installed at the Academic Block Main Campus and the Auditorium will be used to determine if it is the best option or not.

 

 

b)     Office space for ERP Staff.

 

3.10     The Director ICT stated that he needs office space for at least 5 to 6 ERP staff.

 

3.11     The Director suggested that multipurpose building can be used for that.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
