MINUTES OF 213TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD

ON MONDAY, JULY 30, 2012

 

The 213th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, July 30, 2012 at 9:00 a.m. at Societies Meeting Room, Adamjee Academic Centre, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

1.      Dr. Muhammad Nishat                                         Associate Dean, FBA

2.      Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                                Registrar

3.      Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                                   Director Projects

4.      Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                                               Director ICT

5.      Mr. Moeid Sultan                                                Acting Director Finance

 

Syed Fahim Uddin, Manager General Maintenance/Security attended the meeting on special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:      

 

 

Item No.1       To consider and approve GEM Consortium Payment.

 

1.1       Dr. Shahid Qureshi, Assistant Professor and Associate Director Center for Entrepreneurial Development, IBA in his note of July 25, 2012 stated that the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) is an international research effort to promote entrepreneurship implemented in more than 80 nations. The GEM studies are conducted under auspices of the Global Entrepreneurship Research Association which is a not-for-profit academic forum. Entrepreneurs and policy makers around the world are using the GEM results to create awareness and enhance learning about the entrepreneurial process with the aim of improving support for entrepreneurs and innovative business ventures.

 

1.2       He reported in his note that after securing Pakistan’s membership in this prestigious world forum of entrepreneurially conscious nations, GEM Pakistan project was launched in June 2010 under the sponsorship of IBA, Karachi. An in-depth analysis of the state of entrepreneurship and start-up business activity in Pakistan was carried out. This included a National Expert Survey and a comprehensive adult population survey (APS) throughout Pakistan to understand the entrepreneurial attitudes, activities and aspirations of the Pakistanis using a large representative sample.

 

1.3       He further reported in his note that IBA conducted the nationwide research activity for the year 2010 and 2011. In the year 2010 and 2011, the coordination fee was paid by USAID. But this time due to the non availability of USAID fund, IBA will have to pay the coordination fee to the GEM Consortium.  It was reported that there are following two parts of the GEM project:

 

1.      Survey cost  and Report preparation                               1.7 Million Pak Rupees 

2.      Coordination Fee to be paid to the GEM consortium            USD ($11,850)  

 

1.4       The approval for Survey cost and Report Preparation has already been given in the last EC meeting. Approval from EC for Coordination Fee to be paid to the GEM Consortium was requested.

 

1.5       Mr. Zafar Siddiqui, Director CED in his email addressed to the EC reported that in last two years, the coordination fee payment to GEM was arranged from USAID with the help of Mr. Sarfraz Mian. However, his efforts for this year funding arrangement did not materialize. He stated that this is an important area for IBA CED to rise in the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor List and thus IBA needs to pay its cost. It was also budgeted for the year 2012-13.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       The Committee approved the payment of Coordination Fee of US$11,850 to the GEM Consortium.

 

 

Item No. 2      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Tender for House Keeping and Janitorial Services.

 

2.1       The Registrar said that in 2009 the EC had agreed in principle that the house keeping and janitorial services should be outsourced. Accordingly, some janitorial staff including a messenger were engaged through MOP. The contract with MOP is reviewed every year.  He said that this year some new buildings, such as Aman CED Building, Tabba Academic Block, Alumni Student Centre, Library, Boys’ Hostel, Girls’ Hostel have been planned to be included in the contract. However, as advised by the Director Projects, SPPRA rule allows extension in scope of work upto 15% only in the existing contract whereas the proposed inclusion would far exceed the allowed scope of work and would require fresh tendering. He further said that the TEC has approved the tendering but in the CPC meeting it was suggested to seek conceptual approval from the EC for floating tender. He said that the Director H.R. had also observed that there were too many people required to be engaged through outsourcing and suggested to reduce the number and give a general term contract (without giving the number).

 

2.2       The Director Projects said that when we give tender we should find out minimum number of persons required so that we may quantify the price.  

 

2.3       The Manager General Maintenance/Security presented details of scope of work of both campuses along with proposed manning requirements.

 

2.4       Dr. Nishat wanted to know as to what is our benchmark i.e. the quality and its acceptance level. He said that our own team is expected to supervise the contractor so the monitoring (vigilance and control) should be very strong.

 

2.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan agreed with Dr. Nishat and added that we should include minimum acceptable number of persons for each building. He suggested that there should be a sheet showing the overall manpower requirements, the number of existing available janitorial staff of IBA, number of outsourced staff available, and the additional staff required to be outsourced for each building.

 

2.6       The Committee suggested that the IBA janitorial staff should be mixed with the outsourced staff. It was also suggested that only those buildings which are already completed or to be completed in the immediate future should be included in the scope of work.  

 

2.7       The Director Projects said that he had sent some comments on the tender documents submitted by the Maintenance Department but the comments have not been fully incorporated in the tender documents.  He particularly referred to the clause for Penalties in case of bad performance, for which he had suggested that the period should be extended from 90 days to one year. He also suggested that complete details of scaffoldings to be deployed on cleaning of facade, and the auditorium be provided. 

 

DECISION

 

2.8       The Committee in principle gave approval for floating tender for House Keeping and Janitorial Services.

 

ACTION

 

2.9       The Manager General Maintenance/Security would work out the overall manpower requirement, the existing IBA staff available, the already outsourced staff and the additional manpower to be acquired through outsourcing.

 

2.10     The Manager General Maintenance/Security would include only those buildings in the scope of work which are complete or to be completed very recently.

 

2.11     The Manager General Maintenance/Security would incorporate the comments on the tender documents suggested by the Director Projects.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
