MINUTES OF THE 97TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, APRIL 19, 2010

 

The 97th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, April 19, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director

  2. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Associate Dean

  3. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN            Registrar

  4. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations

  5. Mr. Ali Taha                                      Director Finance

  6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                   Director Projects

  7. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                           Director CED

  8. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                          Director H.R.

  9. Mr. M. Faisal Zia                                Senior Manager IT

 

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects attended the meeting on special invitation.

                                               

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Clarity on the additional designation of the Controller of Examinations – “Administrator City Campus” or “Coordinator City Campus”.

 

1.1       The Registrar stated that all the transport resources are under him. However, at City Campus it is looked after by Mr. Faisal Rizvi and is under the control of the City Campus Coordinator.  He further said that Mr. Mobin Khalili has prepared a SOP for transport which is perfect and he is grateful to him for that. But he said that there is a little problem of designation.  He said that as per the Support System Mr. Mobin Khalili has been designated as “Coordinator City Campus” whereas he wants to retain the designation of Administrator City Campus.

 

1.2              The Director wanted to know as to what has been approved by the Board.  To this the Registrar replied that the Board has approved the designation as Coordinator.

 

1.3              The Director said that the Transport is under the Registrar.  He said that they have matrix management as far as the City Campus is concerned. Staff from various departments working at the City Campus will also be reporting to Mr. Mobin Khalili but administratively they will be under the control of the respective heads of departments. The City Campus Coordinator will also write about their performance and this will be integrated into the Performance Evaluation Report finalized by the Head of Departments. He said that the name and designation are unimportant.   

 

1.4              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that as he has been managing the City Campus for over a number of years so he would like to continue with the designation he has been using.

 

1.5              The Director said that to him designations are unimportant but the powers given to individuals should be exercised. He further said that once the designations have been decided by the Board, he would not go again to the Board for such minor issues.

 

DECISION

 

1.6       It was clarified that Mr. Mobin Khalili would henceforth use the designation of “Coordinator City Campus” instead of “Administrator City Campus” but each department head would solicit the comments from City Campus Coordinator in respect to the staff working at the City Campus under them.  

 

ACTION

 

1.7       The Registrar would change the designation to read as “Coordinator, City Campus” in the SOP for Transport.

 

 

Item No. 2      Location of Visiting Faculty Hostel and New Girls’ Hostel in the Staff Colony, Main Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that the Executive Committee in its meeting held on November 16, 2009 had approved the location of the new underground and overhead water tanks in the Southern right hand side corner of the Staff Town.  The Consultant M/s. NESPAK prepared the designs accordingly.  

 

2.2              Mr. Shameem stated that M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative have recommended that the Visiting Faculty Hostel be located at the North-west corner for the following reasons:

 

·         The building becoming a part of the already developed society

·         Hierarchy

·         Privacy of the existing residences, and

·         Services already provided by the road

 

2.3              Mr. Shameem proposed the location of the new Girls’ Hostel in the South-west of the Staff Town for the following reasons:

 

·         It will be close to the existing girls’ hostel and the under-construction Girls’ Hostel Extension. The proximity will promote a community feeling among the students staying in the two hostels.

·         There is enough space for lavish landscaping and outdoor sports activities for the female students. The site offers an opportunity for maximum usability for the girls’ hostel.

·         There will be enough space available for expansion of hostel facilities in the future

·         The site is close to but quite separate from the existing faculty and staff residences.

 

2.4              Mr. Shameem Ahmed requested the Executive Committee to approve the location of the Visiting Faculty Hostel at the North-west corner and that of new Girls’ Hostel in the South-west of the Staff Town.

 

DECISION

 

2.5              The Committee approved the location of the Visiting Faculty Hostel at the North-west corner and that of new Girls’ Hostel in the South-west of the Staff Town as proposed by the Consultant and Mr. Shameem Ahmed.

.

 

Item No. 3      Renewal of SAP Connection with UCC QUT.

 

3.1       Mr. Imran Khan, Assistant Professor, FCS and Project Manager EAAP stated that they have to renew SAP connection with Queensland University of Technology, Australia (SAP University Competency Center) for next 12 months starting from April 1st. He presented the following details of the cost for renewal:

 

Package details                      Annual Cost (in Euro)

 

Economic Plus                                     3000

SCM system                                       2400

ABAP system                                      2400

IDES Dataset                                       400

AIS Dataset                                         400

 

Total                                       Euro    8600

 

3.2       Mr. Imran informed the Committee that they started this SAP University Alliance Program at IBA last year. Since last summer, they have conducted two training workshops (Fundamental of ERP using SAP, Fundamental of Accounting and Finance using SAP and Accounting Information System using SAP) on SAP for the faculty of IBA and 3 elective courses (ABAP Programming I, ABAP Programming II and Accounting Information Systems (AIS) using SAP) for the students of IBA at both undergraduate and graduate levels.

 

3.3       Mr. Imran further stated that the students have shown great interest in AIS course so they would be offering this course in Summer 2010 and Fall 2010. For this summer they have planned to train their faculty on SCM module of SAP so that they can offer a new elective on Supply Chain Management using SAP in the Fall semester.

 

3.4       Mr. Imran requested EC’s approval for the renewal of SAP UCC connection to further continue their plans.

 

3.5              The Director Projects suggested to present the proposal first to the Central Purchase Committee and then take it to the A&FC as it is a renewal case.

 

3.6              Mr. Zafar Siddiqui wanted to know as to how many people know about the SAP connection with UCC QUT.  He said that even he did not know about it.  He suggested to market it as a soft course and use executive education also so that additional revenue can be generated. He suggested Mr. Imran Khan to give a write up for the newsletter. He further suggested that whenever a new initiative is taken, the Public Affairs Department should be taken into loop.

 

3.7              The Director said that there should be shared responsibility with joint program of the FCS and ICT.

 

3.8              Mr. Faisal Zia reported that they are planning to have a CISCO alliance.

 

DECISION

 

3.9              The Committee referred the proposal to the Central Purchase Committee for consideration and if approved by the CPC then it should be presented to the A&FC.

 

ACTION

 

3.10          Mr. Imran Khan would take the proposal of renewal of SAP Connection to the CPC for consideration.

 

 

Item No. 4      Proposal for concessional fee to be increased by 40%

 

4.1              The Director Finance suggested that for the purpose of Budget for the year 2010-11, the following may be considered in light of the fact that IBA has abolished concessional fee effective from July 2009:

 

·         To reduce the gap between concessional fee and regular fee they may increase the tuition fee for concessional program students with 40% increase effective from Fall 2010. 

 

·         In previous years they used to increase tuition fee by 20% for concessional students.

 

·         If 40% increase is allowed then the fee structure would be:

 

Tuition Fee For regular students                                 Rs.102,600/- 

(i.e. after increase of 10% only)

    

Tuition fee for concessional students                         Rs.. 63,600/- 

(if increased by 40%)

The tuition fee for concessional student would be 62.00% of the regular fee. 

     

·         The minimum total revenue would be increased by Rs.8.5 million for the year.

 

·         The above increase in revenue would wipe out the deficit for BS programme where the fee would be only Rs.55,000/- per student per semester and expected number of students would be lesser than any other program.

 

DECISION

 

4.2              The Committee after detailed discussion decided to propose in the budget a 20% increase in concessional program and 10% increase for non-concessional program. 

 

ACTION

 

4.3              The Director Finance would propose in the budget for 2010-11 a 20% increase in concessional program and 10% increase for non-concessional program.

 

           

 

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)                  Storage of IT equipments ready for delivery.

 

5.1              Mr. Faisal Zia reported that some I.T. equipments have already been imported by the vendor and are ready for delivery. However, as the storage facility for such equipments is not yet ready at IBA, these will have to be stored at vendors’ warehouse. He said that the vendors will not charge for providing storage facility at their warehouse and they are also willing to take the responsibility of safe keeping, security and insurance of the equipments till such time they are handed over to IBA. However, he said that as per agreement IBA will have to make 60% payment on receiving the consignment which would simultaneously be stored in the vendors’ warehouse.

 

5.2       The Director said that it is to be ensured that no one else would have access to the place where the equipments would be stored and finally what has been stored exactly the same is received at the time of delivery. He also suggested to consult the Head of Internal Audit of IBA if payment can be made in this case where the possession of the equipments will remain with the vendors.

 

DECISION

 

5.3       Mr. Faisal Zia would consult with the Head of Internal Audit about 60% payment in the case where the possession of equipments will remain with the vendors.

 

5.4       Mr. Faisal Zia would also ensure that if the equipments are stored at vendors’ warehouse, the vendors accept full responsibility of the safe keeping, security and insurance and delivery of the same equipments as are stored.

 

b)                  Disposal of old PCs

 

5.5       The Registrar suggested that the old PC’s should be disposed off as early as possible.

 

5.6       The Director said that requests from staff have been received to sell those PC’s to them at discounted price. He said that the PC’s can be sold to the staff on “as is where is” basis and there would be no after sales support services.  The terms of sales may include this condition.

 

5.7       Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested that IBA should give the PC’s to the staff at 50% of the market price. 

 

DECISION

 

5.8       The Committee decided that Mr. Faisal Zia would make a categorization of the replaced PCs and send it to the Disposal Committee which will fix the price of such PCs.

 

5.9       The Committee further decided that the remaining PCs would be donated to schools run by NGOs. 

 

5.10     The Committee requested the Director H.R. to identify NGO schools both in and outside Karachi.

 

 ACTION

 

5.11     Mr. Faisal Zia would make a categorization of the replaced PCs and send it to the Disposal Committee which will fix the price of such PCs.

 

5.12     The Director H.R. would prepare list of NGO schools both in and outside Karachi and give it to the Registrar.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
