Item No. 2      To consider Reservations about Icon Agreement.

 

2.1       The Director Projects presented the following summary of deficiencies in performance of IBA’s Principal Architect, M/s ICON:

·         The contract agreement was signed by Principal Architect, Mr. Hanif Daud who is not present in Pakistan for the last six months due to which IBA is losing the expertise and experience of the main Architect on almost all the projects of IBA.

·         The Agreement was signed with ICON based on the experience of Mr. Hanif Daud and Mrs. Saman Mehmood. Not only are both of them in USA for the last six months, they have not maintained any liaison with IBA during this period also.

·         The Consultant (ICON) missed many deadlines one after the other due to which significant delays occurred on different projects such as existing Administration Building, Main Campus. Existing Library, Main Campus & existing FCS Building, City Campus and as a result the increase in cost was caused.

·         Since the signing of Agreement the Consultant did not submit any master timeline plan for all the projects to IBA as per Clause 3.6 of the Agreement.

·         The Consultant also had to visit the site and inspect progress of work periodically as per clause 5.1.13 which did not happen most of the time and due to which important site decisions were delayed.

·         As per Agreement clause 5.1.3 the Consultant has to attend all necessary meetings arranged by IBA on their behalf. This obviously is not happening as the chief Architect is out of country for the last six months. Although his representatives do attend the meetings, the prolonged absence of sub-consultants i.e. Electrical, HVAC and Plumbing from weekly site meetings is a genuine cause of concern.

·         As per Agreement clauses 3.3 and 7.1, the Consultant shall exercise all necessary skills, care and diligence in discharge of its duties and shall use their best knowledge, technical skill and care in accordance with the professional standard and ethics. But till now, ICON has failed to deliver on these matters.

·         The air-conditioning system design for the buildings carried out by HVAC sub-consultant is posing problems.

·         The drawings and tender documents usually prepared by the consultant as per clause 5.1.9 have not been up to the mark and are thus liable to revision on many an occasion.

·         IBA has repeatedly pointed out those deficiencies in their staff especially in their quantity surveying and contract section. They have paid no heed to this, because of which the BOQs are defective and IBA faces lots of variations in quantities.

·         IBA is paying 5.85% fee to ICON as per their agreement. The question arises whether they are entitled for this fee when the Principal Architects are away and their expertise are not readily available to IBA.

 

2.2       In view of the above, the Director Projects suggested that the case be sent to IBA’s Legal Advisor for his advice regarding the sanctity and viability of the Agreement with ICON under the present circumstances.

 

DECISION

 

2.3       The Director advised the Director Projects to document all deviations from the agreement and to fix penalties where deviations are as per agreement. He further advised to go through legal procedure.  The Committee agreed with the Director.

 

Item No. 3 To consider and approve appointment of single firm of Project Managers for three Projects.

 

3.1       The Director Projects reported that the following works were in the design stages and were scheduled to be tendered between June 2011 to August 2011:

 

a)      Detailed Supervision and Project Management Estimated Value Rs..... million for Master Planning, Designing & top Supervision of External Development of Infrastructure at IBA Main Campus 

         

b)      Detailed Supervision and Project Management Works estimates can only be Services for Survey, Need Analysis, Planning, known after designing. Designing and Top Supervision of Electrical System at IBA Main Campus

 

c)      Detailed Supervision and Project Management Estimated Value Rs..... million Services for Planning, Designing and Top Supervision of Sport Ground and Ancillary Works at IBA Main Campus

 

3.2       The Director Projects said that all the above works are dependant on one another as they are in the same area and within close proximity. It will be economical to appoint one Project Management Company for all the three works. He requested the Executive Committee to approve the appointment of the Project Managers for three combined works as detailed above so that RFP can be prepared accordingly.

 

3.3       The Director wanted to know as to why a Project Management company is needed for external development work and what the Project Management firm would be doing.

 

3.4       The Director Projects said that digging of trenches and taking their measurements which are typical type of work will be done under a team of supervisors and at every stage they have to get the testing done. He said that the Consultant is paid for the site work but certification of the bills is the job of the supervisor which they cannot do because they do not have the staff to do such work.

 

3.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects said that he was in favour of appointing a single Project Manager for the three projects. He said that the electrical infrastructure should be all-embracing, i.e. it should include Staff Colony, Boys' Hostels' area and the Main Campus; the work must include the distribution network from the 3 new HT connections for which they are building 3 separate sub-stations, on KESC's demand. He further said that he has sent to the Executive Committee the proposed additional scope of services they need from Electrical Consultant who has already been appointed.

 

3.6       The Director said that he has no objection in including the additional scope of services in the scope of the project but he said that his concern was that for Rs..... million project, they would have to pay Rs..... million to the Project Management firm.

 

3.7       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the fee payable to a project manager is usually about 3 per cent.

 

3.8       The Director Projects said that appointing an independent Project Management firm is the requirement of the A&FC/BoG and by appointing a Project Management firm they may reduce the cost of the Project by eliminating unnecessary or redundant things without comprising on the quality and specifications. He said that this can only be done through the Project Management firm and not by the Consultant because the later due to their fee may not suggest reduction in the cost of the project.  So through the Project Management IBA will be getting value added services.  

 

DECISION

 

3.9       In view of the above explanations given by the Director Projects, the Committee approved the appointment of a single firm of Project Managers for the combined works of external development, electrical infrastructure at the Main Campus, Staff Colony, Boys’ Hostel and students’ play ground at IBA Main Campus.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve Scope of additional services required from the Electrical Consultant KP & Associates.

 

4.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that the additional services are needed in respect of the approved expansion of electrical infrastructure at the three locations (Main Campus, Staff Colony and Boys' Hostel) for which they are obtaining 3 new power connections from KESC, and for which they are building 3 new sub-stations. He said that the additional services are required for designing power distribution network from the three sub-stations. He presented the proposed scope of work and requested the Executive Committee to review the scope and, if necessary, provide guidance for improving it.

 

4.2       The Director wanted to know as to why complete scope of work was not defined in the original document through which they were awarding the work to M/s. KP & Associates.

 

4.3       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that since the development work at the Main Campus was to take about two years, he thought there was no hurry to pursue the 2500 KW connection application for the Main Campus. Later, however, on the Director Projects’ requirements, the application was pursued. He said that as the Director Projects had already appointed an electrical consultant, it was more economical in terms of time to increase the Electrical Cosultant’s scope of services instead of appointing another electrical consultant. He further said that the scope of work will increase but the fee for the Consultant agreed in percentage of the cost may be reduced after negotiations.

 

DECISION

 

4.4       The Director said that there should be no violation of the PPRA rules. He asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to forward his proposal to the Head of Internal Audit for his comments and after obtaining the comments from the Head of Internal Audit, resubmit the proposal along with comments to the EC. The Committee agreed with the Director.

 

ACTION

 

4.5       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would obtain comments from the Head of Internal Audit on his proposal for increasing the scope and present them to the EC for consideration.

 

 

Item No. 5      To consider and approve award of the Interior Design of Aman Tower to M/S. EA Consulting.

 

5.1       The Director Projects reported that Aman Tower would be a building having mixed usage. It would have many departments, functions of which would be different from one another such as CEE, Library, Seminar Hall, Residential suites, Lecture theatres, faculty offices etc. He said that the interior spaces of Gymnasium, Canteens, Kitchen, and Dining Halls etc. need to follow a certain common theme in colour, texture of walls, ceiling, floor and furniture. He reported that Architects M/s. EA Consulting are in the design development stage and need to take into account the following so that economy in design of the building interior could be achieved:

 

·         Air-conditioning reticulation/distribution could be taken to the last termination point, i.e. air devices and FC units, since internal partitioning and Reflected Ceiling Plans would be finalized.

 

·         Electrical and data/voice reticulation/distribution could be taken to the last termination point, i.e. desks and wall outlets, since Internal Partitioning and Furniture Layout Plans would be finalized.

 

·         Lighting and illumination could be taken to the last termination point, i.e. luminaries,      since Reflected Ceiling Plans and Internal Partitioning would be finalized

 

·         Flooring and wall finishes for dedicated areas could be finalized and provided for, which may save in costs and redundancy at a later stage.

 

5.2       The Director Projects further said that apart from the obvious ease and facilitation of design and specification decisions, they estimate savings in contractors’ costs and overheads if contracts that will be awarded after finalization of internal configuration could be merged with the main contracts. He said that the scope of services of the Architects would include the following:

 

a)   Conceptual Design

b)   Schematic Design

c)   Final Design                    

d)  Tender Documents

e)   Construction Supervision          

f)   Professional Liability

Item No. 7      To consider and approve Payroll Cycle.

 

7.1       The Director Finance reported that at present the pay day is last day of the month and after processing of payroll, banks also take time to transfer the salary to the salary accounts of IBA employees which are spread over Karachi in various banks, and they further take 3 to 4 days’ time. He said that to meet the deadline Payroll staff works very late, at times, up to midnight. The late sitting is because of certain important information and documents being received very late from various departments especially the Over Time which is received in the Finance Department after 22nd of each month whereas the due date for submission of Over Time Sheet is 22nd. The processing of overtime and other information, their verifications and audit also take time. He suggested to strictly follow the time schedule for the documents / information essentially required to complete the Payroll process. He requested that the following essential documents / information must be received by the Finance Department on 22nd of each month:

 

·         New joining

·         Resignations

·         Contract Renewal cases

·         Medical Expense/ Courier Charges/ Fuel Recovery

 

7.2       The Director Finance further requested the Executive Committee to allow the Finance Department to consider the overtime or other benefits to be paid next month if information not provided by 22nd of the month in order to enable the Finance Department to process the current month Payroll smoothly and timely. He said that if the time line i.e. 22nd of each month for submission of essential information including the overtime is agreed and approved by the Executive Committee then the Finance Department would be able to finalize the payroll and send the disbursement information to the banks on the 27th of each month whereby the salary of all IBA employees would be credited to their account by 30th of each month.

 

DECISION

 

7.3       The Committee approved the proposal and requested the Director Finance to send an email to the department heads requesting them to provide details by 22nd of each month.
