Item No. 2      To consider the proposal for supply and installation of flood lights at cricket ground.

 

2.1 The Director Projects reported that as per decision of the Executive Committee he showed the presentation of the design of cricket stadium prepared by NBCL to Mr. Asad Ilyas. He said that while the design of the pavilion was reviewed by Mr. Asad Ilyas, he (Mr. Asad Ilyas) suggested that it would be better if flood lights are installed at the cricket ground instead of construction of the Pavilion. He further suggested that the cricket ground would be used for night matches to generate revenues and from those revenues the pavilion can be constructed afterwards. The estimated cost of the flood lights (which have already been designed) is approximately Rs.12.0 million. The allocation for the Pavilion is Rs.10.0 million. Foundations for the poles have already been constructed and the network of the conduits has already been laid.

 

2.2 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to consider this suggestion and accord approval in principle so that Mr. Asad Ilyas be asked to prepare a cost-benefit analysis and present it to the Executive Committee.

 

2.3 The Registrar said that it has already been decided in the EC meeting that we do not need night lighting at this stage. He apprehended that holding matches and events at night may create various problems including the security issue.

 

2.4 Mr. Asad Ilyas said that in his opinion it is not the right decision not to have lights for night matches. He said that as the participants and spectators would be alumni and their family members and the corporate people, there would not be any security problem.  He further said that if there is any additional amount involved in installing flood lights instead of pavilion that can easily be recovered by arranging corporate matches.

 

DECISION

 

2.5 The Committee deferred this item and requested Mr. Asad Ilyas to present his proposal to the EC in its next meeting for installing flood lights instead of construction of pavilion, estimated revenues and expenses, and the security issue.

b)   Horticulture and greenery arrangements.

 

3.9  The Registrar reported that the architects had given an estimate of Rs.4 million for horticulture and greenery work at the Main Campus. However, the Dean & Director asked him to get it done in-house in consultation with the architects.  Accordingly, the architect was requested to provide a plan for the Tabba Academic Block.  He reported that they placed the plants on the ground floor only but the architect was not satisfied with it and advised to involve Ms. Anjum Pervez.  Mr. Anjum Pervez advised to remove the plants because according to her the quality of the soil was not good. All the planters were thus emptied by the projects department and excavation was done up to 3.5ft from where all the bad soil and debris were removed through the existing contractor. Further sweet sand as well as the manure was procured through the same contractor under the approval of Ms. Anjum Parvez. The planters were made ready by the Projects department to receive the plants.

  

3.10  The Registrar said that Ms. Anjum Pervez gave an estimate of Rs.1,900,000/- out of which Rs.625,000/- was for the supervision of plantation and transportation of plants.   The Registrar said that he asked the Purchase Officer to obtain rates of the same spec from the market.  Mr. Sohail Khan obtained the rate of the recommended plants which was around Rs.450,000/-  There is a three times difference between the market rate and what Ms. Anjum Pervez demanded!  He suggested that we should get the plants from the market.  He said that as these are to be planted before the inauguration of the building i.e. May 24, 2013, they have to make urgent purchases.  Mr. Moeid Sultan suggested that it has to be routed through CPC for urgent purchase.

 

DECISION

 

3.11 The Committee in principle approved the purchase of plants from the market.

 

ACTION

 

3.12 The Registrar would obtain approval of CPC for urgent purchase of plants from the market.

 

 

c)  Out of Schedule Exams.

 

3.13 Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is an increasing frequency of out of schedule exams because of which the Examination Department is stuck up in conducting exams only through out the year although there are a lot of other work to do. He said that faculty members too feel irritated by making and grading extra papers, a couple of faculty members have expressed their disagreement with the policy of allowing students make-up exams as a regular practice.   He said that the out of schedule exams are conducted mostly for the students who go outside city/country to participate in an event/competition. He quoted figures that the Examination Department had to coduct out of schedule exams of 24 subjects for 4 stduents for finals of Fall Semester 2012, exams of 32 subjects for 13 students in the Ist term and 20 exams for 5 students in the 2nd term of the current semester. (These figures do not include make up exams allowed on medical grounds.). He said that one of the possibilities that can be considered by the Associate Deans to ease out the pressure of extra work for the Examination Dept. and the faculty is to average out the results of those who miss the exams due to their participation in the events/competition.

 

3.14 Dr. Muhammad Nishat said that this can be looked into at the Academic Committee.  He requested Mr. Khalili to provide statistics on this.

 

DECISION

 

3.15 The Committee referred the issue to the Academic Committee.

 

ACTION

 

3.16 The Controller of Examinations would provide statistics to the Chairman, Academic Committee on the out of schedule exams.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
