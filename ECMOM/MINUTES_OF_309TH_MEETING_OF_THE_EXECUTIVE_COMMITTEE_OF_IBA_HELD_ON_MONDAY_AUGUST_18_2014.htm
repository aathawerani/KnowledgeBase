MINUTES OF 309TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON
MONDAY, AUGUST 18, 2014

The 309th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, August 18, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Hussain Dean & Director
2. Dr. Mohammad Nishat Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN Registrar
5. Mr. Moeid Sultan Director Finance
6. Mr. Abdul Rehman Sr. Manager Contracts*
7. Mr. Imran Batada Head of ICT Department
8. Mr. Adnan Hameed Head of HR

 

* Attended the meeting as nominee of Director Projects.

The following items were discussed:


Item No.1 To consider and approve award of work for Supply and Fixing of Furniture for IBA Aman Tower, City Campus.

1.1 Mr. Abdul Rehman, Senior Manager Contract reported that advertisement was floated in order to pre-qualify the furniture contractors. In response to the advertisement following contractor submitted their profile and the documents:

     1. M/s Interwood
     2. M/s Work Man
     3. M/s Versant Interior
     4. M/s Habib & Sons
     5. M/s Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd.
     6. M/s Sheetal
     7. M/s Gujrat Steel
     8. M/s Premier Furniture
     9. M/s DNAZ Entrepreneur

1.2 Among above contractors following contractors were found qualified and approval for their pre-qualification was obtained from Procurement Committee as well as the Executive Committee:

     1. M/s Interwood
     2. M/s Work Man
     3. M/s Versant Interior
     4. M/s Habib & Sons
     5. M/s Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd.
     6. M/s Sheetal

1.3 Mr. Abdul Rehman said that invitations of bids were sent to all pre-qualified bidders and the following contractors collected the bids. M/s Interwood regretted to collect the tender documents due to their preoccupation:

     1. M/s Versant Interior
     2. M/s Habib & Sons
     3. M/s Simab (smc-pvt.) Ltd.
     4. M/s Sheetal

1.4 Among above four, only three contractors submitted their bids on July 26, 2014. M/s Habib Sons regretted due to some financial constraints of their company. The bids were opened in front of IBA officials and the representative of bidders. The bids were then sent to EA Consulting for their evaluation and recommendation of best evaluated responsive bidder. As per the EA Consulting report, there were Arithmetical corrections in the bids of contractors. The following were the final bid prices of the contractors:

     1. M/s Sheetal                     Rs.45,998,205/-
     2. M/s Versant                     Rs.46,589,950/-
     3. M/s Simab                        Rs.64,088,926/-

1.5 The Engineering Estimate of Rs.45,178,900/- was submitted by the EA Consulting after several detailed meetings with Director Projects. The bids were compared with Engineering Estimate. The 1st Lowest bidder is 1.81% higher than Engineering Estimate, 2nd Lowest bidders is 3.12% higher than Engineering Estimate whereas the 3rd lowest bidder is 41.85% higher than Engineering Estimate. M/s EA Consulting recommended the award of work to M/s Sheetal as lowest bidder.

1.6 Mr. Abdul Rehman reported that the case was presented in Procurement Committee meeting held on August 16, 2014. After reviewing all procurement procedures the Procurement Committee recommended the award of work to the lowest bidder M/s Sheetal.

1.7 Based on the recommendations of M/s EA Consulting , and the Procurement Committee, the IBA Projects Department requested the Executive Committee to approve the award of work to the lowest bidder M/s Sheetal at a cost of Rs.45,998,205/-

1.8 The Dean & Director said that for standardization it is necessary that those who are already working or have worked at IBA should do the work. He asked if M/s. Sheetal have worked for IBA. Mr. Abdul Rehman reported that M/s. Sheetal have been given the work for the Library and have been asked to deliver all furniture for the Library by January 2015. He clarified that earlier they have not worked for IBA.

1.9 Dr. Sayeed Ghani suggested to check the quality of work of M/s. Sheetal and if it is acceptable then they may be given approval for award of work.

DECISION & ACTION

1.10 The Registrar was requested to check the quality of furniture for Library sample of which has been provided by M/s. Sheetal. After inspection and subject to satisfaction of the Registrar, the A&FC may be recommended to consider and approve the award of work to M/s Sheetal - the lowest responsive bidder.

1.11 The Director Projects would take action based on the recommendation of the Registrar.


Item No. 2 Up-dates on the following projects/work.

2.1 External Development Work: Mr. Abdul Rehman reported that for the completion of roads November 2014 has been given the deadline and for front side October 2014 is the deadline. He reported that for the Courtyard is being done by Venture Crest. The gates drawings will be given by Mr. Zayd Bilgrami by August 20. He further said that they have asked them to give the design for one gate only. The Dean & Director advised to get Gate # 4 ready. He asked as to how much time it would take to complete. Mr. Abdul Rehman replied that it would take about 2 1/2 months and it will be given to Total Construction as Variation Order.

2.2 Parking Lot: The Dean & Director said that as he had asked earlier the abandoned office of the Contractor should be demolished and the debris should be cleaned. The Registrar said that he had also requested earlier that the debris between the monument garden and the Library should be cleared. Mr. Abdul Rehman promised that he would get it done.

2.3 Admin Building: The Dean & Director asked as to why the façade (roof top) of the Admin Building is still incomplete. Mr. Abdul Rehman said that M/s. Al-Shafi who are the contractors of this building have promised that by the end of August they would complete it. He reported that the third floor of the building is almost complete and furniture is being fixed. The Dean & Director suggested that the offices should be shifted to the floors which are complete. He said that he would shift to Admin Building from Aman-CED as soon as the first floor is ready.

2.4 MP Building: Mr. Abdul Rehman said that tender for the Interior of MP Building was floated but as there was no response, the date has been extended. He said that it would take 5 to 6 months time to complete the work. He reported that the Architects have been asked to give design of MP Building façade. For the Northern wall the architects have been asked to prepare the lay out.

2.5 Water Supply Connection at Aman Tower: Mr. Abdul Rehman reported that initial survey has been completed on August 12, 2014. The bank challan will be given by August 25 and after payment of bank challan it will be completed by October 2014.

2.6 SSGC: After making all payments on August 20, route permission from Cantonment Board has been taken.

2.7 Electricity: Mr. Abdul Rehman said that on August 11, 2014 K-Electric has given drawings which has been submitted to Cantonment Board for permission. By November 15, 2014 the work will be completed but in MJS Auditorium temporary supply will be available. Tender for 500 KVA has been floated. Financial bids would be opened on August 20. It will take 2.5 months for the generator to come. The Registrar said that water, electricity and drainage are main issues. The Dean & Director said that it should be fully functional when we inaugurate the building. The Registrar suggested to get generator on rent until the generator is procured. Mr. Abdul Rehman said that if the bid is opened in favour of M/s. S.M. Jaffer Brothers then they can get the generator soon as they have ready stock. The Registrar suggested to get the connections ready so that generator when received can be fixed immediately. The Dean & Director asked if connection with sub-station is available. The Registrar said that the trench connecting the Aman Tower with sub-station is ready; only the connection has to be provided. He further said that the bulk power is available; it is only to be distributed.

DECISION

2.8 The up-dates given above were noted and approved.


There being no other issue, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
