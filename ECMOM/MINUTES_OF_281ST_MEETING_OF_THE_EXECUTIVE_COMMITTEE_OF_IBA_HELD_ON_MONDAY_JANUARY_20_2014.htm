The following items were discussed:


Item No. 1 Presentation on “IBA Fire Alarm System”.

1.1 The Director Projects said that individual buildings do not have integrated fire alarm system therefore, he has requested Mr. Khalid Pervez of KP Associates to give a presentation on Integrated Campus Fire Alarm System. The Dean & Director wanted to know as to what is the response time if there is any detection of fire and what are the procedures to mobilize fire fighting manpower if there is any fire incident. Mr. Mushtaq said that presently they do not have any automatic suppression system. It is through manual hoses that fire is suppressed. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that different buildings are isolated from each other and there is no connection among these buildings. He said that he has proposed to integrate these buildings with a Central Command Station so that if there is a fire, the information should reach the Central Command Station and fire fighting measures may be initiated. The Dean & Director said that it is the first step; detection is only one part of the system, the second is timely response and suppression and the third is the rescue and evacuation. The whole system should be interlinked. Mr. Khalid Rishi said that he has prepared SOP for that and said that it is a joint work as far as responsibility is concerned. He said that he has the approval of a Fire fighting officer and his assistant and has requested Mr. Adnan Hameed to arrange their hiring. The Dean & Director said that it is not a full time job; the fire fighting officer can be assigned other duties too. The Registrar said that the fire fighting equipment has not yet been tested. As far as fire fighting equipment is concerned, we have not drilled it. Syed Fahim Uddin said that at Aman CED Building they have done it.

1.2 Mr. Khalid Pervez made a presentation on Integrated Campus Fire Alarm System. He presented an overview of 11 buildings/facilities at the Main Campus. Emphasising on the need for Integrated Fire Alarm System he said that integrating the system and connecting it to a Central Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP) will enable monitoring the entire campus from one location. He said that the proposed scope of work is:

  • Combined Monitoring at two places
  • Developing Campus Wide Network
  • Hooking up the following:

    • Multi-purpose
    • ACED
    • New Academic Block


1.3 Mr. Khalid Pervez said that to develop a network the FACPs must be of Addressable type and have networking capability. He clarified that the difference between addressable system and conventional system is that the addressable system pin points single device and identifies which one has problem whereas the conventional one identifies at a larger level. He said that the following buildings have conventional type of FACPs:

  • Admin Block
  • Library
  • Auditorium
  • Old Academic Block
  • Students Centre


1.4 Mr. Khalid Pervez suggested that the existing FACPs be replaced with those of Addressable type along with other interface cards. He presented the following cost estimates:

Cost of System as per BoQ                         Rs.2.45 million
Cost to integrate all buildings                      Rs.4.71 million
Additional Cost                                         Rs.2.26 million

1.5 Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there two options: (a) Follow the original scope and build campus wide network, hooking up three buildings, or (b) Build a full fledge network with additional cost of Rs.2.26 million.

1.6 The Dean & Director asked as to what is the main component of this additional cost. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the fire alarm control panel which is to be replaced in the above mentioned five buildings. The Dean & Director further asked as to what are the draw backs of conventional system. Mr. Khalid Pervez clarified that conventional systems are non-networkable. The Registrar said that all sensors are connected to one panel and if any sensor is activated the light will be on. He wanted to know if there is any simple and cost effective solution. He said that a security guard will also be available in the building whose duty it will be to communicate to the Control Room if there is any fire incident. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that there are human aspects; maybe the guard is not available at that time. The Director Projects said that we have invested billions of rupees on our buildings. He said that spending Rs.2.26 million additional to save our property is not too much amount. The Dean & Director said that we should take preventive measures so that the probability of occurrence of any fire incident becomes very low. He said that spending additional Rs.2.26 million for fine tuning the detection system is not justifiable if other alternative means are available and are equally effective. He said that the risk of human error is halved because six of the buildings are already connected to the control panel through addressable devices. Mr. Khalid Rishi suggested that one or two mega pixel cameras with audio in front of the panel can be placed and it will be connected to the control panel. The Dean & Director asked Mr. Khalid Pervez if the existing IP cameras can be integrated to the system. Mr. Khalid Pervez said that the US Fire Alarm Code requires an independent fire network, it cannot be mixed up. The Dean & Director requested Mr. Khalid Pervez and Mr. Khalid Rishi to jointly find an alternate solution which meets our requirements which is cost effective and suggest to the EC for approval after review by the Director Projects and Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed and their recommendations.

DECISION & ACTION

1.7 Mr. Khalid Pervez and Mr. Khalid Rishi in light of the above suggestions would jointly find an alternative cost effective solution and present it to the EC for approval after review by the Director Projects and Mr. Mushtaque Ahmed and their recommendations.

Item No. 4 Suggestions on Design Aspects of Prayer Hall.

4.1 Mr. M Asif Jaffer, Assistant Professor and Patron IQRA Society IBA, stated that as agreed earlier by IBA management, the existing design of the Prayer Hall needs to be customized in accordance with our needs. He proposed the following suggestions for consideration by management. He informed that for a month or so he was in the process of consolidating feedback from IBA community on this who were showing their concern and suggesting on some of the design aspects:

  • There are almost 40% girl students, even in IQRA Society there are more girls than boys. Overall number of students is also increasing but the new girls’ prayer area is smaller than the older one. It is even difficult to accommodate praying girls there, let alone having any lectures or sessions. Thus there is immediate need to provide more space for female namazis. It was proposed that the front part of the old mosque be retained in its existing form for girls. The open lawn area can be demolished. A number of problems can be solved without compromising the overall campus planned architecture, without any need to customize the new prayer place and even avoiding the need to convert the large break out rooms in the Tabba South Wing, which the Registrar mentioned he would reserve for girls’ prayers and IQRA sessions once the library shifts out.



  • The present girls’ area in the new prayer hall then can be used for IQRA sessions / Quran reading room for boys, thereby having least disturbance to those offering prayer.


  • There is a need to cover the inner walls of prayer area with glass wall around to protect it from noise (disturbing), dust (right now when one offers prayer there, dust sticks on clothes) and cold. The plan has already been discussed with in-house experts and an aluminium frame and dark glasses with windows in between, fixed from top and bottom, would be ideal. This way we shall keep advantage of day light and probably shall not require air conditioning system in summers as fans and windows will suffice.



4.2 Mr. Asif Jaffer said that mosques, in earlier Muslim history, were not only the places of prayers but also a center for many positive community and social activities. He said that his assumption is that the Dean & Director Dr. Ishrat Husain had also proposed this when in the inauguration ceremony he referred and talked to some of IQRA members that we should have IQRA sessions there as well.

4.3 The Dean & Director said that according to External Development Plan the old mosque was to be demolished. He wanted to know as to what would be the impact if it is retained. The Director Projects said that he had discussed this with Mr. Shabih and reported that they would retain the mosque and at the same time have pathways by cutting and re-fixing some portion. He said that the veranda of the mosque would be removed and the partition between boys and girls would also be removed as it would be for the females only. He said that he would ask the architect to develop some landscaping in front of the old mosque and also beautify the mosque. He further said that the ablution area at boys’ side would be removed and the existing ablution arrangement at girls side would be expanded to make arrangement for four from existing two.

4.4 About covering the inner walls of prayer area of the Prayer Hall, the Registrar said that it involves an estimated expenditure of Rs.1 million. However, he said that the first work of wind-wall would be started in March this year and he would try that majority of the work is done in-house. The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to talk to Mr. Aamir Abbasi for the additional expenditure of Rs.1 million.

4.5 The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to retain the old mosque as a functional mosque with suggested modifications and the adjoining walkways incorporated into the Master Plan. He also suggested to ask the Contractor Venture Crest to make the suggested changes and beautify the old mosque free of cost.

DECISION

4.6 The Committee approved the suggestions made by Mr. M. Asif Jaffer on the design aspects of the Prayer Hall and retention of the front part of the old mosque in its existing form for girls with the suggested modifications and beautification.

4.7 The Committee also agreed to hold IQRA sessions at the Prayer Hall.

ACTION

4.8 The Registrar would talk to Mr. Aamir Abbasi for the additional expenditure of Rs.1 million.

4.9 The Director Projects would ask the Contractor M/s. Venture Crest to make the suggested changes into the old mosque and beautify the old mosque and its landscaping free of cost.

4.10 The Registrar would start the first work of wind-wall of the Prayer Hall in March this year and he would try that majority of the work is done in-house.


Item No. 5 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.
