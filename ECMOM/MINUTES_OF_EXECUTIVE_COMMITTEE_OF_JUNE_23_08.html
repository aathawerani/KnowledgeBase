MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF THE IBA HELD ON JUNE 23, 2008

A meeting of the Executive Committee of the IBA was held on Monday, June 23, 2008 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus.  The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                               Chairman, MIS & CS
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                               Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Shamsuzzoha Jafri                        H.R. Manager
  7. Mr. Moeid Sultan                               Acting Head of Finance & Accounts

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Manager Projects attended the meeting by special invitation.

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Payment of Bills to the Contractor.

1.1       The Manager Projects reported that the Contractor has stopped work due to financial constraints.  He said that the Contractor has submitted bills for amounts for which work has not been completed yet.

DECISION:

1.2       The Committee decided that the Contractor should submit the bills and whatever the amount of work completed should be paid for by the IBA after proper verification. The Contractor should deposit the purchased material against which payment should be made.  

1.3       It was further decided that payments for material may be advanced by the Bank and the IBA will pay to the Bank.  The material will serve as security for the advance.

1.4       Alternatively, the IBA may pay post-dated cheque. However, its documentation should be fool proof.

ACTION

1.5       Mr. Moeid Sultan, Acting Head of Finance & Accounts will facilitate bridge financing with the UBL.

Item No. 2      Non-existence of Material Cost Escalation Clause in the Contract

2.1       Mr. Shameem Ahmed pointed out that there is no material cost escalation clause in the contract for IBA projects. Similarly, he said that there is no contingency clause.

2.2       The Director said that if there is significant increase in price due to government policy, one option is to cancel the contract and renegotiate. This will disrupt the whole process. Other option is to adopt cost escalation factor which is advised by the Pakistan Engineering Council and revise the cost.

DECISION:

2.3       The Committee decided that the Audit & Finance Committee should be informed that there is no cost escalation clause in the project contract.

2.4       The Committee decided that revised PC-1 on the basis of tender documents for Centre for Entrepreneurial Excellence, Visiting Faculty Residence and new Boys’ Hostel may be submitted to the HEC

ACTION

2.5      The Manager Projects will prepare a paper informing the A&FC about the non-existence of cost escalation clause and the proposal to adopt the cost escalation factor suggested by the PEC.

2.6      The Manager Projects will prepare revised PC-1 for CEE, VFR and new Boys’ Hostel and send to the HEC for approval.

Item No. 3      Junk Disposal Policy

3.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani informed the Committee of the junks lying on the roof of CCS building. He said that due to cumbersome process the junks cannot be disposed easily.

DECISION:

3.2       It was decided that the Committee would frame Junk Disposal Policy which will be approved and implemented. It would include obtaining a condemnation certificate from the competent authority regarding economic life of the equipment / material.

Item No. 4      Provision of sitting space for the students.

4.1       The Registrar said that as a result of construction on the ground floors of Academic and Admin blocks, the students will have no room to sit on.  The canteen is too small to accommodate large number of students.

DECISION

4.2       It was decided that one floor on the Canteen may be added to accommodate the students.

Item No. 5      University Alliance Program

5.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani presented details of the University Alliance Program (UAP) and said that organizations such as Oracle, Microsoft, SAP should have offices at the IBA to provide training as well as for internships.

DECISION

5.2       The Committee decided that the funds should be raised from private sector including IBA Alumni, HEC, Software Houses.

5.3       One faculty member from MIS & CS department should be deputed to this program.

Item No. 6      Acquisition of Research papers by professor Edward N. Lorenz

6.1       The Committee considered a note from Dr. Waseem A. Khan and Dr. Sayeed Ghani regarding acquisition of seven research papers by Prof. Edward N. Lorenz @12.00 pound starling each.

DECISION

6.2       The Committee approved the request for acquisition of seven research papers at a total cost of pound sterling 84.00. 

Item No. 7      Microsoft SharePoint 2007 Training

7.1       The I.T. Manager in his note of June 16, 2008 stated that M/s. Kalsoft Academy was organizing a 5 days’ hands-on Microsoft SharePoint training to enhance knowledge and skills of IT staff on Implementing Windows SharePoint Server and Services. He stated in his note that the training is useful for the IBA and requested that he (Mr. Azam Sardar, I.T. Manager) and Mr. Abdul Qadir (Network Manager) be allowed to attend the training program.  The total discounted cost for two participants would be Rs.41,500/-  The registration of participants would be confirmed on 100% advance payment of training fee. 

DECISION

7.2       The Committee approved the nomination of Mr. Azam Sardar and Mr. Abdul Qadir for Microsoft SharePoint 2007 Training and the payment of fee of Rs. 41,500/- in advance.

Item No. 8      Training for Phase 1 of ERP – Campus Management System

8.1       Dr. Sayeed Ghani presented a training proposal from M/s. Techlogix for the PeopleSoft Campus Management System to be started from July 1 to July 12, 2008 for 30 to 35 key persons of the IBA which would be conducted at the IBA City Campus. The fee for training of PeopleSoft Campus Solution as given by M/s. Techlogix is Rs.275,000/- (The fee for training will be credited back to IBA in case IBA chooses to award implementation of PeopleSoft Campus Solutions to Techlogix)  As the training will continue from morning till evening, the participants would be served lunch and tea during the training period for which Dr. Sayeed Ghani sought approval of Rs.100,000/-   He said that training for remaining modules (HRMS and Financials, etc.) is not included in the current training program and may be done subsequently.

DECISION

8.2       The Committee approved the proposal.

Item No. 9      Discussions and Decisions on Miscellaneous Issues

9.1       The Committee decided that individuals on full time basis should be appointed for each project such as new Boys’ Hostel, CEE, VFR. 

9.2       The Committee decided that Visiting Faculty Residence is not on priority as the existing facility is sufficient at the moment.

9.3       Regarding expansion program at the City Campus the Director requested Mr. Mobin Khalili to find out title deeds of the City Campus Building.

9.4       Mr. Shameed Ahmed, Manager Projects was requested to obtain details from the Military Estate Officer about construction of buildings and the owner of the adjacent plot at the City Campus.

9.5       The Manager Projects was requested to get a survey of the Staff Town done to determine the needs with regard to repair and maintenance.

9.6.      The Committee decided that maintaining cars and other IBA vehicles would be the responsibility of the Administration Department.

9.7       The Committee decided that the existing vehicles fully depreciated and having lost the economic life should be disposed and new vehicles should be purchased.

9.8       The Committee decided that carpeting should be removed and floor polishing should be done if the flooring is in good condition.

9.9       The Committee decided that it is in the interest of the IBA to provide copy of the Program Announcement to every candidate.

9.10     The Committee decided that instead of two heads i.e. Full time teaching, and Part Time teaching used in budget amount allocation, there should be one head; namely Teaching which would cover both part time and full time.

9.11     The Committee decided that faculty members going abroad on scholarship will also be paid from Faculty Development Program if the amount of scholarship is insufficient to meet all necessary expenses.

9.12     The Committee further decided that the IBA would pay travelling and lodging expenses to faculty members who are invited to read papers in conferences of high standard and for which they cannot avail HEC funding.

9.13     The Committee decided that the staff would be encouraged to develop their professional skills of which there would be benefit to the IBA rather than encouraging in obtaining degrees.

9.14     The Finance Department will ensure that the approved IBA Budget is placed on the intranet.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
