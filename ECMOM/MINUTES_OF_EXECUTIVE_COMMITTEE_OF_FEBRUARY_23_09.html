MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON FEBRUARY 23, 2009 (THIRTY SIXTH MEETING)

 

The 36th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, February 23, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                               Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                        Associate Dean
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Associate Dean
  4. Mr. Inayat N. Din                              Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                     Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                     Director Projects
  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                          Director I.T
  9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director H.R.
  10. Mr. Zafar Siddiqui                              Director CED
  11. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer               (the incoming Registrar)

 

The following items were discussed:

Item No. 1      Budget 2009-10 – Status report from Director Finance.

1.1              The Director Finance presented the status report on the budget formats submitted to Finance Department till February 23, 2009. He stated that except Board Secretariat, the budget formats have not been received from any department.  However, he said that the department heads have assured him that they would submit the budget formats within two to three days.

1.2              The Director Finance also presented the time line for finalization and presentation of the budget to the Audit & Finance Committee.

DECISION

1.3              The Director requested the members Executive Committee to expedite the submission of the budget formats as the draft budget is to be presented to the A&FC by March 31, 2009.

1.4              Because of the delayed response the Director asked the Director Finance to revise the time line for finalization of the draft budget.

1.5              The Director said that we have to decide whether the placement to be given to HR or to Alumni but he added that at this moment he would not disturb the existing arrangement. He requested the Director H.R. and the Director Alumni to jointly prepare the budget for Placement functions.

ACTION

1.6              The members of the Executive Committee will submit their budget formats in three days time to the Finance Department.

1.7              The Director H.R. and the Director Alumni will prepare the budget for Placement functions.

Item No. 2      To consider note from the General Manager Projects regarding Structural Testing of Buildings at City Campus.

2.1       The General Manager (P&D) in his note of February 20, 2009 stated that the Council for Works and Housing Research is a government organization under the Ministry of Science & Technology and provides non-destructive testing and many other technical services related with construction industry. The services are normally provided at their standard prices.

2.2       The General Manager (P&D) requested the Executive Committee to approve engaging the Council in principle for structural testing required at the City Campus urgently.  He stated that the Council being a government organization, can be engaged for the work without inviting competitive bids.  He further stated that his department had advertised for competitive bids in April 2008 but did not receive any response.

2.3       The Director Projects briefing the Executive Committee said that they are planning to construct and add one more floor on the existing building at the City Campus.  He said that to know whether the beams and columns will be able to take the load of another floor or not such tests are necessary.

DECISION

2.4       The Committee requested the Director Projects to consult the Internal Auditor if we can assign the work to the Council which is a government organization or we need to place a second tender. On the basis of the advice of the Internal Auditor the Director Projects would take the required action.  

ACTION

2.5       The Director Projects would consult the Internal Auditor and take action according to his advice.

Item No. 3      To consider proposed Policy on disposal of wastes / old records.

3.1       The Registrar presented the proposed policy on disposal of wastes / old records.

3.2       The Director wanted to know whether the policy conforms to the government audit manual to which the Director Finance replied that he would write to the AGPR for the advice. In the meanwhile he said that he has obtained information on the disposal policy practiced at the Karachi University. He said that the Karachi University has classified the records into three categories; Academic Records, Accounting Records and Old Assets.  He said that he was told that the Karachi University has never destroyed accounting records. Academic records are destroyed / disposed off after three years through tender or auction after approval from the Syndicate. He said that he would also visit NED University to know what their disposal policies are. 

3.3       The Director said that where there is a clear rule we will follow that but where there is a lack of clarity we will follow the best practice.   

3.4       Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer suggested that individual departments should weed out the documents and prepare a list of documents suggested for disposal.  The Disposal Committee will examine and decide if the documents suggested for disposal are to be disposed off or not and also maintain a record of disposed items.

3.5       Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer also advised that we should hire containers of 40’ x 20’ in which disposable items may be stored.

DECISION

3.6       The Committee agreed to the suggestion of hiring containers for placing disposable items.

3.7              It was decided that the Committee will review the draft policy on disposal of wastes / old records and send their comments to the Registrar who will finalise the draft policy and present before the Executive Committee.

 

Item No. 4      To consider note from Director Projects regarding final evaluation report for award of the job detailed design, procurement, detailed supervision and project management of the Centre for Entrepreneurship and Incubation Centre to the successful bidder.

4.1       Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz, Director Projects giving background stated that he had issued RFP to seven Consultants for Planning, Designing, Detailed Supervision and Project Management of Centre for Entrepreneurship and Incubation Centre against which four Consultants submitted their proposals which were analysed and evaluated in the light of the criteria already given in the RFP. M/s. Icon did not submit the bid but sent a letter to the Director IBA stating that they considered it unethical to bid as they had already designed the building. It would therefore, be unfair for other bidders. Later the Executive Committee constituted a Committee comprising Director Projects, Director I.T., and General Manager (P&D) to conduct negotiation process as per the RFP clauses and give their recommendations to the Executive Committee. Three of the four bidders technically qualified which were (1) M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative, (2) M/s. Mot MacDonald Pakistan, and (3) M/s. ACE-Arts.  He said that the bid of ACE-Arts was very high – 13.25% whereas the bid of M/s. Najmi Bilgrami was 8.25% and that of M/s. Mot MacDonald was 6%. He said that they obtained certain clarifications from M/s. Najmi Bilgrami and M/s. Mot MacDonald.  M/s. Najmi Bilgrami clarified that they had not included the fee for furniture design. However, they offered a rebate and reduced the fee to 7.5%  M/s. Mot MacDonald had laid the condition that 10% escalation would be charged. The Director Projects requested them to remove that condition to which they agreed and confirmed in writing.

4.2       Referring to subsequent meeting held by A&FC the Director Projects informed the Committee that the matter was discussed in detail by A&FC members. He further informed that A&FC decided that:

a)           In future project management be separated from architects scope of services and Project Managers be pre-qualified for engaging independently to provide detailed supervision services.

b)          As the scope of services of project under consideration has changed, issuance of RFP on new projects was in order.

c)           Efforts be made to separate project management job in agreement with architects / consultants.  If this was not possible then RFP issued by IBA should be adhered to.

4.3       In a subsequent meeting of M/s. Icon with Director IBA it was decided that M/s. Icon’s design will be given to two competing architects whose consent will be obtained by Director Projects. If this design was acceptable to them then the architects will provide us a rebate equivalent to preliminary design fee.

4.4       The Director Projects informed that both the competing companies accepted the design. M/s. Najmi Bilgrami Collaborative Limited offered a rebate of 1% thus reducing their fee to 6.5%.  M/s. Mot Macdonald Pakistan reduced their fee to 5.85% from 6%.

4.5              The Director Projects then presented the special committee’s view point along with the analysis of both the bids for consideration of the Executive Committee.

DECISION

4.6       The Executive Committee after due deliberations did not approve special committee’s analysis and referred back the matter to the Special Committee with the advice that the Special Committee should revisit the two bids and give their recommendations based on objectivity and tangible justifications.

ACTION

4.7       The Director Projects will arrange a meeting of the Special Committee for re-visiting the bid by assigning weightage to various factors and give their recommendations as per advice of the Executive Committee.  

Item No. 5      To consider note from Registrar on the application of Mr. Khalid Latif, F/o Mr. Khurram Khalid, a dropped out student of IBA.

5.1       Prof. Inayat N. Din, Registrar in his note of February 21, 2009 stated that Mr. Khurram Khalid because of his Acute Psychotic Disorder could not appear in the Fall Semester 2008 final examinations.  As such his CGPA fell below the required level.  He stated that since his medical doctor is very optimistic about the positive impact of medication and the applicant needs a lengthy treatment and rest before he resumes normal work, he requested the Executive Committee to:

a)      Cancel his 3rd semester in which he could not appear in the final examinations because of medical reasons and

b)      grant him permission to resume his MBA-III semester studies from Fall 2009 on humanitarian grounds.

5.2              Mr. Mobin Khalili said that Mr. Khurram Khalid has a history of psychological disorder.  He said that Mr. Khurram had taken one year medical leave then joined MBA Program and again in the MBA 3rd semester final examination he could not appear and thus technically got ‘F’ in all subjects which resulted in his drop out.

DECISION

5.3       The Committee did not approve of the request and decided that the fee paid by Mr. Khurram Khalid for the MBA 4th semester may be refunded to him.

ACTION

5.4       The Registrar will convey the decision to Mr. Khalid Latif, father of Mr. Khurram Khalid.

5.5       The Director Finance will arrange refund of the fee deposited by Mr. Khurram Khalid for the MBA 4th semester.

Item No. 6      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)         Note from Director Finance on IBA staff not using IBA designated bank accounts.

6.1       The Director Finance in his note of February 17, 2009 stated that some staff members do not get salary in their bank account on time. They are from those employees who are not maintaining their bank accounts with IBA designated banks.  Two such cases were reported in the month of February where the employees received their salary very late and in another case the pay order was lost in transit from our bank (UBL) to another bank.  He said that IBA is operating two banks (1) United Bank Limited and (2) National Bank of Pakistan for the disbursement of salary, remuneration and other allowances.

6.2              The Director Finance suggested that to avoid such problems and the hassle faced both by the employees and the Finance Department, the IBA should have a policy whereby all employees shall be bound to operate accounts with IBA designated banks for the purpose of salary and allowances.

6.3              The Director did not agree to the suggestion of the Director Finance that the employees should be forced to operate accounts with IBA designated banks.  He advised the Director Finance to ask the UBL to do their marketing and convince people to have their accounts with their banks. For this purpose they may be allowed to set up their booth.

DECISION

6.4              The Committee agreed to the observations of the Dean and Director.

b)   Clarification on Internal Audit Functions       

6.5              The Director Finance sought clarification on the internal audit functions specially with regard to payments.

6.6              The Director clarified that under the IBA Act it is mentioned that no expenditure shall be made from the funds unless a bill for its payment has been audited by the Audit Officer. He said that he has talked to the Head of Internal Audit and that the existing practice of audit before the payment is released will continue.  

c)      Promotion of Mr. Imran Batada, Sr. Manager MIS

6.7              Dr. Arshad Siddiqi raised the issue of promotion and fixation of market based salary of Mr. Imran Batada, Sr. Manager MIS.

6.8              The Director said that in view of ERP and other enhanced responsibilities we are up-grading the position of Senior MIS Manager.  For this he said that Mr. Imran Batada along with other internal candidates may be considered against an internal job vacancy. He advised the Director H.R. to circulate an ad internally for this position so that the recommended candidate may be presented before the Selection Board.  The Director I.T. will provide job specifications for this position.

ACTION

6.9              The Director I.T. will provide job specifications for the position of Sr. Manager MIS with enhanced responsibilities to the Director H.R. for circulation.

d)     Delay in submission of examination results.

6.10          The Controller of Examinations stated that due to non-submission of results on time by a few faculty members, the compilation of results / GPA calculation is also delayed.

DECISION

6.11          The Director said that Associate Deans will ensure that faculty members submit their results on time.  The Associate Deans will be assisted by Program Coordinators who will monitor the whole system.

6.12          The Director advised the Director H.R. to include an undertaking in the appointment letter for faculty position that the appointee would submit exam results on time with a sense of responsibility.

There being no other issues, the meeting terminated with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
