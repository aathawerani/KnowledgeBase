MINUTES OF THE 64TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON SEPTEMBER 7, 2009

The 64th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, September 7, 2009 at 9:30 a.m. at the IBA, Main Campus. The following were present:

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                              Director
  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                        Professor
  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                              Chairman CS & MIS
  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar
  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                Controller of Examinations
  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                     Director Finance
  7. Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                    Director Projects
  8. Dr. Arshad B. Siddiqi                          Director I.T.
  9. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                          Director CED
  10. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director H.R.

The following items were discussed: 

Item No. 1      To discuss email from Director on Maintenance of IBA Portal and website.  

1.1     The Director referring to his email on maintenance of IBA Portal and website said that it was his 7th email regarding maintenance of IBA Portal and website.  He said that there should be somebody to take care of the portal and the website. He pointed out that the list of faculty on the IBA portal is up-dated but on the website it is not. There is no list of full time faculty on the IBA website. Details regarding students’ society are different on the website from what is on the portal. He said that on one side we are going towards excellence but on the other our face to outside work is showing completely different picture. He said that we have hired very high calibre people at a very high salary so that the responsibilities assigned to persons are properly discharged. He further observed that we are telling everybody that we have improved our faculty and brought good faculty in last one year but it is not shown in our website. He wanted to know as to how this problem can be overcome. He said that we should develop a system whereby every department provides updated, accurate and validated information to the Web Administrator. The Web Administrator will be responsible to up-date the information within 48 hours. Mr. Imran Batada’s task is to ensure that the information provided by the concerned offices are updated and placed on the portal/website by the Web Administrator. The frequency and format of the contents to be decided by the concerned department and the Web Administrator. He said that validation and up-dating of faculty information is H.R’s responsibility. Providing information relating to scholarships will be the responsibility of the Registrar and the academic matters will be the responsibility of Associate Deans. 

1.2     Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that we do not have HR system. He suggested that until ERP is installed, Mr. Imran Batada should be asked to develop a small HR system  

DECISION 

1.3     The Committee decided that in light of the observations by the Dean & Director, the Director ICT would prepare a Standard Operating Procedure for placing and up-dating information on the IBA portal as well as IBA website. 

1.4      The Web Administrator will be responsible to up-date the information within 48 hours. Mr. Imran Batada’s task is to ensure that the information provided by the concerned offices are updated and placed on the portal/website. 

1.5      Mr. Imran Batada to develop a small HR System until ERP is installed.

ACTION 

1.6      The Director ICT would prepare a SOP for placing and up-dating information on the IBA Portal as well as IBA website. 

1.7      The Director ICT would ask Mr. Imran Batada to develop a small HR system.

Item No. 2      To consider and approve the plan of temporary offices to be constructed on the first floor of the Multipurpose Building. 

2.1     The Director Projects presented the plan of temporary offices proposed to be constructed on the first floor of the Multipurpose Building. He said that according to the plan 18 rooms can be built with a total seating capacity of 25 persons. He said that persons in the second floor of the Admin Block can be shifted to the first floor of the Multipurpose building when renovation work of the Admin Block is started. 

2.2     The Registrar said that although the proposal of constructing 18 rooms on the first floor of multipurpose building looks good however, it would require huge amount.  He suggested that we should have open seating rather than cubicles for everybody.  He proposed that except two or three rooms, the rest should be free seating and it should be placed on the hall.

2.3     The Director said that the faculty would not like that because they want privacy. He said that he was in favour of moveable partition.  

2.4      The Director Projects suggested that the offices of his Department as well as those of Mr. Shamim’s department which consist of 12 rooms and are very well furnished can be shifted to the hall in the first floor of multipurpose building.  

2.5     The Director liked the proposal of the Director Projects but wanted to know if the Director Projects would be able to accommodate 32 faculty and other members to the ground floor who would be moved during renovation of the first floor of the Admin. Block. 

DECISION

2.6     The Director Projects would issue tenders for the construction of first floor of the multipurpose building and for the renovation of the whole Admin Block and follow an open floor configuration without internal partitions.  

2.7      The Committee decided the sequencing of evacuation and renovation of the first, second and third floors of the Admin Block (Main Campus). According to the sequence the offices of Testing Services and Day Program (Mr. Manoj’s Office) at the 3rd floor along with the offices of the Project Departments on ground floor would be shifted to the 1st floor of the multipurpose building when the first floor of the multipurpose building is complete. Faculty members and others on the first floor of the Admin Block would be shifted to the rooms on the ground floor to be vacated by the Projects Departments. After the renovation of the first floor is complete, they would be moved back to the first floor. The second floor which accommodates the offices of Admin and Finance would be renovated at the end by moving these offices to the ground floor. 

Item No. 3     To consider the application from Mr. Lutuf Hussain Minhas, Assistant Librarian for two months paid leave on medical ground.  

3.1     Mr. Lutuf Hussain Minhas in his application of September 2, 2009 stated that he had to undergo open heart surgery at Liaquat National Hospital on August 13, 2009 and for some reasons he underwent re-surgery within 25 hours of first operation.  He stated that the doctors have advised him complete bed rest for two to three months. However, he mentioned that as he has no leave in balance therefore, the leaves he avails will be treated as leave without pay which he cannot afford as he has no other source of income to support his family.  He requested the Committee to grant him two months paid leave with effect from August 5, 2009. 

DECISION 

3.2      The Committee allowed two months leave with pay w.e.f. August 5, 2009 to Mr. Lutuf Hussain Minhas on humanitarian ground. The Committee further decided that the two months’ salary would be paid from the Welfare Fund.

ACTION

3.3     The Director Finance would pay two months’ salary to Mr. Lutuf Hussain Minhas from the Welfare Fund. 

Item No. 4      To consider and approve revised policy on Medical Facilities.           

4.1      Mr. Hussain Kazani, Manager H.R. presented the revised policy on medical facilities for IBA faculty and staff.  He proposed the following amendments in the proposed policy: 

     ·        Facility in non-panel hospitals as per the agreed rates of LNH be extended
·         Out-station dependents should also be covered.
·         In case of non-availability of facilities at LNH, employees can avail the same in other hospitals (panel or non-panel) at actual cost by informing H.R.
·         Deduction schedule of the amount paid over and above the LNH approved rate. 

4.2       The Manager H.R. also presented the format of the proposed Health Card, changes in the procedure, employees’ responsibilities, room entitlement and exclusions of facilities.

4.3      It was suggested by a member that instead of the proposed Health Card, Smart Card in which all information of employees is stored should be introduced.  

4.4      It was also suggested that a list of panel hospitals should be included in the Health Card.  

4.5      The Director Projects wanted clarification on the benchmark of LNH rate as he said that in his appointment letter he has been allowed Aga Khan University Hospital also. To this the Director clarified that such cases would be dealt with in accordance with the terms and conditions mentioned in their appointment letter.   

DECISION

4.6     The Committee commended the Director HR and the Manager H.R. for presenting comprehensive policy on medical facilities, endorsed the proposed amendments in the policy and recommended to the A&FC for approval with the following observations: 

a)            Rates from NMC, South City, Indus, and Tabba hospitals should be obtained and if the rates are reasonable, then they should be included in the panel.

b)            List of facilities available at LNH be placed on the portal as well as on the staff handbook. 

ACTION

4.7     The Director H.R. would arrange to obtain rates from NMC, South City, Indus, and Tabba hospitals and if the rates are reasonable, these hospitals would be included in the panel.

4.8              The Director H.R. would present the revised medical facilities policy to the A&FC for approval.

Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a)      Requirement of Performance Bond

5.1     The Director said that he had asked the Head of Internal Audit to compare the bidding documents for IT equipments with the PPRA and find out what is missing in the documents. He informed the Committee that the Head of Internal Audit has suggested that 10% Performance Bond should be included in the bidding documents.  

5.2     The Director ICT requested that in view of the facts that the dollar exchange rate has gone up, a substantial discount is available, and the payment schedule is spread over three years, the requirement of 10% performance bond should be waived.  

5.3      The Director said that he discussed this issue with the Head of Internal Audit and told him that we already have Retention Money and in case of non-performance we will be able to deduct the money. The amount of retention money will be higher than the amount of performance bond to which he said that the Head of Internal Audit agreed. He further said that the agreement was vetted and approved by the IBA’s Legal Advisor as well as by the lawyers of the vendors. He said that we have a discount which will not be available if we go for performance bond. Because of these reasons he said that he is of the view that it is not reasonable to ask for a Performance Bond. But, the Director said that, it has to be endorsed by the Executive Committee.

DECISION 

5.4     The Committee waived the requirement of Performance Bond as there would be enough retention money in lieu of Performance Bond. However, this should be reported to the A&FC. 

ACTION

5.5      The Director ICT would report the decision of the Executive Committee to the A&FC. 

b)     ACs for rooms in the multipurpose building.

5.6      The Registrar reported that faculty members taking classes in the multipurpose building are requesting for ACs in the room as there is no window in the class room.  Similarly there is a need to arrange a BBA class on the ground floor as in the absence of a ramp it is very difficult for the handicapped students to attend their classes on the first floor. 

5.7     The Director Projects stated that the ramp will be ready by April 2010. He suggested that in original plan, faculty lounge is on the ground floor and until the ramp is ready, faculty lounge may be used for classroom.

DECISION 

5.8      The Committee agreed to the suggestion of the Director Projects to hold a BBA class in the faculty lounge.

5.9      The Committee did not approve the request for installation of ACs in the classroom of multipurpose building.  

c)      Space for Storage of IT Equipments. 

5.10   The Director ICT requested the Director Projects to arrange temporary space for storage of IT equipments which would be coming in. 

DECISION 

5.11   The Committee requested the Director Projects, the Registrar, and the Director ICT to find some solutions for the temporary storage of IT equipments. 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
