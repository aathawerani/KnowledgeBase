Item No. 3      Progress Report on Data Centre Project.

 

3.1       The Director ICT reported that the CD of the Construction Drawings for Data Centre of City Campus and Main Campus submitted by ICON on September 8, 2011 was issued to M/s. H.K Shah for their scrutiny and addendum.  As it is in PDF format M/s. H.K. Shah have asked for the CAD version) so that any changes required in the design can be incorporated) but it is still pending. 

 

3.2       The Director ICT further reported that the information provided to M/s. H.K. Shah by other related parties is incomplete and the Electrical Connectivity of City Campus Data Centre with the Computer electrical sub-station with Gen Set has not yet been finalized, the related activities will be moved forward according to the following revised schedule:

 

o   Approval of City Centre Data Centre Design by       September 20, 2011

o   Addendum Issuance at City Campus Data Centre    September 24, 2011

o   Tentative Bidders meeting                                  September 26, 2011

o   City Centre bid closing date                                October 03, 2006

 

3.3       The Director ICT informed the Committee that Mr. Mujtaba, Project Manager of M/s. H.K. Shah has taken over the project.  He proposed that Mr. Mujtaba be allowed to present up-date on the Data Centre to the EC every Monday. 

 

3.4       The Committee noted that earlier the City Centre bid closing date was September 20, 2011 which was extended to September 30, 2011 and now it has been proposed as October 03, 2011.  The Committee asked the Director ICT not to extend the date and ask M/s. H.K. Shah to complete the process on the earlier communicated date. The Committee also did not agree to the proposal for inviting Mr. Mujtaba to up-date the EC and requested the Director ICT that he should continue to give up-date to the EC on the Data Centre.

3.5       The Director ICT reported that he has not agreed on the proposed extended dates and has asked M/s. H.K. Shah to complete the work on the agreed date.  He further said that penalty may be imposed if the work is not done on agreed date. He clarified that the earlier dates were given by the ICT Department but after review M/s. H.K. Shah, for the first time, have requested for extension of dates.

 

3.6       The Committee also took into consideration an email of September 19, 2011 on the subject received from the Dean & Director and copied to the EC. Purport of the said email is as under::

 

“ Director is unhappy and frustrated  with the passive approach on this task and desires the Director ICT to play, in his capacity as Task Manager, a more pro-active role centered on better coordination with every one, anticipation of the bottlenecks and taking of action through managerial interventions to rectify matters before the deadlines are reached. Getting things done despite others' recalcitrance and foot dragging is what the manager's task is. A bureaucrat thinks that by going through the motions, writing emails, giving directives to subordinates and others, waiting for others' inputs to reach by the deadline he has done his duty. A manager is one who ensures that every one's inputs into the process arrive on time and if there are problems resolve them before hand. He is quite justified that in case others do not cooperate to escalate the problem to the next in command so it becomes his responsibility to get it done. If he also fails then we conclude that the matter could not be resolved for reasons that were beyond our control. This is the last time that the extension in time has been given.”

 

DECISION

 

3.7       The Committee asked the Director ICT to prevail upon M/s. H.K. Shah to complete the work on the date conveyed to them earlier.  However, if it is not possible then the proposed extension may be allowed with the condition that if the work is not completed on the agreed date then they would be imposed a penalty to be decided by the EC.

 

 

Item No. 4      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To consider and approve IBA Fixed Assets Policy

 

4.1       The Director Finance while presenting the draft IBA Fixed Assets Policy, said that the purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for capitalizing, depreciating, transferring, disposing, and maintaining control of capital expenditure or fixed assets at IBA.  The following were the main contents of the Fixed Assets Policy:

 

o   Scope

o   Fixed Asset Definition and Guidelines

o   Fixed Asset Classification

o   Intangible Assets and Amortization

o   Asset under Construction

o   Acquisition and Capitalization Policy

o   Fixed Asset Recognition and Measurement

o   Minimum Monetary Limits For Capitalization

o   Other Factors for Capitalization

o   Tagging of Fixed Assets

o   Donation and Government Grants

o   Depreciation Policy

o   Impairment of Fixed Assets

o   Revaluation of Fixed Assets

o   Transfer of Fixed Assets

o   Disposal & Sale of Fixed Assets

o   Financial and Physical Control

o   Fixed Assets Records

o   Insurance of Fixed Assets

           

4.2       The Director Finance said that earlier, managers’ responsibility was not fixed for the fixed assets they were provided with but now they are owners and responsible for all the items provided to them.

 

4.3       The Director Projects pointed out that there is no mention of air-conditioning plant and equipments. 

 

4.4       The Director Finance reported that IBA insurance covers all Fixed Assets of IBA which includes air-conditioning plant and equipments as well.  He said that they have the list of assets with complete description and amount.

 

4.5       Dr. Muhammad Nishat suggested to include all those items which are to be insured.

 

4.6       The Director Finance replied that they have got the fixed assets insured against fire and hazards. He further said that “Equipments”, and “Office Equipments” have been separately mentioned in the policy and they cover plants and equipments also.   He said that the minimum financial limit for capitalization of an item as fixed asset is Rs.3000/- and above for furniture & fixture and Sports equipments.

 

4.7       The Director Projects said that usually insurance for plant and equipments covers the total replacement cost and not just the depreciated value as is done in case of vehicles.

 

4.8       The Director Finance reported that they get the insurance coverage under “Fire and Hazards” on the basis of the valuation of the assets (buildings and fixtures) provided by the Projects Department each year and these are insured for the sum communicated to them and the insured value is claimable.

 

DECISION

 

4.9       The Committee recommended to the A&FC for approval the Fixed Assets Policy as presented by the Director Finance.

 

 

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
