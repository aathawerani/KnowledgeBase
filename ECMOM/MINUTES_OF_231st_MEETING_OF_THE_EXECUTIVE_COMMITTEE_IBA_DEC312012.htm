MINUTES OF 231ST MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 31, 2012


The 231st meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, December 31, 2012 at 9:30 a.m. at Breakout Room, Aman-CED Building, IBA Main Campus. The following were present:

1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                                Dean & Director
2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                         Associate Dean, FBA
3. Dr.Sayeed Ghani                                Associate Dean, FCS
4. Capt. (R) Ahmed Zaheer, PN                Registrar
5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                                 Controller of Examinations
6. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                   Director Projects
7. Mr. Zafar A. Siddiqui                           Director CED
8. Dr. Arshad Siddiqi                               Director ICT

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects, and Mr. Fahad Jawed, Manager Finance attended the meeting on special invitation.

The following items were discussed:


Item No. To consider and approve the construction of Spiral Stair Case at the Administration Building at City Campus.

1.1 The Director Projects said that while main stair case on the southern side of the Academic Block, City Campus, was being removed, it was proposed that a spiral stair case may be constructed to provide the fire escape as well as the access to the class rooms and video conference room, the ‘Gents Bath Room’ on the ground floor. He said that the Architects M/s. ICON have designed spiral stair case. He presented the drawing along with the engineers’ estimate. He said that the Architects did not include the installation of the rubberized vinyl sheets to cover the treads of the staircase which are very essential for the safety of the users. As such approximate amount of Rs. 42,000/- has been added to the estimate.

1.2 The Director Projects requested the Executive Committee to approve the estimate of Rs.160,000/- so that the tenders for the spiral stair case may be invited along with the tenders of the store rooms and Workshop for which the designs and estimates have already been approved. He said that the Budget is available from the Administration Building budget, financial closure of which has not yet been completed due to a dispute with the contractors M/s ZCL.

1.3 The Dean & Director said that there is already a stair case which is being used and that is serving the purpose well. He said that in his opinion there is no need of another stair case. Mr. Mobin Khalili also agreed with the Dean & Director.

DECISION

1.4 The Committee did not approve the proposed stair case.


Item No. 2 To consider and approve the award of work to the lowest bidder for supply and installation of Sewage Treatment Plant.

2.1 The Director Projects said that this item was presented in the last EC meeting where it was decided that the Consultant and the Project Managers who have evaluated the bid documents should be invited to satisfy the EC that there would be no compromise on the quality of equipment and work if the contract is awarded to the lowest bidder. He said that representatives of NBCL, and NESPAK have been invited, as desired by the EC.

2.2 Mr. Shabih Haider of NBCL said that there are two components in STP; Civil, and MEP. For MEP, the engineer’s estimate was Rs.11 million whereas the lowest bidder quoted Rs.10.5 million which was very close to the engineer’s estimate. For the civil section, there was a variation and the lowest bidder quoted Rs.3.4 lesser than the engineer’s estimate. He said that when they made a detailed comparison they found major variations in two items; excavation and concrete for which they under-quoted. He said that one of the reasons may be that the other three bidders who quoted the high price are joint ventures involving greater overheads and profits. He said that the standard practice is that before award of contract they hold a meeting with the contractors and ensure that the contractor follows the drawings, and BoQ. He said that in this case also they would obtain written confirmation of compliance from the contractor.

2.3 To the observation that EC needed to ensure that IBA does not get substandard equipment, the representative of SMC said that they specified three or four companies’ equipment and the equipment mentioned by the lowest bidder is one of them which is acceptable. Moreover, they would get an undertaking from the Contractors that the contractors would comply with the specifications. The representative of NESPAK added that the submittals would be obtained prior to the letter of award.

DECISION

2.4 Having been satisfied with the clarifications given by the Architect and Project Manager, and based on the recommendations of the Technical Evaluation Committee and Central Purchase Committee, the Executive Committee recommended to the A&FC to approve the award of work to the lowest evaluated bidder M/s. RM Gulistan Engineers & Contractors at a cost of Rs.20, 866,109/- for the supply and installation of Sewage treatment plant at IBA Main Campus.

ACTION

2.5 The Director Projects would ensure that an undertaking from the Contractors that they would comply with the specifications and the submittals are obtained prior to issuance of the work order. He would present the recommendation of EC to the A&FC for approval.


Item No. 3 Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

a) Waiver of penalty imposed on Wateen.

3.1 The Director ICT said that the laying of optical fibre cable between various network segments in IBA, was executed by M/s Wateen. However, due to various other projects in progress at IBA, and related constraints, the optical fibre project couldn’t continue at the pace with which it was supposed to. Timelines couldn’t be met, resulting in significant delay in completion of the project. The delay was mainly due to the reasons, as outlined below:

1. As mentioned above, various construction projects are currently under progress at IBA, due to which ground level is uneven at various places. It was advised by Projects to ICT to lay the cable through aerial route, as it wasn’t possible at that time to lay the cable underground. This advice was particularly followed to avoid any delay to commission the network in the upcoming finished building projects. It was further assured by Projects Department that laying the optical fibre through underground channel shall be covered in the scope of external development work undertaken by Projects.

2. Another major factor which was the cause of significant delay in the said project was the permission from Karachi University for digging the trench through their premises for laying fibre optic from IBA Main Campus to hostels and staff colony. We continually followed up the matter with the KU. Meanwhile as the matter was discussed with the Director HEC Projects, he informed that he had already taken permission from Karachi University for digging of a 36 inch trench for IBA for various development works. The Director ICT said that when he asked the Director HEC Projects if they can use the trench for laying of optical fibre, the Director HEC Projects, after discussing the matter with Dean & Director, replied in the affirmative.

3.2 The Director ICT said that as stated above the overall delay in completion of the Optical Fibre project was totally due to the reasons beyond our control and there was no delay on part of M/s Wateen. The laying of optical fibre at City Campus was completed as per the plan and there was no delay of any sort in completion of that particular phase of the project. Furthermore, M/s Wateen have favoured IBA many times by putting extra efforts and doing work which was beyond their scope, for example, laying of Optical Fibre Cable in Aman CED Building, connecting Optical Fibre from new boys’ hostel to the old hostel etc. However, as per the contract a delay penalty of 2% is to be imposed upon Wateen, even though the delay was not caused by Wateen. He requested the EC to waive off the penalty imposed on M/s Wateen, for delay in completion of the project of laying of optical fibre cable between various network segments of IBA.

3.3 Mr. Shameem Ahmed clarified that Wateen was given a contract for laying optic fibre cable from Main Campus to hostels and staff colony but permission for digging could not be obtained from Karachi University. In the meantime, he said that they got permission from Karachi University for laying pipeline through the same way from where optic fibre cable was to be laid. He said that they used the same trench for both optic fibre and the pipeline.

3.4 The Dean & Director said that if the Director ICT is convinced that the delay was due to the circumstances beyond control and there was no fault of Wateen then he should have taken action accordingly. Mr. Fahad Jawed said that when the delay was anticipated it should have been taken during the execution of the project to the CPC for time extension because auditors raise objections if the project is not completed on the agreed time period.

DECISION

3.5 The Committee decided that it is not a case of waiver of penalty rather it is a case of extension of time through CPC.

ACTION

3.6 The Director ICT would request the CPC with full justification for extension of time in the completion of the project.


b) To consider leave request for Ms. Maha Khan, student of BBA-I

3.7 The Registrar said that he has received an email from the cousin of Ms. Maha Khan, a student of BBA-I in which she has reported that Ms. Maha Khan’s brother, Shahzeb Khan, was shot dead on 25th December, in a cross fire by unknown assailants. He was 20 years old and was on his way back from his eldest sister’s valima. This is an untimely, shocking death for the family and both his mother and sister Maha, are in shock as they saw the murder happen from their building balcony. It was reported that the family is under medical care and the doctors have recommended rest and a week-long dosage of anti-depressants.

3.8 The Cousin of Ms. Maha requested a week off for Ms. Maha from 29th December to 6th January 2013 with the hope that she is out of shock and able to appear in her exams from 7th January. During this time she will not be able to attend any classes or appear for presentation/quizzes. It was requested to allow her to appear in these tests/quizzes/presentation later.

3.9 The members were aggrieved to know that the deceased Shahzeb Khan was the brother of Ms. Maha Khan and prayed that Almighty Allah give the bereaved family ample courage and fortitude to bear this irreparable loss.

3.10 Mr. Mobin Khalili suggested that Ms. Maha may be convinced to appear in the final exam as there is a gap between the incident and the final exam. He said that since it would be the semester final exam, as per rule make-up is not allowed. He said that if she is not in a position to appear in the final, then ‘I’ may be given. However, he said that it would be difficult to re-arrange quizzes and presentation. The Registrar suggested that the average marks of the previous quizzes should be considered for the missed ones and the absences should be condoned.

DECISION

3.11 The Committee requested the Registrar to talk to Ms. Maha Khan and see if she can appear in the final exam, otherwise, ‘I’ would be given to her. The Committee further decided that Dr. Muhammad Nishat would talk to the concerned teachers and advise them to award the average marks for the quizzes / presentation she would miss.


c) Landscaping for prayer hall and other places:

3.12 The Director Projects said that to create greenery around the Prayer Hall he has received a proposal from the Architects M/s NBCL for the landscaping along with its estimate and its financing through the same donor. He said that earlier they had removed this item from the Prayer Hall BOQ, as it was to be included in the Master Plan, but then that was also removed from the Master Plan while doing Value Engineering Exercises. The estimated amount for the landscaping is Rs.1.7 million, which can be reduced to Rs.1.5 million. He said that the donor has agreed to further donate for the landscape based on two points:

1. IBA acknowledges them for this further contribution.
2. IBA commits to maintain the landscaped area in years to come.

3.13 The Director Projects said that if the Committee agrees to the two points he would ask the donor to directly pay to Mrs. Anjum Pervaiz, so that the work could be done expeditiously.

3.14 The Dean & Director asked Mr. Fahad Jawed to show the increased amount of donation for Prayer Hall by the donor. Mr. Fahad Jawed said that a letter committing the additional Rs.1.5 million would be required from the donor.

3.15 The Dean & Director said that landscaping around the prayer hall is part of the project; landscaping elsewhere will be part of the external development project. The Director Projects informed that the external development work at Main Campus will start from 1st of January 2013 and that of City Campus will start in March 2013. The Dean & Director suggested that the sequence should be such as the parking lot of CED be developed first and then the ring road connecting CED so that by that time all the heavy traffic movement will be over. The Dean & Director asked the Registrar to execute, in consultation with the Director Projects, the landscaping plan prepared by the Architects. He also advised the Registrar to identify green patches where grass is to be laid and employ the IBA staff to work on these patches.

DECISION

3.16 The Committee decided to acknowledge for the additional amount to be paid by the donor directly to Mrs. Anjum Pervaiz for the landscaping around the Prayer Hall and to maintain the landscaped area in the years to come.

ACTION

3.17 The Director Projects would obtain acceptance letter from the donor for the additional amount. On receipt of the acceptance letter, Mr. Fahad Jawed would increase the amount of donation by the additional amount.

3.18 The Registrar, in consultation with the Director Projects, would execute the landscaping plan prepared by the Architects and identify green patches where grass is to be laid and employ the IBA staff to work on these patches.

3.19 The Director Projects would develop the parking lot of CED first and then the ring road connecting CED.

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
