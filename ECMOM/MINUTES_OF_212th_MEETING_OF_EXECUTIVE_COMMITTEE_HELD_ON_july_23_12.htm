Item No. 2      To consider requirements of stores and technical workshops at City Campus.

 

2.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there is no proper store and electrical workshop at City Campus. The makeshift room where Mr. Jawad and his staff were sitting has been demolished. He said that he needs a permanent room to store examination scripts.  To find a suitable space for the store and technical workshop, he said that he had held a meeting with the Director Projects and his team which was also attended by Mr. Jawad, and Mr. Rizwan Rizvi.  He said that they identified the area in front of Academic Block where photo copy shop and canteen are located for the construction of electrical workshop and other stores (general maintenance and Admin Stores) with little re-modelling so that the same may be matched with the existing photo stat shop and canteen. However, he said that in view of the urgency to vacate the existing electrical store near old KESC Sub-station it was decided that a temporary store may be constructed at left hand side of extended portion of FCS block which will later on be re-constructed properly as the main store of FCS Building. He said that for the Examination Store measuring 20’ X 15’, they have identified the roof top of the Academic Block where iron staircase is already there to reach the roof top.  He said that the Projects Department was requested to present the drawings and estimated costs of the proposed workshops and stores which should be simple, functional and less costly.

 

2.2       The Registrar proposed that if they can wait for a year or so, then there would be plenty of space available. Mr. Mobin Khalili said that there are many faculty members on queue who are to be accommodated in the offices to be vacated.

 

2.3       The Dean & Director said that at the planning stage he had suggested to keep ample space for store in Aman Tower so that at one place all the store requirements of City Campus could be kept. He said that the space identified for technical workshop is fine but constructing a store on the roof top may open up a Pandora box.  

 

2.4       The Director Projects clarified that initially provision for store was made at the Aman Tower but when they revised the plan of Aman Tower, store was removed due to shortage of space and now there is no store at Aman Tower.  He said that there is no problem in making store at roof top because aesthetically it would not look awkward and since Academic Block is Ground plus one, so it would be easy to move around.  He further said that at Aman Tower the construction cost is very high so it would not be wise to use this building for this purpose.  He said that he would use prefab roofs for the store.  He presented the drawings of the electrical and carpenter workshops and store for examination scripts. The total estimated cost for the store and technical workshops would be around Rs.1.5 million.

2.5       The Dean & Director asked the Director Projects to complete the construction work of the store and technical workshops at Rs.1.0 million.

 

DECISION

 

2.6       The Committee approved the locations and the drawings for the technical workshops and store.

  

ACTION

 

2.7       The Director Projects would get the technical workshops and store constructed at the approved locations at an estimated cost of Rs.1.0 million.

 

 

Item No. 3      Presentation by Ms. Rahila Narejo on IRC Trainings.

 

3.1       Ms. Rahila Narejo, of Narejo Human Resources presented highlights of the progress on the International Resource Centre (IRC) Project till July 23, 2012 that was sponsored by British Council. Briefing the EC on IRC Purpose, she said that the purpose of IBA’s International Resource Centre (IRC) is to open our doors to the world and expand the scope of our academic activities beyond our geographical borders. The IRC aims to provide students with both an understanding of our local, diverse community, as well as the tools to become active leaders and responsible members of the increasingly interconnected, global society. The IRC also seeks to develop faculty expertise supporting its internationalization efforts on and off campus. These aims are realized through curricular and co-curricular offerings; interaction with students and faculty from a broad range of cultures; and opportunities to travel, study and perform research abroad. The IRC serves to develop and promote international academic excellence with regards to:

 

• International Research Collaboration and Commercialization

• Transnational Education

• Student, Faculty, and Staff Exchange and Linkages

• International Student Recruitment

• Entrepreneurship Linkages

 

3.2       About the IRC Strategy she said that in order to fully realize IBA’s internationalization purpose and vision, we must:

• increase our international population through visiting and full-time students;

• determine how best to incorporate more global perspectives into our curriculum;

• increase the diversity of our faculty;

• increase opportunities for students and faculty to travel abroad for purposes of study,

  research and cultural awareness;

• develop funding sources for international programming on campus and abroad;

• increase our presence and name recognition globally;

• increase co-curricular offerings with an international focus, including internships,            

  volunteer opportunities, lectures, and more;

• support development of increased international competency for faculty and staff;

• focus on specific regional opportunities that advance IBA’s internationalization vision.

 

3.3       She also covered in her presentation the draft IRC strategic intent policy, Responsibilities assigned for each segment, Training content overview, 6-month Action Plan, and next steps.

He asked Mr. Lashari to work with the Registrar on how to tap this market so that students from Middle East can come here. Item No. 5      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      Request to review the EC decision of July 16, 2012 on re-take of Midterm Exam.

 

5.1       Mr. Mobin Khalili requested the EC to review its decision under Item No.6 of the 211th meeting held on July 16, 2012.  He said that the decision of the Academic Board quoted by Mr. Saleem Umer is regarding the final exams only.  He said that the EMBA students by and large have a tendency of showing defiance to rules & regulations and like to violate it whenever possible. Attention of all concerned has been drawn to this tendency so many times but no corrective measures have been taken so far. Policy regarding cell phones is repeatedly notified and is also announced by the invigilators before the start of exams, despite that these students kept hiding their cell phones in their pockets.  He said that he cannot second Mr. Saleem Umer’s request that these students should be ‘forgiven”; it was not an unintentional mistake but they deliberately and intentionally violated the rule.  He disagreed with the decision because the approved policy does not allow make-up exam under these circumstances.

 

5.2       The Dean & Director said that if it is not in the policy to give ‘I’ in such cases for mid-term exam, then the policy can be revised by the Academic Board to include mid-term exam also.  He suggested that we should fix cell phone jammers and whenever there is exam at any campus, mobile phone jammers should be activated within the radius of the examination place.  He said that from next mid-term exam, jammers should be activated around the whole Academic Block and FCS block at City Campus and the whole Academic Block at Main Campus during the exam.

 

5.3       Mr. Mobin Khalili said that SMS or phone calls can be jammed but if the material has been saved in the mobile phone that can be accessed. The Dean & Director said that the students have to deposit their cell phones.  Body search can also be done for the material and mobile phone.

 

5.4       Mr. M. Saleem Umer said that it is not correct that the students violated the rules and have the tendency of violating the rules.  The students come direct from their offices and in their hurry they forget to deposit the cell phones. He said that there was a student who had not even written the question number when the cell phone, on announcement, was handed over by him.

 

5.5       The Dean & Director said that the EMBA program students complain that they are treated like a children not as mature person and equated with BBA students.  He said that those who handed over their phones on second announcement have not violated the rule. However, if any of them misbehaved with the Examination staff then the rule will apply.

 

DECISION

 

5.6       The Committee decided that a revised policy allowing ‘I’ on violation of any examination rule / carrying cell phone inside the examination hall in mid-term exam also be presented to the Academic Board for approval.

 

5.7       The Committee also decided that a mobile phone jammer be fixed around the whole Academic Block and FCS block at City Campus and the whole Academic Block at Main Campus and activated during the exams.

 

ACTION

 

5.8       Mr. M. Saleem Umer would propose a revision in the policy of Cancellation of Exams allowing Incomplete ‘I’ on violation of any examination rule / carrying cell phone inside the examination hall in mid-term exam also and present it to the Academic Board for approval.  

 

5.9       The Director ICT would look into the possibility of fixing mobile phone jammer around the whole Academic Block and FCS block at City Campus and the whole Academic Block at Main Campus which could be activated during the exams.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
