MINUTES OF THE 100TH MEETING OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE OF IBA HELD ON MONDAY, MAY 10, 2010


The 100th meeting of the Executive Committee of IBA was held on Monday, May 10, 2010 at 9:30 a.m. at the Conference Room of IBA Main Campus. The following were present:
 

  1. Dr. Ishrat Husain                             Director

  2. Dr. Muhammad Nishat                       Associate Dean

  3. Dr. Sayeed Ghani                             Associate Dean

  4. Capt. (Retd.) Ahmed Zaheer PN          Registrar

  5. Mr. Mobin Khalili                               Controller of Examinations

  6. Mr. Ali Taha                                    Director Finance

  7. Engr. Rehan-ul-Ambia Riaz                 Director Projects

  8. Dr. Arshad. Siddiqi                            Director ICT

  9. Ms. Ayesha A. Menai                         Director H.R.

                                                               

Mr. Shameem Ahmed, Director HEC Projects attended the meeting by special invitation.

 

The following items were discussed:

 

 

Item No. 1      Decisions Implementation Reports.

 

STATUS REPORT FROM MR. SHAMEEM AHMED

 

1.1       Sports Ground:   Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that he would attend to it as soon as he would put his major projects on rails.

 

The Director Projects reported that he had a meeting with Mr. Asad Ilyas and had discussed about the cricket and football grounds.

 

The Director asked Mr. Shameem Ahmed to give this project to Mr. Rehan-ul-Ambia. He advised Mr. Rehanul Ambia to select the architects who have developed UBL, NBL and Qaddafi Stadiums.

 

1.2              Tree Plantation:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that they are developing some plants in-house instead of buying from outside. He requested more time to start actual plantation.  He further said that by the time the plants become a bit bigger, water arrangement would also be made. 

 

The Registrar said that there are requirements of 10,000 gallons of water for AC plants. He said that the Consultants have not made any provision for storage of water.

 

The Director Projects said that they have tendered for constructing underground water tank exclusively for AC plant.      

 

Construction of New Boys’ Hostel:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the Consultant has requested some more time and has promised that the tender documents would be ready by May 15, 2010.

  

New Girls’ Hostel:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that a contract agreement with FNMH has been signed. The consultant is working on a new schematic design which will incorporate a wing for Sindh Talent Hunt Program. It will be ready and presented before the Technical Evaluation Committee in the third week of May 2010.

 

The Director advised the Director Projects to renovate VPR.  He also said that there is a need to construct a few houses because housing is one of the attractions for faculty.

 

New Water & Sewerage Lines at Main Campus:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that an approval from the University of Karachi is awaited. 

 

The Director said that he himself would talk to the Vice Chancellor.  He requested Mr. Shameem Ahmed to include this in the agenda for his meeting with the Vice Chancellor.

 

Girls’ Hostel Extension: Mr. Shameem reported that the foundation work has been completed. Masonry work has started. The slab will be cast in early June.

 

KESC: Mr. Shameem Ahmed stated that based on guidelines provided by KESC, the new sub-station will be designed by a pre-qualified contractor or a new consultant to be hired. An action has been taken in this regard. Payment of the KESC’s Rs.5 million cost estimate has been postponed until the end of June in order to save the interest they earn. Payment of Rs.2 million for extension of load has, however, been made. Actual physical construction work is likely to start at the end of June and completed in August 2010.

 

Visiting Faculty Hostel:  Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the schematic design submitted by the consultant was found to have many deficiencies. These have been pointed out to him. A final schematic design will be ready and approved by the end of May 2010.

 

HEC Projects:  Mr. Shameem reported that a revised PC-1 for the old (2004) has also been submitted. Both Revised PC-1s were considered by HEC in its pre-CDWP/DDWP meeting on April 17, 2010 in which he was also a participant. However, no approval has yet been given by HEC.

 

The Director advised Mr. Shameem Ahmed to invite Mr. Jalil of HEC for preparation of PC-1

 

DECISION

 

1.3              The up-date given by Mr. Shameem Ahmed was noted and approved along with the comments / advice recorded above.

 

1.4              The Committee decided that the Status Reports from other members would be discussed in the next EC meeting.

Item No. 3      To consider and approve Group Life Insurance Policy for Contractual employees.

 

3.1              The Director H.R. referring to the proposal she had presented to the Executive Committee on March 8, 2010 regarding Group Life Insurance for contractual employees, stated that after reviewing the revised quotes from both the short-listed companies i.e. EFU and State Life Insurance Corporation (SLIC), SLIC rates were found lowest. She also presented the comparative statement for the information of the EC.

 

3.2              The Director H.R stated that at present they are giving 50% premium to regular employees of BPS 1 – 15. In order to align the policy with regular employees she made the following suggestions:

 

·         Contractual employees equivalent to BPS 1 – 15 or equivalent job band may be given 50% coverage of premium for which the financial impact for IBA will be approximately Rs.50,000/- per annum.

 

·         Contractual employees equivalent to BPS 16 – 22 or equivalent job band may be provided coverage on the rates quoted by the SLIC. There will be no financial impact on IBA.

 

DECISION

 

3.3              The Committee agreed to the suggestions of the Director H.R. as recorded in 3.2 above.

 

ACTION

 

3.4              The Director H.R. would present the proposal of Group Life Insurance Policy for Contractual employees to the A&FC for approval.

 

 

Item No. 4      To consider the proposal for Catastrophic Diseases Coverage.

 

4.1              The Director H.R. referred to the proposal of Mr. Rais Ahmed Khan, Assistant Admin. Officer for including Radiotherapy in the agreement of panel hospitals and to allocate fund for dangerous diseases and the decision of Executive Committee requesting the Director H. R. to obtain proposals from EFU, SLIC and NJI for providing insurance coverage for catastrophic risks to IBA employees and their dependents. The Director H.R. stated that the above named insurance companies were contacted and they have given them the said proposals. She also presented a summary of the proposals received for coverage of catastrophic diseases. According to the summary, only NJI offered to provide this coverage to employees and spouses of the IBA upto the age of 60 + children. Parents are not covered in this policy.  The premium per insured would be Rs.200,000/- per annum.

 

DECISION

 

4.2              The Committee did not consider the proposal feasible and decided that cases of catastrophic diseases would be handled by case to case basis from welfare fund.

 

 

Item No. 5      Nine months financial results.

 

5.1              The Director Finance presented nine months’ financial results. He said that expenses on entertainment, overtime and fuel have very significantly increased which need to be controlled.

 

5.2              The Director asked the Director Finance to incorporate 54 scholarships under Sindh Endowment Fund. He suggested that the overtime should be controlled and department wise overtime be informed to him.

 

5.3              The Registrar said that because of three generators diesel cost has increased.

Item No. 7      Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

 

a)      To witness the functioning of surveillance cameras at the IBA Main Campus.

 

7.1              The Registrar said that in the aftermath of the terrorist act at International Islamic University, Islamabad, there was a meeting held in Governor’s house where it was instructed to install surveillance cameras at the campus. He said that they had two options; IP Option which was expensive and another was DBR which they have adopted. It has archival and retrieval facilities for 30 days. 

 

7.2              Major (R) Qamar Abbas showed the coverage of the surveillance cameras from different angles and of various locations.   

 

7.3              The Director observed that the resolution needed improvement. He said that the car numbers, person’s face, driver’s face, students entering into the library etc should be very clear and should give full identity. He also suggested to remove vibrations. He wanted to know as to why boys’ hostel has not been included.  He also advised the Security Coordinator, Major (R) Qamar Abbas to train other people how to operate the system.

 

7.4              The Registrar said that for boys’ hostel a separate arrangement will have to be made.

 

7.5              Mr. Mobin Khalili wanted to know if any back up camera is available in case any camera goes out of order.  Major ® Qamar Abbas replied that they have one spare camera for each campus.

 

DECISION

 

7.6              The Committee requested the Security Coordinator to show it again by incorporating the feedback given above.  

 

 

b)      Merit Scholarship

 

7.7              The Director Finance reported that out of 48 students who have been given merit scholarships, only 21 could maintain the required GPA of 3.5 which is a condition for the merit scholarship.

 

DECISION

 

7.8              The Director requested the Registrar to write to all 48 students that the scholarship has been given on the basis that their GPA will be 3.5 or more.  If they maintain their GPA of 3.5 they will continue to receive merit scholarship otherwise it will be suspended until they improve it.

 

c)      Outsourcing of help desk people.

 

7.9              The Director ICT said that he wants to outsource help desk people

 

7.10          The Director wanted to know the cost on outsourcing and saving on head counts.

 

DECISION

 

7.11          The Director ICT would provide details to the EC about the cost on outsourcing and saving on head count for consideration.

 

There being no other issues, the meeting was concluded with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
