Item No. 2                  To consider and approve retendering the Project “Construction of a new KESC sub-station and relocation of the four generators at the City Campus.

 

2.2       Mr. Shameem Ahmed reported that the contractor who was recommended by the Consultant and was approved by the EC for the construction of a new KESC sub-station and relocation of the four generators at the City Campus has backed out.  He said that they sought NESPAK’s advice who advised that under the circumstances IBA may consider Siemens who were the next lowest bidder.  Accordingly he said that they approached Siemens but they got an unacceptable and evasive reply from Siemens.  Despite that he said that they pursued the matter with Siemens further. But instead of clearly withdrawing the additional terms and conditions, Siemens insisted on introducing terms and conditions which were not a part of the original tender documents. The conditions which were over and above the tender documents were:

 

a)      The prices were valid for 30 days (whereas it has to be valid for 90 days).

b)      Prices of Cable and Copper related items will be valid for 7 days.

 

2.3       Mr. Shameem said that Mr. Sohail Wajahat of Siemens was contacted but since he is on the IBA BoG and due to conflict of interest, he did not interfere into this issue.

 

2.4       Mr. Shameem said that under the circumstances they seem to have no choice but to cancel all bids received and opened on December 4, 2010 and retender the project. He requested the Executive Committee to approve cancellation of bids and re-tendering for the work.

 

2.5       The Registrar suggested to separate the two jobs; building of KESC Sub-station, and shifting of generators. He said that Mr. Jawad can do the latter job. 

 

2.6       Mr. Shameem Ahmed said that KESC will only fix their cable and IBA will have to relocate the whole cable and synchronization of all generators through ATS.

 

DECISION

 

2.7       The Committee having no choice, allowed to cancel all bids received and opened on December 4, 2010 and to retender the Project.

 

ACTION

 

2.8       Mr. Shameem Ahmed would take action on the decision noted in 2.7 above.

 

 

Item No. 3      To consider and approve set up of a Networking & Communication Lab – FCS.

 

3.1       The Director ICT said that to meet the technology demands of new advancement in networking and communication, the Faculty of Computer Science Department is taking appropriate step towards industry based education and building a Networking and Communication Lab which will also incorporate Network Security, Voice and Routing / Switching needs of industry across all over the globe.  He said that ICT had budgeted Rs.5.2 million for a basic level lab focusing on initial level of Routing and Switching; the associate level of CCNA.  He said that it would have been enough to setup this lab by procuring academic designed bundle and considering Cisco discounts for predesigned equipment only.  However the concept of FCS for this lab has changed considerably thus the cost would be increased due to the following reasons:

 

·         Additional networking and communication equipment have been added which were not included in the basic academic bundles.

·         Level of the course has also been increased from initial to Expert.

·         Initial course was only for Routing and Switching; later on Security and Voice Equipment have also been added into the BoQ.

·         Some of the equipments included in original predesigned equipment package will become useless for IBA and should not be paid for that.

 

3.2       The Director ICT said that Cisco was offering around 75 % discount on their pre-designed initial level equipment for CCNA, however, since ICT wants to offer higher level courses this discount will not be applied on all equipments. Thus to establish the new FCS Networking and Communication Lab, he requested the EC to approve the following:

 

·         In principle approval for initiating the purchase of CISCO equipment for FCS Network and Communication Lab.

·         In principle approval for the BoQs for tendering as per PP&P.

 

3.3       The Director ICT pointed out that although the above requirements were Budgeted for the year 2010-11, however considering the requirement it may have to be altered through re-appropriation (as per TAP) which would be done later once the actual cost is received.

 

3.4       The Director said that the ICT department had assured that with Rs.2.2 million they would be able to get the CISCO Lab ready.  He said that he was not happy with the revised proposal but because it is for academic purposes, he would have to revisit this cost estimate.

 

3.5       Mr. Faisal Zia said that major changes are due to changed configuration of the equipment to be ordered. He said that on pre-designed bundle the 75% discount is still available.

 

3.6       Dr. Sayeed Ghani said that in IT Budget they have Rs.3 million so it would not require re-appropriation from any other head.

 

3.7       The Director said that he does not want to compromise on the quality but at the same time he would not allow such a change because, he said that, earlier Mr. Faisal Zia had said that it would be done for Rs.2.2 million and then he presented a revised proposal of Rs.5.2 million. 

 

DECISION

 

3.8       The Committee decided that Mr. Raza Abedi and Dr. Sayeed Ghani will have a meeting and see if it meets the original idea and report to the EC.
